Union Pacific (UNP) Q3 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 11:23AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, October 23, 2025 at 8:45 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Vincenzo Vena
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jennifer Hamann
  • Executive Vice President, Operations — Eric Gehringer
  • Executive Vice President, Marketing and Sales — Kenny Rocker

Takeaways

  • Adjusted EPS -- $3.08, up 12% due to core pricing gains and operational efficiencies.
  • Operating Revenue -- $6.2 billion, representing a 3% increase.
  • Freight Revenue -- $5.9 billion, increasing 3% with core pricing and favorable mix offsetting lower volumes and fuel surcharges.
  • Volume -- Slightly declined, reducing freight revenue by 25 basis points.
  • Fuel Surcharge Revenue -- $602 million, down $33 million, with lower fuel prices causing a 50 basis point impact on freight revenue.
  • Other Revenue -- $317 million, 2% lower as revenue declines from Metropolitan operations were partially offset by a favorable contract comparison.
  • Operating Expenses -- $3.7 billion, up 1% as decreased compensation and lower equipment costs offset higher purchased services and local taxes.
  • Workforce Productivity -- Increased 6%, reaching an all-time quarterly record.
  • Operating Ratio (Adjusted) -- 58.5%, a 180 basis point improvement.
  • Operating Income -- $2.5 billion, up 6%.
  • Net Income -- $1.8 billion, with reported EPS of $3.01.
  • Cash from Operations -- $7.1 billion, rising 6% or $381 million.
  • Long-term Debt Repayment -- $1 billion repaid during the period, lowering the adjusted debt-to-EBITDA ratio to 2.6x.
  • Dividend -- Third quarter marked the 19th consecutive annual dividend increase.
  • Bulk Segment Revenue -- Up 7%, driven by a 7% volume gain, with coal demand and increased export grain shipments cited as contributors.
  • Industrial Segment Revenue -- Increased 3% on matching volume and a 1% higher average revenue per carload; offset by lower fuel surcharges and business mix.
  • Premium Segment Revenue -- Declined 2% as volumes fell 5% despite a 3% increase in average revenue per car.
  • International Intermodal Volume -- Fell 17%, creating a challenging comparison.
  • Domestic Intermodal Segment -- Achieved record-breaking volumes, according to remarks.
  • Freight Car Velocity -- Improved 8% to 226 miles per day, with September exceeding 230 miles per day, both best-ever performances.
  • Terminal Dwell -- Set a record at just over 20 hours for the quarter; locomotive dwell reduced to a new record of 14.9 hours.
  • Train Length -- Increased 2% to just above 9,800 feet, a quarterly record.
  • Active Workforce -- Decreased 4% against flat volumes.
  • Fuel Consumption Rate -- Improved by 1% and set a best-ever quarterly record.
  • Merger Progress -- Merger application with the Surface Transportation Board (STB) expected by end of November or early December; over 400 customer letters of support have been received.
  • EPS Growth Outlook -- Management reiterated its 3-year CAGR target of high single to low double-digit growth.
  • Volume Guidance (Q4) -- Current run rate is down 6%, particularly affected by international intermodal comparisons.
  • Share Repurchases -- Paused as a result of the pending merger; cash flow prioritized for debt reduction.

Risks

  • Management cited that, "volumes are currently running down 6%" for the fourth quarter, with international intermodal facing "tough comparison against last year's strong growth," creating "a headwind to earnings and margin expansion compared to last year's record fourth quarter."
  • Jennifer Hamann discussed that, "productivity, as you know, also is challenged when you have volumes coming down," indicating cost leverage pressures if volume weakness persists.
  • Core pricing and mix benefits have been "tempered maybe a little bit on the coal side," and "tougher comparisons" are anticipated for coal contracts "move into the fourth quarter and into the first part of next year."

Summary

Union Pacific (UNP 0.15%) reported double-digit growth in adjusted earnings per share and generated record workforce productivity, operational efficiency, and service metrics. Management highlighted strong business mix and core pricing as drivers for revenue and margin gains, while also noting a significant debt reduction and robust cash flows that supported an ongoing strategy of operational excellence. The company affirmed its long-term EPS growth targets and detailed ongoing merger progress with the Surface Transportation Board, while also acknowledging meaningful operational headwinds for the upcoming quarter due to declining volumes and pause in share repurchases.

  • Record operational performance spanned multifactor productivity, fuel consumption per gross ton-mile, and reductions in terminal dwell, underscoring capacity for incremental service improvement if volume conditions improve.
  • Merger process with Norfolk Southern (NYSE:NSC) remains on track, with more than 400 customer support letters submitted and formal STB application targeted by December, suggesting broad stakeholder alignment excluding noted objections from certain industry groups.
  • Management distinguished the company's capacity to adapt resource buffers and cost structure to near-term macroeconomic or demand volatility, asserting flexibility to sustain service quality without compromising margin discipline.
  • Future shareholder returns may be deferred until post-merger debt targets are achieved, as leadership has prioritized balance sheet strength in current capital allocation strategy.
  • Cross-segment performance was mixed, with pronounced weakness in international intermodal contrasting with record-setting domestic results and sector-specific gains in bulk and industrial units, reinforcing the need to monitor changing end-market and commodity trends.

Industry glossary

  • Surface Transportation Board (STB): The federal agency responsible for regulating railroad mergers and overseeing rail service, rates, and competition in the United States.
  • Operating Ratio: A key railroad efficiency metric calculated as operating expenses divided by operating revenue; lower values reflect improved margin performance.
  • Intermodal: Freight transport that utilizes multiple modes, such as rail and truck, but retains freight in the same container or trailer throughout the journey; divided into domestic and international segments.
  • Terminal Dwell: The average time railcars spend stationary in a terminal or yard; lower dwell typically signals greater fluidity and network efficiency.
  • Gross Ton-Miles: The amount of freight transported (in tons) multiplied by miles moved; used to assess railroad productivity and fuel efficiency.
