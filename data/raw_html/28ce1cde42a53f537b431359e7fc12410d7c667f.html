Earnings Call Transcript

Huntsman (HUN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 18, 2026 at 11:33AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 18, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman, CEO and President — Peter R. Huntsman
  • Chief Financial Officer and Executive Vice President — Philip M. Lister
  • Vice President, Investor Relations and Corporate Development — Ivan Mathew Marcuse
  • Executive Vice President — Mike Harrison

TAKEAWAYS

  • Cost Savings -- Achieved a $100 million annualized run rate in cost reductions, driven by approximately 500 headcount cuts and the closure of seven facilities.
  • In-Year Savings Target -- Projecting $45 million of incremental cost savings for 2026, excluding inflation impacts.
  • EBITDA to Free Cash Flow Conversion -- Converted 45% of EBITDA to free cash flow, described as "a higher percentage than many in the industry."
  • Revolving Credit Facility -- Secured an $800 million revolver, extended securitization capacity to $300 million, and held over $400 million in cash at year end, totaling approximately $1 billion in liquidity.
  • Leverage Ratios -- Philip M. Lister stated, "I am not concerned at all in 2026 about the leverage ratios," citing more than $200 million of add-backs in the agreement’s EBITDA calculations.
  • Polyurethanes Segment Volume -- North America polyurethanes volumes grew slightly in Q4, with business wins offsetting expected seasonal declines.
  • Tariffs Impact -- Imports of Chinese-origin MDI into the U.S. decreased by about 80% following significant tariffs.
  • Cost Headwinds -- Rising natural gas costs generated a $10 million headwind in the polyurethanes business during the first quarter, not anticipated several weeks prior.
  • Cash Conversion Cycle -- Reduced by 10% in 2025, with a further reduction targeted in 2026.
  • Normal Turnaround Schedule -- Only standard, planned plant maintenance activities are expected for the year with no abnormal downtime anticipated.
  • Price Increase Announcements -- Notified customers of MDI price increases in North America and Europe to address higher benzene and natural gas costs.
  • AI Implementation -- Plans to "selectively [use] AI tools" to further reduce costs and enhance R&D capabilities where it is economically justifiable.

RISKS

  • Mike Harrison stated, "We have got rising natural gas costs in the first quarter that right now, as I sit here, represent a $10 million headwind that we were not anticipating a couple of weeks ago in our polyurethanes business."
  • European demand and profitability remain challenged due to "a very wide structural issue with the industry of small facilities," persistent "high energy costs," and uncertain policy actions as described by Mike Harrison.
  • Polyurethanes segment margins remain "under pressure" and depend on the successful implementation of announced price increases and the pace of volume recovery.
  • Mike Harrison highlighted, "Europe continues to struggle with high energy costs," constraining competitiveness relative to other regions.

SUMMARY

Huntsman (HUN +0.96%) closed 2025 with substantial progress on restructuring, eliminating 500 positions and shuttering seven plants to reach a $100 million annual cost savings milestone. Management confirmed successful liquidity measures, establishing an $800 million revolver and maintaining total available funding of $1 billion, while expressing confidence that leverage ratios are not a concern for 2026. The company led in converting EBITDA to free cash flow at a rate described as above industry peers and projects $45 million more in cost savings in the coming year, after excluding inflation. Tariffs on Chinese-origin MDI imports reduced U.S. inflows by about 80%, creating new regional supply-demand implications, while management indicated positive early signs in European and North American pricing though volume recovery remains weather and macro-driven. Huntsman intends to continue strategic modernization, including selective AI-driven initiatives and a readiness to pursue M&A, joint ventures, or asset sales supporting structural transformation.

  • Peter R. Huntsman mentioned, "we will be willing to engage with interested parties and push where there is an opportunity for value to be created," signaling potential pursuit of industry consolidation.
  • The company has "set expectations internally to generate enough cash to cover our dividend," underscoring disciplined capital allocation.
  • Management highlighted growing aerospace business, forecasting growth "slightly better than the production rate" for new aircraft, with significant exposure to wide-body planes which remain well below 2019 production rates.
  • Downstream demand and inventories across supply chains are described as "quite low," which may lead to potential shortages if demand rebounds sharply in 2026.
  • Philip M. Lister asserted, "any plans" to access equity markets to bolster the balance sheet, reflecting a preference for internal funding and strategic debt management.
  • Management views the Americas as the key opportunity for margin expansion in Advanced Materials, awaiting recovery in regional PMI and construction activity.
  • Recent capacity additions in North American MDI production are expected to be in the "low to mid single digits" percent, with the anticipated supply impact occurring gradually instead of all at once.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • MDI: Methylene diphenyl diisocyanate, a primary chemical used in the manufacture of polyurethane foams and other polymers.
  • LTM: Last twelve months; refers to a metric calculated over the most recent twelve-month period.
  • System House: A specialty blending facility that customizes polyurethane systems for customer-specific applications.
  • Debottlenecking: Process improvements or investments to increase production capacity of an existing plant, typically by removing throughput constraints.
