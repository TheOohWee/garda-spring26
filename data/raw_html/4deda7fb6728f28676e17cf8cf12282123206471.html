Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $435 million for the quarter, a decrease of 1.7% reported and 2.5% organic due to a strong prior year comparison.

  • Full year adjusted EPS -- $2.23, down from $2.56, affected by higher costs from compliance and supply issues; adjusted EBITDA margin of 19.4%, down 60 basis points.

  • Guidance — full year 2026 -- Revenue expected at $1.66 billion to $1.7 billion, 1.6%-4.1% reported growth and 0.8%-3.3% organic growth; adjusted EPS expected in the $2.30-$2.40 range, with a $0.32 tariff impact included and EBITDA margin improvement of approximately 40 basis points.
  • MediHoney -- No MediHoney revenue included in 2026 guidance; remediation continues into the year, timeline not accelerated.
  • Braintree and SurgiMend -- Braintree facility expected online by end of June; SurgiMend return to market anticipated in the fourth quarter, contingent on process validations and inventory build; full PMA approval for SurgiMend and DuraSorb would expand the biologic and synthetic portfolio for breast reconstruction.

  • Tariffs -- 2025 included $20 million in tariff expenses ($16 million attributed to IEEPA); 2026 guidance maintains prior tariff assumptions, excluding any recovery post-Supreme Court ruling, as Section 122 tariffs’ details remain uncertain.

Risks

  • Gross margin declined by 350 basis points in the quarter due to remediation, tariffs, and unfavorable product mix, with adjusted EPS pressured by these same factors, while adjusted EBITDA margin increased by 30 basis points.
  • "We do not have -- we haven't accounted for any revenues for MediHoney for this year in our numbers. We have been remediating that product. It's one of those products that the remediation has continued into 2026." — ongoing MediHoney remediation delays a revenue contributor.
  • Negative free cash flow of $5.4 million for the quarter driven by working capital and restructuring costs; free cash flow conversion was -8.5%.
  • Full year organic revenue growth was negative 0.7%, reflecting ongoing supply constraints and remediation efforts in the core business.

Summary

Integra LifeSciences (IART +0.71%) reported fourth quarter revenue of $435 million, with declines in both reported and organic growth attributed to difficult prior year comparisons and ongoing supply constraints. Management confirmed that 2026 guidance excludes any potential impact from new Section 122 tariffs, as timing and implementation details remain uncertain after the recent Supreme Court ruling. The company relaunched PriMatrix and Durepair ahead of schedule and expects the new Braintree facility to support the return of SurgiMend later in 2026, but MediHoney will not contribute revenue as remediation work continues.

  • Adjusted EBITDA margin for the fourth quarter improved 30 basis points, primarily due to cost management actions mitigating gross margin declines from tariffs and remediation costs.
  • Global neurosurgery revenues grew organically by 1.4%, with double-digit gains in select franchises such as CereLink and MAYFIELD Capital, and high single-digit growth internationally, especially in China and Canada.
  • The company maintained leverage within its 5x allowable maximum, closing the year at 4.5x, and anticipates approaching its 2.5x-3.5x target range by the end of 2026 through improved free cash flow and lower capital outlays.
  • Management's full year 2026 outlook expects operating cash flow to exceed $200 million, driven by reductions in project spending and working capital improvement, supporting deleveraging plans.

Industry glossary

  • EU MDR: European Union Medical Device Regulation, the regulatory framework governing medical device approval and compliance across EU member states, requiring substantial investment in process and documentation upgrades for conformity.
  • PMA: Premarket Approval, the FDA process of scientific and regulatory review to evaluate the safety and effectiveness of Class III medical devices.
  • IEEPA: International Emergency Economic Powers Act, a U.S. federal law authorizing the President to regulate commerce in response to declared national emergencies, under which prior tariffs were imposed.
