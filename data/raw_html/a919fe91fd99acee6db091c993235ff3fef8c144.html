  • HAMR and ePMR roadmap -- HAMR and next-generation ePMR drive qualifications have begun with separate hyperscale customers this month.
  • Purchase orders and agreements -- Firm purchase orders with top seven customers through calendar 2026; LTAs with three of top five customers extending to 2027 and 2028.
  • UltraSMR JBOD platform launch -- UltraSMR-enabled JBOD platforms announced, broadening UltraSMR adoption and providing higher storage density.

  • Dividend announcement -- Board approved quarterly cash dividend of $0.125 per share, payable March 18, 2026, to holders of record as of March 5, 2026.
  • HAMR qualification timeline -- HAMR qualification process was pulled forward, with progress already underway for at least two hyperscale customers.

Summary

Western Digital (WDC +1.64%) reported record exabyte shipments and solid profit expansion, capitalizing on strong cloud demand and increasing adoption of high-capacity, software-enhanced drives. Management established longer-term commercial certainty by executing firm orders and extended agreements with industry leaders through 2028. Aggressive cost discipline, elevated gross margins, and proactively accelerated technology roadmaps were highlighted as core factors driving current and projected financial outperformance.

  • The UltraSMR mix increase was described as accretive to margins due to both capacity uplift and software-based implementation.
  • Nearline drive mix shift was credited for both top-line and gross margin expansion, underpinned by multi-year LTAs and visible exabyte allocations.
  • Chief Financial Officer Kris Sennesael stated, "we are guiding to 47%, 48%, so 47.5% at the midpoint, which is up 740 basis points on a year-over-year basis."
  • Tiang Yew Tan indicated, "we're pretty much sold out for calendar '26," referencing firm purchase orders with major customers.
  • HAMR and next-generation ePMR drives are advancing in qualification, targeted for early deployment with key cloud service providers.
  • Strategic investments in internal laser IP and quantum collaboration signal ongoing commitment to innovation-led differentiation.
  • Shareholder returns accelerated, with $1.4 billion returned via buybacks and dividends since program launch.
  • The company expects fiscal Q3 revenue growth of approximately 40% year over year, with further gross margin improvement and stable operating leverage.

Industry glossary

  • ePMR (Energy-assisted Perpendicular Magnetic Recording): A magnetic recording technology enhancing hard drive data density and performance by improving write capabilities through energy assistance.
  • HAMR (Heat-Assisted Magnetic Recording): A next-generation hard disk technology using localized heating to enable higher areal densities than conventional magnetic recording.
  • UltraSMR (Ultra Shingled Magnetic Recording): A high-capacity hard drive format utilizing overlapping data tracks and proprietary software to maximize storage efficiency, exceeding standard SMR density.
  • LTA (Long-Term Agreement): Structured multi-year contract specifying volume and pricing terms between vendor and customer.
  • JBOD (Just a Bunch of Disks): A storage architecture combining multiple hard drives in a single enclosure without RAID redundancy, permitting scalable, high-density data storage.
