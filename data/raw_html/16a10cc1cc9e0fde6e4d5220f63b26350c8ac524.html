Earnings Call Transcript

Timken (TKR) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 4, 2026 at 12:15PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, Feb. 4, 2026 at 11:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Lucian Boldea
  • Chief Financial Officer — Michael Discenza
  • Director of Investor Relations — Neil Frohnapple

Takeaways

  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share -- $1.14 for the quarter, exceeding the high end of the company’s guidance range.
  • Total Revenue -- $1.11 billion, up 3.5% from the prior year, with organic sales growth of 1.3% and more than 2% contribution from foreign currency translation.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $178 million, flat with the previous year, with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 16% compared to 16.6% a year ago; margins would have been nearly flat year over year, excluding currency impacts.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $141 million for the quarter and $406 million for the full year, reflecting a $100 million increase from the prior year.
  • Debt Reduction -- Net debt was reduced by over $130 million in 2025, ending with a net debt to adjusted EBITDA ratio of two times.
  • Capital Return -- $36 million returned to shareholders in the quarter.
  • Engineered Bearings Segment Sales -- $714 million, up 0.9%, with organic sales down 1% (pricing up, volume down); currency effects added nearly 2%. Segment adjusted EBITDA was $115 million (16.1% margin), down from $122 million (17.2%) last year, due to unfavorable mix and incremental tariff costs.
  • Industrial Motion Segment Sales -- $397 million, up 8.4%, with 5.6% organic growth and 2.8% from currency translation. Segment margins increased, driven by higher volumes and pricing despite tariff and mix headwinds.
  • Regional Performance -- Americas sales were flat, Asia Pacific up 4% (with India and other areas offsetting China declines), EMEA up 4% led by Industrial Motion.
  • Pricing Actions -- Pricing contributed a $25 million top-line benefit for the quarter, adding over 2% to revenue, helping offset a $30 million year-over-year tariff headwind.
  • Cost Management -- Material and logistics costs declined year over year, especially within engineered bearings, and SG&A expenses fell due to cost-control efforts and lower bad debt accruals.
  • Backlog -- Backlog at year-end increased high single digits, with off-highway, general industrial, wind, and aerospace as primary contributors.
  • 2026 Organic Sales Outlook -- Midpoint guidance for 2% organic sales growth, underpinned by higher pricing and modest volume increases.
  • Total Revenue Outlook for 2026 -- Projected to rise 2%-4%; currency expected to contribute approximately 1%, mainly due to a weaker US dollar.
  • Adjusted EPS 2026 Guidance -- $5.50 to $6.00, an increase of 8% at the midpoint, with expected year-over-year earnings growth in every quarter and a rough 54%/46% split between first and second half, respectively.
  • 2026 Adjusted EBITDA Margin Target -- Implied high 17% range at the midpoint, up from 17.4% in 2025; guidance implies an incremental margin of about 30%.
  • 2026 Free Cash Flow Outlook -- Approximately $350 million targeted for the full year, or about 105% conversion on GAAP net income at the midpoint.
  • Introduction of 80/20 Portfolio Discipline -- Strategic initiative expanded across the operating enterprise, aiming to exit underperforming businesses, simplify operations, and drive future margin improvement, with implementation benefits expected over several quarters.
  • Leadership Appointments -- New Chief Technology Officer, Vice President of Marketing, and Regional President roles established to enhance innovation and commercial execution, aligning with growth strategies.
  • Auto OEM Pruning -- Customer negotiations for exits nearly complete; material revenue declines related to auto OEM business projected in 2027, with expected margin uplift in 2026 and 2027.
  • Industrial Automation Growth -- The company reports 20% linear motion business growth in the Americas off a small base, driven by automation trends.

Risks

  • Michael Discenza stated, "labor inflation will be a a headwind next year. Well as variable compensation," highlighting anticipated inflationary pressures on manufacturing and SG&A costs for 2026.
  • Lucian Boldea said, "Given the volatility of the ongoing trade situation, despite macro uncertainty," indicating persistent macroeconomic and geopolitical uncertainties that could impact results.
  • Tariffs acted as a $30 million year-over-year headwind for the quarter, with future impacts from ongoing tariff changes and mitigation strategies still evolving.

Summary

Management delivered $1.14 adjusted EPS and $1.11 billion in revenue for the quarter, emphasizing a balanced capital allocation that includes debt reduction and shareholder returns alongside ongoing investment. The 2026 outlook calls for 2%-4% total revenue growth and an 8% rise in adjusted EPS at the midpoint, underpinned by both pricing gains and operational efficiencies. Expansion of the 80/20 portfolio discipline will fuel future profitability by simplifying the organization and focusing on higher-growth and higher-margin areas. Planned leadership changes and targeted innovation investments are positioned to support faster expansion in automation and core growth segments. The completion of auto OEM business exits is expected to drive margin improvements as portfolio actions progress through 2026 and 2027.

  • Boldea detailed a progressive back-end loaded roadmap for the 80/20 initiative, with major financial impact and multiyear targets to be disclosed at the May Investor Day.
  • Discenza confirmed that 2026 tariff benefits (10¢–15¢ EPS) include pricing actions, with recapture of margin expected as the company exits 2026.
  • Operational excellence initiatives already yielded a $100 million year-over-year increase in annual free cash flow, with strong working capital management identified as a core driver.
  • Regional footprint flexibility and new regional leadership roles are intended to create local market agility and facilitate export growth, particularly from bases in India and China.
  • While Industrial Motion posted high single-digit segment growth, Engineered Bearings' margin was depressed by negative mix and tariff exposure, illuminating ongoing end-market divergence.

Industry glossary

  • 80/20 discipline: A framework focused on prioritizing the top 20% of products, customers, or activities generating 80% of profit, used to streamline portfolios and operations, maximize margin, and reallocate resources.
  • OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer): Companies that produce parts or equipment which may be marketed by another manufacturer; in this context, represents key Timken customer verticals such as auto and heavy truck production.
