Earnings Call Transcript

Moderna (MRNA) Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Dec 11, 2025 at 2:43PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, Feb. 14, 2025, at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Stéphane Bancel
  • Chief Financial Officer — James Mock
  • President — Stephen Hoge

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $3.2 billion, reflecting a 53% decline, with primary impact from lower product sales.
  • Net Product Sales -- $3.1 billion, at the lower end of revised guidance; U.S. contributed $1.7 billion, and ex-U.S. contributed $1.4 billion, with $400 million coming from non-recurring advanced purchase agreements.
  • Quarterly Revenue -- $966 million for Q4, down 66%, driven by earlier COVID vaccine launch, declining international sales, and phaseout of advanced purchase agreements.
  • Loss -- Net loss for the year was $3.6 billion; Q4 net loss was $1.1 billion, translating to loss per share of $9.28 annually and $2.91 quarterly.
  • Cost Reductions -- $2.6 billion in cost savings, reducing defined cash costs to $6.3 billion and projecting further reduction to $5.5 billion in 2025 and $5 billion in 2026.
  • Operating Expense Guidance -- GAAP expenses projected at $6.4 billion for 2025, including $0.9 billion in noncash charges; capital expenditures projected at $0.4 billion.
  • Product Pipeline Progress -- Ten prioritized programs targeted for approval within 3 years, with three Biologics License Applications (BLAs) filed for next-gen COVID, RSV for adults aged 18-59, and flu+COVID combination vaccines.
  • Cash and Investments -- $9.5 billion at year-end, expected to decline to approximately $6 billion by end of 2025.
  • R&D Expenses -- $4.5 billion in 2024, down 6%, and guided to $4.1 billion in 2025, with flexibility for further reduction depending on sales outcomes.
  • mRESVIA Sales -- $25 million in full year, with expectation of further growth as approvals expand internationally.
  • Guidance for 2025 -- Total revenue expected between $1.5 billion and $2.5 billion; first-half sales projected at $0.2 billion due to respiratory vaccine seasonality.
  • Status of Clinical Trials -- RSV and next-gen COVID vaccines have FDA PDUFA dates of June 12 and May 31, respectively; flu+COVID vaccine approval likely to require positive flu efficacy data from ongoing Phase III study.
  • Norovirus Clinical Hold -- Phase III trial under FDA clinical hold after one Guillain-Barré syndrome (GBS) case, though full current season enrollment is already completed.
  • CMV Vaccine Study -- Interim analysis did not meet early efficacy endpoint; study continues to final analysis, with results expected in 2025 and company remaining blinded to outcomes.
  • INT Cancer Vaccine -- Phase III in adjuvant melanoma fully enrolled; data timing depends on event accrual, possibly in 2026.

RISKS

  • Cost of sales remained high at 79% of product sales in Q4, as volume declines negatively affected cost ratios and included a $238 million noncash charge from contract manufacturing termination.
  • FDA clinical hold on the Norovirus vaccine Phase III trial following a GBS case signals potential regulatory risk, though management does not expect timeline impact for current season analysis.
  • RSV and COVID vaccine market sizes are heavily dependent on uncertain vaccination rates, competitive share shifts, and regulatory licensure timing in Australia, Canada, and the U.K, as acknowledged in forward guidance discussion.

SUMMARY

Moderna (MRNA 0.62%) reported a sharp decline in full-year revenue to $3.2 billion and outlined continued pressure in COVID vaccine sales, compounded by declining advance purchase orders and increased competition. Management signaled focus on a narrowed pipeline, with ten programs prioritized for approval within three years, targeting substantial operating expense cuts and cost discipline. Guidance for 2025 forecasts a significant revenue step down, with no new product launches modeled into expected sales, and management's cost targets implying further streamlining if commercial performance lags. Regulatory and trial uncertainties remain: FDA reviews for next-generation and combination vaccines are pending, approval for major pipeline assets may be contingent on new efficacy data, and a key Norovirus trial is under clinical hold pending safety review.

  • CEO Stéphane Bancel emphasized that reaching break-even on cash costs will require further cutbacks if revenue disappoints, and confirmed operating expenses can be adjusted below projected $5 billion if sales objectives are not met.
  • CFO James Mock explained that over half of trial spending remains concentrated in respiratory programs, which are expected to taper, offering flexibility for future R&D reductions if necessary.
  • Stephen Hoge confirmed the Norovirus vaccine trial is currently on FDA clinical hold but expects no major timeline changes due to completed enrollment this season.
  • Three BLAs were filed in 2024, but regulatory feedback indicates approval of the flu+COVID combination may require interim efficacy data specific to the flu component, which remains unreported and may impact timing.
  • CMV vaccine efficacy at interim was inconclusive, as the Data and Safety Monitoring Board informed the company that the early efficacy criteria was not met, and recommended that the study continue to its final analysis.
  • Guidance range for 2025 revenue is heavily dependent on stable market share, vaccination rates, and on-time licensure of manufacturing facilities outside the U.S; delays or declines could push actual sales to the low end.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PDUFA date: The Prescription Drug User Fee Act deadline—the U.S. FDA's target date for completing review of a drug or vaccine application.
  • BLA: Biologics License Application—the U.S. regulatory submission for approval to market a biologic product, such as a vaccine.
  • mRESVIA: Moderna’s proprietary RSV (respiratory syncytial virus) vaccine, newly commercialized in 2024.
  • Spikevax: Moderna’s commercial COVID-19 vaccine.
  • Priority Review Voucher: A transferrable voucher allowing faster FDA review of another drug application.
  • INT (mRNA-4157): Moderna’s investigational personalized cancer vaccine program, developed in partnership with Merck.
  • GBS (Guillain-Barré syndrome): A rare neurological disorder under investigation in association with the Norovirus vaccine clinical trial.
  • CMV: Cytomegalovirus, a viral infection targeted by a major Moderna vaccine candidate.
  • Norovirus: An infectious virus causing acute gastroenteritis, subject of a key Phase III Moderna vaccine trial now under FDA clinical hold.
  • RSV: Respiratory syncytial virus, cause of severe respiratory illness, the target of Moderna's mRESVIA vaccine.
