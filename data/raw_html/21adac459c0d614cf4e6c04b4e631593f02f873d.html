Earnings Call Transcript

Herbalife (HLF) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 6, 2025 at 6:29PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, August 6, 2025 at 5:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chief Executive Officer — Stephan Gratziani

President and Chief Financial Officer — John DeSimone

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

Adjusted EBITDA margin declined 30 basis points in fiscal Q2 2025 (ended June 30, 2025), driven entirely by unfavorable currency impacts.

North America net sales declined 4% in fiscal Q2 2025, primarily due to approximately 6% volume declines, partially offset by pricing.

Volume was down 3.1%, or $39 million year over year in fiscal Q2 2025, nearly offset by $38 million in favorable pricing.

Asia Pacific and China each reported a 2% year-over-year decline in net sales in fiscal Q2 2025, with respective volume declines of 3% and 6%.

TAKEAWAYS

Net Sales-- $1.3 billion, a 1.7% decrease; at the midpoint of the guidance range, with flat performance on a constant currency basis.

Adjusted EBITDA-- Adjusted EBITDA was $174 million, surpassing the high end of guidance, though down slightly year over year due entirely to currency headwinds.

Gross Profit Margin-- 78%, a 10 basis point increase, largely due to favorable pricing and reduced inventory write-downs, partially offset by currency and cost inflation.

Operating Cash Flow-- Operating cash flows were $96 million, indicating continued operational strength.

Net Income Attributable to Herbalife Nutrition-- Net income attributable toHerbalife Nutrition(HLF +0.20%) was $49 million, with adjusted net income of $61 million.

Adjusted Diluted EPS-- Adjusted diluted EPS was 59¢, impacted by an $0.11 FX headwind.

Adjusted EBITDA Margin-- Adjusted EBITDA margin of 13.8% was down 30 basis points year over year.

Leverage-- Total leverage ratio at 3x as of June 30, 2025; $55 million in debt repaid, including a $50 million redemption of 2025 notes.

Distributor Network-- Over 2 million distributors globally, with nearly 38,000 attendees at key training events and flat new distributor growth year over year, except for 16% growth in Latin America.

Product Launches and Digital Initiatives-- Launch of protocol beta app with 7,000+ distributor participants and debut of Multiburn weight loss supplement; staged global rollout planned beginning in Q4 for U.S. and Puerto Rico, expanding in 2026.

Regional Performance-- Latin America up 9% in constant currency despite a 1% reported sales decline; Mexico local currency sales up 9% with 4% higher volume; EMEA flat reported, down 1% in local currency; India up 2% in local currency; North America and China both down 2%-4% reported, pressured by volume declines.

Guidance Update-- Raised and narrowed full-year 2025 net sales guidance to down 1% to up 3% (reported), flat to up 4% (constant currency); adjusted EBITDA guidance raised to $640-$660 million (reported), $685-$705 million (constant currency).

Capital Allocation-- CapEx at $23 million, below guidance; full-year 2025 CapEx guidance reduced to $75-$95 million.

Tax Rate-- Adjusted effective tax rate of 27.7%, down from 32.3% in fiscal Q2 2024; full-year 2025 guidance range revised to 27%-28%.

Subscription Model Expansion-- Multiburn and select products are now offered via automatic monthly subscription in the U.S., with early uptake exceeding internal expectations as of July 2025.

SUMMARY

Management confirmed that FX headwinds reduced net sales by approximately 170 basis points, less severe than forecasted but still a clear negative factor. Executives highlighted that July 2025 marked the first month of year-over-year volume growth in North America since April 2021, with global volume recovering sequentially as well. Current quarter guidance projects a return to year-over-year net sales growth of 0.5%-4.5% on a reported basis, attributing momentum to product launches and distributor engagement.

DeSimone stated, "Our adjusted EBITDA was $174 million, exceeding the high end of our guidance range," but noted the margin decline was "driven entirely by unfavorable currency impacts."

CEO Gratziani positioned the protocol app launch as a foundational product and data platform, with more than 7,000 distributors participating in the beta and an upcoming wider release in Q4 2025 set to integrate further personalized health features.

Latin America was cited as the only major region with consistent volume and constant currency sales growth, while pressure remained in Asia Pacific and China due to volume declines.

Executives emphasized an ongoing shift toward a more scalable, subscription-based business, with Multiburn and upcoming wellness products expected to play increasing roles in future reported results.

An explicit strategy to deleverage by $1 billion (from 2024 levels) remains on track, with no meaningful debt maturities due until 2028 and several refinancing opportunities highlighted after April 2026.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Protocol (app): Herbalife Nutrition's proprietary wellness and personalization platform integrating digital assessments, AI-driven recommendations, and data-enabled distributor-customer engagement.

Multiburn: A non-pharmaceutical, subscription-based weight loss supplement launched by Herbalife Nutrition in the U.S. market.

Link Biosciences: Acquired entity providing technology for personalized nutritional supplement formulation based on consumer health data.

DMO (Distributor Methods of Operation): Distinct models or strategies employed by Herbalife Nutrition distributors to engage customers and sell products.
