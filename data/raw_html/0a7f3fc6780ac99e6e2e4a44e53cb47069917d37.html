  • Chief Financial Officer — John A. Haudrich

Takeaways

  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Increased 11%, with margin expansion of 220 basis points, driven by Fit to Win benefits that offset net price and volume headwinds.
  • Free cash flow -- Improved to $168 million for fiscal year 2025, supported by higher adjusted earnings, favorable working capital, and a 30% reduction in capital expenditures.
  • Segment operating profit -- Rose 30% to $177 million, with 280 basis points of margin expansion; Americas profit increased 40% despite a 10% volume decline, while Europe grew 8% amid a 3.5% volume decrease.
  • Fit to Win savings -- Delivered $300 million in benefits in 2025, exceeding the prior target, with the three-year cumulative goal raised to at least $750 million.
  • Revenue -- Held stable at approximately $1.5 billion, as flat average selling prices and favorable FX largely offset mid-single-digit volume declines.
  • Volumes -- Total shipments and tons declined 2.5% overall; unit shipments were down 1.5%, reflecting a shift toward lighter-weight, higher-margin bottles.
  • Capital allocation -- Leverage reduced by approximately 0.5x to 3.5x, with a plan to reach about 2.5x by year-end 2027.
  • Guidance for 2026 -- Projected adjusted EBITDA of $1.25 billion–$1.30 billion (up to 7%), adjusted EPS of $1.65–$1.90 per share (up to 19%), and free cash flow of approximately $200 million; includes a $150 million energy cost increase as legacy low-rate European contracts expired.
  • Portfolio mix management -- Exited or repriced negative economic profit business, shifting the mix toward higher-quality segments; about 1% of volume moved out of lower-margin business in 2025, with similar actions planned for 2026.
  • Market conditions -- Shipment declines were concentrated in beer and spirits, especially in the Americas, partly due to consumer affordability and inventory adjustments in the value chain.
  • Network optimization -- Actions to eliminate about 13% of excess capacity by mid-2026; underutilized capacity dropped from 13% (2024) to 6% (2025), targeting roughly 3% in 2026 as European actions conclude.
  • First quarter 2026 outlook -- Management expects the most challenging year-over-year comparison, with volumes likely down mid- to high-single digits due to prior-year tariff prebuying and seasonally higher taxes.
  • Forecast accuracy improvement -- Management reports supply chain forecasting success rate rose from approximately 50% to nearly 69% through 2025.
  • Americas profit drivers -- Despite a 10% volume decline, higher net price realization and Fit to Win savings supported profit growth; approximately half the volume decline attributed to inventory adjustments, with consumption softness and weather disruption also cited.
  • European operations -- Profit rise was led by higher production after prior inventory reduction and continued execution of network actions; further improvement expected post-completion of capacity adjustments.

Summary

O-I Glass (OI +0.56%) reported sequential earnings and margin gains, attributing improved adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow to accelerated cost reduction programs and portfolio modernization. Management increased the cumulative Fit to Win savings target by $100 million, aiming to counteract persistent market uncertainty while reiterating long-term adjusted EBITDA guidance of at least $1.45 billion by 2027. Company leaders outlined supply chain efficiency gains, mix upgrades favoring premium segments, and disciplined capital management as ongoing foundations for sustainable value creation. Operational headwinds were mitigated through deliberate network optimization and direct actions to exit low-margin and negative-profit business lines.

  • Management anticipates improved volume performance in the latter half of 2026, following a challenging first quarter comparison and as market comparisons ease.
  • Operator-driven capacity reductions in Europe are expected to be largely complete by mid-year, resulting in tighter alignment between supply and demand, particularly in core categories such as wine and beer.
  • Forecasting initiatives lifted demand-planning accuracy, with the company reporting a 68%–69% success rate by year-end 2025, supporting working capital and cash performance goals.
  • Commercial strategies are shifting to expand presence in higher-margin segments—premium nonalcoholic beverages, waters, juices, and food—while actively de-emphasizing segments facing capacity and inventory pressures.

Industry glossary

  • Fit to Win: O-I Glass multi-phase, enterprise-wide cost reduction and operational efficiency transformation program focused on SG&A streamlining, network optimization, and value chain transformation.
  • Economic profit (EP): O-I Glass measure of value creation, representing returns generated above the cost of capital.
  • NAB: Nonalcoholic beverages, a key product segment within O-I Glass glass packaging portfolio.
  • IDS: Inventory days of supply, a metric for tracking inventory levels relative to sales velocity.
  • TTF: Title Transfer Facility; a benchmark natural gas price in Europe impacting O-I Glass energy input costs.
