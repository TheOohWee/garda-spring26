UnitedHealth (UNH) Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 11:50AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, October 28, 2025 at 8:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman — Stephen Hemsley
  • Chief Executive Officer, UnitedHealthcare — Timothy Noel
  • Chief Executive Officer, Optum — Patrick Conway
  • Chief Financial Officer — Wayne DeVeydt
  • Chief Operating Officer, Optum Health — Krista Nelson
  • Chief Executive Officer, Optum Insight — Sandeep Dadlani
  • President, UnitedHealthcare Employer & Individual — Dan Schumacher
  • Senior Vice President, UnitedHealthcare — Robert Hunter

Takeaways

  • Adjusted earnings per share -- $2.92, slightly above internal expectations for the period.
  • Revenue -- Over $113 billion, representing 12% year-over-year growth, driven by net domestic membership increases of more than 780,000 lives year-to-date.
  • Medical care ratio -- 89.9% versus 85.2% in the prior-year quarter, with full-year tracking toward the lower end of previous guidance.
  • Operating cost ratio -- 13.5%, reflecting increased technology and employee investments, notably $450 million in broad-based employee incentives and UnitedHealth Foundation contributions.
  • Domestic membership -- Ended the quarter with over 50 million members.
  • Operating cash flow -- $5.9 billion in the quarter, with year-end expectation of $16 billion or 1.1x net income.
  • Debt-to-capital ratio -- 44.1% due to the $3.4 billion Amedisys acquisition in the quarter; targeted to decline toward 40% in the second half of 2026.
  • Share buyback and acquisitions -- Both paused to prioritize capital structure and debt reduction, with "anticipation we may be in a position to reinstate our historical capital deployment practices later in the year."
  • UnitedHealthcare Medicare Advantage membership -- Management expects a contraction of approximately 1 million members in 2026 due to plan exits, network reductions, and benefit adjustments.
  • ACA business rate actions -- Rate filings in almost all 30 states with “average rate increases of over 25%,” expected to reduce enrollment by around 66% to restore margin profile.
  • Optum Health value-based care membership -- Anticipated shrinkage of approximately 10% in 2026, following exits from 200,000 lives in lower-performing PPO contracts.
  • Optum Health margins -- Expected to close 2025 just under 3% (value-based care portfolio under 1%), with improvement projected in 2026 despite persistent Medicare funding cuts.
  • Commercial segment pricing -- Approximately 60% of group commercial insured offerings repriced for 2026 to account for sustained elevated trends; margin recovery expected to reach target 7%-9% range by 2027.
  • Medicare Advantage cost trend -- Full-year 2025 trend of approximately 7.5%, with 2026 expectations at a 10% trend, reflecting ongoing elevated care activity and provider coding practices.
  • Medicare Supplement trend -- Medical cost trends remain above 11%.
  • Medicaid margin outlook -- Margins are forecast to be breakeven for 2025, with further declines expected in 2026 because "states have not funded in line with actual cost trends."
  • Investment in AI -- Accelerated deployment across both UnitedHealthcare and Optum businesses to advance member experience, payment integrity, and clinical operating models.
  • Optum Insight AI launches -- New products (Optum Real, Optum Integrity One, Crimson AI) reported productivity increases (e.g., 73% for outpatient claims coding), with "average return on investment for any provider system investing in Crimson AI has been 13:1."
  • Planned portfolio rationalization -- Company is exiting select international markets and consolidating Optum Health locations, with a preliminary estimate of a "non-GAAP substantially noncash low single-digit billion-dollar charge."
  • Repricing and cost initiatives -- Repricing of UHC risk businesses, targeted plan/product exits, narrowed networks, and cost control intended to yield “margin improvements in 2026,” with acceleration targeted for 2027 and beyond.

Risks

  • Medicare funding headwinds in 2026 represent a “more than $6 billion headwind to the overall enterprise” due to the final year of V28 program changes.
  • Declines in Medicaid profitability expected: "the path to recovery will be more challenging" with inadequate state funding expected to continue through 2026, resulting in margin decline from breakeven in 2025.
  • Projected membership contraction in Medicare Advantage and ACA segments due to product exits and rate actions could reduce overall growth in 2026.
  • Medical cost trends remain "historically high" across Medicare and Commercial lines, pressuring short-term earnings despite repricing efforts.

Summary

UnitedHealth Group (UNH 0.84%) delivered 12% year-over-year revenue growth in the quarter, supported by domestic membership gains, but medical care and operating costs continued to rise. Management confirmed that major portfolio rationalization, ACA retrenchment, and deliberate plan exits in underperforming Medicare and Optum Health segments would drive significant membership reductions and near-term margin disruption in 2026. AI investments and repricing actions are positioned as key drivers for margin recovery and renewed earnings growth heading into 2027. New product launches within Optum Insight signal an acceleration in technology-driven business transformation targeted at both margin enhancement and long-term competitive positioning.

  • Company is realigning capital structure following a $3.4 billion acquisition, with near-term suspension of buybacks and acquisitions until leverage targets are met.
  • Management estimates the ongoing business model reset may yield a “non-GAAP substantially noncash low single-digit billion-dollar charge.”
  • Optum Health expects fee-for-service productivity initiatives to show incremental improvement in regions like the East; leadership asserts network and provider curation will achieve a more focused value-based model in 2026.
  • Optum Rx forecasts new membership growth for 2026 will be outweighed by losses from UnitedHealthcare, even as customer retention and new wins remain strong.
  • CFO Wayne DeVeydt affirmed the company will maintain its current dividend payout policies, with prioritization of debt reduction and future return to historical capital deployment strategies.

Industry glossary

  • Medical care ratio: Percentage of premium revenues spent on clinical services and medical claims payments.
  • Value-based care (VBC): Payment model rewarding providers for outcomes and cost efficiency versus service volume.
  • Medicare Advantage (MA): Medicare-approved health plans from private companies that provide Medicare benefits to enrollees.
  • STARS rating: CMS quality rating system for Medicare Advantage and Part D plans; impacts funding and member attraction.
  • ACA: Refers to health insurance plans compliant with the Affordable Care Act, targeting the individual and small group markets.
  • V28: Industry-wide set of Medicare funding cuts/phased payment policy changes referenced as a major headwind for UnitedHealth Group and peers.
  • Operating cost ratio: Non-medical operating expenses as a proportion of total revenues.
  • Debt-to-capital ratio: Measure of financial leverage showing debt as a percentage of total capital (debt plus equity).
  • I-SNP: Institutional Special Needs Plan, a type of Medicare Advantage plan for individuals in long-term care institutions.
