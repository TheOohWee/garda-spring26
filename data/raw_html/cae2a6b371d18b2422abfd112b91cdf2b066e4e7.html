  • Fourth quarter same-store NOI growth -- 0.7% year over year, with 1.7% core revenue growth and a 4% rise in core expenses.

  • Net debt to adjusted EBITDA -- 5.3x at year end.

  • Share repurchase -- $500 million program authorized in October 2025; 3.6 million shares repurchased for approximately $100 million since then.

  • Blended rent growth guidance for 2026 -- Mid-2% range assumed.
  • 2026 core FFO per share outlook -- $1.90-$1.98; 2026 AFFO per share outlook is $1.60-$1.68.
  • Dispositions -- $550 million projected for 2026, planned as primary funding source for additional share repurchases.
  • New wholly owned home deliveries -- $250 million anticipated in 2026 at the midpoint.

  • Build-to-rent concessions -- Specials applied only to BTR communities during slower months, not to scattered site product.
  • Advocacy and regulatory costs -- $0.02 per share estimated in 2026 earnings bridge related to advocacy and regulatory matters.

SUMMARY

Invitation Homes (INVH +0.08%) completed the ResiBuilt acquisition, bringing in-house development capabilities and signaling modest accretion to 2026 AFFO, with all near-term ResiBuilt activity remaining third-party and fee-based. Management highlighted steady performance in leasing and occupancy metrics, pointing out strong renewal rent growth, while new lease rates declined amid transitory supply pressure in key Sunbelt markets. Share repurchase remains a top capital allocation priority, funded primarily through property dispositions, with $100 million already deployed since program inception. Executive commentary indicated ongoing engagement with regulators on legislative clarity, affirming confidence that SFR operators like Invitation Homes offer key value and affordability to residents. The company reaffirmed its disciplined cost control ethos, flagging higher expense guidance for property taxes and insurance due to tougher year-over-year comparisons, while estimating $0.02 per share of advocacy costs as part of their 2026 plan.

  • Management expects blended new lease and renewal spreads to narrow as the year progresses, with higher new lease growth anticipated by mid-summer.
  • Fee-build activity at ResiBuilt will remain largely third-party for the near term, providing capital-light revenues, while future selective development for company balance sheet and JV partners is under review.
  • Occupancy guidance embeds an assumption of average 96.3% for 2026, with a modest projected increase in turnover due to pricing competition.
  • Leadership reported that approximately 16%-17% of move-outs in the last quarter were due to home purchase, lower than the long-term historical 20%-25% range.
  • No debt maturities are expected before mid-2027; management reaffirmed the intention to maintain high liquidity and fixed-rate debt structure as interest rates remain elevated.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Build-to-rent (BTR): A real estate development strategy focused on constructing purpose-built single-family rental homes, often in professionally managed communities.
  • Fee-build: A development arrangement in which the builder constructs homes for third-party clients, earning fees without holding inventory risk or using significant balance sheet capital.
  • Same-store NOI: Net Operating Income derived from properties held throughout both the current and prior reporting periods, excluding acquisitions and dispositions, used to measure core portfolio growth.
  • Scattered site: Single-family rental homes located across discrete non-contiguous parcels, as opposed to organized BTR communities.
