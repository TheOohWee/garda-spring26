TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $2.8 million, with $2.5 million from ZUNVEYL product sales and $259,000 in licensing revenue from the CMS Pharma collaboration.
  • Full-Year Revenue -- $10.2 million, including $6.8 million in ZUNVEYL net product sales.
  • ZUNVEYL Demand Acceleration -- 4,941 bottles dispensed, marking 62% quarter-over-quarter growth; December reached a high of 1,859 bottles dispensed.
  • Prescribing Expansion -- 50% increase in prescribing physicians and 69% rise in cumulative homes with prescriptions, compared sequentially to Q3.
  • Unique Facility Penetration -- Nearly 4,000 unique long-term care facilities reached since launch, including 1,986 homes engaged and 729 homes placing orders during the quarter.
  • Repeat Orders -- 82% of ordering homes in Q4 placed repeat orders; 69% of prescribers were repeat writers.
  • PBM Contracts -- Contracts now cover 2 of the 4 largest pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs) for long-term care; a second national PBM contract was signed at year-end.
  • Operating Expenses -- $10.7 million in the quarter, up from $2.7 million in the prior-year period, including $258,000 in cost of goods sold and $10.4 million in SG&A and R&D expenses.
  • Operating Loss -- $7.9 million for the quarter and $22.7 million for the year, reflecting expanded commercialization costs.
  • Net Loss -- $6.9 million, or $0.30 per share, for the quarter; $20.7 million, or $1.17 per share, for the year, inclusive of fair value gains on warrant liabilities.
  • Cash Position -- $66 million in unrestricted cash and equivalents at year-end, including $38 million from the recent equity offering and over-allotment exercise.
  • Expense Outlook -- 2026 full-year operating expenses expected in the $54 million to $58 million range, driven by new clinical studies and expanded sales and marketing.
  • Clinical Program Initiation -- BEACON (long-term care, efficacy and tolerability) initiated in February with top-line data expected in Q4 2026; CONVERGE (polypharmacy and adherence) starts in April with data in Q3 2026; RESOLVE (outpatient) initiates in Q2.
  • Sublingual Formulation -- Comparative PK study against tablet and intranasal forms scheduled for Q2, supporting potential clinical entry in early 2027 via 505(b)(2) FDA pathway.
  • CMS Pharma Milestone -- Anticipated business development milestone payment upon regulatory approval in China, timing still to be determined.
  • Sales Force Expansion -- Field team fully staffed as of March, with new representatives focused on psychiatry customers and long-term care medical directors.
  • Peer-to-Peer Education -- National speaker training completed for 48 key opinion leaders, with rollout of educational programs to support prescriber adoption in 2026.
  • Nursing Home Success Stories -- Real-world reports indicate improvements in patient behaviors and cognitive function, as shared directly by providers.
  • Payer Access Plans -- Broader plan-level coverage and reduced prior authorization friction anticipated to accelerate in Q2 and Q3, with implementation focus on regional accounts of contracted PBMs.

SUMMARY

Alpha Cognition (ACOG 5.49%) showcased rapid uptake of its lead asset ZUNVEYL, highlighted by a steep quarter-over-quarter rise in bottles dispensed and significant expansion of repeat provider adoption across long-term care facilities. Management unveiled concrete timelines for key real-world outcome studies—BEACON, CONVERGE, and RESOLVE—with data expected to inform clinical positioning, payer negotiations, and future promotional strategies within 2026. The company emphasized advancement toward payer coverage by securing contracts with two major PBMs and outlined expectations for operational profitability by 2027, underpinned by a robust cash position and ongoing commercial investment.

  • Michael McFadden stated, "we maintained a strong financial foundation, which we believe keeps the company well funded with a clear path toward operational profitability in 2027."
  • Lauren D’Angelo highlighted accelerating demand and the transition to durable, repeat utilization, noting the Q4 inflection as a critical turning point for penetration and scale.
  • Management confirmed initiation of national peer-to-peer education to amplify prescriber confidence and adoption, marking a shift from early sales-force-driven growth to broader clinical influence.
  • The operational plan prescribes intensified focus on expanding coverage at the plan level within existing PBM contracts, as well as completion of clinical and regulatory milestones to drive both near-term access and future product pipeline evolution.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PBM (Pharmacy Benefit Manager): Entities managing prescription drug benefits for health plans, negotiating pricing and access with manufacturers and pharmacies.
  • SG&A (Selling, General, and Administrative expenses): Combined operating expenses not directly attributable to product manufacturing, including sales force, marketing, and administrative functions.
  • PK Study (Pharmacokinetic Study): Research assessing the absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion of a drug in the body.
  • 505(b)(2) Pathway: A regulatory approval process allowing companies to rely in part on existing data for a previously approved drug while adding new clinical or preclinical data for a modified or new formulation.
