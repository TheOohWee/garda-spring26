Earnings Call Transcript

Enterprise Financial (EFSC) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 10:54AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, October 28, 2025 at 11 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — James Lally
  • Chief Financial Officer and Chief Operating Officer — Keene Turner
  • Chief Banking Officer — Douglas Bauche

TAKEAWAYS

  • Diluted EPS -- $1.19, compared to $1.36 in the prior quarter and $1.32 a year ago.
  • Net Interest Income -- Increased $5.5 million sequentially, marking the sixth consecutive quarter of growth.
  • Net Interest Margin -- Increased by 2 basis points to 4.23% from the prior quarter and up from 4.17% a year earlier despite over 100 basis points in Federal Reserve rate cuts.
  • Loan Growth -- Annualized growth rate of 6%, or $174 million net of $22 million in guaranteed loan sales, with origination yields at 6.98%.
  • Deposit Growth -- Core deposits, excluding brokered CDs, increased $240 million sequentially, with noninterest-bearing accounts remaining at 32% of total deposits.
  • Specialty Deposits -- $189 million growth quarter over quarter and 22% year over year, driven by property management, community association, and legal escrow verticals.
  • Branch Acquisition -- Closed on 12 branches, adding approximately $650 million in deposits and $300 million in loans, with expected annualized run-rate expense of just under $18 million and immediate $4.5 million incremental expense in the fourth quarter.
  • Nonperforming Assets -- Increased $22 million sequentially to 83 basis points of total assets, with 60% attributable to a $12 million life insurance premium finance loan and $68.4 million in seven Southern California commercial real estate loans.
  • Net Charge-Offs -- Rose to $4.1 million from $1 million in the prior quarter, but year-to-date net charge-offs remain below historical averages.
  • Tangible Book Value -- Grew by over 15% on an annualized quarterly basis and 12% year over year.
  • Dividend -- Quarterly dividend raised by $0.01 to $0.32 per share for the upcoming quarter.
  • Tangible Common Equity Ratio -- Stood at 9.60% at quarter end, up from 9.4% sequentially.
  • Common Equity Tier 1 Ratio -- Reached 12%, the highest in company history, as reported by Turner.
  • SBA Loan Sales -- Sold $22 million in SBA loans for a $1.1 million gain; management noted future sales may depend on market conditions.
  • Provision for Loan Losses -- Increased to $8.4 million from $3.5 million in the prior quarter, reflecting net charge-offs and NPA growth.
  • Allowance for Credit Losses -- At 1.29% of total loans, or 1.4% excluding government-guaranteed balances.
  • Tax Credit Recapture -- $24 million solar credit recapture offset by insurance; $30.1 million grossed up in noninterest income and income tax expense with no effect on net income this quarter.
  • Expense Guidance -- Run-rate for the current quarter estimated at $107 million, with an additional $4.5 million from branch acquisition normalized in the fourth quarter; full-year branch expenses to add approximately 3.5% to annual totals.
  • Fee Income Outlook -- Fourth quarter noninterest income expected to fall between second and third quarter levels, with $2 million in annualized acquisition-related fees expected to start contributing by year end.
  • Deposit Costs -- Deposit beta assumption remains at 40%, with $1 million added quarterly cost for each 25 basis point rate increase.

RISKS

  • Nonperforming Assets and Credit Expense -- Lally stated, "We did experience an increase in provision for loan losses in the quarter primarily due to a $22 million increase in nonperforming assets and net charge-offs," and Turner reported net charge-offs increased to $4.1 million, with provision for loan losses rising to $8.4 million from $3.5 million sequentially.
  • Resolution Uncertainty for Problem Loans -- Bauche noted, "So again, this looks like this may be heading through some litigation. I think precise timing of the resolution of that case is a bit uncertain. But what is certain is full coverage of cash surrender value covering our principal balance and collectibility."
  • Fee Income Volatility -- Turner acknowledged, "we kind of went from the maybe the best case scenario of fee income in the second quarter to a baseline here in the third quarter," noting unpredictability in tax credit and SBA sale income lines.

SUMMARY

Enterprise Financial Services Corp (EFSC 1.90%) delivered sequential improvements in net interest income and margin, supported by robust loan and deposit growth and further balance sheet expansion following a 12-branch acquisition. Despite positive core operating trends, credit metrics deteriorated with a notable increase in nonperforming assets and net charge-offs, prompting a higher provision for loan losses and ongoing uncertainty regarding the timing of full resolution for large nonaccrual relationships. Management expressed confidence in near-term earnings resilience, stable capital ratios at record levels, and the ability to defend net interest margin into 2026, while expense growth will be driven by recent market expansion and branch integration activities.

  • Turner stated, "When you account for the recapture plus the taxes on the anticipated insurance recovery, the gross up in noninterest income and income tax expense is $30.1 million during the quarter. Since there is no impact on net income for the third quarter, we have excluded these items from the earnings per share bridge."
  • Lally commented on capital deployment, indicating ongoing priority for organic growth and client integration, with further M&A or share repurchases as potential but secondary options.
  • The company's specialty deposit verticals—focused on property management, community association, and legal escrow—posted 22% year-over-year growth and management intends to continue investing in these businesses for future growth.
  • Core efficiency ratio was reported at 61% as the company navigates higher deposit, legal, and credit-related costs amid integration and resolution of problem assets.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • NPAs (Nonperforming Assets): Loans or assets that are not generating the contractually obligated interest or principal payments, often due to borrower delinquency or default.
  • CET1 (Common Equity Tier 1): The core capital measure of a bank's financial strength as defined by regulatory standards, consisting of common equity and retained earnings.
  • Tax Credit Recapture: IRS-mandated return of tax credits previously claimed, typically due to the sale, disposition, or other loss of qualifying assets triggering reimbursement requirements.
  • SBA Loan Sales: The practice of selling U.S. Small Business Administration-guaranteed loans into the secondary market, allowing banks to generate immediate noninterest income and free balance sheet capacity.
  • Deposit Beta: A metric quantifying the sensitivity of a bank’s deposit costs to changes in benchmark short-term interest rates.
  • Core Efficiency Ratio: Operating expenses as a percentage of net revenue, excluding certain nonrecurring items, measuring operational cost efficiency.
