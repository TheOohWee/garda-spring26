TAKEAWAYS

  • Reported Earnings -- $2.9 billion, or $7.17 per share, including a $239 million pretax impact from Los Angeles refinery accelerated depreciation.
  • Adjusted Earnings -- $1 billion, or $2.47 per share, flat sequentially versus the previous quarter.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $2.8 billion, with a $780 million working capital benefit from inventory reduction, partially offset by lower prices impacting receivables and payables.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $756 million in the quarter, of which $274 million was share repurchases.
  • Capital Spending -- $682 million in the quarter, funding both growth and sustaining projects.
  • Net Debt to Capital Ratio -- 38% at quarter-end.
  • Segment Earnings Drivers -- Sequential gains in refining, renewable fuels, and midstream, nearly offset by declines in chemicals and marketing and specialties.
  • Midstream Adjusted EBITDA -- Approximately $1 billion for the year, up 40% since 2022, with execution of Coastal Bend and Dos Picos Two cited as key contributors.
  • Run-Rate Adjusted EBITDA Guidance -- $4.5 billion targeted for midstream by year-end 2027, based on ongoing organic expansions including new gas plants and pipeline capacity.
  • Refining Adjusted Controllable Cost per Barrel -- $5.96 in Q4, with a target of approximately $5.50 by 2027; excluding Los Angeles idling costs, Q4 would have been $5.57 per barrel.
  • WRB Acquisition Impact -- Full consolidation increased exposure to Canadian heavy crude differentials by 40%; management estimates each $1 widening in heavy crude differential adds $140 million annual earnings.
  • Asset Portfolio Actions -- Acquired remaining 50% of WRB, sold 65% interest in Germany and Austria retail marketing, and idled the Los Angeles refinery in 2025.
  • Heavy Oil Processing -- System operating at maximum heavy crude utilization, with anticipated benefit to economics even as clean product yield is modestly reduced.
  • System-Wide Refining Capacity Increases -- Structural upgrades in four refineries resulted in a 25,000 barrels per day total increase, raising system capacity by approximately 2%.
  • 2026 Operating Guidance -- Global O&P utilization expected in the mid-90% range; refining utilization anticipated in the low 90% range; turnaround expense forecasted at $170 million-$190 million for Q1 and $550 million-$600 million for the full year.
  • Dividends and Debt Framework -- Dividend commitment around $2 billion per year, capital spend projected at $2.4 billion, with the balance of operating cash flow allocated to share buybacks and debt reduction under a 50%+ shareholder return policy.
  • Chemicals Segment EBITDA -- $845 million share from CPChem in 2025, with management citing US base plant utilization at 90% compared to 65% in Asia Pacific and Europe.
  • Asset Monetization -- Over $5 billion in assets monetized in 2025.
  • Western Gateway Pipeline Progress -- First open season completed with shipper commitments; current efforts focused on expanding delivery points into the LA market and securing further customer commitments.
  • Safety -- Management reported 2025 as the best safety year on record for the company.

SUMMARY

Phillips 66 (PSX 1.13%) reported significant portfolio repositioning in 2025, highlighted by the full acquisition of WRB, divestiture of European marketing interests, and the idling of the Los Angeles refinery. Management stated these moves advance a multi-year cost streamlining and capital allocation strategy designed to maximize asset value, boost system flexibility, and focus on competitive market segments. The company prioritized returning over half of net operating cash flow to shareholders through dividends and buybacks while signaling strong midstream earnings growth and clarified pathways to achieve a more integrated, lower-cost, and expansion-ready refining and logistics portfolio.

  • Management described a visible line of sight to a 2027 midstream adjusted EBITDA run-rate of $4.5 billion, primarily from organic project delivery, with "the real step changes" in earnings expected as new gas plants and expansions fill and ramp up.
  • Cost reduction initiatives in refining are underpinned by over 300 active projects, with the company tracking toward a $5.50 per barrel controllable cost target, which is "directionally heading towards that $5.50 target that we're up" according to refining leadership.
  • Full exposure to Canadian heavy oil has materially increased, with management highlighting that "each dollar is worth $140 million in yearly earnings" following the WRB consolidation.
  • Turnaround costs for 2026 include full-year WRB integration, with management stating the increase is driven by basis change and not underlying operational escalation, and annual per-barrel turnaround guidance remains unchanged when normalized for these factors.
  • System-wide refining flexibility and reliability have been structurally improved, as projects in Bayway, Billings, and Sweeny have lifted stated capacities by 25,000 barrels per day, allowing for higher utilization and margin capture when market conditions permit.
  • The Western Gateway pipeline project garnered regulatory and commercial backing, with open seasons yielding customer commitments and management characterizing the project as a potential post-2027 midstream growth lever not yet included in forecasts.
  • Management signaled that asset divestitures and targeted M&A remain on the table, stating that select opportunities would be considered if they enhance competitive positioning in key regions such as the Gulf Coast or Mid Continent.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • WRB: A joint venture refinery previously co-owned by Phillips 66 and Cenovus Energy, now fully consolidated by Phillips 66 following the acquisition of the remaining 50% interest.
  • Clean Product Yield: The proportion of high-value refined products (such as gasoline, distillates, and jet fuel) produced from crude oil processing, excluding heavier residuals and byproducts.
  • Crack Spread: The price differential between crude oil and the finished petroleum products refined from it, indicating refining margin potential.
  • Turnaround Expense: Planned maintenance and upgrade spending to temporarily shut down processing units for inspection, repair, or improvement in refineries.
  • PAD II: Petroleum Administration for Defense District II, a geographic designation for the Midwest US refining and distribution region.
  • NGL: Natural Gas Liquids, hydrocarbon liquids extracted from natural gas production, including ethane, propane, butane, and natural gasoline.
  • O&P Utilization Rate: The operating rate for Olefins & Polyolefins units—the main chemical production facilities for ethylene, polyethylene, and related petrochemicals.
  • VGO Project: A plant improvement involving Vacuum Gas Oil optimization at a refinery, designed to enhance feedstock flexibility and yields.
  • CPChem: Chevron Phillips Chemical Company, a joint venture co-owned by Phillips 66 that operates petrochemical plants and projects globally.
  • Western Gateway: A pipeline infrastructure project targeting expanded refined product delivery from the US Gulf Coast and Mid Continent into the California market.
