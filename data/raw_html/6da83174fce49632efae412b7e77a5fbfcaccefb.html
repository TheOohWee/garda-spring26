Birkenstock (BIRK) Q4 2024 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 6, 2026 at 1:42PM EST

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- EUR 1.8 billion for the full year, representing 22% constant currency growth, exceeding the company's stated 20% expectation.
  • Fourth quarter revenue -- EUR 456 million, up 22% constant currency, with B2B increasing 26% and DTC up 18% relative to the same period last year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin -- 30.8% for the year, above the high end of management's 30%-30.5% expected range; Q4 adjusted EBITDA margin was 27.4%, up 190 basis points year-over-year.
  • Closed-toe silhouettes -- Drove growth at more than twice the rate of the group, now account for nearly one-third of group sales, and about half of top 20 selling silhouettes.
  • APMA segment growth -- APMA delivered 42% annual growth, nearly double the overall business pace; APMA accounts for 12% of total revenue mix.
  • Own retail store expansion -- 20 new stores opened globally during the year, taking the total to 67; company plans a 50% increase in store count in fiscal 2025.
  • Pairs sold -- Volumes climbed 14% year over year; average selling price rose 8% driven by mix and targeted price hikes.
  • Wholesale (B2B) revenue -- Grew 23% annually, with over 90% of growth from existing wholesale doors.
  • DTC channel -- Direct-to-consumer sales grew 21%, maintaining approximately 40% penetration of company revenues; digital comprised 90% of DTC sales.
  • Gross profit margin -- 58.8% for the year, down 330 basis points, mainly due to temporary under-absorption from added capacity (150 basis points), with additional impacts from mix and FX.
  • Operating cash flow -- EUR 429 million generated, marking 20% growth in operating cash flow year over year.
  • Net leverage -- Reached 1.8x at fiscal year end, below the 2.0x target; company targets 1.5x by end of next year.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Adjusted EPS of EUR 1.28 for the year, a 30% rise; Q4 adjusted EPS of EUR 0.29, up 107% from the prior-year quarter.
  • Membership base -- Exceeded 8 million members, up over 30% year over year, with members spending on average 30% more than non-members.
  • 2025 guidance -- Management expects revenue growth of 15%-17%, EBITDA margin improvement of up to 50 basis points, gross margin improvement toward the 60% target, and approximately EUR 80 million of capital expenditures mainly for capacity and retail expansion.
  • Segment reorganization -- Effective 2025, APMA becomes APAC (includes India), and EMEA merges Europe with Middle East and Africa; Americas segment unchanged.

Summary

Birkenstock Holding (BIRK 1.78%) reported above-guidance revenue and EBITDA margin for the year, driven by expansion in closed-toe products, rapid APMA growth, and continued DTC and B2B gains. Cash generation improved through enhanced working capital management, and capital deployment included both production investments and aggressive debt reduction. Management confirmed that 2024’s gross margin pressure from capacity expansion will fade, guiding to margin recovery as absorption improves. The company anticipates a 50% increase in retail store count in 2025 and a recalibrated segment structure to align with operational efficiencies. Strategic priorities for 2025 focus on balancing B2B and DTC, new store rollouts, and meeting demand within a disciplined distribution model.

  • CEO Reichert said, "Q1 will likely come in the higher end of our 15% to 17% annual revenue growth guidance range," citing a robust holiday season despite last year's elevated comp.
  • APMA region leadership indicated a strategy to double share versus mature markets, with a long-term goal of 30% regional sales mix.
  • B2B and DTC channels are expected to contribute balanced growth, with channel-specific seasonality impacting quarterly margins.
  • Retail store payback periods remain under 18 months per management, supporting continued fleet expansion.
  • Full absorption of margin drag from new factory investments is expected by the third quarter of 2026.
  • Leadership confirmed a cautious approach to APAC growth, managing exposure across multiple geographies to mitigate geopolitical risk.
  • The company plans to use excess cash primarily for further debt reduction, prioritizing repayment of the most expensive U.S. term loan.
  • According to company commentary, full price realization is over 95% in wholesale and over 90% in DTC channels, reflecting continued inventory discipline and brand scarcity.

Industry glossary

  • APMA/APAC: Regional business segments; APMA is Asia Pacific, Middle East, and Africa (prior to fiscal 2025); APAC is Asia Pacific (including India, starting fiscal 2025).
  • B2B: Business-to-business sales channel encompassing third-party retail partners and wholesale distribution.
  • DTC: Direct-to-consumer channel including company e-commerce and owned retail stores.
  • Own retail: Company-operated physical retail stores, distinct from partner or franchise doors.
  • Full price realization: The share of product sales achieved at initial ticket price without discounting.
  • Under-absorption: Temporary reduction in gross margin due to fixed overhead costs allocated to underutilized new production capacity.
  • Door count: Number of physical retail locations, including both owned and partner-operated doors.
  • Mono brand partner doors: Third-party retail stores exclusively or predominantly dedicated to the Birkenstock brand.
  • ASP: Average selling price per unit sold.
  • Sell-through: The rate at which inventory is sold through retail to end consumers versus sitting on shelves or in warehouses.
  • Hash: Industry term for inventory imbalances or broken size runs in the retail channel.
