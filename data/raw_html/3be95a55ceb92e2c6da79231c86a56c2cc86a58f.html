Earnings Call Transcript

Iridium (IRDM) Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 10, 2026 at 4:39PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, February 13, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Matthew J. Desch
  • Chief Financial Officer — Vincent O'Neill

Takeaways

  • Service revenue -- 5% annual growth, with 2024 described as a "transition year" due to prior voice/data price changes, D2D product rework, and a change in satellite useful-life accounting.
  • Operational EBITDA (“OEBITDA”) -- $117 million for the quarter, up 3% year over year; full-year result exceeded guidance.
  • Total revenue -- $213 million for the quarter, up 9% year over year, from service revenue, equipment return to normal demand, and increased government contracts.
  • Pro forma free cash flow -- $306 million for the year; company forecasts $302 million in 2025 using midpoint of OEBITDA guidance and stated expense assumptions.
  • Shareholder returns -- Over $400 million returned through buybacks (11% of outstanding shares) and nearly $500 million when including dividends in 2024.
  • Share buybacks -- 14 million shares repurchased in 2024 at a $28.92 average price; buyback authorization has $430 million remaining, covering activity through 2027.
  • Commercial service revenue -- $127.3 million in the quarter, up 5%, including voice/data up 3% with APAC market share gains attributed to competitor outages.
  • Commercial IoT revenue -- 15% growth in the quarter, aided by strength in personal communications, despite a net decline of 15,000 IoT subscribers due to a major customer’s plan change.
  • IoT subscriber seasonality -- Plan change at a large commercial IoT customer increased churn of 250,000 seasonal subscribers but does not impact Iridium’s fixed contract revenue.
  • Broadband revenue -- $13.4 million in the quarter, down 9% year over year; full year broadband revenue down 3% with ARPU pressure from companion use cases.
  • Hosted payload and other data -- $15.4 million in the quarter, up 2% from last year; propelled by new PNT service, partially offset by the absence of a prior one-time benefit.
  • Government service revenue -- $26.8 million in the quarter, reflecting a contractual step-up in the U.S. government EMSS agreement effective September 15, 2024.
  • Subscriber equipment revenue -- $21.6 million for the quarter, up 38% as channel demand normalized post-pandemic supply chain disruptions; normalization brings margins closer to pre-pandemic levels.
  • Engineering and support revenue -- $37.4 million for the quarter, up 20% on new government contract awards and Space Development Agency work.
  • 2025 guidance -- Service revenue expected to grow 5%-7%; OEBITDA forecasted at $490 million-$500 million (midpoint implies 5% annual growth); CapEx budgeted at $90 million, with SG&A growth moderating to mid-single-digits and R&D declining as capitalized project work peaks.
  • Net leverage -- Ended 2024 at 3.6x OEBITDA, up from 3.1x, driven by the Satelles acquisition and opportunistic buybacks; management anticipates <2x by 2030.
  • Dividend -- Board intends to raise quarterly dividend to $0.15 per share beginning Q3 2025, equating to a >5% annual increase.
  • Cash balance -- $94 million as of December 31, 2024; management considers $50 million-$60 million adequate for ongoing needs, with future buyback pacing tied to maintaining that buffer and leverage targets.
  • D2D/NTN progress -- Live testing of Iridium NTN Direct with suppliers expected by mid-2025; commercial launch targeted for 2026 with meaningful financial impact building 2027-2029.
  • Iridium Certus developments -- Over 30 Certus 100 products certified in 2024 and midband technology driving new industrial and IoT revenue streams, broadening ARPU opportunities.
  • Partnership expansion -- Onboarding of 30+ new partners and certification of over 60 new network applications in 2024, aimed at supporting long-term growth and standards-based IoT/D2D offerings.
  • Aireon equity stake -- Aireon’s financial rebound and expansion in aviation analytics services (beyond core surveillance) highlighted; Iridium maintains a 27% stake and is open to increasing it if terms are favorable.
  • PNT outlook -- PNT (Position, Navigation, and Timing) services are noted as the largest driver in hosted payload and are seeing escalating interest and regional sales momentum for 2025-2026.

Risks

  • O'Neill stated, "Margin, however, will be down modestly as a one-time cost benefit realized in 2024 will not recur in 2025," specifically affecting equipment segment profitability.
  • Vincent O'Neill noted, "We saw a spike in deactivations from that customer in the fourth quarter as they started to phase out their annual plans, including plans that allow subscribers to toggle between active and inactive status on a monthly basis throughout the contract year. The number of inactive subscribers varied seasonally, but averaged about 250,000 over the last year. I think you'll see the impact of these changes over the next few quarters. Subscribers who previously used these annual plans are now expected to only be subscribers in months, when they anticipate using the service. This means going forward, you will see greater seasonality in our subscriber numbers from quarter-to-quarter versus prior year periods," introducing visibility issues in subscriber metrics.
  • Net leverage rose from 3.1x to 3.6x OEBITDA, attributed by O'Neill to additional term loan issuance for the Satelles acquisition and increased buyback activity.
  • O'Neill remarked, "projections is expected to increase modestly year over year," reflecting the expansion of the company’s credit facility.

Summary

Iridium Communications (IRDM 1.30%) reported improved service revenue and OEBITDA in the fourth quarter, with disciplined capital allocation through substantial share repurchases and a planned dividend raise. Management attributed seasonal volatility in IoT subscriber numbers to contract-driven changes, emphasizing these do not affect fixed revenue flows. The company reaffirmed its guidance for 5%-7% service revenue growth and OEBITDA of $490 million-$500 million in 2025, with new products and partnerships announced to support mid- and long-term expansion. Capital structure strategy was reiterated, with leverage expected to decline organically over the decade as free cash flow is prioritized. Strategic initiatives were also detailed in aviation analytics, PNT services, and D2D/NTN advances, positioning the company for ongoing growth in mission-critical communications markets.

  • Desch said, "Iridium has retired close to 30 million shares of stock and returned over $1.2 billion of capital in dividends and share repurchases" since 2021, citing capital returns as a core differentiator among satellite operators.
  • O'Neill reported, "the Q4 improvement in equipment sales shows continued strong support for Iridium services" as normalization follows pandemic-driven channel disruptions.
  • Desch addressed industry dynamics, describing Iridium’s offerings as "complementary to Starlink's effort," with global coverage and mission-critical focus distinguishing its service profile from broader VSAT disruptors.
  • O'Neill indicated fixed contract terms with major IoT partners would shield near-term reported revenue from subscriber plan changes that are increasing quarterly seasonality.
  • The company expects the new Iridium NTN Direct D2D product to enter commercial market in 2026, noting chip development timelines may cap the initial financial contribution until 2027 and beyond.

Industry glossary

  • OEBITDA: Operational EBITDA, defined by Iridium as earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for certain items to better reflect ongoing operating performance.
  • EMSS: Enhanced Mobile Satellite Services, the fixed-price contract vehicle for U.S. government global satellite communications services.
  • PNT: Position, Navigation, and Timing, an Iridium hosted payload offering for resilient, satellite-delivered secure timing and geolocation capabilities.
  • D2D: Direct-to-Device, enabling direct satellite connectivity to consumer and IoT devices without intermediary ground terminals.
  • NTN: Non-Terrestrial Networks, newly standardized communications linking satellites directly to consumer and machine endpoints.
