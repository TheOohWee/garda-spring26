Seagate (STX) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 12:04PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, October 22, 2024 at 5:00 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Dave Mosley
  • Chief Financial Officer — Gianluca Romano

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $2.17 billion, up 15% sequentially and 49% year over year, surpassing the midpoint of guidance due to stronger-than-expected mass capacity mix and pricing.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Profit -- $723 million, rising 24% quarter over quarter, with margins expanding by 240 basis points sequentially to 33.3%, the highest in over ten years, primarily led by the HDD segment.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Income -- $442 million, a 35% sequential increase, delivering a 20.4% non-GAAP operating margin.
  • Non-GAAP Earnings Per Share (EPS) -- $1.58 per diluted share, at the high end of guidance, supported by improved product mix and cost discipline.
  • HDD Exabyte Shipments -- 128 exabytes, up 20% sequentially, with nearline shipment reaching 109 exabytes versus 84 exabytes in the previous quarter.
  • Mass Capacity Revenue -- $1.7 billion, up 21% sequentially, now representing a record 93% of total HDD exabytes.
  • Legacy Products Revenue -- $270 million, contributing approximately 12% of total revenue, as expected declines were offset by mass capacity growth.
  • Other Businesses Revenue -- $164 million, representing 8% of revenue, with 25% year-over-year growth mainly from systems, SSD, and refurbished drives under circularity programs.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $27 million, reflecting working capital normalization to support higher demand, with management expecting improvement in upcoming quarters.
  • Capital Expenditure -- $68 million, with plans to stay at the low end of the 4%-6% long-term target range for the fiscal year.
  • Quarterly Dividend -- Increased nearly 3% to $0.72 per share, totaling $147 million returned to shareholders during the quarter.
  • Liquidity and Debt -- Ended the quarter with $2.7 billion in liquidity and a total debt of $5.7 billion, with over 90% of maturities in fiscal 2027 and beyond and a net leverage ratio of 3.2 times.
  • Product Ramp -- Aggressive scaling of 24TB and 28TB PMR platform, now the second-highest revenue generator and representing over 20% of nearline revenue in the period.
  • Mozaic HAMR Platform -- Expanded customer qualifications completed across segments, with revenue ramp for qualified customers expected mid-calendar 2025.
  • December Quarter Guidance -- Projected revenue between $2.15 billion and $2.45 billion, operating margin in the low-20% range, and non-GAAP EPS of $1.85 ± $0.20.
  • Build-to-Order Model -- Provides multi-quarter demand visibility, with most agreements characterized as fully committed orders.
  • Factory Utilization -- Over 90% in key fabs, with incremental exabyte capacity obtainable via technology transitions instead of adding manufacturing lines.
  • Pricing -- Gianluca Romano stated, "the pricing environment continued to be positive for the industry" and continues to support gross margin improvement.
  • HAMR Impact on Margins -- Management indicated, "no reason why HAMR should not be accretive to our gross margin [in the short term]" due to cost advantages per terabyte over legacy PMR technology.

Risks

  • Macroeconomic uncertainty in China remains "a headwind for the VIA business in that market," which could affect regional growth dynamics.
  • Management cautioned that additional supply to meet unexpectedly high demand would require "very long lead time" investments and could not be rapidly deployed.
  • Free cash flow remains constrained by working capital normalization and may take "a couple of more quarters" to fully rebound, potentially affecting near-term cash profile.

Summary

Seagate Technology (STX +0.47%) reported substantial sequential and year-over-year growth in revenue, gross profit, and margin metrics, driven primarily by increased mass capacity and nearline HDD demand from global cloud and enterprise customers. Management confirmed aggressive ramping of high-capacity PMR products and broader adoption of its Mozaic HAMR platform, with volume qualification for key customers targeted for mid-calendar 2025. The build-to-order sales model has enabled strong demand visibility and high factory utilization, positioning Seagate to manage predictable exabyte growth without further manufacturing expansion. The company raised its quarterly dividend and projects continued margin expansion, citing technological advantage and favorable pricing trends. Management highlighted that any need for additional supply due to unanticipated demand surges would require significant lead time, and ongoing macroeconomic headwinds in China could present risk to specific business segments.

  • Dave Mosley emphasized that market share will "re-equilibrate" as cyclical demand returns and the company's technology transitions progress.
  • The company stated HDDs offer "approximately 6 times lower cost per terabyte" and "10 times lower embodied carbon per terabyte" compared to NAND storage, supporting both economic and sustainability positioning.
  • The ongoing technology transition enables exabyte capacity gains through aerial density improvements rather than new factory investments.
  • Visibility from build-to-order contracts currently extends up to nine months, lending confidence to fiscal year planning.
  • HAMR product ramp is expected to be accretive to margin even at initial volumes, according to management comments.

Industry glossary

  • PMR (Perpendicular Magnetic Recording): Traditional HDD data recording method, with bits magnetized perpendicularly to the platter surface to increase density.
  • HAMR (Heat-Assisted Magnetic Recording): Next-generation HDD technology utilizing localized heating to enable higher data storage density per platter.
  • Nearline Drives: Enterprise HDDs designed for secondary storage and cloud applications, offering high capacity and reliability but not for primary real-time workloads.
  • CMR (Conventional Magnetic Recording): Standard HDD data writing technique preceding SMR, known for broad compatibility and performance.
  • SMR (Shingled Magnetic Recording): HDD recording method increasing capacity by overlapping tracks, requiring specific host support and compatible workloads.
  • VIA (Video and Image Applications): Segment focused on surveillance, smart city, and analytics workloads that require large-storage and video-optimized drives.
  • CSP (Cloud Service Provider): Third-party enterprises supplying cloud storage and computing resources to customers across multiple industries.
