Leggett and Platt LEG Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 13, 2026 at 8:11AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 12, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Karl Glassman
  • Chief Financial Officer — Benjamin Burns
  • President — J. Hagale
  • Executive Vice President, Strategy and Investor Relations — R. Smith
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Ryan Kleiboeker

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $939 million for the fourth quarter, a decline of 11%, attributed mainly to sales weakness at a specific customer, retailer merchandising changes in Adjustable Bed and Specialty Foam, continued weak residential demand, supply chain disruptions in Automotive, and lower Hydraulic Cylinders demand.
  • Segment Sales Breakdown -- Bedding Products down 11%, Specialized Products down 21% (driven by Aerospace divestiture), Furniture, Flooring & Textile Products down 3% for the quarter.
  • Quarterly Earnings -- EBIT of $32 million and adjusted EBIT of $48 million, with adjusted EBIT down $8 million primarily due to lower volume and loss from the divested aerospace business, partly offset by metal margin expansion and restructuring benefit.
  • Earnings Per Share -- Reported EPS of $0.18; adjusted EPS of $0.22, up 5% compared to adjusted EPS of $0.21 in the prior year's quarter.
  • Full-Year Sales -- $4.05 billion, down 7%, due to ongoing residential weakness, specific customer issues, merchandising changes, divestitures, automotive and hydraulic cylinder demand, and restructuring-related sales losses.
  • Full-Year EBIT and Adjustments -- EBIT increased by $786 million due to non-recurrence of prior year goodwill impairment; adjusted EBIT declined by $4 million to $263 million, reflecting lower volume, partially offset by restructuring benefit and metal margin expansion.
  • Full-Year EPS -- $1.69 reported; $1.05 adjusted, flat with 2024 adjusted EPS.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $338 million, up $33 million, driven primarily by working capital improvements; year-end adjusted working capital at 11.6% of annualized sales, a 140 basis point decrease.
  • Debt Reduction -- Debt reduced by $376 million supported by Aerospace divestiture proceeds, operational cash flow, and real estate sales; net debt to adjusted EBITDA improved from 3.8x to 2.4x.
  • Restructuring Plan -- Substantially completed; expected to deliver a $70 million EBIT run rate benefit with a total cost of $80 million, half non-cash.
  • Guidance for 2026 Sales -- Expected between $3.8 billion and $4.0 billion, a decrease of 1%-6%, including a negative 3% impact from 2025 divestitures.
  • 2026 Segment Volume Outlook -- Volume projected flat to down low single digits overall: Bedding Products and Specialized Products segments down low single digits; Furniture, Flooring & Textile Products flat.
  • 2026 Adjusted EPS Guidance -- $1.00 to $1.20; includes restructuring cost impact ($0.02 to $0.11 per share), costs from Somnigroup unsolicited offer ($0.05 to $0.08 per share), and gains from real estate sales ($0.11 to $0.25 per share).
  • 2026 EBIT Margin Guidance -- Adjusted EBIT margin expected in the 6.3%-7.0% range for the year.
  • 2026 Operating Cash Flow -- Expected between $225 million and $275 million; no working capital benefit anticipated, with tight cash flow focus.
  • 2026 Capital Expenditures -- CapEx of $100 million to $115 million, reflecting delayed initiatives and replacement for bedding facility equipment lost to fire.
  • 2026 Segment Margin Expectations -- Bedding margins up by 150 basis points; Specialized margins down 150 basis points (post-Aerospace adjustment); Furniture, Flooring & Textile margins to remain flat.
  • Balance Sheet Priorities -- Most excess cash flow in 2026 will be used to reduce net debt; focus remains on leverage target of 2x, with capital allocation priorities unchanged: organic growth, strategic acquisitions, share repurchases, and dividends.
  • Customer and Market Trends -- Housing, consumer confidence, and general affordability emphasized as primary industry demand drivers; management does not forecast market recovery in 2026 guidance.
  • Aerospace Divestiture -- Completed in Q3; after-tax proceeds used to retire commercial paper and accelerate deleveraging.
  • Somnigroup Discussions -- Company signed a nondisclosure agreement and six-month standstill with Somnigroup concerning an unsolicited offer; management will not comment further on these discussions.
  • Product and Geographic Initiatives -- Opened furniture facility in Vietnam; focused on expanding Specialty Foam, automotive innovation pipeline, textile penetration in medical nonwovens, and regional sourcing in Automotive highlighted as ongoing strategic efforts.
  • Ongoing Cost Actions -- Additional cost improvement opportunities under review, including further footprint optimization and a small Flooring Products facility consolidation in North Carolina.
  • Segment-Specific Demand -- Bedding market expected flat to up low single digits; Specialized Products sales (excluding Aerospace) expected flat to slightly up as currency benefits offset automotive and hydraulic declines; Furniture, Flooring & Textiles growth led by Geo Components and nonresidential Fabric Converting with continued softness in Home Furniture and Flooring.
  • Metal Margin Expansion -- The benefit seen in second half of 2025 is projected to continue through all of 2026 in Bedding segment.

RISKS

  • Residential markets, comprising about half of revenue, remain in a "multiyear depression" with demand "well below average cycle levels," and management cannot predict timing of recovery.
  • 2026 sales guidance incorporates an expected decrease of 1%-6% and anticipates flat to low single-digit volume declines in two major segments.
  • Bedding Products and Specialized Products segments expect year-over-year volume declines, with Specialized margins expected to decrease by 150 basis points.
  • Persistent weak consumer demand in Home Furniture and Flooring, as well as raw material price pressures and currency drag, continue to constrain margins in Furniture, Flooring & Textile Products.

SUMMARY

The restructuring plan significantly outperformed initial expectations, delivering $70 million in anticipated annual EBIT benefit at a lower total cost than planned and is now essentially complete. The company reduced net debt by $376 million, primarily from the Aerospace divestiture, improved working capital, and real estate sales, lowering its leverage ratio to 2.4x. Guidance for 2026 projects lower revenue and flat to declining volumes in key segments, with adjusted EPS expected to decrease or remain flat based on the midpoint of the forecast. Management does not assume a market recovery in its guidance and highlights that residential end markets remain deeply depressed. Excess cash flow will primarily target further deleveraging, with capital allocation priorities unchanged even amid ongoing strategic activity, including its nondisclosure agreement and standstill with Somnigroup.

  • Plans to expand in Specialty Foam, automotive innovations, and nonresidential textiles remain a focus, with a new Vietnam facility operational for Home Furniture supply chain optimization.
  • Further cost and efficiency benefits may arise from additional footprint optimization actions, though management has not yet quantified these opportunities.
  • Exposure to macro drivers such as housing, affordability, and consumer confidence underscores continued caution in the company's 2026 outlook.
  • Quarterly results are expected to follow historical seasonality, with management flagging a potential weather-driven sales dip in the first quarter.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Metal Margin: The earnings or profit margin derived from the difference between input metal costs and selling prices of finished metal components, often referenced in steel-based product businesses.
  • Adjusted EBIT: Earnings before interest and tax adjusted for one-time or non-cash charges, used for clearer analysis of recurring operational performance.
  • Geo Components: Segment focused on geosynthetics and related materials for construction, landscaping, and infrastructure applications within the company’s Textile Products offering.
  • Footprint Optimization: The process of streamlining or restructuring facilities and operations to improve cost efficiency and margins, often involving consolidations or closures.
