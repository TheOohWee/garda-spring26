NNN REIT (NNN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 12:12PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, February 11, 2026 at 10:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Stephen Horn
  • Chief Financial Officer — Vincent Chao

TAKEAWAYS

  • Annual Acquisitions -- Over $900 million in acquisitions completed, the highest annual volume in company history.
  • AFFO per Share -- $3.44, up 2.7% from 2024, with Q4 AFFO per share of $0.87, up 6.1% year over year.
  • Core FFO per Share -- $3.41 for the year and $0.87 for Q4, each up 2.7% and 6.1% respectively year over year.
  • Annualized Base Rent -- $928 million at quarter end, an increase of nearly 8% compared to 7% in the prior quarter.
  • Occupancy -- 98.3% at year end, up 80 basis points sequentially from the previous quarter, in line with the company's long-term average.
  • Dividend -- Quarterly dividend of $0.60 announced, a 3.4% increase, producing a 5.5% annualized yield and a 69% AFFO payout ratio.
  • AFFO per Share Guidance -- 2026 guidance range set at $3.52 to $3.58; midpoint represents 3.2% year-over-year growth.
  • Acquisition and Disposition Guidance -- 2026 acquisition midpoint of $600 million funded primarily with $130 million of dispositions and approximately $210 million in free cash flow; approach remains leverage neutral.
  • Portfolio Size -- 3,692 single-tenant properties at year end.
  • Cap Rates -- Q4 acquisitions at a 7.4% initial cash cap rate; company anticipates slight compression (5-10 basis points) in cap rates moving into mid-2026.
  • G&A Expense -- G&A was 4.9% of total revenue for the quarter and 5.1% for the year; slight increase attributed to end of a free rent period, promotions, and a new executive hire.
  • Leverage and Debt -- Net lease leverage steady at 5.6x, with weighted average debt maturity of 10.8 years; no encumbered assets and $1.2 billion of available liquidity reported.
  • Disposition Activity -- $190 million in dispositions for the year, $82 million in Q4 which included 18 income-producing assets and 42 vacant; average vacant disposition cap rate was 6.4%.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Approximately $51 million generated after dividend in Q4.
  • Lease Renewal and New Leasing -- 55 of 64 renewals completed ahead of the average 85% renewal rate, with renewed rent roll at 104% of prior rent; four new leases to new tenants at 109% of prior rent.
  • Guided Bad Debt -- 2026 outlook includes a 75 basis-point bad debt assumption.
  • Weighted Average Lease Escalator -- Portfolio escalator remains at 1.5% after recent acquisition activity.
  • Lease Term -- Weighted average lease duration for new acquisitions exceeded 18 years, and portfolio average is 10.2 years.

RISKS

  • Chao stated, "we have included 75 basis points of bad debt in our full year outlook, which we think is prudently conservative to start the year," reflecting elevated credit loss assumptions despite no material changes to the watch list.
  • Horn commented, "ideally, you want to be slightly above that, but we do deal with retailers. So with lease terms that come up, so we do get assets back," indicating the risk of occupancy declines given tenant turnover.
  • Company anticipates a higher blended cap rate for 2026 dispositions, with Horn saying, "on income-producing assets, I would expect a little bit of an elevated cap rate selling the assets."

SUMMARY

Management announced a 36th consecutive annual dividend increase and reported record acquisition volume funded by retained cash flow and dispositions without increasing leverage. The company completed a $400 million note repayment, executed a $300 million delayed draw term loan, and reduced revolver interest rates through a facility amendment. Free cash flow and a conservative payout ratio contributed to self-funding of investment to support per share earnings growth. Portfolio pruning continued with significant disposition activity, especially of vacancies and select income-producing assets, to maintain leasing strength and prepare for future tenant issues. Annualized base rent growth was driven by acquisitions, with the guidance midpoint for 2026 incorporating $600 million in new acquisitions and a 3.2% projected AFFO per share growth. Company leaders emphasized stability in cap rates to start the new year with an expectation for mild compression later in 2026.

  • No material changes occurred in the company’s watch list, and tenant exposure remains idiosyncratic, notably including AMC and At Home.
  • Renewal execution resulted in over 100% recapture rates and higher run rates for both renewal and newly leased space, indicating ongoing demand for assets.
  • Management stated that most of the Q4 improvement in occupancy was due to vacant asset sales rather than leasing, pointing to continued emphasis on disposition as a vacancy resolution tool.
  • Rent coverage in the portfolio is described as stable or increasing in certain segments, with no reductions relative to historic average levels.
  • Weighted average lease escalators were unaffected by 2025's acquisition activity, confirming long-term portfolio stability on rent growth assumptions.
  • The company addressed G&A increases as temporary, driven by end-of-period incentives and organizational investments.
  • Management continues to expect normalized annual lease termination fees between $3 million and $4 million, following an elevated $11 million in 2025.
  • The car wash sector represents a stable and high-rent-coverage investment, with management emphasizing selectivity and low-risk exposure.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AFFO: Adjusted Funds From Operations; a REIT-specific measure of recurring cash flow that excludes certain non-cash and non-recurring items.
  • Cap Rate: Capitalization Rate; the ratio of net operating income to property asset value or purchase price, used to measure investment yield.
  • NOI: Net Operating Income; rental and other income from property operations after deducting operating expenses but prior to financing costs and taxes.
