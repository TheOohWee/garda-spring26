Coinbase (COIN) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 6:02PM EST

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $1.9 billion in fiscal Q3 ended Sept. 30, 2025, driven by expanded product offerings and platform usage.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $801 million in fiscal Q3, reflecting higher profitability in core operations.
  • Net income -- $433 million in fiscal Q3, with adjusted net income of $421 million after excluding significant below-the-line items.
  • Consumer spot trading volume -- $59 billion in fiscal Q3, representing a 37% sequential increase, attributed to new assets and advanced trader engagement.
  • Consumer transaction revenue -- $844 million, up 30% quarter-over-quarter, with a higher mix of advanced trading impacting the average fee rate.
  • Institutional transaction revenue -- $135 million, a 122% sequential increase, mainly due to derivatives and the completed Deribit acquisition.
  • Derivatives volume (Coinbase + Deribit) -- Over $840 billion in the quarter, supported by all-time highs in U.S. derivatives volumes and market share.
  • Subscription and services revenue -- $747 million, growing 14% sequentially, anchored by USDC balances and loan activity.
  • Assets on platform -- $516 billion at quarter end, signaling expanding client adoption.
  • USDC average held on platform -- $15 billion, positioning Coinbase as the largest contributor to USDC's all-time high market cap of $74 billion.
  • Operating expenses -- $1.4 billion, a 9% decrease quarter-over-quarter, with technology & development, general & administrative, and sales & marketing up 14% collectively to $1.1 billion, largely due to headcount and USDC rewards.
  • Full-time employees -- 4,795 at quarter end, reflecting 12% sequential headcount growth.
  • Spot tradable assets in U.S. -- Expanded from 300 to over 40,000, enabled by DEX integration.
  • Outlook for October transaction revenue -- Expected to be approximately $385 million.
  • Fiscal Q4 subscription and services revenue guidance -- Estimated at $710 million-$790 million.
  • Fiscal Q4 technology & development and general & administrative expense guidance -- Combined forecast of $925 million-$975 million, about half due to Deribit and Echo acquisitions.
  • Fiscal Q4 sales and marketing guidance -- Anticipated range of $215 million-$315 million, to be determined by marketing and USDC balances.
  • Deribit acquisition -- Contributed $52 million revenue and $30 million expense (including $16 million deal-related amortization) since closing on August 14.
  • White glove service for advanced traders -- Rolled out for select customers, focusing on high-value trader retention and experience.
  • Coinbase developer platform -- Adopted by 264 institutions, including major financial firms and fintechs, as a recurring strategic infrastructure revenue driver.
  • Stablecoin market cap -- Overall stablecoin market cap reached $300 billion, underlining broader payment adoption.
  • Everything Exchange milestone -- December 17 product event scheduled to showcase ongoing asset class expansion and innovation progress.

Summary

Coinbase (COIN 0.69%) reported a marked acceleration in both spot and derivatives trading volumes, with consumer spot trading growing faster than the global market and the derivatives portfolio achieving all-time highs after the Deribit acquisition. Management provided direct guidance for fiscal fourth quarter 2025, forecasting continued revenue strength across transaction, subscription, and services lines, while also signaling higher operating expenses linked to M&A and headcount. The company achieved a significant expansion in listed assets via DEX integration and emphasized progress toward its strategic ambitions, including broad cross-asset trading and scaling the Everything Exchange framework.

  • Armstrong said, "We executed on that vision by expanding spot coverage, growing our derivatives offering and laying the groundwork for new asset classes on our platform."
  • Haas stated, "We saw strong native unit inflows across USDC balances in Coinbase products, average loan balances across our institutional financing products and assets under custody."
  • The management emphasized the impact of automation, with 65% of customer support already automated and plans to deploy LLM agents for compliance in 2026.
  • Armstrong highlighted the successful deployment of redundancy and infrastructural investments, enabling uninterrupted operations during record trading volume on October 10, noting the platform "operated very well without disruption while other exchanges experienced outages."
  • Haas remarked, "Deribit is already the market leader in options. They had over 75% market share for options. Notably, this is all non-U.S. There is path to grow the market for options in the U.S. That is going to be a multi-quarter road map of bringing both the regulatory licenses and product to bear in the U.S, but we think that's a huge opportunity for us," pointing to multi-quarter regulatory and product roadmaps ahead.

Industry glossary

  • DEX (Decentralized Exchange): A peer-to-peer trading platform enabling direct cryptocurrency transactions without an intermediary.
  • USDC: USD Coin, a fiat-collateralized stablecoin pegged to the U.S. dollar, used for payments and settlement on blockchain networks.
  • CFTC-regulated 24/7 perpetual style futures: Derivative contracts overseen by the Commodity Futures Trading Commission, enabling continuous trading of crypto futures without expiration.
  • Sequencer fees: Transaction fees earned for validating and ordering blockchain transactions, particularly on Layer 2 solutions like Base.
  • Base (Layer 2): Coinbase's Ethereum-compatible scaling solution used to process transactions more efficiently and with lower fees.
  • White glove service: High-touch, concierge-level support for select high-value traders, offering personalized account management and expedited resolution.
