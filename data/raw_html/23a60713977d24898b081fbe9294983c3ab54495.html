Management conveyed that market momentum from Q4 persisted into early Q1, supported by lower rate volatility and increased MBS market liquidity. The company emphasized a barbell strategy in its coupon selection, shifting toward both deep discount MBS and premium bonds with robust call protection as market conditions evolved. Funding conditions improved significantly in 2026, with repo rates averaging SOFR plus 15 basis points and haircuts at historical lows. Management confirmed liquidity at 54% of shareholders’ equity, with collateralized assets and hedged positions tailored to guard against prepayment risk amid rising refinance potential. The firm said it anticipated further policy announcements that may affect the mortgage market, but noted that the lowest hanging fruit has been picked regarding administrative measures to compress spreads further.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • MBS (Mortgage-Backed Securities): Debt securities backed by a pool of residential mortgages, whose principal and interest payments are passed through to investors.
  • CPR (Conditional Prepayment Rate): A measure of the annualized rate at which prepayments are made on a pool of mortgages, typically stated as a percentage.
  • TBA (To-Be-Announced): A forward contract for the purchase or sale of agency mortgage-backed securities, where specific securities are defined at a later date.
  • DUS: Delegated Underwriting and Servicing—agency commercial mortgage-backed securities issued by Fannie Mae with specific prepayment protections.
  • SOFR (Secured Overnight Financing Rate): A benchmark interest rate for dollar-denominated derivatives and loans, based on the cost of borrowing cash overnight collateralized by U.S. Treasury securities.
  • OIS (Overnight Index Swap): An interest rate swap in which a fixed rate is exchanged for a floating rate tied to a published overnight index such as SOFR.
  • OAS (Option-Adjusted Spread): The yield spread of a fixed income security relative to a benchmark yield curve, adjusted for embedded options such as prepayment risk.
  • Repo (Repurchase Agreement): A form of short-term borrowing for dealers in government securities; the dealer sells the securities to investors and agrees to repurchase them at a later date.
  • GSE (Government-Sponsored Entity): A financial services corporation created by the U.S. Congress, such as Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac, to enhance the flow of credit to specific sectors of the economy.
  • G-fees: Guarantee fees charged by GSEs to guarantee principal and interest payments on MBS, usually expressed in basis points of the loan balance.
