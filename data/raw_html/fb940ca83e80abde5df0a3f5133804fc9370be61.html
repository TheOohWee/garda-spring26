MSC Industrial (MSM) Q2 2026 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 1, 2026 at 5:24PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Apr. 1, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Martina McIsaac
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Gregory Clark
  • VP, Investor Relations and Treasurer — Ryan Mills

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Average Daily Sales (ADS) -- 2.9% year-over-year growth, below the previously outlined midpoint of 4.5%, due to headwinds from organizational changes, weather, and a partial government shutdown.
  • Gross Margin -- 41.1%, an increase of 10 basis points year over year and 40 basis points sequentially, reflecting effective pricing actions.
  • Volume -- Decreased 4% year over year, including a 100 basis point decline attributable to weather and the partial government shutdown.
  • Pricing Contribution -- Price actions contributed approximately 6.5% to ADS growth in the fiscal second quarter.
  • Operating Margin -- Reported at 7.1%, while adjusted operating margin reached 7.5%, representing a 40 basis point improvement year over year, and falling within the guided range of 7.3%-7.9%.
  • Adjusted Operating Expenses -- Improved by 20 basis points as a percentage of sales compared to the prior year, supported by headcount reductions and productivity enhancements.
  • GAAP and Adjusted EPS -- GAAP EPS was $0.76, up from $0.70 prior year; adjusted EPS increased 14% to $0.82, from $0.72 prior year.
  • Free Cash Flow Conversion -- Achieved 173% in the quarter and 86% fiscal year-to-date, trending toward the annual target of approximately 90% of net income.
  • Capital Returned to Shareholders -- $49 million during the quarter and $110 million fiscal year-to-date in dividends and share repurchases.
  • Solution Growth (Vending & In-Plant) -- Vending machines installed increased 8% to around 30,400, and In-Plant programs rose 9% to 423; both segments accounted for 20% each of total net sales, with average daily sales in each up 8%.
  • Sales Force Optimization -- Structural sales and service changes resulted in a permanent reduction of 130 customer-facing roles, with 158 headcount down sequentially, and total company headcount reduced by over 400 in the last 12 months.
  • Third-Quarter Guidance -- Average daily sales growth expected in the 5%-7% range, gross margin around 41%, and adjusted operating margin in the 9.7%-10.3% range.
  • Pricing Outlook for H2 -- Management expects a 6.5%-7% year-over-year price benefit in the second half, given tougher comparisons in the fourth quarter.
  • Balance Sheet -- Net debt stood at approximately $466 million, or 1.2x EBITDA; AR securitization increased by $50 million during the quarter.

SUMMARY

Management highlighted that a supplier growth forum generated nearly 10,000 opportunities with a potential value of approximately $500 million spanning the near- and long-term, aiming to boost cross-selling and partnership-driven expansion. Sales to core customers improved about 6% year over year and continued to outpace company-wide growth rates, while National Accounts shifted from flat in February to mid-single-digit growth in March, demonstrating recovery momentum post headcount adjustment. Inventories were intentionally built up to ensure supply security as industrial demand recovers; the company reported no significant signs of prebuying or customer restocking despite ongoing geopolitical and pricing pressures.

  • Martina McIsaac said, "We have greatly simplified and aligned our sales and service organizations," with an aim to drive future profitable growth through increased accountability and sales management focus.
  • The operations and procurement teams accelerated adoption of AI for planning and productivity, supporting improved headcount and compensation trends within distribution centers.
  • Management confirmed that further cost structure optimization through automation and AI remains a strategic priority to target mid-teens operating margin levels.
  • Indicators such as the Manufacturing Business Index (MBI) posting consecutive readings above 50 reinforced management's confidence in a broad-based industrial recovery, particularly within fabricated and primary metals segments.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • In-Plant Program: An onsite inventory management service integrating MSC staff and logistics directly within the customer's facility to support operational efficiency.
  • Vending (Machines): Automated dispensing systems placed at customer sites to enable controlled access and real-time tracking of inventory for MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Operations) products.
  • OEM Fastener Business: Division of MSC focused on supplying original equipment manufacturer-grade fasteners as part of its core sales portfolio.
