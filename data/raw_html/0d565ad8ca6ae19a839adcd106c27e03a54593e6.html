Varonis (VRNS) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 29, 2025 at 9:10AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, October 28, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chief Executive Officer — Yakov Faitelson

Chief Financial Officer and Chief Operating Officer — Guy Melamed

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

Weaker-than-expected renewals in both federal and non-federal on-prem subscription businesses caused results to fall below company expectations.

Management expects "increased uncertainty" according to Yakov Faitelson in the remaining on-prem subscription (OPS) business due to the decision to end-of-life the self-hosted solution by December 31, 2026.

Gross margin (non-GAAP) declined to 79.4%, compared to 85% in Q3 2024.

Net income decreased to $8.4 million, or $0.06 per diluted share, compared to $13.8 million, or $0.10 per diluted share, last year.

TAKEAWAYS

Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) -- $718.6 million, up 18% year-over-year, with 76% of ARR coming from SaaS.

Total Revenue -- $161.6 million, representing 9% year-over-year growth.

SaaS Revenue -- $125.8 million in SaaS revenue, indicating ongoing adoption momentum.

SaaS ARR Share -- Reached 76% this quarter; management targets 83% of total ARR from SaaS by year-end 2025.

Term License Subscription Revenue -- $24.8 million in term license subscription revenue; Maintenance & Services Revenue -- $10.9 million in maintenance and services revenue (non-GAAP), with renewal rates remaining over 90% in that segment (non-GAAP).

Non-GAAP Gross Profit -- $128.3 million (non-GAAP), yielding a gross margin of 79.4%.

Non-GAAP Operating Expenses -- $128.1 million in non-GAAP operating expenses, with operating income of $0.2 million and a margin of 0.1%.

ARR Contribution Margin -- ARR contribution margin was 16.3%, up from 15% last year.

Free Cash Flow (YTD) -- $111.6 million of free cash flow, up from $88.6 million in the same period last year.

Net Income -- Net income was $8.4 million, or $0.06 per diluted share, compared to $13.8 million, or $0.10 per diluted share, last year.

Cash, Cash Equivalents, and Marketable Securities -- $1.1 billion as of September 30, 2025.

Share Repurchase Authorization -- $150 million approved by the Board, citing a "strong balance sheet with over $1 billion in liquidity, and our healthy free cash flow generation" as of September 30, 2025, according to Guy Melamed.

Headcount Reduction -- 5% reduction in headcount to reallocate resources to areas with the highest ROI.

Guidance Update -- Q4 2025 revenue expected at $165 million to $171 million (4%-8% growth); non-GAAP operating income guidance of breakeven to $3 million for Q4 2025; non-GAAP net income per share of $0.02–$0.04 for Q4 2025.

Full-Year Guidance -- For fiscal 2025 (period ending Dec. 31, 2025), ARR of $730 million to $738 million (14%-15% growth); total revenue of $615.2 million to $621.2 million (12%-13% growth); free cash flow of $120 million to $125 million; non-GAAP operating loss projected at negative $8.2 million to negative $5.2 million; non-GAAP net income per share of $0.12–$0.13.

End of Life Announcement -- All self-hosted solution support to cease December 31, 2026; management expects greater uncertainty for remaining OPS business accordingly.

Federal Business -- Approximately 5% of total ARR; underperformance cited as reason for reducing federal team size.

Strategic Acquisitions -- Cyral acquisition produced a next-gen database activity monitoring SaaS product; SlashNext acquisition led to launch of Interceptor, an AI-native e-mail security solution.

Microsoft Partnership -- Ongoing investment to deepen integration for secure Copilot adoption, highlighted in strategic commentary.

SUMMARY

Varonis Systems (VRNS 6.22%) completed its SaaS transition ahead of schedule, with SaaS now comprising a majority of ARR and becoming the primary growth driver. The company experienced unexpected declines in renewal rates for both federal and non-federal on-prem subscription businesses—especially in the final two weeks of the quarter—prompting increased conservatism in guidance and a significant headcount reduction. Management responded by accelerating the end-of-life announcement for its self-hosted solution and introduced new SaaS-native products stemming from recent acquisitions. Strategic partnerships, particularly with Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT), remain a focal point for platform extension and future revenue opportunity. Operating income and non-GAAP net income declined year-over-year, while free cash flow and cash reserves increased year-to-date, supporting a new $150 million share repurchase authorization announced October 28, 2025.

Management stated, "we are baking in additional conservatism to our guidance and have assumed even lower renewal rates in our OPS business for Q4 2025."

Guy Melamed explained, "we plan to end the year with 83% of our total ARR coming from SaaS," referring to fiscal 2025 and projecting acceleration of the SaaS business relative to total operations.

Management confirmed, "federal business has always been around 5% of our total ARR," and cited headwinds from federal underperformance as a driver for resource reallocation.

The end-of-life of the self-hosted solution is expected to affect customer migration dynamics and remaining on-prem subscription revenue streams.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): Recurring revenue components of subscriptions and contracts, measured on an annualized basis, used to gauge business momentum in SaaS and recurring models.

OPS (On-Prem Subscription): Varonis's legacy on-premises subscription business, distinct from SaaS in terms of delivery and management.

MDDR (Managed Data Detection & Response): Varonis's managed, SaaS-based security service for automated data threat detection and remediation.

DAM (Database Activity Monitoring): Software that monitors and analyzes database activity for security and compliance, with Varonis's offering delivered natively through SaaS following the Cyral acquisition.

DSPM (Data Security Posture Management): Solutions that assess and manage data security risks within cloud environments.

Copilot: Microsoft’s AI-powered assistant product, referenced in the context of Varonis security for organizations adopting Copilot.
