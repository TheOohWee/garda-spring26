UPS (UPS) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 11:31AM EST

Tuesday, July 29, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated Revenue -- United Parcel Service (UPS +0.06%) reported $21.2 billion with consolidated operating profit of $1.9 billion and operating margin of 8.8%.
  • Diluted Earnings Per Share -- $1.55, including a net charge of $29 million or $0.04 per share due to transformation strategy costs and business divestiture impacts.
  • U.S. Domestic Package Segment -- Revenue of $14.1 billion, average daily volume (ADV) down 7.3%, operating profit of $982 million, and operating margin of 7%.
  • International Package Segment -- Revenue of $4.5 billion, up 2.6%, ADV up 3.9%, operating profit of $682 million, and operating margin of 15.2%.
  • China–U.S. Trade Lane Volume -- Year-over-year ADV declined 34.8% during May and June, lowering overall international operating margin.
  • China to Rest of World Trade -- ADV increased by 22.4%, while export ADV overall increased 6.1%.
  • Ground Saver Product -- ADV declined 23% year over year, with higher-than-anticipated delivery expenses totaling $85 million pressuring cost structure.
  • Amazon Volume Glide Down -- Amazon ADV declined 13% for the first half of 2025, with management projecting pace to accelerate to approximately 30% declines in both Q3 and Q4.
  • Network Reconfiguration & Closures -- 74 buildings closed and 155 operations completed, enabling progress on strategic goals and $3.5 billion expense reduction target.
  • Automation Penetration -- 64% of volume processed through automated facilities versus 60% last year.
  • Supply Chain Solutions Segment -- Revenue of $2.7 billion, $594 million lower than last year, with 90% of revenue decrease due to the Coyote divestiture.
  • Healthcare Logistics and Digital -- Healthcare logistics revenue grew 5.7%, and UPS Digital (including Roadie and Happy Returns) revenue grew 26.4%.
  • Cash Flow and Shareholder Returns -- $2.7 billion year-to-date cash from operations, free cash flow of $742 million, and $2.7 billion in dividends paid year-to-date.
  • Expense Reduction Progress -- 25 million operational hour reduction target for the year on track, with nearly 9,500 operational positions eliminated year-to-date.
  • Capital Allocation -- 2025 capital expenditures planned at approximately $3.5 billion; $5.5 billion in dividends expected, pending approval; $1 billion in share repurchases completed; and projected tax rate of 23.5% for the year.

RISKS

  • Management explicitly cited "a very unsettling time" due to tariff and trade uncertainties, stating, "we are not providing any forward-looking revenue or earnings guidance."
  • Ground Saver's delivery algorithm failed to meet modeled efficiency, resulting in an unplanned $85 million in added expense with ongoing uncertainty about future cost solutions.
  • Attrition rate for part-time and full-time employees related to Amazon glide down trailed internal models, adding cost pressure and introducing uncertainty for timing of expected savings.
  • Year-over-year volume on the China–U.S. trade lane plunged 34.8% for May and June, with management flagging this segment as the most profitable trade lane and acknowledging related margin pressure.

SUMMARY

Management reported lower U.S. average daily volume but demonstrated improved revenue quality, with positive product and customer mix shifts partially offsetting declines. The strategic removal of Amazon volume, substantial network reconfiguration, and adoption of automation are on track to deliver the targeted $3.5 billion expense reduction, although execution timing faces challenges from slower-than-modeled attrition. Revenue in international markets increased, but heavy declines in China–U.S. lanes created significant pressure on international margins. Ground Saver's underperformance drove unexpected costs, and leadership is re-engaging with USPS and revising delivery models in response. Management withheld quarterly guidance due to ongoing volatility and uncertainty around tariffs, peak season visibility, and labor cost outcomes.

  • CEO Tome noted, "we are not providing any forward-looking revenue or earnings guidance," attributing this to "so much uncertainty out there" linked to both trade policy and customer forecasting.
  • Key expense savings efforts spanned variable, semi-variable, and fixed operational costs, with 25 million operational hour reductions and additional facility closures expected through year-end but subject to labor market dynamics.
  • International ADV increases were enabled by flexible capacity redeployment and prior investment in Asian hubs, with Malaysia, Vietnam, and India cited as growth contributors.
  • UPS leadership reiterated a commitment to a "stable and growing dividend," affirming balance sheet strength and robust free cash flow as support despite sector headwinds.
  • Management confirmed that voluntary separation packages for full-time drivers are essential to achieving cost targets; specifics on ultimate uptake and associated financial impact remain outstanding.
  • Healthcare logistics and digital business units demonstrated outsized growth relative to the core, with management targeting further expansion through acquisitions and service innovation.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Average Daily Volume (ADV): The average number of packages handled or shipped daily, a core operating metric for logistics firms.
  • Ground Saver: UPS's low-cost, deferred-delivery ground product, formerly SurePost, aimed primarily at e-commerce shippers seeking value delivery options.
  • De Minimis Exception: A trade policy threshold that allows for low-value goods to enter a country duty-free; its removal increases import tariffs on previously exempt shipments.
  • CEIV Certified Cold Chain: Certification confirming compliance with standards for end-to-end cold chain logistics of pharmaceuticals and complex healthcare products.
