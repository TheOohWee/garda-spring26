monday.com (MNDY) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 23, 2025 at 10:55AM EST

Monday, May 12, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Co-Chief Executive Officer & Co-Founder — Roy Mann
  • Co-Chief Executive Officer & Co-Founder — Eran Zinman
  • Chief Financial Officer — Eliran Glazer

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $282 million, representing 30% year-over-year growth.
  • Operating Profit -- Record quarterly operating profit achieved in Q1.
  • Net Income -- $58.4 million, compared to $31.7 million in the year-ago quarter.
  • Diluted Net Income per Share -- $1.10, calculated on 53 million fully diluted shares outstanding.
  • Gross Margin -- 90% for the quarter; medium to long-term expectations are in the high 80s percentage range.
  • Net Dollar Retention (NDR) -- Stable at 112% on a trailing four-quarter weighted average; guidance for year-end calls for a level slightly below 112%.
  • Research & Development Expense -- $53.8 million, or 19% of revenue, up from 16% of revenue a year ago.
  • Sales & Marketing Expense -- $135.9 million, or 48% of revenue, compared to 56% a year ago.
  • Employee Headcount -- 2,695 as of quarter-end, an increase of 187 from Q4; headcount is expected to grow approximately 30% in fiscal year 2025.
  • Cash & Cash Equivalents -- $1.53 billion at quarter close, up from $1.41 billion at the end of Q4.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $109.5 million for the quarter; adjusted free cash flow margin was 39%.
  • User AI Actions -- Over 26 million AI actions performed to date, up more than 150% since year-end 2024.
  • Enterprise Segment Growth -- Explicitly identified as the fastest growing segment, with new capabilities launched for the enterprise tier.
  • Chief Revenue Officer Appointment -- Casey George appointed as the company's first global C-level leader, bringing nearly 30 years of enterprise software experience.
  • Q2 2025 Revenue Guidance -- $292 million to $294 million, representing year-over-year growth of 24%-25%.
  • Q2 2025 Non-GAAP Operating Income Guidance -- $32 million to $34 million, with non-GAAP operating margin expected between 11%-12%.
  • Full-Year 2025 Revenue Guidance -- $1,220 million to $1,226 million, up 25%-26% year over year; includes less than 1% negative impact from FX.
  • Full-Year 2025 Non-GAAP Operating Income Guidance -- $144 million to $150 million with expected non-GAAP operating margin of approximately 12%.
  • Full-Year 2025 Adjusted Free Cash Flow Guidance -- $310 million to $316 million, with adjusted free cash flow margin of 25%-26%.

Summary

monday.com (MNDY 5.96%) reported 30% revenue growth, record operating profit, and the highest ever quarterly cash flow, with user AI actions increasing by over 150% and new enterprise product features launched. Management expects Net Dollar Retention to be slightly below 112% by year-end, citing macroeconomic uncertainty as the primary factor for this conservative outlook. Q2 and full-year 2025 guidance were provided, incorporating less than a 1% negative FX impact and conservative expectations for customer expansion. Product adoption momentum was noted across all segments, especially enterprise, with new enterprise-focused features such as AI-driven risk insights and portfolio reports. The company highlighted an expected 30% increase in headcount, primarily invested in enterprise sales and R&D. Cash and cash equivalents increased to $1.53 billion, and adjusted free cash flow margin reached 39% in the period.

  • Casey George was hired as Chief Revenue Officer, "first global C-level leader" at the company, tasked with driving further enterprise market expansion.
  • AI features saw significant adoption, but management stated, "the monetization is still early in early stages and we're experimenting with it."
  • Although NDR remained stable, CFO Glazer explained that the "guidance because we did take a more conservative approach with regards to our expectations for the end of the year because of the macroeconomic situation and the uncertainties that are still exist."
  • Geographically, demand was described as "healthy and consistent across all regions in Q1"; North America remained the strongest while Europe stabilized but lagged in performance.
  • Mid-market and enterprise customers comprised 70% of ARR in monday service, illustrating successful upmarket penetration outside core work management.
  • Q1’s strong free cash flow was attributed to seasonality, with Q2 expected to include higher compensation and event-driven outflows per management commentary.

Industry glossary

  • Net Dollar Retention (NDR): The company’s weighted average metric reflecting the percentage of recurring revenue retained and expanded from existing customers over a trailing four-quarter period, showing customer success and account expansion.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow Margin: Adjusted free cash flow divided by revenue, expressing the proportion of cash generation after operational and certain capital expenses, adjusted for major headquarters build-out costs.
  • MCP: monday.com’s capability allowing large language models (LLMs) to interact programmatically with its Work OS platform for advanced automation and integration use-cases.
  • AI Actions: AI-driven tasks within monday.com, including workflow automation, risk analysis, and decision support, that enhance user productivity and operational efficiency.
