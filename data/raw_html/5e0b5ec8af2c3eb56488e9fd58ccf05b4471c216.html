Takeaways

  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $80,200,000 for the quarter, a new company record, excluding a $9,000,000 non-recurring gain from Clean Planet Energy.
  • Full-Year Adjusted EBITDA -- $232,300,000 for 2025, up from $127,600,000 in 2024, reflecting growth but only partial-year contributions from key acquisitions.
  • EBITDA Exit Run Rate -- Over $320,000,000 annually at year-end, driven by full ownership of Long Ridge, the Wheeling acquisition, and the ammonia contract at Jefferson.
  • Rail Segment Q4 Adjusted EBITDA -- $41,300,000, comprised of $22,000,000 from Transstar and $19,300,000 from Wheeling, marking first full quarter for the acquired asset.
  • Wheeling Q4 Results -- Revenue of $43,000,000, up 8% year over year, and adjusted EBITDA up 34% year over year, with integration into Transstar initiated in late December.
  • Cost Synergy Progress -- More than half of targeted $20,000,000 in annual cost savings from the Wheeling integration implemented, remainder scheduled for first half of 2026.
  • Long Ridge Q4 EBITDA -- $36,200,000, a new high, despite 27.5 days of outages that reduced EBITDA by about $3,500,000; gas production reached 105,000 MMBtu/day, also a record.
  • Jefferson Q4 Performance -- EBITDA of $13,600,000 and revenue of $23,500,000, benefiting from the first month of the long-term ammonia export contract.
  • Repauno Phase 2 Status -- Construction on schedule, with 80,000 barrels/day capacity expected to generate $80,000,000 annual EBITDA upon start-up in early 2027.
  • Term Loan Refinancing -- Closed a $1,300,000,000 term loan at a 9.75% coupon, replacing Wheeling acquisition bridge loan; structure allows prepayment at reducing premium and further reduction if Long Ridge is sold.
  • Rail M&A Pipeline -- Four actionable acquisition targets, mostly local and directly connected to existing assets, under consideration for further rail segment growth.
  • Jefferson Organic Growth Prospects -- Three advanced contract negotiations could yield over $50,000,000 incremental annual EBITDA, involving ammonia, refined products, and Utah crude expansions with minimal additional CapEx.
  • Clean Planet Energy Write-up -- $9,000,000 one-time EBITDA gain from exchange of JV stake for 49% of global entity; Clean Planet expected to contribute recurring EBITDA from 2027.
  • Repauno Phase 3 Progress -- Permits secured for expansion, aiming to double capacity via two storage caverns (640,000 barrels each); anchor customer negotiations and construction estimates ongoing.

Summary

FTAI Infrastructure Inc. (FIP +4.68%) reported record quarterly and full-year adjusted EBITDA, reflecting substantial growth from recent acquisitions and contract wins, with only partial-year benefit from several key transactions. Management disclosed an EBITDA exit run rate meaningfully above reported numbers and highlighted successful refinancing of a major term loan, which enhances financial flexibility and supports near-term deleveraging priorities. Expanding integration of the Wheeling railroad, advancement of multiple high-EBITDA growth opportunities at Jefferson and Repauno, and targeted rail M&A underscore the company's strategy to scale its infrastructure platform and improve cash flows.

  • CEO Nicholson stated, "we exited the year at an EBITDA run rate of just over $320,000,000 annually, meaningfully higher than our reported figures."
  • The company estimates over $50,000,000 of incremental EBITDA potential from combined-rail system revenue initiatives, including increased volumes from Nippon Steel’s $100,000,000 recycling unit and new propane carloads tied to Repauno Phase 2.
  • Long Ridge monetization process is progressing within management expectations, with a target to announce a transaction in the first half of the year and proceeds earmarked for debt reduction.
  • Three new contracts at Jefferson—covering additional ammonia, refined products, and Utah crudes—each could add $10,000,000 to $25,000,000 in annual EBITDA and require almost no additional capital investment.
  • Permits for Repauno Phase 3 have been secured, and construction could begin by late 2026, pending anchor customer commitments, with operational completion required before management would consider asset monetization.
  • The Clean Planet Energy gain was excluded from core results as a one-time event; future recurring EBITDA is expected as global operations scale up beginning 2027.

Industry glossary

  • MMBtu: One million British thermal units, a standard unit of measurement for natural gas quantity.
  • PPA: Power Purchase Agreement, a long-term contract to buy electricity directly from a producer.
  • CapEx: Capital expenditures, referring to funds used by a company for acquiring, maintaining, or upgrading assets.
  • Transstar: FTAI Infrastructure Inc.'s legacy freight railroad subsidiary integrated with newly acquired Wheeling and Lake Erie Railroad assets.
