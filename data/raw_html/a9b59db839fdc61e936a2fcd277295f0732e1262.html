Earnings Call Transcript

Scholastic (SCHL) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 19, 2026 at 5:37PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, March 19, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Peter Warwick
  • Chief Financial Officer and Executive Vice President — Haji Glover
  • President, Scholastic Education Solutions; Chief Growth Officer — Jeffrey Mathews

TAKEAWAYS

  • Sale-Leaseback Proceeds -- Over $400 million in net proceeds generated by the completed sale-leaseback of major facilities, optimizing the balance sheet.
  • Share Repurchase Actions -- $147 million returned to shareholders through repurchasing more than 4,400,000 shares at an average price of $33.30 per share since December; nearly exhausting the $150 million authorization.
  • New Share Repurchase Authorization -- A $300 million share buyback program was announced, including a $200 million modified Dutch auction tender offer at $36 to $40 per share, representing about 25% of shares outstanding if fully executed; $100 million reserved for open-market repurchases.
  • Consolidated Revenue -- $329.1 million reported, down from $335.4 million, mainly due to the timing of key publishing releases and year-to-date softness in Education.
  • Adjusted Operating Loss -- $24.3 million compared to $20.9 million, with the partial-year sale-leaseback impact accounting for $3 million; excluding this, the loss was $21.3 million, close to the prior-year figure.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Approximately breakeven, negatively affected by a $6.7 million impact from the sale-leaseback; excluding this, adjusted EBITDA was $6.7 million, similar to last year.
  • Net Loss -- $3.5 million versus $1.3 million, with adjusted loss per share at $0.15, higher than last year’s $0.05 per share loss.
  • Children’s Book Publishing and Distribution Revenue -- $197.6 million, a 3% decline, attributed to publishing timing; segment profit improved to $8.9 million from $7.6 million due to higher Book Fair revenues.
  • Book Fairs Revenue -- Rose 2% to $113.3 million from growth in fair count and revenue per fair; management expects continued expansion.
  • Book Clubs Revenue -- $14.6 million, stable from last year’s $15.2 million, as increased participation offset lower start-of-year engagement.
  • Trade Publishing Revenue -- $69.7 million, down 10% due to release timing and adverse weather; full-year expectation revised to slightly below last year given tough prior-year comps.
  • Entertainment Revenue -- $16 million, up from $12.8 million, fueled by increased episodic deliveries and production services; segment EBITDA was slightly negative but projected to reach profitability in the fourth quarter.
  • Education Revenue -- $56.1 million, a 2% drop amid tight school budgets, but segment operating loss narrowed to $5.2 million from $6.9 million due to cost controls and operational improvements.
  • International Revenue -- $58.7 million, down from $59.3 million; adjusting for a $3.5 million forex benefit, comparable revenues declined chiefly due to Dog Man publication timing.
  • Cash and Debt Position -- Quarter ended with net cash of $90.6 million, reversing from net debt of $136.6 million in the prior fiscal year; credit facility fully repaid.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $407 million, boosted by real estate transaction proceeds, compared to negative $30.7 million last year.
  • Fiscal 2026 Guidance -- Adjusted EBITDA reaffirmed at $146 million to $156 million, including a $14 million partial-year sale-leaseback drag; free cash flow guidance reaffirmed above $430 million; full-year revenue expected to be flat versus last year.
  • Capital Allocation Targets -- New long-term net leverage goal of 2.0x-2.5x adjusted EBITDA announced; management stated it will be reached over time, not immediately.
  • Tariff Expense -- Company anticipates $10 million in incremental tariff costs in product cost for the current year and is monitoring for future changes.

RISKS

  • Adjusted operating loss increased to $24.3 million from $20.9 million, reflecting expenses tied to the sale-leaseback and timing-related revenue declines.
  • Net cash used by operating activities rose to $30.5 million from $12 million, primarily due to higher tax payments from sale-leaseback proceeds.
  • International segment adjusted operating loss increased to $4.7 million from $2 million due to lower revenues.
  • Trade Publishing revenue expected to end slightly below the prior year, impacted by challenging fourth-quarter comps and short-term headwinds in retail book demand.

SUMMARY

Scholastic Corporation (SCHL +0.05%) delivered results in line with expectations while using a major $400 million sale-leaseback transaction to strengthen its balance sheet and unlock capital for shareholder returns and growth initiatives. The board authorized a $300 million repurchase program featuring a $200 million tender offer priced at $36 to $40 per share, targeting a reduction of approximately one quarter of outstanding shares if fully executed. Adjusted EBITDA guidance for the year remains at $146 million to $156 million, including a $14 million impact from new lease expenses, while full-year revenue is now forecasted to be flat, with management noting continuing softness in Education and challenging Trade comps. Children’s Book Publishing and Book Fairs showed positive operational metrics through increased fair activity, higher average revenues, and improved margins, partially offsetting declines in Trade, International, and Education segments.

  • Company ended the quarter in a net cash position of $90.6 million, eliminating prior reliance on its credit line and lowering interest expense.
  • Greenlighting and production activity grew meaningfully in the Entertainment segment, setting expectations for profitability in the coming quarter and further growth into fiscal 2027.
  • Management introduced a long-term targeted net leverage range of 2.0x-2.5x adjusted EBITDA, signaling ongoing balance sheet optimization and prioritization of future capital returns.
  • Supplemental curriculum demand in Education declined, but teacher, family, and community-oriented revenue streams are proving less volatile and represent an area of targeted growth investment.
  • Management confirmed incremental tariff costs of $10 million for the year, stating continued review of policy changes that could further affect cost of goods.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Sale-Leaseback: A transaction involving the sale of property—often real estate—with an agreement to immediately lease it back, freeing up capital while retaining operational use.
  • Modified Dutch Auction Tender Offer: A repurchase mechanism where shareholders specify prices within a set range at which they will sell shares, allowing the company to buy back at the lowest price that enables the targeted volume.
  • Scholastic Dollars: A reward currency provided to schools for hosting Scholastic Book Fairs, redeemable for Scholastic products.
  • eWallet: A digital payment tool allowing families to preload funds for student use during Book Fairs.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, excluding specified one-time or non-operating items; used as a core profitability metric.
