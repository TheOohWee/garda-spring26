Ambac (AMBC) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 11, 2025 at 9:19AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Nov. 11, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Claude LeBlanc
  • Chief Financial Officer — David Trick

RISKS

  • David Trick reported, "the loss ratio increased to 84.5% in 2025 from 74.4% in 2024," citing adverse development in runoff commercial auto programs as a key factor.
  • David Trick stated, "Adjusted EBITDA from continuing operations to stockholders was a loss of $3 million compared to a sub $2 million gain in 2024," attributing the decline to higher expenses and one-time items not recurring in 2025.
  • The combined ratio for Everspan rose to 112.9% from 100.5%, indicating underwriting deterioration despite lower reported losses and LAE.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Corporate rebranding -- Ambac Financial Group (AMBC 4.14%) transitioned to Octave Specialty Group, establishing a new brand and identity as a pure-play specialty P&C insurance platform.
  • Share repurchase -- Executed repurchases totaling 3,100,000 shares, representing 6.5% of weighted average shares outstanding.
  • Corporate expense reductions -- Implemented initiatives resulting in more than a $10 million decrease in run rate adjusted corporate expenses, driven by actions like the termination of the headquarters lease.
  • Financial guarantee exit -- Closed the sale of the legacy financial guarantee business to Oaktree for $420 million.
  • M&A activity -- Completed the purchase of ArmadaCare and converted the PIVX investment to a majority stake, bringing the 2025 MGA startup cohort to three businesses.
  • Insurance distribution revenue -- Increased by 80% to $43 million, with organic growth at 40% for the period; no profit or contingent commissions included in the organic growth calculation.
  • Insurance distribution adjusted EBITDA -- Segment produced $10 million of adjusted EBITDA on a 23% margin (operating basis) and $6 million to shareholders at a 13.9% margin, both materially higher than 2024 figures.
  • Group net loss -- Reported a net loss from continuing operations of $32 million, or $0.67 per diluted share, compared to a loss of $18 million, or $0.43 per share, in 2024, mainly due to higher intangible amortization, interest, and G&A costs.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Loss of $3 million versus a gain just under $2 million in 2024, with declines primarily from the absence of FX gains, lower Everspan contribution, and higher corporate expenses.
  • Everspan premiums -- Net written premium of $18 million and net earned premium of $17 million, both sharply down from $33 million and $27 million, respectively, due to nonrenewals in auto programs.
  • Everspan expense and combined ratios -- Third quarter expense ratio was 28.4% up from 26.1%; combined ratio was 112.9% up from 100.5%, with a five-point increase in the sliding scale commission benefit partially offsetting adverse development.
  • Expense savings outlook -- Company targets approximately $30 million in adjusted corporate expenses for 2026 and expects over $17 million in reported expense savings from ongoing initiatives when fully realized.
  • Future capacity -- Total reported capacity of $1.5 billion for 2025 does not include ArmadaCare or Everspan, with management stating sufficient capacity is in place and strong capital provider interest.
  • Strategic growth drivers -- Nine new MGAs launched in 2024 and 2025, with Octave Ventures achieving 47% year-to-date organic revenue growth and planned margin expansion as MGAs scale to profitability.
  • EBITDA margin target -- Management reiterated an aspirational $80 million EBITDA goal for 2028, underpinned by both organic initiatives and buy-in options for the remaining 40% of BEAT over four years.

SUMMARY

Ambac Financial Group completed its transition to Octave Specialty Group, finalizing the sale of its financial guarantee business and executing multiple acquisitions, notably ArmadaCare and PIVX. Capital management was marked by share repurchases and expense reduction initiatives, including actions expected to exceed $17 million in reported savings. The insurance distribution segment reported substantial revenue and adjusted EBITDA growth, while the Everspan segment experienced lower premiums and a higher expense structure due to program nonrenewals. Management outlined a strategic focus on organic MGA launches, disciplined M&A, and driving operating leverage, with a stated aim to achieve a $80 million EBITDA target by 2028.

  • David Trick stated, "no profit commissions or contingent commissions that are included in the revenue numbers for the calculation of organic growth," confirming revenue quality in the insurance distribution segment.
  • Management reported sufficient capacity for expected growth with $1.5 billion in third-party capacity, excluding new contributions from ArmadaCare and Everspan.
  • Everspan's current annual run rate for written premium is projected to reach $370 million to $380 million unless year-end activity alters the trend, with expectations for growth to remain controlled into the next year.
  • Interest expense is expected to decrease materially following debt reduction, with full-year interest expense for 2026 likely at the average quarterly level from 2025.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • MGA (Managing General Agent): An insurance intermediary with underwriting authority, operating between insurers and agents, often managing niche programs and distribution relationships.
  • Loss and LAE (Loss Adjustment Expense): The sum of insurance claim losses and associated costs required to investigate and settle those claims.
  • Sliding scale commission: A commission structure where the rate payable to a producer adjusts based on the claims experience or profitability of the insurance program.
  • Combined ratio: A measure of underwriting profitability, the sum of the loss ratio and expense ratio; values above 100% indicate underwriting losses.
  • In-force programs: Insurance programs currently active and generating premium as opposed to those in runoff or discontinued.
  • Call/put dates: Contractual timeframes in which the right to purchase (call) or sell (put) a stake in a business can be exercised, typically relevant for partial MGA ownership transitions.
  • De novo loss: Initial losses recognized from newly launched insurance ventures before breakeven is achieved.
  • Runoff business: Portfolio or products actively managed but not accepting new business, generally in wind-down mode.

As Everspan is experiencing steady growth in earned premium sequentially, we continue to expect the expense ratio to improve. For the quarter, Everspan was breakeven on an adjusted EBITDA, which was down from $1.6 million in 2024.

We outlined in our investor materials certain select expense reduction initiatives, which include, for example, the termination of our corporate headquarters lease.
