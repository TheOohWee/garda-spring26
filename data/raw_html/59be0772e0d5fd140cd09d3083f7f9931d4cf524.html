Southside Bancshares (SBSI) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 29, 2026 at 2:17PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, January 29, 2026 at 12 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Keith Donahoe
  • Senior Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Julie Shamburger

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Income -- $21 million for the period, up $16.1 million or 327.2% from prior quarter, driven by the absence of the previous quarter’s restructuring loss.
  • Diluted Earnings Per Share -- $0.70, an increase of $0.54 per share compared to last quarter.
  • Full-Year Net Income -- $69.2 million, a decrease of $19.3 million or 21.8%, attributed to security portfolio restructuring.
  • Net Interest Margin -- 2.98%, a 4 basis point linked-quarter increase, with further expansion expected due to forthcoming $93 million subordinated debt redemption in mid-February.
  • Net Interest Income -- $1.5 million or 2.7% increase over previous quarter, primarily from lower funding costs and moderate loan growth.
  • Fourth-Quarter Loan Production -- $327 million, down from $500 million in the third quarter, with only $25 million funded and the remainder to be funded over six to nine subsequent quarters.
  • Total Loans Outstanding -- $4.82 billion at period end, a linked-quarter increase of $52.7 million or 1.1%, led by $29 million in construction loans, $24.1 million in commercial real estate loans, and $14.8 million in commercial loans, partially offset by a $6 million decrease in municipal loans and a $5.7 million decrease in 1-4 family residential loans.
  • Loan Pipeline -- Rebounded to just over $2 billion from $1.5 billion mid-quarter, with 42% term loans and 58% construction/commercial lines of credit.
  • Nonperforming Assets -- Increased by $2.6 million, mainly due to a $2.4 million residential condo loan, but remained low at 0.45% of total assets and concentrated in a previously disclosed $27.5 million multifamily loan.
  • Allowance for Credit Losses -- Decreased slightly to $48.3 million, with allowance for loan losses down 1 basis point to 0.94% of total loans.
  • Securities Portfolio -- Increased $147.9 million or 5.8% to $2.70 billion, based on $373 million in mainly MBS purchases, offsetting $82 million in municipal securities sales plus maturities/payments; duration reduced to 7.6 years (total securities) and 4.8 years (AFS portfolio).
  • AFS Securities Net Unrealized Loss -- Decreased to $767,000 from $15.4 million prior quarter, primarily from portfolio restructuring and market improvement.
  • Deposits -- Declined by $96.4 million or 1.4% quarter over quarter, with a $233.5 million reduction in brokered deposits, offset by increases of $40.8 million in retail deposits and $86.3 million in public fund deposits.
  • Efficiency Ratio -- Improved to 52.28% from 52.99%, due to higher total revenue.
  • Noninterest Expense -- Stable at $37.5 million, with a projected 7% year-over-year increase in 2026 (including ~$2.3 million-$2.4 million higher software/data spending and a one-time $800,000 charge for the subordinated note redemption).
  • Income Tax Expense -- $3.8 million, up $3.6 million compared to the previous quarter, reflecting normalization after prior restructuring-related loss.
  • Share Repurchases -- 369,804 shares repurchased averaging $28.94 per share; 762,000 shares remain authorized for future buybacks.
  • Liquidity -- $2.78 billion in available liquidity lines reported as of period end.
  • Capital Ratios -- All reported above regulatory "well capitalized" thresholds.
  • Strategic Focus -- Core system moving off-premise and data platform development highlighted as key 2026 tech expenses intended to drive future efficiencies, M&A readiness, and improved data utilization.
  • M&A Positioning -- Management stated openness to acquisitions in "target areas" such as Dallas, Houston, and Austin, with a focus on strategic expansion rather than size for its own sake.
  • 2026 Fee Income Outlook -- Budgeted $1.5 million increase in trust income, supported by team expansion and geographic extension into North Texas markets; additional growth expected in treasury fees and brokerage services.

RISKS

  • Net Loss from Securities Sales -- Fourth-quarter restructuring of available-for-sale securities resulted in a $7.3 million net loss, explicitly reported by management.
  • Loan Growth Payoffs Headwind -- Management flagged a "high number of those maturities" from construction projects, with loans potentially moving off the balance sheet as projects stabilize, presenting a loan growth challenge in 2026.
  • Noninterest Expense Increase -- A 7% rise is budgeted for 2026, driven by higher compensation costs, technology investments, and a one-time $800,000 charge, which may pressure future efficiency ratios.
  • Nonperforming Loan Concentration -- Nonperforming assets remain "concentrated in the previously disclosed $27.5 million multifamily loan," with resolution contingent on borrower refinancing.

SUMMARY

Management reported a quarter-on-quarter rebound in profitability, underpinned by lower funding costs and moderate loan growth, after taking a significant loss from the restructuring of available-for-sale securities in the prior period. The net interest margin improved and is forecast to benefit further with the imminent redemption of $93 million in subordinated debt. Credit quality stayed stable, with a modest increase in nonperforming assets but continued concentration in a single large multifamily loan. Management is executing technology investments that are expected to drive cost efficiency and support M&A readiness, while deposit balances shifted away from brokered sources toward retail and public funds. The company is actively pursuing strategic acquisitions in selected Texas markets and anticipates a material increase in trust and fee income, bolstered by recent talent additions and market expansion.

  • President and CEO Donahoe said, "we are open to discussions" on M&A in Dallas, Houston, and Austin and remain focused on opportunities that fill geographic gaps rather than growth for its own sake.
  • Chief Financial Officer Shamburger stated that software and data processing expenses will rise $2.3 million-$2.4 million due to the migration of the core system off-premise and the launch of a data platform initiative.
  • Bank will redeem $93 million in subordinated notes on February 15, which management expects will "enhances future net interest income while providing additional balance sheet flexibility."
  • Repurchase activity increased in the latest quarter, with no buybacks since year-end and 762,000 shares remaining under current authorization.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AFS (Available-For-Sale) Securities: Debt or equity securities that are not classified as held-to-maturity or trading, and whose unrealized gains and losses are reported in other comprehensive income until realized.
  • Subordinated Debt: Debt that ranks below other loans and securities with respect to claims on assets or earnings, typically used for regulatory capital purposes in banks.
  • MBS (Mortgage-Backed Securities): Financial instruments secured by pools of mortgages, providing investors with periodic payments derived from the underlying loan payments.
  • CRE (Commercial Real Estate): Loans secured by income-producing commercial properties, such as office buildings, industrial complexes, retail properties, and multifamily residences.
  • C&I (Commercial and Industrial) Loans: Loans made to businesses, not secured by real estate, generally used for working capital, equipment purchase, or business expansion.
  • Nonperforming Assets: Loans or assets that are not generating expected returns, commonly due to delinquency or default; includes loans past due or in nonaccrual status.
