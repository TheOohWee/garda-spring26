Sensient (SXT) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 4, 2025 at 11:18PM EST

July 25, 2025, 1:30 p.m. ET

Risks

Paul Manning stated, "If the United States wanted to convert tomorrow, they couldn't because there's simply not enough raw material available," highlighting a supply chain constraint that may limit the pace of conversion.

Revenue in the Flavors and Extracts Group declined by 3.2% in local currency in fiscal Q2 2025, explicitly due to lower sales volumes and higher costs in the Natural Ingredients business, which management anticipates will persist until year-end.

Color segment Personal Care remains "a little bit soft," especially in Europe and North America, according to Manning, with no immediate improvement expected.

Takeaways

Adjusted EBITDA growth-- Adjusted EBITDA increased 14% in local currency for fiscal Q2 2025, reflecting margin improvements and operational leverage.

Adjusted EPS growth-- Adjusted EPS grew 21% in local currency for fiscal Q2 2025, driven by margin improvements and cost control.

Revenue-- Reported revenue was $414.2 million for fiscal Q2 2025, up from $403.5 million in fiscal Q2 2024, with Color Group and Asia Pacific Group as strong contributors.

Color Group revenue-- Local currency revenue grew 6.6% (non-GAAP) for fiscal Q2 2025; adjusted EBITDA margin increased to 25.1% from 22.2% versus the prior year, and local currency operating profit grew 22.1% for fiscal Q2 2025.

Asia Pacific Group-- Asia Pacific Group revenue grew 7.6% in local currency for fiscal Q2 2025, and operating profit increased 8% in local currency; adjusted EBITDA margin reached 22.3%, up from 22.0% in the prior year's second quarter.

Flavors, Extracts, and Flavor Ingredients-- Flavors, Extracts, and Flavor Ingredients product lines saw 4.6% local currency revenue growth in fiscal Q2 2025, despite an overall group revenue decline.

Flavors and Extracts Group profit-- Local currency operating profit increased 8.6% for fiscal Q2 2025, with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 17.8%, up 160 basis points versus the prior year.

Natural Ingredients business-- Continues to face lower sales volumes and higher costs, with improvement expected only after late Q4 2025; an improved cost position is forecasted for crop sold mainly in 2026.

Cash flow from operations-- Cash flow from operations was $48 million for fiscal Q2 2025, up 10.2% compared to last year's comparable period, attributed to stronger earnings and working capital management.

Capital expenditures-- Capital expenditures totaled $21 million for fiscal Q2 2025, with full-year CapEx guidance raised to "around $100 million or slightly more" for fiscal 2025, and further increases in capital expenditures are expected for 2026.

Net debt to credit adjusted EBITDA-- Net debt to credit adjusted EBITDA was 2.4x as of June 30, 2025.

Tariffs-- The annual impact is now expected to be "slightly less than the $10 million" previously communicated (refers to annual tariff impact as discussed on the fiscal Q2 2025 earnings call); price increases have partly offset initial tariff impacts.

Guidance revision-- Adjusted EBITDA guidance for 2025 was raised from "mid to high single digits" to "high single digits" (non-GAAP, local currency); adjusted EPS guidance was reaffirmed at "high single digit to double digit" growth in local currency for 2025.

Regulatory environment-- More than half of U.S. states have legislation affecting synthetic colors; West Virginia will prohibit synthetic color sales starting January 2028, and Texas requires warning labels by 2027, driving nationwide conversion to natural colors.

Customer pipeline and sales wins-- "strong new sales wins across each of our groups," with robust sales pipelines regionally, attributed to an innovative product portfolio and execution focus.

R&D and technology-- Significant investment in proprietary Microfine and butterfly pea flower extract technologies, cited as enabling vibrant natural color solutions across major categories and facilitating successful customer conversions.

Capacity and supply chain-- Manning stated, "today's manufacturing footprint would be insufficient to convert that $110 million of existing synthetic revenues," underscoring the necessity of expanded CapEx and continued supply chain investments.

Summary

Sensient Technologies(SXT +0.51%) delivered substantial growth in local currency adjusted EBITDA and EPS in fiscal Q2 2025, driven by standout performance from the Color Group and Asia Pacific Group, and supported by disciplined cost management. Management explicitly raised full-year adjusted EBITDA (non-GAAP) guidance for 2025, citing accelerating customer activity related to the mandated synthetic-to-natural color conversion in the U.S., and outlined a further increase in capital expenditures to address upcoming demand, raising full-year 2025 capital expenditure guidance to around $100 million. Regulatory momentum in the U.S. was clearly defined by new state laws, contributing to broad-based engagement from food manufacturers and new branded commitments, with many targeting 2027 for natural color transitions. The magnitude and timing of natural color revenue inflection remain centered on 2027 due to both customer deadlines and fundamental physical supply chain limitations in raw material sourcing and processing. Sensient management stated that natural color revenue is expected to increase more significantly beginning in 2027 to ensure customers' synthetic color products are off store shelves starting January 2028, and that current supply chain capacity is a critical limiting factor.

Paul Manning emphasized, "if you don't have botanicals, the train stops," illustrating that supply chain investment remains pivotal to achieving market share in the U.S. conversion to natural colors.

Tobin Tornehl clarified, "Pricing is really immaterial for us. So low single digits. So volume growth mix, you know, which is a result of a lot of the new wins that we're experiencing across all three of our groups," directly connecting gross margin gains to mix and new business, rather than inflationary price effects.

Natural Ingredients headwinds were explicitly tied to recent adverse crop conditions and inventory dynamics, with crop improvements and cost relief forecast to materialize mainly in 2026.

Current CapEx guidance for fiscal 2025 and further increases for fiscal 2026 are almost entirely focused on natural color capacity expansion, supported by clear customer demand escalation and regulatory deadlines.

Color segment operating leverage was specifically attributed to favorable product mix and cost structure optimization, with similar trends noted in Flavors and Asia.

Industry glossary

Microfine: Sensient's proprietary natural color technology designed for vibrant coloration in snacks, bakery, and confectionery applications, offering superior performance versus standard natural colorings.

Butterfly pea flower extract: A natural colorant stabilized by Sensient, providing blue, green, and purple hues for food and beverage applications, recently approved by the FDA for expanded uses.

Portfolio optimization plan: Sensient's ongoing initiative to streamline its operating structure and product offerings, with associated costs excluded from adjusted results for comparability.

Natural Ingredients business (S&I): Sensient's division specializing in dehydrated vegetables and botanicals used in flavors and food products, currently impacted by crop yield and cost volatility.
