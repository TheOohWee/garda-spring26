Saia (SAIA) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 10, 2026 at 11:51AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, February 10, 2026 at 10:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Frederick J. Holzgrefe
  • Chief Financial Officer — Matthew J. Batteh

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $790 million for the quarter, a record and up 0.1% year over year.
  • Operating Ratio -- 91.9% for the quarter, compared to 87.1% last year, reflecting a rise due to $4.7 million of unexpected reserve increases from prior-year accident claims.
  • Diluted Earnings Per Share -- $1.77 for the quarter, down from $2.84 last year.
  • Shipments & Tonnage -- Shipments per day decreased 0.5%, and tonnage per day decreased 1.5% compared to last year.
  • Revenue per Shipment -- Excluding fuel surcharge, declined 0.5% year over year to $297.57, but increased 1.1% sequentially from the third quarter.
  • Fuel Surcharge Revenue -- Rose 6.1%, representing 15% of total revenue (up from 14.1%).
  • Yield (Ex-Fuel Surcharge) -- Increased 0.5%; total yield including fuel surcharge up 1.6%.
  • Operating Expenses -- Grew 5.6% as salaries, wages, and benefits increased 6.1% due to employee cost inflation and wage increases.
  • Depreciation & Amortization -- $62.9 million in the quarter, an increase of 16.4% driven by investments in equipment, real estate, and technology.
  • Cost per Shipment -- Rose 6.1% versus last year, with group health insurance accounting for over 30% of the increase due to healthcare inflation.
  • Southern California Volume & Revenue Impact -- Regional volume declined 18%, causing an estimated $4 million reduction in revenue for the quarter.
  • Preventable Accident Frequency -- Down 21% year over year in 2025, despite 2.4% growth in internal miles driven.
  • Lost Time Injuries -- Decreased 10% year over year.
  • Cargo Claims Ratio -- Improved to a record 0.47% for the quarter and 0.5% for the year.
  • Cash & Debt Position -- Ended the year with just under $20 million in cash and $164 million in total debt, down from $200 million.
  • Network Expansion -- 39 terminals opened since 2022, with 21 added in 2024; these contributed to an approximate 80 basis-point revenue market share increase in 2025.
  • Excess Capacity -- Management estimates broad-based 20%-25% excess capacity across the network, with up to 50% in new markets.
  • Incremental Margins -- Management expects 30%-40% incremental margins on excess capacity as volume recovers, with potential for higher levels in ramping terminals.
  • CapEx Guidance -- Planned 2026 capital expenditures are $350 million-$400 million, with current focus on maintenance CapEx after substantial prior investments.
  • Contractual Renewals -- Averaged 4.9% contract rate increases in the quarter, accelerating to 6.6% in January 2026.
  • PT (Purchase Transportation) as % of Miles -- PT accounted for 12.1% of total miles in 2025 versus over 18% in 2021, driven by network expansion and optimization.
  • Headcount Management -- Excluding linehaul drivers, Q4 headcount was down 6.4% year over year and 2% sequentially from Q3.
  • Terminal Profitability -- Ramping terminals became profitable for the full year and are expected to reach company-average margins within a three-year horizon under typical economic conditions.

Risks

  • Quarterly operating ratio deterioration was "impacted by unexpected adverse developments on a few cases arising from accidents that occurred in prior years," requiring reserve increases of $4.7 million.
  • Salaries, wages, and benefits saw a 6.1% increase, with healthcare inflation described as a "headwind" and group health insurance driving more than 30% of year-over-year cost per shipment growth.
  • "Mix headwinds continue to impact our results with slight decreases in weight per shipment and length of haul" and continued volume decline in the high-revenue Southern California market.
  • Management noted the self-insurance cost environment remains "inflationary," with "cost per claim continued to rise due to increased cost of litigation and increases in settlement values."

Summary

Saia (SAIA +0.15%) provided direct guidance that its national network contributed to increased market share and record quarterly revenues, despite muted demand and significant cost pressures. Management quantified sequential and year-over-year margin headwinds, attributing deterioration primarily to nonrecurring adverse reserve developments and persistent healthcare inflation. Capital allocation remains focused, with 2026 CapEx targeting maintenance investment following a $2 billion network expansion and plans for balance sheet prudence. Strategic commentary pointed to visible incremental leverage on recent capacity investments, as the company reported ramping terminals turned profitable and expects significant incremental margin as volumes recover. Contractual rate increases gathered pace into January 2026, and customer acceptance of pricing actions exceeded recent norms, underlining management's confidence in further monetizing the national platform.

  • Monthly operational detail showed October shipments per day declined 3.4%, November recovered 2.6%, and December rose 0.6%; January experienced a 2.1% drop, or slight growth excluding weather impacts.
  • Network optimization initiatives, including advanced analytics, are being cited for ongoing improvements in density, efficiency, and internalization of linehaul, contributing to reduction of purchase transportation from 18% to 12.1% of miles since 2021.
  • While the company highlighted positive safety trends, both executives emphasized the ongoing inflationary risk from legal and insurance costs embedded within operating expenses, with near-term profitability visibility dependent on macroeconomic improvement and further network scaling.
  • Management stated, "We have, you know, it is going to vary by market, but I would say on a broad base, 20% to 25% excess capacity," positioning Saia for accelerated incremental returns with any sustained volume recovery.

Industry glossary

  • PT (Purchase Transportation): Third-party or contractor-provided linehaul or delivery transportation services, used as a variable flex supplement to a carrier’s owned fleet.
  • Operating Ratio (OR): Key LTL industry profitability metric calculated as operating expenses divided by operating revenue, expressed as a percentage; lower ratios indicate higher profitability.
  • GRI (General Rate Increase): Industry-standard annual contract and tariff price hike applied to customers by LTL carriers, intended to offset rising underlying cost structures.
  • Ramp(ing) Terminals: Newly opened or recently acquired facilities that have not yet matured to the efficiency, density, and profitability of legacy locations; referenced for their incremental contribution as they scale.
