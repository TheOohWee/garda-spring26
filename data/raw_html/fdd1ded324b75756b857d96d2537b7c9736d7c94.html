SuperCom (SPCB) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Apr 9, 2026 at 8:50AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Nov. 14, 2024 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & CEO — Ordan Trabelsi

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Year-to-date Revenue -- $21.3 million reported, up from $20.9 million for the same period prior year.
  • Gross Profit (9 months) -- $10.7 million, representing a 35% increase, with a gross margin of 50.1% compared to 30.7% previously.
  • Q3 Revenue -- $6.91 million, compared to $6.78 million in Q2 of last year.
  • Q3 Gross Profit -- $3.2 million with a gross margin of 46%, down from $4.0 million and 59% gross margin in prior-year Q3; decline attributed to "project mix and timing."
  • Net Income (9 months) -- $2.52 million, a reversal from a net loss of $2.4 million prior year.
  • EBITDA (Q3) -- $1.1 million, down from $2.5 million in prior-year Q3, ascribed to timing and project composition.
  • Free Cash Flow (Q3) -- Positive $1.2 million, contrasted with a negative figure for prior-year Q3.
  • Operating Cash Flow (9 months) -- Positive, in contrast to a multi-year history of negative operating cash flow: negative $9.7 million (2021), negative $4.7 million (2022), negative $2.4 million (2023).
  • Cash Position -- $6.2 million at period end, marking the highest level reported in recent years.
  • Outstanding Shares -- Approximately 2 million shares at quarter-end, with "Zero in the third quarter." new shares issued in Q3 due to debt conversions.
  • Long-term Liabilities -- Reduced by $4.5 million year over year, attributed to debt-to-equity conversions at up to a 100% premium to market price.
  • Key Contract Win (Israel) -- Awarded a 5-year national electronic monitoring contract with the Israeli Prison Service Agency, covering an estimated 1,500 enrollees initially, and allowing for four 1-year extensions.
  • U.S. Market Expansion -- New contract wins announced in New York, West Virginia, and Maryland, including a Baltimore-based provider generating approximately $250,000 in annual recurring revenue.
  • European Market Activity -- Secured national tracking program orders, including more than $2.9 million in recent European government orders, with over $13.5 million total new orders described for the period.
  • Win Rate (Europe) -- Claimed "over 65%" success rate in competitive European tenders.
  • Flagship Project (Romania) -- Progressed on a $33 million, 6-year national electronic monitoring contract with capacity for up to 15,000 monitored offenders monthly.
  • Product Portfolio Developments -- Launch of new AI-driven analytics in electronic monitoring products including PureProtect and PureOne, with deployments and positive feedback cited in the U.S. market.

SUMMARY

During the call, SuperCom (SPCB +1.08%) reported a year-to-date turnaround in both profitability and cash flow, highlighting a reversal from historical losses to positive net income and operating cash. Management underscored major contract wins, including the Israeli national electronic monitoring project and new U.S. contracts, strategically expanding the company’s global footprint. The company also noted the launch of AI-driven product innovations and cited growing penetration in both the U.S. and European government markets, emphasizing successful competitive displacement and recurring revenue growth.

  • Ordan Trabelsi stated, "Free cash flow generation of $1.2 million this quarter and a cash balance of $6.2 million at it, underscore the tangible results along with an ability to support further potential growth opportunities."
  • Management described the Israeli contract structure as a "lease model," initially covering 1,500 enrollees with potential expansion as additional government programs are deployed.
  • On cash discipline, Trabelsi noted a focus on "reducing our use of cash and over time, as I said, we want a negative $9.7 million to negative $4.7 million to $2.4 million, now positive free cash flows."
  • The Q3 gross margin decline to 46% from 59% was explicitly linked to project mix and the timing of deployment stages.
  • Management highlighted premium-value debt-to-equity conversions effectuated with up to a 100% premium to market price, supporting the company's strategy to lower long-term liabilities while avoiding dilution in the third quarter.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Electronic Monitoring (EM): Technology-based supervision of offenders outside correctional facilities, typically using GPS or RFID devices for house arrest, parole, or domestic violence programs.
  • Debt-to-Equity Conversion: Agreement by which creditors exchange outstanding debt for equity shares, often reducing financial leverage on the balance sheet.
  • Recurring Revenue: Revenue that is highly likely to continue in the future, typically derived from ongoing service contracts or subscription-based models.
