Earnings Call Transcript

Church & Dwight (CHD) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 3, 2026 at 10:39AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, Aug. 1, 2025 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Richard A. Dierker
  • Chief Financial Officer — Lee B. McChesney

TAKEAWAYS

  • Organic Sales Growth -- 0.1%, surpassing the prior outlook range of minus 2% to flat due to improved category consumption trends.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $0.94, exceeding management's outlook of $0.85 by $0.09, and up 1% year over year.
  • Adjusted Gross Margin -- 45.0%, a 40 basis point decrease year over year, with productivity and higher-margin mix adding 170 basis points, offset by negative 140 basis points from inflation and tariffs, 40 basis points from volume/price/mix, and 30 basis points from the ZICAM/Orajel recall.
  • U.S. Consumer Segment Organic Sales -- Declined 1%, with volume growth offset by negative price/mix and muted by retailer destocking (estimated 100 basis point drag in the quarter).
  • ARM & HAMMER Liquid Laundry Detergent -- Consumption increased 3.2%, above category growth of 1.3%; share reached 15%.
  • THERABREATH Mouthwash -- Consumption grew 22.5% despite category decline; household penetration at 11% compared to 65% for the category; maintained #2 mouthwash position with 21% share.
  • HERO Acne Category -- Consumption rose 11.4% versus acne category growth of 1.5%; remains #1 in acne care with a 22% share, household penetration at 9% versus 28% for category.
  • BATISTE Dry Shampoo -- Global consumption declined nearly 7% in the quarter due to competitive pricing and supply issues, which management confirmed are now resolved.
  • International Segment -- Sales growth of 5.3%, with organic growth at 4.8%, led by HERO, THERABREATH, and FEMFRESH; share gains achieved for all power brands internationally.
  • Full-Year 2025 Outlook -- Management maintains guidance for reported and organic sales growth of 0%-2%, gross margin contraction of 60 basis points, and adjusted EPS growth of 0%-2%, incorporating Touchland acquisition, product recall costs, and wind-down of exited businesses.
  • Strategic Portfolio Review Actions -- Management exited FLAWLESS, SPINBRUSH, and WATERPIK showerhead businesses; ongoing strategic review of vitamin business, considering divestiture, joint venture, or substantial restructuring.
  • Touchland Acquisition -- Closed in July; management states it is "the fastest-growing brand in the hand sanitizer category in the U.S.," now contributing as the eighth power brand with $70 million to $80 million in annualized revenue expected this year.
  • Online Sales -- Accounted for 23% of global sales, with further broad-based growth across e-commerce channels.
  • Cash from Operating Activities -- $416.5 million for the first half, down $83 million year over year, primarily due to working capital timing and lower cash earnings.
  • Q2 2025 Share Repurchases -- $300 million repurchased via open market and accelerated share repurchase programs.
  • Gummy Vitamin Business -- Category up nearly 4% for the third straight quarter, but company consumption down about 25% as weighted distribution (TDPs) declined.
  • Retailer Destocking -- Described as a 100 basis point headwind in Q2, likely to have slight but diminishing effect in future quarters.
  • Q3 2025 Guidance -- Management expects organic sales growth of 1%-2%, adjusted gross margin contraction of approximately 100 basis points, and adjusted EPS of $0.72 (down 9% year over year).

RISKS

  • Management singled out the gummy vitamin business as "a drag on the company's organic growth," with consumption down approximately 25% and noted as contributing to mixed results within the vitamin segment.
  • "Adjusted gross margin was down 40 basis points" and expected to contract 60 basis points for the year amid persistent inflation, tariffs, and impacts from product recalls and business exits.
  • Cash from operations declined $83 million in the first half due to "working capital timing and lower cash earnings," and capital expenditures decreased significantly.
  • BATISTE dry shampoo experienced a nearly 7% decline in consumption, attributed to competitive pricing, economic pressure, and earlier supply chain issues within the quarter.

SUMMARY

Management reported improved trends in category consumption and stated that organic sales grew 0.1%, surpassing the previously communicated outlook. Performance exceeded internal targets for both gross margin and adjusted EPS. Strategic actions advanced during the quarter included the completed Touchland acquisition, exit of three underperforming brands, and renewed focus on revitalizing the vitamin business through a multi-option review process. International and e-commerce channels delivered broad-based share gains, while continued innovation—especially in core brands—remains central to future growth. Working capital actions, expanded credit facilities, and $300 million in share buybacks strengthened capital allocation flexibility for the balance of 2025.

  • Consumer uncertainty moderated after early-year lows, with management observing that "category consumption is looking a bit better than 3 months ago."
  • Touchland is expected to contribute positively to gross margin in future years, but business exits will neutralize that effect in 2025 according to CFO McChesney.
  • Retailer destocking was quantified as a 100 basis point drag in Q2, with impacts expected to persist only slightly in future quarters as inventory stabilizes.
  • Marketing expenditure as a percentage of net sales is targeted at 11% for the year, with Q3 expected to be the highest at 12.5%-13% as part of the company's "Evergreen model."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • TDPs: Total Distribution Points; a weighted distribution metric reflecting the breadth and depth of product placement across retail outlets.
  • Evergreen Model: Church & Dwight's stated long-term financial framework combining consistent marketing investment and operational discipline to target stable organic sales and margin growth.
