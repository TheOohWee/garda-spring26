TAKEAWAYS

  • Leadership Transition -- Ben Heraud named CEO effective March 31, 2026, with Talman Pizzey remaining as Board member and adviser to support continuity.
  • Full-Year Revenue -- $2.1 billion, up 4.4% on a constant currency basis and 3.6% as reported, marking the highest combined full-year revenue to date.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $312 million for the year and $76.4 million for the quarter, with annual margin of 14.8% and Q4 margin of 15.0%.
  • Segment Revenue — I&M -- $1.1 billion for the year, with year-over-year results roughly flat and Q4 revenue of $258 million, down 2% in the quarter due to lower outage and capital project spending.
  • Segment Revenue — Consulting Engineering (CE) -- $714 million for the year, up approximately 8% from the prior year, supported by infrastructure and data center demand; fourth quarter revenue of $181 million rose 2%.
  • Segment Revenue — Geospatial (GEO) -- $298 million for the year, reflecting roughly 6% growth, with Q4 revenue of $70 million rising 2% despite federal funding delays.
  • Adjusted Gross Margins -- Q4 combined adjusted gross margin was 38.8%, up 277 basis points from the prior year; full-year adjusted gross margin was 37.6%, up 14 basis points.
  • Data Center Revenue -- Nearly $70 million, more than doubling year over year, with a "line of sight to nearly $100 million" in 2026.
  • Combined Year-End Backlog -- $1.07 billion for CE and GEO, up about 10% from the prior year's $970 million, representing incremental contracted work.
  • Integration Synergies -- $25 million of annualized cost synergies remain on track, with about half expected to be realized during 2026 and full realization by mid-2027.
  • Liquidity and Debt -- Year-end liquidity of $551 million, comprising $440 million cash and $111 million revolving credit facility availability; total term loan debt at $1.6 billion.
  • Cost Management -- Adjusted SG&A of $124 million in Q4, or 24.4% of revenue; integration efforts aim to address elevated SG&A and drive margin expansion.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $34 million reported for the year and $56 million on a combined basis, representing 2.2% and 2.7% of revenue, respectively.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- A new $200 million share buyback program was approved by the Board this week to offer capital allocation flexibility.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Revenue expected to reach $2.15 billion to $2.25 billion and adjusted EBITDA projected at $330 million to $355 million, indicating approximately 4% growth at midpoint.
  • Q1 2026 Expectations -- Revenue guidance of $470 million to $485 million and adjusted EBITDA outlook of $55 million to $60 million, reflecting seasonal patterns.
  • Compensation Change -- NV5 short-term incentive moved from stock to cash compensation, decreasing adjusted EBITDA guidance by $8 million for 2026.
  • Tuck-in Acquisitions -- Three small acquisitions completed in the quarter, with full-year contribution across all segments described as 12% to the combined business.

SUMMARY

TIC Solutions (TIC +0.14%) announced a CEO transition, reported record combined revenue and EBITDA for the year, and highlighted a 10% increase in year-end backlog for its CE and GEO segments. The Board authorized a $200 million share repurchase program, and integration initiatives are progressing as planned to deliver $25 million in annualized cost synergies. 2026 guidance projects continued revenue and margin growth, driven by cross-segment collaboration, disciplined cost management, and a focus on recurring end markets.

  • Kristin Schultes stated that first quarter adjusted EBITDA typically represents roughly 15%-18% of full-year EBITDA, alerting investors to material quarterly seasonality in earnings.
  • The federal funding lapse was referenced as a specific factor impacting GEO award and approval pacing, though management described the effect as limited and temporary.
  • Ben Heraud explained, "Our embedded run and maintain programs and call-out activity grew in the year," with growth in these areas providing visibility and resiliency despite declines in outage and capital project business.
  • The GEO Agent AI-enabled geospatial platform will begin client rollout in the initial months of 2026, expected to create new analytics and workflow opportunities for the segment.
  • Capital allocation in 2026 will include continued pursuit of tuck-ins alongside the authorized share repurchase, with organic reinvestment prioritized for the highest-return business lines.
  • A $250 million private placement of shares and prefunded warrants was completed in October to bolster balance sheet flexibility and reduce leverage.
  • Management stated, "We maintain pricing integrity even when competitors were more aggressive, and we will not trade long-term economics for short-term volume," emphasizing a disciplined approach to pricing and resource deployment within the I&M segment.
  • Ben Heraud noted that the Middle East is a relatively small piece of business, around 1%, and the impact from the region is immaterial for TIC.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • I&M: Inspection and Mitigation — TIC segment specializing in inspection, testing, and industrial maintenance services.
  • CE: Consulting Engineering — TIC segment focused on engineering design, consulting, and specialty services.
  • GEO: Geospatial — TIC segment providing mapping, analytics, and location-based technology solutions.
  • NDT: Nondestructive Testing — Analysis and inspection methods that do not damage or alter the tested materials or components.
  • DSO: Days Sales Outstanding — Metric indicating average time to collect receivables from clients.
  • Backlog: The total value of contracted work not yet completed or recognized as revenue.
  • Tuck-in: A small-scale acquisition intended to be easily integrated into an existing business segment or operation.
