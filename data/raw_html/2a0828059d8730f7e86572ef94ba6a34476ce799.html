Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $39% year-over-year growth marks the 27th consecutive quarter exceeding 30% top-line growth.
  • Operating Income -- $724 million, increasing by 30% year-over-year and highlighting ongoing operating leverage despite investment cycles.
  • Active Buyers -- 75 million unique buyers on the platform, with 7.8 million new buyers added in the quarter across Latin America.
  • Brazil Items Sold Growth -- Items sold in Brazil accelerated to 42% from 26% the previous quarter, following the reduction of the free shipping threshold.
  • Brazil Unit Shipping Costs -- 8% sequential decline (quarter-over-quarter) in unit shipping costs, attributable to scale efficiencies and process improvements.
  • Marketing Spend -- Held steady at approximately 11% of revenues, with affiliate channel investment up 4x year-over-year to attract new, especially younger, users.
  • Mercado Pago User Growth -- Monthly active users accelerated, with Net Promoter Score (NPS) reaching record highs in Brazil due to product enhancements.
  • Argentina Revenue -- Revenue grew 39% in USD and 97% in local currency, with items sold up 34%, but trends slowed through the quarter due to macroeconomic volatility.
  • Credit Portfolio -- Year-over-year loan portfolio growth of 100% in Argentina, and older credit card cohorts in Brazil remain profitable.
  • Fulfillment Capacity -- No unplanned fulfillment centers opened; capacity increased 41% year-over-year, enabling management of strong shipment volume spikes.
  • Merchant Initiatives in Brazil -- Implementation of relative value notice and search algorithm adjustments intended to surface the most competitive offerings, though “too early to discuss results.”
  • Chile and Colombia Growth -- Chile posted its third consecutive quarter of accelerated GMV growth, while Colombia improved GMV growth by more than 10 percentage points sequentially.
  • Credit Card Product Launch -- New credit card rolled out in Argentina late in the quarter, but not enough data yet to evaluate performance.
  • Principality -- 11-point improvement in Brazil and 2 points in Mexico, defined as clients directing at least 50% of their income through Mercado Pago.

Summary

The earnings call revealed MercadoLibre (MELI 1.08%) maintained 39% revenue growth and $724 million in operating income, continuing its streak of outperforming on top-line metrics despite short-term cost pressures and heavy investment. Brazil experienced a notable surge in e-commerce activity, reflected by a 42% jump in items sold and significant operating efficiencies, while Argentina faced headwinds from macroeconomic instability leading to slower growth, increased funding costs, and reduced NIMAL. Strong user acquisition and product innovation drove engagement, with 7.8 million new buyers and record NPS, as the company balanced ecosystem expansion in 1P, 3P, credit, and fulfillment. Each geography, including Mexico, Chile, and Colombia, contributed discrete growth and market share gains, fueled by targeted logistics, marketing, and merchant engagement strategies even as competitive and funding environments evolved.

  • CFO Ariel Szarfsztejn stated the 28% sequential shipment volume jump in Brazil “naturally puts pressure in our network capacity,” but highlighted that existing capacity handled the surge without operational disruption.
  • Management intends to maintain current levels of marketing investment, emphasizing a “sophisticated methodology to acquire users to make sure that those users that we bring are actually contributing to profitability.”
  • Credit card cohorts older than two years in Brazil are now profitable, according to Giménez, with the overall portfolio’s profitability mix dependent on the pace of issuance and maturing cohorts in each geography.
  • Merchant onboarding and marketplace vibrancy were aided by increased listings in Brazil’s targeted BRL 19 to BRL 79 price range after shipping incentives.
  • Osvaldo Giménez explained, “we are already the second largest financial institution in terms of monthly active users,” with Mercado Pago outpacing local competitors on user growth and downloads.
  • Strategic logistics investments, including robotics and automation, seek enhanced productivity and unit cost reductions, but continued optimization is expected over the medium term.
  • Initiatives in B2B commerce and partnerships, such as with Casas Bahia, are at an early stage but viewed as long-term drivers of portfolio diversity and market penetration.
  • Agentic AI tools for sellers and fintech users were launched, with management focusing on developing proprietary technology for customer experience and productivity improvements.

Industry glossary

  • GMV (Gross Merchandise Volume): The total value of merchandise sold over a specified period through MercadoLibre’s marketplace platforms, before deducting returns or cancellations.
  • TPV (Total Payment Volume): The aggregate value of payments processed on Mercado Pago, including both marketplace and off-platform transactions.
  • NIMAL: Net Interest Margin After Losses — a credit portfolio profitability metric considering lending revenues minus funding costs and loan losses.
  • 1P / 3P: “First Party” (MercadoLibre sells inventory directly) vs. “Third Party” (external sellers list items on the platform).
  • Principality: Share of clients who route at least half of their income through the Mercado Pago account, as measured by income flow or verified via survey/open banking data.
  • NPS (Net Promoter Score): Customer experience metric measuring willingness to recommend Mercado Pago to others.
  • BRL: Brazilian Real, the legal currency of Brazil.
  • Pix: Brazil’s instant payment system, used for online and in-store transactions on the Mercado Pago platform.
  • Agentic AI: Artificial Intelligence designed to act as an autonomous “agent,” performing personalized, conversational, and productivity-focused tasks for users or sellers.
