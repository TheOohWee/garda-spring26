Earnings Call Transcript

Radian (RDN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 5:11PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 19, 2026 at 11 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Richard Thornberry
  • Chief Financial Officer — Daniel Kobell

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Net Income from Continuing Operations -- $159 million for the quarter and $618 million for the year, with per-share net income rising due to an 8% reduction in share count.
  • Revenue -- $301 million in the quarter and $1.2 billion for the year, maintaining stable business momentum.
  • New Insurance Written (NIW) -- $15.9 billion for the quarter and $55 billion for the year, representing 6% year-over-year growth.
  • Mortgage Insurance in Force -- $283 billion at year-end, up 3% year over year, reaching an all-time high.
  • Book Value Per Share -- Increased 13% year over year to $35.29; dividends added an extra 3% to book value during the year.
  • Return on Equity (ROE) -- Achieved 13.5% in the quarter and 13.1% for the year.
  • Persistency Rate -- Remained strong at 82% for the quarter, with about half of the portfolio holding mortgage rates at or below 5.5%.
  • In-force Premium Yield -- Stayed stable at 38 basis points, with management expecting this to continue through 2026.
  • Investment Portfolio -- Totaled $6.1 billion, generating $249 million of net investment income during 2025.
  • Primary Default Inventory -- Rose to approximately 25,000 loans at quarter-end, resulting in a 2.56% portfolio default rate, attributed to seasonal trends and seasoning of loans.
  • Cure Rates -- Remained consistently positive, with recent quarterly cure trends "meaningfully exceeding" expectations, aided by embedded equity.
  • Loss Provision -- $57 million for new defaults in the quarter; positive reserve development for prior periods totaled $35 million; net provision expense was $22 million.
  • Operating Expenses -- $56 million for the quarter and $246 million for the year, both below annual guidance for continuing operations.
  • Capital Returns -- Radian Guaranty distributed $795 million to the holding company; $576 million was returned to stockholders through dividends and share repurchases during the year.
  • Share Repurchase -- 13.5 million shares repurchased at a cost of $430 million during the year, supported by capital strength.
  • Available Liquidity at Holding Company -- $1.8 billion at year-end, including a $195 million fourth-quarter dividend and a $600 million intercompany note from Radian Guaranty; $200 million drawn on revolving credit facility in January.
  • Inigo Acquisition -- Closed earlier in the month for $1.67 billion, funded entirely with available liquidity and excess capital, with no new equity raised; expected to double annual revenues and be accretive to EPS and returns.
  • Net Purchase Price Multiple (Inigo) -- Approximately 1.4x Inigo's estimated year-end tangible equity of $1.16 billion.
  • Post-Acquisition Liquidity -- Holding company liquidity estimated at $350 million after Inigo closing; management expects at least $600 million in dividends from Radian Guaranty to the holding company in 2026, including a $140 million dividend in the first quarter.
  • PMIERs Cushion -- $1.6 billion at year-end, significantly above required capital levels.
  • Excess of Loss Reinsurance -- Completed a reinsurance agreement covering $373 million of select policies written from 2016 through 2021 for additional risk mitigation.
  • Leverage Ratio -- Declined to 18.3% at year-end; management projects it will remain below 20% by the end of 2026.
  • Discontinued Operations -- $62 million of capital extracted from businesses held for sale in the quarter; carrying value reduced to $110 million by year-end with divestiture targeted for completion by third quarter 2026.
  • Organizational Updates -- Management promotions announced to align leadership with the company's post-Inigo strategy.

Summary

The call emphasized that the Inigo acquisition, funded without new equity issuance, immediately positions Radian Group (RDN 1.07%) for significant revenue growth, global expansion, and increased strategic flexibility. Management confirmed the company's expectation that Inigo will contribute mid-teens returns through the cycle without dependence on synergies and will drive around $170 million in incremental annual pretax net income from redeployed capital. Leadership highlighted a multi-year focus on capital discipline, with management reiterating plans to resume share repurchases as liquidity builds post-acquisition. The company stated that the integration risk from the Inigo transaction remains "fairly low," with limited systems alignment required and no near-term changes to Inigo's management, brand, or operational autonomy.

  • Chief Financial Officer Kobell affirmed, "no change to what we provided in terms of financial guidance and feel very confident that we'll be able to deliver on that."
  • Management described continued "steady progress" on divestitures, with capital returns from discontinued operations adding to immediate liquidity and the process on track for third quarter completion.
  • During Q&A, the company clarified that the expected $170 million incremental net income from Inigo is a pretax figure, with detailed purchase accounting—such as goodwill and intangible allocation—to follow in the next quarterly report.
  • Chief Executive Officer Thornberry said, "we think our shares are undervalued," underlining confidence in future capital returns and strategic transformation benefits.
  • Plans include fully repaying the $200 million credit facility draw within the current year, while prioritizing liquidity maintenance and potential resumption of share repurchases.

Industry glossary

  • NIW (New Insurance Written): The dollar value of new mortgage insurance policies originated during a specific period.
  • Persistency Rate: The percentage of insurance policies that remain active within the portfolio over a specified period, indicating policy retention.
  • PMIERs (Private Mortgage Insurer Eligibility Requirements): Regulatory capital requirements imposed by the GSEs for private mortgage insurers to ensure financial strength and solvency.
  • In-force Premium Yield: The average annualized premium income earned on the current portfolio, expressed as basis points of insurance in force.
  • Cure Rate: The proportion of delinquent loans that become current or otherwise exit default status within a period, without resulting in a claim for the insurer.

We have the unique opportunity to leverage our high-performing Mortgage Insurance business, which is expected to continue generating excess capital alongside a growing and global specialty insurance business. The acquisition significantly expands our expertise, capabilities and geographic reach, greatly increasing our total addressable market and position us to deploy capital strategically for attractive returns. We expect this transaction to double our annual revenues, be accretive to EPS and returns and provide greater strategic flexibility to deploy capital across multiple insurance lines through various business cycles. Inigo has a proven track record in the Lloyd's market, fueled by a high-performing culture and an experienced team with a strong focus on their customers.

Their business model and the approach align closely with our Mortgage Insurance business, particularly in their commitment to strong risk management through disciplined underwriting, leveraging data and analytics and disciplined capital allocation. As part of Radian, Inigo will operate as a stand-alone business unit in London, maintaining its management team, brand and culture. We are excited to welcome Inigo's CEO, Richard Watson, and his talented team to Radian and look forward to working together to build long-term and sustainable value for all our stakeholders as a global multiline specialty insurer.

I am very proud of these teams as they have effectively managed their businesses while working through this process. Last week, we announced an important organizational update. We also promoted Dan Kobell and Rob Quigley, two highly experienced and accomplished financial executives with extensive financial management expertise. We were pleased to grow our net income from continuing operations per share in 2025, driven by a combination of strong earnings as well as an 8% reduction in our share count. Additionally, in our Mortgage Insurance business, we saw growth in both insurance in force and new insurance written with NIW growing 6% year-over-year. We also returned dividends to our stockholders in 2025 that accounted for an additional 3% of book value. Turning now to the key drivers of our results.

Our total revenues continued to be strong at $301 million in the fourth quarter and $1.2 billion for the full year. Slides 14 through 16 in our presentation include details on our Mortgage Insurance portfolio as well as other key factors impacting our net premiums earned. Our large, high-quality primary Mortgage Insurance portfolio grew 3% year-over-year to another all-time high of $283 billion. Contributing to this growth was $55 billion of NIW in 2025, including $15.9 billion in the fourth quarter.

These figures compare favorably to $52 billion in 2024 and $13.2 billion in the fourth quarter of the prior year. Our proprietary mortgage data and analytics, which drive our MI pricing strategy, together with our disciplined and informed approach to risk management have contributed to a healthy and profitable portfolio, creating long-term economic value and generating strong returns for our shareholders. As shown on Slide 14, our quarterly persistency rate remained strong at 82% in the fourth quarter, a small decrease from the prior quarter due to higher refinance activity. As of the end of the fourth quarter, approximately half of our insurance in-force portfolio had a mortgage rate of 5.5% or lower.

Given current mortgage interest rates, these policies are less likely to cancel due to refinancing in the near term, and we, therefore, continue to expect our persistency rate to remain strong. As shown on Slide 16, the in-force premium yield for our Mortgage Insurance portfolio remained stable as expected at 38 basis points. With strong persistency rates and the current industry pricing environment, we expect the in-force premium yield to remain generally stable in 2026. As shown on Slide 17, our investment portfolio of $6.1 billion consists of well diversified and highly rated securities, generating net investment income of $249 million in 2025. Our provision for losses and related credit trends continue to be positive with strong cure activity.

This increase in total defaults reflects normal seasonal trends and the expected continued seasoning of our large insurance in-force portfolio. As we have noted in the past, our new defaults continue to contain significant embedded equity, which has been a key driver of recent favorable credit trends, including higher cure rates and reduced severity for policies that result in claims submission.

As shown on Slide 21, our cure trends have been consistently positive in recent periods, meaningfully exceeding our initial default-to-claim expectations for these loans. Cure rates in the fourth quarter exhibited typical seasonal trends in line with similar periods from prior years. Slide 22 shows the components of our provision for loss. We maintained our initial default-to-claim rate of 7.5% on new defaults, which resulted in $57 million of loss provision for new defaults in the fourth quarter. Throughout 2025, our provision for losses benefited from favorable reserve development on prior period defaults, primarily due to more favorable cure trends than initially estimated. This continued in the fourth quarter with $35 million of positive reserve development.

As a result, we recognized a net provision expense of $22 million in the fourth quarter. Now turning to our other expenses. For the fourth quarter, our other operating expenses were $56 million, down from $62 million in the third quarter. For the year, our other operating expenses were $246 million, below our previously communicated annual expense guidance of $250 million for continuing operations. With the Inigo acquisition and following the planned divestiture of our Mortgage Conduit, Title and Real Estate Services businesses, we will continue to look for opportunities to enhance our efficiency as we simplify our business model to focus on mortgage and specialty insurance. During the fourth quarter, we completed an excess of loss reinsurance agreement covering approximately $373 million on certain policies written from 2016 through 2021. Our PMIERs cushion was $1.6 billion at year-end, significantly above our required PMIERs capital level.
