W. P. Carey (WPC) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 11:06AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Oct. 29, 2025 at 11 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Jason Fox
  • Chief Financial Officer — ToniAnn Sanzone
  • Head of Asset Management — Brooks Gordon
  • Chairman — Peter Sands

TAKEAWAYS

  • AFFO per share -- $1.25, up 5.9% year over year, driven by higher investment activity and internal rent growth.
  • Full-year AFFO guidance -- Raised to $4.93 -- $4.99 per share, targeting 5.5% growth at midpoint.
  • Investment volume guidance -- Increased to $1.8 billion -- $2.1 billion for 2025, following $1.65 billion invested year to date at a 7.6% weighted average initial cap rate.
  • Same-store rent growth -- Sector-leading contractual same-store rent growth in the mid-2% range, with 2.4% reported for the quarter and a 2.5% full-year expectation.
  • Lease structures -- Majority of 2025 investments featured fixed escalations averaging 2.7%, and weighted average lease term of 18 years, with initial cap rates translating to mid-9% average yields.
  • Disposition volume guidance -- Increased to $1.3 billion -- $1.5 billion, including additional operating self-storage asset sales; achieving spreads of approximately 150 basis points over investments.
  • Portfolio composition -- Warehouse and industrial assets comprised over 75% of investment volume year to date, with the majority of deals in the U.S., but materially increased activity in Europe during the third quarter.
  • Portfolio occupancy -- Declined to 97% due to temporary vacancies, with roughly one quarter resolved or near resolution, and half in active remedy.
  • Rent loss guidance -- Reduced to $10 million from a prior $10 million -- $15 million range; actual rent loss visibility is $7 million, representing 45 basis points of ABR, including Hellweg downtime.
  • Operating property dispositions -- 37 self-storage properties and 1 student housing property sold; fourth-quarter NOI from remaining operating properties expected at $7 million -- $9 million, and projected to reduce in 2026.
  • Liquidity position -- Ended the quarter with $2.1 billion in liquidity, comprising credit facility availability, cash, 1031 exchange funds, and unsettled forward equity.
  • Capital structure activity -- Issued $230 million in forward equity via ATM at $68.50 per share, and $400 million bonds at 4.65% coupon; weighted average interest rate at 3.2%.
  • Dividend -- Quarterly dividend increased by 4% to $0.91 per share, annualized yield at 5.4%, with a year-to-date payout ratio near 73% of AFFO per share.
  • Net debt to adjusted EBITDA -- 5.8x including unsettled equity forwards; 5.9x excluding them; both within target range.
  • G&A, property, and tax expense guidance -- General and administrative expense trajectory of $99 million -- $102 million; property expenses $51 million -- $54 million; tax expense reduced to $41 million -- $44 million midpoint.
  • Capital projects pipeline -- $70 million slated for fourth-quarter completion, with $180 million more underway for 2026.

RISKS

  • Portfolio occupancy drop -- Occupancy declined to 97% due to temporary vacancies; return to normal levels is contingent on the timely resolution of pending re-leasings and sales.
  • Continued exposure to Hellweg -- CFO Sanzone stated, "The balance of our reserve includes ongoing caution towards Hellweg," reflecting persisting tenant-specific risk despite current rent payments.

SUMMARY

W. P. Carey (WPC +0.17%) reported increased AFFO per share and narrowed guidance upward, attributing the performance to robust investment activity and lower than anticipated rent loss. Management highlighted sustained transactional momentum and an expanded deal pipeline, especially in industrial and European assets, with spreads benefiting from steady cap rates and funding by accretive non-core disposition activity. Updated guidance further reflects significant operating self-storage asset monetization, and continued focus on active capital deployment through 2026, underpinned by healthy liquidity, pending equity inflows, and disciplined leverage. The company expressed strategic confidence in maintaining double-digit total shareholder returns via blended AFFO growth and a competitive dividend yield, supported by proactive re-leasing and asset repositioning in the face of select tenant headwinds.

  • Fox emphasized "double-digit total shareholder returns in 2026" as management's target, citing both prospective AFFO growth and dividend policy.
  • Year-to-date investment cap rates averaged 7.6%; management noted recent cap rate stability, and the prospect of incremental competition from private equity investors, especially in the U.S.
  • Third-quarter investment activity marked a 50 -- 50 geographic split between North America and Europe, in contrast to the predominantly U.S.-focused activity earlier in the year.
  • Fixed escalations on new leases averaged 2.7%; CPI-linked escalations represented roughly one quarter of 2025 deals, mostly in Europe where inflation linkage remains customary.
  • Disposition activity, particularly in self-storage, executed at cap rates near 6%, allowing for positive investment spreads and further portfolio streamlining.
  • Brooks Gordon confirmed active asset management plans to meaningfully reduce exposure to the Hellweg tenant by mid-2026, with the aim of exiting the top 25 tenants.
  • Weighted average initial investment cap rates translate to mid-9% yields due to structured rent escalations and long lease terms.
  • The company described its Euro hedging program as limiting 2025 AFFO currency exposure to less than 20%, with a "cash flow hedging program beyond that" minimizing further FX risk impact.
  • Management anticipates 2026 same-store rent growth to likely exceed the current year's 2.5% expectation, supported by both fixed and CPI-based lease escalations.
  • Operating property NOI will decline further in 2026 as more hotel and student housing assets are monetized or redeveloped following sales or conversion from legacy net lease structures.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AFFO (Adjusted Funds from Operations): A REIT-specific performance metric representing cash generated from operations after deducting recurring capital expenditures.
  • ABR (Annualized Base Rent): The total amount of rent contractually due from tenants over a 12-month period, normalized for comparability.
  • Cap rate: The ratio of net operating income to asset purchase price, commonly used to evaluate real estate investment return.
  • Sale-leaseback: A transaction in which a company sells a property it owns and simultaneously leases it back, freeing up capital while retaining use of the property.
  • Build-to-suit: A real estate development approach where a property is constructed or renovated to meet the specific needs of a tenant before lease commencement.
