Medtronic (MDT) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 17, 2026 at 10:30AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Feb. 17, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — Geoffrey Martha
  • Chief Financial Officer — Thierry Pieton
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Ingrid Goldberg

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $9 billion, reflecting 8.7% reported and 6% organic growth, a 50-basis-point acceleration from the previous quarter and 50 basis points above guidance.
  • Cardiovascular Portfolio Revenue Growth -- 11% with U.S. growth at 13%, marking the highest segment growth in a decade aside from COVID periods.
  • Cardiac Ablation Solutions (CAS) -- 80% year-over-year growth, with pulsed field ablation (PFA) contributing 80% of segment revenue.
  • Cardiac Rhythm Management (CRM) -- Accounted for 15% of company revenue and grew 5%, driven by double-digit Micra growth and over 70% growth in Aurora EV ICD.
  • Peripheral Vascular Health -- Delivered high single-digit revenue growth, underpinned by strength in the endovenous portfolio.
  • Structural Heart -- Achieved low single-digit growth, with stronger results internationally and continued share gains in Europe, partially offset by a softer U.S. performance.
  • Neuroscience Portfolio -- Grew 3%, with Cranial and Spinal Technologies (CST) delivering mid-single-digit growth, including 8% in core spine.
  • Medical Surgical Portfolio -- Recorded 3% growth; Endoscopy revenue rose 10%, and Acute Care & Monitoring saw a 7% increase, while Surgical advanced by 1% due to strength in specific subsegments.
  • Diabetes Business (MiniMed) -- Reported 15% total and over 8% organic growth, led by double-digit international gains and a U.S. lift from new products Simplera Sync and Instinct.
  • Adjusted Gross Margin -- 64.9%, ahead of expectations; 30 basis points benefited from pricing, while mix was negative 100 basis points, mainly due to CAS and Diabetes; tariffs impacted costs by $93 million (110 basis points).
  • Adjusted Operating Profit -- $2.2 billion, resulting in an adjusted operating margin of 24.1%.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $1.36, $0.03 above the midpoint of the guidance range.
  • Fiscal 2026 Guidance -- Organic revenue growth target reiterated at 5.5%; EPS guidance maintained at $5.62 to $5.66; fiscal year gross margin projected to rise slightly excluding tariffs.
  • M&A and Investment Activity -- Notable acquisitions included Cat Works and strategic investments in Enteris, with management committed to increasing both organic and inorganic investments across the portfolio.
  • MiniMed Separation -- On track for completion by calendar year-end 2026, via a two-step IPO and split process with temporary EPS dilution of $0.01 to $0.02 per month during interim period.
  • Hugo Robotic Surgical System -- Received FDA clearance in urology and initiated U.S. installations; Touch Surgery AI ecosystem surpassed 1,000 global installations with a 20%+ sequential increase.
  • Stealth AXiS Surgical System -- Secured FDA clearance for spine procedures; navigation drives 70% of U.S. spine surgeries, providing a platform for portfolio expansion.
  • Simplicity (Renal Denervation) -- Direct-to-consumer campaign drove a 50x quarter-over-quarter increase in website visits; over 200 new accounts opened, and coverage expanded to 100 million U.S. lives.
  • Altaviva (Tibial Neurostimulation) -- Early launch metrics noted, including training of 500+ physicians; device offers same-day activation and up to 15 years of battery life.
  • CAS and Diabetes Mix Dynamics -- Management noted gross margin headwinds due to lower margin capital and early-stage manufacturing ramp, with expectations for improvement as scale increases and MiniMed separates.

RISKS

  • The structural heart segment experienced low single-digit growth with "a little softer" results in the U.S. due to competitive pressure and annualization of Evolut FX Plus launch.
  • Neurovascular saw "challenged over the last quarters due to China VBP and to the recall of Vantage," although these issues are "mostly behind us."
  • Management cautioned that the temporary EPS dilution during the MiniMed separation will be $0.01 to $0.02 per month, with accretion only occurring upon final split.
  • Tariffs are estimated to be a headwind of $185 million in fiscal 2026, including a projected $75 million impact in the fourth quarter.

SUMMARY

Medtronic (MDT 0.80%) delivered 6% organic revenue growth and reported strength across key segments, notably Cardiovascular and Diabetes, with significant product launches driving clinical adoption. The company confirmed its full-year guidance, citing "disciplined execution" as new innovations from Simplicity, Altaviva, Stealth AXiS, and Hugo scale in the U.S. and international markets. Strategic investments and acquisitions in high-growth areas reinforced management's commitment to durable long-term growth, while operational mix and external factors, such as tariffs and forthcoming portfolio changes, weigh on near-term margin progression.

  • New product cycles, notably in CAS, CRM, and Diabetes, contributed meaningfully to sequential improvement in both growth rate and geographic balance.
  • Management emphasized that recurring gross margin headwinds from CAS and Diabetes are expected to abate as recurring device sales increase and MiniMed completes its separation.
  • Future revenue and margin trajectory reflect Medtronic's reiteration of 5.5% organic revenue growth guidance, flat to slightly higher gross margin (excluding tariffs), and sustained R&D and M&A investment focus.
  • Unique leading indicators—such as a 50x increase in Simplicity site visits and rapid Touch Surgery installation growth—signal early traction of newly launched technologies.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • CAS (Cardiac Ablation Solutions): A Medtronic segment focused on the treatment of cardiac arrhythmias using energy-based ablation technologies.
  • PFA (Pulsed Field Ablation): An energy modality used in cardiac ablation that employs pulsed electric fields to ablate tissue with precision.
  • CRM (Cardiac Rhythm Management): Technologies and systems for managing heart rhythm disorders, such as pacemakers and defibrillators.
  • MiniMed: Medtronic's Diabetes business unit, encompassing insulin pumps and continuous glucose monitoring solutions.
  • CST (Cranial and Spinal Technologies): The portfolio of products addressing spine and neurosurgery markets, including surgical navigation and robotics.
  • Stealth AXiS: Medtronic’s new AI-powered navigation and robotics surgical platform for spinal procedures.
  • Hugo: Medtronic's robotic-assisted surgery system, now FDA-cleared for use in urology in the U.S.
  • Touch Surgery: Medtronic's digital ecosystem leveraging AI for data connectivity and analytics in the surgical suite.
  • Altaviva: Tibial neurostimulation device for urge urinary incontinence introduced by Medtronic.
  • Simplicity (Symplicity): Medtronic’s renal denervation system for hypertension management.
  • NeuroGuard: Medtronic’s carotid stent product with commercial opportunity across Neurovascular and Peripheral Vascular segments.
  • VBP (Volume-Based Procurement): A centralized purchasing initiative in China affecting pricing and volumes for medical devices.
