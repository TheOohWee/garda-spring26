Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $192.7 million, up 13% year over year, marking the company's 100th consecutive quarter of revenue growth.
  • Recurring revenue -- Increased 14% year over year for the quarter; for the full year, 20% growth to $751.5 million.

  • Recurring revenue customers -- Approximately 54,600, with one P customers flat sequentially and three P customers declining by 350.
  • Average revenue per user (ARPU) -- Approximately $14,350 for the year.
  • Cash reserves -- Ended the year with $151 million in cash and cash equivalents.
  • Share repurchases -- 76% of free cash flow deployed to repurchase $115 million in shares; Board increased repurchase authorization by $200 million to $300 million in total.

  • Guidance: Full year 2026 adjusted EBITDA -- Expected range of $261 million to $265.5 million, a 13%-15% increase.
  • Guidance: Full year 2026 fully diluted EPS -- Expected in the range of $2.50 to $2.58; non-GAAP diluted income per share in the range of $4.42 to $4.50.
  • Operating margin expansion -- Management anticipates two percentage points of adjusted EBITDA margin improvement annually, primarily through gross margin gains.
  • AI product launch -- "MAX," a new agentic AI capability embedded in the SPS supply chain network, launched for beta customers with initial features in fulfillment; customer feedback has been favorable.

  • CFO transition -- Kimberly Nelson retiring after nearly 20 years; Joseph Del Preto appointed as incoming CFO effective March 16, 2026.

Risks

  • Kimberly Nelson stated, "we ended up at the lower end of our revenue guidance. We," citing continued macro headwinds, spend scrutiny, and delayed purchase decisions by customers.
  • Amazon policy changes contributed to reduced performance for the revenue recovery business, with Nelson noting, "we did see more to the lower end of what our expectation would be," alongside effects from the take rate model.
  • Chad Collins said, "these are much smaller businesses, they do have a higher churn rate, and so there is going to be just some natural churn based on the size of the customer there," as three P customer count declined by 350 sequentially with focus shifting to one P customers, indicating persistent churn risk in the three P segment.
  • Down-sell activity has impacted customer connections and volumes since Q2 2025; Nelson indicated, "we'll have lapped the headwinds by the end of the first half of 2026," implying ongoing impact until mid-year 2026.

Summary

SPS Commerce (SPSC 1.61%) reported its 100th consecutive quarter of revenue growth, with full-year revenue reaching $751.5 million and recurring revenue increasing 20%. Management highlighted the successful launch of MAX, their new embedded AI agent, as a strategic initiative to expand cross-sell opportunities and product innovation. The company authorized a $200 million share repurchase expansion, bringing total authorization to $300 million, and is executing a CFO transition from Kimberly Nelson to Joseph Del Preto in March 2026. Two independent directors were added to the Board as part of a cooperation agreement following engagement with Anson Funds, underscoring corporate governance developments. SPS Commerce expects revenue growth to accelerate in the back half of 2026 as headwinds from down-sell activity and macro-related customer delays subside.

  • Management emphasized that ARPU growth will continue to be the principal driver of revenue, though new enablement campaigns are expected to contribute to customer additions later in 2026.
  • "Our reimagined retail go-to-market strategy is enabling more strategic conversations with retailers," positioning the company for increased cross-sell activity and customer engagement.
  • The company stated it expects to "increase our adjusted EBITDA margin by two percentage points annually" through cost discipline and operational efficiencies, especially in gross margin expansion.
  • Ongoing innovation in analytics and data integration was cited, with major replatforming efforts intended to further support AI-based value delivery and future product launches.
  • Capital allocation was balanced between continued M&A pipeline activity, investments in revenue recovery one P, and increased focus on share repurchases backed by robust free cash flow.

Industry glossary

  • One P (first-party seller): A seller that supplies goods directly to a retailer, such as Amazon, who then sells to end customers; contrasted with third-party (three P) sellers on platforms.
  • Three P (third-party seller): An independent seller that lists and sells products directly to customers via a retail platform, such as Amazon Marketplace, without transacting through the first-party retail structure.
  • Revenue recovery: SPS Commerce’s solution targeting the recovery of billing or deduction discrepancies for suppliers, offering a cross-sell opportunity across trading partners.
  • MAX: Newly launched SPS Commerce agentic AI capability embedded within the company’s platform, enabling automated workflows and customer interactions based on proprietary network data.
  • Enablement campaigns: SPS Commerce programs to onboard and digitize retail vendors, formerly known as community enablement, driving customer growth by activating trading partners within retail networks.
  • MCP (model communication protocol): Protocol enabling agent-to-agent, or system-to-system, communication for AI-driven integrations between SPS Commerce and external systems such as ERPs.
