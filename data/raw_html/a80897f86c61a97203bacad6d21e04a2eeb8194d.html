Sportradar (SRAD) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 23, 2025 at 11:50AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Monday, May 12, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Carsten Koerl
  • Chief Financial Officer — Craig Felenstein
  • Head of Investor Relations — James Bombassei

Takeaways

  • Total Revenue -- €311 million, representing 17% year-over-year growth and marking the highest quarterly revenue in company history.
  • U.S. Revenue -- Grew 31% year over year, now accounting for 28% of total company revenues.
  • Net Retention Rate -- Achieved 122%, indicating increasing client adoption of additional products and services.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- €59 million, up 25% year over year, with adjusted EBITDA margin expanding by 120 basis points to 19% (on an adjusted basis).
  • Free Cash Flow -- Generated €32 million for the quarter, reflecting a free cash flow conversion rate of 54%.
  • Betting Technology and Solutions Revenue -- €250 million, up 14%, fueled by a 13% increase in betting and gaming content and 15% growth in streaming and betting engagement products.
  • Odds and Live Data Revenue -- Increased 11% year over year, benefiting from U.S. market growth and broader product uptake.
  • Managed Betting Services -- Grew 16% year over year, primarily driven by higher Managed Trading Services turnover and margin.
  • Sports Content, Technology, and Services Revenue -- Reached €61 million, up 33% year over year, led by 36% growth in Marketing and Media Services mainly from increased ads business and expanded affiliate marketing.
  • Sports Rights Expense -- Rose 14% year over year to €104 million, primarily from ongoing ATP content success.
  • Adjusted Personnel Expenses -- €80 million, up 16%, attributed to increased headcount yet declining as a percentage of revenue.
  • Adjusted Purchased Services -- €44 million, up 24%, reflecting higher marketing affiliate costs and increased cloud and IT spending.
  • Profit -- €24 million profit, an improvement of €25 million, supported by a €28 million foreign currency gain, offset by higher share-based compensation.
  • Cash and Cash Equivalents -- Ended the quarter with €358 million, an increase of €10 million, and no debt outstanding.
  • Share Repurchases -- Repurchased $65 million of stock post-quarter at an average price of $21.83 per share (total repurchases now $86 million at an average price of $17.96), nearing half of the $200 million program’s authorization.
  • 2025 Guidance -- Maintained revenue guidance of at least €1.273 billion (15% year-over-year growth) and adjusted EBITDA of at least €281 million (26% growth); FX headwind of €10 million revenue and €3 million-€4 million EBITDA compared to original target.
  • IMG Arena Acquisition -- Expected to close in Q4, structured with no financial outlay and instead $125 million in received cash payments from Endeavor, plus up to $100 million in prepayments to sport rightsholders; anticipated to be immediately accretive to adjusted EBITDA and cash margins.
  • Product Penetration -- Only 40% of clients use four or more products, highlighting room for expanded cross-sell and take rate increases.
  • Technology Integration -- Computer vision now automates data collection for approximately 50% of covered events, with a long-term target of about 90% automation.
  • Brazil iGaming -- Onboarded 50 clients in Brazil for Managed Trading Services and iGaming marketing, with quarterly iGaming services growth of 31% in Brazil.
  • Contracted Revenue -- Approximately $2 billion in contracted revenue commitments secured over the next two years.
  • Long-Term Targets -- Management reaffirmed expectations of 15% revenue CAGR through 2027 and margin expansion to 27%; free cash flow conversion targeted at 60% over the coming years.

Summary

Sportradar Group AG (SRAD +1.44%) delivered record financial and operational results featuring double-digit revenue growth, margin expansion, and strong U.S. performance. Management highlighted tangible cost visibility due to locked-in sports rights contracts averaging six years, minimizing margin risk from input escalation. The upcoming IMG Arena portfolio acquisition, structured with net cash inflow and no cash payment, is set to boost both margin and cash generation immediately upon closing. Sportradar's ongoing investment in AI and computer vision aims to automate the vast majority of live event data capture, unlocking new product innovation and monetization pathways. Management confirmed solid progress toward its three-year revenue CAGR and margin expansion targets, underpinned by €2 billion of committed contractual revenues.

  • Management stated that 84 global iGaming brands now use Sportradar marketing services, and they see scalable potential for this initiative beyond Brazil.
  • CEO Carsten Koerl said Sportradar covers three of the four major U.S. leagues, encompassing about 70% of relevant betting GGR, providing a strong differentiation in the U.S. market.
  • CFO Craig Felenstein explained that underlying business performance is ahead of original expectations, but reported 2025 financial guidance remains unchanged only due to adverse foreign exchange movements.
  • Management confirmed that U.S. in-play betting rates stand at about 34%-35%, with a clear path to reach international benchmarks of 70%-80% as market education and player adoption progress.
  • Company leadership emphasized disciplined M&A, requiring all acquisitions—including potential iGaming scale-ups—to be accretive across revenue, EBITDA, and margin metrics.
  • The recently completed secondary offering increased the company's share trading liquidity and free float, with management participating directly via share repurchases.
  • New NBA and MLB micro markets and visualization tools such as 4Sight Streaming and Virtualized Live Match Tracker are expanding player engagement and betting opportunities.
  • Affiliate and marketing services revenue remains highly diversified by client geography and segment, and management reported a direct link between lower customer acquisition costs (CAC) and commercial uptake.

Industry glossary

  • MTS (Managed Trading Services): End-to-end risk management and trading solution for sportsbook operators encompassing odds setting, liability management, and live event management.
  • Take Rate: The percentage of gross gaming or wagering revenue Sportradar derives from its clients, reflecting the depth of product cross-sell and value capture.
  • GGR (Gross Gaming Revenue): The total amount wagered by players minus payouts, serving as a key metric for betting revenue pools.
  • In-Play Betting: Wagering on outcomes while an event is taking place, also referred to as live betting or in-running markets.
  • Micro Markets: Betting opportunities on highly granular in-game events or statistics, such as a single play or player action.
  • 4Sight Streaming: Sportradar’s proprietary next-generation AI-driven streaming and betting engagement solution for live sports.
  • CPA (Cost Per Acquisition): The amount spent by a sportsbook or iGaming operator to acquire each new betting customer through marketing activities.
  • Virtualized Live Match Tracker: A real-time animated tool visualizing live gameplay and event data for bettors within sportsbook platforms.
  • emBET: An interactive solution enabling in-game wager placement directly within existing streaming or other media environments.
