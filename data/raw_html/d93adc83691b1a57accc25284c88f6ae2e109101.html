Waste Connections (WCN) Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 11:14AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Oct. 22, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Ronald Mittelstaedt
  • Chief Financial Officer — Mary Anne Whitney

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $2.46 billion, up $120 million or 5.1% year over year, with acquisitions contributing about $77 million net of divestitures.
  • Core pricing -- 6.3% for the quarter, tracking toward approximately 6.5% for the full year, exceeding initial expectations.
  • Volumes -- Down 2.7%, reflecting purposeful shedding of low-margin contracts and ongoing macro-related sluggishness, with about 70 basis points of the decline attributed to contract shedding.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $830 million, up 5.4% year over year; margin of 33.8%, up 10 basis points despite a 20 basis point headwind from commodity declines.
  • Underlying solid waste margin expansion -- Increased 80 basis points, with margin gains highest in areas related to employee retention and lower openings.
  • Commodity revenues -- Recycled commodities and RINs down 30%-35% year over year; combined revenues fell 27%, causing about a 70 basis point margin drag, offset partially by contributions from new facilities.
  • E&P waste revenues -- Up 7% year over year, driven by Canadian R360 performance; U.S. legacy business declined slightly.
  • Free cash flow -- Adjusted year-to-date figure reached $1.08 billion, up more than $135 million; capital expenditures notably higher, supporting outlook for $1.3 billion for the full year.
  • Dividend -- Board authorized an 11.1% increase to the regular quarterly cash dividend, marking the 15th straight year of double-digit annual increases.
  • Share repurchases -- 2.4 million shares, or nearly 1% of shares outstanding, were repurchased; the issuer bid allows up to 5% annually.
  • M&A activity -- $300 million in annualized revenues from acquisitions closed or under agreement year-to-date, with more expected in Q4 and early 2026; includes closing of two large Florida companies (one in Q3, one expected in Q4).
  • Sustainability progress -- Achieved over 19% multiyear emissions reduction, a more than 55% reduction in voluntary employee turnover since late 2022, and safety incident rates down over 25% to new lows.
  • Chiquita Canyon landfill remediation -- Leachate removal now outpaces reaction generation, with odor complaints down more than 95% and all planned extraction wells and synthetic capping projects in progress.
  • Technology investments -- Ongoing digitization and AI deployment focused on pricing, forecasting, and route optimization, with rollout covering about 1/7 of P&Ls so far; early data show a 30%-40% reduction in churn for similar price increase levels.
  • Outlook for Q4 -- Revenue guidance for $2.36 billion; adjusted EBITDA margin anticipated to rise about 90 basis points year over year to 33.3%, aided by a reduced headwind from commodity revenues.
  • CapEx on RNG projects -- Revised 2025 spending guidance to $75 million-$125 million (down from $100 million-$150 million), with $25 million-$50 million projected to carry over into 2026.
  • Labor costs -- Labor inflation moderated to 4.1% in Q3, projected to further decline toward mid-3% in 2026.
  • Arrowhead landfill -- Tons processed reached about 7,500 per day in the period, up materially from acquisition levels; infrastructure investment enabling dedicated unit train operations expected to commence late in the fourth quarter.
  • Net interest expense and tax rate -- Net interest expense was $79 million and the effective tax rate reported at 23.6%.
  • Leverage -- Debt-to-EBITDA ratio comfortably within the targeted 2.75x range.

RISKS

  • Commodity headwinds -- CFO Whitney reported, "recycled commodities and renewable energy credits, or RINs, continued to slide during Q3, both ending the quarter down 30% to 35% year over year," resulting in a margin drag of 70 basis points, and CEO Mittelstaedt explicitly said, "commodities, if they stayed where they are, about a 20 to 25 basis point dilutive impact on a year-to-year basis and."
  • Chiquita Canyon landfill costs -- Mittelstaedt acknowledged, "At this point, the outlays are running somewhat ahead of our expectations because we've taken additional steps to decrease the impacted area and accelerated some of those steps on our leachate treatment activities."
  • Purposeful volume shedding -- CFO Whitney stated, "about 70 basis points of that has been this intentional shedding," with further impact possible from ongoing contract reviews.

SUMMARY

Waste Connections (WCN 1.35%) delivered above-expectation third-quarter results, supported by disciplined pricing, continued portfolio optimization, and incremental margin expansion amid persistent commodity headwinds. Management confirmed no revision to full-year guidance, reaffirming the strength of the business model and financial flexibility for both organic initiatives and M&A. Technology investments in pricing and operational analytics are already contributing to both improved retention and margin lift, with management citing a multiyear digitization process continuing through 2027. RNG project timelines remain consistent, with notable revenue and EBITDA contributions only expected to materialize post-2026, while strategic capital allocation remains focused on core waste acquisitions and opportunistic share repurchases.

  • CEO Mittelstaedt said, "To date, we bought back approximately 2.4 million shares or almost 1% of shares outstanding," with active share repurchases supplementing increased dividend returns.
  • CFO Whitney stated, "Core pricing of 6.3% in Q3 puts us on pace for full year core pricing of approximately 6.5%," illustrating measurable improvement over the initial outlook and reflecting adoption of new digital tools.
  • Management disclosed the Chiquita Canyon landfill capping and remediation process is proceeding as planned, with current outlays in 2025 "ahead of our expectations" but underlying environmental metrics steadily improving.
  • Leadership reiterated the priority of price-led organic growth, with volume contributions expected to remain muted absent macro acceleration, and offsetting headwinds from intentional contract shedding and industry-specific cost exposures.
  • Planned 2026 margin expansion is framed by "normalized" solid waste growth, but offset by 30 to 40 basis points of anticipated headwinds from both commodities and M&A integration effects.
  • Expansion of technology-driven customer segmentation for pricing has led to a reported 30%-40% reduction in churn on like-for-like price actions, suggesting compounding operational benefits as rollout accelerates.
  • Arrowhead landfill rail and infrastructure upgrades are expected to enhance throughputs further in late 2025 and into 2026, while Seneca Meadows permitting and additional market entry in commercial waste zones in New York proceed as anticipated.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RINs (Renewable Identification Numbers): Regulatory credits associated with renewable energy production, affecting landfill gas and recycling revenues.
  • C&D (Construction and Demolition): Waste segment comprising materials generated by construction, renovation, and demolition projects, tracked separately in volume and tonnage.
  • Roll-off pulls: Service events where large waste containers are removed and replaced; a key metric in construction and industrial accounts.
  • Chiquita Canyon landfill: Major landfill asset, now the subject of ongoing remediation efforts and associated cost disclosures.
  • MSW (Municipal Solid Waste): Standard category for community-generated non-hazardous waste handled in collection, transfer, and landfill operations.
  • R360: Waste Connections' branded E&P (exploration and production) waste management business, with operations in the U.S. and Canada.
  • OCC (Old Corrugated Containers): A recyclable cardboard product and a principal commodity within the recycling revenue stream.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: A non-GAAP measure of profitability used by Waste Connections to evaluate core operating performance, excluding certain expenses and non-cash items.
