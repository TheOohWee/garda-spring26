GE (GE) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 22, 2026 at 12:31PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Oct. 21, 2025 at 7:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — H. Lawrence Culp Jr.
  • Chief Financial Officer — Rahul Ghai
  • Vice President of Investor Relations — Blaire Shoor

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Orders -- Up 2% in the quarter, with services growth offset by timing in equipment and defense; year-to-date orders increased 13% and services orders grew 31%.
  • Total revenue -- $11.3 billion, up 26%, with both segments exceeding 25% growth.
  • Operating profit -- $2.3 billion, up 26%, supported by broad-based deliveries.
  • EPS -- $1.66, up 44%, aided by higher operating profit, reduced tax rate, and lower share count.
  • Free cash flow -- $2.4 billion, an increase of 30% with over 130% conversion.
  • Commercial Engines & Services (CES) segment -- Revenue up 27%, services revenue up 28%, internal shop visit revenue up 33%, and spare parts revenue up more than 25%; segment profit $2.4 billion, up 35%.
  • Total engine deliveries -- Increased 41% year over year and 18% sequentially; commercial units up 33% and LEAP engine deliveries up 40% in the quarter.
  • Defense & Propulsion Technologies (DPT) segment -- Revenue up 26%; engine volume up 83% and segment profit $386 million, up 75% year over year.
  • Operating margins -- Flat at 20.3%; CES margins expanded to 27.4% (up 170 basis points), DPT margins to 13.6% (up 380 basis points).
  • Backlog -- Approximately $175 billion, with DPT backlog at $19 billion (up $1.5 billion year over year).
  • Material input from priority suppliers -- Up 35% year over year, with shipments exceeding 95% of target for third consecutive quarter.
  • LEAP shop visits -- Internal output grew over 30% and external shop visits increased approximately twofold; significant progress achieved in turnaround time reductions.
  • R&D investment -- Approximately $3 billion annually, supporting durability improvements and next-generation engine testing.
  • 2025 guidance (raised) -- Revenue growth now expected in high teens (was mid-teens); CES revenue growth low 20s (was high teens); DPT growth high single digits (was mid- to high single digits); operating profit $8.65 billion–$8.85 billion (raised $400 million at midpoint); CES profit $8.45 billion–$8.65 billion (raised $450 million at midpoint); DPT profit $1.2 billion–$1.3 billion (raised $50 million at midpoint); EPS $6.00–$6.20 (raised $0.40 at midpoint); free cash flow $7.1 billion–$7.3 billion (raised $500 million at midpoint).
  • 2026 preliminary outlook -- Commercial services revenue growth is expected to normalize to double-digit rates, with margin expansion opportunities limited by increased 9X losses and inflationary pressures.
  • Significant wins -- New fleet commitments by Korean Air (103 Boeing aircraft powered by GE engines and services) and Cathay Pacific (GE9X engines for 14 additional 777-9s).
  • Capital deployment -- $24 billion planned shareholder returns for 2024–2026, and a $300 million investment in BETA Technologies to co-develop a hybrid electric turbo generator.
  • Tax rate -- Reduced from approximately 20% to 15%, contributing $0.10 to EPS improvement.
  • Days sales outstanding -- Improved by 3 days year over year.

Summary

General Electric (GE 1.49%) reported significant quarterly revenue, profit, and free cash flow growth, prompting a comprehensive upward revision of annual guidance in all key financial metrics. Management cited persistent commercial services strength, improved supply chain execution, expanded backlog, and strong order activity as primary catalysts for margin enhancement and earnings progression. Strategic investments and operational advancements—particularly in LEAP engine durability, supply chain resilience, and next-generation platforms—underpin the company’s competitive positioning and future growth algorithm.

  • H. Culp stated, "we're raising our full year guidance across the board," reflecting heightened confidence in financial trajectory and operational performance.
  • Leadership highlighted FLIGHT DECK, the proprietary lean operating model, as a critical driver of delivery acceleration and supply chain yield improvements throughout both CES and DPT.
  • Expanded LEAP shop visit networks and rapid uptake of new technologies, such as analytics-based maintenance and advanced HPT blade durability kits, may position the company for sustained aftermarket revenue outperformance.
  • Management indicated that capital allocation priorities will remain focused on reinvestment in organic growth and shareholder returns, with disciplined flexibility for targeted M&A and emerging technology partnerships.

Industry glossary

  • FLIGHT DECK: GE Aerospace’s proprietary lean operating framework used to optimize manufacturing, supply chain, and service outcomes.
  • LEAP: A family of high-bypass aircraft engines co-developed by GE and Safran Aircraft Engines, widely used in narrow-body commercial aircraft.
  • HPT blade: High-Pressure Turbine blade component critical for engine durability and thermal efficiency.
  • MRO: Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul; facilities or network performing engine servicing and parts repair for customer fleets.
  • Shop visit: Scheduled engine maintenance event where the engine is removed from service for inspection or repair.
  • ASK: Available Seat Kilometers; an airline industry metric representing capacity offered for passenger traffic.

So there's a lot of pent-up demand here on volume. And that's a big piece why the total demand is kind of outpacing the departures growth. The second is the increase in work scopes. So if you look at GE90 as an example, the GE90 is now going -- like 70% of the shop visits haven't gone in for a second shop visit. H. Culp: No, just maybe that, right? We're just in the process of wrapping up our strategy reviews. We'll spend the fourth quarter working through the details of the '26 budget.
