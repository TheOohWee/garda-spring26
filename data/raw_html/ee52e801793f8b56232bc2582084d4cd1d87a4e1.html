Takeaways

  • Worldwide revenue -- $56.3 million for the quarter, up 15%.
  • U.S. revenue -- $53.5 million, representing 13.9% growth against a challenging prior-year comparison.
  • International revenue -- $2.9 million, up 38.8% with strong demand for iFuse TORQ.
  • Full-year revenue -- $200.9 million, reflecting 20.2% growth.
  • Gross profit (quarter) -- $44.5 million, an increase of 14.8%.
  • Gross profit (full year) -- $159.9 million, up 21%.
  • Gross margin -- 79% for the quarter and 79.6% for the full year, approximately 200 basis points above prior 2025 guidance.
  • Operating expenses (quarter) -- $47 million, increasing 6.2% driven by revenue-generating activity and R&D investment.
  • Operating expenses (full year) -- $182.2 million, up 8.9%.
  • Net loss (quarter) -- $1.6 million, or $0.04 per diluted share, compared to $4.5 million, or $0.11, the year prior.
  • Net loss (full year) -- $18.9 million, or $0.44 per diluted share, narrowed by 38.8%.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (quarter) -- $5.1 million, representing a 176.2% improvement.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin (quarter) -- 9.1%.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (full year) -- $8.9 million, swinging from a $5.1 million loss to positive EBITDA.
  • Free cash flow (quarter) -- Nearly $500,000 generated, marking the first quarter of positive free cash flow, ahead of prior 2026 expectations.
  • Cash and equivalents -- $147.8 million at year-end, increasing $2.1 million from the prior quarter.
  • Physician adoption -- Record 1,640 physicians performed procedures in the quarter, an 18% increase.
  • Territory manager force -- 89 quota-carrying managers; revenue per territory rose 18% to $2.1 million.
  • Product launches -- Intra TI received FDA 510(k) clearance and began alpha launch; a third Breakthrough Device expected to submit for clearance in the third quarter and potentially commercialize late 2026.
  • Reimbursement milestones -- Secured NTAP for iFuse TORQ TNT and TPT for iFuse Bedrock Granite, including CMS policy updates leading to a 17% increase in Medicare reimbursement for office-based labs and a new musculoskeletal APC with nearly $28 million payment for specific outpatient procedures.
  • Smith+Nephew partnership -- Strategic arrangement signed to provide trauma market access at level 1 and level 2 trauma centers nationwide, effective beginning March with ramp-up expected through the year.
  • 2026 outlook -- Revenue guidance of $228.5 million to $232.5 million, representing 14%-16% growth, with back-half weighting anticipated, annual gross margin of approximately 78%, and operating expense growth of 12.5% midpoint.
  • ASC exposure -- 35% of SI joint fusion sales now occur in ambulatory surgery centers, up from negligible five years ago.

Summary

SI-BONE (SIBN 6.21%) reported double-digit revenue and profit growth, with positive adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow inflection, driven by expanding product adoption and favorable reimbursement gains. Management outlined major product launches, a new trauma segment partnership, and a broadened direct sales force as catalysts for future revenue growth. Guidance anticipates continued operating leverage and investment in innovation, with multiple tailwinds including policy shifts, commercial expansion, and new market entries positioned to support sustained profitability and addressable market expansion.

  • The inclusion of the open SI joint fusion code in TPT calculation by CMS is expected to further improve reimbursement pathways.
  • International launches for TNT are being brought forward to 2026 based on strong demand, advancing availability ahead of prior plans.
  • Physician utilization data reveals that only 25% of SI joint fusion physicians have adopted additional procedures from the company, indicating material cross-sell opportunity.
  • Leadership transitions place Nicholas Kerr as Chief Commercial Officer and Anshul Maheshwari as Chief Operating Officer; longstanding reimbursement expertise remains in-house with Jeff Ziegler’s promotion.

Industry glossary

  • NTAP (New Technology Add-On Payment): Supplemental Medicare reimbursement for hospital inpatient cases using qualifying new technologies, enhancing payment for costly, innovative devices.
  • TPT (Transitional Pass-Through payment): Additional Medicare reimbursement for outpatient procedures with new technologies meeting specific CMS criteria, temporarily increasing facility payments.
  • 510(k) clearance: U.S. FDA process permitting marketing of a medical device shown to be substantially equivalent to an existing legally marketed device.
  • APC (Ambulatory Payment Classification): CMS payment system grouping outpatient procedures into categories for facility reimbursement, updated to reflect procedural complexity.
  • ASC (Ambulatory Surgery Center): Specialized healthcare facility focused on outpatient surgical care.
  • OBL (Office-Based Lab): Medical practice setting where minimally invasive procedures are performed outside of hospital or ASC environments.
