Earnings Call Transcript

EPAM (EPAM) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 6:48PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Nov. 6, 2025 at 2:26 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Balazs Fejes ("FB")
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jason Peterson

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $1.394 billion, representing 19.4% reported growth and 7.1% organic constant currency growth.
  • AI-Native Revenues -- Achieved a third consecutive quarter of double-digit sequential growth, signaling accelerating demand for AI-focused services.
  • Vertical Growth -- Five of six industry verticals grew year-over-year, with financial services up 32.7% reported and 6% organic, software and hi-tech up 19.1%, life sciences and healthcare rising 11.8%, consumer goods, retail, and travel increasing 9.9%, and business information & media flat.
  • Emerging Verticals -- Delivered 38.9% reported and 15.1% organic constant currency growth, primarily from strength in energy and materials.
  • Geographic Performance -- Americas represented 58% of revenue and rose 16% reported and 3.9% organic; EMEA accounted for 40% and grew 24.9% reported and 11.8% organic; APAC comprised 2% and advanced 17.7% reported and 14.2% organic constant currency.
  • Gross Margin -- GAAP gross margin was 29.5% (from 34.6% previous year); non-GAAP gross margin was 31% (from 34.3%), reflecting higher variable compensation and lower profitability from recent acquisitions.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Reached $286 million, marking an all-time high for EPAM (EPAM 1.16%), surpassing seasonality impact.
  • GAAP Diluted EPS -- $1.91 for the quarter; non-GAAP diluted EPS $3.08, down $0.04 from the prior year.
  • Utilization Rate -- 76.5%, up from 76.4% year-over-year, but down from 78.1% sequentially from Q2 2025.
  • Headcount -- Quarter-end total employees reached 62,350, with 56,100 in consulting, design, engineering, and architecture—17.5% total and 6.4% organic increase.
  • Share Repurchase -- Approximately 493,000 shares repurchased for $82 million; new $1 billion repurchase program announced.
  • Q4 Revenue Guidance -- Projected at $1.380 billion to $1.395 billion, implying 11.1% year-over-year growth at midpoint, with 4.4% organic constant currency growth and 2.4% positive FX impact.
  • Full-Year Revenue Outlook -- Expected in the $5.430 billion to $5.445 billion range, a 15% year-over-year increase at midpoint; inorganic contributions estimated at 9.1% for 2025.
  • Profitability Guidance -- GAAP income from operations forecasted at 9.4%-9.7%; non-GAAP income from operations at 15%-15.3% for the full year.
  • AI Project Scale -- Of all active AI-native projects in Q3, 60%-70% have expanded from proof-of-concept to larger programs involving both proprietary and open source components.
  • Agentic QA Launch -- Management claimed "Agentic QA is shown to be 10x more efficient than manual testing," with a 50% reduction in manual efforts and 30% reduction in testing costs, addressing 90% of standard manual checks.

SUMMARY

The management stated that more than 90% of employees have completed mandatory AI literacy training, with approximately 95% of engineers having finished foundational AI education. Three proprietary IP offerings—AI/RUN Transform, DIAL, and Agentic QA—were highlighted as fueling differentiated project delivery. The company indicated a sustained shift in AI projects toward medium- and large-scale production implementations, with EPAM increasingly acting as the replacement for partners unable to deliver on advanced capabilities. Management expects further improvements in account margin and profitability, citing a targeted focus on deal selectivity and pyramid balancing for workforce optimization.

  • Balazs Fejes stated, "We believe that as complexity rises so does enterprise risk, raising the importance of highly advanced engineering with proven enterprise-grade quality execution."
  • Jason Peterson reported, "cash flow from operations in the quarter exceeded the impact of typical seasonality and resulting in the highest level of quarterly cash flow from operations in EPAM's history."
  • Management signaled that NEORIS's largest client faced declines due to U.S. tariffs and economic instability in Mexico, modestly weighing on organic growth for Q4.
  • The AI/RUN strategy aims to anchor future growth across both "AI innovation business transformation and AI native engineering transformation," supported by agnostic frameworks and industry-specific accelerators.
  • Management noted that pricing has improved versus the prior year and anticipates low single-digit increases as the company enters 2026.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AI/RUN: EPAM’s integrated suite of frameworks, talent models, and tools designed to accelerate AI-native engineering and delivery for enterprise-scale projects.
  • Agentic QA: An EPAM proprietary quality assurance tool leveraging agentic AI to enhance testing efficiency and reduce costs in software development.
  • SDLC: Software Development Life Cycle; the traditional sequence of stages for developing software from conception through deployment.
  • ADLC: Agentic Delivery Life Cycle; a framework for orchestrating and managing complex agent-powered process automation initiatives.
  • PDLC: Product Development Life Cycle; the full journey of creating, launching, and scaling new product offerings.
  • NEORIS: A recently acquired EPAM business focused on digital solutions, with particular strength in Latin America.
  • First Derivative: An acquired business unit with roots in financial services consultancy and process automation.
