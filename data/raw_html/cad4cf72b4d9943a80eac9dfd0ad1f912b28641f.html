Risks

  • Advertising monetization did not meet internal targets, as CEO Dufay said, "the advertising, so basically monetization on the advertising side, as I was mentioning earlier, is still lower than our expectations in Q4 included across the whole year, actually, and that's the reason for deviation on the bottom line on our end, unfortunately."
  • Algeria exit will result in short-term employee and lease exit costs as well as asset liquidation costs, though anticipated to improve operational focus long-term.

Summary

Jumia Technologies (JMIA 0.43%) delivered substantial acceleration in revenues and order volume, with solid gains in active customer acquisition and upcountry expansion. Management reinforced confidence in achieving adjusted EBITDA breakeven and positive cash flow in Q4 2026, targeting full-year profitability the following year. Market share advances materialized across several segments, and operational initiatives—such as renegotiated logistics contracts and technology enhancements—are designed to support further margin improvement and cost leverage.

  • Management stated that market expansion or new country entry is postponed until full-year profitability is achieved, while current core geographies are expected to deliver both scale and profitability.
  • Fulfillment and tech capacity is considered sufficient for anticipated growth over the next two years, with major CapEx not expected until at least 2027 in most countries.
  • Third-party commissions and marketplace take rates were raised across most countries in early 2026, with enhancements in advertising tools and processes to increase ad revenue from below 1% toward a 2% medium-term target.
  • Recent VAT and tax legislation on nonresident digital platforms in Ivory Coast and Ghana was cited as supporting a more level competitive playing field for Jumia versus global entrants.
  • Buy now, pay later adoption in Egypt was identified as a significant driver for high-value item conversion, while limited rollout is anticipated in other regions due to legal and ecosystem constraints.

Industry glossary

  • GMV (Gross Merchandise Value): The total value of merchandise sold over a given period via Jumia's e-commerce platform, before deducting any fees or returns.
  • Perimeter effects: Adjustments made to reported growth figures to exclude impacts from business exits or segment changes, ensuring like-for-like comparisons.
  • First-party sales: Transactions where Jumia sells goods directly to customers, purchasing inventory itself.
  • Third-party sales: Revenue from marketplace sales where independent sellers list products on Jumia's platform.
  • Fulfillment expense per order: Average logistics and shipping cost incurred by Jumia for delivering each customer order, excluding pure digital orders.
  • BNPL (Buy now, pay later): A consumer finance service allowing customers to purchase products immediately and pay for them over time through installments.
