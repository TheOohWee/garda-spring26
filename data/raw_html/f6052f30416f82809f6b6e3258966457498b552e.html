US Physical Therapy (USPH) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 26, 2026 at 12:17PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Feb. 26, 2026 at 10:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Christopher J. Reading
  • Chief Financial Officer — Carey P. Hendrickson
  • Chief Operating Officer — Eric Joseph Williams

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Average Visits per Clinic per Day -- 32.7, a new company record, up from 30.6 in the prior-year quarter.
  • Net Promoter Score -- 93.5, with 95% of patients categorized as "active promoters" and only 1% as detractors, based on company-wide surveys.
  • Physical Therapy Revenue -- $168,300,000, an increase of $24,800,000, or 17.3%, mainly from acquisitions (Metro PT contributing $19,600,000).
  • Industrial Injury Prevention (IIP) Revenue -- Up 22.6%, with organic revenue growth of 18.4% versus the prior year.
  • Gross Profit Margin (PT Segment) -- 21.1%, compared to 20.1% in the prior-year quarter, the highest since 2023.
  • IIP Gross Profit Margin -- 22%, up from 21.4% a year ago; organic gross profit growth was 21.8%.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $26,900,000, an increase of $4,700,000, with margin rising to 17.5% from 16.4%.
  • Net Rate per Patient Visit -- $105.33, up from $105.05 last year, despite headwinds from a 2.9% Medicare rate reduction and a payer policy change in Michigan.
  • Same-Store Growth in Mature Facilities -- Slightly above 1%, described as "a little bit lighter" than usual due to staffing tightness and cost control.
  • Medicare Revenue Headwind -- The impact from sequential Medicare cuts reached $25,000,000 cumulatively, with a $5,000,000–$6,000,000 year-over-year effect, representing 8%-8.5% of prior-year earnings.
  • Salaries and Related Costs per Visit -- Increased 0.7% year over year, marking the smallest rise since 2023.
  • Total Operating Cost per Visit -- Decreased year over year.
  • Visit Volumes -- Total of 1,532,263 clinic visits and 28,493 home care visits in the quarter, with home care visits reported separately for the first time.
  • Workers’ Compensation Business -- Accounted for 10.4% of net patient revenues, with visit volume up 8.4%.
  • Commercial Net Rate per Visit -- Approximately $105.50, up 1%-1.5% year over year and 2.2% from the first quarter; Michigan policy change reduced rates by about $0.30 per visit.
  • Cash-Based Services Revenue -- about $900,000 attributed to these offerings, showing continued ramp-up since a staged rollout in the spring.
  • Clinic Additions -- Over 50 net new clinics compared to the prior year, with Metro PT clinics averaging about 45 visits per day.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- Board authorized up to $25,000,000 for repurchases through 12/31/2026.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Guidance Raised -- New range set at $93,000,000–$97,000,000, up from $88,000,000–$93,000,000.
  • Term Loan and Cash Position -- $135,000,000 in term loan fixed at 4.7% through mid-2027; $34,100,000 in cash at quarter end.
  • Home Care Visit Growth -- In-home visits increased from 22,943 in the first quarter to 28,493 in the second quarter.
  • AI and Technology Initiatives -- Deployment of AI-driven documentation tools and semi-virtualized front desk processes aimed at improving clinician efficiency and reducing labor needs.
  • Recruitment and Retention Progress -- 25% increase in student clinical rotations in 2025, adoption of new tracking systems, and lowest turnover rates in seven years for January–June.

Summary

U.S. Physical Therapy (USPH 0.65%) reported significant revenue growth, margin expansion, and operational efficiency improvements, driven primarily by acquisitions and robust IIP segment performance. Management emphasized increased investments in recruiting and retention, rapid expansion in home care services, and the introduction of AI-backed tools to further optimize operations. Capital allocation will continue to prioritize acquisitions, particularly in the injury prevention segment, with the newly authorized buyback cited as a flexible secondary option. A staged rollout of cash-based offerings produced measurable incremental revenue, while newly reported home care metrics mark an evolution in disclosure practices. Management expects a 1%-1.75% Medicare rate increase in 2026, potentially adding $2,000,000–$3,000,000 to topline results.

  • Metro PT acquisition led to notable net rate improvements, rising to $107.50 per visit in the second quarter from $101 at acquisition.
  • Cost control initiatives and improved visit-per-day volumes contributed to a declining operating cost per visit and higher incremental profitability.
  • Growth in workers’ compensation patient mix and strategic contract negotiations supported net rate stability despite policy headwinds in Michigan.
  • Injury prevention organic growth outperformed management’s budget expectations, with development efforts ongoing for additional large-scale contracts.
  • AI-powered clinical documentation tools are now in use by up to 250 therapists, targeting reductions in administrative workload and turnover.
  • Management stated, "This is going to be probably the strongest de novo year that we have had since I have been with the company," highlighting de novo clinic openings as a major growth driver.
  • Physical capacity was described as non-limiting, with future visit volume growth dependent on staffing rather than clinic space constraints.
  • Board remains focused on acquisitions as the primary capital deployment, positioning share repurchases as opportunistic and subordinate in priority.
  • Implementation of an enterprise-wide financial and HR system will extend through 2026, with related costs added back in adjusted EBITDA calculations and $221,000 recorded year to date.

Industry glossary

  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for certain nonrecurring or noncash items specified by management.
  • IIP (Industrial Injury Prevention): Segment responsible for onsite injury prevention services, performance optimization, and related services to industrial clients.
  • Metro PT: Physical therapy provider in New York acquired by USPH in November of the prior year.
  • Net Promoter Score (NPS): Metric gauging customer loyalty and satisfaction based on the likelihood of referring the service.
  • AquaNovo: Small, unannounced de novo clinic format within select high-reimbursement markets discussed in the call.
  • Cash-Based Services: Revenue streams not submitted through insurance, paid directly by patients for specialized or supplemental physical therapy services.
