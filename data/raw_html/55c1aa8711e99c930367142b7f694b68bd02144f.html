J.P. Morgan Chase (JPM) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Jan 15, 2026 at 1:33PM EST

Date

Tuesday, Jan. 13, 2026, at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Financial Officer — Jeremy Barnum
  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Jamie Dimon

Takeaways

  • Net income -- $13 billion for the quarter, with full-year net income of $57.5 billion, excluding significant items outlined in the filing footnote.
  • Earnings per share (EPS) -- $4.63 for the quarter and $20.18 for the year.
  • Return on tangible common equity (ROTCE) -- 18% for the quarter and 20% for the year.
  • Reserve build -- $2.2 billion NCCV associated with the Apple Card portfolio forward purchase commitment, impacting results.
  • Total revenue -- $46.8 billion this quarter, up 7% on higher markets revenue, asset management fees, and auto lease income; full-year revenue of $185 billion.
  • Net interest income excluding markets (NII ex markets) -- Growth driven by higher deposits and revolving balances in card, partially offset by lower rates.
  • Expenses -- $24 billion, up 5%, mainly due to higher volume and compensation, with partial offset from an FDIC assessment accrual release.
  • Standardized CET1 ratio -- 14.5%, down 30 bps sequentially due to capital distributions and higher risk-weighted assets (RWA).
  • Apple Card transaction RWA impact -- Advanced RWA rose by $110 billion on closing, expected to drop to $30 billion near term; standardized RWA up $23 billion from this commitment.
  • CCB (Consumer & Community Banking) net income -- $3.6 billion (or $5.3 billion excluding Apple Card reserve build), with revenue up 6% to $19.4 billion, mostly from higher card balances and deposit margin.
  • Debit and credit sales volume -- Up 7% across income groups.
  • Franchise growth -- 1.7 million net new checking accounts, 10.4 million new card accounts, and record households in wealth management were added during the year.
  • CIB (Corporate & Investment Bank) net income -- $7.3 billion; revenue up 10% to $19.4 billion, driven by increased markets, payments, and security services revenue.
  • Investment banking fees -- Down 5%, attributed to a strong prior year and deals deferred to 2026.
  • Markets revenue -- Fixed income up 7% with growth in securitized products, rates, and emerging market currencies; equities up 40% led by prime; lower credit trading revenue partially offset gains.
  • AWM (Asset & Wealth Management) net income -- $1.8 billion with a 38% pretax margin and revenue at $6.5 billion, up 13% on asset management fee growth, net inflows, and increased performance fees.
  • AWM long-term net inflows -- $52 billion for the quarter and $29 billion for the full year, positive across all asset classes.
  • AWM liquidity net inflows -- $105 billion for the quarter and $183 billion for the year.
  • Record client asset net inflows -- $553 billion for the year.
  • Corporate segment net income -- $3.7 billion, with revenue at $1.5 billion.
  • Nonbank financial institution lending exposure -- Approximately $160 billion as defined internally, lower than regulatory report figure due to exclusions such as subscription lending.
  • Expected 2026 net interest income (NII) in markets -- About $95 billion, following the forward curve's two assumed rate cuts, modest firmwide deposit growth, and card loan growth slightly below prior year.
  • Total NII 2026 guidance -- Approximately $103 billion, with markets NII expected at $8 billion.
  • 2026 adjusted expense guidance -- Approximately $105 billion, reflecting meaningful year-over-year growth aimed at strategic investment and growth opportunities.
  • 2026 card net charge-off rate guidance -- Expected at about 3.4%, linked to consumer resilience despite unfavorable delinquency trend.

Risks

  • Jeremy Barnum stated that "where price controls make it no longer a good business, that would present a significant challenge," indicating material risk to the card segment should regulation proceed.
  • Management described the integration of the Apple Card business as requiring "two years for a reason," with substantial technology adaptation, implying both operational risk and higher near-term expenses related to integration.
  • Elevated advanced RWA from the Apple Card transaction is flagged as temporary, but the near-term increase to $110 billion signals interim pressure on capital ratios.

Summary

J.P. Morgan Structured Products B.V. (JPM 0.11%) reported quarterly revenue growth of 7% to $46.8 billion, with the Apple Card acquisition increasing both reserves and risk-weighted assets. Asset and Wealth Management achieved record client asset net inflows of $553 billion, supporting growth in management and performance fees. Management reaffirmed 2026 guidance for net interest income at $103 billion and adjusted expenses at $105 billion, citing ongoing investment across technology and business lines. The firm also outlined plans to continue capital deployment, emphasizing returns through the cycle rather than annual efficiency targets. Strategic outlook includes measured deposit and card loan growth, but signals moderation compared to prior year levels.

  • Jeremy Barnum highlighted that CCB's net checking account openings and client asset growth have not fully translated into proportional deposit growth due to persistent, though moderating, yield-seeking flows.
  • Advanced RWA associated with the Apple Card deal is anticipated to decline from $110 billion to $30 billion in the near term, but capital distribution outpaced net income this quarter, temporarily reducing CET1 ratio.
  • Jamie Dimon observed that macroeconomic tailwinds and deregulation support a positive short- to medium-term outlook, but stressed heightened geopolitical and fiscal risks that could eventually impact the industry.
  • Management reported persistent competitive pressure in consumer deposits and persistent optimism for AWM driven by adviser and banker hiring, product innovation, and continued inflows.
  • Barnum addressed regulatory capital questions by refraining from a set “management buffer,” emphasizing that capital numbers are already set to handle maximum stress, and Jamie Dimon noted, "There's no scenario where capital is gonna be the issue."

Industry glossary

  • Advanced RWA: Risk-weighted assets calculated under the advanced approaches framework, typically more sensitive to portfolio and model changes than standardized RWA.
  • NII ex markets: Net interest income excluding the contribution from trading and markets-related business, isolating core lending and deposit-driven NII.
  • SCB: Stress Capital Buffer; an additional capital requirement set by regulators following stress test results.
  • NVFI: Nonbank financial institution; institutions providing lending, investing, or other financial services without a banking license, often subject to different regulatory standards.
