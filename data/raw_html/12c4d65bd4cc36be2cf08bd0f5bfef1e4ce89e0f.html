Earnings Call Transcript

Teva (TEVA) Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 2:06PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, January 29, 2025 at 8 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Richard Francis
  • Head of Global Research & Development and Chief Medical Officer — Eric Hughes
  • Chief Financial Officer — Eli Kalif

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $16.5 billion, up 9% in local currencies, at the high end of outlook and excluding the prior Sanofi milestone payment.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $4.8 billion, representing growth of 9% year over year, also excluding the Sanofi upfront payment.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $2.49, an increase of 10% compared to the prior year, on a comparable basis.
  • Free cash flow -- $2.1 billion, up 10% year over year, excluding the Sanofi revenue impact.
  • Net debt -- Approximately $14.5 billion at year-end, with a net debt to EBITDA ratio close to 3x, tracking toward a 2x target by 2027.
  • AUSTEDO performance -- U.S. revenue of $1.642 billion, growing 34% with total global sales near $1.7 billion; 2025 guidance set at $1.9 billion to just over $2 billion.
  • UZEDY revenue -- $117 million for the year, surpassing internal target; 2025 forecast indicated at $160 million.
  • AJOVY sales -- Delivered over $0.5 billion in revenue, growing 18% and strengthening market share across all regions, with $600 million targeted for 2025.
  • Generics segment -- Sales closed at $9.5 billion, up 11%, with all regions contributing to growth; Europe grew 6% and international 15%.
  • TAPI (Teva Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients) -- Achieved four quarters of growth and full-year return to growth; business now classified as “held for sale.”
  • Non-GAAP gross profit margin -- 54.8% for the quarter; improved about 150 basis points year over year excluding the Sanofi payment effect.
  • Non-GAAP operating margin -- 27.6% in fiscal Q4, slightly below prior year, reflecting elevated R&D and sales investments.
  • Litigation payments -- $522 million paid in 2024, an increase of $57 million over 2023, relating to legacy settlements.
  • 2025 revenue guidance -- Projected at $16.8 billion to $17.4 billion, implying 2%-5% growth, excluding pending asset divestitures and future milestone receipts from Sanofi.
  • 2025 gross margin guidance -- Targeted to remain stable at 53%-54%, despite FX headwinds and prior-year asset sales.
  • 2025 non-GAAP EPS guidance -- Provided at $2.35-$2.65 per share.
  • 2025 free cash flow guidance -- Expected in the range of $1.6 billion to $1.9 billion, lower due to account receivable securitization changes and higher anticipated legal settlement outflows.
  • Biosimilars portfolio -- Now includes 18 assets, with plans to launch seven in the US and four in Europe during 2025-2027.
  • R&D expense allocation -- Increasing to above 6% of revenue in 2025 as part of a shift toward innovation-focused investments.
  • FX impact -- Foreign exchange reduced 2024 revenue by approximately $257 million and gross profit by $187 million.
  • Sanofi collaboration -- Anti-TL1A program expenses to be shared equally; upfront $500 million payment in fiscal Q4 2023 recognized in prior year’s figures.
  • Upcoming pipeline milestones -- Duvakitug entering Phase 3 in 2025; olanzapine LAI NDA submission anticipated in H2 2025; ICS/SABA (DARI) pediatric recruitment near completion, largest Phase 3 study in Teva history.
  • Credit ratings -- All major agencies upgraded Teva’s ratings and outlook in the last six months, citing strengthened balance sheet and growth outlook.

Risks

  • GAAP operating loss of $29 million in fiscal Q4 2024, compared to $755 million operating income in fiscal Q4 2023, driven by the absence of the Sanofi upfront payment and a new API goodwill impairment charge.
  • 2025 free cash flow guidance of $1.6 billion to $1.9 billion reflects a decline, “mainly due to” reduced account receivable securitization and “higher legal settlement outflows.”
  • Medicare Part D redesign immediately impacts UZEDY from January 1, with CEO Francis stating, “we take that hit straight away.”
  • AUSTEDO is also affected by the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) in 2025, with CEO Francis noting, “this IRA redesign doesn't just touch UZEDY, it actually hit AUSTEDO.”

Summary

Teva Pharmaceutical Industries Limited (TEVA 0.45%) reported 9% year-over-year revenue growth in local currency terms in 2024, excluding the contribution from the Sanofi upfront payment, and an improved cash flow profile, with all major business lines contributing to top-line expansion. Management confirmed that innovative products AUSTEDO, UZEDY, and AJOVY were principal drivers of both revenue and market share gains, and set higher internal 2025 targets for each. The biologics and biosimilars pipeline advanced, with explicit plans for seven US and four European launches through 2027, while clinical program milestones for key new compounds were specified for the coming year. In capital allocation, the company emphasized ongoing deleveraging alongside increased R&D investment, while all major credit ratings agencies upgraded their outlooks following operational and financial improvements. Free cash flow guidance for 2025 was reduced to $1.6 billion–$1.9 billion due to stated changes in receivables management and litigation expense forecasting.

  • The generics business delivered 11% sales growth with international markets expanding 15%, while Teva Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (TAPI) completed a year of positive growth and is now classified as held for sale.
  • Management stated that the impact of sales erosion related to Revlimid is planned for as a “cliff” without a soft landing, with mitigation expected from launches of 16 complex generics and multiple biosimilars in 2025–2026.
  • Teva’s non-GAAP gross profit margin improved year over year by 150 basis points excluding the prior Sanofi milestone, and the 2025 margin is guided to remain stable at 53%-54% despite foreign exchange headwinds and legacy product divestitures.
  • During Q&A, executives specifically cited Part D redesign and IRA effects as immediate negative factors for UZEDY and AUSTEDO growth profiles, and also detailed a projected step-up in R&D allocation above 6% of revenue for 2025.

Industry glossary

  • TL1A: Target ligand for anti-inflammatory drugs, as referenced in Teva’s duvakitug clinical development program in inflammatory diseases.
  • ICS/SABA (DARI): Teva’s dual-action rescue inhaler combining an inhaled corticosteroid (ICS) and a short-acting beta-agonist (SABA), under late-stage development.
  • Olanzapine LAI: Long-acting injectable formulation of olanzapine for schizophrenia, advancing toward regulatory submission in 2025.
  • TAPI: Teva Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients segment, supplying APIs used in pharmaceutical manufacturing.
