By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 18, 2026 at 11:58AM EST

Date

Thursday, March 6, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President — David deVilliers III
  • Chief Executive Officer — John Baker III
  • Chief Financial Officer — Matthew McNulty

Risks

  • CEO Baker stated, "we expect NOI in 2025 to remain flat if not slightly below 2024," citing non-repeatable one-time Mining Royalty segment gains, and negative near-term impact from Industrial/Commercial vacancies.
  • Significant Cranberry Business Park occupancy declines are expected, as management disclosed, "feet defaulted on us, they’re not coming back. That in and of itself will take Cranberry from 96% occupancy to 60% occupancy."
  • President deVilliers III indicated earnings drag from new project deliveries before lease-up, stating, "This will impact NOI negatively until it is occupied and stabilized, where after the operating expenses can be passed through to tenants and receive rent revenue."
  • Construction cost uncertainty linked to tariffs was noted, with management observing that, "Construction costs are entering a period of uncertainty as we await the impact of tariffs on steel, lumber, and gypsum."

Takeaways

  • Quarterly net income -- $1.68 million, down 41.7% from $2.88 million, which previously included a one-time $1.98 million gain from a loan guarantee termination at Bryant Street Project.
  • Annual net income -- $6.39 million, up 20.4% from $5.3 million, primarily driven by improved Multifamily results.
  • Pro rata NOI (quarter) -- $9.1 million, up 21%, with Multifamily projects delivering an added $4.6 million over last year.
  • Pro rata NOI (year-to-date) -- $38.1 million, up 26%, due to performance in six stabilized Multifamily projects.
  • Three-year pro rata NOI CAGR -- 29.5% on a trailing 12-month basis over the past 3 years.
  • Estimated NAV (illustrative, management view) -- $34.63 per share to $39.22 per share; management cautions this does not necessarily reflect actual realizable value.
  • Commercial and industrial segment occupancy -- Stood at 95.6% at quarter-end across 550,000 square feet in Maryland.
  • Commercial and industrial segment revenues/NOI (quarter) -- $1.3 million revenues, and $992,000 NOI, down 11%, and 15%, respectively, attributed to a 50,000 square foot tenant default currently in eviction.
  • Mining and royalty segment revenues/NOI (quarter) -- $3.5 million both revenue and NOI, up 19%, and 34%, respectively, driven by 16 locations with Florida, and Georgia predominance.
  • Multifamily segment occupancy -- Apartments 92.8% occupied, retail 62.6% occupied across 1,827 apartments, and 125,000+ square feet of retail in Washington D.C., and South Carolina.
  • Multifamily segment revenues/NOI (quarter) -- Total $14.1 million revenue, $7.6 million NOI; FRP share: $8.2 million revenue, $4.3 million NOI, primarily reflecting new joint ventures from January, and July 2024.
  • Same store comparison (multifamily, selected assets) -- FRP share: $3.4 million revenue, and $2.1 million NOI, up 2%, and 12.2%.
  • Multifamily renewals -- Renewal success rates over 60%, with renewal rental rates trending higher by over 2.5% in the quarter.
  • Perryman industrial warehouse (Maryland) -- 258,000 square feet, shell completion now delayed to Q2 2025 because of weather; asset will move to the Industrial and Commercial segment, but NOI impacted until stabilized.
  • Lakeland, FL industrial project -- 200,000 square feet; estimated $141 per square foot cost, $9 triple net rents; vertical construction expected Q2 2025; FRP plans 90% partnership stake.
  • Broward County, FL industrial project -- 182,000+ square feet in two buildings, estimated $327 per square foot, $20 triple net rents; vertical construction also expected Q2 2025 with loan term sheets executed in Q4.
  • Cecil County, MD project -- 900,000 square foot distribution center delayed by permitting issues, with approvals now expected in early 2026.
  • Harford County, MD land -- Permitting underway for four new buildings (635,000 square feet) targeting 2026 vertical construction.
  • Industrial/commercial segment growth plan -- Platform to expand from 550,000 square feet to 2.7 million square feet as projects complete; four main buildings total 850,000 square feet, and $146 million project cost.

  • Development underwriting targets -- "Somewhere in the 6.5% to 7% return on cost in the first year of stabilization" for most projects, with core-market industrial assets justifying lower returns.
  • Construction activity commentary -- Management views industrial development pipeline in 2025 as being back to pre-pandemic (2018–2019) activity levels, noting "new starts... have absolutely fallen off the cliff."
  • Cranberry & Chelsea leasing -- About 400,000 square feet of industrial space (largely Cranberry, and new Chelsea) scheduled for rollover or vacancy in 2025, with Cranberry expected "to get very, very low" in occupancy before backfilling at higher rents.
  • Management’s multiyear objective -- "Doubling our Industrial and Commercial segment from 800,000 square feet with the addition of Chelsea to 1.6 million square feet" over the next 5 years.
  • Multifamily development pipeline -- Two active future projects (Estero, FL, and Greenville, SC) could add 810 units, and $6 million stabilized NOI; entitlements in Washington, D.C. targeted to enable vertical construction potentially by 2027.
  • Management perspective on asset disposal -- "We have no intention to sell anything right now... we’re focused on the NOI, and cash flows, and growing those."

Summary

FRP Holdings (FRPH 0.97%) reported robust year over year pro rata NOI growth driven by Multifamily and Mining segment outperformance, yet management explicitly guided to flat or modestly lower NOI in 2025 as one-off gains abate and near-term leasing headwinds emerge in the Industrial portfolio. Development capital deployment will accelerate, with $71 million in equity targeted for 2025 across industrial and multifamily projects, expanding the industrial platform toward 2.7 million square feet and positioning several core pipelines for delivery by 2026–2027. Management highlighted that rental rate rollups on industrial vacancies are expected, but backfilling key assets—especially at Cranberry Business Park—could depress occupancy for an extended period before yields improve. Capital allocation, underwriting returns on new projects (6%-7% return on cost), and timing of lease-up amid construction cost volatility and macro headwinds will heavily influence intermediate-term performance.

  • John Baker III explained that a "one-time payment" in the Mining segment significantly boosted 2024 NOI, warning this level will not recur in 2025.
  • Management expects "over 430,000 square feet" to become vacant or roll over in the Industrial/Commercial segment, enabling higher future market rents, but contributing to a short-term NOI decline until re-tenanting is achieved.
  • FRP Holdings has identified a potential $35 million of 2025 capital deployment for Multifamily development and entitlement, including major projects in Estero, Florida, and Greenville, South Carolina.
  • CFO McNulty stated, "it’s somewhere in the 6.5% to 7% return on cost in the first year of stabilization," with slight variation between segments and geography, outlining discipline in development underwriting standards.

Industry glossary

  • Cap rate: A real estate valuation measure calculated as Net Operating Income divided by property value, used to compare profitability and relative value of assets within the sector.
  • Triple net rent (NNN): Lease arrangement in which the tenant pays base rent plus all operating expenses, including real estate taxes, insurance, and maintenance.
  • Entitlement: The process of obtaining approvals from local jurisdiction to develop land for a desired use, including zoning, site plan, and permit authorization.
  • PUD: Planned Unit Development; a designed grouping of both varied and compatible land uses, such as housing, recreation, and shopping, within one contained development or subdivision.
