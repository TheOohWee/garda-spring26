Match Group (MTCH) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $878 million for Q4, up 2% year over year and flat on a foreign exchange neutral basis.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $370 million in Q4, up 14%, with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 42%; margin would have been 41% excluding discrete items.
  • Full Year Free Cash Flow -- Exceeded $1 billion in 2025, enabling $789 million in share repurchases and $186 million in dividends, reducing diluted shares outstanding by 7% year over year.
  • Tinder Direct Revenue (Q4) -- $464 million, down 3%, or down 5% FX neutral, with 8.8 million payers (down 8%) and RPP up 5% to $17.63.
  • Tinder Product Metrics -- Global spark coverage improved from down 1% in December 2024 to up 4% in December 2025, and new registrations improved from down 12% in Q2 to down 5% in Q4 year over year.
  • Tinder Marketing Spend (2026) -- Budget increased by $50 million to approximately $230 million to support product turnaround and user growth.
  • Hinge Direct Revenue (Q4) -- $186 million, up 26%, with payers up 17% to 1.9 million and RPP up 8% to $32.96; adjusted EBITDA up 54% to $67 million (margin 36%).
  • Hinge European Expansion -- Ended 2025 with more than 3.3 million MAUs in European expansion markets, up from 200,000 at launch; expected to deliver more than $100 million of direct revenue in 2026 in these markets.
  • Hinge International and Product Rollout -- Entered Mexico and Brazil in 2025 with faster-than-expected early growth; plans for three additional Latin American markets and an India launch in 2026, where organic MAUs grew 40% to over 1 million without marketing spend.
  • FaceCheck Impact -- FaceCheck implementation led to more than a 50% reduction in bad actor interactions on Tinder with minimal revenue impact; global rollout planned for Tinder and key Hinge markets in 2026.
  • E and E Segment -- Q4 direct revenue of $145 million, down 7%, payers down 14% to 2.1 million, RPP up 8% to $22.53; adjusted EBITDA of $48 million flat, margin 33%.
  • Match Group Asia -- Q4 direct revenue $66 million, down 2%, payers up 3% to 1 million, RPP down 5%; adjusted EBITDA up 2% to $16 million, margin 25%.
  • Q1 2026 Guidance -- Total revenue expected at $850 million to $860 million, up 2%-3% year over year (down 1% to flat FX neutral), with adjusted EBITDA of $315 million to $320 million and margin of 37% at midpoint.
  • Full-Year 2026 Guidance -- Total revenue between $3.41 billion and $3.535 billion, approximately flat at midpoint, adjusted EBITDA of $1.28 billion to $1.325 billion (margin 37.5%), and free cash flow of $1.085 billion to $1.135 billion, up 8%.
  • Capital Return Strategy -- Board declared a quarterly dividend of $0.20 per share, a 5% increase; company plans to use 100% of free cash flow for share buybacks, dividends, and employee equity award net settlement over time.
  • Share Buyback Activity -- 7.3 million shares repurchased in Q4 for $239 million at an average price of $33 per share and total 24.7 million shares repurchased in 2025 for $789 million at an average price of $32 per share.
  • Expense Management -- Q4 total expenses down 7%; general and administrative costs decreased 22% (to 10% of total revenue) due to lower legal fees and gain on sale of an LA office building; cost of revenue fell 6% (to 25% of total revenue) led by alternative payment savings.
  • Operational Leverage -- Trailing-twelve-month gross leverage 3.2 times; net leverage reduced to 2.4 times in Q4 with $1 billion of cash on hand.
  • Workforce and Platform Measures -- Savings from workforce reductions and alternative payment initiative reinvested into Tinder and Hinge product and marketing.
  • 2026 Segment Outlooks -- Tinder direct revenue projected to decline at a similar rate as 2025, Hinge revenue expected to grow in the low to mid-20% range, E and E direct revenue to decline in low double-digits, and Match Group Asia direct revenue to decline in high single-digits.
  • Product Event Disclosure -- Tinder to host its first product event in Los Angeles on March 12 to showcase upcoming AI-driven features and roadmap innovation, webcast available to investors.

SUMMARY

The call highlighted explicit management commitment to completing the Tinder turnaround and funding Hinge's international expansion, with quantifiable reinvestment from operational efficiencies. Management directly stated flat total revenue expectations for 2026, reinforcing near-term revenue trade-offs in favor of user experience improvements, especially at Tinder and Hinge. Capital return to shareholders remains a clear priority, demonstrated by a 7% year-over-year reduction in diluted shares and a 5% dividend increase, funded entirely from robust free cash flow generation.

  • Management emphasized a shift to bottom-of-funnel, performance-driven Tinder and E and E brand marketing, a change guided by results from "Project Prism."
  • Executive commentary attributed measured MAU turnaround in 15 countries representing one-third of Tinder MAU to "improvements to our recommendation algorithm," double date, FaceCheck, and focused marketing.
  • Direct analysis of user give-back initiatives revealed the company "underspend our revenue give back budget." in Q4, resulting in less negative impact on revenue than forecast, but with continued experimentation planned at a $60 million annualized pace.
  • Management projected no structural change to Tinder profitability from current user investments, stating that future KPIs such as payer growth are expected to benefit from recent MAU stabilization and content innovation.
  • Hinge's rapid MAU and revenue scaling in European, Latin American, and APAC entry markets was directly cited by management, with the new "one MG" (multi-brand) coordination expected to accelerate international ramp and yield monetization improvements over time.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RPP: Revenue per payer, a key metric reflecting average revenue generated per paying user in a given period.
  • FXM/FXN: Foreign exchange neutral/foreign exchange, referring to growth or declines measured excluding currency fluctuation effects.
  • MAU: Monthly active users; the count of unique users engaging with a platform in a single month.
  • FaceCheck: An AI-driven user verification tool designed to improve platform safety by confirming the identity of users via facial biometrics.
  • DoubleDate: A product feature on Tinder enabling users to match and interact as pairs, appealing specifically to Gen Z and female users.
