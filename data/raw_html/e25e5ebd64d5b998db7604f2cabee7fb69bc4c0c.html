SenesTech (SNES) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 12, 2026 at 6:10PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

March 12, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Interim Executive Chair — Dr. Jamie Bechtel
  • Chief Financial Officer — Thomas C. Chesterman

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $2.2 million in fiscal 2025 (period ended Dec. 31, 2025), up 20%, with growth adjusted to 30% excluding a $200,000 impact from the transition to direct Amazon sales.
  • E-commerce revenue -- Rose 88% in fiscal 2025, with e-commerce now representing over half of total company revenue.
  • Gross margin -- Improved to 62.5% in fiscal 2025 from 54.1% in fiscal 2024, attributed to favorable product mix and e-commerce contributions.
  • Net loss -- Reported at $6.4 million, widening slightly from $6.2 million; includes $131,000 in one-time legal expenses and $135,000 in non-cash operating lease expense.
  • Adjusted net loss -- $5.6 million, excluding one-time legal and non-cash lease expenses.
  • Adjusted EBITDA loss -- $5.3 million, improving from $5.8 million.
  • Liquidity -- Ended with $8.6 million in cash and short-term investments.
  • Legal resolution -- The Leafotech litigation was resolved and all litigation dismissed; the CFO stated, "The results were immaterial to us both financially and operationally."
  • Amazon transition -- Direct management began in the quarter, enabling improved product presentation, revenue retention, and marketing optimization.
  • International expansion -- Regulatory approval and shipment to New Zealand completed; initial stocking order shipped through exclusive partner Evicom.
  • Municipal traction -- Field deployments ongoing in Chicago and Baltimore; New York City trial concluding in the middle of Q2, with follow-up bidding discussions underway.
  • Retail pipeline -- Multiple presentations to major retailers completed; large retail orders could rapidly increase revenue, with the CFO stating, "One pallet each. That is a $2 million order."
  • Inventory -- Year-over-year inventory increased 25% to support potential large retail orders, not slower turnover.
  • Marketing focus -- Social media activity paused in favor of "strengthening our core commercial efforts," with communications cadence expected to increase later in the year.
  • CEO search -- Formal search now underway; the Interim Executive Chair said, "We are looking for a leader who can take the company through its next phase of growth."

SUMMARY

SenesTech (SNES 1.24%) delivered double-digit revenue and gross margin growth in fiscal 2025, with significant acceleration in e-commerce as it brought Amazon sales in-house. A legal dispute with Leafotech concluded with no material operational or financial consequences. The company remains active in U.S. and international expansion, securing regulatory clearance and partnership in New Zealand, and moving toward possible large retail orders. Ongoing municipal deployments, especially in major U.S. cities, position the business for future contract opportunities. Leadership transition continues as the formal CEO search progresses.

  • The CFO stated that "improvement should be expected" regarding net loss reduction, while maintaining conservative external disclosures.
  • E-commerce and B2B sales teams will be expanded, aiming for increased ROI and deeper channel penetration.
  • The company expects potential "explosive" growth if major retailers proceed with significant purchase orders following ongoing evaluations.
  • Inventory build was intentional to ensure readiness for possible large retail demand surges.
  • Australia regulatory response on product registration is anticipated by spring, which could further international opportunities.
  • The board intends to align CEO compensation with equity incentives and shareholder value.
  • New species expansion is not a focus until rat and mouse verticals are fully realized, due to regulatory complexities.
  • Sales efforts in the agricultural sector, notably almonds, sugarcane, and poultry, continue to expand with sector-specific distributors in California.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • EVOLVE: Fertility control bait for rodent population management, developed by SenesTech.
  • ContraPest: SenesTech's flagship liquid contraceptive bait targeting both male and female rats.
  • B2B: Business-to-business sales channel, targeting professional and institutional buyers, such as municipalities and pest management firms.
  • D2C: Direct-to-consumer sales, such as products sold online via SenesTech's website or e-commerce platforms.
