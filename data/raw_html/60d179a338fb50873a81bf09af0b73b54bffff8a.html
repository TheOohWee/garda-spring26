Earnings Call Transcript

Expro (XPRO) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Aug 6, 2025 at 12:45PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, July 29, 2025, at 11 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Mike Jardon
  • Chief Financial Officer — Sergio Myworm

For analyst commentary or quotes, please email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue: $423 million in revenue for Q2 2025, up $32 million, or 8% sequentially from Q1 2025.
  • EBITDA: EBITDA reached $94 million for Q2 2025, an increase of $18 million, or 24% quarter over quarter, exceeding the prior guidance range of $80 million–$90 million.
  • EBITDA margin: 22%, representing a 200 basis point improvement sequentially and year over year. The rate set a quarterly company record for EBITDA margin.
  • Free cash flow (adjusted): $36 million in adjusted free cash flow, equivalent to 9% of revenue on an adjusted basis. This figure excludes one-time items for comparability and refers to adjusted free cash flow (a non-GAAP measure) as defined and reconciled in the Q2 2025 earnings materials.
  • Regional revenue breakdown: North and Latin America: $143 million (up $8 million sequentially). Europe and Sub-Saharan Africa: $132 million (up $20 million). Middle East and North Africa: $91 million in revenue (slightly down). Asia Pacific: $57 million in revenue (up $6 million sequentially).
  • Segment margins: Middle East and North Africa EBITDA margin at 36% (down 70 basis points sequentially). Asia Pacific segment EBITDA margin was 26% of revenues (up 500 basis points).
  • Share repurchases: $15 million in year-to-date share repurchases, $5 million in share repurchases, $61 million remaining under the $100 million authorization. There is a plan to repurchase approximately $40 million in stock for full year 2025.
  • Full-year company outlook: Reaffirmed revenue guidance of approximately $1.7 billion for the full year 2025, and at least $350 million of EBITDA for the full year 2025. Full-year 2025 adjusted free cash flow is targeted at plus or minus $110 million.
  • Liquidity: $343 million available at quarter-end ($207 million cash and equivalents; $136 million in revolver capacity), with subsequent credit agreement extension to July 2029. The total revolving credit facility was expanded to $400 million in July 2025, plus a new $100 million bridge facility.
  • Operational innovation: Deployed three new technologies as industry firsts—Brute Armor Packer, Remote Clamp Insulation System (RCIS), and Generation X Remote Plug Launcher/Skyhook Cement Line Makeup Device—resulting in contract wins and efficiency gains.
  • Cost savings initiatives: DRIVE 2025 program targeting $30 million in run-rate cost reductions, as announced on the Q1 2025 earnings conference call, with 50% expected to be realized in 2025.
  • Major project awards: Five-year contract in Guyana with revenue in excess of $120 million. Contracts in North Africa (approximately $100 million and $60 million), and additional wins in Indonesia, Brazil, and the North Sea supporting contracted revenue visibility.

Summary

Expro's (XPRO +0.00%) quarter marked new records for EBITDA margin and a robust increase in adjusted free cash flow, benefiting from continued strength in international and offshore markets. Management announced a refined free cash flow reporting methodology, aligning metrics with industry peers and highlighting plans for continued transparency around adjustments. New and renewed contract awards diversified across regions and service lines, with $595 million of new orders supporting significant future revenue visibility. Amended and expanded credit facilities, entered into subsequent to June 30, 2025, materially increased liquidity, positioning the company for both operational resilience and possible M&A opportunities. Three recently commercialized technologies secured customer adoption and new work scopes, contributing to future margin expansion by enabling safer and more efficient operations.

  • CFO Sergio Myworm introduced a new adjusted free cash flow definition, stating it will be reported CFFO minus CapEx beginning in Q2 2025, and it will exclude truly one-time items "to come up with an adjusted free cash flow that is more reflective of the steady-state business performance."
  • CEO Mike Jardon said, "We still anticipate, and I would be very disappointed if we do not expand margins in 2025 versus 2024," reaffirming a company-wide focus on operational leverage and cost discipline.
  • The company successfully executed a new credit agreement, extending debt maturities to July 2029. Revolving commitments were increased from $340 million to $400 million subsequent to Q2 2025. A $100 million 364-day bridge facility was added to support liquidity needs entered into subsequent to June 30, 2025.
  • Management confirmed plans to repurchase approximately $40 million in shares in 2025, front-loaded toward the latter half of 2025 as adjusted free cash flow generation increases. This signals ongoing prioritization of shareholder returns.
  • Market commentary by Jardon noted that recent order strength "really was just kind of the timing of it. You know, we continue to see a robust level of bidding and tendering activity," indicating a sustained pipeline as opposed to single-event concentration.

Industry glossary

  • Brute Armor Packer: High-pressure, high-tensile well packer designed for deepwater applications, offering leading sealing integrity and retrievability.
  • Remote Clamp Insulation System (RCIS): Automation technology to install control line clamps on tubing during completions, minimizing manual labor and safety risk.
  • Generation X Remote Plug Launcher/Skyhook Cement Line Makeup Device: Technology suite enabling fully remote, hands-free cementing plug operations to enhance safety and control for offshore wells.
  • DRIVE 2025: Company-wide operating efficiency and cost optimization initiative targeting permanent structural cost savings across business segments.
