Asana (ASAN) Q4 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 2, 2026 at 6:05PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, March 2, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Dan Rogers
  • Chief Financial Officer — Sonalee Parekh
  • Incoming Chief Financial Officer — Aziz Meghji
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Eva Leung

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $205.6 million, reflecting 9% year-over-year growth.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin -- 9%, corresponding to $18.2 million in non-GAAP operating income, representing a 10-percentage-point improvement from the prior year.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $25.7 million, equating to a 13% margin.
  • Net Income -- $19.9 million, or $0.08 per diluted share.
  • AI Studio Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) -- Exceeded $6 million with over 50% quarter-over-quarter growth in the fourth quarter.
  • Dollar-based Net Retention Rate (NRR) -- 96% overall; core customer NRR at 97%; $100,000+ customer cohort at 96%.
  • Core Customers -- 25,928 spending $5,000 or more annually, representing 76% of total revenue and growing at 10% year over year; 817 customers spend $100,000 or more, growing at 13% year over year.
  • International Revenue Growth -- 11% increase year over year; international revenue outpaced total company growth.
  • Sector Performance -- Technology sector ARR stabilized to flat performance after seven quarters of decline; non-tech verticals grew in the teens percentage range.
  • Remaining Performance Obligation (RPO) -- $524.8 million, up 22%; current RPO grew 17%, making up 78% of total RPO.
  • Deferred Revenue -- $333.9 million, representing 10% year-over-year growth.
  • Gross Margin -- 88%, with management expecting to maintain this level in the next fiscal year.
  • Operating Expense Improvements -- R&D expenses were 23% of revenue (down from 29%); sales and marketing expenses 43% (down from 45%); G&A expenses 13% (down from 17%), all measured against revenue versus the year-ago quarter.
  • Share Repurchase Activity -- $58 million of Class A shares bought back in the quarter at an average price of $12.75 per share; board increased authorization by $160 million, bringing total authorization to nearly $200 million.
  • AI Adoption Momentum -- Over 200 customers onboarded to the AI teammates beta program; management expects general availability to sales-led customers in Q1 and self-serve in the second half of the year.
  • AI Revenue Outlook -- AI offerings expected to contribute nearly 15% of new ARR in the upcoming fiscal year.
  • Guidance for Q1 Fiscal 2027 -- Revenue expected between $202.5 million and $204.5 million (8.1%-9.2% year-over-year growth); non-GAAP operating income of $15 million-$17 million; non-GAAP net income per share projected at $0.07-$0.08.
  • Full-Year Fiscal 2027 Guidance -- Revenue projected between $850 million-$858 million (7.5%-8.5% growth); non-GAAP operating margin of at least 9.5%; non-GAAP net income per share of $0.36-$0.37.
  • Stock-Based Compensation Planning -- Weighted-average diluted shares outstanding expected to be approximately 243 million for the full year.
  • CFO Transition -- Sonalee Parekh will depart after the quarter, with Aziz Meghji promoted to Chief Financial Officer; leadership described as "seamless transition" by management.

RISKS

  • Chief Financial Officer Sonalee Parekh said, "self-serve, which is primarily SMB, remains a headwind" and cited an ongoing "two-point drag on ARR growth" from product-led growth (PLG) business.
  • Management expects "minimal contribution" from AI teammates in the upcoming fiscal year due to launch timing, with "a more meaningful ramp in Q4."
  • Sonalee Parekh stated, "Our guidance does not assume a recovery in that motion in fiscal year 2027."
  • Chief Financial Officer Sonalee Parekh clarified that "tech vertical in Q4. However, it is too early to call a bottom," and stabilization is not assumed in the outlook.

SUMMARY

Asana (ASAN 4.71%) reported year-over-year revenue growth with expanding margins, highlighted by AI product traction and disciplined capital allocation. Management announced a leadership transition, promoting Aziz Meghji to Chief Financial Officer. The company continues to see robust enterprise demand, as evidenced by accelerated billings and remaining performance obligation growth. Fiscal 2027 guidance incorporates ongoing headwinds in self-serve SMB and product-led channels. Strategic investments in AI and go-to-market initiatives are expected to drive future growth and further margin expansion, while guidance remains conservative on PLG recovery and tech sector stabilization.

  • Channel partner influence is rising, with 20% of AI Studio fourth-quarter deals including a partner, and management positioning the channel as a "meaningful driver of incremental ARR" long term.
  • Sales-led commercial execution improved, with "Sales productivity and attainment both increased," and top 10 renewals exceeding 100% NRR.
  • No philosophical shift in financial strategy was indicated by the incoming CFO, Aziz Meghji, who stated, "there is no philosophical shift in the guidance strategy or the financial strategy."
  • Gross margin is projected to remain in the high-80% range as innovation investments shift towards AI products.
  • Management reiterated its multiproduct AI strategy, citing "AI Studio and AI teammates are becoming foundational to our platform strategy" and noting expansion in both workflows and buying centers as additive and mitigating churn risk.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Agentic Enterprise: An organizational model where humans and AI agents collaborate at scale on workflows, requiring persistent context, memory, and governance.
  • AI Studio: Asana's product integrating large language model-driven intelligence directly into workflow architecture, enabling users to design and automate complex business processes.
  • AI Teammates: AI-enabled workflow agents within Asana that act alongside human team members for productivity and workflow execution.
  • Work Graph: Asana's semantic memory system mapping work relationships among people, tasks, and outcomes, serving as foundational context for workflow automation and AI integration.
  • Core Customer: Defined as a customer spending $5,000 or more annually with Asana, based on annualized GAAP revenue in a given quarter.
  • PLG (Product-Led Growth): A go-to-market strategy leveraging product usage and customer experience to drive acquisition, expansion, and retention, referenced as a distinct channel for Asana.
  • SLG (Sales-Led Growth): Traditional go-to-market motion in which direct sales efforts drive revenue, used alongside PLG at Asana.
  • NRR (Net Revenue Retention): A metric representing the percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers after accounting for churn, downgrades, and expansions over a set period.
  • RPO (Remaining Performance Obligation): The contracted revenue that has not yet been recognized but is expected to be earned in future periods.
  • cRPO (Current Remaining Performance Obligation): The portion of RPO expected to be recognized as revenue in the next 12 months.
  • AEO (Answer Engine Optimization): Search optimization strategy aligning content and product discovery with AI-driven answer engines, distinct from traditional SEO.
