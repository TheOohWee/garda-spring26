Fastenal (FAST) Q1 2025 Earnings Call

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 21, 2025 at 1:27PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, Apr 11, 2025

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Dan Florness: Chief Executive Officer

Jeff Watts: President and Chief Sales Officer

Holden Lewis: Chief Financial Officer

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

Q1 2025 Sales Growth: 3.4% year-over-year, with daily sales up 5%

Operating Margin: 20.1%, down 50 basis points from Q1 2024

Gross Margin: 45.1%, down 40 basis points year-over-year

EPS: $0.52, flat compared to Q1 2024

Pricing Actions: Implemented in April 2025, expected to contribute 3%-4%, potentially doubling in H2 2025

Inventory: Up 11.9%, reflecting efforts to improve product availability and support customer growth

Capital Spending Forecast: $265 million to $285 million for FY2025, up from $214 million in 2024

SUMMARY

Fastenal reported modest growth amid sluggish end-market demand, with management citing self-help initiatives and easier comparisons as key drivers. The company faces significant challenges from newly implemented tariffs, particularly on fasteners and steel-based products, in 2025. necessitating proactive pricing actions and supply chain diversification efforts.

Customer site data reveals strong performance in larger accounts, while smaller accounts underperformed

Management emphasized the need to improve e-commerce capabilities to address underperformance in smaller customer segments, particularly in the context of Q1 2025.

"We have been proactively engaging with our customers for several months and in April, we took our first actions, which we believe will contribute 3% to 4% of price in the second quarter of 2025 with the potential for that to double in the second half of 2025."

The company increased its dividend from $0.43 to $0.44 per share for Q1 2025, Increased its dividend from $0.43 to $0.44 per share, with the potential to reach $1 billion in total dividend payments if the current rate is maintained throughout the year.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

FMI (Fastenal Managed Inventory): Point-of-use technologies deployed by Fastenal to manage customer inventory, including vending machines and other digital solutions

On-site: Customer locations where Fastenal provides more than $50,000 in monthly product and services
