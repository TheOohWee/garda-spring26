Principal (PFG) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Aug 7, 2025 at 8:40AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, July 29, 2025, at 10 a.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Deanna D. Strable
  • Chief Financial Officer — Joel J. Pitz
  • President, Retirement and Income Solutions — Christopher J. Littlefield
  • President, Principal Asset Management — Kamal Bhatia
  • President, U.S. Insurance Solutions — Amy Friedrich

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted Non-GAAP Operating Earnings: $469 million in adjusted non-GAAP earnings for Q2 2025, with non-GAAP EPS of $2.07, reflecting an 18% increase in non-GAAP EPS over 2024.
  • Reported Non-GAAP Operating Earnings: $489 million in non-GAAP operating earnings for Q2 2025, with non-GAAP operating EPS of $2.16, including a $32 million one-time accrual release.
  • Return of Capital to Shareholders: $320 million distributed to shareholders in Q2 2025, split between $150 million in share repurchases and $170 million in common stock dividends.
  • Common Stock Dividend: Raised for the eighth consecutive quarter in Q2 2025 to $0.78 per share common stock dividend for Q3 2025, an 8% increase over both the year-ago quarter and trailing twelve-month period; payout ratio aligns with the targeted 40% dividend payout ratio.
  • Total Company Managed AUM: $753 billion in total company managed AUM for Q2 2025, up 5% sequentially, driven by positive market performance and FX tailwinds.
  • Net Cash Flow: Negative $2.6 billion net cash flow for Q2 2025, showing sequential improvement attributed to positive flows from global institutional clients in several product lines.
  • RIS Sales: $6 billion in RIS sales for Q2 2025, showing 7% year-over-year growth, driven by Workplace Savings and Retirement Solutions (WSRS) and RILA products.
  • RIS Margins: 40% margin achieved for Q2 2025; top-line growth of 3% for Q2 2025, with 5% pretax operating earnings growth year over year.
  • Specialty Benefits Operating Earnings: Specialty Benefits operating earnings increased by 10% compared to the year-ago quarter, with operating margin expansion of 100 basis points over the same period and a 130 basis-point improvement in FBD loss ratio in Q2 2025 compared to Q2 2024.
  • Life Insurance Sales: Premium fees rose 5% in life insurance compared to 2024; Pretax operating earnings of $23 million for Q2 2025 decreased due to higher mortality claim severity.
  • Principal Asset Management Sales: $33 billion in Principal Asset Management sales for Q2 2025, up 19% year over year; investment management sales increased 24% in Q2 2025 over Q2 2024, including $10 billion from international clients.
  • Investment Management Fee Revenue: Investment management fee revenue increased 6% compared to second quarter 2024; management fees rose 4% and operating margin expanded 250 basis points on higher average AUM and performance fees.
  • International Pension Segment: Net revenue increased 2% compared to the year-ago quarter on a constant currency basis; pretax operating earnings grew 7% year over year; operating margin expanded 180 basis points to 47%.
  • Capital Position: $1.4 billion in excess and available capital at Q2 2025 quarter-end, including $800 million at the holding company and $350 million above the targeted risk-based capital ratio, estimated at 400% at quarter end.
  • Capital Return Targets: Company aims to return $1.4 billion–$1.7 billion in capital for full year 2025, including $700 million–$1 billion in share repurchases.
  • Tax Rate: Effective tax rate was 18% for Q2 2025, in line with the full-year targeted range of 17%–20% for 2025.
  • Expense Management: Pitz stated, "margins have improved 140 basis points year over year at the enterprise level ... a product of expenses growing at a slower rate than revenue."
  • Performance Fees Outlook: Bhatia stated, "fee levels would be similar to 2024" for the remainder of 2025 following higher-than-expected alternative debt strategy performance fees.
  • Divestiture of Post Advisor Group: Management expects the AUM divestiture will have an immaterial earnings impact and no effect on medium-term asset management revenue growth or operating margin targets.
  • SMB Segment: Transfer deposits grew 27% year over year in Q2 2025.

SUMMARY

The earnings call revealed sequential improvement in net cash flows driven by global institutional client inflows. Principal Financial Group(PFG 0.56%) raised its quarterly dividend again, extending its growth streak and reinforcing its policy of a 40% dividend payout ratio. Management confirmed strong margin expansion at the enterprise level, supported by expense controls, with stated discipline to reallocate capital when targeted returns are not met in businesses such as Pension Risk Transfer. Capital deployment remains on track for the company's stated 2025 return objectives, with higher levels of share repurchases expected in the back half of the year.

  • Pitz referred to a $32 million after-tax benefit from a one-time expense accrual release in Q2 2025, resulting in a substantial increase in reported non-GAAP EPS.
  • Chief Executive Officer Strable noted, "Volatility continued to underpin markets in the second quarter," acknowledging that daily equity market averages depressed fee revenue in Q2 2025, but the May/June rebound improved the AUM outlook.
  • Within Principal Asset Management, Sales to international clients reached $10 billion in Q2 2025, a nearly 50% year-over-year increase, and Asia institutional AUM nearly doubled compared to the prior year.
  • Bhatia explained the source of higher-than-expected performance fees for Q2 2025, saying they were "primarily generated from our alternative debt strategies, predominantly direct lending and real estate debt."
  • Life insurance pretax operating earnings declined year over year due to higher net mortality claim severity, despite business market growth and improved sales.
  • Friedrich forecasted that Specialty Benefits premium growth would increase in the second half of 2025 but would fall short of the previously communicated full-year 6%–9% growth target.
  • Management highlighted improvements across RI flows in Q2 2025, with stabilization in participant withdrawal rates and strong retention, while acknowledging that elevated markets suppressed net cash flows.
  • Littlefield clarified that Pension Risk Transfer sales were moderate in Q2 2025 due to a smaller opportunity pipeline and the company's decision to prioritize returns over volume, stating, "we were very disciplined and remain in making sure that we prioritize returns over volume."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RILA: Registered Index-Linked Annuity, an annuity product combining growth potential linked to a market index with some downside risk protection.
  • PRT: Pension Risk Transfer, a transaction where a pension plan sponsor transfers the obligation to pay plan participants’ future benefits to an insurer.
  • WSRS: Workplace Savings and Retirement Solutions, Principal’s suite of retirement savings products focused on employer-sponsored plans.
  • FBD Loss Ratio: Fully Insured Benefits Division loss ratio, measuring claims incurred against premiums earned in the benefits segment.
  • SMB: Small- and Mid-sized Business segment, referring to client organizations of smaller scale within Principal’s operational focus.
