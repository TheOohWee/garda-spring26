Earnings Call Transcript

Blue Bird (BLBD) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 10, 2026 at 2:20PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, February 5, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Phil Horlock
  • Chief Financial Officer — Razvan Radulescu

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $46 million, marking the company’s second highest first-quarter result, and equating to a 14.6% margin, representing a 40 basis point decrease year over year.
  • Unit Sales -- 2,130 buses delivered, slightly above the prior year and achieving the highest seasonal Q1 volumes in over 15 years.
  • Net Revenue -- $314 million, only $4 million below last year, as price increases in combustion models nearly offset a lower electric vehicle (EV) mix.
  • EV Sales -- 132 units sold, down 36% versus last year, with an EV mix of 6% this quarter.
  • Combustion Engine Bus Percentage -- 94% of units sold were internal combustion engine vehicles, yet margin performance equaled last year at 15%.
  • Order Backlog -- Ended Q1 with 4,400 buses in backlog, including a record 17% EVs; backlog reflects roughly $760 million in future revenue potential.
  • EV Backlog -- 765 EVs at quarter-end, 22% higher versus end of fiscal 2024 and 82% above the prior year’s Q1; total EV backlog and sales approach 1,000 units.
  • Average Bus Revenue Per Unit -- $135,000, down from $138,000 due to lower EV mix; each internal combustion bus sold at about 6% higher prices than last year, compensating for reduced EV contribution.
  • Parts Segment Revenue -- $26 million in Q1, realizing 6% year-over-year growth in the company’s historically weakest seasonal quarter.
  • Gross Margin -- 19.2%, 80 basis points lower than last year primarily owing to increased labor costs from the now fully effective USW labor agreement.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $31 million, $1 million above last year’s result.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $26 million for Q1, benefiting from profitability and working capital improvement.
  • Liquidity -- Ended the quarter with a record $280 million liquidity position, up $95 million year over year, and $136 million in cash.
  • Debt Reduction -- Reduced debt by more than $40 million compared to the prior year.
  • Share Repurchases -- Completed $10 million of buybacks in Q1, totaling $20 million over six months; $40 million remains available in the Board-authorized program.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $22 million in Q1, a $23 million increase over last year.
  • Alternative Powered Buses -- 51% of sales were alternative fuel vehicles; major competitors typically see 10%-15%, underscoring the company’s segment leadership.
  • DOE Plant Grant -- Signed a contract for $80 million in Department of Energy (DOE) funding, covering half the $160 million plant expansion investment in Fort Valley; grant disbursement timelines linked to federal funding status.
  • Pricing Power -- All non-EV bus orders since January carry a conditional 5% tariff surcharge clause, and the company has prepared to apply such surcharges to backlog buses if tariffs are enacted.
  • Guidance Affirmation -- Reaffirmed fiscal 2025 revenue guidance of $1.4 billion-$1.5 billion and adjusted EBITDA target of $200 million, with a widened range of $185 million-$215 million and margin outlook of 13.5%-14.5%.
  • EV Order Funding -- 75% of the current 1,000 EVs sold or in backlog have funding in place; funding for the remaining 250 units hinges on disbursements from the EPA Clean School Bus Program.
  • Federal Funding Developments -- A late-breaking EPA CFO memorandum clarified that “the disbursement of funds will continue,” overturning the pause initially imposed under new executive orders and allowing Clean School Bus Program and DOE plant funding to proceed during ongoing litigation.
  • Tariff Exposure -- Potential 25% tariffs on Mexican and Canadian imports and 10% on Chinese imports are being monitored; China sourcing exposure is described as limited, and all incremental costs are intended to be passed through to customers via price increases.
  • EV Cost Reduction -- EV school bus lease prices have been reduced by $25,000, reflecting progress toward total cost of ownership parity with internal combustion engine buses.
  • Inventory Strategy -- The company increased raw inventory in Q1, predominantly EV components, to secure supply chain continuity amid tariff uncertainty.
  • CEO Succession -- John Wyskiel will succeed Phil Horlock as CEO on February 17; Horlock will remain on the Board for continuity.

RISKS

  • Portion of EV orders (250 units) remains at risk due to pending federal funding; distribution was paused for up to 90 days before the EPA clarified it may resume disbursements.
  • Potential implementation of a 25% tariff on Mexican and Canadian imports and a 10% tariff on Chinese goods could increase the company’s cost of goods sold, though management states these costs will be passed on to buyers.
  • Adjusted EBITDA guidance range for fiscal 2025 was widened on both the lower and upper ends due to uncertainties from executive orders, EPA funding timing, and tariff imposition.
  • Gross margin contracted 80 basis points compared to last year, attributed to higher labor costs under the USW labor agreement now in full effect.

SUMMARY

The call presented detailed updates regarding Blue Bird (BLBD 0.91%)'s operational and financial trajectory, showing a near-record first-quarter profit and reaffirmation of full-year guidance despite pronounced policy and funding uncertainties. Management explicitly cited a record order backlog led by alternative fuel and electric buses, robust cash flows, and active cost and price management amid evolving tariff threats. Strategic actions included securing DOE project funding, implementing conditional price surcharges, and advancing EV cost reduction targets. Recent EPA clarification restored federal disbursement momentum, directly supporting a quarter of EV orders and critical plant expansion funding.

  • Board-approved share repurchases continued, with $20 million executed over six months and $40 million still available for future buybacks.
  • The company maintained strong liquidity at $280 million and further reduced debt by over $40 million from the prior year.
  • Unit sales for alternative fuel-powered buses reached 51% of Q1 volume, well above industry norms, highlighting the company’s segment dominance and customer mix resilience.
  • Progress toward cost parity was demonstrated by a $25,000 reduction in EV school bus pricing, supporting management’s strategy to make EVs more competitive regardless of federal subsidy dynamics.
  • CEO transition was confirmed, as John Wyskiel is set to assume leadership, underscoring management continuity and experience as strategic priorities.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Clean School Bus Program: A federally funded EPA initiative allocating multi-billion dollar grants for electric and alternative fuel-powered school bus procurement.
  • USW Labor Agreement: Collective bargaining agreement with the United Steelworkers union affecting wages, benefits, and operating costs at the company’s manufacturing facilities.
