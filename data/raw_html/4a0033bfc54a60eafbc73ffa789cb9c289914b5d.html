Establishment Labs (ESTA) Earnings Transcript

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $64.6 million, up 45.2% year over year, with $17.3 million from Motiva sales in the U.S.
  • Full-year revenue -- $211.1 million, representing 27.2% annual growth.
  • Gross profit margin -- 70.5% in Q4, 200 basis points higher than the previous year, with fiscal year 2025 margin increasing 330 basis points, mainly due to higher-margin U.S. sales.
  • U.S. Motiva sales -- $45.6 million in 2025, achieving roughly 20% augmentation market share upon exit of the year and described as “something that took the last new entrant almost ten years to achieve.”
  • Net loss from operations -- $(3.9 million) in Q4, down 79% year over year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $5.5 million positive in Q4, compared to $(13,100,000) loss last year.
  • SG&A expenses -- $44 million in Q4, flat year over year.
  • R&D expenses -- $5.4 million in Q4, supporting continued product innovation.
  • Cash balance -- Ended 2025 with $75.6 million after a $4.9 million quarterly increase, driven by lower operating cash use and option exercises.
  • 2026 revenue guidance -- $264 million to $266 million (25.1%-26% growth), with the U.S. projected to contribute over 30% of total sales.
  • 2026 OUS (Outside U.S.) growth -- Global business outside the U.S. expected to grow at a single-digit rate.
  • Gross margin outlook -- Guidance for a 200- to 300-basis point decrease for 2026.
  • Operating expenses guidance -- $195 million to $200 million for 2026, with underlying cash expenses of $175 million to $180 million.
  • Adjusted EBITDA forecast -- Positive every quarter in 2026, projecting continued improvement in profitability.
  • Cash flow guidance -- Management expects to achieve positive cash flow in 2026 without additional capital required; free cash use to be less than half of 2025 levels.
  • Sales force expansion -- Up to 15 additional U.S. sales representatives planned for 2026, with most hires completed; majority are seasoned industry professionals.
  • Account penetration -- Over 1,500 U.S. accounts onboarded since launch, with both January and February [of 2026] exceeding expectations and further account growth expected throughout the year.
  • Preserva launch -- Full commercial U.S. launch in March 2026 after strong early experience; average Preserva procedure earning over twice traditional revenue and priced at a premium, with surgeons charging 30%-50% more than standard augmentation.
  • Preserva user data -- 81% of U.S. prelaunch organic leads reported Preserva as their exclusive interest; 15% of patients would not have pursued augmentation without Preserva.
  • Minimally invasive platform -- Preserva now available in 33 global markets, with over 700 accounts opened and 200 U.S. surgeons expected to be trained by year-end.
  • Minimally invasive platform guidance -- Forecasted to exceed $30 million in global demand for 2026.
  • Geographic revenue breakdown (Q4) -- EMEA 41%, U.S. 26.8%, Latin America 18%, APAC 14.1% of global sales.
  • Direct market growth -- European direct markets delivered greater than 20% sales growth for the third straight quarter, led by the U.K., Germany, and Spain.
  • Mia -- Outperformed $8 million-$10 million guidance and doubled account base versus 2024.
  • FDA filings -- Submitted Motiva Implants for U.S. approval in primary and revision breast reconstruction in December 2025; Motiva Flora Breast Tissue Expander in 200 facilities nationwide.
  • Management transition -- New CFO Cassandra Harris appointed effective March 9, 2026; Rajbir Singh Denhoy transitioning to SVP, Global Strategy.
  • Index inclusion efforts -- Work ongoing for eligibility and likely inclusion in indices such as the Russell within the year.

Summary

Establishment Labs (ESTA 3.01%) recorded an acceleration in top-line growth, achieving 45.2% year-over-year revenue expansion in the fourth quarter largely driven by rapid U.S. adoption and robust margin performance. The company maintained cost discipline, keeping SG&A flat and limiting cash operating expense expansion below revenue growth, leading to significant improvement in net loss and a shift to positive adjusted EBITDA for two consecutive quarters. Strategic guidance for 2026 projects top-line growth in the mid-20% range, with a step-up in U.S. revenue contribution and sustained OUS momentum, while operational leverage and commercial execution remain focal points. Pipeline initiatives, including the full U.S. commercial launch of Preserva and FDA filings for reconstruction and new product sizes, position Establishment Labs Holdings to drive higher pricing, broaden its addressable market, and reinforce share gains. Leadership changes, including a new CFO and the elevation of Rajbir Singh Denhoy to SVP, Global Strategy, mark the company's focus on scalability and execution as it enters its next growth phase.

  • The management team cited “no need for additional capital” and forecast positive cash flow by year-end 2026 based on current visibility and cash balances.
  • Preserva’s high ASP and appeal to previously untapped patient segments could provide both dollar market expansion and a lift in procedure volumes, with “at least 200” U.S. surgeons to be trained by year-end.
  • Motiva achieved a “roughly 20% augmentation market share” in the U.S. by the end of its first full year, a pace of market entry described by management as unprecedented in the sector.
  • Guidance does not factor in revenue from U.S. reconstruction or small sizes approvals for 2026; material impact is expected to follow upon anticipated FDA clearance.
  • Efficiencies in cost structure, along with pricing from innovations like Preserva and a favorable mix shift, are expected to drive further margin expansion in 2027 and beyond.
  • Management emphasized active preparation for potential inclusion in benchmark indices, which may influence capital market positioning in the coming year.

Industry Glossary

  • OUS: "Outside the United States," referring to business operations and financial metrics from markets beyond the U.S.
  • Preserva: A proprietary minimally invasive breast augmentation procedure platform from Establishment Labs Holdings, designed for smaller incisions, minimal anesthesia, faster recovery, and premium pricing.
  • Motiva Flora Breast Tissue Expander: An implantable device aiding in breast reconstruction by stretching tissue in preparation for permanent implants.
  • Mia: Establishment Labs Holdings’ product family for minimally invasive breast enhancement, targeted for global markets.
  • ASP: Average Selling Price, a metric for the revenue received per item sold, particularly relevant as Establishment Labs Holdings launches higher-priced innovations.
