Bloom Energy (BE) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Oct 28, 2025 at 6:36PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Oct. 28, 2025, at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Founder, Chairman, and Chief Executive Officer — KR Sridhar
  • Acting Principal Financial Officer — Maciej Kurzymski
  • Senior Vice President of Finance and Investor Relations — Michael Tierney

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $519 million in the quarter, up 57% year over year, marking the fourth consecutive quarterly revenue record as of fiscal Q3 2025 (period ending Sept. 30, 2025).
  • Non-GAAP gross margin -- 30.4%, an increase of 510 basis points from the prior year's 25.2% (non-GAAP, fiscal Q3 2024), attributed to product cost reductions and manufacturing efficiency.
  • Operating income -- $46.2 million non-GAAP, compared to $8.1 million non-GAAP in the same quarter last year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $59 million non-GAAP, up from $21 million non-GAAP in the prior year period.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $0.15 in the quarter, reversing a $0.01 non-GAAP loss a year ago.
  • Product margins -- 35.9% non-GAAP in the quarter, with service margins at 14.4% non-GAAP, with the service segment delivering its seventh consecutive profitable quarter (non-GAAP).
  • Cash flow from operating activities -- $20 million inflow in the quarter, primarily from working capital improvements.
  • Ending cash balance -- $627 million on the balance sheet at the end of the quarter.
  • Capacity expansion -- Bloom Energy (BE +4.10%) is doubling its production capacity to 2 gigawatts by December 2026, to support approximately four times the full-year fiscal 2025 revenue.
  • Brookfield partnership -- Brookfield (BN 0.45%) has announced an initial $5 billion investment in AI infrastructure, making Bloom Energy Corporation the "preferred on-site provider" according to KR Sridhar for its global portfolio, with a Bloom-powered European AI inference data center project planned for announcement by the end of the year.
  • Lighthouse customers -- Bloom Energy Corporation states it has secured lighthouse customers and strong commercial pipelines in seven AI ecosystem channels, including Oracle (ORCL +0.17%), Amazon (AMZN +2.05%) (via AWS and AEP), Equinix (EQIX 0.10%), CoreVeev, a global gas provider, and major infrastructure funds.
  • Order fulfillment lead time -- The company delivered its Oracle AI factory order in 55 days, ahead of its 90-day delivery commitment, as announced in August 2025.
  • Service business -- Achieved a seventh consecutive quarter of profitability (non-GAAP) as of fiscal Q3 2025 with expectations for continued double-digit service margins (non-GAAP).
  • 2025 outlook -- Management expects fiscal 2025 to "be better than our previously stated annual guidance for our financial metrics," according to KR Sridhar.
  • Product cost reductions -- Double-digit annual reductions in product cost have persisted for over a decade, with management expecting this trend to continue.
  • Channel expansion -- Management reports commercial momentum is "absolutely accelerating" in both AI and traditional segments, with capacity constraints not expected to limit growth.
  • Financing mix -- The majority of transactions are Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) structures, with direct CapEx sales occurring periodically.
  • Related party revenue -- $288 million disclosed in fiscal Q3 2025, specifically tied to joint ventures with Brookfield resulting from small equity investments.

Summary

Bloom Energy reported record non-GAAP financial results for fiscal Q3 2025. Highlights include rapid revenue growth, significant expansion in profitability, and robust commercial momentum in both AI-driven and traditional market segments, as reflected in non-GAAP results for the quarter. Management stated the 2-gigawatt capacity expansion due by December 2026 is progressing as planned and will ensure the company is not a bottleneck for customer growth. The newly expanded partnership with Brookfield positions Bloom Energy as a preferred global supplier for AI infrastructure projects, with a European deployment announcement expected before year-end. Strategic investments in talent, R&D, and commercial capabilities are designed to support continued margin accretion, technological leadership, and further penetration into new geographies and industry verticals. Management continues to emphasize operating and financial discipline, with clear operating leverage being demonstrated by improvements in cash flow, margins, and segment profitability.

  • CEO Sridhar stated, "We are now competitive in large power-hungry markets of the Midwest, Mid-Atlantic, Mountain West, and Texas, and many European and Asian cities," indicating ongoing geographic market expansion.
  • Management cited prolonged product life, higher reliability, and efficiency, with fuel cells now producing "10 times more power in the same footprint than they did ten years ago."
  • "Our mass-produced modular power systems allow us to power sites as small as your neighborhood retail store and as large as a Giga AI factory," said Sridhar, underscoring highly flexible deployment capabilities.
  • Sridhar described accelerated momentum as "palpable" and "absolutely accelerating" across both AI and traditional commercial markets.
  • Kurzymski clarified no material capital commitments for Bloom Energy under the Brookfield JVs, aside from small project-specific equity contributions, as confirmed by management on the fiscal Q3 2025 earnings call.
  • Sridhar stated legacy turbines and engines cannot support net-zero solutions using natural gas, while Bloom Energy Corporation can integrate carbon capture for "almost net zero" according to KR Sridhar operations, which is driving demand in Europe.
  • The CFO search was described as active, conducted with "a sense of urgency, but no sense of rush," according to KR Sridhar.

Industry glossary

  • Lighthouse customer: A foundational reference account within a target vertical or geography, intended to validate a new technology and accelerate broader adoption among peer customers.
  • Power Purchase Agreement (PPA): A contractual structure whereby a customer purchases electricity generated by Bloom Energy Corporation's installed systems over a fixed long-term period, typically enabling project financing and off-balance sheet treatment for the customer.
  • Inference data center: Data center sites designed to run AI workloads that interpret or 'infer' information, often with extremely low-latency requirements and in proximity to end-users, differing from central AI training facilities.
