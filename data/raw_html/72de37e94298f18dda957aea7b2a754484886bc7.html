UL Solutions (ULS) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 10:07AM EST

Thursday, February 19, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Jennifer F. Scanlon
  • Chief Financial Officer — Ryan D. Robinson

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $3.1 billion, representing a 6.4% increase, with organic growth of 6.2%.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (Full Year) -- Grew 20.7%, reaching a margin of 25.9%, up 300 basis points.
  • Q4 Revenue -- $789 million, up 0.8%, with organic growth of 5.7% amid challenging prior-year comparables.
  • Q4 Adjusted EBITDA -- $217 million, up 28.4%, yielding a margin of 27.5%, a 460 basis point improvement.
  • Industrial Segment Revenue (Q4) -- $352 million, an increase of 7.3%, with organic growth of 6.1%; all service lines contributed to growth.
  • Consumer Segment Revenue (Q4) -- $335 million, rising 8.4%, with organic growth of 7.1%, led by demand in non-certification testing and technology.
  • Software and Advisory Revenue (Q4) -- $102 million, flat year over year in total and organic terms.
  • Free Cash Flow (Full Year) -- $403 million, up from $287 million, and improved from 10% to 13.2% of revenue.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $197 million invested, equaling 6.5% of revenue, focusing on new labs and infrastructure expansion.
  • Dividends Paid -- $104 million returned to shareholders throughout the year.
  • Borrowings Repaid -- $253 million in outstanding debt reduced during the year.
  • Employee Health & Safety (EHS) Software Business Divestiture -- Announced sale for $210 million, removing approximately $56 million from annual revenue, expected to close in Q2; proceeds to be used initially for debt repayment and strategic reinvestment.
  • Restructuring Charges -- $37 million in Q4 pre-tax, relating to ongoing restructuring activities that aim to improve annual operating income by $25 million to $30 million upon completion by Q1 2027.
  • 2026 Outlook: Organic Revenue Growth -- Mid-single-digit guidance, with Industrial expected to grow faster than Consumer, and an anticipated 50 basis point FX tailwind.
  • 2026 Outlook: Adjusted EBITDA Margin -- Projected to be between 26.5% and 27%, inclusive of the EHS divestiture and restructuring benefits.
  • Dividend Increase -- Quarterly dividend to rise by 11.5% in 2026.
  • Segment Realignment -- Advisory services (5% of 2025 revenue) moved to Industrial, with Software and Advisory segment renamed to Risk and Compliance Software to focus on core ULTRIS offerings.
  • Laboratory Expansion -- New and expanded facilities in Germany, Italy, Japan, China, and the U.S. aimed at battery, HVAC, fire science, and IoT testing, reflecting continued infrastructure investment.
  • New Certification Services -- Launched offerings for battery-powered vehicles and equipment complying with UL 2850 and UL 2701 standards, and expanded ECOLOGO certification to industrial products.
  • ULTRIS Software Progress -- Rolled out AI-powered releases supporting compliance and sustainability goals.
  • Effective Tax Rate -- Increased to 26.6% in 2025 from 16.9%, due to OECD Pillar Two implementation and absence of prior-year tax reserve release.
  • Lab Headcount -- Maintained flat, with increased revenue per employee and productivity through technology adoption and process improvements.

SUMMARY

UL Solutions (ULS 0.18%) reported significant margin expansion and cash generation, emphasizing disciplined capital allocation and a record year for organic growth across all regions without reliance on external events. Management announced a strategic portfolio shift, highlighted by the planned sale of its EHS software business and an internal realignment to focus resources on higher-growth software offerings aligned with core testing and certification capabilities. Capital deployment prioritized ongoing laboratory infrastructure expansion globally, with emphasis on facilities serving critical growth end markets such as energy transition, connected devices, and data centers. A dividend increase and continued deleveraging reinforce ULS's balance sheet strength and commitment to returning value to shareholders.

  • Management stated, "Over 55% of our global and strategic accounts customers currently purchase at least one of our ULTRIS risk and compliance software offerings," underscoring digital platform penetration.
  • The CEO explained, As we are out there looking at things like the energy transition, or digitalization that is really pushing AI data centers, and even the needs in the sustainability space. You know, those are three of our biggest megatrends. All of those are yielding, as we look at it, double-digit growth.
  • The CFO clarified that in both certification and non-certification testing, "there were similar contributions from price and volume in the revenue growth."
  • Segment realignment moves advisory revenue of $139 million from Software and Advisory to Industrial, to better align with technical and sectoral growth opportunities.
  • Restructuring is expected to be "substantially completed by the end of the first quarter of 2027," with most changes affecting the Consumer segment in 2026.
  • Management highlighted that "Q1 is typically our lowest revenue quarter," due to seasonality from the Lunar New Year and fewer workdays in Asia.
  • The planned divestiture of the EHS software business is expected to improve software segment growth rates, as that business had lagged the rest of the software portfolio.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • TIC: Testing, Inspection, and Certification — assurance services offered to manufacturers across industries to meet regulatory and quality standards.
  • ULTRIS: UL Solutions Inc.'s proprietary risk and compliance software platform used for product compliance, supply chain visibility, and data-driven decision-making.
  • ECOLOGO Certification: A sustainability-focused product certification program for industrial and energy automation products launched by UL Solutions.
  • EMC Testing: Electromagnetic Compatibility testing, ensuring products comply with regulatory limits on electromagnetic emissions and susceptibility.
  • EHS Software: Employee Health and Safety software, focused on compliance, risk, and incident management for workplace safety, now divested from ULS's software portfolio.
