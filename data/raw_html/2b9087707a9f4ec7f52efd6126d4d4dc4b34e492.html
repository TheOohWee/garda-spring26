Earnings Call Transcript

Centuri (CTRI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 25, 2026 at 11:35AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Feb. 25, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Christian Brown
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Greg Izenstark

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $3.0 billion for the year, representing a 13% increase.
  • Base Revenue -- $2.9 billion, up 18%, as defined excluding storm restoration services.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $39 million, a 49% rise, with adjusted net income per share of $0.43.
  • Fourth-Quarter Revenue -- $859 million, an increase of 20%, including $855 million in base revenue, up 28%.
  • Fourth-Quarter Net Income -- $30 million, or $0.32 per share, including a $23.7 million income tax benefit from deferred tax asset allocations.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $249 million for the year, up from $238 million.
  • Backlog -- $5.9 billion at year-end, up 59%, with bookings of over $4.5 billion and a year-end book-to-bill of 1.5x.
  • MSA Contribution -- 78% of revenue and 82% of year-end backlog came from Master Service Agreement (MSA) contracts.
  • Base Gross Profit -- $234 million for the year, rising 35%, with a base gross margin of 8%, up from 6.9%.
  • Segment Revenue Growth -- U.S. Gas: $1.3 billion (+5%); Canadian: $247 million (+25%); Union Electric: $800 million (+21%); Nonunion Electric: $569 million (+51%).
  • Improved Gross Margins -- Canadian segment: 18.6% (up from 15.9%); Union Electric: 8.7% (up 110 basis points); Nonunion Electric: 8.5% (up from 5.9%).
  • Headcount Expansion -- Employee count increased by nearly 15% over 13 months.
  • Fleet Strategy -- Shift to 50/50 buy versus lease, investing $135 million in fleet assets, supporting higher free cash flow.
  • Deleveraging -- Net debt to adjusted EBITDA reduced to 2.5x from 3.6x, using $251 million in equity offering proceeds for debt paydown and acquisition funding.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Base revenue expected between $3.15 billion and $3.45 billion; base gross profit of $255 million to $285 million; total revenue guidance inclusive of storm restoration services at $3.24 billion to $3.54 billion; adjusted EBITDA of $280 million to $310 million; adjusted net income of $55 million to $75 million; net CapEx forecast at $75 million to $90 million.
  • Data Center Opportunity -- $1.4 billion of active pipeline opportunities, with more than $2.0 billion identified as potential in early stages.
  • Fourth-Quarter Free Cash Flow -- $106 million reported.
  • Operational Initiatives -- Efforts highlighted to reduce gas segment seasonality, target at least 20% fleet efficiency improvement, and drive higher bid margins.

RISKS

  • Management flagged gas segment first-quarter seasonality as an area being addressed through a three-year initiative, with the potential for continued margin volatility until fully resolved.

SUMMARY

Centuri Holdings (CTRI +0.90%) reported new company records in revenue and bookings, supported by broad-based organic segment growth and strengthened by substantial backlog expansion. The business completed structural moves, including full separation from its former parent and the acquisition of Connect Atlantic Utility Services, to advance its electric market platform and geographic reach. Management emphasized the sustainability of growth through persistent operational initiatives, further headcount addition, and a renewed sales pipeline approach, aiming for ongoing expansion in both MSA and bid work revenues. Centuri Holdings 2026 guidance reflects higher adjusted EBITDA and net income targets, incorporating conservative assumptions for storm-related work and capex discipline through its evolving fleet funding model.

  • Christian Brown said, "we are not sized to capacity," highlighting available internal capacity to support incremental growth if market demand strengthens beyond current backlog.
  • Greg Izenstark stated that net debt to adjusted EBITDA is forecast at around 2.0x by year-end 2026, driven by ongoing deleveraging and lower interest expenses.
  • Free cash flow conversion of adjusted EBITDA is targeted at 50% on a long-term basis, according to Izenstark, suggesting further improvements in cash generation efficiency.
  • Brown described the data center pipeline as highly disciplined, stating, "those data centers where we have got dialogue engaged with the developer or with the end user who has capital and is going to deploy capital," are included in official forecasts.
  • Guidance does not anticipate significant storm revenue variation, with management confirming that "The storm impact thus far this year has been pretty minor."
  • Management identified additional capacity for workforce expansion beyond the 12%-15% annual range already achieved, with new hiring approaches spanning apprenticeships and cross-industry recruitment.
  • Brown noted that any upside beyond current guidance would come from "winning new bids," with a detailed pipeline outlined for near-term awards and a significant portion of 2026 base revenue secured by year-end backlog and renewals.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • MSA (Master Service Agreement): A long-term contract that governs recurring service work, typically for utility clients, providing revenue visibility and continuity.
  • Base Revenue/Base Gross Profit: Company performance metrics that exclude storm restoration services to represent recurring, fundamental operational results.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: The ratio of new contract bookings to revenue recognized in a given period; a value above 1.0x indicates growing backlog.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, modified to exclude certain specified items for comparability.

Our drive now is to capture these market tailwinds in our pipeline to build up more backlog to not only do more this year, but build a bigger backlog going into 2027.

So the block and tackling is fundamental to being a predictable service provider, which is our desire and our commitment, and then using the pipeline to continue driving growth across all our opportunities and all of our businesses such that we have a greater backlog going into 2027, and we have committed to double-digit growth year over year, and we still stand by that. We believe the pipeline affords that opportunity without relying upon things we cannot control, like storms, and we will continue to drive the businesses to at least meet that obligation.

Christian Brown: I think we have got—if you look at slide nine again and refer back to it, we are not sized to capacity. That is apprentices, that is veterans coming into our veterans program, it is resourcing from parts of the country where we can see people coming out of other adjacent industries, it is cross-training and cross-developing.

So in terms of capacity, we will commit in our guidance a conservative level of performance that we know we can meet based upon the backlog we have, the resources we can see and have, and things we can control. But on the people side, it is our focus, and we are doing a number of things, some of which I alluded to on the last call, to operate as one Centuri Holdings, Inc. and not as individual opcos, meaning that we can have a more longer-range plan, a more wholesome one-company approach to resourcing across the entire continent. Christian Brown: Yeah.
