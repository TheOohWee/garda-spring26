Takeaways

  • Loan originations -- $2.6 billion, up 40% year over year, with all product lines contributing to the increase.
  • Return on tangible common equity (ROTCE) -- 11.9%, more than tripling from the prior period and above guidance.
  • Marketplace revenue -- Increased 36% year over year, driven by higher marketplace sales volumes and improved loan sale pricing.
  • Net interest income -- $163 million, a 14% rise year over year, reaching an all-time high.
  • Noninterest income -- $103 million, up 38%, supported by marketplace sales volumes and credit performance.
  • Net interest margin -- 6%, representing a 56 basis point annual increase, with a sequential decline attributed to higher cash balances for future growth.
  • Total deposits -- $9.8 billion at period end, growing 8% year over year.
  • Noninterest expense -- $169 million, up 19%, mainly due to higher planned marketing investments.
  • Provision for credit losses -- $47 million, reflecting a stable credit environment, with a higher proportion from longer-duration major purchase finance loans.
  • Net charge-off ratio -- Decreased 80 basis points over the prior year, with expected seasonality from vintage seasoning.
  • Total assets -- $11.6 billion, a 9% increase year over year.
  • Share repurchase and acquisition program -- $12 million deployed in the quarter at an average price of $17.65 per share as part of the previously announced $100 million program.
  • Net income before taxes -- $50 million, more than quadrupling year over year.
  • Effective tax rate -- 16.9% for the quarter, aided by a nonrecurring research and development tax credit benefit; normalized rate expected around 24% going forward.
  • Diluted earnings per share (EPS) -- $0.35 for the quarter, with full-year EPS of $1.16, marking a year-over-year increase of over 100%.
  • Fair value accounting transition -- Full move to fair value option for all new held-for-investment loan originations starting next quarter, expected to simplify financials and align revenue/loss recognition.
  • Q1 2026 outlook -- Expected loan originations of $2.55 billion to $2.65 billion and diluted EPS of $0.34 to $0.39, reflecting 28%-33% and 240%-290% year-over-year growth, respectively.
  • Full year 2026 guidance -- Projected originations of $11.6 billion to $12.6 billion (up 21%-31%) and EPS expected at $1.65 to $1.80 (up 42%-55%).
  • New product developments -- Launched rated structured certificate product for insurance capital and entered into a direct forward flow agreement with a major US insurance company.
  • Deposit product expansion -- LevelUp Savings and LevelUp Checking both growing by double digits, with high engagement from personal loan borrowers.
  • Home improvement financing -- Preparations for entry underway; technology integration and initial partnership signed, with launch on track for midyear.
  • Brand repositioning -- New brand slated to debut later in the year to reflect product expansion beyond traditional lending.

Summary

LendingClub (LC 1.00%) delivered substantial loan origination growth, advanced its transition to fair value accounting, and outlined specific guidance ranges for 2026 that signal sustained expansion and profitability. Management described new investor channels through insurance partnerships, accelerated deposit growth in digital savings and checking, and integration of home improvement financing capabilities as major growth vectors not previously reflected in historical performance. Strategic investments in marketing, technology, and customer engagement proliferate across all business lines, while recent product and process enhancements drive multiple touchpoints for future expansion. Adjustments to accounting practices, including the cessation of origination fee and marketing cost deferrals, will alter reported revenue and expense profiles in the upcoming quarters. Initiatives to strengthen long-term shareholder value now include tactical use of the repurchase and acquisition program, as well as a major rebranding strategy encompassing LendingClub's broader ambitions.

  • Management confirmed “product categories were growing last year, and we're expecting future growth this year,” identifying marketing channel investment and product innovation as foundation for further market share gains.
  • Sanborn highlighted “over 150 billion cells of data” powering proprietary underwriting, maintaining “40 to 50% better credit performance versus our competitive set.”
  • LaBenne noted a “7.1% discount rate for the quarter” on fair value adjustments, with mix changes in products like major purchase finance influencing this metric.
  • Marketplace loan sale pricing “improving back towards our historical range,” supporting demand from both traditional and new investor types such as insurance capital.
  • Sanborn emphasized that home improvement and major purchase loans will increase portfolio duration, but management “not expecting a major shift” in annualized credit loss rates due to asset mix.
  • LaBenne described that fair value adoption “removing the front-loaded CECL impact,” with CECL expense declining as legacy portfolio amortizes off.
  • Outlook assumes a “healthy economy with stable macro conditions,” with “Additional investments in marketing to fuel 2026 growth.”
  • LaBenne clarified that marketing and origination fee deferrals end with the fair value transition and “both line items should increase from Q4 2025 to Q1 2026, independent of any changes in origination volume.”
  • CFO stated, “we will continue to have an inventory of held-for-sale loans. We've, as we've said before, we found that program to be very helpful to onboard new investors and to make opportunistic sales and maybe better prices than we would otherwise get.”
  • Sanborn cited AI deployment across “over 60 initiatives underway across the company,” including document verification and customer analytics productivity.
  • New CECL accounting guidance “be a positive for bank investors that are considering purchasing from us,” potentially reducing CECL impacts for third-party bank loan buyers.
  • Management indicated the rebrand reflects the business’s evolution beyond lending, but stated, “I wouldn't expect, you know, we're not gonna go from being a highly data-driven, you know, efficient curve-oriented direct response marketer to getting stadium rights in 2026. You're not gonna see a big step change in how we think about marketing.”

Industry glossary

  • ROTCE (Return on Tangible Common Equity): Net income available to common shareholders divided by average tangible common equity, measuring profitability attributable to shareholders after deducting intangible assets.
  • CECL (Current Expected Credit Losses): Accounting standard requiring estimation and recognition of expected credit losses over the life of financial assets such as loans at origination or purchase.
  • Fair value option: An accounting method allowing financial assets and liabilities to be measured at estimated current market value, with changes reflected in earnings.
  • Major purchase finance: LendingClub's loan products used to finance large consumer purchases, often with longer durations than core debt consolidation loans.
  • Origination fee deferral: Pre-fair value accounting practice of spreading loan origination fees as income over the expected loan life rather than recognizing them upfront.
