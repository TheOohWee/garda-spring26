Keurig Dr Pepper (KDP) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jul 24, 2025 at 9:44AM EST

Thursday, July 24, 2025 at 8 a.m. ET

Chief Executive Officer — Timothy Cofer

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

RISKS

Keurig Dr Pepper Inc. (KDP +0.57%) anticipates "some margin pressure in the back half of 2025" as rising cost pressures—including higher commodity inflation and tariffs—are expected to moderate EPS growth and operating income.

In the U.S. coffee segment, management said, "we still expect some impact. And that's why we are planning for some segment OI pressure in the second half," explicitly citing rising green coffee costs, tariffs, retail inventory management, and pricing elasticity as headwinds.

Gross margin contracted 110 basis points year-over-year in Q2 2025 as inflationary pressures "more than offset pricing and productivity savings."

TAKEAWAYS

Net Sales Growth: Net sales increased 7.2% in constant currency for Q2 2025, driven by volume mix, price, and the GHOST acquisition.

U.S. Refreshment Beverages Segment: Net sales grew 10.5%, with volume mix up 9.5% (including a 6.6 percentage point boost from GHOST) and net price up 1% in Q2 2025.

U.S. Coffee Segment Performance: Net sales declined 0.2%, with net price realization up 3.6% and volume mix down 3.8% in Q2 2025.

International Segment Sales: Net sales increased 5.7%, led by net price realization of 5.3% and volume mix up 0.4% in Q2 2025, despite challenging conditions in Mexico.

EPS Growth: Earnings per share grew by double digits in Q2 2025, with first-half EPS growth reaching nearly 10%.

Productivity Savings: Management confirmed progress toward the high end of its 3%-4% full-year productivity savings goal for 2025.

Energy Portfolio Expansion: The combined energy drink brands reached over $1 billion in annual run rate net sales and a 7% market share in Q2 2025, up from less than 1% several years ago.

Free Cash Flow: The company generated $325 million in free cash flow in Q2 2025, an improvement from the previous quarter.

Leverage Ratio: Leverage at the end of Q2 2025 was 3.3x, with an ongoing target of 2.5x or below.

Pricing Actions: Additional pricing moves are planned in the coffee segment in response to ongoing cost inflation, with category elasticity described as "manageable" but closely monitored. These actions are expected to take effect in the back half of 2025.

Ownership of Dilo Brands: Completed acquisition of Dilo, expanding presence in the powdered drink mix and liquid water enhancer category.

Route-to-Market Expansion: Direct store delivery (DSD) capabilities are being expanded, with Dr. Pepper distribution set to increase across parts of California, Nevada, and the Midwest later this year.

SUMMARY

Keurig Dr Pepper reported broad-based top-line momentum driven by U.S. refreshment beverages and energy drink portfolio expansion in Q2 2025. The company maintained its guidance for mid-single-digit constant currency net sales growth and high-single-digit EPS growth for FY2025, noting sustained investments in productivity, distribution, and innovation. Management highlighted a focused balance between near-term cost management and long-term strategic initiatives, including tuck-in acquisitions and digital transformation. Foreign exchange is now projected to be a 0.5 percentage point headwind to full-year 2025 results, equating to an approximate $0.01 EPS impact. Capital allocation remains disciplined, prioritizing growth investment, debt reduction, dividends, and opportunistic share repurchases.

Cofer said, "this year, we are on track for our ninth consecutive year of market share growth with Brand Dr Pepper."

Retail sales of the electrolyte sports hydration brand grew over 30%, gaining more than 1.5 percentage points of segment share in Q2 2025.

International segment performance included market share gains in mineral water (Mexico) and K-Cup pods (Canada) despite adverse macro and weather impacts.

New product launches such as Dr Pepper Blackberry and Bloom Pop, as well as innovation in ready-to-drink and prebiotic beverages, were highlighted by management, with Bloom Pop set to launch in Q3.

Investments in marketing technology aim to increase return on investment, with Cofer noting, "you're going to also see it materialize in a more personalized digitally-enabled consumer engagement."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

DSD (Direct Store Delivery): Distribution method in which products are delivered directly to retail stores, enabling greater in-store execution and merchandising control.

TDP (Total Distribution Points): A metric indicating the weighted count of outlets where a product is sold, reflecting the breadth of distribution.

OI (Operating Income): A financial measure representing profit earned from core business operations, excluding interest and tax expenses.
