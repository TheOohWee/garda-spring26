Bank of Hawaii (BOH) Q1 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 11:12AM EST

DATE

Monday, April 21, 2025 at 2 p.m. ET

  • Chairman, President & Chief Executive Officer — Peter S. Ho
  • Vice Chair & Chief Risk Officer — Brad Shairson
  • Vice Chair & Chief Financial Officer — Dean Y. Shigemura

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Interest Income -- $125.8 million, up 4.6% linked quarter, representing the fourth consecutive sequential increase.
  • Net Interest Margin -- Rose to 2.32%, an increase of 13 basis points from the prior quarter.
  • Deposits -- Period-end deposits reached $21 billion, growing 7.3% annualized on a linked basis, with noninterest-bearing deposits stable.
  • Loans -- Period-end loans totaled $14.1 billion, up 1.1% annualized linked quarter; loan portfolio remains 93% Hawaii, 4% Western Pacific, and 3% Mainland.
  • Deposit Costs -- Fell for a second straight quarter, with March average at 1.6% and additional declines expected from time deposit repricing.
  • Credit Quality -- Net charge-offs at $4.4 million (13 basis points annualized), up 3 basis points sequentially, while nonperforming assets declined 2 basis points; delinquency rate dropped 4 basis points to 30 basis points.
  • Allowance for Credit Losses -- Ended at $147.7 million, down $800,000 from the prior period, equating to 1.05% of outstanding loans.
  • Noninterest Income -- Reported as $44.1 million, adjusted to $44.6 million after removing a $600,000 Visa-related charge, down $800,000 sequentially, impacted by market volatility in Trust Services and lower derivative transactions.
  • Expenses -- Totaled $110.5 million, including $2.8 million in seasonal payroll/benefit costs and a $2.3 million FDIC special assessment reimbursement; expectations remain for 2%-3% core expense growth versus normalized 2024 base.
  • Net Income and EPS -- Net income was $44 million and earnings per share were $0.97, rising $4.8 million and $0.12, respectively, versus the prior quarter.
  • Return on Common Equity -- Stood at 11.8%, up from 10.3% linked quarter.
  • Capital Ratios -- Tier 1 capital ratio improved to 13.9%, with total capital ratio at 15%.
  • Interest Rate Positioning -- Fixed asset ratio ended at 56%; $2.2 billion in active swaps at an average fixed rate of 3.97% and $500 million in forward starting swaps at 3.09%.
  • Dividend -- Common dividend declared at $0.70 per share for the second quarter, with $28 million paid to common shareholders and $5.3 million in preferred dividends during the quarter.
  • Leadership Transition -- CFO Dean Y. Shigemura to retire June 30; Brad Satenberg appointed as successor effective that date.
  • Loan Growth Outlook -- Management maintains low single-digit growth guidance, supported by a solid commercial pipeline and increased consumer mortgage/HELOC applications.
  • Margin Target Commentary -- CEO Peter S. Ho stated, "if things trend out the way they have for the past several quarters now, kind of that the type of number at 2.50% is definitely within range."
  • CRE Portfolio -- Commercial real estate loans totaled $4 billion (29% of total), with sector diversification and all weighted average LTVs between 53%-56%.
  • Tariff Exposure -- Brad Shairson reported detailed analysis showing only 4% of the loan portfolio ($640 million) has potential direct industry exposure, largely mitigated by client profiles.

SUMMARY

Bank of Hawaii (BOH 1.34%) reported sequential improvements in both net interest income and net interest margin, driven by favorable asset repricing and moderating deposit costs. Management reaffirmed that a 2.50% margin is within reach by year-end if noninterest-bearing deposits remain stable and deposit costs continue trending lower. Despite economic uncertainty and external tariff pressures, the bank’s credit quality metrics remained favorable, with low net charge-offs and modest criticized loan levels. Forward-looking comments highlighted a solid commercial loan pipeline and increased consumer lending activity, though caution was advised due to the fluid market environment. Leadership announced a planned CFO transition effective June 30, introducing an established internal successor.

  • Active hedging program expanded with $200 million of new pay-fixed swaps and $200 million in forward starting swaps, altering the fixed asset mix and extending duration protection.
  • 74% of time deposits are set to mature within the next six months, which management expects will lower overall deposit funding costs further as they are repriced at lower rates.
  • Ongoing expense initiatives are targeted to moderate growth in core expense run-rate in the second half of the year after seasonal and incentive-related first quarter impacts.
  • Reported minimal direct exposure to tariffs, with only a small segment of clients in potentially affected sectors and further risk mitigated by Hawaii’s service-oriented economy and lenders’ conservative secured positions.
  • Board maintained the quarterly dividend at $0.70 per share, with both common and preferred dividends fully paid during the period.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ACL (Allowance for Credit Losses): Reserve set aside for estimated losses on loans and leases, calculated based on historical loss experience, current conditions, and reasonable forecasts.
  • CRE (Commercial Real Estate): Loans secured by commercial property such as office buildings, retail centers, industrial facilities, and multifamily housing.
  • LTV (Loan-to-Value): The ratio of loan amount to the appraised value of the collateral property, used to assess lending risk.
  • HELOC (Home Equity Line of Credit): A revolving credit facility secured by residential property, allowing borrowers to draw funds up to a set limit.
  • Swaps (Interest Rate Swaps): Derivative contracts in which parties exchange fixed and floating interest rate payments to manage exposure to changes in market rates.
  • Visa Class B conversion ratio: An adjustment factor impacting the value or accounting treatment of certain Visa shares, resulting in period charges or credits.
  • FDIC Special Assessment: A temporary surcharge imposed by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation to cover extraordinary losses or expenses, subject to later reimbursement or reversal.
