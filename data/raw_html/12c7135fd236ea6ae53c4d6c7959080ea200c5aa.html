Joby Aviation (JOBY) Q3 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 6, 2025 at 9:33AM EST

  • Chief Financial Officer — Rodrigo Brumana
  • Executive Chairman — Paul Sciarra

Takeaways

  • Power-on Testing of TIA Aircraft -- Joby initiated power-on testing for the first aircraft to be used in the Type Inspection Authorization (TIA) process, marking entry into the final certification stage with the FAA.
  • Certification Progress -- All five TIA aircraft are currently in production, and each will be used to accelerate parallel FAA flight testing.
  • Operational Demonstrations -- The Joby S4 aircraft completed high-frequency daily test flights in California, the first A-to-B mission to Monterey, and a public demonstration at the California International Air Show.
  • International Operations -- Aircraft are operational and cleared for daily transition flights at the Dubai Air Show, with expectations to ramp commercial operations in Dubai in 2026, prior to FAA type certification.
  • Manufacturing Ramp -- Joby produced 15 times more FAA-conforming parts year-to-date in Marina than in all of 2024, and began propeller blade production in Dayton, Ohio, while adding over 100 manufacturing roles in the quarter.
  • Autonomy Initiatives -- The SuperPilot autonomy system completed over 7,000 miles and 40 flight hours of autonomous operations with a Cessna 208 during summer 2025, and Joby has partnered with NVIDIA for integration of real-time data compute on future aircraft.
  • Hybrid Aircraft for Defense -- Ground testing of a turbine–electric aircraft developed with L3Harris is underway, with imminent flight testing and recent FAA airworthiness activity on-site.
  • Financial Position -- Cash and short-term investments reached $978 million at the end of Q3 2025, with an additional $576 million in net proceeds from an equity offering in October 2025.
  • GAAP Net Loss -- Net loss was $401 million for Q3, primarily due to $229 million in non-cash charges related to warrants and earn-out revaluation resulting from share price increases in Q3 2025.
  • Revenue -- Revenue totaled $23 million for Q3 2025, including $14 million from Blade (August 29–September 30) and $9 million from defense contract completions and engineering services; Agility Prime revenue will not recur.
  • Operating Expenses -- Total operating expenses were $204 million for Q3 2025. This represents a $36 million quarter-over-quarter increase due to Blade expenses, acquisition costs, higher staffing, and manufacturing investment.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA loss was $133 million for Q3 2025 (non-GAAP). This was $1 million higher than the prior quarter and $12 million higher than the same period last year, reflecting increased investment in design, manufacturing, and early commercialization.
  • Production Scaling with Toyota -- Joby emphasized accelerated scaling efforts through close partnership with Toyota to match rising early demand and international interest.
  • Blade Acquisition Impact -- Blade added $6 million in one-time costs in Q3 2025, established operations on key airport and commuter routes in New York and Europe, and will enter low season in Q4.
  • Global Market Preparation -- Regulatory, infrastructure, and pilot training readiness efforts are underway with new simulator acceptance in Montreal and route expansions anticipated in Dubai.

Summary

Joby Aviation (JOBY +0.72%) marked a major milestone by commencing power-on testing of its first FAA TIA aircraft, indicating close proximity to the final certification stage. Management confirmed that all five TIA test aircraft are progressing in parallel, and that manufacturing output has significantly increased in 2025 to meet accelerating demand in U.S. and international markets, particularly in Dubai. The company’s autonomy program demonstrated substantial operational maturity with thousands of autonomous flight miles logged and forged a partnership with NVIDIA for future upgrades. A hybrid-electric aircraft entered ground testing for defense applications and is positioned to compete for Department of War procurement. Quarter revenue was dominated by Blade, now integrated into Joby’s route network, but legacy Agility Prime defense revenue will not continue.

  • Paul Sciarra stated, "we do think there are going to be interesting revenue-generating opportunities that come out of the IPP program," but also clarified that not all test phase activity will be directly commercial.
  • The company highlighted that the principal bottleneck to accelerating Dubai or U.S. commercial launches is manufacturing pace, not market demand or regulatory readiness.
  • Management disclosed that full-scale TIA FAA-pilot testing is anticipated to synchronize with aircraft and pilot training readiness, with the potential for progress to accelerate significantly near the end of the certification process as test reports are submitted.
  • The Blade network is expanding new commuter routes in New York, with first public commuter service between Westchester and Manhattan, and management expects further route and infrastructure expansions post-certification.
  • International regulators in Dubai granted permission for daily public transition flights during the Dubai Air Show, signifying regulatory momentum outside the U.S.
  • Joby’s partnership with Toyota was described as closer than ever, with Toyota identified as critical for rapid scale and supply chain leverage.

Industry glossary

  • TIA (Type Inspection Authorization): The FAA process allowing aircraft to enter final certification flight testing using production-conforming prototypes evaluated by FAA pilots.
  • FAA-conforming parts: Components manufactured and documented in accordance with FAA-approved specifications, suitable for certification testing.
  • SuperPilot: Joby’s internally developed, AI-based autonomy and flight management system, integrated from the Xwing acquisition.
  • EIPP/IP Program: U.S. government executive initiative to enable pre-certification commercial operations of mature eVTOL aircraft in select markets before full FAA type certification.
  • Agility Prime: U.S. Department of Defense program aimed at accelerating adoption of commercial eVTOL for military applications.
  • VTOL: Vertical Takeoff and Landing capability, referring to aircraft that can take off and land vertically without a runway.
