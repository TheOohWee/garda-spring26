Walmart (WMT) Q4 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 9:24AM EST

Date

Feb. 19, 2026, 8 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and CEO — John Furner
  • Executive Vice President and CFO — John David Rainey
  • Executive Vice President, Walmart U.S. — Dave Gagina
  • President, International — Chris Nicholas
  • President, Sam's Club — Latrice Watkins

Takeaways

  • Consolidated Revenue Growth -- Up 4.9% in constant currency, with eCommerce up 24% globally.
  • Adjusted Operating Income -- Increased 10.5% in constant currency, more than double the revenue growth rate.
  • Walmart U.S. Comparable Sales -- Grew 4.6% in the quarter.
  • eCommerce Revenue—Walmart U.S. -- Rose 27%, with 35% of store-fulfilled orders delivered in under three hours.
  • eCommerce Revenue—China -- Increased 28%, now over 50% of segment sales mix.
  • Flipkart Delivery -- Orders in India delivered in less than fifteen minutes across over 30 cities.
  • Sam's Club U.S. -- Club-fulfilled delivery sales doubled; membership income up more than 6%.
  • Global Advertising Revenue -- Increased 37% this quarter; Walmart Connect U.S. up 41%; Visio advertising saw triple-digit growth.
  • Membership Income -- Consolidated income up more than 15%, led by over 35% growth at Sam's Club China and double digit growth for Walmart Plus in the U.S.
  • Inventory Management -- Inventory up 2.6%, approximately half the rate of sales growth; technology and automation cited as drivers.
  • International Operating Income -- Up more than 26%, attributed to improved eCommerce economics and cost efficiencies.
  • Omnichannel Fulfillment -- U.S. customers using delivery in under three hours increased more than 60% for the year.
  • Advertising and Membership Profit Contribution -- Combined represented nearly one third of quarterly operating income.
  • Capital Strategy -- Board authorized a $30 billion share repurchase program, the largest to date.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- Totaled $42 billion, with free cash flow up 18% for the fiscal year.
  • Sales and Operating Income Guidance -- For the upcoming year, expects constant currency sales growth of 3.5%-4.5% and operating income growth of 6%-8%.
  • EPS Guidance -- Expects $2.75-$2.85 for the full year; Q1 EPS forecast at $0.63-$0.65.
  • Sparky AI Engagement -- About half of app users have tried Sparky; those users display average order values approximately 35% higher than non-Sparky users.
  • Advertising Revenue—Full Year -- Globally up 46% to $6.4 billion.
  • Membership Fees—Full Year -- Exceeded $4.3 billion.
  • eCommerce Sales Mix -- Q4 represented 23% of total sales, an increase of 550 basis points over two years.
  • Capex Plans -- Capital expenditures targeted at approximately 3.5% of sales, with automation and store remodels peaking this year and next.
  • Store Network Investments -- Opened 12 new stores and remodeled 674 stores in the past year.
  • Rollbacks in Walmart U.S. -- 6,200 price rollbacks, up 23% from last year.

Summary

Walmart (NYSE: WMT) reported broad-based revenue and profit growth, driven by digital expansion and margin-rich business lines. Management emphasized the scalability of its technology platforms and the measurable efficiency gains from automation, particularly in inventory management and supply chain operations. Strategic priorities for the year ahead include advancing AI-driven commerce capabilities, accelerating omnichannel fulfillment, and leveraging advertising and membership initiatives for profit growth.

  • Walmart expects maximum fair pricing legislation on pharmacy to cause a 100 basis point sales headwind this year.
  • Store-fulfilled eCommerce orders now benefit from a dense U.S. footprint, enabling 95% of customers to receive delivery in three hours or less.
  • The Sparky agentic AI initiative is central to Walmart’s customer engagement strategy and is targeted for further expansion and capability growth.
  • Marketplace categories such as cook-and-dine, fashion, and home decor grew more than 40%, with the company investing for growth over immediate profitability in these areas.
  • Management reported that eCommerce in the U.S. has enjoyed double-digit incremental margins, with platform scale lowering marginal costs for continued digital growth.
  • International strategic focus centers on scaling technology and supply chain automation benefits to global markets using a "build once, scale globally" platform model.

Industry glossary

  • AgenTic Commerce / Agentic AI: AI-driven commerce where software agents interpret consumer intent, generate personalized solutions, and automate fulfillment across digital and physical channels.
  • Walmart Connect: Walmart’s advertising business platform, offering digital marketing solutions to brand partners across online and in‑store channels.
  • Visio: Walmart's digital advertising business/division acquired to expand the company's adtech offerings and omni-channel reach.
  • Sparky: Walmart's proprietary agentic AI shopping assistant embedded in digital platforms for app users, designed to increase customer order value and engagement through personalization.
  • Walmart Plus: Walmart’s U.S. membership subscription program offering delivery, shipping, and customer benefits common to other major retail memberships.
