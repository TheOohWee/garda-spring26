Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $190 million for the quarter, reflecting 11% growth year over year.
  • Clinical revenue -- Increased 16% year over year, with volume and share gains in key segments.
  • NGS revenue growth -- 23% year over year, "well ahead of NGS market growth rate," and accounted for about a third of clinical revenue.
  • Same store revenue -- $170 million, up 14% year over year, driven by a 6% test volume increase and 7% AUP growth; excludes Pathline acquisition impact.
  • Adjusted gross profit -- $5.8 million increase, representing 7% year-over-year growth.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $13.4 million for the quarter, a 10% increase, with sequential margin improvement in each quarter of 2025.
  • Test volume -- Public test volumes rose 11% year over year, with average revenue per clinical test up 5%.
  • Average revenue per clinical test (AUP) -- Sequentially up $12, or 3% from Q3; excluding Pathline, AUP increased $15, or 3% from Q3, and was up 7% year over year.
  • Total operating expenses -- $97 million, rising by $1 million, or 1%, over the prior year.
  • Cash flow -- Positive $1 million from operations in the quarter, with total cash at quarter-end at $160 million, slightly down from Q3.
  • Full year total revenue -- $727 million, representing 10% year-over-year growth.
  • Clinical revenue full year -- Up 15%; growth was 13% excluding Pathline.
  • Nonclinical revenue full year -- Declined 24%, matching revised expectations.
  • Adjusted gross profit full year -- $335 million, up $23 million, or 8%, with a 46% margin (down 111 basis points due to Pathline, nonclinical revenue decline, and liquid biopsy launch costs).
  • Full year cash flow from operations -- Positive $5 million, with free cash flow improving by over 35% versus 2024.
  • Adjusted EBITDA full year -- $43.4 million, a 9% improvement.
  • 2026 revenue guidance -- $793 million to $801 million, with Q1 growth expected around 10%, Q2 at 8%-9%, Q3 at 9%-10%, and Q4 slightly above 10%.
  • 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- $55 million to $57 million, projecting 27%-31% growth.
  • RADAR ST launch -- Full clinical launch set by end of February; initial focus on HPV-negative head and neck cancer and a subset of breast cancer, with "mid-single-digit millions" revenue contribution expected in 2026.
  • Additional RADAR ST indications -- Two more solid tumor submissions to MolDX, with potential to double testing market opportunity upon approval.
  • Sales force expansion -- Plan to add over 25 oncology sales specialists by Q3 to support RADAR ST and broader oncology offerings.
  • Pantracer portfolio update -- Nearly doubled Pantracer tissue test volumes in 2025 versus 2024; Pantracer Pro for solid tumor therapy selection launched in February.
  • Pantracer LVX reimbursement -- Awaiting MolDX decision; modest 2026 revenue contribution expected if approved.
  • Contract exits -- Intentionally exited high-volume, low-value contracts with AUP in the low $200s, shifting strategic focus to higher value testing and margin growth.
  • AUP vs. volume growth mix -- Management expects AUP to drive upper single-digit growth, with test volumes targeted for low- to mid-single-digit increases as strategy shifts to higher value testing.
  • Pathline acquisition -- Accretive to profitability anticipated starting 2026, with increased presence in the New Jersey market.
  • 2026 gross margin outlook -- Expected improvement of 100-120 basis points, supported by price increases, portfolio rationalization, and operational efficiency gains.
  • LIMS integration -- Migration from eight legacy systems to a unified platform underway; workflow efficiencies to emerge late 2026, with more pronounced impact expected in 2027 and 2028.
  • Pharma revenue -- Declined by over 25% in Q4; book expected to erode modestly (5%-10%) in 2026, with growth possible in 2027 pending RADAR ST traction.
  • Geographic penetration -- Pathline enhances Northeast reach; more than 25% hematology testing market share maintained nationally.
  • Customer penetration metric -- In 2025, 14% growth in clinicians ordering five or more tests; 40% of active pathologists and oncologists ordered five or more NeoGenomics tests.
  • Provider satisfaction -- Net promoter score of 79 reported, with upward trend in 2025.
  • Liquidity position -- Quarter-end cash of $160 million and expectation to achieve free cash flow positivity in 2026.

Summary

NeoGenomics (NEO 3.15%) reported double-digit revenue and clinical growth, highlighted by strong adoption of NGS and liquid biopsy products. Management confirmed the full clinical launch of RADAR ST, with additional solid tumor submissions pending and a coordinated sales strategy focused on targeted indications. The company is transitioning away from low-value, high-volume contracts, emphasizing higher margin test mix to support profitability and margin expansion. Leadership outlined investments in operational efficiency through LIMS integration, sales force expansion, and ongoing product development to secure future reimbursement growth. Clear signals point to continued double-digit revenue and EBITDA expansion in 2026, with free cash flow growth, while pharma revenues are expected to remain soft until 2027.

  • Management noted, "NGS revenues grew by 23% over prior year in the quarter and accounted for around a third of total clinical revenue."
  • Guidance incorporates "mid-single-digit millions" in RADAR ST revenue, with timing of additional indication approvals serving as a potential upside lever.
  • Execution of the Pathline acquisition is anticipated to benefit profitability beginning this year, expanding Northeast U.S. presence.
  • Management detailed, "Average revenue per clinical test increased sequentially from Q3 by $12, or 3%, and was up 5% from prior year."
  • Quarter-end liquidity of $160 million supports continued investment in commercial expansion, R&D, and technology upgrades.

Industry glossary

  • NGS: Next Generation Sequencing; advanced DNA/RNA analysis methods enabling precise molecular and genetic diagnostics.
  • MRD: Minimal Residual Disease; molecular testing to detect small numbers of cancer cells not visible via standard testing.
  • MolDX: A molecular diagnostics coverage program determining Medicare reimbursement for advanced molecular tests.
  • AUP: Average Unit Price; the average revenue per test performed.
  • Pantracer: The branded portfolio from NeoGenomics for solid tumor therapy selection tests, including liquid and tissue modalities.
  • LIMS: Laboratory Information Management System; software platform centralizing and streamlining lab workflow and data tracking.
  • Pathline: A New Jersey-based laboratory acquired by NeoGenomics, increasing regional market penetration.
  • OSS: Oncology Sales Specialist; dedicated personnel focused on selling oncology test products.
  • LBX: Pantracer LVX; a noninvasive, blood-based test to inform therapy decisions for solid tumors, pending MolDX coverage.
  • RADAR ST: Circulating tumor DNA test for MRD in solid tumors, with initial use in head and neck and breast cancers; newly launched with additional submissions in process.
