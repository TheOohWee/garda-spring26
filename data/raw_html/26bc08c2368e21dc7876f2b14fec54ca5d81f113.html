Nexa Resources (NEXA) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 27, 2026 at 11:36AM EST

Friday, February 27, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Ignacio Rosado
  • Chief Financial Officer — José Carlos del Valle
  • Senior Vice President, Mining Operations — Leonardo Nunes Coelho
  • Head of Investor Relations — Rodrigo Cammarosano

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Revenue -- $903 million in the quarter, up 18% sequentially and 22% year over year, driven by higher average metal prices, increased by-product contribution, and mining performance.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $300 million for the quarter, representing a 33% margin and showing growth quarter over quarter and year over year.
  • Net Income -- $81 million for the quarter, or $0.38 per share, reflecting improved operational execution and pricing.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $51 million generated in the quarter; negative $105 million for the full year, including debt reduction and dividend payments.
  • Net Leverage -- Improved to 1.7x from 2.2x, supported by higher trailing EBITDA and net debt reduction.
  • Quarterly Zinc Production -- 91,000 tons, a 9% increase from the previous quarter, with Aripuanã reporting its highest output to date.
  • Full Year Zinc Production -- 316,000 tons, meeting consolidated mining guidance.
  • Mining Segment EBITDA Margin -- 50% for the quarter, driven by higher metal prices and improved execution.
  • Mining Cash Cost (net of by-products) -- Negative $0.58 per pound in the quarter; -$0.30 per pound for the full year, both beating guidance.
  • Smelting Segment Metal Sales -- 142,000 tons in the quarter and 567,000 tons for the full year, at the midpoint of guidance.
  • Smelting Cash Cost -- $1.41 per pound for the quarter; $1.28 per pound for the year, in line with guidance.
  • Smelting EBITDA Margin -- 6% for the year, reflecting challenging market and operational constraints.
  • CapEx (Full Year) -- $352 million, slightly above guidance due to Brazilian real appreciation, with $125 million in the quarter.
  • Cerro Pasco Integration Project CapEx -- $12 million in the quarter, $42 million for the full year, below the initial $44 million plan.
  • Liquidity -- $842 million at quarter-end, including a $320 million undrawn sustainability-linked revolving credit facility.
  • Debt Maturity Profile -- Average debt maturity extended to 7.6 years with an average cost of 6.49%.
  • Silver Streaming Agreement -- From Q2 2026, the committed delivery percentage drops from 65% to 25%, increasing Nexa Resources S.A.'s exposure to silver prices.
  • Hedging Program -- A small portion of silver production in Peru hedged at a floor of around $52 and a cap of around $84.
  • ESG Performance -- CDP rating maintained at B for climate change and water security; new ESG targets to be disclosed in 2026 with third-party verification.

SUMMARY

Nexa Resources S.A. (NEXA +1.80%) achieved its highest quarterly operational and financial performance of the year, marked by strong revenue and EBITDA growth, and clear progress in mining cost control. Aripuanã reached record output, and the installation of the fourth tailings filter is on schedule, targeting full operational capacity in the second half of 2026. The step-down of the Cerro Lindo silver streaming agreement beginning in the next quarter will directly enhance Nexa Resources S.A.'s realized silver price exposure and EBITDA leverage. The average debt maturity profile now stands at 7.6 years, and liquidity covers all financial commitments for the next five years, supporting further deleveraging. ESG governance advanced, with detailed recalibrated targets set for disclosure in 2026, anchored in third-party verification and methodological consistency.

  • The company emphasized that full operational stability at Aripuanã during the rainy season marks a shift compared to previous years’ weather-driven throughput constraints.
  • Management confirmed no operational or logistics disruptions from recent El Niño rainfall in Peru, stating, "production hasn't been impacted."
  • Cerro Pasco Integration Project Phase 1 execution remains on track, with annual CapEx aligned to prior year levels; Phase 2 planning is underway with focus on resource drilling and future mine plan optimization.
  • The company stated it is not prioritizing additional silver streaming transactions currently: José Carlos del Valle said, "It's not something that's a high priority right now."
  • Brownfield exploration at key mines continues to drive mineralized extensions, supporting future life-of-mine increases.
  • Dividend payments of $48 million were distributed, coupled with continued early debt repayments and bond refinancings to strengthen the capital structure.
  • No timeline has been set for accessing high-grade reserves in Phase 2 of the Cerro Pasco project; additional reserve inventory analysis is expected within one to two years.
  • Ayawilca project actions remain on hold pending further regulatory and governmental dialogue following the disapproval of the project's environmental impact study.
  • Management indicated that further long-term investments may focus selectively on copper asset opportunities as debt is reduced and cash flow generation stabilizes.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Silver Streaming Agreement: A contract requiring Nexa Resources S.A. to deliver a fixed portion of silver production at a predetermined price, typically in exchange for upfront financing.
  • CDP Rating: An external environmental performance assessment published by CDP (formerly Carbon Disclosure Project), widely referenced in ESG benchmarking.
  • Aripuanã: Nexa Resources S.A.'s polymetallic mining operation in Mato Grosso, Brazil, central to current production ramp-up.
  • Cerro Pasco Integration Project: Asset integration and mine life extension initiative linking the Cerro de Pasco and Atacocha sites in Peru.
