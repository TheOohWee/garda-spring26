QuantaSing (QSG) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Sep 3, 2025 at 11:57AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, November 27, 2024 at 7 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chief Executive Officer — Peng Li

Chief Financial Officer — Tim Xie

Investor Relations Director — Leah Guo

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

Total Revenue-- Total revenues (GAAP) were RMB810.4 million for the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2025, representing a 6.8% year-over-year decrease linked to the strategic focus on the silver economy market.

Individual Online Learning Services Revenue-- RMB709 million, accounting for 87.5% of total revenue.

Enterprise Services Revenue-- RMB47.8 million, a change of 30.2% from a year ago and now comprising 5.9% of total revenue, reflecting a shift away from non-core activities.

Gross Margin-- Gross margin was 83.4%, down from 86.4% in Q1 FY2024, attributed to the change in business mix toward product-focused offerings with higher associated costs.

Total Operating Expenses-- Total operating expenses were RMB573.7 million, down 18.8% year-over-year, as a result of disciplined cost management during the business transition.

Sales and Marketing Expenses-- Sales and marketing expenses were RMB550 million, a 17% reduction from the same period last year.

Net Income-- Net income (GAAP) was RMB80.7 million, with a 10% net margin, demonstrating ongoing profitability (GAAP and non-GAAP) despite lower revenues.

Adjusted Net Income-- Adjusted net income was RMB88 million, resulting in a 10.9% adjusted net margin.

Cash and Short-Term Investments-- Cash and short-term investments totaled RMB1,193.7 million as of September 30, 2024, up RMB167.4 million from the previous quarter, supporting operational flexibility.

Private Label Gross Merchandise Value (GMV)-- Private label GMV reached RMB15 million, highlighting initial progress in the private label strategy.

Registered User Base-- The registered user base reached 134 million and a reported customer satisfaction rate of 98.5%, providing a strong foundation for continued product expansion.

Dividend-- Special dividend announced in October, supported by growth in cash resources and positive operating cash flow.

Research and Development Expense-- Research and development expense was RMB28.1 million, a 35.9% year-over-year decrease, reflecting focus on targeted product development.

Gross Billings (Individual Online Learning)-- Gross billings from individual online learning services were RMB713.7 million, a 6.3% year-over-year decline.

SUMMARY

As the company continues a planned transition toward serving China's growing silver economy, it maintains profitability and strengthened cash reserves throughout the quarter. Management highlighted a significant shift in business mix, with private label wellness, food, and medicine products being integrated with educational offerings and offline events to foster deeper engagement among users over 50. Cost reductions across all major operating categories contributed to sustained net and adjusted profitability, enabling both increased cash flow and a special dividend to shareholders.

CFO Xie said, "we have discontinued providing specific guidance during this transition period," indicating a pause in forward-looking financial projections while the business model evolves.

CEO Li described the offline service pilot in Beijing's six urban districts, designed to refine operational models before wider expansion.

Leadership attributed the reduction in gross margin in Q1 FY2025 to the shift toward more product-focused offerings within the silver economy market.

Management cited government policy initiatives to support the silver economy as a potential long-term demand driver for the company's evolving service offerings.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Silver Economy: The economic ecosystem focused on products and services for the aging population, especially individuals aged 50 and above.

Gross Merchandise Value (GMV): The total value of merchandise sold over a specific period through the company's platforms, used here to quantify the scale of private label product activity.
