Ecopetrol (EC) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 18, 2026 at 12:11PM EST

Wednesday, August 13, 2025 at 10:00 a.m. ET

TAKEAWAYS

  • Hydrocarbon Production -- Semester average of 751,000 barrels of oil equivalent per day, the highest level in a decade, driven by contributions from Colombia (Caño Sur and CPO-09) and the Permian Basin in the United States.
  • Lorito Discovery -- Declared commercially viable; estimated at approximately 250 million barrels of recoverable resources, with 109 million barrels classified as certified contingent resources after the 45% acquisition in block CPO-09.
  • Commercial Viability in Brazil -- The Orca discovery (formerly Gato do Mato), with proven reserves incorporation beginning in 2025, following recognition by the Brazilian National Agency of Petroleum and Biofuels.
  • Investment Execution -- Year-to-date investments of $2.58 billion, with 62% deployed in Colombia, 17% in Brazil, and 21% in the United States and other countries.
  • Operational Efficiencies -- Captured COP 2.2 trillion in efficiencies, exceeding the semester target by 27%, and achieving COP 1.5 trillion in hydrocarbon segment optimizations, mainly through cost reductions and margin improvements.
  • EBITDA Margin -- Achieved 40% in the first half, surpassing the annual target of 39%, with exploration and production accounting for 54% of total EBITDA and electric transmission (ISA) contributing 17%.
  • Net Income -- Reported COP 4.9 trillion, a decline of COP 2.5 trillion year over year, attributed primarily to a 22% drop in Brent crude prices, increased blockades, new taxes, and the RBSE adjustment in Brazil.
  • Debt Metrics -- Gross debt-to-EBITDA ratio stood at 2.4x (1.7x excluding ISA); net debt-to-EBITDA at 2.2x (1.6x excluding ISA); no increase in debt expected for organic activities in 2025.
  • Dividend Distribution -- Full payment of dividends completed, delivering a 10% return to shareholders; payout ratio at 59%, within the 40%-60% policy range.
  • Renewable Energy Progress -- Accumulated 630 megawatts in renewable energy portfolio, with 208 megawatts operational, and on track to achieve a 900-megawatt target by year-end, supported by the acquisition of 100% of the Windpeshi wind project.
  • Downstream Segment -- EBITDA increased 53% versus the same period in 2024, with throughput recovery to 413,000 barrels per day in Q2 and integrated gross refining margin rising 37% year over year to $12.5 per barrel.
  • Social and Sustainability Investments -- COP 180 billion allocated to sustainable territorial development and COP 43 billion invested in Liu of Taxes initiatives, benefiting roughly 350,000 people.

RISKS

  • Brent crude price fell 22%, materially reducing revenue and profits. Management stated, "The solid operational progress during the quarter was partially offset by the decline of the crude price. Brent fell by 22% compared to the second quarter of 2024, impacting both revenue and profits."
  • Net income dropped by COP 2.5 trillion, "represented mainly by the following external factors -- market factors," including commodity price decline, increased blockades, new taxes, and regulatory adjustment to ISA's financial formula in Brazil (RBSE), which resulted in a COP 0.1 trillion impact on net income and a COP 0.6 trillion impact on EBITDA.
  • Transported midstream volumes decreased by 6% compared to second quarter 2024 and by 4% over the prior year’s first half due to increased external events such as blockades, attacks, and lower third-party production.
  • The cost per barrel transported and refining cash cost rose by 2% and 3%, respectively, driven by lower transported volumes and reduced throughputs, partially offset by efficiency gains.

SUMMARY

Ecopetrol S.A. (EC +1.37%) reported record semester oil and gas production and advanced key upstream and renewable energy projects, while market volatility and regulatory changes pressured financial results. The company confirmed commerciality of major discoveries in Colombia and Brazil, integrating new resources and initiating development planning. Cash flow remained positive, supported by accelerated efficiency measures, disciplined capital allocation, and conservative debt management, maintaining investment-grade financial ratios despite lower net income.

  • Management stated there were no imminent changes to the dividend policy, with flexibility to adjust capital spending and payouts if oil prices approach the $50 per barrel breakeven. They emphasized, "our focus is on managing higher efficiencies" and prioritizing "production and reserves."
  • Investments in clean energy reached $300 million to $400 million for the year, including the Windpeshi wind farm and additional partnerships, with savings from renewables contributing over COP 70 billion to cost reductions.
  • The FEPC balance decreased to COP 2.5 trillion, with expected stabilization near COP 5 trillion by year-end and no further regulated fuel price adjustments anticipated for 2025.
  • The company maintained full-year production guidance of 740,000-750,000 barrels of oil equivalent per day, with projects in execution and incremental output from Colombian developments and the Permian Basin.
  • No major refinery maintenance shutdowns are scheduled for the second half, and operational reliability projects in Cartagena remain underway, aiming to "reach a moderate risk level because of the interventions made in this refinery."
  • On value-added tax disputes for gasoline and diesel imports, Ecopetrol and Reficar are compliant with authorities but do not recognize any obligations for prior periods; approximately COP 3.3 trillion of new VAT payments in 2025 are expected to be recoverable.
  • Asset portfolio reviews and M&A activity will prioritize "assets where we have little growth opportunities" and reallocating capital to higher-return and strategic ventures.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RBSE: Remuneração Base dos Serviços Existentes; regulatory mechanism in Brazil affecting remuneration for transmission assets in the basic system network, subject to formula adjustments with direct EBITDA and cash flow impacts.
  • FEPC: Fuel Price Stabilization Fund; Colombian government mechanism to manage retail fuel price volatility, with balances and accruals directly affecting company cash flows.
  • Lifting cost: The average cost incurred to produce a barrel of oil or oil equivalent, excluding finding and development expenses—an essential metric for operational efficiency.
  • Contingent resources: Estimated quantities of oil and gas potentially recoverable from known accumulations, but not yet classified as proved reserves due to one or more contingencies.
  • Workover: Operations to restore or enhance production from an existing oil or gas well, often reflected in efficiency metrics.
  • Throughput: Volume of crude oil or feedstock processed by a refinery over a specified period.
