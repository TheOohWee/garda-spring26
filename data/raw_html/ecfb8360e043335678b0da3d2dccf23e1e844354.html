TG Therapeutics TGTX Q3 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 5:53PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Nov. 3, 2025 at 11:56 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Michael Weiss
  • Chief Commercial Officer — Adam Waldman
  • Chief Financial Officer — Sean Power

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • U.S. BRIUMVI net sales -- $152.9 million, reflecting both sequential and year-over-year growth for the quarter.
  • Total revenue -- $161.7 million, representing a 93% year-over-year increase and 15% sequential rise from fiscal Q2 2025 (period ended June 30, 2025).
  • Product revenue -- $159.3 million, mainly driven by BRIUMVI sales, with ex-U.S. contributing $6.4 million through collaboration with Neuraxpharm.
  • Operating expenses -- $86.6 million, up from approximately $71 million in fiscal Q2 2025 due to increased R&D and commercial spend, but on track to meet full-year guidance of $300 million-$320 million.
  • Share repurchase -- 3.5 million shares reacquired for $100 million at an average price of about $28.50; an additional $100 million authorization granted by the Board.
  • Cash, equivalents, and investment securities -- $178 million at quarter-end, supporting operational and pipeline flexibility.
  • Net income (GAAP) -- $390.9 million, or $2.43 per diluted share, including a one-time $365 million income tax benefit from releasing a deferred tax asset valuation allowance.
  • Updated 2025 U.S. BRIUMVI net revenue guidance -- Raised from $570 million to $575 million to now approximately $585 million, reflecting increased demand and execution.
  • BRIUMVI clinical data -- ULTIMATE I and II open-label extension: nearly 90% of patients remain free from disability progression after 6 years; sixth-year annualized relapse rate at 0.012 (1 relapse every 83 years).
  • Persistence and repeat prescribing -- Both cited as exceeding management expectations, further driving growth.
  • BRIUMVI market position -- Described as the preferred anti-CD20 therapy in academic, community, and VA settings.
  • ENHANCE trial (single infusion schedule) -- Enrollment completed; top-line data expected by mid-2026, with potential simplified regimen launch targeted in 2027.
  • Subcutaneous ublituximab phase III trial -- Enrollment on target for completion in first half 2026; top-line pivotal data anticipated late 2026 or early 2027; a launch in 2028 may follow if results are positive.
  • Subcutaneous market opportunity -- Management estimates subcutaneous formulations could nearly double BRIUMVI’s addressable market, citing market share for self-administered CD20 therapies at approximately 35%-40% and growing.
  • Direct-to-consumer (DTC) marketing -- National TV, digital, and social campaigns debuting in Q3 elevated branded search, digital engagement, and patient brand awareness above pre-campaign baselines, with ongoing investment in Q4 planned.
  • Profitability -- Achieved a sixth consecutive quarter of profitability, attributed to BRIUMVI revenue growth and expense management.

Summary

TG Therapeutics (TGTX 2.60%) delivered strong commercial and financial performance this quarter, with U.S. BRIUMVI net sales and total revenue both showing substantial growth. The company completed its $100 million share buyback authorization and secured board approval for an additional $100 million program. The release of a $365 million deferred tax asset valuation allowance contributed materially to reported GAAP net income. New long-term clinical data from the ULTIMATE I and II studies underscored BRIUMVI’s sustained efficacy and safety profile over six years of treatment. Subcutaneous ublituximab development milestones, ongoing clinical trials, and management's focus on prudent capital allocation were highlighted as key elements shaping future growth beyond 2025.

  • Chief Commercial Officer Waldman said, “Persistence and repeat prescribing both exceeded expectations,” reflecting strong patient and physician engagement.
  • Direct-to-consumer campaign launch resulted in “Branded search activity, website traffic and quality website visits and overall brand -- a patient brand awareness are all elevated relative to pre-campaign baselines.”
  • Management views future pipeline launches as potential catalysts for new growth “in '27, '28 and '29.”
  • CEO Weiss stated, “We view subcutaneous ublituximab as a major opportunity that can nearly double the total addressable market for BRIUMVI.”
  • Chief Financial Officer Power noted, “This represents our sixth consecutive quarter of profitability, driven by BRIUMVI revenue growth and disciplined expense management.”
  • Field force expansion and increased R&D spend primarily drove the quarter’s higher operating expenses, according to management commentary.

Industry glossary

  • BRIUMVI: Intravenous anti-CD20 monoclonal antibody therapy developed for relapsing multiple sclerosis (MS).
  • ULTIMATE I and II trials: Long-term clinical studies evaluating BRIUMVI’s efficacy and safety in patients with relapsing forms of MS.
  • Subcutaneous (subcu) administration: Delivery of a therapeutic agent via injection under the skin, often allowing for patient self-administration.
  • Dynamic share: Market measurement focused on new patient starts or therapy switches within a class, rather than total market share.
  • Deferred tax asset valuation allowance: An accounting reserve against potential future tax benefits, released when realization of those tax assets becomes probable.
  • Direct-to-consumer (DTC) marketing: Promotional strategies targeted directly at patients to drive treatment initiation and engagement.
  • ENHANCE trial: Study evaluating a simplified BRIUMVI dosing regimen combining initial day 1 and day 15 infusions into a single administration.
  • PI3K-delta: A key enzyme targeted in certain lymphoid malignancies by investigational therapies like Umbralisib.
  • CAR T therapy: Adoptive cell immunotherapy using engineered T-cells to target specific disease antigens.
