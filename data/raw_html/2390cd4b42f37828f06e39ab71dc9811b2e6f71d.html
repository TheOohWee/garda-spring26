Earnings Call Transcript

Castle Biosciences (CSTL) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 26, 2026 at 5:43PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 26, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Derek J. Maetzold
  • Chief Financial Officer — Frank Stokes

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total revenue -- $87 million for the quarter and $344.2 million for the year, with guidance for the next year at $340 million to $350 million.
  • Test report volume -- 105,053 reports from core drivers for the year, up 37%, with TissueCypher volumes increasing 86% to 39,014.
  • DecisionDx-Melanoma volume -- 39,083 test reports with 9% growth and 1,795 first-time ordering clinicians added during the year.
  • Patient penetration -- DecisionDx-Melanoma reached 31% and TissueCypher 11% penetration by year-end.
  • Adjusted gross margin -- 77.6% for the quarter and 79.8% for the year, compared to 81.2% in the prior year periods.
  • Net results -- Net loss of $2.3 million for the quarter and $24.2 million for the year, versus net income of $9.6 million and $18.2 million in the previous year, respectively.
  • Free cash flow -- Generated $28.3 million for the year; capital expenditures were $36 million.
  • Cash position -- $299.5 million in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities as of year-end.
  • Advanced ADTX test launch -- Limited release initiated in late November, over 500 orders in the initial five weeks across more than half of 150 targeted dermatology accounts.
  • M&A and pipeline initiatives -- Acquisition of ProVise, collaboration with Cybase for EIS technology, and plans for new GI disease tests and FDA device submissions in the coming year.
  • Guideline recognition -- The American Gastroenterological Association highlighted TissueCypher as the first prognostic test for Barrett’s esophagus risk; DecisionDx-Melanoma’s published clinical data were only partially cited by NCCN in new guidelines.
  • Sales and marketing expenses -- $138.1 million for the year, increased by personnel and activity spend, with salesforces in both dermatology and GI remaining below 100 representatives each.

RISKS

  • Gross margin decline -- Gross margin decreased to 69.2% for the year from 78.5%, impacted by "loss of revenue from DecisionDx-SCC and the one-time adjustment of an acceleration of amortization of approximately $20.1 million."
  • Return to net loss -- Net loss of $24.2 million for the year after net income of $18.2 million the prior year; diluted loss per share was $0.83 compared to earnings per share of $0.62 previously.
  • No revenue from DecisionDx-SCC -- No revenue included for DecisionDx-SCC in guidance and no timeline certainty for its reimbursement or commercial contribution.
  • Advanced ADTX ramp -- Revenue from the advanced ADTX test expected to be immaterial in the coming year, with larger impact not expected until at least 2027 or 2028.

SUMMARY

Castle Biosciences (CSTL 3.88%) delivered substantial year-over-year core test volume growth, particularly in TissueCypher, while posting a net loss for the year as gross margins contracted and several headwinds emerged. The company launched its advanced ADTX test for atopic dermatitis in late November with strong initial clinician engagement but disclosed that revenues from this new product will be insignificant in the near term. Management outlined a multi-phase strategy that includes an expanded commercial footprint, the integration of the ProVise acquisition and Cybase collaboration, and plans for an FDA submission to support commercial reimbursement opportunities for DecisionDx-Melanoma. Guidance for next year anticipates steady revenue growth concentrated in core franchises, with M&A expected to remain a tool for portfolio expansion. Castle Biosciences maintains a significant cash reserve and disciplined capital allocation to support ongoing product and pipeline development activities.

  • Frank Stokes emphasized, "Our revenue guide reflects continued growth in our core dermatologic and gastrointestinal franchises, driven primarily by continued TissueCypher momentum, immaterial revenue expected from our advanced ADTX test, and no revenue included for DecisionDx-SCC."
  • Derek J. Maetzold said, "Our compelling body of evidence for DecisionDx-Melanoma also continues to expand, with now 58 peer-reviewed publications supporting the clinical use of the test, which is not only a key driver for test adoption."
  • TissueCypher’s peer-recognized value as the first prognostic test for Barrett’s esophagus progression risk supports ongoing ordering momentum and reinforces its strategic position in the GI portfolio.
  • Annual operating expenses rose by $63.6 million to $387 million, with increased headcount and commercial investments, offset only partly by reductions in stock-based compensation and lower clinical studies cost in R&D.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • DecisionDx-Melanoma: A multi-gene expression test predicting metastasis risk and guiding sentinel lymph node biopsy decisions in melanoma patients.
  • TissueCypher: A molecular test to assess progression risk in Barrett’s esophagus, informing interventions to prevent esophageal cancer.
  • Advanced ADTX: A gene expression-based diagnostic launched for guiding systemic treatment in moderate to severe atopic dermatitis patients aged twelve and older.
  • EIS (Electrical Impedance Spectroscopy): Technology under evaluation in collaboration with Cybase, used for predicting atopic dermatitis flares via a handheld device.
  • LCD (Local Coverage Determination): A regional reimbursement policy from Medicare Administrative Contractors impacting test coverage and commercial availability.
  • Merlin-001 Study: Referenced clinical study evaluating tests used for sentinel lymph node biopsy decision-making in melanoma.
  • SEER Database: The National Cancer Institute’s Surveillance, Epidemiology, and End Results database used to compare outcomes for patients tested with DecisionDx-Melanoma.
  • ASP (Average Selling Price): The average reimbursement or price received per test, referenced as a key driver for commercial expansion decisions.
