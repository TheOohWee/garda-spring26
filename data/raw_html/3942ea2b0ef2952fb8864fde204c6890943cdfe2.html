FNB (FNB) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 19, 2026 at 9:19AM EST

DATE

Friday, October 18, 2024 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Vincent J. Delie, Jr.
  • Chief Financial Officer — Vincent J. Calabrese
  • Chief Credit Officer — Gary L. Guerrieri

TAKEAWAYS

  • Operating Net Income -- $122 million, or $0.34 per diluted common share, was reported after adjusting for $15 million in significant items including an $11.6 million loss on the indirect auto loan sale and a $3.7 million software impairment.
  • Loan Sale Impact -- A $431 million performing lower-yielding indirect auto loan sale improved the loan-to-deposit ratio by 120 basis points and the CET1 capital ratio by 10 basis points.
  • Total Loans -- Period-end balance reached $33.7 billion, with $391.4 million, or 1.2% linked quarter growth, excluding the loan sale.
  • Total Deposits -- Ended at $36.8 billion, rising by $1.8 billion, or 5.1% sequentially, driven by new production and seasonal inflows.
  • Non-Interest-Bearing Deposits -- Remained stable near $10 billion, comprising 27% of total deposits versus 29% in the prior quarter.
  • Non-Interest Income -- Reached an all-time high of $89.7 million, up 2% sequentially, led by a $1.1 million increase in capital markets income and partially offset by a $1.4 million decline in mortgage banking operations due to a $2.8 million MSR impairment, with life insurance claims adding $3.1 million.
  • Net Interest Income -- Rose to $323.3 million, up $7.4 million or 2.4% sequentially, benefiting from an 8 basis point increase in earning asset yields and a reduction of $1.6 billion, or 28%, in total borrowings.
  • Net Interest Margin -- Held stable at 3.08% for the quarter.
  • Operating Non-Interest Expense -- Totaled $234.2 million, increasing $8.4 million from the prior quarter, mainly from a $5.1 million rise in salaries and benefits and a $2 million increase in marketing.
  • Efficiency Ratio -- Reported at 55.2%, up from 54.4% last quarter, described as “peer-leading.”
  • Tangible Book Value Per Share -- Rose $1.31, or 15%, to $10.33 year over year.
  • CET1 Capital Ratio -- Achieved a record 10.4% at quarter-end.
  • Credit Metrics -- Total delinquency stood at 79 basis points (up 15 bps sequentially), NPLs and OREO at 39 bps (up 6 bps), net charge-offs at 25 bps (YTD 17 bps), criticized loans down 22 bps, and funded reserve at $420 million or 1.25% of total loans (up 1 bp).
  • NPL Coverage -- Reached 352% when including unamortized loan discounts.
  • Loan-to-Deposit Ratio -- Improved to 91.7%, nearly 5 percentage points lower than the prior quarter’s 96.5%.
  • Guidance: Loan and Deposit Growth -- Full-year loans expected to grow mid-single-digits including loan sales; projected year-over-year deposit growth increased to mid-single-digits from prior low-single-digit outlook.
  • Guidance: Fourth Quarter -- Non-interest income targeted between $85 million and $90 million, non-interest expense between $225 million and $235 million, and provision expected within $20 million to $30 million range.
  • Deposit Pricing Actions -- Management reported reducing deposit rates by 50 basis points on several products after Fed’s 50 basis point cut in September, with $7 billion in non-maturity deposits currently priced at or above 4.25%.
  • Deposit Acquisition -- $1 billion in new deposits were sourced at an average rate of 4.25%, mainly in short-term money market and five-month CDs.
  • Asset Sensitivity -- Management noted the balance sheet remains asset sensitive but is moving toward neutral; 47.5% of loans reprice within three months.
  • Capital Markets Performance -- Reported a record year for debt capital markets income and active pipeline for new commercial deposit accounts.
  • Non-Owner CRE Portfolio Metrics -- Delinquency at 69 bps and NPLs at 31 bps, with the NPL increase attributed to a single credit moved to nonaccrual.

SUMMARY

Management emphasized the tactical actions taken to reshape the balance sheet, including the indirect auto loan sale and aggressive deposit gathering, without sacrificing credit quality or long-term growth positioning. Executives detailed their deliberate approach to managing liquidity, capital, and pricing in response to the shifting interest rate environment, noting the flexibility to reprice $11 billion of liabilities and $5.4 billion in CDs within six months. Strategic initiatives, including expanded capital markets capabilities, new commercial deposit pipelines, and ongoing risk management system investments, were presented as integral to navigating both present economic conditions and future Fed policy changes. Management affirmed that M&A would only be pursued where deals are immediately accretive to earnings and do not cause material tangible book value dilution, explicitly stating, we're going to stay true to our strategy.

  • Leadership noted expanding market share in nearly 90% of its MSAs and attaining a top-five deposit share in about half of its operating markets.
  • All the things we've said that are embedded in our business model have proved out in this quarter. according to Delie, referencing deposit and loan growth success despite industry skepticism toward regional banks.
  • Management expects the efficiency ratio to remain favorable, aided by anticipated cost savings and operating improvements from headquarters consolidation and operational hires specializing in process engineering and AI-driven efficiency.
  • Deposit pricing actions taken in response to recent Fed moves are expected to contribute to a targeted 15% deposit beta by year end, with room for upside through further repricing as competitors also lower rates.
  • Loan growth pacing may remain stable going into 2025, with management attributing current tepid demand to pre-election uncertainty and clients waiting for further rate cuts, but expressing confidence in the company’s future growth potential once economic conditions shift.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • MSA (Metropolitan Statistical Area): Defined geographic region used in banking to measure deposit market share and expansion success.
  • Indirect Auto Loan: Loan originated through a dealership or third party, rather than directly with the borrower, often held in portfolios for asset yield management.
  • NPL (Non-Performing Loan): A loan on which the borrower is not making interest payments or repaying principal, typically over 90 days past due.
  • OREO (Other Real Estate Owned): Foreclosed property held on a bank’s balance sheet pending disposal or sale.
  • MSR (Mortgage Servicing Right): The contractual right to service a mortgage loan for a fee, including collecting payments and managing escrows.
  • Loan Beta / Deposit Beta: Measures the degree to which loan or deposit rates adjust in response to changes in benchmark interest rates, such as the federal funds rate.
