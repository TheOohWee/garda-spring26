Spotify (SPOT) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 4, 2025 at 7:01PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, July 29, 2025 at 12:00 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chief Executive Officer — Daniel G. Ek

Co-President & Chief Business Officer — Alex Norström

Co-President & Chief Product and Technology Officer — Gustav Söderström

Chief Financial Officer — Christian Luiga

Vice President, Head of Investor Relations — Bryan Daniel Goldberg

RISKS

Advertising growth lagged expectations: CEO Daniel G. Ek stated, "the one area that hasn't yet better expectations is our Ads business. We've simply been moving too slowly and it's taken longer than expected to see the improvements we initiated to take hold."

Operating income was below guidance: CFO Christian Luiga reported, "Our operating income of EUR 406 million was EUR 133 million below guidance, primarily due to social charges in the quarter, which were EUR 98 million above forecast due to share price appreciation.

Q3 revenue outlook impacted by currency headwind: Luiga noted, "Our revenue outlook incorporates a sizable headwind of approximately EUR 200 million due to unfavorable currency movements over the last quarter."

TAKEAWAYS

Monthly Active Users (MAU): Reached 696 million monthly active users, an increase of 18 million in Q2 2025, exceeding guidance by 7 million.

Paid Subscribers: Rose to 276 million subscribers, adding 8 million net subscribers, up 12% year-on-year and 3 million above guidance.

Premium Revenue: Increased 16% year-on-year on a constant currency basis, driven by subscriber gains and higher ARPU from price increases.

Advertising Revenue: Grew 5% year-on-year on a constant currency basis, with automated sales channels as the largest contributor.

Excluding podcast and Partner program effects: Advertising saw low double-digit year-on-year growth in constant currency.

Gross Margin: Expanded to 31.5%, about 230 basis points higher year-on-year, in line with guidance.

Free Cash Flow: Achieved EUR 700 million free cash flow, resulting from improved operating income and net working capital.

Cash and Short-term Investments: Ended the quarter at EUR 8.4 billion.

Q3 Forecast: Targeting 710 million MAU, 281 million subscribers, EUR 4.2 billion revenue, 31.1% gross margin, and EUR 485 million operating income.

Programmatic Ads and Automation: Management highlighted, The programmatic business, which I think will set us up nicely for 2026.

Share Repurchase Program: Board increased total authorization to $2 billion, with approximately $1.9 billion unused prior to the quarter.

Global Audio Music Stream Share: 65% of global audio music streams are on Spotify, per Luminate.

Video Podcast Engagement: More than 430,000 video podcasts are now available; consumption is "growing 20x faster than audio-only consumption since 2024."

Video Podcast User Growth: Over 350 million users have streamed video podcasts on Spotify

DJ Engagement: User engagement with DJ has nearly doubled over the last year.

Monthly Active Advertisers: Monthly active advertisers grew by more than 40% year-over-year.

Market Share Among Paid Streamers: Holds a 45% subscriber share in regions excluding China and Russia, according to media research.

Audiobook Add-On Subscription: Launched in 13 markets, expanding access to audiobooks and allowing family plan extension.

SUMMARY

Spotify Technology S.A. (SPOT 2.18%) reported robust user and subscriber growth, surpassing internal guidance, as both Premium and MAU figures rose significantly. Revenue growth was led by Premium segment gains. and supported by ARPU improvements attributed to prior price increases. Advertising revenue was hindered by lagging execution and portfolio changes, with management confirming slower-than-expected development and a key leadership transition in the Ads unit. Currency fluctuations exerted a notable negative impact, prompting a EUR 200 million forecasted revenue headwind. Strategic investments continued in generative AI product enhancements, automation of ad channels, and international expansion of content formats across video, audiobooks, and music, with management reaffirming focus on long-term margin expansion and deployment of a newly upsized $2 billion share repurchase authorization.

CFO Christian Luiga said, "Our operating income guidance reflects temporarily elevated growth in operating expenses in quarter 3 due to timing factors, and we remain well positioned to deliver year-on-year expansion in operating margin for the full year of 2025."

Management stated the multi-format strategy is performing, citing user engagement gains and "clear jumps in performance" following recent AI-powered product optimizations.

Co-President Alex Norström indicated long-term ambition for 10%-15% global population subscriber penetration, setting a new goal of "1 billion subs," while the current subscription rate stands at over 3% of the global population.

CEO Daniel G. Ek confirmed, it did positively impact our conversions, as seen in the quarter.

Management identified a la carte transactions as a third monetization pillar alongside advertising and subscriptions, facilitated by improvements in in-app payment capabilities.

The technology team detailed retooling for generative AI, describing a transition to "agentic infrastructure" and citing unlimited improvement potential of large language models with greater engagement data.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Programmatic Ads: Automated, technology-driven purchase and placement of advertising inventory in real-time marketplaces.

ARPU: Average Revenue Per User; a key metric reflecting revenue received per paid subscriber or user in a specified period.

SAC to LTV Ratio: A metric comparing subscriber acquisition cost (SAC) to estimated customer lifetime value (LTV) to guide marketing investment efficiency.

MCPs (Model Context Protocols): Internal term referring to wrappers or structures enabling agentic AI models to interact flexibly with Spotify’s APIs and infrastructure.

SPP Monetization: Revenue generation activities tied to Spotify’s Podcast Platform, including exclusive and open hosting and advertising models.

DMA: Digital Markets Act, a regulatory framework from the European Union impacting digital platforms’ business models and payment systems.

DMCC: Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Bill, a proposed UK regulation to enhance competition and consumer protection in digital markets.

BHAG: Big Hairy Audacious Goal; term used by management for setting ambitious strategic objectives such as the announced 1 billion subscriber target.
