Summary

LyondellBasell (LYB +2.01%) reported $1.2 billion in EBITDA and $1.88 earnings per share, with cash from operations reaching $670 million despite declining profitability from refining and oxyfuels. Management emphasized portfolio transformation via progress on the MoReTec-1 advanced recycling project, the completion of the APK acquisition, and advancement toward a staged refinery shutdown and repurposing in Houston. The European asset review is in progress, targeting strategic changes by 2025 to increase portfolio exposure to cost-advantaged operations. Integration of new low-carbon and recycling technologies positions the company to achieve its Circular & Low Carbon Solutions (CLCS) goal of $1 billion incremental EBITDA by 2030.

  • Executives confirmed that the refining business will be reported as discontinued operations from Q1 2025, with net cash benefits predicted for the year, and less than $50 million in annual ongoing site costs thereafter.
  • The O&P-Americas segment experienced both the quarter's strongest EBITDA since Q2 2022 and high plant utilization, while exposure to Hurricane Beryl was mitigated by merchant ethylene sales.
  • Management stated that EBITDA potential in the North American O&P segment remains unchanged, but the earnings power of European operations has declined due to higher energy costs and regulatory pressures.
  • In the Technology segment, Q3 licensing revenue shortfalls are expected to recover in Q4 as delayed customer milestones are completed.
  • Progress on the European asset review may lead to restructuring, asset sales, or other measures, with leadership anticipating significant clarity during 2025, but immediate outcomes are not expected.
  • Peter Vanacker highlighted that the company is leveraging feedstock flexibility and infrastructure to minimize investment requirements for new circular and renewable product lines in both Europe and North America.

Industry glossary

  • MoReTec: LyondellBasell's proprietary technology for advanced, catalytic chemical recycling of mixed plastic waste into cracker feedstocks for circular polymer production.
  • Olefins & Polyolefins (O&P): Company segments producing and marketing ethylene, propylene, polyethylene, and polypropylene products.
  • Crack Spread: The differential between the purchase price of crude oil and the selling price of refined petroleum products, indicating refinery margins.
  • CLCS (Circular & Low Carbon Solutions): LyondellBasell's strategic business unit focused on producing circular and renewable polymers and associated materials with lower carbon footprints.
  • PO/TBA: Propylene Oxide/Tertiary Butyl Alcohol assets, facilities that enable joint production of propylene oxide and tertiary butyl alcohol-based oxyfuels using butane feedstock.
  • Merchant Ethylene: Ethylene sold outside of integrated in-house demand, directly into the market.
  • Hydrotreaters: Refinery units designed to remove impurities from hydrocarbon feedstocks, relevant here for upgrading recycled and renewable feeds for use in crackers.
