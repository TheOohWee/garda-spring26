Vertiv (VRT) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 12:09PM EST

  • Executive Chairman — David M. Cote
  • Chief Executive Officer — Giordano Albertazzi
  • Chief Financial Officer — Craig Chamberlain

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Organic fiscal Q4 orders growth -- Up 152% year over year and up 117% sequentially, reflecting broad demand strength across all regions and markets.
  • Book-to-bill ratio -- 2.9 times, driven by significant fiscal Q4 order intake, with backlog reaching $15 billion, more than double the prior year and up 57% sequentially.
  • Fiscal Q4 organic net sales -- Increased 19%, led by the Americas segment growing 46% organically, while APAC declined 9% and EMEA declined 14%.
  • Fiscal Q4 adjusted operating margin -- 23.2%, up 170 basis points from the prior year, with adjusted operating profit at $668 million, a 33% increase.
  • Fiscal Q4 adjusted diluted EPS -- $1.36, a 37% increase from fiscal Q4 2024 and $0.10 above prior guidance.
  • Adjusted free cash flow (fiscal full year ended Dec. 31, 2025) -- Approximately $1.9 billion, with an adjusted free cash flow conversion rate of 115%.
  • 2026 adjusted diluted EPS guidance -- $6.02 on projected organic sales growth of 28% and adjusted operating margin of 22.5%.
  • The Americas segment sales -- Up 50% in fiscal Q4 with adjusted operating profit of $568 million, an increase of 77%, and margin rate rising by 450 basis points.
  • APAC and EMEA segment results -- APAC fiscal Q4 sales declined 10%, operating margin down 270 basis points to 9.9%; EMEA sales declined 8%, margin down to 22.1% from 26.6% prior year.
  • Life cycle services orders growth -- Exceeded 25% year over year, evidencing growth in Vertiv Holdings Co (VRT +2.46%)’s services portfolio.
  • Capital expenditures (CapEx) -- Planned increase to 3%-4% of sales for 2026, up from the historical 2%-3% range, supporting expansion initiatives and growth trajectory.
  • PerchRight acquisition -- Strengthens Vertiv Holdings Co's fluid management capabilities, targeting chilled water and liquid-cooled AI data centers, and expected to scale globally through the services network.
  • Adjusted operating profit (fiscal full year 2025) -- $2.1 billion, up 35%, and $30 million above guidance, driving a margin expansion of 100 basis points to 20.4%.
  • 2026 cash flow and investment outlook -- Adjusted free cash flow forecast at $2.2 billion, up 17%, with continued investments in capacity, technology, and higher CapEx offsetting increased tax expense.
  • Discontinuation of quarterly order and backlog disclosure -- Management announced future earnings will no longer report actual orders, forecast, or backlog to avoid excessive volatility; full-year disclosure to remain in the 10-K and market commentary on calls will continue.

Summary

Vertiv Holdings Co (VRT +2.46%) reported trailing twelve-month organic orders growth of 81%, with fiscal fourth quarter orders up over 250% and a backlog of $15 billion extending deliveries into a twelve-to-eighteen-month window. Management highlighted the rapid adoption of prefabricated solutions such as OneCore and SmartRun, which are enabling larger, system-level orders and accelerating customer project timelines. The PerchRight acquisition has expanded the company’s value proposition in fluid management for advanced data center environments. Margins expanded due to profitability and price-cost execution, despite regional disparities, and operating leverage is expected to contribute further to results in 2026. Management is increasing CapEx to support growth while expressing confidence in pipeline visibility and active capacity ramp, with binding, cash-advancing orders forming the basis of reported backlog.

  • CEO Albertazzi said, "The momentum is certainly very strong," and described the backlog as "more elongated into the twelve to eighteen-month window," providing significant sales coverage into 2026 and 2027.
  • Management confirmed pipeline growth remains robust even after substantial order intake, with no evidence of depletion, and reiterated that backlog represents legally binding purchase orders, often with advance payments.
  • The Americas continues to be the company’s primary growth driver, with guidance for high 30% growth, while APAC is guided for mid-20% growth, and EMEA expected to be flat to down mid-single digits in 2026.
  • Vertiv Holdings Co highlighted ongoing capacity expansion through both facility footprint and productivity measures, with several new locations coming online to meet the increasing demand.
  • Large orders driving the current backlog are not attributed to "anything unusual" in pricing or incentives, but are the result of sustained market demand and strengthening customer trust for system-level solutions.
  • Pricing remains favorable, as stated explicitly by management: "2025 pricing exceeded inflation, and we expect the same in 2026."
  • The company is shifting disclosure strategy, with a commitment to avoid reporting quarterly orders and backlog, citing excessive volatility and limited investor benefit.

Industry glossary

  • Book-to-bill ratio: The ratio of orders received to units shipped and billed; a ratio above 1.0 indicates demand outpacing delivery.
  • OneCore: Vertiv Holdings Co’s end-to-end, prefabricated data center solution designed to scale rapidly with gigawatt-level capacity in modular blocks.
  • SmartRun: A prefabricated white space infrastructure solution from Vertiv Holdings Co that accelerates data center fit-out and readiness for multi-generation silicon deployments.
  • PerchRight: Recently acquired Vertiv Holdings Co business providing advanced fluid management capabilities for data center cooling, especially in liquid-cooled environments.
  • CDU (Coolant Distribution Unit): A device used in liquid-cooled data centers to control and distribute coolant to IT equipment racks for heat management.
