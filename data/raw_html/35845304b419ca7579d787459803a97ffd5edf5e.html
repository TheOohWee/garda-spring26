Payoneer (PAYO) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 26, 2026 at 3:37PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 26, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — John Caplan
  • Chief Financial Officer — Bea Ordonez

TAKEAWAYS

  • Processed Volume -- $87 billion processed in 2025 across 190 countries and territories.
  • Revenue ex-Interest Income -- $900 million to $940 million guidance for 2026, reflecting a projected 12% midpoint growth, with a 300 basis point reduction attributed to checkout business optimization and customer portfolio changes.
  • 2025 Revenue ex-Interest -- 14% growth, with B2B revenue up 28% and B2B now comprising 30% of revenue ex-interest, up from 20% in 2023.
  • Adjusted EBITDA ex-Interest -- Increased from negative $25 million in 2023 to positive $40 million in 2025; 2026 guidance midpoint is $90 million, expecting more than a 2x increase year over year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (Total) -- $272 million for 2025, representing a 26% margin and up year over year despite a $25 million interest income headwind.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $146 million generated in 2025, representing nearly 200% free cash flow conversion.
  • Share Repurchases -- $175 million repurchased in 2025, including $80 million in Q4; $192 million authorization remains at year-end 2025, with plans to continue buybacks at similar levels.
  • Customer Cohort Profitability -- All customer cohorts now profitable; ideal customer profile base grew 8% and associated ARPU increased over 50% since 2023.
  • Customer Funds -- $7.9 billion held at year-end 2025, up 13% year over year, outpacing processed volume growth.
  • Interest Income Hedging -- Secured $130 million for 2026, $110 million for 2027, and $90 million for 2028 through hedging, covering roughly 51% of customer funds at year-end 2025.
  • Fourth-Quarter Revenue -- $275 million with 9% increase in revenue ex-interest, driven largely by B2B and pricing strategies.
  • ARPU Performance -- Q4 2025 ARPU excluding interest income up 21%, marking the sixth consecutive quarter of 20%+ expansion.
  • B2B Growth -- Q4 B2B volume grew 21% sequentially, with significant contributions from China, Asia Pacific, and EMEA; full-year B2B growth was 28% in revenue ex-interest.
  • SMB Take Rate -- Q4 2025 take rate of 113 basis points, up 4 basis points year over year, reflecting ongoing pricing initiatives and focus on higher take rate business lines.
  • Upmarket Customer Mix -- Customers with over $600,000 annual average volume now represent 42% of total revenue, driving 60% of overall growth and identified as fastest-growing segment.
  • Region-Specific Growth -- UAE business delivered over $1 billion in volume and $15 million revenue in 2025, with revenue up nearly 50% year over year; driven primarily by IT and digital marketing clients.
  • Enterprise Payout Performance -- Full-year enterprise payout volumes up 17%, with Q4 growth at 27%; enterprise now features expanded partnerships (e.g., Airbnb, TikTok Live, Alibaba).
  • Acquisition of Boundless -- Acquired for $13 million plus up to $4 million in earn-outs, enhancing workforce management offerings.
  • Stablecoin Initiatives -- Launched waitlist and onboarded first customers for stablecoin product; applied for a U.S. uninsured national trust bank charter to integrate stablecoin with core offerings.
  • Checkout Migration -- Transition to Stripe's solution ongoing, cited as accretive to revenue less transaction costs and to adjusted EBITDA; migration and customer portfolio optimization expected to result in a 300 basis point revenue headwind in 2026 but yield favorable margin and yield dynamics.
  • Operating Expenses (OpEx) -- Total OpEx of $246 million for Q4, up 6% year over year due to IT/communications and labor, including impact from the EasyLink acquisition; 7% midpoint OpEx growth for 2026 guidance, with constant-currency growth at 4%.
  • AI-First Strategy -- AI and agentic models deployed to drive code delivery, funnel efficiency, and operational cost savings, with impact observed across engineering, go-to-market, customer support, and compliance functions.
  • 2026 Profitability Outlook -- Adjusted EBITDA guidance of $275 million to $285 million, targeting 25% margin and $8 million year-over-year gain despite a $42 million decrease in interest income; adjusted EBITDA ex-interest between $85 million and $95 million expected to reach double-digit margin for the first time as a public company.

RISKS

  • Bea Ordonez stated, "Our core revenue guidance includes an approximately 300 basis point headwind to our growth rate, reflecting anticipated churn related to our transition to Stripe's Checkout solution as well as from changes to our acquisition focus and onboarding flows."
  • Interest income is expected to decrease by $42 million in 2026, though hedging may partially offset the impact.
  • Macroeconomic uncertainty, interest rate declines, and tariff policy changes were cited as factors impacting future balance growth and volume trends.

SUMMARY

Payoneer Global Inc. (PAYO +1.43%) delivered a record year marked by accelerated growth in B2B activities, notable margin expansion, significant free cash flow generation, and fundamental shifts in its customer portfolio and business mix. Management outlined ambitious 2026 targets, prioritizing mid-teens growth and profitability through upmarket strategy, portfolio optimization, and technology investments, particularly in AI and stablecoin innovation. The transition to Stripe Checkout and deliberate churn management created near-term revenue headwinds but are projected to enhance long-term margin strength and operational efficiency. The company remains proactive in capital allocation, executing substantial share buybacks, expanding its regional footprint, and pursuing regulated offerings, including a proposed U.S. trust bank to underpin its stablecoin strategy.

  • Management highlighted "all customer cohorts are profitable," backing claims of structural improvements across the base and ARPU growth of over 50% for the ideal customer profile since 2023.
  • Payoneer outlined that over 51% of year-end 2025 customer funds are hedged, with $130 million of 2026 interest income secured despite expectations for lower rates.
  • The upmarket strategy is already material: customers averaging over $600,000 annual volume generated 42% of revenue and were responsible for 60% of overall growth.
  • UAE growth and regional expansion into India, Mexico, and Indonesia illustrate targeted investments to facilitate larger, multi-entity, cross-border client acquisition.
  • Stablecoin and blockchain integration, in partnership with Bridge (a Stripe company), represent a business model evolution, with the trust bank charter intended to position Payoneer at the core of digital currency payment infrastructure.
  • The Boundless acquisition and India tech hub expansion signal an ongoing commitment to platform capability growth and operational leverage.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ARPU: Average Revenue Per User; a metric tracking the average revenue generated per active customer account, excluding interest income when specified.
  • Agentic AI: Artificial intelligence models capable of autonomously executing complex, decision-based tasks, deployed to improve operational efficiency and customer experience in fintech platforms.
  • Stablecoin: A class of cryptocurrencies designed to maintain stable value relative to a reference asset, often used for faster and borderless digital payments.
  • SMB Take Rate: The percentage of transaction volume Payoneer earns from fees charged to small- and medium-sized business customers for payment services.
