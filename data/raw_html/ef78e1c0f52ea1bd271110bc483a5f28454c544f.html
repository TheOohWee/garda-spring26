TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Income -- $44.7 million, or $0.70 per diluted share, was reported for the quarter.
  • Net Interest Income -- $135.5 million, increasing $1.9 million sequentially, driven by loan growth and lower deposit costs.
  • Loan Growth -- Total loans grew by $257 million quarter over quarter, with production of $572 million and pay-offs of $471 million.
  • Deposit Growth -- Total deposits increased by $63 million, while interest-bearing deposits rose $189 million; all brokered deposits ($127 million) were paid off and not held at year-end.
  • Deposit Cost -- Total deposit cost decreased by 16 basis points to 2.35% during the quarter.
  • Non-Interest Income -- Decreased $55.1 million from Q3 due to the prior quarter's $53.3 million one-time pre-tax gain from the sale of the insurance agency; adjusted non-interest income fell $1.7 million sequentially, mainly from seasonal mortgage declines.
  • Non-Interest Expense -- $114.7 million, down $7 million from Q3, primarily due to a $9.2 million reduction in merger and conversion expenses; excluding these, non-interest expense was $112.7 million, up $1.9 million sequentially.
  • Capital Ratios -- All regulatory capital ratios remain above "well-capitalized" thresholds but declined moderately from Q3 after earlier gains tied to a capital raise and the insurance agency sale.
  • Provision for Credit Losses -- $3.1 million was recorded in the quarter; net charge-offs totaled $1.7 million.
  • Allowance for Credit Losses (ACL) -- ACL as a percentage of total loans declined by 2 basis points quarter over quarter to 1.57%.
  • Asset Quality -- Criticized loans as a percentage of total loans declined 13 basis points to 2.89%, and non-performing assets as a percentage of total assets decreased 3 basis points to 0.68%.
  • Net Interest Margin (NIM) -- Adjusted NIM (excluding purchase accretion and recoveries) increased 2 basis points to 3.34%.
  • Adjusted Loan Yields -- Decreased by 14 basis points to 6.27% quarter over quarter.
  • New and Renewed Loan Yields -- Averaged around 7.35% during the quarter, with December spot yields at about 7.05%.
  • Loan Portfolio Composition -- Production by geography: about 14% Tennessee, about 10% Alabama/Florida Panhandle, about 28% Mississippi, about 15% Georgia/Central Florida, about 33% commercial corporate lines; by type: roughly 12% consumer/HELOC/1-4 family, about 23% small business/banking, about 34% commercial/C&I, about 32% corporate banking.
  • Variable-Rate Loans -- The book totals $6 billion, more than 90% of which reprice within a month of rate change; 75% have rates at or below 6.5%.
  • Fixed-Rate Loans Repricing -- $600 million to $700 million will reprice over the next 12 months at a weighted average rate near 5.5%.
  • Securities Repricing -- $200 million of securities will reprice in the coming year, with current yields in the mid-2% range.
  • Certificates of Deposit Maturity -- Approximately $2 billion in CDs maturing in the first half, with blended rates in the low-4% range; current replacement rates cited at approximately 3.75% to 4%.
  • Expense Guidance -- Management guided to a 2% to 3% increase in expenses for 2025, with efforts to constrain that growth highlighted.
  • Merger Update -- The merger with The First remains on track for completion in the first half of 2025; regulatory engagement described as responsive, with management expressing continued optimism on integration and business momentum.

SUMMARY

Renasant (RNST 0.86%) reported sequential growth in both net interest income and loans, supported by disciplined deposit pricing and strategic pay-down of brokered funding. Management confirmed that all regulatory capital ratios remain above "well-capitalized" thresholds despite moderate declines following exceptional items in the prior quarter. The company emphasized a stable outlook for net interest margin with potential modest expansion projected in 2025, reversing prior expectations for margin compression. Expense trends reflect underlying pressure from operational and healthcare-related costs, though management maintained guidance for limited growth in 2025. Variable-rate loan exposure remains significant, with most repricing rapidly, while upcoming maturing CDs may create funding cost relief as they renew at lower rates following recent trends. The pending merger with The First is proceeding on the announced timeline, with management highlighting complementary strengths and limited customer overlap.

  • David Meredith clarified that an uptick in classified loans resulted from existing criticized credits being downgraded as ongoing underperformance persisted, not from new portfolio deterioration.
  • Management described operational losses, especially fraud and Reg E dispute costs, and higher self-funded health claims as key factors elevating core non-interest expense in the quarter, noting these trends were unusually high and industrywide.
  • Jim Mabry stated, "our outlook is for some modest expansion in the margin," citing improved deposit beta and funding costs as primary drivers of the revised forecast.
  • Management conveyed confidence in the company's core deposit growth engine and expressed intent to be a net purchaser of securities in the first quarter if loan growth lags, marking a shift from prior quarters.
  • Mitch Waycaster emphasized positive organic loan growth momentum across business lines and regions, supported by a robust pipeline entering 2025.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Brokered Deposits: Deposits obtained through intermediaries rather than directly from bank customers, often at higher cost and used to quickly increase funding.
  • Criticized Loans: Loans identified as showing potential weakness that may jeopardize full repayment, categorized as either special mention or classified assets under regulatory guidance.
  • Net Interest Margin (NIM): The ratio of net interest income to average earning assets, reflecting core bank profitability from lending and deposit activities.
  • ACL (Allowance for Credit Losses): Balance sheet reserve set aside to absorb estimated future losses on loans and other financial assets.
  • CDs (Certificates of Deposit): Fixed-term deposit products with specified interest rates and maturities, used for customer and wholesale funding.
  • HELOC: Home Equity Line of Credit, a revolving credit facility secured by residential property equity.
  • Reg E: Regulation E, federal rules governing electronic fund transfer rights and dispute resolutions for consumers.
  • Purchase Accounting Accretion: Adjustment to interest income arising from the revaluation of acquired loans or securities in a merger or acquisition.
  • OAM (Other Assets Mentioned): Internal loan category for credits requiring management attention but not yet meeting criteria for formal criticism or classification.
  • Special Mention: Regulatory classification for assets presenting potential weaknesses that deserve close attention but do not yet jeopardize the ability to meet repayment.
