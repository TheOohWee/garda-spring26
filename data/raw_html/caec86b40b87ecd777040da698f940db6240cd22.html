Takeaways

Total revenue -- $14.7 million for the three months ended September 30, 2025, up 10% compared to the prior year period, primarily due to increased U.S. procedure volume and expanded commercial reach.

U.S. revenue -- $13.5 million in U.S. revenue for fiscal Q3 2025 (period ended September 30, 2025), reflecting a $1.2 million, or 10%, year-over-year increase, with 420 implants performed versus 394 in the prior year period.

European revenue -- $1.2 million in European revenue for fiscal Q3 2025, representing a $100,000, or 12%, year-over-year increase, offset by a decline in units to 50 from 56 in the prior year period.

Active U.S. centers -- 250 as of September 30, 2025, up from 240 at June 30, 2025, indicating ongoing territory development.

Sales territories -- U.S. territories increased by three to reach 50; European territories held at five.

Gross profit -- Gross profit was $12.8 million, a year-over-year increase of $1.5 million, or 15%, driven by higher average selling prices and improved manufacturing efficiencies.

Gross margin -- 87%, up from 83% in the prior year period, reflecting both ASP improvement and lowered cost per unit.

R&D expense -- $3.1 million, up 26% compared to the prior year period, attributed to higher compensation and consulting costs, offset by a decrease in clinical trial expenses.

SG&A expense -- $21.9 million, a 1% increase compared to the prior year period.

Interest expense -- Increased by $500,000, reflecting higher term loan borrowings.

Net loss -- $12.9 million, or $0.49 per share, compared to $13.1 million, or $0.57 per share, in fiscal Q3 2024, on 26.2 million weighted average shares outstanding.

Cash and cash equivalents -- $85.1 million as of September 30, 2025.

Net cash used in operations and investing -- $10 million net cash used in operating and investing activities, compared to $10.4 million in fiscal Q3 2024.

2025 guidance – revenue -- Expected range narrowed to $55.6 million–$56.6 million from the previous $55 million–$57 million for full-year 2025 revenue guidance.

2025 guidance – gross margin -- Raised to 85%-86% for full-year 2025, up from prior guidance of 83%-84%.

2025 guidance – operating expenses -- Updated to $98 million–$99 million, above the prior $96 million–$98 million range.

Regulatory -- Category one CPT code for Barostim finalized for implementation as of January 1, 2026, expected to eliminate experimental denial rationale and standardize physician reimbursement effective January 1, 2026.

Reimbursement -- CMS maintains outpatient procedure payment of approximately $45,000 under APC 1580; “significant progress” cited with Medicare, Medicare Advantage, and commercial payers.

Clinical trial initiatives -- An IDE application for a new 2,000-patient randomized controlled trial was submitted to the FDA in mid-October 2025, with feedback expected in late November; trial expected to expand total addressable market through broadened inclusion criteria.

Salesforce -- “First wave” of new hires from earlier in 2025 now contributing meaningfully, with onboarding and productivity acceleration ongoing; annual salesforce turnover returning to 10%-20% “normal” range per management.

Account penetration -- Over 20% of active implanting centers achieved three or more implants, with no structural barriers limiting further ramp.

Outlook for growth -- Management targets mid-teens percentage revenue growth for 2026, with full acceleration to “mid to 20 percents” expected longer term, in 2027 and beyond.

Registration statement -- Existing registration statement scheduled to expire on November 15, 2025; company intends to refresh in connection with imminent 10-Q filing.

Summary

Full-year 2025 revenue and gross margin guidance were both raised, while operating expense expectations increased for full-year 2025, indicating more investment to support growth. Management confirmed the transition to a category one CPT code effective January 2026, removing a key reimbursement friction and formalizing physician payment processes. The FDA review for an IDE covering a 2,000-patient randomized trial that could triple the eligible patient base is actively progressing as of mid-October 2025, with feedback expected in late November 2025, with CMS reimbursement anticipated at current outpatient rates (currently $45,000 under APC 1580) for such trial patients. Management emphasized improving commercial productivity, network expansion, and medical education as core drivers sustaining momentum and supporting the company’s strategy to establish Barostim as standard of care.

President and Chief Executive Officer Hykes cited, “Our Salesforce transformation is progressing well, and turnover is returning to more normal levels.”

Chief Financial Officer Oasheim stated ASPs for the quarter were north of $31,000 on a worldwide basis for fiscal Q3 2025. That's a significant step up from fiscal Q3 2024, when ASPs were just below $30,000 per unit globally.

Management highlighted, “If we can reach agreement with FDA on the IDE protocol, we will then approach CMS to apply for Category B IDE coverage, a second and necessary condition in order for us to move forward with the trial.”

Leadership communicated an intention to continue measured expansion of territories by approximately three per quarter, as indicated for 2026, and plans for operating leverage as revenue outpaces OpEx growth in 2026.

Industry glossary

Barostim: CVRx neuromodulation device for heart failure, targeting patients with reduced ejection fraction.

APC 1580: A CMS-structured Ambulatory Payment Classification grouping, setting specific outpatient reimbursement for certain new technology medical procedures.

Category one CPT code: Permanent reimbursement code for established medical procedures, removing experimental/investigational denial risk and standardizing physician payments.

IDE (Investigational Device Exemption): FDA authorization enabling a device to be used in a clinical trial to collect safety and effectiveness data.

Category B IDE coverage: CMS policy facilitating Medicare payment for devices in clinical studies when the device is considered non-experimental/investigational but not yet fully approved.

APP (Advanced Practice Provider): Non-physician healthcare practitioner such as a nurse practitioner or physician assistant involved in patient management.

NT proBNP: Biomarker used to assess heart failure severity and stability for patient selection in cardiac device trials.

Guideline-directed medical therapy: Evidence-based pharmacological and device treatments recommended in clinical guidelines for heart failure management.
