Takeaways

  • Adjusted operating earnings per share -- $2.77 for the year, surpassing the guidance midpoint, with a 7.4% annual growth rate since 2021 for Exelon Corporation (EXC 1.75%).
  • GAAP earnings per share -- $2.73 for the year and $0.58 for the quarter; non-GAAP quarterly EPS was $0.59.
  • 2026 operating earnings guidance -- $2.81 to $2.91 per share, with the midpoint above prior disclosures and indicating over 6% midpoint-to-midpoint implied EPS growth.
  • Rate base growth -- Approximately 8% annualized for 2025 and a projected CAGR of 7.9% through 2029.
  • Capital investment plan -- $41.3 billion projected through 2029, with over 70% of the plan-to-plan increase allocated to transmission projects.
  • Transmission investment pipeline -- $1.2 billion of incremental investment recommended in the recent PJM reliability window, plus $12 billion to $17 billion in additional identified opportunities over the next decade.
  • Regulatory developments -- Final settlements reached for Atlantic City Electric ($54 million revenue recovery at 9.6% ROE), and Delmarva Gas ($21.5 million revenue recovery at 9.6% ROE); ongoing rate cases and new filings detailed for Pepco Maryland, Delaware (Delmarva Power), and ComEd.
  • Return on equity range -- 9% to 10% remains targeted for the consolidated utility, with average earned ROE since separation at approximately 9.4%.
  • Customer bill trends -- Portion of average customer bill attributable to Exelon Corporation has grown only 0.10% since 2021, while supply-side costs for residential customers in the Mid-Atlantic have increased up to 80% over five years.
  • O&M cost management -- Annual operations and maintenance savings of $580 million delivered, with expense growth targeted to remain flat from 2024 to 2026, and not to exceed 2.5% CAGR through 2029.
  • Credit metrics -- Year-end average consolidated credit metrics reached 13.5%, providing 1.50% of headroom vs. Moody’s downgrade threshold, and 1.00% to 2.00% financial flexibility over S&P and Moody’s thresholds for the outlook period.
  • Dividend growth -- Annual dividend growth rate projected at 5%, with a dividend of $1.68 per share expected in 2026.
  • Job and economic impact -- Since 2021, Exelon Corporation has employed 20,000 people, sustained 50,000 jobs, and generated nearly $60 billion in economic activity for its communities.
  • Reliability performance -- Top quartile reliability maintained, with rankings of one, two, four, and seven in 2024 benchmarking, and nearly 2 million annual customer interruptions reduced since 2021.

Summary

Management communicated a strategic capital allocation shift toward transmission infrastructure, driven by accelerating load growth, data center demand, and regulatory clarity. New multiyear grid plans and competitive project wins in PJM and MISO regions strengthen the company's differentiated pipeline and improve visibility into future returns. Equity needs over the four-year capital plan are forecast at $3.4 billion, managed through forward contracts and deliberate ATM activity to minimize market impact. Regulatory settlements and ongoing advocacy on affordability, and supply-related policy position the utility to navigate inflationary and supply pressures while sustaining customer value. The company expects annualized EPS and rate base expansion to approach the high end of guided long-term ranges, while maintaining a balanced funding mix and disciplined O&M expenditure approach.

  • The company issued $1 billion of convertible debt in December, advancing more than half of its targeted long-term holding company debt for 2026.
  • The capital plan’s geographic and project diversification ensures no single regulatory jurisdiction represents more than 30% of total investment allocation, and no individual project comprises more than 3% of expenditures.
  • Management affirmed, "have delivered within our capital within 2% since separation," underscoring execution reliability on capital plans and operational targets.
  • Recent customer relief initiatives included a $60 million fund for low- and moderate-income customers to offset higher energy supply costs.
  • Leadership voiced intent to maintain a 40% equity funding ratio for incremental capital opportunities, with less than 2% of annual market capitalization needed for expected equity raises.
  • Grid resilience investments were validated during Winter Storm FERN, with less than 1% of customers experiencing outages amid record demand conditions.
  • Management cited Charles River Associates' study stating utility-generated power could reduce PJM customer costs by $9.6 billion to $20 billion in 2028–2029, and lower energy shortage outage risk by roughly 85%.

Industry glossary

  • PJM: Pennsylvania-New Jersey-Maryland Interconnection; a regional transmission organization (RTO) managing the electric grid and wholesale markets in parts of the eastern U.S.
  • MISO: Midcontinent Independent System Operator; manages transmission and ensures grid reliability across the Midwest and parts of the South.
  • Rate base: The value of property on which a public utility is allowed to earn a specified return, subject to regulatory approval.
  • Transmission security agreement (TSA): A contract allocating the costs and responsibilities for specific transmission projects to generators or load-serving entities, supporting system reliability and new load interconnections.
  • O&M: Operations and maintenance; recurring costs required to operate and maintain utility assets.
  • ROE: Return on equity; a measure of profitability indicating how much return a company generates on equity invested by shareholders.
  • CEJA: Climate and Equitable Jobs Act; state legislation in Illinois outlining clean energy goals, grid modernization, and labor standards.
  • CRGA: Clean and Renewable Grid Advancement Act; referenced Illinois legislation addressing transmission and reliability priorities.
  • ATM: At-the-market; a method for companies to raise equity capital incrementally by selling shares directly into the open market at prevailing prices.
  • AFUDC: Allowance for funds used during construction; an accounting practice allowing utilities to capitalize financing costs incurred during construction of utility assets.
