NPK International (NPKI) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 26, 2026 at 10:35AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 26, 2026 at 9:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Matthew S. Lanigan
  • Chief Financial Officer — Gregg S. Piontek

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $277 million for fiscal 2025, up 27% year over year, with a 31% increase in the fiscal fourth quarter.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $22 million in the fiscal fourth quarter, up 41% sequentially and 27% year over year; full-year adjusted EBITDA grew 38% year over year.
  • Gross margin -- 37.7% in the fiscal fourth quarter, up from 31.9% in the prior quarter and slightly below 39.2% in the same quarter last year, primarily due to cross-rental costs.
  • Rental revenues -- $124 million for fiscal 2025, increasing 39% year over year; 18% sequential and 35% year-over-year growth in the fiscal fourth quarter.
  • Product sales -- $25 million recognized in the fiscal fourth quarter, up 4% sequentially and 62% year over year; full-year product sales advanced 30% year over year.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $0.13 per diluted share in the fiscal fourth quarter, versus $0.07 in the prior quarter and $0.08 in the prior year's fourth quarter; full-year adjusted EPS improved 83% year over year.
  • Share repurchases -- 4% of outstanding shares repurchased in 2025 at an average price of $6.70 per share, reducing the share count by approximately 2 million for the year.
  • After-tax return on net assets -- 11% for fiscal 2025, a significant improvement over the prior period.
  • SG&A expenses -- $15.4 million in the fiscal fourth quarter, including $1.8 million in acquisition and severance costs and $0.4 million from Grassform Plant Hire; the remainder represents recurring expenses.
  • Net CapEx -- $12 million in the fiscal fourth quarter, primarily for rental fleet growth; $43 million for the full year.
  • Rental fleet -- Approximately 215,000 composite mats at year-end, reflecting a 16% expansion (including 20,000 mats added through the Grassform Plant Hire acquisition).
  • Operating cash flow -- $73 million generated for the full year, plus $17 million from the Fluids divestiture and $42 million used for the Grassform Plant Hire acquisition.
  • Net debt -- $12 million at year-end, with $17 million in total debt and $5 million in total cash; $139 million available under the credit facility.
  • 2026 guidance -- Projected revenue of $305 million-$325 million and adjusted EBITDA of $88 million-$100 million; midpoints reflect 14% revenue and 25% adjusted EBITDA growth.
  • CapEx outlook -- $45 million-$55 million planned for 2026, with $35 million-$45 million dedicated to fleet expansion, excluding manufacturing expansion projects.
  • Effective tax rate -- 26% for the fiscal fourth quarter (excluding a $1.5 million benefit) and 28% for the full year; expectation for mid to upper twenties in 2026.
  • Deferred tax assets -- $40 million in NOLs and tax credits carried into 2026, expected to significantly limit near-term cash tax payments.
  • Sector exposure -- Over two-thirds of 2025 revenue, including 60% of rental and service revenue, driven by the power transmission sector; more than 80% of 2025 product sales revenue derived from utility companies.
  • Pipeline growth -- Entering 2026, quoted volumes are about 30% higher than the previous year, with roughly two-thirds tied to existing customers and one-third to new geographies.
  • ERP system implementation -- Enterprise resource planning system successfully implemented across legacy operations in early 2026, aiming for further SG&A efficiencies.

Summary

NPK International (NPKI 0.13%) delivered fiscal fourth quarter and full-year results that exceeded internal expectations, driven by sustained rental fleet utilization and strong product sales. Management emphasized the successful execution of 2025 priorities, including organic rental growth, the acquisition of Grassform Plant Hire, and the rollout of a new ERP system, each contributing to improved operating leverage. Strategic guidance for fiscal 2026 focuses on further rental-driven growth, continued investment in fleet and manufacturing capacity, and disciplined capital allocation balancing acquisitions and shareholder returns.

  • Management stated, "At this point, we still anticipate Q3 and some activities to be the major seasonality impact on the business. We get some offset from activities in the UK, but with the bulk of the density of our work being here in the U.S, you should expect to see that. You will see a dampening, I expect, with the UK, but largely following the same trend as historical," indicating the expected persistence of third-quarter revenue seasonality despite international activities.
  • Gregg S. Piontek said, "The one thing to highlight there in terms of that broad range is your biggest wildcard is the product sales side, and it goes back to my commentary about the project-centric nature of it," citing the project-centric nature and customer CapEx timing as primary drivers of forecast range width.
  • The company communicated, "what we are seeing is early stages of some improvement in pricing in the market now," though overall 2026 revenue guidance is primarily volume-driven.
  • After integrating the Grassform Plant Hire acquisition, management signaled continued pursuit of inorganic growth opportunities, while stressing that share repurchases remain "programmatic, opportunistic," and secondary to fleet and capacity investments.
  • The organization enters 2026 with a "robust commercial pipeline" and intends to expand the DuraBase composite mat rental fleet by a "low to mid-teens percentage."

Industry glossary

  • Composite mat: A large, engineered panel used to provide stable, temporary access roads or work surfaces, typically replacing timber mats in energy and construction infrastructure projects.
  • DuraBase: Proprietary composite mat product line from NPK International targeting heavy-duty worksite access applications.
  • Cross-rent: The short-term rental of equipment from third-party owners to meet temporary project demand or supplement the company's own fleet.
  • Net CapEx: Capital expenditures net of asset sales or reimbursements, representing the actual cash outflow toward long-term asset growth.
  • NOLs: Net operating loss carryforwards, tax attributes that allow a company to offset future taxable income, lowering cash tax outlays.
