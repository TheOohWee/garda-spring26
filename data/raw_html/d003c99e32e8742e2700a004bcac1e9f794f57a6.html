TAKEAWAYS

  • Leasing Activity -- Nearly 5,000,000 square feet leased in 2025, the highest annual new leasing volume in company history.
  • Anchor Leasing -- 28 anchor leases signed for approximately 645,000 square feet at a 24% blended comparable cash spread and 26% gross returns on capital.
  • Portfolio Transactions -- Two joint ventures with GIC established, totaling around $1,000,000,000 of gross asset value.
  • Asset Dispositions -- 13 properties and two land parcels sold in 2025 for approximately $622,000,000; resulted in a 400 basis point drop in ABR from power centers and removal of 21 watchlist anchor boxes equating to roughly 578,000 square feet.
  • Share Repurchases -- $300,000,000 in repurchases completed at a 9% core FFO yield, representing 6% of outstanding shares, using proceeds from the dispositions.
  • Leased Rate Trend -- Total portfolio leased rate increased by 120 basis points sequentially, with the small shop lease rate up 50 basis points sequentially and 110 basis points year over year.
  • Embedded Rent Growth -- Portfolio embedded rent bumps reached 1.8%, up 25 basis points versus 2024; company targets 2%.
  • Development Pipeline -- Expansion of One Loudoun to add 86,000 square feet of retail, 33,000 square feet of office, 169 hotel rooms, and 429 luxury multifamily units; 65% of retail space already leased.
  • NAREIT and Core FFO -- Reported NAREIT FFO per share of $0.52 and core FFO per share of $0.51 for the quarter; for the year, $2.10 and $2.60 respectively, with core FFO up 3.5% year over year.
  • Same Property NOI -- Grew 2.9% for the year, which is 100 basis points above initial guidance; four-year average is 4%.
  • Signed-Not-Open Pipeline -- Signed-not-open NOI stood at $37,000,000, up $4,000,000 sequentially; 70% of this is expected to come online in 2026.
  • Guidance for 2026 -- NAREIT and core FFO per share expected in the $2.06–$2.12 range; includes assumptions of 2.75% same property NOI growth, $121,000,000 in net interest expense, and a 100 basis point bad debt reserve.
  • Capital Allocation Plans -- Guidance assumes $110,000,000 of 1031 acquisitions in the first half, offset by $115,000,000 of noncore asset sales later in the year; City Center disposition included in planned asset sales at "mid fifties" million dollars.
  • Balance Sheet Position -- Reported $1,000,000,000 in liquidity and net debt to EBITDA at 4.9 times; emphasized continued commitment to long-term leverage target in low- to mid-5 times range.
  • Interest Expense Outlook -- Expected to be a $0.03 per share tailwind for 2026 due to lower line of credit balances and higher capitalized interest from development activity.
  • Recurring but Unpredictable Items -- $0.04 headwind to 2026 guidance, with such items modeled at just under $13,000,000, close to the three-year average run rate.

RISKS

  • Guidance Headwinds -- Heath R. Fear said, "Our philosophy with establishing guidance is always to set expectations based on things we have clear visibility to while maintaining a pathway to outperformance. While our 2025 allocation activity is expected to be accretive on a full year basis, the timing of dispositions and associated deployment of proceeds are acting as a $0.02 headwind into 2026."
  • Bad Debt Reserve -- The 2026 bad debt reserve was set at 100 basis points of total revenues, higher than the prior year's general reserve of 85 basis points, "mostly probably due to The Container Store," indicating increased credit risk concerns.
  • Unpredictable Income Decline -- Recurring but unpredictable income modeled lower for 2026 ($13,000,000 vs. $21,500,000 in 2025), contributing a $0.04 drag to guidance.

SUMMARY

The company entered two joint ventures with GIC totaling around $1 billion of asset value and disposed of $622 million in primarily lower-growth, larger-format assets, resulting in a changed mix toward neighborhood, grocery, lifestyle, and mixed-use centers. Management indicated $300 million in shares were repurchased at a 9% core FFO yield, funded primarily by asset sales, prioritizing arbitrage between public and private market valuations. NAREIT and core FFO per share guidance for 2026 is set at $2.06–$2.12, with expectations for front-loaded disposition activity, moderate acquisition volume, and acceleration of same property NOI in the second half of the year. The balance sheet remains strong with $1 billion in liquidity and net debt to EBITDA at 4.9 times, supporting additional opportunistic capital deployment. Portfolio transformation included bringing in clients such as Whole Foods, Trader Joe's, and luxury retailers at Legacy West, emphasizing elevated leasing velocity and a focus on long-term embedded rent growth.

  • Legacy West outperformed original underwriting, adding luxury tenants and opening future relationship potential.
  • Disposition strategy contributed to a 400 basis point decrease in portfolio ABR exposure to power centers, mitigating risk concentration.
  • Company highlighted a currently wide spread between FFO yield and asset sale yields, influencing ongoing capital allocation decisions.
  • Ongoing entitlement processes for Carillon and Ontario land parcels are expected to extend into 2027, with no current NOI impact but future potential value realization.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ABR (Annualized Base Rent): Rental income earned from leases on an annualized basis, used as a key measure of recurring portfolio revenue.
  • 1031 Exchange (Section 1031 Exchange): A tax-deferral strategy allowing reinvestment of proceeds from property sales into like-kind assets to defer capital gains taxes.
  • Signed-Not-Open (SNO or S&O) Pipeline: The total amount of net operating income from leases that have been signed but have not yet commenced due to pending tenant occupancy.
  • Gross Returns on Capital: A metric expressing the cash returns generated from invested capital, often cited for newly signed lease activity.
  • Cotenancy Clause: Lease provision allowing tenants to alter terms or exit if key neighboring tenants vacate, relevant to negotiations described in anchor leasing.
