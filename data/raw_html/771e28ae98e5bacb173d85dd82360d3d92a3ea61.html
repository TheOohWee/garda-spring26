Vornado (VNO) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 10, 2026 at 11:42AM EST

Tuesday, August 5, 2025 at 10 a.m. ET

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Steven Roth
  • President and Chief Financial Officer — Michael J. Franco
  • Executive Vice President of Office Leasing & Co-Head of Real Estate — Glen J. Weiss

TAKEAWAYS

  • Stock Performance -- Shares rose 42% over the trailing 12 months, nearly double the S&P 500 return, outpacing all other office REITs cited by management.
  • Geographic Concentration -- The company is 90% focused on Prime Pitch Manhattan, with single major properties in Chicago and San Francisco possibly for sale "for the right deal at the right time."
  • First Half Leasing -- 2.7 million square feet leased, including 2.2 million square feet in Manhattan office; the figure includes a 1.1 million square foot master lease with NYU at 770 Broadway, the largest New York office transaction since 2019.
  • Average Starting Rents -- Non-NYU Manhattan office leasing averaged $97 per square foot for the first half, with GAAP mark-to-markets of +10.7% and cash mark-to-markets of +7.7%.
  • Q2 Manhattan Office Leasing -- 1.5 million square feet leased in Manhattan during the quarter, including NYU; excluding NYU, 400,000 square feet leased at $101 per square foot starting rents, with GAAP mark-to-markets of +11.8% and cash mark-to-markets of +8.7%.
  • PENN 1 Leasing and Occupancy -- 12 deals for 183,000 square feet executed in PENN 1 at $101 per square foot average starting rents, raising occupancy to 91%.
  • PENN 2 Leasing and Occupancy -- Following a 203,000 square foot Verizon headquarters lease (signed after quarter-end), occupancy is now 62%, with 50% of 1.4 million square feet in pipeline proposals focused on PENN 2.
  • Pipeline Activity -- 560,000 square feet of leases are signed or in negotiation for Q3, with more than 1 million square feet in earlier proposal stages; half of the pipeline is designated for PENN 2.
  • Comparable FFO -- $0.56 per share, flat year over year and above analyst consensus of $0.53 per share; 2025 comparable FFO is still expected to be "essentially flat" with 2024's $2.26 per share.
  • NYC Office Occupancy -- Increased to 86.7% from 84.4% sequentially, primarily due to the NYU lease.
  • Balance Sheet Improvements -- $1.5 billion of net proceeds from sales, financings, and the NYU transaction year-to-date; $965 million paid down in debt, $540 million increase in cash, and net debt-to-EBITDA reduced by 1.4x turns to 7.2x.
  • Liquidity Position -- Immediate liquidity stands at $2.9 billion ($1.36 billion cash plus $1.56 billion undrawn credit lines).
  • Rent Growth Outlook -- Market rents above $100 per square foot are now common, and management expects further increases; every $10 per square foot rent increase in PENN District's 5 million square feet would add $50 million annually to the bottom line.
  • Development and Redevelopment -- Progress on a 475-unit rental residential project on 34th Street; plans to revamp 700 feet of Seventh Avenue retail frontage as the first phase of district revitalization.
  • Major Financing Transactions -- $450 million financing of 1535 Broadway (April), $675 million refinancing of Independence Plaza (June), and $450 million refinancing of PENN 11 (July); preferred equity reduced from $1.828 billion to $1.079 billion.
  • PENN 1 Ground Lease Dispute -- A motion has been filed in New York County Supreme Court challenging the recent arbitration panel ruling on the PENN 1 ground lease rent reset; management called the motion "entirely without merit."
  • Dividend Policy -- Management expects at least $0.74 per share in dividends for 2025, matching last year, with consideration of a more regular quarterly dividend to be reviewed at year-end.
  • San Francisco and Chicago Assets -- THE MART and 555 California are not being actively marketed but remain available for sale under favorable conditions; recent improvement in San Francisco's leasing environment noted.
  • 350 Park Avenue Project -- A 1.8 million square foot tower is advancing, anchored by Citadel as a 60% partner; building design is complete, and City Planning approval has been obtained, with demolition expected to begin in spring.

SUMMARY

Vornado Realty Trust (VNO +1.38%) reported sequential growth in New York office occupancy, robust leasing activity, and highlighted substantial liquidity improvements that have decreased leverage and strengthened the balance sheet. Management identified ongoing rent growth in premier Manhattan assets and outlined ongoing and future redevelopment projects poised for further value creation, particularly in the PENN District. The company also stressed the strategic flexibility afforded by potential asset sales in Chicago and San Francisco and confirmed consistent dividend expectations for 2025, with the potential for quarterly reinstatement under review.

  • Management stated, "the next few years have the potential to be one of the strongest periods of rental growth we've seen in decades," directly tying tight Class A supply and rising rents to income upside.
  • Activation of major leases, especially in the PENN District, is not expected to materially drive earnings until 2027, with management reiterating, "full positive impact of the lease-up of PENN 1 and PENN 2 in 2027."
  • The average lease term for Q2, excluding NYU, was 6.8 years, which management characterized as "an outlier" and not a new trend, reiterating that the company's average is normally at least 10 years.
  • Tenant improvement spending has stabilized, with Glen J. Weiss stating, "TI's have stabilized -- haven't come down yet, but we are seeing free rent come down," indicating tightening deal terms as market conditions improve.
  • San Francisco's 555 California saw 600,000 square feet leased recently, with "tour volume is great, almost daily," indicating improving demand and occupancy.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Mark-to-Market: The percentage change in rent on a new lease compared with the expiring lease for the same space, reported on either a GAAP or cash basis.
  • TI (Tenant Improvements): Capital expenditures made by the landlord to customize rental space according to tenant requirements.
  • PENN District: Vornado’s office campus centered around Pennsylvania Station in Manhattan, including PENN 1, PENN 2, and Farley buildings.
  • Preferred Equity: Hybrid investment that sits between senior debt and common equity in a property’s capital structure, typically offering fixed returns with residual upside.
