Earnings Call Transcript

Deere (DE) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 11:29AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 19, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Financial Officer — Josh Jepsen
  • President, Worldwide Construction & Forestry and Power Systems — Brian Campbell
  • Manager, Investor Communications — Chris Seibert
  • Executive (host/moderator) — Josh Beal

Takeaways

  • Net Sales and Revenues -- $9.611 billion, up 13%, with equipment operations net sales up 18% to $8.001 billion.
  • Net Income -- $656 million, or $2.42 per diluted share, attributable to Deere & Company.
  • Equipment Operations Operating Margin -- 5.9%, reflecting lower production costs excluding tariffs and margin pressure from unfavorable mix and tariffs.
  • Production & Precision Ag (PPA) Net Sales -- $3.163 billion, up 3%, driven by currency translation; operating margin at 4.4% due to higher tariffs, sales mix, and warranty costs.
  • Small Ag & Turf (SAT) Net Sales -- $2.168 billion, up 24%, from higher volumes and positive price/currency effects; operating margin reached 9% with $196 million operating profit.
  • Construction & Forestry (C&F) Net Sales -- $2.670 billion, up 34%, supported by shipment volumes and currency; operating profit $137 million, operating margin 5.1%.
  • Segment Guidance—PPA -- Full-year net sales expected down 5%–10%, price realization +1.5%, currency translation +3%, operating margin forecasted between 11%–13%.
  • Segment Guidance—SAT -- Full-year net sales expected up ~15%, price/currency each +2%, operating margin between 13.5%–15%.
  • Segment Guidance—C&F -- Full-year net sales expected up ~15%, price realization +2.5%, currency +2%, operating margin 9%–11%.
  • Financial Services Net Income -- $244 million in the quarter; full-year outlook raised to $840 million, primarily due to lower credit loss provisions.
  • Net Income Guidance -- Raised to $4.5 billion–$5.0 billion for the fiscal year, with effective tax rate projected at 25%–27%.
  • Operating Cash Flow Guidance -- Equipment operations cash flow expected at $4.5 billion–$5.5 billion, increased by $500 million at both ends of the range.
  • Order Book Trends -- Construction & Forestry order bank up over 50% sequentially; Large Ag tractor order books now provide visibility into the fourth quarter.
  • Used Equipment Inventory -- North America Large Ag used combine inventory roughly 15% below March 2024 peak; 2022/2023 8R tractors down more than 40% from March, and down over 20% sequentially in the quarter.
  • Product Innovation -- New Deere-designed 20-ton class excavators introduced at CONEXPO, marking the start of a multi-year roll-out targeting 40% of the North American construction equipment market.
  • M&A Activity -- Completed acquisition of Tenna, a technology platform providing fleet insights, to complement fleet management and digital strategy in Construction & Forestry.
  • Shareholder Return -- Nearly $750 million returned to shareholders in the quarter through dividends and share repurchases.

Risks

  • PPA operating margin decreased due to "higher tariffs, unfavorable sales mix, and higher warranty expenses."
  • "the Large Ag equipment industry in the U.S. and Canada to decline 15% to 20% this year," highlighting continued market weakness in this core segment.
  • South American tractor and combine industry sales forecasted to drop approximately 5%, attributed to subdued commodity prices, high interest rates, and stronger real.
  • Annual tariff expenses projected at $1.2 billion, with significant impact on cost structure; "price-cost neutral for the full year" guidance reflects inability to fully offset tariffs with pricing actions.

Summary

Deere & Company (DE 2.10%) reported double-digit growth in net sales across all segments, with both Small Ag & Turf and Construction & Forestry exceeding 20% top-line increases, attributed to stronger shipment volumes and favorable currency movements. Management revised full-year guidance upward for net income and operating cash flow, citing improved end-market order trends and discipline in cost and inventory management. The Construction & Forestry order book expanded over 50% sequentially, providing increased visibility and accelerating segment guidance. Product initiatives advanced with the launch of Deere-designed excavators and completion of the Tenna acquisition, reinforcing the company's digital and fleet management strategy. Used equipment inventory improved, notably in high horsepower tractors, suggesting healthier resale markets and improved replacement cycle dynamics.

  • Management characterized 2026 as "the bottom of the current cycle," supported by a modest outlook for mid-single-digit net sales growth in equipment operations.
  • "production costs were lower year over year for all business segments in the first quarter," exclusive of tariffs, highlighting progress on operational efficiencies.
  • In Construction & Forestry, the company observed "contractor confidence" and broad-based strength, especially within infrastructure and large projects, leading to an elevated order backlog and optimism for the remainder of the year.
  • Late model harvest automation adoption increased, as "99% of the combines that were ordered this year through the EOP had some level of harvest automation, and nearly 80% of that were taking the highest level, the Ultimate package."
  • Global row crop production and commodity demand stability, along with U.S. government support programs and improving used equipment markets, were cited as near-term supports for North American Large Ag, despite continued margin pressure in this segment.

Industry glossary

  • EOP (Early Order Program): A sales program allowing dealers or customers to place advance equipment orders that define annual production schedules.
  • Pool Funds: Dealer incentive funds allocated to manage used equipment inventory levels and promote channel health in specific equipment categories.
  • SmartGrade, SmartDetect, SmartWeigh: Integrated Deere-developed precision technology suites for improving task efficiency and accuracy on construction equipment.
  • UFLPA Tariffs: Tariffs associated with the Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act, impacting component sourcing and segment tariff expenses.
  • Engaged Acres: Total number of acres where Deere technology is actively deployed, utilized for strategic measurement of digital adoption.
