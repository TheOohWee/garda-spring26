Earnings Call Transcript

LyondellBasell (LYB) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 30, 2026 at 1:22PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Aug. 1, 2025 at 11:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Peter Z. E. Vanacker
  • Chief Financial Officer — Agustin Izquierdo
  • Executive Vice President, Global Olefins and Polyolefins — Kimberly A. Foley
  • Executive Vice President, Intermediates & Derivatives — Aaron Ledet
  • Executive Vice President, Advanced Polymer Solutions — Torkel Rhenman

TAKEAWAYS

  • EBITDA -- $715 million for the quarter, reflecting sequential improvement due to less downtime and lower feedstock costs.
  • Earnings Per Share -- $0.62, as directly stated in the call.
  • Shareholder Returns -- More than $500 million returned through dividends and share repurchases in the quarter, with a $1.37 per share dividend confirmed for payout on Sept. 2.
  • Cash Balance -- $1.7 billion at period end, above the $1.5 billion target cash level, with total liquidity at $6.35 billion.
  • Cash Improvement Plan -- On track to deliver $600 million in incremental cash flow for 2025, $100 million above the prior target, with cumulative benefits of at least $1.1 billion anticipated for 2025 and 2026.
  • CapEx Guidance -- 2025 CapEx forecast reduced $200 million to $1.7 billion; 2026 CapEx planned at $1.4 billion, excluding $110 million benefit if European asset sale completes.
  • Olefins and Polyolefins Americas Segment EBITDA -- $318 million, up by more than 25% over the previous quarter, driven by higher integrated polyethylene margins and reduced downtime; crackers operated at approximately 90% while the segment averaged 85%.
  • Olefins and Polyolefins Europe, Asia, International Segment EBITDA -- $46 million, up sequentially due to lower naphtha and LPG feedstock costs and stronger seasonal demand.
  • Intermediates and Derivatives EBITDA -- $290 million, increasing by $79 million from the prior quarter, primarily because of improved styrene and propylene oxide margins; $35 million cash benefit from precious metal sales this quarter.
  • Advanced Polymer Solutions EBITDA -- $40 million, similar to the previous quarter despite continued softness in automotive, construction, and electronics markets.
  • Technology Segment EBITDA -- $34 million, down sequentially, caused by inventory cost adjustments and a less favorable sales mix, with licensing activity remaining subdued and low double-digit million EBITDA impact from inventory and exchange rate corrections.
  • European Asset Sale -- Proposed sale of four European O&P assets announced in June, expected to reduce recurring CapEx by $110 million per year upon closing.
  • Flex-2 Project -- Construction deferred to preserve cash, maintaining $300 million lower CapEx in 2026; project rationale described as intact, with timing to be reassessed as market conditions improve.
  • MoReTec-1 -- Chemical recycling plant construction is ongoing in Cologne, Germany, with operations expected to support profitability and company’s circular solutions strategy.
  • Dividend and Buyback Plans -- No further share buybacks planned for 2025 or 2026, reinforcing commitment to preserving the dividend and investment-grade credit rating as underwriting capital allocation decisions.

RISKS

  • CEO Vanacker noted, "this is the longest downturn in my 35 years in the industry," highlighting persistently weak macro conditions and the need for ongoing cash conservation.
  • Ongoing delays in planned growth investments, including the deferment of the Flex-2 and MoReTec-2 projects, are directly framed as a response to uncertainty in the timing of the market recovery.
  • Automotive, building, and construction demand remains constrained, with new housing starts and existing home sales showing continued weakness. Automotive production volumes remained sluggish, and ongoing weakness in new housing starts was described by management.
  • Oxyfuels margins remain "near winter lows" and are "expected to remain low for the remainder of the summer season due to low crude prices and weak gasoline crack spreads."

SUMMARY

LyondellBasell (LYB +2.01%) reported sequential EBITDA improvement and reaffirmed its strategy of disciplined cash management and capital redeployment. Management confirmed a proposed sale of four European O&P assets and ongoing construction of its MoReTec-1 chemical recycling facility in Germany. Dividend payout continuity was strongly reiterated, with no further share buybacks planned for 2025 or 2026. CapEx guidance for both 2025 and 2026 was revised lower, reflecting deferred investment in Flex-2 and MoReTec-2 projects to prioritize liquidity amidst what was described as the industry’s longest downturn.

  • CEO Vanacker emphasized that actions continue to focus on securing the dividend while maintaining the benefits of the investment-grade rating.
  • Management stated, "our cash improvement plan is on track to improve our cash flow over the years 2025 and 2026, as set by at least, I mean, $1.1 billion."
  • Kimberly A. Foley highlighted increased export demand and noted, "Domestic demand is up year-on-year, quarter-on-quarter and month-on-month, hence, the support for the June increase," pointing to rare positive market momentum.
  • Olefins and Polyolefins Americas segment operating rates are guided to remain strong at 85% in the third quarter, with additional expected margin tailwinds due to the June domestic price increase and lower cracker downtime.
  • The company cited a 75% EBITDA-to-cash conversion rate over the past 12 months, approaching the long-term target of 80%, and continues to stress disciplined working capital and fixed cost management.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • MoReTec: LyondellBasell's proprietary advanced chemical recycling technology, designed for scalable processing of plastic waste into feedstock for new plastics.
  • Flex-2 project: Deferred U.S.-based investment initiative targeting monomer balance and growth, subject to future market conditions.
  • PPWR (Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation): European Union regulation aiming to enhance circularity, including recycled plastic requirements.
  • SUPD (Single-Use Plastics Directive): EU directive regulating the reduction and management of single-use plastic products.
  • PO/TBA unit: Production facility for propylene oxide and tertiary butyl alcohol, integral to LyondellBasell’s U.S. Gulf Coast operations.
