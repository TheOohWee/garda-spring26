Wynn (WYNN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 5:50PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 12, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Craig Billings
  • Chief Financial Officer — Julie Cameron-Doe
  • President, Wynn Las Vegas — Brian Gullbrants

TAKEAWAYS

  • Wynn Las Vegas EBITDA -- $240.8 million in adjusted property EBITDA and an EBITDA margin of 35%, with hold adding just over $8 million to EBITDA.
  • Wynn Las Vegas Operating Revenue -- $688.1 million was reported, with daily operating expenses (excluding gaming tax) at $4.6 million, an increase of 4.1% from last year due to payroll, repairs, and bad debt.
  • Encore Boston Harbor EBITDA -- $57 million in adjusted property EBITDA on $210.2 million in revenue, generating a 27.1% margin; slot revenues rose over 2%, setting a segment record.
  • Macau Operations EBITDA -- Adjusted property EBITDA reached $270.9 million on $967.7 million in operating revenue, equating to a 28% EBITDA margin; low VIP hold reduced EBITDA by just over $16 million and mass hold was about 250 basis points below the previous year.
  • Group and Convention Outlook -- Forward visibility remains strong, with expectations to increase both group room nights and rates for Las Vegas in 2026 compared to 2025.
  • Encore Tower Remodel Impact -- The Las Vegas Encore Tower remodel, starting mid-May, will remove approximately 80,000 room nights through a 12-month process and may result in a slight headwind for full-year performance.
  • Macau Volume Growth -- VIP turnover increased 48% and mass drop rose 18% year-on-year, but EBITDA was impacted by low hold; January volumes slightly exceeded fourth-quarter levels.
  • Chairman's Club at Wynn Palace -- A 63,000 square foot expansion targeting premium customers will open for Chinese New Year, tripling the club’s size to nearly 100,000 square feet with new gaming and amenities.
  • Liquidity Position -- Global cash and revolving credit availability stood at $4.7 billion, split into $2.9 billion in Macau and $1.8 billion in the U.S., supporting a net leverage ratio just over 4.4x.
  • Quarterly Dividend -- A $0.25 per share cash dividend was declared, payable March 4, 2026, to shareholders of record on February 23, 2026.
  • CapEx and Al Marjan Island Spend -- Approximately $171.2 million in capital expenditures was reported in the quarter for various projects, with an additional $79.2 million equity contribution to Wynn Al Marjan Island, bringing cumulative equity invested to $914.2 million and remaining commitment estimated at $450 million to $550 million.
  • Outlook for OpEx -- Las Vegas daily OpEx (excluding major event periods) is guided in the $4.3 million–$4.5 million range; Macau OpEx per day is positioned between $2.7 million–$2.9 million, consistent with prior guidance.
  • Non-USD Revenue Diversification -- Management projects over 55% of annual revenue will be generated in non-U.S. dollar-denominated markets after Wynn Al Marjan opens.
  • Wynn Al Marjan Island Construction -- The project reached its 70th-floor topping milestone, with interior fit-out underway and exterior glass 80% complete, and rooms expected to go on sale in late Q3 or early Q4 2026.
  • Boston Hotel Expansion -- No direct hotel development by Wynn in Boston is planned; rather, land leasing under a memorandum of understanding is intended for third-party hotel development adjacent to Encore Boston Harbor.

RISKS

  • Low VIP and mass table hold in Macau reduced fourth-quarter EBITDA by just over $16 million, and mass hold was about 250 basis points below the prior year, negatively impacting EBITDA margin.
  • The upcoming Encore Tower remodel in Las Vegas will remove about 80,000 room nights in 2026, creating a modest headwind for the year, with the process extending into 2027.
  • Rising operating expenses across segments linked to payroll, bad debt, and cost of living increases present margin pressures, with Las Vegas daily OpEx coming in above guided range for a heavy event quarter.

SUMMARY

Wynn Resorts, Limited (WYNN 0.85%) reported resilient fourth-quarter results, led by stable Las Vegas and Encore Boston Harbor operations, despite margin pressures from higher operating expenses and weak table hold in Macau. The company highlighted forward momentum with increased VIP and mass volumes in Macau, progress on Wynn Al Marjan Island construction, and an expanded Chairman’s Club catering to top-tier clients. Liquidity and capital allocation remained disciplined, reflected in strong cash reserves and a continued shareholder dividend. Operational strategies emphasized rate optimization, targeted group business, and variable reinvestment to sustain share gains across geographies.

  • Management reinforced that third-party hotel development in Boston, not on Wynn's own balance sheet, is part of a larger land-lease strategy supporting urban revitalization and future integration benefits to Encore Boston Harbor.
  • Approximately $171.2 million in capital expenditures during the quarter addressed multi-market property enhancements and maintenance, while the incremental investment in Wynn Al Marjan Island moved total equity deployed to $914.2 million.
  • Chairman’s Club expansion was completed in record time and positioned to serve premium customers immediately during Chinese New Year, with expectations to "set a new standard for premium gaming space in Macau."
  • Growth strategies focus on affluent global clientele and geographic diversification, with a strategic shift toward non-U.S. dollar revenue streams accounting for over 55% of projected portfolio revenues post-Al Marjan launch.
  • Leadership transition was announced as Julie Cameron-Doe prepares to retire as CFO before the next earnings call; no successor or interim appointment details were offered on the call.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • VIP Hold: The realized win percentage on high-denomination table games; low VIP hold can cause material EBITDA volatility.
  • Adjusted Property EBITDA: Segment operating profit before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, rent, and certain other non-recurring items; core measure of resort-level profitability.
  • Mass Drop: Cash wagered by mass-market players at table games, a key volume metric for Macau operations.
  • ADR (Average Daily Rate): The average daily price paid by hotel guests, a primary driver of hotel revenue performance.
  • RevPAR (Revenue per Available Room): Total room revenue divided by available rooms, capturing occupancy and rate performance.
  • Hold: The percentage of total gaming wager that the casino retains as revenue; “low hold” refers to realized results falling below statistical expectations.
  • GGR (Gross Gaming Revenue): Total casino win or gaming revenue before deducting promotional allowances and taxes; used as a key industry growth indicator, especially in Macau.
