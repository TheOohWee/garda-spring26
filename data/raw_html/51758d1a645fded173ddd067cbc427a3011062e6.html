Earnings Call Transcript

Novartis (NVS) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 5, 2026 at 11:49AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, October 29, 2024 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Vas Narasimhan
  • Chief Financial Officer — Harry Kirsch

TAKEAWAYS

  • Sales Growth -- Net sales increased 10% in constant currency, reflecting sustained momentum across major brands.
  • Core Operating Income -- Core operating income rose 20% in constant currency, supported by portfolio volume expansion and margin improvement.
  • Core Margin -- Core margin reached 40.1%, up 340 basis points year over year.
  • Core EPS -- Core earnings per share were $2.06, a 20% increase from the prior year.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Free cash flow totaled $6 billion, noted as the highest ever for any quarter in company history.
  • Entresto Sales -- Entresto delivered a 26% global sales increase with 25% TRx growth and continued strong international expansion; U.S. loss of exclusivity is forecast for mid-2025.
  • Cosentyx Performance -- Cosentyx showed 28% sales growth overall, with U.S. sales up 38% and ex-U.S. sales up 16% in constant currency, underpinned by strong uptake in hidradenitis suppurativa (HS) and IV formulation.
  • Kisqali Expansion -- Kisqali achieved 43% sales growth, including a 50% increase in the U.S., gaining a Category 1 NCCN guideline recommendation for the full population and broad FDA approval for early breast cancer.
  • Pluvicto Trajectory -- Pluvicto sales grew 50%; when adjusted for a one-time price effect in Europe, growth was 36%, and the PSMAfore filing acceptance positions the therapy for a launch that could triple eligible patient numbers in 2025.
  • Kesimpta Uptake -- Kesimpta realized 28% growth, or 56% after removing a one-time Europe-related revenue deduction, and exceeded 100,000 patients treated worldwide.
  • Leqvio Growth -- Leqvio demonstrated accelerating uptake, with 4,600 ordering facilities and reimbursement in 39 countries, and outpaced the advanced lipid-lowering market.
  • Scemblix Surge -- Scemblix sales increased 72%, with a 26% rise in TRx share and 18% quarter-over-quarter demand growth, augmented by the FDA granting Priority Review for first-line chronic myeloid leukemia (CML).
  • Fabhalta Launch -- Fabhalta secured U.S. accelerated approval for IgA nephropathy following positive interim Phase III results and exhibited high patient access and compliance in initial launch metrics.
  • Raised Guidance -- Management raised full-year 2024 guidance for the third time, now expecting low double-digit sales growth and core operating income growth in the high teens, assuming no U.S. generic entries for Tasigna, Promacta, or Entresto in 2024.
  • Capital Return -- Novartis paid $7.6 billion in dividends and continued executing a $15 billion share buyback program, with approximately $8 billion remaining through 2025.
  • FX/Guidance Impact -- Currency headwinds reduced net sales by 1% and core operating income by 3%; similar effects are embedded in full-year and preliminary 2025 modeling assumptions.
  • Pipeline Adjustments -- The XXB program was terminated due to a safety signal in heart failure and insufficient blood pressure reduction on standard of care.

RISKS

  • Kirsch indicated that foreign exchange is expected to reduce core operating income by 3% to 4% for the full year if current rates persist.
  • Narasimhan disclosed the XXB pipeline termination, stating, "We did suffer a few setbacks with XXB. We will terminate this program. We saw a safety signal in heart failure. And overall, the hypertension blood pressure reduction, we saw on top of standard of care was not sufficient to meet the CPP we think we need to achieve for this medicine."

SUMMARY

Novartis (NVS 0.65%) delivered double-digit net sales and core operating income growth, setting a company record for quarterly free cash flow and achieving a 40.1% core margin. Management implemented its third guidance upgrade this year, reflecting robust commercial expansion and ongoing portfolio optimization. Near-term milestones include the PSMAfore launch for Pluvicto and broad commercialization initiatives across recent approvals, with generic exposure for key drugs forecast mid-next year.

  • Narasimhan said, "the 10% and the 20% growth. We've raised our full-year 2024 guidance for the third time; this is showing the underlying momentum we're seeing across our growth drivers and new launches."
  • Shareholder capital returns remain a priority, supported by ongoing M&A, bolt-on transactions, and a sizable buyback commitment.
  • Kisqali's expanded indication more than doubles its eligible patient population relative to its prior metastatic-only use, increasing addressable market scale.
  • CFO Kirsch noted, "free cash flow was basically $6 billion, the highest we have ever achieved in any one quarter."
  • The company is preparing additional submissions and manufacturing capacity expansions to capture future growth in key global markets.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • TRx: Total prescriptions, a metric for cumulative drug dispensation volume.
  • NBRx: New-to-brand prescriptions, reflecting prescriptions written for patients newly initiated on a specific drug.
  • HS: Hidradenitis suppurativa, a chronic inflammatory skin condition and new growth indication for Cosentyx.
  • IV: Intravenous formulation, referring here to Cosentyx's new mode of administration.
  • PSMAfore: A clinical program/population under investigation for Pluvicto in metastatic prostate cancer, expected to triple addressable patients upon launch.
  • LoE: Loss of exclusivity, indicating when a product faces generic competition due to patent expiry.
  • PNH: Paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria, a rare disease indication for Fabhalta.
  • IgA nephropathy: A chronic kidney disease treated by Fabhalta following accelerated approval.
  • CML: Chronic myeloid leukemia, the lead indication for Scemblix expansion.
  • NCCN: National Comprehensive Cancer Network, issuer of guideline recommendations referenced for Kisqali.
  • Lp(a): Lipoprotein(a), a cardiovascular disease risk marker and target for Pelacarsen in the pipeline.
  • RLT: Radioligand therapy, a growth platform for both pipeline and bolt-on M&A activity.
  • siRNA: Small interfering RNA, a modality for gene-silencing therapies under development at Novartis.
  • APPLAUSE/ALITHIOS/APPEAR-C3G: Names of clinical trials for various pipeline assets discussed during the call.
