Highwoods (HIW) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 12:18PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 11, 2026 at 11:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Theodore J. Klinck
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Brendan Maiorana
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Operating Officer — Brian M. Leary

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Funds from operations (FFO) -- $0.90 per share in Q4, including $0.06 per share of land sale gains; full-year FFO was $3.48 per share.
  • Initial 2026 FFO guidance -- $3.40-$3.68 per share ($3.54 midpoint), a 5.7% increase at midpoint compared to prior year’s initial outlook.
  • Development pipeline pre-leased -- 78% pre-leased, up from 72% last quarter, and 56% one year ago.
  • Leasing activity (Q4) -- 526,000 square feet of second-generation space leased (221,000 square feet new leases); 95,000 square feet of first-generation leases in development pipeline.
  • Net effective rent growth -- Net effective rents rose 20% compared to 2024, surpassing the company’s historic peak from 2022.
  • Asset acquisitions -- $472 million invested in 2025 acquisitions, including the $223 million purchase of 600 at Legacy Union in Charlotte; January activity included $318 million of properties in Raleigh and Dallas (HIW share: $108 million, plus $13 million preferred equity).
  • Dispositions -- $66 million of non-core assets sold in Q4; an additional $42 million closed in Richmond post year-end; another $190 million-$210 million of dispositions targeted by mid-2026.
  • Portfolio leased rate -- Over 89% leased at year-end; Charlotte portfolio at "mid-nineties" percent leased.
  • Same property NOI -- 2026 same property cash NOI expected to be roughly flat; GAAP same property NOI projected 150 basis points higher than cash basis.
  • Weighted average lease terms -- Q4 deals averaged nearly six years; expansions outpaced contractions by 2.5 to one in Q4, and over three to one for 2025.
  • Bond issuance -- $350 million of unsecured bonds issued in Q4, providing liquidity and positioning the company to repay a $300 million March 2027 maturity without new 2026 bond issuance.
  • Asset rotation effect on portfolio age -- Portfolio weighted average vintage improved by more than two years to 2007 following asset rotation activities.
  • Projected occupancy -- Year-end 2026 occupancy guided to 87.5%, with expected quarterly progression reflecting development completions and asset recycling.
  • Rent spreads -- Cash rent spreads positive in Q4; GAAP rent spreads for 2025 at 16.4%.

Summary

Management emphasized that most of the $50 million-$60 million NOI growth potential from the eight key properties is expected to materialize in 2027 and beyond, rather than in 2026. The company’s asset recycling program, swapping older and non-core properties for newer Sunbelt BBD assets, is projected to be leverage-neutral and modestly accretive to near-term FFO after all planned dispositions are completed. Highwoods Properties (HIW +3.00%) reported that no lease expirations greater than 100,000 square feet are scheduled before mid-2027, limiting large move-out risk for the next several years. The $223 million 600 at Legacy Union acquisition is temporarily dilutive in 2026 due to low initial occupancy (44% occupied, 89% leased) and is projected to generate GAAP NOI of approximately $10 million in 2026 and more than $18 million in 2027 upon stabilization. Management noted that 2026 FFO will be temporarily reduced by $0.09 per share, attributable to timing of bond issuance, stabilization of new acquisitions, and asset rotation, but confirmed that this reduction will not persist into 2027 or later years.

  • Brendan Maiorana said, "We plan to fund these acquisitions on a leverage-neutral basis, primarily through the sale of non-core assets or other properties where value has been maximized."
  • The new shelf registration with the SEC was described as a routine process every three years, and management confirmed, "the FFO per share outlook that we provided in last night's release assumes no ATM issuances during 2026."
  • Future investment activity could include up to $200 million of new development starts, and up to $250 million in acquisitions, as included in the 2026 outlook.
  • Cash dividend coverage was temporarily short in 2025 by $13 million-$14 million, mainly due to elevated leasing capital expenditures, with expectations of lower capital spending and improved coverage in 2026.
  • The portfolio's exposure to potential AI-driven tenant disruption was addressed directly by management, who said, "We really haven't. I mean, obviously, what we try and tell you on the call is what we're seeing with boots on the ground. And, you know, we all see the narrative on AI, whether it be soft companies a week or so ago, financials the other day. It's just not what we're seeing from our customers and on the demand. I mean, we continue to see in-migration coming to our markets. Companies are taking more space, not less space. Our own operating portfolio, expansions continue to outpace contractions."

Industry glossary

  • BBD: Best Business Districts; primary submarkets with high tenant demand and superior asset performance within major Sunbelt metros.
  • GAAP NOI: Net Operating Income calculated according to Generally Accepted Accounting Principles, including straight-line rent adjustments and amortization.
  • Land sale gains: One-time income recognized from the sale of land parcels, which can meaningfully impact reported FFO in certain periods.
  • Mark-to-market: The practice of resetting in-place rents to prevailing market rates as leases expire or are renewed, driving potential upside in effective rents.
  • ATM program: At-the-market equity offering mechanism allowing REITs to issue shares opportunistically into the open market to raise capital.
  • Joint venture (JV): Structure whereby Highwoods shares equity ownership of properties with institutional or strategic partners, enabling capital efficiency and market access.
