TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $447 million for the quarter, up 9.8%, reflecting higher pricing (5%), volumes (4%), and favorable foreign exchange (1%).
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $85 million or 19.1% margin for the quarter, identical margin to prior year despite higher absolute value.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $0.66 for the quarter, up from $0.58, with adjusted EPS at $2.73 for the year.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $31 million for the quarter and $158 million for the year, driven by working capital improvement partly offset by higher non-trade receivables.
  • Gross Margin -- $127 million for the quarter, up from $107 million, supported by favorable pricing and volumes and partially offset by higher logistics, duties, and manufacturing costs.
  • Full-Year Sales -- $1.764 billion, a 5.7% increase, with growth attributed primarily to pricing and higher volumes, partially offset by FX headwinds.
  • Capital Returns -- $78 million returned to shareholders during the year through $61 million in share buybacks and $17 million in dividends; $69 million remains authorized for repurchase.
  • Segment Realignment -- The Cook Filter acquisition established a new Industrial Solutions segment; Cook Filter revenues included from January 7 close.
  • 2026 Revenue Guidance -- Company expects $1.945 billion to $2.015 billion, a projected increase of 10%-14%.
  • Power Solutions Outlook -- Anticipated revenue range of $1.79 billion to $1.85 billion, reflecting approximately 3% midpoint year-over-year growth; aftermarket to remain flat.
  • Industrial Solutions Outlook -- Revenue expected at $155 million to $165 million, with guidance excluding $3 million in pre-acquisition “stub” sales.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin Guidance -- Expected to be 19.5%-20.5% for the company in 2026.
  • 2026 Adjusted EPS Guidance -- $2.75 to $3 per share provided in guidance.
  • Cook Filter Data Center Exposure -- 8% of acquired business tied to data centers and experiencing “high teens” percentage growth.
  • Foreign Exchange Impact -- U.S. dollar expected to weaken, contributing a 1% revenue tailwind in 2026.
  • Leverage and Liquidity -- Amended five-year credit facility with $1 billion term loan and $500 million revolver; total liquidity of $701 million post-acquisition; leverage ratio approximately 2.1x.
  • Tariffs and Pricing -- Company expects 1% core pricing in 2026, with no incremental tariff-related pricing assumed; expects to remain price-cost neutral on tariffs based on existing trade agreements as of February 1.
  • Share Gain Expectations -- Management anticipates 1%-2% share growth on top of existing gains now embedded in the base business.
  • One-Time Items -- Fourth-quarter $8 million asset impairment charge excluded from adjusted results; $16 million in one-time separation costs for the full year.
  • Gross Margin Synergies -- Synergies identified from the Cook Filter acquisition are expected primarily from procurement and innovation, with integration targeted for completion in the first six months of 2026.

SUMMARY

Full-year adjusted free cash flow improved to $158 million, attributed to better working capital management despite a rise in non-trade receivables related to VAT recoveries. Share repurchase guidance for 2026 indicates an intended buyback of $20 million to $40 million, reflecting ongoing capital allocation discipline. The industrial air filtration “platform” was formally established with the acquisition of Cook Filter, introducing a new reporting segment and diversified exposure, notably to high-growth data center applications. Management reiterated a disciplined integration focus for Cook Filter through mid-year, with procurement synergies already underway and innovation workshops launched to leverage in-house filtration media expertise. The outlook for the Power Solutions segment indicates U.S. heavy- and medium-duty markets may be flat to up 10% due to anticipated weaker first-half demand and recovery later in the year. The 2027 NOx emissions standards remain expected by management to hold, with pre-buy activity in 2026 included in guidance. Cited foreign exchange and potential regulatory shifts could have further revenue and product-cycle impacts.

  • The company transitioned fully to a global Atmus distribution network, granting direct control over customer experience and aftermarket growth channels.
  • Cook Filter’s commercial and industrial HVAC business is expected to grow broadly in line with GDP, with high variability in 2026 market conditions outlined in guidance.
  • Management described ongoing initiatives to reduce costs and drive supply chain transformation as key to margin resilience and future incrementals.
  • Integration of Cook Filter is prioritized for operational continuity before addressing insourcing and larger innovation opportunities in the segment.
  • Adjusted earnings per share gains resulted partly from lower separation costs and improvements in overall efficiency, despite a slightly higher effective tax rate of 22.1%.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • First Fit: Sales of filters installed by original equipment manufacturers on new vehicles or machinery, distinct from aftermarket replacement.
  • Stub Period: The time between the close of an acquisition and the company’s next scheduled reporting period, impacting annual revenue recognition.
  • NOx Standards: Regulatory limits for nitrogen oxides emissions from vehicles, affecting product design and timing of commercial vehicle sales cycles.
