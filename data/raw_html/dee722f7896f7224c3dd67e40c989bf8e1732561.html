Bio-Rad (BIO) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 6:07PM EST

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 12, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Norman Schwartz
  • President and Chief Operating Officer — Jonathan DiVincenzo
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Roop Lakkaraju

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Net Sales -- $693 million for the quarter, up 3.9% on a reported basis, and 1.7% on a currency-neutral basis, driven primarily by growth in Clinical Diagnostics.
  • Life Science Segment Sales -- $268 million, down 2.6% reported, and 4% currency-neutral, attributed to constrained academic and biotech funding.
  • Clinical Diagnostics Segment Sales -- $425 million, up 8.4% reported, and 5.6% currency-neutral, with gains led by quality control and blood typing products.
  • GAAP Gross Margin -- 49.8%, down from 51.2% in the prior-year quarter due to higher supply chain costs.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 52.5%, a decrease from 53.9%, with $13 million in write-offs linked to portfolio rationalization.
  • SG&A Expense -- $221 million, or 31.9% of sales, up from 30.6%, primarily due to higher employee-related costs.
  • Non-GAAP R&D Expense -- $66 million, versus $68 million last year, with the decrease tied to prior in-process R&D charges.
  • Operating Loss -- $119 million GAAP loss, including $173 million of impairment charges for discontinued or reprioritized R&D programs.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin -- 12%, a decline from 13.8% in the previous year, reflecting lower gross margin impact.
  • Non-GAAP Net Income -- $68 million, or $2.51 per diluted share, compared to $81 million, or $2.90 per share, last year.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $119 million for the quarter; $375 million for the year, translating to a 138% conversion ratio versus non-GAAP net income.
  • Share Repurchases -- 1.2 million shares retired in 2025 at a total cost of $296 million; no shares repurchased in the quarter.
  • 2026 Guidance, Currency-Neutral Revenue Growth -- Projected at 0.5%-1.5% for the year, with Q1 expected to be down low single digits year over year, and sequential improvement each quarter.
  • Life Science Segment 2026 Guidance -- Currency-neutral growth expected between 0% and 0.5%; core business (ex-process chromatography) guided to nearly 4% growth.
  • Process Chromatography Guidance -- Expected to decline mid-teens percentage due to government regulations changes and customer efficiencies; projected to be a mid-single-digit growth area long-term.
  • ddPCR Platform -- Delivered mid-single-digit year-over-year growth; QX700 instrument sales accelerated and are projected to maintain mid-single-digit expansion in 2026.
  • Clinical Diagnostics 2026 Guidance -- Currency-neutral growth projected at 1%-2%, with quality controls growing mid-single digits; remainder of Diagnostics portfolio set for low single-digit growth.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin Guidance -- Full-year range projected between 54% and 54.5%.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin Guidance -- Anticipated between 12% and 12.5%, reflecting improvement initiatives and a 50-basis-point headwind from process chromatography.
  • Available Share Repurchase Authorization -- $285 million remains under the Board-authorized buyback plan.

RISKS

  • Process chromatography business faces near-term revenue declines, as "recent changes to government regulations on certain therapeutics usage and vaccines as well as our customers' improved production efficiencies" are expected to cause a mid-teens percentage decline.
  • Gross margin was negatively impacted by "higher-than-anticipated supply chain costs," notably from expedited freight and service costs following the extended U.S. government shutdown, which management described as "execution-related rather than structural."
  • Year-over-year decrease in non-GAAP operating margin, which management attributed to lower gross margins and segment mix headwinds.
  • Ongoing cautious spending in academic and government end markets continues to weigh on Life Science instrument demand, with management stating academic institutions are "focused on maintaining staffing levels and sustaining ongoing research rather than purchasing capital equipment."

SUMMARY

Bio-Rad Laboratories (BIO +0.63%) reported net sales and operating margin within revised guidance, but gross margin fell short of management expectations due to higher supply chain expenses and execution challenges. The quarter saw Diagnostics return to growth, driven by strength in quality control and blood typing products, while Life Science revenue weakened largely because of academic and biotech funding constraints and a decline in process chromatography. Management outlined focused cost-control and operational initiatives designed to deliver sequential margin and productivity improvements, guided modest top-line growth, and detailed segment-level expectations for 2026. Company leadership emphasized ongoing portfolio optimization, the anticipated mid-2026 accretion of the Stilla acquisition, and continued commitment to disciplined innovation, particularly within digital PCR and quality control platforms.

  • Company ended the quarter with $1.541 billion in cash and short-term investments, and $741 million in inventory, showing a reduction from the prior year-end.
  • Net cash from operating activities reached $165 million for the quarter, with annual net cash from operations at $532 million, both improved versus the previous year.
  • Management confirmed that savings from completed 2025 restructuring are "reflected in our 2026 outlook."
  • No shares were repurchased during the fourth quarter, but total buybacks since Q1 2024 amount to $494 million, reducing share count by 6.6%.
  • Bio-Rad deferred its Investor Day while continuing a business transformation focused on product portfolio assessment and cost structure improvement.
  • Free cash flow guidance for 2026 was given at $375 million to $395 million.
  • The Stilla acquisition is expected to be accretive by mid-2026, approximately 6–12 months ahead of initial expectations.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ddPCR (Droplet Digital PCR): A digital polymerase chain reaction platform that partitions samples into thousands of droplets for precise nucleic acid quantification, used widely in genetic, clinical, and research applications.
  • Process Chromatography: A segment of bioprocessing that involves separating and purifying biological molecules, often used in the manufacturing of therapeutics and vaccines.
  • QX700: Bio-Rad’s digital PCR instrument family, cited as a growth driver within the company's ddPCR portfolio.
  • Quality Controls: Reference standards or reagents for validating accuracy and reliability of diagnostic test results in clinical laboratories.
  • VBP: Volume-based procurement, a policy approach often affecting pricing and reimbursements for medical products in China.
