Aptiv (APTV) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 6:42PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, October 30, 2025 at 11:06 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Kevin P. Clark
  • Chief Financial Officer and Vice Chairman — Varun Laroyia

Risks

  • Varun Laroyia disclosed, "The impairment charge of $648 million for Wind River is the result of our regular impairment testing of our reporting units. The charge reflects slower than originally expected growth in the business over 2023 and 2024, owing to delays in 5G adoption and the launch of software-defined vehicles."
  • Clark stated, "timing of some program awards slated for the fourth quarter could shift into 2026," highlighting potential variability in new business bookings.
  • Laroyia indicated a "revenue headwind of approximately $80 million" in the fourth quarter from "recent known disruptions and production adjustments at a few of our OEMs in North America and Europe."
  • Clark noted "an outlook for the fourth quarter that prudently incorporates an element of conservatism to reflect the amplified trade tensions beginning to impact semiconductor supply chains."

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $5.2 billion in the third quarter, up 6% on an adjusted basis, reflecting strong performance across core markets and higher vehicle production volumes in North America and China.
  • Operating income -- $654 million in the third quarter, an increase of 10%, benefiting from volume growth and operating leverage.
  • Operating income margin -- Expanded by 30 basis points in the third quarter, offsetting 130 basis points of FX and commodities headwind, primarily from the Mexican peso and copper.
  • Earnings per share -- $2.17, up 19% as a result of higher operating income, share repurchases, and lower interest expense, partially offset by increased tax expense.
  • Operating cash flow -- $584 million, with capital expenditures of $143 million; nearly $250 million deployed for share repurchases and debt paydown.
  • Third quarter bookings -- $8.4 billion in new awards, bringing year-to-date bookings to roughly $19 billion; guidance for $31 billion for the full year remains, but some awards could shift into 2026.
  • Segment revenue -- Advanced Safety & User Experience (AS & UX) segment reported flat sales on an adjusted basis with over 20% growth in Wind River, offset by roll-off of a legacy infotainment program and China program cancellations; Engineered Components Group (ECG) up 6% to $1.7 billion on an adjusted basis; Electrical Distribution Systems (EDS) up 11% to $2.3 billion on an adjusted basis.
  • Non-auto end market revenues -- Delivered double-digit growth, with non-automotive revenue approaching $3 billion year-to-date.
  • Wind River impairment -- Noncash goodwill impairment charge of $648 million "reflects slower than originally expected growth," but management expects mid-teens revenue growth for Wind River in 2025.
  • Regional results -- North America revenue grew 14%; Europe declined 3%; China was flat, with ECG offsetting AS & UX headwinds.
  • Segment margins -- AS & UX adjusted operating income down 16%; ECG adjusted operating margin up 20 basis points; EDS adjusted operating income up 54% with over 200 basis points of adjusted margin expansion.
  • Balance sheet -- Over $1.6 billion in cash, $4.2 billion in liquidity; gross leverage at 2.4x, net leverage at 1.8x, now in line with pre-ASR program levels.
  • Guidance -- Full-year adjusted revenue outlook of $20.3 billion, adjusted growth of 2%; adjusted EBITDA and adjusted operating income at $3.22 billion and $2.45 billion, respectively; adjusted EPS range $7.55-$7.85, up 23% at midpoint; operating cash flow expected at approximately $2 billion.
  • Conservatism in outlook -- Fourth quarter guidance incorporates an $80 million revenue headwind from known customer and supply chain disruptions, and additional conservatism for trade-tension-related semiconductor supply issues.
  • EDS separation -- Aptiv is progressing with the planned separation of the Electrical Distribution Systems business by the end of Q1 2026.

Summary

Aptiv (APTV 0.57%) reported record financial results, driven by strong vehicle production in North America and China, notable growth in non-automotive markets, and robust new business awards. The company incurred a $648 million goodwill impairment related to Wind River, citing slower 5G and software-defined vehicle adoption, but reaffirmed long-term structural growth for that unit. Management increased full-year guidance based on third quarter strength, while explicitly incorporating expected revenue headwinds in the fourth quarter from OEM disruptions and geopolitical impacts on supply chains. The upcoming separation of the EDS business, targeted for Q1 2026, was cited as a strategic initiative intended to enhance shareholder value and focus. Bookings momentum continues, although management warned some large awards may shift to 2026 due to customer timing and trade-policy uncertainty.

  • Clark emphasized the company's high USMCA compliance, stating, "As a reminder, our direct exposure to tariffs is minimal, in large part because of a high compliance with USMCA and our low level of non-USMCA imports into the United States. In the limited areas where we have exposure and cannot change sourcing owing to the industry setup, we have largely been able to pass on the incremental costs." in limited areas where tariff exposure exists and sourcing cannot be shifted.
  • Laroyia stated that non-auto end market business is "rapidly approaching almost $4 billion" and is growing "high single digits, almost double digits," outpacing auto segment growth rates.
  • Clark said, "sitting here today, 2025 revenues from Smart Vehicle Architecture will be about $150 million to $200 million and growing at about a 10% sort of growth rate into the out years."
  • Management stated accelerated program launches and high bookings with China OEMs, highlighting export opportunities and positioning with top domestic manufacturers focused on global markets.
  • Clark described the Gen 8 radar product as "industry-leading" and outlined incremental share opportunities through displacement of legacy sensor technologies and expansion of advanced safety solutions.

Industry glossary

  • Smart Vehicle Architecture (SVA): Aptiv's platform for modular, scalable electrical and electronics vehicle integration, enabling zonal controllers and advanced software-defined vehicle capabilities.
  • ADAS: Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems—vehicle features designed to automate and enhance driving safety.
  • Wind River: Subsidiary specializing in real-time operating systems, software, and edge AI for mission-critical automotive and industrial applications.
  • USMCA: United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement—trade pact governing automotive and component sourcing requirements in North America.
