Iridium (IRDM) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 10, 2026 at 4:40PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Oct. 17, 2024 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Matt Desch
  • Chief Financial Officer — Tom Fitzpatrick
  • Incoming Chief Financial Officer — Vincent O'Neill
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Ken Levy

Risks

  • Net subscriber additions in IoT declined this quarter "due to a change that the same personal communications customer made as they simplified their available subscription plan," which management expects to continue for several quarters, though "to fully abate by the end of 2025."
  • Sequential net broadband subscriber losses and channel instability identified, with Tom Fitzpatrick stating, "it as not stable. I mean, there's a lot of partners that are dealing with the new reality," though expectations are for eventual improvement.

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $212.8 million, representing an 8% increase, reflecting growth in commercial business lines and engineering and support revenue.
  • Operational EBITDA -- $124.4 million, a record result primarily driven by service revenue expansion.
  • Commercial service revenue -- $133.3 million, up 6%, with growth attributed to IoT, hosted payload, and the Satelles acquisition.
  • Commercial voice and data revenue -- $57.7 million, a 3% increase due to subscriber growth and sustained Push-to-Talk demand.
  • Commercial IoT revenue -- $43.7 million, up 14%, benefiting from a two-year contract with a major partner and continued demand for personal and industrial satellite communications.
  • Hosting and other data services revenue -- $16.4 million, up 9%, primarily reflecting contributions from Iridium PNT following the Satelles acquisition.
  • Government service revenue -- $26.5 million, reflecting an annual EMSS contract bill rate increase to $107 million effective Sept. 15.
  • Subscriber equipment revenue -- $22.2 million, up 9%, marking a positive shift after previous declines and signaling normalized channel demand.
  • Engineering and support revenue -- $30.7 million, representing a 22% increase, led by U.S. government activity and expanded Space Development Agency work.
  • Shareholder returns -- $146 million returned via dividends and repurchases in the quarter; 4.7 million shares repurchased, reducing share count by roughly 4%, and setting a quarterly record since the program's inception.
  • Buyback authorization -- Total repurchase authorization increased by $500 million in September, leaving $552 million outstanding as of quarter-end.
  • Dividend -- $0.14 per common share paid, with an expected $65 million in dividends to be returned for the year.
  • Cash and equivalents -- $159.6 million on hand at Sept. 30.
  • Term loan balance -- $1.8 billion outstanding after a July increase to facilitate additional share repurchases.
  • Net leverage -- Ended the quarter at approximately 3.5 times EBITDA; the company expects leverage to remain below four times EBITDA through 2026, targeting below two times by the end of 2030.
  • Capital expenditures -- $18.6 million in the quarter; full-year 2024 CapEx expected to be $70 million, focused on new product initiatives such as Iridium NTN Direct.
  • Proforma free cash flow projection -- Projected at approximately $300 million for the year, with a 64% EBITDA-to-free cash flow conversion rate and free cash flow yield of about 8.9%.
  • Project Stardust/Iridium NTN Direct -- Service slated for early 2026; Iridium technology included in 3GPP Release 19, supporting integration with standard chipsets for consumer and IoT applications.
  • Satelles acquisition impact -- Transitioned PNT from a wholesale to a retail model, expanding addressable market and positioning Iridium as a leader in alternate PNT.
  • Space Development Agency contract -- Cumulative value escalated to approximately $400 million, with $260 million remaining to be recognized through 2029.
  • Guidance update -- Full-year service revenue growth expected to be approximately 5%; full-year operational EBITDA guided to $465 million-$470 million.
  • Personnel change -- CFO Tom Fitzpatrick retiring at year-end, to be succeeded by Vincent O'Neill.

Summary

The call detailed new product launches and partnerships, such as the integration of Iridium Communications (IRDM 1.30%) technology into 3GPP Release 19 and collaboration with Nordic Semiconductor, that broaden Iridium Communications' reach in direct-to-device and standards-based IoT markets. Management disclosed a significant expansion in engineering and support revenue, underpinned by an enlarged Space Development Agency contract, and confirmed the acceleration of PNT business growth after the Satelles acquisition. The firm emphasized record capital returns to shareholders, further buyback capacity, and updated guidance, including elevated EBITDA and pro forma free cash flow conversion rates. Personnel transition was addressed with the upcoming CFO succession to Vincent O'Neill in preparation for continued execution of long-term strategy.

  • Desch said, "Iridium satellite technology is slated to be included in the next release of the 3 GPP standard known as Release 19," enabling market timing alignment for new direct-to-device services in early 2026.
  • Commercial IoT revenue increase for the quarter was linked to a two-year contract, with Tom Fitzpatrick noting, "contractual revenues for this customer in 2025 will increase materially from 2024" and further growth in 2026 anticipated.
  • Year-to-date commercial IoT revenue growth rate was stated as 19%, surpassing previously stated "mid-teens" guidance.
  • Equipment revenue recovery was attributed to normalization following post-inventory drawdowns, with improvement expected to accelerate into the fourth quarter based on channel feedback.
  • Desch confirmed that PNT activity and market engagement expanded rapidly after Satelles joined Iridium, with added business development resources to address global demand.
  • Fitzpatrick affirmed, regarding leverage: "expect net leverage remain below 4 times of EBITDA through 2026." and plan for "net leverage to fall below 2 times of EBITDA by the end of 2030."

Industry glossary

  • L band: A radio frequency range (1-2 GHz) used in satellite communications for mobile and maritime applications, offering high reliability and resistance to atmospheric interference.
  • NB IoT (Narrowband Internet of Things): Cellular technology for wide-area, low-power communication between devices and satellite networks, especially relevant for remote monitoring.
  • PNT (Position, Navigation, and Timing): Satellite- or ground-based technology providing highly accurate location and time data, crucial for critical infrastructure and navigation applications.
  • GMDSS (Global Maritime Distress and Safety System): An internationally agreed system for ships ensuring automatic distress alerting and continuous communications for maritime safety.
  • LRIT (Long Range Identification and Tracking): A regulation requiring ships to transmit identity and position for international maritime security and tracking.
  • SSAS (Ship Security Alert System): An emergency signaling system on ships enabling discrete security alerts to authorities when under threat or attack.
  • 3GPP Release 19: The forthcoming release of mobile telecommunications standards defining features and network requirements; inclusion permits broader device compatibility for Iridium's services.
  • EMSS (Enhanced Mobile Satellite Services): A U.S. government program leveraging commercial satellite networks (including Iridium's) for secure, global, mobile communications.
