Garmin (GRMN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 18, 2026 at 3:39PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, February 18, 2026 at 10:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & CEO — Clifton Pemble
  • CFO & Treasurer — Douglas Boessen

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated Revenue -- $2.13 billion for the quarter, up 17%, marking the first quarter above $2 billion and a new fourth-quarter record.
  • Annual Revenue -- $7.25 billion, up 15%, resulting in record revenue for every business segment.
  • Operating Income -- $614 million for the quarter, up 19%, and $1.88 billion for the year, up 18%.
  • Gross Margin -- 59.2% for the quarter and 58.7% for the year, both comparable to prior periods despite high tariff impacts.
  • Operating Margin -- Expanded to 28.9% for the quarter and 25.9% for the year, each up 60 basis points.
  • Pro Forma EPS -- $2.79 for the quarter (up 16%), $8.56 for the year (up 16%).
  • Segment Revenue Growth -- Fitness led with 33% growth (2025), Marine up 10%, Aviation up 13%, Outdoor up 5%, Auto OEM up 9%.
  • Fitness Segment -- Revenue $2.36 billion, operating income up 50% to $726 million; gross margin 60% (up 130bps), operating margin 31% (up 360bps).
  • Outdoor Segment -- Revenue $2.05 billion, gross margin 66%, operating margin 34%, operating income $690 million.
  • Aviation Segment -- Revenue $987 million, gross margin 75%, operating margin 26%, operating income up 22% to $257 million.
  • Marine Segment -- Revenue $1.18 billion, gross margin 55%, operating margin 21%, operating income $251 million.
  • Auto OEM Segment -- Revenue $665 million, gross margin 17%, operating loss $49 million.
  • Geographic Revenue -- Americas quarterly revenue exceeded $1 billion for the first time, growing 21%; EMEA up 14%; APAC up 8%.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $430 million for the quarter, $1.4 billion for the year, each rising year over year by $30 million and $24 million, respectively.
  • Balance Sheet -- $4.1 billion in cash and marketable securities at quarter end; inventories rose to $1.8 billion; accounts receivable increased to $1.3 billion due to strong fourth-quarter sales.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $270 million for the year (up $77 million), with 2026 expectations of $400 million driven by a new Thai manufacturing facility.
  • Dividend and Buyback -- Proposed dividend increase to $4.20 per share (up 17%), and $500 million share repurchase authorized through December 2028.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Revenue expected at $7.9 billion (+9%), gross margin 58.5% (down 20bps), operating margin 25.5%, pro forma EPS $9.35 (+9%).
  • Tax Rate -- 2025 pro forma effective tax rate of 17.4% (up 70bps), with 2026 expected to decline to 16% (down 140bps) due to new U.S. tax law provisions.
  • Segment Outlook -- Fitness projected to be the largest contributor to consolidated 2026 growth; Outdoor growth expected to accelerate, particularly in the back half of the year; Marine and Aviation growth forecasts aligned with recent trends; Auto OEM revenue anticipated to decline in 2026 as legacy programs end, though losses are expected to narrow.

RISKS

  • Auto OEM segment is expected to see revenue decline in 2026 due to the peak and subsequent wind-down of BMW domain controller volumes, with ongoing operating losses, though management expects those losses to narrow with R&D reallocation.
  • Guidance factors in ongoing supply chain and memory component cost pressures; gross margin forecast for 2026 is lower due to "higher product costs" as stated by management.
  • Operating expense rose 14% in the quarter, primarily attributed to higher R&D and SG&A expenses.

SUMMARY

Garmin Ltd. (GRMN +0.20%) delivered record-setting financial performance in all business segments, highlighted by a first-time quarterly revenue above $2 billion and annual revenue gains in every region. Management stated they will no longer provide explicit segment-level growth targets, opting to focus on consolidated guidance and qualitative segment commentary, reflecting a strategic shift in their approach to outlook communication. Inventory and accounts receivable balances increased substantially relative to prior periods, reflecting intentional inventory build and strong shipment trends in the fourth quarter. The proposed 17% dividend increase and $500 million new share repurchase authorization underline the Board’s confidence in sustained future cash flows. Guidance for 2026 incorporates a moderate revenue growth target and margin headwinds from input cost inflation, mitigated in part by anticipated improvements in segment mix and favorable changes in U.S. tax legislation.

  • Management cited the Fitness segment as the expected leading driver of 2026 consolidated revenue growth, supported by market share gains in wearables and continued expansion of both user base and new product offerings.
  • Clifton Pemble commented that industry-wide supply chain constraints, particularly in memory, are incorporated in guidance, and emphasized vertically integrated manufacturing and proactive inventory management as mitigation levers.
  • Pemble clarified that most new customers in Fitness remain new to Garmin and that elevated retail engagement through the holiday season contributed to channel momentum.
  • The Board authorized a $500 million share repurchase through 2028, replacing a prior $300 million program, and dividends paid during 2025 totaled $664 million.
  • Auto OEM’s Mercedes-Benz program will see its revenue ramp mainly from early 2027, with only negligible financial contribution in late 2026.
  • Outdoor segment growth is anticipated to accelerate in 2026, largely back-half weighted, though specific product launch timing was not disclosed.
  • Segment operating leverage was cited as a critical driver behind higher margins in 2025, especially within Fitness and Outdoor.
  • Garmin’s ratable (services and subscription) revenue remains below 10% of consolidated sales, but is growing at or above the rate of transactional sales according to management.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • inReach: Garmin’s two-way satellite communication technology, enabling devices to send SOS and connect beyond cellular coverage.
  • Domain Controller: An automotive electronic control unit that integrates multiple functions in a vehicle’s electrical architecture, centralizing digital operations such as infotainment and advanced driver assistance.
  • BOM (Bill of Materials): The full list of raw materials, components, and assemblies required to assemble a final product.
  • MYLAPS: A race timing and activity tracking business acquired by Garmin, enabling digital event services and expanding the company’s engagement in athletic event markets.
  • Connect Plus: Garmin’s subscription-based digital service platform offering enhanced health, fitness, and nutrition features for its device users.
