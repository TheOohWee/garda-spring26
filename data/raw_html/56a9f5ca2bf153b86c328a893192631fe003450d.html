Community Bank CBU Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 1:38PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Oct. 22, 2024 at 11 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Dimitar Karaivanov
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Joseph E. Sutaris

Takeaways

  • Earnings per share -- $0.83, up $0.01 year over year and down $0.08 compared to the previous quarter, with the sequential decrease attributed to higher provision for credit losses and noninterest expenses.
  • Operating diluted EPS -- $0.88, $0.01 higher year over year, but $0.07 lower than the previous quarter.
  • Pretax pre-provision net revenue per share -- $1.29, up $0.13 or 11.2% compared to the same quarter last year, and flat versus the previous quarter.
  • Total operating revenues -- $189.1 million, up $13.7 million or 7.8% year over year, and up $5.9 million or 3.2% versus the previous quarter, marking five consecutive quarters of growth.
  • Net interest income -- $112.7 million, a $3.4 million or 3% increase versus the previous quarter, and the second sequential quarter of net interest income expansion.
  • Net interest margin -- 3.05%, up one basis point from 3.04% in the previous quarter.
  • Deposit cost -- Cost of deposits was 1.23%, unchanged from the previous quarter.
  • Total cost of funds -- Rose to 1.44% from 1.37% in the previous quarter, primarily due to higher borrowing costs.
  • Noninterest revenues -- Operating noninterest revenues were up in all four businesses and represented over 40% of total operating revenues.
  • Banking noninterest revenue -- Up $3 million or 17.1% year over year due to increased mortgage banking, deposit service, and swap fee revenues.
  • Employee benefit services revenue -- Increased $3.2 million or 10.7% year over year, reflecting growth in participants and asset-based fees.
  • Insurance services revenue -- Rose $1.5 million or 12.7% year over year, driven by both acquired and organic growth.
  • Wealth management services revenue -- Increased $1 million or 12.1% year over year, reflecting more favorable market conditions.
  • Noninterest expenses -- Totaled $124.2 million, up $7.7 million or 6.6% year over year and up $5.2 million or 4.4% versus the previous quarter; salary and benefit costs rose $7.3 million or 10.4% due to wage adjustments, incentive plans, and acquisitions.
  • Provision for credit losses -- $7.7 million recorded, up from $2.9 million year over year and $2.7 million in the previous quarter.
  • Effective tax rate -- 23%, up from 21.2% in the same quarter last year.
  • Loan growth -- Loans increased $227.8 million or 2.3% for the quarter and $801.6 million or 8.5% year over year; this was the 13th consecutive quarter of loan growth, with gains in both business and consumer lending.
  • Deposit growth -- Total deposits rose $338.3 million or 2.6% for the quarter, including seasonal municipal inflows; deposits increased $445.4 million or 3.4% over the past year.
  • Liquidity position -- Immediately available liquidity totaled $4.49 billion, representing 200% of uninsured deposits net of collateralized and intercompany balances.
  • Loan-to-deposit ratio -- 76.1%, providing room to shift lower-yielding assets into higher-yielding loans.
  • Regulatory capital -- Tier 1 leverage ratio stood at 9.12%, which substantially exceeded the well-capitalized threshold of 5%.
  • Nonperforming loans -- $62.8 million or 61 basis points of loans outstanding, up from $36.9 million or 39 basis points year over year and higher than the previous quarter, mainly due to one commercial relationship moving to nonaccrual status.
  • 30-89 day delinquencies -- $47.2 million or 46 basis points, up slightly from $45.1 million or 45 basis points in the previous quarter.
  • Net charge-offs -- $2.8 million or 11 basis points annualized, up from $1.2 million or five basis points the prior year.
  • Allowance for credit losses -- $76.2 million or 74 basis points of loans, up $4.7 million from the previous quarter and $11.2 million year over year; coverage exceeded eight times the trailing 12 months' net charge-offs.
  • Deposit mix -- Nearly two-thirds of deposits are noninterest-bearing or low-rate checking and savings accounts.
  • Deposit beta -- Full cycle deposit beta of 24% during the Fed's 2022-2024 hiking phase was among the industry's lowest, reflecting stable core deposits.
  • Loan repricing -- About $1.5 billion of fixed-rate loans are expected to reprice over the next 12 months and current new bookings are in the 7.25% area versus prior averages a little over 5%, providing uplift to yield.
  • Branch expansion -- First new location opened in Hanover Square, Syracuse, as part of an 18-branch strategic plan, with rollout scheduled to continue through 2025 and a goal of expense neutrality through consolidation elsewhere.
  • Expense growth outlook -- Joe Sutaris said, "mid-single-digit growth rate is probably, I think, a fair expectation" for total noninterest expense, with recent investments delivering early benefits.
  • M&A approach -- Management remains active in evaluating acquisitions but stated, "the risk and reward equation did not work for us" in the third quarter.

Summary

Community Bank System (CBU 0.18%) delivered record total operating revenues, with sequential and year-over-year expansion in both net interest income and noninterest revenues. Management expects net interest income and net interest margin to continue rising in the near term, supported by favorable loan repricing dynamics and stabilization in deposit costs. A significant increase in provision expense was driven by qualitative adjustments, while asset quality, though showing higher nonperforming loans and charge-offs, remains described as "strong" by management with reserves offering substantial coverage. Operating expense growth reflected further investments in people and platforms across business segments, while a newly launched strategic branch plan is underway with the first site open and additional locations slated for 2025. Liquidity and capital positions were highlighted as well above regulatory requirements, and organic growth momentum was cited across all businesses—including retail, commercial, and municipal banking, employee benefits, insurance, and wealth management—with positive client acquisition trends noted.

  • Management indicated that outsized loan growth this quarter was not expected to represent a new trend, citing exceptional closings and a return to prior run rates anticipated going forward.
  • Approximately $1 billion in rate-sensitive deposits may be available for further repricing as rates move lower, with about 50% of the first recent cut passed through on the most sensitive buckets.
  • The effective tax rate rose primarily due to the absence of prior-year balance sheet repositioning benefits.
  • Performance-based incentive compensation expense was revised upward in the third quarter, as the company adjusted accruals in light of full-year expectations.
  • Branch expansion is being paired with retail location consolidations, aiming to deliver net neutral expense impact despite phased timing of openings and closures.
  • No specific reserve was directly attributed to the transferred commercial loan that drove the increase in nonperforming loans this quarter; management expects it to remain fully paying.
  • Recent deposit trends for retail and commercial clients showed slight improvement over historical medians for the first time since mid-2022, though management emphasized the significance of this sample size of one.
  • Strategic investments in talent, capacity, and infrastructure were highlighted as both necessary and already yielding benefits, but the investment pace is expected to moderate relative to 2023 levels.
  • Management stated, "We continue to operate from a position of strength and actively gain market share," reflecting confidence in continued new client acquisition and organic growth.
  • Cash and liquidity resources, including Federal Reserve and FHLB borrowing capacity, cover approximately 200% of uninsured deposits after adjustments.

Industry glossary

  • Deposit beta: The percentage of interest rate changes that banks pass on to customers in the form of deposit rate adjustments, measuring deposit cost sensitivity during rate cycles.
  • CECL: Current Expected Credit Loss, a forward-looking accounting standard for determining reserves on loan portfolios.
  • Nonaccrual status: Classification indicating a loan is no longer accruing interest due to potential collectability concerns.
  • PPNR: Pretax pre-provision net revenue, a measure of core banking profitability before considering credit loss provisions.
