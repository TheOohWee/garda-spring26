Axos Financial (AX) Q1 2026 Earnings Transcript

Oct. 30, 2025 at 10:06 p.m. ET

  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Derrick Walsh

Risks

  • President and Chief Executive Officer Gregory Garrabrants said, "fraud is one of the most dangerous potential issues that any lender has," highlighting continued vigilance around syndicated deals where the sort of lender-on-lender violence language wasn't as strong as it should have been in certain cases.
  • Slowing loan yields in certain lending categories are expected due to "recent Fed actions." Management states this will require offsetting by reducing cost of funds to maintain targeted margin levels.
  • Noninterest expenses are expected to increase by approximately $8.5 million per quarter following the Verdant acquisition.

Takeaways

  • Net loan growth -- $1.6 billion linked quarter, including $1 billion from the Verdant acquisition closed on Sept. 30, 2025.
  • Net interest income -- $291 million for the quarter, up $11 million sequentially, or 15.6% annualized, benefiting from broad-based loan growth.
  • Year-over-year net interest income (excluding one-timers) -- Up $16 million or 5.8% from fiscal Q1 2025 to fiscal Q1 2026, after removing the $17 million one-time benefit in the prior year from FDIC loan prepayments.
  • Net interest margin -- 4.75%, down 9 basis points from 4.84% in the prior quarter; excess liquidity contributed a 7 basis point drag.
  • Noninterest income -- Increased by approximately 13% year-over-year due to higher banking service fees, mortgage banking income, and prepayment penalty fees.
  • Book value per share -- Increased 17% year-over-year.
  • Return metrics -- Return on average common equity at nearly 16%, and return on average assets at 1.8% for the quarter.
  • Total deposits -- $22.3 billion, up 6.9% sequentially, and 11.5% year-over-year; 94% in demand, money market, and savings accounts.
  • Noninterest-bearing deposits -- $3.4 billion, up approximately $350 million from prior quarter, driven by treasury management and client cash sorting.
  • Credit quality -- Net charge-offs to total assets were 11 basis points for the quarter ended Sept. 30, 2025, down 5 basis points sequentially and 6 basis points year-over-year; nonaccrual loans to total loans improved from 79 to 74 basis points linked quarter.
  • Net income -- Net income was approximately $112.4 million in the quarter ended Sept. 30, 2025, up from $110.7 million in the quarter ended June 30, 2025; diluted EPS was $1.94 for the quarter ended Sept. 30 versus $1.92 for the June quarter.
  • Adjusted net income and EPS -- Adjusted net income of $119 million and adjusted EPS of $2.06 for the quarter ended Sept. 30, 2025, up 7.3% sequentially and almost 30% annually, excluding deal-related Verdant items.
  • Total originations for investment -- Over $4.2 billion for the 3 months ended Sept. 30, 2025, up 11% sequentially or 44% annualized, excluding single-family warehouse lending.
  • Average loan yields -- For the 3 months ended Sept. 30, 2025, average loan yields were 7.99% overall, 7.66% for non-purchased loans, and 15.81% for purchased loans; new loan rates during the quarter were 7.2%-8.25% depending on segment.
  • Axos Clearing AUC -- Total assets under custody or administration rose from $39.4 billion to $43 billion, with $1.1 billion net new assets in the quarter.
  • Verdant acquisition impact -- Added $1.2 billion of loans, leases, and equipment operating leases; expected to bring $150 million to $200 million of incremental net new loans per quarter starting in the following quarter.
  • Subordinated debt actions -- Issued $200 million in September at 7% fixed for five years, utilized partly to pay off $160 million of older sub debt; this action reduced net interest margin by 1 basis point.
  • Allowance for credit losses -- Added $7.8 million for Verdant, totaling $15.6 million or roughly 1.5% of acquired loan and lease balances, despite Verdant’s historical losses below 50 basis points.
  • Expense outlook -- Personnel and professional services expense growth targeted below 30% of combined net interest and noninterest income growth.
  • Tax rate -- Effective tax rate was 25% for the three months ended June 30, 2025; the quarter ended Sept. 30, 2025 benefited from California methodology changes and RSU vesting; forward guidance is 26%-27%.
  • Guidance -- Loan growth expected in the low to mid-teens range annually for the remainder of fiscal 2026.
  • AI and technology developments -- Artificial intelligence is being broadly deployed to increase efficiency and accelerate new product development, notably in risk, compliance, and software projects.
  • Strategic M&A perspective -- Management sees current regulatory environment as "favorable" for additional strategic and accretive M&A transactions.
  • Pipeline -- As of Oct. 24, 2025, loan pipeline was approximately $2.2 billion, with significant contributions expected from commercial, single-family, multifamily, and specialty verticals.

Summary

Axos Financial (AX 1.25%) delivered sequentially higher net income and EPS while integrating $1 billion in loans from the Verdant acquisition, which management expects to provide $150 million to $200 million in incremental quarterly loan growth going forward. The quarter was marked by robust deposit inflows, particularly in noninterest-bearing accounts, further lowering the cost of funds, and strong credit quality, with net charge-offs decreasing and nonaccrual ratios improving. Expenses rose modestly, with explicit guidance to limit key cost categories’ growth relative to revenue, and the tax rate saw a one-time drop from regulatory changes that will partly carry forward. Technology modernization and expanded AI application were emphasized as strategic levers to drive operating leverage, improve efficiency, and accelerate product roll-outs in both banking and securities segments.

  • Management projects that the Verdant acquisition will be accretive to EPS by 2%-3% in fiscal 2026 and 5%-6% in 2027.
  • Secured wholesale term funding from the acquired Verdant loans will remain outstanding until eligible for payout but is expected to transition to lower-cost internal deposits as loan growth scales.
  • New floor plan lending and proprietary technology platforms are set to further enhance revenue streams, with scheduled rollouts over the next two quarters.
  • Regulatory and risk oversight remain in sharp focus, with management highlighting "continual" vigilance around structural lending risks and proactive measures to mitigate exposure to fraud.

Industry glossary

  • Net interest margin (NIM): The ratio of net interest income to average earning assets, expressing the profitability of a bank's core lending and deposit activities.
  • On-balance sheet securitization: The process of pooling and structuring loans as securities recorded as assets on the company’s balance sheet, rather than being sold off-balance sheet.
  • AUC (assets under custody or administration): The total value of assets a financial institution holds on behalf of clients for safekeeping or administrative purposes.
  • Floor plan lending: Specialized revolving credit lines extended to auto, equipment, or other dealers to finance inventory purchases.
  • CECL (current expected credit losses): An accounting standard requiring banks to estimate and reserve for lifetime expected credit losses upon loan origination or acquisition.
  • FDIC purchased loans: Loans acquired by banks out of FDIC-facilitated transactions (often from failed banks), sometimes carrying unique accounting and accretion benefits.
