TAKEAWAYS

  • Core FFO per Share -- $0.66 for the quarter and $2.55 for the year, reflecting a 6.3% increase including $0.10 per share from two one-time items.
  • Same-Store Cash NOI Growth -- 5.4% in the fourth quarter and 4.3% for the year, reported as above initial guidance.
  • Leasing Performance -- 31 leases commenced totaling 3 million square feet in Q4 with cash leasing spreads of 16.3% and straight-line leasing spreads of 27.4%.
  • Annual Leasing Spreads -- 24% cash and 38.2% straight-line for the year; Q4 cash leasing spreads would have been 20% excluding five fixed-rate renewals.
  • Portfolio Retention -- 75.8% in Q4 and 77.2% for the year on expiring square footage.
  • Occupancy -- Company entered 2026 at nearly 98% occupancy; average occupancy expected to range from 96% to 97% in 2026.
  • 2026 Leasing Pipeline -- 69% of expected 2026 expirations addressed, with projected cash leasing spreads between 18% and 20%.
  • 2026 Same-Store Guidance -- Cash NOI growth guided between 2.75% and 3.25%; retention targeted at 70%-80%; 50 basis points of credit loss budgeted.
  • Acquisition and Disposition Guidance -- Targeted 2026 gross acquisitions of $350 million to $650 million at cash cap rates of 6.25%-6.75%; dispositions forecasted at $100 million to $200 million.
  • Q4 2025 Acquisitions -- $285.9 million closed across seven buildings with a 6.47% cash cap rate; assets 97% leased with average rental escalators of 3.5%.
  • Development Platform -- 3.5 million square feet development or recent completions across 14 buildings, with 59% completed and 73% of completed space leased at year end.
  • New Development Activity -- Construction started on a 186,000 square foot project near Kansas City projecting a 7.2% cash yield and delivery in Q1 2027.
  • Recent Leasing in Development -- A 78,000 square foot lease signed in Charlotte after the quarter, bringing that building to 39% leased, with full stabilization expected in Q1 2027.
  • Dividend Update -- Dividend raised 4% after year end, the largest increase since 2014, and payment schedule shifted from monthly to quarterly.
  • Liquidity and Leverage -- Year-end liquidity reported at $750 million; net debt to annualized run-rate adjusted EBITDA at 5.0x.
  • 2026 Core FFO Guidance -- Core FFO per share forecasted at $2.60 to $2.64, factoring in a $0.03 per share headwind from term loan refinancing.
  • Capital Markets Activity -- $157.4 million in proceeds settled from forward ATM sales during December.
  • Development Leasing Guidance for 2026 -- Company guiding to 907,000 square feet in development leasing, including a build-to-suit project.
  • Cost of Capital -- Cost of debt estimated at 5.5%-5.75%; cost of equity described as in the low 6% range, with more than $100 million in annual retained cash flow expected after dividend distributions.
  • Market Demand Distribution -- Strongest demand noted in Midwest and Texas distribution markets; Southeast port markets cited as weaker.

SUMMARY

Management highlighted an active transaction environment, with acquisition activity in the fourth quarter spurred by increased seller participation and continued pipeline momentum into 2026. Matts S. Pinard indicated that annual leasing activity expectations for 2026 reflect the largest expiring square footage in company history, with a significant portion of renewals already addressed and notable leasing spreads projected. The outlook for new industrial supply points to 180 million square feet or less of deliveries nationally in 2026, with a potential inflection in vacancy rates anticipated in the later half of the year. Recent development projects, including light manufacturing and build-to-suit opportunities, are expected to drive additional leasing and portfolio diversification. The dividend increase and ongoing focus on free cash flow retention signal an emphasis on balance sheet strength and funding flexibility for new investment without accessing equity markets.

  • Matts S. Pinard described tenant demand as "real," with a broad base across third-party logistics, food and beverage, and a growing presence of data center-related tenants.
  • Management expects credit loss for 2026 to be 50 basis points, compared to 22 basis points realized in the prior year, and clarified that "No new tenants on the watch list."
  • The transaction pipeline as of Q1 2026 stands at $3.6 billion, with first quarter acquisition activity projected to be seasonally light but ahead of the pipeline seen the prior year.
  • Executives stated, "we have the ability to operate this business plan without accessing the the equity capital markets," given forecasted leverage, retained earnings, and anticipated funding needs.
  • Concessions, including free rent and tenant improvement packages, are described as stable, with typical free rent at "you are seeing a half a month of free rent per year right now, but that has been stable since '25." and no material changes in tenant improvements observed since 2025.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FFO (Funds From Operations): A REIT-specific measure of operating performance representing net income, excluding gains or losses from sales of properties and adding back depreciation and amortization.
  • NOI (Net Operating Income): Rental and other property-related revenues minus property operating expenses, excluding interest, depreciation, and amortization.
  • Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate): The ratio of a property's net operating income to its purchase price, used to estimate return on investment.
  • ATM (At-the-Market) Offering: A program allowing a company to sell shares incrementally into the open market at prevailing prices, used for flexible capital raising.
  • Build-to-Suit: A property built and customized according to the specifications of a particular tenant.
