Earnings Call Transcript

LiveWire (LVWR) Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 10, 2026 at 10:11AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, Feb. 5, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Jochen Zeitz
  • Chief Executive Officer, LiveWire — Karim Donnez
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jonathan Root

Takeaways

  • Consolidated revenue -- $5.2 billion, declining 11% from the prior year, with HDMC revenue down 15%, HDFS revenue up 9%, and LiveWire revenue down 31%.
  • Consolidated operating income -- $417 million, representing a 47% decrease compared to 2023.
  • Full-year earnings per share (EPS) -- $3.44, down from $4.87 the prior year.
  • Harley-Davidson Motor Company (HDMC) operating margin -- 6.7% for the year, a decline from 13.6% in 2023.
  • HDMC wholesale motorcycle shipments (Q4) -- 14,000 units in Q4, compared to 30,000 units a year earlier, reflecting a 53% decrease.
  • HDMC gross margin (full-year) -- 28%, versus 32.3% the previous year, a 430 basis point reduction.
  • Global retail motorcycle sales -- Down 7% for the year, with North America down 4%, and international down 13%.
  • Tactical inventory management -- Dealer inventory decreased by more than 19% sequentially from Q3, and over 4% year over year.
  • Productivity cost savings -- Cumulative productivity savings reached $257 million since program inception, with a further $100 million targeted for 2025.
  • Operating cash flow -- $1.1 billion in 2024, up $309 million from the prior year, driven by favorable changes in finance receivables and working capital.
  • Share repurchases -- 12.5 million shares bought back in 2024 at a total value of $450 million, representing 9% of shares outstanding at the year's start.
  • LiveWire operating loss -- $110 million for 2024, with unit sales of 612 electric motorcycles; operating loss guidance for 2025 is $70 million to $80 million, and expected unit sales of 1,000 to 1,500.
  • LiveWire cash burn -- "Below target" for 2024, and forecasted reduction of 40% in 2025 to $60 million or less.
  • Market position -- LiveWire achieved a 65% U.S. market share in the 50-plus horsepower on-road EV category, and recorded 46% global retail growth year over year in its Electric Motorcycle segment.
  • HDFS operating income -- $248 million, up 6% from the prior year; operating margin was 24%.
  • HDFS retail credit loss ratio -- Ended 2024 at 3.3%, compared to 3% at year-end 2023.
  • Outlook for 2025 (HDMC) -- Flat retail unit sales anticipated, with wholesale units expected to be flat to down 5%, and operating margin guidance between 7% and 8%.
  • 2025 dealer inventory plan -- Inventory to be reduced by more than 10% by year-end, with a greater than 30% reduction targeted in the first half of 2025.
  • Capital investment plans -- 2025 capital investments projected at $225 million to $250 million, in line with previous years, focused on product development, automation, and LiveWire investments.
  • Share repurchase commitment (2025 plan) -- Planning to buy back $350 million of common shares in 2025.
  • Management’s longer-term margin target -- "We expect to deliver a double-digit margin in 2026, climbing to our 15% target in the years following," contingent on volume improvement and new product launches.

Risks

  • Chairman Zeitz cited, "continued cyclical headwinds for discretionary products, including the high interest rate environment affecting consumer confidence and creating affordability issues for our customers."
  • Chief Financial Officer Root stated, "we are mindful of overall macroeconomic uncertainty and softness in high-ticket consumer discretionary spend, particularly after the second half of 2024’s slowdown."
  • HDFS recorded increased retail credit losses, with the retail credit loss ratio rising to 3.3% from 3% a year earlier, attributed to "higher loan balances and continuing normalization of used bike prices, which have led to lower recovery values and auctions."
  • Management said: "We are realistic and cautious, not having reliable signals yet in either direction as we start the year. Hence our guidance to flat retail sales for the year."

Summary

Harley-Davidson (NYSE: HOG) reported material declines in both revenue and profitability for the year, driven primarily by reduced wholesale volumes, lower global retail sales, and challenging macroeconomic conditions. Inventory management actions resulted in a pronounced reduction in dealer inventories, setting up for further reductions in 2025. LiveWire (LVWR 1.77%) delivered substantial year-over-year retail sales growth but continued to incur significant operating losses, albeit with lower forecasted cash burn for 2025. Management reiterated a long-term margin target of 15%, conditioned on a stabilization and recovery in discretionary consumer demand, and detailed plans for additional cost savings and a disciplined approach to capital allocation, including share repurchases and focused capital investment.

  • Management indicated 70% of U.S. Touring customers remain unfamiliar with the latest product lineup features, presenting a base for future demand conversion.
  • HDMC's Q4 operating loss of $214 million was primarily the result of intentional reductions in wholesale shipments in advance of new model launches and continued efforts to manage inventory.
  • Cost productivity initiatives yielded $110 million in 2024 and cumulatively $257 million, with management expecting to exceed the stated $400 million target by 10%, albeit a year later than planned.
  • Dealers’ inventory quality improved with a higher mix of newly redesigned Touring models on showroom floors, supporting a more strategic and targeted approach to dealer support.
  • LiveWire will enter the European maxi-scooter EV segment in 2026, leveraging the S2 platform and KYMCO partnership, aiming to broaden market reach without substantial new platform investment.
  • Management highlighted a shift to more localized and dealer-centric marketing investments with the launch of the Marketing Development Fund, described as the largest per-unit and absolute spend in company history.

Industry glossary

  • HDMC: Harley-Davidson Motor Company segment, responsible for core motorcycle manufacturing and sales.
  • HDFS: Harley-Davidson Financial Services segment, handling retail and commercial finance operations.
  • STACYC: Segment focused on electric balance bikes, operated by LiveWire.
  • S2 platform: LiveWire’s modular electric motorcycle platform supporting multiple model variants.
  • Maxi-scooter: Large, highway-capable step-through scooter, typically electric in this context, targeting urban and European markets.
