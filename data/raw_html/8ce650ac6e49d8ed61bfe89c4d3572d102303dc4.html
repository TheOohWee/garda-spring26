Greystone (GHI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 1, 2026 at 8:38AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, March 16, 2026 at 12:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kenneth Rogozinski
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jesse Coury

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Loss -- GAAP net loss was $2.6 million, or $0.17 per unit basic and diluted.
  • Cash Available for Distribution (CAD) -- CAD was $2.8 million, or $0.12 per unit.
  • Book Value -- Book value per unit (diluted) was $11.70 as of December 31.
  • Unit Price Discount -- Closing unit price was $5.87, representing a 50% discount to book value as of December 31.
  • Portfolio Composition -- Debt investment portfolio totaled $1.28 billion, or 85% of total assets, comprised of mortgage revenue bonds, governmental issuer loans, and property loans.
  • Mortgage Revenue Bonds Held -- 83 mortgage revenue bonds were held as of December 31, financing affordable multifamily, seniors, and skilled nursing properties across 12 states.
  • Funding Activity -- $38.7 million in funding for mortgage revenue bonds and governmental issuer loan commitments was provided in the fourth quarter, offset by $12.1 million of redemptions and paydowns.
  • Liquidity Position -- Unrestricted cash and cash equivalents were $39.5 million; $49.2 million was available on secured lines of credit.
  • Interest Rate Sensitivity -- An immediate 100 basis point increase in rates would decrease net interest income and CAD by $1.1 million, or $0.049 per unit; a 100 basis point decrease would have the same magnitude effect in the opposite direction.
  • Occupancy -- Stabilized mortgage revenue bond portfolio physical occupancy was 86.7%, down from 87.8% at September 30, with Texas properties cited for higher vacancies due to increased unit supply.
  • JV Equity Losses -- The proportionate share of losses from non-vantage JV equity investments was approximately $7.4 million, or $0.32 per unit, driven by operating losses and noncash depreciation from assets completing construction in 2025.
  • Foreclosure and Transition of Assets -- Deed in lieu of foreclosure completed on four South Carolina mortgage revenue bond properties in January and February 2026, with direct ownership transferred to the partnership and results to be reported in the MF Properties segment.
  • Distribution -- New quarterly unitholder distribution is $0.14 per BUC, set at a level deemed sustainable during investment portfolio repositioning.
  • Remaining JV Funding Commitments -- Market rate multifamily joint venture funding commitments totaled $19.5 million; seniors housing JV funding commitments stood at $7.7 million as of December 31.
  • Future Mortgage Investment Funding Commitments -- Outstanding commitments for mortgage revenue bonds and related investments were $11.6 million as of December 31, expected to be funded over 12 months.
  • Market-Rate JV Equity Strategy -- Management explicitly stated it is actively pursuing exit of all remaining market rate multifamily JV equity investments and reinvesting proceeds into tax-exempt mortgage revenue bonds.

RISKS

  • Four South Carolina mortgage revenue bond properties failed to meet originally underwritten operating levels, prompting foreclosure and direct property ownership transition.
  • Occupancy for stabilized Texas properties declined due to recent increases in local multifamily supply, with management noting uncertainty around near-term recovery.
  • A significant driver of the reported GAAP net loss for the fourth quarter is the proportionate share of losses from non-vantage JV equity investments of approximately $7.4 million.

SUMMARY

Greystone Housing Impact Investors LP (GHI +2.58%) disclosed a $2.6 million net loss and a 50% market price discount to book value, attributing the loss primarily to noncash operating losses from JV equity investments brought online in 2025. Cash available for distribution, a non-GAAP measure, remained positive at $2.8 million, and $39.5 million in unrestricted cash plus $49.2 million in available credit provided liquidity to meet funding needs. During the quarter, management executed foreclosure on four underperforming South Carolina properties, which will now be directly managed. The company continued to shift its strategy by seeking full exit from market rate multifamily JV equity investments, with intentions to reallocate capital to tax-exempt mortgage revenue bond investments intended to provide recurring and tax-advantaged earnings.

  • Management described the discount between market price and net book value as a function of market conditions and not due to questionable asset values, stating that detailed internal analysis is conducted to assess stabilized exit values for those properties and whether those exit values support the return of capital at a minimum. If not, impairments are required to be reported, and none have been reported to date.
  • The interest rate sensitivity analysis indicated near-term exposure is limited, with most debt categories largely insulated from rate fluctuations except for $217 million without designated hedging.
  • Recent direct property ownership after foreclosure will shift operational reporting to the MF Properties segment, changing asset composition.
  • The unitholder distribution level remains at $0.14 per BUC, with further changes subject to the speed of capital recycling out of JV equity positions and into higher income-producing assets.
  • Outstanding mortgage investment funding commitments are expected to be deployed over 12 months, potentially supporting income growth as portfolio repositioning continues.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • BUC: Beneficial Unit Certificate, representing an ownership interest in a partnership or trust, used here for partnership units.
  • CAD: Cash Available for Distribution; a non-GAAP measure reflecting distributable operating cash after specified adjustments.
  • Mortgage Revenue Bond: A bond issued to finance real estate projects that generate rental income, typically tax-exempt and secured by mortgage collateral.
  • MF Properties Segment: The business segment reporting direct ownership, management, and operation of multifamily housing properties, as opposed to bond or loan investments.
  • Governmental Issuer Loan: A loan originated by a governmental body, often used for affordable housing construction, typically repaid at maturity through permanent financing.
