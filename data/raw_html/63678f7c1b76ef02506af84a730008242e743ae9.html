Sportradar (SRAD) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 23, 2025 at 11:28AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Aug. 5, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Carsten Koerl
  • Chief Financial Officer — Craig I. Felenstein
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations and Corporate Finance — James Bombassei

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $318 million, representing 14% year-over-year growth driven by higher product uptake, new client contributions, and strong trading results from Managed Trading Services.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $64 million, up 31% year over year, resulting in a 20.1% margin and expanding approximately 250 basis points.
  • Free cash flow -- $84 million generated in the first half, with a 68% conversion rate compared to 62% the prior year, supported by higher operating cash flow despite increased sports rights payments.
  • Cash position -- $312 million in cash and cash equivalents, with no debt outstanding at quarter end.
  • Market performance – United States -- U.S. revenue grew 30% and now represents 28% of Sportradar Group AG (SRAD +1.44%)'s revenue mix.
  • Rest of world revenue -- Grew 9% year over year, with performance partially affected by the timing of advertising campaigns.
  • Betting technology & solutions segment -- Revenue of $259 million, up 12%, with streaming and engagement products up 12% and odds and live data up 9%.
  • Managed betting services -- Segment revenue up 21%; Managed Trading Services turnover increased 23%, reaching €45 billion, and 50 new sportsbooks signed in 2024 with 42 more in the integration pipeline.
  • Sports content, technology & services segment -- Revenue rose 22% to $59 million, led by 16% year-over-year growth in marketing and media services.
  • Customer metrics -- Net retention rate reached 117%; 40% of clients now take four or more products, presenting further upselling potential.
  • Guidance raised -- Full-year 2025 revenue now expected to reach at least €1.278 billion, representing at least 16% growth, and adjusted EBITDA of at least €284 million, implying at least 28% growth and at least 210 basis points in adjusted EBITDA margin expansion.
  • Share repurchase -- $86 million repurchased at an average price of $17.96; program now nearly halfway through its planned $200 million authorization.
  • Cost structure -- Sports rights expense increased 11% to $106 million, adjusted personnel expense rose 12% to $80 million, and adjusted purchased services expense was up 14% to $44 million; all categories declining as a percentage of revenue.
  • Pending acquisition -- The IMG ARENA acquisition is expected to close in the fourth quarter, anticipated to be accretive to revenue, adjusted EBITDA, and free cash flow; current guidance excludes this impact.
  • Product adoption and upselling -- 4Sight Streaming generated a 30% uplift in turnover for covered ATP events in a recent client case; micro market offerings have expanded to new sports, boosting betting tickets and turnover on MTS.

Summary

Sportradar Group AG (SRAD +1.44%) demonstrated broad-based revenue and margin growth during the quarter, underpinned by strong expansion in the U.S, continued product innovation, and disciplined cost control. Strategic focus remained on increasing client penetration across the portfolio, evidenced by meaningful upselling and elevated retention metrics. The company raised its full-year outlook, reflecting sustained operating momentum and confidence in long-term market opportunities. Management highlighted ongoing technology investments, including successful early adoption of advanced AI tools for developer productivity and client support enhancements. The pending closure of the IMG ARENA acquisition is expected to elevate future content offerings and accelerate core financial metrics. Guidance acknowledged currency headwinds, while reiterating that all major sports rights are secured to support near- and medium-term objectives.

  • Carsten Koerl stated, "Since the legislation of the U.S. sports betting in 2018, the market has grown from approximately $300 million in GGR to nearly $14 billion last year," demonstrating significant ongoing U.S. market potential.
  • Innovations in product and pricing models have enabled clients to dynamically adjust their sports and product mix throughout contract duration, facilitating revenue optimization.
  • Marketing and affiliate service expansions contributed to higher media-related segment growth, with Integrity Services nearly doubling its contribution year over year.
  • The company's iGaming test market in Brazil achieved integrations with 50 operators, positioning Sportradar Group AG as the largest player in the region's early regulatory environment.
  • Management noted that 4Sight has been deployed across additional sports, with forthcoming launches planned for Major League Baseball and soccer, intended to further stimulate live betting engagement.
  • Quarterly profit reached $49 million, compared to a loss of $1.5 million a year earlier, with results assisted by an unrealized foreign currency gain of $54 million.
  • Carsten Koerl described the company's "laser focus on execution and efficiencies," emphasizing durable growth and disciplined capital allocation, including ongoing share repurchases and a balanced approach to organic and M&A investments.

Industry glossary

  • MTS (Managed Trading Services): A Sportradar offering that manages the trading and risk for sportsbook operators, including odds calculation, bet monitoring, and settlement.
  • 4Sight Streaming: Sportradar's enhanced live sports streaming product integrating immersive engagement tools, aimed at increasing betting activity during live events.
  • Micro markets: Betting opportunities on granular in-game occurrences, such as specific player actions or events within a match, enabling increased frequency of bets.
  • UTR Pro Tennis Tour: A global series of professional tennis tournaments for up-and-coming players, delivered as part of Sportradar's content portfolio.
  • Net retention rate: Measures the percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing clients over a period, including upsells, expansions, and churn.
  • Integrity Services: Sportradar's specialized offering for monitoring and safeguarding sports integrity, including match-fixing prevention and detection tools provided to league partners.
  • GGR (Gross Gaming Revenue): Total amount wagered by players minus the winnings paid out, representing the actual revenue retained by betting operators before expenses.
