  • Fiscal Year 2026 Free Cash Flow Guidance -- Raised to $800 million to $840 million (midpoint margin 28.9%).
  • Business Mix Dynamics -- Late-quarter shift toward bookings with future start dates deferred revenue recognition, primarily driven by customer preference for flexible license timing and increased OEM partner contribution.
  • Bookings Performance -- Slightly ahead of expectations for the period, with full-year bookings growth outlook unchanged from prior guidance.
  • US Federal Business -- Achieved double-digit year-over-year growth, with ongoing expectation of elevated variability.
  • Supply Chain Commentary -- No material Q1 impact cited, but management is monitoring for potential future effects from industry component shortages and longer lead times.

Summary

Nutanix (NTNX 6.85%) reported quarterly performance in line with previous guidance but announced a downward revision in full-year revenue expectations due to increasing revenue deferral associated with flexible license start dates and greater OEM involvement. Management confirmed that anticipated full-year bookings growth remains unchanged, and free cash flow guidance has been modestly increased even as more revenue shifts into subsequent periods. Expanded RPO reporting and sustained customer demand were highlighted, along with an unchanged long-term outlook for recognized revenue and continued elevation of operating and free cash flow margin targets.

  • President and Chief Executive Officer Rajiv Ramaswamy asserted, "these changes do not impact our free cash flow expectations for FY '26, which we are modestly increasing."
  • Chief Financial Officer Rukmini Sivaraman emphasized, "If land and expand bookings had come in with the proportion of future start dates that we had assumed, Q1 revenue would have been above the high end of the guided range."
  • Management specifically identified increased customer demand for start-date flexibility and expanded third-party OEM channel presence as primary drivers of deferred revenue recognition.
  • Rajiv Ramaswamy does not disclose bookings specifics but stated quarterly bookings were "slightly ahead of our expectations" and that RPO grew by 26% year-over-year.
  • US Federal business experienced double-digit annual growth, yet future results may fluctuate due to policy and personnel changes as well as government funding uncertainty.
  • Expanded partnerships with Cisco, Dell, and Pure Storage were highlighted as contributing to increased OEM-driven business, impacting both timing and composition of recognized revenue.
  • While supply chain constraints did not affect Q1, management acknowledged a moderate tightening may occur going forward due to macro component shortages.

Industry glossary

  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): The total annualized value of recurring revenue components, such as subscriptions or support contracts, as measured at the end of a reporting period.
  • NRR (Net Dollar-Based Retention Rate): A measure of recurring revenue retained from existing customers over a period, including upsells, expansions, and churn.
  • RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations): The total value of contracted revenue not yet recognized, including deferred revenue and noncancelable backlog.
  • Land and Expand: Sales approach focusing on initial customer acquisition with the intent to broaden usage or purchasing scope over time.
  • OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer): External partners that integrate the company’s software with their hardware, influencing revenue recognition timing due to shipment-based criteria.
