McDonald's (MCD) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 5, 2025 at 10:20AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Nov. 5, 2025, at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Christopher J. Kempczinski
  • Executive Vice President & Chief Financial Officer — Ian Frederick Borden

RISKS

  • Management noted, "QSR traffic from lower-income consumers declining nearly double digits in the third quarter, a trend that's persisted for nearly two years," according to Christopher J. Kempczinski, indicating pressure on guest counts among price-sensitive segments.
  • Christopher J. Kempczinski stated, "We continue to remain cautious about the health of the consumer in the US and our top international markets and believe the pressures will continue well into 2026," highlighting ongoing consumer weakness.
  • Ian Frederick Borden said, "[the company will] continue to operate in an environment where sales have been a little bit more subdued and inflation has been a little higher than what," confirming ongoing margin pressures due to inflation and moderated sales growth.
  • Both executives cited beef inflation as a cost concern, with Borden saying, "beef inflation is up. A fair bit. I think the strength of our supply chain means our beef costs are, I think, certainly up less than most. The still elevated, but I think our basket of goods means we still, have confidence in that kind of low to mid single digit range," which may impact margin performance ahead.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Global Comparable Sales -- Global comparable sales rose 3.6% in Q3 2025, with growth across all segments, reflecting value initiatives and strong execution in a challenging consumer environment.
  • System-wide Sales Growth -- Global system-wide sales growth exceeded 6% in constant currency for a second consecutive quarter, driven in part by new unit openings.
  • US Comparable Sales -- US comparable sales increased 2.4%, with higher-income consumer visits rising almost double digits while lower-income traffic declined nearly double digits.
  • International Operated Markets (IOM) Comp Sales -- Comparable sales in International Operated Markets gained 4.3%; Germany achieved its strongest comp sales in two years, and Australia captured share for a second straight quarter.
  • International Developmental Licensed Markets Comp Sales -- Comparable sales in International Developmental Licensed Markets rose 4.7%, led by Japan, which posted nearly two years of positive guest count growth.
  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share -- Adjusted earnings per share were $3.22, including a $0.04 foreign currency translation benefit; constant currency adjusted EPS declined 1% due to a higher effective tax rate.
  • Restaurant Margin Dollars -- Restaurant margin dollars surpassed $4 billion for the first time, up 4% in constant currency, signaling operational resilience.
  • Adjusted Operating Margin (YTD) -- Adjusted operating margin year-to-date was 47.2%, up from 46.7% in the prior year period, reflecting system-wide execution and portfolio management.
  • G&A Expenses -- G&A expenses rose year-over-year, driven by a $40 million marketing spend for the EVM relaunch, higher incentive compensation, and strategic investment timing.
  • Effective Tax Rate -- The effective tax rate was 22.8%, with management projecting a full-year rate of 21%-22%, narrowing the prior guidance range.
  • Dividend Increase -- A 5% dividend increase was announced in October 2025, marking the forty-ninth consecutive year of dividend growth.
  • Extra Value Meals (EVMs) -- Extra Value Meals were relaunched in September 2025 with minimum discounts of 15%, accounting for 30% of US transactions and supported by targeted corporate funding, which is expected to phase out after Q1 2026.
  • Chicken and Beverage Innovation -- US Snack Wraps launch drove high trial; A 500-restaurant beverage test launched in September exceeded satisfaction expectations and lifted average checks.
  • Digital Engagement -- US MONOPOLY campaign spurred app downloads; The company reported about 45 million 90-day active US digital users as of Q3 2025.
  • New Restaurant Development -- 1,000 new units were added in China in 2025; maintaining guidance for reaching 50,000 global restaurants by 2027.

SUMMARY

McDonald's (MCD 1.25%) reported continued global comparable sales growth in Q3 2025, with a distinct divergence between higher- and lower-income consumer traffic in the US, while intensified inflation and industry pressures impacted margins and guest count trends. The Extra Value Meal relaunch, now supported by targeted funding and set to transition to franchisee-only backing after Q1 2026, addresses immediate competitive value perception but weighs on short-term profitability. Strategic emphasis on differentiated menu offerings in chicken and beverages aligns with segment gains and consumer demand shifts, and digital activations, such as the MONOPOLY campaign, are contributing to higher customer engagement metrics. Committed capital allocation policies—evidenced by the 5% dividend increase announced in October and sustained development pipeline progress—underscore the company’s focus on long-term shareholder returns and operational expansion, despite an uncertain macro environment.

  • Executives explicitly stated that inflation, particularly in beef, remains higher than historic norms and continues to pressure restaurant-level margins, with commentary referencing the current year and Q3.
  • Digital and marketing initiatives delivered measurable business results, including incremental customer occasions and digital engagement via app campaigns in both the US and key international markets.
  • Japan and Germany were highlighted for sustained market share gains in Q3 2025, with Germany achieving its best comp sales performance in two years, driven by localized menu innovation and value campaigns.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Extra Value Meals (EVMs): Bundled menu meal offerings at set price points, typically used to drive value perception and traffic in quick-service restaurants.
  • IOM (International Operated Markets): McDonald’s reporting segment encompassing company-operated international locations, such as Germany, Australia, and the UK.
  • International Developmental Licensed Markets (IDL): Segment of the business operating under franchise/licensing model in international markets, including regions like Japan and China.
