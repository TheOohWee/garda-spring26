PG&E (PCG) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 23, 2025 at 1:12PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Thursday, October 23, 2025 at 11 a.m. ET

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

Core EPS -- $0.50 for the third quarter and Core EPS of $1.14 for the first nine months of 2025, with the narrowed 2025 full-year non-GAAP core EPS guidance at $1.49 to $1.51.

2026 EPS Guidance -- Projected non-GAAP core EPS of $1.62 to $1.66 for 2026, representing a 9% midpoint increase over the 2025 midpoint guidance.

Long-Term EPS Growth Target -- Management set a target of at least 9% annual core EPS growth from 2026 through 2030.

Five-Year Capital Plan -- $73 billion capital plan through 2030, Supporting an average annual rate base growth of 9% from 2026 through 2030 and not requiring new equity issuance.

Wildfire Metrics -- Year-to-date CPUC reportable ignitions down over 35% from 2024 as of October 20, 2025, marking the lowest level since records began in 2015.

Undergrounding Progress -- 1,000 miles of power lines constructed and energized underground in the highest fire risk areas as of October 2025, at a 25% lower cost than initial projects.

O&M Savings -- 5¢ EPS contribution in the third quarter, 8¢ year-to-date for the first nine months, with the company on track to meet or exceed its 2% nonfuel O&M reduction target for 2025.

Tax Planning Benefit -- Accelerated deductibility methods provided an additional non-GAAP core EPS tailwind in both Q3 and the first nine months of 2025, considered upside for customer benefit and future years' protection.

Dividend Policy -- Targeting a 20% dividend payout ratio by 2028 and maintaining that level through 2030.

Fitch Credit Rating -- Parent company rating returned to investment grade, with a continued focus on maintaining FFO to debt in the mid-teens.

Data Center Pipeline -- Over 9.5 GW data center pipeline as of the third quarter of 2025, with modest attrition in earlier project phases but growth in final engineering now at 1.6 GW, of which approximately 95% is expected online by 2030.

Customer Rate Outlook -- Supported by ongoing expense reductions and anticipated load growth.

Advanced Grid Technologies -- Deployment of 8,500 new sensor devices in 2025, building on 10,000 installed in 2024, along with rollout of AI-enabled continuous monitoring and advanced grid-edge meters.

Financing Plan -- Long-term plan through 2030 built not to require new common equity, emphasizing flexibility and credit metrics supportive of investment-grade ratings.

SUMMARY

PG&E Corporation (PCG 0.48%) provided a narrowed 2025 earnings outlook and initiated upward guidance for 2026 EPS, reinforcing confidence in its long-term growth trajectory. The company highlighted tangible wildfire mitigation progress, record-setting ignition reductions year-to-date through October 20, 2025, and major advances in undergrounding while explicitly quantifying cost and performance improvements. Strategic plans through 2030 leverage substantial capital investments—without new equity needs—as management pursues comprehensive risk management and enhances customer affordability, with the plan explicitly built not to require new common equity through 2030. New regulatory, legislative, and credit rating milestones were described as ongoing tailwinds, with direct implications for future capital allocation and investor return frameworks. Leadership prioritized further technology deployment for grid reliability and outlined specific pipeline metrics supporting incremental growth from data center customers.

CEO Poppe said, "We're feeling the positive momentum of this process," in reference to wildfire fund policy and legislation.

CFO Burke stated, "This continues to be a no big bets plan," describing the capital investment approach that emphasizes incremental projects.

PG&E achieved a milestone of zero structures destroyed due to CPUC reportable fires in high-risk areas under high-risk conditions for a third consecutive year, according to Patricia Kessler Poppe.

Management reported achieving nonfuel O&M savings above target for three consecutive years and indicated confidence in maintaining this trend.

PG&E's capital plan forecasts for 2026 through 2030 exclude $2.9 billion of CapEx to be securitized under SB 254, clarifying the rate base forecast methodology.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

CPUC reportable ignition: An incident involving utility equipment that meets California Public Utilities Commission criteria for mandatory reporting due to fire risk.

GRC (General Rate Case): The regulatory proceeding in which a utility requests approval from the CPUC for revenue requirements and rates over a multi-year period.

SB 254: 2025 California legislation strengthening the Wildfire Fund structure and utility risk mitigation obligations.

O&M (Operations & Maintenance): Nonfuel operating expenses related to system maintenance and operations.

FFO to debt: The ratio of funds from operations to total debt, a credit metric used by rating agencies for financial strength assessment.

FERC formula rate: A transmission rate-setting process governed by the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission allowing cost recovery under a fixed methodology.
