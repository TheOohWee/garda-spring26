TAKEAWAYS

  • Full-Year Revenue -- $28.5 billion, representing 20% growth.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $2.9 billion for the year, a company record.
  • Adjusted Diluted Earnings Per Share -- $10.75, up 20%.
  • Fourth Quarter Revenue -- $7.8 billion, a quarterly record.
  • Fourth Quarter Adjusted EBITDA -- $845 million, marking an all-time quarterly high.
  • Fourth Quarter Adjusted Diluted EPS -- $3.16, cited as a record.
  • Full-Year Free Cash Flow -- $1.7 billion, highest annual figure to date.
  • Full-Year Cash Flow from Operations -- $2 billion, another company record.
  • Quarterly Free Cash Flow -- $946 million, a fourth quarter record.
  • Quarterly Cash Flow from Operations -- $1.1 billion, setting a new Q4 record.
  • Backlog -- $44 billion at year end, described as broad-based and record-setting.
  • Acquisitions -- Eight completed during the year, with three in Q4 (Tri City Group, Wilson Construction Company, Billings Flying Service) for $1.7 billion in upfront consideration.
  • Workforce Expansion -- Approximately 11,100 employees added in the year; headcount reached roughly 69,500 at year end.
  • 2026 Outlook -- Forecasts continued double-digit growth in revenue, net income, and adjusted EBITDA; adjusted EPS expected to grow over 20%.
  • 2026 Free Cash Flow Guidance -- $1.8 billion midpoint, including $250 million to $350 million in expected capital expenditures toward vertical supply chain projects.
  • Vertical Supply Chain Investment -- Planned $500 million to $700 million capital allocation over several years focused on power transformer manufacturing for high-voltage transmission.
  • Leverage Ratio -- Remained below two times following Q4 acquisitions.
  • Data Center End Market -- Now approximately 10% of overall business; backlog in this segment growing rapidly.
  • Electric Infrastructure Margins -- Management cited a 10.3% midpoint for 2026, which is consistent with previous years.
  • Organic Workforce Growth -- Approximately 6,000 employees over the coming year, noted as margin pressure factor.

SUMMARY

Management emphasized persistent visibility for multi-year structural demand, pointing to long-term customer commitments across sectors and a diversified, solutions-based platform. The call clarified that the significant backlog is primarily driven by broad programmatic spend instead of large, discrete transmission projects, with management stating no major 765 kV work is booked for 2026. The company is actively investing in vertical supply chain initiatives to reduce exposure to industry-wide transformer supply constraints, aiming to ensure reliability for key customers and projects. Leaders stated that most customer deals are now shifting toward multi-year, programmatic agreements instead of short-term bidding, a transition expected to enhance return on invested capital. Direct discussion highlighted that the company does not intend to invest directly as an equity partner in large infrastructure assets and cited self-funded capital projects for supply chain expansion. Project pipeline visibility now extends through 2032 for certain end markets, with management anticipating continued broad-based backlog growth, especially in renewables and gas generation, pending final bookings.

  • Management confirmed ongoing moderate M&A activity, favoring selective bolt-on acquisitions to add capabilities or address client needs, but does not foresee the need for transformative deals.
  • Earl C. “Duke” Austin stated, “Anytime you have a tight craft labor market, Quanta does very well,” and described data center-related craft labor as the tightest currently observed among business lines.
  • The company intends to use artificial intelligence to improve productivity and reduce operational costs, particularly across engineering functions, but explicitly stated, “We grow people, hire people,” emphasizing internal talent transition over workforce reduction.
  • Adjusted EBITDA, adjusted diluted EPS, and record free cash flow growth were characterized as achievements stemming from long-term strategy, disciplined capital deployment, and a multi-year focus on quality of earnings with risk-adjusted project selection.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Backlog: The total value of contracted work not yet completed, serving as a forward-looking indicator of future revenue.
  • Vertical supply chain: The company’s strategy to directly invest in and control critical elements of the materials or manufacturing process, such as transformer production, to reduce project risk and ensure supply reliability.
  • Programmatic spend: Recurring, multi-year customer investment agreements rather than discrete, one-off project contracts.
  • FIT: Refers to Feed-In Tariff, a mechanism often used in renewable energy to guarantee price and market access for producers.
  • 765 (kV) projects: High-voltage electric transmission projects involving 765 kilovolt lines, typically among the largest infrastructure initiatives in the sector.
  • TAM: Total Addressable Market; the overall revenue opportunity available within a market segment.
  • CCGT: Combined Cycle Gas Turbine, an energy generation technology combining gas and steam turbines to improve efficiency.
