Celsius (CELH) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 5:35PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Nov. 6, 2025 at 11:06 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — John Fieldly
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jarrod Langhans

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Risks

  • Langhans warned of "a really noisy quarter" in fiscal Q4 driven by integration timing, promotional spending, incremental freight, increased returns, and tariff pressure, which may result in "more pressure on our gross margins in fiscal Q4 2025 relative to the prior 3 quarters."
  • Fieldly confirmed that increased product returns related to the Alani Nu transition could "be a scenario that plays out" as a net sales drag, acknowledging that the "return pickup is unknown right now."
  • Management expects an increase in sales and marketing expenses to 23%-25% of sales in fiscal Q4 as the company invests heavily in campaigns and transition costs.

Takeaways

  • Consolidated revenue -- $725 million, up 173%, reflecting accelerated growth from acquisitions and portfolio expansion.
  • Celsius brand U.S. scanner growth -- 13%, attributed to favorable product mix and increased distribution points, while internal revenue growth for Celsius brand was 44% due to timing and inventory factors.
  • Alani Nu revenue growth -- Up 99%, driven by strong limited-time offerings and core SKU expansion.
  • Rockstar Energy contribution -- Approximately $18 million combined revenue impact, with $11 million recorded as revenue and $7 million in other income per GAAP, all in its first month under Celsius Holdings ownership.
  • Gross margin (quarter) -- 51.3%, up from 46%, reflecting scale, lower promotional spend, mix benefits, but partially offset by tariffs and lower margins from new brands.
  • Year-to-date sales -- Up 75%, or $770 million, with Alani Nu as the main contributor and Celsius brand up 12% over the nine-month period.
  • Distributor transition costs -- $247 million expense for Alani Nu's U.S. DSD network conversion, fully funded by PepsiCo (NASDAQ:PEP) and cash neutral to Celsius Holdings due to long-term reimbursement structure.
  • Operating expense management -- General and administrative expenses held at approximately 6% of sales (excluding acquisition costs), down from 9%, evidencing improved efficiency.
  • Debt reduction and cost of capital -- Post–quarter-end, company cut debt by $200 million and reduced annual interest expense by about $20 million for 2026 by lowering term note by 75 basis points.
  • Market share -- More than 20% U.S. energy drink market share (tracked channels), and 31% portfolio growth rate, nearly double the category, based on Circana data.
  • PepsiCo partnership expansion -- Celsius Holdings (CELH 3.99%) now serves as "U.S. Strategic Energy Drink Captain," gaining greater control over planograms, SKUs, and commercial strategy, also receiving added PepsiCo Board representation.
  • International expansion -- Company reported above-expectation results in Australia and further foundation-building in markets including the U.K., Canada, and the Nordics.
  • Upcoming integration effects -- Company projects most financial benefit from Alani Nu's Pepsi system integration to appear in fiscal Q1 2026, owing to phased load-in commencing Dec. 1, 2025.
  • Fourth quarter expense outlook -- Sales and marketing forecasted at 23%-25% of sales, with added gross margin pressure expected from integration activities, increased promotional spending, and tariff-related headwinds.
  • Leadership hires -- New executive appointments include Rishi Daing (Chief Marketing Officer), Garrett Quigley (President, Celsius International), and Ghire Shivprasad (Chief Human Resource Officer).

Summary

The earnings call outlined that Celsius Holdings (CELH 3.99%) delivered rapid portfolio growth and significantly increased market reach through acquisitions and deeper PepsiCo (NASDAQ:PEP) integration. Management expects fiscal Q4 to be marked by operational complexity and near-term margin compression due to transitional costs and network reconfiguration. Forward guidance highlights ongoing investment in brand building, debt reduction, and disciplined capital allocation, with an emphasis on realizing the full impact of integration actions in 2026.

  • PepsiCo increased its ownership stake and added a Board seat, described as "a vote of confidence in our shared long-term trajectory," underscoring strategic alignment.
  • Fieldly stated that Rockstar Energy is targeted for margin improvement by the first half of 2026 through sourcing and production integration, following Alani Nu’s margin enhancement trajectory.
  • Alani Nu's Witches Brew achieved its highest-ever sales week, and upcoming limited-time offerings are expected to sustain velocity and core SKU growth.
  • International initiatives are supported by new executive leadership and focused investments, especially in Australia, with plans to leverage cross-brand synergies globally.

Industry glossary

  • DSD (Direct-Store Delivery): Distribution method where products are delivered directly to retail stores, bypassing central warehouses.
  • Planogram: Retail shelf diagram or layout specifying product placement for optimal sales and visibility within stores.
  • ACV (All-Commodity Volume): Measurement of a product’s retail distribution, expressed as a percentage of total available sales volume in the market.
  • TDP (Total Distribution Points): A calculation reflecting both the breadth (ACV) and depth (number of items) of a product’s distribution.
  • LTO (Limited Time Offering): A product or flavor available for a defined, temporary period to stimulate consumer interest and trial.
  • Scanner data: Point-of-sale information captured at retail registers, used to measure sales performance and market share.
  • Circana: Industry analytics provider tracking retail sales and market share in consumer goods channels.
