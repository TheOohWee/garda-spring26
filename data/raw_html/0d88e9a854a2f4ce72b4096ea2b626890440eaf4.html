Earnings Call Transcript

Byrna (BYRN) Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Apr 9, 2026 at 8:29AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, Feb. 7, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Bryan Ganz
  • Chief Financial Officer — Lauri Kearnes

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Revenue -- $28 million for the quarter, representing a 79% increase due to the advertising strategy shift and normalization of the less-lethal category.
  • Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) Revenue -- DTC channels byrna.com and amazon.com increased by $8.9 million over the prior period.
  • Gross Profit Margin -- Reached 62.8% versus 57.8% in the prior year’s quarter, attributed to higher-margin DTC sales, cost reductions, and manufacturing scale.
  • Operating Expenses -- $13.5 million, up from $9.7 million, reflecting increased selling costs, marketing, and higher payroll in marketing and engineering functions.
  • Net Income -- $9.7 million compared to a net loss of $0.8 million in the comparable quarter, aided by a $5.6 million income tax benefit from released tax valuation allowances.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Non-GAAP adjusted EBITDA was $5.2 million, compared to $400,000 in the prior-year quarter.
  • Cash and Short-term Marketable Securities -- $25.7 million combined, up by $5.2 million, primarily due to reallocating cash into marketable securities.
  • Inventory -- $20 million at period end, compared to $13.9 million three years prior; expecting a $5 million inventory build for the launch of the Compact Launcher (CL) in midyear.
  • Debt Load -- Company reported having no short or long-term debt.
  • Unit Sales Milestone -- Cumulative sales topped 500,000 launchers as of November, indicating scale and market normalization.
  • Return on Advertising Spend (ROAS) -- Achieved more than five times ROAS since launching the celebrity endorsement program in September 2023.
  • Retail Expansion -- Opened one new store in Nashville and committed to four new stores in total, each expected to take four to six months to ramp.
  • Store Performance -- Las Vegas location generated over $1 million in sales at more than 65% gross margin; brick-and-mortar channel conversion rate cited at 80% compared to over 1% online.
  • Partnership with Sportsman’s Warehouse -- Signed a letter of intent for a “store within a store” model at eleven initial locations, targeting expansion to one hundred by the end of 2026; Byrna Technologies Inc. funds half the build-out cost, estimated at $7,500 per store.
  • USCCA Partnership -- New channel opened with nearly one million USCCA members gaining access to Byrna products, and Byrna Technologies Inc. customers gaining access to USCCA training and insurance.
  • Production Expansion -- Monthly launcher production increased to 24,000 units, up 33%, enabled by a second shift and a 10% wage increase at Fort Wayne, Indiana.
  • New Domestic Ammunition Facility -- Opened near Fort Wayne; houses eight machines with eventual capacity for ten million payload rounds per year, including 1.5 million fin tail rounds.
  • Supply Chain Strategy -- Dual-sourcing of magazine components (India and South Africa) and commitment to exit China by midyear, with 100% U.S. sourcing targeted for core models by the end of 2025.
  • International Sales Growth -- Mexico sales grew to $890,000; Canada sales increased to $2.47 million, both expected to double in the following year; new distributor model in Canada to reduce tariff risk.
  • Major International Order -- Cordoba province police in Argentina committed to purchase 1.7 million rounds of ammunition to be shipped in 200,000 round increments through 2025.
  • Compact Launcher Launch -- On track for summer release, with 30,000 units targeted for pre-launch inventory.
  • Guidance Discussion -- Management expects strong double-digit revenue growth in 2025, with gross and EBITDA margins projected to improve as sales scale and product mix shifts toward higher-margin offerings.

RISKS

  • CEO Bryan Ganz said, “I do not expect there to be a sixth consecutive growth quarter,” with the first quarter anticipated to be below the fourth quarter due to seasonal slowing.
  • New retail stores are expected to be loss leaders initially, resulting in “some negative impact on our operating expense mark percentage,” and requiring four to six months to ramp to profitability.
  • Operating expenses are expected to remain elevated due to increased marketing and new store openings, which may delay operating leverage realization.

SUMMARY

Byrna Technologies (BYRN 10.24%) reported 79% revenue growth with marked improvement in gross and adjusted EBITDA margins, underscoring the operational leverage from a strategic shift toward DTC sales and cost initiatives. Management detailed expanded brick-and-mortar and retail partnership rollouts, notably with Sportsman’s Warehouse, and a material increase in production capacity supporting multiple new product initiatives. International demand, highlighted by the significant Argentina ammunition order and strong growth in Mexico and Canada, further diversifies the topline and reduces regulatory risk via new distribution strategies. The company also confirmed a transition to U.S. sourcing for key components and the opening of a new domestic ammunition facility to address future supply chain concerns.

  • Byrna Technologies Inc. cited a transformed advertising policy environment under the new U.S. administration, resulting in increased media access and influencer marketing traction.
  • While the loss of Governor Mike Huckabee as a celebrity endorser was acknowledged, CEO Bryan Ganz stressed it did not have any significant impact on the company’s numbers.
  • Brick-and-mortar expansion is supported by data-driven case studies, with store conversion rates far above online. Management highlighted the “store within a store” model for rapid scaling.
  • Management expects a $5 million inventory build specifically for the Compact Launcher ahead of its midyear launch, significantly affecting working capital deployment.
  • Separation from Chinese sourcing is expected by midyear, with U.S. component sourcing for SD, LE, and CL models by year-end 2025, further mitigating tariff exposure and supporting “made in America” positioning.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ROAS (Return on Advertising Spend): A marketing efficiency ratio measuring revenue earned per dollar spent on advertising, important for evaluating the impact of celebrity endorsement and influencer marketing spend.
  • DTC (Direct-to-Consumer): Sales channel in which products are sold directly to customers rather than through third-party retailers, driving margin improvement and brand control.
  • USCCA (United States Concealed Carry Association): A large U.S. firearms safety, training, and insurance membership organization, now in partnership to offer Byrna products and training access.
  • CL (Compact Launcher): Byrna’s new, smaller-format handheld launcher targeted for midyear 2025 release, designed for concealed carry and higher margins.
