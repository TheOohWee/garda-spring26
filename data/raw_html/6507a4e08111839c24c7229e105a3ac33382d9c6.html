Coca-Cola (KO) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Jul 22, 2025 at 1:44PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

  • Tuesday, July 22, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — James Quincey
  • President & Chief Financial Officer — John Murphy

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Volume: Consolidated global unit case volume declined 1%, impacted by adverse weather, a difficult prior year comparison, and consumer pressure in select markets.
  • Organic Revenue Growth: Organic revenue grew 5%, with a Price/mix increased 6%, driven by approximately five percentage points of pricing actions and one point of favorable mix.
  • EPS: Comparable earnings per share grew 4%. Comparable EPS reached $0.87, despite 5% currency headwinds and a higher effective tax rate.
  • Margin Expansion: Comparable gross margin increased by approximately 80 basis points (comparable, non-GAAP), and Comparable operating margin increased by 190 basis points.
  • Free Cash Flow: $3.9 billion in free cash flow (excluding the Fairlife contingent consideration payment) for the first half of 2025, up approximately $600 million year-over-year due to underlying business performance and lower tax payments, offset by prior year working capital benefits.
  • Net Debt Leverage: Net debt leverage of two times EBITDA, at the low end of the targeted 2-2.5x net debt leverage to EBITDA range.
  • Guidance: Updated full-year 2025 expectation for organic revenue growth of 5%-6%, and Comparable currency-neutral EPS growth of approximately 8% for 2025.
  • Currency Impact: Management anticipates a 1%-2% currency headwind to full-year 2025 comparable net revenues and a 5% headwind to comparable EPS.
  • Segment Insights: EMEA volume increased across all three operating units, with growth led by Europe, Eastern and Western markets, and key brands like Coca-Cola Zero Sugar, Sprite, and Fuze Tea.
  • Brand Performance: Volume for Coca-Cola Zero Sugar, Diet Coke, Fanta, Fairlife, BodyArmor, and Powerade each grew.
  • Fairlife: Fairlife posted double-digit volume growth, although this moderated due to capacity constraints. Further moderation in Fairlife volume growth is expected until the New York facility comes online in early 2026.
  • Strategic Execution: Management highlighted a continuation of "value share gains" for the seventeenth consecutive quarter, and a focus on executing granular, contextually relevant action plans in challenged markets.
  • Productivity Initiatives: Approximately one-third of first-half 2025 comparable margin expansion was attributed to faster realization of productivity initiatives, especially from marketing transformation and disciplined operating expense management.
  • Innovation: Sprite became the number three sparkling soft drink in the US following the introduction of new variants, including Sprite plus Tea, which management scaled based on "strong consumer demand and positive social media reaction."

SUMMARY

Coca-Cola (KO 0.91%) reported 5% organic revenue growth and 4% comparable EPS growth, overcoming a 1% global volume decline tied to adverse June weather and challenging comparisons. Margin expansion was supported by accelerated productivity realization and disciplined cost management. Management affirmed full-year organic revenue growth guidance of 5%-6% (organic, FY2025) and raised comparable currency-neutral EPS growth expectations to approximately 8% for 2025, but explicitly acknowledged a 1%-2% top-line and 5% bottom-line currency headwind.

  • President & CFO John Murphy stated, "We now expect 2025 comparable earnings per share growth of approximately 3% versus $2.88 in 2024."
  • The U.S. market showed sequential volume improvement, and restored brand equity scores among Hispanic consumers by late June after targeted advertising addressed earlier declines.
  • Capacity constraints continued to limit Fairlife growth, rather than "a weakening of the proposition relative to the competition," according to Quincey.
  • Asia Pacific experienced mixed performance, with volume declines in ASEAN and South Pacific offsetting growth in Australia and the Philippines; China saw volume increases driven by trademark Coca-Cola performance.
  • Updated operational strategy focuses on agility and rapid market pivots using digital tools, AI-driven pack price optimization, and marketing designed for local relevance.
  • Despite persistent external volatility, management maintained confidence in achieving updated guidance and sustaining long-term top-line and earnings momentum.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Unit Case: Standard volume measure in the beverage industry, representing 24 eight-ounce servings of finished beverage.
  • Organic Revenue: Revenue growth excluding the impact of currency fluctuations, acquisitions, and divestitures.
  • Comparable EPS: Earnings per share adjusted to exclude certain items to provide a normalized measure of profitability.
  • Value Share: The company's share of the total market based on revenue rather than volume.
  • Price/Mix: Combined effect of changes in pricing and sales mix on revenue growth.
  • Refillables: Reusable beverage containers, part of affordability and sustainability initiatives in select markets.
  • Fairlife: Coca-Cola’s high-protein, value-added dairy brand.
  • All-Weather Strategy: Coca-Cola’s approach to maintaining growth and agility across varied and dynamic market environments.
