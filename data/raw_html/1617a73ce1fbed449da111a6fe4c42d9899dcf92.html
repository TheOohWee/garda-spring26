Adobe ADBE Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Jun 12, 2025 at 10:29PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, June 12, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Shantanu Narayen

TAKEAWAYS

Total Revenue: $5.87 billion in revenue for Q2 FY2025, up 11% year-over-year, as reported and in constant currency.

GAAP EPS: $3.94 GAAP earnings per share for Q2 FY2025; Non-GAAP EPS: $5.06 non-GAAP earnings per share for Q2 FY2025, representing 13% year-over-year growth.

Digital Media Revenue: $4.35 billion for Q2 FY2025, Digital Media revenue grew 11% year-over-year as reported.

Digital Media Ending ARR: $18.09 billion Digital Media ARR for Q2 FY2025, up 12.1% year-over-year for Digital Media ending ARR.

Digital Experience Revenue: $1.46 billion revenue for Q2 FY2025

Digital Experience Subscription Revenue: $1.33 billion subscription revenue for Q2 FY2025, up 11% year-over-year.

Cash Flow from Operations: $2.19 billion in cash flows from operations for Q2 FY2025, a record for the second quarter.

Remaining Performance Obligations: $19.69 billion for Q2 FY2025

Monthly Active Users (MAU): Over 700 million monthly active users across product categories as of the end of the quarter, with over 25% year-over-year MAU growth for Acrobat and Express combined.

Express Capabilities Adoption in Acrobat: Express capabilities adoption in Acrobat grew approximately 3x quarter over quarter and ~11x year-over-year.

Subscription Revenue by Group: Business Professionals and Consumers — $1.6 billion in subscription revenue for the business professionals and consumers group in Q2 FY2025, 15% year-over-year growth for the business professionals and consumers group subscription revenue. Creative and Marketing Professionals — $4.02 billion in subscription revenue for the creative and marketing professionals group in Q2 FY2025, 10% year-over-year growth.

Generative AI Usage: Total generations across Firefly and Creative Cloud topped 24 billion as of quarter end.

Firefly App: paid subscriptions nearly doubled sequentially.

Partner Integrations: Express add-ons with partners (Google Ads, Vimeo, Bitly) grew over 25% quarter-over-quarter.

Q3 Guidance: Total revenue expected at $5.875 billion–$5.925 billion for Q3 FY2025; GAAP EPS $4–$4.5 for Q3 FY2025; non-GAAP EPS $5.01–$5.2 for Q3 FY2025; Digital Media revenue $4.37 billion–$4.4 billion.

Full-Year FY2025 Outlook Raised: Total revenue now $23.5 billion–$23.6 billion for FY2025; Digital Media revenue $17.45 billion–$17.5 billion for FY2025; Digital Media ending ARR growth targeted at 11% for FY2025.

AI Book of Business: Management reported AI-influenced ARR in the billions, and tracking above $250 million target for AI Direct ARR by end of FY2025.

SUMMARY

Adobe (ADBE 2.00%) delivered record revenue in the reported period and raised full-year guidance on key metrics, citing ongoing strong adoption of AI-powered and creative solutions. Management detailed rising demand and accelerated monetization across key segments, emphasizing progress in user growth, product integration, and enterprise adoption. Expanded offerings, heightened partner activity, and notable momentum in both customer acquisition and retention.

Wadhwani stated, “Traffic to the Firefly app grew over 30% quarter over quarter.”

Firefly’s paid subscription base nearly doubled quarter over quarter, while time Adobe subscribers to Firefly grew over 30% quarter over quarter.

Chakravarthy highlighted that “Gen Studio for performance marketing” posted over 45% sequential growth.

Multiple executives reported strong demand for AI-powered solutions, with Firefly services and custom models experienced 4x year-over-year ARR growth.

Management confirmed rollout of Creative Cloud Pro and Firefly app subscriptions, pricing new Firefly tiers at $10, $20, and $200 per month, respectively, to drive segment expansion.

Recent global customer wins cited included Cisco, County of Los Angeles, the National Football League, and the Premier League.

Industry partnerships expanded, with major platforms including Meta, Google, and additional ad channels integrating Adobe’s Gen Studio.

Durn confirmed a $3.5 billion share repurchase in Q2 FY2025, with $10.9 billion of buyback authorization remaining as of quarter end.

Subscription revenue for combined creative and marketing professionals accelerated to a 10.1% year-over-year growth rate.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

ARR (Annualized Recurring Revenue): The annualized value of active subscription contracts at the end of a reporting period, reflecting the company’s subscription business momentum.

MAU (Monthly Active Users): The number of unique users engaging with a product or service in a given month, a key metric for user adoption and engagement.

PLG (Product-Led Growth): A go-to-market approach that drives customer acquisition, expansion, and retention through direct product usage rather than traditional sales-led tactics.

CRPO (Current Remaining Performance Obligations): The portion of remaining performance obligations expected to be recognized as revenue within the next 12 months.

Firefly: Adobe’s AI-powered application and foundational models for creative ideation, image/video generation, and content production across design workflows.

Gen Studio: An integrated platform enabling content planning, creation, management, and campaign activation using generative AI for marketing and creative teams.

Creative Cloud Pro: Adobe’s higher-value subscription bundle offering expanded AI and creative features, including access to Firefly, positioned above standard Creative Cloud packages.

Express: Adobe’s web and mobile application featuring AI-assisted design tools aimed at business professionals and content creators for rapid design and publishing.

AEP (Adobe Experience Platform): Adobe’s data and customer journey orchestration backbone for experience management, segmentation, and campaign optimization at scale.
