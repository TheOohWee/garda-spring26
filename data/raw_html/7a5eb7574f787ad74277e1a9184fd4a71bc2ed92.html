Earnings Call Transcript

Zoetis (ZTS) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 11:09AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, May 6, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kristin Peck
  • Chief Financial Officer — Wetteny Joseph
  • Head of Investor Relations — Steve Frank

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $2.2 billion, with reported growth of 1% and 9% organic operational growth reflecting 4% price and 5% volume uplift.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $662 million, rising 4% on a reported basis and 6% on an organic operational basis, highlighting substantial earnings growth after accounting for currency and divestitures.
  • Companion Animal Revenue -- $1.5 billion, increasing 9% operationally with Simparica up 19% to $367 million, and key dermatology up 10% to $387 million.
  • OA Pain Monoclonal Antibodies (mAbs) -- $147 million in global revenue, up 15%; US OA pain mAbs sales were $65 million, gaining 14%, with Librela at $47 million (+17%) and Solensia at $18 million (+7%).
  • International Segment -- 11% organic operational revenue growth, with companion animal rising 10% and livestock up 12% operationally; Simparica sales reached $106 million (+23%), and key dermatology revenue was $138 million (+15%).
  • US Segment -- 6% organic operational revenue growth, with companion animal revenue up 8% and livestock down 2% on an operational basis.
  • Gross Margin -- Adjusted gross margin reached 72.1%, expanding by 140 basis points, with foreign exchange providing a 220 basis points tailwind; margin pressure was attributed to higher manufacturing costs, offset by favorable mix from the MFA divestiture.
  • Retail Channel -- US retail and alternative channels represented 21% of US business and grew approximately 40% in the quarter, with more than 40% of Apoquel and Simparica Trio US sales through retail.
  • Guidance -- Full-year revenue view set at $9.425 billion to $9.575 billion, with 6%-8% organic operational revenue growth reaffirmed; adjusted net income guided to $2.775 billion to $2.825 billion, with organic operational growth of 5%-7% reflecting impacts of currently enacted tariffs.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- Guidance increased to $6.20 to $6.30, with the EPS rise primarily linked to favorable currency rates and share repurchases, partially offset by enacted tariffs.
  • Tariff Exposure -- CFO Joseph said, "our guidance today reflects the net impact of the tariffs that are actually already in place and so that’s net of any mitigations that we’ve already put together… primarily from imports related to APIs, for a few of our livestock products coming from China," as well as some diagnostic products and veterinary accessories.
  • Companion Animal Market Penetration -- Librela achieved 86% clinic penetration in the US per Joseph, with 1 million dogs on the drug out of 27 million medicalized US dogs with osteoarthritis pain.
  • Simparica Trio Label Expansion -- CEO Peck stated, "with Simparica Trio’s US label expansion to include flea tapeworm protection," indicating ongoing innovation to sustain franchise growth.
  • MFA Portfolio Divestiture -- Livestock organic operational growth was 7% excluding MFA impact; margins benefited from the divestiture.
  • New Long-Acting OA mAb -- Peck confirmed the expected approval of a distinct long-acting osteoarthritis monoclonal antibody with a three-month duration and a unique binding site, anticipated within the year.
  • Poultry Vaccine Update -- "We received conditional licensure from the USDA for avian influenza vaccine for chickens," per Peck, supporting preparedness for evolving disease threats.

RISKS

  • Peck acknowledged, "So uptake maybe slower in the near term, our long-term confidence remain strong as we build a durable differentiated market with significant room to grow, especially given the sizeable and underpenetrated opportunity ahead.", and consumer spending trends are leading to a "more measured approach to managing chronic conditions," which may impact the timing or frequency of care.
  • Joseph cited "operational declines of 2% in livestock are primarily driven by declines in jacwin due to aggressive price competition and the timing of price adjustments in the prior year, as well as the timing of supply of Ceftiofur."
  • CFO Joseph highlighted enacted tariffs as a current headwind: "net of mitigations, we’re calling about $20 million of headwind that we’re putting here;" future tariff impacts are uncertain and not in guidance.
  • Peck noted, "to clarify, our outlook does not include any potential impacts from future tariffs as it’s still too early to know how the situation will unfold."

SUMMARY

Zoetis (ZTS 1.77%) reported 9% organic operational revenue growth, driven by broad-based price and volume gains and continued franchise strength in companion animal and livestock segments. Management reaffirmed 6%-8% organic operational revenue growth guidance and updated adjusted net income expectations to 5%-7% growth to incorporate tariff-related cost impacts. The company is accelerating investments in education and real-world studies to expand OA pain franchise adoption, while pursuing life-cycle innovation, including a forthcoming long-acting monoclonal antibody targeting osteoarthritis pain. Strategic emphasis remains on diversification across species, channels, and geographies, with veterinary and alternative channels contributing to higher compliance and lifetime value per patient. Operating backdrop uncertainty persists due to tariff dynamics, FX volatility, and consumer spending pressure, but Zoetis management emphasized resilience, agility, and their global manufacturing network as key mitigation levers.

  • The three key franchises—Simparica, key dermatology, and OA pain—are expected to deliver double-digit growth for the full year, supported by innovation and first-mover advantage in core categories.
  • Guidance now includes $20 million of net tariff headwinds on adjusted net income, primarily from Chinese API imports, diagnostic products, and accessories; future impacts from yet-to-be-enacted tariffs are not incorporated.
  • Conversion to Apoquel chewable reached approximately 31% in the US and 57% in Europe, reflecting varying levels of adoption between regions and supporting defensibility of the dermatology franchise.
  • Management confirmed no delays have emerged in the FDA or USDA review of pipeline products, but continues to monitor regulatory staffing and industry dynamics closely.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • OA pain mAbs: Monoclonal antibody therapies developed to treat osteoarthritis pain in companion animals.
  • MFA: Medicated Feed Additives, a former Zoetis product segment focused on livestock feed supplementation, now divested.
