Earnings Call Transcript

MainStreet (MNSB) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 11:13AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Monday, April 21, 2025 at 2 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Jeff Dick
  • Chief Financial Officer — Tom Chmelik
  • Chief Accountant — Alex Vari
  • Chief Lending Officer — Tom Floyd

Takeaways

  • Net interest margin -- 3.3%, up 34 basis points sequentially, attributed mainly to lower deposit costs and balance sheet adjustments.
  • Earnings per share -- $0.25, reflecting current period profitability under core banking focus.
  • Return on average assets -- 0.46%, supported by improved interest income and expense control.
  • Return on average equity -- 4.78%, aligned with stated performance objectives.
  • Non-performing loans -- $21.7 million, expected to decline to $10.5 million following a court-approved payoff in June.
  • Avenue banking-as-a-service discontinuation -- Management confirmed, "we've discontinued our Avenue banking-as-a-service initiative," and related intangible was fully written off in fiscal Q1 2025 (period ended March 31, 2025).
  • Operating expense reduction plan -- Targeted 12.5% decrease in operating expenses in fiscal Q2 2025, with additional reductions planned for following quarters.
  • Loan growth -- Gross loans increased by $1 million quarter over quarter, with non-owner occupied commercial real estate up $25 million, multifamily up $12 million, owner-occupied up $6 million, offset by a $47 million decline in construction loans.
  • Deposit trends -- Non-interest-bearing and low-cost transactional deposits increased by $74 million, reflecting relationship-driven deposit gathering.
  • Retail CDs and wholesale CDs repricing -- $113 million of retail CDs and $112 million of wholesale CDs repriced at lower rates during late fiscal Q4 2024 and fiscal Q1 2025, supporting funding cost reduction.
  • CRE concentration ratio -- Commercial real estate capital ratio decreased to 388% from 394% in the prior quarter.
  • Credit facilities for liquidity -- Over 35% of deposit portfolio supported by credit facility availability at quarter end.
  • Stock buyback -- 25,000 shares repurchased at $17.88 per share in January, with $3 million capacity remaining under the current plan.
  • CRE lending policy -- Management is "slowing our investor's CRE lending until we see some political and economic stability."
  • Government contract portfolio utilization -- 29 asset-based lines totaling $9.2 million outstanding, with 11% utilization against $80.9 million in commitments.
  • Loan reset profile -- 76% of loan portfolio has rate resets beyond six months; remaining 24% will reset within six months, 40% of the latter have a weighted average floor of 6.32%.
  • Allowance and stress test -- Fiscal Q4 2024 stress test estimated a worst-case loss of $44.2 million; bank maintains "strongly capitalized" status throughout stress scenarios.
  • Expense reductions linked to Avenue -- "Nearly all" of the fiscal Q2 2025 cost reductions are related to the Avenue shutdown, per management statements.
  • Restricted stock impact -- Restricted shares are non-dilutive and included in reported share count, with changes mainly reflecting annual vesting and compensation accruals.

Risks

  • Avenue discontinuation led to a full write-off of related intangibles and will result in one-time severance and contract termination costs estimated up to $120,000 for fiscal Q2 2025.
  • An increase in 30-to-89 day delinquencies occurred; "those have been brought current." post-quarter, but resolutions for remaining balances remain in progress.
  • The bank is intentionally "slowing our investor's CRE lending" in response to political and economic uncertainty, which may constrain short-term loan growth.

Summary

MainStreet Bancshares (MNSB 1.57%) used this earnings call to confirm a decisive shift in strategy with the full discontinuation of its Avenue banking-as-a-service initiative, citing escalating costs, an elongated timeline, and a failure to create shareholder value. Leadership detailed a substantial sequential improvement in net interest margin to 3.3%, attributing gains largely to repricing of both retail and wholesale CDs at materially lower rates and targeted growth in low-cost deposits. The bank expects its reported non-performing loans to drop by more than half in the coming quarter as a significant legal recovery is completed, with management emphasizing ongoing rigorous credit oversight and stress-tested capital strength. Operating expense projections call for a double-digit percentage reduction in fiscal Q2 2025, driven primarily by Avenue-related cost cuts, with potential one-time severance and contract charges yet to be quantified.

  • Management is redeploying all focus to core banking operations and traditional deposit-growth channels, describing future technology plans as limited to the maintenance of a legacy app and basic core banking improvements.
  • No current direct exposure exists to federal government-occupied CRE, and the board remains open to evaluating strategic alternatives in partnership with advisors but believes current market conditions warrant caution.
  • Loan growth for the quarter was essentially flat in aggregate, with expansion in select real estate categories counterbalanced by contraction in construction lending and tight controls on commercial real estate concentrations.
  • Jeff Dick confirmed that while Avenue has been "shut down," a minimal maintenance team remains, and management may seek opportunities to salvage value but no sale or recovery is currently included in forward guidance.

Industry glossary

  • Banking-as-a-Service (BaaS): A business model enabling non-bank firms to offer financial services using licensed bank infrastructure and regulatory compliance.
  • CRE: Commercial real estate; loans secured by income-producing property including office, retail, industrial, or multifamily buildings.
  • CD: Certificate of deposit; fixed-term deposit instrument typically used by banks for funding.
  • Loan-to-deposit ratio: A key banking metric measuring the proportion of funds loaned out relative to total deposits, indicating liquidity and risk profile.
