Risks

  • Pervasive medical cost trend pressure— Tim Noel reported, "Our current view for 2025 reflects $6.5 billion more in medical costs than we anticipated in our initial outlook," with increases observed broadly across Medicare, commercial, and Medicaid lines of business.
  • Margin compression in Optum Health— Patrick Conway stated, "Optum Health earnings in 2025 are approximately $6.6 billion below our expectations ... with value-based care margins compressed to about 1% in 2025, versus over 3% in 2024 and nearly 5% in 2023."
  • Medicaid non-dual segment losses in 2026— Tim Noel said, "We contemplate negative margins at that time anywhere from negative one to negative one point seven percent for Medicaid in 2026."
  • Continued membership decline expected in ACA and commercial markets— Tim Noel noted, "due to the projected expiration of premium subsidies across the ACA market, our membership should decline significantly," with commercial segment declines linked to trend-driven pricing actions.

Takeaways

  • Revenue— $111.6 billion for Q2 2025, representing a 13% increase over the prior year.
  • Adjusted EPS— Adjusted earnings per share were $4.08, below the same period last year, primarily due to medical cost trends at UnitedHealthcare and Optum Health.
  • Medical cost impact— $6.5 billion in unexpected medical costs for 2025, with $3.6 billion from Medicare, $2.3 billion from commercial (split between ACA and employer), and remaining pressure from Medicaid.
  • Medicare Advantage cost trend— 2025 full-year Medicare Advantage medical cost trend now estimated at 7.5%, up from an initial expectation "just over" 5%, which was in line with the normalized trend experience in 2024.
  • Medicare Supplement trend— Medicare Supplement trend is expected to be over 11% in 2025, compared to an 8%-9% range in recent years.
  • ACA exchange losses— $600 million of unfavorable impacts recognized from ACA exchange business in Q2 2025, including a premium deficiency reserve reflecting higher market-wide morbidity.
  • Group fully insured trend— Commercial group fully insured medical cost trend is approaching 11% for 2025, about 100 basis points above initial projections.
  • Behavioral health in Medicaid— Medicaid behavioral health cost trend is running at 20% for 2025, contributing to persistent margin challenges.
  • Optum Health value-based care margins— Operating margin in value-based care is approximately 1% for 2025, compared to over 3% in 2024 and nearly 5% in 2023.
  • V28 risk model impact— The V28 industry-wide risk model change is estimated to create an $11 billion headwind for Optum Health over three years, with $7 billion realized to date.
  • Plan exits— Over 600,000 Medicare members will be affected by 2026 product exits, mainly in PPO offerings.
  • Optum Health revenue— Optum Health revenues were $25.2 billion in Q2 2025, down $1.8 billion year-over-year due to contract adjustments and Medicare funding reductions.
  • Optum Insight revenue— Second-quarter 2025 Optum Insight revenues of $4.8 billion increased by $285 million, or 6% year-over-year, but outlook for 2025 was reduced due to slower recovery from last year's cyber event and paused portfolio actions.
  • OptumRx growth— OptumRx revenue grew $6 billion, or 19%, to $38.5 billion in Q2 2025; earnings growth for 2025 was constrained by $510 million in headwinds from portfolio actions, new business investment, and GLP-1 drug costs.
  • Guidance update— Adjusted 2025 earnings outlook is set at a minimum $16 per share; full-year revenues are forecasted to approach $448 billion for 2025.
  • Medical care ratio guidance— Full year 2025 medical care ratio expected at 89.25%, plus or minus 25 basis points, up from an initial midpoint of 86.5%.
  • Dividend increase— UnitedHealth increased its dividend by 5% in June 2025.
  • Cash flow— Full-year 2025 expected cash flow from operations is about $16 billion, or 1.1 times net income.
  • Share count— Updated 2025 share count projected at 912–914 million, compared with an earlier outlook of 918–923 million shares.

Summary

UnitedHealth Group (UNH 0.84%) management disclosed a significant underestimation of 2025 medical cost trends across Medicare, commercial, and Medicaid, resetting expectations for earnings and margins through 2026. The call detailed major strategic and operational changes, including leadership turnover, new technology investments, and remediation efforts to address pricing and risk model shortfalls, notably V28. Segment outlooks highlighted sharp earnings compression in Medicare and value-based care, extensive plan exits, and cost pressures in behavioral health and pharmacy. Management emphasized a multi-year earnings recovery path, capital allocation restraint, and an updated commitment to margin and portfolio discipline.

  • Management confirmed Optum Health's long-term margin target is now 6%-8%, with value-based care at 5%, incorporating longer maturation timelines and sustained investment in new cohorts, as stated in the company's long-term outlook.
  • Patrick Conway said, "Optum Health earnings in 2025 are approximately $6.6 billion below our expectations," driven by roughly $3.6 billion in value-based care, $2 billion in discontinued portfolio actions, and $1 billion in services and prior period impacts.
  • The planned Amedisys acquisition remains an active capital priority, with company leaders stressing its strategic relevance for in-home and value-based care integration.
  • Cost reduction and AI adoption -- expected to yield nearly $1 billion in 2026 -- are positioned as enablers for operational execution and longer-term earnings growth, particularly within Optum Health.
  • The full-year medical care ratio for 2025 is projected at 89.25%, up sharply from the previous outlook, reflecting rising service intensity, coding, and lagging rate adjustments, especially in government programs.

Industry glossary

  • V28 risk model: The 28th version of CMS-HCC risk adjustment used to determine payments to Medicare Advantage plans, introducing substantial funding and scoring changes impacting risk-based care revenue.
  • Premium deficiency reserve: A financial reserve established when expected future policy benefits and expenses exceed related premium revenue, as recognized in the ACA exchange business for higher-than-anticipated morbidity.
  • GLP-1s: Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists, a class of specialty drugs impacting pharmacy benefit costs.
  • Dual Special Needs Plans (D SNPs): Medicare Advantage plans that enroll beneficiaries eligible for both Medicare and Medicaid.
  • Medicare Advantage (MA): A type of Medicare health plan offered by private companies that contract with Medicare to provide all Part A and Part B benefits.
  • Accountable care arrangements: Healthcare payment and delivery arrangements where providers are financially responsible for patient outcomes and cost efficiency.
  • Optum Insight: UnitedHealth’s information and technology segment focused on data, analytics, and managed services for the healthcare ecosystem.
