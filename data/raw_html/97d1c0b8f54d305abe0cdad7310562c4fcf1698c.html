RISKS

  • Eduard Grabscheid said, "The weakening U.S. dollar against global currencies has created a year-over-year headwind for our operating expenses and is reflected in our operating profit guidance."
  • Management highlighted persistent macroeconomic and geopolitical uncertainty, noting, "Despite significant macroeconomic and geopolitical challenges, the JFrog team rose to new heights."

SUMMARY

Cloud revenue accounted for 46% of total revenue in 2025, and management guided to baseline cloud growth of 30%-32% for 2026. Security core products expanded to over 10% of ARR and 16% of RPO at year-end, driven by bundled enterprise security solutions and multiyear commitments. JFrog (FROG 2.79%) emphasized the surge in software binaries—driven by both human developers and AI agents—as a major demand driver for its platform across DevOps, security, and MLOps, resulting in record expansion metrics among large customers and further validating its positioning as the system of record for modern software supply chains. Looking ahead, management projected a 17.5% increase in total revenue for 2026 and baseline cloud revenue growth of 30%-32%, while cautioning that FX headwinds and global uncertainty may impact operating expenses and profit margins.

  • Shlomi Ben Haim described the ongoing attack landscape as "that we anticipate will only grow," underlining continued structural demand for supply chain security solutions.
  • The consolidation of about 300 lower value customer entities into parent organizations during the year was executed to align the customer base with enterprise growth priorities.
  • Advanced integrations, such as MCP server and agent-based security, support business-to-agent workflows and new use cases for software artifact management and governance.
  • Partnerships with NVIDIA Enterprise AI Factory and Hugging Face secured key positions for JFrog as a model and artifact registry provider in both enterprise and open-source channels.
  • Gross margin stability is expected to be maintained even with increased cloud adoption, as management projects annual gross margins for 2026 in the 82%-83% range.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): The value of contracted recurring revenue normalized to a one-year period as of a specific date.
  • RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations): The total expected revenue from contracted customers yet to be recognized, including both current and non-current portions.
  • DevSecOps: An approach integrating security practices within DevOps processes to ensure secure software delivery and operation.
  • MLOps: The process and set of practices for managing the life cycle of machine learning models within production environments, including deployment, monitoring, and governance.
  • JFrog ML: JFrog's platform module focused on machine learning model management and life-cycle automation.
  • MCP server (Model Coordination Protocol server): An integration layer within JFrog's platform enabling AI agents and LLMs to securely interact with the ecosystem.
  • AppTrust: JFrog’s governance tool designed to automate policy enforcement and regulatory compliance in artifact workflow pipelines before production release.
  • JFrog Curation: A security product that serves as a firewall, enforcing artifact and package policies to prevent compromised or malicious components from entering the software supply chain.
