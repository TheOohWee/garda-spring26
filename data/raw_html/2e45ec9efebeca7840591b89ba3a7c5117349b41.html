Darden DRI Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 18, 2025 at 9:56AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, December 18, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Rick Cardenas
  • Chief Financial Officer — Rajesh Vennam
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Courtney Aquilla

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Total sales -- $3.1 billion, an increase of 7% driven by 4.3% same restaurant sales growth, 30 net new restaurants, and the Chewy’s acquisition.
  • Adjusted diluted net earnings per share -- $2.08, up 2.5%, with adjusted EBITDA of $466 million for the quarter.
  • Shareholder returns -- $396 million returned via $174 million in dividends and $222 million in share repurchases.
  • Margin performance -- EBITDA margin of 18.7%; adjusted effective tax rate was 13.2% with $243 million in adjusted earnings from continuing operations (7.8% of sales).
  • Commodity impact -- Food and beverage expenses rose 90 basis points, mainly due to near-record beef costs causing 5.5% commodities inflation; segment margins fell year over year except at Olive Garden.
  • Labor and expenses -- Labor inflation was 3.3% and labor costs increased 10 basis points, but productivity gains offset pricing below labor inflation; marketing expenses declined 10 basis points.
  • Restaurant openings -- 17 new units opened, generating 40 additional operating weeks; full-year guidance raised to 65–70 openings.
  • Olive Garden sales -- Same restaurant sales grew 4.7% (traffic +1.7%, catering +1.1%, pricing +2.6%) and total sales rose 5.4% with a segment profit margin of 21.8%, 30 basis points above last year despite a 20 basis point margin impact from lighter portions menu and delivery fees.
  • Never Ending Pasta Bowl -- Maintained $13.99 starting price for the fourth year; refill rates reached a record, with "all-time high guest satisfaction scores."
  • First-party delivery -- Uber Direct sales represented 4% of Olive Garden’s total sales, about half incremental; first-party delivery pulled younger, affluent, higher-frequency guests with higher check averages.
  • Lighter portions menu -- Olive Garden saw a double-digit increase in affordability perceptions and higher frequency among lighter portion guests; 40% of restaurants offered it in the quarter, expanding early in Q3, with full rollout set for January.
  • LongHorn Steakhouse sales -- Same restaurant sales up 5.9% and total sales rose 9.3% with a segment margin of 16.2%; team member turnover hit a record low, and all-time high Steaks Road Correctly scores were achieved.
  • Other business segment -- Sales increased 11.3% (same restaurant sales up 3.1%), with Yard House performance leading and segment margin at 13.4%, 60 basis points lower due to commodity pressures.
  • Fine dining segment -- Sales grew 3.3% (same restaurant sales up 0.8%) and segment margin was 14.8%, down 280 basis points owing to beef costs; Ruth’s Chris and Capital Grille highlighted for traffic and sales improvements.
  • Pricing vs. inflation -- Company priced 130 basis points below inflation in the quarter; LongHorn pricing ran 320 basis points below inflation in the quarter, with management stating a bias to minimize pricing and expecting some modest price increases at LongHorn going forward.
  • Guidance update -- Fiscal 2026 expectations: 8.5%-9.3% sales growth, 3.5%-4.3% same restaurant sales growth, $750–$775 million in capital spending, 3.5% total inflation, 4%-5% commodities inflation. Fiscal year ends May 31, 2026.
  • Third quarter outlook -- Mid-single-digit EPS growth forecasted; management projects earnings per share growth to sequentially accelerate in Q3 and Q4 as pricing catches up to inflation and beef costs moderate.
  • Labor turnover -- Company-wide and brand-level team member turnover reached record lows, while hourly wage inflation was about 3%, lower than pre-pandemic averages.
  • Marketing spend -- Year-over-year marketing expense expected to rise roughly 10 basis points with cost savings funding incremental activity; Uber Direct marketing was paused in Q2 in favor of prioritizing other promotions.
  • Demographic trends -- Casual brands saw year-over-year visit growth from middle and higher income guests (largest gains from higher-income households); strong traffic growth from guests 55 and over, but lower-income visits declined.
  • CapEx outlook -- Capital expenditures guidance is $750 million–$775 million, mainly driven by accelerated restaurant openings and pipeline investments.

Summary

Darden Restaurants (DRI 1.26%) emphasized that its portfolio exceeded sales expectations with each segment achieving positive same restaurant sales and highlighted the positive gap to industry benchmarks widening throughout the quarter. Management confirmed that Olive Garden’s first-party delivery and lighter portions menu are incrementally supporting sales growth as rollouts expand, and noted Yard House has launched delivery while LongHorn considers further participation. The company updated its fiscal 2026 guidance (fiscal year ending May 31, 2026) to reflect stronger year-to-date performance but cautioned on persistent commodity inflation, specifically beef, which pressured segment margins except at Olive Garden. Executives stated that wage and labor conditions have improved and turnover is at record lows, and they reiterated capital deployment priorities in shareholder returns and disciplined unit expansion. Darden expects pricing to approach inflation by year-end as cost pressures begin to abate, especially for beef, allowing sequential EPS growth acceleration in the latter half of the fiscal year.

  • Adjusted G&A expenses were 60 basis points favorable due to sales leverage and lower incentive compensation accruals, though these savings were offset in the tax line by hedging effects.
  • The acquisition of Chewy’s in October contributed positively to top-line growth, particularly within the “other business” segment, as did the addition of new restaurants across the portfolio.
  • Yard House’s Oktoberfest event and Ruth’s Chris $55 three-course menu were identified as key drivers of increased guest engagement and sales momentum in their respective brands.
  • Company management confirmed a measured approach to pricing, with the stated “bias is to minimize pricing,” and signaled only moderate price actions despite inflationary headwinds.
  • Management indicated pipeline strength is sufficient to sustain or increase future year unit growth, emphasizing no anticipated pullback in expansion pace for fiscal 2027 and beyond.

Industry glossary

  • First-party delivery: In-house controlled food delivery service, as opposed to third-party aggregators, allowing Darden direct customer access and data ownership.
  • Segment profit margin: Operating profit from a specific business segment divided by that segment’s sales, before company-wide interest and other expenses.
  • Steaks Road Correctly: An internal quality metric at LongHorn Steakhouse measuring the frequency steaks are cooked to the ordered specifications.
