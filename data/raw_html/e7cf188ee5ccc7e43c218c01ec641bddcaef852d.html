Earnings Call Transcript

Sysco (SYY) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 12:09PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Oct. 29, 2024 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chair of the Board and Chief Executive Officer — Kevin P. Hourican
  • Chief Financial Officer — Kenny Cheung

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Total Revenue -- $20.5 billion, reflecting 4.4% growth driven by U.S. Foodservice volume increase of 2.7% and moderate inflation.
  • U.S. Foodservice Volume -- 2.7% increase comprised of 5.5% national volume growth and 0.2% local volume growth.
  • International Segment Performance -- Sales rose 3% while adjusted operating income grew 12%, attributed to Sysco brand expansion, operational improvements, and new higher-margin categories.
  • SYGMA Segment Results -- Sales up 7.3% and operating income up 38.5% after customer mix reset.
  • Adjusted Operating Income -- $873 million, representing 2.2% year-over-year growth.
  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share (EPS) -- $1.09, up 1.9% as reported.
  • Gross Profit -- $3.8 billion, an increase of 2.9%; gross profit margin was 18.3%, down 27 basis points due to customer mix, timing of strategic sourcing benefits, and reduced Sysco brand penetration.
  • Product Inflation -- 2.2% for the enterprise, in line with expectations.
  • Sysco Brand Penetration -- Down 59 basis points to 36.5% in U.S. Broadline and down 55 basis points to 47% in U.S. local results.
  • Adjusted Operating Expenses -- $2.9 billion, or 14.1% of sales, improving 18 basis points year over year due to supply chain and corporate efficiencies.
  • Corporate Expenses -- Down 14.5% on an adjusted basis from prior year, credited to efficiency actions taken in FY 2024 and during the quarter.
  • Cash Returned to Shareholders -- More than $359 million distributed in the quarter via share repurchases and dividends.
  • 2025 Fiscal Guidance Reaffirmed -- Net sales growth expected at 4%-5%, adjusted EPS growth forecasted at 6%-7%, and adjusted tax rate projected at approximately 25% due to the global minimum tax.
  • Second-half Margin Outlook -- Management expects gross profit margins to expand in the back half from improved local sales, Sysco brand penetration, and strategic sourcing execution.
  • Mexico Joint Venture Divestiture -- Planned exit from Mexico market with an expected $500 million annual revenue impact, described as immaterial to profits.
  • Recent Acquisition -- Campbells Prime Meat, a leading specialty meat company in Scotland, added to expand specialty offerings in the U.K. market.
  • Local Sales Compensation Change -- New sales consultant compensation model launched July 1, shifting earnings toward incentive-based pay, expected to drive customer count and sales growth in the second half.
  • Free Cash Flow and Liquidity -- Operating cash flow of $53 million and an increase in free cash flow of $81 million, with total liquidity at $3.3 billion.
  • Net Debt Leverage Ratio -- Ended the quarter at 2.7x, within management’s stated target range.
  • Supply Chain Productivity -- Pieces per labor hour improved low to mid-single digits year over year, and retention of supply chain staff more than doubled.

Summary

Sysco (SYY 2.89%) reported 4.4% revenue growth and 2.2% adjusted operating income expansion, with results keeping the company on track for full-year guidance. Management highlighted acceleration in national sales, robust SYGMA and international income growth, and successful actions to return cash to shareholders. Second-half gross margin is projected to improve from local business initiatives, strategic sourcing benefits, and higher Sysco brand penetration. Company leaders emphasized disciplined hiring and revamped sales incentives, forecasting positive operating leverage and productivity gains. Ongoing business portfolio optimization includes a planned Mexico JV divestiture and the integration of acquired Campbells Prime Meat assets.

  • CFO Cheung stated, We remain on target to return $2 billion back to shareholders this fiscal year through share repurchase and dividends.
  • September was our strongest month of the quarter, both top and bottom line. Next and is continuing into this quarter. Cheung added, suggesting building operational momentum.
  • Operational efficiency improvements reduced corporate expenses by 14.5%, freeing resources for future investments.
  • Sysco leaders confirmed their confidence in achieving 4%-5% net sales growth and 6%-7% adjusted EPS expansion for full-year 2025.

Industry glossary

  • Broadline: Distribution model serving a wide variety of food and non-food products to foodservice operators from a centralized supply chain.
  • SYGMA: Sysco's subsidiary specializing in customized foodservice distribution to chain restaurant customers.
  • Strategic Sourcing: Procurement approach focused on maximizing value and efficiency by leveraging supplier negotiations, category management, and geographic buying power.
  • Total Team Selling: Sysco initiative that cross-sells specialty business offerings (e.g., produce, protein) to customers already using Broadline services.
  • Adjusted EPS: Earnings per share figure excluding certain items to better reflect ongoing performance, as defined by the company.
