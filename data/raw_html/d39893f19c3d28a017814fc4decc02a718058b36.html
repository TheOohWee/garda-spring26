Earnings Call Transcript

DXC Technology (DXC) Q3 2026 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 30, 2026 at 9:05AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, January 29, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Raul Fernandez
  • Chief Financial Officer — Robert Del Bene
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Roger Sachs

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $3.2 billion, down 4.3% organically year over year, within DXC Technology Company (DXC 7.52%)’s guidance range.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio -- 1.12 for the quarter, bringing the trailing 12-month ratio to 1.02 and marking the fourth consecutive quarter above 1.
  • Adjusted EBIT Margin -- 8.2%, slightly above the high end of guidance, with a year-over-year decline of 70 basis points mainly due to planned investments in offering development and marketing.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $0.96, an increase from $0.92 year over year, attributed primarily to a lower share count, net interest expense, and taxes, partially offset by lower adjusted EBIT.
  • Segment Revenue: CES -- Down 3.6% organically year over year; book-to-bill ratio for CES was 1.2, and trailing 12-month CES book-to-bill reached 1.13.
  • Segment Revenue: GIS -- Declined 6.2% organically year over year, which was in line with full-year guidance; GIS book-to-bill improved to 1.09 for the quarter.
  • Segment Revenue: Insurance -- Up 3.2% year over year, driven by growth in the software business, specifically customer migrations to cloud-based Assure and new AI-enabled smart apps.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $266 million for the quarter, totaling $603 million year-to-date, and tracking toward the full-year guide of approximately $650 million.
  • Total Debt Reduction -- Debt declined by $465 million to $3.6 billion after refinancing a €650 million bond and prepaying $300 million of a $700 million bond; net debt reduction since the start of fiscal 2025 has reached approximately $970 million.
  • Share Repurchase Activity -- $190 million repurchased year-to-date, including $65 million in Q3; projected to reach $250 million for the fiscal year, up from initial guidance of $150 million.
  • Fast Track Initiative Progress -- DXC expects these AI-infused offerings to achieve 10% of run rate revenue by the end of Q2 fiscal 2029; management stated “development time lines and early client interest tracking ahead of our initial plans.”
  • Notable New Contract -- Secured a major master vendor engagement with the London Metropolitan Police to lead their enterprise transformation, citing replicable public sector expertise and execution.
  • FY26 Q4 Guidance -- Expects organic revenue decline of 4%-5%, adjusted EBIT margin of 6.5%-7.5%, and non-GAAP diluted EPS of $0.65-$0.75.
  • Updated Full-Year FY26 Guidance -- Projects organic revenue decline of approximately 4.3%, adjusted EBIT margin of approximately 7.5%, non-GAAP diluted EPS near $3.15, and free cash flow around $650 million.
  • FY27 Capital Allocation Outlook -- Plans include retiring $400 million in bonds and repurchasing $250 million of shares in the first half, matching FY26 repurchase levels.
  • AI Deployment Internal Use -- DXC has implemented AI at scale for all 115,000 employees, facilitating production timelines from weeks instead of months, and integrating multiple major AI providers with technical portability.

RISKS

  • Delays in closing large business process services (BPS) deals in insurance, as management noted, “a couple of large BPS opportunities that have now been delayed to 4Q and will impact our 4Q revenue forecast for insurance.”
  • Continued organic revenue declines of 4%-5% projected into Q4, reflecting ongoing top-line contraction in core segments.
  • Management cited persistent softness in U.S. operations and slower conversion of short-term projects, impacting sequential improvement expectations.
  • Investments in new offerings and AI initiatives continue to place near-term pressure on adjusted EBIT margin.

SUMMARY

Management emphasized a unified dual-track strategy—stabilizing core heritage businesses while accelerating AI-driven, productized solutions—with measurable operational and brand transformations initiated this quarter. DXC closed a flagship contract with the London Metropolitan Police and reported early success from its Fast Track AI initiatives, targeting 10% of run rate revenue contribution by fiscal 2029, supported by internally-developed platforms such as Core Ignite and agentic security solutions. Capital deployment priorities were clarified, with shareholder returns increasing to $250 million in repurchases for the year and $400 million earmarked for debt retirement in early FY27. Management highlighted the accelerating impact of AI in internal operations, noting improved development speed and margin potential, yet underscored that ongoing revenue declines and delayed deal conversion will impact short-term segment results.

  • Speaker Fernandez confirmed, “the era of rates times hours is ending,” outlining a systematic move toward value-based and transactional pricing models in new offerings.
  • Speaker Del Bene signaled a shift in free cash flow seasonality, with this fiscal year’s working capital benefits pulled forward to the first half.
  • Further details on Fast Track product commercialization, revenue ramp, and AI portfolio composition will be provided at the June Investor Day, including live demonstrations and expanded forward guidance.
  • Company executives repeatedly positioned large-scale AI deployment and proprietary legacy platforms as a competitive moat, enabling both cost reductions and defensible, repeatable product expansion.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: A metric indicating the ratio of new business bookings to the revenue billed within a period, signaling demand trends and future revenue visibility within services and technology firms.
  • Fast Track Initiatives: DXC’s internally-branded portfolio of AI-driven, productized solutions and IP intended to accelerate revenue growth and margin expansion, distinct from traditional services.
  • CES: Customer Experience and Support, a reporting segment at DXC focused on advisory, analytics, and consulting solutions; contextually refers to the former “GBS” segment in the transcript.
  • GIS: Global Infrastructure Services, DXC’s segment handling legacy modernization, IT outsourcing, and cloud adaptation services.
  • Core Ignite: A DXC-developed, AI-powered platform that connects legacy banking mainframes to fintech and new digital products without core system replacement, enabling modernization for existing clients.
  • Agentic Security Operations Center (powered by 7AI): An internally-deployed, AI-based security monitoring and incident response system, resolving the majority of threats automatically and now offered externally.
  • Business Process Services (BPS): Outsourced services focused on automating and optimizing front/back office processes, referenced here in relation to insurance segment deals.
  • Assure: DXC’s cloud-based software solution for insurance carriers, supporting digital migration and AI-enabled productivity enhancements.
