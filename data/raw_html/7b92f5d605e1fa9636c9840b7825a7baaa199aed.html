Earnings Call Transcript

US Foods (USFD) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 12:18PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 12, 2026 at 9:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — David Flitman
  • Chief Financial Officer — Dirk Locascio

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $39.4 billion, reflecting 4.1% growth through continued market share gains.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Increased 11% to above $1.9 billion for the full year; fourth quarter grew 11% to $490 million.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin -- Expanded by 30 basis points to a record 4.9% for the year; fourth quarter margin rose 35 basis points to 5%.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- Achieved $3.98 for the year, up 26%; fourth quarter adjusted diluted EPS was $1.04, up 24%.
  • Independent Restaurant Case Volume -- Rose 4.1% in the fourth quarter, with net new independent account growth of approximately 4.7%.
  • Healthcare and Hospitality Sales -- Accounted for over 25% of total sales; healthcare grew 2.9%, hospitality increased 3.1% in the fourth quarter.
  • Chain Restaurant Volume -- Declined by 3.4% in the fourth quarter, attributed to industry slowdowns and prior strategic exits.
  • Adjusted Gross Profit per Case -- Up $0.23 or 2.9% in the fourth quarter; full year adjusted EBITDA per case increased nearly 10%.
  • Cost of Goods Savings -- Exceeded $150 million for the year, driven by strategic vendor management; three-year savings goal increased to $300 million versus previous $260 million.
  • Inventory Management Improvements -- Yielded $40 million gross profit benefit in 2025, surpassing prior expectations.
  • Indirect Cost Savings -- Realized $45 million in 2025; projected to exceed $100 million by 2027.
  • Private Label Penetration -- Rose 90 basis points to nearly 54% among independent restaurant customers; 25% of those customers have over 70% penetration.
  • Cash Flow -- Generated nearly $1.4 billion in operating cash flow; invested $410 million in capital expenditures.
  • Share Repurchases -- Repurchased 11.9 million shares for $934 million in 2025; $1.1 billion remains authorized.
  • Tuck-in Acquisitions -- Completed 2 acquisitions for $131 million focused on local market density.
  • Net Leverage -- Ended the year at 2.7x, within the 2x to 3x target range; Moody’s upgraded credit rating to Ba1.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Expects net sales growth of 4%-6%, total case growth of 2.5%-4.5%, and independent case growth of 4%-7%.
  • Adjusted EBITDA and EPS Outlook -- Projects adjusted EBITDA growth of 9%-13% and adjusted diluted EPS growth of 18%-24% for 2026.
  • Sales Force Headcount -- Seller headcount grew nearly 7% in 2025, with external hires at mid-single-digit percentages.
  • Sales Compensation Change -- Transition to 100% variable commission begins mid-2026, with the majority expected to convert over 2-3 years; pilot feedback is “very positive.”
  • Operational Efficiency -- Descartes routing deployment improved cases per mile by 2% in 2025; operational quality composite improved 15% during the year.
  • Technology Initiatives -- Embedded AI in MOXe platform enabled new ordering features that ease customer workload and increase seller capacity.
  • Pronto Delivery Service -- Generated over $1 billion in sales, live in 46 markets, with expansion planned for 10-15 additional markets in 2026; Next Day variant implemented in 24 markets.
  • Employee Safety -- Injury and accident frequency rates improved 16% from the prior year, with more than 60% of safety equipment deployment completed.
  • Philanthropic Giving -- Donated $12.5 million to hunger relief and other causes, including more than 5 million pounds of food and supplies.

RISKS

  • Fourth quarter chain restaurant volume declined 3.4%, primarily due to industry-wide traffic softness, government shutdown, and weather disruptions, which also hindered volume acceleration late in the quarter.
  • Severe weather in early 2026 led to 35% more distribution center closure days compared to the prior year quarter, negatively impacting volume and costs, and contributing to only “upper single-digit” adjusted EBITDA growth expected for the first quarter.

SUMMARY

US Foods Holding Corp. (USFD 0.88%) delivered record earnings, margin expansion, and robust share gains across key customer segments, while implementing productivity initiatives that exceeded prior targets. The company advanced its three-year cost savings and operational improvement plans, raised its long-term savings goals, and drove digital innovation, particularly through AI and next-day delivery services. Operational efficiency gains, expanded private label penetration, and steady capital deployment through share buybacks and acquisitions supported increased returns, while management provided specific 2026 guidance for double-digit EPS and EBITDA growth. The detailed transition to a variable compensation model, proven capital allocation discipline, and credit rating upgrade were explicitly highlighted as strategic differentiators for long-term value creation.

  • Management stated, “we delivered record adjusted earnings per share of $3.98. Our adjusted EPS growth of 26% led the industry and was more than twice our double-digit adjusted EBITDA growth rate.”
  • Dirk Locascio said, “result, adjusted EBITDA margin expanded 35 basis points to 5%.”
  • David Flitman emphasized, “Actually, since we started -- first started talking about this, all of our experienced cohorts, and we have different experience cohorts in different buckets. They've all improved since we've started to talk about this. So that's a very encouraging sign.”
  • Implementation of center ride powered industrial equipment is expected to be fully completed by year-end to further reduce injuries.
  • Pronto’s rollout, including Pronto Next Day, produced “double-digit levels of uplift” in volume from participating markets according to management.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • MOXe: US Foods’ proprietary digital platform leveraging AI to streamline ordering, customer engagement, and sales productivity.
  • Descartes: Enterprise routing software deployed by US Foods to optimize delivery routes and improve operational efficiency.
  • Pronto: US Foods’ small truck, fast-turn delivery service targeting rapid replenishment in select urban and local markets.
