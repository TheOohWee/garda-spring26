Petco (WOOF) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 25, 2025 at 7:51PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Nov. 25, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Joel Anderson
  • Chief Financial Officer — Sabrina Simmons

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • Sabrina Simmons stated, "in this quarter, tariffs began to more meaningfully impact our cost of goods sold," with a sequentially larger effect anticipated in the fourth quarter, which may pressure gross margins.
  • Net sales declined 3.1% and comparable sales fell 2.2%, driven partly by 25 net store closures in 2024 and an additional nine net store closures year to date, indicating ongoing sales headwinds.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- Decreased 3.1%, with comparable sales down 2.2%; store closures were a key driver of the gap between total and comparable sales.
  • Gross Margin -- Expanded approximately 75 basis points to 38.9%, driven by improved pricing and promotional discipline.
  • SG&A Expense -- Decreased by $32 million, resulting in a 97 basis point leverage gain, particularly driven by expense management and flat year-over-year marketing spend.
  • Operating Margin -- Increased more than 170 basis points due to combined margin expansion and expense leverage.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA rose 21% to $99 million, with the adjusted EBITDA margin expanding nearly 140 basis points to 6.7% of sales.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Generated $61 million in the quarter and $71 million year to date, both significantly above the prior year.
  • Inventory -- Ended the quarter down 10.5%, while in-stock levels for customers improved.
  • Cash and Liquidity -- Closed with $237 million cash and $733 million total liquidity, including undrawn revolver availability.
  • Store Footprint -- Ended with 1,389 U.S. stores after nine net closures year to date.
  • Guidance -- Raised full-year adjusted EBITDA outlook to $395 million-$397 million (up roughly 18% at midpoint); net sales now expected to decline 2.5%-2.8% for the year; Q4 adjusted EBITDA guidance is $93 million-$95 million.
  • Service Segment Performance -- Services business remains the fastest-growing category and is cited as a core competitive moat due to in-person delivery and integration with stores.
  • Tariff Impact -- Management confirmed tariffs began affecting cost of goods sold this quarter, particularly in private label supplies, with greater sequential impact expected in Q4.
  • Transformation Initiatives -- Four-pillar strategy focused on merchandising, store experience, integrated services, and omni-channel execution remains central to the transition toward growth in 2026.
  • Membership Program -- New membership program is currently being piloted in select districts, with broader rollout planned in 2026.
  • Use of Cash Flow -- Priority is internal investment, especially to ramp utilization of existing veterinary assets, followed by deleveraging, which is resulting in a net debt to EBITDA ratio targeted below 3.5x if guidance is met.

SUMMARY

Petco Health and Wellness (WOOF 1.76%) management reported substantial margin and cash flow improvement while noting top-line contraction driven by store rationalization and a pivot away from unprofitable sales. Leaders highlighted expanding gross and operating margins, as well as an increased adjusted EBITDA outlook despite increased tariff-related cost pressures. Strategic resource allocation is shifting toward growth drivers for 2026, including an integrated store and services model and a new customer membership program. Leaders confirmed sustained discipline in working capital and inventory, which underpinned improved free cash flow and set the foundation for selective reinvestment to accelerate profitable sales growth.

  • Joel Anderson explained that the organization is building on core pillars and executing "with discipline" to fuel sales growth in 2026 after a focused push to correct profitability this year.
  • SG&A reductions were achieved broadly, especially in G&A, enabling operating leverage and providing the company with "the option to begin selectively investing behind the business" in Q4 as part of a multi-year transformation.
  • Sabrina Simmons articulated that internal investments will focus primarily on existing vet hospital utilization, avoiding major new capital requirements in the near term.
  • Leadership emphasized that sequentially larger tariff headwinds will impact Q4 margin results, with additional investments in marketing, labor, or promotions to be weighed as a function of overall profitability targets.
  • The four-pillar strategy is already being tested in some markets, with leadership confirming a timeline for more detailed growth guidance in March and an expectation for net closures to slow in 2026.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, excluding non-recurring, non-core, or non-cash items, used as a proxy for core operating performance.
  • Comparable Sales (Comp Sales): Metric reflecting sales performance at stores open for a full year or more, isolating underlying performance from footprint changes.
  • SG&A: Selling, General, and Administrative expenses, covering overhead costs, excluding cost of goods sold.
  • Basis Point: Equal to one one-hundredth of a percentage point, commonly used to describe changes in margins or rates.
  • Omni Experience: Integrated approach to customer engagement across physical stores, digital channels, and services.
  • Membership Program: Customer loyalty initiative offering benefits in exchange for enrollment, intended to drive repeat visits and higher spend.
