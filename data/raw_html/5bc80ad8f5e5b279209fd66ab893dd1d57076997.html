Earnings Call Transcript

W.P. Carey (WPC) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 11:04AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Oct. 30, 2024, at 11 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Jason Fox
  • Chief Financial Officer — Toni Sanzone
  • Head of Asset Management — Brooks Gordon
  • Chairman — Peter Sands

TAKEAWAYS

  • AFFO Per Share -- $1.18 for the third quarter, up $0.01 sequentially from the prior quarter, primarily due to accretive investment activity and rent growth, mostly offset by higher interest rates.
  • Full-Year AFFO Guidance -- Narrowed to $4.65–$4.71, midpoint unchanged, incorporating incremental dividend income from Lineage offset by lower other lease-related income and delayed transaction closings.
  • Investment Volume -- $167 million of new investments closed in the third quarter; an additional $231 million closed since quarter-end, bringing year-to-date volume to approximately $1 billion.
  • Pipeline Visibility -- Over $500 million identified, with most expected to close in North America and a confident path to the $1.5 billion midpoint of full-year investment volume guidance.
  • Weighted Average Cap Rate -- Closed investments year-to-date reflect a 7.6% weighted average going-in cap rate; average yield exceeds 9% when including rent escalators.
  • Tenant Concentration (Watchlist Focus) -- Hellweg, Hearthside, and True Value collectively account for 4.7% of annualized base rent (ABR), with True Value contributing 1.4% after its Chapter 11 filing and current with rent through year-end.
  • Potential Rent Loss Guidance -- Management expects up to 100 basis points of net rent loss (excluding True Value), citing "no visibility into anything specific" but highlighting this as a directional placeholder.
  • Dispositions -- Year-to-date asset sales total $82 million for the third quarter and are guided to a full-year range of $1.3 billion–$1.4 billion, slightly raising the bottom end of prior guidance.
  • Same-Store Rent Growth -- Contractual same-store rent increased 2.8% year over year and is expected to average at the same rate for the full year; comprehensive same-store growth was 20 basis points.
  • Re-Leasing Spreads -- Achieved 103% recapture of prior rents, notably via the conversion of 16 operating self-storage properties to net leases and extension of Extra Space's lease terms to 25 years.
  • Occupancy -- Net lease portfolio ended the quarter at 98.8% occupancy; weighted-average lease term at 12.2 years.
  • Liquidity -- Total liquidity was approximately $2.6 billion, including over $800 million of cash and a minimally drawn $2 billion revolver; no need to raise equity for current and 2025 investment plans.
  • Leverage Metrics -- Debt to gross assets stood at 41.1%, within the target range, and net debt to EBITDA was 5.4 times, below the target range's lower end.
  • Interest Expense -- Reached $72.5 million for the third quarter, up from the prior quarter due to the full impact of $1.1 billion in bond refinancings; average interest rate on debt rose to 3.4% from 3.1%.
  • General and Administrative Expense -- Third quarter G&A was $22.7 million; full-year forecast tightened to $98 million–$100 million.
  • Segment Mix Shift — Retail Emphasis -- 25% of the current portfolio is retail, with about three-quarters outside the U.S.; retail constitutes 40% of the investment pipeline, primarily skewed to the U.S.
  • Asset Sale Proceeds -- Q3 operating property NOI was $20.7 million, down $1.1 million sequentially, attributed to self-storage conversions to net lease and hotel sales.
  • Lineage Investment -- Lineage dividend income was $2.1 million during the third quarter; guidance assumes $2.9 million in dividends for the fourth quarter.

RISKS

  • True Value's bankruptcy poses "uncertainty" for 2025, with management stating, "While it's too soon to have any degree of certainty over which scenario will play out, we're already actively working with brokers and options to re-tenant or dispose of the properties with a focus on mitigating the potential disruption to our earnings. Once we have clarity on the bankruptcy proceedings and the likely outcomes for our properties, including better visibility into the timing and specific cash flow impacts, we will, of course, update the market."
  • Hellweg, accounting for part of 4.7% of ABR among the three watchlist tenants, "still faces a challenging demand environment," signalling ongoing risk exposure despite being current on rent.
  • Leading indicator for 2025 rent loss is approximately 100 basis points, reflecting management's preliminary view that "credit events within our portfolio net of recoveries could total approximately 100 basis points, excluding the."
  • Operating self-storage NOI declined by 4.1% on a same-store basis compared to the prior year, indicating persistent weakness in that subsegment.

SUMMARY

W.P. Carey (WPC +0.17%) management tightened full-year AFFO guidance and reinforced liquidity, maintaining that no equity issuance is needed to fund pipeline investments through 2025. The strategic shift into U.S. retail expands diversification, now constituting 40% of the investment pipeline, with management targeting 30%–40% long term. New investment activity picked up after a late-summer slowdown, with achieved cap rates in the mid-7% and yields above 9%, including rent escalators. The disposition program, particularly of office and self-storage assets, is expected to reach $1.3 billion–$1.4 billion, enabling further balance sheet flexibility. Caution prevails regarding tenant credit events, as management anticipates up to 100 basis points of potential rent loss—excluding True Value, whose outcome remains unresolved in bankruptcy proceedings—but expects no meaningful earnings disruption in 2024.

  • Self-storage conversions included repositioning around 20% of annualized storage NOI into long-term net lease revenue with investment-grade tenancy from Extra Space.
  • Interest expense increased after major bond refinancings, raising the weighted average cost of debt to 3.4%.
  • Comprehensive same-store rent growth remains below the contractual rate, with management attributing the historical 100 basis point gap partly to credit losses, vacancies, and re-leasing; for 2025, the gap may widen.
  • Management explicitly stated, flow to fund the investments in our pipeline during the fourth quarter and into 2025 without the need to issue equity.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ABR (Annualized Base Rent): Total annual rent associated with the portfolio's net lease assets, used to gauge tenant concentration and diversification.
  • AFFO (Adjusted Funds From Operations): A non-GAAP REIT performance metric representing cash available for distribution after recurring capital expenditures, debt service, and other nonoperating items.
  • Net Lease: Real estate lease agreement where the tenant pays most property expenses (taxes, insurance, maintenance) in addition to rent, reducing landlord’s operating risk.
