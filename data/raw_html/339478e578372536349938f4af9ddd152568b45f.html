Corebridge Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 10, 2026 at 10:25AM EST

Date

Tuesday, November 4, 2025 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kevin Hogan
  • Chief Financial Officer — Elias Habayeb
  • Head of Investor Relations — Isil Muderrisoglu

Takeaways

  • Adjusted Pretax Operating Income -- $678 million, excluding VII and notable items, with a $98 million charge from the annual actuarial assumption update disclosed.
  • Operating Earnings Per Share (EPS) -- $0.99 per share, including the notable actuarial charge; run rate operating EPS of $1.21 when adjusting for long-term alternative investment return expectations and notable items, representing a 6% increase year over year.
  • Adjusted Run Rate Return on Equity (ROE) -- 12.9%, up 70 basis points year over year after adjusting for alternative investment returns and notable items.
  • Total Premiums and Deposits -- $12.3 billion, with record performance in Individual Retirement and significant contributions from Institutional Markets (GICs and pension risk transfer).
  • RILA Product Sales -- Nearly $800 million in the quarter and more than $1.7 billion year-to-date; recently approved for sale in New York, with launch expected by year-end.
  • General Account Net Inflows -- $1.4 billion, increasing 27% and supporting 6% general account growth year over year.
  • Fee Income -- Up 7% year over year, primarily from favorable market impacts; fee income now constitutes approximately 60% of Group Retirement core revenue.
  • Capital Returned to Shareholders -- $509 million in the quarter, including $381 million of share repurchases; over $1.4 billion returned year-to-date through buybacks and dividends.
  • Parent Holding Company Liquidity -- $1.8 billion, including partial proceeds from the variable annuity reinsurance transaction.
  • Bermuda Strategy -- $18 billion of reserves ceded since inception, with management highlighting the “financial optionality” created by the strategy.
  • Alternative Investment Returns -- $0.11 per share below long-term expectations this quarter, with ongoing underperformance in real estate equity and a fourth quarter outlook expected “below our long-term expectations of 8% to 9%.”
  • Spread Income -- Down only 1% year over year, despite 100 basis points of Fed rate cuts in 2024; mitigated by asset optimization and business growth.
  • Group Retirement Advisory and Brokerage Assets -- Up 9% year over year to $17.6 billion, setting a new record; out-of-plan assets reached $28.8 billion.
  • Life Insurance Digital Senior Products -- Sales grew 19% year over year, with fully digital offerings expanding despite overall new business sales declining 6%.
  • Institutional Markets Total Reserves -- Increased by $8 billion, or 19%, and marked the sixth consecutive quarter of GIC issuances over $1 billion.
  • Expense Trend -- Short-term increase in compensation-related and one-time medical expenses, as anticipated in previous guidance.
  • Leadership Transition -- Chief Financial Officer Elias Habayeb to depart in April 2026; an executive search is underway with a 6-month transition and completion of 2025 financials secured.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- Elevated repurchase levels expected, enabled by a $2 billion increase authorized by the Board in June.
  • Guidance Reaffirmation -- Management reiterated “a 12% to 14% return on equity, an average 10% to 15% annual EPS growth rate over time and a 60% to 65% payout ratio,” with commitment to maintaining the Life Fleet RBC ratio above target.

Summary

Corebridge Financial (CRBG 1.37%) reported the highest sales since its IPO, driven by record activity across its Individual Retirement and Institutional Markets businesses. The company completed a major variable annuity reinsurance transaction, simplifying its balance sheet and materially increasing liquidity. Strong product demand, particularly for RILA products and pension risk transfer solutions, led to general account net inflows and reserve growth, while continued fee-income momentum is reshaping Group Retirement revenue streams. Operating performance showed resilience as business and asset growth offset headwinds from Fed rate cuts and alternative investment underperformance. Leadership changes were detailed, with a clear and structured succession plan in place to avoid disruption and support strategic continuity.

  • Hogan said, We are now the only company to have a top 10 ranking across all 4 major annuity product categories as measured by LIMRA.
  • Habayeb stated, we delivered run rate operating EPS of $1.21, which represents a 6% year-over-year increase and an adjusted run rate ROE of 12.9%, which is up 70 basis points versus the prior year.
  • The company expects the VA reinsurance transaction to be accretive to the pre-recast EPS by the second half of 2026 once we complete the share repurchases funded by the proceeds from the transaction.
  • Group Retirement is experiencing an ongoing transition from a spread-based to a fee-based revenue stream, with fee income now accounting for 60% of core revenue.
  • Leadership confirmed there will be a 6-month overlap and dedicated executive resources to ensure a seamless CFO transition and finalize 2025 reporting, maintaining investor confidence during the handover.

Industry glossary

  • RILA: Registered Index-Linked Annuity, a variable annuity product providing both upside potential and downside protection linked to equity index performance.
  • GIC: Guaranteed Investment Contract, an insurance product that guarantees principal and fixed or floating interest rates, commonly used in institutional retirement products.
  • PRT: Pension Risk Transfer, a transaction where a pension plan sponsor transfers pension obligations to an insurer, typically via buyout or buy-in structures.
  • VII: Variable Investment Income, an earnings measure reflecting non-core or non-recurring investment results segregated from adjusted operating income.
  • DAC Amortization: Deferred Acquisition Cost amortization, representing the periodic expense of acquisition costs deferred under accounting rules.
  • COLI/BOLI: Corporate-Owned Life Insurance / Bank-Owned Life Insurance, life insurance policies where the corporate or banking institution is the policy owner and beneficiary.
  • Life Fleet RBC Ratio: Statutory Risk-Based Capital ratio for the company’s life insurance subsidiaries, representing solvency strength relative to regulatory thresholds.
