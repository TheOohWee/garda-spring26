We have increasing conviction that our initial execution plan and structuring was flawed. Takeaways

Normalized FFO per share— $1.28 in normalized funds from operations (non-GAAP) per diluted share in Q2 2025, up 21.9% year over year. The full-year 2025 normalized FFO midpoint guidance was raised to $5.10 per share, an increase of $0.13.

Seniors housing operating portfolio same-store NOI growth— 23.4% same-store NOI growth for the seniors housing operating portfolio, marking the eleventh consecutive quarter above 20% growth.

Organic revenue growth— 10%, aided by a 420-basis-point occupancy gain; UK portfolio occupancy rose 600 basis points, with 27% same-store NOI growth.

RevPOR growth— Approximately 5% RevPOR growth, reflecting pricing power across all regions; the spread between RevPOR and ExpPOR produced a 330-basis-point SHOP margin expansion.

Milestone achieved— SHOP assets’ annualized NOI surpassed $2 billion, while overall annualized company revenue reached $10 billion for the first time.

Total portfolio same-store NOI growth— 13.8% year-over-year total portfolio same-store NOI growth.

Outpatient medical same-store NOI growth— 2.6% year-over-year same-store NOI growth for the outpatient medical business, with occupancy at 95% and retention at 94.2%.

Expense growth— ExpPOR up only 0.2%, the lowest reported; utility costs increased just 2.8% year over year and declined 2.1% per occupied day year over year.

Operating margin— Increased 330 basis points year over year to 30.7%.

Investment activity— $9.2 billion closed or under contract year to date as of late July 2025, already surpassing 2024 volume by 50%.

On-balance sheet Q2 transactions— $1 billion, with 90% sourced off-market, covering 2,500 units and a blended occupancy of 76% (component figures for the $9.2 billion year-to-date: $3.7 billion closed, $5.5 billion under contract).

Triple net senior housing same-store NOI growth— 5.1% occupancy growth guidance for the full year; trailing twelve-month EBITDAR coverage at 1.19 times for the period ending March 31, 2025.

Long-term post-acute same-store NOI growth— 2.7% year-over-year same-store NOI growth for the long-term post-acute portfolio; trailing twelve-month EBITDAR coverage at 1.9 times for the period ending March 31, 2025.

Gross equity raised— $2 billion, plus $1.25 billion in senior unsecured notes at a 4.825% blended coupon.

Cash and restricted cash— $4.5 billion in cash and restricted cash on the balance sheet at quarter end; net debt to adjusted EBITDA at 2.93 times (lowest in history), with year-end run rate expected at approximately 3.5 times.

Total liquidity— $9.5 billion in total liquidity, supported by A ratings from S&P and Moody’s.

Dividend increase— Quarterly dividend raised 10.4% to reflect the board’s confidence in cash flow durability and growth trajectory.

2025 guidance— Net income of $1.86–$1.94 per share, normalized FFO of $5.06–$5.14 per share, and total portfolio same-store NOI growth of 11.25%-13.25% for the full year.

SHOP portfolio segment guidance— 18.5%-21.5% same-store NOI growth for the senior housing operating portfolio, full-year guidance; revenue growth forecast at 9.2%, with expected RevPOR rise of 5.1% and a 360-basis-point occupancy gain for the full year; expense growth forecast at 5.25% for the full year.

Summary

Welltower(WELL +0.61%) delivered double-digit normalized FFO and NOI growth in Q2 2025, along with an upgrade to the full-year 2025 normalized FFO outlook, which management attributes to continued performance strength and a robust investment pipeline. Large-scale acquisitions and disciplined capital allocation have driven $9.2 billion in year-to-date transaction volume as of late July 2025, while liquidity and leverage metrics reached historic company bests. Management highlighted the roll-out of the Welltower Business System, which involved process optimization and training over 8,000 site employees, as a contributor to margin expansion and operational efficiency.

CEO Mitra emphasized ongoing efforts to realign talent management and incentive structures across the organization and operating partners, targeting unified long-term value creation.

The company reported ongoing challenges with the Holiday by Atria portfolio, with NOI recovery still pending, but disclosed a 560-basis-point occupancy improvement since the beginning of 2025 for these assets (not yet reflected in same-store numbers).

Substantial balance sheet flexibility was underscored by increased cash flow retention and diversified funding, including reentry into bond markets as an A-rated issuer.

Guidance for same-store NOI and normalized FFO does not factor in further acquisitions beyond the already closed or announced $9.2 billion pipeline for fiscal 2025, signaling potentially higher upside if the current activity completes as expected.

Industry glossary

SHOP: Seniors Housing Operating Portfolio; a business segment in which the REIT operates senior housing assets and shares in operating results, as opposed to collecting fixed rent.

RevPOR: Revenue Per Occupied Room; a metric tracking revenue productivity per unit occupied in senior housing portfolios.

ExpPOR: Expense Per Occupied Room; a metric reflecting operating expense per occupied unit in senior housing.

EBITDAR: Earnings before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization, and Rent; a cash flow proxy used in healthcare real estate underwriting.

WBS: Welltower Business System; the company’s proprietary operating and data platform designed to drive process optimization, efficiency, and portfolio management with its partners.
