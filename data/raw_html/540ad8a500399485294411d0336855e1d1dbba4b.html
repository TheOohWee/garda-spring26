Takeaways

  • Full-Year Revenue -- Nearly $18 billion, representing 8.8% growth, led by strong brand demand and execution.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $15.64 for the year, reflecting 33% year-over-year growth, and $2.80 for the fourth quarter, above guidance.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- Nearly $6.5 billion generated in 2025, supporting $2 billion in shareholder returns via dividends and share buybacks.
  • Fourth Quarter Net Yields -- Increased 2.5% in constant currency on 10% capacity growth, driven by demand across all key products.
  • Net Cruise Costs (Excluding Fuel) -- Decreased 6.3% in constant currency, aligned with guidance as operational efficiencies improved.
  • Bookings -- Company is about two-thirds booked for 2026 at record rates within historical load factors, positioning for optimized pricing and yield growth.
  • 2026 Revenue Outlook -- Revenue projected to increase at a double-digit rate, with net yield growth guided to 1.5%-3.5% and capacity set to rise 6.7%.
  • Caribbean Performance -- Caribbean yields have grown 35% since 2019, with regional capacity to rise 8% and continued expected yield growth despite industry supply increases.
  • Digital Engagement -- Active users on the company app rose 25% year over year in fourth quarter; e-commerce traffic grew 10% in 2025 with improved conversion rates.
  • Capital Investments -- $5 billion planned for strategic initiatives in 2026, including delivery of Legend of the Seas and expansion of exclusive destinations such as Santorini Beach Club, Cozumel Beach Club, and Perfect Day Mexico.
  • Fleet Expansion -- Commitment for 10 additional Celebrity River Cruises ships, bringing the fleet to 20 vessels by 2031, and launch of the Royal Caribbean Discovery class with two firm orders and four options.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Guidance -- For 2026, expected to be just under $8 billion, a 13% increase, with margins just above 40%.
  • Liquidity -- Quarter-end liquidity was $7.2 billion with leverage well below 3x, supporting investment-grade metrics.
  • Loyalty Program Innovation -- Launch of Points Choice enables guests to earn and redeem points across all three major vacation brands, aimed at deepening customer engagement and retention.
  • AI and Technology Deployment -- Broad adoption of AI and disruptive technologies to support commercial and operational improvements, including margin enhancement and guest personalization.

Summary

Royal Caribbean Cruises (RCL +0.38%) reported a record-setting year in 2025, highlighting significant earnings growth, margin expansion, and expanded capital returns. The company announced major fleet initiatives, including 10 new Celebrity River Cruises vessels and the innovative Discovery class for Royal Caribbean, to accelerate long-term growth and strengthen its competitive positioning in the global vacation market. App engagement and e-commerce conversion rates demonstrated continued digital traction, while the new Points Choice loyalty program aims to drive cross-brand engagement. Shareholder returns, strategic investments in exclusive destinations, and expanded deployment in high-demand regions form a central part of RCL’s 2026 value proposition and differentiated guest experience.

  • Jason Liberty stated, "We experienced the best seven booking weeks in the company's history since the last earnings call," underscoring sustained demand momentum entering 2026.
  • Naftali Holtz said, "roughly 80% of the people that booked are actually existing customers, but they've never RiverCruise before," indicating river initiatives are expanding wallet share from the existing base.
  • New ships and hardware are expected to drive approximately half of guided yield growth, with the balance coming from like-for-like improvements across itineraries.
  • Digital direct-to-consumer channels and travel partner networks are both contributing meaningfully to increased bookings at higher rates.
  • RCL noted its differentiated Caribbean market strategy, citing "Over 70% of guests on these itineraries sailing on the Royal Caribbean brand will visit a private destination this year."
  • The company highlighted continued efficiency advances from enterprise AI applications in yield management, supply chain, and marine operations to support margin goals.

Industry glossary

  • Adjusted EPS: Net income per share excluding certain non-recurring, non-cash, or exceptional items, reflecting normalized profitability.
  • APCD: Available passenger cruise days, a capacity metric calculated as the total number of passenger days available based on ship deployment and size.
  • Net Yield: Revenue per APCD, net of commissions, transportation, and other direct expenses, used as a key profitability and pricing measure.
  • Perfect Day: RCL’s exclusive private island destination experiences, central to its premium Caribbean product strategy.
  • Discovery Class: New class of ships announced by Royal Caribbean, designed to introduce innovative guest experiences and support future brand growth.
  • Points Choice: RCL’s next-generation loyalty program allowing customers to earn and redeem points interchangeably across its three main brands.
  • Legend of the Seas: The company’s newest Icon class ship, debuting in Europe with a focus on broadened guest experiences and higher yields.
  • Royal Beach Club: A collection of exclusive beach resort destinations owned and operated by RCL, enhancing differentiated itineraries and product offerings.
  • LNG: Liquefied natural gas, a lower-emissions marine fuel increasingly adopted by RCL for environmentally efficient fleet operations.
  • Dry Dock: Periodic ship maintenance involving removal from service for upgrades or repairs, impacting capacity availability and yield comparisons.
