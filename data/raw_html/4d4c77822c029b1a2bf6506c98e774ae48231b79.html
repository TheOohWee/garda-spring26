Etsy (ETSY) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 10:08AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 19, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kruti Patel Goyal
  • Chief Financial Officer — Lanny Baker

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Depop divestiture -- Etsy (ETSY 1.37%) announced a definitive agreement to sell Depop to eBay (NASDAQ: EBAY) for $1.2 billion in cash, with closing expected in 2026, enabling a renewed focus on the core Etsy marketplace.
  • Record revenue -- Consolidated revenue reached $882 million, up 6.6%, excluding Reverb, establishing a new quarterly high.
  • Marketplace GMS -- Etsy (excluding Reverb and Depop) recorded GMS of $3.6 billion, up 2.4% year over year; Etsy marketplace GMS rose 0.1% year over year and declined 1% on a currency-neutral basis, representing a 220 basis point improvement from the third quarter.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Consolidated adjusted EBITDA was $222 million, with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 25.2%.
  • Standalone marketplace profitability -- Etsy marketplace adjusted EBITDA margin was slightly above 30%, the annual peak, though marginally lower year over year due to higher cost of revenue and increased G&A costs.
  • Take rate -- The take rate expanded by 170 basis points to 24.5% compared to the same period last year, attributed to Reverb divestiture, advertising gains, and Depop growth.
  • Mobile app metrics -- Mobile app downloads grew 4%, with app GMS growth accelerating to 6.6% and the app representing 46% of total GMS, up from 41% at the end of 2023; home page clicks per visit on the app increased 14% year over year.
  • Active buyers and sellers -- Trailing twelve-month active buyer count in the US grew sequentially, with 6.8 million new buyers and 10.4 million reactivated buyers, for a total of 17.2 million gross additions (up 2.7%); active sellers rose 1.5% sequentially to 5.6 million.
  • Buyer spend and habitual buyers -- Trailing twelve-month GMS per active buyer reached $121, with habitual buyers down 8.6% year over year and declining 1.4% quarter to quarter.
  • Depop performance -- Depop generated Q4 GMS of $300 million (up nearly 38%), and full-year GMS of $1.1 billion; its lower take rate and negative adjusted EBITDA margin reduced the consolidated take rate by 80 basis points and consolidated adjusted EBITDA margin by 350 basis points for the year.
  • AI and agentic traffic -- Agentic platform traffic to Etsy grew 15 times versus the prior year but remains below 1% of total; ChatGPT-originated orders skew higher in value and show engagement from both new and lapsed buyers.
  • Share repurchases and liquidity -- Etsy ended the period with $1.8 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and investments; $133 million in stock was repurchased during the quarter, totaling $777 million for the year and reducing shares outstanding by approximately 14.4 million.
  • Guidance for Q1 2026 -- Core Etsy GMS is expected to be $2.38 billion to $2.43 billion, implying 2%-4% year-over-year growth; take rate is projected at 25.5%, with adjusted EBITDA margin between 28%-30%.

Summary

Etsy (ETSY 1.37%) detailed the sale of Depop to eBay (NASDAQ: EBAY) for $1.2 billion in cash, positioning the company to direct resources toward strengthening its core marketplace. Leadership emphasized a clear four-priority strategic framework focusing on early engagement, machine learning-driven matching, loyalty, and human connection as central to driving marketplace improvements. Record revenue and improving buyer and seller metrics demonstrate initial payoffs from reorganizational changes, enhanced product personalization, and targeted marketing spend. Growth in agentic and AI-driven discovery channels is emerging, with management highlighting early but promising data from ChatGPT and other integrations. Product and marketing investments are projected to remain disciplined, with further expansion into mobile app experiences and social channels expected throughout 2026.

  • CEO Kruti Patel Goyal described reorganization efforts that consolidated product and engineering teams around customer outcomes, unified trust and safety with customer support, and shifted marketing from a channel-first to a customer-first approach.
  • “it is our most personalized and engaging platform,” according to Goyal, referencing the acceleration in GMS and home page engagement.
  • Kruti Patel Goyal noted, “return our core marketplace to growth,” citing sequential improvement in US buyer GMS and stabilization in buyer metrics.
  • Early agentic shopping channel data suggests potential to unlock retention and higher lifetime value, as management observed that ChatGPT-originating buyers often have a previous Etsy relationship.
  • Liquidity and capital allocation priorities will emphasize “general corporate purposes, continued share repurchases, and investment in the Etsy marketplace,” with no major acquisitions planned and ongoing commitment to returning excess cash to shareholders.
  • App users drive 40% higher lifetime value versus non-app users, and expansion into social channels such as TikTok and Pinterest (NYSE: PINS) is central to acquiring younger demographics.

Industry glossary

  • GMS (Gross Merchandise Sales): The total value of completed transactions between buyers and sellers on the platform within the reporting period.
  • Agentic shopping: AI-powered discovery and purchasing via conversational agents (e.g., ChatGPT, Google (NASDAQ: GOOGL), Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT) Copilot) that can select, recommend, and facilitate commerce across platforms.
  • Take rate: The percentage of GMS captured as revenue by the platform, derived from transaction, payment, and service fees.
  • Habitual buyers: Buyers who have made six or more purchases and spent at least $200 in the trailing twelve months.

What we are doing to improve the performance of our core marketplace, why I am confident in these actions, and what you can expect as we go forward. But first, I will take a few minutes to review yesterday's announcement of the definitive agreement we signed to sell Depop to eBay for $1,200,000,000 in cash. This transaction will allow us to focus exclusively on the compelling opportunity we see in front of us to grow the Etsy, Inc. marketplace in ways that matter most to our buyers and sellers.

We believe it is a great outcome for Etsy, Inc. shareholders and a positive next step for all involved. Of course, it is also a bittersweet moment for me personally given my time as Depop's CEO. I am incredibly proud of what the Depop team has built: a truly differentiated brand and product grounded in clear purpose and strong community. We have been proud to support Depop's evolution, helping it reach the next generation of shoppers and become the fastest growing fashion resale marketplace in the US. And we believe that eBay's desire to invest in Depop will further strengthen its position in the circular economy. Lanny will cover more specifics on the transaction a bit later.

Now back to Etsy, Inc. When I returned last year as Chief Growth Officer, I conducted a deep diagnostic of the business to better understand the root causes of recent growth challenges and where our biggest opportunities are. I spent time speaking directly with buyers and sellers, listening closely to our teams, and pressure testing what I was hearing with customer research, data insights, and trend analysis. The number one takeaway for me was that Etsy, Inc.'s value proposition for buyers and sellers remains differentiated and deeply resonant. At the same time, the diagnostic made clear that we had not translated strength consistently through our customer experience.

For instance, we saw that buyer appreciation for what makes Etsy, Inc. special remains high, but perceptions of differentiation have softened over time. As our sellers' inventory has grown significantly in both scale and breadth, we have not reliably helped buyers understand what they are seeing, why it belongs on Etsy, Inc., or how to find the right item for their intent. That clarified a major opportunity. If we get better at how we match buyers to the right items, and make the human story behind our sellers more visible, we can turn our scale back into an advantage and reassert what makes Etsy, Inc. distinct.

As another example, we have seen our buyer demographics aging, with older users growing faster than younger ones. Our research shows that is not an appeal problem, it is a presence gap. Hence, our work to improve our app and shift our marketing mix to more intentionally engage and acquire younger shoppers. We have been weighted toward lower funnel moments, showing up once a buyer already has something specific in mind. The opportunity is to move earlier, and in some cases before a mission even begins, using inspirational content to spark shopping journeys, not just respond to them, in the places and formats where discovery increasingly happens, especially for younger buyers.

On top of that, frequency and retention, even among our most valuable buyers, have not been where we want them to be. We have become much more effective at closing a transaction, but underinvested in creating reasons to return. Optimizing for conversion alone is not enough. Long-term growth requires making Etsy, Inc. a destination for inspiration, discovery, and ongoing engagement. Finally, it was clear that as Etsy, Inc. grew, a disproportionate share of our investment went toward improving core e-commerce table stakes: things like conversion, price competitiveness, reliability, and shipping. Those investments were necessary but not sufficient, and we did not invest enough in the aspects of Etsy, Inc. that make Etsy, Inc. feel special and different.

I believe that trade-off helped us compete more effectively on fundamentals, but it has also limited our ability to fully capture demand for unique, meaningful commerce and unlock more of the e-commerce TAM. These learnings directly shape the strategic priorities we introduced last spring, designed to turn Etsy, Inc.'s strengths into more durable, long-term growth.

As a reminder, these four priorities are: showing up earlier in the shopping journey, increasingly meeting customers at the start of their missions wherever they begin; working to get much better at using machine learning to match buyers with the right items, marrying their interests and their intent, so we can turn the abundance of our marketplace into a clear advantage; deepening loyalty with our most valuable customers so they feel seen, appreciated, and genuinely valued; and leaning into the human connection that differentiates Etsy, Inc., so shoppers experience the stories, creativity, and passion that make every item and every purchase feel special.

Alongside these priorities, we have made important changes to how we operate to drive clearer focus and better execution. At a high level, we have reorganized the company around customer outcomes rather than functional silos. We consolidated product and engineering so teams own end-to-end experiences and can move faster and with clearer accountability. We unified the teams responsible for trust and safety and customer support under one leader, protecting our community while delivering fast, thoughtful help when it matters most. And we realigned marketing from a channel-first model to a customer-first one, with teams anchored to outcomes like frequency, trust, and lifetime value, rather than optimizing individual channels in isolation.

The result is an organization with clearer ownership of customer outcomes and fewer handoffs, built to move faster and execute more consistently against our priorities. That increased focus and execution is beginning to show up in our results. Our goal last year was to return our core marketplace to growth, and we achieved that in the fourth quarter. While we still have work ahead, the trajectory is clearly improving. From the first to the fourth quarter of last year, Etsy, Inc.'s marketplace GMS comparisons improved by nine percentage points, and Q4 US buyer GMS grew for the first time in four years.

As Lanny will cover in more detail, our consolidated Q4 performance met or exceeded our expectations across the board. We delivered record revenue. We did so while continuing to invest for growth at both Etsy, Inc. and Depop, all while maintaining very healthy profitability. This performance reinforces our confidence that the changes we have made so far are working and that we are headed in the right direction. For example, the work we have done on our app is making it our most personalized and engaging platform, with year-over-year GMS growth accelerating to 6.6% in Q4, and home page clicks per visit increasing 14% year over year, and GMS share continuing to grow.

Our personalized owned marketing programs are doing a better job engaging, with push and email clicks up more than 25% while message volume stayed disciplined. And satisfaction with our customer support is improving for both buyers and sellers, with particularly strong gains on the seller side, up 15.5% since last year. These are all indicators that we are on the right track, which is why we are doubling down on our priority for 2026. We have a clear plan to drive more visits, better engagement, higher conversion and spend, and healthier retention. What matters now is continued discipline and execution, quarter after quarter.

When we do that well, we feel confident that those investments will compound into the kind of sustainable growth we all believe Etsy, Inc. is capable of: growth rooted in Etsy, Inc.'s differentiation and unique value to our customers. Etsy, Inc. began with a simple belief: that technology should empower creative entrepreneurs, not replace them. Just over 20 years later, we are at an inflection point, one where the power of AI technology has the potential to make commerce on Etsy, Inc. more human than ever, enhancing our differentiation and strengthening our unique customer value.

On the seller side, AI is already helping to automate routine tasks, so sellers can spend more time on what only humans can do: creating, designing, and connecting with customers around the globe. On the buyer side, it is making discovery easier and more relevant, helping Etsy, Inc. show up in more of the moments where inspiration begins. At the same time, AI-powered agentic shopping presents meaningful opportunities for the unique items on Etsy, Inc. to shine. These tools offer deeper insights into our listings, enabling our sellers' items to starkly stand out against the sea of impersonal, undifferentiated, mass-produced goods. So we are moving fast to stay at the forefront of this inflection point.

Since our last call, we have expanded our agentic shopping partnerships, adding integrations with Microsoft Copilot and Google, as well as an agentic payments agreement with Stripe. While still a very small part of our overall traffic and GMS, agentic traffic to Etsy, Inc. in Q4 was about 15 times what it was last year, underscoring just how rapidly this channel is emerging. And early indicators support our hypothesis that agentic discovery can be additive to our ecosystem.

Using ChatGPT as an example, we are seeing evidence that in addition to bringing new buyers to Etsy, Inc., a meaningful share of buyers engaging through ChatGPT have a prior relationship with us, including lapsed buyers, indicating that agentic shopping could be a great unlock for retention and better lifetime value. Orders originating from ChatGPT also tend to skew higher value compared to some of our more mature acquisition channels. And in addition to instant checkout sales, we are seeing strong engagement with listings on Etsy, Inc. resulting from ChatGPT discovery.

There is a lot on our AI and Agentic for Commerce roadmap with these important partnerships as well as through the development of product experiences on Etsy, Inc., and we will keep you informed of our progress. Wrapping up, I want to be clear about what you should expect from me. We have more work to do to return Etsy, Inc. to sustained, durable growth. My intention is to earn your confidence over time with clear priorities and transparency about what we are learning along the way. At the same time, we will continue to shape Etsy, Inc.'s longer-term direction by leaning into what makes this marketplace truly distinctive: human creativity and meaningful connection.

I have been a part of two significant Etsy, Inc. turnarounds already. First, starting in 2018 as Chief Product Officer, when we began to significantly up-level our shopping experiences that enabled tremendous growth. And more recently, running Depop, where we identified and deepened the platform's core differentiation and value proposition through improved personalization and discovery. In both cases, the biggest unlock was not a single bold idea or strategic initiative. It was sharp focus and strong execution, pinpointing what matters most to customers and building the operating discipline to deliver consistently. That is the muscle we have been strengthening to deliver another turnaround, one which propels Etsy, Inc. to our next great chapter.
