Americold Realty Trust (COLD) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

DATE

Thursday, February 19, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Robert Chambers
  • Chief Investment Officer and Interim Chief Financial Officer — Scott Henderson
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Rich Leland

RISKS

  • Management expects revenue headwinds in 2026, stating, "continue to believe that both will be headwinds for us in 2026."
  • Guidance incorporates expectations for "flat to down by as much as 300 basis points" in economic occupancy and a decline in overall revenue per pallet by "approximately 100 to 200 basis points" due to ongoing demand and contract renewal pressures.
  • No increase in consumer demand assumed for 2026, and management cautions that anticipated benefits from new initiatives "will take time to fully realize."

TAKEAWAYS

  • AFFO per share -- $0.38 in the fourth quarter, representing a year-over-year increase and finishing above the midpoint of revised full-year guidance.
  • Full-year AFFO per share -- $1.43 for 2025, matching internal forecasts.
  • Quarterly growth in NOI, EBITDA, and AFFO -- Achieved for the first time since Q3 2024, driven by higher occupancy and cost reductions.
  • Economic occupancy -- Increased 280 basis points sequentially from Q3 2025, attributed to seasonal harvest, holiday volumes, and portfolio management actions.
  • Services revenue per pallet -- Rose 2.4% in Q4; storage revenue per pallet increased 0.3% amid competitive pressures.
  • Services margins -- Nearly 14% in Q4; full-year margin at 12.7%, up approximately 1,000 basis points over two years.
  • Fixed commitment contracts -- Approximately 60% of rent and storage revenues now fixed, compared to less than 40% a few years ago.
  • Underperforming site actions -- Exited or idled 10 sites and a joint venture in Brazil in 2025, removing over 22 million cubic feet and 65,000 pallet positions; 9 additional sites identified for 2026.
  • Development and expansion projects -- Three projects completed on time and on budget in 2025; four developments in progress remain on schedule and within budget.
  • Cost structure initiatives -- Executed a plan to unlock $30 million in annualized cost savings, mainly in indirect labor and SG&A, largely completed by year-end.
  • Project Orion cash spend -- Projected reduction in transformation-related spend by approximately $50 million for 2026.
  • Same-store revenue guidance -- Forecasted at $2.2 billion to $2.27 billion for 2026, reflecting management's revenue expectations.
  • Same-store NOI guidance -- Guided to $735 million to $785 million for 2026.
  • Total company NOI guidance -- Expected $780 million to $845 million, including impact from non-same-store and Transportation segments.
  • SG&A forecast -- Projected core SG&A of $218 million to $228 million in 2026, with a ~$7 million reduction at the midpoint year over year.
  • Core EBITDA guidance -- Projected $570 million to $620 million for 2026.
  • Interest expense -- Anticipated between $170 million and $180 million for full-year 2026.
  • Maintenance CapEx -- Planned spend between $60 million and $70 million in 2026, consistent with prior-year levels.
  • Leverage ratio -- Reported at 6.8x as of Q4 end; deleveraging described as a high priority for maintaining investment-grade status.
  • Dividend policy -- Management reiterated commitment to maintaining both the dividend and an investment-grade rating.
  • 2026 AFFO per share guidance -- Forecasted between $1.20 and $1.30, based on current operating environment and excluding unannounced transactions.

SUMMARY

Americold Realty Trust (COLD 0.17%) reported sequential and year-over-year gains in AFFO, EBITDA, and NOI, supported by higher occupancy, ongoing cost reductions, and a greater share of fixed revenues. The company emphasized progress in deleveraging, with leverage reduction to 6x or lower described as essential for investment-grade maintenance, and asset sales or joint ventures under evaluation with details expected in the first half of 2026. Portfolio optimization continued through the exit or idling of underperforming assets, and further actions in 2026 are planned to maximize facility profitability and capital allocation. Service margin improvements, new client wins such as the On The Run contract in Australia, and international channel expansion indicate ongoing business development activity, though no unannounced transactions or demand inflection is included in 2026 forecasts. Operational initiatives reduced indirect expense, with an additional $50 million cash spend reduction planned from Project Orion, positioning the business for adaptive capital management amid persistent market headwinds.

  • Management noted that "customers are continuing to manage their inventory tightly," impacting both physical and economic occupancy as fixed contract renewals increasingly align with actual usage.
  • Geographic review is underway, but international segments in Europe, Asia Pacific, and the Middle East are currently "performing well and in line with our expectations," with no disclosed asset-specific plans.
  • The company expects higher volume and AFFO in the second half of 2026 due to the agricultural harvest and holiday demands, following seasonally lower results in Q1.
  • Recent debt activity included entering a $250 million term loan and repaying a $200 million maturity, temporarily reducing the U.S. revolver balance to zero at year-end.
  • Announcements of new cold storage supply have "slowed down materially" in the U.S., with excess capacity—particularly at distribution nodes—characterized by underperformance among new market entrants.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AFFO: Adjusted Funds From Operations; a REIT-specific cash flow metric that excludes nonrecurring items and certain non-cash expenses, representing core distributable cash.
  • NOI: Net Operating Income; the profit after deducting operating expenses from gross income, excluding interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, specific to property operations.
  • Economic occupancy: The ratio of space for which revenue is being received to total available space, reflecting the occupancy rate based on contractual or actual revenue generation.
  • Fixed commitment contract: Multi-year agreements in which customers commit to a predetermined storage volume, providing revenue predictability for Americold and cost certainty for customers.
  • SG&A: Selling, General, and Administrative expenses; non-direct costs incurred in running the business, exclusive of property operating costs.
  • Project Orion: Internal transformation initiative focused on technology and business process improvements within Americold.
