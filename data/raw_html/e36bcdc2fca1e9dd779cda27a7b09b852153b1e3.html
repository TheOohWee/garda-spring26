Applied Digital Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 15, 2026 at 6:55PM EST

Jan. 7, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

  • Chairman and CEO — Wesley Cummins
  • Chief Financial Officer — Mohammad Saidal Mohmand
  • Investor relations (opening remarks) — Matt Glover

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $126.6 million, representing a 250% increase from $36.2 million in the prior year, driven primarily by $73 million in tenant fit-out services for the HPC Hosting Business, and $12 million recognized lease revenue from the CoreWeave lease at Polaris Forge 1.
  • Data center hosting segment revenue -- $41.6 million, up 15%, contributed by increased ASIC capacity across two North Dakota facilities.
  • Segment operating profit (data center hosting) -- Approximately $16 million on a $131 million asset base for the quarter.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $20.2 million, as presented and reconciled in the earnings release.
  • Net loss -- $31.2 million, corresponding to $0.11 per share; adjusted net income was $0.1 million, or $0.00 per share.
  • Cost of revenues -- $100.6 million, a year-over-year increase primarily attributable to $69.5 million in tenant fit-out services, and higher data center hosting–related expenses.
  • SG&A expenses -- $57 million, up from $26 million, mainly due to a $23.8 million increase in stock-based compensation, $4.7 million in higher professional services, and $1.2 million in higher personnel expenses.
  • Interest expense -- $11.5 million, compared to $2.9 million previously, reflecting higher project-level debt.
  • Cash position -- $2.3 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and restricted cash at quarter-end, not including $382.5 million in additional financings completed after the quarter.
  • Total debt -- $2.6 billion, with maturities mostly due in 2030; total equity was $2.1 billion at quarter-end.
  • Major new leases -- A $5 billion, 15-year lease with a U.S.-based investment-grade hyperscaler for 200 megawatts at Polaris Forge 2, and 400 megawatts contracted for CoreWeave at Polaris Forge 1, together representing $16 billion in prospective lease revenue across North Dakota campuses.
  • Capital structure -- $2.35 billion private offering of 9.25% senior secured notes due 2030 completed to finance buildings at Polaris Forge 1, with the intent to refinance at lower rates after operational launch.
  • Construction pipeline -- Two campuses under simultaneous construction totaling 600 megawatts, with further advanced discussions on three potential campuses totaling 900 megawatts.
  • Strategic spin-out -- Entered a non-binding letter of intent to combine Applied Digital Cloud with EKSO Bionics for formation of ChronoScale, expecting Applied Digital to retain over 80% ownership pending close in the first half of 2026.
  • Trailing 12-month cloud revenue -- Cloud business generated over $60 million in revenue, supported by $313 million in assets.
  • R&D investment -- $15 million invested in Corintis for advanced liquid cooling, as part of a $25 million funding round targeting high-density AI workloads.

Summary

Applied Digital (APLD +2.62%) initiated lease revenue generation from its first 100-megawatt data center after investing over $1 billion, marking a new phase for the company. The company secured two major hyperscale leases totaling 600 megawatts and approximately $16 billion in prospective lease revenue, while entering advanced negotiations for three additional sites. A planned spin-out of the cloud business into ChronoScale is designed to increase capital flexibility and pursue AI compute contracts, with Applied Digital anticipating over 80% ownership after completion. The company ended the quarter with $2.3 billion in cash and $2.6 billion in debt, excluding $382.5 million of additional financing received post-quarter. Investments in both construction scalability and next-generation liquid cooling through Corintis position the company to expand service offerings and future-proof data center operations.

  • Management reported significant improvement in contract terms, with leases now featuring "100% make-whole" cancellation provisions, and non-transferability except to customers with equal or better credit.
  • Wesley Cummins stated, "The demand profile for the past 6 months has been extraordinarily robust," shifting emphasis from customer demand to Applied Digital's own capacity to scale construction and delivery.
  • Three additional campuses in advanced discussions each have the potential to scale to 2 gigawatts, providing a clear internal path to at least 5 gigawatts of total company capacity by 2032 without further expansion.
  • CFO Mohammad Saidal Mohmand outlined a repeatable, capital-efficient financing model leveraging facilities from Macquarie Equipment Capital and Macquarie Asset Management, enabling Applied Digital to retain majority site ownership and reduce reliance on public markets.
  • Management announced the launch of Applied Digital Cares, a community initiative supporting local education, health, innovation, and regional development tied to operational growth.
  • Wesley Cummins confirmed ongoing evaluation of third-party "stranded power" opportunities, and noted multiple inbound offers for additional regional expansion.
  • Spin-off partner ChronoScale is set to operate as a GPU-accelerated compute platform, leveraging early deployments of NVIDIA H100 GPUs to address AI market needs.

Industry glossary

  • Hyperscaler: Major cloud or compute infrastructure providers with investment-grade status, referenced as long-term counterparties in leasing agreements.
  • Polaris Forge: Applied Digital’s branded North Dakota data center campuses, differentiated as Forge 1 and Forge 2 for project tracking and lease identification.
  • ASIC: Application-specific integrated circuit — hardware deployed within Applied Digital data centers for specialized, high-performance computing workloads.
  • Tenant fit-out services: Construction or customization provided to data center tenants to ready space for hosting HPC equipment, recognized as a key revenue driver.
  • ChronoScale: A planned stand-alone entity to be formed from the combination of Applied Digital Cloud and EKSO Bionics, intended as a dedicated GPU compute platform for demanding AI workloads.
  • Corintis: A portfolio company focused on advanced liquid cooling technology for high-density AI chips, supported by Applied Digital’s investment.
