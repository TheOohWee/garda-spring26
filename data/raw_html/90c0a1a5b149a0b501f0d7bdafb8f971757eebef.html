Mosaic (MOS) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 25, 2026 at 12:42PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Feb. 25, 2026 at 11:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Bruce Bodine
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Luciano Siani Pires
  • Executive Vice President, Commercial — Jenny Wang
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Jason Tremblay

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue Resilience -- North America segment gained market share as sales volumes outperformed industry declines in potash and phosphate shipments.
  • Phosphate Production -- Produced 1,700,000 tons in the fourth quarter despite an extended Bartow turnaround and deliberate curtailments, with full-year output at its highest in three years for Florida and record mining at Miski Mayo.
  • Phosphate 2026 Guidance -- Management expects at least 7,000,000 tons production this year, with operating rates at U.S. facilities trending toward 80% as asset reliability initiatives strengthen utilization.
  • Potash Outlook -- Esterhazy site returned to full rates post-safety incident; record production expected in 2026 with targeted output roughly 9,000,000 tons, matching 2025 levels despite planned Carlsbad sale.
  • Cost Savings Achieved -- Achieved $150,000,000 of cost savings ahead of schedule in 2025, led by mine optimization, reduced fixed labor, and supplier consolidation; targeting an additional $100,000,000 reduction for 2026.
  • Phosphate Conversion Costs -- Cash cost of conversion in the fourth quarter was $112 per ton, down ~$20 sequentially due to improved production volumes and structural cost actions.
  • Potash Cash Costs -- Production averaged $75 per ton in 2025, elevated due to the Colonsay extension but otherwise within Analyst Day target ranges.
  • Mosaic Fertilizantes Costs -- Blended rock cost per ton fell to $97, the lowest since 2021.
  • Sulfur Impact -- End-of-year sulfur price spike expected to "significantly compress margins in our Phosphates and Mosaic Fertilizantes segments well into 2026," prompting Brazil asset curtailments at Araxá and Fospar.
  • Inventory Build -- Working capital use reduced cash flow by $960,000,000, driving an $829,000,000 increase in net debt; excess phosphate inventory stands at 240,000 tons ($140,000,000 in potential working capital release).
  • Working Capital Outlook -- $300,000,000-$500,000,000 working capital release anticipated through inventory reduction and demand recovery, improving EBITDA-to-cash conversion rates from mid-30s% in 2025 toward historic ~70% levels.
  • Capital Expenditure Plans -- 2026 CapEx guided to ~$1,500,000,000—up from 2025—due to multiple gyp stack, clay settling area, and tailings dam projects; management identifies this as a temporary "ceiling." CapEx expected to trend toward $1,000,000,000 by 2030.
  • Phosphate Demand Dynamics -- U.S. phosphate demand dropped sharply due to affordability and government support uncertainties but is "emerging" as spring planting approaches; international demand remains strong, with China restricting exports and DAP netbacks higher abroad than at NOLA.
  • Mosaic Biosciences Performance -- Net sales doubled to $68,000,000 in 2025, with 40%+ gross margins; five products were launched and 60-plus registrations achieved across 16 countries. Doubling net sales again is the 2026 goal.
  • Asset Divestitures -- Divestments include Patos de Minas, Taquari, and a pending Carlsbad sale; $170,000,000 proceeds expected with $60,000,000 in asset retirement obligation reductions.
  • Brazil Market Strategy -- Credit constraints in Brazil limit fertilizer shipment and volume growth; company opts for prudent credit risk management over aggressive market share, with intent to expand as conditions normalize.
  • Capital Allocation Outlook -- Free cash flow expected to exceed minimum dividend after CapEx and other spend, supporting debt reduction and potential for extraordinary shareholder returns pending operational progress and margin recovery.

RISKS

  • Management noted, "sulfur prices spiked at the end of the year, which we expect will significantly compress margins in our Phosphates and Mosaic Fertilizantes segments well into 2026."
  • Bruce Bodine said, In 2025, we faced significant market volatility. We moved quickly to protect margins and profitability. We have idled Araxá and Fospar in Brazil, our lowest margin operations, until further notice.
  • Cash flow "remains constrained by lower EBITDA, a result of the sharp increase in sulfur prices since December," per Luciano Siani Pires, with Q1 2026 EBITDA facing a $250,000,000 headwind.
  • Persistent credit issues in Brazil and high interest rates are creating "major shift in the industry." Increased retailer and grower bankruptcy filings are constraining volume outlook and requiring a conservative sales strategy.

SUMMARY

The Mosaic Company (MOS 2.02%) entered 2026 following a year marked by pronounced demand drops and working capital absorption, which resulted in higher net debt and curtailed production at certain assets. Management expects a multi-hundred-million-dollar working capital release in 2026 as inventories normalize and U.S. and international fertilizer demand recover. Mosaic Biosciences delivered substantial top-line growth with high-margin expansion, positioning it as a scalable growth vector for the company. Capital allocation is weighted toward essential maintenance and regulatory projects in 2026, temporarily elevating CapEx but set to decline in subsequent years according to detailed project timelines shared on the call. Free cash flow in 2026 is projected to surpass required dividends after capital outlays, supporting incremental debt reduction and, contingent on operating results, potential resumption of extraordinary shareholder distributions.

  • Management disclosed that $900,000,000 was raised in November through three-year and five-year notes primarily to address the fourth quarter surge in working capital and retire short-term commercial paper.
  • Luciano Siani Pires stated that "EBITDA-to-cash flow from operations conversion rate reached the low point in the mid-30s range in 2025 versus a more normalized level of 70%," and that "as working capital unwinds, we expect a meaningful improvement in this conversion rate."
  • Production improvements at key U.S. phosphate facilities achieved or approached 80% operating rates, with the largest plant, New Wales, expected to attain this after completing its Q1 turnaround.
  • Mosaic's management confirmed that Araxá and Fospar operations in Brazil will remain idled until further notice due to unprofitable margin structure from raw material inflation.
  • Segment commentary revealed that Fertilizantes blended rock costs fell to $97 per ton, with EBITDA maintained near $50,000,000 in Q4 despite sharp volume and margin pressures.
  • No revaluation of inventory occurred in North America, with Luciano Siani Pires stating, "there is absolutely no revaluation of inventory. There are no gains recorded on EBITDA."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • P2O5: Chemical abbreviation for phosphorus pentoxide, a key measure of phosphate concentration in fertilizer production.
  • Stripping Margin: The difference between selling price of a fertilizer (such as DAP) and its primary variable input costs, typically sulfur, ammonia, and phosphate rock; a key profit metric for the phosphate segment.
  • Gyp Stack: Engineered structure for storing phosphogypsum, a byproduct of phosphate fertilizer manufacturing, frequently subject to regulatory capital requirements.
  • SSP: Single superphosphate, a phosphate fertilizer produced primarily in Brazil and referenced within Mosaic Fertilizantes operations.
  • NOLA: New Orleans, Louisiana, a major U.S. fertilizer pricing and trade hub referenced for benchmark DAP prices.
