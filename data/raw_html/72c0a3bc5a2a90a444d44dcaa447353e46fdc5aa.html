Earnings Call Transcript

AutoZone (AZO) Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 3, 2026 at 11:26AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, March 3, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Philip Daniele
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jamere Jackson
  • Vice President, Treasurer, Investor Relations, and Tax — Brian Campbell

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Sales -- $4.3 billion, representing 8.1% growth.
  • Earnings Per Share (EPS) -- $27.63, down 2.3%; excluding a $59 million non-cash LIFO charge, EPS would have increased 7.1%.
  • Gross Margin -- 52.5%, down 137 basis points due to the LIFO charge; excluding LIFO, gross margin was slightly positive year over year.
  • Domestic Same Store Sales -- Grew 3.4%.
  • Domestic DIY Same Store Sales -- Increased 1.5%, reflecting both steady ticket growth and lower traffic.
  • DIY Average Ticket -- Rose 5.2%, driven by same SKU inflation "up north of 6%."
  • DIY Traffic -- Declined 3.6%, matching the prior quarter's pace.
  • Domestic Commercial Sales -- Increased 9.8% to $1.2 billion; commercial sales now represent 27% of total company sales and just over 32% of domestic auto parts sales.
  • Commercial Average Weekly Sales per Program -- $15,400, up 4.8%.
  • International Same Store Sales -- Up 2.5% on a constant currency basis; unadjusted comps were up 17.1% due to foreign exchange impact.
  • Foreign Exchange Tailwinds -- Strengthening of the Mexican peso generated a $74 million benefit to sales, $23 million to EBIT, and $0.95 per share to EPS.
  • Store Openings -- 64 globally opened this quarter versus 45 in the prior year period, bringing totals to 6,709 U.S., 913 Mexico, and 152 Brazil stores.
  • Store Growth Target -- Now expects 350 to 360 new stores for the full year, up from 304 last year.
  • CapEx Investments -- Nearly $1.6 billion being invested this fiscal year, with a similar amount expected next year; focus is on accelerated store growth, distribution network, and technology.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $15 million generated in the quarter, compared to $291 million last year, due to CapEx and payables timing.
  • Share Repurchases -- $311 million in buybacks; $1.4 billion remains under authorization.
  • Inventory Per Store -- Increased 8.1%; total inventory rose 13.1% from last year, due to both new stores and inventory investments.
  • SG&A Expenses -- Rose 8.7%; on a per-store basis, SG&A was up 3.9% compared to 5.8% in the prior quarter.
  • LIFO Charges -- $59 million non-cash in the period; expects ~$60 million per quarter for the remainder of fiscal 2026, totaling $277 million versus $64 million last year.
  • Leverage Ratio -- Finished at just over 2.5x EBITDAR.
  • Mega Hub Stores -- Five opened this quarter, ending at 142; on track for 30 new Mega Hubs for the year, with a target of approximately 300 at full build-out.

RISKS

  • DIY traffic declined by 3.6%, with management citing, "traffic in the middle four-week segment was down more than the other eight weeks."
  • Severe winter weather caused commercial sales to increase only about 1% during affected weeks 10 and 11, versus over 12% growth in the other 10 weeks.
  • Year-to-date free cash flow declined from $856 million to $645 million, attributed to CapEx and payables timing.
  • International sales growth in Mexico remains challenged by a "continued soft macro environment" and "slower economic growth."

SUMMARY

Management reported that the non-cash LIFO charge was the primary reason for declines in both gross margin and EPS, while core operating metrics exhibited growth. Strong commercial sales were noted despite significant temporary disruption from severe weather that materially impacted late-quarter performance and suppressed otherwise double-digit commercial gains. Strategic investments in accelerated store openings, Mega Hub expansion, and distribution center capacity are on pace and expected to contribute to future top-line and EBIT growth trajectories.

  • Plans call for a continued annual pace increase in global store openings toward 500 stores by 2028, with management stressing, "we are in the middle innings." of the investment cycle.
  • Management expects same SKU inflation to remain mid-single digit through the third quarter, supporting sequential average ticket growth before easing in the fourth quarter.
  • Tax refund season and prior winter weather may drive incremental traffic as management highlighted, "Cold weather with ice and snow lead to higher failure rates." and may result in increased spring and summer maintenance demand.
  • Commercial Mega Hub stores are surpassing initial models for returns and performance, delivering increased parts availability and network benefits that drive share gains in both commercial and DIY channels.
  • International expansion is positioned to deliver meaningful contribution as macroeconomic headwinds in Mexico moderate, supported by ongoing investments in new stores and distribution infrastructure.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • LIFO: "Last-In, First-Out" inventory accounting method; current year charges reflect rising replacement costs flowing through cost of goods sold.
  • DIFM: "Do-It-For-Me," the commercial segment where parts are sold to professionals who install them for consumers.
  • Mega Hub: Large-format store carrying more than 100,000 SKUs and serving as a fulfillment and supply source for regional satellite stores, driving a sales lift and improved part availability.
