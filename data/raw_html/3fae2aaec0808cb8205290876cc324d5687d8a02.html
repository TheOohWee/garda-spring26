Pentair (PNR) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Oct 21, 2025 at 5:02PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Oct. 21, 2025, at 9 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — John Stauch
  • Chief Financial Officer — Bob Fishman

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Sales -- $1.022 billion, up 3% year over year, driven by growth in the pool and flow segments.
  • Adjusted operating income -- Adjusted operating income was $263 million, up 10% year over year, reflecting transformation benefits and margin expansion.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Adjusted EPS was $1.24, up 14% year over year and a quarterly record.
  • Return on sales (ROS) -- Adjusted return on sales (ROS) -- 25.7%, up 160 basis points year over year, attributed to transformation savings.
  • Core sales growth -- Core sales growth -- 3% year over year, with pool up 6%, flow up 4%, and water solutions flat.
  • Flow segment sales -- $394 million, up 6% year over year, with residential up 3%, commercial up 5%, and industrial up 10%.
  • Flow segment income -- Flow segment income -- up 15% year over year, with ROS rising 200 basis points to 24%.
  • Water solution segment sales -- $273 million, down 6% year over year; core sales flat, commercial down 6% (including a 9% negative impact from the commercial services sale), residential down 6% from portfolio exits.
  • Water solution segment income -- Up 6% year over year to $68 million, with ROS up 280 basis points to 25% due to transformation savings.
  • Pool segment sales -- $354 million, up 7% year over year, benefiting from price, volume, and the Gulfstream acquisition.
  • Pool segment income -- $116 million, up 3% year over year, but ROS decreased 120 basis points to approximately 33% compared to a challenging prior-year comparison.
  • Free cash flow -- Free cash flow -- $719 million in free cash flow, up 14% year over year, marking a company record.
  • Return on invested capital (ROIC) -- Return on invested capital (ROIC) -- 16.7% year to date, up from 15.2% a year ago.
  • Net debt leverage ratio -- 1.3x, down from 1.4x a year ago, inclusive of the HydroStop acquisition.
  • Share repurchases -- $175 million repurchased year to date.
  • HydroStop acquisition -- Acquired for $292 million ($242 million net of tax benefits); anticipated $50 million future cash tax benefit; expected to add approximately $10 million in Q4 2025 sales at 30% ROS, and $50 million in sales for 2026.
  • Transformation savings -- $56 million in transformation savings delivered year to date, on track to reach $80 million in 2025, net of strategic growth investments.
  • Full-year 2025 guidance raised -- Now projects approximately 2% sales growth and adjusted EPS of $4.85-$4.90, a 12%-13% increase for full-year 2025.
  • Segment outlooks -- Flow sales expected up low single digits, water solutions down mid-single digits (core sales down low single digits), and pool sales up about 7% for full year 2025.
  • Q4 2025 outlook -- Sales to rise 3%-4% in Q4 2025, adjusted operating income up 4%-8%, adjusted EPS of $1.11-$1.16 (up 3%-7%), with HydroStop contributing approximately $10 million in revenue at 30% ROS.
  • Tariffs impact -- $75 million expected impact for 2025, consistent with previous guidance; management states “tariff uncertainty continues.”
  • Dividend growth -- Dividend increased for forty-nine consecutive years, reinforcing a policy of returning capital to shareholders.
  • CFO transition -- Bob Fishman to leave effective March 1, 2026; Nick to assume CFO duties following a transition period.

Summary

Pentair (PNR 0.38%) reported record third-quarter results, with margin expansion across core segments underpinned by continued execution on transformation initiatives and disciplined capital deployment. Management raised full-year sales and adjusted EPS guidance for 2025, citing confidence in operational performance as well as near-term momentum. The acquisition of HydroStop was highlighted as a strategic addition to commercial flow offerings, with accretive financial expectations and integration underway.

  • CEO Stauch said, "Our 8020 actions are well underway and show early signs of success in driving top-line growth," noting that a deeper review will be provided at the March Investor Day.
  • Management reiterated a commitment to balanced capital allocation, including ongoing buybacks, dividends, and targeted M&A, with emphasis on maintaining a high-teen ROIC.
  • The company continues to mitigate elevated tariff costs through pricing strategies and alternative sourcing, but notes that unresolved tariff developments related to China and Mexico could require further action.
  • The flow segment saw reliable contributions from all subcategories in Q3, with commercial flow achieving its thirteenth consecutive quarter of year-over-year sales growth, according to Bob Fishman.
  • Transformation savings are driven by value-based pricing, sourcing (waves three and revisit of earlier efforts), productivity initiatives, and organizational discipline in G&A spending.
  • Digital and product technology investments are increasing, and digital enablement was described as a growing priority, driving both operational efficiency and customer experience.
  • Management confirmed no significant abnormal trends in inventory and described channel inventories as at historical levels.
  • CEO Stauch acknowledged increasing low-cost competition, especially for "commodity-based products" at the lower end, stating, "we have to make sure that we're offering better value to our customers."
  • Current pool pricing is said to be holding, with management guiding for a 6%-7% price increase in the segment for 2026, and approximately a 1%-2% company-wide price carryover benefit into 2026.

Industry glossary

  • 8020 actions: Company initiative focused on prioritizing top customers (Quad 1) and optimizing resources to maximize value and profitability, often by reducing emphasis on lower-performing customer segments.
  • Core sales: Organic sales growth metric excluding the impact of acquisitions, divestitures, and currency effects, reflecting underlying operational performance.
  • Return on sales (ROS): Operating income as a percentage of sales, a primary profitability measure for the company.
  • Transformation savings: Cost savings achieved through multi-year strategic initiatives, including sourcing optimization, pricing excellence, productivity improvements, and organizational discipline.
  • Two-step distribution: Sales model where products pass through a distributor before reaching the end customer, cited as 75% of Pentair’s business.
