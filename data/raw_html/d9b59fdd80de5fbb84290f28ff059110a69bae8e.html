Earnings Call Transcript

Insight (NSIT) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 6, 2026 at 8:12AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, October 31, 2024 at 9:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Joyce Mullen
  • Chief Financial Officer — Glynis Bryan
  • Senior Vice President of Finance and CFO, North America — James Morgado

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $2.1 billion, an 8% decrease in both U.S. dollars and constant currency, driven by an 11% decline in product sales.
  • Hardware Revenue -- Down 13%, with large enterprise and corporate clients in North America identified as the main sources of weakness.
  • On-Prem Software Revenue -- Down 9%, primarily due to a partner consolidation and a shift in revenue classification to services.
  • Devices and Infrastructure -- Devices declined low single digits, while Infrastructure decreased double digits year over year; sequentially, devices remained flat and Infrastructure fell high single digits.
  • Gross Profit -- Increased 6%, fueled by strong Cloud and Insight Core services growth, partially offset by lower product sales.
  • Insight Core Services Gross Profit -- $81 million, a 14% increase, reflecting benefits from recent acquisitions.
  • Cloud Gross Profit -- $129 million, up 33%, driven by growth in Infrastructure-as-a-Service and Software-as-a-Service and the positive effect of the partner consolidation.
  • Gross Margin -- 20.7%, rising by 270 basis points, attributed to a higher mix of cloud and Insight Core services revenue from acquisitions.
  • Adjusted SG&A Expenses -- Rose 8% due to acquisitions, while organic adjusted SG&A declined year over year from prior expense management actions.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $129 million, flat year over year, with margin improving by 50 basis points to 6.2%.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- $2.19, an 8% decline, primarily attributed to increased interest expense from higher debt taken on for acquisitions and share repurchases.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $125 million generated in the quarter; $418 million year-to-date, and expected to exceed $500 million for the year, above the upper end of normal guidance.
  • Share Repurchases -- $165 million in Q3 and $200 million year-to-date, with a new $300 million program authorized and none yet utilized.
  • Total Debt -- $1.1 billion at quarter end, compared to $673 million a year ago, with $1.8 billion available under the ABL facility and $1.5 billion accessible.
  • Guidance for 2024 -- Gross profit growth projected in the mid-single-digit range, gross margin expected at 19%-20%, and adjusted diluted EPS now guided to $9.40-$9.70.
  • Operating Expense Reductions -- Anticipated $20 million to $25 million of annualized savings to be fully realized in 2025.
  • SADA Contribution -- SADA unit accounts for about one-third of revised guidance shortfall, with the pivot away from large enterprise resales cited as a factor.
  • Segment Performance -- Commercial client hardware growth posted two consecutive quarters of improvement, while EMEA and APAC also delivered strong quarterly results.
  • Cloud Segment Headwind -- Cloud gross profit growth expected to moderate in Q4 due to SADA's shift and enterprise retail reductions.
  • Partner-Driven Revenue Shift -- On-prem software changes from partner consolidation in EMEA shifted enterprise revenue to agent relationships, now booked as service fees.

RISKS

  • CEO Mullen stated, "Q3 didn't meet our expectations, and we anticipate the IT spending environment will remain cautious in the near term," prompting guidance reductions for 2024.
  • Glynis Bryan said, "Q3 did not meet our expectations and also did not provide the foundation that we need to achieve our prior 2024 guidance," reflecting sales weakness in key enterprise and corporate groups.
  • Adjusted diluted EPS declined 8%, attributed to increased interest expense from higher debt linked to acquisitions and share repurchases.
  • Management does not expect the typical year-end hardware budget flush from enterprise clients, and SADA is projected to be dilutive in Q4 due to the reduced focus on large enterprise resales.

SUMMARY

Management reduced full-year guidance due to continued weakness in hardware spending among North American enterprise and corporate clients and a slower-than-anticipated hardware recovery. SADA's transition away from large enterprise resales is expected to weigh on fourth-quarter results and account for roughly one-third of the revised guidance shortfall. Gross profit and margin expansion were sustained by double-digit growth in cloud and Insight Core services, partially offsetting shortfalls in legacy product categories. Repurchased shares totaled $200 million year-to-date, and a new $300 million authorization remains unutilized. Operating cash flow generation is projected to surpass $500 million for the year, and operating expense reductions of $20 million-$25 million will be fully realized next year.

  • The company is shifting its SADA strategy toward corporate and mid-market clients to align with evolving partner priorities and improve future contribution.
  • Cloud gross profit growth will moderate in Q4 due to both SADA's seasonality shift and reduced enterprise agreements after partner consolidation.
  • Gross margin expansion to 20.7% in the quarter stemmed from a favorable mix, amplified by recent acquisitions and increased cloud and service contributions.
  • Management identified the partner consolidation and new agency model in EMEA as a key driver of recent software revenue declines outside North America.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Insight Core Services: Proprietary suite of services including consulting, implementation, and managed services across cloud, data, and digital infrastructure provided by Insight Enterprises.
  • SADA: Acquired Google Cloud-focused solutions business, now repositioned from enterprise resale to corporate and mid-market services within Insight Enterprises.
  • ABL Facility: Asset-based lending facility, a secured credit line backed by receivables or inventory and providing company liquidity.
