Ecopetrol (EC) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 18, 2026 at 12:15PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, November 14, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Ricardo Barragan
  • Corporate Vice President of Hydrocarbons — Rafael Guzmán
  • Vice President of Energy Transition — Bayron Triana Arias
  • Chief Financial Officer — Alfonso Camilo Munoz
  • Vice President of Strategy and New Businesses — Julian Lemos Valero
  • Chief Compliance Officer — Rodolfo García Paredes
  • Vice President of Refining — Felipe Trujillo

TAKEAWAYS

  • Production -- Average production reached 751,000 barrels of oil equivalent per day, at the upper end of guidance and reflecting record international output and the highest domestic production levels since 2021.
  • Midstream Throughput -- Transported volumes averaged 1,118,000 barrels per day, up 1% year over year and 3% sequentially, driven by operational normalization and refinery recovery.
  • EBITDA -- EBITDA for the quarter was COP 12.3 trillion with a 41% margin, an 11% increase over the previous quarter.
  • Net Income -- Net income for the quarter was COP 2.6 trillion, reflecting a 42% sequential increase.
  • Refining Throughput -- Consolidated refinery throughput was approximately 429,000 barrels per day, the segment's second highest quarterly mark.
  • Efficiency Program -- Achieved COP 4.1 trillion in efficiencies by September, 40% above the internal target for the period.
  • Costs -- Total unit cost in Hydrocarbons was $45.5 per barrel, down $1.8 year over year, and lifting costs decreased to $11.8 per barrel, a $0.44 decline from 2024.
  • CapEx -- Year-to-date investments totaled $4.179 billion, representing 72% of the annual plan, with 62% directed at Hydrocarbons, 13% at Energy Transition, and 25% at Transmission and Toll Roads.
  • Liquidity -- Cash position stood at COP 14.1 trillion at quarter end, a COP 1 trillion increase from the prior quarter, with liquidity secured until year-end.
  • Debt Metrics -- Gross debt-to-EBITDA ratio was 2.4x; excluding the ISA acquisition and related debt, the ratio was 1.7x, below industry median.
  • Renewable Energy Capacity -- Operational renewable self-generation capacity reached 254 megawatts after La Iguana solar project's commissioning.
  • Natural Gas Initiatives -- Announced commercialization of offshore Sirius field gas with volumes up to 249 million cubic feet per day and production expected in 2030.
  • Environmental Progress -- Reduced greenhouse gas emissions by 379,000 tons of Tier 2 equivalent as of September.
  • Dividend Policy -- CFO Alfonso Camilo Munoz stated, "Our proposal for the distribution of dividends for next year will be within that range, closer to the medium range," referring to the 40%-60% policy.
  • Permian Basin Strategy -- Management confirmed there is "not interested in the divestment of Permian," but potential decisions remain subject to Board review.
  • Exchange Rate Sensitivity -- Every COP 100 of exchange rate variation affects annual net profit by COP 700 billion, with a COP 600 billion net positive impact year-to-date specifically related to EBITDA from the exchange rate.
  • Credit Facilities -- A COP 700 billion revolving credit line was secured at IBR + 2.65% as a liquidity backstop.
  • Regulatory Approvals -- Received ANLA environmental approval for LNG reception and regasification infrastructure in Coveñas.
  • Operational Contingency -- Temporary suspension of operations in the northern part of the Tibu field; impacted gas output is less than 1% of total Colombian production and has been replaced by other sources.

SUMMARY

Ecopetrol S.A. (EC +1.37%) delivered increased operational throughput and financial recovery, with international production and record refinery utilization supporting sequential profit growth despite a 15% decline in Brent prices year-to-date. Active cost discipline and successful portfolio efficiency initiatives helped mitigate the adverse effects of inflation and foreign exchange volatility. Investment activity aligned to the group's strategy, with significant progress on decarbonization and renewable expansion, including the commissioning of new solar capacity and environmental certifications. The company maintained healthy liquidity and secured additional credit lines to reinforce its cash position, signaling ongoing resilience in adapting to a changing energy landscape. Strong internal controls enabled the company to address regulatory, financial, and portfolio management challenges while safeguarding future competitiveness.

  • The company executed major refinery maintenance programs, driving a 22% year-over-year increase in gross refining margin through higher throughput amid supportive market conditions.
  • Operational cash management and sale of short-term securities contributed to securing liquidity and reducing short-term debt, with no material working capital impact from the FEPC fuel stabilization fund in recent quarters.
  • Strategic gas initiatives included new supply optionality programs in the Caribbean and commercial advances on the Sirius and Florena fields, with sales contracts and infrastructure development underway.
  • Environmental commitments advanced via the Coral green hydrogen project (55% complete) and ISO 37001 anti-bribery certification, reinforcing sustainability positioning.
  • Asset portfolio management involved the launch of multi-modal asphalt exports, continued asset optimization, and exploration projects, with drilling of 10 new wells and ongoing partner negotiations to enhance returns from non-core assets.
  • Hedging strategies covered 11%-15% of dollar-denominated revenues, helping offset Colombian peso appreciation during the reporting period and supporting equity stability.
  • Management reiterated its intention to maintain low lifting costs through divestments of high-cost assets and expanded use of partners for capital allocation efficiency.
  • Continued engagement with authorities and communities facilitated the timely progress of key infrastructure and prior consultation processes across major projects.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ANLA: Colombia's National Environmental Licensing Authority, responsible for issuing permits and approvals for major energy and infrastructure projects.
  • FEPC: Fuel Price Stabilization Fund, a government mechanism impacting cash flows by subsidizing or taxing domestic fuel prices relative to international benchmarks.
  • Tier 2 equivalent: An internationally recognized greenhouse gas emission standard, used for regulatory compliance and emissions reduction tracking.
  • IBR: Indicador Bancario de Referencia, Colombia's interbank reference interest rate benchmark.
  • GBTUD: Gigabritish Thermal Units per day, a measurement of natural gas flow rates.
  • JOA: Joint Operating Agreement, the legal structure governing rights and obligations between partners in an oil and gas asset.
