Takeaways

  • Total net sales -- $1.55 billion, a 5.4% increase driven by price and new business wins, with a notable impact from the InTech DIY acquisition and a 5% decline in market volumes.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $275.3 million, up 13.9%, reflecting timing benefits from price increases versus tariff costs, and an increase of $33.6 million over the prior year.
  • Hardware and Protective Solutions (HPS) net sales -- $1.2 billion, a 7.8% rise, supported by pricing actions and gains in PowerPro screws and rope and chain categories.
  • HPS adjusted EBITDA -- $196.3 million, up 26% from the prior year due to execution by the sales and service teams.
  • Robotics and Digital Solutions (RDS) net sales -- $220.2 million, a year-over-year increase of 1.6%, attributed to the rollout of new MinuteKey 3.5 kiosks.
  • RDS margin metrics -- Adjusted gross margin at 73% and adjusted EBITDA margin at 30%, both near historical norms.
  • Canadian segment net sales -- Down 0.6% due to soft market volumes and over a two-point foreign exchange headwind; adjusted EBITDA margin just under 10%.
  • Fourth quarter net sales -- $365.1 million, a 4.5% year-over-year increase.
  • Q4 adjusted EBITDA -- $57.5 million, rising 2.3% over the prior-year period.
  • Adjusted gross profit margin (full year) -- 48.7%, up 60 basis points, with Q4 margin at 47.6% (down 10 basis points versus the prior year and slightly down sequentially).
  • Adjusted SG&A as a percentage of sales -- 31% for the year, a drop from 31.6% in the prior year; Q4 at 31.8% versus 31.5% a year earlier.
  • Free cash flow (full year) -- $35.1 million, impacted by $65 million in tariffs, down from $98.1 million the prior year.
  • Net debt -- $665.8 million at year-end, improving $8 million from 2024; liquidity at $306 million, including $279 million in revolving credit and $27 million in cash.
  • Leverage ratio -- Net debt to trailing twelve-month adjusted EBITDA at 2.4x, down from 2.8x.
  • Capital expenditures -- $70 million versus $85 million in 2024, reflecting a moderation in MinuteKey 3.5 kiosk investment pace.
  • Share repurchases -- $12.4 million used to buy back 1.4 million shares at an average price of $9.07 per share.
  • 2026 net sales guidance -- Projected range of $1.6 billion to $1.7 billion (midpoint implies 6.3% growth).
  • 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- Expected between $275 million and $285 million, with midpoint of $280 million, reflecting a 1.7% increase.
  • 2026 adjusted gross margin guidance -- Targeted at 46%-47%, stepping down as tariff pricing and costs fully normalize.
  • 2026 free cash flow guidance -- $100 million to $120 million, with a 90%+ conversion of adjusted net income.
  • 2026 capex -- Expected at $70 million–$75 million, continuing investment in MinuteKey 3.5 and merchandising solutions.
  • 2026 leverage expectation -- Planned net debt/adjusted EBITDA near 2.1x (excluding M&A), with typical seasonal inventory build in Q1 and free cash flow generation in subsequent quarters.

Risks

  • Adjusted gross margins projected to decline in 2026 due to full normalization of tariff pricing and costs, following a temporary benefit in 2025.
  • Free cash flow in 2025 fell sharply to $35.1 million, significantly impacted by $65 million in tariff-related costs.
  • Management explicitly cited persistently low existing home sales—"unchanged from the thirty-year lows we saw during 2024"—as a headwind for home improvement demand.
  • Canadian segment faced continued pressure from weak market volumes and currency headwind, with net sales down 0.6% and margins below 10%.

Summary

Hillman Solutions Corp. (HLMN 1.15%) delivered record sales and adjusted EBITDA in 2025, primarily driven by pricing actions and new business wins amid ongoing softness in market volumes. Management guided for continued revenue growth in 2026, but expects profit margin normalization as tariff-driven cost benefits recede and macro headwinds, such as low existing home sales, persist. Capital deployment included reduced capex and share buybacks, while leverage and liquidity metrics improved, supporting flexibility for organic expansion and M&A activity.

  • Management emphasized confidence in capturing new business and growing the "pro" segment, highlighting dedicated team expansion and a multi-channel focus.
  • Planned Investor Day on Mar. 19, 2026 promises further disclosure on long-term growth strategies and organic targets across business units.
  • M&A pipeline remains active with expectations of one to two bolt-on acquisitions in 2026, as paused and new opportunities emerge.
  • Rollout of 1,800 MinuteKey 3.5 kiosks in 2025 supported segment growth, with nearly 3,500 machines now deployed and further deployments scheduled in 2026 as major customer rollouts continue.
  • Guidance indicates net sales growth will outpace adjusted EBITDA increases as margins revert to historical levels.

Industry glossary

  • MinuteKey 3.5: The latest generation of Hillman’s key duplication kiosk offering, featuring enhanced capabilities such as auto key duplication and expanded self-service functions ("endless aisle").
  • Fill rate: The percentage of customer orders shipped in full on the first attempt, used as a key operational metric in retail manufacturing.
  • Pro: Contractor and trade professional segment targeted by Hillman for new products, dedicated business development, and expanded channel growth.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization as modified by company-specific adjustments for clearer performance analysis.
  • Adjusted SG&A: Selling, general, and administrative expenses adjusted for non-recurring or non-cash items, used to assess core operating efficiency.
