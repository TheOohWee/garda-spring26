Ross Stores (ROST) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 3, 2026 at 5:40PM EST

DATE

March 3, 2026

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Jim Conroy
  • Group President and Chief Operating Officer — Michael J. Hartshorn
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Bill Sheehan
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations — Connie Kao

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Sales (Q4) -- $6.6 billion, up 12%, driven by accelerated business momentum and expansion efforts.
  • Comparable Store Sales (Q4) -- 9% growth, predominately led by increased transactions and customer count, with all major categories and regions positive.
  • Net Income (Q4) -- $646 million, compared to $587 million in the prior year, reflecting improved operating performance.
  • Earnings Per Share (Q4) -- $2.00, versus $1.79 last year, excluding a $0.14 per share gain from a prior-year facility sale, resulting in 21% EPS growth excluding that benefit.
  • Operating Margin (Q4) -- 12.3%, flat versus last year’s 12.4% including a nonrecurring 105 basis point benefit; excluding the prior-year benefit, operating margin increased by 95 basis points.
  • Inventory Growth -- Consolidated inventories up 8% year over year; packaway inventory represented 37% versus 41% a year earlier.
  • Store Count (Year End) -- 2,267 total stores (1,904 Ross Dress for Less and 363 dd’s DISCOUNTS), after opening 80 Ross and 10 dd’s DISCOUNTS locations and closing nine stores.
  • Merchandise Margin (Q4) -- Improved by 10 basis points, with CFO Sheehan stating gains were "mainly driven by the better buying."
  • SG&A Rate (Q4) -- Increased 75 basis points primarily from the previous year’s facility sale; excluding this, SG&A was down 30 basis points.
  • Full-Year Sales -- $22.8 billion, representing 8% growth over the prior year’s $21.1 billion.
  • Comparable Store Sales (Full Year) -- 5% growth, building on 3% growth reported for the previous year.
  • Full-Year EPS -- $6.61, compared to $6.32 last year; adjusted for prior-year facility sale gain and $0.16 per share in tariff-related costs, EPS increased 10%.
  • Share Repurchase -- 1,500,000 shares repurchased in Q4, completing the $2.1 billion, two-year program; new authorization of $2.55 billion over two years, a 21% increase.
  • Quarterly Cash Dividend -- Increased by 10% to $0.45 per share.
  • 2026 Guidance (Q1) -- Comparable sales expected to rise 7%-8%; EPS forecasted at $1.60-$1.67; total sales projected to increase 10%-12%.
  • 2026 Guidance (Full Year) -- Comparable sales up 3%-4%, EPS expected at $7.02-$7.36, and total sales growth of 5%-7% anticipated.
  • 2026 Operating Margin Guidance -- Projected range of 12.0%-12.3%, compared to 11.9% in 2025, on improvement in merchandise margin and distribution costs.
  • Planned Store Openings (2026) -- 110 new stores (85 Ross and 25 dd’s DISCOUNTS), representing 5% unit growth, concentrated more heavily in summer and fall openings.
  • Capital Expenditures (2026) -- $1.1 billion projected, with spend allocated among new stores, distribution centers, store maintenance, and technology.
  • Store Productivity -- Noted as "one of our best years in a while," with new stores performing above expectations across both new and existing markets.
  • Marketing Spend -- CEO Conroy said, "as a rate of sales, we have not changed our marketing spend," but left open the possibility of minor increases for experimentation.
  • Customer Demographics -- Growth seen "broad-based across income demographics and age demographics, including 18- to 34-year-old customers," per CEO Conroy.

SUMMARY

Ross Stores (ROST 1.67%) reported double-digit revenue, earnings, and EPS growth for the fourth quarter, with broad-based positive comparable sales across merchandise categories and regions. Management highlighted continued positive trends in customer traffic and new store productivity, enabling an acceleration in store expansion plans. The company introduced an increased share repurchase program, a 10% dividend hike, and provided guidance for further EPS and margin improvement in 2026.

  • The new store productivity outperformance occurred in both established regions like California and Florida, and new markets, contributing materially to total sales growth.
  • Packaway inventory as a percent of total ended the period at 37%, down from 41% last year, improving inventory flexibility.
  • Executive commentary confirmed that fourth-quarter merchandise margin gains were "mostly by better buying" rather than lower markdowns.
  • Incremental sales and profit initiatives included merchandising improvements, marketing refinements, and increased supply chain efficiency, with significant planned investment in distribution centers and technology.
  • CFO Sheehan guided for first quarter operating margin compression versus last year, citing persistently higher distribution center costs, packaway-related timing, and higher incentive compensation—all partially offset by expected merchandise margin improvement.
  • Expansion into the New York Metro Area and Puerto Rico marked important milestones for Ross Dress for Less’s geographic footprint during the quarter.
  • Leadership noted the sequential improvement in the home category, which had been pressured by tariffs, as well as continued strength in cosmetics, shoes, and juniors apparel.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Packaway Inventory: Merchandise purchased well in advance and stored for future sale to enable timely in-store allocation and support inventory flexibility.
  • IMU: Initial Markup; the difference between cost and retail price at the time merchandise is first offered for sale, before markdowns or discounts.
