  • President — James Zelter

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted Net Income -- $5.2 billion, up 14% year over year, equating to $8.38 per share.
  • Fee-Related Earnings (FRE) -- $2.5 billion, an increase of 23% year over year, driven by record asset management inflows and operational scale.
  • Spread-Related Earnings (SRE) -- $3.4 billion, up 9% year over year on a normalized basis.
  • Total Origination Volume -- Over $305 billion, up nearly 40% year over year, with $282 billion in debt, comprised of 80% investment grade (average rating: single A) and 20% sub-investment grade (average rating: single B).
  • Origination Spread -- Investment-grade origination delivered 290 basis points above treasuries (220 basis points above rated corporates); sub-investment grade origination achieved 490 basis points above treasuries (200 basis points above comparable high-yield).
  • Capital Formation -- Record $228 billion inflows, consisting of $100 billion organic and $45 billion inorganic into Asset Management, and $83 billion into Athene.
  • Athene Net Invested Assets -- $292 billion, reflecting 18% growth year over year.
  • 2025 Inflows by Channel -- Athene saw $34 billion from retail, $35 billion from funding agreement issuance, and $12 billion from reinsurance, with the rest from pension group annuities and new channels.
  • FRE Margin -- Approximately 57%, stable versus prior year and aligned to stated targets.
  • Global Wealth Fundraising -- $18 billion, up nearly 50% year over year, with nine strategies over $500 million and three exceeding $1 billion in annual fundraising.
  • Third-Party Insurance Platform -- $135 billion AUM across 30 mandates, bolstered by $15 billion of new mandates and $16 billion in third-party sidecar growth.
  • Quarterly SRE & Blended Net Spread -- $865 million SRE for the quarter, with a 120 basis point blended net spread ex-notables, compared to 121 basis points in the prior quarter.
  • Fee-Generating AUM -- $79 billion, reflecting a 25% increase year over year.
  • Capital Solutions Fees -- $226 million in Q4, surpassing $800 million for the full year, driven by 125 transactions in the quarter and about 430 for the year (60% credit-driven).
  • Individual and Third-Party Insurance Growth -- Individual market inflows over $18 billion; third-party insurance origination exceeding $15 billion, both cited as major expansion areas.
  • Software Exposure -- Less than 2% of total AUM, with zero exposure to growth software in private equity and only 0.5% of Athene’s balance sheet (mainly investment grade with Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT) and Oracle (NYSE: ORCL)).
  • Dividend Increase -- Annual per-share dividend to be raised by 10%, from $2.04 to $2.25 in 2026, with a stated plan for roughly 10% annual growth going forward.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Management reaffirmed expectations for 20% plus FRE growth and approximately 10% SRE growth, translating to about $3.85 billion in SRE (assuming 11% alternatives return).
  • Athene–ARI Transaction -- Subject to approval, Athene to acquire $9 billion of commercial mortgage assets with 7.7% yield, providing 50–75 basis points additional spread versus new commercial mortgage loans, but replacing rather than expanding SRE assets.
  • ADS (Direct Lending Vehicle) -- Over $25 billion in AUM, annual return of roughly 8%, and net inflows exceeded $5 billion despite industry redemptions.
  • Globalization -- Origination in 2025 split as $245 billion North America, $40 billion Europe, and $15–$20 billion Asia-Pacific, illustrating focus on geographic expansion.
  • Private Equity Flagship Funds -- Flagship funds delivered 39% gross and 24% net returns over three and a half decades; Fund XIII posted a 22% net IRR.
  • Hybrid Value and AAA Vehicle -- Hybrid value up 16% for the year; AAA asset vehicle exceeds $25 billion in size, maintaining a 12% inception-to-date return and 23 quarters of consecutive positive performance.
  • Organic Inflows -- $182 billion firmwide, with approximately two-thirds from third parties.
  • Annualized Fee Revenue from Bridge Acquisition -- Bridge produced $105 million fee-related revenue and $60 million expenses in its first four months; quarterizing is viewed as the near-term run rate.
  • Performance Fees -- $588 million realized in Q4 from carry, including notable gains from Fund Ten, Accord Plus, and credit hedge fund strategies.
  • Multi-Year Tax Rate Expectation -- Targeting around 20%, acknowledging quarterly variability.
  • Shareholder Capital Return -- $1.5 billion returned via dividends and repurchases in 2025, alongside continued strategic investment.

SUMMARY

Record adjusted net income for the full year was supported by double-digit growth in both fee-related and spread-related earnings. Management emphasized the strategic expansion from a single institutional market to six distinct channels, each requiring separate products, technology, and client approaches. Global origination has accelerated, with 2025 showing nearly 40% higher originations, and the business is actively broadening its global footprint across North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. Management highlighted continued dividend growth plans and increased capital return, while introducing platform enhancements such as open architecture collaborations and transition to daily NAV. Recent asset acquisitions, including the ARI portfolio, are being incorporated into guidance as part of maintaining a 10% SRE growth target rather than driving incremental gains. Leadership cited ongoing macroeconomic volatility and a need for risk-aware discipline, with a "principal's mindset" guiding deployment of both capital and product innovation.

  • Dialogue referenced product innovation enabling access to alternatives for traditional asset managers and retirement channels, including developments in daily liquid and NAV-driven offerings.
  • Management affirmed organic growth drivers in both credit- and equity-oriented strategies, with alternative asset origination scalability seen as the limiting factor for further expansion.
  • "origination, product, and investing teams working in sync across the firm." are highlighted as the key differentiator producing over $60 billion of value to investors in 2025.
  • Exposure to software assets is intentionally minimal, with less than 2% of AUM and zero growth software in private equity, reflecting a highly selective portfolio posture.
  • Leadership reinforced that future FRE margin expansion of "something like a 100 basis points annually" is targeted through platform efficiency and international scaling.
  • Retirement Services' guidance for $85 billion inflows in 2026 relies on profitability discipline, not volume, with spread-based competitive advantages noted as a key differentiator.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FRE (Fee-Related Earnings): Pre-tax earnings generated from management fees, capital solutions fees, and other recurring asset management-related activities, excluding performance-based income.
  • SRE (Spread-Related Earnings): Earnings derived largely from investment spread between asset yields and cost of liabilities, especially within retirement services.
  • ACS (Apollo Capital Solutions): The firm's platform for originating and syndicating structured financing and capital solutions, contributing to fee generation.
  • ADS: Apollo's flagship direct lending vehicle, focused on senior secured corporate lending.
  • AAA: Apollo's large hybrid private credit vehicle, emphasizing stable returns and low volatility for institutional clients.
  • PRIV: Apollo's public ETF, designed to offer liquid access to private investment grade credit.
  • ARI (Apollo Commercial Real Estate Finance): REIT entity whose commercial mortgage loan portfolio is being acquired by Athene.
  • PRT (Pension Risk Transfer): Transaction type where a plan sponsor transfers pension obligations to an insurer.
  • Sidecar: Investment vehicles established alongside primary funds or platforms to make co-investments, often in insurance or alternatives.
  • Daily NAV: A product feature allowing investment vehicles to report and transact at net asset value on a daily basis, enabling higher liquidity for investors.
