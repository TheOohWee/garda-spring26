VeriSign (VRSN) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 6:57PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Oct. 23, 2025 at 6:27 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Executive chairman and CEO — D. Bidzos
  • Chief financial officer — John Calys
  • Vice president, investor relations — David Atchley

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Domain name base -- .com and .net domain name base reached 171.9 million, representing a 1.4% year-over-year increase.
  • New registrations -- 10.6 million new domain registrations during the quarter; net additions were 1.5 million.
  • Revenue -- $419 million, up 7.3% year-over-year.
  • EPS -- Diluted earnings per share of $2.27, reflecting a 9.7% year-over-year increase.
  • Renewal rate -- Preliminary renewal rate of 75.3%, up from 72.2% in the prior year quarter.
  • Geographic trends -- Sequential domain base growth occurred across all three main regions; the U.S. and EMEA were identified as strongest contributors.
  • Shareholder returns -- Returned $72 million via dividends, and $215 million via share repurchases, totaling $287 million for the quarter.
  • Cash and liquidity -- $618 million in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities at quarter-end.
  • Share repurchase authorization -- $1.33 billion remained under the authorized program, with no expiration date.
  • Operating cash flow -- $308 million, with free cash flow reported at $303 million, both up from $253 million and $248 million, respectively, in the prior year quarter.
  • Updated guidance -- Full-year revenue projected at $1.652 billion to $1.657 billion; operating income guidance set at $1.119 billion to $1.124 billion.
  • Declared dividend -- Cash dividend of $0.77 per share declared, payable Nov. 25, 2025, to shareholders of record as of Nov. 18, 2025.
  • Expense drivers -- Operating expense was $135 million, an increase from both last quarter ($129 million) and prior year ($121 million), primarily due to higher incentive compensation and legal costs.
  • Marketing program impact -- Management attributed growth and improved renewal rates to enhanced marketing programs, stating these initiatives are "accretive," and increasingly responsive to registrar feedback.
  • AI influence -- CEO Bidzos noted, "AI is having a positive impact on registrations as well as on the utilization of our DNS resolution services."
  • Google AdSense exposure -- CEO Bidzos said, "we view our exposure as minimal" regarding recent changes to Google AdSense, clarifying most affected domain traffic models lie outside VeriSign's core business.
  • .web update -- Litigation remains pending, with "the final hearing is still scheduled for mid-November 2025." No new substantive developments were reported during the quarter.
  • gTLD process -- The ICANN 2026 round of new generic Top-Level Domains is intended to start in Q2 2026, with no future auctions planned; timing of deployment may extend into 2027.

Summary

VeriSign (VRSN 3.74%) reported year-over-year growth in its core .com and .net domain portfolio, citing improved underlying demand and enhanced channel engagement. Full-year revenue and operating income guidance was reaffirmed within narrow ranges, while management highlighted ongoing operational discipline and cash generation. Product innovation and registrar-focused programs were directly linked to improved renewal and registration metrics, with management emphasizing expansion in the U.S. and EMEA. Management addressed industry concerns regarding Google AdSense changes, indicating minimal business impact and clear strategic differentiation from affected market segments. The company gave an update on the .web litigation timeline and provided insight into preparations for the upcoming ICANN gTLD round.

  • Management stated that increased use of artificial intelligence is "a primary driver" behind growing registration and resolution activity, underscoring the evolving importance of DNS infrastructure.
  • Marketing program effectiveness was detailed, with cost accounting handled as a revenue reduction, rather than a component of SG&A, providing further clarity to expense line item interpretation.
  • Cash returns to shareholders of $287 million, combined with $1.33 billion in remaining repurchase authorization, position the company for flexibility in capital allocation.
  • No segmentation detail was disclosed for domain monetization purposes, but management distinguished parked resale domains from those dependent on ad revenue models.

Industry glossary

  • Agentic web: An emerging model where AI agents autonomously perform actions across websites and services, leveraging DNS infrastructure for persistent, resolvable identities.
  • gTLD: Generic Top-Level Domain; an internet domain extension such as .com or .net overseen by ICANN.

We're seeing solid underlying demand for our domain names and continued registrar engagement with our programs which together have enhanced the pace of growth in new registrations.

Given these continued positive domain name base trends, we now expect the growth in the domain name base to be between 2.2% and 2.5% for 2025. Our financial and liquidity position remained stable with $618 million in cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities at the end of the quarter. At the end of the quarter, $1.33 billion remained available under the current share repurchase program, which has no expiration. So AI companies need data, and they're continuously scouring the Internet to get it. Data is not static. And so AIs like search engines are constantly fetching fresh data from websites to augment their existing data. This is enabled by the DNS. Registrars in the DNS ecosystem are well positioned to layer new value-added services on this infrastructure, again, all enabled by the DNS. So from what we see today, the trends are very positive.
