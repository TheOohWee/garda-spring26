Atlassian (TEAM) Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 6:01PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 5, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer & Co-Founder — Michael Cannon-Brookes
  • Chief Financial Officer — Joe Binz
  • Head of Investor Relations — Martin Lam

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Cloud Revenue -- Reached $1 billion for the first time, representing 26% year-over-year growth.
  • Annual Run Rate Revenue -- Surpassed $6 billion.
  • Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) -- Rose by 44% year over year to $3.8 billion and accelerated for three consecutive quarters.
  • Net Revenue Retention (NRR) -- Exceeded 120%, with sequential improvement for the third quarter in a row.
  • Million-Dollar Deals -- Record number of deals greater than $1 million in annual contract value, nearly doubling year over year.
  • AI Feature Adoption -- Robo surpassed 5 million monthly active users, with Teamwork Collection passing 1 million seats sold in less than nine months.
  • AI Impact on Customer Expansion -- Companies using AI code generation create 5% more Jira tasks, see 5% higher Jira monthly active users, and expand seats for Jira 5% faster than non-AI users within the software segment.
  • Seat Expansion Rates -- Double-digit seat expansion in Teamwork Collection compared to standalone applications.
  • Service Management Metrics -- Jira Service Management passed 65,000 customers and claims enterprise growth over 60% year over year; more than two-thirds of service collection customers now use it for non-IT use cases.
  • Cloud Migration Contribution -- Migrations contributed a mid- to high-single-digit percentage to cloud revenue growth and are projected to maintain this contribution for the full fiscal year.
  • Midterm Guidance -- Management reiterated a 20%+ compounded annual revenue growth target through fiscal 2027 and 25%+ non-GAAP operating margin for fiscal 2027.
  • Pricing Model Comments -- Seat-based pricing remains the preferred framework, complemented by select consumption-based models for features like Forge, extra AI credits, and Bitbucket pipelines.
  • Enterprise Penetration -- Atlassian Corporation serves more than 350,000 customers, including over 80% of the Fortune 500 and 60% of the Forbes AI 50.
  • Cross-Sell Momentum -- Teamwork Collection package, which bundles multiple products and additional AI credits, delivers higher seat expansion and adoption rates, outpacing standalone application conversion.
  • Buyback and Guidance Update Impact -- Management noted raised guidance and a significant buyback acceleration, though immediate impact on the share price was addressed as not correlating with operational performance.
  • Non-Technical Seat Expansion -- Significant seat growth reported in HR, finance, and other business teams beyond traditional technology user base.

SUMMARY

Atlassian Corporation (TEAM 3.02%) reported a record-setting $1 billion cloud revenue quarter and achieved annual run rate revenue above $6 billion, citing accelerating demand for AI-powered solutions and multi-year enterprise commitments. The company disclosed that AI adoption is materially increasing usage and seat expansion within core products, and that over 1 million seats were sold for its Teamwork Collection in less than nine months. Management reaffirmed its midterm revenue growth and margin targets, while reiterating proactive pricing discipline and a leading position in enterprise workflow transformation.

  • CFO Joe Binz emphasized broad-based seat expansion momentum across both technical and non-technical users, with enterprise and SMB segments both contributing to stable growth rates.
  • CEO Michael Cannon-Brookes cited customer priorities now centering on AI integration, compliance, and unified systems of work, driving a shift to long-term cloud platform deals.
  • Jira Service Management, now used by more than 65,000 customers and half the Fortune 500, is experiencing its fastest scaleup and strong adoption for non-IT workflows.
  • Guidance for mid- to high-single-digit cloud migration contributions suggests continued transition from data center deployments, with incentives for cloud upgrades maintained.
  • The company reported that Teamwork Collection has double-digit higher seat expansion rates than standalone products, strongly correlating with its AI platform and bundled pricing.
  • Management addressed pricing strategy by highlighting hybrid models and customer preference for predictability, with selective use of consumption-based pricing for advanced workloads.
  • The company stated that agent-based and AI-enhanced workflows are now operating at scale in both Jira and Service Management, contributing to increased customer stickiness and multi-year commitments.
  • Atlassian Corporation remains confident in achieving at least 20% compounded annual revenue growth and expanding margins through fiscal 2027, with ongoing risk adjustments in short-term forecasts.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations): Contracted revenue obliged to be recognized in future periods, representing visibility into booked business not yet delivered.
  • NRR (Net Revenue Retention): A measure of recurring revenue growth from existing customers, accounting for expansions, contractions, and churn.
  • Teamwork Collection: Atlassian Corporation’s cloud-based suite bundling multiple collaboration apps and advanced AI credits on a seat-based subscription model.
  • Jira Service Management (JSM): The company’s service desk and workflow automation platform spanning IT, HR, finance, and operations.
  • Agentic Workflows: Automated processes executed by software agents or AI within business applications, often replacing or augmenting human input for routine tasks.
  • MAU (Monthly Active Users): The count of distinct users actively engaging with a product or feature within a calendar month.
