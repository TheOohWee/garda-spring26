TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Assets Under Construction and Completed -- Exceeded $328 million, supported by ongoing development at phase two in Miami, Bradley International, and Addison in Dallas, with new ground broken at Salt Lake City and more sites scheduled for 2026.
  • Revenue -- Increased by 87% to $27.5 million, attributed to the acquisition of Camarillo and higher revenue from both new and existing campuses.
  • Operating Expenses -- Rose to nearly $28 million, primarily due to a higher number of operating campuses and accrual-based expensing of ground leases.
  • SG&A Expense -- Showed a small quarter-end dip because of reduced cash compensation for senior management; management targets a cash-based ceiling of $20 million for SG&A.
  • Positive Operating Cash Flow -- Achieved for the first time, largely due to "the realization of $5.9 million in upfront rent, part of an extension of an existing tenant that closed in December."
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Hit breakeven on a run-rate basis in December, with fourth-quarter adjusted EBITDA at approximately negative $1 million, an improvement for the third straight quarter.
  • Obligated Group Revenues -- Rose 49% year over year and 18% sequentially in the fourth quarter, with a moderate increase anticipated for 2026 and a further uptick upon new project completions in 2027.
  • Re-leasing Uplift -- "Twenty-two percent is the average markup from the last year of the previous lease to the first year of the new lease" for mature leases in Miami and Nashville.
  • Rent Escalators on Multiyear Leases -- Annual escalators have moved from a "It used to be with a floor of 3%. Today, it is a floor of 4%." on new leases.
  • Aggregate Ground Lease Square Footage -- Stands at 4,160,000 square feet across the portfolio, encompassing active, funded, and future projects.
  • Construction Program -- Approximately 750,000 square feet under construction entering 2026, with ramp-up planned, and development schedule showing accelerating campus deliveries and scale.
  • J.P. Morgan Debt Facility -- Finalized five-year tax-exempt drawdown facility, undrawn at year-end 2025, now available to fund active projects with $200 million committed.
  • 2026 Series Subordinate Bonds -- Secured $150 million at a 6% fixed interest rate, three times oversubscribed, with 18 institutional investors and a call option in year four; proceeds to amplify leverage.
  • Illustrative Run-Rate Campus Economics -- Management targets $40 per square foot in rent, $5 fuel margin, $9 operating expense, and $36 NOI per square foot as a model for new deals.
  • Cash and Treasuries -- Closed 2025 with $48 million in liquidity, now enhanced by $150 million bond proceeds and $200 million available from the J.P. Morgan facility.
  • Prototype Build Cost -- Management cited "hard cost below $250 a foot," attributing declines to vertical integration (steel fabrication, general contracting) and scale-driven efficiency.
  • Expansion and Waitlists -- Management reported consistent inability to meet demand at airports such as San Jose, citing their strategy to maximize net operating income via premium site acquisition and expansion in familiar markets.

SUMMARY

Sky Harbour Group Corporation (SKYH +0.00%) reported record consolidated revenue, citing 87% growth, and established positive operating cash flow, driven largely by upfront rent receipts from long-term tenant extensions. Management emphasized a deliberate shift in guidance metrics toward net operating income (NOI) capture over simple counts of airports or square footage, foreshadowing a more nuanced approach to capital formation and site development. The company outlined accelerated expansion with a fully funded pipeline facilitated by $150 million in subordinate bond financing and a $200 million J.P. Morgan debt facility, alongside expectations of continued efficiency improvements via vertical integration and development phasing.

  • A new leasing approach enables rapid occupancy gains through short-term, below-target leases, followed by renegotiation to above-target, long-term contracts, supporting stable revenue ratchets upon stabilization.
  • Campuses entering prelease and initial lease-up phases command meaningfully higher average rents than legacy properties, attributable to management's targeted site selection and pre-commitment strategy.
  • Management committed to further cost reductions in future project builds, maintaining that ongoing vertical integration and process repetition will continue to compress build costs below the already achieved $250 per square foot baseline.
  • Guidance for 2026 will prioritize NOI as the leading operational metric, with detailed targets forthcoming in the next quarterly disclosure.
  • Asset monetization via ultra-long-term prepaid leases (described as hangar "sales") is viewed strictly as a capital management lever, with management stating such deals will occur only at compelling present-value terms.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Obligated Group: The ring-fenced set of campuses whose operating and financial results are consolidated for credit and bond covenant purposes within Sky Harbour Capital.
  • NOI (Net Operating Income): The income from campus operations after operating expenses, before debt service and other non-operating costs.
  • Ground Lease: A long-term lease of land on which Sky Harbour constructs and operates hangar facilities.
  • Floor (as in CPI escalator): The minimum guaranteed annual rent increase, set here at 3%-4% regardless of actual inflation changes.
  • Triple Net Lease: Lease agreement where the tenant is responsible for property taxes, insurance, and maintenance in addition to rent.
