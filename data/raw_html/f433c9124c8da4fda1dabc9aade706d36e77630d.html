Cinemark (CNK) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 5, 2025 at 11:18AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Nov. 5, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

President and Chief Executive Officer — Sean Gamble

Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Melissa Thomas

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

Industry Box Office -- North American box office reached $2.5 billion in Q3 2025, a decrease of approximately 10% year over year, due to a less robust film slate compared to the prior year.

Market Share -- Cinemark (CNK 1.62%) exceeded North American box office performance by nearly 250 basis points in Q3 2025, achieving its highest third-quarter domestic market share on record.

Global Attendance -- Welcomed 54.2 million guests globally in Q3 2025, reflecting a 10% year-over-year decrease, which management attributed to last year's exceptionally strong film lineup.

Global Revenue -- Generated $857.5 million in global revenue in Q3 2025.

Adjusted EBITDA -- Achieved $177.6 million in adjusted EBITDA in Q3 2025 with a corresponding margin of 20.7% for the quarter despite lower overall attendance levels.

Domestic Revenue -- U.S. operations produced $683.6 million in revenue in Q3 2025 and $140.2 million in adjusted EBITDA, with a 20.5% adjusted EBITDA margin.

International Revenue -- International revenues totaled $173.9 million in Q3 2025, with $37.4 million in adjusted EBITDA and a 21.5% adjusted EBITDA margin.

Admissions and Ticket Price -- Domestic admissions revenue was $348.5 million in Q3 2025, and the average ticket price was $10.50, a 5% year-over-year increase in average ticket price, primarily linked to pricing strategies and alternative content mix.

Concessions Per Capita -- Domestic concession per cap reached a record $8.20 in Q3 2025, up 3% year over year, driven by pricing initiatives, a favorable shift in product mix, and merchandise sales.

Alternative Content -- Nontraditional programming contributed 16% of the domestic box office in Q3 2025, representing the second-highest quarterly dollar amount for the segment in company history.

Net Income and EPS -- Reported net income attributable to Cinemark of $49.5 million in Q3 2025 with diluted earnings per share of $0.40.

Free Cash Flow -- Generated $38 million in free cash flow in Q3 2025 and ended the quarter with $461 million in cash.

Debt Extinguishment -- Completely retired remaining pandemic-related debt, including full settlement of $460 million in convertible notes and related warrants in Q3 2025, eliminating all outstanding COVID-related debt as of the end of Q3 2025.

Share Repurchases -- Repurchased 7.93 million shares in March 2025, offsetting warrant-related share issuance and resulting in no net dilution for long-term shareholders.

Share Count -- As of September 30, 116.5 million shares were outstanding, with an additional 2.2 million shares issued in Q4 2025 to further settle warrants.

Shareholder Returns -- Authorized a new $300 million share repurchase program in Q3 2025.

Net Leverage Ratio -- Ended the quarter with a net leverage ratio of 2.4 times, within management’s target range of two to three times.

Capital Expenditures -- Invested $106 million in the first nine months of this year with a full-year target of $225 million, weighted toward the fourth quarter due to the timing of several projects.

G&A Expense -- General and administrative expense was $61.9 million in Q3 2025, primarily up due to wage inflation, increased headcount, software costs, and share-based compensation.

SUMMARY

Cinemark (CNK 1.62%) fully extinguished its pandemic-related debt, addressing all convertible notes and related warrants, with the next debt maturity not until 2028. Management introduced a $300 million share repurchase program in Q3 2025 and raised the quarterly dividend, with plans for measured execution aligned to capital priorities. Despite a 10% year-over-year decline in global attendance and industry box office, the company achieved record third-quarter domestic market share and maintained structural share gains above 100 basis points versus pre-pandemic levels. The company emphasized a disciplined approach to M&A, ongoing investment in circuit quality, and continued focus on leveraging strategic and data-driven pricing to enhance revenue and margin opportunities. Share count adjustments from warrant settlements will result in diluted share count nearly matching the basic share count in 2026, aside from share-based compensation effects.

Sean Gamble stated, "we have settled the final outstanding warrants related to our convertible notes, thereby fully extinguishing the remaining portion of our COVID-related debt as of November 5, 2025."

Melissa Thomas indicated that growth in capital expenditures could occur in 2026 "above what we're targeting in 2025" due to deferred maintenance and ROI-driven projects, with further details to come after budget finalization.

Alternative content has represented more than 10% of the box office for the past three years, with management highlighting further upside in anime, faith-based, and multicultural segments.

The share repurchase and dividend increases were explicitly tied to a strengthened balance sheet and confidence in sustained cash generation.

Management maintained a cautious outlook that margin improvement for the year is dependent on box office results in the fourth quarter and noted the absence of a major animated release impacting year-to-date performance.

Ongoing investments in premium formats, including upgrades to IMAX and ScreenX and expansion of Cinemark XD, were confirmed, supporting competitive differentiation in premium amenities.

Pricing power remains an area of focus, with actions driven by elasticity analysis and the goal of maximizing both attendance and per-guest revenue without eroding perceived value.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Per Cap: Industry abbreviation for per-capita spending, usually referencing concessions, indicating average spending per guest.

Alternative Content: Refers to nontraditional theatrical programming such as anime, multicultural, faith-based, music, and specialty films, separate from mainstream studio releases.

Adjusting EBITDA Margin: The ratio of adjusted earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization to total revenue, providing an indicator of core operating profitability.

Net Leverage Ratio: Financial metric that measures net debt divided by adjusted EBITDA, used to assess balance sheet risk and capital allocation discipline.
