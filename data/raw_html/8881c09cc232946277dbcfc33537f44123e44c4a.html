By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 2:28PM EST

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Core Earnings Consistency -- Quarterly core earnings ranged from $109 million to $177 million post-FEMA Puerto Rico claims, indicating stability after one-off items phased out.
  • Full-year EBITDA Plus Gains Forecast -- Revised annual guidance to $1.25 billion‑$1.5 billion, up from prior estimates, incorporating realized and potential gains from asset sales.
  • Jamaica Asset Sale -- Completed for $1.055 billion with $778 million net proceeds and a $430 million gain, marking a major deleveraging transaction and exceeding original price and timing forecasts.
  • Debt and Balance Sheet Actions -- Amended credit agreements allowed early repayments ($270 million on the revolver, $55 million on Term Loan A) and eliminated select debt covenants, retaining nearly $400 million in proceeds to address 2026 maturities and deferring significant debt until late 2027.
  • Segment Operating Margin -- Delivered $106 million for Q1, down from $240 million in Q4 2024, correlating with lower one-off contributions.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Reported at $82 million for the quarter, reflecting the absence of material one-off gains.
  • Net Loss -- GAAP net loss of $200 million, or $0.73 per share, with no material one-time items for Q1.
  • Liquidity -- Ended Q1 with $448 million cash, $275 million available on the revolver, and $393 million of added cash from Jamaica sale, producing over $1.1 billion pro forma liquidity.
  • Brazil Power Plant Progress -- CELBA plant reached 95% completion, targeting COD in the second half; PortoCem at 54% completion with major turbine installations ahead of schedule.
  • Contracted Margin -- Five major long-term supply/demand contracts commit 109 TBtus of supply and generate $500 million in annual margin, representing about half of total available volumes.
  • FSRU Subcharter Gains -- Subchartered surplus FSRUs, resulting in cumulative $143 million, $59 million, $110 million, and $312 million nominal profits over 3‑10 year contracts; present value estimated at $236 million (10% discount rate).
  • Barcarena Complex Contracts -- Norsk Hydro receiving gas under a 15-year, Henry Hub plus $6.04/MMBtu contract (with annual U.S. CPI adjustment), 90% take-or-pay on roughly 30 TBtus per year; CELBA secured with a 25-year PPA, 100% take-or-pay for 18 TBtus during the second semester, indexed to 91% of JKM plus $3.36/MMBtu and U.S. CPI adjustment.

SUMMARY

The company executed a $1.055 billion Jamaica asset sale, providing net proceeds of $778 million and a $430 million gain, immediately enhancing liquidity and reducing leverage. Management articulated a shift toward asset-level financing and balance sheet simplification, targeting refinancing and deferral of substantial debt maturities until the second half of 2027. Construction milestones include near-finalization of the CELBA power plant in Brazil and significant advancements at PortoCem, both tied to long-duration, inflation-linked contracts with high-credit counterparties. Executives expect material margin expansion by replicating these long-term arrangements across additional portfolio volumes to potentially double annual contracted margin to $1 billion. Strategic focus remains on finalizing a FEMA claim, progressing the Nicaragua restructuring, and capitalizing on regulatory-driven opportunities in Brazil and Puerto Rico as market conditions evolve.

  • Chairman Edens said, "EBITDA plus gains to be $1.25 billion to $1.5 billion for the year, which is higher than our previous estimate."
  • Leandro Cunha cited, "combined cycle power plant is expected to reach COD in the second half of this year, while the second power plant, a 1.6 gigawatt open cycle plant is on track for COD by mid next year."
  • Chief Financial Officer Guinta reported, "We ended Q1 with $448 million of cash on hand and $275 million available under our revolving credit facility. Add to that, the $393 million of cash proceeds after debt pay down and you have over $1.1 billion of pro forma liquidity at the end of Q1."
  • The company confirmed, "following the Jamaica sale, our goals are very, very clear. Number one, first and foremost is to simplify the balance sheet and do so by extending the duration to match underlying assets and lower our debt costs."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FSRU: Floating Storage and Regasification Unit—a ship or barge used to store liquefied natural gas (LNG) and regasify it for delivery to shore terminals.
  • COD: Commercial Operation Date—the date on which an energy asset begins revenue-generating operations under contracted terms.
  • JKM: Japan/Korea Marker—a benchmark price for spot LNG delivered to Northeast Asia.
  • PPA: Power Purchase Agreement—long-term contract for supply of electricity between a power generator and a buyer, defining price and delivery terms.
  • TBtu: Trillion British thermal units, a standard measure of energy volume used in gas contracts.
