Intrusion (INTZ) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 25, 2026 at 9:12AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, March 24, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Anthony Scott
  • Chief Financial Officer — Kimberly Pinson

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $1.5 million for the quarter, representing a 25% sequential decline and 12% decrease year over year, attributed to a delay in incremental funding for a major U.S. government contract.
  • Full-Year Revenue -- $7.1 million, up 23% from the prior year, primarily driven by an earlier contract expansion with the U.S. Department of War.
  • Consulting Revenue -- $1.1 million for the quarter, down from $1.5 million in the prior quarter, and $1.3 million in the prior year quarter.
  • Shield Revenue -- $0.4 million for the quarter, compared to $0.5 million in the previous quarter, and $0.3 million in the fourth quarter of 2024.
  • Gross Profit Margin -- 74% for the quarter, lower than the prior year period; full-year margin of 76%, down roughly 93 basis points from 2024.
  • Operating Expenses -- $4 million for the quarter, up $0.3 million sequentially, and $0.8 million year over year, mainly due to increased sales and marketing spending.
  • Net Loss -- $2.8 million, or $0.14 per share for the quarter, compared to $2 million in the prior year period; full-year net loss of $9.1 million or $0.46 per share, increasing by $1.3 million from last year.
  • Cash Position -- $3.6 million in cash and cash equivalents at year-end.
  • Contract Delay Impact -- CEO Scott confirmed a contract timing delay cost at least $0.5 million in quarterly revenue, with the expense already recognized and revenue expected to be nearly 100% margin when captured.
  • Product Expansion -- Shield Cloud launched on AWS and Microsoft Azure marketplaces, broadening sales channels.
  • Shield Stratus Launch -- Rolled out as a cloud-native packet filtering solution, touted as integrating with AWS gateway load balancer.
  • Law Enforcement Pipeline -- P.O.S.S.E. program pilot shows high adoption and has begun distribution across Texas, Missouri, Oklahoma, and Iowa, providing access to hundreds of public safety agencies and schools.
  • Sales Leadership -- Hired Valencia Reaves as Public Sector VP of Sales, and Patrick Duggan as Director of Channel Sales & Partnerships, to strengthen business development.
  • Future Debt Financing -- CFO Pinson stated intent to seek a small debt financing round to support growth initiatives, and cited ongoing discussions.

RISKS

  • Quarterly revenue fell 25% sequentially and 12% year over year due to delayed U.S. government contract funding, with CFO Pinson noting, "The timing of this award was affected by funding and procurement constraints associated with the U.S. government shutdown and continuing resolution."
  • Operating expenses rose by $0.3 million sequentially, and $0.8 million year over year, reflecting sustained higher sales and marketing costs without immediate top-line offset.
  • CFO Pinson acknowledged that increased sales and marketing expenses may continue to grow, stating, "We will continue to invest in sales and marketing. What we saw in the first quarter approximates the run rate, but we will see some increases from here."
  • Full-year net loss increased by $1.3 million compared to the prior year, reaching $9.1 million.

SUMMARY

Intrusion (INTZ 3.44%) management attributed the quarterly revenue decline to the delay in incremental funding of a large U.S. Department of War contract, emphasizing that related service costs have already been recognized and anticipated revenue will benefit margins when received. Product development and channel expansion included new launches of Shield Cloud on AWS and Azure, the introduction of Shield Stratus, and expanded distribution via the P.O.S.S.E. law enforcement program through PortNexus, which reported high pilot adoption and threat mitigation results. CFO Pinson reported plans for new debt financing to support continued investment, and noted a year-end cash balance of $3.6 million. Management expects revenue from delayed government contracts will be recognized in future periods, and highlighted expanding sales leadership to increase penetration in government and commercial markets.

  • CEO Anthony Scott said the company aims for breakeven operations and would be “extremely disappointed” if not achieved by the third quarter of 2026, with growth prospects tied to additional contracts similar in size to the $3 million annual Department of War agreement.
  • Shield revenue opportunity is structurally high margin due to a software licensing model for the PortNexus school and public safety deployment, as Anthony Scott said, “for us, it's very high, yes, because we don't actually have to go do any install or anything. We just license our software, PortNexus' team is responsible for the installs.”
  • Critical infrastructure solutions are currently deployed for water assets in the Asia-Pacific region, with strong referenceability and management expectation of regional expansion opportunities linked to U.S. government interests.
  • Management described cybersecurity industry trends in generative AI as a tailwind increasing demand for proactive network monitoring solutions due to more sophisticated and scalable threats.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • P.O.S.S.E. program: A law enforcement-focused initiative launched in partnership with PortNexus, utilizing Intrusion Shield to secure public safety networks for sheriffs’ departments and schools.
  • Shield Stratus: Intrusion’s cloud-native, packet filtering cybersecurity offering integrating with AWS gateway load balancer.
  • Department of War: The U.S. government department referenced as a major Intrusion client for critical infrastructure contracts; context suggests this refers to the U.S. Department of Defense.
  • PortNexus: Partner company embedding Intrusion’s Shield technology in school safety and law enforcement solutions distributed across multiple states.
