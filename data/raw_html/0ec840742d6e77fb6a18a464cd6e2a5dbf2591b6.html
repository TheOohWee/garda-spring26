Feb. 12, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer and President — E. Jean Savage
  • Chief Financial Officer — Eric R. Marchetto
  • Director, Investor Relations — Leigh Anne Mann

TAKEAWAYS

  • EPS -- $3.14 for the full year, a 73% increase year over year.
  • Adjusted Return on Equity -- 24.4%, up 67% from prior year.
  • Revenue -- $2.2 billion for the year and $611 million for the fourth quarter, both down due to lower Rail Products deliveries.
  • Leasing and Services Revenue -- Up 5.5% year over year, driven by higher lease rates and net fleet growth.
  • Segment Operating Profit (Leasing & Services) -- Increased 53% year over year, including a $194 million noncash gain from railcar partnership restructuring.
  • Gains on Railcar Sales -- $91 million full year, with $56 million in the fourth quarter.
  • Fleet Utilization -- 97.1% at year end; renewal success rate at 73% in Q4.
  • Future Lease Rate Differential (FLRD) -- Moderated to 6%, but renewal rates were 27% higher than expiring rates.
  • Net Lease Fleet Investment -- $350 million for the year, matching the high end of guidance.
  • Rail Products Operating Margin -- 5.2% for the year, within guidance, despite a 46% drop in deliveries.
  • Headcount Rationalization -- Workforce reductions in 2025 have right-sized the company and maintained profitability.
  • Onetime Credit Loss -- A charge in the fourth quarter (within Rail Products), cutting the segment margin by 190 basis points for the quarter.
  • Railcar Partnership Restructuring -- Trinity (TRN +0.35%) took full ownership of approximately 6,235 railcars and Napier Park took ownership of about 10,850 railcars, generating a $194 million noncash gain.
  • Fleet Composition -- 101,000 wholly owned railcars on the balance sheet and 45,000 under management in investment vehicles.
  • RIV Program Fee Income -- Approximately $20 million annually, contributing scale to leasing operations.
  • Railcar Asset Value -- Book value of $6.3 billion with management estimating market value 35%-45% higher.
  • Cash Flow from Operations -- $367 million for the year.
  • Capital Returned to Shareholders -- $170 million returned in 2025 via dividends and repurchases.
  • Dividend Increase -- Quarterly dividend raised to $0.31 per share in December, marking 7 consecutive years of growth and a 9% annualized growth rate.
  • Liquidity -- $1.1 billion via cash, revolver, and warehouse capacity at year end.
  • Loan to Value Ratio -- 70.2% on the wholly owned lease fleet, affected by restructuring and TRP 2021 fleet addition.
  • 2026 Guidance: EPS -- $1.85-$2.10 anticipated for the year.
  • 2026 Guidance: Industry Deliveries -- Anticipates ~25,000 North American industry deliveries, below replacement level.
  • 2026 Guidance: Rail Products Margin -- 5%-6% expected for the year.
  • 2026 Guidance: Secondary Market Gains -- $120 million-$140 million projected.
  • 2026 Guidance: Leasing & Services Margin -- 40%-45% expected, incorporating gains and further structuring activities.
  • 2026 Guidance: Net Lease Fleet Investment -- $450 million-$550 million expected, covering new originations, fleet sales, and modifications.
  • 2026 Guidance: CapEx -- $55 million-$65 million for operating/admin purposes, focused on automation, technology, and modernization.
  • 2026 Guidance: Tax Rate -- Approximately 25%-27% expected for the year.
  • AI Initiatives -- Trinity is embedding artificial intelligence in manufacturing, logistics, and financial workflows, delivering improved working capital and productivity.
  • Three-Year Net Lease Fleet Investment Target -- $750 million-$1 billion (2024-2026); $531 million invested to date with guidance to reach the top end of the target.
  • Three-Year Cash Flow from Operations Target -- $1.2 billion-$1.4 billion (2024-2026, excluding noncash gains); $1.1 billion achieved so far, expected to exceed the range.
  • Three-Year Adjusted ROE Target -- 12%-15%; actual average for 2024-2025 was 19.5%.
  • Order Environment -- Industry orders were about 5,800 units last quarter, contributing to visibility for 2026’s 25,000 industry shipment expectation.

SUMMARY

Management introduced an EPS guidance range of $1.85 to $2.10, indicating continued confidence in earnings stability and leasing cash flow visibility. Asset sales and partnership transactions generated a $194 million noncash gain, shining a spotlight on the embedded value in Trinity's railcar fleet, which management estimates is substantially above book value. The company disclosed that the RIV (Railcar Investment Vehicle) program now comprises 45,000 managed cars and contributes about $20 million in servicing revenue annually. Leaders highlighted that AI is fully integrated into business operations, not a pilot project, with material improvements in working capital and execution predictability already realized. Guidance reflects proactive cost management, technology investment, and further simplification of the fleet structure, with the company planning to contribute remaining partially owned railcars to managed vehicles in the next quarter.

  • CEO Savage stated, "Lease rates continued to trend higher, supported by healthy demand, even as the pace of growth moderates in certain railcar categories."
  • CFO Marchetto said, "We expect the secondary market to remain active and anticipate gains of $120,000,000 to $140,000,000 in 2026."
  • During the Q&A, Savage confirmed, "We have over 200,000 railcars that are over 40 years old. And when you look at current inquiry levels, they are increased, which is encouraging."
  • Marchetto highlighted, "Our average lease rate continues to go up quarter over quarter and year over year."
  • CEO Savage reported, "headcount rationalization decisions we made in 2025 have right-sized the organization for the current reality and allow us to maintain profitability."
  • The company expects to operate with sufficient manufacturing margin despite a competitive landscape and called current industry delivery expectations "well below replacement levels but reflective of current industry backlogs."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RIV (Railcar Investment Vehicle): Special-purpose entity used for pooling, financing, and managing railcar fleets, often in partnership between Trinity and private investors, with associated fee income and asset management responsibilities.
  • FLRD (Future Lease Rate Differential): The percentage difference between lease rates on renewing contracts versus expiring contracts, indicating the direction and magnitude of lease repricing.
  • TRP 2021 Fleet: A distinct subset of Trinity's railcar assets referenced within partnership restructurings, involved in recent full-ownership transfer transactions.
