Earnings Call Transcript

Wolverine (WWW) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 26, 2026 at 11:12AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 26, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Chris Hufnagel
  • Chief Financial Officer — Taryn Miller
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Jared Filippone

TAKEAWAYS

  • Annual Revenue -- $1.874 billion, up 7% on a reported basis and 6% on a constant currency basis, with a $14 million foreign currency tailwind and a 70 basis point benefit from a 53rd week concentrated in DTC.
  • Annual Gross Margin -- 47.3%, up 300 basis points, driven by lower supply chain costs, a favorable mix shift to full price sales, and a 50 basis point benefit from tariff mitigation net of higher tariffs.
  • Annual Adjusted EPS -- $1.35, up 53% from $0.88, reflecting margin expansion and improved profitability.
  • Annual Adjusted Operating Margin -- 9%, a 170 basis point increase, due to gross margin gains outpacing investment and cost increases.
  • Net Debt and Leverage -- Net debt reduced by $81 million to $415 million, ending with bank-defined leverage at 2 times.
  • Operating Free Cash Flow -- $126 million versus a $90 million midpoint of guidance; cash flow was above target mainly due to working capital timing.
  • Share Repurchase -- $15 million repurchased in Q4 at an average price of $16.13, with $135 million remaining under authorization; no material impact on annual EPS.
  • Q4 Revenue -- $517 million, up 5% reported and 3% constant currency, exceeding the $506 million midpoint of guidance.
  • Q4 Gross Margin -- 47% (up 340 basis points year over year), 70 basis points above expectations, aided by product cost reductions, full price mix, and an 80 basis point net benefit from tariff mitigation.
  • Q4 Adjusted Operating Margin -- 11%, up 110 basis points year over year and 50 basis points above guidance, with gross margin expansion offsetting higher investments and incentive costs.
  • Q4 Adjusted EPS -- $0.45, up 13% from $0.40, above the $0.39-$0.44 outlook.
  • Merrell Annual Performance -- High single-digit growth for the year; Q4 revenue up 5% with balanced growth across regions and channels, including mid-single-digit DTC growth.
  • Merrell Market Share -- Gained share in U.S. hike; Moab Speed 2 nearly doubled sell-through at U.S. retail, and lifestyle collections like Wrapt and Jungle Moc also saw growth.
  • Merrell 2026 Outlook -- Mid-single-digit revenue growth expected, driven by new product launches, marketing investment, and expanded city strategy (London and New York added).
  • Saucony Annual Performance -- Record annual growth of 30%; Q4 revenue grew 24%, with performance up over 20% and lifestyle growing faster; DTC up mid-teens in Q4.
  • Saucony 2026 Outlook -- Low to mid-teens revenue growth targeted globally, with lifestyle segment expected to drive international gains and domestic lifestyle to contract due to a door-count reset.
  • Endorphin Azura Launch -- Introduced 25 days prior; described as “the biggest debut launch in the history of the brand,” with demand on saucony.com far ahead of forecast and exceptional early retail sell-through.
  • Sweaty Betty Annual Performance -- Q4 revenue up mid-single digits (5%); delivered four quarters of sequential improvement and strong DTC momentum in the U.K.
  • Sweaty Betty 2026 Outlook -- Revenue expected to decline low single digits, with U.K. and EMEA DTC growth offset by the absence of the 53rd week and continued U.S. business reset.
  • Wolverine Brand Performance -- Q4 revenue down approximately 11%, but retail sell-through trends improved and work boot market share increased; namesake brand expected to deliver flat revenue in 2026.
  • Active and Work Groups in Q4 -- Active Group revenue up 10% (ahead of high single-digit guidance); Work Group revenue down 12% (slightly better than expectations).
  • 2026 Revenue Guidance -- $1.96 billion-$1.985 billion (reported growth of 5.2% at the midpoint), with foreign currency a $14 million benefit and 53rd week absence representing a 70 basis point headwind.
  • 2026 Segment Guidance -- Active Group up mid-single digits; Work Group flat; Merrell up mid-single digits; Saucony up low to mid-teens; Sweaty Betty down low single digits; Wolverine brand flat, with H2 improvement anticipated.
  • 2026 Gross Margin Guidance -- Approximately 46%, down 130 basis points, driven by $60 million in higher tariffs (300 basis point unmitigated impact), partially offset by pricing, full price mix, and cost savings.
  • 2026 Adjusted Operating Margin Guidance -- 9.1%, up 10 basis points, with gross margin decline offset by revenue growth leverage and cost discipline.
  • 2026 Adjusted EPS Guidance -- $1.35-$1.50; interest and other expenses guided to $23 million (down from $28 million), and effective tax rate projected at 18%.
  • 2026 Operating Free Cash Flow Guidance -- $105 million-$120 million, with $20 million planned capital expenditures.
  • Q1 2026 Guidance -- Revenue $445 million-$450 million (8.5% reported growth at midpoint, 5.1% constant currency), gross margin 47.5% (down 10 basis points), and adjusted EPS $0.20-$0.22 versus $0.19.
  • Tariffs in 2026 -- Entire $60 million unmitigated impact assumed through year-end; an announced but not implemented 15% tariff rate could reduce tariff costs by $5 million-$7 million if enacted for all of 2026.
  • Inventory and Accounting -- FIFO accounting change implemented in 3Q 2025, with a $1.4 million benefit to Q4 COGS; impact already included in previous guidance.

RISKS

  • Tariff Exposure -- Taryn Miller said, "Our 2026 guidance reflects the continuation of the tariff rates that went into effect in August 2025. Based on that assumption, we now estimate the full year unmitigated impact from higher tariffs to be approximately $60 million, or an incremental $50 million versus 2025."
  • Sweaty Betty U.S. Headwinds -- Chris Hufnagel said, "remains a near-term headwind as the brand establishes a healthier foundation for future growth."
  • Gross Margin Pressure -- Gross margin guidance for 2026 is down 130 basis points due to higher tariff costs, only partially offset by mitigation actions.

SUMMARY

Management delivered year-end revenue and adjusted EPS above guidance midpoints, led by broad-based strength in Merrell and Saucony, which each captured share in key categories. The company reduced net debt, improved leverage, executed a $15 million share buyback, and aligned cash generation with strategic investments. Full-year 2026 guidance projects continued low- to mid-single-digit growth, solid international and DTC expansion for core brands, and significant expense leverage even as tariffs compress gross margin. The change to FIFO inventory accounting boosted reported margins in Q4 but was fully factored into prior guidance and will not skew future comparisons.

  • Saucony saw record annual and quarterly growth, with the Endorphin Azura launch described as outperforming internal forecasts and marking a new high-water mark for new product introduction velocity.
  • While Merrell’s growth was geographically balanced, management signaled that upcoming city strategy expansions are expected to reinforce the sales trajectory beyond North America.
  • Sweaty Betty’s distribution repositioning and U.S. retrenchment were cited as negatives for short-term revenue but are expected to yield a premium DTC foundation in future periods.
  • Management stated its mitigation strategy against tariffs remains unchanged, reflecting actions like price increases, disciplined promotional activity, and product cost savings already underway.
  • Wholesale and DTC growth are both expected to contribute meaningfully in 2026, though the absence of a 53rd week will be a revenue headwind concentrated in the DTC channel’s Q4 comparison.
  • The company confirmed no material acceleration in inventory buying is planned in response to tariff developments, due to ongoing policy uncertainty.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • 53rd Week: An extra week included in fiscal reporting, impacting year-over-year comparisons and channel mix, notably within DTC results.
  • Tier Zero: Highly curated, influential retail accounts targeted for brand-defining, trend-setting releases and collaborations.
  • FIFO Accounting: Inventory accounting method ("First-In, First-Out") where the oldest inventory costs are recognized first in cost of goods sold.
  • Active Group/Work Group: Wolverine’s reporting segments, with Active focused on performance and lifestyle brands and Work covering industrial/work brands including Wolverine.
