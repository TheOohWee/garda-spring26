TAKEAWAYS

  • Total GAAP Revenue -- $5 billion for the quarter, up 37% year-on-year, and $16.8 billion for the year, up 29% year-on-year, driven by e-commerce GMV and digital financial services growth.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $591 million for the quarter versus $127 million last year, and $2 billion for the year versus $1.2 billion, reflecting improved profitability.
  • Net Income -- $238 million in the quarter, a reversal from a $112 million net loss in the prior year's quarter; $448 million for the year, up from $163 million last year.
  • Shopee GMV -- Surpassed $100 billion for the year, with fourth-quarter GMV of $28.6 billion, up 23% year-on-year.
  • Shopee Orders -- Over 10 billion for the year; fourth-quarter orders grew 20% year-on-year to 3 billion.
  • Shopee adjusted EBITDA -- $152 million for the quarter compared to a $225 million adjusted EBITDA loss prior year; $156 million positive for the year compared to a $214 million adjusted EBITDA loss prior year.
  • E-commerce Marketplace Revenue -- $3.2 billion for the quarter, up 41% year-on-year; full year at $9 billion, up 38% year-on-year.
  • E-commerce Value-added Services Revenue -- $0.8 billion for the quarter, up 21% year-on-year, stemming from logistics-related services.
  • Advertising Revenue and Take Rate -- Advertising revenue grew more than 50% year-on-year in the fourth quarter, with ad take rate improving by over 50 basis points relative to last year.
  • Live Streaming in Southeast Asia -- Accounted for 15% of Shopee's physical goods order volume; average daily unique streamers grew over 40%, and viewers over 30%, year-on-year in the fourth quarter.
  • Shopee Express Logistics -- Delivered nearly half of Asian SPX Express orders within 2 days during the first quarter; overall logistics cost per order reduced by $0.05 year-on-year in the fourth quarter.
  • AI-driven Efficiency -- AI-enabled chatbot handled 80% of customer queries, reducing customer service cost per contact by nearly 30% year-on-year, and accelerated resolution of buyer returns in Asia by over 40% year-on-year.
  • SeaMoney Revenue -- $733 million in the quarter, up 55% year-on-year, and $2.4 billion for the year, up 35% year-on-year.
  • SeaMoney Adjusted EBITDA -- $211 million in the fourth quarter, up 42% year-on-year; $712 million for the year, up 29% year-on-year.
  • Loan Book Size -- Consumer and SME loans outstanding reached $5.1 billion at year-end, up 64% year-on-year, with $4.2 billion on-book and $0.9 billion off-book loans.
  • Active Borrowers -- SeaMoney reported over 26 million active borrowers and added 5 million first-time borrowers in the fourth quarter.
  • Nonperforming Loan Ratio -- 90-day NPL held at 1.2% at quarter-end, indicating stable risk exposure.
  • Digital Entertainment Bookings -- $543 million in the quarter, up 19% year-on-year, and $2.1 billion for the year, up 19% year-on-year.
  • Free Fire Daily Active Users -- Surpassed 100 million average DAU, up 28% year-on-year, maintaining its status as the world's largest mobile game by DAU and downloads.
  • Free Fire Bookings Growth -- Annual bookings grew 34% year-on-year, with Africa cited as a rapid growth region (e.g. Nigeria user base up 90% year-on-year in December).
  • Guidance: Shopee GMV -- Management expects 20% GMV growth and margin improvement for 2025, assuming current FX rates.
  • Guidance: Loan Book -- Management expects loan book growth to "meaningfully" outpace Shopee GMV growth in 2025.
  • Guidance: Garena -- Management expects double-digit year-on-year growth in both user base and bookings for 2025.

SUMMARY

Sea Limited (SE +0.54%) reported record top- and bottom-line performance, converting rapid revenue acceleration across all segments into sharply higher profitability. The company explicitly credited direct operational improvements—particularly AI adoption, logistics efficiency, and expanded content features—for driving both margin expansion and customer engagement. Strategic guidance anticipates Shopee GMV growth of around 20% in 2025, loan book growth for SeaMoney to meaningfully outpace Shopee GMV growth, and Garena to achieve double-digit growth in both user base and bookings. Management presented results as validating the business model's structural strengths and self-sufficiency, positioning Sea Limited to potentially accelerate market share and user penetration in 2025.

  • SeaMoney's credit adoption is accelerating in both on- and off-platform scenarios, with management highlighting expanding off-Shopee loans as about half of the loan book in Asia.
  • AI-driven automations are cited as resulting in cost savings—for example, average customer service resolution times fell by more than 40% and the company reduced its service cost per contact nearly 30% year-on-year, supporting scalable profitability.
  • Logistics improvements, such as reductions in per-order costs and progress in same-day delivery (noted in Indonesia, Thailand, Malaysia, and Vietnam), are directly linked to higher user retention and increased order frequency, according to management.
  • Partnerships, such as YouTube collaborations in Southeast Asia, are explicitly attracting new buyers and increasing average daily orders, with Indonesia's YouTube-attributed orders up sixfold since program launch.
  • In Brazil, advances in digital lending penetration and fulfillment services are attributed to rising average order value and sustained Shopee profitability, while management notes a "CapEx-light" and rental-based fulfillment deployment strategy.
  • Management stated, "we are actively reviewing all the possibilities" for potential uses of growing cash reserves, including dividends, share buybacks, acquisitions, and further AI investments.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • DAU (Daily Active Users): Number of unique users interacting with a digital platform or service on a daily basis, indicating user engagement.
  • GMV (Gross Merchandise Value): The total value of merchandise sold through a marketplace within a given period, reflecting overall trading volume.
  • NPL (Non-Performing Loans): Loans in which payments are overdue by a specified period—here, more than 90 days—serving as a measure of credit risk.
  • SPayLater: SeaMoney’s proprietary "buy now, pay later" service, enabling installment purchases within Shopee and, increasingly, off-platform transactions.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted to exclude certain non-recurring or non-core expenses or income.
  • Take Rate: The percentage of GMV retained by a marketplace as revenue, encompassing commissions and advertising fees.
