Regeneron (REGN) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 28, 2025 at 4:17PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Oct. 28, 2025, 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

Board Co-Chair, Co-Founder, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Dr. Leonard Schleifer

Board Co-Chair, Co-Founder, President, and Chief Scientific Officer — Dr. George Yancopoulos

Executive Vice President, Commercial — Marion McCourt

Takeaways

Total revenues -- $3.8 billion (non-GAAP) for fiscal Q3 2025, up 1%, reflecting higher Dupixent and Libtayo sales and EYLEA HD in the U.S., partially offset by lower U.S. EYLEA sales and Bayer collaboration revenue.

Diluted net income per share -- $11.83 (non-GAAP) for fiscal Q3 2025 with net income of $1.3 billion.

Dupixent global net sales -- $4.9 billion for fiscal Q3 2025, increasing 26% on a constant currency basis, with U.S. net sales rising 28% to $3.6 billion.

Libtayo global net sales -- $365 million for fiscal Q3 2025, up 24% on a constant currency basis; U.S. net sales up 12% to $219 million.

EYLEA HD U.S. net sales -- $431 million for fiscal Q3 2025, up 10% quarter-over-quarter; unit demand increased 18% quarter-over-quarter, partially offset by lower net price.

EYLEA U.S. net sales -- $681 million for fiscal Q3 2025, down 10% quarter-over-quarter, reflecting unit demand declines during conversion to EYLEA HD and ongoing pricing pressure.

Bayer collaboration revenue -- $345 million (non-GAAP) for fiscal Q3 2025, including $312 million from share of net profits outside the U.S. (non-GAAP); EYLEA and EYLEA 8 mg sales outside the U.S. were $854 million (non-GAAP), including $232 million for EYLEA 8 mg.

Sanofi collaboration revenue -- $1.6 billion (non-GAAP) Sanofi collaboration revenue for fiscal Q3 2025, including $1.5 billion from profit share (non-GAAP), with a 34% year-over-year increase in Regeneron’s share of profits (non-GAAP).

Gross margin -- 86% on net product sales in fiscal Q3 2025, lower than the prior year due to product mix and higher manufacturing investments.

R&D expense -- $1.3 billion (non-GAAP) for fiscal Q3 2025, representing ongoing late-stage pipeline investments, with an expectation of a mid-teens percentage increase in 2026 R&D spending.

SG&A expense -- $541 million (non-GAAP) for fiscal Q3 2025, down 12% compared to the prior year, primarily due to lower charitable contributions.

Free cash flow -- $3.2 billion in free cash flow (non-GAAP) for the first nine months of 2025, with $16 billion in cash and marketable securities net of debt at quarter end.

Share repurchases -- $2.8 billion in the first nine months of 2025, the highest allocation for open market repurchases in company history; the full-year 2025 total is expected to reach $4 billion through dividends and repurchases.

EYLEA HD market share -- Represents approximately 40% of Regeneron’s U.S. retina franchise as of fiscal Q3 2025, with continued anticipation of sequential demand growth in the moderate to high single digits for the upcoming quarter before potential label enhancements.

Libtayo FDA approval -- Recently approved in high-risk adjuvant cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma, positioned as the only PD-1 antibody for this use, with an estimated 10,000 U.S. patients eligible.

Catalent facility regulatory update -- FDA classified Catalent Indiana as "official action indicated" (OAI), resulting in a complete response letter for the EYLEA HD prefilled syringe sBLA, with the only approvability issue being unresolved inspection findings.

EYLEA HD manufacturing plans -- Regeneron plans to submit an alternate prefilled syringe filler by January 2026; an application for an alternate vial filler is already submitted, with a PDUFA date in late December.

Regeneron U.S. manufacturing investment -- Over $7 billion in manufacturing and infrastructure planned for New York and North Carolina in coming years.

Pipeline progress -- Positive Phase III or registration-enabling data announced for six programs in immunology, neurology, allergy, and rare diseases, with multiple pivotal programs in hematology/oncology, obesity, thrombosis, and allergy set to expand in the coming months.

Lynozyfic approval and launch -- U.S. and EU approval for relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma, with early commercial progress, and broad registrational programs set for earlier disease lines and precursor settings.

Cemdisiran Phase III data -- Positive results for generalized myasthenia gravis, showing statistically significant improvement in the MG-ADL score; regulatory submission planned for early 2026.

Sanofi development balance -- Reduced to $900 million by quarter-end, expected to be fully repaid by the end of fiscal Q3 2026.

Summary

Regeneron Pharmaceuticals (REGN 2.47%) reported steady revenue growth driven by Dupixent, Libtayo, and EYLEA HD, with market performance and product expansions contributing to financial and operational results. Management outlined steps to address EYLEA HD manufacturing challenges, including regulatory setbacks related to Catalent's facility, and provided timelines for alternate filler submissions and FDA actions. The company highlighted R&D pipeline momentum, including positive pivotal data in multiple indications and plans for an increase in R&D investment next year to support expansion into new markets such as hematology and oncology.

Schleifer stated, "our priorities are closely aligned" with the U.S. government's goals to lower drug costs and enhance U.S. domestic manufacturing capacity.

CFO Fenimore disclosed that Regeneron has repurchased approximately $2.8 billion of its shares, the most ever allocated to open market repurchases in any full fiscal year in company history, through the first nine months of 2025.

FDA's classification of Catalent Indiana as "official action indicated" has delayed approval for EYLEA HD's prefilled syringe, with Schleifer confirming, "the sole approvability issue relating to unresolved inspection findings at Catalent."

Regeneron's ongoing pipeline investments yielded positive Phase III data across six distinct programs, with Yancopoulos highlighting the breadth of milestones ahead, including new clinical studies in obesity and hematologic cancers.

Lynozyfic supports rapid expansion to earlier disease settings, according to Yancopoulos.

Fenimore indicated a "mid-teens percentage increase in R&D expense in 2026 relative to 2025" to fuel advancement of pivotal trials.

Industry glossary

VEGF: Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor; target protein for therapies treating retinal vascular diseases like macular degeneration.

sBLA: Supplemental Biologics License Application; regulatory submission to expand indications or modify labeling for approved biologics.

PDUFA date: Prescription Drug User Fee Act date; statutory deadline by which the FDA must act on a drug application.

REMS: Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy; regulatory requirement to manage serious medication risks.

MG-ADL: Myasthenia Gravis–Activities of Daily Living scale; validated questionnaire to assess symptom severity in myasthenia gravis.

PNH: Paroxysmal Nocturnal Hemoglobinuria; rare blood disorder targeted by complement inhibitors in Regeneron's pipeline.

FcRn: Neonatal Fc Receptor; target for therapies modulating immune response, referenced in myasthenia gravis treatment discussion.
