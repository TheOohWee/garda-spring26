AngioDynamics (ANGO) Q3 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 6, 2026 at 12:22PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, April 2, 2025 at 8:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Jim Clemmer
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Stephen Trowbridge

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $72 million in AngioDynamics (ANGO 2.77%) fiscal 2025 third quarter, up 9.2% year over year, driven by both MedTech and U.S. Med Device platforms.
  • MedTech revenue -- $31.3 million in fiscal 2025 third quarter, growing 22.2% year over year, comprising 44% of total revenue compared to 39% a year ago.
  • Med Device revenue -- $40.7 million in fiscal 2025 third quarter, increasing 0.9% year over year, marking a return to growth for the segment.
  • Auryon platform revenue -- $13.9 million, up 17.3% year over year; Auryon has achieved double-digit year-over-year growth for 15 consecutive quarters post-launch.
  • Mechanical thrombectomy revenue -- Up 46.7% year over year, including AlphaVac and AngioVac products.
  • AlphaVac revenue -- $3 million, up 161.4% year over year and 20% sequentially from fiscal Q2 2025, highlighting accelerating adoption for pulmonary embolism treatment.
  • AngioVac revenue -- $6.8 million, up 23.1% year over year; Trowbridge said, "I think this level kind of mid-6s and seeing that as a growth platform moving forward is the right way to think about AngioVac going forward."
  • NanoKnife revenue -- $6.3 million, increasing 5.3%, with disposable revenue up 16.2% and capital sales decreasing 21.6% in the quarter.
  • Gross margin -- 54%, up 290 basis points year over year and surpassing internal expectations.
  • MedTech gross margin -- 62.5%, a 100 basis point increase, led by higher AngioVac revenue and greater Auryon penetration in hospitals.
  • Med Device gross margin -- 47.4%.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $1.3 million, compared to a loss of $3.6 million in the prior-year quarter.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Adjusted loss of $0.08 per share, an improvement from an adjusted loss of $0.16 per share in the prior-year quarter.
  • Cash position -- $44.8 million in cash and cash equivalents as of February 28, 2025.
  • Cash usage -- $13.2 million used in operating activities in the quarter, $1.8 million in capital expenditures, and $1.4 million for Auryon unit placements and evaluations.
  • Net cash change -- Approximately $10 million used, attributed mainly to BD Bard settlement payments and working capital for the Spectrum Vascular manufacturing transition.
  • Revolver facility -- Secured commitment for a $25 million revolving line of credit from J.P. Morgan, expected to close within weeks, described as "prudent financial housekeeping."
  • Free cash flow outlook -- Management affirmed the target for positive cash flow for the full fiscal year 2026.
  • Updated fiscal 2025 guidance -- Revenue expected between $285 million and $288 million, up 5.3%-6.4% year over year; MedTech net sales growth of 14%-16% (previously 12%-15%); gross margin of 53%-54% (up from 52%-53% prior); adjusted EBITDA of $4 million-$5 million (prior $1 million-$3 million); adjusted loss per share of $0.31-$0.34 (improved from $0.34-$0.38).
  • NanoKnife regulatory and reimbursement milestone -- FDA expanded prostate indication received in December 2024; CPT 1 code effective January 1, 2026, with initial reimbursement readout expected summer 2025; CEO Clemmer cited, "It is important to highlight that the expanded indication was contemplated in our guidance for fiscal 2025 and that we don't expect to see a material inflection in a projected performance wherein the adoption and utilization until our recently approved CPT 1 code goes into effect and payers adopt coverage."
  • R&D spending -- $6.9 million, representing 9.6% of sales, down from $8.1 million, or 12.2% of sales, mainly due to completion of PRESERVE and APEX trials.
  • SG&A expense -- $36 million, representing 50% of sales, versus $34.2 million, or 51.9% previously.
  • Macro/tariff exposure -- Trowbridge stated, "we do not believe today that tariffs will materially impact our business," citing U.S.-centric manufacturing and supply base.
  • Med Device segment strategic execution -- Management reported successful commercial return to growth after previous declines.
  • Clinical data initiatives -- AMBITION BTK trial and registry for Auryon launched in January 2025, targeting clinical outcomes in patients with critical limb ischemia below the knee.
  • AlphaVac/AngioVac commercial expansion -- CEO Clemmer said, "you're going to see over this course of this calendar year expansion in that AlphaVac, AngioVac sales force."
  • Auryon international launch -- Fiscal Q4 is expected to see initial EU revenues following CE marking; sequential growth anticipated but no immediate material revenue impact.

Summary

Management raised fiscal 2025 guidance for revenue, MedTech segment growth, gross margin, adjusted EBITDA, and adjusted loss per share based on better-than-expected execution and profitability. The company confirmed sequential adoption growth in AlphaVac and AngioVac product lines, with commercial expansion planned throughout 2025. Plans for broader NanoKnife prostate adoption hinge on the permanent CPT 1 code effective January 2026, with the current quarter showing prostate as the top procedure for NanoKnife for the first time. The cash position remained robust despite outflows tied to legal settlements and transition manufacturing, with a $25 million revolving credit facility secured for added liquidity. AngioDynamics highlighted imminent clinical trial milestones, including the AMBITION BTK study, designed to validate Auryon's use for below-the-knee applications.

  • Trowbridge indicated MedTech now comprises a higher portion of total revenue, reflecting a strategic intent to shift the mix toward higher-margin platforms.
  • The European market for Auryon is not expected to be as large as the U.S., tempering near-term revenue expectations from international regions despite the product launch.
  • Management stated integration of AngioVac and AlphaVac is gaining commercial traction and facilitating cross-selling within target hospital accounts.
  • R&D expenditures are set to normalize at approximately 10% of sales moving forward in support of long-term MedTech growth.
  • CEO Clemmer noted that transformation of the supply chain and shifting cost base are anticipated to contribute meaningfully to profitability over the next two fiscal years.

Industry glossary

  • AlphaVac: AngioDynamics' mechanical thrombectomy device for treating blood clots, including pulmonary embolism.
  • AngioVac: A medical device used to remove blood clots or unwanted material from the vasculature.
  • Auryon: Atherectomy laser platform targeting peripheral artery disease including below-the-knee lesions.
  • MedTech segment: AngioDynamics' business unit focused on advanced medical technologies delivering higher-margin growth, as distinct from traditional medical device sales.
  • PRESERVE study: Clinical trial assessing NanoKnife's efficacy and quality of life outcomes in prostate cancer ablation.
  • APEX-AV trial: Clinical study evaluating AlphaVac F1885 System for treating pulmonary embolism.
  • BTK (below the knee): An anatomical reference for medical device usage in lower limb arterial procedures.
  • CPT 1 code: Category I Current Procedural Terminology code enabling reimbursement for specific medical procedures after payer adoption.
