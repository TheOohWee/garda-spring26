Earnings Call Transcript

Western Union (WU) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Aug 6, 2025 at 6:30PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Monday, July 28, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Devin McGranahan
  • Chief Financial Officer — Matt Cagwin

Risks

  • Management cited ongoing "headwinds associated with the current geopolitical environment" and explicit market softness in U.S. Outbound corridors, with transactional activity down in both retail and digital channels due to increased immigration enforcement and policy uncertainty, particularly affecting The Americas.
  • Adjusted revenue (excluding Iraq) declined 1% year over year in fiscal Q2 2025 (period ended June 30, 2025), with adjusted operating margins pressured by lower contributions from Iraq and higher consumer fraud losses, despite cost savings and favorable currency impact.
  • Branded digital revenue growth from U.S. to Latin America, especially to Mexico, slowed in fiscal Q2 2025, with management reporting aggregate volume declines and no material shift from retail to digital channels.
  • Growth rates in the V Go business in North America turned negative after prior double-digit gains in fiscal Q2 2025, which management directly linked to tightening immigration policy and channel disruption.

Takeaways

  • GAAP revenue: $1.0 billion for fiscal Q2 2025, with adjusted revenue of $1.0 billion. Adjusted revenue (excluding Iraq) was down 1% year over year.
  • Adjusted operating margin: 19% in fiscal Q2 2025, unchanged from the prior-year period, with offsetting effects from lower Iraq profits, higher fraud losses, cost savings, and foreign currency benefits.
  • Adjusted earnings per share (EPS): $0.42 in fiscal Q2 2025, down from $0.44 in fiscal Q2 2024, with the decline attributed to lower Iraq-related profits and higher interest expense, partially offset by a reduced share count. This relates to adjusted (non-GAAP) results for fiscal Q2 2025.
  • Consumer-to-consumer transactions: Down 3%, or 2% excluding Iraq, driven by lower volumes from North America and U.S. Outbound corridors amid immigration-related disruptions.
  • Average principal per transaction (PPT): Rose more than 5% year over year on a constant currency basis. There were fewer transactions but a higher average principal per transaction.
  • Branded digital business: Branded digital business transactions increased 9%, and adjusted revenue grew 6%. This growth marked the seventh straight quarter of mid-single-digit or higher adjusted revenue gains for the branded digital business.
  • Payout-to-account transactions: Nearly 30% growth in the quarter. Now accounting for almost 40% of branded digital transactions; management highlighted this as supporting higher margins and improved retention.
  • Consumer services revenue: Contributed 14% of total quarterly revenue. Segment adjusted revenue grew 41%, driven by the Travel Money business and EuroChange acquisition (EuroChange contributed approximately 2% to revenue growth).
  • Retail Europe performance: Spain and the United Kingdom delivered revenue growth exceeding 15%. UK Post contract shifted to exclusive status.
  • Cash flow from operations: $148 million year to date, up from $60 million in the prior-year period, with the final transition tax payment completed last quarter.
  • Capital expenditures: $54 million year to date, 15% lower than the prior year.
  • Balance sheet: $1.0 billion in cash and equivalents, $2.7 billion in debt. Leverage ratios of 2.8x gross and 1.8x net.
  • Capital returned to shareholders: Over $150 million in the quarter and $300 million in the first six months of 2025, exceeding 10% of current market cap in the first half of 2025 and annualizing to a 20% cash-on-cash return at current prices.
  • 2025 outlook: Adjusted revenue guidance is $4.0 billion-$4.1 billion. Operating margin is 19%-21%, and adjusted EPS is $1.65-$1.75, based on current trend assumptions.
  • Remittance tax exposure: Newly enacted U.S. 1% remittance tax—effective 2026—primarily affects retail cash transactions, with digital and card-based retail transactions generally excluded. Management estimates less than 20% of revenue exposed, potentially as low as 15% as debit adoption increases, based on management commentary during the earnings call.
  • AI implementation: Management reported over 50% reduction in customer service handle times where AI-powered customer service representative assistants are active, and over 90% sampling of service calls via AI in the most recent period, with ongoing use in treasury and engineering functions to improve efficiency and capital allocation.
  • Stablecoin initiatives: The company is piloting on-chain treasury settlement rails and exploring on/off-ramp solutions to facilitate fiat-to-crypto transfers, with pilots underway in South America and Africa.
  • Operational efficiency program: $40 million in year-to-date cost savings for the first half of the year. Cumulative savings of more than $150 million, completing the two-year program ahead of schedule.

Summary

Western Union's (WU 1.33%) results showed declining top-line metrics attributed to macro and policy-driven slowdowns in key U.S. and Latin American corridors, with executives emphasizing resilience through diversification and margin stability. Management reported progress in digital adoption, payout-to-account growth, and accelerated efficiency measures, positioning the company to absorb regulatory and operational shocks with balance sheet strength and shareholder returns. Near-term guidance assumes modest improvement rather than further deterioration in retail performance, while structural headwinds from immigration and regulatory pressures remain closely monitored by leadership seeking to offset risks with digital and consumer services expansion.

  • Devin McGranahan stated, "we are optimistic about the potential of our business" despite acknowledging that North America retail faces persistent challenges and that recent policy changes have suppressed transaction growth in major corridors.
  • Matt Cagwin confirmed, "There's not a massive amount of improvement but there's not a deterioration further in in the guide," clarifying that full-year projections depend on achieving at least modest operational progress in core retail businesses.
  • Stablecoin and AI pilot programs are specifically positioned to reduce dependencies on legacy banking infrastructure and drive future cost savings, with management reporting active engagement from infrastructure partners and technology providers globally.
  • New remittance tax laws in the U.S. are seen by management as a catalyst for digital wallet adoption and cost-reducing payment mix shifts, with minimal anticipated incremental cost for compliance due to already planned technology rollouts.
  • Negotiations for key retail and postal partnerships resulted in renewals -- 12 out of 15 major U.S. deals secured -- and new exclusive agreements in Europe, supporting regional resilience and mitigating the risk of further volume loss.

Industry glossary

  • CMT (Consumer Money Transfer): The segment focused on consumer-to-consumer cross-border and domestic money transfers via retail and digital channels.
  • EuroChange: A European travel money provider acquired by Western Union, materially contributing to consumer services revenue growth.
  • PPT (Principal Per Transaction): The average dollar amount sent per transaction, commonly used to measure remittance value trends.
  • Payout-to-account: Money transfer transactions directed to recipients' bank or mobile accounts rather than cash pickup.
  • V Go: Western Union's U.S. retail and digital wallet platform offering hybrid money transfer and digital financial services.
  • WUPOS: Western Union's proprietary point-of-sale transaction platform used at retail and agent locations.
