Crane (CR) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 4, 2025 at 7:08PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, July 29, 2025 at 2:00 p.m. ET

Call participants

President and Chief Executive Officer — Max H. Mitchell

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

Adjusted EPS— Adjusted EPS was $1.49 for Q2 2025, reflecting 6.5% core sales growth, driven primarily by the Aerospace & Electronics and Process Flow Technologies segments.

Core orders— Core orders increased by nearly 20%, mainly due to strength in Aerospace & Electronics.

Full-year adjusted EPS guidance raised— The full-year adjusted EPS outlook has been raised to a range of $5.50-$5.80, up from the previous range of $5.30-$5.60.

Pending acquisition— Agreement announced to acquire PSI (Precision Sensors and Instrumentation) from Baker Hughes, including Druck, Panametrics, and Reuter Stokes, with the acquisition anticipated to close January 1.

Aerospace & Electronics segment revenue— $258 million, up 12%, driven by broad-based commercial and military demand; nearly all growth was organic.

Record Aerospace & Electronics backlog— Backlog above $1 billion, up 29% year-over-year and 9% sequentially, with strength in both commercial and defense orders for 2026 and 2027.

Aftermarket & OEM sales growth— Aerospace & Electronics aftermarket sales rose 18%, with commercial aftermarket up 9%, military aftermarket up 37%, OEM up 9%, and military OEM up 9%.

Process Flow Technologies revenue— $319 million, a 7% increase, including 3% core sales growth, a 3% contribution from recent acquisitions, and 1% from favorable currency effects.

Process Flow Technologies segment orders— Core FX-neutral orders increased 4% year-over-year but decreased 1% sequentially from Q1 2025; backlog decreased 4% year-over-year.

Process Flow Technologies margin— Adjusted operating margin of 20.7%, up 20 basis points, with core operating leverage at 35%, the high end of the targeted range.

Tariff exposure— Tariff-related costs for 2025 are forecast at roughly $30 million, down from $60 million guided last quarter, largely due to reduced China-related tariffs.

Net cash position and leverage— Pro forma leverage after the PSI transaction expected at roughly 1 time net debt to EBITDA, below the company's 2 times-3 times targeted range.

Segment growth outlooks— Aerospace & Electronics core sales growth for 2025 now projected at high single digits to low double digits, with 35%-40% expected leverage; Process Flow Technologies core growth anticipated at the lower end with 30%-35% volume leverage.

M&A pipeline— The company is actively pursuing additional acquisitions, with deal sizes ranging from under $100 million to $1 billion, and a “robust funnel” across both core segments.

GTF program— GTF aftermarket sales accounted for less than 5% of total commercial aftermarket and are growing approximately 15% in 2025, expected to accelerate to 30% growth in 2026.

Summary

Crane(CR +0.95%) delivered solid core sales and margin improvements, supported by robust demand in Aerospace & Electronics, resulting in a raised full-year adjusted EPS outlook to a range of $5.50-$5.80. Management emphasized the strategic importance of the pending PSI acquisition, which will expand proprietary sensing technologies and enhance resilience in key industrial and aerospace markets through Druck, Panametrics, and Reuter Stokes. The company maintains a strong balance sheet and proactive capital allocation, underscored by a rich pipeline of acquisition opportunities spanning both Process Flow Technologies and Aerospace & Electronics.

CFO Richard A. Maue explained the accounting reclassification of $9 million in business interruption insurance recoveries related to Hurricane Helene, moving it from operating to non-operating income for clearer reporting.

COO Alejandro A. Alcala described structural changes granting the Aerospace & Electronics segment greater capacity for future M&A and organic initiatives, following the established Process Flow Technologies model.

In Process Flow Technologies, record cryogenics backlog, strong biopharma and space launch demand, and successful acquisitions were cited as contributors to growth, despite softness in chemicals and regional CapEx delays.

Pricing and productivity are expected to fully offset remaining tariff cost increases, with copper-related tariff exposure noted as minimal (less than $1 million) in 2025.

Reuter Stokes positions Crane to capitalize on nuclear market growth, including traditional, restart, and small modular reactor opportunities, with additional potential in homeland security and medical segments.

Management cited order strength in both commercial and defense programs, including air defense and communication platforms, and noted that large orders are phased and support multi-year revenue visibility.

Industry glossary

PSI: Precision Sensors and Instrumentation, comprising the Druck, Panametrics, and Reuter Stokes brands acquired from Baker Hughes, focused on high-reliability sensing technologies for industrial and aerospace markets.

GTF: Geared Turbofan, a type of aircraft engine platform relevant to aftermarket sales growth.

Core FX-neutral: Sales, orders, or backlog figures adjusted to exclude the impact of foreign exchange rate changes, reflecting true underlying performance.

OEM: Original Equipment Manufacturer, referencing initial equipment sales to aircraft or industrial platforms as distinct from aftermarket sales.

Aftermarket: Sales involving replacement parts, maintenance, and upgrades provided after original equipment delivery, critical for recurring segment revenue.

Cryogenics: Engineering and manufacturing related to extremely low-temperature processes and products, such as those needed for space launch platforms and LNG storage.
