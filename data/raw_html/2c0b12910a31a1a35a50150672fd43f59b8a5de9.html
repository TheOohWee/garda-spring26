Takeaways

  • Core EPS -- Core earnings per share reached $1.36 for Q3 2025, up 64% year-over-year, with management highlighting this as a record result.
  • Trailing Twelve-Month Core ROE -- Trailing twelve-month core return on equity was 13.8%.
  • Total Revenues -- Quarterly revenues climbed 6% in Q3 2025.
  • Supplemental and Group Benefits Sales -- Individual supplemental sales increased 40%, with Group Benefits reporting record sales; all business lines posted higher sales.
  • Full-Year 2025 Core EPS Guidance -- Full-year 2025 core EPS guidance was raised to a range of $4.5-$4.7, assuming $65 million catastrophe losses and $473-$477 million in total net investment income guidance.
  • Property & Casualty Profitability -- Property & Casualty combined ratio was 91.4% year-to-date Q3 2025; auto combined ratio of 96.4% year-to-date, property combined ratio was 83.1% year-to-date.
  • Pretax Catastrophe Losses -- Year-to-date pretax catastrophe losses fell to $56 million from $91 million.
  • Life and Retirement Earnings -- Core earnings for this segment held steady at $15 million for Q3 2025, with net written premiums and contract deposits rising to $170 million.
  • Life and Retirement Sales Growth -- Life sales grew 16%, while retirement deposits increased 9%.
  • Supplemental Benefits Utilization and Ratio -- Policyholder utilization stayed below historical norms; The blended benefits ratio averaged 37% year-to-date versus a 39% long-term target.
  • Property Net Written Premiums -- Increased 20% to $99 million in Q3 2025, with property combined ratio improving to 75.3 and policyholder retention near 89%.
  • Shareholder Capital Returns -- $20 million repurchased in shares through October and $43 million paid in dividends through October; $57 million remains authorized for repurchases as of October 2025.
  • Debt Issuance -- $300 million in senior notes due 2030 issued at a 4.7% coupon in September 2025, over five times oversubscribed.
  • Expense Management Initiatives -- Termination of a legacy dormant pension plan is expected to yield over $1 million in annualized pretax run-rate savings, with the transaction expected to be finalized in the fourth quarter.
  • Expense Ratio Target -- Management reiterated intent to lower the expense ratio by 1.5 points over the next three years.
  • GenAI Adoption -- GenAI deployment to automate call notes in customer care, reducing representative administrative time by 20% to 30% daily, is expected to deliver productivity gains.
  • Strategic Partnerships -- New partnerships with Teach For America and Grand Canyon University expand access to educators and support targeted financial wellness initiatives.
  • Growth in Distribution Network -- The benefit specialists network expanded nearly 30% over the past year.

Summary

Horace Mann Educators (HMN 1.78%) management emphasized that each business segment is performing at or above targeted profitability metrics, providing room to accelerate scale-building investments while maintaining discipline on expenses. The updated full-year 2025 core EPS guidance range of $4.5-$4.7 factors in both strategic investments and significant non-recurring expense items, including a planned $3-$7 million foundation donation. Management clarified that lower catastrophe losses contributed to the profitability metrics for 2025 but do not prompt expectations for materially lower future catastrophe loss assumptions. Segment-specific rate increases for next year are targeted in the mid-single digits for auto and high-single digits for property, in line with or ahead of expected loss trends.

  • CFO Ryan Edward Greenier noted, "Auto net written premiums of $132 million increased slightly over the prior year," while household retention in auto fell to 84% but was characterized as stable.
  • Group Benefits delivered record sales of $6 million in Q3 2025, nearly twice the prior year's figure, though management described this segment as "relatively small" according to Marita Zuraitis and subject to quarterly fluctuation.
  • Fixed income new money yield exceeded book yield by over 100 basis points year-to-date, with management saying it was the "fifteenth consecutive quarter that new money yields in the core portfolio have exceeded book yield."
  • Annual actuarial reviews in Q3 2025 led to a $3.5 million life reserve decrease (GAAP only) and a $5.4 million GAAP retirement reserve increase, impacting only GAAP earnings and noted as non-cash with no effect on free cash flow.
  • Recent capital actions included retiring short-term maturities via the new $300 million senior note issuance in September at a record-tight spread, indicating robust investor demand.
  • Despite recognizing elevated near-term expense levels, management asserted investments are balanced to support both growth-building scale and operational efficiency to reach long-term profitability goals.

Industry glossary

  • Combined Ratio: A measure of underwriting profitability calculated as the sum of incurred losses and expenses divided by earned premiums; below 100% indicates underwriting profit.
  • Persistency: Percentage of insurance policies or contracts remaining in force over a specified period, reflecting retention and customer loyalty.
  • Catastrophe (Cat) Losses: Insurance claims and losses resulting from large-scale events such as natural disasters; impacts profitability and volatility.
  • GenAI (Generative Artificial Intelligence): A class of AI technologies that automate content creation or repetitive information-processing tasks to improve operational efficiency.
  • Bonds—Book Yield vs. New Money Yield: Book yield is the existing average yield earned on a portfolio, while new money yield refers to the yield on newly purchased assets; the differential indicates potential for net investment income growth.
