TAKEAWAYS

  • After-Tax Distributable Earnings -- $214 million, or $0.53 per share of Class A common stock, cited in prepared remarks.
  • Dividend Declaration -- $0.45 per share of Class A common stock, payable December 1, 2025 to holders of record as of November 14, 2025.
  • Total Assets Under Management (AUM) -- $286 billion, up 20%, driven by $44 billion of capital raised, $24 billion of value creation, and offset by $26 billion of realizations over the last 12 months.
  • Fee-Earning Assets Under Management -- $163 million, a 15% increase, as explicitly stated in the call.

  • Year-to-Date Capital Raised -- Over $35 billion, surpassing the full-year 2024 total.

  • Credit Fundraising -- $4.8 billion raised in the quarter; nearly $12 billion raised year-to-date, establishing a record for the franchise.
  • Record Credit Dry Powder -- Over $16 billion available, with non-fee-earning credit AUM at nearly $11 billion, representing over $100 million in annual revenue potential as fees activate.
  • Real Estate Credit Fundraising -- Final close of $2.1 billion for the inaugural real estate credit strategy (TRECO), exceeding the initial $1.5 billion target by more than 35%.
  • Insurance Channel Penetration -- Capital from insurance clients has grown over 60% in two years, comprising 40% of TRECO's final close and more than 25% of credit fundraising in the quarter.
  • Private Wealth Channel -- More than $1 billion raised in the quarter, with $900 million in inflows for T-POP since launch, propelled by expanded platform distribution.
  • Record Deployment -- $15 billion invested, up over 70%, with credit platform contributing $8.3 billion (more than double year over year) and private equity $4.6 billion (up nearly 40%).
  • Fee-Related Revenue and Margin -- $509 million in fee-related revenue, $225 million in fee-related earnings, with a 44% FRE margin, which management affirms aligns to guidance of exiting the year in the mid-40s percent range.
  • Distributable Earnings -- $230 million for the quarter, including $30 million from realized performance allocations, as specifically allocated to exit events such as Sai Life Sciences and Samhwa.
  • Realizations -- $8 billion realized in the quarter (up nearly 40%), contributing to differentiated DPI but with performance allocations tempered by fund stage, as per management explanation.
  • Portfolio Performance -- Private equity revenue and EBITDA growth of approximately 17% and 20%, respectively, over the last 12 months; portfolio aggregate appreciation of 3% for the quarter and 11% over 12 months.

  • Real Estate Performance -- TPG's real estate portfolio appreciated 3.5% for the quarter, nearly 16% over 12 months; TPG AG real estate portfolio up 2% in the quarter and 3.5% over 12 months.
  • Net Accrued Performance Balance -- Grew by nearly $200 million to $1.2 billion, including $100 million of accrued carry acquired through the Peppertree transaction.

  • Liquidity and Leverage -- $1.7 billion in net debt and $1.8 billion in available liquidity as of September 30, supporting ongoing growth initiatives and flexibility.
  • Innovative Fund Launches -- Launch of a liquid-securities open-ended credit fund and rated note feeder for insurance capital, as directly detailed in the call.
  • Transaction and Monitoring Fees -- $38 million generated in the quarter; $163 million over the last 12 months, with expectations of future growth as product breadth increases.

SUMMARY

TPG (TPG 1.26%) reported a surge in fundraising and deployment, surpassing all of last year's capital formation with over $35 billion raised to date and a record $15 billion invested this quarter. Management highlighted sequential and year-over-year growth in fee-related earnings and maintained guidance for margin improvement as the scale of new fund launches and cross-platform distribution expands. Portfolio metrics indicated continued outperformance versus market benchmarks in credit, private equity, and real estate, supporting a favorable outlook for future realizations and fee income conversion.

  • Management emphasized growing penetration in insurance and private wealth channels as significant contributors to current and future fundraising velocity.
  • Recent credit rating upgrade by Fitch to A- provides validation of balance sheet strength and was specifically announced in the call.
  • Transaction activity included full exits with strong returns, such as Sai Life Sciences, Samhwa, and a partial realization at CR Fitness, alongside the IPO of Beta Technologies.
  • Additional emphasis was placed on new product development—particularly evergreen and interval funds—as part of the multiyear strategy to diversify revenue streams and investor base.
  • Management stated, "We are reiterating our guidance to exit this year in the mid-40s. As I've said all along, that is not an end point for us," indicating sustained focus on operating leverage beyond current levels.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • T-POP: TPG's perpetually offered private equity vehicle designed for ongoing capital inflows, specifically distributed through private wealth channels.
  • TRECO: TPG Real Estate Credit Opportunities, the firm's inaugural real estate credit fund platform highlighted for exceeding fundraising targets.
  • TCAP: TPG's nontraded Business Development Company (BDC), offering private credit exposure to wealth management clients.
  • FRE (Fee-Related Earnings): A non-GAAP profitability metric representing manager earnings from recurring management and related fees, net of related expenses but excluding performance fees and incentives.
  • DPI (Distributions to Paid-In Capital): An industry metric for realized returns, representing the ratio of capital returned to investors relative to contributed capital.
  • Dry Powder: Uninvested, committed capital available for future opportunities, often referenced as a measure of readiness for deployment.
  • Accrued Carry (Accrued Performance Balance): Unrealized, potentially payable performance incentives due to the manager once relevant fund hurdle rates and return benchmarks have been met.
