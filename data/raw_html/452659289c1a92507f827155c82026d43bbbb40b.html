Penguin Solutions (PENG) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 7, 2026 at 9:19AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Jan. 6, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Mark Adams
  • Chief Financial Officer — Nate Olmstead

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $343 million, representing a 2% sequential increase and 1% year-over-year rise, with no hyperscale hardware revenue recognized in the quarter.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 30%, down 0.8 percentage points year over year and 0.9 percentage points sequentially, attributed mainly to the wind down of the high-margin Penguin Edge business.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Income -- $42 million, up 1% compared to the same quarter last year and up 6% sequentially.
  • Non-GAAP Diluted EPS -- $0.49, flat year over year and up 14% sequentially.
  • Segment Revenue Composition -- Advanced Computing: $151 million (44% of total), Integrated Memory: $137 million (40%), Optimized LED: $55 million (16%); Advanced Computing fell 15% year over year due to Penguin Edge and hyperscale hardware sales dropping out, while Integrated Memory increased 41% year over year, and Optimized LED declined 18% year over year.
  • Advanced Computing Growth (Ex-Edge/Ex-Hyperscale) -- Excluding Penguin Edge and hyperscale hardware, Advanced Computing net sales grew 52% year over year.
  • Services and Product Sales -- Services net sales: $65 million, down 9% year over year; Product net sales: $279 million, up 3% year over year.
  • Operating Expenses -- $61 million, down 4% year over year and down 6% sequentially, reflecting lower personnel-related and subcontract services costs.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $45 million, up 1% year over year.
  • Cash Flow from Operations -- $31 million, representing a 125% increase over the same quarter last year, mainly due to lower investments in net working capital.
  • Cash, Equivalents, and Short-term Investments -- $461 million, up $68 million year over year and up $8 million sequentially.
  • Inventory -- $213 million, down from $247 million a year ago; days of inventory at 38, down from 49 days a year ago and 51 days sequentially.
  • Accounts Receivable -- $342 million, up from $276 million a year ago; days sales outstanding at 51, up from 45 last year, and flat sequentially.
  • Stock Repurchase -- $15 million spent to buy back approximately 791,000 shares; $96.5 million remains authorized for repurchase as of Nov. 28, 2025.
  • Business Activity -- Two new Advanced Computing customers signed in defense and education/research; master agreements executed with major financial and oil and gas institutions, with purchase orders anticipated imminently.
  • Integrated Memory Demand and Expansion -- Strong demand across networking, telecommunications, and computing, with accelerated adoption of DDR5 and emerging Compute Express Link (CXL) deployments in early production through OEM partners.
  • Optical Memory Appliance (OMA) -- Ongoing investment and collaborations in photonic transport technology to boost GPU/CPU/memory interconnect performance.
  • Optimized LED Outlook -- Weak demand in China and among some large U.S. OEMs, though segment operating income rose to $3.5 million, up 24% sequentially.
  • Zilia Technologies Divestiture -- Signed agreement to sell remaining 19% stake in Zilia Technologies for $46 million, expected to close in the fiscal third quarter.
  • Full Year Revenue and EPS Outlook -- Midpoint guidance maintained at 6% net sales growth and $2 non-GAAP diluted EPS; guidance excludes hyperscale hardware and assumes continued Penguin Edge wind down.
  • Segment Guidance Ranges -- Advanced Computing: minus 15% to plus 15% year over year; Integrated Memory: 20%-35% growth; Optimized LED: minus 15% to minus 5% year over year.
  • Second Half Weighting -- 53%-54% of full year net sales projected for the second half, reflecting anticipated AI opportunity bookings and shipments.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin Outlook -- 29%, plus or minus 1 percentage point, reduced by 50 basis points due to higher mix of lower-margin memory sales.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses Guidance -- $250 million, plus or minus $10 million for the fiscal year.
  • Non-GAAP Tax Rate -- Expected at 22% for the fiscal year, with possible adjustment for global and U.S. tax changes or shifts in geographic income mix.
  • Cash Conversion Cycle -- 35 days, improving by 11 days year over year and 14 days sequentially, primarily from faster inventory turns and changes in purchase/payment timing.
  • ICE ClusterWare Platform -- Actively customizing compatibility with open source industry software and integrating with NVIDIA's software for AI deployments.

RISKS

  • Non-GAAP gross margin decreased 0.8 percentage points year over year and 0.9 percentage points sequentially, primarily due to the wind down of the high-margin Penguin Edge business, according to Nate Olmstead.
  • Optimized LED segment revenue fell 18% year over year, and segment demand remains weak in China and select large U.S. OEM customers.
  • Management continued to cite "supply chain constraints, especially as they relate to our Advanced Computing and Integrated Memory businesses," noting extended lead times for some key components.
  • Nate Olmstead said, "I also expect sequentially advanced computing to be down Q1 to Q2," and flagged typical LED business softness in Q2 due to Chinese New Year.
  • Advanced Computing net sales were down 15% year over year in fiscal Q1 2026, reflecting both the wind down of Penguin Edge business and hyperscale hardware sales in Q1 last year, which did not recur in Q1 this year.

SUMMARY

Penguin Solutions (PENG +0.74%) delivered sequential and year-over-year revenue growth despite the complete absence of hyperscale hardware revenue, maintaining full-year guidance and signaling ongoing customer diversification. Management highlighted rapid progress in securing new enterprise, financial, and oil and gas clients, with signed master agreements poised to convert to near-term purchase orders, likely affecting second-half performance. They reported record Integrated Memory growth, significantly raised segment guidance, and detailed product innovation in Compute Express Link and optical memory technology. Structural changes continued with the announced sale of the company's remaining Zilia Technologies stake, furthering its strategic transition from a holding company to an AI infrastructure solutions provider.

  • Penguin Solutions is restructuring its software stack by making ICE ClusterWare compatible with open source and NVIDIA platforms to increase market addressability and enhance support for large-scale AI deployments.
  • Cash conversion cycle improved materially, down 11 days year over year and 14 days sequentially, supporting enhanced operational efficiency and working capital management.
  • Second-half sales are projected to be weighted at 53%-54% of full-year revenues, with AI project bookings expected to drive the shift following greater new client onboarding.
  • The company expects the wind down of Penguin Edge and exclusion of hyperscale hardware sales to represent about a 14 percentage point and 30 percentage point headwind to total and Advanced Computing net sales growth, respectively, for fiscal 2026.
  • Operating expenses and capital outlays remain tightly managed, with cash, equivalents, and short-term investments building to $461 million, and $15 million invested in share repurchases during the quarter.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Compute Express Link (CXL): An open industry-standard interconnect protocol enabling high-speed, high-capacity connections among CPUs, GPUs, and memory, designed for advanced data center and AI workloads.
  • ICE ClusterWare: Penguin Solutions' proprietary software platform for cluster management, orchestration, and integration of high-performance computing and AI infrastructure.
  • Optical Memory Appliance (OMA): Penguin's emerging solution integrating photonic transport to improve bandwidth and performance between memory and processors in advanced computing systems.
  • Sovereign Cloud: Cloud computing infrastructure set up and managed to comply with the data locality and sovereignty requirements of a particular country or government entity, often outside the U.S.
