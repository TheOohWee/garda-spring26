DexCom (DXCM) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 6:04PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Feb. 12, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Jacob Steven Leach
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jereme M. Sylvain

Takeaways

  • Worldwide revenue -- $1.26 billion, up 13% on a reported basis and 12% organic growth, driven by continued new customer demand.
  • US revenue -- $892 million, representing 11% growth, with noted improvement in sell-through trends linked to the G7 15-day product launch.
  • International revenue -- $368 million, up 18% as reported and 15% organic, with France among the fastest-growing markets due to expanded type two access.
  • Gross profit and margin -- Gross profit of $799.8 million and a margin of 64%, up from 59%, with sequential improvement over the quarter attributed to resumed ocean shipping and lower scrap rates.
  • Operating expenses -- $468.3 million for 2025, compared to $451.7 million in 2024, reflecting investments in new manufacturing capacity and business expansion.
  • Operating income -- $331.5 million, or 26% of revenue, versus $209.5 million, or 19% of revenue, in the prior year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $422.2 million, representing 34% of revenue, versus $300.1 million (27% of revenue) previously.
  • Net income and EPS -- Net income of $265.1 million and $0.68 per share for the quarter.
  • Cash position -- Closed the quarter with approximately $2.0 billion in cash and cash equivalents, following the settlement of $1.2 billion convertible notes and $300 million in share repurchases.
  • Free cash flow -- Surpassed $1.0 billion in free cash flow for the first time.
  • 2026 revenue guidance -- Anticipated range of $5.16 billion to $5.25 billion, implying 11%-13% growth, including expected incremental contribution from Stelo and new product innovations.
  • Margin guidance -- Projected full-year non-GAAP gross profit margin of 63%-64%, operating profit margin of 22%-23%, and adjusted EBITDA margin around 30%-31%, assuming margin expansion of 200-300 basis points.
  • G7 15-day launch -- Nationwide US availability as of January, with initial positive customer and physician feedback on accuracy and reduced sensor changes per month.
  • Smart Basal early access launch -- Commencing this month in the US, targeting improved type two diabetes basal insulin management and prescriber workflow efficiency.
  • Direct EHR integration -- Now live or onboarding at over 160 health systems, enabling streamlined access to customer CGM data across platforms.
  • Stelo revenue -- $130 million contributed in 2025, with 2026 expected to add approximately one percentage point of total revenue growth.
  • International product expansion -- Plans to launch Stelo and additional CGM products for international segments in 2026 to address untapped market demand.
  • Utilization rates -- Type one automated insulin delivery (AID) users show over 90% utilization; type two basal at 80%-85%; type two non-insulin users in registry reporting similarly high rates in reimbursed environments.
  • Manufacturing investments -- Ireland facility to start late in the year, with initial OpEx impact before costs transition to COGS.
  • Rebate dynamics -- G7 15-day pricing and rebate rates align with existing G7 ten-day structure; no expected revenue-per-month differences as a result of the new product.
  • Coverage expansion -- Approximately 6.5 million commercial type two non-insulin lives now covered, with ongoing progress toward the potential addition of 12 million new Medicare-covered lives upon CMS expansion.

Summary

Management emphasized that DexCom (DXCM 2.53%) surpassed $1.0 billion in free cash flow for the first time, signaling substantial financial flexibility. DexCom completed a broad US rollout of the G7 15-day sensor, which received favorable early adoption feedback for its accuracy and wear time, and initiated the early access launch of Smart Basal to address type two diabetes insulin dosing. Internationally, DexCom cited France as one of its fastest-growing markets, reflecting broader access wins and positioning for further geographic revenue expansion through targeted product launches. Guidance for 2026 projects continued top- and bottom-line growth, anchored by product innovation, international expansion, and incremental contributions from Stelo. DexCom outlined ongoing progress in expanding payor coverage for type two non-insulin users, with approximately 6.5 million commercial lives now covered, and highlighted the potential impact of adding up to 12 million new Medicare lives pending CMS policy decisions.

  • Jacob Steven Leach stated, "We are currently in the process of building greater awareness of this product's availability in the market," indicating a strategic push to accelerate G7 15-day sensor adoption.
  • Chief Financial Officer Jereme M. Sylvain said, "We do not necessarily need a record new patients to hit the the low end of our guidance. And you would wanna hit a record new patient to certainly hit the top end of the range and and beyond," clarifying the link between new patient growth and revenue guidance.
  • DexCom reported, "warranty rates coming down and and certainly our complaint rates coming down," following resolution of prior sensor deployment and supply chain challenges.
  • DexCom confirmed a May 2026 investor day for additional long-term strategic and financial updates.

Industry glossary

  • Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM): Real-time, wearable biosensor technology providing ongoing glucose measurements for people with diabetes.
  • AID (automated insulin delivery): Systems that automatically adjust insulin delivery based on CGM data to optimize glycemic control.
  • Basal insulin: Long-acting insulin regimen used to control blood glucose between meals and overnight.
  • Stelo: DexCom's consumer platform offering CGM-based wellness applications and over-the-counter access, aimed at type two non-insulin and prediabetes populations.
  • PBMs (pharmacy benefit managers): Third-party administrators of prescription drug programs that negotiate drug benefits and coverage.
  • DME (durable medical equipment): Medical devices that provide therapeutic benefits and are intended for repeated use under a physician's care; in CGM, DME refers to non-pharmacy distribution channels.
