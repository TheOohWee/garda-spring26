Citi Trends (CTRN) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 17, 2026 at 9:54AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, March 17, 2026 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Executive Chairman — Kenneth Seipel
  • Chief Financial Officer — Heather Plutino

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Comparable store sales growth -- 8.9% increase in the quarter, marking the sixth consecutive quarter of positive comps and a 15.3% two-year stack.
  • Total Q4 sales -- $230.4 million, up 9.1% over Q4 2024.
  • Q4 adjusted EBITDA -- $11.9 million, up 67% year over year and $4.8 million higher than Q4 2024.
  • Gross margin Q4 -- 39.9%, a 20 basis point increase, driven by lower markdowns and improved allocation process, but slightly below internal expectations due to freight and markdowns at quarter end.
  • Adjusted SG&A Q4 -- $80 million, up from $76.7 million, including $1.8 million in higher incentive compensation, with leverage of 160 basis points to 34.7% of sales.
  • Store activity Q4 -- Three store closures and no openings.
  • Full-year net sales -- $820 million, an 8.9% increase, with 9.7% comparable store sales growth and two-year comp growth of 13.1%.
  • Full-year adjusted EBITDA -- $11.8 million, a $26 million increase compared to 2024, with adjusted EBITDA margin of 2.1% (reflecting methodology change).
  • Inventory levels -- Year-end total inventory down 7.4%, and in-store inventory down 2%, reflecting supply chain and allocation efficiencies.
  • Balance sheet -- $66 million in cash, no debt, and no drawings on the $75 million revolver at year end.
  • Store fleet modernization -- Three store openings, 62 remodels, and four closures in the year, completing 2025 with 590 stores and approximately 30% of the fleet updated to a new format.
  • Marketing impact -- The flagship Joy video generated over 55,000,000 views and engagements, demonstrating the power of authentic storytelling that reflects the communities served.
  • Q1 2026 comparable store sales trend -- High single-digit growth quarter-to-date, building on a 10% first quarter comp in the prior year.
  • 2026 sales and EBITDA guidance -- Projected total sales growth of 6%-8%, comp growth of 5%-7%, and adjusted EBITDA of $34-$38 million, representing a near-doubling from the 2025 adjusted base.
  • 2026 capital investment -- Planned capital expenditures of $35-$40 million, primarily for 25 new stores and 50 remodels, targeting an updated fleet share of 42% by year-end.
  • AI and technology investments -- Implemented AI-based allocation and planning systems, improved inventory monitoring, and plans for advanced in-store security cameras and productivity tech upgrades by end of second quarter.
  • Closeout and extreme value penetration -- Closeouts currently about 30% of the assortment mix and expected to grow; extreme value products targeted to reach 10% over time, with current penetration under 5%.
  • Planned store expansion -- 25 new store openings in 2026 (2 already opened), with remaining phased around major seasonal periods and 40 openings targeted for 2027.
  • Loyalty program progress -- CRM program piloted in select stores, with full launch expected in the back half of the year, subject to messaging refinement and marketing alignment.
  • Strategic initiatives -- Early-stage synergistic acquisition opportunities are being explored, with no decisions made; strategic focus remains on core business and disciplined, data-driven expansion.
  • Adjusted financial reporting change -- Beginning in 2026, equity-based compensation is excluded from adjusted SG&A and adjusted EBITDA; 2025 expense was $5.4 million, with 2026 projected at $5.5-$6 million.

SUMMARY

Citi Trends (CTRN 0.97%) entered 2026 with operational momentum, reporting consistent transaction growth and increased basket size, which management attributes to a sharpened merchandising strategy focused on core markets. Technology upgrades—including AI-driven merchandise allocation, real-time dashboards, and automated security systems—were highlighted as drivers of improved margin and inventory velocity. The company announced disciplined national expansion, guided by predictive analytics delivering approximately 90% accuracy in the sales prediction for new stores, with a 2027 pipeline already under active planning. Early-stage acquisitions and a data-centric loyalty program were framed as incremental long-term levers, not immediate contributors.

  • Management confirmed that the plan for 2026 calls for top-line growth in the mid to high single digits, while more than doubling adjusted EBITDA performance.
  • Citi Trends disclosed no current debt, a cash balance sufficient for planned expansion, and ongoing leverage of fixed costs from sales growth.
  • 2026 capital expenditure priorities are dedicated to strategic market penetration via store formats, remodels, and technology enhancement.
  • The revised non-GAAP reporting for equity-based compensation was introduced to increase clarity for investors about operating results.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Four-wall contribution margin: Store-level profit margin calculated as sales minus controllable expenses, excluding corporate allocations, to assess location profitability.
  • CRM (Customer Relationship Management): Marketing and data system focused on tracking, engaging, and driving repeat shopping via loyalty programs and personalized outreach.
  • Closeouts: Merchandise procured at significant discounts resulting from vendor excess inventory, discontinued SKUs, or season-end stock, often sold below typical retail prices.
