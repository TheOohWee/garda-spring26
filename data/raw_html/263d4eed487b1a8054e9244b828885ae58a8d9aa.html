Gilead (GILD) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 10, 2026 at 5:45PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Feb. 10, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — Daniel O'Day
  • Chief Commercial Officer — Johanna Mercier
  • Chief Medical Officer — Dietmar Berger
  • Chief Financial Officer — Andrew Dickinson
  • Executive Vice President, Kite — Cindy Perettie

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total product sales -- $28.9 billion for the year, up 1% and above guidance; $7.9 billion in Q4, up 5% year over year, driven primarily by HIV and liver disease sales growth, offset by expected decline in Vecluri sales.
  • HIV business -- Full-year sales reached $20.8 billion, up 6%, with Biktarvy sales up 7% to $14.3 billion and Descovy sales up 31% to $2.8 billion, both driven by demand and higher realized prices.
  • YES2GO -- Achieved $150 million in 2025 sales, with Q4 sales of $96 million; management guides for approximately $800 million in revenue for 2026, supported by 90% payer coverage and significant commercial momentum.
  • Liver disease -- Sales totaled $3.2 billion, up 6%, with Libdelzi driving quarterly growth of 42% sequentially to $150 million following a key US competitor withdrawal.
  • Trodelvy -- Annual sales reached $1.4 billion, up 6%, and are supported by positive Phase III results in metastatic triple-negative breast cancer, with NCCN guideline updates expanding recommended indications.
  • Cell therapy -- Full-year revenue was $1.8 billion, down 7%, reflecting competitive pressures; management expects a further 10% decline in 2026.
  • Vecluri -- Generated $911 million in 2025, down 49% or $900 million, mainly due to lower COVID-19 hospitalizations.
  • Operating metrics -- Non-GAAP product gross margin was 86.4%, operating margin was 45% (48% excluding IPR&D and nonrecurring revenue); non-GAAP diluted EPS was $8.15, increasing $0.40 after adjusting for Simbae impact.
  • Shareholder returns -- $5.9 billion returned to shareholders in 2025, representing 63% of free cash flow, including $1.9 billion in share repurchases.
  • 2026 guidance -- Total product sales expected at $29.6 billion to $30 billion; base business sales (excluding Vecluri) projected at $29 billion to $29.4 billion, with 4%-5% growth anticipated and 2% headwind from drug pricing and ACA shifts.
  • HIV segment 2026 outlook -- Anticipated 6% growth in total HIV sales, with YES2GO guided for $800 million revenue; headwinds of about 2% due to policy changes, but underlying HIV business expected to grow 8% absent these factors.
  • Pipeline and launches -- Four potential product launches targeted in 2026: Trodelvy (first-line metastatic TNBC), Viclen (HIV), Anidocel (multiple myeloma), and bruleviratide (hepatitis D); up to 10 launches possible through 2027.
  • Clinical milestones -- Five Phase III readouts and five FDA decisions expected in 2026, spanning HIV, liver, and oncology, supporting Gilead Sciences' pipeline diversification.

RISKS

  • Cell therapy revenues are projected to decline 10% in 2026, due to increased competition and trial-related volume headwinds, as explicitly stated by management.
  • HIV segment faces approximately a 2% growth headwind in 2026, directly attributed to a new drug pricing agreement and proposed Affordable Care Act changes impacting lower price channel mix.
  • Vecluri revenues declined $900 million, or 49%, due to lower COVID-19 hospitalizations, with further softening expected as guided by management.

SUMMARY

Gilead Sciences (GILD 2.18%) delivered record HIV and liver disease growth, surpassing sales guidance despite headwinds from Medicare Part D redesign and Vecluri declines. The HIV prevention portfolio expanded rapidly, with YES2GO achieving $150 million in its launch year and projected to reach $800 million in 2026, backed by strong coverage and access metrics. Elevated pipeline activity is evident, as management targets four launches and five pivotal readouts in 2026, supported by robust late-stage clinical results in HIV, oncology, and liver disease. Shareholder returns remained high at 63% of free cash flow, underlining disciplined capital allocation and expense management.

  • Biktarvy’s share in the US HIV treatment market now exceeds 52%, with uninterrupted year-over-year gain since launch.
  • "have achieved our goal of 90% coverage well ahead of our one-year target. This includes all major payers." (Mercier)
  • Management confirmed there are no major product LOEs until 2036, diminishing portfolio patent risk for the next decade.
  • Anidocel’s launch in fourth-line multiple myeloma is planned for second-half 2026 with expectations of modest contribution in year one, but “over time to become the market leader” due to efficacy and differentiated safety profile.
  • Trodelvy is now uniquely recommended by NCCN guidelines for both first- and second-line metastatic triple-negative breast cancer irrespective of PD-L1 status, with duration of treatment in first-line roughly double that in second-line.
  • R&D expenses were $5.7 billion, flat on a dollar basis, while acquired IPR&D of $1 billion matched annual investment targets; SG&A fell 5% reflecting disciplined OpEx control amid ongoing launch investments.
  • Of the $900 million Medicare Part D redesign headwind absorbed in 2025, HIV growth would have reached 10% year-over-year absent these effects, highlighting underlying volume and pricing strength.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Libdelzi: Gilead Sciences' recently launched treatment for primary biliary cholangitis, a chronic liver disease.
  • YES2GO: Twice-yearly injectable pre-exposure prophylaxis for HIV prevention, newly launched by Gilead Sciences.
  • Biktarvy: A once-daily, single-tablet regimen for HIV treatment combining bictegravir, emtricitabine, and tenofovir alafenamide.
  • Trodelvy: An antibody-drug conjugate for metastatic triple-negative breast cancer, also under investigation for other tumor types.
  • Vecluri: Remdesivir, used as an antiviral for COVID-19 hospitalization, known globally as Veklury.
  • PrEP: Pre-exposure prophylaxis; a preventive treatment for people at high risk of HIV, referenced in portfolio context for YES2GO and Descovy.
  • LOE: Loss of exclusivity; expiration of product patent protection exposing a drug to generic competition.
  • Kite: Gilead Sciences' cell therapy business unit focused on CAR T therapies in oncology.
  • Anidocel: Investigational BCMA-targeting CAR T therapy for relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma, aiming for potential launch in late 2026.
  • MRD: Minimal residual disease; a measure of the small number of cancer cells that remain in the body after treatment, used as a trial endpoint.
  • IPR&D: In-process research and development; acquisition-related spend on pipeline or early-stage assets.
  • PD-L1: Programmed death-ligand 1; a biomarker relevant in tumor immune response and therapeutic decision-making.
  • PBC: Primary biliary cholangitis, a form of chronic liver disease targeted by Libdelzi.
