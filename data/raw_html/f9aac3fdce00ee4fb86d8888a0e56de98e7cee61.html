Earnings Call Transcript

Kaspi.kz (KSPI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 2, 2026 at 9:31AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

March 2, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • CEO — Mikheil Lomtadze
  • CFO — David Ferguson
  • Operator

Takeaways

  • Consolidated revenue -- Exceeded $8 billion for the full year, incorporating both Kazakhstan and Turkey business lines for the first time.
  • Net income -- Flat year over year at approximately $2.1 billion, with reported 10% growth for the full year, at the lower end of previous guidance due to smartphone category weakness in Q4.
  • Kazakhstan revenue growth -- Increased 15% in Q4 and 19% for the full year.
  • Kazakhstan underlying net income growth -- 10% in Q4, and 13%-18% for the full year, reflecting resilience despite higher rates and taxes.
  • Payments platform (Kazakhstan) TPV -- 14% growth in Q4, 19% for the year; Q4 transactions up 12%, full-year up 14%.
  • Payments revenue (Kazakhstan) -- Grew 7% in Q4 and 12% for the year; net income increased 4% in Q4 and 13% for the year, with Q4 margins impacted by launch costs for Kaspi Alakan.
  • Kaspi Alakan “Pay by Palm” -- Achieved registration by nearly 500,000 customers and adoption by almost 6,000 merchants in Almaty, with 10% penetration among connected merchant transactions within 90 days of launch.
  • Marketplace platform GMV (Kazakhstan) -- Grew 12% in Q4, 19% for the year; smartphone GMV down 24%, but January data indicated recovery.
  • Marketplace take rate (Kazakhstan) -- All-time high with 13.1% in Q4 and 12.7% for the year, driven by advertising and delivery revenue streams.
  • E-commerce GMV (Kazakhstan) -- Rose 9% in Q4 and 16% for the year; ex-smartphones, growth was 27% for the year.
  • E-commerce purchases -- Q4 growth of 70%-83%, indicating rising consumer activity despite smartphone segment headwinds.
  • E-grocery GMV (Kazakhstan) -- Up 53% for the year, with consumer base nearing 1.4 million.
  • M-commerce GMV (Kazakhstan) -- Down 4% in Q4 including smartphones (up 3% excluding) and up 7% for the year including smartphones (11% excluding); m-commerce take rate at 9.4% in Q4 and 9.2% for the year.
  • Kaspi Travel GMV -- Increased 6% in Q4 and 14% for the year, with some take-rate expansion noted.
  • Marketplace revenue (Kazakhstan) -- Ex-smartphones, up 21% in Q4 and approximately 30% for the full year; Q4 net income declined 7%, up 6% for the year, due to mix shift toward lower-ticket, delivery-intensive items.
  • Fintech platform (Kazakhstan) TFE -- Grew 4% in Q4 and 13% for the year; segment pricing yield stable at 24%, cost of risk at 2.2%, nonperforming loan ratio reached approximately 6% and is expected to persist, due to improved recoveries.
  • Fintech loan portfolio growth -- Up 27%-31% for the year; savings and deposits up 16%-18% for the same period.
  • Fintech revenue and net income -- Revenue up 19% in Q4 and 20% for the year; net income up 49% despite impacts from higher interest rates, increased taxes, and stricter reserve requirements.
  • Hepsiburada (Turkey) purchases -- Number of purchases grew 19% in Q4; engaged purchasers increased 19%, monthly active consumers up 15%, engaged consumer count 29% higher than prior period.
  • Hepsiburada (Turkey) GMV and revenue -- GMV up 13% in Q4 and 7% for the full year; revenue up 18% in Q4 and 13% for the year.
  • Next-day shipping coverage (Turkey) -- Improved from 47% to 63% over the year, strengthening logistics capability.
  • Dividend guidance -- 850 Kazakh tenge per share declared for the last quarter, with management stating this rate is sustainable for the current year in line with pre-acquisition levels.
  • 2026 guidance update -- Growth targets for GMV, TPV, and TFE now include Turkey; adjusted EBITDA guidance base at 1.6 trillion tenge, with targeted 5% growth.
  • Marketplace GMV mix shift -- E-commerce segments (Kazakhstan and Turkey) comprised 54% of total Marketplace GMV in 2025, expected to rise to 60% in 2026.
  • Strategic focus -- Company to prioritize frequent consumer engagement, continued Turkey development near EBITDA breakeven, expansion of value-added services in both main markets, and measured investment in new geographies and products such as fintech offerings and Pay by Palm.

Risks

  • David Ferguson stated, "as of today, no reduction in interest rates; higher taxes in Kazakhstan in 2026—this is not just a 2025 event. The bank tax only went up from January 1 this year, so that will add 200 bps to the tax rate. There are also higher National Bank reserve requirements, which will have an impact on the bottom line."
  • Management noted that the smartphone category GMV dropped 24% in Q4 and persisted as a drag on Marketplace and Fintech segment results until showing recovery only in January.
  • Launch costs for Kaspi Alakan, the “Pay by Palm” service, negatively impacted Payments segment net income margin in Q4.
  • CFO Ferguson emphasized, "do not assume in this guidance any reduction in rates. That may be a difference between some buy-side expectations and ours."

Summary

Kaspi.kz (KSPI 0.86%) delivered consolidated revenue of over $8 billion and maintained annual net income near $2.1 billion despite macro headwinds and regulatory pressures. Management reported that Marketplace and E-commerce platforms sustained segment-leading growth and established all-time high take rates, largely driven by delivery and advertising monetization, while Fintech and Payments experienced slower revenue growth and margin compression. Turkey’s Hepsiburada platform accelerated purchase expansion and delivery infrastructure during integration, with strategy anchored around consumer engagement and breakeven EBITDA. Management committed to stable dividend payouts for the year and introduced updated growth guidance encompassing both Kazakhstan and Turkey lines, with a dedicated capital allocation framework. Operational focus was reaffirmed on cross-market product migration, merchant finance, rapid scaling of Pay by Palm, and continued reinvestment to drive future profitability, with leadership clearly articulating a measured approach toward further capital return and expansion.

  • Fourth-quarter results were impacted by higher delivery costs due to a consumer shift toward more frequent, lower-ticket purchases, with management raising delivery prices starting January to mitigate direct margin pressure.
  • Hepsiburada demonstrated higher order frequency and engagement metrics echoing Kazakhstan’s prior trajectory, as new logistics and next-day delivery initiatives contributed to rising user activity.
  • Dividend guidance was maintained in line with legacy pre-acquisition levels, with management reiterating that higher Kazakh taxes and reserve requirements will constrain net income until these are fully absorbed in the cost base by year-end.
  • Strategic remarks highlighted that value-added service revenues—particularly advertising—are sustaining multi-segment take-rate expansion despite soft smartphone sales, while investments in innovative offerings like Pay by Palm and new geography rollouts are prioritized over near-term profitability targets.

Industry glossary

  • TPV: Total Payment Volume; aggregate value of all payments completed through the platform within a period.
  • TFE: Total Fintech Earnings; the sum of revenue generated across all fintech products and services offered.
  • GMV: Gross Merchandise Value; cumulative sales volume transacted via the marketplace within the defined reporting period.
  • Take rate: Percentage of GMV or TPV retained by the platform as revenue, often through transaction fees or value-added service charges.
  • Kaspi Alakan: Company-branded “Pay by Palm” biometric payment solution launched in 2025, rapidly scaling in Almaty.
  • Hepsiburada: The Turkey-based e-commerce business acquired by Kaspi.kz, integrated into consolidated results.
  • m-commerce: Mobile commerce; in-person shopping at brick-and-mortar merchants through platform-linked mobile applications.
  • Ex-smartphones: Financial measure excluding the smartphone sales segment, used to assess core growth unaffected by specific category dynamics.
