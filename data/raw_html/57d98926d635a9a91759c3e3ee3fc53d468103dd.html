Earnings Call Transcript

Wiley (WLY) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 5, 2026 at 11:17AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

March 5, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Interim CEO and Chair — Matthew Kissner
  • Chief Financial Officer — Craig Albright
  • EVP, General Manager, Research — Jay Flynn
  • SVP, Investor Relations — Brian Campbell

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • AI revenue -- $42,000,000 year to date, ahead of last year’s $40,000,000, with company guidance of $45,000,000–$50,000,000 for the full year.
  • Research publishing output -- Submissions increased 26%, article output up 11%, and revenue up 4% excluding AI, outpacing the broader market.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin -- Guidance raised to the high end of 25.5%-26.5% range (up from 24% prior year), with 250 basis points improvement reported for the quarter.
  • Operating cash flow -- Nearly doubled year over year to $103,000,000, with free cash flow up by $57,000,000 and tracking to an annual target of ~$200,000,000.
  • Shareholder returns -- Share repurchases doubled in the quarter to $70,000,000 year to date, with $120,000,000 combined repurchases and dividends in nine months, up 37%.
  • Recurring revenue -- 70% of research publishing revenue is from recurring contracts, with customer retention above 99% and 82% of the renewal cycle completed.
  • Open access segment -- Author-funded open access revenue is growing consistently above 20%; Advanced Science journal revenue grew 50%.
  • Geographic performance -- Substantial submission growth in China (+43% year to date), India, North America, and Europe, plus renewed gains in Japan and momentum in the Middle East.
  • Corporate expense reduction -- Adjusted EBITDA corporate expenses down 21% in the quarter and 12% year to date; $17,000,000 in total cost savings, with ~85% attributed to the tech transformation.
  • Virtusa partnership -- Announced a roughly $150,000,000 five-year managed services agreement to drive technology modernization, expected efficiency, and margin expansion.
  • Segment dynamics -- Learning segment revenue declined 2% in the quarter (down 7% year to date); Professional segment down 5%, offset by 1% growth in Academic; EBITDA for Learning down 8% year to date.
  • Advanced journal portfolio -- Advanced brand expected to exceed $70,000,000 revenue in the current fiscal year and posting double-digit growth.
  • Nexus partners -- Nexus content licensing service now at 36 publishing partners, with continuing expansion efforts.
  • Clinical outcome assessments (COA) -- Revenue grew from $800,000 in 2021 to nearly $7,000,000, underpinned by strategic agreement with IQVIA and engagement with top 20 pharma companies.
  • Open Evidence partnership -- Multiyear partnership for content integration with a five-year multimillion-dollar licensing agreement, and a minority equity stake taken by Wiley (WLY 0.36%).
  • AI recurring revenue trajectory -- Slightly under 10% of current AI revenues are recurring, with management expecting this proportion to triple next year.
  • India market initiative -- Wiley supports expanded research access in India via the One Nation, One Subscription program, now reaching over 6,000 institutions and 18,000,000 users.
  • CapEx -- Current year capital expenditures expected to remain in line with the prior year’s $77,000,000 total.

Summary

The company reported record AI revenue and announced that the proportion of recurring AI-related earnings is set to triple in the near term, highlighting a deliberate shift to sustainable subscription-based models. Recent strategic partnerships, including with Virtusa, Open Evidence, and IQVIA, are positioned to deliver expanded technology capabilities, new recurring revenue streams, and differentiated content offerings across industries. Accelerated cost discipline resulted in double-digit margin expansion, increased cash flow, and significant shareholder distributions through both dividends and record share buybacks.

  • The Advanced open access brand now represents a material portion of publishing growth, and eight new journals are planned for launch.
  • Company migration of 80% of journals onto the Research Exchange platform is accelerating the transition to AI-ready content and digital workflows.
  • Federal funding for U.S. scientific research remained resilient, stabilizing the research subscriptions core amid macro uncertainty.
  • Wiley’s entry into daily clinical workflows through Open Evidence moves company assets beyond traditional publishing into high-value, embedded decision-support domains.
  • Management indicated a focus on continued portfolio optimization, discipline in capital allocation, and an active M&A pipeline targeting high-impact research content.
  • Share repurchases are projected to reach about 3,000,000 shares for the year, with a current dividend yield of approximately 4.5% ensuring continued cash returns to shareholders.
  • The company expects to benefit from further AI-driven productivity gains and ongoing margin expansion in fiscal 2027, as tech partnerships and the AI Center of Excellence scale up.
  • CFO Craig Albright said, "We continue to deploy capital strategically to expand our journal portfolio and content advantage."
  • India submissions surged 43% year to date, reflecting tangible progress in emerging markets and broad international diversification of research output.

Industry glossary

  • Open access: Publishing model where research articles are made freely available to readers, with costs commonly supported by author or funding body fees.
  • Clinical outcome assessments (COA): Scientifically validated instruments used to measure patient responses and outcomes in pharmaceutical clinical trials.
  • Research Exchange platform: Wiley's proprietary digital publishing system supporting peer review, AI-ready data integration, and streamlined journal production workflows.
  • Subscription knowledge feeds: Recurring, data-driven content products that deliver verified research to corporate and institutional clients for AI training and analytics.
  • Nexus content licensing: Wiley’s service for licensing proprietary content to third-party publishers and AI developers.
