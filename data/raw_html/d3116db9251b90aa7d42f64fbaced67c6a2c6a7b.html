Signet (SIG) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 2, 2025 at 9:40AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, December 2, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — JK Simancic
  • Chief Financial and Strategy Officer — Joan Hilson

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $1.4 billion for the quarter, reflecting 3% same store sales growth and a 7% increase in average unit retail.
  • Adjusted Operating Income -- $32 million, attributed to higher sales and operating efficiencies in gross margin and SG&A.
  • Gross Margin -- Rate expanded by 130 basis points, including 80 basis points of merchandise margin expansion, 30 basis points of occupancy leverage, and 20 basis points from distribution efficiencies.
  • Average Unit Retail (AUR) -- Fashion AUR grew 8%, mostly due to increased LGD (lab-grown diamond) fashion mix and higher gold prices; bridal AUR grew 6% from higher mix of LGD wedding and anniversary bands.
  • Service Revenue -- High single-digit growth, with extended service agreements attachment rates up over 1.5 percentage points.
  • Merchandise Margin -- 50 basis points expansion year-to-date, 80 basis points in Q3, despite higher tariffs and gold costs.
  • Inventory -- $2.1 billion, down 1% year over year, despite nearly 50% higher gold costs and higher tariffs.
  • Share Repurchases -- Approximately $28 million (about 300,000 shares) in the quarter; year to date, $180 million repurchased (2.8 million shares), over 6% of diluted shares outstanding; $545 million authorization remaining.
  • Guidance Updates -- Raised low end of full-year same store sales guide to down 0.2%, maintaining high guide at plus 1.75%; introduced Q4 same store sales range of plus 0.5% to down 5% and full-year adjusted operating income range of $465 million to $515 million.
  • Capital Expenditures -- Projected at $145 million to $160 million for the year, including accelerated real estate refreshes.
  • Brand Segment Results -- Kay, Zales, and Jared combined posted 6% same store sales growth; Jared comp sales were up 10% in fashion, led by collections such as Italia de Oro.
  • Lab-Grown Diamond (LGD) Penetration -- LGDs accounted for 15% of fashion sales, roughly doubling prior year; now 40% of bridal business.
  • Modern Marketing Initiatives -- Double-digit growth in impressions with low to mid single-digit spend increase, using brand ambassadors and digital-first content.
  • Store Refreshes -- Recently renovated Kay, Jared, and Zales stores delivered mid single-digit sales lifts; repositioning of Kay stores on track for just over two-year payback.
  • Holiday Inventory Position -- Company made significant investments in LGD fashion at sub-$1,000 price points and increased inventory in key gifting categories.
  • Guidance Sensitivity -- Q4 low-end guidance scenario forecasts mid single-digit unit declines in both bridal and fashion; high end allows for low single-digit unit declines in bridal.
  • Tariff Mitigation -- Margin expansion efforts and strategic sourcing offset majority of India tariff increases, which rose from 5% to above 50%.
  • Promotion Strategy -- Reduced discounting by 25% at Jared; overall approach focused on fewer promotions, deeper targeting, and retention of pricing flexibility for Q4.
  • Liquidity and Free Cash Flow -- Quarter-end cash at $235 million; total liquidity of $1.4 billion with an undrawn ABL; free cash flow improved by over $100 million in the quarter, over $150 million year to date.

Summary

Management reported three consecutive quarters of positive same store sales and a doubling of adjusted operating income compared to Q3 of last year. The company expanded merchandise and gross margins through refined pricing and supply chain strategies, counteracting cost pressures from tariffs and gold. Tariff risk was mitigated by moving some production to the U.S. and other countries, with further margin protection from a growing services business. Recent investments in LGD inventory and a streamlined promotional cadence were designed to address consumer value sensitivity entering a pivotal holiday period. Management raised the lower end of annual guidance due to strong Q3, successful tariff mitigation, and a cautious view of Q4 in light of recent consumer softness, especially among brands serving lower- to mid-income segments.

  • Simancic said, "we believe we're well positioned for the holiday season with a focused assortment aligned to key categories and price points supported by a modernized marketing approach."
  • speaker 0 stated, "Free cash flow improved by more than $100,000,000 for the quarter and by more than $150,000,000 year to date, from timing of receipts that will shift payment to the fourth quarter and inventory discipline."
  • The Q4 guidance range builds in "flexibility for the current macro environment," with 70% of the quarter remaining at the call date.
  • Discounting at Jared fell by 25%, reflecting management's strategic focus on "a much more fulsome playbook" for pricing and promotion.
  • James Allen is expected to generate a 120 basis point negative comp impact for the back half of the year.
  • The company continues to close underperforming stores, particularly in declining malls, while investing in higher-performing locations for key brands.

Industry glossary

  • AUR (Average Unit Retail): The mean selling price per item sold, an indicator of pricing power and product mix.
  • LGD (Lab-Grown Diamond): Synthetic diamonds produced in a laboratory setting that are chemically and physically identical to mined diamonds.
  • ESA (Extended Service Agreement): Add-on protection plans or warranties offering additional coverage beyond standard policies on jewelry purchases.
  • GMM (Gross Merchandise Margin): Total margin generated from merchandise sales before other operating costs, measuring direct product profitability.
  • ABL (Asset-Based Lending facility): A credit line secured by company assets, typically inventory or receivables, providing flexible liquidity.
