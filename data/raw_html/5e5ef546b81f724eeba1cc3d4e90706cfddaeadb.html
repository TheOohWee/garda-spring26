Earnings Call Transcript

Ecopetrol (EC) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 18, 2026 at 12:16PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, March 5, 2026 at 9 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Ricardo Roa Barragán
  • Executive Vice President, Hydrocarbons — Juan Carlos Hurtado Parra
  • Vice President, Energy Transition — Bayron Triana Arias
  • Chief Financial Officer — Alfonso Camilo Muñoz
  • Corporate Vice President, Strategy and New Businesses — Julián Fernando Lemos

Takeaways

  • Net income -- COP 9 trillion, near the financial plan target, achieved despite a 15% reduction in Brent prices and the absence of COP 1.6 trillion in nonrecurring gains booked in 2024.
  • EBITDA -- COP 46.7 trillion, at a 39% margin, with 51% from E&P, 43% from Transportation and related segments, and 6% from Refining.
  • Production -- 745,000 barrels per day, matching 2024 levels, with domestic crude output at 517,000 barrels per day—the highest in five years.
  • Reserves replacement ratio -- 121%, the highest in four years, with 1.944 billion barrels of oil equivalent reported and organic growth adding 314 million barrels.
  • Transportation volumes -- Above 1.1 million barrels per day; segment EBITDA reached COP 11 trillion, and net income was nearly COP 5 trillion.
  • Gross refining margin -- Rose to $13.1 per barrel, a 32% increase from $9.9 per barrel in 2024, driven by operational and commercial initiatives.
  • Renewable energy capacity -- Achieved 951 megawatts installed, surpassing the original 2030 target; operational renewable capacity grew 94% from 186 to 381 megawatts during the year.
  • Efficiency program -- Accumulated COP 16 trillion in savings over three years, with 2025 annual contribution of about COP 6.6 trillion, exceeding the updated annual target by 1.3x.
  • Unit hydrocarbon cost -- $46 per barrel, down $1.7 or 3.4% versus 2024; lifting cost dropped to $12.2 per barrel, $0.3 below 2024, driven by cost discipline and synergies.
  • Dividend proposal -- Board proposed COP 110 per share, representing 50% of net income, pending approval at the March 27 shareholder assembly.
  • Free cash flow -- COP 11 trillion, supported by strong operational cash generation and early collection of COP 7.7 trillion from FEPC.
  • Organic investment (CapEx) -- Totaled $6.3 billion, with $3.9 billion allocated to Hydrocarbons, $750 million to Energy Transition and Gas, and about 25% allocated to Transmission and Road.
  • Net debt metrics -- Gross debt-to-EBITDA closed at 2.3x; excluding ISA, the ratio was 1.6x, both within the group's strategic thresholds.
  • Dividend total transfer -- COP 35 trillion returned to the nation via dividends, taxes, and royalties in 2025.
  • Environmental indicators -- Reduced CO2e emissions by 561,000 tons (165% of annual target) and reused 181 million m³ of water (10% increase over 2024).

Risks

  • Alfonso Camilo Munoz cited a "15% annual decline in Brent prices," contributing to a year-over-year decrease in net income, partially offset by efficiency and commercial strategy effects.
  • CFO Alfonso Camilo Muñoz reported that external factors, including tax changes, blockades, and a non-deductible VAT on fuel imports, reduced net income by COP 1 trillion.
  • An unresolved tax controversy with DIAN regarding import VAT (COP 9.6 trillion exposure including penalties/interest) remains pending in court though management asserts a "very low risk to lose this controversy."
  • Operational disruptions from heavy rains and lightning impacted production at Rubiales, Caño Sur, Castilla, and Chichimene fields, though issues have been restored.

Summary

Ecopetrol (EC +1.37%) reported near-record profitability, with operational discipline enabling stable production and efficient cost management despite a sharp Brent price decline. Management delivered a 121% reserves replacement ratio and advanced its energy transition strategy, achieving earlier‑than‑planned renewable milestones and the integration of assets such as Statkraft’s portfolio. The earnings call highlighted disciplined capital allocation and a proposed dividend equaling 50% of net income, with a rigorous focus on maintaining solid liquidity, cost leadership, and prudent debt profiles as external headwinds persisted.

  • CFO Alfonso Camilo Muñoz stated, "efficiency programs delivered a record target of approximately COP 6.6 trillion, exceeding the adjusted annual target of COP 5 trillion by 1.3x."
  • Management confirmed organic CapEx of $6.3 billion was within the investment plan and weighted 63% to core Hydrocarbons projects, ensuring flexibility to adjust based on Brent price scenarios.
  • "By the end of 2025, the Transportation segment posted one of its best historical performance in EBITDA and net income," according to Parra, driven by expanded evacuation capacity and flexible operations.
  • CEO Ricardo Roa clarified no contracts were changed with the National Agency of Hydrocarbons; the accounting of royalties shifted to monetized reserves, "validated not only by SEC, but other methodologies that audited these reserves."
  • Muñoz disclosed the breakeven profit improved to $50 per barrel, with a targeted 2026 breakeven of $46 per barrel including a tax component of "$9 to $10 per barrel."
  • The company reported accumulated energy optimization of 4.79 petajoules in 2025, reaching 99% of its strategic goal ahead of schedule and supporting cost and emissions reductions.
  • Management outlined a disciplined response to external shocks, leveraging commercial strategy, cost control, and hedging to offset much of the negative impact from market and regulatory events.

Industry glossary

  • FEPC: Fondo de Estabilización de Precios de los Combustibles—Colombia’s fuel price stabilization fund, impacting working capital and government transfers in volatile price environments.
  • ISA: Interconexión Eléctrica S.A.—Ecopetrol’s subsidiary operating transmission, power infrastructure, and road concessions, instrumental for the group’s diversification.
  • 1P reserves: Proved reserves—oil and gas quantities with reasonable certainty to be recoverable from known reservoirs under existing economic and operating conditions.
  • Lifting cost: The per-barrel cost required to produce and maintain hydrocarbon wells, exclusive of exploration and capitalized development expenditures.
  • Crude differential: The price spread between Ecopetrol’s crude oil sales and benchmark prices, driven by crude quality, logistics, and market mix.
  • MEM: Colombia's wholesale energy market—where large‑scale electricity contracts are traded to manage cost and supply risks.
