Lincoln (LINC) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 23, 2026 at 11:59AM EST

  • Chief Financial Officer and Executive Vice President — Brian Meyers

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $142.9 million for the quarter, a $25.2 million or 21.4% increase, attributed to a 17% rise in average student population and a 3.7% increase in revenue per student.
  • Student Starts -- Up 15.7% in the quarter, marking the thirteenth consecutive quarter of year-over-year growth with nearly 4,000 new students enrolled.
  • Same Campus/Program Growth -- Existing programs operating over one year grew student starts by 4% on a same campus and same program basis.
  • Segment Performance -- Transportation and Skilled Trades, comprising about 80% of student population, achieved 23.4% start growth and approximately 7.5% organic growth; Healthcare and Other Professions, about 20% of population, declined 2% in starts, aligned with expectations after culinary program exit.
  • Net Income -- Increased by over 70% to $12.7 million.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Reached $29.1 million for the quarter, up 51.2%, with EBITDA margin expanding more than 400 basis points to 20.4%.
  • SG&A Efficiency -- SG&A expenses improved to 49.8% of revenue versus prior 51.6%.
  • Bad Debt Expense -- Lowered to 10.9% of revenue from 13.1%, citing enhanced collections and financial process improvements.
  • Full Year Results -- Revenue grew 19.7% to $518.2 million; average student population rose 17.9%; adjusted EBITDA increased 60% to $67.1 million; and adjusted net income grew 64% to $28.4 million.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $59.3 million generated in the full year, more than doubling the prior year.
  • Ending Cash and Liquidity -- Company finished the year with nearly $29 million in cash and around $90 million in total liquidity; no debt outstanding at year-end.
  • Capital Expenditures (CapEx) -- $88 million in 2025, with 70% allocated to growth initiatives and the remainder to maintenance and equipment.
  • New and Relocated Campuses -- Executed three major campus projects during the year: relocations in Nashville, Tennessee and Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and a new campus opening in Houston, Texas.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Company projects revenue of $580 million–$590 million; adjusted EBITDA of $72 million–$76 million; net income of $20 million–$23 million; student start growth of 8%–13%; and capital expenditures of $70 million–$75 million.
  • EBITDA Calculation Update -- Beginning in 2026, adjusted EBITDA will no longer exclude preopening costs, first-year campus operating losses, or prelaunch expenses; only non-cash stock-based compensation will be added back, increasing EBITDA transparency.
  • Capacity Utilization -- Management stated the company is at approximately 60% capacity utilization, with significant room to increase utilization and margin by adding more student bodies to existing infrastructure.
  • Graduation and Placement Rates -- Graduation rate declined by approximately 200 basis points to 67.5%; placement rate increased by about 250 basis points to 82.8%.
  • High School Channel -- About 20% of students entered directly from high school in 2025; management expects that figure to rise due to expanded initiatives and growing acceptance by school districts.
  • Program Divestiture -- Company exited the culinary and cosmetology programs, focusing on programs with stronger ROI and improved regulatory metrics; all remaining programs passed government gainful employment thresholds.

Summary

Lincoln Educational Services Corporation (LINC +1.54%) reported a substantial increase in quarter and full year revenue, with student start momentum driven by both organic growth and campus expansions. The company emphasized continued robust enrollment in Transportation and Skilled Trades, and noted that recent program divestitures and campus relocations strengthen the operational mix for future periods. Management introduced a new approach to adjusted EBITDA reporting, heightening financial transparency and comparability for investors commencing in 2026. Expansion initiatives, particularly in the high school and workforce partnership channels, provide incremental drivers for medium-term growth as reflected in the company's multi-year guidance targets.

  • Management explicitly guided to 8%–13% student start growth for 2026, supporting double-digit revenue gains despite varying tuition growth and program mix effects.
  • Planned campus developments in Hicksville, New York and Roulette, Texas, as well as select expansions at the East Point and Houston campuses, position the company for geographic and capacity expansion.
  • Leadership highlighted a 70% allocation of 2026 capital expenditures to growth projects, with most spend anticipated in the first half, confirming intention to maintain a no-debt profile by year-end despite interim credit facility use.
  • Placement rate increased to 82.8%, reflecting management's focus on career outcomes for students.
  • All educational programs currently meet or exceed gainful employment standards as assessed by recent government updates.

Industry glossary

  • Student Starts: The number of new students beginning a program in a given period, serving as a primary growth and revenue driver for education providers.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for non-cash stock-based compensation (and, prior to 2026, preopening and startup costs), used to evaluate core operating profitability.
  • Gainful Employment: Federal regulatory standard measuring whether educational programs lead to sufficient earnings for graduates relative to student debt, impacting program eligibility and continued operation.
