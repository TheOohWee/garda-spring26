Earnings Call Transcript

ManpowerGroup (MAN) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 29, 2026 at 10:03AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, January 29, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — Jonas Prising
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jack McGinnis
  • President & Chief Strategy Officer — Becky Frankiewicz

TAKEAWAYS

  • Reported Revenue -- ManpowerGroup (MAN 6.23%) reported $4.7 billion for the quarter, reflecting organic constant currency growth of 2%.
  • System-wide Revenue -- $5.1 billion, incorporating expanding franchise base.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $100 million, down 2% in constant currency; margin was 2.1%, equal to prior year and at guidance midpoint.
  • Full-Year Earnings Per Share -- Negative $0.29 reported; adjusted EPS was $2.97, down 38% in constant currency.
  • Full-Year Revenues -- $18 billion, a 2% constant currency decrease; system-wide of $19.5 billion.
  • Adjusted SG&A Expense -- $686 million, down 4% in constant currency and 3% organically; SG&A as a percentage of revenue at 14.4% in constant currency.
  • Gross Profit Margin -- 16.3% for the quarter, down due to mix shifts toward enterprise accounts, lower permanent recruitment in Europe, and other services.
  • Manpower Brand Performance -- Quarterly revenue up 5% organically, with US segment growing by 7% and maintaining six consecutive quarters of US growth.
  • Experis Brand Performance -- Revenue declined 6% year over year, improving from a 7% decline in the previous quarter.
  • Talent Solutions Performance -- Revenue declined 4% year over year, improving from an 8% decline in the previous quarter; MSP sub-segment grew, while RPO activity declined.
  • Regional Highlights -- Southern Europe revenue grew 1% after thirteen consecutive quarters of decline, with Italy up 7% and France down 3% but sequentially improving for four consecutive months.
  • Asia Pacific Middle East (APME) -- Revenues up 6% organically with Japan, the largest market, up 7%.
  • Cash Flow -- Full-year free cash flow was an outflow of $161 million, compared to an inflow of $258 million last year; Q4 free cash flow reached $168 million.
  • Debt and Liquidity -- Year-end cash of $284 million; total debt $1.1 billion; net debt $806 million; successful refinance of €500 million note.
  • Day Sales Outstanding (DSO) -- Increased to 55 days from 52, attributed to enterprise client mix.
  • Technology Implementation -- PowerSuite operates across nearly 90% of the business; front office 87% complete, back office 75% with Italy going live year-end.
  • AI Tools Impact -- Integrated AI recruiter toolkit expanded to more than 12 markets, resulting in a 7% increase in placement rates.
  • Restructuring Costs -- $13 million in the quarter, related to actions in Spain, France, The Netherlands, Germany, and Peru.
  • Guidance for Q1 2026 -- EPS forecasted in the $0.45-$0.55 range, constant currency revenue guidance of down 1% to up 3%, and projected EBITDA margin up 10 basis points.
  • Tax Guidance -- Full-year global tax rate estimated at 45%; may fall to 43.5%-44% if the US WOTC is renewed and applied retroactively.

RISKS

  • Jack McGinnis said, "Gross profit margin came in just below our guidance range, driven by lower permanent recruitment in Europe," highlighting segment-specific weakness impacting profitability.
  • Full-year 2025 free cash flow reported as an outflow of $161 million, citing the impact of timing items previously benefiting the prior year without repeat in 2025.
  • US Experis Healthcare IT project volumes are expected to decrease in 2026, with management noting "an increased rate of revenue decline compared to the fourth quarter" for the overall US business.
  • Germany reported a 22% revenue decrease year over year, with ongoing weakness and only a potential for improvement in the rate of decline.

SUMMARY

Management highlighted stabilization in key revenue metrics, with sequential improvement across several major regions and targeted ongoing cost reductions. The technology transformation, anchored by PowerSuite and AI deployment, was a central theme with tangible progress in recruiter efficiency and margin aspirations. Market commentary identified enterprise demand and flexible staffing models as persistent drivers, while Europe and North America showed cautious signs of inflection but not a comprehensive recovery.

  • Management reaffirmed commitment to a long-term EBITDA margin target of 4.5%-5%, expecting year-over-year improvement even under modest recovery scenarios.
  • Becky Frankiewicz emphasized, "the intersection of AI and workforce readiness is an urgent priority," as ManpowerGroup aims to differentiate through technology-driven solutions.
  • Asia Pacific and Latin America markets were specifically cited as outperforming, with Japan achieving record profitability and consistent growth.
  • SG&A leverage was achieved through structural and discretionary cost actions, notably shifting Northern Europe from loss to profit in the quarter.
  • Enterprise client expansion contributed to DSO increases but also generated strong year-end cash flow, balancing liquidity pressures with improved collection in Q4.
  • Leadership noted that permanent recruitment softness in Europe continues to weigh on gross profit margins, with enterprise mix largely stabilized and future gross margin upside tied to recovery in higher margin services.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PowerSuite: ManpowerGroup's proprietary, end-to-end operating and technology platform central to global process standardization and AI integration.
  • AgenTiK: ManpowerGroup’s AI coding assistance tool deployed in the Experis brand to enhance technical recruitment and delivery efficiency.
  • SoFi.ai: ManpowerGroup's proprietary AI ecosystem built on PowerSuite to augment human expertise with real-time labor market insights and recruiter tools.
  • MSP (Managed Service Provider): Outsourced management of contingent workforce programs and staffing vendors, typically under the Talent Solutions brand.
  • RPO (Recruitment Process Outsourcing): End-to-end management of clients’ hiring processes, part of Talent Solutions.
  • TAPFIN: ManpowerGroup’s branded MSP offering providing managed workforce solutions at enterprise scale.
  • OUP (Operating Unit Profit): Segment-level profitability metric, referenced by ManpowerGroup in segment reporting.
  • WOTC (Work Opportunity Tax Credit): US federal tax credit referenced by management in ManpowerGroup’s tax rate guidance.
