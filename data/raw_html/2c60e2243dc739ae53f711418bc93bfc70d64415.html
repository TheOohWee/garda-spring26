Asana (ASAN) Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Sep 3, 2025 at 7:27PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Sept. 3, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Co-Founder and Chair of the Board — Dustin Moskovitz
  • Chief Executive Officer — Dan Rogers
  • Chief Operating Officer and Head of Business — Anne Raimondi
  • Chief Financial Officer — Sonalee Parekh
  • Head of Investor Relations — Eva Leung

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • Chief Financial Officer Sonalee Parekh said, “Potential pressure from downgrade activity persists, particularly in the technology vertical, as a larger volume of renewals is concentrated in the second half of fiscal 2026 (ending Jan. 31, 2026). This may result in net retention rate (NRR) reverting to Q1 levels.”
  • Chief Operating Officer Anne Raimondi cited “Top-of-funnel pressure given the evolving search landscape has the potential to weigh on small business customer growth in the second half of fiscal 2026.”

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue-- $196.9 million in Q2 fiscal 2026, up 10% year over year and 1% above the high end of guidance.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin-- Non-GAAP operating margin was 7% for Q2 fiscal 2026, an improvement of almost 1,600 basis points year over year and 240 basis points above the guided midpoint.
  • Core Customer Revenue-- Grew 12% year over year in Q2 fiscal 2026, representing 76% of total GAAP revenue.
  • $100,000+ Customer Cohort-- 770 customers spending $100,000 or more on an annualized basis in Q2 fiscal 2026, up 19% year over year, with net adds in this group also up 19%.
  • International Revenue-- Rose 13% year over year for Q2 fiscal 2026, outpacing US growth of 8%.
  • Net Retention Rate (NRR)-- Overall and core customer NRR at 96% for Q2 fiscal 2026. $100,000+ NRR was 95%, stable sequentially. In-quarter NRR improved for the overall and core cohort, while the $100,000+ cohort declined mainly due to a large renewal.
  • Gross Margin-- Gross margin held steady at 90% in Q2 fiscal 2026 and is expected to remain at this level for fiscal 2026.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow-- $35.4 million, or 18% on a margin basis, for Q2 fiscal 2026 (non-GAAP).
  • Share Repurchase-- Repurchased $27.8 million of Class A common stock (nearly 2 million shares) at an average price of $14.20 in Q2 fiscal 2026. $128 million remains authorized.
  • AI Studio ARR-- More than doubled quarter over quarter in Q2 fiscal 2026, with strengthening adoption in new and existing customer workflows.
  • Foundational Service Plan (FSP) Adoption-- Customers adopting FSPs increased seat utilization by 20% within three months of adoption.
  • Guidance Update-- Fiscal 2026 revenue guidance is now $780 million to $790 million, representing 8%-9% annual growth, with non-GAAP operating income forecast of $46-$50 million, or a 6% margin.
  • RPO and Deferred Revenue-- Remaining performance obligations (GAAP) were $507.3 million (up 29%) for Q2 fiscal 2026, with 75% to be recognized in the next 12 months. Deferred revenue at the end of Q2 fiscal 2026 was $313.6 million, up 8%.
  • SMB Business Performance-- Continued double-digit growth in SMB for Q2 fiscal 2026, but management guided for ongoing headwinds from AI-driven search trends impacting traffic and small business acquisition, as reflected in updated Q3 and fiscal 2026 guidance.

SUMMARY

Asana's (ASAN 4.71%) management indicated that AI Studio's rapid adoption is accelerating expansion opportunities, particularly through cross-functional workflows and packaged smart workflow templates, as discussed in Q2 fiscal 2026. Leadership highlighted broad international and non-tech sector momentum in Q2 fiscal 2026, with notable large-scale customer expansions in financial services, manufacturing, and retail. A higher proportion of customer renewals in the technology sector is set for the second half of fiscal 2026, introducing concentrated NRR risk and renewal-related downgrade exposure. Strategic resource shifts, such as cost optimization and shifts to more cost-effective hiring locations, contributed to significant margin expansion while supporting continued investment in go-to-market initiatives and AI capabilities.

  • Chief Executive Officer Dan Rogers said, “We've more than doubled our AI Studio ARR quarter over quarter in Q2 fiscal 2026,” emphasizing that prebuilt agents and AI teammates are planned to deepen platform engagement and measurable ROI.
  • Chief Financial Officer Sonalee Parekh said, “We delivered a 7% non-GAAP operating margin, or $14 million of non-GAAP operating income in Q2 fiscal 2026,” highlighting disciplined spending and operating leverage as key drivers of profitability.
  • Chief Operating Officer Anne Raimondi noted “Self-serve” remains a major growth driver, with roughly 40% of customers purchasing through this channel in Q2 fiscal 2026, and early adoption of AI Studio Plus exceeding initial tier limits.
  • Management noted that customer expansion examples include Morningstar reporting cost savings of over $600,000 and nearly 15,000 annual person-hours from AI Studio-powered productivity gains.
  • International event attendance and pipeline generation more than doubled in both Sydney (early May) and Tokyo (early August), supporting growth in Japan and the EMEA regions.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Work Graph: Asana's proprietary structured model for mapping relationships between projects, tasks, goals, and teams to enable contextual workflow management.
  • AI Studio: Asana's no-code platform for building, deploying, and scaling AI-powered workflow automation within customer environments.
  • Smart Workflow Gallery: A suite of prebuilt, AI-powered automation templates available within Asana to accelerate workflow creation and adoption.
  • Foundational Service Plan (FSP): A service plan designed to boost customer health and retention by increasing seat utilization and engagement with Asana features.
  • Agentic Enterprise: A term used by Asana referencing organizational workflows enabled by context-aware, embedded AI agents working alongside human users.
