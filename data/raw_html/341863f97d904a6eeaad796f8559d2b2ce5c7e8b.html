Sonos (SONO) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 3, 2026 at 5:25PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 3, 2026, 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Tom Conrad
  • Chief Financial Officer — Saori Casey

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $546 million, down 1% year over year, finishing above the midpoint of guidance (guided range: down 7% to up 2%).
  • GAAP gross profit -- Up 5% year over year, supported by a nearly 300 basis-point (bps) improvement in GAAP gross margin, driven by lower costs, favorable FX, and one-time items, partially offset by unfavorable product mix.
  • GAAP gross margin -- 46.5% in the quarter; non-GAAP gross margin reached 47.5%, both modestly above the high end of guidance and up nearly 300 bps year over year.
  • Operating expenses -- GAAP operating expenses were $153 million, down 21% year over year; non-GAAP operating expenses were $137 million, down 19% year over year, with Q1 expenses noted as unseasonably low due to the timing of product launches.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $132 million, up 45% year over year; represents as much as generated in all of fiscal 2025, with margin expanding 760 bps to 24.2%, the highest in four years.
  • Non-GAAP earnings per share -- $0.93, up 37% from $0.68 year over year.
  • Share repurchases -- $25 million deployed in the quarter at an average price of $16.79, reducing share count by 1.2%, with $105 million remaining under authorization.
  • Net cash balance -- Ended at $363 million, including $51 million of marketable securities; period-end inventory was $125 million, down 11% year over year and 27% sequentially.
  • Free cash flow -- $157 million, up from $143 million year over year; CapEx was $6 million, down from $13 million.
  • Regional revenue performance -- Americas up 1% year over year; EMEA down 4%; APAC down 5%; growth markets continued to outperform the rest of business.
  • Product segment performance -- Plug-ins segment achieved double-digit growth, led by ERA 100, which drove three consecutive quarters of accelerating new customer growth after a price reduction; Arrow 100 new customer starts up over 40% year over year in Q1.
  • Installer channel contribution -- Installer and integrator partners represent 22% of business, and are positioned for further expansion following the Sonos Amp Multi launch.
  • Q2 guidance -- Revenue projected between $250 million and $280 million (down 4% to up 8% year over year); midpoint does not include contribution from AMP Multi, which is not generally available in Q2.
  • Q2 gross margin guidance -- GAAP gross margin of 44%-46%; non-GAAP approximately 220 bps higher; guidance embeds higher memory costs.
  • Q2 operating expenses guidance -- GAAP operating expenses expected between $150 million and $160 million, down 11% at midpoint year over year; non-GAAP to be about $16 million lower than GAAP.
  • Q2 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- Range of negative $18 million to positive $10 million; first half adjusted EBITDA implied at $128 million, up 42% year over year.
  • Full-year 2026 guidance -- Revenue expected between $796 million and $826 million, flat at midpoint year over year; GAAP operating expenses guided down 16%; non-GAAP operating expenses guided down 9%.
  • Product roadmap -- New product launches planned for later in 2026, with Sonos Amp Multi recently introduced to professional installers, and further product launches set for the back half of the year.
  • Market share -- Company claims approximately 6% share of the $24 billion global premium audio market, with continued dollar share gains in premium home theater in the US, and EMEA this quarter.
  • Customer lifetime value upside -- Management estimates moving from current average of 4.5 devices per multi-product household to six per household could generate an incremental $5 billion in revenue, while converting single-product households to multi-product could represent a $7 billion opportunity.
  • Tariffs and cost environment -- Approximately 300 bps headwind to gross margin in the quarter from tariffs, largely mitigated by tactical pricing actions; memory cost inflation remains a headwind, but is contained due to low memory requirements per product and supplier diversification.

Risks

  • Chief Financial Officer Saori Casey stated, "tariff expense was an approximately 300 basis point headwind to gross margin, which we were able to offset with mitigation actions led by the pricing adjustments," indicating persistent external cost pressures.
  • Casey noted that guidance for Q2 gross margin "embeds higher memory costs," with management confirming ongoing inflation in memory pricing.
  • Guidance projects Q2 adjusted EBITDA as low as negative $18 million, reflecting anticipated operating expense increases and seasonal revenue declines.
  • Casey described Q1 operating expenses as "unseasonably low due to timing of product launches and associated spend," implying that expense increases are expected, and may pressure profitability in subsequent quarters.

Summary

Sonos (SONO 0.54%) delivered $546 million in revenue during the first quarter, with adjusted EBITDA matching the total of all fiscal 2025, and a significant year over year operating margin expansion. Geographic results were mixed, as the Americas achieved modest growth, while EMEA and APAC experienced declines, and installer channel revenues now comprise 22% of business, indicating ongoing strategic emphasis on professional partnerships. Management expects new product introductions, including Amp Multi and additional launches later in 2026, to accelerate revenue trends in the back half of the year, while full-year guidance remains flat at the midpoint, citing flat to modest sequential revenue and gross profit growth for the first half. Cost mitigation from tariffs and memory price inflation remains a focus, with gross margin management centered on supplier actions and tactical pricing levers.

  • Management intends to expand the Sonos system's value proposition with a unified hardware-software ecosystem, and tap into emerging trends such as conversational AI, aiming for durable, repeatable growth.
  • Customer acquisition via Arrow 100 has continued to accelerate for three consecutive quarters, supported by pricing actions, and customer cohorts starting with Arrow 100 show high repeat purchase behavior.
  • Sonos has outlined an explicit five-dimension growth strategy: product innovation, customer advocacy, intentional marketing, geographic expansion, and capturing demand from external trends, with each lever viewed as compounding and interdependent.
  • Share repurchases in Q1 reduced share count by 1.2%, reflecting ongoing capital return efforts within a $105 million remaining authorization.
  • Inventory declined 11% year over year, and 27% sequentially, pointing to tighter operational discipline as new product launches approach in the back half of 2026.

Industry glossary

  • Plug-ins segment: Sonos’s business line comprising certain expandable audio devices and accessories, often driving multi-room installations.
  • Installer channel: The segment of sales and partnerships with professional audio integrators, custom home installers, and system designers who incorporate Sonos products into large-scale or custom home projects.
  • ERA 100 / Arrow 100: Product names referencing Sonos’s entry-level plug-in speakers positioned as customer acquisition and expansion drivers.
  • Amp Multi: A recently launched Sonos amplifier designed specifically for professional installers, enabling multi-zone audio distribution and integrated system management for sophisticated residential audio environments.
  • Channel fill: A temporary inventory buildup at distributors or retailers associated with product launches, used in Sonos’s transcript to explain year-over-year product segment comparability.
