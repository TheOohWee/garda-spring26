Centerspace (CSR) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 18, 2026 at 10:59AM EST

Date

Feb. 18, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Anne M. Olson
  • Senior Vice President, Investments and Capital Markets — Grant P. Campbell
  • Chief Financial Officer — Bhairav Patel

Takeaways

  • Strategic review -- The ongoing Board-led evaluation of strategic alternatives remains active, with no assurance of any resulting transaction or timetable provided.
  • Same-store NOI growth -- Achieved 3.5% annual growth, which management stated “outpaced peers” and was attributed to steady occupancy and expense discipline.
  • Q4 same-store NOI -- Increased 4.8% year over year, as reported by Bhairav Patel.
  • Q4 same-store revenue -- Rose by 1% year over year, driven by a 1.5% increase in average monthly revenue per occupied home, partially offset by a 40 basis-point decline in occupancy.
  • Same-store expense trend -- Decreased by 5.1% year over year in Q4, with controllable and non-controllable expense reductions, including lower repairs, maintenance, administrative, and marketing costs.
  • Rent dynamics -- Q4 blended leasing spreads were up 10 basis points, as new lease spreads fell 4.8% while renewals rose 3.9%, producing a blended rate in positive territory.
  • Retention metrics -- Quarterly retention was 55.2% (full-year 58.2%), demonstrating relative affordability among residents according to management.
  • Market segmentation -- North Dakota led with 4.5% blended rent increases; Minneapolis posted a 1.1% increase; Denver saw a 4.3% decrease in blended rent trade-outs for Q4.
  • Denver supply & absorption -- 2025 marked the second-highest absorption year in the post-pandemic era, but continued elevated new supply pressured rents.
  • 2025 transaction activity -- Executed $493 million in transactions, with entry into Salt Lake City, expansion in Fort Collins, exit from Saint Cloud, MN, and selective pruning in Minneapolis.
  • Balance sheet actions -- Expanded unsecured credit facility by $150 million and assumed $76 million of long-term debt, which strengthened liquidity and the debt profile.
  • Share repurchases -- Repurchased 3.5 million shares in 2025 “reinforcing [the company’s] belief in the value of [its] stock.”
  • Full-year 2026 guidance -- Targets core FFO per diluted share of $4.93 at the midpoint, with same-store NOI up 75 basis points, revenue up 88 basis points, and expenses up 150 basis points (all midpoint).
  • Revenue guidance assumptions -- Assumes blended leasing spreads of approximately 2%, mid-95% occupancy, and retention near 52%.
  • Regulatory impact in Colorado -- Company expects a $1 million year-over-year decline in expense recoveries (RUBS) in 2026 due to regulatory changes.
  • CapEx plans -- Forecasts value-add CapEx of $2.5 million to $12.5 million, with recurring CapEx per home at a $1,300 midpoint, partially limited by “higher cost of capital and execution risk.”
  • Leverage & liquidity -- Net debt to EBITDA improved to 7.5x; weighted average interest rate is 3.6% with 6.9 years average maturity; liquidity is nearly $268 million versus $99.2 million of debt maturing in the next two years.
  • Labor & turnover -- On-site compensation was flat to down in Q4; management attributed this to “less turnover, more steadiness in that employment,” along with lower vendor turnover leading to stable operations.
  • Bad debt and rent affordability -- Bad debt remained steady, and management said, “our rent-to-income has held steady, if not lowered slightly,” due to resident income growth outpacing rent increases.

Risks

  • Bhairav Patel stated, regulatory changes are expected to temper revenue growth in our Colorado portfolio, with expense recoveries expected to be down nearly $1,000,000.
  • Denver faces continued concessionary pressure into 2026 with two to four weeks of concessions on a per-move-in basis, and blended spreads are expected to be down for the year.
  • Retention rate forecast for 2026 is lower at around 52%; management described this as being measured amid occupancy declines in Q4 and sequential/year-over-year dips.
  • Value-add CapEx range narrowed by management due to higher cost of capital and execution risk, and ongoing strategic alternatives review, causing deferred or selective capital deployment.

Summary

Centerspace (CSR +3.14%) closed the year with Q4 same-store NOI up 4.8% and reported a 3.5% annual increase, leveraging expense reductions and steady portfolio performance driven by strength in Midwest markets. The ongoing strategic review continues without further disclosure, while 2025 portfolio activity included $493 million of transactions, selective market pruning, and substantial share repurchases. Guidance for 2026 centers on stable core FFO of $4.93 per share, with limited revenue growth (mid-1% blended leasing spreads) and expense guidance reflecting higher controllables and regulatory headwinds, particularly in Colorado. Management emphasized the strength of the balance sheet, improved leverage profile post-dispositions, and robust liquidity compared to near-term debt maturities.

  • The company completed a major expansion of its unsecured credit facility and assumed long-term debt at attractive rates to bolster liquidity.
  • Operational retention continued trending downward, with a cautious outlook for 2026 reflecting both recent and forecasted turnover pressures.
  • Denver remains a challenged market due to elevated supply and concessions, while North Dakota and other Midwest markets are expected to offset regional softness.
  • Management cited ongoing unpredictability from regulatory developments as a key driver of both revenue and expense variability, particularly in Colorado.

Industry glossary

  • NOI (Net Operating Income): Rental income minus operating expenses (excluding interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization), a key measure of property-level profitability for REITs.
  • FFO (Funds From Operations): A cash flow metric for REITs that adjusts net income by adding back non-cash depreciation and amortization, and excluding gains or losses from property sales.
  • RUBS (Ratio Utility Billing System): A method of allocating utility costs to tenants based on occupancy or square footage instead of direct metering.
  • Blended leasing spreads: The weighted average percentage change in rent for both new leases and renewals compared to previous lease terms.
  • CapEx (Capital Expenditures): Investments in property improvements, renovations, or routine replacements, differentiated as value-add (to upgrade or reposition properties) or recurring (to maintain current operations).
