RISKS

  • CEO Choi said, "We fell significantly short of guidance," with the Americas segment responsible for the shortfall and a $150 million Q4 miss.
  • Commercial rental days in the Americas dropped 11% in November due to FAA flight reductions, air traffic control disruptions, and TSA delays.
  • Americas RPD deterioration of 3.7% exceeded guided decline, as industry overcapacity and lack of length-of-rent restrictions depressed pricing.
  • "recalls became a more meaningful operational and financial headwind than we anticipated," with 14,000 vehicles grounded and $40 million direct impact in Q4 alone.
  • $50 million deliberate increase to PLPD insurance reserves, with underlying "new incident trends are improving," but baseline reset to manage future risk.
  • Depreciation expense remains elevated in early 2026 as the "catch-up" for overestimated residual values, especially for the Americas fleet subject to used vehicle price swings.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $748 million for the full year, under original guidance by approximately $150 million due entirely to performance in the Americas segment.
  • Americas Rental Days -- Expected growth of 3% turned to flat volume as November’s 11% decline in commercial days reversed earlier trends.
  • Net Depreciation Per Unit (Americas) -- $338 monthly in the fourth quarter, exceeding initial estimates well below $300, as used vehicle prices dropped by nearly $1,000, or 4.3%, from October to November before partially recovering in December.
  • Revenue Per Day (RPD, Americas) -- Decreased by 3.7%, versus guidance of an approximate 2% decline, driven by industry-wide demand weakness and excess supply.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Miss Attribution -- $40 million from lower rental days and weaker RPD, $60 million from higher gross depreciation and lower gains on sale, and approximately $50 million from a deliberate year-end increase in PLPD insurance reserves.
  • EV Fleet Write-Down -- Approximately $500 million impairment recorded, coinciding with monetization of $180 million in previously unusable federal EV tax credits and an accelerated reduction of EV economic life from 36 to 18 months.
  • Fleet Disposition Activity -- Record vehicle sales in January and continued high volumes in February, with expectations for ongoing elevated activity through the March–April tax refund period.
  • Recalls Impact -- 14,000 grounded vehicles at quarter-end; $40 million direct cost in the fourth quarter from depreciation, interest, and parking, before accounting for lost profits and gains on sale.
  • Global Workforce Reduction -- One-time, company-wide action implemented in January to align organizational structure with the 2026 plan and support a leaner operating model.
  • Business Portfolio Actions -- Zipcar UK exited in December; U.S. Zipcar operations restructured in January for sustainability, with further reviews of non-core segments planned.
  • OEM Partnership Strategy -- 2026 plan includes rebalancing fleet sourcing, prioritizing OEMs with consistent execution and reliability, and reducing volume to partners not meeting those standards.
  • First Quarter Expectations -- Elevated depreciation per unit ($400 monthly) will generate lower EBITDA, offsetting more stable revenue conditions; performance expected to normalize in subsequent quarters.
  • 2026 Operational Focus -- Emphasizing higher fleet utilization and disciplined capital allocation to control earnings volatility and prioritize margin durability.
  • Americas Revenue Outlook -- 2026 EBITDA guidance assumes modest revenue growth in the Americas following three years of segment decline.

SUMMARY

Avis Budget Group (CAR +0.96%) reported a material adjusted EBITDA shortfall, missing full-year guidance by approximately $150 million as Americas performance lagged and was impacted by demand volatility, unfavorable used car markets, and insurance reserve increases. Segment-specific rental day weakness coincided with rapid pricing deterioration, compounded by a $500 million year-end EV fleet impairment that generated $180 million from monetized tax credits but reset future depreciation. Management outlined a multi-pronged 2026 strategy emphasizing tight fleet utilization, rigorous cost control including organization-wide layoffs, and more selective OEM relationships, alongside the restructuring or exit of non-core businesses.

  • Choi said, "as a deliberate reset that strengthened our balance sheet and reduced future risk," describing the EV impairment and related tax credit transaction.
  • Depreciation per unit is expected to fall into the low-$300 range after a first-quarter reset at $400, with models now calibrated to reflect current used car market conditions rather than previous tariff-influenced estimates.
  • Brian J. Choi stated, "we are prioritizing utilization over fleet growth," marking a strategic shift from past practices that emphasized maximum vehicle availability during periods of high demand.
  • The leadership team’s first full-year plan, built under new operating philosophies, assumes Americas revenue will grow in 2026, reversing three years of decline.
  • Actions taken, including a global reduction in force and renewed performance management rigor, are intended to fund customer experience initiatives and capital investments without increasing cost base volatility.
  • Expansion of the Avis First premium offering to commercial clients and continued partnership with Waymo, whose Dallas autonomous program is soon to serve public riders, remain core elements of long-term strategic execution.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RPD (Revenue Per Day): Average daily revenue earned from rental transactions, a key pricing and utilization metric in rental fleet operations.
  • PLPD (Personal Liability and Property Damage): Insurance reserves set aside for claims related to liability and property damage occurring during vehicle rentals.
  • DPU (Depreciation Per Unit): The average monthly depreciation expense recorded per owned vehicle in the active rental fleet.
  • OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer): Automakers from whom the company sources vehicles for its rental fleet.
  • Manheim Index: A wholesale used-vehicle price index published by Manheim, widely used to benchmark fleet asset values in the U.S. rental industry.
