MiMedx (MDXG) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 25, 2026 at 5:43PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 25, 2026, 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Joseph H. Capper
  • Chief Financial Officer — Douglas C. Rice
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Matthew Notarianni

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $118 million for the quarter, representing 27% year-over-year growth, with wound and surgical categories both growing at or above 25%.
  • Adjusted Gross Profit Margin -- 86% for the quarter, up approximately 200 basis points compared to the prior-year period due to product mix.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $29 million for the quarter, or 25% of net sales; full-year adjusted EBITDA was nearly $106 million, with a margin over 25%.
  • GAAP Net Income -- $15 million, or $0.10 per diluted share, for the quarter, compared to $7 million, or $0.05 per diluted share, in the prior-year period.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $25 million generated in the quarter, resulting in an end-of-year net cash position of $148 million, a sequential increase of $24 million.
  • Wound Product Sales -- $79 million for the quarter, up 28%, driven by demand for new products EpiExpress and Emerge.
  • Surgical Product Sales -- $39 million in the quarter, up 25%, led by AmnioFix, AmnioEffect, and particulate products.
  • GAAP Gross Margin -- 84% for the quarter, compared to 82% in the prior year.
  • Sales and Marketing Expenses -- $61 million, or 52% of net sales, up from $48 million in the prior-year period due to higher commissions.
  • R&D Expenses -- $5 million, or 4% of net sales for the quarter, up 33%, reflecting costs for randomized controlled trials (RCTs), and new product development.
  • Medicare Reimbursement Changes -- A price cap of $127 per square centimeter for skin substitutes was instituted, impacting wound care market dynamics, and causing provider and volume disruptions.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- Up to $100 million authorized for stock buybacks over two years, providing additional capital allocation flexibility.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Full-year revenue expected in the $340 million to $360 million range; adjusted EBITDA margin projected in the mid- to high-teens; gross margin anticipated in the mid- to upper-70% range.
  • Surgical Specialty Expansion -- Surgical portfolio recently expanded by the launch of AmnioFix Thyroid Shield, and the licensing of three 510(k)-cleared products for surgical applications.
  • EpiEffect RCT -- Enrollment for the EpiEffect randomized controlled trial is nearly complete, with a final readout and publication expected in the coming months.

  • "claims processing has slowed to a trickle as providers adjust to the new prior authorization requirements." within WISER model states, contributing to stagnant wound care volumes.
  • "some providers have completely shut down their businesses." and Some products are being dumped in the market at very low prices, causing even more chaos.
  • Management expects "quarterly revenue to be the lowest in Q1 with substantial increases in each successive quarter," specifically highlighting near-term pressure on top-line performance due to payer changes and market adjustment delays.

SUMMARY

MiMedx Group (MDXG 3.60%) delivered record quarterly and full-year results in revenue and EBITDA, driven by both wound care and surgical segments. Management acknowledged acute short-term disruption following the Medicare reimbursement reset for skin substitutes, causing lower Q1 volumes, decelerated claims, and competitive price concessions in wound care. Strategic execution resulted in bolstered liquidity, with a $148 million net cash balance, and an authorized $100 million share repurchase program to enhance capital deployment options.

  • Leadership anticipates significant sequential revenue growth quarter by quarter in 2026, as the wound care market stabilizes, reimbursement processes adjust, and new product rollouts gain traction.
  • Investments in R&D continue, with a near-completed randomized controlled trial for EpiEffect, and a new RCT for ChorioFix planned to support long-term differentiation in clinical efficacy.
  • Expansion in surgical business is supported by new commercial hires and portfolio additions, including AmnioFix Thyroid Shield and collagen-based wound matrices, targeting high-growth specialties.
  • Management provided initial 2026 guidance, expecting full-year revenue between $340 million and $360 million, operating margins to reset lower in the transition, and recovery to historical double-digit top-line growth by 2027.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PURION process: Proprietary tissue processing method designed to preserve biological activity during placental tissue allograft preparation.
  • 510(k)-cleared: U.S. Food and Drug Administration process for premarket notification, permitting medical devices to be marketed based on substantial equivalence to an already legally marketed device.
  • EpiEffect RCT: Company-sponsored randomized controlled trial evaluating the efficacy of the EpiEffect wound care product.
  • WISER model: A reimbursement framework requiring prior authorization for certain therapies, implemented in select U.S. states and referenced for its impact on claims processing speed.
  • LCD: Local Coverage Determination; a decision by a Medicare Administrative Contractor regarding whether a particular service or item is covered in their jurisdiction.
  • OPPS: Outpatient Prospective Payment System, Medicare's reimbursement methodology for outpatient hospital services.

This surgery carries inherent risk due to the proximity of the recurrent laryngeal nerve and the parathyroid glands, which can be vulnerable to injury. Damage to the laryngeal nerve can result in significant complications including loss of voice and an increased risk of aspirating food or fluid, which can in turn lead to serious respiratory complications such as aspiration pneumonia, posing additional health risk and prolonging patient recovery.

Equally important are the parathyroid glands, which play a critical role in maintaining calcium balance in the body. Under conditions of surgical stress, these glands can become temporarily dormant, leading to pathologically low serum calcium levels or hypocalcemia. While calcium levels may gradually recover, in some cases, they fail to normalize, necessitating lifelong calcium supplementation. Slow recovery of parathyroid function can also extend hospital stays, increase healthcare costs, and delay return to normal activity. Recent evidence highlights the efficacy of AmnioFix Thyroid Shield as a protective adjunct in thyroid surgery, significantly reducing or even eliminating postoperative complications.

In cases where the nerve injury does occur, use of AmnioFix Thyroid Shield appears to accelerate the restoration of normal vocal and swallowing functions. Additionally, the use of AmnioFix FibroLyse Shield has been shown to minimize parathyroid gland damage during thyroidectomy. This protective effect promotes a faster recovery of the parathyroid function and helps restore blood calcium levels to normal more rapidly. Such benefits not only improve patient outcomes, but also reduce the likelihood of extended hospitalizations, offering both clinical and economic advantages. In summary, AmnioFix Thyroid Shield represents a valuable innovation in thyroid surgery, providing critical protection for both the recurrent laryngeal nerve and parathyroid glands.
