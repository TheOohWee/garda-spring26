Skillsoft (SKIL) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 9:45AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, June 9, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Executive Chair and Chief Executive Officer — Ronald W. Hovsepian
  • Chief Financial Officer — John Wilbert Frederick
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations & Corporate Development — Stephen Poe

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $124.2 million, representing a 2.8% decrease year over year.
  • TDS Revenue -- $99.1 million, up 1% over the prior year.
  • Global Knowledge (GK) Revenue -- $25.1 million, down $4.7 million or 15.7% year over year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $22.1 million, which increased by $3.2 million from the prior year and reached 17.8% of revenue versus 14.8% last year.
  • GAAP Net Loss -- $38 million compared to $27.6 million in the previous year; net loss per share was $4.57 versus $3.42.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $2.5 million compared to an adjusted net loss of $390,000 a year ago; adjusted net income per share was $0.30 against an adjusted net loss per share of $0.05.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $26.2 million, an increase of $15.8 million from $10.4 million in the prior year.
  • TDS LTM Dollar Retention Rate (DRR) -- 99%, compared to 100% last quarter and 99% a year ago.
  • Total Operating Expenses -- $102.1 million, down $6.8 million or 6.2% from the prior year.
  • Cost of Revenue -- $32 million, a 6.5% decrease year over year, with variable cost reductions cited.
  • Cash, Cash Equivalents, and Restricted Cash -- $131 million at quarter end.
  • Total Gross Debt -- $580 million at quarter end, down from approximately $581 million at fiscal year-end.
  • Total Net Debt -- $449 million, an improvement from approximately $477 million at the end of the fourth quarter.
  • Fiscal 2026 Guidance Reiterated -- Revenue expected in a range of $530 million to $545 million; adjusted EBITDA targeted between $112 million and $118 million.
  • Fiscal 2026 Free Cash Flow Guidance -- Projected in the $13 million to $18 million range.
  • Leadership Changes -- Appointment of John Wilbert Frederick as CFO and Raianne Reiss as Chief Marketing Officer, with a stated focus on accelerating go-to-market strategy.
  • Cost Reductions -- Management described "cost reduction initiatives that we executed in the back half of last year" as driving year-over-year operating expense improvements.
  • AI Product Expansion -- Launch of CAISY, an "AI-powered coach" now supporting more than 40 languages, and release of the Percipio certification dashboard.
  • International Public Sector -- Management noted growth in "large-scale non-U.S. public sector deals" within Global Knowledge.
  • Seasonality -- Frederick stated the first quarter is "our highest seasonal free cash flow quarter," with the second quarter "traditionally our lowest" and warned much of the Q1 benefit will reverse in Q2.
  • TDS Revenue Growth Trend -- Leadership referenced "3 consecutive quarters of revenue growth" in TDS.
  • Product and Go-To-Market Investment -- Management said, "With reinvestment happening predominantly in the first half of this fiscal year, we expect to see positive free cash flow and top line growth for the full fiscal year."

RISKS

  • Management cited "macro uncertainty, which translated into lower discretionary spending by our customers and slightly elongated decision-making," resulting in lower live learning revenue, particularly in the U.S. public sector.
  • Frederick cautioned, "in Q1, we benefited from the timing of collections and certain disbursements and would expect to see much of that benefit reverse in the second quarter, in fact, causing a cash usage in the second quarter."
  • Hovsepian stated, "certain verticals are experiencing heightened customer caution," and added, "we are seeing some elongation in decision-making and softening in discretionary spend."
  • Federal sector live learning revenue was described as primarily impacted by "the hit we took came from the federal sector, right? That came from U.S. federal sector."

SUMMARY

Skillsoft Corp. (SKIL +5.21%) reported a single-digit revenue decline and continued reductions in operating expenses, while generating positive non-GAAP adjusted net income and substantially higher free cash flow. The shift of customer discretionary spending and soft decision-making cycles was cited as leading to weaker U.S. public sector demand, with management flagging a seasonal reversal of cash flow strength in the following quarter. New AI tools and dashboard features were introduced to differentiate the Percipio platform, aiming to support future top-line performance as product and go-to-market investments increase through the first half of the year.

  • Leadership highlighted a completed resource reallocation program, with recurring TDS revenue described as a key source of visibility entering future quarters.
  • Management underscored their confidence in maintaining full-year guidance contingent on macro stabilization, emphasizing deal pipeline strength in Global Knowledge.
  • The addition of a new Chief Marketing Officer is intended to improve demand generation and brand awareness as the company advances its growth plan.
  • Investments targeting sales coverage and marketing are expected to yield multi-year benefits, with management replying that, "in the back half, that's where we start to see the benefits from what should be a multi-year set of benefits."
  • Federal and public sector conditions remain fluid, and potential policy impacts are stated as areas under close ongoing review.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Talent Development Solutions (TDS): A business segment focused on comprehensive talent management and skill-building services for organizations.
  • Global Knowledge (GK): A segment offering live instructor-led training and related digital learning products.
  • CAISY: Skillsoft's proprietary artificial intelligence–powered coaching tool supporting multilingual learner interaction.
  • DRR (Dollar Retention Rate): The percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers during a trailing twelve–month period, excluding the impact of new or lost customers.
