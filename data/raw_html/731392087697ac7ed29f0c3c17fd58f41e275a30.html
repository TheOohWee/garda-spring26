Ballard Power (BLDP) Q3 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 13, 2025 at 1:34PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, November 13, 2025 at 11:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Marty Neese
  • Chief Financial Officer — Kate Igbalode
  • Chief Operating Officer — Sumit Kundu

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $32.5 million, representing a 120% year-over-year increase, primarily driven by bus and rail segment deliveries.
  • Bus Segment Revenue Concentration -- Bus and rail accounted for more than 70% of total revenue, highlighting the strategic importance of these markets.
  • Net Order Intake -- Approximately $19 million in new orders added during the quarter.
  • Gross Margin -- Positive 15%, marking a sequential and year-over-year improvement, supported by reduced product costs and a net reduction in onerous contract provisions, though management noted this includes one-time benefits.
  • Excluding One-Time Items -- Gross margin would have been slightly negative, but this reflects continued progress relative to the prior year and quarter.
  • Total Operating Expenses -- $34.9 million, down 36% year-over-year, or 55% lower when excluding restructuring costs.
  • Cash Operating Costs -- Decreased 40% year-over-year due to restructuring actions and ongoing cost management.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Negative $31.2 million, improved from negative $60.1 million in the previous year, demonstrating material sequential progress.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $22.9 million used in operations, a reduction from $28.6 million in the comparable period.
  • Ending Cash Position -- $25.7 million in cash and cash equivalents with no bank debt or near-term financing requirements.
  • Capital Expenditures Guidance -- Now expected at $8 million to $12 million, lowered from prior $15 million to $25 million guidance, reflecting deferred facility investments and capital discipline.
  • Largest Marine Order To Date -- A single 6.4 megawatt order to ECAP and Samskip placed during the quarter in the marine segment.
  • Strategic Facility Decision -- Decision not to pursue development of the Texas Gigafactory, with management citing current global capacity sufficiency and a commitment to capital discipline.
  • China JV Reduction -- Further reduction in involvement with the Weichai Ballard joint venture in China, reallocating resources to North America and Europe.
  • Service Offerings Expansion -- Introduction of enhanced service offerings, including digital maintenance, extended warranties, and technical training, designed to strengthen customer value and engagement.

SUMMARY

Ballard Power Systems (BLDP +6.25%) management stated the commercial bus and rail segments were the primary revenue drivers, reflecting strategic market alignment and positive customer response. The company highlighted successful product innovation, including the launch of the next-generation FCmove SC, garnering early positive feedback from OEMs. Progress in core stack life and operational durability was emphasized, alongside the successful debut of the DNV-certified FCwave product in marine applications. Capital discipline was addressed via deferral of large-scale investment in Texas and lowering of current year capital expenditure estimates while retaining a cash buffer. Management reiterated the focus on margin expansion, operational cost reduction, and long-term movement towards cash flow positivity, although the path remains ongoing.

  • Kate Igbalode stated, "Gross margin improved to 15%," which management clarified was bolstered by one-time items and is not likely to be a ratable baseline.
  • Chief Financial Officer Igbalode forecasted that, "one time benefits, our gross margin would be slightly negative," but confirmed sequential improvement and projected "low to mid single digits on our gross margin" for 2026.
  • Management reported Ballard is re-entering the material handling market, offering an air-cooled fuel cell stack with "at least 2x the state of the art" in durability compared to competitors.
  • Sumit Kundu identified that "order pipeline is taking additional time as we work with current customers to secure more sustainable contract terms," with some orders shifting to later quarters for long-term sustainability.
  • Chief Financial Officer Igbalode announced operating expenses (excluding restructuring) are now expected to be below the low end of prior guidance range, further tightening the cost base.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FCmove SC: Ballard's next-generation fuel cell engine module designed for higher power density, compact size, and improved operational efficiency in bus applications.
  • FCwave: Ballard's fuel cell engine product certified for marine use under DNV (Det Norske Veritas), designed for large vessel propulsion and auxiliary marine power.
  • Onerous contract provisions: Balance sheet reserves or adjustments for contracts expected to generate losses, due to delivery at costs exceeding the revenue recognized.
