Lennar (LEN) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 13, 2026 at 2:55PM EST

Date

Friday, March 13, 2026 at 11:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Home deliveries -- 18,515 homes delivered, with average sales price at $374,000, down 8% year over year.
  • Gross margin -- 15.2%, attributed to improving discipline across construction, land, and overhead.
  • Net margin -- 5.3%, resulting in net income of $229 million and earnings per share of $0.93.
  • SG&A -- 9.8% of revenue, described as slightly above expectations.
  • Sales incentives -- 14.1% on deliveries, nearly unchanged from the previous quarter’s 14.5% rate, with new order incentives trending lower.
  • Inventory turn -- 2.5x, up from 1.7x a year ago; return on inventory reached 17.4%.
  • Land-related metrics -- Less than 5% of land on balance sheet, homebuilding inventory cut from just under $20 billion two years ago to $10.5 billion; 86% land bank delivery rate versus 52% a year earlier.
  • Financial services -- Operating earnings of $91 million, with the decline attributed to a product mix shift toward ARMs, which are "generating significantly lower earnings."
  • Liquidity and debt -- Ended with $2.1 billion in cash, $5.2 billion in total liquidity, and a homebuilding debt-to-capital ratio of 15.7%.
  • Inventory composition -- Ended with about 5,000 completed unsold homes, or three per community, with 1,678 active communities up 6% year over year.
  • Cycle time reduction -- Single-family detached cycle time down to 122 days, an 11% year-over-year improvement and an all-time low for the company.
  • Technology and efficiency -- Direct construction costs down 2.5% quarter over quarter and 7% year over year, supported by technology-driven tools.
  • Marketing and customer engagement -- Qualified leads up 10% year over year; average response time to inquiries improved to 35 seconds, a 71% improvement year over year.
  • Shareholder returns -- Repurchased two million shares for $237 million and paid $123 million in dividends during the quarter.
  • Guidance for fiscal Q2 (ending May 31, 2026) -- New orders expected between 21,000 and 22,000 homes; deliveries between 20,000 and 21,000; average sales price between $370,000 and $375,000; gross margin guided to 15.5%-16%; SG&A targeted to 8.9%-9.1%; expected EPS range of $1.10-$1.40.
  • Full year target -- Maintaining guidance for 85,000 home deliveries in fiscal 2026.

Summary

Lennar (LEN 0.02%) reported higher inventory turn, improved cycle times, and a greater proportion of land held through off-balance sheet vehicles, signaling enhanced operational efficiency. Management stated, "we believe our Q2 -- our Q1 margin of 15.2% should represent the low point for the year," indicating expectations for sequential margin recovery. Asset-light land banking relationships delivered 86% of home sites this quarter, a notable increase from 52% a year ago, supporting both liquidity and lower risk. Cash totaled $2.1 billion, while shareholder returns remained a priority, as evidenced by $237 million in share repurchases and $123 million in dividends. Product mix changes in Financial Services, including increased ARM origination, lowered segment earnings and margin.

  • "We have lowered our direct cost 12 of 13 quarters sequentially, and we are down 12% over the last two years," according to Grove.
  • The company remains number one by market share in 22 of the top 50 homebuilding markets and a top three builder in 42, reflecting sustained national leadership.
  • Technology initiatives were credited with a 7% improvement in customer engagement quality scores and an 11% quarter-over-quarter increase in kept sales appointments.
  • Active community count rose to 1,678, with new communities opening into fiscal Q2, enhancing future growth prospects.
  • Management transitioned to a new generation of leadership in fiscal Q1, expecting this "fresh legs and new energy and overhead has benefited at the same time."
  • While SG&A increased year over year in the first half, management emphasized cost reductions would begin flowing through earnings later in fiscal 2026 as technology transition costs decline and retirements lower overhead.

Industry glossary

  • ARMs (Adjustable-Rate Mortgages): Home loans with interest rates that adjust periodically, generally yielding lower initial earnings for the lender compared to fixed-rate loans.
  • Even flow production: Operational strategy to closely align production start pace with sales rates, minimizing inventory volatility and supporting cost efficiencies.
  • Core plans: Standardized home designs repeatedly used across divisions to increase efficiencies, reduce costs, and improve cycle time and build quality.
