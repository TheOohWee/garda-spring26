Lands' End (LE) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Sep 10, 2025 at 8:07AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Sept. 9, 2025, at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Andrew McLean
  • Chief Financial Officer — Bernie McCracken
  • Senior Director of Financial Planning and Analysis — Tom Altholz

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Risks

  • Europe revenue declined 15% year over year, driven by supply chain challenges and macroeconomic pressures.
  • U.S. eCommerce sales decreased 11%, primarily due to a slow start to the swim season.
  • Gross profit decreased by 6% compared to last year, and adjusted EBITDA declined 18% year-over-year, impacted by tariffs, Europe performance, and slow seasonal demand.

Takeaways

  • Total Revenue-- $294 million, representing a 7% decrease.
  • GMV-- Flat year-over-year, indicating volume resilience despite revenue decline.
  • Gross Margin-- 49%, an increase of 90 basis points, aided by full-price selling and expanded licensing.
  • U.S. eCommerce Sales-- Down 11%, with management citing a slow start to swim as a key driver.
  • Third-Party Marketplace Revenue-- Up approximately 14% year-over-year, led by Macy's, Amazon, and improvement at Kohl's.
  • Outfitters Sales-- Increased 5% year-over-year, attributed to enterprise contract wins and longer contract durations.
  • School Uniform Channel Revenue-- Grew at a high single-digit rate, propelled by new school account acquisitions.
  • Europe Revenue-- Decreased 15% year over year, with management referencing supply chain bottlenecks on seasonal goods.
  • Licensing Revenue-- Grew 19% year over year, driven by licensee momentum across expanded channels.
  • SG&A Expenses-- Decreased by $6 million; however, rose 130 basis points as a percentage of sales due to deleverage from revenue decline.
  • Adjusted Net Loss-- Adjusted net loss of $1.9 million, or $0.06 per share.
  • Adjusted EBITDA-- $14 million adjusted EBITDA, an 18% decrease attributed to tariffs, Europe, and swim season softness, partly offset by marketplace and licensing growth.
  • Inventory-- $302 million at quarter-end, down 3%, reflecting inventory discipline and tariff mitigation.
  • Debt-- Term loan balance at $241 million; ABL outstanding at $35 million; total long-term debt flat year-over-year.
  • Share Repurchase-- $2 million repurchased, leaving $9 million under the existing authorization.
  • Tariff Impact-- Management expects to mitigate near-term tariff effects for the remainder of FY2025 and has incorporated current rates into guidance.
  • Q3 Guidance-- Net revenue of $320 million-$350 million; GMV projected for mid to high single-digit growth; adjusted net income $3 million-$7 million; EPS $0.10-$0.02; adjusted EBITDA $24 million-$28 million for Q3 FY2025.
  • Full-year Guidance-- Net revenue of $1.33 billion-$1.4 billion; GMV low to mid single-digit growth; adjusted net income $19 million-$27 million; EPS $0.62-$0.88; adjusted EBITDA $98 million-$107 million; $25 million in capital expenditures planned for FY2025.
  • Strategic Alternatives-- "The Board's previously announced process to explore strategic alternatives remains ongoing," according to Andrew McLean.

Summary

Lands' End (LE 4.57%) management highlighted increasing business momentum, citing a resilient GMV, and licensing and third-party channels as key diversifiers offsetting weakness in core eCommerce. Executives underscored success in asset-light strategies, with over half of new customer growth coming from third-party, marketplace, and licensing initiatives, which required minimal capital outlay. Modular supply chain redesign enabled rapid tariff mitigation and the flexibility to adjust sourcing as global trade policies shifted. Expanded AI personalization and targeted catalog strategies drove engagement, especially among the 35- to 50-year-old demographic, supporting multi-category purchasing and higher basket sizes.

  • "Our sales and margin over Labor Day weekend was the best we have had in the last decade," CEO McLean reported, marking record performance in key franchises.
  • Management confirmed pricing actions passed some tariff costs to consumers, "As little as we possibly can," while also sharing the load with vendors and implementing internal efficiency measures.
  • The launch of the Lands' End Essentials line on Amazon delivered a notable lift in new customer acquisition and was described as a catalyst for migration to the traditional brand website.
  • Europe's market is being leveraged as a testing ground for distributed commerce and collaboration models, with leadership citing early positive results in channel expansion and digital repositioning.
  • Asset-light, distributed commerce and licensing are positioned as primary growth drivers for the company's strategic outlook, with management expressing "the sky is the limit in terms of the licenses we can go after."

Industry glossary

  • GMV: Gross Merchandise Value — the total value of merchandise sold across all channels before returns and allowances, not reflecting net revenue.
  • Outfitters: Lands' End, Inc. business segment focused on B2B sales, primarily commercial and enterprise uniform contracts.
  • ABL: Asset-Based Lending — a revolving credit facility secured by company assets.
  • Distributed Commerce: Multi-channel retail approach allowing consumers to shop and transact outside the company’s primary website, directly through third-party marketplaces, social platforms, and licensing partners.
  • Resolver: Company-specific term for a traditional, loyal customer who typically returns for replacement purchases of favorite items.
  • Revolver: Internal term for a new or evolving customer cohort, typically exploring broader assortments and cross-category purchases.
