GM (GM) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 12:49PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, October 21, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chair and Chief Executive Officer — Mary Barra
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Paul Jacobson
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer, GM Financial — Susan Sheffield

Takeaways

  • EBIT-Adjusted -- $3.4 billion, down $700 million year over year, with a $1.1 billion gross tariff impact partially offset by over 30% through cost and operational actions.
  • U.S. Market Share -- 17%, up 50 basis points, marking the highest third-quarter level since 2017.
  • Dealer Inventory -- 527,000 units, a 16% reduction year over year, including a nearly 30% sequential decrease in EV inventory since the end of the second quarter.
  • North America EBIT-Adjusted Margin -- 6.2%, with margins reaching approximately 9% when excluding tariffs.
  • EV Deliveries -- 67,000 units, a record quarter with 16.5% U.S. EV share; company reporting the #2 position in U.S. EV market share.
  • Warranty Expense -- $900 million headwind year over year; overall warranty cash outlays stabilized over recent months.
  • China Market Share -- 6.8%, an increase of 30 basis points, with China equity income at $80 million for the quarter.
  • GM International ex China EBIT-Adjusted -- Nearly $150 million, described as stable year over year.
  • GM Financial EBT-Adjusted -- $800 million, with a $350 million dividend paid to the parent company this quarter.
  • Stock Repurchases -- $1.5 billion in the quarter, $3.5 billion year-to-date; diluted share count reduced 15% year over year to 954 million shares.
  • 2025 Guidance Raised -- EBIT-adjusted of $12 billion to $13 billion, EPS adjusted diluted of $9.75 to $10.50, adjusted automotive free cash flow of $10 billion to $11 billion, driven by strong business performance and expanded MSRP tariff offset eligibility.
  • Tariff Exposure -- Gross tariff impact for 2025 now projected at $3.5 billion to $4.5 billion, reduced from prior $4 billion to $5 billion owing to expanded offset, with plans to mitigate around 35% through internal initiatives.
  • Special Charges -- $1.6 billion recognized in the third quarter: $1.2 billion in noncash impairments (mainly Orion Assembly EV-to-ICE transition and battery asset write-downs) and $0.4 billion in cash costs for supplier contract terminations.
  • Software and Services Revenue -- Nearly $2 billion recognized year-to-date; OnStar subscriber base at 11 million, up 34% year over year; Super Cruise customers exceed 500,000, nearly doubling year over year.
  • Deferred Revenue -- Up 14% from the second quarter to nearly $5 billion, attributed to software and services growth.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $2.1 billion invested this quarter; full-year capital spending expected at the lower end of $10 billion to $11 billion guidance.
  • U.S. Production Expansion -- More than doubling planned Chevrolet Equinox output at Fairfax Assembly (Kansas); investments support plan to produce over 2 million vehicles annually in the U.S. after projects come online.
  • Engine Investment -- Close to $1 billion allocated for next-generation V8 production in New York.
  • BrightDrop Update -- Decision to cease BrightDrop output at CAMI Assembly, citing slower-than-expected commercial EV van market growth and regulatory/fleet incentive changes; further charges anticipated in the fourth quarter.
  • Shareholder Returns -- Reaffirmed capital return priorities, indicating further share repurchases ahead under current allocation policy.

  • "Now with an evolving regulatory framework and the end of the federal consumer incentives, it's clear that near-term EV adoption will be much lower than planned," causing higher variable costs and underutilized EV capacity.
  • Paul Jacobson reported, "Warranty expense was a $900 million headwind year over year in the third quarter. This is too high, and we need to do better."
  • Planned actions terminating BrightDrop production and resetting EV/ICE capacity will result in additional special charges in the fourth quarter.

Summary

General Motors (GM 0.40%) raised its full-year 2025 guidance for EBIT-adjusted, adjusted EPS, and automotive free cash flow, incorporating strengthened product demand, expanded eligibility for tariff offsets, and ongoing cost discipline. Management detailed a significant $1.6 billion in special charges tied to strategic manufacturing realignments including the transition at Orion Assembly and the end of BrightDrop production, with further charges anticipated in the fourth quarter. While reporting record EV deliveries and robust software service growth, General Motors directly acknowledged sharp external risks, including supply chain pressures on semiconductors from China, elevated warranty costs, and muted near-term EV demand and fleet incentives.

  • General Motors will more than double Chevrolet Equinox capacity at Fairfax Assembly and invest nearly $1 billion in new V8 engine production, advancing its plan to produce more than 2 million vehicles annually domestically.
  • U.S. incentives remained below industry average for the 10th consecutive quarter, and strict inventory discipline reduced ICE and EV inventories amid volatile demand.
  • Mary Barra said, "we are maintaining our capital discipline while addressing this production and creating new jobs in the United States," emphasizing balanced growth and financial strategy.
  • The North American EBIT-adjusted margin would have been about 9% without tariff impacts, placing performance within General Motors’ historical target range.
  • GM solidified a #2 position in the U.S. EV market, but expects EV demand softness to persist through early 2026, aligning manufacturing to actual demand levels.
  • Software and services, especially OnStar and Super Cruise, are expected to deliver continuing double-digit revenue growth with gross margins around 70% through the decade.
  • Management plans further share repurchases as the diluted share count dropped 15% year over year, with capital returns reinforced by strong free cash flow and lower capital expenditure guidance.
  • Executive leadership signaled a clear priority: restoring North American EBIT margins to the historical 8%-10% range via cost action, tariff mitigation, and careful EV portfolio management.

Industry glossary

  • MSRP Offset Program: A federal mechanism allowing automakers to partially recover import tariff costs via credits based on manufacturer’s suggested retail price for U.S.-produced vehicles and select parts.
  • LMR (Lithium Manganese Rich): A new battery chemistry under development intended to reduce cost and improve performance in electric vehicles.
  • ICE: Internal Combustion Engine, referring to traditional gasoline or diesel vehicle powertrains.
  • CAFE Credits: Regulatory compliance credits issued for meeting or exceeding U.S. Corporate Average Fuel Economy standards.
  • Super Cruise: General Motors’ advanced driver assistance system enabling hands-free driving on compatible roads.
  • Equity Income: Proportionate share of net income earned by General Motors from joint ventures, notably in China.
