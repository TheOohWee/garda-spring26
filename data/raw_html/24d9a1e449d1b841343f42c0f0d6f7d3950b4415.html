Synchrony (SYF) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 27, 2026 at 9:30AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, January 27, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Brian Doubles
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Brian Wenzel
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations — Kathryn Miller

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Earnings -- $751 million for the quarter, or $2.40 per diluted share, inclusive of a $0.14 restructuring charge.
  • Return on Average Assets -- 2.5% in the quarter, directly stated by management.
  • Return on Tangible Common Equity -- 21.8% for the quarter, as reported in prepared remarks.
  • Purchase Volume -- $49 billion, a quarterly record and up 3%, with digital platform purchase volume up 6%.
  • Dual and Co-branded Card Volume -- 50% of total purchase volume, up 16% over last year.
  • Net Interest Income -- $4.8 billion, an increase of 4%, primarily due to product pricing and policy changes and reduced funding liabilities costs.
  • Net Interest Margin -- 15.83%, up 82 basis points, with principal drivers including higher receivables yield and lower liability costs.
  • Efficiency Ratio -- 36.9%, up about 360 basis points, mainly reflecting higher expenses and RSA impact on net revenue; would have been about 180 basis points lower excluding restructuring charge.
  • Net Charge-off Rate -- 5.37%, down 108 basis points, with stated long-term target range of 5.5%-6% reaffirmed for guidance.
  • 30+ Day Delinquency Rate -- 4.49%, down 21 basis points, while 90+ day rate fell 23 basis points to 2.17%, all below 2017-2019 historical average.
  • Ending Loan Receivables -- $104 billion, a decrease of 1%, attributed to higher payment rates, lower average active accounts, and lower first-half purchase volume.
  • Payment Rate -- 16.3%, up 45 basis points, and 155 basis points above pre-pandemic Q4 average.
  • Net Revenue -- $3.8 billion, flat year over year as higher net interest income was offset by increased RSAs.
  • RSAs (Retailer Share Arrangements) -- $1.1 billion, or 4.3% of average loan receivables, up $175 million on improved program performance.
  • Provision for Credit Losses -- $1.4 billion, down $118 million, mainly due to a $294 million drop in net charge-offs, partially offset by a $76 million reserve build.
  • Other Expenses -- $1.4 billion, up 10%, driven by higher employee costs and technology investment, including a $67 million restructuring charge from a voluntary early retirement program.
  • Allowance for Credit Losses -- 10.06% of loan receivables, down 29 basis points sequentially and 38 basis points year over year.
  • Capital Return -- $1.1 billion in Q4, split between $952 million in share repurchases and $106 million in cash dividends; full-year capital return totaled $3.3 billion.
  • Capital Ratios -- CET1 at 12.6%, Tier One Capital at 13.8%, Total Capital at 15.8%; all declined about 70 basis points compared to a year ago.
  • Full-Year Earnings -- $3.6 billion, or $9.28 per diluted share; return on average assets 3% and return on tangible common equity 25.8%.
  • Deposit Funding -- Deposits at 84% of total funding; direct deposits up $2.9 billion and broker deposits down $3.8 billion, with secured debt at 9% and unsecured debt at 7%.
  • Liquid Assets -- $16.6 billion, representing 13.9% of total assets, a 3% decline and 45 basis points lower than the prior year.
  • Guidance for 2026 -- Projected net earnings per diluted share of $9.10 to $9.50, with mid-single-digit ending receivables growth and net charge-off rate aligned to the 5.5%-6% long-term target.
  • Program Developments -- Added or renewed over 25 partners in the quarter, with more than 75 in 2025; 97% of interest and fees from top 25 partners renewed through 2028, and top five partners renewed through 2030 and beyond.
  • Digital and Multi-Product Strategy -- Digital wallet sales and unique provisioned accounts more than doubled, an 18% rise in visits, and a 17% increase in 2025 sales attributed to channel enhancements.
  • Pay Later Product -- Now at more than 6,200 merchants, with cited data showing "at least a 10% average increase in sales" when offered alongside revolving products.

RISKS

  • Chief Executive Officer Brian Doubles stated, "any price controls like an APR cap would not make credit more affordable. It would eliminate credit for those that need it. A cap would require issuers to significantly reduce the amount of credit they're able to provide. That disproportionately impacts the consumers at the lower income level."
  • Chief Executive Officer Brian Doubles said, "this would be a huge hit for them. When you look at, like, the severe impact on both the consumers and businesses, there's no question this would be very bad for the economy."

SUMMARY

Synchrony Financial (SYF 0.11%) reported record purchase volume in the quarter, with strengthening in co-brand, digital, and diversified purchase segments.

Management disclosed that 97% of top 25 partner interest and fees are contractually renewed through 2028, and described substantial progress in scaling digital wallet initiatives. Outlook for 2026 targets mid-single-digit ending receivable growth and net charge-off ratios matching long-term targets, while the earnings guidance range incorporates EPS impact from new growth programs, reserves for asset expansion, and technology investment. Capital ratios, though reduced, remain robust by management’s own benchmarks, and the company’s funding mix continues to heavily favor direct deposits over brokered alternatives. Multi-product deployment, especially the incremental impact of Pay Later and digital wallet strategies, is expected to drive both incremental customer acquisition and deepen partner engagement.

  • Chief Financial Officer Brian Wenzel confirmed net interest margin rose 82 basis points, attributing 44 basis points to higher loan receivables yield from product pricing and policy changes.
  • Management expects Retailer Share Arrangements to remain within the 4%-4.5% range of average receivables, with further increases tied to stronger program performance.
  • Significant investments cited include AI, cloud infrastructure, and expansion of the health and wellness platform; accompanying reserve requirements comprise the largest component of growth-related expenses.
  • Pay Later product uptake across the portfolio is being tracked as incremental — with "any cannibalization" of legacy card flows and clear confirmation that these accounts comprise net new customer acquisition.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RSAs (Retailer Share Arrangements): Partner profit-sharing agreements in which a portion of program income is contractually paid to retail partners, typically as a percentage of loan receivables or revenue.
  • PPPCs (Product Pricing and Policy Changes): Company-initiated adjustments to product terms (such as APR, late fees, or pricing structures), designed to optimize yield and manage risk in the loan portfolio.
  • CECL (Current Expected Credit Loss): An accounting standard requiring recognition of expected lifetime credit losses on financial assets at the time of origination, impacting loan loss reserve calculations.
  • Pay Later: Installment-based financing product—offered alongside revolving credit—which provides consumers with short- to medium-term, fixed payment options at the point of sale.
