Vertiv Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Oct 22, 2025 at 1:37PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Oct. 22, 2025 at 11 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Executive Chairman — David M. Cote

Chief Executive Officer — Giordano Albertazzi

Chief Financial Officer — David J. Fallon

Vice President, Investor Relations — Lynne M. Maxeiner

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

Adjusted Diluted EPS -- Adjusted diluted EPS of $1.24, up about 63% year over year in fiscal Q3 2025 (period ended Sept. 30, 2025), reflecting higher operating profit and a lower effective tax rate.

Organic Net Sales Growth -- 28%, driven by the Americas up 43% and APAC up 21% in fiscal Q3 2025; EMEA organic sales declined 4%.

Book-to-Bill Ratio -- 1.4x, indicating continued demand momentum.

Trailing Twelve-Month Organic Orders Growth -- 21%, with Orders up 60% year over year and 20% sequentially.

Total Backlog -- Backlog reached $9.5 billion as of fiscal Q3 2025, up about 30% year over year and up 12% sequentially (from Q2 to Q3 2025), providing visibility into 2026.

Adjusted Operating Profit -- Adjusted operating profit reached $596 million, up 43% year over year, with adjusted operating margin at 22.3% in fiscal Q3 2025.

Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- Adjusted free cash flow was $462 million, up 38% year over year, representing about 95% adjusted free cash flow conversion in fiscal Q3 2025.

Net Leverage Ratio -- Net leverage was 0.5x at the end of the quarter, expected to decrease to 0.2x by year-end 2025.

Regional Segment Commentary -- Margin expanded by 400 basis points in The Americas, while operating leverage was critical for margin expansion in APAC. EMEA margins significantly below prior year, attributed to lower sales and increased fixed costs alongside regional capacity investments.

Restructuring Charges -- $30 million incurred globally, primarily in EMEA, in fiscal Q3 2025, anticipated to generate approximately $20 million in annualized benefit beginning in 2026.

Q4 2025 Guidance -- Adjusted diluted EPS projected at $1.26 (up 27%) in Q4 2025, net sales projected at $2.85 billion in Q4 2025, and adjusted operating profit projected at $639 million in Q4 2025.

Full-Year 2025 Guidance Raised -- Adjusted diluted EPS projected at $4.10, 44% higher than 2024 for full-year 2025. Net sales projected at $10.2 billion (27% organic growth) for full year 2025, adjusted operating profit projected at $2.602 billion (33% higher) for full year 2025 (non-GAAP), and adjusted free cash flow guidance raised to $1.5 billion for full year 2025.

Operating Margin Targets -- Full-year adjusted operating margin now expected to be 20.2% in 2025, about 80 basis points higher than 2024.

Capital Investments and R&D -- Engineering and R&D spend expected to grow 20%+ in 2026, with further acceleration possible.

Tariffs -- Management expects to materially offset current tariff impacts by the end of Q1 2026 through pricing and supply chain actions.

Service Business -- Recurring and accretive to margin; service engineer headcount up to approximately 4,400 to 4,500, with ongoing expansion aligned to backlog geography.

System and Technology Leadership -- Ongoing investments in prefabricated solutions (e.g. Smart Run, Great Lakes acquisition) and system integration to maintain multiple GPU generations ahead.

Incremental Margin Guidance -- Long-term incremental margins targeted at 30% to 35%, with 2025 below this range due to tariff headwinds.

SUMMARY

Management highlighted a 1.4x book-to-bill ratio and 30% backlog growth in fiscal Q3 2025 (period ended Sept. 30, 2025) as key drivers of future sales visibility, particularly positioning Vertiv (VRT +2.60%) for 2026 and beyond. Full-year financial guidance for all key metrics was raised, anchored by strong order momentum, positive pricing seen outpacing inflation, and operational execution. EMEA performance lagged due to power constraints and regulatory challenges, leading to restructuring efforts expected to benefit margins in 2026. Significant capital deployment was discussed, including planned engineering and R&D increases and a capacity expansion strategy that maintains deployment ahead of expected demand curves. Tariff countermeasures are progressing and are expected to substantially offset headwinds as the company enters Q1 2026.

Management indicated service business is gaining traction, with field engineering capacity actively scaled to meet growing installation and lifecycle needs in major markets.

The CEO underscored enhanced collaboration with leading technology partners, specifically noting the upcoming 800V DC portfolio aligned with Nvidia's (NVDA +2.59%) 2027 product cycles.

Speaking to competitive dynamics, management directly addressed recent industry innovations, characterizing Vertiv’s response as "relentless investment" and a commitment to stay "at the center" of technology advancement.

A leadership transition was announced, as Chief Financial Officer David J. Fallon retires and Craig Chamberlain assumes the CFO post.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Book-to-Bill Ratio: The ratio of orders received to products billed in a given time period, indicating demand strength relative to shipments.

Incremental Margin: The ratio of change in operating profit to the change in sales revenue, analyzing profit flow-through on additional revenue.

800V DC Portfolio: Advanced direct current power infrastructure designed for high-density, next-generation data centers, supporting emerging technologies such as NVIDIA's platforms.

Smart Run: A prefabricated, modular white space system enabling efficient data center build-outs and rapid deployment.

Free Cash Flow Conversion: The proportion of net income or operating profit translated to free cash flow, reflecting cash efficiency.

SIOP: Sales, Inventory, and Operations Planning — an integrated business management process for aligning supply and demand, used here for both product and service workforce planning.
