Humana (HUM) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 9:10AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, February 11, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — James Rechtin
  • Chief Financial Officer — Celeste Mellet
  • President, Enterprise Growth — David Dintenfass
  • President, Medicare — George Renaudin

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted EPS -- $17.14 for 2025, in line with expectations and above initial guidance of approximately $16.25, achieved despite "higher than initially planned investments."
  • Insurance Segment Benefit Ratio -- 90.4% for full year 2025, slightly better than guidance, with benefit set aside for a potential "doc fix" that was invested in network management and technology.
  • Medicare Advantage (MA) Membership Growth -- Approximately 1 million new members in AEP, representing 20% growth; full-year 2026 individual MA membership growth expected at approximately 25%.
  • Retention Rate -- Improved by over 500 basis points year over year.
  • New Sales Composition -- Over 70% of new sales were switchers from competitor plans, nearly 30% were “bounce-back” members, and over 75% came from higher lifetime value sales channels, a nearly 10-percentage-point improvement year over year.
  • STARS Performance and Headwind -- Net STARS headwind for 2026 is approximately $3.5 billion, including both individual and group MA, with 45% of members in four-plus-star plans, and about 30% of new sales in below-four-star contracts.
  • MA Margin Outlook -- 2026 individual MA margin is expected to double year over year "normalizing for STARS," though initial guidance assumes actual margin is "slightly below breakeven" after accounting for the 2026 STARS headwind.
  • Adjusted EPS Guidance for 2026 -- At least $9, with the anticipated decline explicitly attributed to the previously communicated 2026 STARS headwind, net of mitigation.
  • Consolidated Operating Expense Ratio -- Significant improvement expected in 2026, primarily from operating leverage due to membership and revenue growth, tactical cost cutting, and transformation efforts.
  • CenterWell and Medicaid Expansion -- Medicaid spans 13 states, including Georgia and Texas planned for launch next year; strategic acquisition in the primary care space is anticipated.
  • Capital Efficiency Achievements -- Despite expected 40% premium growth between 2024 and 2026, statutory capital requirements will increase by less than 20%, offsetting over $3 billion of required capital for 2026 membership growth.
  • Debt-to-Capital Ratio -- Expected to remain largely flat year over year while funding membership growth and select M&A, with a focus on maintaining credit ratings.
  • Operational Progress in Onboarding -- January saw reduced complaints to Medicare and increased health risk assessment completion rates, both of which are positive STARS-related outcomes.
  • Transformation Initiatives -- Tactical efficiency improvements include supplier consolidation, early retirement programs, expanding outsourcing, process standardization, and increased automation; cost savings largely expected to accelerate in 2027 and 2028.

RISKS

  • Celeste Mellet noted, "after accounting for the 26 stars headwind, our initial guidance assumes individual MA margins are slightly below breakeven."
  • Guidance for 2026 includes a "higher than typical" level of conservatism "to account for the dynamic environment," reflecting uncertainty in core assumptions.
  • "net STARS headwind for 2026 including individual and group MA, is approximately $3.5 billion," due partly to membership and revenue growth in contracts below four stars.

SUMMARY

Humana (HUM 2.62%) reported full-year 2025 adjusted earnings in line with expectations, while highlighting a 20% surge in Medicare Advantage membership and a 500-basis-point improvement in retention. Management expects continued growth with approximately 25% MA membership expansion in 2026, but acknowledges a significant $3.5 billion STARS-related headwind that will drive individual MA margins slightly below breakeven despite underlying operating improvements. CenterWell and Medicaid initiatives are expanding, capital efficiency actions have reduced required funding for growth, and transformation efforts are set to deliver more pronounced cost savings by 2027 and 2028. Strategic discipline in pricing, margins, and capital allocation—along with proactive operational capacity management—remain at the forefront of the company’s approach amid annual funding and regulatory uncertainties.

  • CenterWell Pharmacy is projected to see a material tailwind in 2026 earnings directly tied to new membership growth, offsetting prior declines.
  • Management confirmed that approximately 90% of the incremental $550 million in transformation investments for 2025 were attributed to medical costs.
  • Value-based contracting stability is expected, with one-third of MA members in full-risk arrangements and paneling rates recovering through the year as new members are assigned primary care doctors.
  • Medicaid now covers 13 states, and upcoming expansion into Georgia and Texas is planned for next year.
  • Debt-to-capital and capital requirements are described as stable, benefiting from completed redomestication and ongoing optimization of reinsurance and risk-transfer structures.
  • Aaron Martin, President of Medicare Advantage as of January, is positioned to succeed as President of Insurance upon George Renaudin's retirement, reflecting a planned leadership transition.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AEP (Annual Election Period): Annual period when eligible individuals can enroll in or switch Medicare Advantage or Part D plans.
  • STARS (CMS Star Ratings): Quality rating system used by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services to evaluate Medicare Advantage and Part D plans on a scale of one to five stars based on clinical and member experience metrics.
  • CTM (Complaint Tracking Module): CMS system recording complaints filed by Medicare beneficiaries with plans, impacting quality ratings.
  • HRA (Health Risk Assessment): Structured evaluation of a member’s health status to guide care and support STARS metrics.
  • MA (Medicare Advantage): Private health plan alternatives to traditional Medicare offering supplemental benefits and managed care features.
  • MLR (Medical Loss Ratio): Ratio of medical claims paid to premiums received, a key measure of insurance plan profitability.
  • MRA (Medical Risk Adjustment): Process of adjusting payments to health plans to reflect the underlying health status of enrollees.
  • CenterWell: Humana’s healthcare delivery brand encompassing primary care clinics, home health, pharmacy, and related services.
  • Doc fix: Term for legislative intervention to adjust or block scheduled reductions to Medicare physician payment rates.
  • V28: A version or variant of CMS risk adjustment or payment policy referenced for impact analysis on the company's financials.
  • PPO/HMO: Preferred Provider Organization / Health Maintenance Organization, representing different managed care plan structures.
  • DSNP (Dual Eligible Special Needs Plan): Medicare Advantage plans serving members eligible for both Medicare and Medicaid benefits.
  • PMPM (Per Member Per Month): A financial metric indicating average monthly revenue, cost, or investment per enrolled member.
