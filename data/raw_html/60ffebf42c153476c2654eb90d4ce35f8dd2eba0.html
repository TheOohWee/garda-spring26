Corebridge Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 10, 2026 at 10:28AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Aug. 5, 2025 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kevin Timothy Hogan
  • Chief Financial Officer — Elias Farid Habayeb
  • Head of Investor Relations — Isil Muderrisoglu

Takeaways

  • Variable annuity reinsurance transaction -- Corebridge closed the AGL portion, representing about 90% of the transaction, generating $2.1 billion in net distributable proceeds, with the remainder expected to close in the fourth quarter, subject to regulatory approvals.
  • Adjusted pretax operating income -- $942 million, with operating earnings per share of $1.36, a 20% increase year over year and no notable items in the quarter.
  • Operating earnings per share (run rate) -- $1.30, representing an 8% year-over-year increase, and an adjusted run rate return on equity of 13.7%, up 90 basis points.
  • Individual retirement net inflows (excluding variable annuities) -- $3.2 billion, up 4% year over year and more than double sequentially, a company high point.
  • Individual retirement record sales -- $6.8 billion in premiums and deposits this quarter, including $500 million from the recently launched RILA product.
  • Group retirement business -- Fee earning assets increased 7% sequentially, and out-of-plan deposits rose 22% within the quarter; base spread income decreased 18% year over year.
  • Life insurance underwriting margin -- Increased 12% year over year, with adjusted pretax operating income up 44%, driven by pricing discipline, automated underwriting, and favorable mortality.
  • Institutional markets -- Reserves grew 17% year over year, supporting a 64% rise in total sources of income; GIC reserves up $4.7 billion, or 40% year over year, with five consecutive quarters of GIC issuances exceeding $1 billion.
  • Insurance company dividends -- $600 million paid to the holding company during the quarter, and 64% year-to-date payout ratio achieved through dividends and share repurchases.
  • Share repurchase authorization -- Increased by $2 billion in June, supported by liquidity of $1.3 billion at the holding company and no material debt maturities until 2027.
  • Expense efficiency -- General operating expenses down 14% since the IPO, primarily due to the Corebridge Forward program and further digitization and modernization initiatives.
  • Management growth targets -- Management reaffirmed targeting average annual EPS growth of 10%-15% and a payout ratio of 60%-65% over time.
  • New money yield versus roll-off -- CFO Habayeb reported a 50 basis point differential favoring new money investments in the second quarter.
  • Automated underwriting -- CEO Hogan noted that 80% of new decisions in the Life Insurance segment now use automated underwriting, producing mostly instantaneous results.
  • Large group retirement plan exits -- CFO Habayeb indicated $1.5 billion in outflows planned for the next quarter, with a less than $5 million annual earnings impact.
  • Advisory and brokerage assets -- Reported at $16.8 billion, up 10% year over year, supporting fee income growth in Group Retirement.

Risks

  • CFO Habayeb said, "Base spread income declined by 6% year over year, driven by the combination of Fed rate actions in 2024 and net outflows in Group Retirement as the business continues to transition from spread to fee income."
  • Management expects "higher levels of fixed annuity and index annuity volumes to be exiting surrender charge periods in the second half of this year," which may cause non-linear patterns in surrender rates and outflows.
  • CFO Habayeb stated, "Base spread income decreased 18% year over year, given the ongoing demographic shift in our customer base and resulting net outflows."
  • On alternatives, CFO Habayeb said, the second half of the year to be below the 8% to 9% for two reasons. We expect continued weakness on valuations around real estate equity in the second half of the year. And then, the uncertainty we saw in the second quarter and it somewhat continuing has slowed deal activity. So given the combination of those two drivers today, our outlook for the second half is, while it might be positive, it will be below the 8% to 9%.

Summary

Corebridge Financial (CRBG 1.37%) completed the majority of its variable annuity reinsurance transaction, resulting in a full exit from Individual Retirement variable annuity risk, significant proceeds, and a simpler, lower-volatility balance sheet. Second quarter operating income and EPS both increased sharply, with non-VA Individual Retirement net inflows and record sales reflecting strong organic growth. Management raised the share repurchase authorization, maintained robust liquidity, and confirmed guidance for double-digit annual EPS growth and a 60%-65% payout ratio. The company highlighted progress in digitization, automated underwriting adoption, and fee-based business expansion while identifying near-term headwinds in spread income, planned group retirement outflows, and alternative asset returns.

  • CFO Habayeb stated, "we expect the transaction to be EPS accretive once we fully deploy the capital that we intend to. And we expect to get there by the second half of 2026."
  • CEO Hogan emphasized "transaction monetized an undervalued book of business with a decreasing financial contribution to Corebridge at an extremely attractive price."
  • Group Retirement business showed continued migration toward fee income, with 1.9 million customers—1.6 million of whom are in-plan only—providing future cross-sell potential.
  • Management described ongoing opportunities for growth in the advisory and brokerage channel, which comprises a $104 billion earnings base, up 3% year over year, and noted expansion of adviser forces.
  • Chief Executive Hogan said, "automated underwriting capability in Life is actually transacting around 80% of our new decisions."
  • The RILA product achieved over $1 billion in cumulative sales across both Individual and Group Retirement within nine months of launch, including new and existing distribution partners.
  • Company liquidity remains above near-term requirements, with no substantial debt maturities expected before 2027.

Industry glossary

  • RILA (Registered Index-Linked Annuity): An annuity product offering returns linked to a specified equity index, subject to a cap and floor, allowing for limited downside risk.
  • GIC (Guaranteed Investment Contract): An insurance product that provides a fixed or floating interest rate return over a set period in exchange for a lump-sum investment, often used for institutional clients.
  • PRT (Pension Risk Transfer): The transfer of defined benefit pension liabilities from plan sponsors to an insurer through an annuity or related structure.
  • VII (Variable Investment Income): Income derived from non-core, fluctuating sources such as private equity, hedge funds, or real estate, not included in core operating earnings.
  • Base spread income: The interest margin earned between yields on invested assets and crediting rates paid on insurance liabilities, excluding nonguaranteed or extraordinary items.
