Earnings Call Transcript

Armstrong (AWI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 24, 2026 at 11:33AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Feb. 24, 2026 at 10:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Victor D. Grizzle
  • Chief Financial Officer — Christopher P. Calzaretta
  • Chief Operating Officer / CEO-Designate — Mark Hershey

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- Increased 12% year over year, marking a second consecutive year of double-digit top-line growth and the fifth straight year of net sales growth.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Rose 14% for the year, with margin expanding 70 basis points; adjusted EBITDA margin reached a record 43.5% in Mineral Fiber for the full year.
  • Mineral Fiber Segment Q4 Sales -- Sales grew 3% in the quarter, driven by 6% average unit value (AUV) growth, partially offset by lower volumes resulting from government-related demand disruptions.
  • Mineral Fiber Q4 Adjusted EBITDA Margin -- Expanded by 460 basis points to 42.1%, attributed to AUV gains and a favorable claims adjustment, representing the highest Q4 margin in segment since 2016.
  • Architectural Specialties (AS) Q4 Net Sales -- Increased 11%, largely driven by 2024 acquisitions (3form and ZAHNER) and organic growth, despite notable project delays.
  • AS Q4 Adjusted EBITDA -- Decreased 3% due to cost imbalance from project delays and higher input costs, with margin compression linked to timing of custom projects.
  • Full-Year AS Adjusted EBITDA Margin -- Achieved 18%, with organic margin at approximately 19%, but below the 19% margin guidance due to Q4 project headwinds; two quarters in the year exceeded the 20% organic margin target.
  • Adjusted Diluted Net Earnings Per Share -- Grew 17%, reflecting robust profitability and operational performance.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- Increased 16% over the previous year, primarily from higher cash earnings and WAVE joint venture dividends, partially offset by higher capital expenditures.
  • Capital Expenditures -- Rose by $26 million as investments continued in manufacturing productivity, Temploc capacity, and digital platform upgrades.
  • 2025 Acquisitions -- Included Parallel Architectural Products and Eventscape; Eventscape brought approximately $30 million in 2025 revenue with full-year contributions to come.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $15 million paid in dividends and $50 million in share repurchases in Q4; $533 million remains authorized for buybacks through 2026.
  • 2026 Guidance — Total Net Sales -- Projected to grow 8%-10%, supported by Mineral Fiber AUV growth of approximately 6% and high single-digit AS organic growth; acquired businesses expected to account for about half of total AS segment sales growth.
  • 2026 Guidance — Adjusted EBITDA -- Expected to increase 8%-12% with margin expansion forecast in both business segments and ongoing SG&A discipline expected to improve the expense ratio.
  • Growth Initiatives’ Contribution -- Reported as a 1 percentage point lift in 2025 (in a down market) and anticipated to accelerate by an additional 0.5 points in 2026, with incremental benefit from data center and energy efficiency innovations.
  • Backlog and Order Intake -- Architectural Specialties reported double-digit year-over-year order intake growth in Q4, indicating sustained momentum into 2026.
  • Segment Mix and Margin Impact -- Data center and energy-saving product lines are accretive to AUV and expected to align with the company’s targeted 60% incremental EBITDA contribution as they scale.
  • WAVE Joint Venture Earnings -- Provided a positive contribution to both Mineral Fiber segment margin expansion and adjusted free cash flow, with 2026 growth expected in the mid single-digit range.
  • Leadership Transition -- CEO transition to Mark Hershey as of April 1, 2026, with assurance of continued strategic priorities, focus on innovation, and profitable growth initiatives.

RISKS

  • Mineral Fiber Volume Pressure -- Q4 volumes were negatively impacted by the U.S. government shutdown, with slower-than-expected recovery in government building MRO demand; January showed partial rebound, but normalization was not immediate.
  • Architectural Specialties Margin Compression -- Project delays in Q4 resulted in a cost imbalance and lower margins; the segment finished below its 19% full-year margin guidance.
  • Home Center Channel Softness -- Continued destocking drove softness in home center demand for Mineral Fiber products during Q4.
  • Seasonal and Market Uncertainty -- Management expects a muted start to 2026 due to winter weather impacts and persistent market choppiness, with stronger growth weighted to the second half; forecast includes these uncertainties.

SUMMARY

Management reported another year of record results, highlighted by double-digit gains in net sales and adjusted EBITDA, underpinned by pricing discipline and operational productivity in both core business segments. The company detailed strategic momentum from portfolio expansion through acquisitions and investments in digital platforms, including energy efficiency and data center offerings, with new products such as Temploc and DynaMax LT contributing to growth. The announced CEO transition maintains strategic continuity, with an emphasis on further scaling growth initiatives that have already demonstrated incremental volume effects even in adverse market conditions. The board reiterated a disciplined capital allocation framework, deploying increased cash toward reinvestment, targeted M&A, and heightened share repurchase activity, all enabled by a robust adjusted free cash flow profile. Management described a multiyear backdrop for outperformance in Architectural Specialties order intake and sustained high-margin profitability in Mineral Fiber, despite discrete Q4 volume and margin headwinds.

  • The AUV growth in Mineral Fiber relies more on like-for-like pricing than product mix, with expectations for a continued pricing bias due to anticipated 2026 inflation.
  • Energy-saving and data center products are in early stages of margin scaling, but management expects them to deliver 60% incremental EBITDA as volumes grow.
  • Architectural Specialties' recent acquisitions provided top-line lift, but project timing and integration work are ongoing as management targets a 20% segment EBITDA margin when normalization resumes.
  • Mineral Fiber volumes remain 14% below 2019 levels, presenting a multi-year recovery and margin expansion opportunity should end markets return to pre-pandemic norms.
  • AI adoption has begun to support sales specification excellence and digital platform initiatives, enhancing the value proposition in competitive bids and architectural decision-making.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AUV (Average Unit Value): Average revenue earned per unit sold, reflecting both pricing and product mix effects in a segment.
  • MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Operations): Demand segment consisting of products and services used for ongoing maintenance and repair in existing buildings.
  • WAVE Joint Venture: Worthington Armstrong Venture, a strategic partnership supplying suspension system products; provides equity earnings and financial contribution to Armstrong World Industries, Inc.
  • AS (Architectural Specialties): Business segment offering specialty ceilings, walls, and architectural materials, including acquired product lines.
  • SG&A: Selling, General, and Administrative expenses, including costs not directly tied to manufacturing.
  • Temploc: Armstrong's energy-saving ceiling solution designed to enhance building passive thermal performance and energy efficiency.
  • ProjectWorks: Armstrong's automated digital design platform, providing visualization and project specification services for architects and contractors.
  • Canopy: Armstrong's online platform for direct sales to underserved customers, expanding product reach and contributing to digital revenue and EBITDA.
