Titan Machinery (TITN) Q2 2026 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Sep 2, 2025 at 5:39PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Aug. 28, 2025, at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Bryan J. Knutson
  • Chief Financial Officer — Robert Larsen

RISKS

  • Chief Financial Officer Bryan J. Knutson stated, "we now expect our equipment margins to remain subdued through the rest of fiscal 2026," which underpins narrowed earnings guidance.
  • Net loss (GAAP) was $6 million, or $0.26 per diluted share, for the second quarter of fiscal 2026 (period ended July 31, 2025), versus adjusted net income of $4 million for the second quarter of fiscal 2025, indicating a negative shift in profitability.
  • Same-store sales in the domestic Agriculture segment declined 18.7% to $345.8 million, attributed to "softer retail demand and our continued efforts to manage inventory to targeted levels."
  • Bryan J. Knutson said, "Given the challenging agriculture industry backdrop, we are pleased with the progress we have made," but highlighted that further progress in inventory reduction "requires the continuation of pricing concessions," which will sustain equipment margin compression for fiscal 2026.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue-- $546.4 million, down from $633.7 million in the prior-year period, reflecting a 14% decline in same-store sales.
  • Gross Profit-- $93.6 million versus $112.4 million, with a gross profit margin of 17.1% compared to 17.7% previously.
  • Operating Expenses-- $92.7 million, a 2.6% decrease, driven by lower variable expenses aligned with declining revenue and cost control measures.
  • Net Loss-- $6 million, or $0.26 per diluted share (GAAP); prior year adjusted net income was $4 million, or $0.17 per share for the second quarter of fiscal 2025.
  • Equipment Inventory-- $954 million as of July 31, 2025, essentially flat in the first half of fiscal 2026; year-to-date equipment inventory reduction from the prior peak totals $365 million as of the second quarter of fiscal 2026.
  • Domestic Agriculture Same-Store Sales-- Decreased 18.7% to $345.8 million; segment pretax loss was $12.3 million, compared to adjusted pretax income of $6.7 million for the second quarter of fiscal 2025.
  • Construction Same-Store Sales-- Down 10.2% to $72 million; segment pretax loss of $1.2 million compared to adjusted pretax income of $0.2 million in the prior year's second quarter.
  • Europe Same-Store Sales-- Increased 44% to $98.1 million (including $4.1 million positive currency impact); segment pretax income of $5.1 million compared to pretax loss of $2.3 million in the same period last year.
  • Australia Same-Store Sales-- Declined 50.1% to $30.6 million, with a 1.4% negative currency impact; segment pretax loss of $2.1 million compared to pretax income of $1.4 million in the prior year's second quarter.
  • Consolidated Equipment Margin Guidance-- The full-year equipment margin forecast is now approximately 6.6% for fiscal 2026, about 100 basis points below previous expectations.
  • Domestic Agriculture Equipment Margin-- First-half margin was 3.1% for the first half of fiscal 2026; full-year fiscal 2026 margin is expected to be approximately 3.8%.
  • Inventory Reduction Target-- $100 million goal reaffirmed, with internal plans "significantly higher" and majority of reduction anticipated in the year's closing months.
  • Revised Revenue Forecast-- Domestic Agriculture revenue is expected to be down 15%-20% for fiscal 2026, Construction revenue is expected to be down 3%-8% for fiscal 2026, Europe segment revenue is expected to be up 30%-40% for fiscal 2026, and Australia segment revenue is expected to be down 20%-25% for fiscal 2026.
  • Adjusted Debt to Tangible Net Worth Ratio-- 1.8, below the bank covenant of 3.5x.
  • Adjusted Diluted Loss per Share Guidance-- Adjusted diluted loss per share guidance has been narrowed to a range of $1.50 to $2 for fiscal year 2026.

SUMMARY

Titan Machinery(TITN +2.48%) reported lower revenue and margins in its domestic Agriculture and Construction segments for the second quarter of fiscal 2026 (period ended July 31, 2025), while the Europe segment achieved significant growth, driven by Romanian demand supported by expiring EU stimulus programs. Management outlined ongoing inventory reduction and optimization initiatives, emphasizing that the pricing concessions required for inventory progress will keep equipment margins at compressed levels through the rest of fiscal 2026. Updated fiscal guidance reflects narrowed earnings expectations, a lower full-year equipment margin outlook, and segment-specific revenue revisions in light of market dynamics.

  • Chief Financial Officer Larsen said, "We are raising our assumptions for each of the domestic Agriculture, Construction and Europe segments while keeping Australia consistent," clarifying the source of the revised revenue guidance.
  • Management stated confidence in achieving the previously communicated inventory reduction target of $100 million for fiscal 2026 and highlighted increased internal ambitions beyond this figure.
  • Larsen provided historical context, noting that domestic Agriculture equipment margins have averaged nearly 10%, and described the present sub-4% levels as the only periods when equipment margin was below 8% were fiscal 2016 and fiscal 2017, which were the core of the prior cycle, and then fiscal 2025 and the current fiscal year (fiscal 2026).
  • Knutson identified the reinstatement of 100% bonus depreciation as a temporary positive for buyers with taxable income and pointed to continued uncertainty regarding $20 billion to $30 billion in potential government aid for the current year.
  • Cash on hand at period end was $33 million as of the second quarter of fiscal 2026, supporting liquidity amid working capital initiatives.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Floorplan Interest Expense: The financing cost associated with carrying inventory, particularly equipment held for sale, commonly backed by bank credit lines.
  • Same-Store Sales: Revenue growth measure from stores or locations open for the same period in both the current and previous year, isolating organic performance.
  • Bonus Depreciation: Accelerated tax deduction permitting 100% write-off of qualifying capital assets in the year placed in service.
  • OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer): Companies that produce equipment sold by Titan Machinery, such as CNH Industrial and other manufacturing brands.
