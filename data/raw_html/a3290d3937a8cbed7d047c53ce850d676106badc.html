Vita Coco (COCO) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 18, 2026 at 10:25AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, February 18, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Executive Chairman — Michael Kirban
  • Chief Executive Officer — Martin F. Roper
  • Chief Financial Officer — Corey Baker

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $610,000,000, up 18%, driven by 26% Vita Coco Coconut Water growth, partially offset by a 19% private label decline.
  • Americas Segment Sales -- $509,000,000, up 15%, with Vita Coco Coconut Water up 24% to $424,000,000, partially offset by private label down 30% to $63,000,000.
  • International Segment Sales -- Up 37%, with Vita Coco Coconut Water up 43% and private label growing 34%.
  • Gross Profit -- $223,000,000, increasing $24,000,000; gross margin was 37%, declining 200 bps due to higher product cost and tariffs, partially offset by favorable mix and pricing.
  • Tariff Expense -- $14,000,000 expensed in the year, with an additional $2,000,000 in inventory to flow through early 2026.
  • SG&A -- $140,000,000, rising with investments in personnel, supply capacity, and increased marketing spend.
  • Net Income -- $71,000,000 or $1.19 per diluted share, up 27%, driven by higher gross profit and FX derivative gains.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $98,000,000 (16% of net sales), up from $84,000,000, primarily due to higher gross profit.
  • Cash and Capital Position -- $197,000,000 in cash and no debt; $32,000,000 full-year cash generation after $27,000,000 working capital for inventory, $11,000,000 in share repurchases, and $8,000,000 capex.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Net sales $680,000,000-$700,000, gross margin ~38%, adjusted EBITDA $122,000,000–$128,000, driven by mid-teens U.S. category growth and continued international acceleration.
  • Private Label Outlook -- U.S. private label net sales projected to grow 20%-25%, paced by new and regained retail regions starting after Q1.
  • Gross Margin Drivers (2026) -- Expected improvement from branded pricing, tariff removal, and reduced ocean freight rates, partially offset by greater promotional and incentive activity.
  • SG&A Guidance (2026) -- Expected to increase mid- to high-single digits, with higher marketing and personnel offset by reduced incentive compensation and projected ~1 point of leverage over prior year.
  • Operational Capacity -- 2026 ocean freight partially secured (25% of requirements fixed); sufficient capacity and inventory are in place to support anticipated growth.
  • Walmart Distribution Reset -- Restored and expanded shelf space and aisle placement, adding roughly 6% to year-to-date U.S. brand trends according to management estimates.
  • International Growth Contribution -- International segment contributed 29% of total net sales growth; strong momentum in U.K. and Germany, with Europe positioned as a multi-year growth driver.

SUMMARY

Vita Coco (COCO +4.44%) management emphasized the transition of coconut water from a niche to a mainstream beverage, supported by broad-based growth in both domestic and international markets. Leadership attributed performance improvement to distribution recovery at Walmart (WMT 1.83%), product pricing, and category momentum, along with expanding global marketing and operational investments. For 2026, the company projects margin expansion and robust net sales gains fueled by international market penetration, new private-label wins, and disciplined cost control. Operational initiatives to reduce freight volatility and allocate marketing resources to reinforce brand positioning against private-label competition were directly highlighted as key strategies for sustaining top-line and profitability growth. Cash deployment priorities remain weighted toward core business expansion, with management reiterating selective openness to M&A when attractive options arise.

  • Martin F. Roper said, "Walmart is adding 5% to 6% to our total scans right now, which is very cool."
  • Management stated that as tariff-exempt inventory cycles in during 2026, cost-of-goods-sold improvements and promotional investment will be phased, resulting in net pricing gains early in the year and more promotional activity by Q3.
  • CFO Corey Baker reported $27,000,000 invested in inventory to support 2026 growth objectives, along with $11,000,000 in share repurchases during the year.
  • Leadership conveyed that additional international market expansion—particularly in Europe—may result in the segment ultimately matching the U.S. business in scale.
  • Management noted ongoing testing of new innovation in pack formats and flavors as well as fresh marketing campaigns aimed at active hydration occasions and partnerships with youth sports organizations.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Scan Data: Retailer point-of-sale data used to track unit sales and category trends in real time.
  • DSD Partners: Direct Store Delivery partners—distributors delivering product directly to retail stores rather than through warehouse shipments.
