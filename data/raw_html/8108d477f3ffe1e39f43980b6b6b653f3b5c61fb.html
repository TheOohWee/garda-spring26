Earnings Call Transcript

Darling (DAR) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 11:32AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 12, 2026 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — Randall C. Stuewe
  • Executive Vice President & Chief Financial Officer — Robert W. Day

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $336.1 million in the fourth quarter, up from $289 million in the fourth quarter of 2024 and $245 million in the previous quarter, showing both year-over-year and sequential growth.
  • Core Ingredients EBITDA -- $278.2 million in the fourth quarter versus $230 million in the fourth quarter of 2024 and $248 million in the previous quarter; fiscal year total of $922 million compared to $790 million in 2024.
  • Total Net Sales -- $1.7 billion, compared to $1.4 billion in the prior-year quarter.
  • Raw Material Volume -- 4.1 million metric tons in the fourth quarter, up from 3.8 million metric tons a year ago.
  • Gross Margin -- 25.1%, up from 23.5% in the prior-year quarter; Feed segment margin 24.6% versus 22.6% a year ago.
  • Feed Segment EBITDA -- $193 million, up from $150 million last year; Feed sales at $1.13 billion, compared to $924 million, on 3.4 million metric tons versus 3.1 million metric tons.
  • Food Segment EBITDA -- $82 million in the fourth quarter versus $64 million last year; segment sales at $429 million, up from $362 million, with gross margins of 27.2%.
  • Fuel Segment EBITDA (including Diamond Green Diesel) -- $85 million, up slightly from $84 million last year, despite an unfavorable $24 million lower-of-cost-or-market inventory adjustment at the Diamond Green Diesel entity.
  • Diamond Green Diesel (DGD) Operating Results -- $57.9 million fourth quarter EBITDA, or $0.41 per gallon; full-year DGD EBITDA of $1.037 billion on one billion gallons sold, equating to $0.21 per gallon.
  • DGD Dividend Flows -- Darling Ingredients contributed $328 million to DGD and received $368 million in dividends, with $285 million from production tax credit sales, $255 million paid during 2025 and the remainder scheduled for 2026.
  • Debt & Liquidity -- Net debt reduced to $3.8 billion as of Jan. 3, 2026, from $4 billion at 2024 year-end; leverage ratio improved to 2.9x from 3.9x; $1.3 billion remains available under the revolving facility.
  • Income & EPS -- Net income was $57 million, or $0.35 per diluted share, versus $102 million, or $0.63 per diluted share, in the fourth quarter of 2024. Non-GAAP adjusted EPS was $0.67 compared to $0.66 a year ago after $58 million in restructuring and impairment charges recorded in the quarter.
  • Brazil Asset Acquisition -- The company expects to close the acquisition of three Potense Group rendering facilities in Brazil for around $120 million, funded from first quarter 2026 cash flow.
  • First Quarter 2026 Outlook -- Core ingredients adjusted EBITDA expected in the range of $240 million–$250 million; DGD production estimated at 260 million gallons with improving margins.
  • Food JV Progress -- Joint venture with PB Leiner and Tessenderlo advancing per plan and regulatory reviews are underway.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $156 million in the fourth quarter; $380 million for the year; 2026 capital spend guided to be approximately $400 million, mostly maintenance.
  • Tax Position -- Fourth quarter income tax benefit of $11 million and full-year benefit of $9.4 million, driven by production tax credits; effective tax rate of negative 15.3% for 2025.
  • Portfolio Rationalization -- Management explicitly stated "we have taken steps to sharpen our portfolio," confirming ongoing asset sales and highlighting recent restructuring and impairment charges.

RISKS

  • Restructuring and impairment charges of $58 million in the fourth quarter, which management linked to a strategic portfolio review of underperforming businesses.
  • Advanced biofuels segment (Fuel segment, including Diamond Green Diesel) adjusted EBITDA dropped by 49% for the year, from $374 million to $192 million, reflecting the effect of a "challenging year for Diamond Green Diesel" and policy uncertainty.
  • Management warned that first quarter volumes and fat prices may be modestly lower versus the fourth quarter due to "severe weather in the Southeast and Eastern Shore" and seasonality, projecting a first quarter adjusted EBITDA pullback in the core business.
  • Uncertainty regarding final Renewable Volume Obligation (RVO) policy, with CFO Day saying, "we are not providing guidance and will reconsider that after we get a final ruling on the RVO," underscoring exposure to regulatory decisions.

SUMMARY

Darling Ingredients (DAR 0.33%) delivered significant adjusted EBITDA growth, margin expansion, and improved net leverage in the fourth quarter, supported by increased volumes in both Feed and Food segments and aided by favorable price lags and a 53-week fiscal year. Management finalized a strategic acquisition bid for three Brazilian rendering plants, further strengthening the company’s footprint and hinting at future asset acquisitions and sales as part of an ongoing portfolio optimization initiative. The Diamond Green Diesel joint venture produced record EBITDA per gallon in the quarter, but annual segment EBITDA declined sharply due to challenging market and policy conditions. Looking ahead, core ingredients first quarter EBITDA is forecasted at $240 million–$250 million, with management pointing to potential tailwinds from anticipated positive policy changes but acknowledging continued exposure to regulatory decisions and commodity volatility.

  • CFO Day said, "Our bank covenant preliminary leverage ratio at year end was 2.9 times versus 3.9 times at year end 2024."
  • CEO Stuewe stated, "there is no change in how we do business there," when asked about pricing lags or supplier formula modifications.
  • Day emphasized that final RVO outcomes will materially impact outlook and guidance for both DGD and Feed margins, stating, "going to depend on the kind of clarity and certainty we have."
  • Stuewe commented that "commodity gelatin part has, what I would say, leveled off and improved from where it was a year ago," and new NexData health and wellness products are gaining repeat orders, supporting Food segment momentum.
  • Day confirmed, "we expect it would be considerably less than what it was in 2025," due to fewer plant turnarounds and cost items.
  • Day explained the fourth quarter capital expenditure step-up as, "Some of this is just the teams wanting to make sure that they get certain things done by the end of the year and paying for the cost of doing that as bills come due."
  • Stuewe reaffirmed M&A caution: "On an M&A perspective, I would still say we are on an M&A holiday," emphasizing organic growth in Brazil, Paraguay, China, and the U.S.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • DGD (Diamond Green Diesel): The company’s 50% joint venture with Valero Energy Corporation producing renewable diesel from animal fats and used cooking oil.
  • RVO (Renewable Volume Obligation): Federal policy mandate setting the minimum volume requirements for renewable fuels required to be blended into transportation fuels in the U.S.
  • LCFS (Low Carbon Fuel Standard): Regulatory program requiring a reduction in carbon intensity of transportation fuels, notably operating in California, which affects renewable diesel and SAF demand.
  • SAF (Sustainable Aviation Fuel): Renewable fuel suitable for use in aviation, produced from feedstocks such as animal fats, and a key area of growth for DGD.
  • NexData: Darling Ingredients' proprietary product line targeting health, nutrition, and wellness markets with functional food ingredients.
  • LCM Inventory Adjustment: Lower-of-cost-or-market accounting adjustment affecting inventory valuation, impacting reported segment EBITDA in volatile periods.
