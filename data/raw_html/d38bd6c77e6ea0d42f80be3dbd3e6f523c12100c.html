Takeaways

  • Brinsupri revenue guidance -- Management projects Brinsupri revenue of at least $1 billion for 2026, citing "additional visibility we have gained into the market access environment and the early performance we have seen from Brinsupri so far this year."
  • First full quarter Brinsupri revenue -- Brinsupri generated $144.6 million in net revenue during its first full quarter.
  • New Brinsupri patient starts -- Approximately 11,550 new patients began Brinsupri treatment in 2025, representing less than 5% of the addressable diagnosed non-CF bronchiectasis patient population.
  • Brinsupri addressable market -- The U.S. addressable market is estimated at 500,000 currently diagnosed non-CF bronchiectasis patients, with roughly 150,000 meeting the two-or-more exacerbations profile.
  • Physician adoption trends -- Of approximately 4,000 prescribing physicians, nearly half have prescribed Brinsupri to a single patient, reflecting the “exploration stage” of launch dynamics outlined by management.
  • Payer access and approval rates -- Over 90% of targeted patient lives have payer access to Brinsupri, with management observing high approval rates even when documentation is required.
  • Gross-to-net guidance for Brinsupri -- For 2026, gross-to-net deductions are expected in the mid-20% to low-30% range, described as consistent with 2025 levels and “not anticipated to have a significant effect on the overall GTN of the product.”
  • ARIKAYCE revenue growth: Japan -- Japan posted 40% ARIKAYCE revenue growth, accounting for more than 25% of its global revenues in 2025.
  • ARIKAYCE global growth: Europe -- ARIKAYCE revenues in Europe increased at a faster percentage rate than Japan, albeit from a smaller base.
  • Company-level 2026 revenue guidance -- Management forecasts total company revenue to double or more compared to 2025, driven by Brinsupri and ARIKAYCE performance.
  • Cash position -- Insmed (INSM 3.00%) ended 2025 with approximately $1.4 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities.
  • Cost of product revenues -- Fourth quarter cost of product revenues was $44.2 million, representing 16.8% of revenues, reflecting improved gross margin profile from Brinsupri.
  • TPIP regulatory milestone -- The FDA granted orphan drug designation for treprostinil palmitil inhalation powder (TPIP) in pulmonary arterial hypertension, citing “plausible high hypothesis that your drug may be clinically superior to the same drugs already approved for the same indication.”
  • TPIP Phase III program -- Phase III trial in pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) will require only one pivotal study for registrational submission, with patients eligible to titrate up to 1,280 mcg TPIP daily.

Summary

Management emphasized that Brinsupri’s U.S. launch exceeded initial internal expectations, with launch indicators—patient starts, physician adoption, and payer access—supporting early billion-dollar revenue guidance for 2026. The company reports an expanding addressable market for Brinsupri, supported by data showing progressive disease dynamics and ongoing identification of undiagnosed bronchiectasis within COPD and asthma patient cohorts. ARIKAYCE saw significant growth in both Japan and Europe, and an upcoming ENCORE Phase III readout could unlock a sevenfold increase in its addressable patient population. Financially, leadership projects cash flow positivity without additional capital necessity, supported by a robust quarter-end cash balance and operational leverage from Brinsupri. The pipeline advanced with orphan drug status for TPIP, which enters a streamlined Phase III pathway for PAH as a result of FDA guidance.

  • Management explicitly stated, “we anticipate total company revenue in 2026 to be more than double the revenue we produced in 2025,” indicating transformational growth expectations from internal launches.
  • Only 15 drug launches in history have achieved $1 billion revenue in their second through fifth full quarters of launch, according to management; Insmed’s target implies expectations to join this select group.
  • The addressable bronchiectasis market may expand further as “the progressive nature of the disease and better patient reporting and documentation” shift more patients into the treatment-eligible segment.
  • Brinsupri’s U.S. billion-dollar revenue forecast for 2026 is “vastly weighted towards The US,” with negligible expected ex-U.S. contribution during that period.
  • Revenue and spending are both expected to increase as the pipeline is resourced, but core business models “a very clear path to cash flow positivity” even before potential business development investments.
  • Payer dynamics are described as ahead of expectations, with the majority of plans supporting either attestation or manageable documentation, and field teams in place to facilitate access and reimbursement procedures.
  • Upcoming ARIKAYCE Phase III ENCORE results, anticipated in March or April, may allow expansion from 30,000 to over 200,000 addressable patients if successful per management commentary.
  • FDA orphan drug designation for TPIP is grounded in “greater placebo corrected improvement in six minute walk distance compared to other approved oral or inhaled formulations” per the official FDA letter quoted during the call.
  • Physician feedback and refill rates for Brinsupri are described as “very positive,” driving confidence in increasing repeat prescribing once initial patient experience is established in physician practices.

Industry glossary

  • Brinsupri: Insmed's brand name for brensocatib, an oral DPP1 inhibitor for non-CF bronchiectasis.
  • ARIKAYCE: Insmed's liposomal amikacin inhalation therapy for Mycobacterium avium complex (MAC) lung disease.
  • TPIP: Treprostinil Palmitil Inhalation Powder, a long-acting inhaled prostanoid prodrug for pulmonary arterial hypertension.
  • Gross-to-net (GTN): The net price realization after accounting for discounts, rebates, returns, and patient assistance programs.
  • ENCORE trial: A Phase III clinical study designed to read out in March or April, potentially expanding ARIKAYCE's indicated population.
  • DPP1 inhibitor: Drug class targeting dipeptidyl peptidase 1, which regulates neutrophil serine protease activity, relevant for inflammatory lung diseases.
  • CT scan: Computed tomography imaging, required to diagnose bronchiectasis for Brinsupri eligibility.
  • Payer access: The proportion of covered lives with formal coverage or policy enabling Brinsupri reimbursement.
