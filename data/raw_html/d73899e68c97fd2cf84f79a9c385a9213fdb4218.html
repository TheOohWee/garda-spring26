Earnings Call Transcript

Mitek (MITK) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Jan 7, 2026 at 10:57AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, December 11, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Edward West
  • Chief Financial Officer — David Lyle

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $44.8 million in Q4, up 4%, driven by 19% growth in SaaS revenue.
  • Annual Revenue -- $180 million for fiscal year, split evenly between fraud and identity and check verification segments.
  • Fraud and Identity Revenue -- $90 million for the year, a 15% increase, now representing over half of total business and cited as the "growth engine."
  • Check Verification Revenue -- $90 million for the year, down from $94 million, attributed to deal timing and a previously recognized large contract.
  • SaaS Revenue Growth -- Accelerated to 21% for the year, with SaaS now at 43% of total revenue.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA was $54 million for the year, a 15% gain, producing a 30% margin, up from 27% last year.
  • Q4 Adjusted EBITDA -- $12.9 million with a 28.7% margin.
  • Non-GAAP Net Income -- $45 million for the year; Q4 non-GAAP net income of $11.1 million ($0.24 per diluted share on 47.3 million shares).
  • Free Cash Flow -- $54 million for the year, equaling 100% of adjusted EBITDA conversion, boosted by nonrecurring benefits (e.g, convertible debt arbitrage).
  • Q4 Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 84%, down approximately 200 basis points, due to higher SaaS services investment.
  • Full-Year Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 85%, down from 86%, reflecting a greater SaaS/services revenue mix.
  • Operating Expense -- Non-GAAP annual operating expense fell 2% to $100.9 million (operating expense intensity improved from 60% to 56%).
  • Fraud and Identity SaaS -- Now over half of portfolio—SaaS growth described as "accelerating" with a mix of 43% of revenue.
  • Check Fraud Defender -- Annual contract value (ACV) up 50%, with consortium data covering more than 25% of all U.S. checking accounts (approaching 50% with pilot banks), but ACV below goal due to delayed enterprise decisions.
  • Automation -- Companywide automation reached approximately 90% of identity transactions.
  • Share Repurchases -- $5 million repurchased during the year; an additional $7.7 million since fiscal year-end, with $13.6 million remaining authorized through May 2026.
  • Cash and Investments -- $196 million at fiscal year-end, with $157 million in total debt and a net cash position of $40 million.
  • Convertible Debt -- $155 million set to be retired by February 1, 2026, with liquidity from term loan and revolver.
  • Internal Controls Remediation -- All material weaknesses have been fully remediated and disclosed as completed.
  • Fiscal 2026 Revenue Guidance -- $185 million to $195 million (implying about 6% growth at midpoint).
  • Fiscal 2026 Fraud and Identity Guidance -- $101 million to $105 million, targeting 15% growth at midpoint.
  • Fiscal 2026 Adjusted EBITDA Margin Guidance -- Projected in the 27% to 30% range, with EBITDA dollars roughly flat due to reinvestment.
  • Tax Rate Guidance -- Fiscal 2026 non-GAAP tax rate expected to decline to roughly 10% (from 21%), attributed to U.S. tax legislation changes.
  • Revenue Classification Change -- Beginning this quarter, revenues are reported in fraud and identity and check verification, with license/maintenance now combined as a single line to mirror how customers purchase.

SUMMARY

Management emphasized sustained client demand for end-to-end fraud and identity solutions, spurred by AI-driven synthetic fraud risks. Leadership attributed the SaaS revenue acceleration and margin improvement to automation, consolidated product platforms, and operational discipline. A large proportion of U.S. checking accounts is now covered by consortium data sets, strengthening differentiation in fraud detection. The company has fully addressed all material internal control deficiencies, marking a compliance milestone cited as significant in the call.

  • Management said “Check Fraud Defender ACV for the year came in below our initial goal, primarily due to the timing of large enterprise deployments.”
  • “Flat non-GAAP net income despite 15% growth in adjusted EBITDA was driven primarily by a higher non-GAAP tax rate—21% in fiscal 2025 compared to 9% in fiscal 2024—affecting bottom-line earnings despite operational improvement.”
  • Chief Financial Officer Lyle confirmed all $155 million in convertible debt will be repaid at maturity, with the company retaining flexibility in cash versus facility use.
  • Forecast calls for a continued shift in investment toward R&D and go-to-market, offset by efficiencies in G&A and marketing, which management asserted will maintain or improve overall operating expense intensity.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Check Fraud Defender (CFD): Mitek's consortium-based SaaS tool that aggregates transactional and behavioral datasets from participating financial institutions to detect and prevent check fraud.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Company's non-GAAP earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for items such as stock-based compensation and restructuring.
  • Annual Contract Value (ACV): The value of all active, recurring contractual agreements expected to be recognized from a single year of service, used to measure SaaS and consortium product momentum.
