Earnings Call Transcript

BGSF (BGSF) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 12, 2026 at 9:51AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, March 12, 2026 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Keith R. Schroeder
  • President, Real Estate — Kelly Brown

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $22 million, representing a 9.4% decrease, attributed to lower billed hours and weak demand from property management clients facing cost pressures.
  • Gross Profit -- $7.7 million, down from $8.7 million; gross margin was 35% and would have been 35.6% excluding $147,000 in out-of-period workers' comp costs.
  • SG&A Expenses -- $9.3 million, lower than the prior year’s $10.5 million, but the figure included $403,000 in strategic review costs and $460,000 in out-of-period expenses mainly from self-insurance and divestiture-related adjustments.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Loss of $947,000, improved from a $1.6 million loss in the prior year, due to significant cost reductions in selling and administrative expenses, despite $1 million lower gross profit.
  • Net Loss from Continuing Operations (GAAP) -- $0.11 per diluted share; non-GAAP adjusted EPS loss from continuing operations was $0.09 per share; consolidated adjusted non-GAAP EPS positive $0.09 per share.
  • Full-Year Net Cash from Operations -- $117,000, which included a $5.2 million escrow receivable related to the professional division sale; settlement expected in Q2.
  • Capital Expenditures -- Minimal at $138,000 for the year.
  • Share Repurchases -- 351,200 shares purchased for $1.5 million in 2025; cumulative total to date is 522,000 shares at a cost of $2.4 million.
  • Debt Position -- All outstanding debt retired following the professional division sale; company now operates debt-free with a strong cash position.
  • Special Dividend and Buyback -- Paid a $2 per share special dividend and announced a $5 million share buyback.
  • Ongoing G&A Outlook -- Future general and administrative costs estimated at $12 million, including roughly $2 million in public company expenses, with ongoing efforts to achieve further reductions.
  • Annualized Cost Savings -- Organizational and incentive changes expected to deliver $1 million in annualized savings, with full impact reflected by Q3.
  • Revenue Trend (Early 2026) -- Management stated that sales for January and February are "slightly ahead" of the prior year, indicating a positive start; expectation communicated for full-year top-line growth in the "mid-single digits" for 2026.
  • PropTech Market Initiative -- Launching first software partnership with Yardi to support the property tech market; initial consultant pool of 8–12 with first-year revenue targeted at $1 million–$2 million, growing organically.
  • AI and Technology Enhancement -- Ongoing investments in AI and platform technology have improved operational efficiency and client engagement, with specific mention of improved front- and back-office processes.
  • Brand Repositioning -- Website to transition to bgstaffing.com after TSA completion to enhance SEO, brand recognition, and marketing effectiveness.
  • Seasonality and Fixed G&A -- G&A expenses described as "pretty flat" across quarters, while variable selling expenses increase in stronger seasonal quarters due to higher bonuses and commissions.
  • TSA Agreement -- Transitional services agreement from the division sale progressing as planned, with wrap-up expected by the end of Q1.
  • Conference Participation -- Scheduled as an exhibitor at major rental housing and real estate industry events in June, positioning for enhanced customer engagement and lead generation.

SUMMARY

Management affirmed the transition to a pure-play real estate staffing organization, with all debt eliminated and significant shareholder capital returned following the professional division sale. The company outlined the launch of new technology-enabled service lines, specifically entering the PropTech support segment through a nationwide partnership with Yardi and a dedicated consultant network. Operational changes—including AI-driven workflow upgrades and process streamlining—were credited for improved year-start revenue momentum, which management characterized as ahead of the 2025 pace. Cost controls were detailed, with ongoing fixed G&A optimization and expectations for $1 million in annualized expense reductions materializing in the coming quarters. The capital allocation strategy continues with ongoing share buybacks and the expectation to settle a $5.2 million escrow in the upcoming quarter.

  • CEO Schroeder said, "We are now estimating ongoing G&A costs to be in the $12,000,000 range, with public company costs estimated at approximately $2,000,000."
  • President Brown described the Yardi venture as "our first software partnership" and sees potential to serve all current customer properties via technology-enabled solutions.
  • Schroeder confirmed revenues in January and February were above prior-year levels, marking the first positive sales trend in multiple quarters.
  • Management specifically expects that "full-year sales in 2026 to be over 2025, in the mid-single digits."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • TSA (Transitional Services Agreement): Contractual arrangement under which the seller of a business division continues to provide certain services to the buyer for a specified transition period post-sale.
  • PropTech: Property technology; innovations and digital platforms that modernize how real estate is managed, operated, and serviced.
  • Yardi Independent Consultant Network: A network of qualified consultants certified to provide support, implementation, and training for Yardi’s property management software solutions.
  • SEO (Search Engine Optimization): Strategies and processes used to increase the visibility and ranking of web content in internet search engine results.
  • AI (Artificial Intelligence): Technology that enables machines or software to mimic cognitive functions such as learning and problem solving, applied here to recruiting and operational workflows.
  • IBNR (Incurred But Not Reported): Estimated insurance claims that have occurred but have not yet been reported to the insurer, impacting self-insurance reserves.
