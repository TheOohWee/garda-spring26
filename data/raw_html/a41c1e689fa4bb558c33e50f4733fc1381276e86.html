SUMMARY

News Corporation (NWSA 0.45%) emphasized accelerated shareholder returns through an expanded $1.3 billion buyback program, with planned execution at a faster pace fueled by post-divestiture proceeds from the Foxtel sale. The company highlighted Dow Jones' shifting revenue mix toward higher-margin B2B services, underpinned by strong growth in Risk & Compliance and Energy, and asserted the durability of digital and recurring revenues. Acquisitions in data and Manga content, coupled with integration initiatives, signal ongoing portfolio concentration on scalable, high-growth verticals. Management described robust digital subscriber and audience expansion within both Dow Jones and Digital Real Estate segments while addressing continued Book Publishing softness attributed to challenging market conditions and difficult prior-year comparables. Legal and commercial strategies to protect intellectual property in the AI age emerged as a management priority, reflecting a broader industry debate with expected financial impacts.

  • Thomson stated, "we expect to begin executing repurchases at an accelerated rate soon after the release of these results," directly linking operational performance and capital returns.
  • The Foxtel Group divestment completed in early April, with financial results now recast as discontinued operations, materially increases balance sheet flexibility.
  • Chandrashekar emphasized that proceeds from the Foxtel sale will "benefit from the approximately $380 million of proceeds from repayment of Foxtel shareholder loans," directly supporting repurchase capacity.
  • Professional Information Business now represents 39% of Dow Jones revenue and around half its profits, with revenue growth of 10% in Q4—the highest quarterly increase this year.
  • Audience engagement at Realtor.com reached record levels, with product investments such as the Zenlist acquisition and RealPRO Select driving higher revenue per lead.
  • LSEG partnership is broadening the digital distribution of Dow Jones content, yielding higher margin subscribers through reduced acquisition and retention costs.
  • Management did not provide a target leverage ratio but described the balance sheet as "extremely conservative" and committed to maintaining financial flexibility.
  • AI licensing negotiations are underway, and management signaled legal action against unauthorized use of proprietary content, naming DeepSeek in reference to "potential abuse."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Foxtel Group: A pay-TV and streaming business previously part of News Corporation's Subscription Video Services segment, recently sold to DAZN.
  • REA Group: News Corp's majority-owned Australian digital real estate portal operator, including realestate.com.au.
  • Risk & Compliance: Dow Jones's B2B product line offering information and analytical services supporting anti-money-laundering, sanctions, and due diligence requirements.
  • LSEG: London Stock Exchange Group, referenced as a new enterprise partner for Dow Jones digital content distribution.
  • Zenlist: A mobile-first real estate platform acquired by News Corporation to enhance Realtor.com’s agent and customer search experience.
  • RealPRO Select: Realtor.com's product offering targeting higher-quality and more profitable lead generation for agents.
