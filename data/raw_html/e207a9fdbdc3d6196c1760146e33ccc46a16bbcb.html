  • Chief Financial Officer — David Jordan

TAKEAWAYS

  • Gross Billings -- $24.3 billion, a 15% increase year over year and 13% in constant currency, setting a new company record.
  • Non-GAAP Diluted EPS -- $3.83, up 24% year over year, establishing an all-time high.
  • TD SYNNEX ex-HIVE Gross Billings -- Increased 10% year over year, with both gross profit and operating income up double digits.
  • HIVE Segment Billings -- Rose over 50% year over year, while ODM Centimeters within HIVE grew 39% year over year, driven by demand in cloud data center infrastructure.
  • Net Revenue -- $17.4 billion, up 10% year over year, exceeding the high end of guidance.
  • Gross Profit -- $1.2 billion, a 15% increase year over year.
  • Gross Margin (as % of Billings) -- 5%, flat year over year.
  • Non-GAAP SG&A Expense -- $698 million or 3% of gross billings; cost to gross profit ratio improved by approximately 100 basis points year over year to 58%.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Income -- $497 million, up 18% year over year; operating margin as a percentage of billings was 2.04%, a five basis point improvement.
  • Interest Expense and Finance Charges -- $88 million, increasing by $1 million year over year.
  • Non-GAAP Effective Tax Rate -- Approximately 24%, up from 21% in the prior year.
  • Total Non-GAAP Net Income -- $313 million, with non-GAAP diluted EPS up 24% year over year to $3.83.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $1.4 billion for both the quarter and full year, exceeding expectations and marking the third consecutive year over $1 billion.
  • Capital Return -- $209 million returned to shareholders in the quarter ($173 million in share repurchases, $36 million in dividends); $742 million for the year and $2.2 billion cumulatively over three years (61% of free cash flow).
  • Net Working Capital -- $2.9 billion, down approximately $300 million from the prior year; gross cash days improved by two days to twelve days.
  • Balance Sheet -- $2.4 billion in cash and cash equivalents; $4.6 billion in debt; gross leverage ratio of 2.4x, net leverage ratio 1.1x.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- $1.2 billion remains available as of November 30.
  • Dividend -- Cash dividend of $0.48 per common share approved, payable January 30, 2026.
  • 2026 Q1 Guidance -- Non-GAAP gross billings expected between $22.7 billion and $23.7 billion (12% increase at midpoint); net revenue of $15.1 billion to $15.9 billion with an anticipated 33% gross to net adjustment; non-GAAP net income of $243 million to $283 million; non-GAAP diluted EPS of $3 to $3.5 on approximately 80.1 million shares outstanding.
  • Seasonality Impact -- "We are anticipating a cash outflow in Q1 in part due to typical seasonality of the business and due to the timing impact that benefited Q4."
  • CapEx Outlook -- "We're probably planning for a similar level of CapEx in '26 relative to '25. And that would include the investments needed to support Hive's continued growth."
  • Strategic Execution -- Management reiterated focus on omnichannel engagement, specialized go-to-market, enablement, and brand visibility as core strategic pillars.
  • AIPCs -- "AIPCs performed incrementally better this quarter," and their increasing weight is viewed positively for future growth.

RISKS

  • "non-GAAP effective tax rate was approximately 24% compared to 21% in the prior year," indicating a higher tax expense burden.
  • "We are anticipating a cash outflow in Q1 in part due to typical seasonality of the business and due to the timing impact that benefited Q4," suggesting temporary pressure on near-term cash flow.

SUMMARY

TD SYNNEX (SNX +0.24%) reported record-breaking gross billings and non-GAAP diluted EPS, driven by double-digit gains across core business lines and exceptional growth in its HIVE segment. Management attributed broad-based regional growth to a diversified technology portfolio and highlighted the expansion of operating income faster than sales across Asia Pacific, Europe, and North America. The company announced a continued commitment to shareholder returns, with an additional $1.2 billion in repurchase authorization and a declared $0.48 dividend for the current quarter. Cash flow performance remains a focal point with free cash flow exceeding $1 billion for three years running and balance sheet optimization including new debt issuance ahead of upcoming maturities. Strategic investments were emphasized, particularly in HIVE and digital platforms, positioning the company for additional opportunities in cloud, AI, and infrastructure markets in 2026.

  • Zammit said, "gross billings in the high single digits year over year, while improving both its gross margin and operating margin profile."
  • Jordan stated, "Globally, PCs have now increased double digits for four consecutive quarters, and we expect continued momentum heading into the initial months of 2026," citing the Windows 11 refresh as a key demand driver.
  • Management confirmed, "seen any demand destruction from higher component cost," with recent ASP increases for memory-related products reflected in short-term guidance.
  • The gross to net adjustment has increased due to a larger mix of software and HIVE netted programs, a shift expected to persist as stated in Q1 guidance commentary.
  • HIVE’s growth comprised contributions from both ODM Centimeters and supply chain services, with the latter characterized as "lumpy opportunistic business."
  • TD SYNNEX plans to maintain CapEx levels into 2026, supporting expansion initiatives, especially in the HIVE segment, to address ongoing and potential new customer programs.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Gross Billings: The total dollar value of customer invoices prior to subtracting reductions for netted or agented transactions.
  • HIVE: TD SYNNEX’s business unit providing assembly, engineering, and supply chain solutions for hyperscaler customers, particularly in cloud infrastructure and data center racks.
  • ODM Centimeters: Term used by TD SYNNEX for the original design manufacturing and customized solutions segment within HIVE that addresses broad hyperscaler needs.
  • ASP: Average selling price for a given set of products, referenced in context with memory components, PCs, servers, and storage devices.
  • AIPCs: AI-enabled personal computers, noted as a higher-ASP, growth-focused product segment within the TD SYNNEX PC portfolio.
  • Gross to Net Adjustment: The reduction from gross billings to recognized net revenue due to netted or agent-arranged sales, with higher software and certain HIVE transactions causing larger adjustments.
