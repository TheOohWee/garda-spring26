Risks

  • Michael McCann said, "we anticipate a softer start in 2026 with revenue building throughout the year," citing delayed healthcare spending and seasonality in industrial manufacturing, which will result in first-quarter revenue similar to last year and lower adjusted EBITDA due to higher SG&A.
  • Jayme Brooks noted, "we do not expect 2026 to have the same gross margin write-ups of $900,000 that we had in 2025," potentially impacting reported margins compared to prior year.
  • Ongoing integration of Pioneer Power remains a margin drag, with management explicitly stating gross margin is below legacy levels; improvement is anticipated to be realized "throughout 2026" and not immediate.

Takeaways

  • Total Q4 revenue -- Limbach Holdings (LMB 2.15%) reported $186.9 million, up 30.1%; ODR revenue reached $145 million, rising 51.8%, with 23.9% organic growth and 27.9% from acquisitions.
  • Q4 GCR revenue -- $41.9 million, down 13%, reflecting a 26.1% decline in organic revenue offset by 13.1% growth from acquisitions.
  • Q4 total gross margin -- 25.7%, down from 30.3%, primarily due to lower margins from Pioneer Power.
  • Q4 adjusted EBITDA -- $27.2 million, up 30%, with adjusted EBITDA margin at 14.6% versus 14.5% a year ago.
  • Q4 net income -- $12.3 million, increasing 25%; diluted EPS grew 24.4% to $1.20; adjusted EPS rose 21.7% to $1.40.
  • Q4 operating cash flow -- $28.1 million, up from $19.3 million; free cash flow at $21.1 million, a $4.5 million increase.
  • Full-year revenue -- $646.8 million, up 24.7%; ODR increased 40.6%, GCR decreased 7%; acquisitions contributed $109.1 million, and organic revenue grew $18.9 million or 3.6%.
  • Full-year adjusted EBITDA -- $81.8 million, up 28.4%, within guidance of $80 million-$86 million.
  • ODR revenue mix -- ODR accounted for 75% of total 2025 revenue; GCR comprised 25%.
  • Gross profit and margin -- Total gross profit increased 17.4% to $169.3 million; total gross margin declined from 27.8% to 26.2%; legacy branch business gross margin was 28.2% excluding acquisitions since 2021.
  • Balance sheet and liquidity -- Net debt was $24.6 million; net debt to adjusted EBITDA ratio of 0.3x; total liquidity of $96.3 million at year-end.
  • 2026 guidance -- Management expects revenue of $730 million-$760 million (13%-17% growth) and adjusted EBITDA of $90 million-$94 million (10%-16% growth); ODR organic growth projected at 9%-12%, with organic total revenue up 4%-8%.
  • Share repurchase program -- $50 million authorized in December.
  • Record Q4 bookings -- $225 million in Q4 bookings versus $187 million in quarterly revenue, providing visibility into 2026.
  • Headquarters relocation -- Announced move to Tampa, Florida, where nearly 40% of the corporate workforce is based.
  • Data center focus -- Data center vertical represented less than 5% of 2025 revenue; company dedicated resources to develop national vertical market teams and was awarded a $10 million specialty infrastructure project in Q4.
  • Acquisition performance -- Pioneer Power’s gross margin is below the level of prior acquisition Jake Marshall at time of purchase (13.4%); Jake Marshall’s margin grew to 28% in 2025 after four years of integration.
  • 2026 free cash flow target -- Targeted at 75% of adjusted EBITDA; CapEx expected at approximately $5 million; no additional rental fleet investments anticipated.
  • Strategic hires -- Two senior executives assigned to accelerate national and local sales as part of the ODR and growth strategy.

Summary

The company reported record revenue and profitability, driven by a continued shift to ODR, acquisitions, and expansion in targeted vertical markets. Management highlighted strategic investments in national and local sales leadership, a $50 million share repurchase authorization, and the relocation of headquarters to Tampa, Florida. The 2026 outlook projects double-digit revenue and EBITDA growth, but first-half results are expected to reflect lower profitability due to seasonality, integration costs, and reduced gross margin write-ups.

  • Pioneer Power's post-acquisition integration is entering a phase focused on gross margin improvement, following a strategy proven with the Jake Marshall acquisition's margin gains from 13.4% to 28% over four years.
  • Healthcare and data center customers are deepening early engagement with the company, resulting in large contract wins including a $15 million multi-hospital project and a $10 million specialty infrastructure award, respectively.
  • Fourth-quarter bookings exceeded current revenue, with a 1.2x ratio cited as providing strong 2026 visibility and indicating forward sales momentum across key verticals.
  • Management stated, "our focus is now on growth," with a three-pillar strategy centered on ODR organic revenue expansion, margin enhancement through bundled customer solutions, and targeted M&A aligned with strategic value and culture fit.
  • National account opportunities are expected to become material to results in 2027, with 2026 described as foundational for strengthening national relationships and capitalizing on the local plus national execution model.

Industry glossary

  • ODR (Owner Direct Relationships): Revenue from direct contracts with property owners, focusing on facility planning, lifecycle solutions, and maintenance, typically featuring recurring or project-based work.
  • GCR (General Contractor Relationships): Revenue from subcontracting to general contractors, usually on larger construction projects with longer cycles and higher transaction size.
  • MEPC: Mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and control systems—core components of building infrastructure addressed by the company’s solutions.
  • REVOLVE customer solutions: The company's bundled suite of differentiated facility service offerings including integrated planning, maintenance, retrofits, new MEPC infrastructure, and energy decarbonization projects.
