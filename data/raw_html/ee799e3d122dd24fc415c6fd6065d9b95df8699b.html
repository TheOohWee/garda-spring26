WD-40 (WDFC) Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 2:46PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, October 17, 2024 at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Steve Brass
  • Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Sara Hyzer

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Net sales -- $156 million for the quarter, an increase of over 11%, marking the second consecutive record sales quarter.
  • WD-40 Multi-Use Product annual sales -- $453 million, up 11%, setting a new annual record for the core brand.
  • Gross margin -- 54.1% for the quarter, improving 100 basis points from the third quarter and 270 basis points from the same quarter last year.
  • Americas segment sales -- $79 million, up approximately 6%; includes 7% WD-40 Multi-Use Product increase, with 63% growth in Latin America.
  • EIMEA segment sales -- $59 million, up approximately 16%, with WD-40 Multi-Use Product volumes up 16% and distributor market sales up 41%.
  • Asia Pacific segment sales -- $18 million, up approximately 21%, driven by a 26% increase in WD-40 Multi-Use Product sales.
  • Full-year revenue (Non-GAAP constant currency) -- $583 million, up 8%, in line with published guidance.
  • Full-year gross margin -- 53.4%, slightly above guidance expectations.
  • Home care and cleaning brands sales -- Approximately $24 million for the year, representing 4% of global business; divestiture targeted for completion in the first half of fiscal 2025.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin -- 17% for both the quarter and full year, with EBITDA up 8% in dollars year over year.
  • Net income -- $16.8 million for the quarter, up about 1%; $69.6 million for the year, with a 6% annual increase.
  • Diluted EPS -- $1.23 for the quarter and $5.11 for the full year; annual EPS up $0.28, or 6%, supported by 13.6 million average shares outstanding.
  • Employee incentive compensation -- $16.5 million accrued for the year, increasing $8.8 million from last year and $4.6 million in the fourth quarter alone due to business recovery.
  • Inventory reduction -- Nearly $40 million, or 34%, from peak levels, now considered stable for ongoing operations.
  • Cash flow from operations -- Approximately $92 million, with $25 million used to pay down short-term higher interest borrowings.
  • Return on invested capital -- 25.5% for the year, up from 23.7%, achieving internal targets.
  • Fiscal 2025 guidance (pro forma, excluding divested brands) -- Net sales projected at $600 million to $630 million in constant currency, representing 6%-11% growth; gross margin expected at 54%-55%; operating income forecasted at $95-$100 million, up 6%-12%; EPS guided to $5.20-$5.45, representing 9%-14% growth over 2024 pro forma results.
  • Gross margin segment performance -- Americas gross margin improved 350 basis points to 52.5%; EIMEA up 190 basis points to 55.5%; Asia Pacific up 70 basis points to 56.4%.
  • Sales opportunity estimates -- Management estimates the global benchmark sales opportunity for WD-40 Multi-Use Product at $1.6 billion, with 28% achieved to date; WD-40 Specialist at $605 million, with 12% achieved.
  • Brazil market transition -- $7 million net sales contribution during the first six months of direct distribution, expected to add $7-$9 million in growth in the following year.
  • Dividend policy -- Quarterly cash dividend of $0.88 per share approved; annual dividends targeted at over 50% of earnings.
  • Pro forma 2024 exclusion of HCCP -- Fiscal year 2024 pro forma net sales would have been $567 million; pro forma gross margin at 53.9%; pro forma operating income $89.3 million; pro forma EPS $4.76.

Summary

WD-40 Company (WDFC 4.04%) reported substantial growth in all three geographic segments, led by record quarterly and annual sales of its flagship Multi-Use Product and robust gross margin recovery that approached long-term targets across trade blocks. The shift to direct distribution in Brazil and sustained premiumization efforts notably contributed to both sales expansion and profitability improvements. Management confirmed the planned divestiture of home care and cleaning brands and moved 2025 guidance to a pro forma basis, clarifying future core performance expectations. Capital allocation remained focused on dividend commitments and deleveraging, while segment-specific performance and supply chain execution supported enhanced operational metrics.

  • Sara Hyzer described the 2025 guidance as “For fiscal year 2025, we are estimating net sales growth from the pro forma 2024 results is projected to be between 6% and 11%, with net sales between $600 million and $630 million in constant currency,” with operating income targeted at “between $95 million and $100 million.”
  • Hyzer stated, “We forecast an annual boost to gross margin of approximately 60 basis points post divestiture.”
  • Brass highlighted the underpenetrated opportunity by saying, “after 71 years, we've achieved only approximately 28% and of the benchmark sales opportunity for WD-40 Multi-Use Product.”
  • The company announced that its ERP implementation now covers 50% of the business, which management expects will “result in smoother implementations going forward.”
  • A change in functional currency for the UK subsidiary to euro from pound sterling will occur September 1, 2024, due to a growing dependence on euro-denominated transactions in the EIMEA region.
  • Management intends to maintain incentive compensation and information technology investments at elevated levels while the ERP rollout and Brazil integration continue in 2025.

Industry glossary

  • EIMEA: Europe, India, Middle East, and Africa geographic segment used for reporting and management in WD-40 Company’s filings and presentations.
  • HCCP: Home Care and Cleaning Product brands, a portfolio within WD-40 Company targeted for divestiture.
  • Smart Straw: A WD-40 product format featuring a permanently attached straw for targeted spray application, positioned as a premium offering in maintenance products.
  • ERP: Enterprise Resource Planning system, a software platform used for integrated management of core business processes and operations.
