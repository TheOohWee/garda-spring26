TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $6.1 million, representing a 12% increase year over year and a 28.5% increase fiscal year-to-date.
  • Gross Margin -- 52.7%, compared with 54% in the prior year and 54.8% in the prior quarter; decline attributed to product mix.
  • Operating Expenses -- $10.1 million versus $7 million a year ago and $6.7 million in the prior quarter.
  • Research and Development Expense -- $7.5 million, up from $4 million in the prior year and $3.8 million in the preceding quarter, due to IP purchase for PLATO and related consulting.
  • Net Loss -- $3 million or $0.09 per diluted share; included $3.6 million in interest and other income, a $6.2 million non-cash warrant valuation adjustment, $2.8 million issuance costs, and a $251,000 tax benefit.
  • Cash and Cash Equivalents -- $70.7 million as of quarter end, up primarily from $46.9 million in net proceeds raised in an October 22, 2025, registered direct offering.
  • Fourth Quarter Outlook -- Net revenues projected between $5.7 million and $6.5 million, with gross margin guidance in the 54%-56% range.
  • Customer Concentration -- KYEC revenue accounted for $1.1 million or 17.9% of net revenue, Nokia $675,000 or 11.1%, and Cadence $233,000 or 3.8% in the period; values diverged materially from comparable prior periods for each customer.
  • Government Funding -- More than $1 million in expected funding for Sentinel proof-of-concept, to offset R&D expenses.
  • Gemini II Benchmarks -- Achieved three-second time to first token (TTFT) for HLON tasks using text and video input at approximately 30 watts system power, with management citing "up to three times faster first token at a lower power than the competitive chip on the same workload."
  • Segment Shipment Mix -- SigmaQuad products constituted 41.7% of shipments, military defense 28.5%.
  • Product Development Progress -- PLATO hardware development underway following IP acquisition, with operations targeted for early 2027, and ongoing Gemini II edge AI proof-of-concept initiatives.

SUMMARY

GSI Technology (GSIT +0.36%) secured increased cash reserves following a significant capital raise, resulting in substantial working capital and equity growth. The company emphasized its differentiated edge AI positioning by reporting competitive Gemini II benchmark results and deepening ties with defense and commercial partners through funded projects and proof-of-concept engagements. New disclosures initiated for cash flow components signal added financial transparency for future reporting periods.

  • Management asserted that edge inference performance metrics such as TTFT and power efficiency are now prioritized over traditional peak training performance for prospective customers.
  • Current and prospective government projects—including SBIR, BAA, and other funding opportunities—may expand non-dilutive capital sources according to company commentary.
  • Government funding for the Sentinel and related programs is designated specifically for offsetting development expenses, not general cash consumption.
  • Shift in customer revenue contributions highlights changes in product and geographic end-market exposures for the period.
  • Company plans further improvements to Gemini II’s TTFT within the next five months in advance of planned government demonstrations and commercial proof-of-concept use cases.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • APU (Associative Processing Unit): A specialized chip architecture designed for tasks involving similarity search and AI inference at memory-level data speeds rather than traditional CPU/GPU compute structures.
  • TTFT (Time to First Token): The elapsed time from input initiation to the model producing its first output token, a critical responsiveness metric in edge AI and surveillance applications.
  • SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research): A U.S. government program that provides non-dilutive R&D funding to small and emerging technology companies focused on federal objectives.
  • BAA (Broad Agency Announcement): A government contracting mechanism to solicit research proposals on specific high-level topics, often outside the traditional procurement process.
  • SigmaQuad: A proprietary high-performance, high-bandwidth static random access memory (SRAM) product line used primarily in telecom and defense applications.

Revenue in the third quarter increased by 12% year over year and 28.5% on a fiscal year-to-date basis. This government funding will offset our cost to build a software stack and the libraries needed for this project. Didier will share more details shortly. Another recent milestone is Gemini II's time to first token benchmark, announced in the press release earlier today. For those who have not reviewed it, please see today's release for the full detail on the benchmark result and the methodology.

Accordingly, we reported three-second time to first token, or TTFT, performance for HLON with text and video input, consuming approximately 30 watts of system power. Compared to third-party testing of competitive platforms, Gemini II's TTFT delivered up to three times faster first token at a lower power than the competitive chip on the same workload. We believe these test results validate Gemini II's fast response for edge use cases that need low power and low latency. In his comments, Didier will expand on the benchmark results. We are making steady progress for the Benzene pretzel, continue to improve Gemini II performance, and completing the 17 of watches.

We are also pursuing early proof of concept and prototyping opportunities for Gemini II in systems in different programs, including zone and unmanned systems, and in selected commercial edge deployments. In parallel, we continue to pursue long-diluted R&D funding through government defense programs and strategic partners. With that, I hand the call over to Didier Lasserre.

Didier Lasserre: Thank you, Lee-Lean. I will start by expanding on some of Lee-Lean's comments. On the Sentinel POC, we expect to receive more than $1 million in government funding. We will record this as an offset to R&D expenses. We plan to use it to complete key software milestones for the project, including software development for GEMMA 312B on our Gemini II ahead of the planned demonstration to the government agencies later this year. Our POC partner, G2 Tech, is receiving additional funds to develop the drone platform for this demo. I am pleased to share that G2 Tech conducted a competitive evaluation, and GSI was selected based on Gemini II's performance, delivering the lowest TTFT at 30 watts.

If the government evaluation later this year is successful, it could lead to a potential Gemini II design win with G2 Tech, and we would move to pursue additional opportunities with other drone and unmanned system customers beyond the POC sponsors. Turning to today's press release, our Gemini II TTFT benchmarks discussed preliminary results showing a three-second time to first token for a multimodal model at the edge using video and text inputs at approximately 30 watts of system power. TTFT is how long it takes the system to produce the first response, which is critical for drones and unmanned systems. The threshold for a useful TTFT in video surveillance to ensure nothing is missed is three seconds.

That means we are sampling the video image every three seconds. If the TTFT is ten seconds, it takes too long, so the surveillance video could miss something. In preparation for the Sentinel demo, we will continue improving TTFT over the next five months to further reduce Gemini II's first time to response. What's exciting for GSI about these Gemini II preliminary benchmark results is that they demonstrate what compute and memory can provide for physical AI: faster time to first token and materially lower chip power. This will help enable a broader set of viable, cost-effective deployments.

For added color, at CES 2026, there was a clear shift towards edge AI and physical AI systems that must make real-time decisions under tight power constraints. In that context, Intel noted that TOPS, the number of operations per second, does not tell the whole story. What matters more in edge AI and physical AI is real-world workload performance and efficiency. That is the takeaway for us as well. For edge inference, performance per watt and responsiveness matter more than peak training metrics. We are confident that our compute and memory APU architecture, designed to reduce data movement, is well-suited for power-constrained edge inference.

Our near focus is to continue validating this with additional benchmarks and customer proof of concepts and convert that progress into design wins for Gemini II. To be clear, we are not trying to compete with folks at training and data centers. Our goal is to be a strong option for fast, low-power edge AI applications. Switching to the customer and product sales breakdown, in 2026, sales to KYEC were $1.1 million or 17.9% of net revenues, compared to $1.2 million or 22.7% of net revenues in the same period a year ago, and $802,000 or 12.5% of net revenues in the prior quarter.

Sales to Nokia were $675,000 or 11.1% of net revenues compared to $239,000 or 4.4% of net revenues in the same period a year ago, and $200,000 or 3.1% of net revenues in the prior quarter. Sales to Cadence Design Systems were $233,000 or 3.8% of net revenues compared to $971,000 or 17.9% of net revenues in the same period a year ago, and $1.4 million or 21.6% of net revenues in the prior quarter. Military defense sales were 28.5% of third-quarter shipments compared to 30% of shipments in the comparable quarter a year ago, and 28.9% of shipments in the prior quarter.

SigmaQuad sales were 41.7% of third-quarter shipments fiscal 2026 compared to 39.1% in 2025 and 50.1% in the prior quarter. I would now like to hand the call over to Douglas Schirle. Go ahead, please.
