Okta (OKTA) Q4 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 4, 2026 at 6:42PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

March 4, 2026, 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer and Co-Founder — Todd McKinnon
  • Chief Financial Officer — Brett Tighe
  • President and Chief Operating Officer — Eric Kelleher
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations — Dave Gennarelli

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • New products bookings mix -- Approximately 30% of bookings in the quarter came from new products, a meaningful increase from prior quarters.
  • Average contract uplift -- When new products were included in deals, the average contract saw an approximate 40% uplift, measured over the entire contract.
  • Okta Identity Governance (OIG) adoption -- OIG now has over 2,000 customers since launch a little more than three years ago.
  • Annual contract value milestone -- Okta surpassed $3 billion in annual contract value.
  • Total contract value closed -- A record total contract value of nearly $1.3 billion was closed during the quarter.
  • Channel engagement -- Channel partners participated in 18 of the top 20 deals, while total contract value via AWS Marketplace grew over 45% for the year to roughly $750 million.
  • Balance sheet position -- Over $2.5 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments at quarter-end.
  • Share repurchase action -- More than 875,000 shares were repurchased and retired for $79 million in January as part of a $1 billion repurchase program.
  • Revenue and margin guidance -- For the upcoming quarter, revenue growth is projected at 9%, current RPO growth at 10%, a non-GAAP operating margin of 23%-24%, and free cash flow margin of 33%-35%.
  • Full year guidance -- For the year ahead, management guided to 9% revenue growth, a non-GAAP operating margin of 25%-26%, and free cash flow margin of 27%-28%.
  • Professional services shift -- The revenue guidance reflects about a one-point reduction from shifting more professional services work to global system integrators, which will decrease professional services revenue but may boost top-line growth through deeper partner engagement.
  • Interest income impact -- Free cash flow margin guidance incorporates a one-point headwind due to an anticipated decrease in interest income associated with the share repurchase program, the intent to settle the remainder of 2026 notes in cash, and the current interest rate environment.
  • Tax rate assumption -- Guidance updates the non-GAAP tax rate to 21% from 26% to reflect recent federal tax law changes.
  • Go-to-market model -- The current go-to-market structure has delivered higher sales productivity and low attrition while enabling the addition of quota-carrying capacity since the second quarter.
  • Suite-based pricing -- This structure is driving larger deal sizes and faster deal closure, though as of now it is still early in adoption and not yet a meaningful contributor to overall run rate.
  • AI agent products -- Okta for AI Agents became available in early access in January, and Auth0 for AI Agents was generally available at the quarter’s start; management highlighted notable early deals and “huge interest” in both platforms.
  • Pricing models for AI agents -- Two models are used for pricing AI agents: either as a multiplier on a person-based license or based on the number of agent connections to systems.
  • Subscription vs. total revenue growth -- Subscription revenue is growing faster than total revenue, with professional services declining from approximately 2% to 1% of total revenue year over year.
  • Net retention discussion -- Management indicated stable gross retention with upsell rates being the driver of net retention range in fiscal 2027, emphasizing future top-line and RPO growth rather than immediate acceleration in net retention.

Summary

Okta (OKTA 7.13%) highlighted the increasing contribution of new product bookings and emphasized its position in the emerging AI agent identity market, underpinned by major contract milestones and record total contract value. Management outlined a strategy to deepen engagement with large enterprises and channel partners, reinforced by a shift of professional services to global system integrators and by continuing disciplined capital allocation, including executing a substantial share buyback. The company affirmed robust liquidity and reiterated a focus on profitable growth, updating its tax rate assumption and maintaining margin guidance while acknowledging planned near-term revenue and free cash flow headwinds from specific operational decisions.

  • Management identified Okta’s unique positioning as both an identity security and infrastructure provider, claiming a comprehensive platform approach in the agentic market.
  • Leadership described substantial current and pipeline demand for both Okta for AI Agents and Auth0 for AI Agents, citing early enterprise adoption and feedback loops driving agile go-to-market and pricing models.
  • Executives explained that suite-based pricing expedites large deal closure and supports broader platform adoption, with early field and customer approvals but minimal run-rate impact to date.
  • The company reported an ongoing increase in sales and marketing capacity, paired with a strategic emphasis on specialization and partner-driven growth, while maintaining disciplined cost allocation.
  • Okta’s product leadership asserted that its AI agent identity offerings act as an extension of prior capabilities and are designed to evolve standards in the agentic enterprise, emphasizing foundational infrastructure value to customers.

Industry glossary

  • Agentic: Refers to identity frameworks, applications, or systems built to secure, govern, and manage AI agents (autonomous software entities) within enterprise environments.
  • CIAM: Customer Identity and Access Management—software that enables organizations to securely manage and authenticate external users (customers, partners) accessing digital applications.
  • OIG: Okta Identity Governance—a cloud-native, integrated governance product for managing, auditing, and controlling user access and entitlements across systems.
  • RPO/CRPO: Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) / Current RPO—forward-looking contracted revenue, with CRPO reflecting obligations expected to be recognized as revenue within the next 12 months.
  • GSIs: Global System Integrators—large consulting and services firms that integrate enterprise IT solutions for customers across regions and industries.
  • Net retention (DBNRR): Dollar-Based Net Retention Rate—a metric indicating how recurring revenue from existing customers grows or contracts from renewals, upgrades, and churn over a set period.
