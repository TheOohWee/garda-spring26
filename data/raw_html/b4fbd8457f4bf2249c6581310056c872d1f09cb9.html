Earnings Call Transcript

lululemon (LULU) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 12, 2025 at 10:58AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, December 11, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Calvin McDonald
  • Chief Financial Officer — Meghan Frank
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Howard Tubin

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $2.6 billion, up 7% on both a reported and constant currency basis.
  • Comparable Sales -- Increased 2% globally; U.S. and Americas comparable sales declined 5%.
  • Regional Revenue -- Americas down 2%, with U.S. down 3% and Canada flat on a constant currency basis; China Mainland up 46%, Rest of World up 19% in constant currency.
  • China Mainland Revenue Guidance -- Now expected at or above the high end of 20%-25% annual growth, excluding the 53rd week.
  • Store Count -- 796 stores at quarter-end after opening 12 net new stores and completing 16 optimizations.
  • Digital Revenue -- $1.1 billion, constituting 42% of total revenue and up 13% year-over-year.
  • Product Category Growth -- Men’s revenue up 8%, women’s up 6%, accessories and other up 12%.
  • Gross Profit -- $1.43 billion, representing 55.6% of net revenue; gross margin declined 290 basis points due to tariffs and higher markdowns.
  • SG&A Expenses -- $988 million, or 38.5% of net revenue; up from 38% last year, but below guidance for deleverage.
  • Operating Income -- $436 million, or 17% of net revenue, compared to 20.5% in the prior year’s comparable quarter.
  • Net Income -- $307 million, or $2.59 per diluted share, down from $2.87 per diluted share a year ago.
  • Inventory -- Increased 11% to $2 billion; unit inventory up 4% with dollar growth outpacing units due to higher tariffs and foreign exchange.
  • Share Repurchases -- Approximately 1 million shares were repurchased at an average price of $181, with $1.6 billion remaining on the share repurchase authorization.
  • CEO Transition -- Calvin McDonald will step down as CEO on January 31, with Meghan Frank and Andre Maestrini serving as interim co-CEOs and Marti Morfitt as Executive Chair.
  • Outlook: Full-Year Revenue -- Now expected at $10.96 billion to $11.05 billion, representing 4% growth (and 5%-6% excluding the 53rd week in 2024).
  • U.S. Guidance -- U.S. revenue expected in the range of negative 1% to 2% versus prior year on a constant currency basis, excluding the 53rd week.
  • Gross Margin Guidance -- Full-year gross margin to decrease approximately 270 basis points, improved from prior guidance for a 300 basis point decline.
  • Operating Margin Guidance -- Full-year operating margin expected to decrease by approximately 390 basis points.
  • EPS Guidance -- Fiscal 2025 diluted EPS now expected between $12.92 and $13.02, down from $14.64 in 2024.
  • Q4 Revenue Guidance -- Expected in the range of $3.5 billion to $3.59 billion; negative 3% to negative 1% versus last year (or 2%-4% growth including the 53rd week).
  • Q4 Margin Outlook -- Gross margin expected to decrease by 580 basis points due to tariffs, de minimis removal, and investments; 410 basis points attributable to tariffs and de minimis.
  • Product Innovation Initiatives -- New style penetration targeted at 35% for spring 2026; product lifecycle management accelerated via chase and fast-track processes aiming for replenishment in 6-8 weeks for strong performers.
  • Action Plan Focus -- Management reiterated three pillars: product creation, product activation (including digital and in-store upgrades), and enterprise efficiency targeting cost mitigation from tariffs and de minimis changes.

RISKS

  • Meghan Frank stated, “it is fair to assume that the negatives will outweigh the positives” for operating margin in 2026, citing a full year of increased tariffs, removal of de minimis provision, and “some pressure next year.”
  • Markdowns rose 90 basis points in Q3, with further increases of 100 basis points expected in Q4, reflecting elevated inventory clearance needs due to underperforming sales.
  • Q4 guidance anticipates gross margin decline of 580 basis points and operating margin deleverage of 680 basis points, primarily from tariffs, de minimis changes, fixed costs, and higher investments.
  • Management identified soft demand post-Thanksgiving and stated, “We have seen some pullback in demand post Thanksgiving in terms of traffic. We've reflected that in our guidance.”

SUMMARY

The call disclosed decelerating U.S. sales and persistent operating margin pressures driven by tariffs, elevated markdowns, and strategic investment. Leadership transition will occur at the end of January 2026, with existing executives assuming interim roles and the CEO search underway. China Mainland and Rest of World regions exhibited strong revenue growth, buoying overall results even as Americas experienced contraction. Inventory growth outpaced sales, but was mitigated by higher-than-expected sell-through and targeted inventory management, with plans for unit inventory growth below sales in 2026.

  • The upcoming product pipeline is set to achieve 35% new style penetration in spring 2026 and is supported by reduced product development timelines and enhanced agility.
  • Updated digital and in-store experiences, new marketing partnerships, and membership initiatives are already producing improved guest engagement and have driven record digital sales on Black Friday.
  • Guidance for the remainder of the year incorporates observed demand softening, higher tariff cost absorption, and an explicit intent to balance markdown risks through inventory and chase strategies.
  • International expansion—including a new flagship in Seoul and additional stores planned for China and EMEA—remains a growth lever amid continued U.S. market softness.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • de minimis provision: A U.S. customs rule allowing duty-free import of shipments valued below a certain threshold, its removal can increase landed cost and impact gross margin.
  • Chase capabilities: Supply chain process enabling rapid replenishment of strong-selling items, typically within 6-8 weeks, to capitalize on demand trends and reduce markdown risk.
  • Product activation: Initiatives to drive customer engagement and sales, including in-store merchandising, digital enhancements, and marketing focused on highlighting new and differentiated products.
