Synaptics (SYNA) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

Takeaways

  • Core IoT Revenue Growth -- 74% year-over-year increase, driven by processor and wireless connectivity products.
  • Total Revenue -- $292.5 million, representing 14% year-over-year growth and above the midpoint of company guidance.
  • Segment Revenue Mix -- 35% Core IoT, 51% Enterprise & Automotive, 14% Mobile Touch for the quarter.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $1.09 per diluted share, up 35% year over year and above the midpoint of guidance.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 53.2%, aligning with the company's previously communicated range.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expense -- $104 million, slightly better than the midpoint of guidance.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin -- 17.6%, increasing by approximately 110 basis points sequentially and 90 basis points year over year.
  • Cash, Equivalents & Short-Term Investments -- approximately $459.9 million balance, up approximately $7.4 million from the prior quarter.
  • Cash Flow from Operations -- $30.2 million for the first fiscal quarter.
  • Share Repurchases -- $7.2 million in Q1, and $15 million through the date of the call.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $12.2 million, partially attributed to lab build-outs for R&D purposes.
  • Days Sales Outstanding -- 37 days at quarter end, down from 41 days in the prior quarter.
  • Inventory Metrics -- $143.1 million in ending inventory, up by $3.6 million, with days of inventory at 94 days (essentially flat).
  • Product Milestone -- Launched next-generation Astra Edge AI processors, with "lead customers" already sampling the SL2600 series and initial production revenue expected in the second half of calendar 2026.
  • Design Win Activity -- "secured design wins" with Astra SL2600 series, ahead of internal expectations, and ongoing expansion in pipeline for Edge AI devices.
  • Collaboration with Google Research -- Integrated open-source Coral NPU in Astra processors, providing a more accessible software development environment for Edge AI.
  • Mobile Touch -- Lower than expected revenue partly due to supply chain constraints; secured "marquee design wins with a top Android phone OEM" for next-generation touch controllers with anticipated revenue impact in the next fiscal year.
  • Enterprise & Automotive Revenue -- Segment flat year over year; enterprise portfolio showed improvement, offset by continued softness in automotive demand.
  • Guidance (Q2) -- Projected revenue of $300 million at midpoint (±$10 million), with non-GAAP gross margin at 53.5% (±1%) and non-GAAP EPS of $1.15 (±$0.15) on 40.4 million diluted shares.
  • Market Opportunity -- Rahul Patel stated, "we feel very comfortable with our guide of 25% to 30% growth for the fiscal year '26." in Core IoT revenue.
  • Wireless Connectivity Development -- Wi-Fi 7 and broad-market wireless products are gaining traction with ongoing customer commitments and additional design wins across consumer, industrial, and enterprise applications.

Summary

Synaptics (SYNA 0.30%) reported broad-based growth driven by Core IoT, achieving a 74% revenue increase in that segment and securing new design wins for its Astra Edge AI processors. Management highlighted a strategic focus on Edge AI, an expanding customer pipeline, and new collaboration with Google Research that integrates open-source architectures for faster AI adoption. Sequential and year-over-year improvements were seen in revenue, margins, and operating fundamentals, while investments were made toward next-generation product development and increased silicon content, especially in foldables and integrated wireless solutions.

  • Synaptics plans to periodically release specific data on design wins and production ramp schedules for the Astra processor line in upcoming quarters.
  • Enterprise segment market share increased over the past year, even as the overall PC market remained relatively flat.
  • Management reiterated anticipated revenue impact from next-gen Mobile Touch products in foldable devices, with content per unit expected to more than double versus current smartphone designs.
  • Industrial applications for new processors are positioned to ramp after consumer device adoption, reflecting longer refresh cycles in industrial markets.
  • The company continues to review potential inorganically driven growth opportunities for IoT, with openness to small tuck-in acquisitions or strategic partnerships.

Industry glossary

  • Edge AI: Artificial intelligence algorithms processed locally on a device ("at the edge") rather than in a centralized data center, enabling real-time inference and data privacy.
  • Design Win: Confirmation that a customer's product under development will use a supplier's component, typically leading to future production revenue.
  • Coral NPU: An open-source neural processing unit developed by Google, specialized for efficient on-device machine learning acceleration.
  • Astra SL2600: Synaptics' next-generation family of Edge AI processors aimed at IoT and edge device markets.
