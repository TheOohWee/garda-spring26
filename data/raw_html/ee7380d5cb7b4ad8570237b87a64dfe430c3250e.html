RISKS

Marie Perry stated, "Assignment revenues totaled $382.4 million in Q2 2025", a decline of 13.9% year over year, reflecting continued softness in portions of the commercial segment that are more sensitive to changes in macroeconomic cycles."

TAKEAWAYS

Total Revenues: Revenue was $1.02 billion in Q2 2025, down 1.4% year over year, but above the high end of the guidance range.

Adjusted EBITDA: Adjusted EBITDA was $108.5 million in Q2 2025, with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 10.6%, at the top end of expectations.

Commercial Consulting Revenues: $325.7 million in Q2 2025, up 15.7% year over year, driven in part by TopBloc's contribution.

Commercial Segment Revenues: $708.1 million in Q2 2025, down 2.4% year over year.

Federal Government Segment Revenues: $312.5 million in Q2 2025, up 1.1% year over year, including approximately $10 million in higher-than-expected license revenue.

Gross Margin: Gross margin was 28.7% in Q2 2025, a decrease of 40 basis points year over year from Q2 2024 to Q2 2025.

Commercial Segment Gross Margin: 33%, an increase of 30 basis points year over year from Q2 2024 to Q2 2025. due to margin expansion in consulting revenues.

Federal Government Segment Gross Margin: 19.2%, down 140 basis points year over year from Q2 2024 to Q2 2025. The decline in gross margin for the federal government segment was attributed to increased low-margin software licenses and the loss of higher-margin Doge work.

SG&A Expenses: $216.8 million in Q2 2025, up from $205.6 million in Q2 2024, including $8.3 million in acquisition, integration, and strategic planning expenses not included in guidance.

Free Cash Flow: Free cash flow was $115.8 million in Q2 2025, representing a conversion rate of about 107% of adjusted EBITDA.

Share Repurchases: $9.5 million was deployed to repurchase approximately 200,000 shares at an average price of $58.69 in Q2 2025, with $470 million remaining under the authorization.

Net Leverage Ratio: Net leverage ratio was 2.46 times at the end of the second quarter.

Commercial Consulting Bookings: Commercial consulting bookings were $417.5 million in Q2 2025, with a trailing twelve-month book-to-bill ratio of 1.2 times.

Federal Contract Awards: $72 million in new federal contract awards in Q2 FY2025, book-to-bill ratio of 1.1 times on a trailing twelve-month basis in Q2 FY2025; backlog was over $2.9 billion at quarter-end, equating to 2.4 times the federal segment's trailing twelve-month revenues.

IT Consulting Revenue Mix: IT consulting represented 63% of total revenues in Q2 2025, up from 57% in the second quarter of the prior year.

Guidance for Q3 2025: Expected revenue of $992 million to $1.012 billion in Q3 2025, adjusted EBITDA of $108.5 million to $113.5 million in Q3 2025, adjusted EBITDA margin of 10.9% to 11.2% in Q3 2025, and net income of $35.8 million to $39.4 million in Q3 2025.

Impact of Doge: Perry confirmed, The Doge impact was less than 2% of total revenues in Q2 FY2025. unchanged for Q2 and expected to continue for Q3.

Commercial Segment Industry Insights: Consumer and industrial accounts grew by mid-double digits year over year. Materials, utilities, industrials, and consumer discretionary experienced mid-teens or higher growth. Financial services, business services, and TMT declined year over year.

Technology Partnerships: The partnership with Workday and the performance of TopBloc exceeded expectations, boosting consulting bookings.

AI Initiatives: The ASGN AI Innovation Center was launched to unify AI expertise; "solution accelerators" and the AI University were created to drive repeatable, lower-cost client solutions and internal upskilling.

Internal Use of AI: Hanson detailed the application of AI across enterprise systems, recruiting, sales, proposal generation, and cybersecurity functions to drive operational efficiency.

SUMMARY

ASGN Incorporated (ASGN 5.26%) reported revenue and adjusted EBITDA above guidance in Q2 2025, with consulting revenues showing strong double-digit growth (up 15.7% year over year) despite overall top-line contraction and margin pressure. Management pointed to persistent macroeconomic uncertainty affecting assignment revenue, but emphasized increased demand for cloud, data, AI, and digital modernization solutions in both commercial and federal segments. The company’s technology partnerships and the launch of its AI Innovation Center represent a strategic pivot toward expanding proprietary intellectual property and further embedding AI into both client offerings and internal operations.

Hanson explained that TopBloc’s annualized revenue and bookings are "tracking just ahead" of prior targets for the full year, supporting consulting momentum.

Federal segment contract awards were challenged by award delays, but growth in national security accounts and a substantial backlog suggest potential for future expansion aligned with increased U.S. defense spending.

Iyer identified that AI adoption is driving increased advisory work around technology architecture, creating new consulting opportunities as clients evaluate build-versus-integrate strategies.

Perry clarified that investment in AI is not detracting from margins and may be additive as initiatives scale in maturity.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Doge: A high-margin federal contract referenced during the call as a source of prior-year revenue now diminishing, impacting federal segment margin.

Book-to-bill ratio: The ratio of contract bookings to revenue recognized over a set period, indicating growth or contraction in future revenue pipeline.

TopBloc: An ASGN-owned Workday consultancy contributing to the company’s commercial consulting revenues and platform implementation strength.

AI Innovation Center: ASGN’s internal initiative to unify commercial and federal AI resources, develop repeatable AI solutions, and foster internal training and external client delivery capabilities.

Solution accelerator: Proprietary, reusable technological components developed by ASGN to address client business challenges with AI, speeding deployment and reducing costs.
