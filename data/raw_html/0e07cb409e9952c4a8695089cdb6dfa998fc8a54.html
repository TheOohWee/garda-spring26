Summary

monday.com Ltd. (MNDY 5.96%) reported 27% revenue growth driven by enterprise expansion and rapid adoption of its multiproduct platform, with notable momentum in the CRM business achieving $100 million in annual recurring revenue. Management emphasized accelerating customer engagement with new AI-powered features, citing 46 million AI actions and early evidence of paid AI credit overages driving incremental revenue. Investment intensity remained high with head count up 172 sequentially and R&D expense at 20% of revenue, while gross margin was maintained at 90% and sales and marketing efficiency improved. Cautious forward guidance reflects both macro uncertainty and disruption from Google’s algorithm changes to SMB customer acquisition, resulting in a more conservative annual outlook. The company introduced strategic executive hires and reaffirmed its focus on disciplined resource allocation between organic product development and potential M&A opportunities.

  • Management projects stable NDR at 111% for the remainder of the year and highlighted strengthening gross retention broadly across customer segments.
  • Forward guidance for Q3 revenue and margin reflects 24%-25% year-over-year growth and a non-GAAP operating margin of 11%-12%, underlying management's expectation of sequential margin compression.
  • The CRM segment reported over 20% year-over-year ACV uplift in Q2, predominantly through seat expansion and higher average deal size, signaling successful upmarket CRM execution.
  • AI feature launches (monday magic, monday vibe, monday sidekick) were described by management as "a major step forward in our evolution from work management to work execution," directly supporting increased product usage and diversification.
  • Sales hiring and organizational expansion were "pretty much in line" with internal plans, with management signaling slower head count growth into 2026 and prioritizing operational leverage.
  • Most product, segment, and expense trends were characterized as consistent across geographies, with no single vertical notably outperforming in H2 guidance.
  • Management said its full year outlook already incorporates potential uncertainties from ongoing Google search engine changes, with other media channels and internal optimization efforts mitigating immediate risk exposure.
  • Free cash flow generation remains a strategic priority, as does exploring selective M&A; no material foreign exchange impact expected for the full year.

Industry glossary

  • Net Dollar Retention (NDR): Metric reflecting percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers, including expansion and contraction, over a defined period.
  • Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR): The value of contractually committed subscription revenue normalized to a one-year period.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow: Non-GAAP measure calculated as net cash from operations less capital expenditures and capitalized software costs, plus costs for headquarters build-out/expansion.
  • ACV (Average Contract Value): Average annualized revenue per customer contract, used to track deal size and upmarket motion.
