BNY Mellon (BK) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 15, 2026 at 6:14PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Jan. 13, 2026 at 11:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Robin Vince
  • Chief Financial Officer — Dermot McDonogh

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Net income -- Record $5.3 billion for the year, driven by broad-based business momentum.
  • Total revenue -- Record $20.1 billion, up 8% year over year, reflecting both fee and net interest income growth.
  • Earnings per share (EPS) -- $7.40, up 28%; excluding notable items, $7.50, up 24% year over year.
  • Return on tangible common equity (ROTCE) -- 26% for the year, rising to 27% in Q4.
  • Pretax margin -- 35% on a reported basis and 36% excluding notable items for the full year.
  • Positive operating leverage -- 507 basis points reported, with 411 basis points excluding notable items, and eight consecutive quarters achieved.
  • Capital return -- $5 billion returned via dividends and buybacks; Q4 payout ratio was 100%.
  • Fee revenue -- Increased 6% year over year; investment services fees up 8%, investment management and performance fees down 2%.
  • Net interest income (NII) -- Up 15% for the year and 13% for Q4, mainly from reinvestment at higher yields and balance sheet growth.
  • Expenses -- $13.1 billion, up 3% year over year due to investments, merit increases, and adverse FX impact, partially offset by efficiency gains.
  • Assets under custody/administration (AUC/A) -- $59.3 trillion, up 14% year over year due to inflows, higher market values, and FX effects.
  • Assets under management (AUM) -- $2.2 trillion, up 7% year over year; net outflows of $3 billion for Q4.
  • Security services segment -- Revenue of $2.5 billion, up 7% year over year; pretax margin reached 34% for Q4 and 33% for the full year.
  • Markets and wealth services segment -- Revenue of $1.8 billion, up 8% year over year; pretax margin was 49%.
  • ETF AUC/A -- $3.8 trillion, up 34% year over year; number of funds serviced increased 22%.
  • Alternatives AUC/A -- Up 10% year over year, with double-digit private markets growth.
  • Corporate trust market share -- Increased by 4 percentage points in CLOs, and 3 percentage points in municipal securities year over year.
  • Clearance and collateral management -- Investment services fees up 15% year over year; collateral balances grew to $7.5 trillion, also up 15%.
  • Medium-term financial targets raised -- Pretax margin target increased by 500 basis points to 38%; ROTCE target increased by 500 basis points to 28%.
  • 2026 guidance -- Total revenue growth projected at approximately 5%; expenses expected to rise 3%-4%, both excluding notable items.
  • AI platform adoption -- Over 130 digital employees deployed, partnering with Google Cloud and OpenAI to advance capacity and workflow productivity.
  • Commercial model progress -- Over the past two years, the number of clients purchasing three or more services increased over 60%.
  • Tokenized deposits initiative -- Enabled on-chain mirrored representations of client deposit balances, signaling a strategic expansion into digital asset infrastructure.
  • Capital ratios -- Tier 1 leverage at 6% (down 9 basis points sequentially), and Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) at 11.9% (up 17 basis points sequentially).
  • Liquidity ratios -- Liquidity coverage ratio at 112%, and net stable funding ratio at 130% remained unchanged quarter over quarter.

Summary

Bank of New York Mellon (BK 0.79%) announced multiple record-setting financial results and strengthened its medium-term performance targets, highlighting a shift toward higher-margin growth and further digital transformation. Management reported broad-based improvements across fee and net interest income and stressed the company’s ability to drive operational leverage and maintain strong capital deployment discipline. Strategic initiatives, including deepened multi-product client penetration and platform investments, position BK to deliver higher value from existing relationships and pursue new digital and front-to-back client capabilities.

  • The company confirmed that 10% of 2025 sales came from entirely new clients, reflecting success from recent commercial efforts and product innovation.
  • Management noted that approximately two-thirds of pretax income now comes from platform-style businesses, up from 55% three years prior.
  • Markets and Wealth Services achieved segment-leading margins near 50%, while Security Services exceeded its long-standing pretax margin target.
  • Expense growth is being closely managed through a combination of efficiency gains and agile reinvestment, with a commitment to harvest roughly $500 million annually for business growth.
  • AI and digital transformation are framed as capacity multipliers, not mere cost-reduction tools, with Eliza and digital "employees" supporting new business models and product delivery.

Industry glossary

  • Tokenized deposits: Digitally represented deposit balances recorded on a blockchain to improve settlement and programmable use.
  • Digital employees: AI-powered software agents automating discrete operational workflows alongside human staff.
  • On-chain mirrored representation: Blockchain-based, real-time reflection of traditional financial account balances for integrated digital asset functionality.
  • Eliza: BNY Mellon's enterprise AI platform, providing multi-model intelligence and workflow automation capabilities.
  • Wove: BNY Pershing's digital platform delivering custody, clearing, and client service tools for investment advisors and wealth managers.
