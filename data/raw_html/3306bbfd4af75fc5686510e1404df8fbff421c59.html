Earnings Call Transcript

Hubbell (HUBB) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 28, 2025 at 12:05PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Oct. 28, 2025 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Gerben W. Bakker

Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — William R. Sperry

Vice President, Investor Relations — Daniel Joseph Innamorato

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

CFO William R. Sperry said, "Grid automation, continuing the trend from the last several quarters. Down double digits driven by project roll-offs that aren't being backfilled with new projects," resulting in a 4% drag on overall growth in fiscal Q3 2025 (period ended Sept. 30, 2025).

Management indicated that utility segment organic revenue recovery has been shaped as expected, but growth timing has shifted, with revenue guidance reduction realized entirely within the utility segment.

TAKEAWAYS

Consolidated Sales -- up 4%, with strength in Electrical Solutions and grid infrastructure, partially offset by grid automation declines.

Adjusted Diluted EPS -- benefiting from $225 million in share repurchases during the first half of 2025 and a lower tax rate from an international restructuring.

Operating Profit -- price and productivity actions offset increased costs and higher restructuring spend.

Free Cash Flow -- Rose 34% to $254 million, on track for 90% net income conversion for the full year 2025.

Utility Solutions Segment -- Sales increased 1% to $944 million; grid infrastructure (three-quarters of the segment) grew high single digits, while grid automation sales declined 18% due to major project roll-offs.

Electrical Solutions Segment -- Delivered 10% total sales growth (8% organic) and notable strength in data centers and light industrial verticals.

Pricing -- CFO William R. Sperry stated, "We were talking about pricing for the year—being in the three-point range, and the quarter was in line with that. And, let's say, reasonably balanced between the segments." (Pricing refers to fiscal 2025.)

2025 Adjusted EPS Outlook -- Raised full-year 2025 adjusted EPS guidance to $18.10 to $18.30 (previously $17.65 to $18.15), representing a midpoint increase of 30¢, mainly reflecting a lower expected tax rate.

Q4 Organic Growth Expectation -- Projected 8%-10% organic growth for fiscal Q4 2025, supported by both segments, with visibility from order acceleration in September and October and anticipated margin expansion in both businesses.

DMC Power Acquisition -- Closed in October for $825 million; expected to be neutral to fiscal Q4 2025 earnings and contribute approximately $0.20 in adjusted earnings per share accretion in 2026, with EBITDA margins above 40% (non-GAAP).

CFO Transition -- Announcement that Joe Capizzoli will succeed retiring CFO William R. Sperry effective in 2026, following significant tenure and leadership development within Hubbell (HUBB +0.35%).

SUMMARY

Management attributed double-digit adjusted earnings growth in fiscal Q3 2025 (period ended Sept. 30, 2025) to high single-digit organic gains in both Electrical Solutions and Grid Infrastructure. Broader positive price-cost productivity offset cost inflation, and share repurchases and tax restructuring further improved bottom-line results. The grid automation headwind, which reduced overall sales growth by roughly 4% in fiscal Q3 2025, is expected to subside in fiscal Q4 as project roll-offs annualize. Organic growth guidance for fiscal Q4 is supported by accelerating order activity in September and October, especially within transmission and distribution. The DMC Power acquisition, integrated at high EBITDA margins, is projected to enhance adjusted earnings per share accretion in 2026. Leadership transition planning was clearly articulated, emphasizing succession continuity and organizational depth.

Utility Solutions segment results reflected broad-based growth in transmission, substation, and distribution end markets, while recovery in telecom and enclosures was noted after previous inventory overhangs.

CFO William R. Sperry described fourth-quarter growth expectations as requiring "a little bit stronger than normal seasonality," citing clear visibility into order books and project pipelines.

Management characterized the outlook for 2026 as aligning with long-term organic growth targets, driven by continued investment in grid modernization, electrification, and resilient data center demand.

CFO William R. Sperry said, we anticipate being neutral to earnings in fiscal Q4 2025 as we set it up to contribute 20¢ next year, confirming both timing and anticipated accretion details.

The operating teams credited commercial realignment, vertical market strategy, and ongoing productivity investments for sustained multi-year margin expansion in Electrical Solutions.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Grid Automation: Systems and devices that enable remote monitoring, protection, and control of the electric grid, supporting modernization and efficiency.

OP Margin: Operating profit divided by revenue, used to assess segment profitability.

Data Center Vertical: Business segment providing specialized electrical and connectivity solutions to facilities supporting cloud and enterprise computing infrastructure.

Adjusted Diluted EPS: Earnings per share metric excluding specified items to reflect underlying performance.

Basis Point (bps): One-hundredth of a percentage point, used to express margin or yield changes.

AMI: Advanced Metering Infrastructure, referring to systems for automated measurement and communication of energy usage.
