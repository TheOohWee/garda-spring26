Ford (F) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 10, 2026 at 6:10PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 10, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Jim Farley
  • Chief Financial Officer — Sherry House
  • President of Ford Blue and Model e — Andrew Frick
  • President of Ford Pro — Alicia Bohler Davis
  • Chief Operating Officer — Kumar Galhotra
  • Chief Executive Officer of Ford Credit — Kathy O'Callaghan
  • Director of Investor Relations — Lynn Antipas Tyson

Takeaways

  • Full-year revenue -- $187 billion, marking the fifth consecutive annual increase, with expansion into hybrid trucks, and paid software subscriptions.
  • Adjusted EBIT -- $6.8 billion, which includes a $2 billion Novelis fire impact and $2 billion in net tariff headwinds; excluding the one-time tariff, adjusted EBIT would have reached $7.7 billion.
  • Total shareholder return -- 42%, with underlying improvements in cost and quality highlighted by management.
  • Free cash flow -- $3.5 billion, ending the period with cash of nearly $29 billion, and total liquidity approaching $50 billion.
  • U.S. market share -- Increased to 13.2%, the best level achieved in six years.
  • Ford Pro performance -- $66 billion in revenue and $6.8 billion in EBIT, achieving a double-digit margin; U.S. Super Duty sales up 10%, and Transit sales up 6%.
  • Ford Model e -- Revenue and volume growth of 7369% each, primarily in Europe; EBIT loss improved to $4.8 billion due to lower Gen one losses and higher European volumes.
  • Software and physical services -- Ford Pro's revenue share from these categories grew 10%, and now contributes 19% to Ford Pro EBIT; overall paid software subscriptions rose 30%.
  • Ford Blue EBIT -- $3 billion delivered, with lower warranty and cost improvements offset by planned and unplanned production losses, and adverse exchange.
  • Ford Credit EBT -- $2.6 billion, up 55%, with distributions of $1.7 billion, and average U.S. FICO scores above 750; recent industrial bank approval expected to further diversify funding.
  • 2026 outlook — adjusted EBIT -- Projected between $8 billion and $10 billion.
  • 2026 outlook — adjusted free cash flow -- Expected at $5 billion to $6 billion.
  • 2026 capital expenditures -- Forecasted at $9.5 billion to $10.5 billion, with roughly $1.5 billion allocated to Ford Energy investments.
  • Novelis impact guidance -- $1 billion year-over-year EBIT improvement expected, with $1.5 billion to $2 billion in temporary costs projected for 2026; costs not expected to repeat in 2027.
  • Segment 2026 EBIT outlook -- Ford Pro: $6.5 billion to $7.5 billion; Ford Blue: $4 billion to $4.5 billion; Ford Model e: ($4 billion) to ($4.5 billion); Ford Credit: $2.5 billion EBT.
  • Industry assumptions -- 2026 U.S. SAAR expected at 16 million to 16.5 million units; industry pricing assumed flat.
  • Planned industrial cost improvements -- Targeting an additional $1 billion of cost reductions after achieving $1.5 billion in 2025.
  • Dividend declaration -- Regular first quarter dividend of $0.15 per share announced.

Risks

  • Sherry House said, "1.5 billion to $2 billion of temporary costs, including tariffs, to ensure continuity in aluminum supply," related to Novelis disruptions, which are not expected to recur in 2027.
  • Management forecasts a $4 billion to $4.5 billion loss for Ford Model e in 2026, with losses persisting until breakeven targeted in 2029.
  • Alicia Bohler Davis cited "a very competitive environment" in European light commercial vehicles, and anticipated continued pressure on pricing, though not manifested yet in early quarters.
  • $7 billion in charges expected in 2026 to 2027 from rebalancing the EV portfolio and asset disposition, with up to $5.5 billion in associated cash expenditures, the majority incurred in 2026.

Summary

Ford (F 0.90%) management aims for an 8% adjusted EBIT margin by 2029, emphasizing disciplined capital allocation, a shift toward affordable EVs, and strengthening Ford Pro's commercial leadership. The call highlighted an updated product strategy—introducing a universal EV platform for high-volume, lower-priced vehicles, and expanding Ford Energy, targeting both grid-scale and data center storage applications. Capital expenditures will rise $1 billion, driven primarily by Ford Energy initiatives, and increased funding for software and multi-energy platforms. Segment outlooks prioritize profitability recovery in Ford Blue and Pro, continued investment in Model e ahead of Gen two product launches, and further expansion of paid software services across all business units. Operational plans include dealer stock optimization, an ongoing focus on warranty and material cost reductions, and new product introductions supporting truck and off-road franchise growth.

  • Sherry House confirmed receipt of a $1 billion U.S. government tariff receivable, contributing to higher free cash flow conversion for 2026.
  • Andrew Frick said, "we grew two points of revenue share and one and a half points of volume share in 2025," reinforcing Ford's expanded truck leadership position.
  • Kumar Galhotra stated, "Expect the mill to start back up somewhere in the middle of the year," providing volume normalization guidance between May and September.
  • Jim Farley described the LFP technology licensing and manufacturing partnership with CATL as a "significant advantage" for Ford Energy and battery storage expansion.
  • Management announced a seven-year streak as the top U.S. auto producer, citing continued domestic manufacturing emphasis, and policy alignment following emission standard resets.

Industry glossary

  • LFP: Lithium iron phosphate, a battery chemistry used for lower-cost, durable energy storage in electric vehicles and stationary applications.
  • SAAR: Seasonally Adjusted Annual Rate of industry-wide vehicle sales, commonly cited to forecast market size.
  • Novelis: Aluminum supplier whose production disruptions materially affected Ford’s 2025 and 2026 financial performance.
  • UAV/UEV: Universal Electric Vehicle platform, Ford's modular EV architecture for high-volume, affordable models.
