Earnings Call Transcript

Arhaus (ARHS) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 15, 2025 at 11:01AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Aug. 7, 2025, 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Founder, Chairman, and Chief Executive Officer — John P. Reed
  • Chief Financial Officer — Michael Alan Lee
  • Chief Marketing and eCommerce Officer — Jennifer E. Porter
  • Senior Director, Investor Relations — Tara Louise Atwood

Takeaways

  • Net revenue -- $358 million, a record quarter, up 15.7%.
  • Comparable growth -- Increased 10.5%, driven by faster conversion of first-quarter demand.
  • Demand comparable growth -- Decreased 3.6% for the quarter; July demand comparable growth rebounded to 15.7%.
  • Year-to-date demand comparable growth -- Up 2.2%, including July.
  • Gross margin -- $148 million, up 19.1% with gross margin rate expanding 130 basis points to 41.4%.
  • Selling, general, and administrative expenses (SG&A) -- $101 million, up 6.8%; SG&A as a percentage of net revenue declined 240 basis points to 28.3%.
  • Net income -- $35 million, up 57.7%, reflecting more than three times earnings leverage on 16% net revenue growth.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $60 million, up 51.2%, with adjusted EBITDA margin at 16.8%, a 390 basis-point increase.
  • Cash position -- Ended with $235 million in cash or cash equivalents; company remains debt-free.
  • Merchandising inventory -- $311 million at quarter-end, a 4.7% increase since Dec. 31, 2024.
  • Sourcing exposure to China -- Now projected at 5% by year-end, higher than previous target of 1% due to tariff policy easing.
  • Showroom projects -- Eight completed year-to-date: one new, six relocations, one renovation.
  • Showroom project guidance (full-year) -- On track to finish 12 to 15 projects, including four to six new openings and eight to nine relocations, renovations, or expansions.
  • New showroom economics -- Traditional Showrooms target at least $10 million net revenue annually, average contribution margin of 32%, payback in under two years.
  • Design studio economics -- Target higher average contribution margin of approximately 35% with payback in under two years.
  • Orders placed via designer -- Average order value nearly four times higher than those placed without a designer.
  • 2025 capital investments -- Approximately $10 million planned, mainly weighted to back half of the year, focused on technology and operational systems.
  • Tariff impact -- Estimated at $12 million net of mitigation in 2025; majority of impact in the second half.
  • 2025 full-year outlook -- Net revenue between $1.29 billion and $1.38 billion (1.5%-8.6% growth), comparable growth range negative 5% to 1.5%, net income $48 million to $68 million, adjusted EBITDA $123 million to $145 million.
  • Fiscal Q3 2025 outlook (period ended June 30, 2025) -- Net revenue expected between $320 million and $350 million (0.3%-9.7% growth), comparable growth range down 4% to up 5%, net income $7 million to $17 million, adjusted EBITDA $23 million to $33 million.
  • Arhaus Bath Collection launch -- New category announced, available online and in selected Showrooms this fall.
  • Buy More Save More program -- Strategy to reward higher purchase amounts, with no changes planned.
  • Interior designer program growth -- Contributed to increased orders over $5,000 and $10,000, supporting higher order value trends.
  • In-source Dallas distribution center -- Transitioned earlier than planned, contributing to operational leverage and improved conversion of backlog.
  • Material weakness remediation -- New ERP implementation identified as key to addressing financial controls.

Risks

  • Chief Financial Officer Lee stated, "we do expect continued choppiness in the second half of the year," with notable macroeconomic and geopolitical uncertainty explicitly identified as ongoing challenges.
  • Incremental 2025 tariffs estimated to have a $12 million net impact, largely affecting second-half results.
  • Showroom and technology investments totaling $10 million, concentrated later in the year, are expected to increase SG&A and may weigh on profitability in the near term.
  • Demand comparable growth was negative 3.6% in the quarter, and management expects ongoing volatility in monthly demand trends due to external factors.

Summary

Arhaus (ARHS +2.72%) reported record net revenue and margin expansion, supported by operational improvements, a successful in-sourced distribution transition in Dallas, and resilience from high-end clients. Leadership reaffirmed full-year guidance, despite significant macro volatility, a $12 million tariff impact, and ongoing month-to-month demand unpredictability. The debut of the Arhaus Bath Collection signals entry into a new product category with a multi-year development cycle. Management committed to strategic investments in technology and infrastructure, expected to deliver long-term operational leverage and improved financial controls.

  • The company intends to review and modify key performance indicator disclosures to clarify the link between written and delivered sales for analysts and investors.
  • Arhaus maintains that its differentiated omnichannel strategy and high-touch designer program are driving increased order values and competitive market share gains.
  • Chief Financial Officer Lee directly addressed investor ERP concerns, emphasizing risk mitigation and P&L leverage as primary objectives in scaling operational systems.
  • Trade and B2B initiatives remain a future growth lever, with leadership signaling forthcoming detailed plans under newly onboarded talent.

Industry glossary

  • Comparable growth: Company-defined metric reflecting sales performance at stores and channels open for comparable periods.
  • Demand comparable growth: Arhaus-specific key performance indicator measuring change in written orders (not delivered sales), highlighting underlying demand volatility.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for non-recurring and non-cash items, as reported by management.
  • In-source distribution center: The process of shifting operations from a third-party logistics provider to internal company control, as demonstrated by the Dallas facility transition.
  • Showroom project: Includes new retail locations, relocations, renovations, and expansions within the company’s physical retail network.
  • Payback period: The length of time required for a new store or investment to generate sufficient net revenue or margin to cover initial setup costs.
  • Material weakness: A deficiency or combination of deficiencies in internal financial controls, increasing the risk that financial statements could be misstated.
