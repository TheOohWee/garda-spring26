Ecolab (ECL) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 10, 2026 at 2:31PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Feb. 10, 2026 at 1 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — Christophe Beck
  • Chief Financial Officer — Scott Kirkland
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Andy Hedberg

Takeaways

  • Adjusted earnings per share growth -- 15% increase, supported by accelerating underlying sales and operating income (OI) margin expansion.
  • Organic sales growth -- 3%, enabled by 3% value pricing and positive volume growth across business lines.
  • Volume growth by segment -- Food and beverage up 5%, pest elimination up 7%, life sciences up 7%, specialty up 7%; underlying volume growth for Ecolab as a whole at 2% after adjusting for basic industries, paper, and lower distributor inventories.
  • Organic operating income growth -- 12%, with organic OI margin reaching 18.5%, a 140-basis-point increase; full-year OI margin at 18%, up 150 basis points.
  • 2026 guidance: Organic sales growth -- Expected at 3%-4%, with value pricing contributing 2%-3% and volume growth projected to accelerate as the year progresses.
  • 2026 guidance: OI margin and growth -- OI margin to expand 100-150 basis points to above 19%, translating to 14%-16% operating income growth.
  • 2026 guidance: EPS growth -- Anticipated 12%-15%, inclusive of added non-cash amortization from the Ovivo acquisition.
  • Reported sales outlook (2026) -- Including Ovivo Electronics, total reported sales expected to grow at an upper single-digit percentage rate.
  • One Ecolab cost savings target (2027) -- Increased again to $325 million, with over $100 million realized by year-end 2025; future savings split approximately equally over 2026 and 2027.
  • Growth engines portfolio -- Now comprise 20% of portfolio including Ovivo Electronics, with collective double-digit growth expected in 2026.
  • Ecolab digital revenue -- Annual sales nearly $400 million, up more than 20% in 2025, with expectations to grow roughly 25% in 2026.
  • CapEx outlook -- Forecasted at approximately 7% of sales for 2026, continuing at elevated levels to support growth engine investments.
  • Pest elimination smart devices -- Expected to surpass 1 million in-field devices in 2026, facilitating customer outcomes and margin leverage.
  • Life sciences segment -- Achieved a 30% full-year OI increase in 2025; long-term segment operating margin target reaffirmed at 30%.
  • Customer retention rate -- "Low to mid-nineties" percent in Institutional & Specialty, with stable attrition including unavoidable closures.

Risks

  • Christophe Beck indicated, "paper and packaging industry, has had a dual challenge. On one hand, okay, a demand that was pretty low. And at the same time, related to it, consolidation of the industry. So consolidation means." Mill closures negatively impacted sales, though management noted recent stabilization.
  • Persistent underperformance in basic industries and paper segments continues to act as a drag on underlying water division growth, though a progressive recovery is expected.
  • Segment performance mixed: "the OI growth in low single digits in Q4," reflecting investment pacing and performance-based compensation, while pest elimination exhibited strong OI due in part to favorable comps.

Summary

Ecolab (ECL +0.41%) outlined record sales, margins, and earnings for 2025, with double-digit increases in adjusted EPS and organic operating income. Management raised its One Ecolab cost-savings target for 2027 to $325 million and described continued high growth in digital solutions, pest elimination, and global high-tech. Integration of Ovivo Electronics is expected to drive further margin expansion and double-digit growth in high-tech and water solutions for semiconductor fabs and data centers. Management provided specific outlooks for organic sales, reported sales, margin, operating income, and EPS in 2026, and addressed segment-specific questions on headwinds, capex, pricing, and digital revenue evolution.

  • Annualized sales productivity over the past five years improved by nearly 30% through sales per head gains and operational leverage.
  • New business wins with core and major regional customers contributed to outperformance among the top 35 global clients, which grew by approximately two percentage points more than the company overall.
  • Digital initiatives—such as the IQ suite and AI-enabled cleaning—are in the early stages of market penetration, currently low-single-digit, but target full connectivity and billable applications across all customer locations.
  • Organic operating income margin in water reached the second-highest level in five years, with cyclical businesses (basic industries and paper) still causing volatility.
  • Company assumes a largely neutral foreign exchange (FX) impact for 2026; guidance accommodates potential tax rate increases and lower other income due to pension assumptions.
  • Operating margin target of 20% by 2027 remains in effect, with additional M&A evaluated strictly for accretive return and margin benefit; management reinforced commitment against dilutive deals.

Industry glossary

  • TVD (Total Value Delivered): Ecolab's framework for quantifying business outcomes, operational performance, and environmental impact delivered to customers.
  • One Ecolab: Company-wide growth and organizational initiative to align resources and unlock cross-segment synergy, targeting significant SG&A savings and sales growth from top global, and regional, customers.
  • IQ suite: Ecolab's set of proprietary, digitally enabled solutions (Dish IQ, Aqua IQ, Kitchen IQ, Beverage IQ) designed to automate and optimize institutional kitchen operations and address labor and cost challenges.
  • Ovivo Electronics: Acquired business specializing in ultra-pure water systems for semiconductor manufacturing, contributing technology to Ecolab's global high-tech and microelectronics market offerings.
