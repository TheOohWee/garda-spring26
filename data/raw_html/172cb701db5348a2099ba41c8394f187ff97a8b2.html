UDR (UDR) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 10, 2026 at 1:34PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, February 10, 2026 at 12 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Tom Toomey
  • Chief Financial Officer — Dave Bragg
  • Chief Operating Officer — Mike Lacey
  • Senior Officer — Chris Benenz

TAKEAWAYS

  • FFO as Adjusted Per Share -- $0.64 for Q4 and $2.54 for the full year, at the midpoint of prior guidance ranges.
  • Same Store Revenue Growth -- Met guidance for 2025; same store expense and NOI growth exceeded expectations.
  • 2025 Full Year Same Store NOI Growth -- Second highest among peers, with revenue growth at or above peer median in 13 of 14 shared markets.
  • 2026 FFO as Adjusted Guidance -- $2.47-$2.57 per share; midpoint of $2.52 is less than 1% below 2025 figure, reflecting absence of one-time items totaling $0.02 per share.
  • 2026 Same Store Revenue Guidance -- Range of 0.25%-2.25%, with a midpoint of 1.25%.
  • 2026 Blended Lease Rate Growth Expectation -- 1.5%-2%, about 100 bps higher than achieved in 2025.
  • 2026 Same Store Expense Growth Guidance -- Midpoint expectation is 3.75%, mainly driven by return to long-term average real estate tax growth and repair/maintenance dynamics.
  • Current Occupancy -- Mid to high 96% range across the portfolio, with rental renewal offers between 5.5%-6% and actual achieved renewal rates in the 5% range through April.
  • Other Income Initiatives -- Expected to contribute approximately 45 basis points to 2026 same store revenue growth, mainly from WiFi rollout, parking, package lockers, storage, and improved pet rent collection.
  • Acquisitions and Dispositions -- Acquired The Enclave at Potomac Club in Q4 for $147 million; contributed four communities valued at $230 million to a JV, and plan to be a net seller in 2026 with $700 million of properties actively marketed.
  • Share Repurchases -- Repurchased nearly $120 million of stock in 2025, including $93 million in Q4 at an average price of $35.56, reflecting a "sizable discount to NAV."
  • Balance Sheet and Liquidity -- 12% of total consolidated debt matures through 2027; year-end liquidity is nearly $1 billion with minimal committed capital.
  • Expense Control -- 2025 same store expense growth was 2.6%, below initial guidance midpoint, supported by a 1,200 bps improvement in resident turnover compared to historical levels.
  • Lease Rate and Occupancy Strategies -- In Q4 2025, lease expirations were strategically shifted (25%) to 2026; occupancy increased to nearly 97% despite challenging demand conditions.
  • Innovation and AI Application -- Ongoing pilots leveraging AI for customer experience, CapEx allocation, screening, reimbursement analysis, and early intervention on at-risk leases, with expectations for incremental NOI above market level growth.
  • Board Changes -- Ellen Gauthier and Rick Clark appointed in place of two departing long-tenured directors, aligning board skills with strategic priorities.

RISKS

  • Dave Bragg said, "consumer confidence, which recently hit its lowest level in a decade," and anticipates "a more muted job growth environment," which may constrain rent growth and operating performance.
  • Management cites "continued uncertainty over tariffs, immigration, and more" as regulatory risks at both market and federal levels, potentially impacting portfolio performance.
  • Disposition plans are subject to tax gain capacity limitations, with guidance assuming use of 1031 exchanges to avoid tax inefficiency.

SUMMARY

UDR (UDR +0.73%) reported Q4 and full-year 2025 FFO as Adjusted in line with prior guidance, while same store expense and NOI growth outperformed initial targets. Strategic capital allocation included opportunistic share repurchases below NAV and a notable $147 million acquisition, funded in part by a $230 million JV transaction yielding immediate repayment of secured debt. The 2026 outlook targets stable or modestly lower FFO per share and NOI growth, with management highlighting operational momentum, effective supply management, and innovation-led revenue expansion as key building blocks. Market sentiment is shaped by near-term headwinds from slower employment growth and regulatory uncertainty, while management expects AI and ongoing expense control to deliver incremental gains beyond the baseline forecast.

  • Management confirmed that a "35% year-over-year reduction in supply completions" should support higher blended lease growth and occupancy, with sequential monthly momentum outpacing initial expectations in early 2026.
  • The company has made measurable progress in resident retention, with turnover improved by 1,200 basis points in 2025 and January 2026 showing an additional 200 bps decline year over year.
  • Sunbelt regions are showing recent acceleration in blended lease rate growth, contributing to improved performance relative to coastal markets.
  • AI initiatives are actively integrated into daily operations, optimizing areas such as resident screening, maintenance, reimbursements, and early detection of lease payment risks.
  • The balance sheet carries low near-term refinancing risk, with only 12% of consolidated debt maturing before 2027 and nearly $1 billion in liquidity at year-end.
  • The debt and preferred equity investment portfolio is expected to decline 10%-25% during 2026, driven by planned maturities and repayments rather than outsized prepayment risk.
  • Advocacy and legal costs related to regulatory ballot measures, notably in Massachusetts and Salinas, are expected but not estimated to be material relative to prior California ballot expenses.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FFO as Adjusted (FFOA): Funds from Operations adjusted for non-recurring or non-cash items, offering a normalized earnings metric for REIT performance comparison.
  • NOI: Net Operating Income, representing total property revenue less property-level operating expenses, excluding depreciation and interest.
  • Blended Lease Rate Growth: Weighted average growth rate combining new lease and renewal lease rates, a key driver of multifamily revenue trends.
  • DPE (Debt and Preferred Equity): Investment strategy involving loans or preferred equity interests in real estate projects, generating income or potential acquisition opportunities.
  • 1031 Exchange: A tax-deferral mechanism allowing REITs to sell a property and reinvest proceeds in like-kind assets while deferring capital gains taxes.
