Service Properties (SVC) Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 26, 2026 at 12:24PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 26, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Christopher J. Bilotto
  • Vice President — Jesse Abair
  • Treasurer and Chief Financial Officer — Brian E. Donley
  • Director of Investor Relations — Kevin Barry

TAKEAWAYS

  • Hotel Asset Dispositions -- 112 hotels totaling approximately 14,600 keys sold during the year for nearly $860 million in gross proceeds.
  • Debt Reduction -- Proceeds from hotel sales and internal cash used to fully redeem $800 million of 2026 maturities, and $300 million of 2027 notes.
  • Q4 Hotel Portfolio Performance -- RevPAR increased 70 basis points year over year, outperforming the U.S. lodging industry's 1.1% decline.
  • Retained Portfolio Hotels -- 77 hotels showed RevPAR up 170 basis points, and occupancy up 140 basis points year over year in the quarter.
  • Hotel EBITDA -- Adjusted hotel EBITDA totaled $21.3 million in Q4, declining 35% year over year due to increased labor costs, overhead, and lack of prior-year business interruption insurance gains.
  • Sonesta-Managed Hotels for Sale -- Seven full-service properties (2,010 keys) being marketed with expected proceeds of $175 million to $200 million, anticipated to increase annual EBITDA by $13 million upon sale.
  • Net Lease Portfolio -- 760 properties, 97% leased, produced $390 million in annual base rents, with a 7.4-year weighted average lease term at year-end.
  • Net Lease Acquisitions -- $101 million invested in new assets during the year, delivering an average GAAP cap rate of 8.3%, a cash cap rate of 7.5%, and weighted average lease term of 14.3 years.
  • Net Lease Financing -- $745 million raised through new five-year mortgage financing collateralized by 158 properties (appraised at $1.1 billion) at a 5.96% coupon, with proceeds to fully redeem $700 million of 8.38% notes due 2029.
  • Annual Interest Savings -- New net lease financing expected to reduce annual cash interest expense by $14 million, or $0.08 per share.
  • Normalized FFO -- Q4 normalized funds from operations were $27.5 million, or $0.17 per share, flat compared to the prior year quarter.
  • Adjusted EBITDAre -- Adjusted EBITDAre was $125.6 million, down $5 million from the prior year quarter.
  • Full-Year 2026 Guidance – Hotels -- RevPAR projected between $108 and $113; hotel EBITDA expected to range from $124 million to $144 million for 94 hotels owned at year-end.
  • Full-Year 2026 Guidance – Net Lease -- Net lease net operating income projected at $383 million to $386 million.
  • Full-Year 2026 Guidance – Consolidated -- Adjusted EBITDA forecasted at $500 million to $520 million, normalized FFO per share at $0.65 to $0.77, and total CapEx of $120 million to $140 million.
  • Capital Expenditures -- Q4 investment of $106 million; full-year 2025 total $238 million, with significant projects including the Nautilus in Miami, and large-scale renovations at multiple Sonesta properties.
  • Net Lease Portfolio Lease Executions -- 536,000 square feet newly leased in Q4, with average cash rent roll-up of 15%, and average term exceeding nine years.
  • Debt Profile and Covenants -- Current debt totals $5.2 billion at a weighted average interest rate of 5.95%; secured debt to total-asset covenant rose to 33% of a 40% maximum limit.
  • Portfolio Mix -- Post-Q4 and anticipated transactions, EBITDA mix expected to be approximately 70% net lease, and 30% hotel by end of 2026.
  • EBITDA Impact from Dispositions -- Nine remarketed focused service hotels generated approximately $3 million positive EBITDA; anticipated net $10 million annual drag reduction from planned hotel asset sales.

RISKS

  • Adjusted hotel EBITDA declined 35% year over year in Q4 due to "elevated labor costs, higher hotel overhead costs, and the impact of non-repeat business interruption insurance recognized in the prior year."
  • Disruptions from hotel dispositions contributed to operational performance pressures, described as "largely transitional" by management.
  • Benefit expense pressures and labor costs continue to "hamper margins," with margin guidance assuming these costs remain elevated.
  • Net lease rent coverage dropped below 2x, mainly attributed to "TA coverage dropping seven basis points quarter over quarter," indicating operational softness in the travel center segment.

SUMMARY

Service Properties Trust (SVC 2.33%) completed substantial hotel asset sales, materially deleveraged through retirements of 2026 and 2027 notes, and arranged a $745 million mortgage financing at improved interest rates. Consolidated hotel RevPAR outperformed the broader U.S. market, though hotel EBITDA faced pressure from expense growth and operating disruptions linked to asset dispositions. Guidance for 2026 indicates measured performance improvement, ongoing capital recycling, and reduced CapEx, with free cash flow expected post-investment. Annualized base rent in the net lease portfolio rose on acquisition activity, and the financing of 158 net lease assets will lower annual interest costs.

  • Hotel portfolio repositioning continues, as the sale of seven Sonesta-managed properties in 2026 targets a $13 million annual EBITDA lift, and the sale process for nine focused service hotels with stable occupancy is underway.
  • Net lease asset acquisitions were funded entirely by proceeds from portfolio dispositions, and cash on hand, reflecting a capital-recycling approach.
  • Weighted average cash cap rates, and long lease terms on new net lease assets signal management's focus on accretive, long-duration income streams.
  • Displacement from significant hotel renovations, especially at the Nautilus, will continue to impact results, but the company expects renovation-related disruptions and CapEx intensity to decrease in future periods.
  • Following refinancing and asset sales, EBITDA mix is projected to remain near a 70% net lease, and 30% hotel allocation, with minimal short-term headroom remaining for incremental secured financings under existing covenants.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RevPAR: Revenue per available room, a hotel industry metric for operating performance.
  • Adjusted EBITDAre: Adjusted Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization, and real estate-related adjustments; common in REIT reporting.
  • Normalized FFO: Normalized funds from operations; a key REIT metric representing recurring, cash-based profitability, excluding specified non-recurring items.
  • Cap Rate: Capitalization rate, calculated as property net income divided by acquisition price or value; indicates return on investment.
  • Net Lease: Lease structure where the tenant pays property taxes, insurance, and maintenance, lowering landlord operating risk and costs.
  • GOP Margin: Gross operating profit margin; hotel revenues less operating expenses as a percentage of total revenue.
  • Displacement: Temporary loss of hotel revenues or profit due to renovations or other operational disruptions.
