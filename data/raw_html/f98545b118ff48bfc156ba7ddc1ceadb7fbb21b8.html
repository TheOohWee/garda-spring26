SUMMARY

Comstock Resources (CRK 2.09%) reported a significant increase in fiscal full-year revenue and EBITDAX, driven by improved realized natural gas prices despite a 14% production decline. The company executed major portfolio optimization with $445 million in asset divestitures, leading to material balance sheet deleveraging and $1.3 billion in liquidity. Management emphasized considerable expansion in drilling inventory and reserves, with Western Haynesville emerging as a key future growth catalyst through active rig deployment, new cost reduction technology initiatives, and a two-gigawatt data center JV with NextEra. Operational flexibility was highlighted, with capital spending plans able to adjust quickly in response to commodity price volatility. The pivot to longer laterals, improved cost structure, and targeted infrastructure investments set up the company for potential margin gains and increased cash flow generation in upcoming periods.

  • Pinnacle Gas Services is expected to transition to free cash flow positivity in the second half of 2026, supported by the Marquet Train Two plant startup and planned preferred equity redemption.
  • Average Western Haynesville EURs cited as three to four Bcf per 1,000 feet, with a current average of 3.5 Bcf per 1,000 feet according to Netherland, Sewell & Associates.
  • Management stated, "we have as many as four rigs that we could with short notice take out of action," confirming operational flexibility if gas prices weaken.
  • Benchmark legacy Haynesville first-year PDP decline rate decreased by one or two percentage points from 40% in 2024, as Western Haynesville becomes a larger part of the portfolio.
  • The company plans to increase Western Haynesville well lateral lengths, with its first 15,000-foot lateral targeted for 2026, supported by rig and drilling technology upgrades.
  • No new asset divestitures are planned for 2026, with management affirming confidence in the remaining drilling inventory.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • IP (Initial Production) rate: The initial rate at which a new well produces oil or gas, typically measured in millions of cubic feet per day for gas wells.
  • PDP (Proved Developed Producing): Reserves that are both proved and developed, and currently producing hydrocarbons.
  • EUR (Estimated Ultimate Recovery): The total volume of hydrocarbons expected to be produced from a well or field over its lifetime.
  • EBITDAX: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, and exploration expense, used as a cash flow and operational profitability metric in the oil and gas sector.
  • Bcfe/Tcfe: Billion/trillion cubic feet equivalent, a volumetric unit combining oil and gas volumes using a standard energy equivalence ratio.
  • Basis differential: The price difference between a local or company-specific natural gas price and a standard reference price such as NYMEX.
  • Frac fleet: A group of hydraulic fracturing equipment and crews dedicated to stimulating wells.
  • Rotary steerable drilling assembly: Advanced drilling technology that allows precise directional control during drilling, improving wellbore quality and efficiency.
  • Horseshoe well: A specific drilling design that reuses stranded short laterals, often in high-yield areas, by curving the wellbore in a U-shape to access more reservoir.
