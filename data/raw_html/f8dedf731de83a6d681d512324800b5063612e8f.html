By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 11:11AM EST

Wednesday, Feb. 11, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Saumya Sutaria
  • Chief Financial Officer — Sun Park

RISKS

  • Sun Park explicitly cited a "wide range of potential outcomes" regarding the impact of premium tax credit expiration, estimating a $250 million hit to 2026 adjusted EBITDA, predominantly in the hospital segment.
  • Guidance assumes a 20% reduction in overall exchange enrollment, with exposure concentrated in states such as Arizona, Michigan, and California, which may increase uninsured rates and negatively impact volume growth and payer mix.
  • Management excluded any incremental contribution from supplemental Medicaid program approvals not yet received, leaving 2026 results exposed if approvals are delayed or denied.
  • Saumya Sutaria stated the "urgency with respect to much of what we are talking about doing is enhanced because of the, you know, what has happened on the exchange marketplace," which may pressure future operating margins.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net operating revenue -- $21.3 billion for the full year, with $5.5 billion in fiscal fourth quarter (period ended Dec. 31, 2025).
  • Consolidated adjusted EBITDA -- $4.57 billion for the year, up 14%, and $1.183 billion for fiscal Q4, a 13% increase.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin -- 21.4% for both fiscal Q4 and full year, up 210 basis points for the year.
  • USPI adjusted EBITDA -- $2.026 billion for the year (+12%), and $2.13 billion to $2.23 billion guidance for 2026.
  • Same-facility USPI revenues -- Up 7.5% for the year; fiscal Q4 same-facility revenues up 7.2%, net revenue per case up 5.5%.
  • USPI case volumes -- Same-facility case volumes increased 1.6% in fiscal Q4; double-digit same-store volume growth in total joint replacements noted for the year.
  • Ambulatory portfolio expansion -- Thirty-five facilities added in 2025 through $350 million of capital investment.
  • Hospital segment adjusted EBITDA -- $2.54 billion for the year (+16%); fiscal Q4 at $603 million (+16%).
  • Hospital revenue per adjusted admission -- Up 5.3% for the year; fiscal Q4 grew 7.5%, even as admissions were flat.
  • Expense structure improvements -- Salaries, wages, and benefits were 40.2% of fiscal Q4 revenues (110 basis point year-over-year improvement); contract labor 2.1% of SW&D.
  • Free cash flow -- $2.53 billion for 2025; $367 million in fiscal Q4.
  • Cash on hand -- $2.88 billion as of Dec. 31, 2025; no line of credit borrowings outstanding.
  • Debt maturities and leverage -- No significant maturities until late 2027; leverage ratio at 2.25× EBITDA (2.85× EBITDA less NCI).
  • Share repurchases -- Eight point eight million shares repurchased for $1.386 billion in 2025; 22% of shares retired since 2022 for $2.5 billion total.
  • 2026 revenue and EBITDA guidance -- Projected net operating revenue: $21.5 billion to $22.3 billion; consolidated adjusted EBITDA: $4.485 billion to $4.785 billion.
  • Guidance headwinds -- $250 million EBITDA impact from expiration of enhanced premium tax credits embedded in 2026 outlook.
  • Supplemental Medicaid -- $1.34 billion in total 2025 supplemental payments; 2026 guidance assumes a consistent normalized baseline.
  • Free cash flow guidance -- 2026 outlook calls for $2.5 billion to $2.8 billion; $1.865 billion after NCI and excluding Conifer tax payments at the midpoint.
  • Fiscal Q1 2026 guidance -- Fiscal Q1 expected to deliver 24% of full-year EBITDA; USPI segment at 22% of its annual EBITDA target.
  • Cost management initiatives -- Management stated new structural cost reductions leveraging automation and technology, beyond traditional annual savings.
  • M&A pipeline -- 2026 target of $250 million for USPI M&A investment, with an active deal pipeline noted.
  • Conifer transaction -- Conifer deal retired $885 million in obligations and returned 23.8% JV equity for $540 million; resulted in $1.9 billion in accelerated cash flow, with after-tax NPV estimated at over $1 billion.
  • Exchange plan exposure -- Hospital segment assumes 20% lower enrollment due to premium tax credit expiration, with management expecting up to 15% to find alternative coverage.
  • Payer mix -- In fiscal Q4, exchanges accounted for 7.5% of admissions and over 6.5% of consolidated revenues.
  • Payer contracting -- 3%-5% payer rate increases achieved; 2026 virtually fully contracted, 80% contracted for 2027.

SUMMARY

Management at Tenet Healthcare (THC 3.28%) provided detailed updates on capital structure, highlighting $2.88 billion in cash, no near-term debt maturities, and flexibility for further M&A and share repurchases. Commentary on the Conifer transaction detailed the $1.9 billion accelerated cash inflow and organizational benefits from retiring joint venture obligations. For 2026, a significant headwind from the expiration of enhanced premium tax credits was quantified, with explicit assumptions for exchange patient migration and payer mix risk. New cost-management efforts described as "structural" were emphasized, centering on technology, automation, and process standardization for sustainable expense reduction. Hospital and USPI segment strategies target service line expansion, physician recruitment, and selective reinvestment to drive disciplined top-line and margin growth.

  • Saumya Sutaria stressed that margin expansion remains a focus but acknowledged more uncertainty and conservative guidance for the hospital segment due to payer dynamics and policy risks.
  • Fourth-quarter exchange volumes were specifically disclosed, providing near-term transparency into exposure and payer mix trends.
  • Supplemental Medicaid revenues are modeled flat year over year, with an out-of-period adjustment called out for clarity.
  • The company plans to deploy free cash flow primarily toward accretive USPI deals, targeted hospital expansion, and opportunistic share repurchases, balancing growth with returns.
  • Contract labor expenses and salary ratios are trending lower, reflecting expense discipline even as case mix and pricing support higher revenues per admission.
  • Future share repurchase cadence is linked to balance sheet strength and confidence in sustained performance at premium valuation multiples.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • USPI: United Surgical Partners International, Tenet Healthcare's ambulatory surgery center network.
  • De novo: New facility developed from the ground up rather than through acquisition.
  • Adjusted admissions: Standardized metric that accounts for both inpatient admissions and equivalent outpatient activity.
  • Enhanced premium tax credits: Temporary subsidies offered through ACA exchanges, affecting patient insurance coverage and provider payer mix.
  • Supplemental Medicaid payments: Additional government payments to hospitals beyond standard Medicaid reimbursements, often subject to approval and variability by state.
  • Conifer: Tenet Healthcare's healthcare business process services subsidiary, providing revenue cycle management and related services.
  • Out-of-period payment: Revenue or cost recognized outside its usual reporting period, often due to timing or transaction structure.
  • EBITDA less NCI: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted to exclude non-controlling interests.
  • ASC: Ambulatory Surgery Center, an outpatient facility where surgeries not requiring hospital admission are performed.
  • Inpatient-only list: CMS-defined list of procedures historically reimbursed only when performed in hospital inpatient settings; phase-out enables shifts to outpatient.
  • DPP: Delivery System Reform Incentive Payment Program, a form of supplemental Medicaid revenue.
  • HICC: Healthcare Insurance Coverage Category; contextually refers to ACA exchange plans within admissions and revenue metrics.
