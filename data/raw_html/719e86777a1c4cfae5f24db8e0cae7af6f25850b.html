Takeaways

  • Net sales -- $1.6 billion, up 12% year over year, attributed to a 14% increase in shipments, primarily from operating the Augusta Mill for a full year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $107 million for the full year, a $71 million improvement versus the prior year, resulting from substantial cost control actions.
  • SG&A as a percentage of net sales -- 6.5%, down from 8.4% in 2024, including $16 million in SG&A savings.
  • Share repurchases -- $17 million in shares repurchased, with $79 million remaining under authorization.
  • Year-end liquidity -- Over $400 million in liquidity preserved on the balance sheet.
  • Major maintenance outage costs -- $50 million total for the year, all three completed on schedule and representing lower costs versus prior year.
  • Workforce reduction -- More than 10% reduction in total workforce in 2025, including about a 40% cut in corporate SG&A headcount.
  • Insurance proceeds -- $23 million initial settlement linked to Augusta acquisition representation and warranty coverage, with $75 million of the $105 million limit remaining for potential future recoveries.
  • Current quarter guidance -- Q1 adjusted EBITDA anticipated at approximately breakeven, reflecting $15 million to $20 million in incremental costs due to severe weather-related disruptions at Odessa and Cypress Bend facilities.
  • Paperboard pricing headwinds -- Approximately $70 million expected headwind in 2026 from price pressure, incorporating the Q4 RISI index change.
  • RISI index exposure -- 40% of volume tied to the RISI folding carton index and 10% to the RISI cup index after the Augusta Mill acquisition.
  • Announced price increases -- $60 per ton for cup grades and $50 per ton for other products, targeting 50% of volume not indexed to RISI.
  • 2026 revenue outlook -- $1.4 billion to $1.5 billion, assuming flat to modest shipment growth and $70 million in pricing headwind, without including effects from new price increases or RISI’s projected price recovery.
  • Planned capital expenditures -- $65 million to $75 million in 2026.
  • Planned working capital improvement -- Approximately $20 million expected, with improvement weighted toward the back half of the year.
  • New product launch -- VOLURA, a lightweight paperboard incorporating mechanical pulp in the middle layer, scheduled to launch in the second quarter.
  • Potential strategic investment -- Engineering feasibility completed for a $60 million conversion to CUK at Cypress Bend, with projected annual capture of 100,000 tons–150,000 tons of independent converter demand if executed.
  • Operating rate context -- Industry SBS operating rates dropped to the low 80% range by the end of 2025 due to 500,000 tons of new capacity and flat demand, per American Forest & Paper Association and RISI data; pricing aligned accordingly.
  • Management on profitability -- CEO Kitch stated, "At these margin levels, we do not believe that Clearwater Paper (CLW 2.62%) can produce the cash flows and returns that are necessary to reinvest in these types of capital-intensive assets in the long run."
  • Outlook for supply-demand balance -- Industry supply is stated to exceed demand by about 400,000 tons–500,000 tons; RISI forecasts imports to decrease 8% and exports to grow 5% in 2026, with a net capacity reduction of 180,000 tons projected.
  • Actions under consideration -- The company may implement extended curtailments and further variabilize costs to balance supply with demand in 2026; additional asset and cost structure review is ongoing.

Summary

Clearwater Paper (CLW 2.62%) reported a sharp year-over-year increase in shipments and net sales, driven by the full integration of the Augusta Mill, aggressive fixed cost reductions, and ongoing execution of operational efficiencies. CFO Sherri Baker disclosed that net loss from continuing operations was $53 million, or $3.28 per diluted share, mainly due to a non-cash goodwill impairment; net income for the quarter was $3 million, or $0.20 per share, including insurance recoveries. Management faces a $70 million pricing headwind for 2026, which is not yet offset by announced price increases or forecasted improvements. Severe weather led to operational disruptions and incremental costs of $15 million to $20 million at two major plants, directly impacting first quarter guidance. The company launched a new lightweight product line, VOLURA, and completed engineering for a CUK investment, but has not yet made a final investment decision. Planned capital expenditures and working capital improvements are designed to maintain financial flexibility while weathering an industry downturn marked by low operating rates and oversupplied conditions.

  • CEO Kitch said, "today's margins and cash flow levels are not tenable for us for an extended period."
  • According to CFO Baker, "SG&A remained below our targeted 6% to 7% range, reflecting our continued cost discipline."
  • No specific mill closures or permanent asset rationalization have been determined, though management expects a strategy update by the end of the second quarter.
  • Exposure to the RISI folding carton index increased to 40% of volume post-Augusta acquisition, making future results sensitive to industry index pricing.
  • The company holds $75 million of remaining insurance claim coverage for issues identified in the Augusta Mill acquisition integration.

Industry glossary

  • SBS (Solid Bleached Sulfate): A premium paperboard substrate, often used for folding cartons and print applications, known for high brightness and surface quality.
  • CRB (Coated Recycled Board): A paperboard made primarily from recycled fibers, used for packaging where premium print quality is less critical.
  • CUK (Coated Unbleached Kraft): A high-strength paperboard substrate, generally used for beverage carriers and demanding packaging applications.
  • FBB (Folding Box Board): A layered paperboard composed of mechanical and chemical pulp, common in European packaging and increasingly present in North America.
  • RISI Index: An industry benchmark published by Fastmarkets RISI, tracking pricing movements for specific grades of paperboard, widely used as a reference for contract price adjustments.
  • CPG (Consumer Packaged Goods): Companies or sectors that manufacture and sell products consumed frequently, such as food, beverages, and household items.
  • QSR (Quick Service Restaurant): The restaurant sector focused on fast-food service models, a major end market for packaging grades like SBS and CUK.
  • VOLURA: The company’s soon-to-launch lightweight paperboard product line, designed to compete with FBB and target the North American bleached market.
