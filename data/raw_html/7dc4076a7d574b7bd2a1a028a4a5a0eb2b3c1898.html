NovoCure (NVCR) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

  • Executive Chairman — William Doyle
  • Chief Executive Officer — Frank Leonard
  • Chief Financial Officer — Christoph Brackmann

Takeaways

  • Net Revenue -- $655 million for the year and $174 million for the quarter, both reflecting 8% year-over-year growth.
  • Ex-U.S. Market Performance -- Active patient growth rates: Germany 10%, France 19%, Japan 29%.
  • U.S. GBM Active Patients -- four percent growth, following a period of flat performance.
  • Foreign Exchange Tailwind -- approximately $11 million for the year and approximately $5 million in the quarter.
  • Optune Lua Revenue -- $10.4 million for the year, including $5.8 million from non-small cell lung cancer; recognized $3.5 million in the quarter, with $2.4 million attributed to non-small cell lung cancer.
  • Gross Margin -- 75% for the year and 76% for the quarter, compared to prior-year figures of 77% and 79% respectively; decline attributed to reduced prior period collections and increased costs, including higher tariffs.
  • R&D Costs -- $225 million for the year (up 7%) and $61 million for the quarter (up 19%), primarily due to KEYNOTE-58, LUNAR-2 trial costs, and regulatory expenses.
  • Sales and Marketing Expense -- $240 million for the year (flat year over year) and $69 million for the quarter (up 2%).
  • G&A Expense -- $178 million for the year (down 6%) and $43 million for the quarter (down 41%), with the decrease driven by lower share-based compensation.
  • Net Loss -- $136 million for the year ($1.22 per share) and $24 million for the quarter ($0.22 per share).
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Negative $34 million for the year and negative $16 million for the quarter.
  • 2026 Revenue Guidance -- $675 million to $705 million, reflecting 3%-8% year-over-year growth at constant currency.
  • 2026 Adjusted EBITDA Guidance -- Range of negative $20 million to breakeven, indicating a stated goal to achieve profitability.
  • 2026 Gross Margin Guidance -- Expected in the mid-70s percentage range.
  • Optune Gio Growth Expectation -- Low to mid-single-digit percentage range in established markets; modest contributions from new launches in Spain, Czechia, and British Columbia are anticipated.
  • Non-GBM Product Revenue Expectation -- Expected contribution of $15-$25 million in 2026, compared to $10 million in 2025, primarily from Optune Lua in Japan and Optune Pax in the U.S. and other regions.
  • Medicare Billing Update -- Company stated, "we do not expect any negative impact on revenue recognition" following the reinstatement of CMS billing privileges retroactive to December 17, 2025.
  • Clinical Milestones -- Anticipated top-line readouts: PANOVA-4 trial next month, TRIDENT trial in Q2; KEYNOTE D58 trial expected to complete enrollment by year-end.
  • Regulatory Approvals and Applications -- Optune Pax received FDA approval for locally advanced pancreatic cancer; regulatory filings submitted for Optune Pax in Europe and Japan; PMA decision pending for brain metastases from non-small cell lung cancer.
  • Field Force Deployment -- No incremental headcount added for Optune Pax launch; existing torso-focused sales team repurposed for pancreatic indication coverage.
  • Leadership Change -- Dr. Nicolas Leupin resigned as Chief Medical Officer on February 25; Dr. Uri Weinberg, Chief Innovation Officer, will also serve as Chief Medical Officer with an aim to accelerate R&D execution.

Summary

NovoCure (NVCR 3.82%) reported its highest annual net revenue to date, driven by international patient growth and new product launches. The company communicated its first formal annual revenue and adjusted EBITDA guidance, emphasizing the movement toward both steady growth and potential breakeven in profitability. Optune Pax gained FDA approval for first-line treatment of locally advanced pancreatic cancer, with commercial launch preparations underway and reimbursement processes in early stages. Management outlined a series of imminent clinical and regulatory milestones anticipated for 2026, including major trial readouts and expanding European and Japanese regulatory submissions. Company leadership roles changed, with the resignation of the Chief Medical Officer and consolidation of R&D leadership functions.

  • Chief Executive Officer Leonard said, "we are committed to returning the company to steady growth," highlighting a new phase of public messaging and performance expectations.
  • Full-year net loss and negative adjusted EBITDA prompted a renewed focus on expense management, with plans to avoid further draws on the current credit facility beyond the $200 million already used.
  • Optune Pax commercial launch leverages existing sales infrastructure and grants physicians greater discretion in patient selection compared to prior launches.
  • Establishment of payer coverage for Optune Pax in the United States is in progress, with inclusion in clinical guidelines expected to play a crucial role in future revenue growth.

Industry glossary

  • TTFields (Tumor Treating Fields): A medical device-based modality that uses alternating electric fields to disrupt cancer cell division in solid tumors.
  • PMA (Premarket Approval): U.S. FDA regulatory process for medical devices requiring evidence of safety and efficacy before commercialization.
  • GBM (Glioblastoma): An aggressive and common form of primary brain cancer targeted by Optune Gio.
  • NCCN Guidelines: National Comprehensive Cancer Network clinical practice recommendations widely adopted by U.S. oncology payers for medical coverage decisions.
  • HCP (Healthcare Professional) Portal: A digital platform allowing physicians to submit prescriptions and manage patient information for company products.
  • DME Supplier Revalidation: CMS-mandated periodic check confirming a supplier’s eligibility to provide durable medical equipment to Medicare beneficiaries.
  • PSUs (Performance Share Units): Share-based compensation granted to employees, vesting based on achievement of specific company milestones.
