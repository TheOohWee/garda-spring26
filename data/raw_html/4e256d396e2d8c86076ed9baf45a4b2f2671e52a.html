Daktronics (DAKT) Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 10, 2025 at 3:45PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, Dec. 10, 2025 at 11 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Interim President and Chief Executive Officer — Bradley Wiemann
  • Acting Chief Financial Officer and Board Member — Howard Atkins
  • Chairman of the Board — Andrew Siegel
  • Incoming President and Chief Executive Officer — Ramesh Jayaraman

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Order Growth -- Orders rose 12% across all business segments year-over-year, marking three consecutive quarters of top-line growth.
  • Product Backlog -- The product backlog reached $321 million, increasing 36% year-over-year and representing a multi-quarter revenue runway.
  • Net Income -- Net income was $17.5 million, or $0.35 per diluted share, up 25.4% on an adjusted basis after accounting for prior-year nonrecurring items.
  • Operating Income -- Operating income reached $21.6 million, compared to $15.8 million in the prior period, with an operating margin of 9.4% approaching the 10%-12.5% company target range.
  • Segment Performance -- Live Events posted 26.5% order growth, while International delivered 23.6% order growth; Commercial segment orders declined 5% driven by fewer large outdoor spectacular projects.
  • Gross Profit Margin -- Gross profit margin was 27%, up from the previous year.
  • Cash Position -- Net cash was $138.3 million, increasing about 20% from $115.5 million at prior year-end.
  • Tariff Expense -- Tariff expenses were $8.8 million compared to $1.5 million in the prior year period, directly impacting margins.
  • Manufacturing Expansion -- Announced facility opening in Saltillo, Mexico, with initial production targeted for late April 2026, supporting growth and supply chain agility.
  • Innovation Roadmap -- Significant product launches included a new 2.5mm chip-on-board narrow pixel pitch offering, next-generation digital billboard, All Sport Lite mobile scoring app, and Venus Control Suite Live content management system; three additional launches slated for the second half of the year.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- Share repurchase capacity increased to $25.7 million, with $20 million newly authorized by the Board.
  • New CEO Appointment -- Ramesh Jayaraman formally introduced, effective as President and CEO beginning February 1.
  • Third Quarter Seasonality -- Management stated third quarter is typically "seasonally slower" due to Thanksgiving, Christmas, and New Year's holidays affecting order-to-revenue conversion.
  • Inventory Efficiency -- Inventory-to-sales ratio improved over five consecutive quarters, boosting balance sheet strength and investable cash.
  • Recurring Revenue Initiatives -- Launched Software-as-a-Service product trial to develop new subscription models, enhancing long-term recurring revenue streams.

Summary

Daktronics (DAKT +0.05%) reported robust year-over-year momentum in orders, profit, and operating metrics, underpinned by margin expansion and disciplined cost management. Management emphasized the strategic significance of the $321 million backlog weighted toward Live Events, which could provide extended revenue visibility as these projects gradually convert over multiple quarters. The company is executing an innovation-led growth strategy, marked by several new product launches, planned manufacturing expansion in Mexico, and the hiring of incoming CEO Ramesh Jayaraman to drive long-term performance.

  • The mix shift in backlog towards Live Events means a higher proportion of future revenue will be driven by complex, customized projects with longer delivery timelines.
  • Segment diversification efforts are leading to increased international contributions and providing resilience as some Commercial segment order volumes moderated.
  • The conversion of the bank credit line to a cash flow facility enhances Daktronics' financing flexibility, with no current borrowings under the facility.
  • Structural improvements in inventory management have helped mitigate tariff costs and strengthen cash flows, though management signaled further efficiency gains may be limited from current levels.

Industry glossary

  • Narrow Pixel Pitch: LED display technology characterized by a small distance between pixels, enabling higher resolution and superior image quality, especially in close-viewing environments.
  • All Sport Lite: Daktronics' mobile application for entry-level scoreboard control, aimed at simplifying scoring for youth and community sports events.
  • Venus Control Suite Live: Daktronics' cloud-hosted content management platform for managing live venue display content remotely.
