Eversource (ES) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 4, 2025 at 7:07PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, August 1, 2025 at 1:00 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Joseph R. Nolan Jr.

Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — John P. Marreira

Vice President, Investor Relations — Rima Q. Hyder

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

RISKS

Moody's downgraded Connecticut Light and Power to Baa1 from A3, citing the Connecticut regulatory environment as the reason for the downgrade.

TAKEAWAYS

Quarterly Earnings— GAAP and recurring earnings were $0.96 per share, up from $0.95 per share in the prior year, with growth in transmission, distribution, natural gas, and water segments, partially offset by higher parent interest expense.

Load Growth— Electric demand growth exceeded 2% through 2025, nearly double the rate observed in the same period last year, driven by electrification and decarbonization trends.

Capital Plan— Five-year capital investment plan increased 10% to $24.2 billion, with $2.2 billion executed through June 2025 and $1.5-$2 billion in additional potential opportunities identified.

FFO to Debt— Moody's FFO to debt ratio reached 11.5% as of Q1 2025, an improvement of over 200 basis points from year-end 2024, with expectations of reaching 13%-14% post-Aquarion sale.

Connecticut SB4— Senate Bill 4 enacted, enabling securitization of storm costs from 2018-2025 and shifting some public benefit program costs from customer bills to state bond funding, expected to modestly reduce customer bill impacts.

Storm Cost Recovery— Deferred storm costs submitted for prudency review in Connecticut totaled $980 million as of December 2023; approximately 85% of $2.0 billion in total deferred storm costs across all states are either recovered or under review.

Regulatory Outcomes— New Hampshire PUC approved a $100 million permanent rate increase for Public Service Company of New Hampshire, effective July 25, 2025, based on a 9.5% ROE and a 50/50 capital structure, and authorized a new four-year performance-based rate mechanism with annual inflation adjustments and a 142 basis point adder.

Equity Issuance— Issued approximately $200 million of equity under the ATM program in June 2025 to provide liquidity; future issuances will be monitored based on Aquarion sale timing and short-term debt needs.

Infrastructure Investment— Advanced metering infrastructure rollout in Massachusetts is underway, with the AMI communication network substantially complete in Western Massachusetts and meter installation started in Weston.

Earnings Guidance— Recurring EPS guidance for 2025 reaffirmed at $4.67-$4.82 per share, with a long-term EPS growth target of 5%-7% off the 2024 base year.

SUMMARY

Eversource Energy(ES 0.85%) management emphasized execution of strategic priorities, including regulated utility infrastructure investment and grid modernization to address rising regional demand. The Connecticut Supreme Court clarified that prudency for cost recovery must be assessed at the time of management's investment decision, prohibiting regulatory hindsight in future rate cases. The onshore substation for the Revolution Wind project, managed by Eversource, is expected to reach substantial completion this month and enter the testing and commissioning phase, reducing critical path risk for the company. Equity needs for the five-year forecast period remain at $1.2 billion, with the majority anticipated in the latter half of the plan and potential reductions pending Aquarion transaction closure and storm cost securitization timing. Management reported an over $1 billion year-over-year improvement in operating cash flows for the first half of 2025, driven by cash flow initiatives and regulatory recoveries. The company maintains top decile reliability performance, supporting service continuity for over 4 million customers across three states.

The Connecticut Supreme Court ruling clarified that "prudency needs to be set at the time management makes the investment decision," and that regulators cannot apply hindsight in cost recovery reviews.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

FFO to Debt: Ratio of funds from operations to total debt, used by credit rating agencies to assess financial leverage and cash flow adequacy.

AMI: Advanced Metering Infrastructure; integrated systems of smart meters and communication networks that enable real-time monitoring and management of energy usage.

PURA: Public Utilities Regulatory Authority; regulatory body for utilities in Connecticut.

ATM Program: At-the-market equity offering program, allowing the company to issue shares incrementally into the market to raise capital as needed.

Performance-Based Rate Mechanism (PBR): Regulatory framework tying utility revenues to agreed performance outcomes, often allowing for periodic adjustments based on inflation or other metrics.

Deferred Storm Cost: Utility expenses related to storm restoration that are tracked separately and eligible for later recovery from customers, subject to regulatory approval.
