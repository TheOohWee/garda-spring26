GEO Group (GEO) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 2:36PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 12, 2026 at 1 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman & CEO — George Zoley
  • Chief Financial Officer — Mark Suchinski

TAKEAWAYS

  • Largest new business wins in company history -- Awarded new or expanded contracts representing up to approximately $520 million in new incremental annualized revenues, with normalization expected by year-end.
  • ICE facility activations -- Activated five facilities totaling approximately 6,000 beds, accounting for annualized revenue of around $400 million, and the hiring of 2,000 new employees.
  • ICE facility population -- Active ICE facility census rose from approximately 22,000 in the third quarter to around 24,000 currently, the highest level for the company to date.
  • Secured transportation services expansion -- Expanded services for ICE and U.S. Marshals Service, adding approximately $60 million in incremental annualized revenue.
  • ISAP 5-program milestone -- Secured a new two-year contract covering 361,000 participants in year one and 465,000 in year two; ankle monitor usage grew from 17,000 to over 42,000 participants during the year.
  • SmartLink mobile app trend -- ISAP participants using SmartLink declined to under 135,000, while case management assignments rose to about 106,000 individuals.
  • Skip tracing contract -- Awarded a new two-year contract valued at up to $60 million annually, following a $10 million revenue pilot in Q4 2025.
  • Revenue (Q4 2025) -- Reported $708 million, up from $608 million in the prior year's fourth quarter.
  • Net income (Q4 2025) -- Reached approximately $32 million, or $0.23 per diluted share, compared to $15.5 million, or $0.11 per diluted share, in Q4 2024.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (Q4 2025) -- Increased to about $126 million, compared to $108 million for the prior year’s fourth quarter.
  • Owned and leased secure service revenues -- Rose by around $70 million, or 23%, primarily due to ICE facility activations, partially offset by asset sales and population changes elsewhere.
  • Managed-only contract revenues -- Grew by approximately $26 million, or 17%, largely due to the North Florida joint venture and increased transportation-related revenues.
  • Adjusted net income (full year 2025) -- Excluding extraordinary items, achieved $120 million, or $0.86 per diluted share, up from $101 million, or $0.75 per diluted share, for the full prior year.
  • Balance sheet strengthening -- Sold two facilities for $322 million, reducing net debt by year-end to approximately $1.5 billion, and lowering annual interest expense by about $30 million.
  • Share buyback program -- Repurchased around five million shares for $91 million by year-end, leaving $409 million available under a $500 million authorization.
  • 2026 guidance -- Forecasts annual revenues of $2.9 billion to $3.1 billion, GAAP net income of $0.99 to $1.07 per diluted share, and adjusted EBITDA of $490 million to $510 million.
  • Q1 2026 outlook -- Expects revenues of $680 million to $690 million, GAAP net income of $0.17 to $0.19 per diluted share, and adjusted EBITDA of $107 million to $112 million, reflecting seasonal and contract transition impacts.
  • Liquidity position -- Ended 2025 with $70 million in cash, expanded revolving credit facility by $100 million, and cited lender support for future liquidity needs.
  • CEO transition -- Announced the retirement of CEO Dave Donahue effective end of February; George Zoley returns as Chairman and CEO under a contract through April 2029.

RISKS

  • Management stated, "If no additional appropriation bill is passed by Congress before the expiration of the current continuing resolution, there will be a partial government shutdown involving the Department of Homeland Security," potentially delaying payment timing and collections, and requiring careful liquidity management.
  • CFO Suchinski noted, "our first quarter 2026 guidance reflects a decline from our fourth quarter '25 results" due to elevated payroll taxes, fewer days in the quarter, and no skip tracing revenue assumed, contributing to margin compression and near-term earnings decline.
  • Continued start-up expenses for recently activated or idle facilities, particularly on the West Coast, are expected to create headwinds and temporarily compress margins until later in the year.
  • Guidance assumes "modest organic growth," but management cautions that timing of new contract awards and facility activations is uncertain, which may affect results if delays persist.

SUMMARY

GEO Group (GEO 1.96%) reported record-setting new contract wins totaling up to $520 million in incremental annualized revenues, led by ICE facility activations and major service expansions. Full-year adjusted net income rose to $120 million, supported by substantial increases in both secure services and managed-only revenues, alongside asset sales strengthening the balance sheet and reducing interest expense. Shareholder returns accelerated via a large-scale buyback authorization, with five million shares repurchased in 2025 and further capital return capacity remaining. A new multi-year ISAP 5 contract, technological mix shift favoring higher-revenue ankle monitors, and expanded skip tracing revenues position the company for future growth, though management noted seasonal margin compression and government funding uncertainties as near-term considerations. A CEO transition will see George Zoley reassume the top executive post as the company enters 2026 with optimism, emphasizing growth opportunities across business segments and increased participation in federal procurement initiatives.

  • Zoley said, "The census across our active ICE facilities has continued to steadily increase from the third quarter at approximately 22,000 to presently approximately 24,000, which is the highest level of ICE populations we've ever had."
  • The company indicated that, if fully activated, 6,000 additional idle beds could generate more than $300 million in incremental annualized revenues.
  • Zoley emphasized the technology mix shift impact, stating, "The number of ISAP participants on GPS ankle monitors has increased from approximately 17,000 in early 2025 to more than 42,000 ankle monitors today," with case management growing to 106,000 assignments.
  • Skip tracing pilot generated $10 million in Q4 2025, with the full contract expected to ramp mainly in the back half of 2026, contributing up to $60 million annually.
  • The company sold two facilities for a combined $322 million, subsequently acquiring the 770-bed San Diego facility for $60 million to further support U.S. Marshals Service contracts.
  • Suchinski reported improved liquidity, "resulting in improvement in our current net debt balance to approximately $1.5 billion," following accounts receivable normalization post-government shutdown.
  • Zoley committed to continued "our focus is to lean in hard when there are opportunities, as you just mentioned. And I think we've been very diligent about making sure that we manage our liquidity and take advantage of the stock buyback program when we can. And so we're going to lean into it hard. We're looking at it, and we'll continue to do that." share buybacks when market opportunities arise and stressed ongoing diligence in capital allocation.
  • No revenue was assumed from new skip tracing contract in Q1 2026 due to timing of contract ramp after pilot phase; management expects contribution primarily in the second half of the year.
  • Management underlined active discussions with ICE for both facility reactivations and potential federal warehouse management opportunities, highlighting federal priorities for larger-scale, high-security facilities.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ISAP: Intensive Supervision Appearance Program—a federal contract for electronic monitoring and case management of non-detained immigrants in ICE proceedings.
  • SmartLink: A mobile phone application used under ISAP for location tracking and check-ins based on facial recognition, voice identification, and GPS.
  • Skip tracing: The process of locating individuals through data verification and address research, typically used to track non-detainees under federal supervision.
  • Managed-only contract: Facility management agreement in which the company provides operational services without ownership of the underlying property.
