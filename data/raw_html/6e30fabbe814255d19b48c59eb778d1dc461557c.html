NOV (NOV) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 4, 2025 at 10:29PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, July 29, 2025 at 3:00 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Clay Williams

Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Rodney Reed

Senior Vice President — Jose Bayardo

Vice President, Investor Relations and Corporate Communications — Amie Burnett

For analyst commentary, email [email protected]

RISKS

Aftermarket spare parts and consumable product sales declined due to softened oil and gas activity. This led to sequential margin compression across several businesses.

Macroeconomic uncertainty, the rapid unwinding of OPEC plus production quotas, and conflict in the Middle East have made customers more cautious. In North America, exploration and production

Tariff and inflation-driven cost pressures, including a June increase in section 232 steel tariffs from 25% to 50%, are increasing expenses and offsetting expected cost savings initiatives.

A sharp decline in spare parts bookings and reduced service utilization occurred in the drilling equipment business, especially impacting the aftermarket segment.

TAKEAWAYS

Revenue: $2.2 billion (GAAP) for fiscal Q2 2025 (period ended June 30, 2025), up 4% sequentially, but down 1% year over year.

Net Income: $108 million, or 29¢ per fully diluted share, on a U.S. GAAP basis.

Adjusted EBITDA: Adjusted EBITDA was $252 million, representing 11.5% of sales.

Energy Equipment Segment Revenue: $1.21 billion, essentially unchanged from the prior year; EBITDA grew $16 million to $158 million, and segment margin rose by 130 basis points to 13.1% for the Energy Equipment segment.

Energy Products and Services Segment Revenue: $1.03 billion, a 2% decrease year over year. Adjusted EBITDA declined $38 million to $146 million, or 14.2% of sales (adjusted EBITDA, non-GAAP).

Free Cash Flow: with 83% EBITDA-to-free-cash-flow conversion over the last twelve months.

Shareholder Returns: 10.9 million shares repurchased for $150 million. Cumulative buybacks since the 2024 launch total approximately 25 million shares.

Dividends: $0.75 per share base dividend and $0.21 per share supplemental dividend paid, totaling $135 million year to date.

Tariff Expense: $11 million incurred; projected to increase to $25 million–$30 million by fiscal Q4.

Cost Reductions: Over $100 million in annualized cost savings targeted by 2026 via business process changes, strategic sourcing, facility consolidations, and market exits.

Working Capital: Improved by about 300 basis points year over year in working capital as a percentage of revenue for fiscal Q3; full-year range expected at 27%–29% of sales.

Third-Quarter Guidance: Consolidated revenue for fiscal Q3 is expected to decline by 1%–3% year over year, with adjusted EBITDA projected between $230 million–$250 million for fiscal Q3.

Segment Guidance: Energy Products and Services revenue to be flat to down 2% and EBITDA $130 million–$150 million for fiscal Q3; Energy Equipment revenue to decline 1%–3% with EBITDA $145 million–$160 million for fiscal Q3.

Backlog: Process systems and subsea flexible pipe businesses combined booked $1.6 billion in orders over the last five quarters as reported for the period ending fiscal Q2 2025.

Book-to-Bill Ratios: Energy Equipment segment had a 66% book-to-bill in fiscal Q2 due to lumpy flexible pipe orders, while certain sub-segments such as turret mooring systems and offshore construction had book-to-bill ratios above 150% in fiscal Q2.

Automation & Digital: 220 NOVOS control systems sold, 134 installed; four robotic drilling upgrade packages commissioned with 11 more in the pipeline; Digital product revenues up 25% year over year.

Production and Midstream Revenue: Achieved highest quarterly revenue since 2019, driven by growth in unconventional gas markets.

SUMMARY

Management cited pronounced macroeconomic uncertainty and customer caution, as evidenced by a 9% decline in the U.S. oil-directed rig count since March and reduced global drilling activity. Amid unfavorable sales mix shifts and sharply lower spare parts bookings, consolidated margins compressed sequentially, with tariffs and inflation explicitly called out as persistent headwinds anticipated to intensify in the near term.NOV(NOV 0.51%) expects the remainder of 2025 to remain challenging, with North American shale activity drifting lower and limited reacceleration in Saudi Arabian drilling before 2026, but points to sizable backlog and international demand for capital equipment as partial offsets. Executives outlined a multi-pronged cost reduction initiative—including facility rationalizations, market exits, and business process redesign—targeting over $100 million in annualized savings by 2026, while emphasizing robust free cash flow conversion and flexible supply chain adaptation amidst elevated tariffs. Strategic commentary focused on anticipated 2026 recovery in offshore and international unconventional markets, with management projecting demand acceleration for NOV's deepwater, LNG, and production technologies as the industry pivots from North American shale to global offshore production and unconventional gas as incremental supply sources.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Book-to-bill: The ratio of orders received (bookings) to completed sales (billings) in a given period, commonly used to assess demand trends in capital equipment businesses.

FPSO: Floating Production, Storage, and Offloading vessel—an offshore vessel that processes and stores oil or gas before offloading to a tanker or pipeline.

FIDs: Final Investment Decisions—major project approvals by customers, often marking commitment to proceed with production or development projects in the energy industry.

NOVOS: NOV's proprietary multi-machine drilling control and automation platform.
