Earnings Call Transcript

Dow (DOW) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 29, 2026 at 9:20AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, January 29, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chair and Chief Executive Officer — James R. Fitterling
  • Chief Operating Officer — Karen S. Carter
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jeffrey L. Tate
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Andrew Riker

TAKEAWAYS

  • Operating EBITDA -- $741 million, reflecting a sequential decline attributed to lower seasonal demand and typical margin compression across key end markets.
  • Cost Savings Delivery -- More than $6.5 billion in near-term cash support identified, with over half realized in 2025, and specific achievement of over $400 million in annual savings, ahead of the targeted $300 million.
  • Segment Performance: Packaging & Specialty Plastics -- Net sales of $4.7 billion; volume declined 2% year over year due to lower merchant olefin sales in EMEAI, while polyethylene volumes rose both sequentially and year over year.
  • Packaging & Specialty Plastics Operating EBIT -- $215 million, down year over year due to lower integrated margins, but up $16 million sequentially, led by cost savings and higher licensing revenue.
  • Industrial Intermediates & Infrastructure -- Net sales of $2.7 billion, down 9% year over year and 5% sequentially; operating EBIT decreased $285 million year over year and $154 million sequentially due to lower margins, partially offset by cost savings.
  • Performance Materials & Coatings -- Net sales of $1.9 billion, a 6% decrease year over year; segment operating EBIT rose $34 million from the prior year, aided by demand for electronics and mobility applications and ongoing cost actions.
  • Incremental Asset Actions -- Completion of the shutdown of a high-cost upstream propylene oxide unit in Freeport, Texas, removing 20% of North American PO industry capacity, and plans to close three higher-cost assets in Europe.
  • Transform to Outperform Program -- Announced and targeted to deliver at least $2 billion in near-term EBITDA improvements, with approximately two-thirds from productivity gains and one-third from growth, requiring $1.1 billion to $1.5 billion in one-time costs including $600 million to $800 million in severance.
  • Workforce Reduction -- Global reduction of 4,500 roles planned in connection with the Transform to Outperform program, in addition to decreasing third-party roles and resources.
  • Path to Zero Project Update -- Two-year delay confirmed, moving Phase One startup to late 2029; 30% of total CapEx spent, with return expectations of at least 8%-10% and no anticipated material impact to cash or tax incentives.
  • Planned Maintenance and Turnarounds -- Headwinds for next quarter include $125 million from Louisiana cracker maintenance and $75 million from lower equity earnings and licensing activity in Packaging & Specialty Plastics, plus $15 million in Industrial Intermediates & Infrastructure.
  • Operating EBITDA Guidance -- First quarter EBITDA expected at approximately $750 million, an anticipated sequential improvement driven by seasonal uplift, margin expansion, and continued cost reductions, though partially offset by higher turnaround spending and lower equity earnings.
  • Liquidity and Balance Sheet -- Cash and cash equivalents exceeded $3.8 billion at year end; over $14 billion in available liquidity, including a recently renewed revolving credit facility through 2030.
  • Dividend Policy -- Implemented a 50% dividend reduction as part of broader cash and cost support measures targeting financial flexibility and credit quality.
  • Asset Sales and Financing -- Generated $3 billion in proceeds from a 49% equity sale in select US Gulf Coast infrastructure assets, executed $2.4 billion in bond issuances, pushing the next material maturity to 2029.

RISKS

  • Chief Operating Officer Carter said, "the building and construction end markets and durables just really offset [lower fixed costs and planned maintenance];" this continuing weakness exerts downward pricing and demand pressure in Industrial Intermediates & Infrastructure.
  • Management noted that consumer confidence "remains near historic lows, continuing to weigh on overall demand," which may constrain near-term recovery.
  • Chief Financial Officer Tate highlighted that "higher planned turnaround spending will weigh on first-quarter results," limiting the benefit from sequential margin and demand improvements.
  • Several asset shutdowns and workforce reduction actions will generate one-time costs of $1.1 billion to $1.5 billion, including $600 million to $800 million in severance, creating material short-term charges.

SUMMARY

Dow (DOW +2.55%) directly reported sequential and year-over-year declines in key segment sales and earnings, mainly driven by continued polymer price weakness, margin compression, and persistent end-market softness. Management announced the launch of the Transform to Outperform program, targeting $2 billion in near-term EBITDA improvement, entailing substantial cost reductions, workforce resizing, and operational simplification. A two-year delay to the Path to Zero project was confirmed, with Phase One startup now anticipated for late 2029 and expected returns of at least 8%-10% intact. The company disclosed continued focus on asset rationalization, including multiple plant closures, and emphasized recent steps to ensure strong financial flexibility, including a $3 billion asset sale, lower CapEx, bond issuances, and a 50% dividend cut. EBITDA guidance for the first quarter points to a modest sequential rebound, though management explicitly cited elevated turnaround costs as a material headwind.

  • Management highlighted that productivity from cost reduction measures offset some but not all of the negative impacts from margin and volume declines across business units.
  • For Packaging & Specialty Plastics, global polyethylene inventory drawdown and higher January/February announced prices signal an expectation for improved integrated margins in early 2026.
  • Rationalization of European capacity and shutdowns of underperforming upstream assets are expected to generate $200 million in annual EBITDA uplift by 2029, starting in 2026.
  • Workforce reduction of 4,500 roles globally, together with streamlined end-to-end processes enabled by automation and AI, is a core component of the new productivity model.
  • On cash flow, management projects $1 billion of benefit for 2026 from cost savings and Transform to Outperform, supplemented by anticipated proceeds from additional asset sales and working capital efficiency gains.
  • Chief Executive Officer Fitterling stated, "Transform to Outperform will become a central driver of new value creation," underscoring its significance in Dow's near-term and long-term performance plans.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Path to Zero: Dow's long-term decarbonized ethylene and derivatives project in Fort Saskatchewan, Alberta, structured to produce low-carbon products and drive cost-advantaged growth.
  • Transform to Outperform: Comprehensive restructuring and operational transformation aimed at delivering at least $2 billion in EBITDA uplift through productivity improvements, growth, and business process modernization.
  • PO (Propylene Oxide): Key industrial chemical used in manufacturing polyurethanes, with relevance to Dow's asset rationalization in North America and industry capacity dynamics.
  • PASP (Packaging & Specialty Plastics): Dow segment addressing high-value polyethylene and related polymers, with significant emphasis on global volume growth and margin sensitivity to export conditions.
