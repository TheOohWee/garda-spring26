Earnings Call Transcript

Destination XL (DXLG) Q3 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 7, 2026 at 9:42AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, December 4, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Harvey Kanter
  • Incoming Chief Executive Officer (post-merger) — James Fogarty
  • Chief Financial Officer — Peter Stratton

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $101.9 million, down from $107.5 million, driven primarily by a 7.4% decrease in comparable sales, partially offset by new store openings.
  • Gross Margin Rate -- 42.7%, compared to 45.1% last year, with deleverage on occupancy costs representing 210 basis points of the decline, and merchandise margin down 30 basis points due to promotions and tariffs.
  • Tariff Impact -- Third quarter margins were reduced by approximately 60 basis points, with a full-year 2025 margin impact expected to be about $2 million.
  • SG&A Expense Ratio -- 44.7% of sales, up from 44.1% last year, with the advertising-to-sales ratio increasing to 6% from 5.7%, as paid search and social channels showed strong returns.
  • EBITDA -- $(2) million for the quarter compared to $1 million in the prior year period.
  • Inventory -- Down 4.6% year over year; clearance inventory at approximately 10%, consistent with targets and last year.
  • Cash and Short-Term Investments -- $27 million at period end, versus $43 million last year, with the decrease explained by $13.1 million in new store development, and $3.3 million in fourth-quarter 2024 share repurchases.
  • Free Cash Flow (YTD) -- Usage of $20.2 million, compared to $7 million used last year, attributed mainly to lower earnings.
  • Merger Structure -- 100% stock-for-stock with Destination XL Group (DXLG +4.73%) shareholders holding 45% and FullBeauty shareholders holding 55% of the combined company.
  • Combined LTM Net Sales -- Approximately $1.2 billion for the last twelve months ending October 2025.
  • Combined LTM Adjusted EBITDA -- Approximately $45 million, projected to reach $70 million with the realization of $25 million annual run-rate cost synergies targeted by 2027.
  • Term Loan at Closing -- $172 million with maturity in August 2029; interest set at LIBOR plus 750 basis points.
  • Combined Sales Mix -- Expected to be 73% direct-to-consumer and 27% from nearly 300 stores.
  • Customer Database -- Combined base reaching approximately 34 million households.
  • Product Mix -- Expected portfolio split of 54% women’s and 46% men’s categories across private and national brands.
  • Merger Approvals -- Unanimous Board approval from both firms, with voting agreements in place covering about 19.4% of DXLG’s current shares, supporting transaction closure in the first half of fiscal 2026.
  • Management and Governance -- Combined company to be led by James Fogarty as CEO and Peter Stratton as CFO; board to comprise nine directors (four from each firm plus one mutually agreed independent director).

RISKS

  • Comparable sales declined 7.4%, and EBITDA turned negative $(2) million for the third quarter of 2025, reflecting ongoing challenges with discretionary spending and category demand.
  • Gross margin rate dropped by 240 basis points to 42.7%, with deleverage on occupancy costs contributing 210 basis points of decline, and merchandise margins decreasing by only 30 basis points, primarily impacted by promotions and tariffs.
  • Year-over-year cash and short-term investments fell by $16 million, with free cash flow usage of $20.2 million year-to-date due to lower earnings.
  • Peter Stratton stated, “sales were below our expectations,” underlining weaker-than-planned operating performance.

SUMMARY

The announced merger between Destination XL Group (DXLG +4.73%) and FullBeauty creates a specialty retailer with approximately $1.2 billion in trailing twelve-month sales and $45 million in adjusted EBITDA, establishing a leading platform in inclusive sizing apparel. The all-stock transaction gives DXLG shareholders 45% ownership of the new entity, which will assume $172 million in term debt maturing August 2029 and aims for $25 million in annual cost synergies by 2027. Management expects that 73% of the merged company’s sales will be from direct-to-consumer channels, and its nearly 300-store footprint will contribute 27% of sales. The combined database of approximately 34 million households will offer increased scale for marketing, inventory, and digital programs, while product assortment is planned to span approximately 54% women’s and 46% men’s apparel from both private and national brands.

  • Leadership transition will see James Fogarty as Chief Executive Officer and Peter Stratton as Chief Financial Officer, with a nine-member board equally split between the legacy firms and one independent member.
  • Management emphasized the synergistic strengths of both businesses in omnichannel strategy, with cross-selling, private label integration, and expanded assortment considered key performance drivers post-merger.
  • Peter Stratton indicated that a significant portion of the targeted $25 million in annual cost synergies will be actioned within the first twelve months after closing, focusing on efficiencies in cost of goods, logistics, and corporate overhead.
  • Voting support agreements from board members and a major shareholder represent 19.4% of DXLG shares, supporting confidence in deal completion during the first half of fiscal 2026.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for nonrecurring or nonoperating items, commonly used to evaluate a company's core profitability.
  • Comparable Sales (Comps): Year-over-year sales performance comparison for stores or channels open at least one year, measuring organic revenue trends excluding store openings/closures or acquisitions.
  • Private Label: Merchandise branded and sold by the retailer itself rather than distributed under a manufacturer or national brand, often with higher margins.
  • Run Rate Cost Synergies: The anticipated full annualized savings achieved from cost reductions or efficiencies when merger integration initiatives are fully implemented.
