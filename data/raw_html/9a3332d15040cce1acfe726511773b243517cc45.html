XP (XP) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 13, 2026 at 8:58AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Feb. 12, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Thiago Maffra
  • Chief Financial Officer — Victor Mansur
  • Investor Relations Officer — Andre Parize

Takeaways

  • Gross Revenue -- BRL 19.5 billion for 2025, up 8% year over year; second half grew just above 10% compared to second half of 2024.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- BRL 5.2 billion in 2025, a 15% increase year over year; fourth quarter figure was BRL 1.3 billion.
  • Return on Equity (ROE) -- 23.9% for 2025, expanding by 94 basis points over 2024.
  • BIS Ratio -- Ended 2025 at 20.4% after BRL 500 million in dividends and BRL 1.9 billion in share buybacks.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- Increased 18% in 2025, totaling BRL 9.81 for the year; BRL 2.56 in fourth quarter, up 15% year over year and 4% sequentially.
  • Total Client Assets (AUC/AUM/AUA) -- Surpassed BRL 2.1 trillion, rising 22% year over year; supported by approximately 18,000 advisers and nearly 5 million clients.
  • Net New Money -- Achieved BRL 32 billion in fourth quarter 2025 (BRL 20 billion retail, BRL 12 billion corporate/institutional); offset by BRL 3 billion in SMB outflows from the retail platform and BRL 23 billion of retail inflows from individuals.
  • Retail Revenue -- BRL 14.6 billion for the year, up 8%; fourth quarter retail revenue was BRL 3.9 billion, increasing 8% year over year and 4% sequentially.
  • Corporate & Issuer Services Revenue -- BRL 2.7 billion in 2025, up 19% year over year; fourth quarter reached BRL 895 million, a 49% increase year over year and up 23% sequentially, driven by DCM activity.
  • Efficiency Ratio -- 34.7% for the last twelve months, stable compared to 2024; SG&A in 2025 was BRL 6.3 billion and BRL 1.7 billion for fourth quarter, growing 10% year over year and 4% sequentially.
  • Adjusted EBT -- Fourth quarter was BRL 1.5 billion (up 20% year over year, 16% sequentially); full year was BRL 5.5 billion (up 10%), with a 29.6% margin, expanding by 52 basis points year over year.
  • Credit Card TPV -- Grew 11% year over year in fourth quarter to BRL 14.6 billion.
  • Life Insurance Written Premiums -- Up 25% year over year in fourth quarter after product range enhancement.
  • Retirement Plan Client Assets -- Achieved BRL 95 billion in fourth quarter, 17% growth year over year.
  • New Product Revenue -- FX, global investments, digital accounts, and consortium generated BRL 258 million in revenue for the quarter, up 21% year over year, and now represent over BRL 1 billion in annual revenues.
  • Share Buybacks and Dividends -- Returned BRL 2.4 billion to shareholders in 2025; 24 million shares repurchased, about 4% of total outstanding.
  • Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA) -- Totaled BRL 119 billion, growing 13% year over year and 11% sequentially.
  • Service Model Index -- Clients above index target made up 39% of AUC; these clients showed 21% higher revenues and more than double net asset inflows versus others.
  • Client Portfolio Returns -- 39% achieved more than 110% of the SELIC rate, 23% reached between 100%-110%, and 28% attained 80%-100% of SELIC; 90% tracked at or above 90% of SELIC rate.
  • Stablecoin Initiative -- Plans to launch a proprietary, dollar-backed stablecoin in first half of 2026 targeting FX diversification and 24/7 liquidity.
  • Wholesale Bank Market Share -- XP represents roughly 50% of equities, futures, options, and ETF trading in Brazil for relevant markets.
  • Guidance for 2026 -- Management reiterated previously disclosed gross revenue guidance of BRL 22.8 billion to BRL 26.8 billion, implying at least a 17% increase; retail net new money expected to remain stable at BRL 20 billion per quarter near-term.

Risks

  • Customer NPS declined to 65 in the fourth quarter from a baseline above 70, attributed to issues related to Ambipar structured notes and negative news flow concerning Banco Master; management described the impact as temporary.
  • Retail net new money has faced ongoing headwinds from SMB withdrawals, with BRL 3 billion withdrawn by SMBs in the fourth quarter, affecting overall inflows.
  • Management highlighted that the business environment for 2026 remains challenging, stating, "we still face a challenging environment for 2026," despite positive figures posted.

Summary

XP (XP +0.33%) reported that total client assets reached a record BRL 2.1 trillion in 2025, representing 22% growth year over year, and adjusted net income expanded by 15% year over year.

Management confirmed strategic continuity, underscored by a maturing adviser network, proprietary technology deployment, and targeted stablecoin and digital asset launches in 2026. Recent corporate restructuring has enhanced capital efficiency, with significant capital returned to shareholders through buybacks and dividends. While guidance for gross revenue remains ambitious with a 17%-20% growth target, XP acknowledged short-term mixed dynamics in retail net inflows and noted temporary declines in Net Promoter Score stemming from isolated client events.

  • Fourth quarter retail inflows were offset by substantial SMB withdrawals, while corporate and institutional net new money reached BRL 12 billion.
  • Technology and AI investments have enabled efficiency gains and are intended to augment rather than replace advisers, especially for higher-net-worth clients.
  • Segment profitability was bolstered by elevated margins in issuer and corporate services, even as banking activity contributed to a higher tax rate for the quarter.
  • Wholesale Bank activities—particularly DCM and capital markets offerings—drove outsized sequential revenue growth in the closing quarter of 2025.
  • Client portfolio returns tracked closely to or above SELIC rates for 90% of advised clients, with differentiated outcomes for those meeting advisory index targets.
  • Management intends to gradually adjust the CET1 and BIS capital ratios to optimize distribution capacity while remaining above peer averages.
  • The stablecoin and expanded crypto initiatives scheduled for rollout in 2026 reflect an effort to diversify digital offerings and strengthen cross-selling within the XP ecosystem.
  • Executive control realignment and reinforced corporate governance were presented as measures to foster long-term management alignment, though immediate operational changes are minimal.

Industry glossary

  • SELIC rate: The Brazilian central bank's overnight rate, serving as the national benchmark for fixed income returns.
  • BIS Ratio: A capital adequacy measurement defined by the Bank for International Settlements, used as a regulatory benchmark for financial institutions.
  • SMB: Small and medium-sized businesses segment, representing non-individual/retail clients investing or transacting on the XP platform.
  • TPV (Total Payment Volume): The total value of credit card transactions processed within a defined period.
  • FGC: "Fundo Garantidor de Créditos," Brazil's deposit insurance fund protecting individual bank depositors.
  • DCM: Debt Capital Markets, an area focused on the structuring and distribution of corporate debt securities.
  • CET1: Common Equity Tier 1, the highest quality of regulatory bank capital according to Basel standards.
  • RWA: Risk-Weighted Assets, a measure of bank assets weighted by risk for capital adequacy calculations.
  • Net Promoter Score (NPS): A standardized metric for measuring customer satisfaction and loyalty.
  • RIA model: Registered Investment Adviser business model, emphasizing fee-based advisory services instead of transactional commissions.
