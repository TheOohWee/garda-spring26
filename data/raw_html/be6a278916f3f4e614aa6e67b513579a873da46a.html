Coinbase (COIN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 13, 2026 at 8:48AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, February 12, 2026 at 5:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Brian Armstrong
  • Chief Financial Officer — Alesia Haas
  • President and Chief Operating Officer — Emilie Choi

Takeaways

  • Total Revenue -- $7.2 billion for the fiscal year, representing a 9% increase year over year.
  • Subscription and Services Revenue -- $2.8 billion, up 23% year over year and over 5.5x peak 2021 levels.
  • Q4 Revenue -- $1.8 billion, a decrease of 5% quarter over quarter.
  • Q4 Transaction Revenue -- $983 million, down 6% quarter over quarter.
  • Q4 Subscription and Services Revenue -- $727 million, down 3% quarter over quarter.
  • Global Trading Volume and Market Share -- Both doubled year over year, setting new all-time highs.
  • Derivatives Volume -- Achieved all-time highs in Q4, supported by the acquisition of Deribit.
  • Assets on Platform -- Coinbase holds 12% of all global crypto assets, more than the next four competitors combined; platform assets have grown about 3x over the past three years.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (Q4) -- $566 million; Adjusted Net Income -- $178 million.
  • GAAP Net Loss (Q4) -- $667 million, primarily due to a $718 million unrealized crypto investment loss and a $395 million loss on strategic investments, including Circle.
  • Cash and Cash Equivalents (Year-End) -- $11.3 billion, with total available resources of $14.1 billion, including crypto assets held for investment and collateral.
  • Share Repurchases -- $1.7 billion deployed to buy back 8.2 million shares, fully offsetting 2025 dilution; the board approved an additional $2 billion authorization in January.
  • Operating Expenses (Q4) -- $1.5 billion, up 9% quarter over quarter, mainly due to acquisition costs and higher USDC rewards.
  • Employees -- Full-time headcount at year-end was 4,951, up 3% quarter over quarter.
  • Everything Exchange Launch -- Went live in Q4, now offering crypto, equities, commodities, and prediction markets; nearly 10,000 equities tickers to be available this month.
  • Subscription Product Diversification -- 12 products each generate over $100 million in annualized revenue; 6 of those products exceed $250 million each.
  • Stablecoins -- All-time high in USDC stored on Coinbase products aligns with the wider USDC market cap reaching about $75 billion.
  • Board-Approved Priorities for 2026 -- Focus points include scaling Everything Exchange, expanding stablecoin payments use cases, and increasing onchain (DeFi/self-custody) adoption.
  • Prediction Markets -- Rolled out across 100% of customers with positive early user engagement; partnership with Kalshi is not exclusive and in-house development remains possible.
  • Q1 2026 Outlook -- Through February 10, approximately $420 million in transaction revenue was generated; subscription and services revenue projected at $550 million to $630 million.
  • Expense Guidance (Q1 2026) -- Technology, development, G&A, and sales and marketing expected to be flat to down quarter over quarter, with specified ranges provided for each.
  • Native Unit Inflows -- Q4 marked the ninth consecutive quarter of net inflows onto the platform, indicating customer engagement despite market volatility.
  • Customer Trading Behavior -- Retail clients demonstrated net buying activity on the platform every week entering the new year, with management noting ongoing "HODL-ing" behavior during market dips.

Risks

  • CFO Haas reported, "On a GAAP basis, we reported a net loss of $667 million, primarily driven by a $718 million unrealized loss on our crypto investment portfolio and a $395 million loss on strategic investments, which includes our investment in Circle."
  • Total operating expenses increased 9% quarter over quarter, with acquisition costs and higher USDC rewards driving the year-end operating expense to $1.5 billion, indicating margin pressure.
  • Guidance for Q1 subscription and services revenue ($550 million to $630 million) reflects "the lower average crypto price environment we are in, lower interest rates and lower staking protocol rewards rates compared to the fourth quarter."
  • CFO Haas identified that Q4 experienced "quarter-over-quarter softer market conditions," with the crypto market cap down 11% quarter over quarter.

Summary

Coinbase Global (COIN 0.72%) executed a major strategic pivot by accelerating its diversified multi-asset Everything Exchange platform, bringing cryptocurrencies, equities, commodities, and prediction markets onto a unified marketplace and achieving all-time highs in trading volume, derivatives activity, and user engagement for new products. Management highlighted surging stablecoin adoption and noted that USDC stored on Coinbase reached a record, contributing to subscription and services revenue growth. The company maintained positive adjusted EBITDA for a twelfth consecutive quarter but reported a significant GAAP net loss due to large unrealized losses on crypto investments and strategic holdings. Share repurchases of $1.7 billion offset share-based compensation dilution, with an additional $2 billion buyback authorization approved. Management outlined Q1 2026 guidance that anticipates lower subscription-related revenue and cautioned against linear extrapolation due to increased market volatility.

  • President Choi confirmed 10 completed acquisitions in 2025, including Deribit and Echo, directly supporting acceleration of the product road map and Everything Exchange expansion.
  • CEO Armstrong stated, "we store 12% of all crypto in the world, more than the next four competitors combined," framing Coinbase’s institutional trust advantage.
  • Base, Coinbase’s Layer 2 chain, achieved new all-time transaction highs as AI agents adopted stablecoin wallets, with strong development momentum and future token exploration mentioned by management.
  • Management noted the successful rollout of prediction markets, especially increased usage during high-visibility events, with additional features and sports integrations planned.
  • CFO Haas clarified that "Q4 marked our ninth consecutive quarter of native unit inflows. This is," supporting the platform’s ongoing asset accumulation flywheel.
  • In Q4, retail users on the platform were net buyers during market declines, indicating resilient trading activity and continued engagement in volatile environments.

Industry glossary

  • Everything Exchange: Coinbase’s cross-asset trading platform offering access to crypto, equities, commodities, and prediction markets on a single venue.
  • Base: Coinbase’s proprietary Ethereum Layer 2 blockchain, designed to enable faster, lower-cost onchain transactions and host applications such as AI-driven agents and stablecoin payments.
  • USDC: USD Coin, a regulated U.S. dollar-pegged stablecoin issued in partnership with Circle, widely used in digital asset trading and payments.
  • Native Unit Inflows: Net increase in absolute units of crypto assets deposited or held on the Coinbase platform, regardless of market price fluctuations.
  • Sequencer Fees: Transaction processing fees earned by Coinbase from operating the Base Layer 2 sequencer, recorded as transaction revenue rather than subscription revenue.
  • HODL: Industry slang for holding crypto assets through periods of market volatility without selling.
  • Deribit: A derivatives exchange acquired by Coinbase, enhancing its offerings in crypto options and futures trading.

We see that the more products people connect into those assets, the more sticky they are. And we use the monetization from that to really complete the flywheel and we invest back in being the more trusted brand and adding more products.

And so as we've added in some of these asset classes like equities and prediction markets and commodities into the Everything Exchange, the first step is it just makes the product more valuable for our existing users, but we're also seeing it help attract more traditional investors who want to come in and onboard and just have an easiest place to trade every asset class in one spot, maybe get better rewards on their credit card, maybe get a better rate lending out their money. Anil Gupta: Next question is from Devin Ryan at Citizens. Stablecoin adoption is a 2026 priority, but we've seen market cap flatline for the last couple of months. Why has that been? And what gives you confidence around growth in 2026? And can you give any color around incremental adoption trends?

Alesia Haas: I'll start here, Brian, if you want to add on. So I think there's 2 things that are happening. One is we've seen risk appetite be relatively range bound. And when you think about stablecoins, first and foremost, product market fit was as a trading pair to enable global traders to move money across the exchange ecosystem. They used it against the longer tail of assets. We've seen a shift now where there's not as much risk appetite for those longer tail. And so we've seen speculation activity come down a little bit. And as a result, stablecoin market cap has not been expanding because there was no risk and leverage expansion.

The second thing that we see is higher velocity of stablecoin payments, settlements, remittances. So we've seen more transaction volume, but not necessarily a higher market cap as a result of that. So we're monetizing stablecoins in incremental and new ways. I think we still have confidence and optimism for 2026 because we are more deeply embedding stablecoins in our products and services. What we've demonstrated is that we have been a key driver of USDC's market cap growth and a key driver of our growth in assets on our platform due to our ability to embed and create differentiated experience with USDC and our products and services.

And so we're excited about our ability to continue to do so and to more deeply create value through the payments priorities that Brian articulated as our second growth area and through just the growth of the Everything Exchange, where we believe that using USDC on our platform will become a great experience for our users.

Brian Armstrong: Yes. I guess the only thing I'd add is that one of the things that gives me confidence about continued growth is just the GENIUS Act passing in the U.S. And we saw, I think, 150 companies in the 3 months following that piece of legislation going into law that came out and announced stablecoin integrations. And it's just -- it's faster, it's cheaper, it's more global. There's no company in the world that wants to pay more money for moving their money, right? So I think that, that's an incredible tailwind to the continued adoption of stablecoins. And in particular, it's important that these stablecoins preserve the ability to have rewards programs.
