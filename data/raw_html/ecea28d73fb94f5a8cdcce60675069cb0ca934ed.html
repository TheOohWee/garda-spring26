Maximus (MMS) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 10:15AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 5, 2026 at 9:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Bruce Caswell
  • Chief Financial Officer — David Mutryn

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $1.35 billion, reflecting a 4.1% decline, of which roughly 1.5% is attributable to divestitures in the outside the U.S. segment; most of the remainder is organic movement.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin -- 12.7%, compared to 11.2% for the prior year period.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $1.85 versus $1.61 in the prior year period.
  • US Federal Services Segment Revenue -- $787 million, an increase of 0.8%, all of which is organic growth.
  • US Federal Services Segment Operating Margin -- 16.5%, up from 12.7% year over year, driven by continuing adoption of technology initiatives.
  • US Services Segment Revenue -- $415 million, down from $452 million in the prior year period.
  • US Services Segment Operating Margin -- 7.1%, a decline from 9% year over year.
  • Outside the U.S. Segment Revenue -- $143 million, compared to $170 million; $19 million of the decline was from divestitures, with the remainder driven by lower program volumes, partially offset by currency benefit.
  • Outside the U.S. Segment Operating Result -- Loss of $1.4 million versus a profit of $8.1 million in the prior year period.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio -- Trailing-twelve-month ratio of approximately 0.5x; quarterly ratio of 0.2x due to light award activity.
  • Sales Pipeline -- $59.1 billion as of December 31, up from $51.3 billion at September 30; 59% of the pipeline is new work, including Medicaid and SNAP opportunities.
  • Proposals Pending and in Preparation -- $6.2 billion in total contract value, a 55% increase over the previous year’s figure of $4 billion.
  • Cash Flow -- Net outflow from operating activities of $244 million and free cash flow outflow of $251 million, reflecting seasonality and temporary collection delays in US Federal Services.
  • Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) -- Increased to 78 days; expected to remain elevated at March 31 and normalize in the second half of the fiscal year.
  • Total Debt and Net Leverage Ratio -- Total debt at $1.58 billion; net total leverage of 1.8x, up from 1.5x as of September 30 due to short-term borrowing needs.
  • Divestiture -- Completed divestiture of the child support business in US Services, which comprised approximately $25 million annual revenue and generated a $9 million gain.
  • Fiscal 2026 Revenue Guidance -- Narrowed range to $5.2 billion to $5.35 billion, with both ends reduced by $25 million (divestiture impact) and the top end further reduced by $50 million for new work delays.
  • Fiscal 2026 Adjusted EBITDA Margin Guidance -- Raised to approximately 14%, a 30-basis-point improvement.
  • Fiscal 2026 Adjusted EPS Guidance -- Increased by $0.10 to a range of $8.05 to $8.35 per share.
  • Fiscal 2026 Segment Margin Guidance -- US Federal Services margin expected in the 16.5%-17% range; US Services segment margin in the 10.5%-11% range; outside the U.S. segment projected at 13%.
  • Technology and AI Initiatives -- Multiple examples cited, including AI-powered document processing, accuracy assistant for SNAP payment error rate reduction, and implementations resulting in measurable cost reduction and throughput improvements.
  • GSA Blanket Purchase Agreement (BPA) -- Maximus (MMS +0.12%) was selected as the single awardee for the GSA’s GXCC services transformation BPA, pending the protest period, with no ceiling amount and a performance period up to five years.
  • Medicaid and SNAP Regulatory Developments -- Upcoming requirements for semiannual Medicaid eligibility determinations and community engagement will create new program demands beginning January 1, 2027; SNAP incentive for states to keep error rates below 6% and shift of administrative funding responsibilities to states in 2027.
  • Forbes Employer Recognition -- Maximus won a spot on the Forbes list of America’s Best Employers for 2026 for a second consecutive year.

RISKS

  • Cash flow was negatively impacted by "expected seasonality around the timing of cash payments in the business, as well as temporary delays of collections in our US Federal Services segment," including lingering effects from the government shutdown.
  • "The lower TTM book-to-bill ratio was impacted by very light award activity in our just completed first quarter," suggesting softness in near-term contract wins.
  • The outside the U.S. segment reported a $1.4 million operating loss, with revenue decrease primarily affected by divestitures and lower program volumes.

SUMMARY

The company raised full-year earnings guidance and narrowed revenue expectations, citing solid first-quarter results and portfolio visibility. Management confirmed that nearly all remaining revenue guidance is supported by existing work, with minimal new contract assumptions and a building pipeline for future growth. Technology initiatives, and specifically new AI-powered solutions, generated both expanded government sector opportunities and measurable productivity gains, including new contracts and competitive wins. U.S. Federal Services maintained positive organic growth and segment margin improvements, while U.S. Services and outside the U.S. segments cited lower volumes and divestiture impacts, though management expects U.S. Services to return to organic growth by the fourth quarter.

  • The CFO indicated, "Cash used in operating activities was a net outflow of $244 million, and free cash flow was a net outflow of $251 million," with DSO temporarily elevated to 78 days.
  • New regulatory requirements for Medicaid and SNAP programs are expected to drive additional state engagement and transactional opportunities over the next two years.
  • The GSA’s blanket purchase award introduces significant long-term federal sector potential, as the BPA has no maximum orders or ceiling and includes advanced cloud, AI, and analytics platform deployments.
  • Divestiture decisions reflect continued, disciplined focus on portfolio profitability and capital deployment to higher-value government service opportunities.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: A metric indicating the ratio of contracts won (booked) to revenue recognized (billed) over a defined period, used to assess business momentum and future revenue visibility.
  • Blanket Purchase Agreement (BPA): A long-term government contract with flexible ordering provisions, allowing agencies to acquire services or products on demand over several years without a maximum ceiling.
  • GXCC (Government Experience Contact Center): A General Services Administration initiative for modernizing and transforming government agency contact centers utilizing advanced technology platforms.
  • DSO (Days Sales Outstanding): The average number of days it takes a company to collect payment after a sale has been made, used as a measure of cash collection efficiency.
  • Accuracy Assistant: Maximus’s proprietary AI-powered tool to help states identify and prevent errors in SNAP payment processing through real-time analytics and data validation.
  • Community Engagement Requirements: Regulatory mandates for Medicaid expansion populations to meet conditions related to work, education, or volunteering, impacting program administration and contract demands.
