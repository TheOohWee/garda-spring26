SiriusPoint (SPNT) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 9:57AM EST

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 19, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Scott Egan
  • Chief Financial Officer — James J. McKinney
  • Chief Underwriting Officer — Liam Blackledge

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue Growth -- Top-line grew 16% in 2025, demonstrating continued expansion across business lines.
  • Book Value Per Share -- Diluted book value per share increased 28% in 2025, including a $1.70 per share rise in the quarter from the Armada sale.
  • Operating Earnings Per Share -- Operating earnings per share grew 49% year over year in 2025.
  • Leverage Ratio -- Leverage projected to fall to 23% by February, reflecting all-time low debt levels after preference share redemption.
  • Return on Equity -- Full-year operating return on equity reached 16.2%, surpassing the company's 12%-15% cycle target; GAAP ROE for the fourth quarter was 44.9% due to Armada sale gain.
  • Combined Ratio -- Core combined ratio was 92.9% in the quarter, with core attritional combined ratio for the year improving 1.5 points to 91.6%.
  • Gross Written Premium (GWP) -- GWP grew 18% in the quarter and 16% for the year, with accident and health premiums rising 23% to nearly $1 billion, now representing 27% of business mix and 43% of Insurance & Services segment GWP.
  • Net Income -- Net income increased 141% to $444 million for the year, with $240 million generated in the quarter.
  • Insurance & Services Segment -- Segment GWP increased 23% in the quarter and 20% for the year, reaching $2.3 billion full-year; fourth-quarter combined ratio was 93.3%, and full-year 91.7% reflects run-rate going into 2026.
  • Reinsurance Segment -- Quarterly GWP up 9% to $341 million; full-year GWP up 3%, with combined ratio for the quarter improving to 92.1% and full-year at 91.8%.
  • Service Fee Income and EBITDA -- IMG expected to generate over $30 million fee income and more than $35 million EBITDA in 2026; two acquisitions (Assist America, World Nomads) each projected to add $4 million-$5 million of annual EBITDA post-integration beginning in 2026 and ramping in 2027.
  • Capital Management -- Announced $100 million share buyback (over 4% of outstanding shares) to be executed over the next twelve months, following receipt of proceeds from Armada and Acadian sales totaling $390 million.
  • BSCR Ratio -- Fourth-quarter Bermuda Solvency Capital Requirement ratio rose to 247%; pro forma post-preferred redemption stands at 232%.
  • Investment Portfolio -- Year-end investment income was $275 million; 81% of portfolio in fixed income (98% investment grade, average rating AA-, duration 3.2 years); reinvested $500 million this quarter at yields above 4%.
  • Underwriting Volatility Reduction -- New property aggregate reinsurance program for 2026 provides $100 million annual limit, with cover attaching at $90 million of annual losses, and purchased at a lower cost but higher total limit than prior year.
  • Reserving Track Record -- Nineteen consecutive quarters of favorable prior-year development, with fourth quarter core favorable development of $15 million (consolidated $22 million).
  • Attritional Loss Ratio -- Improved to 60.8% for Insurance & Services segment in 2025 due to enhanced risk selection and portfolio mix management; guidance for potential +/- 0.5 point variation in 2026 depending on business mix.
  • Preference Share Redemption -- Company to redeem $200 million of 8% preference shares, reducing leverage and supporting capital flexibility.

Summary

SiriusPoint (SPNT 0.91%) reported 16% annual revenue growth and a 141% rise in net income, attributed partly to the sale of Armada and robust accident and health premium growth, which now contributes almost $1 billion in annual written premium. Management disclosed two strategic acquisitions in travel insurance and assistance services, expected to add $8 million-$10 million in annual EBITDA post-integration. The Board approved a $100 million share repurchase program representing over 4% of current shares outstanding, supported by a strong pro forma BSCR ratio and record low leverage. Insurance & Services segment growth outpaced Reinsurance, marking a structural mix shift that management confirmed aligns with its strategic focus on lower-volatility lines and disciplines capital deployment.

  • CEO Egan cited that 60% of 2025 growth came from lines less correlated to P&C pricing cycles, with accident and health "the largest contributor."
  • Operating return on equity improved for a third consecutive year, with Egan noting it "is the highest the company has achieved."
  • Management reaffirmed its focus on underwriting performance above top-line growth for 2026, referencing early-year performance as "in line with our planning assumptions."
  • Growth in casualty and surety is expected to continue, with CFO McKinney stating, "more the early innings of kind of the total build out from a premium perspective on the surety side."
  • Property catastrophe exposure remains limited at only 5% of the business, and new retrocession structures are designed to further lower earnings volatility.

Industry glossary

  • BSCR Ratio: Bermuda Solvency Capital Requirement ratio, a regulatory capital adequacy metric for Bermuda insurers and reinsurers.
  • MGA: Managing General Agent — a specialized insurance intermediary or agency with delegated underwriting authority from insurers.
  • OUE: Other Underwriting Expense ratio — non-acquisition underwriting costs expressed as a percentage of premium.
  • Attritional Loss Ratio: The percentage of incurred losses, excluding catastrophes and prior period development, assessed against earned premium for core underwriting profitability.
