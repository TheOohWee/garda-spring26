Western Alliance (WAL) Q1 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 11:24AM EST

Date

Monday, April 21, 2025 at 12 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Ken Vecchione
  • Chief Financial Officer — Dale Gibbons
  • Chief Banking Officer, Regional Banking — Tim Bruckner

Takeaways

  • Pre-Provision Net Revenue -- $278 million, marking a $31 million or 12% increase year over year driven chiefly by higher net interest income.
  • Net Interest Income -- $651 million, up 9% year over year and down $16 million sequentially, primarily due to two fewer calendar days in the quarter.
  • Net Interest Margin (NIM) -- 3.47%, down just 1 basis point from the prior quarter, with adjusted NIM including deposit costs rising 17 basis points to 2.75%.
  • Deposit Growth -- $3 billion in the quarter, led by gains in noninterest-bearing deposits, savings, and money market balances.
  • Loan Growth -- Held for investment loans up $1.1 billion sequentially; C&I loans now comprise 44% of the portfolio, an increase from 39% last year.
  • Provision Expense -- $31 million, significantly below Q4's $60 million, supporting coverage for $26 million in net charge-offs and boosting CRE reserves.
  • Net Charge-Offs -- $26 million for the quarter, equivalent to 20 basis points of average loans, and aligning with management's full-year view.
  • Nonaccrual Loans -- Decreased by $25 million sequentially to $451 million, now 82 basis points as a percentage of funded held-for-investment loans.
  • Asset Quality -- Net charge-offs declined 5 basis points compared to the prior quarter; criticized assets increased by $254 million, largely from rises in special mention and classified loans.
  • Total Assets -- Grew by $2.1 billion to reach $83 billion, with a $1.4 billion reduction in borrowings attributed to seasonal mortgage warehouse deposit rebound.
  • Total Equity -- Increased $508 million, including $293 million proceeds from a REIT preferred equity issuance, which raised the Tier 1 leverage ratio from 8.1% to 8.6%.
  • Tangible Book Value Per Share -- Rose by $1.83 from year-end to $54.10, a 14% increase year over year aided by organic profitability and improved AOCI position.
  • Adjusted Efficiency Ratio -- 56%, an improvement from 57% in the comparable period last year.
  • Deposit Growth Segment Detail -- HOA (Homeowner Association deposits) up $900 million, surpassing $10 billion for the first time; Corporate Trust deposits increased by nearly $300 million.
  • CET1 Capital Ratio -- Decreased 13 basis points sequentially to 11.1% due to strong loan growth, remaining above management's target floor.
  • Asset Composition -- Mortgage warehouse loans total $8 billion with very short duration, and residential portfolio of $14 billion skewed toward high FICO, low LTV loans; peer comparison provided to contextualize reserve levels.
  • 2025 Full-Year Guidance -- $5 billion loan growth and $8 billion deposit growth expected, with net interest income and noninterest income each projected to rise 6%-8%.
  • Noninterest Expense Outlook -- Expected between flat and a 5% decline for the year, benefiting from anticipated lower ECR costs with two rate cuts assumed.
  • Full-Year ECR Cost Estimate -- Updated range of $485 million to $535 million, with Q2 ECR costs expected at $140 million to $150 million.
  • Asset Quality Guidance -- Management expects full-year net charge-offs to remain near 20 basis points of average loans.
  • Effective Tax Rate -- Projected to be approximately 20% for 2025.

Summary

Western Alliance Bancorporation (WAL 0.70%) reported 12% year-over-year growth in pre-provision net revenue and a 9% net interest income increase, establishing solid profitability despite a sequential net interest income reduction linked to fewer calendar days. Management issued unchanged full-year guidance, projecting $5 billion in loan growth and $8 billion in deposit increases for 2025 while forecasting net interest income and noninterest income to rise 6%-8%, supported by healthy pipelines and continued focus on asset quality. Executives highlighted a deliberate capital strategy, maintaining the CET1 ratio above 11% and utilizing a REIT-based preferred equity raise to enhance Tier 1 leverage and secure tax-efficient funding. The bank's adjusted efficiency ratio improved to 56%, and tangible book value per share rose 14% year over year. Management expects full-year ECR costs of $485 million to $535 million, with net charge-offs to persist around 20 basis points. Executives reaffirmed stable loan performance, minimal international exposure, and a resilience supported by diversification, while dismissing M&A or share repurchases in favor of capital deployment for organic expansion.

  • Ken Vecchione said, "we expect Q2 loan growth to actually top Q1," referencing momentum in national homebuilder, warehouse lending, and lender finance groups.
  • Dale Gibbons said the reserve methodology "is conservatively weighted to economic scenarios more pessimistic than economists are forecasting," noting the allowance for credit losses model considers peak unemployment above 6% and significant CRE valuation stress.
  • Executives confirmed REIT-based preferred equity issuance produces "lower-cost after taxes" funding due to tax-deductible dividends, unlike a holding company issuance.
  • Deposits from Homeowner Associations ("HOA") and Corporate Trust continued to post significant inflows, with HOA surpassing $10 billion in deposits and Corporate Trust deposits now exceeding $800 million.
  • Treasury management service fee income and service charges showed a "significant" year-over-year increase, attributed to expanded commercial client product penetration.
  • New loan production yields declined by 3 basis points from the portfolio average, while deposit costs decreased by 29 basis points within the same period, per management's statement.
  • N/A projects "sequential" net interest income and margin growth across upcoming quarters, driven by loan growth and deposit cost optimization.

Industry glossary

  • HOA: Homeowner Association deposit balances managed as a specialized vertical within the institution, serving residential community associations.
  • ECR: Earnings Credit Rate; the rate paid on commercial deposit balances used to offset service fees, central to commercial account cost management.
  • MSR: Mortgage Servicing Right; contractual right to service a mortgage in exchange for a fee, with fair value changes impacting noninterest income.
  • CRE: Commercial Real Estate; loan or asset category backed by income-producing property, often a focus of risk assessment during economic cycles.
  • AOCI: Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income; a component of equity reflecting unrealized gains and losses on certain balance sheet items, subject to changes with interest rates.
  • PPNR: Pre-Provision Net Revenue; total revenue less noninterest expenses, excluding loss provisions, indicating core bank profitability.
  • CET1: Common Equity Tier 1 capital, a key regulatory capital measure representing the core equity capital held by banks to withstand losses.
