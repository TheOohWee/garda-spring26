Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $87.1 million for the quarter, reflecting 36% year-over-year growth after adjusting for the Maxim Effort divestiture.
  • Full-year revenue -- $290.1 million, with normalized growth of 36% after adjusting for prior-year Maxim Effort contributions.
  • Gross margin (Q4) -- 82%, an increase of 530 basis points, with over 300 basis points attributed to improvement in the core Performance TV (PTV) business.
  • Full-year gross margin -- 77%, up 560 basis points, with more than 300 basis points from operating improvements and the balance from the Maxim Effort divestiture.
  • Active PTV customers -- 3,632 over the trailing twelve months, representing 63% growth.
  • Customer expansion rate -- "well north of 115%," indicating spend growth among retained customers.
  • Operating expenses (Q4) -- $50.9 million, resulting from expanded sales and marketing investments.
  • Full-year operating expenses -- $200 million.
  • Net income (Q4) -- $34.5 million, GAAP EPS of 47¢.
  • Full-year net loss -- $6.4 million, or GAAP loss per share of 13¢, primarily driven by a one-time $23 million charge related to the IPO and convertible notes settlement.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (Q4) -- $28.1 million, up 36% from the prior year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin (Q4) -- 32.3%, compared to 29.6% in the previous year.
  • Full-year adjusted EBITDA -- $68 million, versus $38.8 million in 2024.
  • Full-year adjusted EBITDA margin -- 23.4%, increasing from 17.2% the previous year.
  • Cash and equivalents -- $210 million at quarter-end, with zero borrowings outstanding.
  • Shares outstanding -- 73.9 million at the close of the quarter.
  • Q1 2026 revenue guidance -- $71.3 million to $73.3 million, midpoint growth of 22.3% normalized for Maxim Effort.
  • Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- $13 million to $14 million.
  • Full-year 2026 revenue guidance -- $345 million to $355 million, midpoint growth of 22.9% normalized for Maxim Effort.
  • Full-year 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- $94.6 million to $99.6 million.
  • AI adoption -- Quick Frame AI tool attracted 5,000 users within its first month; new AI tools are scheduled for release through 2026.
  • Sales investment -- 53 new hires in sales and marketing during the quarter, with a stated focus on SMB and mid-market customer acquisition.
  • Gross margin target -- Long-term gross margin guidance reiterated at 75%-80% by management.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin target -- Long-term target confirmed in the 35%-40% range.
  • Customer profile -- Over 95% of customers launched their first TV commercial using the MNTN platform.
  • Performance attribution & measurement -- Platform leverages an identity graph covering nearly all U.S. households and cross-device measurement; integrates with approximately 11 third-party attribution platforms for reporting.
  • Inventory access & Magnite partnership -- MNTN secures inventory through both direct network deals and programmatic technology partners, notably Magnite; relationship provides access to unique ad units, including paused ads and live sports inventory.
  • Paused ad units -- Early deployment underway, with management stating, "the jury is still a little bit out" regarding relative performance, as volume data remains limited.

Summary

MNTN (MNTN 4.20%) reported 63% customer growth and double-digit revenue expansion, led by rapid adoption of its Performance TV software among small and midsized businesses. Management emphasized full automation and frequent AI-driven product releases as central to its long-term growth playbook. The company highlighted strong cash reserves, a debt-free balance sheet, and robust customer budget retention, while also reiterating long-term profitability targets for gross margin and adjusted EBITDA. Market access continues to expand through both direct partnerships and programmatic integrations, introducing new premium inventory formats and AI-powered campaign capabilities.

  • Management described SMB-oriented product design as a permanent competitive moat, contrasting with enterprise-focused competitors.
  • Hires in sales development are explicitly "focused on the SMB market," with all driving demos as a precursor to onboarding.
  • MNTN's identity graph enables detailed cross-device attribution, with performance data concurrently delivered to external attribution systems and internal analytics.
  • Gross margin improvement was attributed to both the Maxim Effort divestiture and infrastructure cost reductions, with future upside linked to ongoing hosting and tech initiatives.
  • The Magnite partnership was called "a really strong partnership," facilitating early access to new ad units across content verticals such as live sports and paused ad inventory.
  • Customer "expansion rate" significantly above 115% indicates retained clients’ rising budgets, distinct from net new logo growth.
  • Quick Frame AI and other model orchestration technologies are core to creative production and advertising efficiency, with proprietary tools orchestrating multiple generative AI models for professional video workflows.

Industry glossary

  • PTV (Performance TV): Software-driven, outcome-based advertising on connected TV (CTV), focused on measurable sales or conversions rather than impressions.
  • SMB: Small and medium-sized business segment, often under-served by traditional TV or agency models and a primary audience for self-service ad tools.
  • Identity graph: A technical dataset mapping device, household, and individual identifiers across platforms to enable targeted advertising and accurate attribution.
  • Paused ad units: Connected TV advertisements displayed when a user pauses streaming content, providing a new engagement window distinct from in-stream ads.
