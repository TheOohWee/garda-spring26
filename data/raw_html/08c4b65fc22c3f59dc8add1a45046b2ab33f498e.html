Dynatrace (DT) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 9, 2026 at 9:12AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, February 9, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Rick McConnell
  • Chief Financial Officer — James Benson

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) -- Dynatrace (DT 4.32%) reported $1.97 billion in ARR, a 16% increase, with stabilization of growth for three consecutive quarters.
  • Net New ARR -- $75 million (FX-adjusted), up 11%, maintaining three consecutive quarters of double-digit expansion.
  • Log Management Consumption -- Surpassed $100 million in annualized usage, with over 100% year-over-year growth; logs remain the fastest-growing product category.
  • New Logo Additions -- 164 customers added, with average ARR per new logo exceeding $160,000 on a trailing twelve-month basis.
  • Average Land Size per New Logo -- Exceeded $200,000, driving new logo ARR above 21% of a strong third quarter in fiscal 2025.
  • Average ARR per Customer -- Nearly $500,000, as expansion within the existing customer base persists, with stated potential to reach $1 million per customer medium-to-long term.
  • Gross Retention Rate -- Remained in the mid-90% range, reflecting the platform’s criticality to operations.
  • Net Retention Rate (NRR) -- Reached 111% on a trailing twelve-month basis, matching the prior two quarters.
  • Total Revenue -- $515 million, up 16%, with subscription revenue at $493 million, also up 16%.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin -- 30%, nearly 100 basis points above the top end of guidance.
  • Non-GAAP Net Income -- $135 million or $0.44 per diluted share, exceeding the high end of guidance by $0.02 per share.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $27 million for the quarter; $463 million on a trailing twelve-month basis, equating to 24% of revenue (30% pre-tax free cash flow margin).
  • Share Repurchase Activity -- $160 million used to repurchase 3.5 million shares at an average price of just over $45 per share; $500 million program substantially completed.
  • New Share Repurchase Authorization -- Board authorized a $1 billion program, double the previous authorization, indicating management’s confidence and view on share valuation.
  • ARR Guidance Raised -- Full-year ARR growth outlook increased by 125 basis points to a range of 15.5%-16%, with guidance pointing to continued double-digit net new ARR growth.
  • Revenue Guidance Raised -- Full-year total and subscription revenue growth outlook increased by 75 basis points at the midpoint to 16% growth.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Income and EPS Guidance Increased -- Operating income outlook raised by $9 million and free cash flow by $13 million; non-GAAP EPS guidance now $1.67-$1.69 per diluted share on an expected diluted share count of 304 million.
  • Platform Innovation -- Dynatrace Intelligence announced as the industry’s first agentic operation system, combining deterministic and agentic AI, embedded for all customers and expected to drive platform adoption and agentic execution-based monetization.
  • Strategic Partnerships and Integrations -- Expanded technical engagements with major hyperscalers, including integration with Amazon Bedrock agent core, embedded support for Azure’s SRE agent, and participation in launching GCP Gemini Enterprise features.
  • Acquisition of DevCycle -- Recently acquired DevCycle to enable progressive delivery for AI-native application feature management, extending reach to developers and platform teams.

SUMMARY

Management highlighted stabilization and double-digit expansion in both the core ARR and net new ARR metrics, with prioritization of ARR as the central driver for growth assessment and guidance. The logs business, now exceeding $100 million annualized consumption and growing over 100%, emerged as the fastest-growing segment and a key accelerant for future ARR. Dynatrace Intelligence, the new agentic AI-powered operations system, was launched and made available to all users, with management outlining two explicit monetization paths tied directly to increased workload consumption and agentic execution. The company initiated a new $1 billion share repurchase authorization and completed its previous $500 million program, signaling substantial capital return and a stated belief in Dynatrace’s undervaluation. The EPS, operating income, and revenue guidance were all raised with quantified increments, and management described durable pipeline visibility and strong close rates in large, end-to-end observability platform deals, supporting an elevated growth outlook.

  • Management expects Dynatrace Intelligence to contribute to revenue through both broader platform usage and usage-based agentic AI workflows, identifying these as pivotal for future performance.
  • Board- and C-level commentary labeled the $1 billion share buyback as the company’s largest, with the explicit message that "our shares are undervalued" and an intention to be "active buyers in the market at current levels."
  • New logo mix is targeted to remain near one-third of ARR, with two-thirds continuing to come from customer base expansion, and company leaders emphasized that significant expansion capacity remains within the installed base.
  • Guidance implies a continuation of double-digit net new ARR growth trends into the next quarter, backed by management’s statements about "very good line of sight" and an "incredibly robust" pipeline.
  • Active engagement with hyperscalers and recent partner integrations are framed as central strategic elements expected to drive enterprise deal volume and position Dynatrace as a consolidator of fragmented market tools.
  • Average ARR per new enterprise logo exceeded $200,000, and five of the quarter’s new logos contributed over $1 million ARR each, illustrating the scale dynamics in the go-to-market motion.
  • "consumption continues to grow north of 20%," outpacing ARR growth, indicating healthy underlying usage momentum across existing deployments.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): The annualized value of all active subscription contracts, a leading driver of growth evaluation in software companies.
  • NRR (Net Retention Rate): The percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers, including upsells, over a set period, indicating expansion and churn dynamics.
  • DPS (Dynatrace Platform Subscription): Dynatrace’s licensing model enabling unified access and consumption across its observability platform.
  • Agentic AI: AI systems capable of autonomous decision-making and action-taking within enterprise software environments.
  • Grail: Dynatrace’s proprietary real-time data lakehouse and analytics engine, built for exabyte-scale observability and security data management.
  • SmartScape: Dynatrace’s real-time dependency graph that dynamically maps technology stacks, providing topological context for AI-powered analytics.
  • DevCycle: A feature management platform acquired by Dynatrace, aimed at enabling progressive delivery for cloud-native and AI-native applications.
  • GSIs (Global System Integrators): Large consulting and IT service providers engaging in strategic software deployment partnerships.
