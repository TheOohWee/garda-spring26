Earnings Call Transcript

Openlane (OPLN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 18, 2026 at 9:52AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Feb. 18, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Peter Kelly
  • Chief Financial Officer — Bradley Herring
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — William Wright

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated revenue -- $494 million in Q4, representing 9% year-over-year growth, despite the 2024 divestiture of the keys business.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $76 million for the quarter, up 5% year over year.
  • Dealer-to-dealer volume (U.S.) -- Over 20% year-over-year unit growth in Q4, outpacing both the industry and physical dealer auction volume trends, with OPENLANE's share gaining relative to AuctionNet’s reported 4% industry growth.
  • Commercial vehicle volume -- Q4 volumes declined 2%, but leadership confirmed, "we saw commercial volumes inflect in December," pointing to expected growth in Q1 2026.
  • Gross merchandise value (GMV) -- $7.1 billion for Q4, up 8% year over year; $29 billion for the full year, an increase of 6%.
  • Marketplace adjusted EBITDA -- $32 million in Q4 (8.2% margin), with margins 60 basis points lower year over year due to revenue mix and increased compensation expense driven by performance.
  • Finance segment receivables -- Average outstanding managed receivables reached $2.5 billion in Q4, up 9% year over year, supported by a 4% rise in average vehicle value and a 2% increase in transaction count.
  • Finance segment net yield -- 13.2% in Q4, down 50 basis points year over year, mainly due to a 90 basis point drop in transaction fee yields from higher loan values.
  • Loan loss provision -- 1.6% in Q4, 24 basis points lower than last year and stable sequentially; management maintains a 1.5%-2% target loss rate range.
  • Adjusted free cash flow conversion -- 89% reported for 2025 due to year-end working capital timing, expected to normalize to 65%-70% going forward after changes in financing cost mapping related to new debt instruments.
  • Repurchase activity -- Over 50% of the Series A convertible preferred stock repurchased in Q4; 1.8 million common shares bought in 2025 at an average price of $24.71 per share.
  • Liquidity -- Unrestricted cash of $142 million and more than $400 million available on revolver facilities at quarter end.
  • Updated reporting structure -- All digital platform-driven revenues consolidated into "auction and related fees"; "SaaS and other revenues" now capture non-volume marketplace streams, providing more transparency and supported by revised segment presentation.
  • 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- Expected in the $350 million-$370 million range, forecasting 5%-11% growth, with the majority of growth attributed to the marketplace segment.
  • Record engagement metrics -- Q4 and full-year digital marketing and inside sales teams drove record new dealer registrations, unique vehicle listings, and buyer/seller engagement.
  • AI integration -- Artificial intelligence now integrated in engineering, inspection, pricing advisory, and operations, with management positioning AI as an "enabler" to drive efficiency and customer value.
  • Private label commercial program launch -- New private label program live, onboarding more than 900 new dealers in the quarter.
  • Share count and debt -- Quarter-end fully diluted share count was 125 million (assuming full conversion of remaining Series A Preferred), with $550 million in debt outstanding.
  • Non-cash events impacting GAAP EPS -- The repurchase of Series A shares resulted in a $242 million deemed dividend impacting Q4 GAAP EPS by $2.20 per share; a $35 million non-cash tax benefit raised GAAP net income and GAAP EPS by $0.32 per share.

RISKS

  • Management cautioned on "headwinds from a lower interest rate and a higher risk environment" for the finance segment in 2026, noting, "even small upward movements within that range could impact AFC's performance."
  • Macro conditions in Canada are expected to remain challenged into 2026, which may limit growth opportunities in the region’s marketplace segment.
  • Updated adjusted free cash flow conversion guidance was revised downward to a 65%-70% range because, as management explained, "a portion of our dividend payment has now shifted to a tax-deductible interest payment" following new debt issuance.

SUMMARY

OPENLANE (OPLN 0.27%) delivered 9% year-over-year growth in revenue, 5% growth in adjusted EBITDA, and over 20% year-over-year growth in U.S. dealer-to-dealer transaction volumes in the quarter, supported by improved customer acquisition and robust adoption of its digital marketplace.

  • Management highlighted acceleration in U.S. dealer-to-dealer growth—exceeding 20% in Q4—while confirming a December inflection in commercial vehicle volumes that sets up anticipated expansion in 2026.
  • The company finalized a major strategic milestone by repurchasing over half of its convertible preferred shares in Q4, a move expected to benefit shareholders.
  • Additionally, OPENLANE revised its forward free cash flow conversion percentage downward to reflect recent debt restructuring, clarifying that absolute cash generation remains "largely unchanged."
  • Adjusted EBITDA growth in 2026 is projected to come "Nearly all" from the marketplace segment, as AFC's contribution is expected to be flat and subject to yield and risk headwinds.
  • AI deployment now enhances areas from vehicle inspections to price discovery and customer-facing operations, which management said is accelerating both efficiency and customer outcomes.
  • The company's engagement strategy centers on expanding digital share, leveraging recent sales staff expansion, record registration activity, and integration of private label programs to attract both new commercial and independent dealers.
  • Active seller and buyer counts in the U.S. marketplace rose more than 20% in Q4, driving transaction volume and customer stickiness according to management commentary.
  • ERP system consolidations, designed to drive data consistency and back-office efficiency, launched in 2025 and are expected to conclude by late 2027.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Off-lease volume: The aggregate number of vehicles returning at the end of lease terms, making them available for remarketing channels such as digital auctions.
  • AFC: The company’s floorplan finance segment, providing short-term, inventory-secured financing solutions for independent auto dealers.
  • Dealer-to-dealer (D2D): Refers to transactions where wholesale vehicles are exchanged directly between automotive dealers, either digitally or via physical auctions.
  • AuctionNet: An industry consortium that compiles and reports physical auction transactions for comparative benchmarking.
  • ERP: Enterprise Resource Planning software used for integrating and managing core business processes in a unified system.
