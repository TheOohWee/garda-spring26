TAKEAWAYS

  • Property Acquisitions -- $95 million acquired during the quarter at a 7% blended cap rate; full-year acquisitions totaled $318 million, both funded primarily through equity raised via ATM forward issuance.
  • Portfolio Granularity -- Most acquisitions in 2025 consisted of midsized transactions ranging from $5 million to $20 million, with no large portfolio deals.
  • Portfolio Tenant Concentration -- Olive Garden, Longhorn, and Chili's accounted for over 51% of portfolio rent; combined portfolio rent coverage was 5.1x for reporting tenants.
  • Same Store Sales Growth -- Chili's reported 9% growth for the quarter ended December 2025 (two-year comp +43%); Olive Garden and Longhorn posted near 5%-6% growth, for the quarter ended November 2025.
  • Bahama Breeze Exposure -- Represents 1.3% of base rent across 10 properties, each averaging $341,000 rent; leases have at least 1.7 years remaining, and management expects several properties to be converted to other Darden brands.
  • Diversification Metrics -- 37% of rent now comes from outside casual dining: automotive service (13%), quick service restaurants (11%), and medical retail (10%).
  • Equity vs. Debt Funding -- Since March 2024, 85% of $520 million in acquisitions funded with equity, the remainder by low-rate term loans; current net leverage stands near 5x, with balance sheet characterized as "overequitized."
  • Debt Structure -- 98% of debt is fully fixed at a 4% blended cash interest rate; 95% of floating-rate debt is fixed through November 2027 at 3%; no debt maturities until December 2026.
  • Liquidity Position -- Over $220 million in available liquidity, excluding forward equity, before reaching 5x leverage.
  • Q4 and FY Metrics -- Q4 AFFO per share was $0.45; full-year AFFO reached $1.78 per share, an increase of 2.9%; Q4 rental income totaled $67.5 million, up 11.1%; annual base rent at period-end was $264.2 million.
  • GAAP G&A Expense -- Annual cash G&A expense was $18 million, or 6.9% of annual rental income, at the bottom of guidance.
  • Occupancy and Collections -- Portfolio occupancy was 99.6%, with rent collections of 99.5% in Q4 and 99.8% for the full year; lease expirations in 2025 and 2026 reduced to 1.5% of annual base rent.
  • Acquisition Pipeline Expansion -- New investments in the quarter included a Sprouts grocery store ($8.6 million basis) and a United Rentals equipment rental property, marking initial entries into these sectors.
  • Debt Market Access -- Current term loan coupon rates available in the 4.5%-5.5% range, materially improved relative to prior periods.
  • Impairment Charge -- A write-down was taken on a vacant Hardee's property acquired at inception, described as a rare occurrence within the 1,325-property portfolio.

SUMMARY

Management cited a multi-year track record of underwriting discipline, noting enhanced platform diversification without reliance on large portfolio deals. The call highlighted proactive exposure reduction in Bahama Breeze, with several properties slated for conversion and strong leasing interest for any closures. Large anchor tenants reported leading sector sales performance, supporting overall portfolio stability. Leadership outlined a capital strategy using predominantly equity to fund recent growth, resulting in a low-leverage, highly liquid balance sheet and access to attractively priced debt for future acquisitions. New sector entries in grocery and equipment rental reflect a methodical expansion strategy, subject to proven underwriting standards and asset resilience criteria.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Net Lease: A real estate lease structure in which the tenant is responsible for property expenses, such as taxes, insurance, and maintenance, in addition to rent.
  • Cap Rate: Capitalization rate; the ratio of a property's net operating income to its acquisition price, used as an indicator of potential return.
  • AFFO: Adjusted Funds From Operations; a non-GAAP REIT performance metric reflecting cash generation from operations after maintenance capital expenditures.
  • ATM Forward Issuance: At-the-market share issuance program where equity is raised through the forward sale of common stock, often to fund acquisitions.
  • Term Loan: A fixed-term debt instrument, often with set repayment schedule and interest rate, used to finance real estate acquisitions.
  • SOFR: Secured Overnight Financing Rate; a benchmark interest rate for dollar-denominated derivatives and loans, now widely used as a reference rate in U.S. lending markets.
  • ABR: Annual Base Rent; total contracted rental income derived from property leases on an annualized basis.
  • Forward Equity Balance: Proceeds from the sale of stock through an ATM program where settlement and receipt of cash occur at a future date.
