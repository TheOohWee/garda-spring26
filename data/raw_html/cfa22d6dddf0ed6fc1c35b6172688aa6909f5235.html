Murphy USA (MUSA) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 30, 2025 at 1:10PM EST

Risks

Donnie Smith reported that year-to-date average per store fuel volumes were approximately 236,000 gallons per month in Q3 2025, prompting a downward revision of full-year fuel volume guidance to a range of 235,000–237,000 gallons per store per month for fiscal 2025 (ending Dec. 31, 2025), below the original guided range.

Takeaways

CEO succession and leadership continuity -- R. Andrew Clyde announced his planned retirement as CEO at year-end, with Mindy West becoming CEO on January 1, 2026, and Clyde remaining as advisor through February 2027.

Capital allocation -- The Board authorized a new $2 billion share repurchase program, to be executed through 2030, after reaching approximately 80% completion of the prior $1.5 billion share repurchase program, and renewed the dividend policy with an annual 10% growth target.

Q3 adjusted EBITDA -- $285 million in adjusted EBITDA was reported, flat compared to the prior year, despite all-in fuel margins running approximately $0.02 per gallon lower.

Fuel performance -- Average per store monthly fuel volumes were down 1.8%, with all-in margins of $0.307 per gallon and retail-only margins approximating $0.32 per gallon in October 2025.

Structural fuel margin -- Management identifies a $0.03–$0.04 per gallon structural uplift in the current margin structure versus 2022 (non-GAAP), which could lead to higher fuel contribution if margin conditions normalize.

Merchandise contribution -- Merchandise margin contribution dollars increased by $24.4 million, or 11.2%, driven by over 20% growth in nicotine categories and about 5% growth in center store categories.

QuickChek performance -- QuickChek reported its fourth consecutive quarter of same-store food and beverage sales growth, with total food and beverage sales up 2.7% and margins down 2.2% for the period.

Operating expenses -- Per store operating expense increased 2.8%. On an absolute basis, expenses were up 5.6%, with two-thirds of this driven by new and larger stores.

Store pipeline and growth -- Eight new stores and eleven raze-and-rebuilds were added; the year-end 2025 projection is over 45 new store openings, with nearly 40 stores under construction for Q4 2025 and into 2026, supporting a pipeline of 50+ annual new stores into the future.

Expense guidance -- Projected monthly per store operating expense for 2025 has been lowered to $36,200–$36,600 (from $36,500–$37,000), and SG&A guidance reduced to $230 million–$240 million for the full year, exclusive of the restructuring charge.

Tax rate -- All-in effective tax rate guidance for 2025 has been tightened to 23.5%–24.5% for the full year, reflecting discrete state refunds and federal energy tax credits.

Promotion-driven sales -- Nicotine promotional dollars have grown at a 12% CAGR since 2020, though the precise time frame is not specified, with recent promotions contributing materially to incremental sales, and management expects this performance to be sustainable going forward.

Dividend increase -- The Board approved an additional 10% dividend increase ($0.63 per share) payable December 1, as stated on the company's Q3 2025 earnings call.

Guidance update -- Full-year 2025 merchandise contribution guidance tightened to $870 million–$875 million, at the upper end of the prior range.

Summary

Murphy USA (MUSA 0.60%) reported a CEO transition plan, with Mindy West set to assume the chief executive role at the start of 2026 and a formal advisory role for outgoing CEO R. Andrew Clyde through February 2027. The Board initiated a new $2 billion share buyback program to begin after the completion of the current $1.5 billion authorization, with the new program set to run through 2030, alongside a reaffirmed 50/50 capital allocation strategy and a 10% target for annual dividend increases. Despite a continued low price, low volatility fuels environment and an approximately $0.02 per gallon decline in all-in margins, EBITDA for Q3 2025 matched the prior-year period, underpinned by robust merchandise performance and disciplined operating cost management. Management highlighted a $0.03–$0.04 per gallon structural uplift in retail fuel margin since 2022 (as discussed during the Q3 2025 earnings call), which may provide significant incremental earnings if the margin environment normalizes. Merchandise results for Q3 2025, led by over 20% growth in nicotine categories and strong center store performance, offset softness in fuel volumes, and full-year 2025 merchandise contribution guidance moved to the upper end of the range. Company-identified cost savings and a completed organizational restructuring contributed to reduced operating expense and SG&A guidance, with future new store growth targets raised to 50+ annually beginning in 2026, following an active raze-and-rebuild and acquisition quarter.

Mindy West stated that "current results are largely attributable to the low volatility and low price environment. Which is masking the potential of our business in a normal environment."

Recent nicotine promotions, particularly in pouches, accelerated volume growth to 120% of the prior year by October 2025, with management expecting the associated "halo effect" to continue into November 2025.

Donnie Smith highlighted that year-to-date improvements in merchandise performance, OpEx efficiencies, and G&A cost savings more than offset the impact of lower fuel volumes for the nine months ended Q3 2025, as efficiency initiatives—including a new labor model and loss prevention efforts—contributed to the improved fiscal 2025 expense outlook.

Mindy West said, "Now that we've become leaner, I think the next step is how do we get more efficient," signaling further focus on automation, workflow optimization, and resource consolidation following the organizational restructuring.

Management confirmed that full-year adjusted EBITDA is expected to land near $1 billion for fiscal 2025, assuming a $0.30 per gallon fuel margin and approximately five billion gallons sold, reiterating confidence in the company's business model through changing market cycles.

Industry glossary

All-in fuel margin: The total gross profit per gallon of fuel sold, combining both retail and any ancillary fuel-related revenues or costs.

Raze-and-rebuild (R&R): The process of demolishing (raze) an existing store and constructing a new facility (rebuild) at the same site to improve operational capabilities or capitalize on updated formats.

Center store: Non-fuel, non-nicotine merchandise categories located in the main area of a convenience store, including packaged beverages, candy, lottery, and general merchandise.

SG&A: Selling, General, and Administrative expenses; operating costs not directly tied to store-level management or merchandise acquisition.

CAGR: Compound annual growth rate; measures the mean annual growth rate of an investment or metric over a specified period, assuming compounding.
