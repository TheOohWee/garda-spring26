Earnings Call Transcript

Insperity (NSP) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 4, 2025 at 9:53AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, Nov. 3, 2025, at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Paul J. Sarvadi
  • Executive Vice President of Finance, Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer — James D. Allison

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • Adjusted EPS of minus 20¢ and Adjusted EBITDA of $10 million for Q3 2025 were both below forecasted ranges, due to higher-than-expected benefits costs.
  • Benefits Cost Trend increased 9.1% in Q3 2025 over Q3 2024, driven, according to management, by "higher than expected outpatient and inpatient utilization and pharmacy costs, including a significant increase in large claims frequency both sequentially over Q2 and year over year."
  • The prevailing view in the industry is that the higher claims trend experienced in 2025 will persist in 2026. According to Jim Allison, this explicitly signals ongoing earnings headwinds.
  • Management cited "a significant earnings shortfall from our initial budget," according to Jim Allison for 2025, driven by "the unexpected increase in health care claims costs."

TAKEAWAYS

  • Paid Worksite Employee Growth -- Increased by 1.2% to 312,842 in Q3 2025, within the company’s forecasted range.
  • Client Retention -- Averaged 99% per month in Q3 2025, consistent with the prior year.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Minus 20¢ for Q3 2025, below management's guidance "primarily due to a further continuation of higher than expected benefits costs," according to Jim Allison.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $10 million in Q3 2025, also below the forecasted range for the same primary reason as adjusted EPS.
  • Gross Profit per Worksite Employee -- $208 per month in Q3 2025, down from $247 in Q3 2024, with management attributing the decline to $20 million in unexpected benefits costs.
  • Benefits Cost Trend -- 9.1% year-over-year increase for Q3 2025 over Q3 2024, with management identifying higher claim frequency and utilization as the drivers.
  • Operating Expenses -- Decreased 4% year-over-year in Q3 2025 and declined $10 million sequentially, reflecting expense management below budget, especially in salaries and G&A.
  • HR scale Investment -- $17 million invested in Q3 2025, with $11 million included in operating expenses, down from $19 million in Q3 2024 (all expensed then); milestone reached for capitalization due to software platform progress.
  • Capital Return -- $22 million in cash dividends paid in Q3 2025; $68 million in dividends year-to-date, and 225,000 shares repurchased for $19 million in 2025.
  • Quarter-End Liquidity -- $120 million in adjusted cash and $280 million available under the credit facility at the end of Q3 2025.
  • Worksite Employee Guidance -- Q4 2025 average paid worksite employees expected at 313,000–315,000, up 1.3%–1.9% over Q4 2024.
  • Full-Year Growth/Profit Guidance -- Full-year adjusted (non-GAAP) EPS forecasted at 84¢–$1.47 and adjusted EBITDA (non-GAAP) at $119 million–$153 million for fiscal year ended Dec. 31, 2025.
  • Operating Expenses Outlook -- Full-year 2025 operating expenses expected to decrease by about 3% from prior year, with Q4 sequential declines anticipated.
  • Health Benefits Costs -- Full-year benefits cost trend projected to remain at 9% for 2025, due to utilization and pharmacy cost increases.
  • Workday Strategic Partnership Investment -- For fiscal year ended Dec. 31, 2025, total estimated at $58 million (including $48 million in operating expenses), compared to $57 million, all expensed in fiscal year ended Dec. 31, 2024.
  • Q4 2025 Profit Guidance -- Q4 2025 adjusted EBITDA forecasted at negative $25 million to $9 million, and adjusted EPS projected at minus 79¢ to minus 16¢.
  • 2026 Management Priorities -- UnitedHealthcare contract renewal through 2028 incorporates new financial terms, plan design, and a pooled claim cap reduction from $1 million to $500,000 per member, targeting a favorable impact of about 2% on gross benefits costs in 2026.
  • HR scale Rollout -- Platform milestones met, with client go-live targeted for March/April 2026, and 2026 HR scale operating expenses projected to be about $15 million lower than the $48 million estimate for 2025.
  • 2026 Recovery Opportunity -- Leadership stated, "we believe that 2026 represents an opportunity to recover a majority of the earnings shortfall we have experienced this year" (referring to fiscal year ended Dec. 31, 2025).

SUMMARY

Insperity (NSP 3.40%) management reported a steep drop in profitability, with both adjusted EPS and adjusted EBITDA for Q3 2025 falling below prior guidance due to elevated health benefits costs driven by increased claim utilization and frequency. Liquidity and capital returns remained healthy, while effective cost containment reduced operating expenses year-over-year and sequentially in Q3 2025. The company executed and capitalized significant investments in the HR scale platform with a major strategic milestone achieved for product rollout in 2026. New pricing measures and a renegotiated UnitedHealthcare contract, including a lower claim pooling cap, are intended to contain benefits costs and support margin recovery in 2026. Management expects operating expenses to decline as HR scale transitions from development to deployment, enhancing future profitability potential. The company’s joint solution with Workday (WDAY 0.49%) is positioned as a growth driver for large client sales and retention.

  • Paul Sarvadi described the UnitedHealthcare partnership extension through 2028 as providing "plan design modifications, and risk transfer alternatives that are projected to significantly reduce Insperity claim costs and mitigate expected trends and large claim risk for the upcoming year."
  • CEO Sarvadi stated, "The projected immediate benefit cost trend combined with the lower large claim pooling level in 2026 presents a timely and important opportunity to reduce costs and lower risk."
  • Momentum in HR 360 and HR scale sales was highlighted, with booked HR 360 sales 45% higher than the prior year in Q3 2025 and record large-account wins attributed to the joint Insperity-Workday solution.
  • Management reiterated that recently implemented pricing increases are "exceeding this continuing higher rate," and early signs show client retention averaged 99% per month in Q3 2025, with a higher sales pipeline entering 2026.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Worksite Employee: An individual employed by Insperity’s client firms and included in the company’s professional employer organization (PEO) service counts.
  • Pooling Level: The maximum annual health claim exposure per plan member before excess claims are transferred to an insurer, used for risk control in large group health plans.
  • HR scale: Insperity’s new cloud HR platform leveraging the Workday partnership, targeting mid-market firms with integrated service and technology solutions.
  • HR 360: Insperity’s flagship professional employer organization solution, offering comprehensive HR outsourcing for clients.
