Summary

Reliance (RS +0.21%) delivered record shipment volumes and gained significant U.S. market share, driven primarily by organic growth in carbon products, while also returning substantial capital to shareholders through dividends and buybacks. Tariff-driven cost increases sharply elevated LIFO expense, pressuring reported margins and earnings, particularly due to aluminum input volatility, with management citing outsized LIFO impacts and continued margin compression in certain product lines. Adjusted FIFO profitability remained strong, supported by price discipline and end market diversity, with forward guidance calling for both higher volumes and selling prices in fiscal 2026, despite management's acknowledgment of persistent risks from trade policy uncertainty and segment-specific demand softness.

  • Fourth quarter volumes and pricing consistently surpassed internal expectations and industry benchmarks, highlighting operating leverage from recent capital investments.
  • Sequentially lower CapEx in fiscal 2026 does not reduce management's stated willingness to increase investment as compelling opportunities or customer needs arise.
  • Management confirmed SG&A expense control through operating leverage, with per-ton costs declining for the year despite inflationary pressures.
  • No material customer substitution away from aluminum was reported despite price volatility, and copper-to-aluminum shifts were only noted in specific architectural subcategories.
  • Record high base prices, longer lead times, and robust demand for structural products were cited, especially within public infrastructure, energy, and data center-related segments.
  • Ongoing inventory overhang continues to constrain the semiconductor and commercial aerospace markets, with only gradual improvement anticipated later in fiscal 2026.
  • Organic shipment growth in 2025 equated to the scale of a $650 million revenue acquisition, a result achieved with lower cost and risk versus potential M&A transactions according to management's commentary.

Industry glossary

  • FIFO (First-In, First-Out): Inventory cost accounting method that reflects current costs by recognizing the value of oldest inventory as sold first, used internally to gauge operational profitability.
  • LIFO (Last-In, First-Out): Alternative inventory accounting method emphasizing the cost of newest inventory first, making results susceptible to material cost surges, especially due to tariffs.
  • Tolling tons: Customer-owned material processed through company facilities under tolling arrangements, excluding those volumes from reported tons sold.
  • Midwest premium: U.S. regional surcharge applied to the base price of aluminum, reflecting local supply-demand dynamics and tariffs.
