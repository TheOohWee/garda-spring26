PROG Holdings (PRG) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 18, 2026 at 10:02AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 18, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Steve Michaels
  • Chief Financial Officer — Brian Garner
  • Director of Investor Relations — John Baugh

Takeaways

  • Consolidated GMV -- Increased 12.1%, with For Technologies contributing approximately 144% year-over-year growth.
  • Progressive Leasing GMV -- Declined 8.6% year over year, primarily due to the Big Lots bankruptcy and tighter decisioning; “underlying GMV in 2025 grew in the mid single digits” after adjusting for these factors.
  • For Technologies GMV -- Delivered $730 million, with triple-digit growth throughout the year.
  • Gross margin (consolidated) -- Improved by 284 basis points to 36.3% in the fourth fiscal quarter ended Dec. 31, 2025, attributed to margin expansion in Progressive Leasing and increased mix from For Technologies.
  • Write-offs (Progressive Leasing) -- Remained within the 6%-8% targeted range, at 7.5% for the full year and 7.6% in the fourth fiscal quarter.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (consolidated) -- Totaled $269 million for fiscal 2025 (11.2% of revenue), flat to prior year and matching high end of original guidance; fourth quarter adjusted EBITDA was $61.5 million.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (For Technologies) -- $9.9 million for the year, representing a 13.5% margin; fourth quarter showed a $1.2 million seasonal loss due to holiday originations.
  • Non-GAAP diluted EPS -- $3.51 for the year and $0.74 in the fourth quarter, both exceeding the outlook provided in October and February.
  • Shareholder returns -- Approximately 1.8 million shares repurchased at an average price of $28.20, and dividends of $0.52 per share paid during 2025.
  • Cash and liquidity -- Ended 2025 with $308.8 million in cash and total available liquidity of approximately $659 million including revolving credit facility.
  • Net leverage -- 1.1x trailing twelve months adjusted EBITDA at year-end 2025, increasing to 2.5x following the Purchasing Power acquisition (excluding non-recourse ABS debt).
  • Vive portfolio divestiture -- Sold in the fourth fiscal quarter of 2025 and reflected as discontinued operations, enabling redeployment of capital.
  • Purchasing Power acquisition -- Completed Jan. 2026; expected to contribute $680 million-$730 million in revenue and $50 million-$60 million in adjusted EBITDA in 2026 with adjusted EBITDA margin initially in the 7%-8% range.
  • For Plus subscription model -- Over 80% of GMV originated from active subscribers, contributing to take rate of approximately 10% over the trailing twelve months.
  • E-commerce GMV mix -- Progressive Leasing reported all-time high of 30% e-commerce GMV in the fourth quarter and 23% for the full year, up from 17% in 2024.
  • Prog Marketplace -- Delivered approximately $82 million GMV in 2025, nearly doubling year over year.
  • Money App -- Drove $45 million incremental leasing GMV in 2025, up from $23 million in 2024, and approached breakeven adjusted EBITDA exiting the year.
  • AI integration -- Internal AI assistant “Piper Plus” resolved over 18,000 inquiries with more than half handled on first interaction; AI further improved decision speed by 75% and enhanced efficiency across multiple functions.
  • 2026 guidance (consolidated) -- Revenue projected at $3.0 billion-$3.1 billion, adjusted EBITDA at $320 million-$350 million, and non-GAAP EPS at $4.00-$4.45, assuming continued soft consumer demand and stable decisioning stance.
  • Capital allocation priorities -- Focus on debt reduction toward net leverage target of 1.5x-2x, integration of Purchasing Power, and continued investment in multiproduct growth initiatives.

Risks

  • CEO Michaels said, “we begin the year with a smaller lease portfolio, down 9.4% year over year which creates revenue headwinds.”
  • Management cited ongoing pressure in discretionary retail categories, particularly furniture and appliances, contributing to a challenging consumer environment.
  • Brian Garner said, “$5,000,000 of onetime costs related to a partner bankruptcy” and increased technology investments contributed to a higher SG&A in the fourth quarter.

Summary

PROG Holdings (PRG 0.64%) reported consolidated fiscal 2025 growth in GMV and successful multiproduct platform execution, with For Technologies and Money App contributing substantial incremental volume. The company completed the divestiture of the Vive portfolio and closed the Purchasing Power acquisition, positioning for revenue diversification and segment margin expansion. Gross margin and adjusted EBITDA margins both improved despite a significant decline in Progressive Leasing GMV, and cash generation supported ongoing capital returns to shareholders. Management issued 2026 guidance reflecting anticipated top-line growth, higher profitability, and continued cost control as the business integrates Purchasing Power and focuses on operational leverage.

  • The company highlighted “consolidated GMV” as a new primary lens for tracking total transaction volume across platforms.
  • AI-driven efficiencies and digital engagement expanded customer self-service options and streamlined operations across Progressive Leasing and For Technologies.
  • Seasonality and revenue cadence for 2026 are expected to be influenced by the integration of Purchasing Power (with the first fiscal quarter as a historical low) and by the timing of lapsing headwinds in the Progressive Leasing portfolio.
  • Cross-product engagement drove notable value, with $45 million leasing GMV attributed to interactions with Money App and For users, equating to a top-10 retailer contribution.
  • Leadership transitions included Lee Wright joining as President of Purchasing Power to accelerate segment growth and integration.

Industry glossary

  • GMV (Gross Merchandise Volume): Total dollar value of transactions processed across lease-purchase, buy now pay later, and direct-to-consumer platforms within a set period.
  • Take rate: Revenue as a percentage of total GMV, representing monetization efficiency for transactional businesses.
  • ABS (Asset-Backed Securities) debt: Non-recourse funding vehicle used by Purchasing Power to finance customer receivables, excluded from consolidated net leverage calculations.
  • Decisioning: Proprietary data-driven process used in underwriting and approving customer applications for lease-purchase products.
