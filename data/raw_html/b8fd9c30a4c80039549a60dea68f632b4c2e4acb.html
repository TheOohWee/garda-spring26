TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Income -- $139 million, an increase of 63%, with diluted EPS of $2.50, up 48%.
  • Return on Equity -- 22.5% for the quarter, with book value per share at $48.17.
  • Operating Revenues -- Just over $1.4 billion, up 52%, and up 20% sequentially.

  • Physical Contracts Operating Revenues -- Up 69%, led by an $83.9 million increase in precious metals, partially offset by a $19.8 million decrease in agricultural and energy.
  • Securities Operating Revenues -- Increased 43%, driven by a 22% rise in volumes and a 35% improvement in rate per million.
  • OTC Derivatives Operating Revenues -- Gained 72%, supported by client activity in Brazilian and European markets; up 8% sequentially.
  • Payments Revenues -- Decreased 4%, though average daily volume rose 11%; up 7% sequentially.

  • Total Fixed Compensation and Other Expenses -- Increased $75.6 million, including $44.4 million related to R.J. O'Brien and Benchmark; up 10% sequentially.
  • Professional Fees -- Rose $13.8 million, primarily due to higher legal fees, while the sequential quarter increase included $8 million of investment banking advisory fees for R.J. O’Brien acquisition.
  • Interest and Fee Income (Client Float) -- Increased $66.1 million (61%), with R.J. O'Brien contributing $63.8 million; average client equity rose 100% and money market FDIC sweep balances increased 5%.
  • Segment Results: Commercial -- Net operating revenues up 65%, segment income up 72%; sequential increases of 50% and 61%, respectively.
  • Segment Results: Institutional -- Net operating revenues grew 86% and segment income increased 78%; up 11% and 4% sequentially.
  • Segment Results: Retail -- Net operating revenues down 34%, segment income down 67%; sequential increases of 25% and 26%, respectively.
  • Segment Results: Payments -- Net operating revenues down 3%, segment income down 1%.
  • Hedging Action -- Entered $1.2 billion in fixed rate SOFR swaps with 2-year duration at an average rate of 3.32% to hedge short-term interest rate exposure.
  • Interest Rate Sensitivity -- A 100 basis point move in short-term rates estimated to change net income by $43.2 million or $0.80 per share annually.
  • Stock Split -- Board approved a Three-for-Two stock split effective March 20, 2026, for shareholders of record on March 10, 2026.
  • R.J. O'Brien Integration -- U.K. entity migration completed, releasing $20 million in capital; full U.S. consolidation on track to complete by fiscal year-end.
  • R.J. O'Brien Cost Synergies -- $50 million target affirmed, with further realization expected as integration proceeds.

SUMMARY

StoneX Group (SNEX 0.41%) reported record net operating revenues, net income, and earnings per share for the quarter, largely driven by the impact of the R.J. O'Brien and Benchmark acquisitions and exceptional performance in precious metals. Management stressed the diversification of its business model as a key advantage, particularly during market shifts impacting multiple product areas. The company completed the major migration of its R.J. O'Brien U.K. entity and confirmed that overall integration and targeted cost synergies remain on schedule. New hedging instruments and digital initiatives in Commercial and Institutional segments are aimed at enhancing client engagement and reducing platform friction. The Board authorized a three-for-two stock split, positioning the company for broader equity participation in March 2026.

  • Philip Smith stated that CoinInvest, now StoneX Bullion, scaled from annual earnings of $1.5 million pre-acquisition to generating that amount in a single day recently, accelerating direct-to-consumer growth in the retail precious metals business.
  • William Dunaway confirmed R.J. O'Brien added $5.8 billion to average client equity during the quarter.
  • The institutional segment benefited from the addition of Benchmark, driving record revenues across equities, fixed income, and prime service lines.
  • Smith noted increasing cross-sell opportunities and ecosystem expansion among regional U.S. banks, emphasizing bundled product offerings as a competitive differentiator.
  • Management confirmed the FX/CFD business experienced a normalized environment following an unusually high prior-year quarter, with the current period’s rate per million aligned with historical averages.
  • Dunaway highlighted that the rate per million in institutional equities reached approximately $320, with future expectations set around the $300 level as the new baseline.
  • StoneX is expanding into environmental, power, and electricity markets in Australia and Europe, targeting new client segments and diversifying revenue streams.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FCM (Futures Commission Merchant): A firm or individual that solicits or accepts orders to buy or sell futures or options on futures and accepts money or other assets from customers to support such orders.
  • SOFR (Secured Overnight Financing Rate): A benchmark interest rate for dollar-denominated derivatives and loans, reflecting the cost of borrowing cash overnight using U.S. Treasuries as collateral.
  • ADR (American Depositary Receipt): A negotiable security that represents securities of a non-U.S. company and trades on U.S. financial markets.
  • CFD (Contract for Difference): A financial derivative allowing traders to speculate on asset price movements without owning the underlying asset, settling the difference in value between open and close.
