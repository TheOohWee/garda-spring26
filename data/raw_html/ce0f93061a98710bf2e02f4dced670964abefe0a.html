Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $91.4 million in the quarter, representing a 17% year-over-year increase, and $343.2 million for the year, up 15% year over year.
  • Total ARR -- $366 million exiting the quarter, up 17% year over year and up $18 million sequentially.
  • Enterprise cohort ARR growth -- 20% year over year, noted as driving higher retention and expansion rates than other segments.
  • Customers with>$100k ARR -- 698, up 18% year over year, with 45 sequential net adds, the largest increase in company history.
  • Customers with>$1 million ARR -- 56, up 33% year over year.
  • Multi-product penetration -- 74% of ARR now from customers with more than one product, a 15 percentage-point increase year over year.
  • Net dollar retention -- Improved to above 105% in the period after ending 2024 at 100%.
  • Gross margin -- 77%, flat year over year and up one percentage point sequentially from the prior quarter.
  • Sales and marketing expense -- 42% of revenue, down one percentage point sequentially.
  • R&D expense -- 18% of revenue, unchanged year over year.
  • G&A expense -- 12% of revenue, down four percentage points year over year.
  • Operating income -- $4.2 million, equivalent to 4.6% of revenue on a non-GAAP basis.
  • Net income per share -- $0.04, up from $0.02 a year ago, with diluted share counts of 141.5 million and 135.7 million, respectively.
  • Free cash flow -- $11.2 million in the quarter (12% margin), and nearly $24 million for the year (7% margin).
  • Rule of 40 metric -- Improved from 15 in 2024 to over 24 in 2025, calculated by adding free cash flow yield and ARR growth.
  • RPO growth -- Current RPO consistently above 20% throughout the year; total RPO grew 35% year over year in the quarter.
  • Average contract duration -- Exceeds 22 months.
  • Expansion dynamics -- 58% of Q4 gross ARR came from expansion, with no individual deal exceeding $1 million.
  • AI agentic analytics platform -- Achieved a 76% success rate on complex production-grade queries, seven times higher than simple text-to-SQL approaches.
  • Agentic query growth -- Agent-driven queries now account for 25% of total queries, up from near zero in October; agents drove most of the incremental query growth.
  • Product adoption -- “Guides and Surveys” cited as the fastest-growing product less than a year after launch.
  • Pricing and packaging change -- Introduced centralized monetization for products via a percentage uplift on the core events-based platform charge, aiming for customer transparency and simplified adoption.
  • InfiniGrow acquisition -- Added AI-native marketing analytics capabilities, connecting spend, behavior, and revenue impact, as well as engineering talent.
  • Buyback authorization -- Board approved an additional $100 million reserve for share repurchases.
  • Outlook: Q1 2026 revenue -- Projected $91.7–$93.7 million, implying 16% annualized growth at midpoint.
  • Outlook: Q1 2026 non-GAAP operating income -- Projected between -$4.5 million and -$2.5 million.
  • Outlook: full-year 2026 revenue -- Projected $390–$398 million, representing 15% growth at the midpoint.
  • Outlook: full-year 2026 non-GAAP operating income -- Projected $7–$13 million, with non-GAAP net income per share of $0.08–$0.13 on 145.9 million diluted shares.
  • Board's stance on M&A -- Balance sheet enables more aggressive M&A for R&D acceleration as appropriate.

Summary

Amplitude (AMPL 6.66%) emphasized the strategic impact of its AI-driven analytics platform, highlighting rapid adoption among both enterprise and AI-native customers as a central driver of growth. Management detailed the measurable effects of recent product launches—including the rapid scaling of agentic workflow queries and the adoption of "Guides and Surveys"—in driving expansion, contract value, and platform stickiness within a consolidating market. Leaders explicitly cited improved operational efficiency, longer contract durations, and successful pricing model simplification, all positioned as fundamental changes supporting future profitability and scalability.

  • Leadership stated that "74% of our ARR is from customers with more than one product," and specified, "Only 51% of our ARR comes from customers with greater than three products," indicating further cross-sell opportunity.
  • Nearly 20% of new ARR booked in the quarter was on the new pricing and packaging pilot.
  • Operator reported use cases with major enterprise customers, including NTT Docomo, Siemens, and a top global music streaming service, mentioning a campaign analysis reduction time at NTT Docomo "by over 90%."
  • The company described its platform's AI-driven agent capabilities as a market differentiator, stating a "76% success rate on complex production grade queries. That is seven times better than a straight text-to-SQL approach."
  • Andrew Casey attributed net retention improvement primarily to cross-sell expansion, with growing impact from data ingestion-based upsells as capacity-related churn issues subside.
  • Management described customer demand as increasingly urgent for analytics tools amid faster product deployment cycles, noting buyers' emphasis on education and enablement to navigate rapid AI and platform transitions.
  • Operator addressed industry competitive concerns by highlighting platform integration and workflow automation, distinguishing its "context system specifically designed for them. Agents" as a key differentiator from generic data warehouse solutions.

Industry glossary

  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): The portion of revenue generated from ongoing subscription contracts, annualized to measure recurring business performance.
  • Agentic analytics: AI-enabled analytics workflows where intelligent agent software autonomously executes, summarizes, and recommends data-driven actions within the analytics platform.
  • MCP (Model Context Protocol): Amplitude's proprietary integration layer allowing both internal and third-party AI agents to execute analytics queries and actions across modules via standardized tool calls.
  • RPO (Remaining Performance Obligation): Contracted subscription revenue not yet recognized; includes both current and future periods of signed agreements.
