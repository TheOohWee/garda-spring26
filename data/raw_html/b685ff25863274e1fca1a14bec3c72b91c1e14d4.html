Hillman (HLMN) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 10:47AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, November 4, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Jon Michael Adinolfi
  • Chief Financial Officer — Robert Kraft

Takeaways

  • Net Sales -- $424.9 million for the quarter, representing an 8% increase.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $88 million, up 36% from the prior year period, achieving a record high for the company.
  • Adjusted Gross Margin -- 51.7%, increasing 350 basis points year over year and 340 basis points sequentially.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin -- 20.7%, up 420 basis points.
  • Net Debt -- $672 million at the period end, improving to a net leverage ratio of 2.5x trailing 12-month adjusted EBITDA.
  • Liquidity -- $277 million available, consisting of $239 million on the credit facility and $38 million in cash.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $9.1 million for the quarter, after absorbing approximately $30 million in tariff-related costs.
  • Guidance for 2025 Net Sales -- Reiterated at $1.535 billion to $1.575 billion, with 4%-7% growth expected.
  • Guidance for 2025 Adjusted EBITDA -- Raised low end to $270 million with a range of $270 million to $275 million, representing 12.7%-14% growth.
  • HPS Segment Growth -- Hardware and Protective Solutions net sales up 10%, with adjusted EBITDA up 57.3% to $65.8 million.
  • HPS Volume Impact -- Market volume in this segment declined 5.5%, offset by price, acquisitions, and new business wins.
  • RDS Segment Growth -- Robotics and Digital Solutions net sales increased 3.3%; adjusted EBITDA margin reached 31.4%.
  • Mini Key 3.5 Rollout -- Over 3,000 kiosks deployed, up by more than 800 units during the quarter.
  • Canadian Segment -- Net sales nearly flat, down 0.2%; adjusted EBITDA margin expected to remain above 10%.
  • Tariff Inventory -- $60 million worth of new tariffs held in inventory at quarter end.
  • Share Repurchase Program (SRP) -- $3.2 million deployed to repurchase 326,000 shares at an average price of $9.72; annual buyback pace targets $20 million to $25 million.
  • Operating Metrics -- Fill rates remained above 95% for the year.
  • Price/Volume Drivers -- Quarter's top-line growth included 10 points from price, 2 from Intex acquisition, and 2 from new wins, partly offset by a 6-point headwind from market volumes.
  • Guidance for 2026 -- Net sales projected to grow in the high single- to low double-digit percentage range; adjusted EBITDA anticipated to grow in the low- to mid-single-digit range, assuming market volumes remain flat and current tariff regime stays in place.

Risks

  • Robert Kraft said, "the price cost timing dynamic we are benefiting from now presents a difficult margin comp next year," pointing to margin risk in 2026.
  • Flat market volumes persisted, with new business wins offset by a 6-point headwind in the quarter and expectations of a 7-point headwind in the second half.
  • Adjusted gross margin is expected to "step down" in the fourth quarter, with the rate expected to return to levels seen in this year's second quarter due to tariff pressures.
  • Significant tariff-related costs, including $30 million in the quarter and $60 million held in inventory, continue to impact both free cash flow and profitability.

Summary

Hillman Solutions Corp. (HLMN 1.15%) delivered record third-quarter results, achieving the highest net sales and adjusted EBITDA in its 61-year history, driven by price initiatives, new business wins, and contributions from the Intex acquisition. The company improved leverage to 2.5x and demonstrated resilient margins, with adjusted EBITDA margin reaching 20.7% and adjusted gross margin expanding to 51.7%. Management reiterated 2025 net sales guidance and raised the low end of adjusted EBITDA guidance, while highlighting strategic investments and disciplined execution, including ongoing share repurchases. Looking ahead, management expects high single- to low double-digit top-line growth and low- to mid-single-digit adjusted EBITDA growth in 2026, noting that current favorable price-cost dynamics may create challenging margin comparisons next year.

  • Tariff management remains a central operational focus, impacting inventory, pricing, and free cash flow, with $60 million of tariffed goods held at quarter end.
  • Deployment of the SRP continued, as the company seeks to offset equity dilution and capitalize when shares trade at a perceived discount to intrinsic value.
  • Quarterly net sales were comprised primarily of price gains, while market volume remained a headwind; price initiatives provided the bulk of top-line improvement.
  • Over 3,000 Mini Key 3.5 kiosks have been deployed, with the company remaining on track to finalize rollout to its 2 largest customers by the end of 2026.
  • Operating execution metrics remained strong, with fill rates above 95% and robust customer relationships cited as central to future growth opportunities.

Industry glossary

  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, excluding items management identifies as non-core or non-recurring.
  • Mini Key 3.5: A proprietary self-service key duplication kiosk central to RDS segment growth and rollout strategy.
  • SRP (Share Repurchase Program): The company's board-approved initiative to buy back outstanding shares, used to manage dilution and potentially enhance shareholder value.
  • HPS (Hardware and Protective Solutions): Hillman's largest business segment, encompassing a wide range of hardware, fasteners, and home improvement products.
  • RDS (Robotics and Digital Solutions): Segment specializing in kiosk-based automation and digital services, notably key duplication solutions.
  • Intex: Acquired business contributing incrementally to sales and growth, first included in results from August 2024.
  • Fill Rate: The proportion of customer orders fulfilled in full and on time, indicating supply chain and operational efficiency.
