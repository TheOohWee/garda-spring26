Earnings Call Transcript

On Holding (ONON) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 23, 2025 at 11:11AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Aug. 12, 2025 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Co-Founder — David Allemann
  • Chief Executive Officer — Martin Hoffmann
  • Co-Founder — Caspar Coppetti

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- CHF 749.2 million in the quarter, representing 32% reported growth and 38.2% constant currency growth.
  • Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) Net Sales -- CHF 308.3 million, up 54.3% constant currency and 47.2% reported, lifting the DTC mix to a new Q2 high of 41.1% of total sales.
  • Wholesale Net Sales -- CHF 441 million, rising 28.8% constant currency and 23.1% reported.
  • Regional Sales — EMEA -- CHF 197.8 million, up 46.1% constant currency and 42.9% reported; identified as the strongest regional growth rate in the past two years.
  • Regional Sales — Americas -- CHF 432.3 million, increasing 23.6% constant currency and 16.8% reported; DTC nearly 40% growth in this region.
  • Regional Sales — APAC -- CHF 119.2 million, advancing 110.9% constant currency and 101.3% reported, with triple-digit growth for a fifth consecutive quarter.
  • Shoe Sales -- CHF 704.9 million, up 36% constant currency and 29.9% reported; both performance and lifestyle portfolios contributed notably.
  • Apparel Sales -- CHF 36.7 million, up 75.5% constant currency and 67.5% reported, with expanding engagement from both repeat and new customers.
  • Gross Profit Margin -- Reached 61.5%, an increase of 160 basis points year over year, with drivers cited as higher DTC mix, lower freight costs, and foreign exchange tailwinds.
  • SG&A (excluding share-based comp) -- 47.7% of net sales, improved from 48.6% in Q2 last year, reflecting operational efficiency gains.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- CHF 136.1 million with an 18.2% margin, up 220 basis points year over year.
  • Net Loss -- CHF 40.9 million, attributed to “a meaningful unrealized foreign exchange impact in our net financial results.”
  • Inventory Balance -- CHF 360.4 million at quarter-end; management described ongoing improvements in inventory health and working capital.
  • Cash Balance -- CHF 846.6 million, down from CHF 871.8 million in the prior quarter, primarily due to foreign exchange revaluation on U.S. dollar holdings.
  • Guidance Raised — Net Sales (Full Year 2025) -- Expected constant-currency net sales growth of at least 31%, now guiding for at least CHF 2.91 billion in reported net sales, up from previous CHF 2.86 billion.
  • Guidance Raised — Gross Profit Margin (Full Year 2025) -- Raised to 60.5%-61%, above the prior 60%-60.5% range, accounting for new tariffs and continued operational efficiencies.
  • Product Franchise Diversification -- Management reported nine footwear franchises contributing over 5% each to total sales.
  • LightSpray Manufacturing -- Launch of first Zurich-based factory leveraging robotic automation intended to simplify supply chain and reduce labor requirements.
  • Apparel and Brand Expansion -- Apparel previewed at Paris Fashion Week and via influencer collaborations; higher profile in sports and fashion signaled as a geographic and category growth driver.
  • Retail Footprint -- Now operating 54 owned stores globally, with planned openings in Palo Alto and Stockholm cited as near-term expansion milestones.

RISKS

  • Martin Hoffmann stated, "the continuous weakness of the U.S. dollar versus Swiss franc in the second quarter, closing near multi-decade lows at 0.79 led to a meaningful unrealized foreign exchange impact in our net financial results, which resulted in a net loss of CHF 40.9 million."
  • Management highlighted exposure to elevated tariffs: "now this number is changing. It's changing to 40% for imports from Vietnam and to 39% from imports from Indonesia."; effects are incorporated into the margin outlook, with mitigation steps possible but not yet initiated.
  • The second half of the year is anticipated to "face ongoing foreign exchange headwinds" due to U.S. dollar devaluation, which may impact reported financials.

SUMMARY

On Holding (ONON 2.58%) reported record quarterly net sales, significant margin expansion, and robust constant-currency growth across all regions and channels. Executive commentary confirmed strategic commitment to premium positioning, with nine footwear franchises each generating more than 5% of top-line sales and growing influence in both performance and fashion markets. Retail and DTC momentum boosted gross margin and supported an increase in both sales and margin guidance for the full year. Management cited the ramp-up of LightSpray technology as pivotal for future cost optimization and supply agility, while macro headwinds such as currency risk and tariffs remain a focus for operational mitigation.

  • Leadership revealed that apparel sales now show accelerated adoption from both first-time and repeat customers, suggesting earlier and broader integration into customer journeys.
  • Hoffmann reported that, despite new tariff burdens, the company expects to sustain a gross profit margin above 60% for the remainder of the year by leveraging price increases and continued operational efficiencies.
  • Caspar Coppetti stated, "you now have 9 franchises that deliver more than 5% of top line," underscoring product diversification.
  • Store-level expansion in Asia and selective global wholesale growth are reflected in a deliberate premium channel strategy, highlighted by the Singapore opening and mid-single-digit expected growth in wholesale doors.
  • Management noted that over 70% of the cash balance is held in U.S. dollars, signifying ongoing exposure to currency valuation swings.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • DTC (Direct-to-Consumer): Sales channel in which a brand sells products directly to end consumers via owned stores or e-commerce rather than through wholesalers or third-party retailers.
  • LightSpray: On Holding's proprietary manufacturing method using robotic arm automation to rapidly construct ultralight footwear components.
