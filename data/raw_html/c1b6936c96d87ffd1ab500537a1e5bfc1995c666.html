DocuSign (DOCU) Q4 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 18, 2026 at 9:10AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, March 17, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Allan Thygesen
  • Chief Financial Officer — Blake Grayson
  • Head of Investor Relations — Matt Sonefeldt

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $837 million for the quarter, up 8% year over year, with full-year revenue of $3.2 billion, also up 8%.
  • Subscription Revenue -- $819 million for the quarter, increasing 8% year over year; $3.2 billion for the year, up 9%.
  • Billings -- $1 billion for the quarter, marking the first quarterly result above this threshold, with 10% year-over-year growth; annual billings reached $3.4 billion, up 10%.
  • Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) -- $3.3 billion, up 8% from the previous year, consistently matching the prior year's ARR growth rate.
  • IAM ARR -- Over $350 million, representing 10.8% of total ARR, up from 2.3% a year prior.
  • International Revenue -- Surpassed 30% of total revenue in the quarter, rising 15% year over year.
  • Dollar Net Retention Rate (DNR) -- 102% for the quarter, up from 101% last year, improving for six consecutive quarters.
  • Customer Base -- Over 1.8 million total customers, up 9% year over year, with 1,205 customers spending over $300,000 annually, a 7% increase.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 81.8% for the quarter and 82.0% for the full year, both down slightly versus the prior year primarily due to cloud migration costs.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Income -- $247 million for the quarter, up 10%; $968 million for the full year, up 9%.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin -- 29.5% for the quarter (up 70 basis points), reaching 30% for the full year (up 30 basis points) for the first time.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $350 million in the quarter, up 25% year over year; over $1 billion for the year, a 33% margin.
  • Cash & Investments -- $1.1 billion at quarter-end with no debt outstanding.
  • Share Repurchases -- $269 million repurchased in the quarter; $869 million for the year, representing 82% of annual free cash flow.
  • Repurchase Authorization -- Expanded by $2 billion for a new total of $2.6 billion remaining authorization.
  • Non-GAAP Diluted EPS -- $1.01 for the quarter, a $0.15 per share increase over last year; $3.84 for the year, up from $3.55.
  • GAAP Diluted EPS -- $0.44 for the quarter, compared to $0.39 the prior year; $1.48 for the full year, down from $5.08 due to a prior-year tax valuation benefit.
  • Employee Count -- 7,044 at year-end, up from 6,838, with most hires in lower-cost locations focused on IAM development.
  • Fiscal 2027 ARR Guidance -- 8.25%-8.75% growth rate, targeting $3.551 billion in total ARR and IAM to represent approximately 18% of total ARR, exceeding $600 million.
  • Fiscal 2027 Revenue Guidance -- $3.484 billion to $3.496 billion, or 8% growth at the midpoint; Q1 revenue expected in the $822 million to $826 million range.
  • Fiscal 2027 Profitability Guidance -- Non-GAAP gross margin expected at 81.5%-82.0%, with non-GAAP operating margin between 30.0% and 30.5%.
  • Fiscal 2027 Share Count Guidance -- Non-GAAP fully diluted weighted average shares outstanding projected at 190 million to 195 million.
  • IAM Product Launches -- New IAM SKUs for HR and procurement, richer legal automation tools, and expanded compliance features announced for rollout.
  • AI Data and Efficiency -- More than 200 million private agreements ingested into Navigator, with "AI processing costs optimized by upwards of 50x" compared to direct LLM prompts.
  • Partner Channel Momentum -- Total partner contributed bookings up over 30% year over year in the quarter.
  • Consumption-Based Pricing -- IAM subscription pricing model to launch in Q1, following validation with 40–50 enterprise customers.
  • Share Count Reduction -- Q4 diluted weighted average shares outstanding fell to 204.7 million, down from 214.5 million the previous year.

RISKS

  • Non-GAAP gross margin declined by 50 basis points in the quarter and 20 basis points for the year due to "ongoing costs associated with our cloud infrastructure migration."
  • GAAP diluted EPS dropped to $1.48 from $5.08 year over year as prior-year results benefited from a one-time tax valuation allowance release.

SUMMARY

Management confirmed that IAM ARR rose from 2.3% to 10.8% of total ARR, with consistent company-wide ARR growth at 8%. Guidance calls for an accelerated shift, targeting IAM to exceed $600 million ARR and 18% of total next year. The quarter marked the first with billings over $1 billion, over $1 billion in free cash flow, and more than 30% of revenue from international business. DocuSign reported its largest quarterly repurchase and expanded the buyback program to $2.6 billion remaining authorization. Pricing innovation, including IAM consumption-based models and new SKUs, is positioned to support further enterprise and functional expansion. Management noted "continued strong adoption" and improving DNR, while stating that retention rates for early IAM renewal cohorts "are better than the company average." Enhanced partnerships with Microsoft and Anthropic were highlighted as strategic channels for enterprise and AI-driven growth.

  • Executives stated, "IAM is tracking as we hoped it would" and expect it to be "a key growth lever" to ultimately reach long-term double-digit top-line growth.
  • The company is moving away from reporting billings as a primary metric, shifting ARR as the top performance indicator going forward.
  • Allan Thygesen said, "we have a huge advantage in using private consented agreements, not just public data," for DocuSign AI models.
  • IAM renewal cycles are expected to increasingly drive platform adoption, with management confirming "gross retention and dollar net retention rates for these IAM early renewal cohorts are better than the company average."
  • Recent launches include AI-powered agentic tools for legal workflows, with new vertical- and function-focused product offerings planned for fiscal 2027.
  • IAM's integration with major third-party AI platforms, such as OpenAI's ChatGPT, Google Gemini, GitHub Copilot Studio, and Salesforce Agentforce, provides broad workflow coverage for customers.
  • Most new engineering code is now AI-assisted, according to management, indicating extensive internal adoption of AI tools for productivity gains.
  • Blake Grayson stated that "Stock-based comp grew 2% year-over-year" and is expected to decline again in the upcoming year as further cost actions are taken.
  • IAM's go-to-market strategy for fiscal 2027 includes a new top-down, C-suite focused sales motion and a partner-led channel that delivered over 30% bookings growth.
  • Enterprise customer projects, including the Aon Meridian case, are described by management as transformational in scope and complexity for both process automation and customer value.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • IAM (Intelligent Agreement Management): DocuSign's AI-native, end-to-end platform designed to automate, analyze, and manage business agreements across functions, integrating advanced workflows and artificial intelligence.
  • Navigator: DocuSign's intelligent contract repository used for AI-powered search, data extraction, and workflow automation leveraging large volumes of private agreements.
  • SCIM: A standards-based system for identity management that enables automated and scalable management of user access across applications, referenced as part of DocuSign's enterprise security and compliance enhancements.
  • Envelope: DocuSign’s unit for digital transaction volume, representing each complete package of documents sent for electronic signature.
  • DNR (Dollar Net Retention Rate): A metric reflecting the change in recurring revenue from existing customers due to expansion, contraction, and churn.
  • SKUs: Stock Keeping Units, referring here to differentiated product configurations or packages tailored by function or vertical (e.g., IAM for HR, procurement).
