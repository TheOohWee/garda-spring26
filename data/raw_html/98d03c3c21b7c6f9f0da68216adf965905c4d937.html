MercadoLibre (MELI) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 26, 2026 at 7:25PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 24, 2026, at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Financial Officer — Martin de Los Santos
  • President, Commerce — Ariel Szarfsztejn
  • Chief Executive Officer, Mercado Pago — Osvaldo Giménez

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Revenues -- Grew 45% year over year, driven by commerce acceleration and fintech expansion.
  • Brazil GMV -- Increased 35% year over year, with a 45% rise in items sold, attributed to a lower free shipping threshold.
  • Mexico GMV -- Registered 35% growth year over year, indicating broad commerce traction.
  • Advertising Revenue -- Surged 67% year over year, fueled by AI-driven bidding algorithms and increased adoption among sellers.
  • Fintech Monthly Active Users -- Grew nearly 30%, marking ten consecutive quarters of similar expansion.
  • Credit Portfolio -- Nearly doubled to $12.5 billion year over year, with about 3 million new credit cards issued in the quarter.
  • Assets Under Management -- Rose 78% year over year, reaching nearly $19 billion.
  • Operating Income -- Full year growth of 22%, with margin compression of 5%-6% related to strategic investments in free shipping, credit card, first-party commerce (1P), cross-border trade (CBT), and network expansion.
  • AI Assistant in Mercado Pago -- Handled 87% of user interactions without human support, covering tasks such as credit management, and money transfers.
  • Brazil Acquiring Total Payment Volume (TPV) -- Increased 25%, with AI-enabled tools improving merchant targeting and portfolio effectiveness.
  • Mexico Acquiring TPV -- Expanded 50%, demonstrating fintech adoption outside Brazil.
  • Credit Card Book NPL (Non-Performing Loans) -- Achieved an all-time low of 4.4% in the fourth quarter.
  • Sales and Marketing Spend -- Increased sequentially by 60 basis points, and year over year by 1.4 points, driven mainly by an expanded affiliate program.
  • Record Net Promoter Scores -- Achieved highest levels in commerce, and fintech across Argentina, Brazil, and Mexico, reflecting reported improvement in user experience.

RISKS

  • Argentina experienced margin compression from higher fulfillment costs, increased bad debt provisions due to new credit card issuance, and elevated funding costs compared to the prior year.
  • International expansion of cross-border trade (CBT), and early-stage growth in smaller countries are expected to sustain margin pressure until further scaling is achieved.
  • Overall margin compression of 5%-6% is attributed to persistent investments in shipping, credit card, first-party commerce, and cross-border initiatives, with the commentary clarifying, "still not profitable on its own" for 1P commerce, and ongoing scaling needs for cross-border fulfillment.

SUMMARY

MercadoLibre (MELI 1.08%) delivered fourth-quarter results marked by accelerating commerce and fintech activity, achieving record high net revenues and user engagement levels. Management provided detailed transparency on the magnitude and allocation of current margin investments, confirming a 5%-6% margin impact from strategic priorities, especially across shipping, credit card, first-party, and cross-border operations. Key technological advances, notably in artificial intelligence, have contributed to improved user experience, operational productivity, and advertising monetization, with AI-based solutions resolutely driving automation and value creation across both commerce and payments ecosystems.

  • Company disclosed that new buyer cohorts brought in since the June value proposition launch showed higher purchasing rates across more categories, with improved retention relative to prior comparison periods.
  • Marketplace seller AI assistants now advise transactions representing 20% of total gross merchandise volume, supporting faster listing improvements and customer support for merchants.
  • Management described recent adjustments to merchant shipping charges in Brazil, aligning fees with item weight and dimensions to better reflect cost structure, but withheld forward financial guidance pending further performance data.
  • Reported that interchange fee regulations in Mexico have been put on hold by regulators, so no current impact to take rates or fee structure is anticipated.
  • Brazilian credit card portfolios older than two years are now profitable on a NIMAL basis, while the average across all geographies remains unprofitable due to recent, rapid user base expansion.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • GMV (Gross Merchandise Volume): The total value of merchandise sold over a given period via the platform, before deducting returns, allowances, and discounts.
  • TPV (Total Payment Volume): Aggregate value of payments processed through Mercado Pago, including on-platform and off-platform transactions.
  • 1P (First-Party Commerce): Sales made directly by MercadoLibre as the principal seller, rather than transactions between third-party sellers and buyers.
  • CBT (Cross-Border Trade): Business segment focused on facilitating international sales and shipments across markets.
  • NIMAL (Net Interest Margin After Losses): Net interest margin, net of credit losses, indicating risk-adjusted profitability of lending products.
  • NPL (Non-Performing Loans): Loans on which the borrower is not making interest payments or repaying any principal, used as a measure of credit portfolio health.
  • Affiliate Program: A performance-based marketing initiative where outside individuals or entities promote MercadoLibre’s services in exchange for commissions or fees.
  • Net Promoter Score (NPS): A metric evaluating customer satisfaction and loyalty based on survey responses to recommend the company’s products or services.
  • Agentic Commerce: Commerce model leveraging AI agents to autonomously conduct shopping, discovery, and transaction processes on behalf of users or merchants.
