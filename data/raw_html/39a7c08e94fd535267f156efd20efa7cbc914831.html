TAKEAWAYS

Revenue: $16.8 billion in revenue for Q2 2025, driven by improvement across Institutional Securities, Wealth Management, and Investment Management businesses.

Earnings Per Share: $2.13 in EPS (GAAP) for Q2 2025, marking six consecutive quarters of durable earnings growth through Q2 2025.

Return on Tangible Common Equity (ROTCE): 18.2% in the second quarter, and 20.6% for the first half of 2025.

Total Client Assets: Total client assets exceeded $8.2 trillion across wealth and investment management in Q2 2025, moving closer to the firm's target of surpassing $10 trillion.

Wealth Management Pretax Profit: A record $2.2 billion in pretax profit at a 28.3% margin in Q2 2025, impacted by the Deferred Compensation Plan (DCP) by approximately 70 basis points.

Net New Assets (Wealth Management): $59 billion in net new assets added in Q2 2025, including a $22 billion headwind from tax outflows, $43 billion in fee-based flows marked a new record (excluding asset acquisitions).

Institutional Securities Revenue: $7.6 billion in Institutional Securities revenue for Q2 2025, $3.7 billion from equities and $2.2 billion from fixed income businesses in the second quarter.

Investment Banking Revenue: $1.5 billion in investment banking revenue for Q2 2025, $500 million from equity underwriting and $508 million from advisory; Debt underwriting delivered $532 million, with declines in non-investment-grade issuance.

Prime Brokerage: Achieved record revenues and all-time high average client balances in the equity segment.

Asset Management Revenue (Investment Management): $1.6 billion in revenue for Q2 2025, Up 12% year over year, $11 billion in long-term net inflows and record assets under management of $1.7 trillion.

Bank Lending Balances: Increased sequentially to $169 billion, with growth largely in securities-based lending.

Total Deposits: with net interest income of $1.9 billion, steady compared to the previous quarter.

Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) Ratio: Reported at 15%, over 200 basis points above the forward requirement, implying flexibility for capital deployment.

Dividend Increase: Quarterly dividend raised by $0.075 to $1 per share.

Efficiency Ratio: The firm's year-to-date efficiency ratio was 70%, attributed to ongoing cost control and productivity initiatives.

SUMMARY

Morgan Stanley (MS 0.29%) reported strong quarterly performance, underpinned by record client assets and recurring fee-based flows in wealth and investment management. Management emphasized progress toward exceeding $10 trillion in client assets and highlighted the firm’s improved capital position, as reflected in a CET1 ratio of 15%. Strategic clarity centered on ongoing investments in platform capabilities, disciplined capital deployment, and targeted inorganic opportunities judged by high strategic relevance. Operational leverage in institutional and wealth channels, evidenced by margin gains and net asset inflows across all business lines, signals sustained earnings durability. Regulatory reform and capital flexibility were explicitly cited as potential enablers for future growth, while management expects steady net interest income levels given current policy rates.

Chief Financial Officer Sharon Yeshaya said, "Fee-based assets stand at $2.5 trillion." highlighting the scale of recurring revenue streams.

CEO Ted Pick said, "organic development is happening and will continue to happen where we think there is operating leverage with smart risk architected deployment around clients." clarifying capital allocation priorities.

Investment management long-term net flows totaled $11 billion, with particular strength in fixed income and customized Parametric portfolios among wealth management clients.

Management indicated readiness for further capital deployment, including potential buybacks and "opportunistically buying back stock" as stated by CEO Ted Pick.

The efficiency ratio improvement stems from prioritization of controllable spending, tangible savings from space exits, and enhanced productivity via technology investment.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

DCP (Deferred Compensation Plan): A plan under which a portion of employee compensation is paid out at a future date, commonly used by financial institutions to align employee interests with long-term firm performance.

Prime Brokerage: A set of services offered by investment banks to hedge funds and other institutional clients, including securities lending, leveraged trade execution, and cash management.

CET1 (Common Equity Tier 1) Ratio: A key regulatory capital metric reflecting the ratio of a bank’s common equity tier 1 capital to its risk-weighted assets, significant for assessing capital adequacy.

Parametric Platform: Morgan Stanley’s proprietary portfolio customization solution focused on personalized investment strategies for clients, especially in tax management and factor exposures.
