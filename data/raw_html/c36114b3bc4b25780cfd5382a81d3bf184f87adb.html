Tyson Foods (TSN) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 2, 2026 at 10:29AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, Feb. 2, 2026 at 9:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Donnie King
  • Chief Financial Officer — Curt Calaway
  • Chief Operating Officer — Devin Cole
  • Chief Growth Officer — Kristina Lambert
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Jon Kathol

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $14.3 billion, an increase of 6.2%, led by beef with notable gains from chicken, pork, and prepared foods; growth calculated excluding a $100 million legal contingency reserve recognized in the quarter.
  • Segment Operating Income -- $811 million, a decrease of 12%, primarily due to beef segment weakness, partially offset by improvements in other segments.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $0.97, down 15%, attributed in part to a higher tax rate for the period.
  • Prepared Foods Sales -- Up 8.1%, fueled by volume gains, improved channel mix, and pass-through pricing, with segment operating income increasing to $338 million, up $16 million.
  • Chicken Segment -- Segment operating income reached $459 million with a 10.9% margin; marked the fifth consecutive quarter of volume and net sales growth, with volume up 3.8% and all-time record poultry volume.
  • Pork Segment -- Segment operating income margin rose 220 basis points to 6.7% due to network optimization, with adequate raw material supply and utilization supporting prepared foods.
  • Beef Segment -- Operating income declined, pressured by higher cattle costs, with volume down 7.3%; facility closures in Lexington, Nebraska and reduction to one shift in Amarillo, Texas occurred after quarter-end and are expected to improve capacity utilization going forward.
  • Retail Sales Outperformance -- Nielsen data shows Tyson Foods (TSN +0.34%) retail branded volume grew 2.5% and dollars grew 3.6%, outperforming total food and beverage retail volume, which declined 1.8% with dollars up 0.9%.
  • Share Gains -- Tyson-branded fresh chicken sales rose 10.7%, while Hillshire Farm lunch meats increased 10.4%, 12.5%, and 7.2% across tracked categories; all four grew dollars and share.
  • Operating Cash Flow & Free Cash Flow -- Operating cash flow of $942 million; capital expenditures were $252 million, resulting in free cash flow of just under $700 million, comfortably covering $177 million in dividends.
  • Liquidity and Leverage -- Ended the quarter with $4.5 billion in liquidity; net leverage decreased to 2.0 times, improving by a tenth of a turn since year-end, and gross debt reduced by $1.4 billion over the prior twelve months.
  • Capital Returns -- Returned $224 million to shareholders via $47 million in share repurchases and $177 million in dividends during the quarter.
  • 2026 Outlook — Sales and Operating Income -- Anticipates full-year sales growth of 2%-4% and adjusted operating income of $2.1-$2.3 billion; CapEx guidance is $700 million-$1 billion with free cash flow expected between $1.1-$1.7 billion, the higher outlook driven mainly by expected working capital improvements.
  • Beef 2026 Guidance -- Expects segment operating loss of $500 million-$250 million for beef due to continued tight cattle supplies, excluding facility closure costs.
  • Pork, Chicken, Prepared Foods, International Guidance -- Forecasts pork segment operating income of $250-$300 million, chicken of $1.65-$1.9 billion, prepared foods of $1.25-$1.35 billion, and international of $150-$200 million; corporate expenses and amortization anticipated at $950-$975 million.
  • Cost Management -- Corporate expenses and amortization were $20 million lower (7.7% reduction); team member costs fell by $13 million compared to the prior quarter, contributing to lower overhead.
  • Reporting Change -- Shifted to reporting segment operating income excluding corporate expenses and amortization at the segment level, increasing transparency and management accountability for volume growth and margin improvement.
  • Product Reformulation Progress -- Branded portfolio proactively removed petroleum-based synthetic dyes and high fructose corn syrup, aligning with new U.S. dietary guidelines encouraging fewer artificial ingredients.
  • Protein Demand -- New U.S. dietary guidelines support increased animal protein consumption; Tyson is positioned as a market leader, producing one out of every five pounds of chicken, beef, and pork in the U.S.

RISKS

  • Segment operating income for beef is projected to be a loss of $500 million-$250 million in 2026, reflecting pressure from persistently tight U.S. cattle supplies.
  • Donnie King said, "Continuing to absorb losses like we have been seeing for the past two years is simply unacceptable," highlighting ongoing risk in beef operations if market conditions fail to improve.
  • Uncertainty remains on the financial impact and market dynamics following facility closures, with management noting the effects will only appear in future quarters; Q1 beef volume was down 7.3%.
  • Input cost inflation persists in prepared foods, notably from beef and pork trim, with a $100 million commodity cost increase in the quarter and pricing only "catching up" with raws, as described by King.

SUMMARY

The first quarter reflected broad-based sales growth, margin expansion in pork and chicken, and disciplined cost control, but beef remains a significant drag due to elevated cattle costs and declining volumes. Management implemented structural changes—both in segment reporting and facility footprint—to drive transparency, decision-making accountability, and improved asset utilization. The guidance for 2026 projects continued revenue growth and robust cash flow, underpinned by operational discipline and favorable channel mix within chicken and prepared foods. The newly adopted U.S. dietary guidelines validate Tyson Foods' protein-centric strategy and branded portfolio positioning. Management maintains confidence in volume growth and efficient capital allocation, as evidenced by ongoing investments, cost savings, and liquidity improvement.

  • Donnie King emphasized, "Protein is a superstar in the story," referencing the sustained and growing demand across U.S. retail and foodservice channels.
  • Tyson Foods' retail brands achieved share gains in every tracked category, with five consecutive quarters of sequential improvement in prepared foods share, volume, and units.
  • The full transition to segment operating income reporting is intended to catalyze volume growth by removing corporate cost allocation as a barrier to segment decision-making.
  • Guidance for 2026 anticipates managed improvements in working capital and ongoing reduction in leverage as key sources of uptrend in free cash flow.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Cutout Value: The estimated market value of a carcass after processing into its component parts (primal and sub-primal cuts), used in assessing segment profitability in meat processing.
  • Drop Credit: Revenue from by-products (such as hides, organs, and rendered products) generated during meat processing, which offset costs in the beef and pork segments.
  • MAP (Marketing Activation Program): Targeted marketing and promotional investment designed to increase volume and market share for branded products in retail and foodservice channels.
  • S&OP (Sales and Operations Planning): Integrated business process for aligning demand, production, and distribution strategies to optimize operational efficiency and delivery.
