Risks

  • Higher compensation per employee expected, with Jennifer Hamann stating, "For 2026, we expect our all in compensation per employee to be up around 4% to 5%," adding cost headwinds despite ongoing productivity efforts.
  • Corporate guidance notes, price may not be a driver of our improving margins in 2026, indicating margin pressures could persist absent pricing power or significant mix tailwinds.
  • 2026 macroeconomic backdrop described as "softer," with S&P Global forecasting deterioration in industrial production, housing starts, and auto sales, highlighting volume and cost headwinds.
  • Fourth quarter operating income fell 5% and freight revenue declined 1% on 4% lower volume, reflecting market and segment pressures.
  • Other expense increased 22% in the quarter, mainly due to higher casualty costs and rising property taxes.
  • Pending merger with Norfolk Southern faces a procedural delay, as Jim Vena said, "we are disappointed that the STB determined we needed provide more information after providing close to 7,000 pages," which introduces regulatory and timing uncertainty.

Summary

Union Pacific (UNP 0.26%) delivered record full-year net income, earnings per share, and freight revenue (excluding fuel surcharges), while achieving best-ever safety and productivity results. Management maintained operational discipline by utilizing 3% fewer employees to move 1% greater volume and holding adjusted operating expenses roughly flat, even as inflation and merger expenses increased. The merger with Norfolk Southern remains on track for a 2027 close, with additional regulatory information requested but no pending change to the timeline. The company targets mid single digit EPS growth in 2026 from its $11.98 2025 base, prioritizing cost management, service reliability, and modest capital spending at $3.3 billion. Share repurchases have been paused to prioritize deleveraging ahead of the merger, with a $1.5 billion long-term debt maturity scheduled in the first half of 2026.

  • Merger synergies identified in the application project at least $2 billion in incremental net revenue and a manageable 6% increase in combined operating inventory, reinforced by investments since the 2023 base year.
  • Record quarterly other income of $264 million was driven primarily by industrial park land sales, providing a one-time benefit to earnings and cash conversion.
  • Management confirmed continued record-setting velocity, dwell, and train length, attributing gains to technology, network investments, and process optimization.
  • The leadership team reemphasized resource buffer management, stating that extra personnel and asset reserves prevent service degradation during weather events and support future growth.
  • Although 2026 rail inflation is expected above 4%, Union Pacific is committed to exceeding inflation with price dollars and offsetting headcount with further operational productivity gains.
  • STB’s new reciprocal switching framework and merger review inject regulatory uncertainty but are framed by management as competitive drivers rather than existential threats.

Industry glossary

  • Operating ratio (OR): A measure of operating expenses as a percentage of revenue, used to assess railroad efficiency; lower values indicate better performance.
  • ARCs (Average revenue per car): The average amount of freight revenue earned per car transported, useful for understanding business mix and pricing.
  • Intermodal: The transport of freight in containers or trailers by multiple modes (rail, truck, ship) without handling the freight itself when changing modes.
  • Terminal dwell: The average time a railcar spends in a terminal from arrival to departure, indicating network fluidity and efficiency.
  • Reciprocal switching: A regulatory policy allowing shippers access to a competing railroad’s network at a defined interchange, enhancing competitive options.
  • STB (Surface Transportation Board): The U.S. federal agency responsible for economic regulation of railroads, including mergers and access policies.
