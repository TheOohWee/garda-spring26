Risks

  • CEO Federico acknowledged, "There is a risk" that advancements in mortgage technology and changing borrower dynamics could erode traditional prepayment protections, potentially increasing refinancing speeds in a sharply lower rate environment.
  • MBS spreads to treasury and swap benchmarks ended the quarter 7 and 14 basis points wider, respectively, indicating adverse market performance for Agency mortgage-backed securities relative to benchmarks.
  • Bernice Bell indicated that higher swap hedge costs and slower deployment of newly raised capital "primarily" contributed to lower net spread and dollar roll income for the quarter.

Summary

AGNC Investment (AGNC 0.10%) reported quarter-end leverage at 7.6x and maintained a robust liquidity position, which management credited for sustaining operations through significant market volatility without forced asset sales. The company executed a nearly $800 million at-the-market equity offering at a substantial premium to book value, opting for a "patient measured" capital deployment strategy in response to continued MBS spread volatility and uncertain investor demand. Key government statements during the quarter reinforced the commitment to maintaining the implicit guarantee of Agency MBS even as GSE reform progresses, reducing perceived credit risk in the asset class.

  • CEO Federico said, "we could certainly also have the capacity to operate with slightly higher leverage" as confidence increases in mortgage spread stability and the resolution of policy uncertainty.
  • About 81% of the asset base now possesses positive prepayment characteristics, an explicit focus resulting from recent acquisitions of higher coupon specified pools.
  • Net interest rate spread narrowed due to incrementally higher swap costs, and Bernice Bell expects swap costs to rise further as hedges roll off and require replacement at less favorable terms.
  • Management emphasized that further capital raises or leverage increases will be dictated by prevailing market spreads, investor demand, and evolving regulatory developments.
  • The stability and liquidity of AGNC’s common equity, along with its $8 billion market capitalization, were identified as advantages for shareholders seeking efficient fixed income exposure.

Industry glossary

  • Specified Pools: Mortgage-backed securities containing loans with targeted characteristics, such as lower balance or prepayment protection, appealing for more stable cash flows.
  • TBA (To-Be-Announced) Position: A forward contract to buy or sell mortgage-backed securities with standardized terms, generally used to manage liquidity and hedging strategies in the MBS market.
  • CPR (Conditional Prepayment Rate): An annualized percentage indicating the rate at which principal is expected to be prepaid on a mortgage pool.
  • Swap-based Hedges: Interest rate risk protection using swaps, enabling MBS investors to offset changes in funding and asset value as rates fluctuate.
  • Duration Dollar Terms: A measure of interest rate risk that quantifies the dollar value change in a portfolio's value for a one-basis-point change in interest rates, based on duration.
  • ATM (At-the-Market) Offering Program: A capital raise mechanism where shares are sold incrementally into the market at prevailing prices, often used by REITs to strengthen liquidity.

This volatility and macroeconomic uncertainty adversely impacted Agency mortgage-backed securities with spreads to treasury and swap rates widening meaningfully. A primary focus of AGNC's risk management framework is maintaining sufficient liquidity to withstand episodes of significant financial market stress. One important measure of this capacity is the percentage of equity that we hold in unencumbered cash and Agency mortgage-backed securities, which are available to meet margin calls in the normal course of business.

This focus enabled us to begin the second quarter with a strong liquidity position and to navigate the financial market volatility without issue and importantly, without selling assets. Moreover, we were able to take advantage of the wider MBS spread environment by raising accretive capital during the quarter and opportunistically deploying a portion of that capital in attractively priced assets. Over the last 2 months of the quarter, most financial markets retraced the April losses, and in some cases, set new record highs. For example, the S&P 500 Index rallied 25% from the April low and ended the quarter about 10% higher. The one notable performance exception was Agency mortgage-backed securities as the current coupon spread to a blend of treasury and swap benchmarks ended the quarter 7 and 14 basis points wider, respectively. Although the Fed and treasury have indicated that beneficial regulatory reforms are forthcoming, bank demand for MBS still appears to be constrained. These statements individually and collectively clarify the administration's approach and more importantly, should provide investors greater confidence that the credit quality of the $8 trillion of outstanding Agency mortgage-backed securities as it is understood to be today, will not be impaired by actions associated with privatization.

In fact, given the explicit statement of credit support made by the President of the United States that the implicit guarantee of Agency MBS will be preserved, investors could reasonably conclude that the credit quality of the outstanding stock of Agency mortgage-backed securities has never been stronger. These statements also make it clear that maintaining stability in the mortgage market and lowering mortgage costs are 2 important guiding principles of GSE reform.
