Earnings Call Transcript

Standex (SXI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 30, 2026 at 2:16PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Friday, Aug. 1, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — David A. Dunbar
  • Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer — Ademir Sarcevic
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Christopher H. Howe

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $222 million, up 23.2% year-on-year, with a 23.4% contribution from recent acquisitions and 1.2% from foreign currency, partially offset by a 1.4% organic decline.
  • Adjusted operating margin -- 20.6%, a record level, up 350 basis points year-on-year and 120 basis points sequentially.
  • Adjusted earnings per share -- $2.28, up 20.6% year-on-year, reaching an all-time high.
  • Free cash flow -- $24.9 million compared to $22.2 million a year ago, reflecting improved operating cash conversion despite higher capital expenditures.
  • Electronics segment revenue -- $115.2 million, up 43.2% year-on-year, with acquisitions contributing 41%, organic growth of 0.3%, and a 1.9% foreign currency impact.
  • Electronics book-to-bill -- 1.03, with orders totaling approximately $118 million, representing a sequential increase of $10 million.
  • Engineering Technologies segment revenue -- $32 million, up 26.8% year-on-year, with a 25% acquisition contribution, 0.9% organic growth, and 0.9% from foreign currency.
  • Scientific segment revenue -- $17.9 million, up 2.3% year-on-year, driven by a 16.1% acquisition benefit but offset by a 13.9% organic decline mainly due to lower demand from channels affected by NIH funding cuts.
  • Engraving segment revenue -- $33 million, up 0.6% year-on-year, with a 1.2% foreign currency benefit and a 0.6% organic decline.
  • Specialty Solutions segment revenue -- $23.9 million, down 1.2% year-on-year, attributed to market softness in segment end markets.
  • Net leverage ratio -- 2.6x, reduced from the prior year through debt paydown and cash repatriation; net debt was $448 million versus net cash of $5.3 million at prior year-end.
  • Sales into fast growth end markets -- $184 million for the fiscal year, exceeding the $170 million target, and expected to exceed $265 million in fiscal 2026 with approximately 45% projected growth.
  • New product sales contribution -- Accounted for approximately 2.8% of total company sales in the quarter, exceeding the 2% goal, and expected to add about 3% incremental sales in fiscal 2026 with over 15 new products planned for release.
  • Adjusted gross margin and adjusted operating income -- Both achieved record levels for the third consecutive quarter.
  • Dividend -- 244th consecutive quarterly dividend of $0.32 per share, a 6.7% increase year-on-year.
  • Capital expenditures guidance -- Anticipated at $33 million to $38 million for fiscal 2026.
  • Amran/Narayan Croatia facility -- Site established to serve European customers, with first product shipments expected within four months and single-digit millions in sales projected for fiscal 2026.
  • Guidance for next fiscal year -- Management expects revenue growth of over $100 million and continued adjusted operating margin expansion, led by mid- to high single-digit organic growth in Electronics, double-digit organic growth in Engineering Technologies, and incremental benefit from recent acquisitions.

Risks

  • Scientific segment organic revenue declined 13.9% due to "lower demand from academic and research institutions that were impacted by NIH funding cuts."
  • Sequential revenue and margin in Engineering Technologies are expected to be "Sequentially, we expect slightly lower revenue and adjusted operating margin due to project timing," while Electronics and Engraving will experience seasonality impacts in Europe, potentially leading to lower sequential results.
  • Management notes that revenue and margin guidance for fiscal 2026 excludes the impact of "unforeseen economic global trade or tariff-related disruptions."
  • Acquisition-related longer customer credit terms and less robust receivables processes have increased DSO to "about 69 or 70" days, though management expects improvements over the coming year.

Summary

Standex International Corporation (SXI 1.60%) delivered record profitability in the quarter, with significant year-on-year growth in revenue and margin driven primarily by acquisitions and expansion into fast growth markets. Management anticipates over $100 million in incremental revenue for fiscal 2026, backed by further contributions from new products, increased capacity, and the integration of recent acquisitions. Segment performance varied, with robust Electronics growth offset by meaningful organic contraction in the Scientific division due to NIH funding headwinds.

  • David A. Dunbar affirmed, "We remain on track to achieve our fiscal 2028 long-term targets of sales of greater than $1.15 billion and adjusted operating margin of greater than 23%."
  • Guidance for the upcoming year features expected sales into fast growth end markets growing by approximately 45%, notably supported by defense, grid, and space commercialization programs.
  • Full-year impact from the Amran/Narayan and McStarlite acquisitions is projected to be "over $60 million," with organic growth and new products adding additional upside potential.
  • Adjusted earnings per share reached $7.98 for the fiscal year, reflecting an approximate 6% increase, with margin expansion enabled by pricing and productivity initiatives.

Industry glossary

  • Book-to-bill ratio: The ratio of orders received (bookings) to units shipped and billed, used as a short-term demand indicator.
  • Fast growth end markets: Customer industries (such as data centers, electrical grid, defense, and space) identified for above-average, secular, multi-year growth potential within Standex’s portfolio.
  • DSO (days sales outstanding): The average number of days it takes a company to collect payment after a sale, used as an indicator of receivables management.
  • Adjusted operating margin: Operating margin calculated after excluding certain items such as restructuring, acquisition costs, and one-time expenses, to better indicate ongoing business performance.
