National Vision (EYE) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 4, 2026 at 10:06AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, March 4, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Alex Wilkes
  • Chief Financial Officer — Christopher Laden
  • Investor Relations — Tamara Gonzalez

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Revenue -- $1.99 billion for the year, up 9%.
  • Adjusted Comparable Store Sales -- Increased 6% for the year; Q4 comp store sales rose 4.8%.
  • Adjusted Operating Income -- Grew 56% to $102.5 million for the year; Q4 adjusted operating income was $17.6 million.
  • Adjusted Operating Margin -- Expanded by 160 basis points to 5.2% for the year; Q4 margin reached 3.5%, an increase of 280 basis points year over year.
  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share -- $0.80 for the year, up from $0.52; Q4 adjusted EPS was $0.15 versus a Q4 loss of $0.04 last year.
  • Managed Care Revenue Mix -- Managed care accounted for 42% of total revenue at year-end; managed care comp sales grew at a low double-digit percentage rate.
  • Customer Traffic -- Total annual traffic declined 0.5%, driven by self-pay declines offset by growth in higher-value customer groups.
  • Average Ticket -- Rose 6% for the year; Q4 average ticket increased 5.8%.
  • Premium Frame Assortment -- The percentage of frames priced above $99 doubled from 20% to 40% by year-end; branded frames expected to reach 70% of assortment by year-end 2026.
  • New and Closed Stores -- 12 America's Best stores opened and 4 closed in Q4; the company ended the year with 1,250 stores.
  • Smart Glasses (Meta) -- Currently in 300 stores; company plans to roll out Meta glasses chainwide by end of Q2 2026 due to above-expectation sell-through.
  • Balance Sheet and Cash Flow -- Year-end cash balance of $38.7 million; full-year operating cash flow was $146.3 million, and net debt to adjusted EBITDA stood at 1.1x.
  • Debt Repayment -- Repaid $101.3 million of convertible notes during the year; total debt at year-end was $245.9 million (net of discounts).
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- New $50 million authorization approved, valid through December 28, 2030.
  • 53rd Week Impact -- Extra week contributed $35.6 million of net revenue and $3.5 million of adjusted operating income for the year; this impact was excluded from comp metrics.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Projected net revenue of $2.03 billion-$2.09 billion; adjusted comp store sales growth of 3%-6%; adjusted operating income of $107 million-$133 million; adjusted EPS of $0.85-$1.09.
  • Store Expansion and Optimization -- Plans to open 30–35 new stores and close 10–15 in 2026, with America's Best stores weighted toward openings.
  • Investments in Offering -- Premium lens penetration targeted to approach 60% for premium materials and 50% for anti-reflective coating from current 50% and 40% respectively.
  • Cost Savings Initiative -- On track for $10 million in annualized cost savings for 2026, contributing to SG&A leverage.
  • Technology Investments -- ERP and data platform upgrades executed, including Oracle ERP and Adobe CRM/Digital Experience Platform roll-outs.

RISKS

  • Chief Executive Officer Wilkes said, "November kind of as expected, but then we did see some slowing in December, in particular with the cash pay customer," indicating lingering volatility in the self-pay segment.
  • Chief Executive Officer Wilkes described the cash pay customer as "still a bit more fickle than what we have previously experienced in years past," which may hamper broad-based traffic recovery.
  • Chief Financial Officer Laden noted Q4 customer traffic declined 2.5% year over year, and explained, "healthy trends in our managed care business continue to offset softer traffic in our cash pay business," highlighting segment divergence.
  • Chief Executive Officer Wilkes stated, "When you market two pair of eyewear for a certain price including an eye exam, and that is your primary lead offer that you deploy most of your marketing spend against, you are speaking to one customer type that is really sensitive to price. As you begin to shift that marketing expense into a marketing expense that is more targeted, you are making a conscious decision to walk away potentially from some of those cash pay consumers as you are reinvesting into the acquisition of the more valuable customer group," reflecting a strategic risk in shifting customer mix.

SUMMARY

National Vision Holdings (EYE 2.54%) reported substantial adjusted margin expansion and double-digit growth in adjusted operating income, driven by a deliberate shift to higher-value customer cohorts and premium products. Management confirmed that managed care customers now account for a greater revenue share, with strong low double-digit comp sales and growing adoption of premium frames and lenses. The rollout of Meta smart glasses exceeded expectations and is slated for full deployment by end of the second quarter, adding a differentiated product category with high transaction value. The 2026 outlook emphasizes continued progress on product mix, targeted marketing investment, and cost savings, while supporting incremental store openings and digital modernization, all underpinned by a new capital return plan.

  • Chief Executive Officer Wilkes confirmed, "We started last year with about 20% of our frame board priced over $99. We ended the year at 40%," providing momentum for ongoing ticket growth from frame premiumization.
  • Chief Financial Officer Laden said the 53rd week accounted for $35.6 million in revenue and $3.5 million in adjusted operating income, clarifying one-time factors affecting headline growth.
  • Chief Executive Officer Wilkes described managed care as "the North Star" with a long-term goal of reaching 50% of revenue, reiterating the multiyear nature of the mix shift strategy.
  • Management cited high prescription lens attachment and premium mix in Meta smart glass transactions as contributing to higher-value sales than legacy product lines.
  • Chief Executive Officer Wilkes reported, "One of the things that I was most pleased by in 2025 was, in a category where eye exams were declining between the mid- to high-single-digit range, we actually grew eye exams in the plus-one range," demonstrating outperformance versus sector headwinds.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Adjusted Operating Income (AOI): Operating income excluding certain non-recurring or non-cash charges, used by management to assess underlying business performance.
  • Managed Care: Revenue derived from customers whose eye care purchases are reimbursed through insurance or managed health plans, generally yielding higher average transaction values.
  • Outside Rx: Customers who purchase eyewear using prescriptions written by eye doctors not affiliated with National Vision Holdings' in-store optometrists.
  • Comp Store Sales (Comps): Growth measure based on sales from stores open for a full comparable period, excluding new and closed locations.
  • Premium Frame Board: The assortment of higher-priced frames (above $99) offered for sale, as opposed to basic or value-priced frames.
  • Smart Glasses / Meta: Wearable eyewear integrating digital technology or augmented reality features; in this context, refers specifically to Ray-Ban Meta collaboration products.
  • Remote Exam Technology: Platform for providing eye exams via telemedicine, allowing doctors to examine patients at multiple locations remotely.
