Zoetis (ZTS) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 11:18AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Monday, November 4, 2024 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kristin Peck
  • Chief Financial Officer — Wetteny Joseph
  • Vice President of Investor Relations — Steve Frank

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $2.4 billion, 11% reported growth and 14% operational growth, with 8% from volume and 6% from price.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $716 million, up 14% reported and 15% operationally.
  • U.S. Sales Growth -- 15% revenue growth; U.S. companion animal up 18%, and livestock up 5%.
  • Adjusted Gross Margin -- 70.7%, an increase of 20 basis points, with gains from price and mix offset by higher manufacturing costs.
  • Simparica Franchise Revenue -- $333 million globally, with 27% operational growth globally and 29% U.S. growth (revenue of $237 million in U.S.); puppy share of Simparica Trio reached 35%.
  • Key Dermatology Franchise -- $449 million in global revenue, 16% operational growth; U.S. revenue $303 million, up 17%, with Apoquel leading and chewable growth offsetting declines in tablets.
  • OA Pain Monoclonal Antibody (mAb) Franchise -- $151 million globally; U.S. sales for Librela and Solensia combined at $73 million ($55 million Librela, $18 million Solensia); international OA pain mAbs $78 million, with $62 million Librela, $16 million Solensia.
  • Librela Penetration -- 85% in U.S. clinics following launch, surpassing earlier product introductions.
  • Livestock Segment -- $758 million in global revenue; U.S. livestock up 5%, international livestock up 15% operationally, mainly from cattle and poultry price and volume gains.
  • Alternative Channels -- Retail and home delivery accounted for nearly 15% of U.S. business, with retail growth at 34% year over year.
  • Medicated Feed Additive (MFA) Divestiture -- Closed in Q4, with an estimated $400 million in 2023 revenue and about 30% margin; will reduce Q4 2024 sales by two months for U.S., and one month internationally.
  • Full Year 2024 Guidance -- Raised and narrowed: revenue of $9.2 billion to $9.3 billion (10%-11% operational growth); adjusted net income of $2.67 billion to $2.695 billion (13.5%-14.5% operational growth); adjusted diluted EPS of $5.86 to $5.92; reported diluted EPS of $5.33 to $5.39.
  • Triple-Combination Parasiticide Market -- Trio is the number one vet-prescribed U.S. parasiticide, treating 13 million dogs in four years, and Zoetis increased share after first year of direct competition, with almost 60% of medicalized dogs still not on triple-combination therapies.
  • International Segment Results -- Revenue grew 7% reported and 13% operationally; international companion animal revenue up 11% operationally, Simparica franchise up 28%, with Simparica Trio up 39% in Europe and China launch cited.
  • China Growth Headwind -- Headwinds estimated to have a roughly 1% negative impact for FY 2024, expected to normalize in 2025.
  • Stocking Dynamics -- Q3 showed "a clean quarter across our entire business," with no significant inventory or stocking effects per Kristin Peck.
  • Price Realization -- Price increases, excluding Argentina, averaged 4.5%-5% across the portfolio; overall growth reflected both price and targeted promotions, with improved compliance in oral medications.

Summary

Zoetis (ZTS 1.77%) reported double-digit operational growth in both revenue and adjusted net income, driven by expansion across companion animal and livestock products. Management underscored the rapid adoption of Librela, with 1 million dogs treated and 85% U.S. clinic penetration, noting substantial opportunity to convert the estimated 8 million dogs on NSAIDs, and additional untreated OA cases. The company completed the divestiture of its Medicated Feed Additive portfolio, which management expects to impact Q4 comparables and help refocus capital toward higher-growth franchises. Internationally, strong price momentum continued in high-inflation markets, especially in cattle and poultry, while China growth headwinds are anticipated to dissipate by 2025. Zoetis raised full-year guidance for both top and bottom-line metrics, citing outperformance in key segments and plans to maintain above-market growth through continued investment in innovation and strategic channel expansion.

  • Apoquel remains the top animal-health prescription globally, supported by high satisfaction, and new lifecycle products such as the chewable formulation.
  • Simparica Trio's clinical adoption accelerated through alternative channels, and continued to lead in puppy share, indicating future market gains as conversion from older therapies increases.
  • Leadership acknowledged that new market entrants typically expand the total addressable market for oral parasiticides, rather than erode Zoetis' existing share, due to first-mover and product differentiation advantages.
  • Operational leverage was cited as a driver for adjusted net income growth exceeding revenue growth, as past field force investments and DTC spending support scale.
  • Segmental performance benefited from strong demand in OA pain and dermatology, with higher compliance and longer therapy duration trends revealed for both canine and feline portfolios overseas.
  • While recent Argentina pricing contributed to top-line gains, management said future growth will more closely reflect historical price trends as inflation moderates.

Industry glossary

  • OA pain mAbs: Monoclonal antibodies targeting osteoarthritis pain, offering a non-NSAID therapy for animals with chronic joint discomfort.
  • Simparica Trio: A triple-combination oral parasiticide chewable for dogs, protecting against fleas, ticks, and heartworms.
  • Alternative channels: Distribution platforms beyond traditional veterinary clinics, including retail, e-commerce, and home delivery, facilitating increased product accessibility and compliance.
  • Medicated Feed Additive (MFA): Veterinary pharmaceutical products incorporated into livestock feed to prevent or treat disease and improve production efficiency.
