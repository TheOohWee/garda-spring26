Fortive (FTV) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Jul 30, 2025 at 3:23PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

  • Tuesday, July 30, 2025, at noon EDT

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Illuminae Charoye
  • Chief Financial Officer — Mark Okerstrom
  • Investor Relations — Christina Jones

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • Adjusted gross margin in the Intelligent Operating Solutions segment fell to 66.1% from just under 70% in the prior year’s Q2, with Mark Okerstrom stated this decline was "primarily due to tariff cost pressures partially offset by pricing countermeasures and growth from our higher margin software businesses."
  • Uncertainty in macro conditions, including "tariff uncertainty, constrained government spending, and evolving healthcare policy dynamics," led to customer demand pressures in June, with Revenue finished approximately $30 million below expectations, according to CEO Illuminae Charoye.
  • Guidance for full-year 2025 assumes a "continuation of the market dynamics we experienced in Q2." and Management explicitly does not forecast material improvement for Q3 and Q4, flagging persistent challenges with "tariff-related cost pressures" and unpredictable "global trade and fiscal policy," as Mark Okerstrom noted.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted EPS (Consolidated and Continuing Ops): Delivered adjusted EPS of $0.90 consolidated and $0.58 for continuing operations, with the latter representing 4% year-over-year growth in adjusted EPS, driven by stable adjusted EBITDA, lower interest expense, and share repurchases.
  • Revenue: Total revenue was just over $1 billion. Down 0.4% year-over-year. Core revenue declined 0.7% year-over-year, with a late-quarter demand shortfall of about $30 million attributed to macro-driven customer deferrals.
  • Free Cash Flow: Generated $180 million of free cash flow in the second quarter, with trailing 12-month free cash flow was up 14% to $939 million on a continuing operations basis. Free cash flow conversion reached 107% of adjusted net income.
  • Segment Performance — Intelligent Operating Solutions (iOS): Revenue and core revenue growth were both flat. Adjusted gross profit was $461 million, slightly down. Adjusted EBITDA grew 2% to $236 million as lower operating costs offset a modest gross profit decline.
  • Segment Performance — Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS): Revenue was $320 million, down 1.3% (down 1.9% core). Adjusted gross margins for the Advanced Healthcare Solutions segment improved to just over 59% from just under 58% on favorable pricing and a shift to higher margin software. Adjusted EBITDA was flat at $86 million, with margin rising modestly to 26.9%.
  • Tariffs: Estimated gross tariff impact for continuing operations is projected at $40 million to $55 million for 2025 and $80 million to $120 million on an annualized basis, with U.S.-China exposure dominating; Countermeasures are expected to fully mitigate gross tariff effects by Q4 2025.
  • Capital Deployment: $140 million deployed to share repurchases. Over 75% of consolidated free cash flow from the time of the spin announcement in September 2024 through completion in June 2025 was used for buybacks; $1.15 billion Ralliant spin dividend received, with $725 million used to pay down yen and euro debt, remainder designated for further repurchases.
  • Balance Sheet: Gross leverage ratio is approximately 2.5x adjusted EBITDA pro forma for debt repayments as of July 2025, aligning with management’s stated target.
  • Recurring Revenue: New Fortive now has 50% recurring revenue in its revenue base as of Q3 2025, with Fluke “double-digit ARR growth” and iOS software renewals showed “very good NDR.”
  • Guidance & Phasing: Initiated full-year adjusted EPS guidance for continuing operations at $2.50 to $2.60 for FY2025; expects “Q3 reported revenue to be broadly similar to Q2,” Core revenue growth in the second half of 2025 is expected to be broadly in the range of the core growth seen in the first half, and A seasonal Q4 uplift is expected, with Q3 adjusted EPS slightly below Q2's $0.58 due to tariff and tax phasing.
  • Cost Discipline: Approximately half of stranded costs from the spin have already been eliminated, with the remainder targeted over the next twelve months, and additional efficiency initiatives underway.
  • Organic Growth Drivers: Management highlighted “innovation acceleration, commercial acceleration, and recurring customer value” as the three organic growth levers, with Fluke and Gordian cited as key contributors to growth, though Fluke backlog increased due to an end-of-quarter mix shift, and Gordian was impacted by year-end government spending pullbacks.
  • M&A Strategy: Management stated a disciplined return-based approach to bolt-on M&A, with ongoing pipeline development but heightened financial and strategic scrutiny for all prospective deals.
  • Reporting Approach: Fortive will provide annual adjusted EPS guidance only, with quarterly color on phasing, in response to feedback requesting simplified investor communication.

SUMMARY

Fortive Corp. (FTV 3.10%) concluded its last quarter as a consolidated company with the completion of the Ralliant spin-off, transforming into a more focused entity centered on Intelligent Operating Solutions and Advanced Healthcare Solutions. Management reported a year-over-year revenue decline and a late-quarter demand shortfall, attributing this to tariff uncertainty, government spending delays, and healthcare policy changes, but noted early July order trends were stabilizing and backlog is expected to convert in future periods. Tariff-related cost headwinds impaired segment adjusted gross margins and contributed to conservative full-year adjusted EPS guidance of $2.50 to $2.60 per share, which assumes a continuation of the market dynamics experienced in Q2 and does not forecast any material improvement in macro conditions. The company executed major capital actions, retiring foreign currency debt and prioritizing share repurchases with proceeds from the spin dividend, reducing leverage to its targeted range. Fortive’s guidance methodology shifts to annual adjusted EPS with limited quarterly granularity, while leadership emphasized confidence in long-term targets and ongoing acceleration initiatives despite current macro-driven revenue volatility.

  • Okerstrom stated, "recent near-term revenue volatility has absolutely no impact on our confidence in the future outlook for our business," reinforcing management's assertion that medium-term financial targets remain on track.
  • Charoye flagged that 50% of the revenue base is recurring for New Fortive beginning in Q3 2025, with Fluke subscription revenues grew at double-digit rates and iOS software “renewal rates are really strong across the board.”
  • Late-quarter revenue softness primarily affected Fluke (professional instrumentation), AHS capital equipment, and Gordian (government-related procurement), each tied to temporary customer deferrals rather than lost business, with project pipelines and backlogs described as intact for deferred demand recovery.
  • Tariff mitigation actions -- pricing, supply chain shifts, and productivity measures -- are expected to fully phase in during the second half of 2025, with a modest negative impact to margins and earnings expected in Q3 before improvement in Q4.
  • Leadership emphasized a “very different capital allocation play call,” balancing accretive bolt-on M&A with disciplined share repurchases and a methodical framework for deal evaluation amid ongoing proprietary pipeline engagement.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • iOS (Intelligent Operating Solutions): Fortive’s segment focused on connected reliability, EHSQ and facility software, and advanced instrumentation across industrial and infrastructure end-markets.
  • AHS (Advanced Healthcare Solutions): Fortive’s segment providing device reprocessing hardware and software, asset tracking, radiation safety, and subscription-based surgical inventory management to healthcare clients.
  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): A metric tracking the annualized value of recurring revenue streams, such as subscriptions, reflecting the strength of Fortive’s SaaS and service-based business models.
  • NDR (Net Dollar Retention): The percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers over a period, after factoring in expansions, contractions, and churn, used as a key software renewal performance indicator.
  • Stranded Costs: Overhead expenses remaining with Fortive after the Ralliant spin-off that are not directly associated with continuing operations, targeted for reduction to optimize profitability.
