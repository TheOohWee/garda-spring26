Seagate (STX) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 11:58AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Jan. 21, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — David Mosley
  • Chief Financial Officer — Gianluca Romano
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations — Shanye Hudson

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $2.33 billion, up 7% sequentially, reflecting higher demand across most markets with particular strength in the cloud segment.
  • Non-GAAP operating income -- $538 million, representing a 22% sequential increase and a four-fold year-over-year rise.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $2.03, reaching its highest level in 12 quarters, at the upper end of guidance.
  • Non-GAAP gross margin -- 35.5%, up 220 basis points sequentially, marking the seventh straight quarter of margin improvement.
  • Hard disk drive (HDD) revenue -- $2.2 billion, an 8% quarter-over-quarter increase, supported by shipment of 151 exabytes versus 138 exabytes previously.
  • Mass capacity revenue -- $1.9 billion, a 9% increase sequentially, with exabyte shipments up to 140 exabytes; mass capacity revenue has risen for six consecutive quarters.
  • Nearline exabyte shipments -- 126 exabytes, up from 114 exabytes, including a revised reporting structure that now incorporates shipments to VIA customers.
  • Legacy product sales -- $275 million, up 2% sequentially on seasonal consumer demand.
  • Other business revenue -- $156 million, slightly down sequentially due to lower SSD sales, partially offset by system demand increase.
  • Non-GAAP gross profit -- $825 million, a 14% sequential increase, reflecting the favorable mix of high-capacity products and ongoing price and cost initiatives.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $591 million, up 19% quarter over quarter.
  • Free cash flow -- $150 million, up from $27 million in the prior period.
  • Capital expenditures -- $71 million, with fiscal 2025 CapEx (ending June 27, 2025) expected at the low end of the 4%-6% of revenue target range.
  • Debt reduction -- $479 million of notes retired in early January, leaving no additional debt maturities until late fiscal 2027.
  • Net leverage ratio -- 2.5x at quarter-end, with management expecting further reduction.
  • Available liquidity -- $2.7 billion, including undrawn revolver facilities.
  • Inventory -- $1.5 billion, increased to support new high-capacity product ramp.
  • Dividend return -- $148 million returned to shareholders via quarterly dividend payments.
  • HAMR Mozaic platform -- Initiated ramp and multiple customer qualifications on next-generation drives; sampled 36TB drives during the quarter.
  • Supply constraint update -- Production issue resolved, but approximately $200 million revenue impact expected for the upcoming quarter; all major build-to-order commitments will be met.
  • Guidance for next quarter -- Expected revenue of $2.1 billion, plus or minus $150 million, with more than 25% year-over-year growth at the midpoint; non-GAAP EPS guided to $1.70, plus or minus $0.20.
  • Gross margin outlook -- Potential for further sequential improvement despite supply constraints, driven by mix and cost efficiencies.

Summary

Seagate Technology Holdings (STX +0.47%) delivered significant sequential and year-over-year improvements in revenue, operating income, and gross margin, anchored by heightened cloud demand and rapid advances in high-capacity nearline and HAMR drives. Management resolved a recently disclosed production issue, while making clear that $200 million of lost March-quarter sales will not be recaptured, although all build-to-order obligations to key customers remain unaffected. Guidance projects continued profitability improvement even amid near-term supply constraints, underpinned by effective execution in technology transitions and expectations for expanded gross margins as high-capacity product mix increases.

  • The company revised its reporting of nearline exabyte shipments to include VIA customer volumes, aligning with shifts in customer drive utilization and prevailing industry practices.
  • No additional capital expenditures or added head/media unit capacity are planned for the HAMR transition; efficiency gains are expected through areal density enhancements rather than further equipment investments.
  • Leadership emphasized that gross margin expansion is being driven by high-capacity product sales, consistent pricing discipline, and cost structure efficiencies, noting both non-HAMR and HAMR products contribute to these gains.
  • The HAMR Mozaic platform now has multiple customers qualified and is accelerating volume shipments, with management identifying increasing customer pull for new drives as the key factor in the ongoing technology adoption cycle.
  • Management's outlook anticipates the majority of incremental HAMR volume ramp and related margin uplift will occur in the second half of calendar 2025, following additional customer qualifications now underway.

Industry glossary

  • HAMR: Heat-Assisted Magnetic Recording, an advanced data storage technology enabling higher areal density and increased drive capacities in hard disk drives.
  • Nearline: HDD or SSD drives designed for high-capacity, non-real-time storage in enterprise and cloud environments, balancing cost, density, and reliability.
  • Mozaic platform: Seagate’s proprietary platform implementing HAMR technology for the next generation of high-capacity drives.
  • VIA: Video, Image, and Analytics; refers to a market segment focused on storing and analyzing large-scale video and image data, often at the network edge.
  • Build-to-order (BTO): Customer engagement model where storage systems are manufactured to specific client configurations and delivery schedules, enhancing supply chain predictability and visibility.
