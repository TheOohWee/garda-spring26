Takeaways

  • Total revenues -- $599 million for the quarter, including $546.6 million from the cabozantinib franchise net product revenues.
  • CABOMETYX US net product revenues -- $544.7 million in Q4, with full-year US cabo franchise net product revenues increasing 17% to about $2.12 billion.
  • Global cabo franchise sales -- $754 million in the quarter and $2.89 billion for the year, including revenue from Exelixis and partners.
  • Neuroendocrine tumor indication -- US CABOMETYX net product revenues exceeded $100 million in 2025 for this segment.
  • Market share dynamics -- CABOMETYX gained three percentage points of TRx share, reaching 46% in Q4. TRx volume increased 15%, outpacing the market basket's 7% growth.
  • Gross to net deductions -- Stood at 28.5% in the quarter, lower than the year's average, driven mainly by reduced PHS and 340B volume; full-year 2026 estimate is 31%-32%.
  • Royalty revenues -- $52.8 million in royalties reported from partners Ipsen and Takeda’s cabozantinib sales for Q4.
  • Operating expenses -- $363 million, excluding restructuring, for the quarter, mainly reflecting higher R&D, NDA filing fees, personnel, and marketing.
  • GAAP net income -- $244.5 million for the year, with GAAP earnings per share of $2.00 basic and $0.88 diluted.
  • Non-GAAP net income -- $259.5 million for the year, or $0.97 basic and $0.94 diluted, after excluding $15 million in stock-based compensation expense net of tax.
  • Cash and marketable securities -- $1.66 billion at year-end.
  • Share repurchase activity -- $954 million spent in 2025, retiring about 24 million shares at an average price of $39.61; $590 million remains under the current $750 million program.
  • ZANZA NDA acceptance -- The FDA accepted the NDA for Zanza plus atezolizumab in third-line-plus colorectal cancer, with a PDUFA date set for December.
  • STELLAR-303 trial outcome -- This pivotal trial met one dual primary endpoint, showing a 20% reduction in death risk in the ITT CRC population. Final results for non-liver metastases population are expected midyear.
  • Pivotal trials and pipeline -- Seven ZANZA pivotal trials are ongoing or soon to start, including STELLAR-316 (patients with molecular residual disease in CRC) and STELLAR-304 (non-clear cell RCC), among others.
  • Commercial team expansion -- The GI sales team was rapidly built out in January to drive CABOMETYX growth and prepare for ZANZA's potential launch.
  • Business development -- Ongoing focus on late-stage assets in GU and GI oncology, with a “back-end loaded pay-for-success” approach and continued share repurchases if shares are deemed undervalued.
  • Guidance disclosure -- 2026 financial guidance was provided at the JPMorgan Conference and is detailed in the company's slide deck.

Summary

Exelixis (EXEL 2.51%) reported product revenue acceleration led by CABOMETYX’s market share growth and penetration in neuroendocrine tumors, with U.S. net product revenues of approximately $2.12 billion for the year. Management stated that CABOMETYX retained its position as the number one prescribed TKI for renal cell carcinoma, while the ZANZA NDA acceptance and upcoming STELLAR trial readouts establish regulatory and commercial inflection points for portfolio expansion in colorectal and renal cell cancers. Executive commentary outlined a multi-franchise strategy targeting GI and GU oncology, highlighted GI sales team expansion, and reaffirmed commitment to both business development and capital returns. The company maintained liquidity with nearly $1.66 billion in cash and marketable securities post year-end, while executing share repurchase activity. The clinical pipeline, featuring seven ZANZA pivotal trials and several early-stage molecules, positions Exelixis to pursue additional oncology franchise opportunities.

  • The company’s operational focus was described as “singular” in building multi-product, multi-tumor franchises anchored by scientific leadership in GU and aspirational expansion into GI oncology.
  • Management confirmed expectations for continued revenue and prescription share growth, driven by both expanded indications and new product launches.
  • Executives cited “decisive” steps to accelerate GI sales force expansion and anticipate immediate impact on CABOMETYX neuroendocrine tumor revenue ahead of ZANZA’s CRC launch.
  • ZANZA’s franchise potential is reinforced by regulatory momentum and the anticipated breadth of pivotal trial initiations in new oncology indications.
  • Financial discipline was noted, with guidance for 2026 provided at the JPMorgan Conference, and cash deployment priorities allocated across pipeline advancement, external asset acquisition, and opportunistic share buybacks.

Industry glossary

  • GU oncology: Cancers originating in genitourinary organs, such as kidney (renal cell), bladder, and prostate.
  • GI oncology: Cancers of the gastrointestinal tract, including colorectal, stomach, and pancreas.
  • TKI: Tyrosine kinase inhibitor, a class of targeted cancer therapies that block specific enzymes involved in tumor growth.
  • PHS: Public Health Service, relevant to discounted drug programs impacting gross to net calculations.
  • 340B: A federal drug discount program for eligible healthcare organizations and covered entities.
  • TRx: Total prescriptions, a measure of prescription drug volume across all settings.
  • PDUFA date: Prescription Drug User Fee Act–mandated FDA target date for regulatory action on an NDA or BLA.
  • ITT population: Intention-to-treat population, comprising all patients randomized in a clinical trial regardless of outcome.
  • NLM population: Non-liver metastasis population in a trial, referring to patients whose cancers have not metastasized to the liver.
  • MRD: Molecular residual disease, presence of cancer-specific DNA after definitive therapy.
  • ADC: Antibody-drug conjugate, a class of targeted cancer therapeutics combining antibodies and cytotoxic drugs.
