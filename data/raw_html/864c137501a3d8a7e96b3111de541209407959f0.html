IDEXX (IDXX) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 2, 2026 at 9:41AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 2, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Jay Mazelsky
  • Chief Financial Officer — Andrew Emerson
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — John Ravis

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $943 million in the quarter, representing 14% reported growth and 12% organic growth.
  • CAG Diagnostics recurring revenue -- Increased 10% organically, with United States recurring revenues up 9% organically, and international recurring revenue up 12% organically.
  • Premium instrument placements -- 6,567 premium instruments placed in the quarter, up 42%, with 1,900 InVue DX placements supporting a 69% organic year-over-year rise in CAG Diagnostic instrument revenues.
  • Installed base expansion -- Global premium instrument installed base expanded 12%, including nearly 6,400 InVue DX instruments for the full year.
  • Consumable revenue -- IDEXX VetLab consumable revenues rose 15% organically, reflecting increased use of new menu additions and installed base growth.
  • Reference lab revenue -- Global Reference Lab revenues grew 9% organically, supported by volume growth and net price improvement.
  • Rapid assay revenue -- Declined 3% organically, affected by continued transition of pancreatic lipase to Catalyst, and pressure on US wellness visits.
  • Imaging revenue -- Increased 13% organically, led by double-digit growth in cloud-based practice information management systems (PIMs).
  • Operating margin -- Fourth-quarter operating margin was 60.3%, up 60 basis points comparably after adjusting for approximately 10 basis points of negative foreign exchange impact.
  • EPS -- $3.08 in the quarter, up 17% year over year on a comparable basis, including a 7¢ per share tax benefit from share-based compensation, and a $0.09 tailwind from foreign exchange.
  • Full-year operating margin -- 31.6%, an increase of 90 basis points on a comparable basis, excluding a 180 basis point benefit from lapping a now concluded litigation expense.
  • Free cash flow -- $1.1 billion for the year, representing 100% conversion of net income, and $125 million in capital spending (3% of revenue).
  • Share repurchases -- $1.2 billion allocated to repurchase 2.4 million shares at an average cost of $506, resulting in a 2.7% reduction in diluted shares outstanding.
  • Leverage ratios -- Year-end gross leverage of 0.5x, and net leverage of 0.4x.
  • 2026 revenue guidance -- $4.632 billion to $4.720 billion, an increase of 7.6%-9.6% reported and 7%-9% organically, with foreign exchange expected to add 60 basis points predominantly in the first half.
  • 2026 CAG Diagnostics recurring revenue guidance -- 8%-10% organic growth expected, with global net price realization of approximately 4% (3.5% in the US), and incorporated decline in same-store visits of approximately 2%.
  • Instrument revenue outlook -- Decline anticipated in 2026 as the company laps the strong 2025 IDEXX InVue DX placements, and expects regional revenue mix changes.
  • 2026 operating margin guidance -- 32%-32.5% reported, representing 30-80 basis points of comparable improvement; foreign exchange benefit of about 30 basis points, and a 20 basis point headwind due to prior year litigation accrual adjustment.
  • 2026 EPS guidance -- $14.29 to $14.80, representing 10%-14% comparable growth, net of a 1% headwind from 2025 litigation adjustment; includes $34 million net interest expense, and $0.22 benefit from foreign exchange.
  • 2026 free cash flow guidance -- Net income to free cash flow conversion ratio projected at 85%-95%, with $180 million (about 4% of revenues) in capital spending, and share buybacks to support a further 1%-2% decline in diluted shares.
  • Fiscal first quarter 2026 revenue guidance -- 11.5%-13.5% reported growth, including 2.5% benefit from foreign exchange; organic growth of 9%-11%, and CAG Diagnostic recurring revenues up 8.5%-10.5%, benefiting from approximately 50 basis points of equivalent days.

Risks

  • Andrew Emerson said, "Pressure on clinical visits remains a headwind to the sector," with US same-store clinical visits declining approximately 1.7% in the quarter, and projected to decline 2% for the full year 2026.
  • Rapid Assay revenues declined 3% organically in the quarter, negatively impacted by reduced US wellness visit volumes, and ongoing product mix transition.
  • Instrument revenue is expected to decline in 2026 as the company laps rapid prior-year growth in IDEXX InVue DX instrument placements, and faces regional revenue mix dynamics.

Summary

IDEXX Laboratories (IDXX 3.72%) delivered 14% reported and 12% organic revenue growth in the fourth quarter, driven by double-digit gains in core CAG Diagnostics recurring revenues, and record premium instrument placements supporting robust consumable growth. Management provided 2026 guidance assuming 7%-9% organic growth, operating margin expansion, and continued aggressive capital deployment to repurchase shares, while also stating a decline is anticipated in instrument revenues as the company laps a high-growth launch period. The company's planned innovation rollout includes international introduction and menu expansion of the CancerDx platform for canine oncology, and controlled initial commercialization of fine needle aspirate (FNA) cytology applications, aiming to further drive utilization. Completed commercial expansions in key international geographies are now fully operational, and expected to generate stronger sales engagement and customer face time, particularly in Germany, the United Kingdom, and Australia.

  • Fourth-quarter operating margin improved to 60.3%, with the majority of the expansion attributed to consumable growth and higher reference lab margins, partially offset by the business mix related to instrument sales surge.
  • Full-year free cash flow matched net income, allowing for $1.2 billion in share repurchases, and modest reductions in overall leverage ratios.
  • IDEXX software, including cloud-based PIMs and pet-owner engagement tools like Velo, contributed double-digit recurring revenue growth and higher client utilization rates, with Velo user base up 40% sequentially.
  • Management attributed robust recurring revenue resilience to high customer retention, continued diagnostic innovation adoption, and ongoing volume expansion even as sector clinical visits remain pressured.
  • Reference Laboratory business realized competitive share gains partly due to new cancer testing capabilities, with CancerDx expanding its usage both as a stand-alone and as part of diagnostic panels.
  • Gross margin improvement in 2026 is expected to primarily drive operating margin expansion, supported by growth in high-margin business segments, and ongoing productivity initiatives.

Industry glossary

  • CAG: Companion Animal Group, IDEXX's primary business segment focused on veterinary diagnostics for pets.
  • InVue DX: IDEXX's point-of-care instrument platform enabling automated cell cytology and other diagnostic testing.
  • CancerDx: IDEXX's proprietary diagnostic panel for detecting canine cancer types, including lymphoma and mast cell tumors.
  • PIMs: Practice Information Management Systems, software solutions for veterinary clinic operations and diagnostics integration.
  • FNA: Fine Needle Aspirate, a cytology technique for sampling masses or lesions in veterinary patients.
  • Velo: IDEXX's cloud-based pet-owner engagement and practice communication platform.
