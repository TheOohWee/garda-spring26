STERIS (STE) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total as-reported revenue growth -- 9% increase, with constant currency organic revenue up 8%, driven by volume and a 200-basis-point price contribution.
  • Gross margin -- Decreased by 70 basis points to 43.9%, as higher tariffs and inflation offset positive price and productivity impacts.
  • EBIT margin -- Decreased by 40 basis points to 22.9%, largely due to lower gross margin, with some offset from operating expense discipline.
  • Adjusted net income -- $249.4 million reported from continuing operations.
  • Earnings per diluted share -- $2.53, representing a 9% increase.
  • Adjusted effective tax rate -- 24.2%, a decline from 24.5%, attributed to changes in geographic mix.
  • Free cash flow -- $736.8 million for the first nine months, with improvement driven by higher earnings and reduced capital spending.
  • Total debt -- $1.9 billion at period end, with gross debt to EBITDA of approximately 1.2.
  • Healthcare segment revenue -- Constant currency organic growth of 8%, with service up 11%, consumables up 8%, and capital equipment up 7%; backlog remained over $400 million.
  • Healthcare EBIT margin -- Decreased by 100 basis points to 24.3%, as tariffs and inflation outweighed volume pricing and restructuring benefits.
  • AST segment revenue -- Constant currency organic revenue increased 8%, led by 9% growth in services and 103% growth in capital equipment.
  • AST EBIT margin -- Increased by 30 basis points to 45.1%, as pricing and volume offset higher labor and energy costs, and adverse mix from capital growth.
  • Life sciences segment revenue -- Constant currency organic revenue rose 5%, driven by 11% growth in consumables and 7% growth in capital equipment; backlog exceeded $100 million.
  • Life sciences margin -- Fell by 20 basis points, as tariffs and inflation exceeded volume and price gains.
  • Tariff impact -- Approximately $16 million in pretax tariffs incurred, mainly affecting the healthcare segment, and $55 million expected for the year.
  • Fiscal 2026 outlook -- Maintained guidance of 8%-9% as-reported revenue growth, 7%-8% constant currency organic revenue growth, and $10.15-$10.30 earnings per share range, while noting "the higher end of that range is less likely" due to increased tariffs.
  • Free cash flow target -- $850 million expected for the fiscal year, with capital expenditures budgeted at $375 million.
  • Healthcare service outlook -- Management expects the high growth rate to decelerate, with guidance aligning to current quarter levels or below.
  • Capital equipment orders -- Healthcare segment orders down 1% year-to-date against challenging prior-year comparisons, while backlog remains stable above $400 million.
  • Life sciences order activity -- Backlog growth is attributed to a recovery in pharma sector spending, and a better macro backdrop compared to the previous year.
  • EMEA commercial push -- Management confirmed active go-to-market and structural changes in EMEA, and ongoing work toward an "integrated model."

RISKS

  • Burton said, "we do have a bit of a slowdown in the second half," noting tough comparisons in AST and decelerating healthcare services growth.
  • Management described increased tariffs as a headwind, stating, "Although with $10 million more in anticipated tariffs, the higher end of that range is less likely."
  • Gross margin and EBIT margin declines were explicitly attributed to inflation and tariff pressures outpacing positive pricing and productivity impacts.
  • Burton cautioned that Q4 free cash flow variability could be affected by timing of capital activity and collections, as well as potential weather-related disruptions.

SUMMARY

STERIS (STE 0.59%) reported high single-digit organic growth across all segments and maintained its full-year outlook, while explicitly flagging Q4 deceleration and tariff pressures as reasons for conservatism at the upper end of guidance. Margin performance diverged by segment, as inflation and tariffs offset price and productivity gains, and margin headwinds were most acute in Healthcare. Segment commentary revealed continued strength in Life Sciences capital orders, stable Healthcare backlog, and robust AST capital equipment performance, with ongoing EMEA structural changes supporting management's expansion strategy.

  • Management described tariff mitigation as "a wide variety" of efforts involving product movement, supplier negotiations, cost reduction, and operational efficiencies.
  • Carestio stated the ASC (ambulatory surgery center) shift creates "new capacity demands" and has had a generally positive impact for STERIS due to higher clinical support needs.
  • Life Sciences backlog growth was directly linked to recovery in pharmaceutical customer spending—management described "much better spot from a macro perspective."
  • AST services growth variability within the quarter was attributed to "There wasn't anything uniquely geographic, wasn't any customer subsegment that we look at that was off. It was just a general softness in the volumes that we're seeing across the global network" early in the period, which had resolved by December.
  • No material PPE or API supply chain onshoring effects were observed, though STERIS could benefit if announced initiatives proceed.
  • Management confirmed ongoing discipline regarding M&A, with smaller bolt-on and channel acquisitions continuing, while larger M&A must meet clear financial and customer value criteria.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AST (Applied Sterilization Technologies): Business segment offering contract sterilization and laboratory services to device and pharma manufacturers.
  • ASC (Ambulatory Surgery Center): Standalone outpatient surgical facilities requiring specialized sterilization and clinical support services.
  • EMEA: Europe, Middle East, and Africa geographic region.
  • API (Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient): Primary component in drug manufacturing relevant to pharmaceutical supply chain discussions.
