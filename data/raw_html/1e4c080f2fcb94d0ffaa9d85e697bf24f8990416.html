Figma (FIG) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 18, 2026 at 6:23PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, February 18, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Dylan Field
  • Chief Financial Officer — Praveer Melwani
  • Head of Investor Relations — Kate DeLeo

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $304 million for the quarter, representing 40% growth year over year, and $1.06 billion for the full year, up 41%.
  • Net Dollar Retention Rate (Paid Customers $10K+ ARR) -- 136%, up five percentage points sequentially from Q3, and the highest rate in the past ten quarters.
  • Gross Margin -- 86% for the quarter and 88% for the full year on a non-GAAP basis.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin -- 14% in the quarter; 12% for the full year, with non-GAAP operating income of $44 million for the quarter and $130 million for the year.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow Margin -- 13% for the quarter; adjusted free cash flow was $38 million, impacted by infrastructure investment, vendor payments timing, and a $25 million IP transfer tax related to the Weavee acquisition.
  • Cash and Marketable Securities -- $1.7 billion at the end of the year.
  • Customer Growth ($10K+ ARR Tier) -- 951 net adds for $10,000+ ARR, and 143 net adds for $100,000+ ARR, which grew 46% year over year.
  • Customer Growth ($1M+ ARR Tier) -- 67 paid customers surpassing $1 million in ARR, reflecting 68% year-over-year growth.
  • International Revenue -- Grew 45% year over year; international customers were ~85% of monthly active users but contributed 54% of revenue.
  • Figma Make Weekly Active Users -- Increased over 70% sequentially from Q3 to Q4.
  • AI Feature Adoption -- Approximately 75% of paid customers with more than $10,000 in ARR consumed AI credits weekly as of the quarter-end.
  • Outlook: Q1 2026 Revenue -- Guidance is $315 million to $317 million, representing 38% growth at the midpoint.
  • Outlook: FY 2026 Revenue -- Guidance is $1.366 billion to $1.374 billion, implying 30% growth at the midpoint.
  • FY 2026 Non-GAAP Operating Income Guidance -- $100 million to $110 million, with a midpoint non-GAAP operating margin of 8%.
  • Figma Make Usage (Enterprise) -- Over 50% of paid customers spending $100,000+ in ARR used Figma Make weekly in Q4; nearly 60% of Makefiles in 2025 were created by non-designers.
  • AI Image Editing Features -- Used more than 10 million times within weeks of launch in December 2025.

RISKS

  • Praveer Melwani stated that sequential adjusted free cash flow declined in Q4, citing "continued investment in infrastructure, and AI, changes in the timing of vendor payments, and a one time $25 million IP transfer tax payment related to our acquisition of Weavie."
  • Full-year non-GAAP operating income margin guidance for 2026 is 8% at the midpoint, down from 12% in 2025, attributed to accelerated AI and infrastructure investment.
  • Stock-based compensation was elevated in 2025 due to IPO-related expenses and one-time vesting events, but management expects improvement in proportion to revenue going forward.

SUMMARY

Figma (FIG 4.82%) delivered a quarterly revenue of $304 million, achieving record annual revenue of $1.06 billion and acceleration in growth rates, as called out in prepared remarks. Customer engagement deepened, demonstrated by an over 70% sequential increase in Figma Make weekly active users and the adoption of new AI consumption models, with 75% of enterprise customers using AI credits weekly. Strategic expansion in international markets and the successful integration of eight product launches—including AI-native features—supported outperformance in net new customer adds, especially at higher ARR tiers. Management reiterated a disciplined approach to scaling, guiding to revenue growth of 30% for 2026 and a deliberate reduction in operating margin to fund continued AI innovation and infrastructure. The company referenced March as the inflection point for monetizing both seats and credits, indicating an anticipated shift in the revenue model not reflected in historical financials.

  • Dylan Field emphasized a strategy of "as models get better, Figma gets better," clarifying ongoing investments to advance AI-driven workflows and position the platform as central to design and development.
  • Praveer Melwani described the new hybrid monetization model—combining seat and credit consumption—as an area of evolving revenue predictability, with initial telemetry and add-on pack structures designed to balance pay-as-you-go bursts and contracted commitments.
  • Expansion in enterprise segments was reinforced by partnerships such as GitHub, which leverages Figma's DevMode MCP to connect design and development workflows, and a near doubling of hyperscaler seat footprints, including significant adoption among product managers.
  • Management highlighted a continued shift in customer usage from traditional designers to expanded teams, with nearly 60% of Makefiles created by non-designers, signaling broader platform utility and the blurring of functional roles across product, engineering, and design.
  • The acquisition of Weavee broadens Figma's capabilities in AI-driven creative workflows for image, video, and motion generation, demonstrated by use cases at scale such as NVIDIA's keynote preparations.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • MCP (Model Connect Platform): Figma's integration layer linking design files and code, enabling bidirectional workflows between development and design environments.
  • DevMode MCP: Specific to development workflows, allows users to pull code and design context between Figma and agentic coding tools.
  • Figma Make: Figma's AI-powered tool for rapid prototyping, open to designers and non-designers, supporting iterative creative processes and automation.
  • AI Credits: Metered units enabling customers to access AI features within Figma products; monetization is shifting from seat-based to a hybrid seat/consumption model.
  • Governance Plus: Figma's enterprise add-on for compliance and centralized governance of design assets and workflows.
