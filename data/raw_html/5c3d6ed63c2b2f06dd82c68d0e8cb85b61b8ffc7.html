Clarus (CLAR) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 5, 2026 at 6:38PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, March 5, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Executive Chairman — Warren Kanders
  • Chief Financial Officer — Michael J. Yates
  • President, Black Diamond Equipment — McNeil Seymour Fiske

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated Revenue -- $65.4 million, down 8% reflecting softer North American wholesale at Outdoor, lower global D2C, and Adventure segment declines, with Peips divested.
  • Outdoor Segment Revenue -- Down 2.1%, with constant currency revenue down 2.9%; Apparel grew 10%, Mountain grew 0.4%, and Climb increased 4.3%.
  • Adventure Segment Revenue -- Down $2.1 million, or 10.4%, primarily from reduced OEM demand, soft US bike market, and transitioning Australian and New Zealand customers; partially offset by European expansion and RockyMounts acquisition.
  • Gross Margin (Consolidated) -- 27.7%, down from 33.4% a year ago, impacted by inventory reserves ($3.4 million Adventure, $500,000 Outdoor), tariffs, and FX losses; adjusted gross margin 33.6% compared to prior year's 38%.
  • Outdoor Segment Gross Margin -- 32.2%-30.3%, below 35.2% prior, reflecting a 630 basis point headwind from tariffs and FX contracts.
  • Adventure Segment Gross Margin -- 16% vs. prior 28.9%; would have been 34.5% excluding $3.4 million inventory reserve.
  • Tariff Impact -- Net unrecovered tariff impact was $3.4 million to Outdoor adjusted EBITDA for the year; expects remaining $2.8 million impact in 2026.
  • FX Contract Impact -- $2.2 million negative EBITDA impact year over year at Outdoor; expects a $1.6 million EBITDA pickup in 2026 due to rolling off these contracts.
  • Inventory -- $64.9 million, up from $53.5 million prior, attributable to change in recognition timing ($7.9 million), tariffs, and FX ($5 million), not incremental purchases.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $1.2 million, or 1.8% margin; $300,000 Adventure, $2.0 million Outdoor, and $1.2 million in adjusted corporate costs.
  • Restructuring Charges -- $900,000 in Q4 related to further organizational streamlining, exit of three product lines, Canadian 3PL exit, European logistics restructuring, and store closures; expects additional $1.5 million in Q1 2026.
  • Legal Costs (Section 16(b), CPSC, DOJ) -- $1.2 million in Q4 and $4.7 million for the year, adjusted out of EBITDA.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $11.6 million for 2025, down from $14.4 million prior due to inventory receipt timing at Outdoor.
  • Cash and Debt -- $30.7 million in cash; total debt of zero at year end.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Full-year sales expected at $255 million-$265 million; adjusted EBITDA $9 million-$11 million (3.8% margin at midpoint); capex $6 million-$7 million; free cash flow $3 million-$4 million.
  • Segment Guidance 2026 -- Adventure: $80 million sales; Outdoor: $180 million sales.
  • Q1 2026 Outlook -- Sales expected between $60 million and $62 million.
  • Pricing Actions -- Outdoor raised prices by $7 million-$8 million total since May 2025 to offset tariffs (75%-80% of $11 million-$12 million impact), with latest increases effective January 2026; Adventure executed price increases in Q4 (RockyMounts ~5%, +$2 million-$3 million in revenue expected for 2026), and broader increases for Rhino-Rack in Q1 2026.
  • Cost Initiatives -- Outdoor headcount down 38% from 2023 baseline (30% ex-manufacturing); Adventure streamlined facilities in New Zealand and Australia, consolidated MAXTRAX and Rhino-Rack under one roof, and shifted to new 3PL in Europe for improved service and lead times.
  • Channel Performance -- North America wholesale (Outdoor) down 10.4% (excluding FX), North America digital D2C down 0.8%; Europe wholesale up 12.1% USD (3.2% constant currency), international distributor channel up 19.3%.
  • Product Mix Evolution -- Mountain, Climb, and Apparel combined accounted for 86% of Outdoor Q4 sales, 90% full-year; Ski now less than 10% of the business and expected to fall to 5%-7% as mix stabilizes.
  • Inventory Quality -- Adventure and Outdoor both took significant inventory write-downs ($3.4 million and $500,000) to address obsolete stock, with Adventure’s step viewed as a one-time "significant step in improving quality."
  • Litigation and Regulatory -- Section 16(b) litigation ongoing; Caption Management claims settled February 2026; DOJ investigation initiated regarding avalanche beacon products, with subpoenas served and Black Diamond former executives named as targets.
  • Innovation & Growth Moves -- Adventure delivered a record number of new vehicle fitments; European platform expansion expected to yield incremental $1 million in sales; RockyMounts contributed $5 million in 2025 with further growth expected.
  • Management's Market View -- Outdoor order book for 2026 described as “holding up” despite cautious retailer behavior and weather uncertainty; Adventure notes market remains “challenging” but sees “green shoots” in Europe and Japan following new distribution and service model.

RISKS

  • Outdoor gross margins declined 280 basis points compared to the prior period due to unrecovered tariffs, FX contract losses, and inventory exits, specifically with tariffs causing a 390 basis point impact, FX contracts a 240 basis point drag, and inventory exits an 80 basis point hit.
  • Adventure segment gross margin fell to 16% from 28.9% driven by inventory reserve write-downs and lower sales volume.
  • Legal and regulatory risk remains material with ongoing DOJ criminal investigation into avalanche beacons, grand jury subpoenas to company and current/former Black Diamond employees, and unresolved regulatory scrutiny by CPSC.
  • Management stated, "to achieve our guided adjusted EBITDA, we need to hit our gross margin targets," underscoring execution risk tied closely to margin improvement rather than cost control alone.

SUMMARY

Clarus Corporation (CLAR 1.09%) delivered an 8% year-over-year revenue decline, with both Outdoor and Adventure segments pressured by tariffs, FX losses, inventory write-downs, and tepid OEM demand in key markets. Price increases, operational streamlining, and product portfolio concentration partially offset these headwinds but were insufficient to prevent sharp gross margin contraction, especially in Adventure, where inventory reserves and market softness hit results hard. The company reaffirmed 2026 guidance with consolidated sales targeted at $255 million–$265 million and adjusted EBITDA of $9 million–$11 million, contingent on improving gross margins amid continued regulatory and macroeconomic uncertainties. Adjusted free cash flow generation remained resilient, and the debt-free balance sheet, combined with reduced headcount and narrowed product focus, allows for strategic patience and capital flexibility as management awaits resolution of significant legal and regulatory actions.

  • Former President John Walbrecht and former Director of Quality Rick Vance have been named as targets in the DOJ's criminal investigation regarding Black Diamond’s avalanche beacons, with the company and multiple employees receiving grand jury subpoenas.
  • Management indicated the recent Supreme Court decision could lead to a $6.5 million recovery of IEEPA tariffs, though the most burdensome 50% Section 232 tariffs remain and are not subject to reversal.
  • Adventure segment’s new distribution warehouse in the Netherlands enabled incremental $1 million in annual revenue, expanding into smaller European customers previously unreachable from Australia.
  • The company does not plan near-term M&A, instead "sit on our cash for at least the first half of the year." to focus on internal execution following recent structural and operational changes.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • IEEPA tariffs: U.S. trade tariffs authorized under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act, affecting certain imported goods; relevant due to possible recovery of paid duties.
  • Section 232 tariffs: U.S. tariffs imposed on imported steel and aluminum for reasons of national security, referenced as the most punitive and non-recoverable for Clarus Corporation.
  • 3PL: Third-party logistics provider; in this context, refers to outsourced warehousing and distribution used to streamline Clarus’s supply chain.
  • PFAS: Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, regulated chemicals sometimes leading to specific product inventory reserves or write-downs.
  • OEM: Original Equipment Manufacturer, large customers purchasing products for integration or resale under their own brand, significant for Clarus’s Adventure segment.

We estimate that the net unrecovered impact from tariffs and duties for the year was approximately $3.4 million to adjusted EBITDA. As we roll forward into 2026, we have now offset nearly 75% of the tariff impact as best we can estimate today, leaving a $2.8 million gap this coming fiscal year. Over time, we believe we can reduce that gap still further through pricing, sourcing, new product introductions, and value engineering.

As noted last quarter, while we benefited from the translation of the higher euro to the dollar, we incurred significant losses on FX contracts in 2025.

The largest drag on the top line was our ski business unit, which was down 30% to prior period due to a combination of our rotation out of low-margin categories like bindings, beacons, and airbags, and the most unfavorable seasonal conditions in 50 years in key ski destinations in the U.S.

Moreover, while our ski apparel line started strong through October and November, the growth trend tapered in December with the unusually poor conditions in both the U.S. West and Europe. Taken together, these three categories of Apparel, Mountain, and Climb grew 3.7% in Q4, accounting for 86% of our sales in the quarter and 90% for the full year.

This is where our simplification strategy is paying off, and we expect this strategy to drive profitable growth at BD in the future. By channel and region, North America wholesale, excluding FX contracts, was down 10.4% due to planned exits in the ski category and somewhat softer replenishment orders in December. North America digital D2C was down 0.8% compared to the fourth quarter of last year, which was a significant improvement in the run rate from previous quarters. Europe wholesale, excluding FX contracts, was up 12.1% in U.S. dollars and 3.2% on a constant currency basis.

Europe digital D2C, which is a relatively small part of the region’s revenue at 7.3% of total sales, was down 290.9%, or 36% in constant currency. Operating expenses, excluding restructuring and legal costs from both periods, were essentially flat year over year. This is $7.9 million of the difference to prior year and is strictly a matter of timing.

The two other factors raising this year’s number are tariffs and currency, which together inflate the value of inventory approximately $5.0 million. We have made great progress in improving the quality and composition of the inventory over the last few years and enter 2026 in good shape.
