Thursday, Feb. 5, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

  • Interim President and Chief Executive Officer — William Austen

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $8.7 billion for the fourth quarter, up 20% year over year and exceeding guidance.
  • Non-GAAP Diluted EPS -- $4.39 for the quarter, a 48% increase year over year, driven by higher sales, value-added mix, and lower interest expense.
  • Global Components Sales -- $5.9 billion for the quarter, up $1.1 billion year over year and $326 million sequentially, with a 6% increase from the prior quarter.
  • Global Components Non-GAAP Operating Income -- $219 million, up approximately $20 million and 10% from the previous quarter.
  • Global ECS Sales -- $2.9 billion, up around $400 million or 16% year over year, above midpoint of guidance.
  • Global ECS Total Billings -- $7.1 billion for the quarter, up 16% year over year, and backlog grew over 75% year over year to another all-time high.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 11.5% of sales for the quarter, down 20 basis points year over year due to regional and customer mix in global components.
  • Operating Expenses -- $669 million, up $53 million sequentially due to higher variable costs to support sales growth and ECS seasonality.
  • OpEx as Percent of Gross Profit -- 67%, down 700 basis points sequentially and 100 basis points year over year.
  • Interest and Other Expense -- $44 million, lowered by reduced average debt levels and declining short-term rates.
  • Book-to-Bill and Backlog -- Management stated, "Our book-to-bill ratio has improved further in all 3 regions and our above parity," with backlog showing healthy sequential growth for four consecutive quarters.
  • Value-Added Services Contribution -- Represented approximately 30% of total company operating income in 2025, up from less than 20% historically, with management aiming to further increase this share.
  • ECS Revenue Composition -- 75% from software/services, 25% from hardware (mainly storage, compute, networking, and security), with fastest hardware growth in networking and security.
  • Recurring Revenue in ECS -- Now accounts for roughly one-third of total ECS billings, with recurring models expanding earnings quality.
  • Return on Working Capital -- Improved 170 basis points year over year to 18%.
  • Return on Invested Capital -- Increased 190 basis points year over year to 11.1%.
  • Cash Conversion Cycle -- Reduced by 7 days year over year.
  • Inventory -- $5.1 billion at quarter-end, with improved inventory turns and normalized pace.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $200 million for the quarter, $64 million for the full year.
  • Gross Debt -- $3.1 billion at year-end, down $44 million sequentially.
  • Share Repurchases -- $50 million in the quarter and $150 million for the full year; $3.6 billion since 2020.
  • Q1 2026 Guidance -- Sales projected between $7.95 billion and $8.55 billion; non-GAAP diluted EPS expected between $2.70 and $2.90.
  • Leadership Update -- Board is still searching for a permanent CEO, with a market update pending conclusion.

SUMMARY

Management described a business environment characterized by gradually improving demand, highlighted by a steadily rising book-to-bill ratio and expanding backlog across all operating regions. Arrow Electronics (ARW +0.27%) demonstrated that value-added and recurring revenue streams are increasing in strategic importance, enabling margin support and quality earnings through targeted portfolio shifts. Seasonality, positive momentum in both components and enterprise solutions, and disciplined cost and working capital management are expected to continue shaping financial performance in the near term.

  • New internal appointments—such as Chief Growth Officers for distribution/services and a Chief Revenue Officer for ECS—are intended to enhance focus on growth initiatives and integrated execution.
  • Management confirmed, "visibility beyond 90 days is still a little bit cloudy," emphasizing near-term outlook uncertainty despite improved backlogs.
  • Elevated productivity and cost-control efforts were emphasized as drivers of margin leverage, with management signaling further focus on efficiency.
  • Strategic investments in digital platforms, specifically ArrowSphere, and recognition from Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT) as Distributor Partner of the Year, signal continued ECS business innovation and channel enablement.
  • Guidance for Q1 2026 explicitly factors in above-seasonal global components performance in all regions, management-flagged shipping day shifts, and macro uncertainties.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: A measure comparing the amount of new orders received (bookings) to the amount shipped and billed to customers within a given period, indicating demand momentum.
  • Backlog: The value of unfulfilled customer orders scheduled for future delivery, representing committed demand not yet recognized as revenue.
  • ArrowSphere: Arrow Electronics' proprietary digital platform for managing, provisioning, and enabling cloud-based IT services and solutions for channel partners.
  • Value-Added Services: Offerings such as supply-chain management, engineering, integration, and design services, in contrast to basic component distribution, designed to deepen customer relationships and improve margins.
