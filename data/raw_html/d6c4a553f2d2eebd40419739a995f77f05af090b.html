PayPal (PYPL) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 3, 2026 at 9:17AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Feb. 3, 2026 at 8:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Interim Chief Executive Officer and Board Member — Jamie Miller
  • Chief Financial Officer — Steve Winoker
  • Incoming President and Chief Executive Officer (from March 1, 2026) and Board Chair (until now) — Enrique Lopez
  • Board Chair — David Dorman
  • Operator

TAKEAWAYS

  • CEO Transition -- Enrique Lopez will assume the roles of president and CEO on March 1, with board member David Dorman becoming board chair; management explicitly framed the change as a response to underperformance in execution, not strategy.
  • Venmo Revenue -- Grew 20% year over year to $1.7 billion, excluding interest income, with active accounts surpassing 100 million.
  • Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) TPV -- Surpassed $40 billion in 2025, up more than 20% year over year.
  • Enterprise Payments -- Returned to double-digit volume growth in the fourth quarter, contributing to nearly half of 6% transaction margin dollar growth for the year.
  • Branded Checkout TPV -- Grew 1% on a currency-neutral basis in the fourth quarter, down from 5% in the third quarter, with deceleration attributed to U.S. retail weakness, international headwinds (notably Germany), and slower growth in travel, ticketing, crypto, and gaming.
  • Biometric Authentication Adoption -- 36% of consumers are "checkout ready," a 15 percentage point improvement over the prior year, with a stated target of reaching nearly 50% by year-end 2026.
  • Conversion Rates -- Vaulted PayPal as default delivers average conversion over 95%; initial scaling of the redesigned checkout experience with biometrics drives a two to five-point conversion uplift in large merchants.
  • Digital Rewards Launch -- PayPal Plus soft launched in the UK, generating mid-single-digit year-over-year growth in branded checkout TPV among enrolled users, with broader rollout planned for Europe and the U.S. in 2026.
  • Debit Card Growth -- PayPal-branded debit card TPV in the U.S. grew over 50% in Q4, with monthly active accounts up over 35%; combined MAA for Germany and the UK exceeded 700,000.
  • Venmo KPIs -- Venmo TPV up 13% in the fourth quarter; monthly active accounts reached 67 million, up 7% year over year; Venmo debit card TPV and MAA each grew 50%; "Pay with Venmo" TPV rose 32%, MAA up 26%.
  • Value-Added Services Revenue -- Other value-added services revenue grew 10% in Q4 to $807 million; full-year growth was 14% to $3.4 billion, driven by merchant and consumer credit strength.
  • Transaction Margin and Profitability -- Transaction margin dollars excluding interest grew 4% in the quarter and 6% for the year; non-GAAP operating income rose 3% in Q4 to $1.6 billion, up 9% for the year to $6.4 billion.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $1.5 billion in share repurchases completed in Q4; $6 billion total for the year; company initiated its first quarterly dividend at $0.14 per share.
  • 2026 Financial Guidance -- Management projects transaction margin dollars to be slightly down or roughly flat, excluding interest and customer balances; non-GAAP EPS guidance is for low single-digit decline to slightly positive growth.
  • Capital Allocation -- Plans call for approximately $6 billion in share repurchases and at least $6 billion in adjusted free cash flow for 2026; capital expenditure projected at $1 billion.

RISKS

  • Management rescinded the multiyear 2027 financial outlook cited at the previous Investor Day, reducing guidance to a single-year (2026) horizon due to macroeconomic headwinds, more intense competition, and slower-than-expected merchant adoption of new products.
  • Online branded checkout saw greater than expected deceleration in Q4, with management attributing the decline to operational execution shortfalls and concentrated macro pressures in key verticals and geographies.
  • 2026 transaction margin dollar growth faces an explicit three-point headwind from increased strategic investment spend, largely targeted to branded checkout and BNPL; management confirmed these investments result in lower upfront economics and will weigh on EPS and margin growth in 2026.
  • Non‑GAAP EPS of $1.23 in Q4 came in $0.04 below the low end of management's prior guidance range, attributed mainly to a higher than expected tax rate and lower operating income.

SUMMARY

PayPal Holdings, Inc. (PYPL 1.61%) announced a CEO transition effective March 1, explicitly positioned as a response to slow execution rather than strategic misalignment, with Enrique Lopez stepping into the role. The management team disclosed broad-based growth led by Venmo, buy now pay later, and enterprise payments, each posting double-digit gains or higher. Despite this, branded checkout growth slowed sharply to 1% on a currency-neutral basis in the fourth quarter, with persistent merchant integration challenges, targeted operational priorities for 2026, and an explicit withdrawal of the multiyear financial outlook. Significant investment will be allocated to product, experience, and rewards initiatives, with a corresponding short-term margin and earnings drag articulated for 2026.

  • Incremental investment targeting consumer rewards and co-marketing was flagged as necessary to improve checkout choice and drive deeper engagement, with PayPal Plus showing initial UK traction prior to full marketing activation.
  • Enterprise payments business nearly doubled its net processing yield and expanded profitability by successfully monetizing 16 value-added services and executing its first omnichannel merchant partnership via Verifone, enabling competition for large RFPs across both online and in-store payments.
  • The company launched its first quarterly dividend and reaffirmed its focus on balanced capital return and growth investment, with share repurchases totaling $6 billion for 2025 and similar plans for 2026.
  • Management stated that roughly 36% of consumers are categorized as "checkout ready" (active on biometric authentication), targeting nearly 50% by year-end 2026 to further lift conversion rates and merchant performance.
  • The transaction take rate declined nine basis points to 1.65% in the fourth quarter, primarily due to increased debit, Venmo, enterprise payments, and merchant co-marketing investments, with management signaling further pressure as strategic repricing and investments continue.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ARPA: Average Revenue Per Account; a measure of revenue generated per monthly active user account, exclusive to PayPal-specific disclosure.
  • TPV: Total Payment Volume; the sum of all payment transactions processed by PayPal on behalf of customers in a given period.
  • TM Dollar: Transaction Margin Dollar; profit generated from core payment activities, excluding certain non-transaction revenues or interest, as defined in PayPal's reporting.
  • Take Rate: Ratio of transaction revenue to TPV, indicating average monetization per dollar processed.
  • Checkout Ready: Internal designation for PayPal users who have enabled biometric authentication or device passkeys, facilitating frictionless checkout flows.
  • MAA: Monthly Active Accounts; the number of distinct user accounts active in a given month, unique to PayPal's unified measurement across its platforms.
  • Branded Checkout: PayPal's core consumer checkout solution offered on merchant sites, distinct from unbranded or white-label payment processing.
  • Pay with Venmo: E-commerce capability enabling Venmo users to pay at eligible merchant checkouts utilizing their Venmo balance or funding sources.
  • Agentic Commerce: PayPal's emerging channel enabling AI-powered agents to access, discover, and transact with merchant catalogs on behalf of consumers.
  • PSP: Payment Service Provider business segment within PayPal, with an emphasis on enterprise merchant payment processing, value-added services, and corresponding volumes.
