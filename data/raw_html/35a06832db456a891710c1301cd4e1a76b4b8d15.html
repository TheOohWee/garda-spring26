Earnings Call Transcript

Butterfly BFLY Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 31, 2025 at 10:45AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, October 31, 2025 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Joseph M. DeVivo

Interim Chief Financial Officer — Megan Carlson

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

Megan Carlson indicated that purchase delays impacted U.S. hospital and enterprise channels, contributing to softness in the quarter and creating timing uncertainty on large deals.

Carlson noted that if regulatory processing delays extend significantly, they could affect deal timing and FDA regulatory submissions.

Management stated, "We continue to see churn in our individual subscription" for software, indicating persistent renewal challenges in this revenue stream.

Carlson reported, "Software and other services revenue was $6.9 million in the third quarter, which was flat to the prior year period," indicating no growth in this segment.

TAKEAWAYS

Revenue -- $21.5 million for Q3 2025, representing 5% year-over-year growth, primarily due to increased average selling prices from more iQ3 sales internationally and higher U.S. volume.

Product Revenue -- $14.6 million, up 8% year-over-year, driven by international pricing strength and growth across e-commerce and veterinary channels.

Software & Services Revenue -- $6.9 million, flat from Q3 2024, with increased licensing and service fees offset by lower renewals and decreased extended warranty sales due to longer iQ3 warranties.

International Revenue -- $5.4 million, rising 4% over the prior year, benefiting from iQ3 market expansion and higher average selling prices.

U.S. Revenue -- $16.1 million, with slight year-over-year growth attributed to e-commerce improvements and veterinary distribution gains.

Adjusted Gross Margin -- 63.9%, an increase from 60% in Q3 2024, reflecting higher selling prices and lower software amortization expense.

Gross Margin -- Negative 17.5%, sharply down from 59.5% in Q3 2024 due to a $17.4 million non-cash inventory write-off tied to lower-than-expected iQ+ demand.

Probe Mix -- iQ3 comprised about 85% of probe volume, with iQ+ at 15%, as the market shifted more rapidly from legacy iQ+ to iQ3 products.

Adjusted EBITDA Loss -- $8.1 million, improving from an $8.4 million loss in Q3 2024, largely on better adjusted gross profit.

Normalized Cash Burn -- $3.9 million; cash and equivalents, including restricted cash, totaled $148 million at quarter-end.

Full-Year Guidance -- Management reaffirmed revenue guidance in the $91 million–$95 million range and tightened adjusted EBITDA loss guidance (non-GAAP) to $32 million–$35 million for full year 2025.

Large Deal Pipeline -- Several large enterprise and medical school deals remain active; closure is needed to achieve high end of Q4 revenue guidance.

AI & Clinical Validation -- A study published at Rutgers Robert Wood Johnson Medical School in September 2025 using Butterfly’s AI lung tool improved clinical management in 35% of cases, reduced hospital stay by 30%, and saved more than $750,000 in direct costs.

Next-Gen Product Milestone -- P5.1 chip development is complete and entering fab production, with an expected launch in a new form factor in the second half of 2026.

AI Platform Advancement -- Apollo AI chip development is underway as of Q3 2025; it is designed to enable high-speed ultrasound processing and on-device AI, with stated 20x processing power over P5.1 and legacy chips.

Cloud Security Certifications -- Company achieved ISO 27001 and other international security certifications, supporting global cloud and enterprise expansion.

New Solution Launches -- HeartFocus, FDA-cleared partner app, now available in Butterfly Garden to guide echo probe placement and enable faster cardiac scans by more providers.

SUMMARY

The quarter’s revenue reached the top end of guidance, driven by a higher international sales mix and robust iQ3 demand. The company took a $17.4 million non-cash write-off for iQ+ inventory amid a rapid shift to iQ3 probes, which also improved blended average selling prices and margins. While software and services revenue held steady, management acknowledged persistent churn among individual subscriptions, and product revenue growth has outpaced software contributions, shifting the segment mix. Full-year financial guidance was reaffirmed, but closure of several delayed enterprise and medical school deals is necessary to achieve the top end of Q4 targets.

DeVivo said that deals have simply been delayed and not closed, confirming that purchase delays are tied to customer budgeting cycles and IT resource constraints rather than lost competitiveness.

According to Carlson, exposure to the ongoing government shutdown is limited in the short term, but if it extends significantly, regulatory processing delays could become a factor for the FDA and funded accounts.

Enterprise customers saw compelling clinical results validated in the JAMA-published POCUS Care trial, providing economic arguments supporting Butterfly’s AI-driven platform adoption.

HomeCare pilot commercialization, multiple national risk-bearing partnerships, and significant ultrasound-on-chip OEM partnership discussions remain pending, with timelines contingent on third-party decisions.

Development of the iQ Station platform and RoHS compliance processes continue, with regulatory decisions in the quiet phase and expected outcomes in the coming year.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Point-of-Care Ultrasound (POCUS): Bedside ultrasound imaging performed and interpreted by clinicians at the patient’s point of care, often outside traditional imaging departments.

Adjusted Gross Margin: Gross profit as a percentage of revenue, excluding the impact of non-cash inventory or unusual one-time charges.

Probe: The handheld ultrasound transducer device attached to Butterfly’s imaging systems; product mix refers to the relative sales shares of iQ3 and iQ+ models.

OEM: Original Equipment Manufacturer; in this context, refers to third-party companies potentially embedding or licensing Butterfly’s ultrasound-on-chip technology.

CMUT: Capacitive Micromachined Ultrasonic Transducer; a microfabrication technology enabling semiconductor-based ultrasound imaging.
