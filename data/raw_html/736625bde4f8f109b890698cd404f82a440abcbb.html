Builders FirstSource (BLDR) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 17, 2026 at 12:37PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, February 17, 2026 at 8:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Peter Jackson
  • Chief Financial Officer — Pete Beckmann

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $3.4 billion in the fourth quarter, a 12% decline driven by lower core organic sales and commodity deflation, partially offset by contributions from acquisitions.
  • Core Organic Sales -- Decreased with single-family down 15%, multifamily down 20%, and repair and remodel down 7%, reflecting weakened housing and consumer demand.
  • Gross Profit -- $1 billion for the quarter, a 19% decrease from the prior year period.
  • Gross Margin -- 29.8% for the quarter, down 250 basis points; full-year margin exceeded 30%.
  • Adjusted SG&A Expense -- $751 million, down $13 million due to lower variable compensation; partially offset by higher costs from recent acquisitions.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $275 million for the quarter, down 44%, with margin at 8.2% (down 470 basis points) due to reduced gross profit and operating leverage.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $1.12, a 52% year-over-year decline; share repurchases contributed $0.04 per share in the quarter.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $109 million for the fourth quarter and $874 million for the full year, resulting in a full-year free cash flow yield of approximately 8%.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $195 million for the quarter, down $179 million, mainly due to lower net income.
  • Net Debt to Adjusted EBITDA -- Approximately 2.7x, with no long-term debt maturities until 2030.
  • Capital Expenditures (CapEx) -- $86 million in the quarter, plus $227 million spent on acquisitions.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- $500 million remains available for future buybacks.
  • Facility Consolidation -- 25 facilities consolidated during the year; 55 in total over two years, while maintaining a 92% on-time and in-full delivery rate.
  • Cost Actions -- $100 million identified for cost actions, with $75 million in year-over-year reductions and $25 million in avoidance, focused entirely on SG&A.
  • Productivity Savings -- $48 million achieved in 2025, largely via supply chain initiatives.
  • Strategic M&A -- Acquisitions included Builder's Door & Trim, Rystin Construction, Lengefeld Lumber, Pleasant Valley Homes, and Premium Building Components, expanding product and geographic reach.
  • Pleasant Valley Homes Acquisition -- Enables expansion in prefabricated component strategy, serving modular and HUD-compliant home demand across 10 Northeastern states.
  • Digital Platform -- Processed almost $7 billion in quotes since early 2024, growing over 130% year over year.
  • Technology Investments -- SAP pilot programs initiated, with broader deployment planned for 2026 and ongoing rollout beyond.
  • 2026 Outlook -- Guides for net sales of $14.8 billion-$15.8 billion, adjusted EBITDA of $1.3 billion-$1.7 billion, and gross margin of 28.5%-30%.
  • 2026 Free Cash Flow Guidance -- Approximately $500 million, reflecting increased working capital needs and lower expected EBITDA.
  • 2026 Q1 Guidance -- Net sales expected at $3 billion-$3.3 billion and adjusted EBITDA at $175 million-$225 million, citing inventory levels and winter weather as factors.
  • Install Business -- Remained 16%-17% of total sales, declining less than the core single-family segment and maintaining margins comparable to installed categories.
  • Commodity Prices -- Assumes 2026 average of $365-$385 per thousand board foot, below the long-term average of $400.
  • Working Capital Shift -- $300 million year-over-year swing from release to investment expected, due to higher projected sales pace and inventory value in 2026.
  • SG&A Inflation Impacts -- Rent and insurance premiums noted as persistent inflationary pressures and embedded in 2026 expense outlook.

RISKS

  • Chief Financial Officer Pete Beckmann said, "Sales decelerated more sharply than expected late in the quarter as homebuilders aggressively delayed starts to work down excess inventory," resulting in weaker-than-anticipated results.
  • Management reported, "we incurred higher-than-expected insurance cost true-ups, which further pressured performance" in the fourth quarter.
  • Persistent inflation in rent and insurance continues to impact costs. Expectations for these expense categories are to remain elevated in 2026.
  • Commodity composite index exited the year "below $350 per thousand board foot." Management stated, "Commodity supply is being curtailed, but not at a pace that we believe will meaningfully lift prices in the near term."

SUMMARY

Builders FirstSource (BLDR +0.02%) reported significant year-over-year declines in sales, gross profit, adjusted EBITDA, and adjusted earnings per share, citing ongoing weakness in U.S. housing starts and continued affordability pressures. The company executed $100 million in SG&A cost actions, consolidated 25 facilities, and maintained a strong delivery rate while deploying capital to both internal investments and targeted M&A, notably expanding modular and prefabricated offerings. Management guided 2026 results to net sales of $14.8 billion-$15.8 billion and free cash flow of approximately $500 million, recognizing greater working capital intensity due to an anticipated ramp in sales during the second half of the year. Technology and digital initiatives advanced, with the digital platform processing $7 billion in quotes and SAP implementation poised for further rollout; AI investments have primarily driven sales efficiency and operational improvement rather than direct headcount reduction. The company highlighted persistent cost inflation in rent and insurance, cautious near-term macro assumptions, and the expectation that multifamily sales contribution will lag overall starts due to a typical 9- to 12-month sales cycle.

  • Management expects Q1 2026 to represent a smaller portion of annual sales as builders accelerate activity after a weak late 2025 and severe winter weather impacts key markets.
  • Dividend and capital deployment discipline remains, with $500 million available for further share repurchases and stated comfort regarding current leverage, supported by no material debt maturities until 2030.
  • Margin guidance for 2026 reflects competitive pressure, start environment, contract resets, and seasonal volatility. Low-end guidance accommodates a range of possible pricing and demand outcomes, not current trends.
  • The install business is performing ahead of core single-family declines, underpinned by builder demand for reliable, integrated onsite execution as labor tightness persists.
  • Management reaffirms that normalized housing starts of 1.0 million to 1.1 million would support previously stated long-term EBITDA and margin targets, with no updates to through-cycle earnings power assumptions.
  • Pleasant Valley Homes and other acquisitions are framed as experiments in filling the affordable home segment via modular construction, leveraging company scale to offer semi-custom solutions. Retail HUD distribution is not a focus.
  • Recent facility closures by competitors have led to local market exits by smaller players. Builders FirstSource can maintain service by consolidating capacity within overlapping regions.
  • Federal attention to housing affordability policy is recognized as a promising but not yet market-moving development.
  • Management cited that most cost actions are already implemented and will be realized incrementally through 2026.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • OSB: Oriented Strand Board – an engineered wood panel used mainly for sheathing in residential construction.
  • HUD-compliant: Housing and Urban Development (HUD) standards for manufactured homes, indicating compliance with federal construction and safety regulations.
  • Installed Business: Refers to products sold with accompanying onsite installation services, often bundled as turnkey solutions for homebuilders.
  • Truss: Structural framework, usually prefabricated, used to support roofs or floors in building construction.
  • Value-Added Solutions: Company-provided products or services that offer enhanced functionality or integration beyond basic building materials, such as prefabrication or custom assemblies.
