American Outdoor Brands (AOUT) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 12, 2026 at 8:58PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

March 12, 2026, at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Brian Daniel Murphy
  • Chief Financial Officer — H. Andrew Fulmer
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Elizabeth A. Sharp

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $56.6 million, a 3.3% decrease year over year, attributed to an ongoing inventory reset at the largest e-commerce retailer and softness in the aiming solutions category.
  • Outdoor Lifestyle Category Sales -- $35.3 million with 5.4% growth year over year, driven by BOG and MEAT! Your Maker brands, and representing over 62% of total company net sales.
  • Shooting Sports Category Sales -- Down 15% year over year, mainly due to decreased demand for aiming solutions; Caldwell brand registered "solid growth" on the new Claycopter platform.
  • Point of Sale (POS) Growth -- 5% increase year over year, marking a third consecutive quarter of positive POS results, led by the outdoor lifestyle category.
  • Traditional Channel Sales -- Down 2.1% year over year; E-Commerce Channel Sales -- Down 4.6% year over year, with inventory reset and tariff pressures impacting channel performance.
  • Gross Margin -- 41%, down 370 basis points from prior year; the decrease driven by new tariffs and a $1.2 million inventory reserve related to aiming solutions. Without the reserve, gross margin would have been 43.1%.
  • Tariff Impacts -- $1.7 million IEEPA tariff expense recognized, first material period of impact; U.S. Supreme Court ruling struck down IEEPA tariffs on February 20, though those paid are amortizing through cost of goods sold as inventory turns.
  • Inventory -- Ended quarter at $110.2 million, down from $124.0 million sequentially, due to working capital management and $13.8 million inventory reduction that includes assets held for sale related to UST.
  • GAAP Operating Expenses -- $27.1 million, up from $25.8 million, reflecting a $3.4 million non-cash impairment for the UST brand, partially offset by lower variable costs and intangible amortization.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses -- $21.0 million, compared to $22.7 million in the prior year, excluding impairment and certain nonrecurring items.
  • GAAP EPS -- Loss of $0.32, versus $0.01 last year; Non-GAAP EPS -- $0.12, down from $0.21 last year on approximately 12.5 million fully diluted shares.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $3.3 million, down from $4.7 million, largely due to the inventory reserve and tariff impact.
  • Cash Flow and Balance Sheet -- $10.4 million in cash, no debt, $1.4 million share buyback executed during the quarter, and a renewed $75 million credit line extended to March 2031.
  • Operating Cash Inflow -- $9.9 million for the quarter, driven by lower accounts receivable and inventory.
  • CapEx Guidance -- Full-year capital expenditure expectations lowered by $500,000 to a range of $3.5 million-$4.0 million.
  • Brand Portfolio Actions -- Decision to divest UST camping and survival brand due to limited returns on innovation; recorded a $3.4 million non-cash impairment and are fulfilling orders from existing inventory while seeking a buyer.
  • New Product Contribution -- Over 26% of net sales attributable to new products in the quarter, reflecting successful innovation strategy.
  • Annual Guidance (Fiscal 2026) -- Maintained for net sales ($191 million-$193 million), gross margin (42%-43%), and adjusted EBITDA (4%-4.5% of net sales).
  • Order Pull-Forward Impact -- $10 million of sales accelerated into prior year Q4 due to tariff avoidance, creating a reported annual sales decline of 13%-14%, but underlying decline would be approximately 5% excluding this effect.

  • Company recorded a $3.4 million non-cash impairment for UST brand and is divesting the segment, acknowledging limited innovation potential and unfavorable market dynamics for traditional camping accessories.
  • Management explicitly cites "uneven retailer ordering patterns," and "consumer uncertainty" as factors in current period operating performance, creating uncertain near-term demand visibility.

SUMMARY

American Outdoor Brands (AOUT 0.62%) delivered third-quarter results that reflected both disciplined working capital management and targeted brand portfolio actions, alongside headwinds from tariffs and category-specific softness. Management recorded a $3.4 million impairment charge to facilitate exit from the UST camping and survival brand, sharpening focus on higher-growth segments. The company is leveraging innovation, as new products accounted for over 26% of net sales and point of sale growth outpaced headline revenue, particularly in outdoor lifestyle categories. Looking forward, guidance for sales, gross margin, and adjusted EBITDA is unchanged as executives anticipate possible future margin volatility due to amortizing capitalized tariff costs, while highlighting available capital for both buybacks and opportunistic investment.

  • Ongoing inventory reductions are proceeding more rapidly than previously planned, as H. Andrew Fulmer noted, "there is opportunity to end a little bit better than the $110,000,000 if we can move some of the slower-moving inventory."
  • Recent U.S. Supreme Court action striking down IEEPA tariffs has created uncertainty about possible future tariff refund benefits, which management does not include in current guidance.
  • Brian Daniel Murphy stated, "we are well positioned to navigate the current environment," but also pointed to "dynamic and evolving" tariff policy and persistent aiming solutions category weakness, indicating ongoing business agility will be required to meet long-term goals.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • IEEPA Tariffs: U.S. government-imposed import duties under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act, affecting recognized inventory and gross margin as products are sold through.
  • Section 232 Tariffs: Import tariffs levied by the U.S. government under Section 232 of the Trade Expansion Act, primarily targeting specific categories of goods such as steel and aluminum, now impacting company cost structure.
  • POS (Point of Sale): Metric representing sales at the retail register, reflecting end-market consumer demand rather than wholesale shipments.
  • Assets Held for Sale: Financial reporting classification for assets a company intends to divest, separated from operating assets on the balance sheet and often accompanied by impairment charges if expected sale proceeds are less than carrying value.
