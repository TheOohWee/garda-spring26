Zoetis (ZTS) Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

  • Chief Financial Officer — Wetteny Joseph
  • Vice President of Investor Relations — Steve Frank

Takeaways

  • Full-year revenue -- $9.3 billion, representing 8% reported growth and 11% operationally, with price contributing six percent and volume five percent.
  • Adjusted net income -- $2.7 billion for the year, with 10% reported growth and 15% operationally.
  • Companion animal portfolio -- 14% operational growth, driven by Simparica, OA pain monoclonal antibodies, and dermatology franchises.
  • Simparica franchise -- $1.4 billion in annual revenue, 28% operational growth; Simparica Trio exceeded $1 billion globally, with 30% U.S. growth to $918 million.
  • OA pain monoclonal antibodies (Librela and Solensia) -- $581 million in global revenue, 80% operational growth; Librela U.S. launch reached $201 million in its first full year.
  • Key dermatology franchise -- $1.6 billion revenue, 17% operational growth globally, with 16% U.S. growth ($1.1 billion) and balanced double-digit volume growth in Apoquel and Cytopoint.
  • Livestock portfolio -- $2.9 billion in annual revenue, five percent operational growth; excluding medicated feed additives (MFA) divestiture, organic operational livestock growth was seven percent globally and six percent in the U.S.
  • Q4 revenue -- $2.3 billion, five percent reported and six percent operational growth; organic operational growth, excluding MFA divestiture, was nine percent.
  • Q4 adjusted net income -- $632 million, up 11% reported and nine percent operationally.
  • Q4 Simparica franchise -- $324 million in revenue globally, 21% operational growth; U.S. Simparica revenue $240 million, up 17%.
  • Q4 key dermatology franchise -- $417 million, 11% operational growth globally; U.S. revenue $270 million, up seven percent.
  • Q4 OA pain monoclonal antibodies -- $150 million globally, 20% operational growth; $71 million combined U.S. revenue, up 22%.
  • Q4 livestock revenue -- $726 million, one percent operational growth; U.S. livestock declined eight percent reported but grew 12% organic operationally on timing of supply and product mix.
  • Gross margin -- 70.7% adjusted for the year, increasing 50 basis points; foreign exchange created a 40 basis point headwind.
  • Shareholder returns -- $1.9 billion in share repurchases (record), $786 million in dividends, total capital returned over $2.6 billion, up $800 million from prior year.
  • 2025 revenue guidance -- $9.225 billion to $9.375 billion, implying 6%-8% organic operational growth; projected FX headwind of $250 million.
  • 2025 adjusted net income guidance -- $2.7 billion to $2.75 billion, 2%-4% operational growth and 6%-8% organic operationally.
  • 2025 adjusted diluted EPS guidance -- $6.00 to $6.10; reported diluted EPS $5.70 to $5.80.
  • 2025 gross margin outlook -- Adjusted cost of sales projected at 28% of revenue, margin improvement expected from favorable mix after MFA divestiture, partly offset by FX.
  • Compensation and SG&A -- 2024 adjusted SG&A up nine percent operationally due to higher compensation and demand-generating activities; 2025 SG&A projected at $2.3 billion-$2.35 billion.
  • R&D investment -- Full-year R&D spend up 12% operationally in 2024; 2025 guided to $680 million-$690 million, now tracking in line with revenue growth after accelerated investment cycle.
  • Triple combination parasiticide market -- Valued at $2 billion globally; expected to double to $4.5 billion by 2028; over 50% of U.S. puppies now on triple combinations.
  • Dermatology market opportunity -- Estimated total addressable market to grow to $2.5 billion by 2028 (11% CAGR), with 20 million dogs globally untreated or undertreated.
  • OA pain category penetration -- Nearly 40% of dogs will suffer from OA; Librela became fourth-largest U.S. product and fastest blockbuster launch in company history.
  • Alternative channel growth -- About 20% of U.S. pet care portfolio revenue now through alternative channels (retail/home delivery), boosting compliance and market expansion.
  • Livestock industry growth alignment -- 2025 organic operational livestock guidance set in line with historical livestock industry growth rates of 2%-4%.
  • 2025 FX and tax headwinds -- Projected FX headwind of $50 million to adjusted net income, and 2025 tax rate expected at 21% (higher end of historical range due to foreign earnings taxes).
  • Share repurchase program -- New, multi-year $6 billion buyback program approved in August 2024; EPS guidance based on current share count, excluding effects of future buybacks.
  • Product guidance exclusion -- Guidance does not include impact from as-yet unapproved products or pending regulatory changes (e.g., tariffs, tax reform); long-acting OA pain products are not included in 2025 forecast.

Summary

Zoetis (ZTS 1.77%) delivered double-digit operational revenue and adjusted net income growth, driven by its companion animal and innovative therapeutic franchises. Management signaled ongoing strength across Simparica, OA pain, and dermatology franchises, citing robust adoption among both veterinarians and pet owners. Strategic moves included the portfolio streamlining via divestiture of MFA and strong capital returns through record buybacks and a newly authorized $6 billion repurchase program.

  • Wetteny Joseph stated, "our guidance does not include products that have not yet been approved," indicating conservatism in 2025 forecasts.
  • Price increases, especially in high-inflation international markets, contributed over one and a half percentage points to total company growth for 2024, but this price-driven tailwind is not expected to repeat in 2025.
  • The company projects organic operational growth in both revenue and adjusted net income at 6%-8% for 2025, with higher gross margins expected from a favorable business mix post-divestiture.
  • Kristin Peck noted that "Librela's U.S. launch is the most successful in our history," while emphasizing continued growth opportunity as penetration moves from severe to moderate OA cases in dogs.

Industry glossary

  • Operational growth: Revenue or profit growth measured excluding foreign exchange impacts.
  • MFA (medicated feed additives): Products added to livestock feed to deliver medicines through diet; Zoetis divested selected MFA assets in Q4 2024.
  • OA pain mAbs: Monoclonal antibodies targeting osteoarthritis pain in companion animals, including Librela and Solensia.
  • Alternative channels: Non-veterinary clinic distribution points such as retail and home delivery for animal health products.
  • Triple combination parasiticides: Products that provide simultaneous protection against three types of parasites, notably fleas, ticks, and worms, often administered monthly.
