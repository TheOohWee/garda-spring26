Vinci Compass (VINP) Q1 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 23, 2025 at 11:39AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, May 12, 2025 at 5:00 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Alessandro Horta
  • Partner and Head of Private Equity, Technology, and Innovation — Bruno Zaremba
  • Chief Financial Officer — Sergio Passos

TAKEAWAYS

  • Fee-Related Earnings (FRE) -- BRL65.7 million in the first quarter of 2025, up 22% year over year, equating to BRL1.04 per share and a margin of 28.4% in Q1 2025.
  • Adjusted Distributable Earnings (ADE) -- BRL62.3 million in the first quarter of 2025, up 26% year over year or 6% per share, at BRL0.98 per share in Q1 2025.
  • Dividend Declaration -- Quarterly dividend of BRL0.15 per common share, payable June 10 to holders of record as of May 27.
  • Assets Under Management (AUM) -- Decreased 7% quarter over quarter, primarily due to a 7% appreciation of the Brazilian real against the U.S. dollar and net outflows in the global IP&S segment.
  • Fee-Related Revenues -- BRL231.6 million, an increase of 117% year over year and 6% sequentially.
  • Management Fees -- BRL195.5 million, up 15% sequentially, with no material catch-up fees recorded this quarter.
  • Advisory Fees -- BRL24.9 million, including BRL22.5 million in upfront fees from TPD alternatives and a contribution from Real Estate Advisory Service.
  • Performance Fees -- BRL1.7 million, originating from global IP&S commingled funds and a separate equity mandate.
  • Quarterly Capital Raised -- BRL1.1 billion in new capital subscriptions, marking the company's most active first quarter for fundraising in recent years.
  • Credit Segment Fundraising -- Over BRL600 million raised in PEPCO II (Peru), close to BRL200 million in MAV III (agribusiness), and approximately BRL200 million in SPS IV (opportunistic credit) during the quarter, with SPS IV's first close achieving total commitments of BRL1.1 billion, matching SPS III's total commitments.
  • Equity Segment Fund Flows -- Foreign investors were net buyers of BRL3.8 billion in the first quarter, compared to net selling of BRL6 billion in the prior quarter.
  • Global IP&S Net Outflows -- Driven by TPD Liquid redemptions and capital returns from closed-end alternatives; BRL7 billion in net outflows from TPD Liquids, partially offset by alternatives inflows.
  • Chilean Pension Fund Market Share -- The company maintains a nearly 20% share as the leading provider of offshore liquid fund access.
  • ICC Fund Closing -- Climate Infrastructure Fund (ICC) scheduled for its final closing in the second quarter, with substantial dry powder earmarked for Brazilian sustainable infrastructure projects.

SUMMARY

Vinci Compass Investments Ltd. (VINP 0.47%) reported substantial year-over-year growth in all key profitability metrics, emphasizing robust progress from recent acquisitions and platform integration. Management highlighted expanding inbound interest from global investors across equities, infrastructure, and credit, citing completed commitments from Asian clients and ongoing SMA discussions. The quarter saw strong demand in credit products, with new flagship and cross-border strategies achieving significant initial closes, including notable traction in both Peru and the pan-Latin America region.

  • Management described the Q1 fundraising environment as "most active first quarter in recent years," signaling a shift in capital flows toward the platform's alternative strategies.
  • Bruno Zaremba stated, "opportunistic credit will be the first of Vinci's original alternative strategies to structure a new vintage with a true, brand-regional mandate," revealing intentions to replicate this model across other strategies in coming years.
  • Sergio Passos noted the average fee rate in the global IP&S segment was 0.13% including upfront fees and 0.17% excluding them, highlighting the revenue normalization effect as upfront fees are non-recurring.
  • Management expects the FRE margin to move into the low 30% range for 2025, contingent on a recovery in corporate advisory and ongoing cost discipline from integration synergies.
  • The Chile Small Cap Investment Fund returned 27.1% year-to-date in U.S. dollar terms, positioning the strategy as a peer leader within regional equities.
  • The company cited market volatility and currency fluctuation, rather than asset write-downs, as direct drivers of AUM and performance-related markdowns, with expectations for recovery if FX stabilizes.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FRE (Fee-Related Earnings): Profit derived from management, advisory, and other recurring fee revenues—excludes performance fees and investment income.
  • Adjusted Distributable Earnings (ADE): After-tax earnings available for payment to shareholders, adjusted for certain non-cash or non-recurring items.
  • TPD (Third-Party Distribution): Platform segment distributing investment products managed by non-affiliated asset managers, comprising both ‘Liquid’ and ‘Alternative’ categories.
  • Upfront Fee: Fee charged at the time of capital commitment or fundraising close, typically not recurring in future periods.
  • Dry Powder: Undrawn capital committed by investors, reserved for future investment deployment.
  • SMA (Separately Managed Account): Customized investment account managed on behalf of a single client, often institutional.
  • Vintage: Specific fund generation or launch within a strategy, often numbered consecutively.
  • Catch-up Fees: Fees collected retroactively from new fund investors to align their participation with earlier closings.
