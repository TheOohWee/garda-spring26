  • Chief Financial Officer — Kelly Lee Tuminelli

Takeaways

  • Total revenues -- Declined 2% in the quarter and 1% for the year, attributed to decreased worksite employee (WSE) volumes, partially offset by higher insurance and professional service pricing.
  • Co-employed WSEs -- Ended the year at 294,000, reflecting an 11% decrease, driven by pricing actions and muted hiring activity.
  • Retention rate -- Fell to roughly 80%, down five percentage points, with pricing increases cited most frequently as the primary cause of attrition.
  • Insurance services revenue per co-employed WSE -- Grew 9%, as average health fee increases exceeded 9%.
  • Insurance cost ratio (ICR) -- Achieved 90.8% for the year and 94% in Q4, with a 0.6-point year-over-year Q4 improvement, remaining within guidance.
  • Professional services revenue -- Declined 7% in Q4 and 6% for the year, partly offset by low single-digit pricing increases, a $7 million net negative from HRIS discontinuation, and a $22 million loss due to the technology fee discontinuation.
  • Advanced services offering (ASO) growth -- Concluded the year with more than 39,000 users at approximately $50 average revenue per user, nearly three times SaaS-only levels, driven by higher-than-expected SaaS conversion and new client wins.
  • Operating expenses -- Reduced by 16% year over year in Q4 and 7% for the full year, reflecting talent optimization and automation efforts.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Produced $57 million for Q4 and $425 million for the year, with a full-year adjusted EBITDA margin of 8.5%.
  • Free cash flow -- Generated $43 million in Q4 and $234 million for the year, a 16% increase, with a free cash flow conversion ratio of 55% (vs. 41% prior year).
  • Shareholder returns -- Returned $235 million through $182 million in repurchases (2.8 million shares) and $53 million in dividends ($1.075 per share).
  • Balance sheet -- Eliminated $90 million revolving credit facility debt, ending with a debt-to-adjusted EBITDA ratio of 2.1 times.
  • 2026 revenue guidance -- Forecasting $4.75 billion to $4.90 billion, affected by lower starting WSE base and expected Q1 attrition from January renewals.
  • 2026 insurance cost ratio guidance -- Anticipates a range of 89.25% to 90.75%, tightened by 50 basis points, with medical cost inflation projected at high single to low double-digit rates and pharmaceutical inflation in the low double digits.
  • Operating expense outlook -- Forecasts mid-single-digit decline for 2026, with some savings to be reinvested in value creation initiatives.
  • 2026 adjusted EPS guidance -- Guides to $3.70 to $4.70; GAAP earnings per diluted share expected in the $2.15 to $3.05 range.
  • Share repurchase authorization -- Increased to $400 million available for buybacks.

Summary

TriNet Group (TNET 4.12%) reported that its final significant repricing of health fees was completed with the January renewal cohort, which the company described as normalizing attrition pressures going forward. Management detailed that early April renewal indicators show the proportion of clients receiving health fee hikes above 30% dropped by more than half versus January, suggesting stabilization of fee increases and improved retention prospects. TriNet Group highlighted its sales pipeline momentum, with January sales growth “nicely” up and four national broker partnerships contributing disproportionately to current and future bookings. The company announced a nearly 20% expansion in sales capacity for 2026, citing both an increase in high-tenure representatives and new Ascend graduate cohorts. TriNet Group is launching TriNet Assistant, an AI-powered HR platform built on decades of internal expertise and positioned for rapid expansion, with plans for further workflow-related integrations across international, IT, and leave management.

  • The CFO stated interest income for 2026 is expected to face a $25 million to $30 million headwind due to rate cuts and reduced cash balances from tax refund timing and distribution.
  • Professional services revenue is forecast in a $625 million to $645 million range, with double-digit ASO services growth partially offset by a $10 million to $15 million SaaS HRIS headwind and a state-specific tax methodology change.
  • The company noted that stabilization of ICR is underpinned by improved actuarial capabilities and a more normalized client pricing distribution.
  • Growth in the ASO business is expected to shift from internal migration to new sales after the SaaS-only business exit early in the year, creating a broader opportunity for brokers and representatives when PEO is not a suitable fit for clients.
  • Management said, “generate good outcomes in 2026 with building momentum,” positioning 2026 as a transition to sustainable growth anchored by recent pipeline and service investments.

Industry glossary

  • WSE (Worksite Employee): Refers to employees whose HR, payroll, and benefits services are managed by TriNet Group, and who may be either co-employed (under the PEO model) or using the HR platform (platform users).
  • ICR (Insurance cost ratio): The ratio of insurance claims and related expenses to total insurance revenue, expressing expense management and pricing adequacy in insurance operations.
  • ASO (Administrative services only): A service model wherein TriNet Group provides HR and benefits administration for clients’ employees without taking on co-employment risk.
  • CIE (Change in employment): An internal measure of net hiring or reduction within TriNet Group’s client base, indicating clients’ workforce change trends over a specified period.
  • PEO (Professional employer organization): A co-employment structure where TriNet Group becomes the employer of record for HR, benefits, and payroll services, sharing certain legal and compliance responsibilities with the client.
  • HRIS (Human resource information system): The company’s previous SaaS-only HR platform, now discontinued in favor of full-service ASO or PEO offerings.
