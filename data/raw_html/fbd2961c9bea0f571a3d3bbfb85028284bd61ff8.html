CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Ronald Mittelstaedt
  • Chief Financial Officer — Mary Anne Whitney

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $2.373 billion in Q4, with net acquisition contribution of $58 million.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $796 million in Q4, up 8.7% year over year, representing 33.5% of revenue and a 110 basis point increase in margin.
  • Full-year 2025 adjusted EBITDA -- $3.125 billion, up 7.7% from the prior year, with a margin of 33% (up 50 basis points), and exceeding 33.6% after normalizing for Chiquita and commodity headwinds.
  • Pricing growth -- Core solid waste pricing rose 6.5% for the full year, above expectations, with Q4 pricing accelerating sequentially to 6.4%, and ranging from 3.7% in exclusive Western markets to over 7% in competitive markets.
  • Volume trends -- Reported volume declined 2.7% in Q4, consistent with prior quarters, impacted by intentional customer shedding, price-volume trade-offs, and cyclically weak business areas.
  • Landfill tonnage mixed -- Q4 same-store total landfill tonnes: municipal solid waste (MSW) up 4%, special waste up 4%, construction and demolition (C&D) down 4%; for full year, C&D down 5%, special waste up 7%, MSW up 3%.
  • Free cash flow -- Adjusted free cash flow for 2025 was $1.26 billion, reflecting approximately 50% conversion of adjusted EBITDA, despite $200 million in cash outlays related to Chiquita Canyon.
  • Capital expenditures -- $1.194 billion for 2025, with $100 million directed to RNG and recycling project development.
  • Acquisition activity -- Closed 19 acquisitions in 2025 totaling $330 million in acquired annualized revenue; the 2026 acquisition revenue contribution is expected at $125 million for already completed deals.
  • Shareholder returns -- Returned over $830 million through $500 million in share repurchases, and more than $330 million in dividends in 2025, with the quarterly dividend increased by 11.1%.
  • Balance sheet and leverage -- Maintained 2.75x debt-to-EBITDA leverage, preserving flexibility for continued acquisitions and capital returns.
  • Chiquita Canyon update -- Leachate generation at Chiquita Canyon decreased from a peak of 400,000 gallons per day to 200,000–225,000 gallons per day by year-end; leachate treatment costs average $0.50–$2.50 per gallon, comprising about 40%-45% of Chiquita-related expenses.
  • 2026 revenue outlook -- Projected at $9.9 billion–$9.950 billion, with 3.5%-4% organic solid waste growth, core pricing expected at 5%-5.5%, and near-flat volumes.
  • 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- Expected in the range of $3.30 billion–$3.325 billion, with 33.3%-33.4% margin, reflecting a 30–40 basis point increase (including a 20–30 basis point commodity drag).
  • 2026 free cash flow projection -- Anticipated at $1.4 billion–$1.45 billion, with normalized conversion of approximately 50% of EBITDA or $1.7 billion, net of non-core impacts.
  • Capital allocation to projects -- 2026 CapEx forecast at $1.25 billion, including about $100 million toward RNG and recycling initiatives, which are expected to be largely completed within the year.
  • Operating cost management -- Labor inflation moderated from 5% early in 2025 to 3.9% in Q4, and is expected to further trend toward 3%-3.5% during 2026; other costs moved from 4.5% toward 2.5%-3% across the year.
  • Technology and AI initiatives -- Two key AI initiatives for 2026 are real-time dynamic customer routing and an enhanced mobile platform, aiming to reduce inbound customer calls and improve route efficiency.
  • Northeast and Arrowhead landfill progress -- Daily tonnes at Arrowhead landfill increased from 2,300–2,500 to 7,500, with expectations for 9,000–9,500 tonnes by the end of 2026, accompanied by improved internalization rates (~60%) in the region.
  • RNG facility ramp-up -- Five RNG projects are online, rest expected to be operational around year-end; initial plant efficiency starts at 40%-50% and ramps to full capacity over one year, with full EBITDA contribution anticipated in 2027.
  • Seneca Meadows expansion -- Expansion process is in technical review, with management expressing high confidence in approval; volumes have been throttled back to preserve airspace, limiting EBITDA risk in a closure scenario.
  • Driver academies impact -- Over 60% of driver hires in 2026 expected to be developed through internal academies, supported by lower turnover rates and improved safety statistics.

RISKS

  • CEO Mittelstaedt said, "the political challenges of resolving this situation continue to exceed our updated expectations primarily because of related regulatory, permitting, legal, consulting and other unanticipated requirements that have drug out and inflated an already burdensome and dysfunctional process."
  • CFO Whitney noted a "commodity-related drag of 20 to 30 basis points" on projected 2026 adjusted EBITDA margins, with no improvement in commodity values assumed in outlook.
  • CFO Whitney stated, "Our outlook assumes no change in the current economic environment," and management did not factor in any material pickup in macroeconomic conditions or commodity prices, presenting upside risk if actual conditions improve but a downside if headwinds persist.
  • CEO Mittelstaedt remarked, "I certainly wouldn't pursue owning an additional landfill in California in the next 200 years," highlighting ongoing business environment risk in California.

SUMMARY

Waste Connections (WCN 1.39%) delivered record Q4 and full-year 2025 adjusted EBITDA margins on an adjusted basis, driven by price-led organic growth, disciplined cost controls, and accretive acquisition activity, despite pronounced weakness in recycled commodity values and persistent operational headwinds at Chiquita Canyon. Management’s 2026 guidance points to continued margin expansion and robust free cash flow generation even as forecasts explicitly exclude any uplift from economic or commodity recoveries, with incremental efficiency gains targeted through technology investments and internal driver academies. The Arrowhead landfill and Northeast corridor initiatives have accelerated internalization and network optimization, while RNG development remains on track for significant EBITDA contribution by 2027. Regulatory risks at Chiquita Canyon and ongoing exposure to low recycled commodity prices remain watch points, though management underscores balance sheet strength and capacity for further acquisitions.

  • CEO Mittelstaedt confirmed the company expects to maintain over 50% of new driver hires through internal academies, with resulting safety and retention improvements projected to support future margin gains.
  • CFO Whitney specified that 2026 free cash flow of $1.4 billion–$1.45 billion incorporates both Chiquita Canyon closure costs ($100 million–$150 million) and RNG project outlays, with both exposures expected to step down from 2026 to 2027.
  • Management emphasized the full-year guidance reflects only known and closed M&A transactions, with any additional acquisitions considered upside to current projections.
  • CEO Mittelstaedt stated, "Dynamic real-time routing allows you to move assets with information that today you don't really have or you have only reactively, not proactively," highlighting the strategic priority of data-driven operational efficiency initiatives.
  • Mary Whitney noted a 15% year-over-year decrease in recycled commodity values has been built into 2026 guidance, equating to a margin drag of 20–30 basis points, mostly concentrated in the first half of the year.
  • CEO Mittelstaedt highlighted that landfill gas (RNG) facility ramp-up typically starts at 40%-50% efficiency, requiring up to a year to reach full output, but affirmed total EBITDA contribution estimates remain in the $100 million–$120 million range once all projects are operational.
  • CEO Mittelstaedt said regulatory outcomes for Seneca Meadows are favorable, and cautioned that an adverse expansion decision would have "far less" EBITDA impact than the Chiquita Canyon closure, given operational throttling and network mitigation.
  • CFO Whitney indicated that the underlying Canadian E&P waste business is performing steadily, with 2026 growth for this segment projected as "flattish" absent episodic project repeatability.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for certain items; used to assess recurring operational performance.
  • Chiquita Canyon: A Waste Connections-owned landfill in California facing an elevated temperature landfill (ETLF) event, regulatory scrutiny, and significant closure-related operational and financial costs.
  • RNG (Renewable Natural Gas): Biogas produced from decomposing organic matter in landfills, upgraded for energy use or sale, and eligible for Renewable Identification Numbers (RINs) under federal biofuel programs.
  • Internalization: The practice of directing collected waste to company-owned disposal or processing assets rather than selling disposal services to third parties, often enhancing margins.
  • C&D (Construction and Demolition Debris): Waste generated from construction, renovation, or demolition projects.
  • Leachate: Liquid that drains or 'leaches' from a landfill, often requiring extensive treatment before disposal due to environmental regulations.
  • Arrowhead Landfill: A Waste Connections landfill in Alabama, central to its Northeast intermodal waste transport and internalization strategy.
  • RINs (Renewable Identification Numbers): Credits used to track renewable fuel production and compliance with EPA Renewable Fuel Standards, influencing RNG project economics.
  • Roll-off pulls: Movements of large, detachable waste containers (used in construction, demolition, and commercial applications) – a demand indicator for bulky/volume-based services.
  • Special waste: Industrial or non-traditional waste types requiring unique handling or disposal solutions, often including contaminated soils or remedial cleanup byproducts.
  • Yield: The net price realization per unit of waste serviced or processed, comprising both price increases and the net impact of service mix and customer churn.
