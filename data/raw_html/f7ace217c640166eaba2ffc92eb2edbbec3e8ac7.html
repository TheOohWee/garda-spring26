Earnings Call Transcript

Atlantic Union (AUB) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jul 24, 2025 at 10:08AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, July 24, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

Chief Executive Officer — John Asbury

Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Rob Gorman

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

Sandy Spring Acquisition: Closed April 1, adding $13 billion in assets, $8.6 billion in loans held for investment, and $1.9 billion in loans held for sale.

CRE Loan Sale: $2 billion of acquired commercial real estate loans were sold on June 26, 2025, exceeding initial pricing estimates and generating a $15.7 million pretax gain.

Net Interest Margin: core NIM increased eight basis points, excluding accretion income.

Net Interest Income: Tax equivalent net interest income was $325.7 million, rising $137.8 million sequentially, primarily from Sandy Spring contributions and merger-related accretion.

Loan Growth: Loans held for investment reached $27.3 billion at quarter-end, up $8.9 billion from the prior quarter; Assuming the Sandy Spring acquisition closed on March 31 instead of April 1, and excluding both the negative loan fair value marks on the acquired loans and the effect of the CRE loan sale, pro forma loan growth was approximately 4% annualized, above internal expectations.

Deposit Balances: Total deposits were $31 billion, increasing $10.5 billion from the prior quarter; adjusted for the acquisition close, pro forma deposits declined by $752.8 million due to intentional brokered and time deposit runoff.

Credit Quality: Net charge-offs were $6.666 million (one basis point annualized), and Nonperforming assets rose to 0.6% of loans, primarily from the newly acquired Sandy Spring portfolio.

Capital Ratios: CET1 capital ratio at quarter-end was 9.8%; bank-level commercial real estate concentration ratio was 284%.

Provision for Credit Losses: Provision for credit losses totaled $105.7 million, including a $100.9 million CECL day-one double count related to Sandy Spring; allowance for credit losses (GAAP) rose to $342.4 million (1.25% of loans held for investment).

Adjusted Operating Results: Adjusted operating net income to common shareholders was $135.1 million, or $0.95 per share; adjusted operating ROATCE was 23.8% and adjusted operating return on assets was 1.46%.

Efficiency Ratio: Adjusted operating efficiency ratio improved to 48.3%.

Dividends: Paid a common stock dividend of $0.34 per share. This amount represents an increase of 6.3% from the previous year's second quarter dividend.

Full-Year Guidance: Projected 2025 year-end loan balances of $28 billion–$28.5 billion and deposits of $31 billion–$31.5 billion; guidance for fully tax-equivalent net interest margin at 3.75%–4% for the full year, net interest income of $1.15 billion–$1.2 billion for the full year, and an allowance for credit losses to loans ratio expected to fall between 1.2% and 1.3% at year-end.

Expense Guidance: 2025 adjusted operating noninterest expense forecast is $670 million–$680 million, excluding estimated $60 million in intangibles amortization for the full year.

Branch Expansion: Plans to open 10 new branches in North Carolina from 2026 through 2028 and expand commercial, wealth, and mortgage teams in North Carolina starting in 2026.

SUMMARY

Atlantic Union Bankshares Corporation integrated Sandy Spring Bank one quarter ahead of schedule, materially increasing both loan and deposit bases and generating immediate merger-related financial benefits. The $2 billion sale of commercial real estate loans provided liquidity, improved the loan-to-deposit ratio, and reduced commercial real estate concentration, unlocking additional growth capacity. Adjusted return metrics reached high levels, including an adjusted operating return on tangible common equity of 23.8% and an adjusted operating efficiency ratio below 50%. Loan growth momentum was supported by a strong pipeline in both legacy and acquired markets, with management projecting continued organic expansion and highlighting Maryland and North Carolina as focal points for strategic growth. Deposit costs continued to decline while Loan yields increased to 6.48% from 6.01% in the prior quarter, contributing to the upward trend in net interest margin, with management emphasizing a gradual "grind higher" in the near term.

Key leadership transition was announced as Chief Financial Officer Rob Gorman disclosed his planned retirement, and a nationwide search for his successor is underway, with no anticipated changes to strategic direction or targets.

Management stated the Sandy Spring acquisition "provided us with significant growth opportunities in the large markets of Maryland and Northern Virginia," and enables accelerated investment in North Carolina expansion.

Rob Gorman said, we're still targeting a mid-40s efficiency ratio for 2026, inclusive of the investment we're making in the Carolinas. This guidance remains unchanged despite organic expansion plans.

Excess cash from the CRE loan sale will be used to pay down high-cost brokered deposits and reinvest in the securities portfolio, with $200 million–$300 million deposit runoff expected in the next quarter.

The company projects CET1 capital to continue rising by 25–30 basis points per quarter through internal capital generation. Share repurchases may be considered when CET1 capital ratio levels approach 10.5%–11%.

Commercial credit quality remained stable, with the increase in nonperforming assets "simply the Sandy Spring portfolio coming in," and Management characterized full-year net charge-off guidance as conservative, with allowance for specific problem credits.

Approximately 23% of total loans are in the Washington Metro Area; management highlighted resilience in these markets and noted recent record defense spending may benefit government contractor exposures.

Loan production is increasing in both C&I and stabilized CRE, with short sales cycles and growing borrower optimism reported late in the quarter.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

CECL (Current Expected Credit Loss): Accounting standard requiring estimation and recognition of all expected loan losses over the life of the asset at origination or purchase.

Net Interest Margin (NIM): Percentage measure of the difference between a bank's income from interest-bearing assets and its cost of funds relative to average earning assets.

ROATCE (Return on Average Tangible Common Equity): Ratio expressing earnings relative to average tangible (excluding goodwill and intangibles) common equity, used as a profitability and capital efficiency indicator.

CRE (Commercial Real Estate): Loan or asset category secured by commercial property, including office, retail, industrial, or multifamily real estate.

Pro Forma Loan Growth: Loan growth calculation adjusted for merger timing and significant portfolio transactions to reflect underlying organic expansion rate.
