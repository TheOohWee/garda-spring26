Q2 Holdings (QTWO) Q3 2024 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 2:55PM EST

DATE

Wednesday, November 6, 2024 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Matthew Flake
  • Prospective Chief Financial Officer — Jonathan Price
  • President — Kirk Coleman
  • Vice President, Investor Relations and Corporate Development — Josh Yankovich

TAKEAWAYS

  • Non-GAAP Revenue -- $175 million, up 13% year over year and increased 1% sequentially from the previous quarter.
  • Subscription Revenue -- Grew 18% year over year and 3% sequentially, now comprising over 80% of total revenue.
  • Total ARR -- $796 million, up 15% year over year; Subscription ARR -- $655 million, up 20% year over year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $32.6 million, representing 19% of revenue and up 66% year over year; increased 9% sequentially.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $35.1 million for the quarter; year-to-date free cash flow reached $70 million, compared to $10 million in the prior-year period.
  • Gross Margin -- 56%, rising from 53.9% in the prior-year and from 55.7% in the previous quarter due to a higher subscription revenue mix and efficiency gains.
  • Services and Other Revenue -- Decreased 11% year over year, reflecting lower professional services demand; this segment declined an average of 12% year over year through 2024.
  • Operating Expenses -- $73 million, or 41.5% of revenue, down from 45.8% a year ago and from 42.7% in the previous quarter, attributed to greater sales and marketing efficiency.
  • Ending Backlog -- Exceeded $2 billion, up $78 million sequentially (4%) and increased $467 million year over year (30%), driven by expansion bookings and strong renewals.
  • Full-Year 2024 Guidance -- Non-GAAP revenue expected between $691.5 million and $694.5 million (11% growth); subscription revenue growth projected at approximately 16%.
  • Full-Year Adjusted EBITDA Guidance -- Projected at $122 million to $124 million, about 18% of non-GAAP revenue.
  • Cross-Sell and Expansion Activity -- Achieved the strongest quarter of the year for cross-sell bookings; Innovation Studio bookings for the first three quarters doubled the 2023 full-year total.
  • Deal Activity and Sales Mix -- Secured 6 enterprise and Tier 1 bookings, including 3 top-50 U.S. banks; largest single digital banking ARR deal with a Tier 2 bank.
  • M&A-Driven Wins -- Gained a Tier 1 customer due to client consolidation via acquisition for the second consecutive quarter.
  • Relationship Pricing Highlight -- Closed a contract with a $90 billion bank, marking one of the top 5 largest relationship pricing deals in company history by contractual ARR.
  • Helix and Fabric -- Secured a major Helix renewal and expansion, plus the first Fabric win with Envisant to support prepaid card growth.
  • Fourth Quarter and Outlook -- Q4 non-GAAP revenue guidance set at $178.1 million to $181.1 million; Q4 adjusted EBITDA forecast at $34.3 million to $36.3 million.
  • 2025 Subscription Growth Outlook -- Full-year subscription revenue growth anticipated to reach approximately 15% in 2025.
  • Rule of 30 Achieved -- Reached the previously stated Rule of 30 total revenue milestone ahead of the late-2024 target.

SUMMARY

Q2 Holdings (QTWO 0.37%) reported double-digit year-over-year growth in revenue, subscription sales, and annualized recurring revenue, highlighting consistent expansion in high-margin, recurring business. Management emphasized strategic progress through record cross-sell achievements and notable wins with both Tier 1 and Tier 2 financial institutions, including an expansion of its Helix and Fabric platforms. Despite year-over-year declines in services and other revenues, efficiency measures improved gross margin and reduced operating expenses as a percentage of revenue, driving robust growth in adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow. The company raised its guidance for the full year, signifying continued strong performance and confidence in recurring revenue acceleration for 2025.

  • Management identified cross-sell momentum and faster realization of expansion revenue as key contributors to outperformance in subscription sales.
  • Leadership transition was completed, with Jonathan Price formally stepping into the CFO role following a period of close collaboration with his predecessor.
  • Q2's backlog growth was attributed to both renewals and expanded customer deployments, with sales and implementation teams working proactively to balance capacity and demand amid an increasing number of holistic, platform-wide deals.
  • In response to questions on M&A activity, management highlighted a company win rate near 90% for customer retention and platform adoption in bank acquisitions, though they declined to provide quantified forward estimates.
  • Guidance acknowledged Q4 faces a difficult comparison period due to exceptionally large 2023 deals, but management still expects sequential strength and continued momentum.
  • The CFO reiterated that operational efficiency initiatives—in pricing, packaging, and delivery—are ongoing, with further opportunity for subscription mix to drive margin improvements in 2025.
  • Several responses referenced “Innovation Studio” as a driver of both customer acquisition and product adoption, particularly among existing clients, with management indicating significant untapped potential in upselling this line.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ARR (Annualized Recurring Revenue): The annualized value of all active recurring revenue contracts at the end of a period, standard in SaaS and fintech.
  • Rule of 30: A software company metric where the sum of year-over-year revenue growth and adjusted EBITDA margin equals or exceeds 30%.
  • Backlog: The contracted amount of future revenue not yet recognized, sometimes called Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) in SaaS reporting.
  • Innovation Studio: Q2 Holdings’ ecosystem and platform fostering partner integrations and cross-selling opportunities for digital banking clients.
  • Helix: Q2 Holdings’ cloud-based core processing and banking-as-a-service platform serving digital-first financial offerings.
  • Fabric: An extension of the Helix platform designed for prepaid and payment product enablement within credit union and banking ecosystems.
