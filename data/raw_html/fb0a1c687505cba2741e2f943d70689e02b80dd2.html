WesBanco (WSBC) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 1:01PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Oct. 23, 2025 at 3 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Jeffrey Jackson
  • Senior Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Daniel Weiss

Takeaways

  • Net income (excluding merger and restructuring) -- $90 million, representing diluted EPS of $0.94, up 68% year over year.
  • GAAP net income -- $81 million, or $0.84 per share.
  • Pretax pre-provision core earnings growth -- Nearly 130% higher year over year.
  • Net interest margin -- 3.53%, a 58-basis-point improvement year over year, with a 6-basis-point decline sequentially from Q2 2025 due to matured CDs.
  • Fee income -- Grew 52% year over year, with noninterest income of $44.9 million; gross swap fees up $2.1 million to $3.2 million.
  • Total deposits -- $21.3 billion, up 53.8% year over year, driven by $6.9 billion from Premier acquisition and $573 million organic growth.
  • Organic loan growth -- 4.8% year over year, 2.2% quarter over quarter annualized, with commercial pipelines at $1.5 billion (over 40% from new markets and LPOs).
  • Commercial real estate payoffs -- $235 million in Q3 2025, creating a 1.5% headwind to loan growth.
  • Efficiency ratio -- Improved by 10 percentage points to 55% year over year, attributed to Premier synergies and cost control.
  • Return on average assets -- 1.3%, an increase over the prior period.
  • Return on tangible equity -- 17.5%, up from the previous year.
  • Total assets -- $27.5 billion, a 49% increase year over year, including $5.9 billion of acquired PFC loans.
  • Noninterest expense (excluding restructuring and merger) -- $144.8 million, 46% higher year over year due to acquisition, intangibles amortization, and FDIC expense.
  • Salaries and wages -- $60.6 million in the quarter; employee benefits $18 million; both up year over year but flat sequentially.
  • Allowance for credit losses -- $217.7 million, representing 1.15% of total loans, reflecting a decline from Q2 2025 due to a runoff in qualitative factors.
  • Criticized and classified loans -- Decreased to 3.2% during the quarter due to upgrades and payoffs.
  • Series B preferred stock -- $230 million issued Sept. 10, with $150 million earmarked to redeem Series A and $50 million to pay down PFC sub debt.
  • Financial center footprint -- 27 additional closures planned, totaling 80 since 2020, expected to yield $6 million in annual pretax savings; 75% of closures within 10 miles of another center.
  • Customer satisfaction -- Upper 80th percentile across all markets, with post-Premier acquisition markets recovered to pre-conversion levels.
  • Commercial production -- Year-to-date new commercial loan production at $2.3 billion, compared to $1.7 billion a year ago.
  • Health care vertical -- $250 million in loans, $80 million deposits, $2 million fees closed in six months; projected $300 million to $500 million loans annualized.
  • Deposit cost -- Funding costs of 256 basis points, down 29 basis points year over year; including noninterest-bearing, cost is 192 basis points.
  • Preferred stock dividends (Q4 2025 projected) -- $13 million composed of $2.5 million Series A, $5.5 million Series A redemption premium, and $4.9 million Series B.
  • Capital levels -- CET1 internal targets at 10.5%-11%, with 15-20 basis points of expected quarterly growth.
  • CD runoff -- $50 million intentional brokered deposit runoff in Q3 2025 without impacting organic growth.
  • Future loan growth guidance -- Mid-single-digit loan growth expected for full-year 2025 and mid- to upper single-digit range targeted for 2026.
  • Planned openings -- New financial centers slated for Knoxville, Tennessee, and Alliance, Ohio, in Q1 2026.
  • Provision for credit losses -- To be primarily determined by loan growth, economic factors, and charge-offs per CFO Daniel Weiss.

Risks

  • Commercial real estate payoffs totaled $235 million in the quarter and are projected to approach $800 million to $900 million for the full year, continuing to present a headwind to loan growth.
  • Noninterest expense increased 46% year over year due to additional operating expenses from Premier, higher amortization, and increased FDIC insurance, pressuring overhead despite offsetting synergies.
  • Health care costs in Q3 2025 exceeded baseline projections by $1 million due to large claims and general cost increases, impacting expense management.
  • Closure-related restructuring charges of $7 million and expected additional personnel costs create short-term expense pressure from footprint optimization.

Summary

WesBanco (WSBC 1.77%) reported significant year-over-year improvements in profitability, efficiency, and fee revenue, alongside successful Premier integration and targeted expansion in key growth markets. Strategic optimization includes a planned 27 financial center closures for approximately $6 million in projected annual pretax savings, and two new center openings to support geographic reach. Management targets mid-single-digit loan growth in 2025, with commercial pipelines and new verticals supporting broader organic growth while continuing disciplined capital build. The company maintains a 10.5%-11% CET1 goal, raised $230 million in new preferred capital, and anticipates further improvement in operating leverage through continued network and digital enhancements.

  • CFO Daniel Weiss said, "We funded loan growth with deposits, and on a year-over-year basis, improved the net interest margin by 58 basis points, grew fee income 52%, and reduced the efficiency ratio by 10 percentage points."
  • President and CEO Jeffrey Jackson cited record commercial loan production, noting $2.3 billion in new loans year-to-date, and described the commercial pipeline as "really strong, about $1.5 billion and have remained strong."
  • Deposit growth was sustained organically and fully funded new loans, as management emphasized momentum in core categories and minimal reliance on higher-cost CDs.
  • The health care vertical delivered $250 million in loans in its first six months and may achieve $300 million to $500 million in annualized originations, offering an emerging growth lever.

Industry glossary

  • LPO (Loan Production Office): A non-branch office focused on originating loans in targeted markets without deposit-taking operations.
  • CECL double count: Refers to the potentially duplicative capital impact from the Current Expected Credit Loss accounting standard until clarified by regulators.
  • PFC: Refers to Premier Financial Corp., the institution acquired in the referenced period.
