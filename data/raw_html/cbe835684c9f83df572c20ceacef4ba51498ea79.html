Earnings Call Transcript

Regeneron (REGN) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 2:43PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, August 1, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Board Co-Chair, Co-Founder, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Leonard S. Schleifer
  • Board Co-Chair, Co-Founder, President, and Chief Scientific Officer — George D. Yancopoulos
  • Executive Vice President, Commercial — Marion E. McCourt
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Christopher R. Fenimore
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations — Ryan Crowe

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenues -- $3.7 billion, up 4%, reflecting growth from Dupixent, EYLEA HD, and Libtayo collaborations.
  • Diluted Net Income per Share -- $12.89, an increase of 12%, with net income reaching $1.4 billion.
  • Dupixent Global Net Product Sales -- $4.3 billion, representing 21% growth on a constant currency basis, driven by expansion across all approved indications, age groups, and geographies.
  • U.S. Dupixent Net Product Sales -- $3.2 billion, up 23%, consolidating leadership in new-to-brand prescriptions for seven of eight FDA-approved indications.
  • Libtayo Global Net Product Sales -- $377 million, a 25% increase on a constant currency basis, now annualizing at $1.5 billion.
  • Libtayo U.S. Net Product Sales -- $248 million, up 36%, benefiting from timing of approximately $20 million in customer shipments (expected to adversely impact next quarter).
  • EYLEA U.S. Net Product Sales -- $754 million, a decline of 39%; sequential unit demand fell 10%, influenced by competitive pressures, patient affordability, and ongoing conversion to EYLEA HD.
  • EYLEA HD U.S. Net Product Sales -- $393 million, an all-time high, with 16% sequential growth in physician unit demand, comprising one-third of total U.S. retina product sales.
  • Bayer Collaboration: EYLEA/EYLEA 8 mg Ex-U.S. Net Sales -- $978 million, up 4% in constant currency, including $242 million from EYLEA 8 mg.
  • Sanofi Collaboration Revenue -- $1.4 billion recognized, of which $1.3 billion was Regeneron's share of profits, growing 30% due to Dupixent volume and improving margins.
  • Research and Development (R&D) Expense -- $1.3 billion, reflecting continued clinical pipeline investment across obesity, hematology, and thrombosis.
  • SG&A Expense -- $542 million, down 19% year over year, attributed to lower general and administrative costs.
  • Gross Margin on Net Product Sales -- 86%, lower due to increased investments in manufacturing operations and higher inventory write-offs.
  • Effective Tax Rate -- 8.3%, lowered by roughly 4 percentage points due to a favorable IRS audit settlement.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $1.7 billion generated in the first half of 2025.
  • Cash and Marketable Securities -- $17.5 billion, with debt at $2.7 billion as of quarter end.
  • Share Repurchases -- $1.1 billion bought back this quarter; $2.2 billion year-to-date, reducing outstanding shares by 3.2 million from December 2024.
  • Financial Guidance Updates -- Midpoint reductions for SG&A, R&D, and COGS by $125 million, with lower gross margin guidance unrelated to current tariff discussions.
  • Lynozyfic FDA Approval -- Lynozyfic, a BCMAxCD3 bispecific for relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma, received FDA approval in July; early launch progress noted with NCCN guideline inclusion as a preferred product.
  • EYLEA HD Label Enhancements -- Anticipated delay in PDUFA decisions for enhancements such as prefilled syringe, 4-week dosing, and macular edema (RVO) addition due to Catalent Indiana LLC FDA site inspection; Regeneron expects expeditious resolution but not before August 2025.
  • Odronextamab BLA Impact -- The Catalent site inspection also triggered an FDA Complete Response Letter (CRL) for the BLA application in relapsed/refractory follicular lymphoma.
  • Pipeline Clinical Milestones -- Phase III readouts expected within six months for C5 program (gMG), fianlimab plus Libtayo (melanoma), garetosmab (FOP), and birch/cat allergy trials; decision pending on further development of itepekimab in COPD.

RISKS

  • EYLEA U.S. net product sales are expected to face "ongoing switches to EYLEA HD, competitive pressures, patient affordability issues and pricing," which management said will "continue to negatively impact EYLEA U.S. net product sales going forward."
  • EYLEA HD label/route enhancements are now "likely to be delayed from their August 2025, PDUFA dates as a result of observations from an FDA general site inspection at the filler" (Catalent Indiana LLC), increasing regulatory and launch timing uncertainty.
  • Third-party funding gaps in patient assistance foundations for retina products continue to "negative impact on the branded anti-VEGF category," as reported by Marion E. McCourt.
  • Libtayo U.S. net sales included a $20 million benefit from shipment timing, which is expected to "adversely impact third quarter U.S. net product sales."

SUMMARY

Regeneron Pharmaceuticals (REGN 2.47%) reported single-digit revenue growth, driven by robust demand for Dupixent and expansion of Libtayo's oncology portfolio. EYLEA HD delivered exceptional sequential sales and unit demand growth, but overall EYLEA franchise sales declined sharply as conversion and competition intensified. Regulatory setbacks emerged following an FDA site inspection at Catalent Indiana, causing anticipated PDUFA delays and a Complete Response Letter for odronextamab. Continued R&D investment was highlighted by imminent late-stage pipeline milestones and substantial capital commitments to U.S. manufacturing infrastructure.

  • Lynozyfic's FDA approval in multiple myeloma establishes a differentiated position with higher complete response rates and inclusion in NCCN guidelines, with early commercial uptake tempered by REMS requirements and pending formulary decisions.
  • Dupixent's addressable market expanded to over 4 million patients in the U.S. through three newly approved indications in the past 10 months, yet current patient penetration remains low and growth runway persists.
  • Regeneron's collaboration profits from Sanofi increased 30%, and the company maintains $17.5 billion in cash and equivalents while actively repurchasing shares; management clarified that full reimbursement of the Sanofi development balance is now anticipated by year-end 2026.
  • Emerging obesity pipeline strategies include combination therapies that demonstrated lean mass preservation in semaglutide-treated patients, with interim Phase II data presented and final results to be disclosed at a major diabetes congress in September 2025.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • BCMAxCD3 bispecific: A dual-targeting antibody therapy designed for multiple myeloma that bridges CD3 on T cells and BCMA on myeloma cells, promoting T cell-mediated tumor cell destruction.
  • PDUFA date: The target date by which the U.S. FDA must respond to a new drug application as set by the Prescription Drug User Fee Act.
  • REMS: Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy, required by the FDA to ensure safe use of certain medicines with specific safety concerns.
  • NCCN: National Comprehensive Cancer Network, which issues clinical practice guidelines widely used in oncology care and reimbursement decisions.
  • CRL (Complete Response Letter): An official communication from the FDA indicating that a drug application cannot be approved in its current form.
  • gMG (generalized myasthenia gravis): A rare neuromuscular disorder causing weakness and fatigue, targeted by Regeneron's C5 program.
  • FOP (fibrodysplasia ossificans progressiva): A rare genetic disorder characterized by abnormal bone formation in muscles and connective tissues.
  • DLBCL (diffuse large B-cell lymphoma): An aggressive form of non-Hodgkin lymphoma, included in Regeneron's pivotal trials for new therapies.
  • RVO (retinal vein occlusion): An eye condition involving blockage of retinal veins, addressed by enhancements pending for EYLEA HD.

The combination of semaglutide with trevogrumab was generally well tolerated.
