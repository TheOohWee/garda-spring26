Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $496.8 million for Q3 2025, up 15% year over year, with growth attributed to increased advertiser traction on the Realize platform.
  • Ex-TAC Gross Profit -- Ex-TAC gross profit was $176.8 million for Q3 2025, up 6.3% year over year; growth was driven by Realize and higher advertising spend from scaled advertisers.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA was $48.2 million for Q3 2025, with a 27.3% margin; reflects company focus on cost discipline and strategic investments to support expansion.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Free cash flow was $46.3 million for Q3 2025, representing a 96% conversion from adjusted EBITDA, driven by high margins and working capital efficiency.
  • Share Repurchases -- Nearly 14% of outstanding shares bought back year to date ($184 million) as of Q3 2025, reducing share count from approximately 337 million at the end of 2024 to about 291 million at the end of 2025.
  • Scaled Advertisers -- 2,064 scaled advertisers in Q3 2025, up 4.4% year over year; average revenue per scaled advertiser grew 10.9% year over year, benefiting from product improvements and easier comparison with last year.
  • Net Income -- $5.2 million net income for Q3 2025; non-GAAP net income was $34.3 million for Q3 2025.
  • App Traffic -- Now represents about one-third of total supply, driven by growth with publisher and OEM partners, and showing double-digit increases.
  • Foreign Exchange Impact -- $2 million headwind on expenses versus Q3 2024, linked mainly to the Israeli shekel; partially offset by positive ex-TAC effects.
  • Credit Facility -- New $270 million revolving credit line secured, lowering interest expenses by $1.6 million and increasing available liquidity to approximately $196 million as of September 30.
  • Q4 2025 Guidance -- Revenue of $532 million-$542 million for Q4 2025, ex-TAC gross profit of $204 million-$210 million, adjusted EBITDA of $83 million-$85 million, and non-GAAP net income of $52 million-$56 million.
  • Full-Year 2025 Guidance -- Revenue of $1.91 billion-$1.93 billion for full year 2025, ex-TAC gross profit of $700 million-$710 million, adjusted EBITDA of $209 million-$214 million, and non-GAAP net income of $139 million-$144 million.
  • Realize Platform Highlight -- Management reported "an inflection point in the business with Realize" and reiterated expectations.
  • Taboola News -- Outpaced company growth rates, with app-based and OEM supply cited as key drivers and described as "completely immune to LLM disintermediation" according to Adam Singolda.
  • Paramount Partnership -- Newly announced collaboration extends Realize’s demand-generation capabilities to CTV environments, enabling advertisers to measure cross-channel outcomes.

Summary

Taboola.com (TBLA +0.00%) delivered revenue above its high-end guidance for Q3 2025, fueled by expanding adoption and higher spend among scaled advertisers on its Realize platform. Ex-TAC gross profit and adjusted EBITDA also increased, though ex-TAC margins were down year over year due to last year’s Yahoo-related test impacts. Share repurchases reduced the share count by almost 14% year to date as of the end of 2025. The company highlighted strong cash generation, high free cash flow conversion rates, and robust liquidity enhanced by a new credit facility. Management raised guidance for both Q4 and full-year 2025, supported by a "an inflection point" in advertiser engagement and platform momentum.

  • CFO Walker said, Ex-TAC gross profit margins were down year over year primarily due to the one-time testing we were doing with Yahoo in Q3 2024. warning of headwinds that distort comparability across periods.
  • Management reported that free cash flow conversion from adjusted EBITDA has consistently exceeded 70% over the last four and eight quarters.
  • Investments increased in marketing and growth initiatives for Realize, with particular success in U.S. markets and among ideal customer verticals, including finance, auto, health, and direct-to-consumer products.
  • App-based traffic growth, driven by partnerships and OEM integrations, now constitutes a significant and increasing portion of Taboola's ad inventory, contributing favorably to engagement and monetization.
  • Walker indicated, "we are no longer required to purchase shares from Yahoo for the remainder of 2025," granting the company greater flexibility for market repurchases.
  • LLM-based AI productivity and campaign optimization tools such as "Abby" have begun to impact advertiser success rates and internal sales operations, but management characterized these initiatives as early-stage for measurable impact outside core platform yield improvement.

Industry glossary

  • Ex-TAC Gross Profit: Revenue remaining after deducting traffic acquisition costs (payments to publishers and partners for ad inventory), representing the company's retained gross profit before operating expenses.
  • Scaled Advertiser: An advertiser spending at least $100,000 per year on Taboola’s platform, considered a key growth and retention metric.
  • OEM: Original Equipment Manufacturer; refers to device makers (such as Apple and Samsung) partnering with Taboola to pre-install or integrate its content and ad recommendations.
  • LLM: Large Language Model; advanced artificial intelligence model used for tasks like content creation, campaign optimization, and productivity enhancements.
  • CTV: Connected TV; digital television viewed via internet-connected devices, offering advertising formats measurable in both TV and online channels.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, and other excluded items, serving as a normalized profitability measure favored by management.
