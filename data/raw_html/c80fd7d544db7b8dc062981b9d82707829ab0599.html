Earnings Call Transcript

MasTec (MTZ) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 31, 2025 at 10:24AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Friday, Oct. 31, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

Call participants

Chief Executive Officer — Jose Ramon Mas

Chief Financial Officer — Paul DiMarco

Vice President, Investor Relations — Chris Mecray

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

Revenue -- Revenue was just under $4 billion, reflecting a 22% year-over-year increase.

Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA was $374 million, up 20% compared to the prior year.

Adjusted EPS -- Adjusted earnings per share were $2.48, nearly $0.20 above consensus according to management.

Backlog -- Backlog was $16.8 billion at quarter-end, up approximately $325 million sequentially and 21% year-over-year.

Communications segment revenue -- $915 million, up 33% year-over-year; EBITDA rose 38% organically year-over-year with EBITDA margin at 11.3% (up 40 basis points year-over-year and 140 basis points sequentially).

Clean energy & infrastructure (CE&I) segment revenue -- $1.4 billion, up 20% year-over-year and 21% sequentially; EBITDA up 36% year-over-year with segment margin at 8.5% (an increase of 100 basis points year-over-year and 110 basis points sequentially).

Power delivery segment -- Power Delivery segment revenue and EBITDA up 17% and 21%, respectively, year-over-year with EBITDA margin increasing 30 basis points to 9.4%, but below internal low double-digit forecasts due to project mix and Greenlink permitting delays.

Pipeline infrastructure segment -- Revenue of $598 million, up 20% year-over-year and 11% sequentially; EBITDA of $92 million with a 15.4% margin, meeting mid-teens guidance; backlog at $1.6 billion, rising 8% sequentially and 124% year-over-year, with $600 million in new bookings.

Book-to-bill ratio -- 1.1 times overall and for CE&I, 1.2 times for pipeline, indicating booking rates exceeded revenue recognition.

Backlog details -- Communications segment backlog was $5.1 billion, CE&I backlog was just above $5 billion, and Power Delivery backlog up 11% year-over-year; pipeline segment highlighted as having visibility beyond reported backlog due to timing of contract finalization.

Greenlink project impact -- Power Delivery revenue reduced to around $250 million for fiscal year 2025 due to permit-related delays (down from original $300 million–$500 million annual expectation) with management expecting completion schedule to remain unchanged and higher activity in 2026.

Cash flow and liquidity -- Cash flow from operations was $89 million and free cash flow was $36 million; net leverage ratio was 1.95 times; total liquidity was approximately $2 billion at quarter-end.

2025 guidance update -- Full-year revenue guidance increased to $14.075 billion for 2025; adjusted EBITDA outlook raised to $1.135 billion for 2025; adjusted EPS forecast $6.40, up 62% over 2024 for full-year 2025.

Capital expenditures -- Near-term CapEx expected to run above depreciation (currently about $300 million annually), with management forecasting approximately $350 million in capital expenditures going into 2026, subject to business growth mix.

Large project awards -- Power Delivery segment awarded its second-largest transmission/substation project (after Greenlink), to start mid-2026 and enter backlog by year-end.

Summary

MasTec (MTZ +1.08%) delivered all-time high third-quarter revenue and backlog, with every segment contributing to backlog gains despite heavy project execution and burn. The company attributed margin improvements to productivity initiatives and operating leverage, although Power Delivery margins lagged internal targets due to lower-than-expected Greenlink project activity and a lack of high-margin storm work. Management disclosed robust visibility in pipeline infrastructure contracts, citing the evolving “book and burn” process and noting actual opportunity exceeds reported backlog, particularly with several projects commencing in 2026 and beyond. The team expressed comfort with the consensus expectation for more than $8 adjusted EPS in 2026, reflecting 25% year-over-year growth.

CEO Mas stated, “We now see the ability to exceed historical high revenue levels in that business. Historically, our high years in that business were about $3 billion in revenue. We're guiding to $2 billion in revenue for 2025, and now have a path to meet or exceed historical levels. I think that's the best way to frame where we see the opportunity—not for 2026, but for beyond. I feel better about the opportunity to do that today than I did last quarter.” in pipeline infrastructure, with management expecting substantial revenue increases into 2027 and beyond.

Clean energy backlog reached its highest level in company history, with CEO Mas highlighting three of the four largest wind jobs secured in the business to date, and a “nine straight sequential increase” in renewables backlog.

Management explained that margin expansion remains a major focus, expecting annual positive progression, and acknowledged that investments in new geographies and customer relationships are temporarily constraining margin upside.

CEO Mas affirmed comfort with managing two concurrent major transmission projects, with a strategic goal to build capacity for three or more over time.

The majority of current projects include battery storage solutions, with significant growth expected in 2025 and further expansion anticipated in 2026, according to CEO Mas.

Management clarified that a portion of the Hugh Brinson pipeline project is included in current backlog, with further components due to be booked as milestones are met.

Chief Financial Officer DiMarco said, “our strong balance sheet provides us significant financial flexibility to pursue a disciplined return-focused capital allocation strategy,” reiterating a focus on organic investment, select acquisitions, and opportunistic buybacks.

Industry glossary

Book-to-bill ratio: A measure of the value of new project bookings relative to revenue recognized, indicating growth potential if above 1.0.

Burn (project burn rate): The rate at which backlog is converted to recognized revenue as projects are executed.

Greenlink project: A large-scale power transmission and substation infrastructure project serving as a benchmark for MasTec's Power Delivery segment.

DSO (days sales outstanding): The average number of days it takes for a company to collect payment on sales made on credit, closely monitored for cash flow impact.
