Earnings Call Transcript

PRA Group (PRAA) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 3, 2025 at 6:15PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, Nov. 3, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

President and Chief Executive Officer — Martin Sjolund

Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Rakesh Sehgal

Managing Director, Investor Relations — Najim Mostamand

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

Noncash goodwill impairment -- Management reported a $413 million nonrecurring impairment charge related to historical European acquisitions in Q3 2025, triggered by the sustained decline in PRA Group’s stock price in Q3 2025, resulting in a net loss of $408 million for Q3 2025.

Negative adjustments to U.S. COVID vintages -- CEO Martin Sjolund stated, while absorbing negative adjustments in our challenging U.S. Core vintages '21, '22, and '23 in Q3 2025, these currently represent around 10% of global ERC.

TAKEAWAYS

Portfolio purchases -- $255 million acquired in Q3 2025, split 60% in The Americas and 40% in Europe, with a year-over-year decline reflecting management’s prioritization of net returns over purchased volumes.

Cash collections -- Increased 14% to $542 million year over year, exceeding global expectations by 8%, led by a 27% rise in U.S. legal cash collections, and a 10% overperformance in Europe.

Nonrecurring goodwill impairment -- A $413 million noncash charge was recorded, directly resulting from lower stock price and tied to historic European acquisitions, with $27 million goodwill remaining for claims servicing as of Q3 2025.

Net loss and adjusted profitability -- Reported net loss was $408 million, but adjusted net income—excluding the impairment charge—was $21 million, translating to adjusted ROACE of 9% and adjusted diluted EPS of 53¢.

Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA reached $1.3 billion for the last twelve months, up 15% year over year, growing faster than cash collections and marking nine consecutive quarterly increases in adjusted EBITDA.

Operating expenses -- Operating expenses totaled $627 million for Q3 2025, but adjusted operating expenses (excluding impairment) were $214 million, up 12% year over year due to continued investment in legal collections.

Net leverage -- Stood at 2.8 times as of September 30, compared to 2.9 times in the prior year period; excluding the $15 million one-time payment, leverage would have been 2.7 times.

Cost reduction initiatives -- U.S. headcount was reduced by over 115 employees during Q3 2025, and agent headcount by 170, yielding gross annualized cost savings of $20 million with around $3 million of those savings offset by increased outsourcing costs.

European performance -- Europe’s cash collections overperformed expectations by 11% year to date 2025, with six consecutive years of overperformance against underwritten targets and further positive adjustments to ERC in the quarter.

2025 guidance reaffirmed -- Management reiterated its targets for $1.2 billion portfolio purchases for 2025, high single-digit cash collections growth target for 2025, and cash efficiency of 60% plus for the full year 2025.

First euro-denominated bond issuance -- A €300 million, seven-year term bond was issued in Europe during Q3 2025 to pay down bank debt, expand investor base, and rebalance the secured/unsecured debt mix; company reports $1.2 billion of credit facility availability as of September 30, 2025.

Southern Europe -- Management identified increased deployment opportunities in Southern Europe as competitive dynamics stabilized, allowing investment at company return thresholds.

SUMMARY

Management reported a $413 million nonrecurring goodwill impairment in Q3 2025 driven by stock price declines, erasing net profit for the period despite continuing operational gains. PRA Group (PRAA 0.42%) grew cash collections by 14% year over year in Q3 2025 and achieved a 27% increase in U.S. legal channel collections year over year, exceeding internal forecasts globally and across its main geographies. Adjusted EBITDA climbed 15% to $1.3 billion for the last twelve months, outpacing cash collections and supporting reductions in net leverage, while operating expense control and headcount reductions yielded $20 million in gross annualized savings, as disclosed on the Q3 2025 earnings call. European operations registered sustained outperformance, and the company expanded its capital structure flexibility through its inaugural euro-denominated bond. Management reaffirmed full-year financial targets for portfolio purchases, cash collections, and efficiency, noting continued investment selectivity and operating discipline amid headwinds from challenging U.S. COVID vintages.

CEO Sjolund stated, "We now have around one third of our calling capacity offshore," highlighting the company’s ongoing call center optimization and shift toward offshoring.

CFO Sehgal confirmed, "ERC at quarter end was $8.4 billion. Up 15% year over year. And up 1% on a sequential basis."

Management clarified that the $15 million one-time payment to a portfolio selling partner was a "very unusual situation and a one-off," with associated economics reflected as both a revenue reduction and a benefit to ERC across several vintages.

The adjusted cash efficiency ratio was 61% for Q3 2025, essentially in line with the prior year when excluding the goodwill impairment impact.

The legal channel represented 46% of America’s core collections in Q3 2025, compared to 38% two years ago.

The company highlighted plans for a second U.S. talent hub in Charlotte, North Carolina, with a new office opening in early 2026.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

ERC (Estimated Remaining Collections): Projected future cash recoveries from all owned portfolios, discounted to present value, and used as a key indicator for intraperiod and long-term revenue generation.

ROACE (Return on Average Common Equity): A measure of profitability calculated using adjusted net income divided by average common equity; used to assess operational return performance.

Purchase Price Multiple: The ratio of purchase price paid for a portfolio to its expected gross cash collections, used to assess pricing discipline and expected profitability at acquisition.
