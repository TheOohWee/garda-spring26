TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $1.285 billion, up 9.1%, driven by both organic expansion and acquisitions.
  • Organic Revenue Growth -- 3.6% consolidated, with deceleration attributed to property rate pressure and lower new business in Retail.
  • Adjusted EBITDAC Margin -- 36.7%, an increase of 100 basis points, supported by incremental interest income and underlying margin expansion.
  • Adjusted Diluted Earnings Per Share -- $1.03, up 10.8% on a higher share base.
  • Retail Segment -- Organic growth of 3%, total revenue up 7.9%; margin decreased by 50 basis points to 27.5% due to Quintes revenue seasonality.
  • National Programs Segment -- 4.6% organic growth; total revenue up 6.1%; EBITDAC margin expanded by 320 basis points to 52.8%, aided by a contingent commission true-up.
  • Wholesale Brokerage Segment -- Organic growth of 3.9%, total revenue up 14.5%; margin increased by 80 basis points to 34.1%, with higher contingent income and acquisition mix impact.
  • Acquisition Activity -- 15 transactions completed in the quarter with $22 million in annualized acquired revenue; 29 acquisitions year-to-date totaling $60 million.
  • Debt and Equity Financing -- $4.4 billion equity and $4.2 billion debt issued for the pending Accession acquisition; equity issued at just over a 3% discount, average debt coupon at 5.4%.
  • Cash Flow from Operations -- $537 million for the first half, an increase of $164 million.
  • Dividend Growth -- Dividends paid per share increased by 15.4%.
  • Effective Tax Rate -- 24.7%, down from 25.3%.
  • Adjusted Interest Income/Expense -- $13 million in incremental interest income and $5 million in additional interest expense due to debt/equity actions.
  • Shares Outstanding -- Weighted average increased by approximately 10 million, primarily from the equity raise.
  • Planned Accession Integration -- Substantially all regulatory approvals obtained; anticipated close on August 1 and integration planning underway.
  • U.S. P&C Market Pricing -- Admitted rates increased 1%-5%, compared to prior periods of 2%-7% and 5%-10%.
  • E&S Property Market -- Second quarter rates declined 15%-30%; pressure intensified late in the quarter.
  • Medical and Pharmacy Cost Trends -- U.S. employee benefit medical costs up 6%-8%, pharmacy costs up over 10% with no slowing expected.
  • Retail New Business -- Decline attributed to both lower new business written and greater rate pressure, as described by J. Powell Brown.
  • Contingent Commissions -- Growth in contingent commissions tied to carrier profitability and positive program performance, partially offsetting organic slowdowns.
  • Upcoming Segment Realignment -- Programs and Wholesale Brokerage to merge into “Specialty Distribution” division beginning next quarter.
  • Revolving Credit Facility -- Outstanding $400 million balance fully repaid during the quarter.
  • Transaction and Integration Costs -- $37 million of onetime costs related to Accession excluded from adjusted profitability metrics.
  • Set Aside for Discontinued Operations -- $750 million reserved for Accession’s historic operations in runoff; J. Powell Brown said, "we felt that it would be appropriate to have a set aside for those operations."

RISKS

  • J. Powell Brown stated, "over half that discrepancy was because of rates, so downward pressure on rates," identifying rate deceleration as a key factor impacting Retail organic growth expectations for Q3 and Q4.
  • R. Andrew Watts acknowledged, "June definitely had a further trail off compared to what we were seeing in April and May," highlighting accelerating rate and new business softness late in the quarter.
  • J. Powell Brown clarified, "it surprised us in the speed of decline in Q2 and particularly in the latter part of the quarter."
  • J. Powell Brown advised, "everyone else needs to factor that in to your organic growth expectations in Q3 and Q4," referencing continued headwinds from property rate deceleration.

SUMMARY

Brown & Brown (BRO 3.01%) reported double-digit growth in revenue and earnings, with margin expansion and increased cash flow from operations. The company completed significant debt and equity financing to support the pending Accession acquisition, which is expected to close imminently following regulatory approvals. Management cited accelerated softening in property and E&S markets, especially late in the quarter, as a significant drag on organic growth, which may pressure results in subsequent quarters. Segment dynamics included margin expansion in Programs from contingent commission adjustments and a planned segment realignment into Specialty Distribution. Management stated no change to long-term margin guidance, emphasizing strong balance sheet positioning and continued M&A activity supported by ample liquidity.

  • Program and contingent commission performance buffered margin impact from slowing organic growth, as carrier profitability improved.
  • Management confirmed acquisition margin improvement typically follows over 12 to 36 months for standalone deals.
  • The share and debt issuances achieved significant oversubscription, supporting the company’s ability to finance integration and maintain delevering flexibility post-closing.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • EBITDAC: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, and change in acquisition earn-out payables; a metric used to assess operating performance excluding certain non-cash and acquisition-related adjustments.
  • E&S (Excess and Surplus): Insurance market segment covering risks that standard carriers will not write, often involving specialty or higher-risk exposures and subject to greater pricing variability.
  • Contingent Commissions: Additional commissions paid by carriers to brokers based on profitability, volume, or growth of the placed business, often recognized after reconciliation of annual carrier/broker results.
  • Set Aside: A reserve established for anticipated future liabilities related to discontinued operations, not attributable to active lines of business.
  • Binding Authority: A wholesale insurance arrangement in which a brokerage or MGA can underwrite, bind, and issue policies on behalf of carriers, usually for a specific class or book of business.
  • MGA (Managing General Agent): A specialized intermediary with delegated underwriting authority from insurers to manage certain kinds of insurance business, including program development and claims administration.
