Earnings Call Transcript

Duluth (DLTH) Q2 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 7, 2026 at 10:06AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Aug. 31, 2023 at 9:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Sam Sato
  • Senior Vice President and Chief Merchandising Officer — Eli Getson
  • Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Heena Agrawal

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $141.6 million, up 1.8%, supported by higher average order value and increased units per transaction.
  • Gross Margin -- 52.3%, a 90 basis point improvement, driven by sourcing initiatives and sell-through of higher-cost inventory.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Loss of $0.02, favorable to last year's loss by $0.04, with adjustments for $1.6 million in restructuring and a $2.4 million one-time sales tax contingency.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $10.6 million, 7.5% of net sales, an increase of $2 million, or 23.9%.
  • Liquidity -- $210 million total liquidity; ending cash balance of approximately $10 million, and $11 million of debt repaid, resulting in no outstanding debt at quarter's end.
  • Women's Business Growth -- Increased 5.8%, led by a 22% rise in the women's first layer category, and a 20% gain in women's bras.
  • Men's Category Trends -- Duluth men's grew 1% overall; Armachillo up 10%, woven shorts up 8%, and Double Flex Denim up 3%.
  • Direct Channel Sales -- Grew 5.6%, reversing from a decline in the prior quarter, with double-digit growth in units per transaction.
  • Retail Store Sales -- Declined 4.4%, though the traffic trend improved, with sequential traffic decline moderating to 4% from 7% in the prior quarter.
  • Mobile Penetration -- 70% of visits, and 57% of sales originated from mobile devices, up 100 and 230 basis points, respectively; mobile conversion improved by 10 basis points, and mobile sales increased nearly 10%.
  • Clearance Inventory -- Rose to 11% of total, up from 7% last year, with 89% of inventory classified as current product.
  • SG&A Expenses -- Increased 4.6% to $76.3 million, or 53.9% of sales; adjusted SG&A rose 1.3% to $73.9 million, leveraging by 20 basis points to 52.2% of sales.
  • Restructuring Charges -- $1.6 million recognized, with an additional $5.8 million anticipated next quarter due to the exit from a legacy fulfillment center; total planned restructuring expense is $7.4 million.
  • Fulfillment Center Optimization -- Adairsville facility now processes nearly 60% of online orders and replenishments, offering 65% lower variable CPU than legacy centers, and annualized run rate savings of approximately $5 million expected to begin late Q4.
  • Product Innovation -- "Newness" increased over 300 basis points; notable launches included Bullpen 3D underwear, souped-up sweats, Heirloom Garden prints, and forthcoming Hanes and Pheasants Forever collaborations.
  • Retail Portfolio Strategy -- Two new stores targeted to open in back half of 2025; 15 stores (about 25% of fleet) up for renewal by 2026, with criteria for remodel, relocation, or exit based on heightened profitability thresholds.
  • Outlook Reaffirmed -- Full-year sales guidance remains $640 million, with adjusted EPS guidance at minus $0.22, and adjusted EBITDA guidance at $39 million; gross margin expansion for the year projected at 150 basis points.
  • Capital Expenditure -- Year-to-date capex was $8 million; full-year capex expected to reduce by more than half to $25 million, focusing mainly on technology investments.

RISKS

  • Agrawal warned of higher clearance inventory leading to gross margin pressure in the third quarter, stating, "we will see higher pressure in Q3 as we try to clean out of it sooner rather than later."
  • Agrawal reported a $2.4 million estimated sales tax contingency for prior fiscal years, increasing SG&A expense and identified as a one-time non-recurring adjustment.
  • Sato indicated ongoing retail store traffic challenges, though trends improved sequentially; retail store sales fell 4.4%, and traffic declined 4%, albeit better than the prior quarter's 7% drop.

SUMMARY

Duluth Holdings (DLTH 0.91%) delivered modest top-line growth and gross margin expansion, enabled by direct-to-factory sourcing efforts and fulfillment network optimization. Management introduced plans to open two new stores in late 2025 while intensifying evaluation of 25% of the existing fleet for potential remodel, relocation, or exit to improve profitability. Investment in technology, product innovation, and omnichannel initiatives drove higher digital engagement, evidenced by rising mobile penetration, and supported a younger, expanding customer base. The company reaffirmed full-year sales and adjusted EBITDA guidance amid ongoing inventory clearance actions and a one-time sales tax adjustment, with gross margin benefits expected to continue into future years.

  • The Adairsville fulfillment center is projected to produce $5 million in annual operational savings, with expected benefits beginning in late Q4, and supporting future SG&A leverage.
  • Strategic marketing initiatives, including partnerships with Amazon Prime Video and Yellowstone, targeted younger demographics, and contributed to improved mobile conversion and digital sales.
  • Customer acquisition skews younger, with new customers averaging five years below the existing base, and the highest gains occurring in users under 50, and in women's penetration rates.
  • Product development yielded measurable innovation as the level of newness sequentially improved in the second quarter and increased by more than 300 basis points compared to last year. Product innovation highlights that resonated with consumers include: the women's business grew nearly 6% this quarter with positive results across both brands, particularly in Duluth. Growth was largely driven by strength in the women's first layer business, which grew by 22%. Success continued across the Armachillo, Buck Naked, and Dry on the Fly collections, and women's bras increased by 20%, driven by the popularity of the TeeLUXE bra as well as plus sizes. Additionally, the Heirloom Garden collection continued to be a favorite, as evidenced by growth of 18% this quarter, bolstered by a variety of new prints.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • First Layer: Category referring to base-layer apparel such as underwear, bras, and lightweight tops worn closest to the body in Duluth Holdings' product segmentation.
  • CPU (Cost Per Unit): Operational metric referencing the per-unit cost to process and fulfill an order in the context of distribution and logistics analytics within Duluth Holdings' fulfillment centers.
  • Four-wall Profitability: Financial performance measure reflecting profits attributable directly to individual store operations, excluding corporate allocation, as applied to Duluth's retail fleet evaluations.
