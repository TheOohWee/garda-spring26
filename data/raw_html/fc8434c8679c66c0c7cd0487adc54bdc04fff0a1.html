Earnings Call Transcript

Advanced Energy (AEIS) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 10, 2026 at 5:45PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, February 10, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Steve Kelley
  • Chief Financial Officer — Paul Oldham

Takeaways

  • Total Revenue -- $489 million, up 6% sequentially and 18% year over year, reaching the high end of guidance.
  • Data Center Computing Revenue -- $178 million, up 4% sequentially and 101% year over year, marking a quarterly record.
  • Semiconductor Revenue -- $212 million, up 8% sequentially and 6% for the year, achieving the company's second-highest annual figure in this segment.
  • Industrial and Medical Revenue -- $78 million, up 10% sequentially and 2% year over year; sequential growth followed three quarters of decline and signals market normalization.
  • Gross Margin -- 39.7% in the quarter, up 60 basis points sequentially and approaching the company's 40% target.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $1.94 per share, rising from $1.74 in Q3 and $1.30 in the prior year quarter.
  • Operating Margin -- 17.8%, up 100 basis points sequentially and 430 basis points year over year.
  • Cash Flow from Operations -- $80 million for the quarter and $235 million for the year, both at record levels.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $38 million in Q4; full-year CapEx was $107 million, or 6% of revenue.
  • Share Repurchases -- 33,000 shares bought back at an average price of $205.38, totaling $6.7 million.
  • Factory Capacity Expansion -- Output more than doubled in The Philippines and Mexico; initial fit-up of the new Thailand factory completed, enabling over $1 billion in annual revenue-generating capacity.
  • 2026 Revenue Outlook -- Management projects "high teens" percentage revenue growth, after achieving 21% growth in 2025.
  • Data Center Revenue Forecast -- Outlook raised to exceed 30% year-over-year growth, solely based on existing customers.
  • Gross Margin Targets -- Management expects to achieve ≥40% gross margin in 2026 and maintains a long-term goal of 43% despite higher data center mix and tariffs.
  • Product Pipeline and Design Wins -- 26 new product launches in 2025; new Everest, EVOS, and NavX technologies are "delivering meaningful improvements in yield and throughput" in semiconductor applications.
  • Inventory Management -- Inventory days declined by three to 125 and turns improved to 2.9; DSO increased to 60 days, DPO to 68 days, and net working capital decreased from 124 to 117 days.
  • Next-Gen Data Center Initiatives -- Engagement in multiple 800-volt projects with marquee customers; management stated, "our total dollar opportunity goes up with 800-volt solutions relative to what we're generating today."
  • Operating Expense Guidance -- OpEx run rate projected to reach around $120 million by Q4 2026, with $450 million to $460 million forecasted for the year.
  • Q1 2026 Guidance -- Revenue expected at approximately $500 million ±$20 million, flat non-GAAP EPS (~$1.94), and gross margin in the 39.5%-40% range.

Risks

  • CEO Kelley cautioned, "allocation situations in both processors and memory, which will limit some of the growth we believe in 2026," citing sold-out memory makers and ongoing supply constraints for processors.
  • Kelley also flagged, "We do anticipate that we'll see some supply chain issues moving forward," highlighting proactive inventory increases for risk management.
  • The industrial and medical segment experienced an 11% year-over-year revenue decline for 2025, reflecting lingering end-market weakness despite recent sequential improvements.
  • CFO Oldham referenced macroeconomic uncertainty as a potential "wild card" for the pace of industrial and medical sector recovery in 2026.

Summary

Advanced Energy Industries (AEIS +1.24%) reported broad-based revenue growth in key end markets with record results in data center computing and ongoing margin expansion. Management outlined continued capacity investments, including the operational readiness of its major new Thailand factory, supporting future top-line targets and providing flexibility to meet anticipated demand surges. During the call, executives emphasized new product adoption and design win momentum in both semiconductor and data center segments, reinforcing confidence in sustained market share gains across multiple verticals.

  • Data center expansion is anchored by sustained AI investment and direct customer engagements, with segment revenue now representing a growing share of company-wide sales.
  • Operational leverage improved as gross and operating margins rose substantially year over year and outpaced the rate of operating expense growth.
  • Strategic priorities include doubling manufacturing output in key geographies, aggressively scaling new product introductions, and maintaining financial flexibility for further acquisitions.
  • Management communicated intent to balance organic growth with opportunistic M&A, specifically aiming to expand scale and product breadth in industrial and medical markets.

Industry glossary

  • EVOS / Everest / NavX: Proprietary Advanced Energy semiconductor power delivery platforms, cited for enabling improvements in wafer process yield and throughput, specifically at advanced process nodes.
  • 800-volt AI data center rack: Next-generation server rack architecture designed for higher power efficiency in large-scale artificial intelligence computing environments; cited as a source of increased revenue opportunity and engineering engagement for AEIS.
  • WFE (Wafer Fab Equipment): Industry term for capital equipment used for semiconductor wafer fabrication; referenced as a benchmark for Advanced Energy's relative growth performance in the semiconductor segment.
