Earnings Call Transcript

AAON (AAON) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 2, 2026 at 10:21AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, March 2, 2026 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Matthew J. Tobolski
  • Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer — Rebecca A. Thompson
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Joseph Mondillo

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $424.2 million, up 42.5%, driven by 138.8% growth in Basics branded sales linked to demand for data center cooling and Memphis facility utilization.
  • Gross Margin -- 25.9%, a slight contraction from 26.1% due to unabsorbed fixed costs at Memphis.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin -- 15.2%, down from 15.8%, reflecting near-term ramp expenses and higher operating costs.
  • Diluted EPS -- $0.39, up 30% compared to the prior year.
  • AAON Oklahoma Net Sales -- $215.5 million, up 11.1% with gross margin of 27.5%, reflecting $6.4 million in incremental overhead tied to Memphis.
  • AAON Coil Products Sales -- $102.4 million, up 93.6%, led by $75.3 million in Basics-branded liquid cooling that grew 100% in the quarter.
  • AAON Coil Products Gross Margin -- 21.3%, up from 16.1%, aided by higher throughput and favorable mix offset by a five-day year-end shutdown.
  • Basics Segment Sales -- $106.1 million, up 109.1%, with gross margin increasing to 27.1% from 18.8% due to the Memphis ramp and a favorable comparison period.
  • Backlog -- Basics backlog at $1.3 billion, up 141%, with a book-to-bill of 2.4; AAON branded backlog up 61% and bookings up 20%.
  • Memphis Facility -- Achieved first quarterly profitability; contributed roughly $25 million to $30 million to Basics revenue for the period.
  • Cash and Debt -- $1.2 million in cash balances and $398.3 million in debt at year end; leverage ratio of 1.77.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $0.5 million for the year versus $192.5 million in 2024, reflecting material working capital investments.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $204.9 million, down 3.9%; planned 2026 CapEx of $190 million.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Anticipates 18%-20% sales growth, gross margin of 29%-31%, SG&A at approximately 16% of sales, and $95 million to $100 million in depreciation and amortization.
  • Production Update -- Tulsa segment production volumes accelerated in January and February, expected to drive margin uplift in Q1 2026.
  • Customer Base -- Basics backlog increasingly diversified across hyperscalers and colocation providers, with new customer additions cited.
  • ERP Implementation -- Remaining ERP rollouts sequenced for Redmond in 2026 and Tulsa in 2027, prioritizing operational stability and lead time reduction.
  • Liquid Cooling Revenue Mix -- Liquid cooling represented 37.8% of total revenue; percentage may moderate as portfolio mix broadens through Memphis.

RISKS

  • Gross margin for the quarter declined year over year, primarily due to unabsorbed fixed costs at the Memphis facility, as well as supply chain constraints in Tulsa that management described as a "single biggest driver" of margin pressure.
  • Debt is expected to "remain elevated for most of the year," with interest expense tracking higher given starting leverage levels, per Rebecca A. Thompson.
  • Lead times in the Oklahoma business are "substantially longer than where we want them to be," particularly mid-20 weeks on high-volume lines, with normalization dependent on balancing production ramp with strong bookings.
  • Gross margin progression "expected to be uneven by quarter as capacity ramps and product mix normalizes," according to management guidance.

SUMMARY

AAON (AAON +2.64%) delivered a 42.5% jump in net sales and more than doubled Basics segment revenues, supported by record data center market demand and increased output from the Memphis facility, which reached profitability for the first time. Management issued 2026 guidance calling for 18%-20% sales growth, a gross margin range of 29%-31%, and SG&A at 16% of sales, but flagged that margin expansion will be incremental and quarterly progression variable as capacity utilization and product mix stabilize. A year-end Basics backlog of $1.3 billion and a robust AAON branded bookings increase signal continued momentum, although cash flow for 2025 was modest due to heightened working capital deployment, and debt remains at $398.3 million entering the new year.

  • Rebecca A. Thompson said, "cash flows from operations to be positive Q3 and Q4 with sequential improvement," and expects the positive trend to continue into 2026.
  • Management indicated further leverage from manufacturing and operational improvements is expected before additional capacity expansions are considered, aiming to "actually unlock more capacity inside the existing investment" beyond the stated $1.5 billion revenue capability.
  • SG&A as a percent of sales is forecast to "see our sequential decline year over year to 16% in our target for 2026," with potential ongoing leverage as new investments are absorbed.
  • ERP system rollouts for Redmond and Tulsa are being staggered to avoid operational disruption and enable focus on production velocity.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Book-to-bill: Ratio of orders received to product shipped and billed within the period, indicating demand strength and backlog change.
  • Alpha Class heat pump: AAON's proprietary air-source heat pump product line targeted at optimized performance and energy efficiency within the commercial HVAC sector.
  • ERP: Enterprise Resource Planning system; integrated software platform for streamlining operations, planning, and information flow across the organization.
  • Colocation provider: Data center operator offering space, power, and cooling for customer-owned IT hardware, often serving cloud or hyperscale clients.
