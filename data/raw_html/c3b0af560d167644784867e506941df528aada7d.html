Xos (XOS) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 13, 2025 at 5:49PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, November 13, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Dakota Semler
  • Chief Operating Officer — Giordano Sordoni
  • Chief Financial Officer — Liana Pogosyan
  • General Counsel — David Zlotchew

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Risks

  • Management explicitly cited that long-term structured pricing with national accounts and recent tariffs are compressing near-term margins, stating, “tariffs that were not contemplated when some of these large programs were originally priced.”
  • Seasonality and customer delivery patterns point to a lighter Q4 from parcel delivery customers, with Dakota Semler noting, “it's generally a lighter quarter from our key customers in Q4.”
  • The company remains exposed to risks regarding continued tariff regime changes, with supply chain adjustments and cost-sharing efforts still ongoing.

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $6.5 million recognized on 130 units, down from $18.4 million on 135 units in the prior quarter and compared to $15.8 million on 94 units one year ago, marked by major shipments to UPS, Bluebird, and FedEx ISPs.
  • Vehicles shipped -- 140 units shipped, including 10 chassis for future revenue recognition, signaling growing demand and ongoing fulfillment of a 200-plus unit order.
  • GAAP gross margin -- 15.3%, an improvement over 8.8% in the prior quarter, driven by product mix and adjusted UPS pricing for tariffs, though still below 18.1% from the prior year.
  • Non-GAAP gross margin -- 16% this quarter versus 1.4% prior quarter, marking nine consecutive quarters above zero.
  • Operating loss -- $7 million, the lowest since going public, compared to $7.1 million prior quarter and $9.7 million the previous year.
  • Non-GAAP operating loss -- $4.8 million, improved from $6.9 million prior quarter and $6.6 million last year.
  • Cash and liquidity -- Cash and equivalents rose to $14.1 million from $8.8 million last quarter, driven by $2.4 million in ATM proceeds, lower inventory, and strong accounts receivable collections.
  • Inventory -- Decreased to $25.2 million from $31 million last quarter, reflecting sales outpacing production.
  • Accounts receivable -- Declined to $15.4 million from $21 million last quarter, with $18.7 million collected during the period.
  • Free cash flow -- Positive $3.1 million for the quarter, the third positive quarter since going public.
  • Convertible note restructuring -- Maturity pushed from August 2025 to quarterly installments ending February 2028, with Alger Mae Automotive Company becoming the largest shareholder after interest accrued to maturity paid in stock.
  • Mesa facility lease termination -- Agreement to terminate is expected to save approximately $20.7 million in cash through 2033; requires 18 monthly payments totaling about $2.8 million and generated a $9.4 million gain in non-operating income.
  • Product diversification -- 18 powertrains delivered to Blue Bird this quarter with approximately 75 new orders post-quarter, and increased deployments of the Xos Hub mobile charging platform.
  • Full-year guidance reaffirmed -- Revenue projected between $50.2 million-$65.8 million, deliveries between 320 and 420 units, and non-GAAP operating loss between $24.4 million-$26.9 million.

Summary

Xos (XOS 3.80%) reported record unit deliveries while shifting product mix toward higher-margin powertrain and charging solutions, resulting in improved gross and operating margins despite softer revenue recognition. The company demonstrated enhanced liquidity through reduced inventory, increased cash balances, and a convertible note extension that aligns with a supportive strategic investor. Xos is investing in scalable operations, expanding powertrain offerings, and positioning the Xos Hub as an energy platform for new markets, supported by demonstrated customer demand and follow-on orders from large OEM partners.

  • CEO Dakota Semler stated, “We are deliberately expanding into higher margin, lower concentration categories, including powertrains and energy infrastructure.”
  • The company amended its convertible note repayment, moving principal outflows from a lump sum to quarterly payments through early 2028, improving near-term financial flexibility.
  • Hub deployments accelerated with growing interest from fleet, utility, and industrial clients, and a 2026 product update is planned to enhance energy management features beyond transportation.
  • Xos realized its third positive free cash flow quarter since going public, reflecting disciplined working capital management and reduced exposure to slow-paying incentive programs.

Industry glossary

  • Step van: A type of medium-duty, walk-in commercial delivery vehicle commonly used by parcel and logistics companies.
  • LFP battery: Lithium iron phosphate battery chemistry, known for safety, longevity, and durability in commercial EV applications.
  • Xos Hub: The company's mobile charging platform designed to provide temporary or portable fast charging for electric fleets and industrial users, with extensions for backup power and peak shaving.
