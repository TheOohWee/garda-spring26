Cryoport (CYRX) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $176.2 million from continuing operations, surpassing previous top-end guidance due to core market momentum.
  • Commercial Cell and Gene Therapy Revenue -- $33.4 million for the year, up 29%; in the fourth quarter, this segment represented 20% of total revenue.
  • Clinical Trial Revenue -- $47.1 million, reflecting a 14% increase.
  • Clinical Trial Portfolio -- Supported a record 760 trials and 20 commercial therapies, with Phase 3 trials at 86 and Phase 2 trials at 361.
  • Market Share -- Supported approximately 70% of cell and gene therapy clinical trials.
  • Life Sciences Services Revenue -- Grew 18%, including 22% growth in biostorage/bioservices.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Improved by $12 million year over year, driven by prior cost reduction initiatives.
  • Gross Margin -- Reported at 47%.
  • Life Sciences Products (MVE) Revenue -- Grew 7% for the year, with Q4 up 2%; MVE segment highlighted for new product launches such as Fusion 800 and CryoVerse.
  • 2026 Revenue Guidance -- Set at $190 million to $194 million, with anticipated positive adjusted EBITDA.
  • Strategic Partnerships -- Established with DHL (including CryoPDP divestiture), Cardinal Health, and Parexel, targeting global and service expansion.
  • Capital Infusion -- Completed CryoPDP sale to DHL, providing significant cash resources.
  • Global Expansion Initiatives -- Opened a supply chain center in Paris and expanded Belgian operations; ongoing U.S. campus consolidation in California.
  • AI Adoption -- Actively deploying generative AI across enterprise operations to streamline workflows, reduce manual processes, and improve accuracy.
  • BLA/MAA Filings and Approvals Pipeline -- Anticipates 13 application filings and nine new therapy approvals in 2026, plus two additional label/geography expansions.
  • Commercialization Outlook -- Three clients expecting new therapy approvals in March and April 2026.

SUMMARY

The earnings call presented new data on Cryoport (CYRX 0.92%)'s continued expansion in commercial cell and gene therapy services, highlighting 20% of revenue now sourced from this fast-growing segment. Management outlined plans to leverage global partnerships and new product launches to sustain momentum, supported by a broad clinical pipeline of 760 supported trials. Capital from the CryoPDP divestiture is being deployed into European and North American infrastructure to bolster operational reach. Technological innovation—in both supply chain management and AI-driven process improvements—was emphasized as a differentiator for future growth. Margin expansion initiatives and guidance for both revenue and positive adjusted EBITDA in 2026 indicate confidence in operational leverage.

  • Direct commentary from management clarified, "We have had a solid start to Q1, Puneet. We are not expecting a light one like some other life science companies are."
  • The MVE segment is being integrated with advanced technology—such as the Fusion 800 cryogenic freezer and CryoVerse platform—to capture new market demand in constrained environments.
  • Mark Sawicki said, "Parexel and Cardinal Health are two very good examples" of partners complementing core supply chain offerings, underscoring the strategic importance of ecosystem collaborations for industry standardization.
  • Clinical trial funding flowing through the sector is expected to impact orders after an average lag of six months, according to management.
  • Client demand prompted completion of a GMP-compliant Belgian facility, now operational and contributing revenue from late 2025, as described by Mark Sawicki.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • BLA: Biologics License Application; the regulatory submission required for U.S. approval of biological therapies.
  • MAA: Marketing Authorization Application; European counterpart to BLA for approval of new biological therapies.
  • REMS: Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategies; FDA-mandated plans to ensure that the benefits of certain drugs outweigh their risks, especially in cell and gene therapy.
  • Dewar: A specialized insulated container for storing cryogenic fluids, used in the transport and storage of biological material.
