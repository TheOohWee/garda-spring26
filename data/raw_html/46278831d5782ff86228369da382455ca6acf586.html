TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Sales -- $79.4 million with comparable sales growth of 0.2%, driven by traffic growth of 0.5%, and offset by a negative 0.3% in price and mix.
  • Regional Comparable Sales -- West Coast at negative 0.6%, Southwest at positive 1.6% for the quarter.
  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) -- 28.4% of sales, increasing by approximately 70 basis points due to tariffs within the quarter.
  • Labor Costs -- 31.1% of sales, improving by 30 basis points from the prior year period, attributed to operational efficiencies.
  • General & Administrative (G&A) Expenses -- 11.7% of sales compared to 20.3% in the prior year quarter; excluding litigation, 11.4% versus 13.2% last year for the quarter.
  • Restaurant-Level Operating Profit -- 19.8% of sales, compared to 20.9% in the prior year period.
  • Operating Income -- $1.5 million, shifting from a $5.8 million operating loss in the prior year period.
  • Net Income -- $2.3 million ($0.18 per share), reversing a $5.2 million net loss (negative $0.46 per share) in the prior year period.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $7.4 million versus $5.5 million in the prior year period.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $2.5 million ($0.20 per share), rising from $1 million ($0.09 per share) last year for the quarter.
  • G&A (Full-Year) -- 13.3% of sales, a 300 basis point reduction from the prior year's 16.4%, with G&A ex-litigation at 12.5% compared to 14.1% previously.
  • Cash and Investments -- $92 million at quarter-end, with no debt.
  • Store Expansion -- 15 new locations opened in the fiscal year; 3 stores opened in the fourth quarter (The Woodlands, TX; Salt Lake City, UT; Boulder, CO), and 3 additional units opened subsequent to quarter end.
  • Unit Growth Guidance -- Expectation to open 16 new units in the next fiscal year, maintaining above 20% annual unit growth.
  • Menu Pricing -- 3.5% price increase effective November 1, with expected effective pricing of 4.5% in the following quarter.
  • COGS Guidance -- CFO Uttz said, "we expect our COGS for fiscal '26 to be at least 30%, around the 30% range."
  • Restaurant-Level Operating Profit Margin Guidance -- Expected at approximately 18% for the full year.
  • G&A Expense Guidance -- Fiscal 2026 guidance is 12%-12.5% of sales.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Growth (Full-Year) -- Over 30% increase year over year.
  • Robotic Dishwasher Retrofit -- Commercial certification obtained, enabling retrofitting at approximately 50 restaurants (previously expected: 5-10); anticipated to yield about 50 basis points labor improvement per retrofitted location.
  • Reservation System -- Upgrades completed; marketing to non-rewards members to start in the upcoming fiscal second quarter.
  • Rewards Program -- Introduction of status tiers under exploratory research as "the first major update" since original launch.
  • New Store Build Cost Guidance -- Average net capital expenditures per unit expected to remain at $2.5 million, offsetting tariff impact with improved tenant allowances.
  • First Quarter Comparable Sales Guidance -- Porten said, "our expectation for Q1 is to come in negative mid-single digits," attributed to comparison dynamics.
  • Positive Full-Year Comp Sales Goal -- Porten said, "our goal remains to deliver positive comps for the year. We think we can get flat to slightly positive."
  • Litigation/Impairment Impact -- No impairment charges recorded in Q4, compared to 2.4% of sales in the prior year quarter; year-over-year G&A reduction partially attributed to lapping prior-year litigation costs.

RISKS

  • CEO Uba acknowledged "negatively impacted our top line results and restaurant-level margins," with CFO Uttz stating tariffs increased Q4 COGS by approximately 70 basis points and warning, "we expect our COGS for fiscal '26 to be at least 30%, around the 30% range."
  • Comparable restaurant sales for the current quarter are expected to be "negative mid-single digits," as stated by Porten, due to challenging prior-year comparisons.
  • Restaurant-level operating profit margin is guided at approximately 18%, which management described as a lower target versus prior performance, citing elevated utility costs and ongoing tariff impact as key contributing factors.

SUMMARY

Kura Sushi USA (KRUS 4.99%) reported quarterly sales of $79.4 million, with a 0.2% increase in comparable sales led solely by traffic gains and offset by negative price/mix. The company delivered record unit expansion, opening 15 locations in the year, and maintained a focus on operational efficiency that lowered G&A to 11.7% of sales for the quarter. Management obtained commercial approval for a proprietary robotic dishwasher, increasing the retrofit scope to 50 existing restaurants, aiming to drive future labor savings. Fiscal 2026 guidance includes expectations for 16 new openings, 20%+ unit growth, and flat to slightly positive same-store sales, despite ongoing tariff-pressured COGS projected at approximately 30%. Adjusted EBITDA and net income improved, but management cautioned about near-term margin pressures and guided to a restaurant-level operating profit margin of about 18% for the coming year.

  • For the first quarter, comp sales are projected to be negative mid-single digits due to the toughest annual comparison.
  • Menu price increases of 3.5% went into effect November 1, with CFO Uttz confirming effective pricing will reach 4.5% in the following quarter.
  • Management confirmed that revenue guidance does not rely on incremental benefit from expanded marketing of the reservation system or new rewards program tiering; these initiatives may provide upside if successful.
  • Average net build costs for new units are maintained at $2.5 million, as higher tariffs have been offset by improved tenant improvement allowances.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • COGS: Cost of Goods Sold; the direct costs attributable to the production of the food and beverages sold at restaurant-level.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for one-time items or non-cash expenses, used to evaluate core profitability.
  • Restaurant-Level Operating Profit Margin (RLOPM): Percentage of sales remaining after deducting direct restaurant operating expenses (excluding corporate overhead), commonly used to assess store-level profitability.
  • AUV: Average Unit Volume; annualized average sales per store unit, a key restaurant performance metric.
  • LTO: Limited Time Offer; a menu item or promotion available only for a restricted period, used to drive short-term customer interest and traffic.
