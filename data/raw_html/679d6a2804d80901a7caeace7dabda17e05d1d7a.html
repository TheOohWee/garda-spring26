DICK'S (DKS) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 12, 2026 at 11:28AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, March 12, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Executive Chairman — Edward Stack
  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Lauren Hobart
  • Chief Financial Officer — Navdeep Gupta

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated Net Sales -- $17.22 billion for the year, up 28.1%, driven by a $3.11 billion partial-year contribution from Foot Locker (NYSE: FL) and 4.5% comparable sales growth in the DICK'S (DKS 0.07%) business.
  • DICK'S Standalone Full-Year Sales -- $14.1 billion, with comps up 4.5% and both average ticket (+4.2%) and transactions (+0.3%) posting gains.
  • DICK'S Full-Year Non-GAAP EPS -- $14.58, an increase of 3.8% on a share count of 81.2 million, exceeding the high end of guidance.
  • Foot Locker Pro Forma Q4 Comp Sales -- Down 3.4%, improved versus prior expectations of a mid- to high-single-digit decline.
  • Foot Locker Inventory Optimization -- Inventory cleanout described as "done," enabling a return to margin expansion and positioning for 1%-3% comp growth in 2026, with pilot Fast Break stores showing "very strong positive comps" and significant gross margin improvement.
  • DICK'S Q4 Comp Sales -- Up 3.1%, building on the prior year's 6.6% growth and reaching a two-year comp stack near 10%.
  • DICK'S Q4 Gross Margin -- Expanded sequentially by 67 basis points, attributed entirely to higher merchandise margin.
  • Consolidated Q4 Net Sales -- $6.23 billion, up 59.9%, reflecting a $2.18 billion Foot Locker contribution and 3.1% DICK'S comp growth.
  • Consolidated Q4 Non-GAAP Operating Income -- $438.6 million or 7.04% of sales, with a $5.9 million operating loss recorded for Foot Locker, consistent with expectations.
  • 2026 DICK'S Comp Sales Outlook -- 2%-4% growth expected, with midpoint two-year stack at 7.5% and slightly higher comp pacing in H1, partly due to World Cup timing.
  • 2026 DICK'S Operating Margin Outlook -- Approximately 11.1% at midpoint, with a potential 10 basis points of improvement at the high end on a non-GAAP basis.
  • 2026 Foot Locker Sales and Operating Income Outlook -- Foot Locker sales expected at $7.6 billion-$7.7 billion with pro forma comp sales of 1%-3% and operating income targeted at $100 million-$150 million.
  • 2026 Consolidated Non-GAAP EPS Guidance -- $13.50-$14.50 based on 91 million diluted shares, reflecting dilution from shares issued in the Foot Locker acquisition.
  • Inventory Position -- Consolidated inventory at year-end was $4.91 billion, up 47%, largely as a result of the Foot Locker acquisition; DICK'S business inventory rose only 1%.
  • Dividend Increase -- Quarterly dividend raised by 3% to an annualized payout of $5.00 per share, the twelfth consecutive year of increases.
  • 2026 Capital Expenditures -- Planned net capex of $1.5 billion, with emphasis on opening approximately 14 House of Sport locations and 22 Fieldhouse locations, and construction initiation on 18 more House of Sport stores for 2027.
  • Synergy Realization -- Company reiterates expected cost synergies of $100 million-$125 million over the medium term, most from procurement and direct sourcing; some benefits are included in the 2026 outlook.

RISKS

  • GAAP gross profit unfavorably impacted by $218 million in inventory optimization charges related to Foot Locker, in line with prior expectations.
  • Consolidated non-GAAP operating margin declined to 8.81% from 11.14%, reflecting the lower-margin acquisition of Foot Locker and associated integration costs.
  • Year-end consolidated inventory rose 47%, which management described as "well positioned".
  • Management expects operating margins in the DICK'S business will "decline in the first half" due to planned investments and synergy realization weighted to the second half.

SUMMARY

The call presented critical momentum in both DICK'S and the newly acquired Foot Locker business, underpinned by clear growth in key categories, strategic inventory optimization, and a defined path to synergy realization. Management outlined concrete expansion plans for House of Sport and Fieldhouse formats, as well as the accelerated rollout of the Fast Break initiative across approximately 250 Foot Locker stores by back-to-school, signaling focused capital deployment within omnichannel and store reinvestment. Guidance points to consolidated growth, but explicitly incorporates Foot Locker integration costs, dilution from share issuance, and back-half-weighted profitability tied to synergy capture and store revamps.

  • Executive Chairman Stack said, "We believe that Foot Locker's inventory is now well positioned," citing comprehensive execution of the "clean out the garage" plan as foundational for margin and sales improvement.
  • CEO Hobart said, "We anticipate our comp sales to be in the range of 2% to 4%," and confirmed high conviction in current category trends, with specific mention of technical running, team sports, and branded partnerships driving demand.
  • Company management noted the Fast Break store concept will reach roughly 250 Foot Locker locations across the U.S. and Europe by the back-to-school timeframe, following strong uplift in pilot stores.
  • GameChanger, described as achieving a "nearly 40% CAGR" and contributing strongly to profitability, is a material gross margin driver alongside merchandising initiatives.
  • On a GAAP basis, acquisition- and write-down-related costs totaling $248.9 million in Q4 ($235.5 million acquisition, $13.4 million asset write-down) weighed on reported earnings, reflecting active portfolio restructuring.
  • Management reiterated a back-half weighting of expected operating income and margin expansion, driven by timing of synergy realization and store initiatives, particularly at Foot Locker.
  • 2026 capital allocation includes $1.5 billion for growth formats and technology, with the dividend raised for the twelfth year, underscoring continued shareholder returns alongside reinvestment.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Comp Sales (Comparable Sales): Sales growth metric for stores open at least one year, excluding new/closed units.
  • Fast Break Initiative: Foot Locker's store productivity and merchandise reset program, entailing SKU reduction, improved product presentation, and pilot store remodeling.
  • DICK'S Fieldhouse: A next-generation store format focused on expanded experiences and category depth.
  • GameChanger: DICK'S-owned SaaS and mobile application for youth sports statistics, streaming, and engagement.
  • DICK'S Media Network (DMN): In-house digital advertising platform leveraging owned web, app, and data to connect brand partners with DICK'S audience.
  • SKU: Stock Keeping Unit; unique identifier for each product and service offered for sale.
