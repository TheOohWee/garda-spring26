Earnings Call Transcript

Ranpak (PACK) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 5, 2026 at 12:19PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Mar. 5, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman & CEO — Omar Asali
  • Chief Financial Officer — Bill Drew
  • Director of Investor Relations — Sara [surname not provided]

Takeaways

  • Volume growth — consolidated -- Volumes increased 3% compared to the prior year’s strong quarter, marking the ninth positive quarter out of the past ten.
  • Net revenue -- Increased 2.2% on a constant currency basis, or 4.4% excluding the impact of warrants. Full-year net revenue grew 5% constant currency and 6.1% excluding the $5 million warrant headwind.
  • North America performance -- Sales rose 5.8% for the quarter and 14% for the year, with void fill products up more than 20% and automation revenue up 91.7% excluding warrants.
  • Europe and APAC revenue -- Combined revenue declined 1.4% constant currency for the quarter and dropped 2.7% for the year, as higher rebate activity and less favorable mix offset gains in protective packaging systems (PPS) and 30% automation growth.
  • Automation segment -- Automation grew nearly 40% for the quarter and approximately 35% for the year on a constant currency basis, reaching $40 million in full-year revenue excluding the impact of warrants.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Fell 10.3% on a constant currency basis for the quarter, or 1.2% when excluding warrant impact. Full-year Adjusted EBITDA down 8.5%, or 2.4% excluding warrants.
  • Gross profit -- Declined 16% for the quarter constant currency, or 10.6% excluding a $2.3 million non-cash warrant impact; full-year gross profit dropped 9% or 5.3% excluding a $5 million warrant impact.
  • SG&A expense -- Excluding RSU expense, SG&A declined 2% constant currency versus prior year, highlighting expense control focus.
  • Capital expenditures (CapEx) -- $30.3 million for the year, a reduction of $2.8 million from 2024 and 45% below 2023 levels.
  • Liquidity & leverage -- Ended the year with $63 million in cash, no draws on the revolving credit facility, and a reported net leverage of 4.4x LTM, with a stated target of 2.5x-3x over 18-24 months.
  • 2026 guidance — revenue & adjusted EBITDA -- Net revenue expected to grow 5%-12.7% constant currency ($415 million-$445 million); Adjusted EBITDA forecast to rise 5.4%-19.9% ($83.5 million-$95 million), both including a non-cash warrant expense of $5 million-$7 million.
  • Automation 2026 outlook -- Targeting 30%-50% automation revenue growth to reach more than $60 million, with segment becoming positively contributive to Adjusted EBITDA.
  • Free cash flow outlook -- CFO Drew said, “We are expecting about $15 million in free cash for the year” based on CapEx of approximately $37.5 million, interest of approximately $34 million, taxes of $3 million-$4 million, and a $5 million working capital use.
  • PPS volume expectations by region -- Management expects mid-to-high single-digit growth in North America, a low-to-mid single-digit range in EMEA (depending on geopolitical developments), and APAC growth driven by local sourcing investments.
  • Key customer impact -- Both Walmart and Amazon are expected to be “double-digit growers” in consumables, and management said existing automation/equipment deployments will ramp PPS volumes through 2026.

Risks

  • CEO Asali said, “We believe the lower end of the range reflects our optimism of growth in North America and automation and a potentially less robust and more expensive environment in Europe if the war persists.”
  • Gross profit and Adjusted EBITDA declines for the quarter and year were attributed directly to “gross profit headwinds” and “less favorable mix” in Europe combined with increased rebate activity.
  • Automation, though now breakeven in Q4, was a “meaningful drag” on full-year profitability, given a negative $6 million contribution to Adjusted EBITDA.
  • Management explicitly stated a “tough comparison in Q1” due to prior-year paper market disruptions and adverse weather, which may weigh on first-quarter performance.

Summary

Management highlighted the completion of transformative partnerships with two leading e-commerce and retail players, expected to deliver more than $1 billion in cumulative revenue over the next 8-10 years and materially influence Ranpak Holdings Corp. (PACK 1.04%)’s future scale. The company underscored structural labor shortages and the acceleration of the “plastic to paper” shift in U.S. enterprise accounts as long-term volume growth drivers. Technology investment—totaling more than $20 million since 2022—has built an “AI-ready” infrastructure, enabling disciplined adoption of automation and operational efficiencies. Ranpak Holdings Corp. also cited substantial secular and regulatory tailwinds, including Europe’s Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR), as catalysts for near-term automation momentum and continued cross-selling opportunities. Balance sheet discipline, including a 45% CapEx reduction since 2023 and commitment to substantial deleveraging, positions the company to pursue further margin improvement and cash generation.

  • Management’s revised guidance was explicitly described as more conservative, having been adjusted in response to “recent events in the Middle East” impacting both top-line and margin assumptions.
  • Strategic collaborations with Medline Industries in healthcare and Pickle Robot in warehouse automation were cited as validation of Ranpak Holdings Corp.’s positioning and differentiation in automated solutions.
  • Customer behavior in the U.S. is described as a “switch from plastic to paper,” cited as a distinct growth driver for the company’s enterprise segment, with implications for new business pipeline.
  • Management noted that the protective packaging space is consolidating, and a strong technology platform was mentioned as a factor supporting future revenue growth and re-rating potential.

Industry glossary

  • PPS (Protective Packaging Systems): Ranpak Holdings Corp.’s suite of solutions providing void-fill, cushioning, and wrapping through on-demand paper conversion equipment.
  • APAC: Abbreviation for the Asia Pacific region, as used in Ranpak Holdings Corp.’s financial reporting and strategy discussions.
  • EMEA: Geographic segment covering Europe, the Middle East, and Africa, referenced in Ranpak Holdings Corp.’s segment results.
  • Warrants: Financial instruments issued by Ranpak Holdings Corp. that impacted GAAP versus non-GAAP reporting for both revenue and Adjusted EBITDA, cited as a $5 million-$7 million non-cash expense reduction in 2026 guidance.
  • PPWR (Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation): European Union directive requiring companies to minimize packaging waste, optimize packaging design, and support circular economy goals, with direct implications for automation adoption.
