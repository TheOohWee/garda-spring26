BorgWarner (BWA) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 10:47AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, February 11, 2026 at 9:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Joseph Fadool
  • Chief Financial Officer — Craig Aaron
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Patrick Nolan

Takeaways

  • Net sales -- $14.3 billion, increasing by about $200 million, supported by 23% growth in light vehicle e-product sales.
  • Adjusted operating margin -- 10.7%, up 60 basis points, despite a 20 basis point net tariff headwind.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Increased 14% year over year, driven by improved operating income and over $500 million in share repurchases.
  • Free cash flow -- Exceeded $1.2 billion, up about 66%, with approximately $630 million returned to shareholders via share repurchases and dividends.
  • Fiscal Q4 adjusted operating income -- $427 million, representing a 12% margin, compared to $352 million and 10.2% margin in the previous year’s fiscal Q4.
  • Customer recoveries and tariffs -- Fiscal Q4 operating income benefited from over 100 basis points in customer recoveries and $11 million in positive net tariff recoveries.
  • Record new product awards -- The company secured a record number of new product awards across foundational and e-product offerings, spanning all business units.
  • Turbine generator system -- Signed master supply agreement with TurboCell, first-year production sales over $300 million expected for data center and microgrid markets, production ramp starting 2027.
  • 2026 sales guidance -- Projected at $14 billion-$14.3 billion, assuming a $200 million currency benefit, with full-year organic sales expected to decrease 1.5%-3.5% due to battery business decline and flat-to-down 3% weighted end markets.
  • 2026 adjusted operating margin guidance -- Expected range of 10.7%-10.9%, aided by a 10 basis point margin lift from charging business exit and further cost controls.
  • 2026 adjusted EPS guidance -- $5.00-$5.20 per diluted share, implying about 4% growth at the midpoint.
  • 2026 free cash flow guidance -- Projected $900 million-$1.1 billion, with a midpoint decline due to higher capital spending ahead of new product launches.
  • Share repurchase activity -- $400 million repurchased in 2025, total return to shareholders (including dividends) of approximately $630 million, or 52% of free cash flow, with 31 million shares repurchased since 2021.
  • Battery business decline -- Management expects this to be a 150 basis point headwind to year-over-year sales growth in 2026, with near-term trends described as difficult to predict.
  • Investment in CapEx -- Capital expenditures guided to 4.5% of sales in 2026, reflecting investments in announced product wins and the new turbine generator system.

Risks

  • Batteries segment expected to experience a "sales decline" due to "lack of North American incentives and weaker European demand," resulting in a 150 basis point headwind to 2026 organic sales growth.
  • Management noted, "near term, sales trends are a little bit difficult to predict." for the battery business, citing ongoing headwinds in North America and Europe.

Summary

BorgWarner (BWA 0.89%) detailed a record-setting year for new product awards and reported substantial free cash flow and margin expansion, supported by double-digit growth in light vehicle e-products. The company announced entry into the data center and microgrid market via a master supply agreement for its turbine generator system, targeting over $300 million in sales during the launch year with production ramp beginning in 2027. Management projects continued margin improvement and EPS growth in 2026, despite anticipating organic sales headwinds from the battery business and flat or declining end markets. Strategic capital spending will rise in 2026 to support future product launches, especially the turbine generator system, with management expecting top-line acceleration starting in 2027. The company returned 52% of 2025 free cash flow to shareholders and maintains $600 million in remaining share repurchase authorization.

  • Joseph Fadool said, "we secured record new business wins across our foundational and e-product portfolios, which we expect will support our ability to deliver long-term profitable growth."
  • Craig Aaron confirmed guidance for adjusted operating margin expansion in 2026 even as organic sales decline, attributing improvement to "charging access, that's 10 basis points of margin expansion," and ongoing cost controls.
  • BorgWarner's content in the new turbine generator system is estimated at 65%, leveraging its global supply chain and manufacturing footprint for rapid market entry.
  • Mid-teens incremental margin is expected on the $300 million turbine generator system sales in 2027, with immediate EPS accretion and a "strong return on invested capital."
  • More than half of BorgWarner's electrification business resides in China, and export strength from Chinese OEM customers is expected to be an ongoing area of opportunity.
  • The company continues to utilize machine learning and generative AI pilots within manufacturing, back office, and R&D, anticipating quality, cost structure, and efficiency improvements with measurable financial impact.
  • Joseph Fadool reiterated that the DRAM memory shortage would have "no impact" on company production, stating, "We don't use DRAM in our products."

Industry glossary

  • IDM (Integrated Drive Module): A drivetrain component that combines one or more electric motors, inverter(s), and software controls into a single compact assembly for EV and hybrid applications.
  • EXD (Electric Cross Differential): An electronically controlled differential that manages torque distribution across an axle, here referenced for a 48-volt system tailored to electrified vehicle architectures.
  • Mid-teens incremental conversion: Operating income growth relative to organic sales growth, expressed as percentage points, here referring to an expected margin increase in the 13%-17% range.
  • PDS (Power Drive Systems): BorgWarner segment or product line focused on electrified propulsion, including e-motors, inverters, and transmission technologies.
