J&J Snack Foods (JJSF) Q1 2026 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 3, 2026 at 10:30AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Feb. 3, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Dan Fachner
  • Chief Financial Officer — Shawn Munsell
  • Director, Investor Relations — Reed Anderson

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Net Sales -- $343.8 million, down 5.2%, with approximately $18 million of the decline from the bakery business, including a $13 million reduction attributable to Project Apollo SKU optimization.
  • Gross Margin -- Improved 200 basis points to 27.9%, supported by Apollo initiatives, plant consolidation, and product mix improvement.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $27 million, representing a 7% increase, with $3 million of realized net savings from Project Apollo.
  • Adjusted Operating Income -- $8 million, compared to $8.2 million in the prior year period.
  • Foodservice Net Sales -- $219.2 million, down 8.3%, with an $18 million decline in bakery and a $3.6 million, or 6.9%, increase in soft pretzel sales.
  • Retail Net Sales -- Increased $1.2 million, or 2.6%, to $45.9 million, driven chiefly by a $1.8 million increase in handheld products volume.
  • Frozen Beverages Net Sales -- Flat at $78.7 million, with beverage growth offset by declines in service and machine sales.
  • Share Repurchases -- Completed $42 million buyback for over 158,000 shares at an average price of about $91.60 per share, and announced a new $50 million repurchase authorization.
  • Operating Expenses -- Increased to $95.4 million, including $6.1 million in nonrecurring plant closure and related expenses.
  • Selling and Marketing Expenses -- Rose 9.9%, or $2.8 million, with increased commissions in Dippin’ Dots retail vending and investments for the summer season.
  • Distribution Expenses -- Decreased $1.6 million, or 3.9%, to 11.1% of sales due to lower overall volume.
  • Administrative Expenses -- Rose 7.8% to $20.4 million; about 300 basis points of the increase came from nonrecurring restructuring charges and legal fees.
  • Cash & Liquidity -- $67 million in cash, no long-term debt, and $210 million of available borrowing under the credit facility.
  • Operating Cash Flow and Capex -- Generated $36 million in operating cash flow and invested $19 million in capital expenditures during the quarter.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $0.33 per diluted share, flat compared with the prior year; reported EPS was $0.05, down from $0.26 due to one-time charges.
  • Project Apollo Run Rate -- Management expects to reach the $20 million annual cost savings run rate in Q2, as plant consolidation completes (with $15 million from plants, $5 million ramping through Q4).
  • Pretzel Performance -- Food service pretzel sales up 6.9%, retail pretzel sales up ~4%, attributed to new formulations and packaging.
  • Frozen Novelties -- Dogsters grew over 20% in volume; Dippin’ Dots sales up about 4% with expansion in retail and theaters.
  • Portfolio Optimization -- Management guides to approximately 3% full-year sales decline from SKU rationalization, largely completed in Q1 via accelerated plant consolidation.
  • Commodity Costs -- Management noted a less adverse commodity environment than the prior year, with tailwinds anticipated for key inputs like eggs and cocoa.
  • Tariffs and One-Time Costs -- Incurred ~$600,000 in net tariff costs and $1 million in product disposal expense, the latter not adjusted out of earnings and characterized as one-time.
  • Retail Innovation Pipeline -- Planned launches for Q2 include new protein and whole grain pretzels, Luigi's mini pops with wellness benefits, Dippin' Dots sundae flavors, and retail rollout of original Dippin' Dots.
  • Theater and Frozen Beverage Developments -- Completed ICEE rollout to a large Southwest convenience chain; continued expansion testing with a major West Coast QSR partner.
  • Tax Rate -- Effective rate of 27% for the quarter.
  • Full-Year Sales Growth Outlook -- Management reaffirms a low single-digit growth target for the remaining portfolio, net of a 3% headwind from SKU optimization, supported by innovation and new business wins.

Summary

J&J Snack Foods (JJSF +0.87%) reported a quarter marked by margin gains and continued execution of cost transformation initiatives, despite net sales pressure from deliberate portfolio optimization. The company completed an accelerated round of plant consolidation, which is expected to deliver the majority of the anticipated $20 million in run rate savings by the next quarter, and it has returned significant capital to shareholders through completed and newly authorized repurchase programs. Ongoing retail and food service pretzel strength, as well as robust growth in Dogsters and Dippin’ Dots, offset declines in other novelty products and bakery, while management maintained guidance for modest underlying revenue growth after adjusting for SKU rationalization. Operating cash flow and liquidity remained strong, enabling further investment in innovation, with several branded product launches and major distribution gains anticipated in the upcoming quarter.

  • The management team expects remaining Project Apollo savings outside plant consolidation to ramp up through the fiscal fourth quarter.
  • One-time product disposal and plant closure expenses had a visible, but not recurring, impact on quarterly results.
  • Retail handheld products rebounded from prior year production constraints caused by a facility fire.
  • Frozen beverage sales mix trends now favor beverage growth, offset by lower service and machine sales revenue in the segment.

Industry glossary

  • Project Apollo: J&J Snack Foods' multi-phase transformation program focused on operational cost savings, plant consolidation, and portfolio optimization.
  • SKU Optimization: The strategic reduction or consolidation of product types (“stock keeping units”) intended to focus on higher-margin items and operational efficiency.
  • QSR: Quick Service Restaurant; food service locations specializing in convenience and speed of service, a target distribution channel for ICEE and similar products.
  • Dogsters: J&J Snack Foods’ frozen novelty treat specifically formulated for dogs, credited with double-digit volume growth during the reported quarter.
