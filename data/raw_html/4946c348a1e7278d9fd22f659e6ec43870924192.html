Monday.com (MNDY) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 9, 2026 at 9:36AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, February 9, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Co-Chief Executive Officer and Co-Founder — Roy Mann
  • Co-Chief Executive Officer and Co-Founder — Eran Zinman
  • Chief Financial Officer — Eliran Glazer
  • Chief Revenue Officer — Casey George

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $334 million in Q4, a 25% increase, and $1.232 billion for the year, up 27%.
  • Operating Margin -- 13% in Q4, 14% for the full year; Q4 margin had a 180-basis-point negative FX impact, primarily from the Israeli shekel's appreciation.
  • Net Income -- $55 million in Q4, and $233.6 million for the year, up from $183.3 million previously.
  • Diluted Net Income Per Share -- $1.04 in Q4 and $4.40 for the year, calculated on 53.1 million fully diluted shares for the year.
  • Gross Margin -- 89% for Q4 and 90% for the full year.
  • Sales and Marketing Expense -- $159.9 million in Q4, 48% of revenue; for the year, $586.8 million, 48% of revenue, down from 51% previously.
  • Research and Development Expenditure -- $67.7 million in Q4, 20% of revenue, increasing from 18%; full year, $238.5 million, 19% of revenue, up from 17%.
  • Headcount -- 3,155 at year-end, up 137 sequentially; mid-teens percentage growth forecasted for 2026, with focus on sales and R&D hiring.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $56.7 million in Q4 (17% margin), $322.7 million for the year (26% margin).
  • Cash Balance -- $1.5 billion in cash and equivalents, down slightly from Q3 due to $135 million share repurchase.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- $735 million remained available at period end.
  • 2026 Guidance: Revenue -- $1.452 billion to $1.462 billion, up 18%-19%.
  • 2026 Guidance: Non-GAAP Operating Income -- $165 million to $175 million, with margin of 11%-12% and expected negative FX impact of 100-200 basis points.
  • 2026 Guidance: Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $275 million to $290 million, margin of 19%-20%, with 100-basis-point negative FX impact included.
  • Overall Net Dollar Retention Rate (NDR) -- 110% in Q4, expected to remain flat in 2026.
  • Enterprise Customer Growth -- Customers with over $50,000 ARR now represent 41% of total ARR; customers over $500,000 ARR grew 74%.
  • AI Product Momentum -- Monday Vibe fastest in company history to reach $1 million ARR; Monday blocks logged over 77 million actions, and Sidekick processed 500,000+ user messages.
  • Monetization Strategy -- Sidekick offered as a paid add-on for pro and below packages and included in enterprise packages; additional Sidekick monetization possible.
  • Product Focus -- 2026 growth driven mostly by existing product lines; new products expected to comprise a larger share in 2027.
  • Long-Term Targets -- 2027 targets withdrawn due to “noise in the market” and choppiness in no-touch SMB segment; 2026 guidance now the focus.

RISKS

  • Eliran Glazer stated, “the 2027 number is currently off the table,” due to persistent macroeconomic uncertainty and continued choppiness in the no-touch business segment.
  • FX headwinds from the Israeli shekel resulted in a 100–200 basis point negative impact on operating margins and a 100 basis point negative impact on adjusted free cash flow margin in 2026 guidance.
  • Roy Mann said, “the cost to acquire and expand self-serve customers have increased over the past year, and the returns on those investments have been below historical levels.”
  • No-touch (self-serve/SMB) channels continue to operate in a choppy demand environment, with persistent challenges expected throughout 2026.

SUMMARY

Management shifted the strategic emphasis to upmarket expansion and AI-driven product adoption while scaling back long-term financial targets due to continued volatility in the SMB segment. The withdrawal of previously published 2027 targets signals a focus on delivering reliable, near-term execution anchored by disciplined expense management and conservative growth assumptions. Higher-cost investment in AI and the enterprise sales motion, as well as a strengthening Israeli shekel, were explicitly cited as reasons for downward margin and free cash flow guidance revisions.

  • Enterprise penetration accelerated, as customers with more than $50,000 ARR now account for 41% of total ARR and gross retention within this group reached 91%.
  • AI product Vibe reached $1 million ARR faster than any prior company launch, with customers actively leveraging new AI workflows and Sidekick’s platform-wide intelligence.
  • Performance marketing and self-serve customer acquisition remain challenged, prompting budget reallocation to higher-ROI upmarket channels and confirming management’s expectation that lower SMB demand will persist through 2026.
  • Gross margin is forecasted to decline into the mid- to high 80% range as AI integrations and infrastructure investment become more prominent; this follows historically high prior-year gross margins.
  • Share repurchase activity continued, leaving $735 million in authorized capital; cash usage priorities include opportunistic buybacks as well as incremental tax obligations as profitability increases.
  • Management confirmed that guidance incorporates recent cost increases in R&D and sales headcount, higher FX exposure, and moderating S&M leverage, without anticipating near-term improvement in the SMB performance marketing channel.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ARR: Annual Recurring Revenue; annualized subscription value recognized from customers.
  • NDR: Net Dollar Retention; measures revenue growth from existing customers after churn, contraction, and expansion.
  • No-touch/Touch Channels: Sales approaches distinguished by level of human involvement; “no-touch” refers to self-serve/low-touch SMB acquisition, “touch” relates to high-touch enterprise sales.
  • Monday Vibe: monday.com Ltd.’s AI-powered application builder for workflow automation and data consolidation.
  • Sidekick: AI-powered central intelligence and insights engine embedded in monday.com Ltd.’s platform.
  • Monday blocks: Modular action-building components on monday.com Ltd.’s platform, enabling workflow automation.
  • SLG: Sales-Led Growth; strategy emphasizing direct, often high-touch, enterprise sales efforts.
