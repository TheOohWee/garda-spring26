CrowdStrike (CRWD) Q1 2026 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jun 3, 2025 at 6:15PM EST

DATE

Tuesday, June 3, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

Chief Executive Officer and Founder — George Kurtz

Chief Financial Officer — Burt Podbere

RISKS

Burt Podbere disclosed, "The company received a request for information from the DOJ and the SEC relating to revenue recognition and reporting of ARR for certain transactions, the July 19 outage, and related matters."

Burt Podbere stated, "GAAP net loss attributable to CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc. was $110.2 million and included $39.7 million of expenses for outage and related matters."

Burt Podbere noted, "we expect to incur Q2 cash charges of approximately $26 million in connection with the aforementioned strategic plan."

TAKEAWAYS

Net New Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR): $194 million (non-GAAP) net new ARR, ahead of management’s expectations, with sequential improvement expected in Q2 FY2026.

Ending ARR: $4.44 billion ending ARR (non-GAAP), up 22% year-over-year (non-GAAP), establishing continued leadership in pure-play cybersecurity software.

Subscription Gross Margin: 80%, reflecting platform efficiency and AI-enabled scale.

Gross Retention Rate: 97%, signaling high customer stickiness for the Falcon platform.

Free Cash Flow: $279.4 million (non-GAAP) free cash flow, representing 25% of revenue

Total Revenue: $1.1 billion total revenue (GAAP), up 20% year-over-year (GAAP); subscription revenue of $1.05 billion, also up 20%. Professional service revenue achieved a record $52.7 million.

Total Gross Margin: 78% total gross margin (GAAP); total non-GAAP operating expenses at $656 million or 59% of revenue.

Non-GAAP Operating Income: $201.1 million non-GAAP operating income, with a non-GAAP operating margin of 18%, above company guidance.

Non-GAAP Net Income: $184.7 million or $0.73 diluted per share (non-GAAP), both exceeding guidance.

Cash & Cash Equivalents: $4.61 billion cash and cash equivalents, a record balance.

FalconFlex Account Value Added: $774 million; total FalconFlex deal value expanded to $3.2 billion (non-GAAP), up 31% sequentially and sixfold year-over-year (non-GAAP).

FalconFlex Customer Metrics: Over 820 accounts adopted; average deal size exceeds $1 million ARR, with 31-month average contract length and over 75% of contracts already deployed.

Reflex Adoption: Thirty-nine FalconFlex customers exhausted their initial “Flex” demand within about five months and returned for additional purchases.

Platform Adoption Deepening: As of Q1 FY2026 (non-GAAP), subscription customers using six, seven, and eight or more Falcon modules account for 48%, 32%, and 22% of the base, respectively.

MSSP Channel: Contributed over 15% of deal value (non-GAAP), achieving record sales including the largest Latin American deal to date via this channel.

International Revenue: Comprised 33% of total revenue, with U.S. at 67%.

Strategic Realignment Plan: Expected to add at least one percentage point to non-GAAP operating margin in FY2027 and drive improved free cash flow margin (non-GAAP) above 30% for FY2027.

Guidance: Q2 FY2026: Total revenue (GAAP) for Q2 FY2026 is expected to be between $1,144.7 million and $1,151.6 million (19% year-over-year growth), non-GAAP operating income is projected to be between $226.9 million and $233.1 million, and non-GAAP diluted EPS is expected to be $0.82–$0.84.

Guidance: Fiscal Year 2026: Revenue (GAAP) for FY2026 is expected to be $4,743.5 million–$4,805.5 million (20%–22% growth), with non-GAAP net income projected at $878.7 million–$909.7 million and non-GAAP diluted EPS expected to be $3.44–$3.56 for fiscal year 2026.

Share Repurchase Authorization: Board approved up to $1 billion in share repurchases, reflecting management’s confidence in long-term strategy and cash flow strength.

CCP Program Impact: There will be a temporary separation between ARR and subscription revenue recognition in FY2026, impacting revenue by $11 million in Q1 FY2026 and is expected to affect each remaining quarter of FY2026 by $10 million–$15 million through Q4.

SUMMARY

CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc. reported sustained high customer retention and growing Falcon platform module adoption, underscoring demand for consolidated cybersecurity solutions. Unique contract structuring via FalconFlex and increasing “reflex” activity have driven rapid expansion, with customers frequently returning for additional product deployments well ahead of initial timelines. Management highlighted cloud, next-gen SIEM, and identity products as primary drivers within large-scale enterprise wins, supported by Frost Radar recognition and key new partnerships including a strategic collaboration with Microsoft focused on unifying adversary intelligence nomenclature. Investments in AI-driven automation across internal operations and the Falcon platform underpin management's confidence in accelerating net new ARR and achieving long-term margin targets for FY2026 and beyond. Cash generation remains robust, supporting both growth investments and new share repurchases aimed at maximizing shareholder value as the company scales toward $10 billion in ending ARR (non-GAAP, as of Q1 FY2026).

George Kurtz said, "Falcon Flex is significantly evolving our go-to-market and customer experience." with over $3.2 billion in closed deal value across more than 820 accounts (non-GAAP) as of Q1 FY2026, and ongoing rapid adoption.

Burt Podbere projected, "the sequential net new ARR growth rate will be at least double what we saw from Q1 to Q2 in the prior fiscal year (Q1 to Q2 FY2025 to Q1 to Q2 FY2026, non-GAAP)." signaling potential acceleration in core growth metrics (non-GAAP) in FY2026.

CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc. announced general availability of AI model scanning and security dashboard capabilities, with adoption expanding as AI agent proliferation increases the attack surface and customer needs.

Management expects the strategic realignment plan to enhance FY2027 non-GAAP operating margin to at least 24%, with a targeted free cash flow margin of more than 30% for FY2027.

Next-gen SIEM delivered triple-digit ending ARR growth in Q1 FY2026 and was repeatedly cited as winning accounts over legacy solutions, including Splunk and QRadar.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR): The annualized value of all active subscription contracts at the end of the reporting period, used to measure predictable, recurring revenue streams for SaaS businesses.

FalconFlex: CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc.'s modular, demand-based platform subscription model allowing customers flexible, multi-module security solution adoption and the option to rapidly expand service (“reflex”) as needs grow.

Reflex (FalconFlex context): When a customer exhausts their initial FalconFlex subscription earlier than planned and purchases a new, often larger, contract to expand or accelerate platform adoption.

Next-gen SIEM: CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc.’s modern Security Information and Event Management offering that replaces traditional legacy SIEM solutions, enables integrated, real-time threat detection, and is optimized for speed and automation.

CCP Program: Customer Choice Program, a one-time initiative granting CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc. customers flexibility to choose more product, time, or both, resulting in temporary effects on revenue recognition versus ARR.

MSSP: Managed Security Service Provider—third-party partners delivering security solutions and services, often including SOC operations, as part of CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc.'s channel sales ecosystem.
