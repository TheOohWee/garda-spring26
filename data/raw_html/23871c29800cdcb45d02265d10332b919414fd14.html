Tronox (TROX) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 10:29AM EST

DATE

Thursday, February 19, 2026 at 9:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Senior Vice President, Chief Financial Officer — D. John Srivisal
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Jennifer Guenther

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $2.9 billion, with the decline primarily resulting from lower pricing and unfavorable product mix.
  • Net Loss Attributable to Tronox Holdings plc -- $470 million, including $233 million of restructuring and other charges, net of taxes, mainly for the Botlek and Fuzhou plant closures.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $336 million, with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 11.6%, affected by higher production costs and reduced pricing.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- Loss of $1.50, reflecting the impact of challenging conditions and restructuring costs.
  • Free Cash Flow (Full Year) -- Outflow of $281 million, including $341 million capital expenditures, with Q4 free cash flow positive at $53 million.
  • Q4 TiO2 Revenues (Sequentially) -- Increased by 5%, driven by a 9% volume rise and offset by a 4% price and mix decline; volumes exceeded the 3%-5% guidance.
  • Q4 Zircon Revenues (Sequentially) -- Rose 32% due to a 42% surge in volumes, exceeding 15%-20% guidance; quarter-over-quarter price down 7%, 10% including mix.
  • Adjusted Q4 EBITDA -- $57 million, a 56% decrease year over year due to lower pricing and higher fixed costs from production actions.
  • Total Debt and Net Debt (Year-End) -- $3.2 billion in total debt and $3.0 billion in net debt, with liquidity at $674 million including $199 million cash.
  • Weighted Average Interest Rate (Q4) -- Approximately 6%, with 77% of interest rates fixed through 2028.
  • Cost Improvement Program -- Achieved over $90 million run-rate savings by year-end, triple the original target, driven by fixed cost and SG&A reductions.
  • Plant Closures -- Fuzhou (China) and Botlek (Netherlands) pigment plants closed, with $30 million and $15 million in fixed cost savings expected, respectively.
  • Q1 2026 EBITDA Guidance -- Projected between $55 million and $65 million, factoring in cost savings and FX headwinds, with additional improvement expected from realized savings.
  • 2026 Capital Expenditures -- Forecasted to be approximately $260 million, a reduction from prior year, with lower mining extension outlays.
  • Debt Maturity -- Next significant maturity set for 2029; no financial covenants on term loans or bonds, and springing covenant on U.S. revolver not expected to trigger.
  • Working Capital Initiatives -- Q4 working capital was a $133 million source (excluding $19 million of restructuring payments), with continued focus for 2026.
  • Sustainable Cost Savings Target -- On track for the high end of $125 million to $175 million run-rate savings by end of 2026, with over 2,000 initiatives identified.
  • Rare Earths Initiative -- Progress on Australian cracking and leaching facility financing with EFA and Ex-Im Bank; ongoing feasibility study and stakeholder engagement.
  • Return to Shareholders -- $48 million dividends paid during 2025.

RISKS

  • Restructuring charges of $233 million, driven primarily by the closures of Botlek and Fuzhou, negatively impacted net results, with additional $6 million in expected restructuring cash charges in 2026 and $15 million related to China.
  • Sequential production actions, including extended downtime at certain furnaces and mining assets, reduced near-term EBITDA but were deemed necessary to improve cash generation and working capital.
  • Foreign exchange volatility, particularly in the Australian dollar and South African rand, creates a $10 million headwind in Q1 2026 compared to Q4 average rates.
  • Continued exposure to unfavorable pricing and mix in both TiO2 and zircon segments, with Asia representing a lower-margin market and ongoing competitive dynamics affecting realized prices.

SUMMARY

Management reported that Tronox Holdings plc (TROX +2.30%) delivered its lowest overall injury rate in more than a decade, reflecting ongoing operational discipline. Executives highlighted that TiO2 sales volumes reached their highest quarterly level in 2025, supported by antidumping duties and market share gains in India, Latin America, and the Middle East, indicating structural shifts in global trade flows. The closure of the Fuzhou (China) and Botlek (Netherlands) pigment plants, along with expansive cost improvement initiatives, directly improved the company’s long-term cost structure by about $45 million annually. A $400 million senior secured note offering in September increased liquidity, while more than 500 cost initiatives are already delivering run-rate savings. Strategic focus remains on cash generation, with guidance for positive free cash flow in 2026, supported by pricing initiatives and anticipated benefits from ongoing commercial and operating actions.

  • Segment revenue growth in zircon outpaced internal guidance, but price and mix remained headwinds, particularly in Asian markets with lower margins.
  • Q4 operating efficiencies were partially offset by higher maintenance and downtime costs in plants, notably at Stallingborough, with an $11 million EBITDA impact now resolved.
  • Executives signaled that a favorable resolution on duties in the coming weeks could quickly restore lost volumes, and management is monitoring global antidumping trends that may affect future regional market share.
  • First quarter 2026 TiO2 pricing is expected to increase 2%-4% sequentially, reflecting executed price rises and improved regional sales mix toward higher-value markets.
  • The company is progressing its rare earth initiative with external financing under discussion and a feasibility study ongoing, but 2026 capital expenditures for this project remain minimal at this stage.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • TiO2: Titanium dioxide, a white pigment used in paints, coatings, plastics, and paper, and a core product for Tronox Holdings plc.
  • Antidumping Duties: Trade measures designed to counteract sales of imported goods below fair market value, significantly impacting competitive dynamics in TiO2 markets, especially in India, Brazil, and Saudi Arabia.
  • SG&A: Selling, general, and administrative expenses; cost category targeted for reductions as part of cost improvement initiatives.
  • Run-rate Savings: The projected, ongoing annualized cost reductions achieved from implemented initiatives at a specific point in time.
