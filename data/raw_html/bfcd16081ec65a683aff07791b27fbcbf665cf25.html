Planet Labs (PL) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 10, 2025 at 9:33PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, December 10, 2025 at 5:00 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer, Chairperson, and Co-Founder — Will Marshall
  • President, Chief Financial Officer, and Chief Operating Officer — Ashley Johnson

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $81.3 million, representing approximately 33% year over year growth and driven primarily by defense and intelligence, civil government sector growth, and satellite services contracts.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $5.6 million profit, marking the fourth consecutive quarter of positive adjusted EBITDA.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 60% for the quarter, compared with 64% in the prior year due to investments and contract mix.
  • Backlog -- $734.5 million, reflecting 216% year-over-year growth, with 37% scheduled for the next twelve months and 61% over the next two years.
  • Recurring ACV -- 97% of end-of-period annual contract value, with 83% from annual or multi-year contracts.
  • Net Dollar Retention Rate -- 109%; with win backs, 110% at quarter end.
  • Customer Count -- 910 at period end, flat sequentially, as the company prioritized larger account opportunities.
  • Regional Revenue Growth -- Asia Pacific and EMEA both grew 38% year over year, North America 30%, and Latin America 7%.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Positive for the third quarter in a row, reinforcing the expectation of being free cash flow positive for the full fiscal year.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $27.7 million, above guidance due to prepayment for hardware and satellite launch deposits.
  • Convertible Debt Financing -- $460 million raised at 0.5% interest, with net proceeds enhanced by purchase of a capped call to avoid dilution up to $18.04 per share.
  • Cash Position -- $677 million in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments at quarter end, $4 million higher sequentially.
  • RPOs (Remaining Performance Obligations) -- $672 million, up 361% year over year, with 33% for next twelve months and 59% over the next two years.
  • Defense and Intelligence Sector Revenue -- Over 70% year-over-year growth, up more than 15% sequentially, led by contract wins including NGA, US Navy, and international clients.
  • LUNO b Program (NGA) -- Awarded a $12.8 million initial contract, focused on AI-driven maritime analytics.
  • EOCL (Electro-Optical Commercial Layer) Contract -- $13.2 million renewal through June 2026; annual run rate of approximately $21.1 million; includes framework for high-resolution Pelican imagery.
  • US Navy Renewal -- Six-month, $7.5 million extension for Pacific vessel monitoring.
  • NATO Global Monitoring Pilot -- Pilot expansion awarded with a seven-figure extension prior to completion of existing pilot.
  • Civil Government Revenue -- Up approximately 1% year over year, up approximately 15% sequentially; highlights include a $13.5 million NASA task order under CSDA and an incremental disaster response order post-quarter valued at just under a million dollars.
  • Commercial Sector Revenue -- Moderately declined year over year and sequentially, aligned with the shift toward focusing on government clientele.
  • AXA Contract -- New operational contract signed; Planet Labs PBC (PL +1.28%) data and imaging will power AXA (EPA: CS)’s GeoClaims platform for improved insurance claims management and client distribution.
  • Satellite Launches -- Two high-resolution Pelican and 36 Super Dove satellites launched; total commercial fleet now at five Pelicans and all 38 new satellites successfully contacted and in commissioning.
  • Berlin Manufacturing Facility -- Announced for next-gen Pelican satellite production, targeting a doubling of manufacturing capacity to support European demand by ramping operations next year.
  • Owl Fleet -- Next-generation, one-meter class monitoring constellation announced with first tech demo launch slated for late 2026 and upgraded onboard compute capabilities.
  • SunCatcher (Google (NASDAQ: GOOGL) Funded R&D) -- Partnership to deploy two prototype AI compute satellites powered by Google (NASDAQ: GOOGL) TPUs in early 2027; leverages technology synergies with Owl project.
  • Bedrock Acquisition -- Closed acquisition of Bedrock Research, boosting in-house expertise in AI, remote sensing, and national security solutions.
  • Q4 2026 Guidance -- Revenue of $76 million–$80 million (27% year-over-year growth midpoint), non-GAAP gross margin of 50%-52%, adjusted EBITDA loss of $5 million–$7 million, and capital expenditures forecasted at $22 million–$26 million.
  • Full-fiscal-year 2026 Guidance -- Revenue expected between $297 million–$301 million, non-GAAP gross margin 57%-58%, adjusted EBITDA profit of $6 million–$8 million, and capital expenditures projected at $81 million–$85 million.
  • Investment Priorities -- Increased CapEx and Q4 margin compression attributed to accelerated investments in satellite services and AI-enabled solutions.

Summary

Planet Labs PBC (PL +1.28%) reported rapid top-line expansion, underpinned by a sharp uptick in government-facing segments and multiple significant contract wins that drove record backlog and performance obligations. Management announced the completion of 38 new satellite launches and a Berlin manufacturing expansion, setting the stage for capacity growth and broader geographic penetration. Landmark awards with partners such as Google (NASDAQ: GOOGL) (SunCatcher), NASA, and AXA (EPA: CS) highlight expanding technology integration and the commercialization of advanced data-driven analytics across verticals.

  • Will Marshall asserted prime positioning in emerging AI-powered space compute markets, referencing Google (NASDAQ: GOOGL)'s selection for R&D collaboration as "a huge compliment to us."
  • Ashley Johnson stated Q4 2026 growth rates will provide a "pretty good benchmark to use as a reference." for fiscal 2027 financial performance and margin planning.
  • Government contract mix trends are shifting toward shorter-duration, larger-value deals, impacting annual contract composition but supporting higher average revenue per customer.
  • Recent investments in AI-enabled solutions are enhancing margin structure in government segments and are viewed as a scalable growth driver for future commercial sector expansion.
  • Management indicated continued profitability targets for fiscal 2027, with resilience in annual free cash flow generation despite Q4 investment ramp-up.
  • Production synergies between Tanager, Pelican, and SunCatcher satellites are enabling operational leverage and cost efficiency across R&D and manufacturing.

Industry glossary

  • Backlog: Forward-looking contracted revenue, including contracts with termination for convenience provisions, used to indicate future revenue visibility.
  • Pelican: Planet’s high-resolution earth observation satellite platform aimed at rapid revisit and low-latency imaging.
  • Super Dove: Medium-resolution monitoring satellite series forming the core of Planet’s broad-area monitoring fleet.
  • Owl: Next-generation monitoring satellite constellation under development with improved one-meter resolution and enhanced onboard computing.
  • EOCL (Electro-Optical Commercial Layer): U.S. government program leveraging commercial satellite imaging capabilities for intelligence and reconnaissance.
  • LUNO b Program: National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency initiative for advanced maritime analytics leveraging AI and commercial satellite partners.
  • CSDA (Commercial Satellite Data Acquisition Program): NASA procurement program for commercial geospatial data services.
  • TPU (Tensor Processing Unit): Google (NASDAQ: GOOGL)’s custom-developed AI accelerator hardware for high-performance machine learning tasks.
  • Backlog: Aggregate of contractual commitments expected to be recognized as revenue in future periods, including contracts with early termination provisions.
