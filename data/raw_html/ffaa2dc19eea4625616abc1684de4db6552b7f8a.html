Absci (ABSI) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 13, 2025 at 12:01PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, November 12, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Founder and Chief Executive Officer — Sean McClain
  • Chief Financial Officer and Chief Business Officer — Zachariah Jonasson
  • Chief Innovation Officer — Andreas Busch
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Alexander Khan

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $400,000 reported for the quarter, reflecting continued progress on partnered programs.
  • R&D Expenses -- $19.2 million, up from $18 million for the same quarter last year, primarily from advancement of internal programs and increased preclinical and clinical activity.
  • Selling, general, and administrative expenses -- $8.4 million, a decrease from $9.3 million for the previous year’s quarter, mainly due to lower personnel spending.
  • Cash position -- $152.5 million in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities as of September 30, 2025, versus $117.5 million on June 30, 2025.
  • Funding outlook -- Management stated, "We believe our existing cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments will be sufficient to fund our operations into 2028."
  • ABS-201 (Androgenetic Alopecia) -- Phase 1/2a trial set to begin dosing next month with proof-of-concept interim data expected in 2026. The planned trial will enroll up to 227 participants in a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled design, with primary endpoints of safety and tolerability and secondary endpoints covering pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, immunogenicity, hair count, and patient-reported outcomes.
  • ABS-201 (Endometriosis) -- Phase 2 proof-of-concept trial planned for 2026, with interim data expected in 2027. Management is prioritizing ABS-201 development in endometriosis due to perceived value creation opportunity and synergy with ongoing AGA studies.
  • ABS-101 (Anti-TL1A) -- Interim Phase 1 results show extended half-life versus first-generation anti-TL1A competitors, but not versus next-generation compounds. No serious adverse events or ADA impact on PK observed. The company will seek a partner and discontinue additional internal clinical development after completing the ongoing Phase 1 study.
  • AI partnership outlook -- Management "anticipate signing one or more drug creation partnerships, including with a large pharma company by year-end."
  • ABS-301 and ABS-501 programs -- Both preclinical programs will be offered to prospective partners before beginning clinical development, reflecting a focus on capital efficiency and core pipeline prioritization.
  • ABS-201 ex vivo data -- New ex vivo human scalp data demonstrated ABS-201's ability to induce the hair growth anagen phase and counteract prolactin-mediated suppression, with repeatable results across all three patient samples studied.
  • KOL seminar -- Company will host an educational event on December 11 to present the clinical development plan and additional human data for ABS-201 in AGA.
  • Clinical development efficiency -- Safety data from the AGA Phase 1/2a study will be leveraged for the endometriosis proof-of-concept trial, allowing parallel advancement and capital efficiency according to management.

Summary

Absci Corporation (ABSI +1.02%) is reallocating clinical development resources to prioritize ABS-201 in both androgenetic alopecia and endometriosis, discontinuing further internal development of ABS-101 after Phase 1 and seeking a partner for that asset. Management projects that existing cash and investments will fund operations into 2028, citing a strengthened liquidity position. The company expects to initiate the ABS-201 AGA trial by next month, with interim efficacy data targeted for the second half of 2026, and plans to launch a phase 2 endometriosis study in 2026 leveraging safety data from the AGA study. An AI-driven partnering strategy remains a key focus, with management stating an expectation of closing one or more drug creation partnerships, potentially with large pharmaceutical companies, before year-end.

  • Chief Executive Officer McClain said, "we have made the strategic decision to prioritize ABS-201 development in endometriosis."
  • Chief Business Officer Jonasson confirmed, "do not currently plan to develop the program ourselves into phase two," citing the competitive landscape and resource discipline.
  • ABS-201 was designed to enable convenience for patients, with preclinical results indicating potential for "a simple, infrequent course of just two to three injections of ABS-201 could deliver durable, multiyear hair regrowth."
  • The company noted positive human proof-of-concept in endometriosis for a competing anti-prolactin receptor antibody (HMI-115), which management believes "derisks the mechanism" for ABS-201 and supports its best-in-class potential based on non-human primate data.
  • Both ABS-301 (first-in-class immuno-oncology antibody) and ABS-501 (anti-HER2 antibody) will be offered for external partnership rather than advanced internally, reflecting strategic prioritization.
  • New ex vivo scalp biopsy data for ABS-201 showed "the ability of ABS-201 to transition hair follicles into the anagen growth phase and to counteract suppressive catagen effects of prolactin."

Industry glossary

  • ADA: Anti-drug antibodies; immune system proteins that may bind a therapeutic and impact its effectiveness or pharmacokinetics.
  • PK: Pharmacokinetics; the study of how a drug moves through the body.
  • PD: Pharmacodynamics; the study of a drug’s biological effects on the body.
  • SAD: Single ascending dose; a clinical trial design where participants receive increasing single doses to evaluate safety and pharmacokinetics.
  • MAD: Multiple ascending dose; a study design where participants receive multiple doses at increasing amounts to assess safety and effects over time.
  • KOL: Key opinion leader; a recognized expert in a particular medical or scientific field.

ABS-201 was designed using our AI platform to antagonize the prolactin receptor and thereby block prolactin signaling. Scientific data support the dual role of prolactin signaling in endometrial lesion formation as well as associated pain. Prolactin and prolactin receptors are overexpressed in the endometrium of patients with endometriosis. Furthermore, while prolactin supports endometrium formation and individualization, regulated expression of prolactin and its receptor have been found in ectopic endometrial lesions. Prolactin receptors are also present in sensory neurons and can sensitize these neurons, potentially leading to increased pain perception. The prolactin pathway is distinct from sex hormone signaling, further differentiating it from current therapeutic mechanisms of action for treating endometriosis.

Preclinical data have shown that prolactin receptor antagonism suppresses postoperative pain in female mice and inhibits endometriosis interna formation. I also want to point out that there is a significant unmet medical need based on the fact that there are not any non-hormonal treatments around in endometriosis. There is, of course, the approved GnRH antagonist treatment, which has the typical side effects of estrogen reduction.
