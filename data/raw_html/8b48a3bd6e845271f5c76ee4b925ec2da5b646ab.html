Elutia (ELUT) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 11, 2026 at 6:27PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, March 11, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Randy Mills
  • Chief Financial Officer — Matt Ferguson
  • Corporate Communications — Sonali Fonseca

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $3.3 million, representing a 16% increase driven by a shift to direct distribution for both cardiovascular and SimpliDerm product lines.
  • Adjusted gross margin -- 66.8%, up 12 percentage points from 56.5% in the prior-year quarter, attributed to direct distribution.
  • Net Loss from Continuing Operations -- $6.5 million, versus a net loss of $7.2 million in the year-ago quarter; excludes divested bioenvelope business.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (non-GAAP) -- Loss of $4.2 million, compared to a $3.4 million loss in the prior-year quarter, reflecting increased operating investment.
  • Cash Position -- $44.4 million, including cash on hand and $8 million in escrow, after full repayment of $28 million in SWK debt.
  • Shares Outstanding -- 42.8 million shares, with an additional 4.5 million pre-funded warrants, and elimination of all Class B shares due to conversion and sale during the quarter.
  • Bioenvelope Divestiture -- Completed $88 million sale of the business to Boston Scientific, funding debt repayment and supporting cash reserves.
  • Nasdaq Compliance -- Company returned to full compliance with all Nasdaq continued listing requirements following resolution of share class overhang.
  • FDA Submissions -- NXT 41 FDA submission completed with expected clearance in 2026; NXT 41X submission and clearance expected toward the end of the first half of 2027, targeting a second-half launch.
  • SimpliDerm Strategic Review -- Company announced exploration of strategic options for the SimpliDerm product line, considering its segregability and EBITDA accretion for a potential acquirer.
  • Manufacturing Capacity -- Current facility supports up to $120 million in NXT 41X product revenue annually with a single shift, according to management.
  • Workforce & Diversity -- 54% of employees are women, 62% of leadership roles held by women, and 50% possess advanced degrees.
  • Board and Leadership Changes -- Addition of Guido Nils to the board and Pete Ligotti as Chief Commercial Officer to prepare for 41X commercialization.
  • Great Place to Work Certification -- Company earned certification, which management cited as correlating with "fourfold" financial outperformance versus noncertified peers, according to referenced survey data.

SUMMARY

Elutia Inc. reported that the bioenvelope business was divested to Boston Scientific for $88 million, enabling full debt repayment and resulting in a cash position of $44.4 million. The company has filed an FDA submission for NXT 41 and plans to follow with NXT 41X, aiming for a commercial launch in the second half of 2027. Leadership is focused on high-value breast reconstruction markets and is preparing for NXT 41X rollout with targeted manufacturing and commercial strategies. The strategic review of the SimpliDerm product line is underway, and management highlighted both workforce diversity and the recent Great Place to Work certification.

  • Management stated the breast reconstruction market opportunity for NXT 41X is $1.5 billion, with approximately 102,000 annual mastectomy reconstructions and biological meshes used in 90% of procedures.
  • CEO Mills said, "we have 100 million lives covered across two of the largest payers, Anthem and UnitedHealthcare, as well as nine regional plans," referring to SimpliDerm reimbursement.
  • A 15%-20% postoperative infection rate was cited as a key driver for the NXT 41X platform, with registry data cited in the call (reported as 12%-37%).
  • Direct local antibiotic delivery demonstrated infection reductions of 62% and 82% in referenced studies, according to management's discussion.
  • Leadership reported a "return to direct distribution" improved both cardiovascular and SimpliDerm revenue streams in the quarter.
  • The conversion and sale of legacy Class B shares eliminated a share overhang and contributed to Nasdaq compliance, as explained by the CFO.
  • Manufacturing in the Gaithersburg facility is positioned to support 41X ramp without additional major investment, as stated by management.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Adjusted Gross Margin: Gross margin excluding intangible asset amortization, as referenced in company non-GAAP presentations.
  • KOL (Key Opinion Leader): Influential physicians and medical professionals actively engaged in product evaluation and market advocacy within targeted specialties.
  • VAC (Value Analysis Committee): Hospital or institutional committee responsible for clinical and economic evaluation of new products for formulary or procurement decisions.
  • Acellular Dermal Matrix (ADM): Biologic material derived from donated human or animal skin processed to remove cells, used for soft tissue reconstruction applications.
