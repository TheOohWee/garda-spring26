LATAM (LTM) Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

DATE

  • Chief Financial Officer — Ricardo Bottas

TAKEAWAYS

  • Passenger Traffic -- Record 82 million passengers transported for the full year, up 15%.
  • Quarterly Passenger Volume -- 21.5 million passengers transported, a 7% increase year over year.
  • Capacity Growth -- Approximately 12% increase in capacity for the quarter and 15% for the year.
  • Load Factor -- Consolidated load factor of almost 86% for the quarter, indicating efficient utilization.
  • Revenue -- Total annual revenue reached $13 billion, representing an approximate 11% increase.
  • Quarterly Revenue -- Total revenues for the quarter rose 4% with Cargo revenue up 29%, marking three consecutive quarters of Cargo improvement.
  • Adjusted Operating Margin -- 13.6% for the quarter and 12.7% for the full year, exceeding guidance.
  • Adjusted EBITDAR -- $866 million for the quarter, 28% higher, and $3.1 billion for the year, a 23% increase.
  • Net Income -- $272 million for the quarter (up 228%), and $977 million for the year, nearly double the prior year.
  • Cost Discipline -- Adjusted passenger CASK ex-fuel was $0.042, down 1.5% from the prior year.
  • Operating Expenses -- Adjusted operating expenses up 1% for the quarter, with a 12% increase in capacity offset by a 23% decrease in jet fuel prices (including hedges).
  • Liquidity -- $3.5 billion at year-end, equivalent to 27% of last 12 months' revenues.
  • Adjusted Net Leverage -- Stands at 1.7x, reflecting ongoing deleveraging.
  • Refinancing Exercise -- Fourth-quarter refinancing significantly reduced the cost of the majority of non-fleet debt.
  • Adjusted Operating Cash Flow -- $2.8 billion for the year, with $243 million positive cash generated after $175 million in mandatory minimum dividend and $207 million for refinancing.
  • Customer Satisfaction -- Net Promoter Score reached a record 51 points overall and 56 for Premium passengers, a 3-point increase, plus lowest complaint rates in key markets.
  • Punctuality -- Ranked among the top five global airline groups in punctuality for the sixth consecutive year.
  • Loyalty Program -- LATAM Pass membership reached 49 million, remaining the largest in the region.
  • Sustainability Recognition -- Reincorporation into the Dow Jones Sustainability Index, ranked fifth globally and first in the Americas for airline group sustainability.
  • Sustainability-Linked Loan -- Secured South America’s first sustainability-linked loan and advanced studies on sustainable aviation fuel with MIT and Airbus.
  • Shareholder Return Policy -- New capital allocation framework allows returns beyond the mandatory 30% dividend, including potential for up to $150 million share buyback subject to shareholder approval.
  • 2025 Guidance -- Capacity expected to increase 7%-9% with 22 new aircraft, revenue forecast $14 billion-$14.5 billion, and adjusted EBITDAR guidance of $3.25 billion-$3.6 billion.
  • International Segment -- Noted continued strong demand, with international bookings high for current season and no significant weakness across long-haul or regional routes.
  • Fleet Plan -- 22 Airbus A320 family and 2 Boeing 787 deliveries planned; engine supply is monitored but incorporated into guidance.
  • Joint Venture Developments -- Delta Air Lines partnership maturing after two years, with expanded capacity and growing integration efforts.

SUMMARY

LATAM Airlines Group S.A. (LTM +0.19%) delivered record financial and operational results, achieving its highest passenger volume, adjusted EBITDAR, and net income to date. Management highlighted effective cost containment, expanded liquidity, and reinforced the company’s leadership in punctuality, sustainability, and customer satisfaction, while emphasizing a robust capital position supporting increased shareholder returns. Updated 2025 guidance signals continued network and revenue growth, with disciplined investment in fleet and sustainability initiatives.

  • Management disclosed that potential share repurchase programs are constrained by market liquidity and require shareholder approval, acknowledging buybacks are rare in Chile’s market environment.
  • Plans for sustained capital allocation prioritize flexibility for growth, potential M&A, and returning capital, with no immediate strategic investments reported but options actively considered.
  • Executives specifically indicated that domestic Colombia remains subject to overcapacity from competing entrants, while overall competition is not expected to materially disrupt LATAM’s pricing or capacity deployment in other markets.
  • Annual improvements in Net Promoter Score and customer service metrics have been attributed to network, product upgrades, increased Wi-Fi deployment, and ongoing loyalty program expansion.
  • LATAM’s sustainability initiatives now include extensive waste recycling and a novel focus on sustainable aviation fuel development backed by academic-industry partnerships.
  • Infrastructure constraints are most notable at Bogotá’s El Dorado airport, though executives expressed confidence in current capacity elsewhere and highlighted regional airport investment programs.
  • Strategic focus continues on maximizing the operational and customer service synergies of the Delta Air Lines joint venture, particularly as new routes and improved connections roll out.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ASK (Available Seat Kilometer): A measure of airline capacity, representing one seat flown one kilometer regardless of occupancy.
  • CASK (Cost per Available Seat Kilometer): Operating cost divided by total available seat kilometers; key industry cost efficiency metric.
  • EBITDAR: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, and aircraft rental; used to assess airline operational profitability.
  • Net Promoter Score (NPS): Customer satisfaction metric indicating likelihood of recommendation, widely used in service industries.
  • LATAM Pass: The company’s proprietary frequent flyer and loyalty program.
