TAKEAWAYS

  • FFO Beat -- Marc Holliday stated, "we printed an FFO beat of 2¢ a share, driven by higher NOI due to lower expenses, net of reimbursements."
  • Same Store Cash NOI -- Higher than internal expectations, supported by improved hospitality performance and lower G&A expenses.

  • Same Store Occupancy -- Achieved 93% at year-end, reflecting an increase of nearly 400 basis points from year-end 2024 lows.
  • Post-Quarter Leasing Momentum -- 142,000 square feet signed in January and a forward pipeline exceeding 1,000,000 square feet reported.
  • 2026 Occupancy Objective -- Targeting 94.8% same store occupancy by year-end, with leasing activity and pipeline supporting this goal.
  • Fee Revenue -- SL Green forecasts generating over $100 million in fee revenue from institutional clients in 2026.
  • Financing Strategy -- $7 billion capital markets plan in progress, including approximately $5 billion earmarked for the refinancing of 1 Madison Avenue, 245 Park Avenue, and the corporate credit facility.
  • Recent Loan Pricing -- Park Avenue Tower financing priced at a 1.58% spread, with AAA tranches representing over 50% of the transaction selling as tight as 112 basis points above the Treasury rate.
  • Asset Sales Plan -- $2.5 billion of asset dispositions expected in 2026, primarily weighted toward the second half, with four deals in contract or term-sheet negotiations.
  • Fund Platform -- Plans to launch fundraising for a new senior credit lending fund, with current fund targeting $150 million to $175 million of deployment per quarter and underwriting mid-teens gross returns.
  • New Metric Disclosure -- Economic occupancy at year-end 2025 reported as 86.7% for the same-store portfolio.
  • FAD Outperformance -- Surpassed initial guidance by $65 million for the full year, including an outperformance of almost $20 million in the fourth quarter.
  • Summit Contribution -- Fourth-quarter operating profit from Summit was impacted by a later than anticipated opening and maintenance expenses, but no change to the 2026 outlook was indicated.
  • Green Loan Services -- Now the largest active special servicer of SASB loans in the U.S, servicing five of the ten largest specially serviced loans.

SUMMARY

SL Green Realty Corp. (SLG +1.80%) reported operating results for the fourth quarter characterized by notable leasing velocity, improved occupancy, and continued execution on a targeted $7 billion refinancing strategy. Management indicated robust investor interest in New York City assets from both domestic and international sources, including recent expansion of capital relationships across Asia, Canada, Europe, and the Middle East. Economic occupancy, a newly disclosed metric, was 86.7% for the same-store portfolio at year-end, with guidance indicating higher year-end 2026 levels and forecasting annual same-store NOI growth of 3.5%-4.5%. Fee-generating business lines and the launch of a new senior credit fund were emphasized as integral to future earnings growth, while asset sales are expected to be back-half loaded and significantly diversified across sectors.

  • Steve Durels reported that 800,000 square feet of the current leasing pipeline are leases under negotiation, with approximately half of the pipeline comprised of financial services tenants.
  • Steve Durels noted that "AI tenant leased a million square feet last year" and that there are 13 known AI requirements for more than 200,000 square feet each.
  • Management clarified that expected occupancy gains in 2026 are minimally dependent on asset sales, highlighting continued organic leasing momentum.
  • Recent partnership with Rockpoint at 100 Park Avenue closed on New Year's Eve, with the building now fully leased and Rockpoint making its first major office investment in six years.
  • The current subordinate credit fund deployment pace was stated as $150 million to $175 million per quarter, with a focus on inefficiencies in the subordinate credit space.
  • Management highlighted that 6th Avenue is exhibiting leasing and rent growth dynamics similar to Park Avenue, pointing to a recoveries in key submarkets.
  • Fourth-quarter "other income" increased due to the timing of transaction closes and additional special servicing fees.
  • The company expects the volume of lease rollover to decline to less than 900,000 square feet per year over the next four years, materially below historic levels.
  • SL Green's asset management business and fee income are positioned as key sources of durable cash flow, set to expand with the new fund launch and ongoing capital markets activities.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FFO: Funds From Operations — a key REIT earnings metric reflecting net income with adjustments for depreciation and gains/losses from property sales.
  • FAD: Funds Available for Distribution — a cash flow metric adjusting FFO for capital expenditures and other non-cash items, relevant for dividend sustainability analysis.
  • NOI: Net Operating Income — rental and other income from properties less property-level expenses, before debt service and corporate costs.
  • Same-Store: Refers to a portfolio of properties held by a company for the full duration of the measurement period, used to compare performance trends over time.
  • Mark to Market: The difference between current contractual rents and current market rents for lease rollovers, used for assessing rental growth potential.
  • SASB: Single-Asset Single-Borrower — a type of commercial mortgage-backed securities structure for individual large-asset financings.
  • TI: Tenant Improvements — landlord-funded buildouts or improvements in connection with new office leasing, often affecting net effective rent.
