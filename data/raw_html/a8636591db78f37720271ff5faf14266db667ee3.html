CACI (CACI) Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $2.2 billion, representing 5.7% year-over-year growth and 4.5% organic growth in the second quarter.
  • EBITDA margin -- 11.8% for the quarter, a 70 basis point year-over-year increase, aided by strong program execution and favorable technology mix.
  • Adjusted diluted EPS -- $6.81, up 14% from the prior year, reflecting higher operating income and a lower share count.
  • Free cash flow -- $138 million in the quarter, with day sales outstanding at 57 days.
  • Book-to-bill ratio -- 0.65 times for the quarter, 1.4 times for the first half, and 1.3 times on a trailing twelve-month basis.
  • Backlog -- $33 billion total, up 3%; funded backlog up 7% from a year ago.
  • Fiscal 2026 guidance raised -- Now projects revenue of $9.3 billion to $9.5 billion (7.8%-10.1% growth), adjusted net income of $630 million to $645 million, adjusted EPS of $28.25 to $28.92 (7%-9% growth), EBITDA margin of 11.7%-11.8%, and free cash flow of at least $725 million.
  • Technology revenue mix -- Nearly 60% of total revenue derives from technology, with management expecting this share to continue increasing.
  • Major contract wins -- Awarded $1.4 billion in new business during the quarter, with weighted average award duration over six years.
  • Electronic warfare segment -- Now generates approximately $2 billion in revenue, supported by demand for software-defined solutions such as Merlin and RMT.
  • Pipeline dynamics -- $6 billion of bids under current evaluation (over 70% new business), and $20 billion in planned submissions over the next two quarters, also weighted toward new business.
  • Leverage and capital structure -- Net debt to trailing-twelve-month EBITDA at 2.4 times, expected to rise to 4.3 times upon close of the ARCA acquisition, with a target to return to the low threes within six quarters.
  • Contract revenue visibility -- 95% of fiscal year 2026 revenue expected from existing programs, 3% from recompetes, and 2% from new business.
  • Operational execution -- Indirect costs have remained below 21% of revenues for three consecutive quarters, attributed to disciplined cost control amid growth.
  • Pending and resolved protests -- The company successfully defended the JTMS contract protest, unlocking a ten-year, $1.6 billion opportunity poised to impact Q4 revenue and future years; other protests remain unresolved but are expected by month-end.
  • Acquisition of ARCA -- The announced ARCA acquisition marks a further expansion in the space and intelligence market, with leverage implications incorporated and backlog details pending transaction close.
  • Impact of government funding dynamics -- Management observed reconciliation funds beginning to flow, affecting border security, space, counter-UAS, and intelligence modernization programs, with financial guidance factoring in a range of potential funding outcomes.
  • Modernization and civil business -- Growth in the civil segment is mainly driven by Customs and Border Protection, Department of Homeland Security, and NASA programs, with CACI winning the three largest agile software development contracts in the federal government.
  • Margin expansion strategy -- Management prioritizes free cash flow while seeking to maintain or modestly expand margins through technology mix improvements and disciplined investment.

Summary

Management increased all fiscal 2026 guidance targets following second quarter results, projecting revenue, margin, and free cash flow improvements. CACI (CACI 5.10%) reported strong order momentum, with a weighted average contract duration of over six years and a backlog now at $33 billion. Leadership highlighted successful technology-led growth, including $2 billion in annual electronic warfare revenue and sustained market share gains through new business wins and pipeline development. Management confirmed that acquisitions, including ARCA, are a cornerstone of its capital allocation, with a clear timeline for deleveraging post-close. Reconciliation funding and modernization priorities are expected to drive further increases in both defense and civil program activity.

  • President Mengucci stated, "Our strategy defines where we are going, what we are building, and how we are executing with discipline and consistency," indicating a commitment to long-term differentiation in national security solutions.
  • The ARCA acquisition is designed to accelerate expansion in high-priority space and intelligence markets, with management noting historic resilience in managing post-deal leverage.
  • Recent government shutdowns contributed to delayed material purchases and temporarily lower award conversions, but management expects improvement as acquisition processes recover.
  • Guidance assumes no contribution from ARCA, and management identified a $300 billion total addressable market, citing its current market share at under 4%.
  • Technology revenue acceleration and sustained cost discipline were described as the main drivers of improved profitability and projected free cash flow per share growth of 65%.
  • Company leaders addressed questions concerning policy and regulatory changes, expressing confidence in CACI’s readiness to adapt to firm fixed price, OTA, and FAR Part 12/15 transition models.
  • President Mengucci emphasized, regarding program risk, "I don't see any risk. In fact, I like the opportunity of what the government has taken a look at," referencing proactive FFP and OTA business model approaches.

Industry glossary

  • Book-to-bill ratio: A measurement of the ratio of orders received to revenue billed in a given period, indicating demand versus fulfillment in the contract business.
  • EBITDA margin: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization as a percentage of revenue, used to gauge operating profitability.
  • FAR Part 12 / FAR Part 15: Federal Acquisition Regulation parts governing commercial (Part 12) and negotiated (Part 15) procurement processes for government contracts.
  • OTA (Other Transaction Authority): A flexible contract vehicle permitting certain government agencies to enter into agreements not subject to standard procurement regulations, often used for R&D and prototyping.
  • RMT: Proprietary electronic warfare system referenced by CACI among its product offerings.
  • Merlin: CACI’s counter-UAS (unmanned aircraft system) technology platform.
  • JTMS: Joint Asset Tracking and Management System, a ten-year $1.6 billion program awarded to CACI.
  • ARCA: Target of CACI’s announced acquisition, a technology provider servicing the space and intelligence community.
  • Reconciliation funds: Special congressional appropriations (sometimes via reconciliation bills) providing incremental funding to government agencies and programs.
  • NASAs end caps: Reference to a particular NASA contracting program in which CACI participates (specific context: enterprise IT/civil work).
