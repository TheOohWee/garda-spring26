Northern Technologies (NTIC) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 3:02PM EST

Tuesday, Nov. 18, 2025 at 9:00 a.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Patrick Lynch
  • Chief Financial Officer — Matthew Wolsfeld
  • Vice President, Natur-Tec — Vineet Dalal

RISKS

  • Gross margin compression noted, with gross profit as a percentage of net sales falling to 37.9% for the quarter due to a less profitable sales mix.
  • "Fiscal year ended Aug. 31, 2025 was marked by order timing shifts and selective softness in our ZERUST oil and gas, and Natur-Tec markets."
  • Operating expenses as a percentage of net sales rose to 44.7% for the year, indicating higher cost pressures from investments and personnel.
  • Northern Technologies International Corporation's Chinese subsidiary incurred a one-time $387,000 penalty from Ningbo Customs, impacting fourth quarter profitability.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated net sales -- $22.3 million, down 4.4% due to order timing shifts and market softness in ZERUST oil and gas, and Natur-Tec segments.
  • ZERUST oil and gas net sales -- $3 million in the quarter, a 29.4% decline attributed to the prior-year period benefiting from $600,000 in order timing shifts.
  • Natur-Tec net sales -- $5.1 million in the quarter, a 10% decrease driven by pricing dynamics and delayed purchasing from a significant North American customer.
  • ZERUST industrial net sales -- Increased 5.8% in the quarter, partially mitigating the overall sales decline.
  • Joint venture sales (Q4) -- $24.4 million, up 4.7%; full-year joint venture sales down 4.9% due to European economic pressures and global trade policy uncertainties.
  • NTIC China net sales -- Rose 12% to $4 million for the quarter and 14% to $16.2 million for the year, reflecting ongoing demand growth and limited exposure to U.S. tariffs.
  • Gross profit margin -- 37.9% for the quarter, down from 43.8%, primarily due to a less profitable sales mix.
  • Operating expenses -- 43.5% of net sales in the quarter, up from 40.7%, driven by strategic investments in ZERUST oil and gas, personnel, and infrastructure.
  • Net income (loss) -- Net loss of $1.1 million, or $0.11 per diluted share for the quarter; full-year net income of $18,000, or $0.00 per diluted share.
  • Non-GAAP adjusted net income (loss) -- Adjusted net loss of $607,000, or $0.06 per diluted share for the quarter; adjusted net loss of $12,000, or $0.00 per diluted share for the year.
  • Effective tax rate -- 67.5% for the year, up from 17.3%, reflecting higher tax expense in foreign subsidiaries and reduced pre-tax income; management expects normalization as North American profitability recovers.
  • Cash and debt position -- $3.7 million in cash and cash equivalents, $12.2 million in debt, including $9.3 million drawn on the revolving line of credit at year-end.
  • Brazil FPSO contract -- ZERUST Brazil secured a three-year, approximately $13 million offshore corrosion protection contract with a major EPC company, with ramp-up expected through 2026-2028.
  • Natur-Tec preferred supplier agreement -- Entered a strategic supplier deal with a major U.S. JanSan, and food service distributor, anticipated to support Natur-Tec sales growth in 2026.
  • Dividend -- Quarterly cash dividend of $0.01 per share paid on Aug. 13, 2025.

SUMMARY

Northern Technologies International Corporation (NTIC 0.13%) announced that ZERUST Brazil won a three-year, $13 million contract to provide corrosion protection for offshore floating production units, representing a major expansion in the global oil and gas market. NTIC China delivered double-digit sales growth for both the quarter and full year, demonstrating demand growth in Asia and minimal tariff exposure. The company entered a new preferred supplier agreement for Natur-Tec in North America, positioning Natur-Tec for rising sales as significant customer orders have already resumed for the coming quarters. Non-GAAP results reflected material declines, as net loss and compressed margins were shaped by adverse sales mix, increased expenses, and discrete foreign tax events. Operating cash flow for the year was $2.4 million, with management targeting reduced leverage and increased cash generation in 2026 as capital investments taper.

  • Chief Financial Officer Matthew Wolsfeld said, "the expectation is that we're going to drive revenue growth in 2026, those gross margin dollars falling down to the operating profit line as we're able to hold operating expenses as stable as possible."
  • Management identified ongoing political and economic headwinds in Europe as impacting joint venture performance, while expecting targeted stimulus measures to create future upside, especially in Germany.
  • Revenue from new compostable food packaging solutions is expected to begin in India by the second or third quarter of 2026; U.S. commercialization may require additional development cycles.
  • Further capital expenditures in North America are planned to be minimal in 2026, with any incremental expansion in Brazil and India to be funded locally.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FPSO (Floating Production Storage and Offloading unit): A sea-based vessel used in offshore oil and gas production that processes and stores hydrocarbons for export or offloading.
  • EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction company): A type of firm contracted to deliver complex construction and engineering projects in the oil, gas, and industrial sectors.
  • JanSan: An abbreviation for Janitorial and Sanitation, referring to maintenance supplies and distribution relevant to food service and industrial cleaning markets.
  • HTS code: Harmonized Tariff Schedule code, an internationally standardized system for classifying traded products.
