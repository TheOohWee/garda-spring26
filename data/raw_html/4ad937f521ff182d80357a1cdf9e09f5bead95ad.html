TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Sales -- $6.2 billion, with 3.9% growth for the fourth quarter, and full-year growth of 1.6% despite portfolio changes.
  • Organic Sales -- 0.7% for the full year; adjusting for exited Vitamins and Minerals Supplement (VMS) business, organic growth was 2% for the year and 1.8% in Q4.
  • Gross Margin -- Increased 90 basis points year over year in Q4 and projected to improve by 100 basis points in 2026, exceeding the Evergreen model standard of 25-50 basis points.
  • EPS -- Achieved $0.86 in Q4, 12% higher than prior year; 2026 outlook targets 5%-8% growth.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $1.2 billion in 2025, with conversion at 127%, and 2026 guidance of $1.15 billion.
  • Dividend -- Announced 4.2% increase to dividends in 2026, marking the 125th consecutive year of dividend payments and 30th consecutive increase.
  • Portfolio Reshaping -- Divested Spinbrush and Vitamin businesses, exited Flawless and showerhead categories, reducing private label exposure from 12% to 5%.
  • Touchland Acquisition -- Drove above-expectation sales; integrated and contributing to both organic and reported growth.
  • Brand Performance -- Four of eight core power brands grew share; Hero and TheraBreath each delivered double-digit growth; ARM & HAMMER attained record 14.5% wash-load share in laundry.
  • Segment Growth Outlook -- 2026 organic growth projected at 3%-4% overall, with U.S. at 3%, international at 8%, and Specialty Products Division at 5%.
  • Marketing Investment -- Planned spend at 11% of sales for 2026, consistent with the company’s growth framework.
  • e-Commerce Penetration -- Online sales grew from 2% to 24% of total, driving above 14% growth for brands such as ARM & HAMMER, Hero, and TheraBreath.
  • Tariff Mitigation -- Reduced tariff exposure from $190 million to $25 million, now continuing downward.
  • International Expansion -- TheraBreath in over 50 countries and Hero in more than 75, with Touchland entering Canada and the Middle East.
  • Innovation Contribution -- More than half of organic growth derived from new product development; 2026 innovation pipeline highlighted by launches in oral care, acne cleansers, premium laundry products, and category extensions.

SUMMARY

Strategic portfolio repositioning, including divestment of low-margin and private-label-heavy businesses, drove a reduction in private label exposure and a shift toward higher-margin brands. The company delivered improved year over year gross margin and record cash flow, maintaining strong dividend growth and financial flexibility for future M&A activity. Management outlined ambitious growth targets for ARM & HAMMER, TheraBreath, and international segments, reinforced by continued investment in innovation and digital platforms. The Touchland acquisition quickly generated positive sales impact and is being purposefully expanded internationally through premium partnerships and selective retail presence.

  • International business registered a 5.5% organic growth in 2025, with a three-year average CAGR of 8%, supported by brand localization and regional innovation initiatives.
  • ARM & HAMMER achieved #1 share in U.S. laundry wash loads and outpaced the category by 1%, while the cat litter portfolio increased market share and reported a 48% repeat rate for its HardBall SKU.
  • TheraBreath holds the #2 position in U.S. mouthwash, reached nearly 22% market share, and is targeting penetration in the $4 billion toothpaste segment via new product launches with clinical backing.
  • Hero skincare business grew at three times the category rate and attained 19% share, with geographic expansion and new product formats planned for 2026.
  • Inventory destocking and the OxiClean delisting at Costco are referenced as prior headwinds, now largely lapped in the sales base entering 2026.
  • SG&A and amortization impacts from Touchland are expected mainly in the first half, with productivity and portfolio mix supporting targeted margin expansion.
  • The company reported e-commerce penetration reached 24%, with omnichannel strategies yielding double-digit engagement rates for flagship brands and leveraging AI-driven content for digital growth.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • VMS (Vitamins, Minerals, Supplements): Refers to the dietary supplement segment, which was exited by the company to improve portfolio mix and margin.
  • Evergreen model: Church & Dwight's long-term operating framework that targets consistent 4% organic growth and 25-50 basis points of gross margin expansion annually.
  • TDP (Total Distribution Points): A retail shelf-space metric indicating how widely a product or brand is distributed across stores and shelf facings.
  • SKU (Stock Keeping Unit): The unique identifier for each distinct product in inventory or on retail shelves.
  • SG&A (Selling, General, and Administrative expense): The operating costs not directly tied to manufacturing, referenced here as a margin pressure due to acquisitions and business exits.
