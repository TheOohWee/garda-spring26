TAKEAWAYS

  • Return on Equity (ROE) -- 12.4% for the quarter, marking seven consecutive quarters of double-digit ROE.
  • Net Asset Value (NAV) per Share -- $15.28, "just off from historical highs," according to Packer.
  • Net Investment Income (NII) -- $0.47 per share, down $0.01 sequentially from Q2 2024.
  • Supplemental Dividend -- $0.05 per share declared for the quarter, bringing cumulative supplemental dividends to $0.47 per share since inception of the program.
  • Base Dividend Coverage -- 127%, among the highest stated peer numbers, with management expressing confidence in sustaining coverage through 2025.
  • Dividend Distributions -- Fourth quarter base dividend of $0.37 per share declared, payable January 15, 2025, to shareholders of record as of December 31, 2024.
  • Portfolio Investments -- $13.4 billion at quarter end.
  • Outstanding Debt -- $7.8 billion, yielding net leverage of 1.23x, positioned within the 0.9x-1.25x target range.
  • Total Net Assets -- $6 billion as of quarter end.
  • Total Liquidity -- $2.1 billion, exceeding unfunded commitments, after raising revolver capacity by $585 million to $2.6 billion.
  • Spillover Income -- $0.41 per share at quarter end, described as a "strong advantage" for supporting future distributions.
  • Interest Rate Sensitivity -- A 50 basis point rate decrease is expected to reduce NII by $0.02 per share over the next quarter, assuming all other factors remain steady.
  • Non-Accrual Rate -- 0.7% of debt portfolio fair value, with one name removed from non-accrual and no new additions during the quarter.
  • First Lien Investments -- Increased to 76% of the portfolio from 69% one year ago, with over 96% of this quarter's originations in first lien or unitranche structures.
  • Investment Diversification -- Average investment is less than 0.5% of the portfolio; 11 new names added in the quarter.
  • Borrower Metrics -- Median EBITDA of portfolio borrowers is $112 million; weighted average EBITDA is $197 million; average loan-to-value (LTV) is 43%.
  • Interest Coverage Ratio -- Portfolio average improved to 1.7x from 1.6x in the prior quarter, with management projecting improvement to high-1x-to-low-2x within a year under the current forward curve.
  • Portfolio Revenue and EBITDA Growth -- "mid-single digits" year over year, with a slight quarter over quarter uptick.
  • PIK (Payment-in-Kind) Exposure -- Over 80% of PIK income structured at initial underwriting; over half in first lien investments; exposure remains stable vs. prior periods.
  • Originations -- $1.2 billion in new commitments; approximately two-thirds allocated to existing borrowers for refinancing or add-ons.
  • Platform Scale -- $128 billion in credit-related assets under management; Blue Owl serves as lead or co-lead lender on 90% of deals and administrative agent on about 65% of investments.
  • Recent Acquisitions -- Atalaya Capital Management acquisition closed in September; IPI Partners acquisition announced in October, expanding platform capabilities in alternative credit and data centers.
  • OBDE Merger Process -- Joint proxy declared effective in mid-October; shareholder meetings scheduled for January 8; anticipated closing January 2025.
  • Loss Ratio -- Cumulative loss ratio since inception is approximately 0.20%, indicating low realized portfolio losses.
  • Dividend Income Variability -- Q3 dividend income was $47 million, down from $51 million in Q2, with management attributing fluctuations to the underlying asset performance rather than structural changes.
  • Fee Income -- Prepayment-related fee income in Q3 was lower compared to Q2, which had "exceptional repayments" due to refinancing activity.

SUMMARY

Blue Owl Capital Corporation (OBDC +1.02%) reported portfolio investments of $13.4 billion, supported by $2.1 billion in liquidity and a strong capital structure highlighted by a recent revolver upsizing. Management stressed their ability to sustain the base dividend through 2025 and characterized portfolio credit trends as stable, with first lien loans comprising a growing share of assets. The company disclosed low non-accruals, increasing spillover income, and improved portfolio interest coverage, while noting that a 50 basis point interest rate decline could modestly reduce net investment income in the near term. Strategic expansion continued with recent acquisitions in alternative credit and data centers, as well as an anticipated merger with OBDE designed to streamline the platform and enhance earnings accretion. No adverse credit migration or heightened amendment activity was identified, with portfolio company earnings and diversification metrics described as favorable for risk-adjusted performance despite muted new M&A volumes.

  • The company completed $1.2 billion of new investment commitments, with most originations allocated to refinancing and add-on deals for existing borrowers rather than new buyout activity.
  • Over 96% of new originations were in first lien or unitranche loans, elevating first lien exposure to 76% of the total portfolio.
  • Administrative agency and lead/co-lead lending roles were maintained in most platform investments, supporting selective portfolio construction.
  • Recent acquisitions and joint ventures expanded addressable markets in credit, infrastructure, and data centers, but management reaffirmed commitment to upper middle-market, sponsor-backed lending as the BDC's primary strategy.
  • Dividend and prepayment-related income remain variable quarter-to-quarter, depending on underlying asset performance and refinancing trends.
  • Following the merger, the combined company is expected to see a decline in common equity investments, but will have room to strategically grow these positions over time.
  • Interest rate sensitivity analysis reveals moderate downside risk to income if base rates fall, though management expects any earnings reduction to occur gradually.
  • Total platform assets under management now exceed $128 billion, providing competitive origination advantages and a deep pool of sponsor and borrower relationships.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Unitranche: A type of single-debt facility that combines senior and subordinated loan tranches into one structure, typically used in direct lending to provide borrowers with streamlined financing.
  • PIK (Payment-in-Kind) Income: Interest paid in the form of additional securities or accrued principal rather than cash, often used for portfolio companies with limited current cash flow.
  • Spillover Income: Undistributed taxable income, usually retained to support future dividend payments in a BDC structure.
  • Administrative Agent: The lender or institution responsible for managing and administering the terms and ongoing activities of a syndicated loan agreement.
