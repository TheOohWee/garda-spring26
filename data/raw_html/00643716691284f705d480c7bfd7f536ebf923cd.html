Takeaways

  • Total Net Revenues -- RMB 3.2 billion, a 6% increase, with e-commerce revenue at RMB 2.6 billion (up 2.5%) and Brand Management revenue at RMB 664 million (up 24%).
  • E-Commerce Services Revenue -- RMB 2 billion, rising by 3.1%, led by digital marketing, IT solutions, and strong luxury online performance.
  • E-Commerce Product Sales Revenue -- RMB 574.5 million, up slightly by 0.5%, driven by Health and Nutrition category gains, partially offset by declines in appliances.
  • Brand Management Product Sales -- RMB 663.7 million, representing 24% growth, attributed to robust contributions from Gap.
  • Gross Margin - Group Product Sales -- 36.5%, expanding 640 basis points year over year.
  • E-Commerce Product Sales Gross Margin -- 18.4%, a 760 basis point increase, driven by category mix optimization.
  • Brand Management Gross Margin -- 52.1%, up from 50.4%, reflecting merchandising and marketing improvements.
  • Gross Profit -- RMB 451.5 million, up 35.9%.
  • Sales and Marketing Expenses -- RMB 1.2 billion, rising by RMB 181 million; BEC contributed RMB 136.9 million of the increase, BBM RMB 49.6 million due to digital and offline expansion.
  • Fulfillment Costs -- Down 11.1% to RMB 683.4 million through continued cost optimization.
  • Technology and Content Expenses -- Declined 20.2% to RMB 116.9 million as tech monetization efficiency improved.
  • G&A Expenses -- RMB 187.9 million, a 2% decrease from further cost controls.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Profit (Group) -- RMB 197.7 million, up 91.4% year over year.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Profit (E-Commerce) -- RMB 195.9 million, a 43% increase.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Profit (Brand Management) -- RMB 1.8 million, marking the segment’s first breakeven quarter.
  • Cash, Equivalents, and Investments -- RMB 2.8 billion at year-end, with operating cash flow up 315% to RMB 420 million.
  • Investment Impairment -- RMB 230 million recognized on e-commerce sector preinvestments and certain equity holdings.
  • Store Network -- 164 Gap stores at year-end after opening 29 new sites in 2025; total BBM stores reached 177 with Hunter additions.
  • Inventory Turnover (BBM) -- Efficiency improved; turnover days decreased by 16% to 114 days.
  • 2028 Operating Profit Target -- RMB 550 million, with growth from both margin expansion in BEC and scaling in BBM.
  • 2026 Growth Outlook -- Management expects single-digit revenue growth for BEC, higher double-digit growth for BBM, and non-GAAP operating profit approximately doubling from 2025 levels.
  • AI Integration -- AI agent technology is focused on “driving our efficiency internally more focus on the bottom line” with proprietary tools developed in-house for process automation; no major partnerships with leading AI firms reported.
  • Gap (China) Performance -- Over 30% growth in January and February; targeting 20% growth for 2026 and 25%-30% annual increases through 2028, driven by store expansion and supply chain optimization.
  • New Store Performance -- Newly opened Gap stores and upgraded "new image" stores demonstrated “double-digit gain in sales productivity,” indicating outperforming legacy locations.

Summary

Baozun (BZUN 6.20%) concluded its multi-year transformation with sequential revenue growth and significant margin expansion, ending the year with both core segments profitable on a non-GAAP basis. Management presented a focused 2028 non-GAAP operating profit target of RMB 550 million, underpinned by ongoing synergies between e-commerce and brand management operations, and announced a double-digit store expansion plan for Gap that supports continued top-line acceleration. Strategic emphasis was placed on internal efficiency gains from proprietary AI deployment, concrete category gains within health and nutrition, new merchandising initiatives for Gap, and accelerated store growth in both mainland China and select overseas locations.

  • The group registered improved inventory turnover and reduced fulfillment costs, supported by digital process enhancements and new retail formats.
  • Leadership highlighted that the “first breakeven quarter for” marks a business inflection point, validating the segment’s repositioning and supporting a multi-year scaling outlook.
  • Management stated, “for Gap, the biggest brands, we will see a very good trend and also the improvements or the capabilities also promising,” referencing brand-specific performance catalysts in product development and omnichannel marketing.
  • Management indicated international operations, led by Hunter in Southeast Asia, contribute positively but remain a minor portion of group growth through 2027.
  • Impairment of preexisting e-commerce investments was recognized “to maintaining a focus and resilience business portfolio,” with ongoing confidence in the health of remaining holdings.

Industry glossary

  • BEC: Baozun E-Commerce, the group’s core technology-driven online retail service platform.
  • BBM: Baozun Brand Management, the division overseeing direct brand management businesses, including Gap and Hunter.
  • GEO: Generative Engine Optimization, company-specific terminology referring to the application of AI-driven content and search optimization for digital commerce.
