TAKEAWAYS

  • Gross Written Premium -- $492.6 million for the quarter, up 32% year over year, reflecting expansion across diversified lines.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $61.1 million for the quarter, representing growth of 48% compared to the prior year period.
  • Adjusted Return on Equity -- 26.9% annualized for the quarter, up from 23.1% year over year, outpacing the company's 20% target.
  • Adjusted Combined Ratio -- 73.4% for the quarter, compared to 71.7% last year, reflecting sustained underwriting profitability.
  • Net Earned Premiums -- $233.5 million for the quarter, an increase of 61% over the prior year period.
  • Full-Year Gross Written Premium -- $2.0 billion, up 32%, with net earned premiums up 57% to $802.6 million.
  • Full-Year Adjusted Net Income -- $216.1 million, an increase of 62%, exceeding the initial yearly guidance midpoint by $30 million.
  • Full-Year Adjusted Return on Equity -- 25.9%, compared to 22.2% the prior year.
  • Casualty Segment Growth -- 120% year over year gross written premium growth in the quarter; casualty accounted for 20% of total premiums by year-end.
  • Crop Franchise Written Premium -- $248 million for the year, above the original $200 million and revised $230 million target, with $40 million generated in the quarter.
  • Earthquake Segment -- Combined residential and commercial franchise declined 2% year over year in the quarter due to a one-time headwind; commercial earthquake rates fell 15% as competitive pressure continued.
  • Inland Marine and Other Property -- 30% growth year over year for the quarter, driven by builders risk and Hawaiian hurricane products as well as record flood production from the Neptune Flood partnership.
  • Reinsurance -- Four quota share treaties renewed at improved economics, including a commercial earthquake quota share 15% lower risk-adjusted and earthquake XOL treaties 15% lower risk-adjusted; June 1 XOL renewal is assumed to decrease by 10% risk-adjusted.
  • Expense Ratios -- Acquisition expense ratio was 13%, and other underwriting expense ratio was 8.1% for the quarter, both increased year over year due to business mix and growth investments.
  • Net Investment Income -- $16 million for the quarter, up 41.3% due to higher yields and investment balances; full-year net investment income was $56 million, a 56% rise.
  • Adjusted Net Income Guidance -- 2026 guidance set at $260 million to $275 million, implying 24% growth at the midpoint and a return on equity above 20%.
  • Gray Surety Acquisition -- Closed January 31, 2026, at $311 million funded with a $300 million term loan at SOFR plus 1.75%, modestly accretive in 2026; Gray Surety and Credit would have represented 6.5% of total annual premium pro forma for 2025.
  • Employee Growth -- Headcount surpassed 500 professionals with additions in underwriting, claims, data, and actuarial functions to support expansion.

RISKS

  • Expense ratios rose, with the acquisition expense ratio at 13% and other underwriting expense ratio at 8.1%, which management attributed to a "mix of business for the quarter resulting in higher commission and lower ceding commission" and organizational investments.

SUMMARY

Palomar Holdings (PLMR 4.29%) reported record quarterly and full-year financial results, with premium and income growth significantly above initial guidance and broad-based expansion across specialty insurance lines. The company executed two acquisitions in 2025 and closed a third, Gray Surety, in January 2026, integrating new verticals and redefining reportable segments to reflect its evolving portfolio. Palomar plans to grow crop premium by more than 30%, shift to higher retention, and deploy technology—including AI—towards underwriting, operational efficiency, and scaling the organization. Changes in the business mix and increased crop retention are anticipated to raise loss ratios and expense ratios, but operating leverage and continued investment in talent are expected to support long-term adjusted return on equity above 20%.

  • Management stated, "We scaled our newer verticals in casualty and crop while maintaining underwriting discipline," highlighting operational progress and segment diversification.
  • The 2026 adjusted net income guidance assumes "a $10,000,000 catastrophe load and a decrease of 10% on our excess of loss property catastrophe reinsurance renewal on June 1," combining exposure growth with lower costs.
  • Chief Financial Officer Chris Uchida said, "Our 2025 adjusted net income more than doubled, technically 2.3 times, from 2023 in two years off of our goal of three to five years, with an ROE well above our target of 20%," underscoring accelerated achievement of key long-term goals.
  • Following restructuring, "fronting is no longer a focus of the business," and existing programs will be recategorized into relevant core lines, consolidating the company's specialty insurance platform.
  • Management emphasized that even with diversification and increased retention, "we are going to be generating an ROE that is in excess of 20%, and we are going to be growing our bottom line at a very attractive rate," reflecting confidence in the sustainability of earnings and capital efficiency.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • E&S: Excess and Surplus Lines; insurance written by non-admitted carriers for unique or higher-risk needs outside the standard market.
  • XOL: Excess of Loss; a form of reinsurance that covers losses above a specific retention limit.
  • IBNR: Incurred But Not Reported; reserves established for claims that have been incurred but not yet reported to the insurer.
  • D&O: Directors and Officers Liability; insurance for company managers against claims due to actions taken in their roles.
  • MPCI: Multi-Peril Crop Insurance; federal insurance program covering multiple types of crop risks.
  • SRA: Standard Reinsurance Agreement; federal contract establishing terms for crop insurers and the USDA.
  • SOFR: Secured Overnight Financing Rate; a reference interest rate for U.S. dollar-denominated derivatives and loans.
