Takeaways

  • Bookings -- Full-year bookings exceeded $7.0 billion, up over 40%, with Q4 bookings increasing more than 50% and nearly matching the entire annual total from two years prior.
  • New Product Bookings -- AIR, AI, and Fūsus booked over $1.0 billion, nearly tripling the previous year’s result, and the AI Era Plan represented about $750 million or approximately 10% of total bookings.
  • International Bookings -- Annual international bookings surpassed $1.0 billion for the first time, driven by two large European cloud deployments and broad regional momentum.
  • Corrections Vertical -- The corrections team delivered the largest single-customer booking in company history, including TASER 10, Body 4, and AI features.
  • Revenue -- Quarterly revenue grew 39% to $797 million, marking the eighth straight quarter and fourth consecutive year of revenue growth above 30%.
  • Software and Services Revenue -- Grew by 40% year over year to $343 million, with contributions from Fūsus, AI, VR, CounterDrone, and evidence management.
  • Connected Devices Revenue -- Increased 38% to $454 million; TASER revenue reached $264 million up 32%; Personal sensors contributed $109 million, up 28%; and platform solutions grew 81% to $81 million.
  • Net Revenue Retention (NRR) -- Expanded to 125%, signaling strong customer adoption of new products.
  • Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) -- Exceeded $1.3 billion, up 35% year over year.
  • Adjusted Gross Margin -- Reached 61.1%, down sequentially, affected by tariffs and product mix, but offset by software strength.
  • Adjusted Operating Expenses -- Were $1.1 billion, up $245 million sequentially, but declined as a percentage of revenue from 39.2% to 38.2% year over year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Increased 46% to $206 million, with a margin of 25.9%, exceeding internal expectations.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- Delivered $217 million, with free cash flow conversion temporarily below the 60% adjusted EBITDA target due to inventory and collections timing.
  • 2026 Revenue Growth Guidance -- Projected at 27%-30%, the highest outlook entering any year, with adjusted EBITDA margin forecasted at 25.5%.
  • Software as Portion of Revenue -- Software comprised 43% of total business.
  • 2028 Financial Targets -- Revenue goal is about $6.0 billion, with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 28% and free cash flow conversion target of 60%.
  • Stock-based Compensation Dilution -- Commitment to less than 2.5% annual dilution going forward, lowering from under 3% previously.
  • Future Contracted Bookings Visibility -- Between 20%-25% of booked value expected to convert to revenue the next year, supporting long-term guidance consistency.
  • M&A Activity -- Completed Prepared acquisition in Q4 and closed Carbyne in early 2026; both had immaterial revenue or EBITDA impact in the reporting period.
  • Product Pipeline -- Apollo DART, described as a technical breakthrough for TASER delivery, is entering field tests and targeted for customer deployments in the next cold season.
  • Rule of 40 Performance -- Historically sustained performance above 50, recently surpassing 55, and model implies ongoing operation at these levels.

Summary

Axon Enterprise (AXON 1.27%) reported a material acceleration in bookings and revenue across hardware and software, underpinned by rapid adoption of emerging AI-driven solutions and significant new wins in corrections and international markets. Management outlined clear guidance for continued strong top-line growth and stable margins, citing visibility from contracted bookings and expanding product suite, while reiterating company-specific execution goals and investment focus. Strategic acquisitions (Prepared, Carbyne) and a broadened customer mix are positioned as ecosystem enablers rather than near-term financial contributors, with operational leverage and software-led expansion supporting margin targets through 2028.

  • Management emphasized that only about 30% of customers are on premium subscription plans, leaving ample runway for upsell and ARPU growth.
  • Product mix shift and AI solutions led to software outpacing hardware growth, setting up a durable tailwind in gross margin over time.
  • International deal flow, particularly outside Commonwealth countries, was called an inflection point, but management stated additional follow-through is required for sustainability.
  • AI Era Plan is estimated at $750 million in bookings, with growing adoption across premium offerings, yet not all new features are deployed across the existing base at initial launch.
  • Management stated, "We are seeing broad-based demand and we are seeing it at increasing scale in a lot of places," highlighting expansion into enterprise, federal, and new verticals such as retail and healthcare.
  • Future margin and revenue guidance incorporate expectations for global tariff headwinds, inflationary component costs, and increased mix from emerging solutions, but maintain confidence due to product adoption trends and backlog.
  • Leadership asserted a strategic intent to embed AI natively into all workflows, leveraging an integrated hardware-software ecosystem as a competitive moat.
  • On risk management, executive comments stressed privacy and data handling as existential concerns, with significant investment in privacy-by-design cited as a customer acquisition driver.
  • Company continues to benchmark business quality against Rule of 40, with sustained output above 50 considered a model validation.

Industry glossary

  • Bookings: Value of customer orders signed within a reporting period, reflecting future revenue under contract.
  • Net Revenue Retention (NRR): Percentage measure showing recurring revenue preserved and expanded from existing customers after churn and upsells.
  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): Total value of recurring revenues on an annualized basis at a given point in time.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, excluding certain items to reflect normalized operating profitability.
  • AI Era Plan: Axon's premium subscription offering bundling advanced AI-driven capabilities across cloud and device platforms.
  • Fūsus: Cloud-based real-time crime center software platform, acquired to enhance situational awareness and camera integration.
  • ALPR: Automated license plate recognition technology within Axon's V-Intelligence product line.
  • Platform Solutions: Integrated product suites offering combined hardware and software for mission-critical workflows.
  • Rule of 40: SaaS industry benchmark where the sum of growth rate and profit margin exceeds 40%.
  • DEDICATED DRONE PRODUCTS (Dedrone, CounterDrone): Axon's counter-drone technology for threat detection and neutralization.
  • PSAP: Public Safety Answering Point, the call center responsible for answering 911 calls.
