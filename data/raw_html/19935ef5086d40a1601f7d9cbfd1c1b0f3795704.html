TAKEAWAYS

  • Core Net New Assets (NNA) -- $519 billion, representing a 42% annual increase, with an organic growth rate of 5.1%.
  • New Brokerage Accounts -- 4.7 million accounts opened, a 13% year-over-year rise, contributing to more than 46 million total client accounts.
  • Managed Investing Net Flows -- Grew 36% to nearly $70 billion, reaching a record high and nearly quadrupling since 2022.
  • Bank Lending Balances -- Increased to an all-time high of $58 billion, up 28% year over year, with pledged asset line originations growing 85% over the prior year.
  • Client Assets -- Surpassed $12 trillion, an 18% annual growth rate, ranking first among peers in total client assets, RIA custodial assets, and daily average trades.
  • Total Net Revenues -- Reached a record $23.9 billion for the year, up 22% from 2024.
  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share -- Hit a record $4.87, increasing 50% year over year and exceeding the upper end of previously updated guidance ranges.
  • Fourth-Quarter Total Revenue -- Achieved $6.3 billion, up 19% year over year, with net interest revenue growing 25% and trading revenue rising 22%.
  • Adjusted Pretax Profit Margin -- Reached just over 52% in the fourth quarter and 50% for the full year, representing an expansion of nearly 800 basis points.
  • Client Trading Activity -- Daily average trades of $8.3 million in the fourth quarter, marking the second-highest quarterly level, and $7.7 million daily average trades for the year, both record figures.
  • Cash Balances -- Quarter-end cash reached $453.7 billion, with a sequential increase of $28.1 billion, or approximately 7%, fueled by over $26 billion of December inflows and seasonal trends.
  • Adjusted Tier 1 Leverage Ratio -- Ended the year at 7.1%, above the company’s 6.75%-7% objective, even after $2.7 billion in common share repurchases during the quarter.
  • Total Capital Return -- $11.8 billion returned to shareholders over the year through various forms, including repurchases and dividends.
  • 2026 Financial Scenario Guidance -- Forecasts total revenue growth of 9.5%-10.5%, full-year organic asset growth around 5%, and net interest margin between 2.85%-2.95%.
  • Expense Planning -- Projected 5.5%-6.5% year over year annual expense growth, aligned with strategic investments and macroeconomic factors.
  • Expected 2026 Adjusted EPS -- Scenario implies $5.70-$5.80, suggesting upper-teen percentage earnings growth.
  • AI and Efficiency -- Over 220 AI use cases adopted, contributing to a 20% reduction in cost per account over five years and bringing expense on client assets (EOCA) from 15 basis points in 2020 to about 11 basis points currently.
  • Alternatives Platform Expansion -- Alternatives offerings, including the soon-to-close acquisition of Forge and investment in Qapita, target higher net worth clients, with less than 40% of RIA clients currently allocated to alternatives.
  • Client Demographic Trends -- One third of new retail accounts opened by Gen Z, nearly 60% of new retail clients under 40, and average retail client age now in the 40s.
  • Trading and Crypto -- About half of new-to-firm retail clients initiated access to thinkorswim, with spot Bitcoin and Ethereum trading planned for launch within the year; Schwab clients represent a roughly 20% share of spot crypto ETPs.

SUMMARY

The Charles Schwab Corporation (SCHW 2.54%) reported record annual growth across core business lines, with leadership emphasizing both expanded client engagement and revenue diversification. Management highlighted that institutional and retail business segments each delivered strong net new asset growth, with the company maintaining industry-leading status in assets and daily trade volume. Executives signaled ongoing momentum for 2026, supporting a scenario of high-single to low-double-digit revenue growth, organic asset expansion, and further margin improvement based on continued efficiency gains and strategic initiatives.

  • Verdeschi stated, "this full year scenario implies potential adjusted earnings of around the $5.70 to $5.80 range, which would represent year-over-year earnings growth in the upper teens."
  • Wurster described strong engagement among former Ameritrade clients, noting they contributed between "1/3 and 40%" of flows to wealth and lending solutions following a challenging transition period.
  • Verdeschi explained, "We have continued to build out these capabilities in the early days of 2026 by putting in place a modest amount of income hedges against our margin loan book."
  • The company is investing in broadening its suite of wealth management, lending, and alternative investment offerings to attract affluent and Gen Z clients, aiming to further increase account penetration rates and asset consolidation.
  • Scenario planning assumed 225 basis points in Fed funds rate cuts and 6.5% equity market returns, while explicitly noting that capital return levels may fluctuate but remain "a meaningful part" of their financial growth narrative.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • NNA (Net New Assets): The dollar value of new client assets brought to the platform minus client asset outflows for the period, reflecting organic growth.
  • EOCA (Expense on Client Assets): Operating expenses as a percentage of total client assets, used to benchmark cost efficiency in wealth management platforms.
  • PAL (Pledged Asset Line): A flexible, non-purpose securities-based lending facility allowing clients to borrow against the value of their investment holdings.
  • RIA (Registered Investment Adviser): An advisor or firm providing fiduciary investment advice, often utilizing Schwab's Advisor Services platform for custody and other support.
  • ETP (Exchange-Traded Product): A public investment vehicle, such as ETFs or ETNs, tracking an index or asset—including crypto—traded on exchanges.
