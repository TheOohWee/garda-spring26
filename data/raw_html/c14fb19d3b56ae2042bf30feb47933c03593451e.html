Pool Corp (POOL) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 12:34PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 19, 2026 at 11 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Peter D. Arvan
  • Senior Vice President & Chief Financial Officer — Melanie M. Hart

TAKEAWAYS

  • Annual Revenue -- $5.3 billion, unchanged year over year with maintenance demand providing stability amid weaker new pool construction.
  • Fourth Quarter Sales -- $982 million, down 1% compared to the same quarter last year, a period with elevated hurricane-related demand.
  • Gross Margin -- 29.7% for the year, up 20 basis points after adjustment for one-time items; fourth quarter reached 30.1%, rising 70 basis points year over year.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $530 million distributed in cash, a 10% increase, including $341 million in share repurchases and a 4% increase in quarterly dividend.
  • Regional Sales Trends -- Florida declined 2% for the year and 9% in Q4; Texas fell 3% yearly but gained 1% in Q4; California down 3% year, 4% quarter; Arizona flat for the year; Europe grew 4% locally in Q4, marking the first such increase in three years.
  • Segment Performance -- Chemical sales declined 1% for the year; building materials flat annually but up 4% in Q4; commercial pool products up 3% for the year; equipment sales flat excluding cleaners for the year and down 3% in Q4; sales to independent retailers fell 3% for the year.
  • Digital Initiatives -- Digital sales accounted for 15% of total annual revenue and reached a record 17% during the pool season; POOL360 Unlocked and artificial intelligence features expanded network access.
  • Sales Center Expansion -- Eight new locations opened and three acquired, totaling 456 sales centers at year end; 10 additional Pinch A Penny franchise stores launched.
  • Inventory -- Year-end inventory was $1.45 billion, up 13% due to early purchases intended to mitigate forecasted cost increases for 2026 season.
  • Debt and Leverage -- Total debt increased by $249 million to $1.2 billion, with year-end leverage ratio at 1.67, aligning with company’s 1.5 to 2 target range.
  • Operating Expenses -- Fourth quarter expenses rose $14 million, or 6% year over year; full-year operating expenses increased 3.5%, driven partly by technology spending and new locations.
  • Cash Flow -- Cash flow from operations reached $366 million (90% of net income) but would have been 107% of net income excluding deferred tax payments tied to hurricane-impacted regions.
  • EPS and Guidance -- Full-year diluted EPS was $10.85 versus $11.30 last year; guidance for 2026 EPS is $10.85 to $11.15 with no projected ASU tax benefit.
  • Product Mix -- Maintenance accounted for 64% of sales, renovation/remodel for 22%, and new construction for 14% in 2025.
  • 2026 Outlook -- Management expects low single-digit net sales growth with gross margin similar to 2025; planned capital expenditures represent 1%-1.5% of net sales and $25 million to $50 million for acquisitions.

RISKS

  • Management stated, "we are still awaiting more positive consumer spending," reflecting continued uncertainty and muted recovery in discretionary sales channels.
  • Fourth quarter operating income declined to $52 million from $61 million, with diluted EPS dropping to $0.85 from $0.98 as incremental technology investments and self-insured medical costs raised expenses.
  • Inventory increased 13% year over year, "In anticipation of estimated cost increases for certain products," increasing working capital requirements and funding needs.
  • 2026 guidance indicates, "be partially offset by the one-time increase in incentive compensation," suggesting expense pressures will limit margin expansion if growth stalls.

SUMMARY

Pool Corporation (POOL +0.66%) delivered stable full-year sales as maintenance demand offset continued weakness in new pool construction and discretionary categories. Gross margin rose both annually and in the fourth quarter due to disciplined pricing and supply chain benefits, despite elevated operating expenses associated with technology, new facilities, and higher medical costs. Early inventory purchases expanded working capital, with management anticipating modest benefits to margins early in the year but normal seasonality thereafter. The company grew its physical and digital network, adding new sales centers and achieving record digital penetration, and projects low single-digit growth and consistent gross margin for 2026 based on a continuation of current market conditions. Debt and leverage increased to support high inventory, expansion, and shareholder distributions, with interest and incentive compensation flagged as expense headwinds in coming quarters.

  • Management noted, "Maintenance activity overall for the year was positive," highlighting the resilience of this core business line amid broader construction declines.
  • Digital connectivity and share of sales reached record levels following the launch of POOL360 Unlocked and artificial intelligence features.
  • Management plans to limit new store openings in 2026 (expecting no more than eight), focusing on extracting operating leverage from prior capacity investments.
  • anticipate vendor cost increases and corresponding pricing pass-throughs to result in a 1% to 2% pricing benefit. but with no significant increase in pool construction or remodel expected in 2026.
  • Florida’s two-year Q4 sales stack remained positive despite 2025’s post-hurricane sales decline, and Texas "showed early signs of recovery late in the year" with a Q4 sales uptick.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • POOL360: Pool Corporation’s proprietary digital ordering and customer engagement platform, now expanded with POOL360 Unlocked and AI-powered features.
  • Pinch A Penny: Franchise network of retail pool supply stores, a significant sales channel for Pool Corporation, particularly in Florida.
  • Greenfield Locations: Newly constructed sales centers not acquired via M&A, central to Pool Corporation’s geographic expansion strategy.
  • Focus List: Company-defined set of underperforming sales centers receiving targeted improvement initiatives.
  • Horizon: Pool Corporation’s irrigation supply distribution brand, linked primarily to new housing construction and thus sensitive to housing cycles.
  • Prozone: In-branch initiative designed to educate builders and service professionals on advanced pool equipment and automation technologies.
  • ASU Benefit: Tax benefit arising from Accounting Standards Update, impacting effective tax rate and diluted EPS reported figures.
