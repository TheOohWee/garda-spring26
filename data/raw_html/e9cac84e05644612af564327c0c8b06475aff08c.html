Sanmina (SANM) Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 10:38AM EST

Date

Monday, November 4, 2024 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Executive Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Jure Sola
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Jon D. Faust
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Paige Melching

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $2.02 billion, up 9.6% sequentially and exceeding the outlook of $1.9 billion to $2.0 billion.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 8.7%, at the high end of the outlook and up 20 basis points sequentially, with improvement attributed to favorable mix in the CPS segment.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses -- $68.2 million, higher than outlook due to targeted investments in research and development, strategic opportunities, and workforce.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Profit -- $107 million, representing 5.3% of revenue; operating margin is flat sequentially and within the short-term target of 5%-6%.
  • Non-GAAP Diluted EPS -- $1.43, based on approximately 56 million shares outstanding, exceeding the high end of the $1.30 to $1.40 outlook with a 14% sequential improvement and a 1% improvement year over year.
  • IMS Segment Revenue -- $1.63 billion, up 10.1% sequentially; non-GAAP gross margin was 7.3%, down 30 basis points sequentially due to unfavorable mix.
  • CPS Segment Revenue -- $418 million, up 7.6% sequentially; non-GAAP gross margin was 13.6%, up 210 basis points sequentially and 280 basis points year over year.
  • Cash and Liquidity -- Cash and cash equivalents of $626 million with no outstanding borrowings on an $800 million revolver, totaling approximately $1.5 billion in liquidity.
  • Inventory -- $1.33 billion at quarter-end, down 4% sequentially; inventory turns improved to 5.4x from 4.9x in the prior quarter.
  • Non-GAAP Pre-Tax ROIC -- 23%, up from 21.1% in the prior quarter and well above the company’s weighted average cost of capital.
  • Leverage -- Gross leverage ratio at 0.51x with no net debt, among the lowest in the industry.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $29 million for the quarter and $231 million for the year, an increase of $186 million year over year.
  • Shareholder Returns -- Repurchased 945,000 shares in the quarter for $65 million; 4 million shares repurchased for $227 million in the year, representing nearly 100% of free cash flow returned to shareholders.
  • Segment Revenue Composition -- Industrial, medical, defense, aerospace, and automotive segments accounted for $1.253 billion, representing 62% of quarterly revenue and up 6% quarter over quarter; communication networks and cloud infrastructure contributed $765 million, or 38% of revenue, up 16% quarter over quarter.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio -- 1:1 for the quarter, indicating balanced order intake and shipments.
  • Fiscal 2025 Guidance -- Revenue outlook of $1.925 billion to $2.025 billion; non-GAAP gross margin of 8.4%-8.8%; operating expenses of $62 million to $66 million; non-GAAP operating margin of 5.3%-5.7%; non-GAAP EPS of $1.30-$1.40; capital expenditures projected around $30 million.
  • Growth Outlook -- Management forecasts high-single-digit revenue growth and expects non-GAAP EPS to outpace revenue growth in fiscal 2025.

Summary

Sanmina (SANM +4.17%) reported sequential growth across most end markets and described fiscal 2024 as a transition year marked by inventory normalization initiatives with customers. Management confirmed disciplined execution with focused investments in R&D, strategic opportunities, and workforce expansion contributing to higher non-GAAP operating expenses. The company highlighted improved liquidity, increased inventory turns, and a strengthened return on invested capital while maintaining no net debt and an active capital return policy. Executives outlined a customer-centric strategy for fiscal 2025, emphasizing targeted program wins and margin expansion from both new projects and operational leverage. Management stated, "we expect fiscal year '25 to be a growth year for us," and noted preparation for increased exposure to high-growth segments such as AI-driven communications and cloud infrastructure, advanced manufacturing for defense, and emerging opportunities in energy, electric vehicles, and healthcare.

  • Management stated that the India joint venture is "ahead of the game" with increased investment and stronger-than-expected performance, expanding export capabilities and market position in the region.
  • Executives detailed ongoing adoption of artificial intelligence internally for operational improvement and externally as a driver of new customer growth, particularly in advanced printed circuit boards and high-performance optical networks.
  • Sanmina's capital allocation priorities remain focused on organic investments, selective strategic acquisitions, and continued share repurchases, with "need to do anything crazy" regarding debt or acquisitions, according to management comments.
  • The company identified improving cash conversion cycles and further inventory optimization as key future levers to enhance free cash flow and profitability.
  • Sanmina confirmed no single customer contributed over 10% of revenue in the quarter, underscoring diversification efforts and reduced customer concentration risk.

Industry glossary

  • IMS (Integrated Manufacturing Solutions): Sanmina business segment providing design, engineering, assembly, and test services for complete products and subassemblies across key industries.
  • CPS (Components, Products and Services): Sanmina business segment focused on high-margin, specialized components, advanced interconnect systems, and subassemblies, including optical modules, printed circuit boards, and high-performance systems.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: An industry metric indicating demand balance, where a ratio of 1:1 reflects parity between orders received and products shipped within the period.
  • Inventory Turns: The number of times inventory is sold and replaced during a period, used as a measure of operational efficiency and inventory management.
  • Pre-Tax ROIC (Return on Invested Capital): A profitability metric measuring the return generated on capital invested in the business, stated before consideration of taxes.
  • Viking Enterprise Solution: Sanmina business unit providing complete design, assembly, and integration solutions for server and storage systems, frequently used in cloud data centers.
