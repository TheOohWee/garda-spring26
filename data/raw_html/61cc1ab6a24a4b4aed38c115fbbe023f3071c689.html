Carter's (CRI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 27, 2026 at 9:47AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, February 27, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Douglas C. Palladini
  • Chief Financial Officer — Richard F. Westenberger

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $925,000,000 for the quarter, up 8%, or up 3% on a comparable 13-week basis excluding the 53rd week.
  • Gross Margin -- 43.2%, down 460 basis points, impacted by approximately $40,000,000 in tariff costs and higher product investments.
  • Adjusted SG&A -- $315,000,000, up 5%, with increases driven by the 53rd week, demand creation, inflationary wage and rent, and higher performance-based compensation.
  • Adjusted Operating Income -- $89,000,000 with a margin of 9.7%, a decrease of $26,000,000 and margin contraction versus 13.4% last year.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $1.90, compared to $2.39, as lower profitability offset top-line growth.
  • Tariff Impact -- Tariffs created a $60,000,000 headwind to 2025 profitability, rising to a projected $200,000,000-plus in 2026, mostly offset by mid single-digit pricing gains.
  • U.S. Retail Performance -- Segment net sales up 9%; third consecutive quarter of comp gains at 4.7%, led by double-digit ecommerce traffic and broad strength across brands and age groups.
  • U.S. Wholesale Performance -- Net sales rose 3%, with exclusive brand growth offsetting a decline in Simple Joys and margin pressure concentrated from tariffs and excess inventory sales.
  • International Performance -- Net sales increased 10%, or 8% on a constant currency basis; Mexico drove nearly 30% growth through new stores and double-digit comps.
  • Inventory -- Year-end net inventories stood at $545,000,000 (up 8%), but physical units down 4%, with incremental tariffs increasing stated inventory by $50,000,000.
  • Liquidity & Financing -- Year-end liquidity exceeded $1,000,000,000; $575,000,000 of new senior notes at 7.375% and a $750,000,000 asset-based credit facility extended debt profile and replaced previous borrowings.
  • Full-Year Operating Cash Flow -- $122,000,000; decline year over year due to lower earnings and higher inventory.
  • 2026 Outlook -- Net sales growth projected in the low to mid single digits and adjusted operating income growth at a similar pace, both back-half weighted; gross margin rate expected to decline modestly.
  • Segment Guidance -- U.S. Retail to grow low single digits with mid single-digit comps; U.S. Wholesale up mid single digits; International up mid single digits across Canada, Mexico, and international partners.
  • First Quarter 2026 Expectations -- Net sales up mid single digits; U.S. Retail to grow high single digits (mid single-digit comps); U.S. Wholesale down low single digits; International up double digits; adjusted operating income planned between $12,000,000 and $15,000,000; projected EPS of $0.02 to $0.08.
  • Cost Savings -- Workforce reduction in Q3 to yield $35,000,000 in 2026 cost savings; ~60 store closures planned in 2026 (totaling 150 by 2028), expected to lower fixed costs and enhance margins.
  • Pricing Mix/AUR -- Mid single-digit AUR increase planned for 2026, driven by reduced promotions, improved product mix, and higher acceptance of premium offerings, with Little Planet exceeding $100,000,000 in sales.
  • Productivity Initiatives -- Three-month faster development cycle, 20%-30% SKU reduction in key brands, and improved adoption of mainline products are intended to improve efficiency and assortment relevance.
  • Consumer Trends -- "Acceleration in acquisition of new consumers" skewing to higher-income cohorts, supporting premium positioning and lifetime value.
  • CapEx -- 2026 capital expenditures planned at $55,000,000, focused on new Mexican stores, distribution upgrades, and technology.
  • 2026 Cash Flow -- Operating cash flow expected between $110,000,000 and $120,000,000.

RISKS

  • Richard F. Westenberger stated, "Tariffs continue to dominate the headlines," and the gross impact of higher tariffs on our P&L in 2025 was approximately $60,000,000. with 2026 exposure projected at more than $200,000,000, creating sustained margin pressure that may only be partially offset by price increases.
  • Rising interest expense will impact 2026 EPS by approximately $0.30 due to late-2025 refinancing, with total net interest expense expected just under $40,000,000.
  • Management flagged a step-up in the effective tax rate to 22% in 2026 (from 19%), raising tax expense due to Hong Kong tax policy change and a greater mix of U.S. income.
  • Retail and Wholesale profitability declined due to "higher product costs reflecting incremental tariff pressure and product investments," only partially offset by pricing actions.

SUMMARY

Management emphasized that all business segments delivered year-over-year sales increases, with U.S. Retail and International outperforming through comp gains and geographic expansion. Consolidated earnings per share and margins declined, offsetting top-line momentum, with both executives citing extraordinary tariff and cost pressures. The company’s 2026 plan focuses on productivity, demand creation, and premium positioning to preserve margins despite persistent cost headwinds, with positive comp momentum in DTC and e-commerce highlighted as key tailwinds for segment growth.

  • Management projects 2026 profitability improvement in the second half, as gross margin pressures lessen with greater pricing capture and mix optimization.
  • Store closures and organizational streamlining are expected to be accretive to profitability by reducing fixed overhead while shifting sales to more productive channels.
  • New debt refinancing introduces higher annual interest costs and cash flow outlays, partially offset by extended maturities and improved liquidity.
  • The company is not including potential positive impacts from evolving tariff policy in 2026 guidance, citing continued regulatory uncertainty and lag effects on inventory and margins.
  • The acquisition of higher-income, higher-lifetime-value consumers is seen as validating ongoing pricing and product mix strategies designed to build brand equity and loyalty.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AUR (Average Unit Retail): The average selling price per unit, reflecting realized pricing power and promotional discipline.
  • SG&A (Selling, General and Administrative Expenses): Operating expenses not directly tied to cost of goods, including payroll, rent, marketing, and overhead.
  • Tariffs: Government-imposed taxes on imported goods, which increase product costs and affect gross margin when passed through the income statement.
  • Comps (Comparable Sales): Year-over-year sales changes for stores or channels open in both periods, a key metric for evaluating organic growth.
