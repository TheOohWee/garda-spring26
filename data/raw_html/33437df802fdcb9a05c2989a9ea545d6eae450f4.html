DoorDash (DASH) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 6:27PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Nov. 5, 2025, 8:41 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Co-Founder, Chair, and Chief Executive Officer — Tony Xu
  • Chief Financial Officer — Ravi Inukonda
  • Head of Investor Relations — Weston Twigg

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Growth Acceleration -- Growth accelerated for the fourth consecutive quarter, with management emphasizing improved unit economics and increasing profit dollars.
  • 2026 Major Investment Areas -- The company identified three principal 2026 investment priorities: building a single global tech platform, developing new products including autonomous delivery and CRM tools, and expanding DashMart Fulfillment Services for increased retailer delivery capability.
  • EBITDA Margin Outlook -- CFO Ravi Inukonda stated, "the EBITDA margin for the existing business, including the investments, think of that as the overall business excluding Deliveroo, I would expect those margins to be up slightly compared to 2025."
  • Deliveroo Integration -- Management reported the Deliveroo acquisition resulted in a business "in better shape than we had expected," driving double-digit growth and supporting continued product improvement and investment.
  • Deliveroo Unit Economics and EBITDA -- Ravi Inukonda specified, "the $200 million, think of that as the contribution from Deliveroo to the overall EBITDA," including investments in product, selection, quality, and personnel.
  • Advertising Business Growth -- CEO Tony Xu highlighted, "ads business in history to get to $1 billion of annualized revenues," with "extra budgets wanting to spend more on the Dash platform than we kind of give ad space to."
  • New Verticals Performance -- The new verticals segment reported increasing order frequency, growing basket size, and ongoing unit economics improvement both sequentially and year-over-year.
  • U.S. Marketplace Momentum -- The U.S. business achieved four consecutive quarters of strengthening performance on an expanding base, with increasing monthly active user penetration and record levels of DashPass and Wolt+ subscribers.
  • Tech Platform Investment Timing -- CEO Tony Xu explained that the rise in tech investment in 2026 is due to actual build-out, with "extra kind of temporary cloud instances" driving expense as they maintain multiple tech stacks.
  • DashMart Fulfillment Strategy -- The DashMart Fulfillment Services offering aims for "near-perfect accuracy" through direct inventory management and enables same-hour or same-day delivery for a wide range of retail partners.

SUMMARY

DoorDash (DASH 1.27%) delivered a data-rich earnings call emphasizing consecutive growth acceleration, improved unit economics, and broad reinvestment strategies targeting long-term scalability. A central focus was the transition to a single global tech platform to drive operating leverage, AI integration, and accelerated feature deployment across markets. Management highlighted the strategic value and competitive momentum gained through the Deliveroo acquisition, with rapid double-digit growth exceeding expectations and substantiated by a $200 million EBITDA contribution. The company continues to expand new products, including autonomous delivery and DashMart Fulfillment Services, and is experiencing record levels of engagement and penetration in core U.S. and international markets. The advertising business reached $1 billion in annualized revenues, underscoring sustained demand and high incremental margins. Investments in 2026 are determined to be broad-based, with margin discipline and a stated goal of increasing EBITDA margin, excluding Deliveroo, despite these elevated investments.

  • Management confirmed that "unit economics continue to improve" for new verticals, and anticipate breakeven through scale and ongoing product quality enhancements.
  • The company is seeing a step change in growth across categories beyond restaurants, with categories such as pets, electronics, health and beauty, and home improvement all cited for notable traction and seasonal surges.
  • Payback periods on investment remain "the same," with the accelerated number of projects attributed to more experiments achieving product-market fit and warranting further investment.
  • The company's approach to Deliveroo integration is to protect performance by enabling standalone operation on legacy systems during tech stack migration, which temporarily elevates costs but preserves user and merchant experience.
  • Ravi Inukonda clarified an "USD 8 million to USD 10 million impact or an expense to EBITDA" due to accounting differences moving from Deliveroo's to DoorDash's definition of EBITDA.
  • Growing demand from retail and grocery partners has moved DoorDash into a leadership position in order volume and customer acquisition in grocery delivery.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • GOV (Gross Order Value): The total dollar value of transactions on the platform before deductions such as discounts, refunds, and adjustments.
  • Unit Economics: Financial metrics representing revenue and cost on a per-order or per-customer basis, used to assess business profitability at the transaction level.
  • DashMart Fulfillment Services: DoorDash's fulfillment solution offering end-to-end inventory management and delivery for retail partners, aiming for high accuracy and fast delivery speed.
  • DashPass: DoorDash's membership program providing customers with benefits such as discounted delivery fees and exclusive offers.
  • Wolt+: Membership program for the Wolt platform, offering similar advantages as DashPass for international markets.
  • EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization; a widely used measure of profitability.
