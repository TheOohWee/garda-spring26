Enbridge (ENB) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 6:46PM EST

Friday, Nov. 7, 2025 at 12:00 a.m. ET

Risks

  • EPS decline -- Patrick Murray reported earnings per share decreased from $0.55 to $0.46, attributing this to the typical seasonal profile of gas utilities where "Q3 tends to be a softer quarter for EPS as EBITDA is seasonally lower, but items such as interest and depreciation remained flat quarter-over-quarter."
  • Mid-Con and U.S. Gulf Coast liquids segment weakness -- Patrick Murray noted that "contributions from the Mid-Con and U.S. Gulf Coast segment are tracking lower due to tighter differentials and strong PADD II refining demand," with these headwinds expected to persist into the fourth quarter.
  • Flat DCF per share -- Higher financing and maintenance costs from the Enbridge Gas North Carolina acquisition, along with increased interest rates, resulted in flat DCF per share compared to last year.

Takeaways

  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Achieved a record third quarter adjusted EBITDA, increasing by $66 million compared to the previous year, driven by contributions from U.S. gas utilities and organic growth in gas transmission.
  • Debt-to-EBITDA ratio -- Reported quarter-end debt-to-EBITDA at 4.8x, staying within the target range of 4.5x to 5.0x.
  • Mainline throughput -- Liquids mainline transported approximately 3.1 million barrels per day, a third quarter record, attributed to strong demand and high asset utilization.
  • Secured capital additions -- Added $3 billion of new growth capital to the secured capital program, including the Southern Illinois Connector and expansions in gas storage, pipeline, and CO2 sequestration projects.
  • Gas storage expansion -- Sanctioned expansions at Egan and Moss Bluff storage for a combined 23 Bcf of capacity and planned expansion of Aitken Creek with 40 Bcf, with half contracted long-term.
  • Power project execution -- Four renewable projects totaling over 2 gigawatts are backed by agreements with Amazon and Meta, with Fox Squirrel and Orange Grove operational, Sequoia Solar entering service in 2026, and Clear Fork in 2027.
  • Rate settlements -- Positive settlements reached for Enbridge Gas North Carolina (approved interim rate effective Nov. 1, allowed return on equity of 9.65% and $34 million revenue requirement increase) and Enbridge Gas, Utah (revenue requirement increase of $62 million with rates to begin Jan. 1, 2026), supporting growth and quick cycle capital returns.
  • Dividend growth commitment -- Maintained a growing dividend within a 60%-70% DCF payout target for 30 consecutive years, underpinned by contracted cash flows and low-risk model.
  • 2025 guidance reaffirmed -- Remains on track for full-year adjusted EBITDA in the upper half of the $19.4 billion to $20 billion guidance range, and adjusted DCF per share near the midpoint of the $5.50 to $5.90 range.
  • Capacity expansion projects -- Mainline optimization Phase 1 (adding 150,000 barrels per day) near FID, targeted for service in 2027; Phase 2 (250,000 barrels per day) proceeding with Energy Transfer as partner, expected service in 2028, both utilizing existing infrastructure.
  • Capital allocation limits -- Gregory L. Ebel and Patrick Murray repeated their commitment to retain annual growth investment within the $9 billion to $10 billion envelope, emphasizing disciplined prioritization of brownfield projects.
  • Customer credit quality -- Over 95% of customers are investment-grade, supporting revenue stability and risk mitigation.

Summary

Enbridge (ENB 0.26%) delivered record adjusted EBITDA in the third quarter, advancing multiple secured growth projects while maintaining leverage within its target range. The company added $3 billion to its secured capital backlog, with new investments in U.S. Gulf Coast storage, pipeline expansions, and carbon sequestration, reinforcing management’s expectation to achieve the upper half of annual EBITDA guidance. Positive regulatory outcomes in its utility businesses will support upcoming revenue and further capital recycling. Management confirmed ongoing expansion in renewables, with new and nearly operational projects secured by long-term agreements with major technology clients. New mainline optimization phases are designed to increase liquids egress using existing assets and partnerships, enhancing system flexibility for customer growth. No change was signaled in the dividend policy or disciplined capital program, and the 2025 outlook was reaffirmed.

Industry glossary

  • Mainline optimization (MLO): Incremental expansion projects to increase capacity of Enbridge’s primary crude oil pipelines using existing infrastructure and permit-light brownfield enhancements.
  • PADD II: Petroleum Administration for Defense District II, the U.S. Midwest regional refining and distribution area influencing regional crude demand and pipeline flows.
  • Bcf: Billion cubic feet (of natural gas), a volume metric frequently used for pipeline and storage capacity disclosures.
  • FID (Final investment decision): The company’s formal board commitment to allocate capital and commence full project execution based on regulatory and customer milestones.
  • Take-or-pay agreement: A contractual arrangement obligating customers to pay for a minimum volume of service, even if unused, providing steady revenue assurance for infrastructure owners.
