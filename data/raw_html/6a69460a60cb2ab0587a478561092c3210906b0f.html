Watsco (WSO) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 17, 2026 at 11:23AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 17, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman and CEO — Albert Nahmad
  • President — A.J. Nahmad
  • Executive Vice President — Barry Logan
  • Executive Vice President and CFO — Paul Johnston
  • Chief Operating Officer — Rick Gomez

Takeaways

  • Gross margin -- Reported at 27.1% for the quarter, representing a 40 basis point increase due to double-digit pricing gains on new A2L products
  • Unit volumes -- Declined seventeen percent in 2025 as stated, with fourth quarter prior-year comparison up more than twenty percent, suggesting the drop reflected unusually strong historical comparables
  • Pricing -- "Our price benefit in '25 for the year was nine percent. For the fourth quarter eleven percent," driven by new product mix following regulatory transition
  • SG&A expense -- Decreased by two percent, including impacts from acquired and new locations, improving operating efficiency
  • Inventory -- $500 million reduction target met by year-end, aided by the conclusion of A2L transition and resulting in inventory levels at approximately eighteen percent-nineteen percent of trailing twelve months' sales, which aligns with the company’s stated ten-year average
  • Cash flow -- Generated record fourth quarter cash flow of $400 million and was debt-free throughout 2025
  • E-commerce penetration -- Accounted for thirty-five percent of sales overall, with some U.S. markets exceeding sixty percent
  • Digital platform activity -- OnCallAir, the company’s contractor digital selling platform, saw a twenty percent increase in gross merchandise value to $1.8 billion annualized and mobile app engagement rose fifteen percent to seventy-three thousand users
  • Dividend -- Annual dividend increased by ten percent to $13.20, marking the fifty-second consecutive year of payments and a payout that exceeds trailing twelve-month earnings per share, underpinned by robust cash flow
  • Parts & supplies segment -- Now represents about thirty percent of total sales, with a new initiative launched to compete more aggressively in this fragmented non-equipment market
  • Commercial & international sales -- Commercial product sales declined by high single digits in the quarter, while international remained weak for Latin America and modestly improved for Canada
  • Commodity products -- Products such as copper tubing and refrigerants account for roughly five percent of total revenue, exhibiting minimal price volatility over the year
  • Technology & AI investment -- Company is accelerating AI and pricing optimization initiatives with the stated goal of gross margins of thirty percent or greater over time

Risks

  • Paul Johnston stated, "the aftermarket and on replacement market was down six percent," attributing this to both channel disruption and a weaker consumer environment
  • Barry Logan noted, "It's down five percent or so in in the first part of the year," referencing January–February sales softness, partially attributed to severe weather but acknowledged as not indicative of peak season demand
  • Continued double-digit equipment unit declines, as referenced by management, may signal lingering demand weakness as the sector exits a period of regulatory transition
  • International operations, particularly in Latin America, experienced ongoing weakness, which management explicitly linked to geopolitical and tariff-related headwinds

Summary

Watsco (WSO +1.29%) closed the year with higher gross margins, robust cash generation, and a notable dividend increase, despite reporting a seventeen percent annual decline in unit volumes following regulatory and market turbulence linked to the A2L refrigerant transition. Management emphasized that e-commerce, technology adoption, and AI initiatives are increasingly central to driving future margin expansion and customer engagement. The company reported successful completion of a major inventory reduction program, bolstered by prudent working capital management and improved inventory positioning aligned with historical norms. Management described the current market environment as entering a phase of "normalization," with new product lines established and contractor readiness to support upcoming seasonal demand. Strategic initiatives in non-equipment segments, along with competitive positioning of commercial and digital offerings, are expected to support future growth and margin enhancement.

  • Management stated, "We continue to invest in innovation and technology that separate us from competitors," reflecting a long-term approach to technology-driven differentiation
  • Inventory turns are targeted to gradually return to pre-pandemic levels of five, up from the low threes, directly linking improved turnover with prospective cash flow growth
  • While replacement and aftermarket volumes contracted six percent, the parts business, specifically compressors and motors, achieved double-digit growth for the year despite a flat-to-down fourth quarter
  • OEM pricing actions and the cessation of legacy product transitions were critical in supporting pricing and margin discipline throughout the year
  • The dividend payout was justified by cash flow performance, even as it temporarily exceeded earnings per share, with management confirming that "Cash flow is actually how the dividend is paid and cash flow is probably closer to $16.18 dollars a share today"
  • The company does not anticipate significant disruptions from commodity price fluctuations since "precisely five percent of overall revenue" and is closely managed on a real-time basis

Industry glossary

  • A2L refrigerant: A new category of low-GWP, mildly flammable refrigerants mandated in recent HVAC regulatory changes, requiring different handling and installation practices
  • SEER: Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio, a standard measure of HVAC system energy efficiency
  • OEM: Original Equipment Manufacturer, referring to companies producing HVAC equipment distributed by Watsco
  • OnCallAir: Watsco’s proprietary digital sales platform used by contractors for configuring, quoting, and selling HVAC systems to homeowners
  • VCR: Internal initiative at Watsco focused on optimizing purchasing and redistribution strategies within the non-equipment product space to enhance gross margin performance
