Corcept (CORT) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 5:36PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Nov. 4, 2025 at 7:26 p.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Joseph K. Belanoff
  • Chief Business Officer — Gary Robb
  • President, Endocrinology — Sean Maduck

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $207.6 million in the third quarter of 2025, up from $182.5 million in the third quarter of 2024, driven by record Korlym tablet shipments and prescription growth.
  • Net income -- $19.7 million in the third quarter of 2025, down from $47.2 million in the third quarter of 2024.
  • Cash and investments -- $524 million at Sept. 30, 2025, after $50 million share buyback during the third quarter.
  • 2025 revenue guidance -- Raised to a range of $800 million to $850 million.
  • Korlym tablet shipments -- Increased 42.5%, reaching an all-time high for the quarter.
  • Patients on therapy -- Approximately 3,250 paying patients were on drug at the end of the quarter.
  • Authorized generic (AG) share -- In terms of our authorized generic in the second quarter, about two-thirds of our business were on the authorized generic. In the third quarter, it ended in the low 70s. Our expectation is by the end of the year, it might creep up a little bit, maybe ending at around 75%. In terms of the net, it's about a 30% discount to Korlym's list price.
  • Specialty pharmacy transition -- New pharmacy vendor (Curant) began onboarding Oct. 1 and is fully online; second and third specialty pharmacies to be added by early next year.
  • Sales force expansion -- Sales team increased to 150 clinical specialists, up from 60 at the start of the year.
  • Relacorilant NDA updates -- FDA PDUFA date set for Dec. 30, 2025 (hypercortisolism); platinum-resistant ovarian cancer PDUFA set for July 11, 2026; European MAA submitted in Oct. with decision expected by year-end 2026.
  • Patent litigation status -- Awaiting appeals court ruling in Teva patent case after Dec. 2023 trial court decision against Corcept; outcome could impact generic entry timeline.
  • R&D and SG&A spending outlook -- R&D expenses expected to remain flat year-over-year; SG&A expenses anticipated to increase in preparation for relacorilant launches and clinical expansion.
  • Gross margin outlook -- Management stated, "No, we have not seen that, and we don't expect that."

Summary

Corcept Therapeutics (CORT 0.80%) reported significant revenue growth and completed a major capital return via share repurchase. The patient base receiving Korlym reached record highs, and operational scale-up included a substantial sales force increase and specialty pharmacy expansion. Regulatory catalysts are approaching for relacorilant in both the U.S. and Europe, with management stating both late cycle and mid-cycle FDA review meetings have occurred as scheduled. Clinical pipeline momentum continues, with several pivotal trial readouts and new study initiations anticipated through 2026.

  • The company indicated that capacity constraints may continue for the next few months during the pharmacy transition, potentially affecting fulfillment pace.
  • Phase II DAZALS ALS results showed 84% reduction in risk of death at one year for patients on dazucorilant compared to placebo (p=0.0009), and a Phase III trial is planned for 2026.
  • Clinical trial pipeline milestones include upcoming MOMENTUM hypertension data, pivotal ROSELLA and BELLA trial updates in ovarian cancer, and MONARCH Phase IIb results in MASH, all expected through late 2026.
  • Sean Maduck projected relacorilant's future annual revenue from hypercortisolism could reach $3 billion to $5 billion, given current medical findings and anticipated market expansion.
  • Management stated that the authorized generic business segment is expected to expand to 75% of total volume by year-end, with consequent blended price impact.
  • Curant and Optime Care are both currently dispensing Korlym, ensuring continuity of supply during the transition until all patients migrate to the new specialty pharmacy partner.
  • Oncology pipeline expansion covers additional tumor types and combination regimens, with a particular focus on achieving guideline-relevant outcomes and new clinical study launches in solid tumors such as endometrial, cervical, and pancreatic cancers.
  • Nenocorilant, a new proprietary GR antagonist for oncology, enters clinical dosing in combination with PD-1 immunotherapy, with preclinical data expected for public release next year.
  • Management clarified no expected material change in gross margin outlook despite volume, pricing, or generic business mix shifts.

Industry glossary

  • Korlym: Corcept's commercial drug, mifepristone, used for Cushing's syndrome with hypercortisolism.
  • Relacorilant: An investigational selective cortisol modulator in late-stage clinical trials for hypercortisolism and multiple cancers.
  • PDUFA date: Prescription Drug User Fee Act date, the FDA deadline for action on a new drug application.
  • Authorized generic (AG): A generic version of a branded drug launched with the original manufacturer's approval, usually at a lower price point but identical formulation.
  • MAA: Marketing Authorization Application, EU regulatory submission for product approval.
  • GR antagonist: Glucocorticoid receptor antagonist, a drug class that blocks cortisol effects at its receptor, central to Corcept’s pipeline.
  • ROSELLA trial: Corcept’s Phase III study of relacorilant plus nab-paclitaxel in platinum-resistant ovarian cancer.
  • BELLA trial: Ongoing Phase II study testing relacorilant-based regimens in gynecologic cancers.
  • MONARCH trial: Phase IIb clinical trial testing miricorilant in patients with metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH).
  • DAZALS trial: Phase II trial evaluating dazucorilant in ALS patients.
  • CATALYST study: Corcept trial establishing prevalence and treatment potential of hypercortisolism, particularly in diabetes.
  • MOMENTUM trial: Study evaluating hypercortisolism prevalence among patients with resistant hypertension.

First, it's anti-apoptotic, so it blunts the effectiveness of chemotherapy. Second, it provides alternative growth pathways for prostate cancer tumors being treated with androgen deprivation medication. And third, it suppresses the immune system, reducing the effectiveness of immunotherapy in the treatment of cancer. Increasing apoptosis by blunting cortisol activity is a very broad platform. It applies to all solid tumors that express the glucocorticoid receptor.

Published research has shown that about 60% of solid tumors express the GR, and they do so at every stage of treatment. Our oncology program is built on the belief that antagonizing the effects of cortisol at the GR can benefit many, many more patients. And a prime example of that is shown by the women from our Phase II and pivotal Phase III ROSELLA studies who received relacorilant in addition to nab-paclitaxel and experienced extended progression-free survival and live longer than the women who were treated with just nab-pac alone.

Now remember, nab-pac is one of the most potent treatments for women with this disease and adding relacorilant led to an even better result and remarkably without adding to the safety burden of the women who received it.
