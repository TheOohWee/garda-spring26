Amgen (AMGN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 3, 2026 at 5:50PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Feb. 3, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman, President & Chief Executive Officer — Robert A. Bradway
  • Executive Vice President, Research & Development — James E. Bradner
  • Executive Vice President, Global Commercial Operations — Murdo Gordon
  • Executive Vice President & Chief Financial Officer — Peter H. Griffith

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue Growth -- Double-digit revenue and earnings per share growth achieved in 2025.
  • Product Performance -- Fourteen products reached blockbuster status with over $1 billion in sales; thirteen showed double-digit sales growth; eighteen achieved record results.
  • Key Products -- Repatha, Evenity, and Tespire each delivered more than 30% year-over-year sales growth.
  • Rare Disease Portfolio -- Exceeded $5 billion in sales, with multiple therapies early in their lifecycle and growing through new indications and geographies.
  • Oncology Portfolio -- Grew at 11% year over year, fueled by bispecific T cell engager (BiTE) medicines and rapid adoption of Imdeltra.
  • Biosimilars Segment -- $3 billion in 2025 sales, with cumulative sales surpassing $13 billion since 2018.
  • Repatha -- Grew 36% year over year to over $3 billion, driven by updated treatment urgency, new direct-to-patient programs, and landmark Vesalius CV trial outcomes.
  • Evenity -- Increased 34% to $2.1 billion; U.S. sales climbed 41% with volume gains and category market share over 60%.
  • Prolia -- Delivered $4.4 billion in sales, up 1%, but faces accelerated sales erosion due to global biosimilar launches.
  • Eplisna -- Rare disease medicine grew 73% to $655 million, following approvals in IgG4-related disease and generalized myasthenia gravis.
  • Tavneos -- Sales reached $459 million with a 62% year-over-year increase; over seven thousand ANCA-associated vasculitis patients treated.
  • Tespire -- Sales rose 52% year over year to nearly $1.5 billion, capturing the leading position for new-to-brand patients in severe uncontrolled asthma.
  • Otezla -- Increased 7% to nearly $2.3 billion; company anticipates sales erosion in 2026 from U.S. pricing and EU generics.
  • Oncology Segment Sales -- Innovative products generated $8.7 billion, with Imdeltra contributing $627 million and Blincyto surpassing $1.5 billion after 28% growth.
  • PAV Blue Biosimilar -- Achieved $700 million in sales; adoption expanded among retina specialists.
  • Operating Margin -- Reported full-year non-GAAP operating margin of 46%.
  • R&D Investment -- Non-GAAP R&D spending rose 22% to $7.2 billion, reflecting late-stage pipeline expansion and $300 million from recent business development transactions.
  • Debt Reduction -- $6 billion in debt retired during 2025.
  • Tax Rate -- Non-GAAP tax rate increased 1.4 percentage points to 15.9% for 2025 due to changes in earnings mix.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $8.1 billion generated for the year.
  • Dividend -- $2.38 per share in Q4, increasing 6% from 2024.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Total revenues projected at $37 billion to $38.4 billion; non-GAAP EPS expected between $21.6 to $23.
  • 2026 Operating Margin Forecast -- Anticipates 45%-46% non-GAAP operating margin.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $2.2 billion in 2025, forecast at $2.6 billion in 2026 to support capacity growth and new product launches.
  • Share Repurchases -- Plans not to exceed $3 billion for 2026.

RISKS

  • Prolia and Otezla expected to face sales erosion in 2026 due to increased biosimilar competition and unfavorable pricing dynamics.
  • Inventory build of approximately $250 million in 2025 could potentially impact first-quarter 2026 sales.
  • Seasonal headwinds anticipated for first-quarter 2026 revenues due to U.S. health insurance cycles and higher patient co-pay obligations.
  • Termination of Rocotinlimab and Bimirituzumab late-stage programs as a result of portfolio reprioritization and below-expectation efficacy, leading to resource reallocation.

SUMMARY

Amgen (AMGN 1.29%) reported double-digit top-line and bottom-line growth, supported by high-performing oncology, rare disease, biosimilar, and general medicine portfolios. The company highlighted the outsized sales contributions of Repatha, Evenity, and Tespire, each increasing more than 30%. The pipeline advanced with major regulatory approvals, a rapidly progressing Meritide program, and extensive Phase III trial activity, signaling a broad evidence-generation effort. Management committed to material R&D investment, reduced leverage, and raised dividends, while guiding to continued revenue and margin strength in 2026 based on core growth brands. Shareholder return policy remains disciplined with share repurchases capped and manufacturing capacity set to expand for upcoming product launches.

  • Robert A. Bradway emphasized, "Fourteen of our products achieved blockbuster status with sales of a billion dollars or more" and flagged Repatha, Evenity, and Tespire as core growth drivers.
  • James E. Bradner reported, "six global phase three studies underway with Miratide," with full enrollment of chronic weight management studies and new indications pursued in cardiovascular and obesity-related conditions.
  • Murdo Gordon noted, "Repatha sales grew 36% year over year in 2025 surpassing $3 billion," with new clinical trial data driving a strategic push with updated guidelines and expanded patient access programs.
  • Peter H. Griffith detailed a "non-GAAP operating margin of 46%" and highlighted continued pipeline investment, supported by higher R&D spend and strategic capital deployment.
  • Recent regulatory actions included successful EU and U.S. approvals for Eplisna in rare diseases, and full FDA approval for Imdeltra in small cell lung cancer.
  • Amgen ended the year with $8.1 billion in free cash flow and retired $6 billion of debt, underlining ongoing shareholder value creation and capital discipline.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • BiTE (Bispecific T cell engager): An engineered antibody construct that directs T cells to target and eliminate cancer cells.
  • PCSK9 inhibitor: A class of drugs reducing LDL cholesterol by inhibiting the PCSK9 protein, enhancing clearance of LDL from the bloodstream.
  • NMOSD (Neuromyelitis Optica Spectrum Disorder): A rare, autoimmune disease characterized by inflammation of the optic nerve and spinal cord.
  • TSLP: Thymic stromal lymphopoietin, a cytokine involved in the initiation of allergic inflammation common in severe asthma.
  • DELHPHY-304 (Trial): A pivotal Phase III clinical study evaluating Imdeltra in small cell lung cancer.
  • ASCVD: Atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, involving buildup of fats and cholesterol in artery walls.
  • CIDP: Chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy, a neurological disorder causing progressive weakness and impaired sensory function.
  • IgG4-related disease: A rare autoimmune condition involving immune-mediated inflammation and fibrosis of organs.
