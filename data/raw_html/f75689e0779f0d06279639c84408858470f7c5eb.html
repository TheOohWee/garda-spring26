Darden (DRI) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 6, 2026 at 1:34PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, September 19, 2024 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Rick Cardenas
  • Senior Vice President, Chief Financial Officer — Rajesh Vennam
  • Vice President, Investor Relations and Corporate Communications — Courtney Aquilla

Takeaways

  • Total Sales -- $2.8 billion, a 1% increase, driven by 42 net new restaurant openings and partially offset by a 1.1% decline in same-restaurant sales.
  • Industry Outperformance -- Same-restaurant sales exceeded industry by 140 basis points; same-restaurant guest counts surpassed industry by 160 basis points.
  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share -- Adjusted diluted net EPS from continuing operations was $1.75, slightly below the prior year’s figure.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $392 million, exceeding prior year, attributed to margin resilience despite softer sales.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $338 million returned to shareholders via $166 million in dividends and $172 million in share repurchases.
  • Olive Garden Sales -- Total sales decreased by 1.5% on same-restaurant sales decline of 2.9%, underperforming the industry by 40 basis points for the first time in years; two-year cumulative same-restaurant sales rose 3.2%, exceeding industry by 480 basis points over that period.
  • Olive Garden Segment Profit Margin -- 20.6%, maintaining significant margin leadership despite sales deleverage.
  • LongHorn Steakhouse Performance -- Total sales up 6.5%, driven by 3.7% same-restaurant sales growth; segment profit margin reached 17.9%, 40 basis points above prior year.
  • Fine Dining Segment -- Sales grew 2% on eight net new units; both Capital Grille and Eddie V’s reported negative same-restaurant sales.
  • Other Business Segment -- Sales declined slightly, with a 1.8% decrease in same-restaurant sales; segment profit margin remained flat at 15.1%.
  • Commodities & Labor Inflation -- Food and beverage cost improved by 50 basis points year over year as commodities were “only slightly inflationary”; total labor inflation was approximately 4%, above total pricing of approximately 2.5%.
  • Adjusted G&A Expense -- 20 basis points favorable year over year on lower incentive accrual; first quarter G&A was $125 million, with full-year G&A expected near $450 million.
  • Tax Rate -- Adjusted effective tax rate was 10.6%, with underlying rate about 12% excluding mark-to-market hedge effects.
  • Never Ending Pasta Bowl Promotion -- Extended to 12 weeks at Olive Garden, with additional marketing and unchanged price point for three years; a new garlic herb sauce launching during the event to maintain guest interest.
  • Menu Adjustments -- Olive Garden is reintroducing Steak Gorgonzola Alfredo and Stuffed Chicken Marsala using upgraded ingredients and streamlined execution; LongHorn added lemon garlic chicken and introduced a top-selling dragon fruit margarita; Yard House enhanced pizza offerings with faster cook times and higher guest satisfaction; Cheddar’s launched value-focused items and reintroduced onion rings, their highest-engagement social media event.
  • First‑Party Delivery Partnership -- A two-year exclusive with Uber Direct initiates pilot delivery at Olive Garden, maintaining in-restaurant price parity for pickup, dine-in, and delivery; guests pay a transparent delivery fee averaging $7 per order.
  • Digital Marketing Expansion -- Olive Garden’s connected TV strategies are being deployed at several brands, leveraging learnings to optimize media investment and promotional effectiveness.
  • Chuy’s Acquisition Progress -- Closing targeted for mid-October with secured financing; management projects the deal to be neutral to adjusted EPS for fiscal 2025, excluding transaction and integration expenses.
  • Team Member Engagement -- Internal survey in July showed team member engagement nearly 30 points above U.S. Gallup benchmark, the highest in company history.
  • Guidance & Inflation -- Full-year earnings growth guidance reaffirmed at 6%-8%, with commodity inflation for the year now expected to be slightly below 2% due to improved trends, though chicken costs present a second-half risk.

Risks

  • First quarter earnings and sales were below company expectations, attributed to a significant decline in July traffic and industry-wide softness, particularly impacting Olive Garden and Fine Dining segments.
  • Fine dining segment continues to experience negative same-restaurant sales, with management describing persistent “challenges,” including weaker performance in urban markets and among aspirational/lower-income cohorts.
  • Labor inflation at approximately 4% exceeded total pricing of 2.5% for the quarter, generating margin pressure despite productivity improvement.
  • Potential cost headwinds anticipated for chicken in the second half as existing favorable supply contracts expire, indicating possible margin risk.

Summary

Management introduced a two-year exclusive first‑party delivery pilot with Uber Direct for Olive Garden, maintaining menu price parity and implementing transparent guest-paid delivery fees. Brand teams cited broad-based innovation, such as menu reinvestment, targeted digital marketing, and operational initiatives to address evolving guest demands for speed and value. The fine dining segment remained challenged, with negative same‑restaurant sales and continued urban softness; management outlined targeted product initiatives but cautioned on recovery timing. Large-scale marketing initiatives at Olive Garden, notably the expanded Never Ending Pasta Bowl and increased advertising of value‑oriented price points, aim to counter traffic declines and re-engage infrequent guests. The pending Chuy’s acquisition is on track for closing with the financial impact slated to be neutral to adjusted earnings per share for the fiscal year, excluding transaction costs.

  • Rajesh Vennam confirmed the company generated $209 million in adjusted earnings from continuing operations, representing 7.6% of sales.
  • Cheddar’s value strategy leverages opportunity buys and targeted limited-time offers, evidenced by the $12.99 pork chop and $8.59 lunch menu launches.
  • Olive Garden’s marketing plans will showcase more explicit price points and value messaging throughout the year rather than relying solely on equity-focused advertising.
  • Approximately 80% of Olive Garden’s off-premise business is small-order takeout, with catering at roughly 20%, and digital transactions exceeding 60% of ToGo orders.
  • Fine dining recovery is expected to be gradual, with management identifying a sharper pullback among aspirational and lower-income guests, and continued better retention in suburban versus urban locations.
  • LongHorn Steakhouse outperformed both segment peers and company brands, with 0.7% positive traffic growth, 3% check growth, and outperformance driven partly by trade-down from fine dining.
  • Management reiterated a commitment to avoid deep discounting practices and maintain pricing strategy below inflation, with upcoming limited-time offers designed to be both simple and margin-accretive.
  • Yard House achieved significant gains in speed and satisfaction by revamping its pizza platform and conducting broad bartender recertification programs to enhance the guest atmosphere.
  • The first quarter saw Olive Garden negative mid-5% traffic, with pricing at 1.9% and a slightly positive mix; LongHorn reported a 0.7% positive traffic and high-2% pricing, clarifying check and mix composition for each segment.
  • Adjusted margin analysis detailed restaurant-level EBITDA of 18.8%, 20 basis points lower year-on-year, with restaurant labor, expenses, and marketing all slightly higher as a percentage of sales but offset by G&A favorability and tax benefit from hedges.

Industry glossary

  • Opportunity buys: The practice of procuring ingredients in bulk at a discount due to supplier availability or surplus, enabling value-focused limited-time menu offers.
  • First-party delivery: Orders fulfilled via direct integration with a delivery provider (like Uber Direct) but initiated and controlled from the restaurant's digital platforms, as opposed to third-party delivery marketplaces.
  • Segment profit margin: Profit generated by a specific restaurant concept (e.g., Olive Garden) after restaurant-level expenses, expressed as a percentage of that segment’s sales.
  • Trade-up: The rate at which guests select higher-priced add-ons or menu items (e.g., protein add-ons to promotional pasta dishes) rather than base or entry-level offerings.
