Earnings Call Transcript

Fox Factory (FOXF) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 26, 2026 at 5:42PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 26, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Michael C. Dennison
  • Chief Financial Officer — Dennis Charles Schemm

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $361.1 million for fiscal fourth quarter ended Dec. 31, 2025, up 2.3%, and $1.47 billion for the year, up 5.3%.
  • Gross margin -- 28.3%, down from 28.9%, due to product line mix and tariffs.
  • Adjusted net income -- $8.3 million, or $0.20 per diluted share, compared to $12.8 million, or $0.31, for the fiscal fourth quarter last year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $35.0 million with a 9.7% margin, versus $40.4 million and 11.5% margin last year.
  • Operating expense -- Included a non-cash goodwill impairment of $295.2 million tied to the share price.
  • Segment sales -- PVG: $116.7 million, AAG: $126.2 million (up 12.5% year over year, 7.1% sequentially), SSG: $118.2 million (down 5% year over year).
  • Fiscal first quarter 2026 outlook -- Net sales expected between $343 million and $369 million, adjusted EBITDA between $27 million and $34 million.
  • Fiscal 2026 full-year guidance -- Net sales of $1.328 billion to $1.416 billion (implying a 6.5% year-over-year decline at midpoint), adjusted EBITDA between $174 million and $203 million, with a targeted margin of 13.7% at midpoint.
  • Cost savings initiatives -- $25 million realized in fiscal 2025 from Phase One, with $10 million carrying forward and a targeted $40 million additional savings from Phase Two for fiscal 2026, totaling $50 million targeted cost reductions.
  • Divestiture actions -- Exiting Phoenix operations and Shock Therapy, UTV, and Geyser businesses, expected to be complete by the end of fiscal first quarter 2026, with proceeds earmarked "100% [for] debt reduction."
  • Tariff impacts -- Fiscal 2025 saw $50 million in gross tariffs (mitigated by $25 million in offsets); fiscal 2026 expects $30 million gross tariff impact with $15 million net headwind after mitigation.
  • Capital expenditures -- Targeting reduction to about 2% of revenue in fiscal 2026 after several years above 3%.
  • Tax rate expectation -- 15%-18% for fiscal 2026, as guided.
  • Debt reduction -- $13 million paid in fiscal fourth quarter and $33 million for the year; year-end balance at $673.5 million. Net leverage ratio at 3.74, within the covenant of 4.5.
  • Board-level actions -- Formation of a transformation committee focused on operational excellence and margin improvement, to begin advising in the coming month.
  • Product pipeline -- New launches include Live Valve aftermarket, expansion into motorized two-wheel, RV partnerships, and new OEM programs (including with Ducati and Ford).

Risks

  • Pervasive margin pressure from product line mix changes and tariffs, as seen in 60 basis points gross margin decline and $15 million net tariff headwind expected in fiscal 2026.
  • Ongoing exposure to dilutive operations in AAG until completion of divestiture, with management stating, "We will also continue to feel the impact from the dilutive Phoenix operations through its divestiture toward the end of the first quarter."
  • Guided top-line contraction of 6.5% due to divestitures, rationalization, and macro softness, with no assumptions for market or tariff improvement in outlook.
  • Adjusted net income declined to $8.3 million from $12.8 million, and adjusted EBITDA margin dropped to 9.7% from 11.5%.

Summary

Fox Factory (FOXF 0.53%) reported modest revenue growth for the fiscal fourth quarter and year but faced compressed margins and a significant, non-cash goodwill impairment. Management launched an aggressive $50 million cost reduction effort for fiscal 2026 by exiting unprofitable business lines and consolidating its footprint, targeting approximately 200 basis points in adjusted EBITDA margin improvement. The company expects meaningful sales contraction, primarily due to divestitures, while sustaining capital and tax discipline and deploying all divestiture proceeds to reduce debt. Board formation of a transformation committee and explicit guidance on cost and margin improvement signal significant restructuring and portfolio focus across operations.

  • Management intends to drive profitability by prioritizing "self-help" margin expansion actions independent of any upturn in market conditions or relief from tariffs.
  • The company is actively reviewing all businesses and product lines for alignment with strict core, synergistic, and durable profitability criteria, with further divestitures possible if those criteria are not met.
  • Early demand for new product launches, including Live Valve aftermarket applications and expanded OEM platforms, may support long-term growth, but management underscored that fiscal 2026 guidance assumes only currently visible conditions.
  • The transformation committee, including external advisors, is expected to identify additional incremental savings beyond the $50 million already targeted.

Industry glossary

  • AAG: Aftermarket Applications Group segment, which includes businesses related to performance and specialty vehicle upfitting.
  • PVG: Powered Vehicles Group segment, focused on automotive OE and powersports suspension and related products.
  • SSG: Specialty Sports Group, covering cycling and related specialty sports equipment.
  • PVD: Power Vehicle Division within AAG, identified as core by management.
  • Live Valve: Fox's proprietary electronic suspension technology for adaptive off-road ride dynamics.
  • OEM: Original Equipment Manufacturer; denotes sales direct to vehicle or equipment manufacturers as opposed to aftermarket channels.
  • SG&A: Selling, General, and Administrative expenses; operating costs not directly tied to production.
  • CapEx: Capital Expenditure, or spending on property, plant, equipment, and related investments.
  • Net leverage ratio: A measure of company debt relative to EBITDA, often used to assess risk relative to loan covenants.
