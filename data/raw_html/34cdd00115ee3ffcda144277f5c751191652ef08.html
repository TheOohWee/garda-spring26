Argan (AGX) Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Sep 4, 2025 at 6:21PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Sept. 4, 2025, at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — David Watson
  • Chief Financial Officer — Joshua Baugher

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Consolidated Revenue-- $237.7 million consolidated revenue for Q2 fiscal 2026, a 5% year-over-year increase and a 23% sequential gain.
  • Gross Margin-- 18.6% gross margin (GAAP) for Q2 fiscal 2026, up from 13.7% in Q2 fiscal 2025, attributable to execution in the Power Industry Services segment.
  • Net Income-- $35.3 million net income for Q2 fiscal 2026, representing a record $2.50 per diluted share, doubling from $18.2 million or $1.31 per share in Q2 fiscal 2025.
  • EBITDA-- $36.2 million EBITDA for Q2 fiscal 2026, up from $24.8 million in Q2 fiscal 2025, with an EBITDA margin of 15.2% compared to 10.9% in the prior-year quarter.
  • Record Project Backlog-- $2 billion at quarter-end, including the Platton Power Station in Ireland and an industrial recycling water treatment contract in Alabama; backlog rose 5% since April 30, 2025.
  • Power Industry Services Revenue-- $197 million for Q2 fiscal 2026, a 13% increase in Power Industry Services revenues, now constituting 83% of consolidated revenue and $35 million in pretax book income.
  • Industrial Construction Services Revenue-- $36 million for Q2 fiscal 2026, down from $50 million in Q2 fiscal 2025, with a record backlog of $189 million and $3 million pretax book income.
  • Telecommunications Infrastructure Services Revenue-- 2% of consolidated revenue; this segment reported record backlog and expectations for further growth.
  • Backlog Composition-- Approximately 61% natural gas projects and 29% renewables in backlog as of July 31, 2025.
  • Major Project Developments-- Achieved first fire on both units of the 950-megawatt Trumbull natural gas project; ongoing work on a 1.2-gigawatt Texas combined cycle plant, two Ireland thermal power stations, and multiple water treatment and recycling projects.
  • Capital Allocation-- $25 million returned to shareholders in the first six months of fiscal 2026; quarterly dividend raised 25% to 37.5¢ per share in September 2024, now $1.50 annualized dividend run rate, with a cumulative 50% increase in under two years.
  • Share Repurchase-- $109.6 million returned to shareholders since November 2021; program authorization increased to $150 million in April 2025.
  • Balance Sheet-- $572 million in cash and investments as of Q2 fiscal 2026, net liquidity of $344 million, no debt, and stockholders' equity (GAAP) of $393 million as of July 31, 2025.
  • Year-to-Date Metrics-- Six-month GAAP revenues of $431.4 million (up 12.1%), net income of $57.8 million, and EBITDA of $66.5 million for the first half of fiscal 2026.
  • Project Execution Outlook-- CEO Watson stated, "Our pipeline is stronger than it has ever been," and confirmed capacity to handle 10 to 12 power projects simultaneously.

Summary

Argan (AGX 0.89%) CFO Joshua Baugher reported income tax expense dropped to $400,000 due to favorable deductions from stock option exercises, compared to $6.1 million in Q2 fiscal 2025. CEO Watson highlighted that risk was reduced following first fire. Management expects to grow project backlog beyond $2 billion before fiscal 2026 year-end and confirms unchanged high demand for large natural gas and renewable power infrastructure. Watson noted disciplined pricing and a focus on long-term partner relationships, citing customers’ willingness to pay amid "record levels of $329 per megawatt per day" in recent PJM capacity auctions for the 2627 delivery year. The company is open to acquisitions that could extend capabilities or geographic reach as part of its strategic capital allocation.

  • Baugher directly stated no formal guidance is issued on gross margin, but "continued strong execution" is expected to result in higher margins than last fiscal year.
  • All three business segments achieved record backlog this quarter, supported by organic growth across the platform.
  • CEO Watson said, "We've intentionally added headcount over the last eighteen months to position ourselves for this currently very strong demand environment."
  • Watson described a robust bidding pipeline, noting, "the fundamentals, electricity consumption is expected to increase 4% annually through 2027 per the IEA," and referenced OEMs being sold out for years, indicating elevated opportunity levels.
  • Backlog growth and size diversity were emphasized, underlining flexible project size capacity.

Industry glossary

  • First fire: Initial operation of a gas turbine in a power plant, marking a critical commissioning milestone and reduced project risk.
  • PJM: Largest regional transmission organization in the U.S., overseeing the wholesale electricity market serving 65 million people.
  • EPC: Engineering, Procurement, and Construction—a contract model covering comprehensive turnkey delivery of infrastructure projects.
