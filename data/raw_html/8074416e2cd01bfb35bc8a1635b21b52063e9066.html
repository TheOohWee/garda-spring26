FRP Holdings (FRPH) Q3 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 18, 2026 at 12:07PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Nov. 6, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — John Baker III
  • Chairman — John Baker II
  • President and Chief Operating Officer — David deVilliers III
  • Vice Chairman — David deVilliers, Jr.
  • Chief Financial Officer — Matthew McNulty
  • Executive Vice President — John Milton
  • Chief Investment Officer — Mark Levy
  • Chief Accounting Officer — John Klopfenstein

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net income -- FRP Holdings (FRPH 0.97%) reported $700,000, down 51% from $1.4 million in the prior year, mainly due to $1.3 million in Altman Logistics Properties acquisition expenses.
  • Adjusted net income -- $281,000 higher, representing a 21% increase, when excluding the Altman acquisition expenses.
  • Pro rata NOI -- $9.5 million, a 16% decrease, attributed to a one-time minimum royalty payment last year; adjusted NOI increased by $104,000 after excluding this payment.
  • Commercial and industrial segment occupancy -- Same-store occupancy dropped by 24%, with new development space at the Chelsea building (258,000 square feet) fully vacant; vacancies now total 51% of the segment’s leasable area.
  • Commercial and industrial revenue and NOI -- Segment revenue was $1.2 million, and NOI was $904,000, representing declines of 16% and 25%, respectively.
  • Mining and royalty revenue -- $3.7 million, up 15%; NOI was $3.8 million, down 26% due to a nonrecurring $1.9 million payment last year.
  • Multifamily portfolio occupancy -- Apartments at 91% occupancy, and retail space at 74% occupancy at quarter-end.
  • Multifamily segment financials -- Total revenues were $14.6 million, and NOI was $8.2 million; FRP Holdings' share of NOI declined 3.2%, while revenues rose 2.9%.
  • Leasing trends in multifamily -- Renewal success rates exceeded 55%, with rent increases averaging over 2.5%; new lease trade-out rates are down amid concessions for new supply.
  • Industrial development pipeline -- Over 1.8 million square feet of new industrial space to be added; Florida projects totaling 750,000 square feet will be available for lease-up in 2026 and are expected to generate annual NOI of about $9 million when stabilized (FRP Holdings' share just over $8 million).
  • Recent acquisition -- Full ownership of Lakeland and Broward County, Florida assets and minority interests in three industrial buildings totaling 510,000 square feet in New Jersey and Florida, all delivering in 2026; FRP Holdings expects to invest up to $8 million and projects a “2x multiple on invested capital” upon sale.
  • Multifamily development pipeline -- Woven project in Greenville, South Carolina (214 units, 13,500 square feet retail, $87 million cost), and Estero, Florida JV (296 units, 28,745 square feet retail, $142 million cost), both under construction and collectively expected to boost NOI by over $4 million after stabilization in 2029.
  • Capital recycling -- Aberdeen Overlook lending venture funded with $31.1 million; $24.7 million in principal and preferred interest has been received to date; a national homebuilder under contract for all finished lots by Q4 2027, and profit expected at $11.2 million (36% on funds drawn).
  • Market conditions -- Management observed “constructive” industrial fundamentals, with rents described as “holding firm,” new construction below pre-pandemic levels, and market vacancy estimated to peak in Q4 2025.
  • Leadership appointment -- Mark Levy joined as Chief Investment Officer following the Altman Logistics Properties acquisition, bringing industrial development expertise and reinforcing a strategy centered on infill, supply-constrained locations.

RISKS

  • Commercial and industrial segment faced vacancies totaling 51% of space, with new Chelsea building at 100% vacancy, directly reducing business segment revenue and NOI.
  • Multifamily NOI declined by 3.2% due to increased operating costs, property taxes, and elevated uncollectible revenue, especially at Maren, with ongoing competitive pressure from significant new supply in the D.C. market.
  • Management acknowledged persistent macro uncertainty, noting that “Continued uncertainty around trade policy and macroeconomic direction has extended decision cycles for many occupiers, particularly for larger blocks of space.”
  • District retail and multifamily assets are still experiencing the effects of pandemic-era policies, with delinquency rates historically as high as 10%-12%, and some tenants “just stopped paying their landlords,” though trends are now improving.

SUMMARY

Management reported that headline net income and NOI declines were largely the result of one-time acquisition and prior-year royalty items, with underlying adjusted profitability trending modestly upward. The industrial development pipeline materially expanded due to the Altman Logistics Properties acquisition, increasing ownership stakes and future NOI contribution from strategic Florida and New Jersey assets. Leasing velocity, especially for mid-bay industrial space, was cited as strengthening in key markets, with new supply scheduled for delivery into 2026 expected to benefit from stabilizing vacancies and firm rent levels. The multifamily business faces lingering occupancy and collection headwinds in D.C. but is seeing stabilization and incremental renewal rent growth as pandemic effects abate. Management highlighted disciplined capital deployment, with recent and planned project investments projected to generate above-market internal rates of return and long-term portfolio expansion.

  • The company expects to act as both sole owner and fee-generating manager in various configurations for new development and joint ventures, following a strategy emphasized as “opportunistic and flexible.”
  • Company leadership described 2025 as a “foundational year for future growth, just not necessarily a growth year,” prioritizing disciplined lease-up over rate concessions.
  • In retail and multifamily in the District, legal reforms to tenant protections are now reducing historic delinquency, and recent occupancy and rent improvements at Bryant Street are providing improved cash flow prospects.
  • Management disclosed the expectation of realizing over a 2x multiple on invested capital in select development projects, signifying explicit targets for capital efficiency in addition to recurring income.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • NOI (Net Operating Income): Rental and related property income less direct property-level operating expenses, used to evaluate real estate performance.
  • Pro rata NOI: The company’s share of NOI from joint ventures or partially owned assets, reflecting economic ownership proportion.
  • Trade-out rate: The difference in rental rate between expiring leases and new leases for the same or similar units, indicating leasing market trends.
  • Promote economics: The portion of profits awarded to the managing partner or sponsor in a joint venture after certain financial returns are met, typically incentivizing performance.
