TAKEAWAYS

  • Firm-Wide Revenue -- $5.5 billion, up 11%, surpassing $5 billion for the first time in company history.
  • Q4 Revenue -- $1.56 billion, a 9% sequential increase, exceeding consensus by $50 million, and up 14% year over year.
  • EPS (Excluding Q1 Legal Accrual) -- $7.92 for the full year; Q4 record EPS of $2.63.
  • Return on Tangible Common Equity -- 25% for the year and over 31% in Q4.
  • Pretax Margin -- 21% for the year, exceeding 22% in Q4.
  • Global Wealth Management Revenue -- $3.5 billion, highest ever, marking the 23rd consecutive record year.
  • Institutional Revenue -- $1.9 billion for the year, up 20%, with Q4 revenue at $610 million, up 28% year over year.
  • Investment Banking Revenue -- Q4 revenue of $456 million, up 50% year over year, with advisory revenue up 46% to $277 million.
  • Equity Capital Raising Revenue -- $95 million in Q4, double the prior year, led by health care, financials, and industrials.
  • Fixed Income Underwriting -- Record $76 million in Q4, up 23%, driven by public finance and corporate issuance.
  • Record Client Assets -- $552 billion at quarter-end, with fee-based assets reaching $225 billion; net new asset growth in the low- to mid-single digits percentage range.
  • Adviser Recruiting -- 181 financial advisers added during the year, including 92 experienced advisers with a trailing twelve-month production of $86 million.
  • Net Interest Income -- At the high end of guidance for Q4 but $2 million below consensus; for 2026, guided to $1.1 billion-$1.2 billion, with $4 billion in anticipated balance sheet growth.
  • Operating Expense Management -- Compensation ratio at 58% for the quarter and year; non-compensation expenses at $307 million in Q4, up 6%, largely due to investment banking activity.
  • Capital Position -- Tier 1 leverage ratio at 11.4%, risk-based capital ratio at 18.3%, and over $560 million in excess capital above the 10% leverage target.
  • Shareholder Actions -- 11% dividend increase authorized for 2026 and a three-for-two stock split effective Feb. 26, 2026 (record date Feb. 12, 2026); 335,000 shares repurchased in Q4, with 7.6 million remaining under authorization.
  • Strategic Transactions -- Sale of Stifel Independent Advisors (SIA) and closure of European equities business, removing $100 million in annual revenue but expected to improve operating leverage and margin profile.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Net revenue forecasted at $6.0 billion-$6.35 billion, reflecting strategic changes; compensation ratio guided to 56.5%-57.5%, and non-compensation operating ratio to 18%-20%.
  • Leadership in M&A Advisory -- Participated in 75% of depository M&A advisory transactions by deal volume in 2025; announced new depository M&A transaction representing Stellar in sale to Prosperity Bank.
  • Public Finance Platform -- Remains the number one negotiated issue manager by deal count, with increased success in larger par value transactions in Q4.

SUMMARY

Stifel Financial (SF +0.14%) delivered record-breaking results for both firm-wide revenue and multiple business segments, citing disciplined growth and sustained adviser productivity as key drivers. Management announced a three-for-two stock split and an 11% dividend increase, emphasizing enduring confidence in capital generation and operating leverage. The strategic sale of SIA and closure of the European equities business are expected to shift the expense and margin structure going forward, with 2026 guidance explicitly accounting for $100 million in revenue reduction from these moves. Planned $4 billion balance sheet growth and targeted recruiting investment may support continued asset and earnings expansion, even as market conditions evolve.

  • Chief Executive Officer Kruszewski stated, "the time, we looked forward, it was our ability to generate $5.2 billion in revenue and $8 a share in a normalized environment," affirming the company surpassed these aims in 2025 despite headwinds.
  • Chief Financial Officer Marischen highlighted that net interest income guidance for 2026 assumes all balance sheet growth will be funded with treasury deposits, remaining "relatively rate agnostic" due to portfolio structure.
  • Leadership identified health care, technology, and financial institutions as sectors with notable momentum in investment banking pipelines.
  • Marischen indicated that the SIA sale and European restructuring will materially lower compensation and non-compensation expense ratios, with $20 million-plus annual non-compensation savings anticipated.
  • Kruszewski noted potential for further adviser recruiting investment, suggesting an increased allocation could accelerate net new asset flows and team productivity.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Fee-Based Assets: Client portfolios where revenue is earned through advisory or management fees rather than per-transaction commissions.
  • Sweep Balances: Client cash automatically moved from brokerage accounts into interest-bearing deposit or money market accounts at financial institutions.
  • Negotiated Issue Manager: Lead underwriter for a public finance deal selected through negotiation rather than a competitive bid process.
  • Bookrunner ("lead left"): The firm leading placement and distribution in a syndicated capital markets deal, typically named in the primary (leftmost) position on investor materials.
