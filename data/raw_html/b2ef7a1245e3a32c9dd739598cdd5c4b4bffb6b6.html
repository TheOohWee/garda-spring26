TAKEAWAYS

  • Core FFO Per Share -- $2.28, surpassing the high end of guidance due to strong North American and U.K. same-property performance.
  • North American Same-Property NOI Growth -- 5.4%, driven primarily by manufactured housing segment strength.
  • Manufactured Housing Same-Property NOI Growth -- 10.1% with 98% occupancy, reflecting high portfolio demand.
  • 2026 Rent Increase Notices (MH) -- Delivered to 50% of residents at an average increase of approximately 5%.
  • Same-Property Annual RV Revenue -- Up 8.1%; same-property RV NOI declined 1.1% as transient RV revenue decreased 7.8% due to strategic conversion to annual contracts.
  • 2026 Annual RV Rental Rate Increases -- Average approximately 4% per management’s update.
  • U.K. Same-Property NOI Growth -- 5.4% supported by 4.8% revenue increase and 4% expense rise.
  • U.K. 2026 Rent Increase Notices -- Sent to Park Holidays homeowners with an average rise of approximately 4.1%.
  • Completed Dispositions -- Approximately $118 million of delayed consent properties and an $18 million land parcel sold in the quarter.
  • Acquisitions -- 14 communities purchased for $457 million using 1031 exchange proceeds; 7 U.K. properties’ ground leases acquired for $124 million.
  • U.K. Ground Lease Advancement -- 28 ground leases totaling $324 million acquired year-to-date; 5 more totaling $63 million pending by Q1 2026, bringing 49 of 53 U.K. communities to freehold ownership.
  • Total Debt -- $4.3 billion at 3.4% weighted average interest rate and 7.4 years weighted average maturity as of September 30; pro forma net debt at $3.7 billion.
  • Net Debt to Recurring EBITDA -- Approximately 3.6x on a trailing 12-month basis.
  • Share Repurchases -- Approximately 4 million shares bought back for $500 million year-to-date at an average price of $125.74 per share.
  • Full-Year 2025 Core FFO Guidance -- Raised by $0.04 at the midpoint to a new range of $6.59 to $6.67 per share.
  • Updated NOI Guidance -- North American same-property NOI growth at 5.1% midpoint (+40 basis points); manufactured housing segment to 7.8% midpoint; RV segment revised to 1% decline midpoint; U.K. to approximately 4% midpoint.
  • Recent Acquisitions’ Cap Rate -- Management confirmed cap rates in the low 4% range; ground lease yields are in the low to mid-4% range.
  • Operational Cost Controls -- Ongoing expense savings result from procurement standardization, technology-driven efficiencies, and favorable bad debt trends.

SUMMARY

The appointment of Charles Young as CEO marked a leadership transition focused on operational excellence, capital discipline, and long-term growth. The company executed major portfolio rebalancing, advancing strategic acquisitions with a disciplined approach and completing $118 million in asset dispositions. Recent ground lease purchases in the U.K. shifted 49 out of 53 communities to freehold, improving both strategic flexibility and earnings accretion. Management communicated confidence in rate setting and RV retention strategies, as evidenced by average proposed 2026 rent increases of 5% for manufactured housing and 4% for annual RV contracts. The capital allocation framework remained conservative, blending debt reduction, portfolio expansion, and share repurchases while pursuing cost efficiencies through technology and procurement initiatives.

  • Charles Young stated his initial priorities include "supporting our team as we deliver on our strategy and commitments" and "assessing opportunities for disciplined long-term growth."
  • Aaron Weiss commented that ground lease acquisitions in the U.K. offer "meaningful flexibility to manage the portfolio strategically over time" and provide incremental earnings benefits.
  • Management observed continued transaction market activity but noted new opportunities are "much more likely to be in the single asset or small portfolio opportunities" with cap rates holding in the low 4% range.
  • The team described cost discipline efforts as having expanded to payroll, supplies, repairs, and technology, supported by a "meaningful standardization, expansion and adoption of our procurement platform."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • 1031 Exchange: A U.S. Internal Revenue Code provision allowing for the tax-deferred exchange of investment properties, provided proceeds are reinvested in qualifying assets.
  • Cap Rate: The ratio of a property’s net operating income to its purchase price, used as a measure of investment yield in real estate transactions.
  • Ground Lease: An arrangement in which a tenant leases land from the owner for a long-term period, often with rights to develop or operate structures; freehold ownership refers to direct ownership of both land and buildings.
  • FFO (Funds From Operations): A standard REIT performance metric representing net income excluding gains or losses from property sales and certain non-cash items.
  • NOI (Net Operating Income): Total property revenue minus direct operating expenses, excluding interest, depreciation, and amortization.
  • Park Holidays: Sun Communities’ U.K.-based holiday park platform, cited in the call as a segment for recurring real property revenue and home sales.
  • MH: Manufactured Housing; factory-built residential units installed at community sites, distinct from RV or apartment units.
  • RV: Recreational Vehicle; in the context of Sun Communities, refers to properties or segments dedicated to RV site rentals, both transient (short-term) and annual (long-term stays).
