Lulus (LVLU) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 13, 2025 at 5:40PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, Aug. 13, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

Chief Executive Officer and Interim Chief Financial Officer — Crystal Landsem

President and Chief Information Officer — Mark Vos

General Counsel and Corporate Secretary — Naomi Beckman-Straus

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Risks

Shoes and separates continued to weigh on top-line results, accounting for most of the 11% year-over-year net revenue decline in fiscal Q2 2025 (period ended June 29, 2025).

Interest expense rose to $856,000 from $270,000 year-over-year in fiscal Q2 2025, including one-time fees related to credit agreement amendments.

Net cash used in operating activities (GAAP) was $1,400,000 in fiscal Q2 2025, compared to $3,700,000 provided in fiscal Q2 2024, due to larger decreases in accounts payable tied to payment timing.

Crystal Landsem noted, "I'm currently serving as interim CFO, while we conduct a search for our next finance leader," indicating ongoing executive team transition.

Takeaways

Net revenue-- $81,500,000 in net revenue for fiscal Q2 2025 (GAAP), down 11% year-over-year, driven by a 16% decline in total orders placed, and partially offset by a 1% increase in average order value.

Gross margin-- 45.3%, nearly flat year-over-year, but improved sequentially from 40.3% in fiscal Q1 2025 due to higher product-related margins, partly offset by increased shipping costs.

Adjusted EBITDA-- $500,000 adjusted EBITDA, compared to a $200,000 loss in the prior year, yielding an adjusted EBITDA margin of 0.6% versus negative 0.2% previously in fiscal Q2 2025.

Net loss-- $3,000,000 net loss for fiscal Q2 2025, a significant improvement from $10,800,000 in fiscal Q2 2024, driven by reduced operating loss and lower income tax provision.

Operating expenses-- Operating expenses decreased 15% year-over-year, with fixed costs down 19%, reflecting targeted cost-saving initiatives in fiscal Q2 2025.

Selling and marketing expense-- $22,000,000, down $2,900,000 year-over-year, due to reduced marketing and merchant processing fees in line with lower revenue in fiscal Q2 2025.

General and administrative expense-- $17,500,000 in fiscal Q2 2025, a decline of $3,900,000, or 18% year-over-year, due to reduced headcount and lower variable costs.

Return rates-- Improved by 114 basis points in fiscal Q2 2025, marking the fourth consecutive quarter of improvement due to fit enhancements and policy changes.

Product margin-- Improved by roughly 170 basis points year-over-year in fiscal Q2 2025, reflecting a shift in sales mix to higher-margin categories and successful margin initiatives.

Inventory-- $37,300,000 at the end of fiscal Q2 2025, a decrease of about $300,000, or 1% year-over-year.

Net debt-- $4,200,000 net debt at the end of fiscal Q2 2025, a $4,400,000 reduction from $8,600,000 at the end of fiscal Q4 2024 (non-GAAP).

Wholesale growth-- Addition of three new major partners, and expansion with boutique retailers contributed to channel growth.

Love Rewards loyalty program-- Achieved double-digit membership growth year-over-year in fiscal Q2 2025, resulting in increased total program membership.

Brand equity-- Reached a new all-time high in fiscal Q2 2025, following sequential improvement after a prior record quarter.

Q3 guidance-- Management affirmed the expectation for positive adjusted EBITDA and continued progress on refinancing efforts, referencing active negotiation of a new asset-based revolving credit facility.

Capital expenditures-- Full-year capital expenditures are now expected to be $2,500,000 for fiscal 2025, the low end of the previously guided $2,500,000 to $3,000,000 range.

Direct sourcing strategy-- Management reiterated its intent to double the direct sourcing sales mix by fiscal 2025 year-end from previously less than 5%, supporting margin expansion.

Tariffs and sourcing-- The company is actively managing potential tariff impacts with vendor collaboration, sourcing diversification, strategic pricing, and continued advancement of its direct sourcing strategy.

Summary

The special occasion and bridesmaids categories produced record sales in fiscal Q2 2025, while shoes and separates underperformed and drove the overall revenue shortfall. Management delivered improved profitability metrics, with positive adjusted EBITDA (non-GAAP) and a reduction in net loss to $3,000,000 from $10,800,000 for fiscal Q2 2025. Efforts to streamline the assortment, drive cost efficiency, and advance the direct sourcing mix contributed to margin and expense improvements (GAAP and non-GAAP measures are both referenced in the company’s financial reporting). The company highlighted continued momentum in brand equity, loyalty membership, and wholesale channel expansion as supporting longer-term growth objectives in fiscal Q2 2025.

President and CIO Mark Vos said, "Love Rewards loyalty program membership saw year-over-year double-digit growth in the second quarter, driving an overall increase in total membership."

Management stated, "we entered into an amendment to our forbearance agreement, fifth amendment to our credit agreement with Bank of America," which extended key credit milestones and signaled continuing active negotiations on future financing.

Company campaigns and influencer partnerships were cited as key drivers of engagement, helping brand equity reach a new record level by quarter end.

Crystal Landsem indicated the company expects to double its direct sourcing sales mix by year-end, a shift that enables greater margin expansion and risk mitigation against supply chain concentration and tariffs.

Industry glossary

Adjusted EBITDA: A non-GAAP measure of profitability that excludes interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, and certain other items, to indicate core operational performance.

Direct sourcing: The practice of procuring goods directly from manufacturers or factories, bypassing intermediaries, to optimize costs, improve margins, and increase supply chain control.

Asset-based revolving credit facility: A loan where the company can borrow funds up to a certain limit, based on the value of its assets such as inventory and receivables, and repay or redraw over time as needed.

SKU rationalization: The process of reducing the number of unique products (Stock Keeping Units) in an assortment to focus on higher-performing items, streamline operations, and improve inventory efficiency.
