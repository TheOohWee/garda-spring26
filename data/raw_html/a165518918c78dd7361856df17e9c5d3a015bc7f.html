Limoneira (LMNR) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 12, 2026 at 6:38PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, March 12, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Harold Edwards
  • Chief Financial Officer — Greg Hamm
  • Managing Director, ICR (hosting) — John Mills

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Net Revenue -- $18.2 million, a 47% decrease driven by the transition to Sunkist for lemon sales and related business exits.
  • Agribusiness Revenue -- $16.8 million, reflecting reduced volumes under the Sunkist cadence and the exit from brokerage and farm management operations.
  • Fresh Lemon Sales -- $11.9 million from 681,000 cartons at $17.41 per carton, with volume decline "entirely related to the change in cadence under the Sunkist agreement."
  • Adjusted Net Loss -- $8.5 million, or $0.48 per diluted share, compared to $2.5 million and $0.14 per share in the prior-year quarter.
  • Non-GAAP Adjusted EBITDA -- ($7.7 million) loss versus ($2.3 million) loss in the prior period.
  • Total Costs and Expenses -- $28.8 million, a 27% reduction, attributed to lower agribusiness volumes and eliminated citrus sales and marketing costs.
  • Sunkist SG&A Savings -- Management stated, "We expect to realize approximately $10.0 million in total annual selling, general, and administrative savings for Fiscal 2026."
  • Specific Q1 Charges -- $2.5 million in transition costs ($1.0 million packing house repairs, $0.5 million Chilean exit, $1.0 million foreign exchange) with $1.0 million of repair cost "recovered from insurance proceeds in the second quarter."
  • Long-term Debt -- $89.9 million with $88.0 million net debt after $1.3 million in cash at quarter end.
  • Avocado Acreage -- 1,600 acres planted, with 800 currently bearing fruit; remaining acreage expected to mature over two to four years, targeting near 100% production growth.
  • Real Estate Monetization Pipeline -- $155 million in expected proceeds over five fiscal years from Harvest at Limoneira, Limoneira Lewis Community Builders 2, and East Area 2.
  • Water Rights Monetization -- Ongoing efforts include "actively working to realize meaningful value from our Class 3 Colorado River water rights and Santa Paula Basin conserved pumping rights."
  • EBITDA Contribution -- Management expects planned 50/50 organic recycling joint venture to be EBITDA-accretive when operational in Fiscal 2027.
  • Full-Year Lemon and Avocado Guidance -- 4.0-4.5 million cartons for lemons and 5.0-6.0 million pounds for avocados.
  • Leadership Transition -- Greg Hamm named Chief Financial Officer, succeeding Mark Palamountain.

SUMMARY

Fresh utilization rates showed improvement, indicating a higher proportion of standard lemons sold into the fresh market, which management said could drive positive margin effects later in the fiscal year. Strategic divestitures and property monetizations remain on schedule, with Windfall Farms and Argentinian asset sales expected to conclude by year end, and the large Limco Del Mar entitlement process progressing as a future earnings lever. The cadence of results will be weighted to the third and fourth quarters due to the Sunkist transition, as explicitly noted by management. Early-stage avocado acreage will drive significant capacity expansion as more trees reach bearing age over the next two to four years. Sunkist partnership benefits include not only cost reductions but "enhanced customer access to premium accounts and major U.S. retailers," along with a stabilized pricing environment across the citrus portfolio.

  • Management reiterated anticipation of sequential improvement in operating and financial results with "third and fourth quarters being the strongest periods of the year," reflecting new seasonal cadence.
  • Insurance proceeds related to packing house repairs totaled $1.0 million, recovered in the second quarter, and an additional $1.4 million in insurance proceeds is expected in the second quarter.
  • There will be no increase to the SG&A savings run rate beyond $10 million in Fiscal 2026; management described these as "steady savings throughout the year."
  • Real estate efforts include Phase 3 of Harvest (550 home lots and 300 apartments), 35 acres of East Area 2 Medical Pavilion with potential monetization starting this fiscal year, and evaluation of 221-acre Lincodelmar for residential development potential.
  • Limoneira's water rights position the company to benefit from potential "long-term fallowing programs" tied to Colorado River regulatory developments, with management expressing intent to announce further monetization specifics next quarter.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Carton: A standard unit for fresh lemon sales, typically representing approximately 40 pounds of fruit.
  • Fresh Utilization: The percentage of fruit sold for fresh consumption, as opposed to processing or juice channels; a key citrus margin metric.
  • Class 3 Colorado River Water Rights: Entitlements allowing usage or monetization of a specified volume of Colorado River water, subject to state and federal regulatory agreements.
