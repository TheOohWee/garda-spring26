Chipotle (CMG) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Oct 29, 2025 at 7:48PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Oct. 29, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Restaurant Officer — Scott Boatwright
  • Chief Financial Officer — Adam Rymer

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Risks

  • Rymer said, "we now anticipate full-year comps to decline in the low single-digit range," citing softening transaction trends persisting into October and an ongoing macroeconomic downturn.
  • Rymer stated, "inflation is accelerating into the mid-single-digit range, primarily due to tariffs and rising beef costs, and we anticipate it will remain in this range in 2026."
  • Boatwright reported, "low to middle-income guests further reducing frequency" and highlighted that "our data, is dining out less often due to concerns about the economy and inflation," impacting sales.
  • Boatwright noted, "digital order accuracy" has declined following incentive plan adjustments, prompting an immediate redesign to address consumer experience issues in digital channels.

Takeaways

  • Sales -- $3 billion, a 7.5% increase, with a 0.3% increase in comparable sales.
  • Digital sales -- 36.7% of total sales.
  • Restaurant-level margin -- Restaurant-level margin was 24.5%, down about 100 basis points from last year.
  • Adjusted diluted EPS -- Adjusted diluted EPS was $0.29, up 7% year-over-year on a non-GAAP basis and 4% on a GAAP basis.
  • New restaurant openings -- 84 new restaurants, including 64 Chipotlanes.
  • Cost of sales -- Cost of sales were 30%, down about 60 basis points from last year, with tariffs impacting the quarter by about 30 basis points.
  • Labor costs -- Labor costs were 25.2%, up about 30 basis points from last year due to lower volumes and wage inflation.
  • Marketing spend -- Marketing costs were 3% of sales, up about 90 basis points from last year, and are expected to remain around 3% of sales for Q4 and the full year.
  • General & administrative expense (G&A) -- $147 million GAAP, $139 million non-GAAP, with $8 million retention equity awards included in GAAP.
  • Share repurchases -- $687 million repurchased at a $42.39 average price, with $652 million remaining authorized at quarter-end.
  • Consumer cohorts -- Management estimates guests with household income below $100,000 represent ~40% of sales, with declining frequency; the 25 to 35-year-old group is particularly affected.
  • Pricing -- Management reported running about 2% price from the increase taken in December, compared to the industry’s 4%; there are no plans to fully offset inflation in 2026, which will pressure margins in the near term.
  • HEAT equipment rollout -- Now in 75 restaurants, with positive field feedback on culinary and labor efficiency; full national implementation is targeted within three years.
  • Menu innovation -- Launch of Adobo Ranch and Red Chimichurri sauces drove incremental transactions, with the latter achieving low double-digit incidents and boosting carne asada trial.
  • Catering and group occasions -- Catering pilot in 60 Chicago locations and “Build Your Own Chipotle” experience for groups of four to six are in early rollout; both aim to expand occasions without cannibalization.
  • Rewards program -- Chipotle U enrollment is increasing, and loyalty initiatives such as Summer of Extras, Chipotle IQ, and FreePotle have accelerated comps among loyalty members.
  • Unit productivity -- New restaurant productivity in North America averages 80%, and year-two cash-on-cash returns remain around 60%.
  • Growth outlook -- 2026 guidance includes 350–370 new restaurants, with 10–15 partner-operated international outlets and 1–2 company-owned locations in Europe.
  • Tax rate -- Effective tax rate was 23.1% and 22.8% non-GAAP; underlying tax rate for fiscal 2025 (ending Dec. 31, 2025) is expected at 25%–27%.
  • Balance sheet -- Quarter-end cash, restricted cash, and investments totaled $1.8 billion; the company has no debt.

Summary

Chipotle Mexican Grill (CMG 0.44%) delivered $3 billion in sales and grew digital channel penetration to 36.7%, yet experienced a sharp decline in transaction trends, most pronounced among low and middle-income households and younger adults. Management forecast low to mid-single-digit declines in comparable sales for Q4 and the full year, with macro headwinds and ongoing inflationary pressure constraining the company’s ability to offset costs through price increases. Strategic initiatives, including menu innovation, HEAT kitchen equipment expansion, catering pilots, and an evolving digital loyalty platform, have partially mitigated headwinds but have yet to reverse the underlying decrease in transaction frequency. The board expanded the company’s share repurchase authorization, and guidance for 2026 targets robust new unit openings, especially internationally, even as consumer uncertainty persists.

  • Rymer said, "we will be rolling off two points of price in early December," signaling further near-term revenue headwinds from pricing rollbacks.
  • Boatwright stated, "Through our research, we found that over 90% of Gen Z consumers say they would visit a restaurant just for a new sauce," underscoring menu innovation’s importance for attracting younger guests.
  • Rymer cautioned, "The overall impact as it impacts our overall comp is increasing," yet noted this has been stable despite accelerated new restaurant growth.
  • Boatwright reported, "accuracy has fallen off" for digital orders after incentive changes, and the company is responding by redesigning its system-wide bonus targets for improved accuracy and guest satisfaction.
  • Management highlighted continued confidence in long-term goals, aiming for 7,000 North American locations and surpassing $4 million in average unit volumes, with a "Recipe for Growth" framework guiding operational, digital, and marketing execution plans.

Industry glossary

  • Chipotlane: Drive-through pickup lane for digital orders unique to Chipotle Mexican Grill restaurants.
  • HEAT equipment package: High-efficiency kitchen equipment suite designed to improve throughput and food consistency, comprising a dual-sided plancha, three-pan rice cooker, and high-capacity fryer.
  • Incident rate: The percentage of checks or transactions with a specific menu item or promotion (e.g., new sauce or dip).
  • LTO: Limited-Time Offer—special menu item or promotion available for a defined period.
  • AUV: Average Unit Volume—average annual sales per restaurant unit.
  • ROAS: Return On Ad Spend—revenue generated per dollar of advertising expenditure.
  • Cash-on-cash return: Ratio measuring yearly pre-tax cash flow relative to the total cash invested in an asset or project, often cited by restaurant operators for new unit economics.
