Earnings Call Transcript

Novanta (NOVT) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 24, 2026 at 9:47AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Feb. 24, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chair and Chief Executive Officer — Matthijs Glastra
  • Chief Financial Officer — Robert J. Buckley
  • Corporate Finance Leader — Ray Nash

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $981 million for the year, representing a record annual result, with fourth quarter reported growth of 9%, organic growth of 2%, and sequential growth of 4%.
  • Bookings -- Increased 25% year over year and 12% sequentially in the fourth quarter, resulting in a book-to-bill ratio of 1.11; all individual businesses achieved double-digit bookings growth and a positive book-to-bill for the first time since 2022.
  • New Product Sales -- Grew over 80% year over year in the fourth quarter and over 60% for the full year, raising the full year vitality index to 22% of sales and 24% in the quarter.
  • Medical End Markets Revenue Mix -- Sales to medical end markets were 53% of total sales, advanced industrial markets accounted for 47%; medical consumables comprised 15% of sales and grew at a double-digit rate.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $61 million in the fourth quarter, up 17% year over year, with a 23.5% adjusted EBITDA margin; $245 million to $250 million expected for full year 2026.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $0.91 in the quarter, up 20%, and $3.29 for the full year, up 7%; 2026 guidance is $3.50 to $3.65, with up to 11% growth and specific impacts from equity fundraise dilution ($0.22-$0.24) and an all-employee grant ($0.14).
  • Gross Margin -- Fourth quarter adjusted gross margin was 45.5%, down 150 basis points year over year, and is expected to expand to approximately 47% for 2026, reflecting a 100 basis point increase, as regional manufacturing transfers complete.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $9 million in the fourth quarter (versus $62 million prior year), with full year at $64 million and 2026 guidance of $145 million to $185 million; lower figures in 2025 trace to higher inventory and transition costs from regional manufacturing moves, with improvement expected as site moves complete.
  • Debt and Cash Position -- Gross debt ended at $260 million, cash balance at $381 million, net debt at negative $121 million, and a net leverage ratio of negative 0.5, due to paydown of over $300 million of revolver and proceeds from a $600 million equity raise in November; total acquisition capacity stands at nearly $1.5 billion.
  • Segment Performance -- Automation Enabling Technologies revenue up 2% with book-to-bill of 1.16 and bookings up 33%; Medical Solutions revenue up 16% with book-to-bill of 1.07 and bookings up 17%; advanced surgery business experienced 15% growth; precision manufacturing revenue declined 3% year over year but grew 8% sequentially; precision medicine sales grew 16% year over year and 4% sequentially, with Keyon acquisition contributing $9 million in the quarter.
  • 2026 Revenue and Growth Guidance -- GAAP revenue expected to be $1,030 million to $1,050 million, representing 4%-6% organic growth; quarterly organic growth guided at 1%-3% in Q1, 5%-7% in Q2, and similar rates through the year.
  • Capital Allocation -- $19 million in stock repurchased in the quarter, nearly $40 million for the year; "acquisitions remain our top capital allocation priority," but opportunistic buybacks will continue based on value against intrinsic future value.
  • R&D and SG&A Expenses -- R&D was $23 million (9% of sales) in the quarter, $95 million (10%) for the year; SG&A was $46 million (18%) in the quarter and $181 million (18%) for the year; 2026 guidance for combined R&D and SG&A is $294 million to $298 million (about 28% of sales).
  • Tax Rate -- Non-GAAP tax rate was 20.5% in 2025, expected to be around 21% in 2026; first quarter rate guided between 19%-20% due to timing of tax benefits.
  • Book-to-Bill and Backlog Detail -- Book-to-bill for precision manufacturing business was 1.2 (50% bookings growth, over $100 million backlog; backlog is about two times revenue); robotics and automation business had a 1.13 book-to-bill (25% bookings growth; backlog about 1.5 times revenue); advanced surgery business had a 1.12 book-to-bill (backlog about two times revenue, 15% yearly growth); precision medicine had a 1.01 book-to-bill (backlog about two times revenue, 22% bookings growth).

RISKS

  • Fourth quarter margins and cash flow came in below expectations set in the prior call, due to prioritized customer deliveries over the pace of regional manufacturing transfers, resulting in higher dual running costs and elevated inventory.
  • Operating cash flow for 2025 dropped to $9 million in Q4 and $64 million for the year, attributed to the same regional manufacturing dynamics and higher inventory, though management expects this is temporary as site moves are completed.
  • 2026 EPS growth will be negatively impacted by $0.22-$0.24 from the 2025 equity fundraise and by a $0.14 effect from an all-employee equity grant, with these items described as headwinds for reported earnings growth.

SUMMARY

Novanta (NOVT +1.47%) reported record annual and quarterly revenue, with broad-based bookings momentum across all businesses and robust new product contributions driving a notable increase in backlog and a book-to-bill above 1. The company demonstrated continued year-over-year margin pressure and below-target cash flow due to deliberate decisions to prioritize customer deliveries during ongoing regional manufacturing transitions, but outlined a clear recovery plan with improved guidance for 2026 margins and cash generation as these initiatives are completed. Management highlighted the strengthening acquisition pipeline, expanded financial flexibility following a major equity raise, and repeated focus on deploying capital toward strategic, high-return growth markets, particularly medical technologies, consumables, and embedded software-enabled solutions.

  • The acquisition of Keyon outperformed initial expectations and delivered $9 million incremental revenue in the fourth quarter, with recurring revenue streams helping support long-term prospects for the precision medicine business.
  • Management emphasized unique market positioning in GPU board drilling, stating, "Our air bearing spindles are currently the only qualified supplier for drilling AI-driven GPU boards," positioning the company as a direct beneficiary of AI compute infrastructure expansion.
  • For the full year, company-wide design wins increased more than 20%, with the vitality index for new product revenue at 24% in the fourth quarter and 22% for the year, demonstrating accelerated innovation impact.
  • Company-wide guidance for 2026 calls for 4%-6% organic growth and full year adjusted EBITDA margin of 24%, with the strongest organic revenue acceleration expected after completion of manufacturing transitions by midyear.
  • The company's net leverage ratio is negative 0.5, reflecting a positive net cash position and expanded acquisition capacity approaching $1.5 billion; stock repurchases continue opportunistically but are secondary to M&A priorities.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Vitality Index: The percentage of revenue derived from new products launched within a defined recent period, indicating innovation-driven sales momentum.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: The ratio of incoming customer bookings to product shipments (billed revenue) in the same period; a value over 1 indicates rising backlog and demand outpacing shipments.
  • Dual Running Costs: Temporary additional expenses incurred when operating both legacy and new (e.g., regionalized) manufacturing operations simultaneously during transition periods.
  • Insufflator: A surgical device used to deliver and regulate gas flow, primarily for medical procedures such as laparoscopic or minimally invasive surgeries.
  • Embedded Software: Software that is integrated into hardware components or subsystems to enable intelligent or automated functions within product platforms.
