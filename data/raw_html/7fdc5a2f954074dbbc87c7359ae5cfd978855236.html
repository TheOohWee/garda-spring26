Martin Marietta (MLM) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 11:23AM EST

Date

Wednesday, Feb. 11, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman, President and Chief Executive Officer — Ward Nye
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Michael Petro
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Jacklyn Gamble

Takeaways

  • Aggregates revenue -- $1.2 billion in the quarter, an 8% increase, reflecting pricing and volume momentum.
  • Aggregates gross profit -- $420 million, up 11%, with gross profit per ton rising 9% to $8.59 and gross margin expanding 93 basis points to 34%.
  • Aggregates segment full-year performance -- Revenue of $5 billion (11% growth), driven by 6.9% pricing increase and 3.8% volume growth; gross profit rose 16% to $1.7 billion, while gross margin widened 143 basis points to 34%.
  • Price-cost spread -- 239 basis points for aggregates in the year, supported by favorable pricing exceeding cost inflation.
  • Other building materials -- Revenue declined 8% to $992 million; gross profit fell 18% to $98 million, mainly due to the Minnesota asphalt business and California divestiture impact.
  • Specialties segment -- Record annual revenue of $441 million and gross profit of $137 million, reflecting both organic growth and Premier Magnesia acquisition contributions.
  • Full-year building materials revenue -- $5.7 billion, up 7% from the prior year.
  • Full-year building materials gross profit -- $1.8 billion, up 13% with gross margin rising 173 basis points to 31%.
  • Cash flow from operations -- Surged 22% to $1.8 billion, a new record, enabling $812 million in acquisitions, $680 million of plant and equipment reinvestments, and $647 million returned to shareholders.
  • Liquidity and leverage -- Ended the year with $1.2 billion in liquidity and a net debt to adjusted EBITDA ratio of 2.3 times, within the target range.
  • Capital expenditures guidance -- 2026 capital spending planned at $575 million, a 29% reduction designed to boost free cash flow for M&A and share buybacks.
  • Adjusted EBITDA guidance -- Midpoint outlook for 2026 consolidated adjusted EBITDA at approximately $2.49 billion, inclusive of discontinued operations.
  • Aggregates guidance -- Low double-digit gross profit growth forecast for 2026, underpinned by low single-digit shipment growth and mid-single-digit pricing improvement.
  • Specialties guidance -- High teens gross profit growth expected in 2026, primarily due to full-year Premier Magnesia contributions.
  • SOAR 2025 achievements -- 208 basis points price-cost spread over five years and a compound annual growth rate above 13% in aggregates gross profit per ton; $16 billion in transactions executed or announced.
  • Total shareholder return -- 126% return over five years, about 30 percentage points above the S&P 500 Index.
  • Infrastructure segment outlook -- Company expects "mid-single digits" growth in infrastructure, which accounted for 37% of business, supported by IIJA funds and robust state DOT budgets up 7% in top ten states.
  • Free cash flow outlook -- Lower capital spending is expected to significantly increase free cash flow dedicated for acquisitions and share repurchases.

Summary

Martin Marietta Materials (MLM +0.96%) delivered record financial and operational results, highlighted by meaningful margin expansion and strategic execution of portfolio enhancements. Management detailed that continued infrastructure investment, significant data center and energy sector activity, and favorable state and local funding frameworks form the basis for high single-digit revenue and adjusted EBITDA growth expectations for 2026. The company emphasized the benefits and anticipated rollout of quarry network optimization initiatives, noting realized cost savings in pilot regions that may provide a broader margin opportunity, pending a full assessment by midyear. Strategy for 2026 is underpinned by a disciplined capital allocation framework, with management confirming confidence in achieving the guided price-cost spread and maintaining a resilient balance sheet while awaiting the pending Quikrete transaction.

  • Management stated, "71% of IIJA highway and bridge funds have been obligated. However, only 48% has been disbursed," suggesting a significant forward pipeline of projects and funding tailwind.
  • Chairman Nye indicated, "we're guiding to 2026 consolidated adjusted EBITDA of approximately $2.49 billion, inclusive of contributions from discontinued operations," with a commitment to update guidance once the Quikrete transaction closes.
  • Network optimization initiatives already reduced cost of goods sold per ton in Western pilot regions in the fourth quarter; company plans to publish a comprehensive impact assessment by midyear.
  • Specialties gross profit guidance for 2026 of $160 million reflects measured expectations and the full-year contribution from Premier Magnesia, with management clarifying expected margin stabilization at pro forma levels.
  • Pricing expectations for 2026 are "largely for what's in place," supported by completed customer discussions; future mix headwinds from M&A or product mix changes could result in optical changes to average selling price.
  • Company reported its aggregates shipment guidance for 2026 at 2% growth at the midpoint, factoring in mixed trends across infrastructure, nonresidential, and residential end-markets with identified upside potential in public works and heavy nonresidential sectors.
  • Liquefied natural gas and data center construction, including multi-double-digit shipment growth in those categories, are expected to supplement public sector demand through the year.
  • Cash deployment priorities remain unchanged: targeted M&A, capacity enhancements, and returning capital to shareholders.

Industry glossary

  • SOAR (Strategic Operating Analysis and Review): Martin Marietta Materials' long-term strategic and operational improvement initiative, referenced in targets SOAR 2025 and SOAR 2030.
  • IIJA (Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act): U.S. federal law providing funding for infrastructure, cited as a major driver of public sector demand and multi-year construction activity.
  • COGS per ton: Cost of goods sold per ton, a key cost-efficiency and margin metric within aggregates operations.
  • DOT (Department of Transportation) budgets: State-specific government funding programs for transportation infrastructure, relevant to regional demand signals within Martin Marietta Materials' footprint.
  • Quikrete transaction: Pending asset exchange with The Quikrete Companies, which upon closing will trigger a restatement of 2026 guidance to reflect acquired asset contributions and discontinued operations.
  • Premier Magnesia: Acquired company contributing to the specialties segment's revenue and gross profit growth.
  • Base stone: Lower-priced aggregate used as foundational material in construction; referenced regarding potential optical impacts to average selling price and mix.
  • PreciseIQ: Martin Marietta Materials' sales technology platform, enabling more agile quoting and improved win rates, planned for full company-wide rollout by mid-2026.
