Roku (ROKU) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 6:24PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 12, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Anthony Wood
  • Chief Financial Officer — Dan Jedda
  • President, Roku Media — Charlie Collier

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Platform revenue growth -- Platform revenue grew 18% for the quarter and full year, hitting $1.2 billion in Q4.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Achieved record quarterly adjusted EBITDA of $169 million and full-year adjusted EBITDA of $421 million, with margin expansion of 255 basis points.
  • Net income -- Net income reached $80 million in Q4, noted as the highest reported to date.
  • Free cash flow -- Record free cash flow of $484 million for the year, over 100% higher year over year, and stated guidance that free cash flow will exceed adjusted EBITDA in the coming periods.
  • Share repurchase -- Bought back $150 million in stock during Q4, achieving near 0% dilution, the company's lowest reported level.
  • 2026 platform revenue and EBITDA outlook -- Platform revenue is projected to grow more than 21% in Q1 and 18% for the full year, with 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $635 million, representing over 50% year-over-year growth and margin expansion to 11.6%.
  • Platform gross margin -- Platform gross margin finished at 52%; company expects 51%-52% range for the current year and does not anticipate significant quarter-to-quarter variability.
  • Streaming households -- Company on track to surpass 100 million streaming households this year.
  • Distribution expansion -- Added new first-party TV launches at Best Buy and Target, broadened partnerships with TV OEMs including TCL and Hisense, and highlighted cost advantages from moving TV production to Mexico.
  • International progress -- Achieved scale in Canada and Mexico, where monetization efforts are ramping; launched premium subscriptions in Mexico and FAST channels in Brazil.
  • Advertising partnerships -- Expanded deep integrations with third-party demand-side platforms, including Amazon, Yahoo!, AppLovin, and others, with partnerships ramping over time rather than delivering immediate material impact.
  • AI application -- Anthony Wood said, "AI is a significant opportunity for Roku. We view it as a powerful tailwind to our business... not a disruptor, and we're integrating it across our entire technology stack."
  • SMB advertising -- Roku Ads Manager is capturing small- and medium-sized business ad spend, with management emphasizing this segment's performance focus but indicating "they're not lower margin for us."
  • Home screen monetization -- Testing new home screen designs and ad units, citing ongoing efforts to increase engagement, impressions, and click-through rates.
  • Owned and operated subscription growth -- Howdy and Frndly integrations are driving incremental revenue and engagement, with plans to expand Howdy off Roku platforms.
  • Stock buyback program -- $250 million remains authorized under the current share repurchase authorization.
  • Deferred tax asset -- Company holds more than $1 billion in deferred tax assets, which is expected to keep cash taxes low for years.
  • Operating expense growth -- Operating expenses increased 3% last year, management guides to mid-single-digit OpEx growth due to engineering investments and declining share-based compensation costs.

Summary

Roku (ROKU +2.43%) reported record platform and financial performance, including all-time highs in adjusted EBITDA, free cash flow, and net income, with management guiding for sustained double-digit platform revenue growth and margin expansion. Data-supported leadership in U.S. streaming households and accelerated international scale were disclosed, with monetization ramping in select non-U.S. markets. Strategic investments in platform features, distribution, first-party TVs, and expanded advertising partnerships were each highlighted as growth enablers. Management explicitly stated that artificial intelligence is viewed as a business tailwind powering both ad and user experience improvements, not a disruptive threat, and detailed ongoing investments in proprietary ad technology and revenue diversification initiatives.

  • Anthony Wood stated, "Nearly half of all TV streaming in the U.S. happens on the Roku platform."
  • Company plans to achieve over $1 billion in free cash flow by 2028, with clear guidance that free cash flow will consistently exceed adjusted EBITDA due to a CapEx-light strategy.
  • Management highlighted, "we expect to see the impact predominantly in the second half of the year as these cycles take time to scale."
  • "Our ARPU is actually quite strong in Canada," according to Dan Jedda, indicating growing international ARPU alongside streaming household expansion.
  • Strategic focus on premium subscriptions and ad tech was explicitly identified as a key driver of multi-year compound revenue growth, alongside secular industry tailwinds from linear TV migration to streaming.

Industry glossary

  • ARPU: Average Revenue Per User, a measure of monetization efficiency per streaming household or account.
  • DSP: Demand-Side Platform, an ad technology platform that enables automated buying of advertising inventory across multiple sources.
  • FAST: Free Ad-Supported Streaming Television, a streaming service format delivering linear, channel-style content supported by advertising rather than subscription revenue.
  • Roku OS: Roku’s proprietary, purpose-built operating system tailored for television streaming devices and smart TVs.
  • SMB: Small- and Medium-Sized Businesses, advertisers with relatively modest budgets targeted by performance and self-serve ad solutions.
  • M&E: Media and Entertainment, referencing the content and advertising sector historically dominant in Roku home screen ads.
