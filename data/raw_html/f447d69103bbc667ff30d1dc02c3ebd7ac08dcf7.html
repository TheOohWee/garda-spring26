Illumina (ILMN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 5:37PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 5, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Jacob Thaysen
  • Chief Financial Officer — Ankur Dhingra
  • Chief Medical Officer — Eric Green
  • General Manager, China — Jenny [surname not provided]
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations — Conor McNamara

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $1.16 billion, up 5% year over year on a reported basis and 4% on a constant currency basis, driven by clinical consumables strength, NovaSeq X placements, and outperformance in China.
  • Clinical Consumables Revenue ex-China -- Grew 20% in the quarter, with management attributing acceleration to increased adoption of sequencing-based diagnostics and greater demand for comprehensive genomic profiling and whole genome sequencing in oncology and genetic disease.
  • China Revenue -- $55 million, exceeding expectations but down $25 million from the prior year, with instruments revenue in the region declining 55% due to export restrictions.
  • Sequencing Consumables Revenue -- $755 million, up 8% year over year and 11% excluding China, driven by high throughput volume growth primarily on NovaSeq X instruments.
  • NovaSeq X Transition -- 55% of revenue now transitioned to NovaSeq X; approximately 90% of research high throughput volumes and more than two-thirds of clinical volumes have converted, with transition expected to be substantially complete by 2026.
  • Total Sequencing Output -- Connected high and mid throughput instruments achieved more than 30% year-over-year growth in gigabase output, led by clinical customer expansion.
  • Sequencing Instruments Revenue -- $154 million, flat year over year; over 100 NovaSeq Xs placed in the quarter, with 60%+ to clinical customers and 890 total installed base at period end.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses -- $502 million, a reduction of 5% or $24 million year over year, reflecting cost optimizations and focused investment allocation.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin -- 23.7% for the quarter, expanding 400 basis points year over year due to increased operating leverage and cost reduction efforts.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $1.35 per diluted share, up approximately 42% year over year and above guidance, with 2025 EPS of $4.84 up 16% year over year.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $267 million for the quarter; $931 million for the full year.
  • Share Repurchases -- 337,000 shares repurchased for $42 million average price of $124.12 per share in Q4; $643 million remaining on authorization at quarter end.
  • Somalogic Acquisition -- Closed for $350 million upfront plus potential royalties/milestones, funded with cash on hand, expected to add 1.5-2 points of revenue growth but dilute 2026 EPS by $0.18 and reduce operating margin by 100 basis points.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Company expects revenue of $4.5-$4.6 billion (4%-6% reported growth, 2%-4% ex-China organic), operating margin of 23.3%-23.5%, and EPS of $5.00-$5.20 including Somalogic dilution.
  • Segment Growth Expectations -- Clinical consumables guided to double-digit to mid-teens growth, research/applied consumables to decline mid- to high single digits, and sequencing instruments flat to slightly down.
  • Multiomics Integration -- Launch of Illumina Connected Multiomics software and planned 2026 introduction of spatial transcriptomics and Constellation MAP Read technology positions company for end-to-end workflow expansion.
  • BioInsight Launch -- BioInsight platform, including the Billion Cell Atlas, introduced with early collaborations from AstraZeneca, Merck, and Eli Lilly, and further pharma engagement expected.
  • Geographical Reporting Structure Change -- From January 2026, company will realign and report under new geographical segments, with historical reconciliation to be provided.
  • Balance Sheet -- Ended quarter with $1.63 billion cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments, and gross leverage of approximately 1.6x gross debt to last twelve months EBITDA.

RISKS

  • Management cited that research and applied consumables revenue continues to face "mid to high single-digit" declines for 2026, reflecting continued funding uncertainty and muted academic demand.
  • In China, instrument sales remain negatively impacted by ongoing export restrictions, with no anticipated step-up in instrument sales in the first half of 2026 and China sales expected to be a one-point headwind to total revenue growth.
  • Guidance assumes Somalogic acquisition will "dilute" 2026 EPS by $0.18 and lower operating margin by 100 basis points.
  • Management noted tariff headwinds of 205 basis points negatively impacted non-GAAP gross margin, more than offsetting sequential margin gains excluding tariffs.

SUMMARY

Illumina (ILMN 2.22%) delivered quarterly revenue growth of 5% year over year and accelerated clinical consumables revenue ex-China, underpinned by strong NovaSeq X adoption and expanding sequencing output. Operating metrics reflected substantial leverage from multiyear cost initiatives, as non-GAAP operating margin rose by 400 basis points to 23.7% and non-GAAP EPS increased 42%, driven by mix shift and disciplined expense management. The company completed the Somalogic acquisition, strengthening its position in multiomics and proteomics, but incorporated EPS dilution and margin impact into 2026 guidance. Forward commentary highlighted double-digit clinical consumables growth, moderation in research and applied segments, and sustained capital returns through share repurchases.

  • Annual free cash flow reached $931 million, supporting share repurchases totaling $42 million in the quarter.
  • The installed base of NovaSeq X instruments expanded to 890, with transition in research nearly complete and clinical conversion reaching more than two-thirds.
  • Management launched the BioInsight platform and Billion Cell Atlas data product, initiating collaborations with major pharmaceutical companies and broadening the addressable market in biopharma.
  • The company will introduce new segment reporting aligned to commercial structure from Q1 2026, increasing transparency for investors.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • NovaSeq X: Illumina’s high-throughput sequencing platform enabling large-scale genomic and multiomic applications.
  • Multiomics: Integration of multiple “omics” technologies—such as genomics, proteomics, and epigenomics—to provide a holistic view of biological systems.
  • BioInsight: Illumina’s data, software, and AI-driven platform aimed at drug discovery through integrated biological modeling and analytics.
  • Billion Cell Atlas: Data product mapping human biology at single-cell resolution, used by biopharma for drug discovery and target validation.
  • Somalogic: Recently acquired company specializing in affinity proteomics with the Aptima platform for high-throughput protein analysis.
  • MRD (Minimal Residual Disease): Detection of small volumes of disease remaining after treatment, particularly relevant in oncology sequencing applications.
  • Pull-through: The downstream revenue generated from consumables sold to users of installed sequencing instruments.
  • Spatial Transcriptomics: A genomic technology for spatially resolving gene expression patterns within tissues.
