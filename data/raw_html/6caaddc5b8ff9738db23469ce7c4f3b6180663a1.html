Adient (ADNT) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 4, 2026 at 10:46AM EST

Wednesday, February 4, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Jerome Dorlack
  • Chief Financial Officer — Mark Oswald
  • Investor Relations — Linda Conrad

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $3.6 billion, reflecting a 4% increase year over year, mainly driven by FX tailwinds from Europe.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $207 million, up 6% year over year, representing a margin of 5.7% (an increase of 10 basis points).
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $28 million, or $0.35 per share, for the quarter.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $15 million in the quarter, exceeding internal expectations, with a full-year outlook now raised to $125 million.
  • Consolidated Sales Growth Drivers -- $149 million year-over-year increase mainly attributable to FX, improved volume, and favorable pricing.
  • Cash Position -- $855 million at quarter end, with total liquidity of $1.7 billion including undrawn credit lines.
  • Share Repurchases -- $25 million was returned to shareholders this quarter through buybacks, with $110 million of repurchase authorization remaining.
  • Debt Position and Leverage -- Gross debt of $2.4 billion and net debt of $1.5 billion as of December 31, 2025; net leverage was 1.7x, within the company’s 1.5x-2x target range.
  • Guidance Raised -- Annual sales guidance increased to approximately $14.6 billion (from $14.4 billion), adjusted EBITDA to $880 million (from $845 million), and free cash flow to $125 million (from $90 million).
  • Onshoring Business Wins -- Approximately 150,000 direct and 25,000 indirect onshoring units awarded, with a total combined incremental revenue opportunity estimated at $500 million ($300 million impacts fiscal 2027, full $500 million in 2028).
  • China Revenue Composition -- Expected exit rate of 60% revenue from domestic China OEMs, and management anticipates continued double-digit growth in China through fiscal 2028.
  • Innovation Initiative -- ModuTech, a modular seat platform, is anticipated to drive upwards of 20% value-chain savings and almost 15% reduction in JIT floor space requirements.
  • EMEA Regional Performance -- Sales trailed the market due to customer mix and deliberate portfolio actions; launches included Ford compact crossover SUV metals business and Mercedes GLB complete seat business.
  • Sustainability Achievements -- 42% reduction in Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions since 2019, 30% of electricity from renewable sources, 6% reduction in water withdrawal year over year, and 80% of suppliers assessed for sustainability.
  • Restructuring Spend -- Fiscal 2026 restructuring primarily in Europe, estimated at $120-$130 million; spending expected to decrease in fiscal 2027.
  • Asia Growth and Launch Costs -- EBITDA negatively impacted by higher engineering and launch costs, though year-over-year equity income from joint ventures improved by $8 million, mainly in China.
  • Term Loan B Repricing -- Refinancing completed post-quarter reduced costs by 25 basis points, saving approximately $1.5 million annually.
  • Joint Venture Equity Income Timing -- Dividend receipts from joint ventures are weighted toward the second half of the year, especially for the company’s largest JV.

Summary

Adient (ADNT 0.39%) delivered revenue and adjusted EBITDA growth, exceeding internal expectations for both free cash flow and margin, while simultaneously strengthening its balance sheet through maintained cash reserves and reduced financing costs. Management raised full-year guidance for revenue, adjusted EBITDA, and free cash flow, citing improved North American vehicle production forecasts and robust new business wins, particularly from onshoring and domestic China OEMs. Significant innovation initiatives, notably ModuTech modular seating, position the company to drive additional operational and capital efficiencies. The company outlined targeted restructuring investments in Europe and emphasized sustainability milestones as operational differentiators valued by global OEM customers.

  • CEO Dorlack asserted, "Adient plc will be a net beneficiary from onshoring," highlighting continued strategic customer alignment and execution in North America and Asia.
  • Asia region’s investment in engineering and launches elevated costs in the short term, but management expects positive business performance for the full year in APAC.
  • European sales underperformed the market, but new business wins are expected to offset program exits and to be margin accretive as production schedules normalize.
  • Management stated restructuring expenditures in Europe will decrease in fiscal 2027, with the future scale of restructuring dependent on customer program decisions.
  • Adient plc refinanced its term loan B, reducing borrowing costs and lowering annual interest expense.
  • Approximately $25 million in cash return was distributed to shareholders via share repurchases, reflecting an ongoing balanced capital allocation strategy.

Industry glossary

  • Onshoring: Shifting manufacturing operations or awarded business from outside U.S. borders into the United States to align with customer localization strategies.
  • JIT (Just-in-Time): Inventory and logistics methodology aimed at minimizing in-process inventory and increasing manufacturing efficiency by delivering parts only as needed.
  • Conquest Business/Win: Gaining a new customer contract or production program from a competitor, typically resulting in incremental revenue opportunity.
  • Scope 1 and Scope 2 Emissions: Standard measures of a company's direct greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1) and indirect emissions from purchased energy (Scope 2).
  • ModuTech: Adient plc’s modular seating platform designed to simplify seat assembly, enable higher automation, and enhance value-chain savings.
