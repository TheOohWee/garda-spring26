Adeia (ADEA) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 4, 2025 at 10:00AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, Nov. 3, 2025, at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Paul E. Davis
  • Chief Financial Officer — Keith A. Jones
  • SVP, Corporate Development and Investor Relations — Chris Chaney

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • The company revised its 2025 full-year revenue guidance downward due to the unlikelihood of closing a license agreement with AMD (AMD +3.50%) following the filing of patent litigation.
  • Non-GAAP litigation expenses are projected to increase by approximately $3 million in the fourth quarter of 2025, due to new legal actions against AMD and ongoing litigation with Walt Disney (DIS 0.62%).

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $87.3 million in revenue for the third quarter of 2025, consistent with management’s previously stated expectations.
  • Non-pay TV recurring revenue -- Increased 31% year-over-year in the third quarter compared to the same period in the prior year, evidencing ongoing expansion into new verticals outside core pay TV.
  • Non-pay TV recurring revenue growth since separation -- Non-pay TV recurring revenue increased 81% since separation in the third quarter of 2025, with growth driven by new agreements in semiconductors, OTT, social media, and e-commerce.
  • License portfolio growth -- Patent assets grew from approximately 9,500 at separation to over 13,000 as of the third quarter of 2025, an increase of more than 35%, driven primarily by internal R&D since separation.
  • Litigation and legal developments -- Multiple patent infringement lawsuits filed against AMD in the Western District of Texas, covering ten patents (seven hybrid bonding, three advanced process node); litigation expected to extend to at least 2027.
  • Disney litigation progress -- Litigation proceeding on all six patents in Delaware; preliminary injunction granted in Brazil and upheld on appeal; three European cases scheduled for 2026 trial.
  • Third-quarter operating expenses -- $37.1 million on a non-GAAP basis in the third quarter of 2025, decreasing by $3.5 million, or 9%, compared to the previous quarter (non-GAAP), primarily from administrative and personnel cost reductions.
  • Litigation expense -- $5.2 million in litigation expense in the third quarter of 2025, down $2 million, or 28%, compared to the prior quarter, mainly due to reduced spending on Canadian cases, partially offset by increased AMD and Disney litigation costs.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $50.7 million, with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 58% in the third quarter of 2025.
  • Cash and equivalents -- $115.1 million at quarter-end, with $17.8 million in cash generated from operations in the third quarter of 2025.
  • Debt reduction -- $11.1 million in principal payments in the third quarter of 2025; cumulative debt repayments since separation now total $311.6 million.
  • 2025 full-year revenue guidance -- Revised non-GAAP revenue guidance to $360 million-$380 million for fiscal year 2025 (ending Dec. 31, 2025), reflecting changes following the AMD litigation; guidance previously incorporated an anticipated AMD license agreement.
  • 2025 operating expense guidance -- Lowered non-GAAP operating expense guidance to $160 million-$164 million for fiscal year 2025, including all expected litigation costs for Disney and AMD actions.
  • Interest expense guidance -- Projected at $40 million-$41 million for the full year 2025.
  • Other income guidance -- Expected in the range of $5.5 million-$6.5 million for the full year 2025.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin guidance -- Adjusted EBITDA margin guidance is approximately 56% for the full year 2025.
  • Dividend -- Paid 5¢ per share in the third quarter, with another 5¢ per share dividend approved for December 15, 2025.
  • Pipeline and deal execution -- Multiple major licensing opportunities remain active and could roll into 2026 if not completed in 2025, potentially driving substantial revenue growth in 2026.
  • RapidCool commercial progress -- Early-stage engagements ongoing following public rollout; identified as a mid- to long-term revenue opportunity with no near-term financial impact disclosed.
  • Semiconductor recurring revenue -- Approximately $5.2 million in the third quarter of 2025, as confirmed by management, with sequential growth attributed to 3D NAND market momentum.

SUMMARY

Adeia (ADEA 0.70%) reduced its annual revenue outlook for fiscal year 2025 (ending Dec. 31, 2025) due to the filing of patent litigation against AMD and the decreased likelihood of closing a related license agreement, while reiterating confidence in its pipeline and the durability of its recurring revenue streams. Management highlighted substantive progress across multiple litigation fronts, including favorable developments in ongoing cases with Disney, Shaw, and Videotron, as well as the expansion of both its customer base and patent portfolio. Strategic focus continues on pipeline opportunities spanning semiconductors, OTT, and e-commerce, with upside potential for 2026 revenue if delayed deals close. The company reinforced capital discipline through cost controls, sustained dividend payments, continued debt reduction, and non-dilutive expansion of the intellectual property portfolio.

  • CEO Davis emphasized, "To the extent that they do not close in 2025, they become a strong catalyst to growth in 2026."
  • Management indicated that, even with revised guidance, "there still remain opportunities which could potentially result in revenue beyond the noted range for 2025."
  • Litigation filed against AMD covers patents that management described as significant, with "eight of them do not expire until mid-2030 or beyond," according to Paul E. Davis, potentially impacting current and next-generation AMD products.
  • Paul E. Davis described the business model as "highly cash-generative," citing over "$500 million in cash from operations."
  • Chief Executive Officer Davis noted, "we're seeing really strength in that recurring revenue business, kind of going forward," and explained that any delay in major deals could position the company for "significant growth that we could see in 2026 as compared to 2025."
  • Growth in the semiconductor vertical is attributed in part to the adoption of hybrid bonding and 3D NAND technologies among major chipmakers, with upcoming industry launches expected to drive further engagement.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Hybrid Bonding: An advanced semiconductor packaging process enabling direct wafer-to-wafer connections, often used in 3D NAND and logic/memory chip design to improve bandwidth and performance.
  • OTT (Over-the-Top): Delivery of media and entertainment content via internet streaming services, bypassing traditional cable or satellite distribution channels.
  • RapidCool: Adeia's proprietary thermal management technology involving direct bonding of a cold plate to chips for efficient semiconductor cooling.
