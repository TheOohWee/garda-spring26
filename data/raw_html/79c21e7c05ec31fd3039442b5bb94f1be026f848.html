Takeaways

  • Total ACV -- Grew 17% as reported and 14% in constant currency, outpacing management's guidance.
  • Pega Cloud ACV -- Increased 33% as reported and 28% in constant currency, an acceleration from 18% growth as reported in the prior year; sequential quarterly acceleration confirmed throughout the year.
  • Net new ACV -- Rose 37% year over year in constant currency, attributed to Blueprint adoption, global sales execution, and heightened partner and client demand for Pega's AI agents.
  • Free cash flow -- Increased 45% to $491 million, $51 million above guidance, reflecting robust subscription model and improved operational execution.
  • Contractually committed backlog -- Increased 28% as reported and 23% in constant currency to over $2 billion for the first time; Pega Cloud backlog represented 74% of total backlog following 36% year-over-year growth.
  • Debt and share repurchases -- $468 million of debt repaid, $498 million in stock repurchased, and dividends paid, all enabled by strong cash flow; specific dividend dollar figure not provided in the transcript.
  • 2026 guidance: Total ACV -- Management projects 15% annual growth in constant currency after a consistent 14% rate during the reporting period.
  • 2026 guidance: Total revenue -- Management guided to $2 billion, an approximate 15% increase and a milestone level for the company.
  • 2026 guidance: Free cash flow -- Management expects $575 million, a 17% increase over 2025.
  • Buyback authorization -- Board approved an additional $1 billion repurchase capacity, extending capital return flexibility.
  • Pega Cloud ACV outlook -- Forecasted to grow "30% in 2026," with management attributing sustained acceleration to Blueprint and AI-powered automation demand.
  • Subscription transition -- Now complete, resulting in greater alignment between ACV and revenue growth for 2026 and increased annual revenue predictability.
  • Professional services revenue (2026) -- Expected to be approximately 10% of the $2 billion revenue guide due to headcount reduction and increased delivery partner reliance.
  • Pega Cloud ACV composition -- Now constitutes more than half of total ACV, further enhancing revenue transparency and predictability.
  • Legal resolution -- The Supreme Court of Virginia and appellate court rulings voided a previous $2 billion trade secret verdict against the company.
  • Net revenue retention rate -- Improved by approximately 150 basis points over 2024, with this level expected to be stable into 2026.
  • Cash and investments -- Year-end balance was $426 million, reflecting liquidity strength.
  • Partner engagement -- Blueprint was enhanced to allow partners to incorporate proprietary intellectual property and jointly deliver projects, with early-stage but positive partner feedback.
  • Contract renewal seasonality -- Management expects net new ACV and associated revenue to be heavily weighted to Q3 and Q4 in 2026, due to contract renewal patterns.

Summary

Pegasystems (PEGA 1.25%) delivered accelerating cloud growth, record backlog, and free cash flow generation above guidance, all attributed in the transcript to rapid adoption of the Blueprint agent, disciplined global sales, and expanded partner activity. Management committed to shareholder returns via elevated buybacks and debt reduction and forecasted further revenue and cash flow growth, highlighting durability of the firm’s operational and cloud transition model. The Supreme Court of Virginia's decision eliminated a major $2 billion verdict overhang referenced in prior quarters, resolving a significant legal risk. Guidance for 2026 signaled continued focus on scaling Pega Cloud ACV, capitalizing on AI-powered product differentiation, and deepening partner channels, with professional services revenue further de-emphasized in the mix. Internal commentary pointed to sustained sales pipeline momentum, improved net revenue retention, and origination of new sales from both legacy modernization and net-new logos from Blueprint-led selling motions.

  • Management stated that revenue growth and ACV growth are now "more closely aligned," and will remain so following the subscription model transition, changing prior modeling assumptions for investors.
  • The company's AI agent architecture emphasizes predictable, repeatable outcomes and regulatory compliance in enterprise workflows, in contrast to competitors' reliance on real-time, prompt-driven generative models.
  • Blueprint delivery was characterized as transformative for reducing customer sales cycle friction, improving ramp time for new sales staff, and unlocking new logo acquisition and partner engagement, with internal performance metrics cited as accelerating across all related pipeline measures.
  • Professional services headcount reductions were "intentional" as part of the AI-enabled delivery shift and increased reliance on partners, with overall client migrations to Pega Cloud described as steady and driven by new activity and expansions.
  • Management characterized client intent for cloud migrations and legacy transformation as accelerating, with both AWS and global consulting partners referenced as strategic channels for workflow modernization, underpinned by Blueprint integration capabilities.
  • Contract duration metrics remained consistent across cloud and on-premise renewals, with no significant quarter-over-quarter changes called out by management.

Industry glossary

  • ACV (Annual Contract Value): The annualized value of all active recurring revenue contracts.
  • Blueprint: Pegasystems' AI-powered design agent for automating sales, delivery, and client implementation processes, enabling rapid workflow creation, modernization, and integration with partner intellectual property.
  • Net revenue retention rate (NRR): A metric indicating the percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers inclusive of upsell, expansion, and churn.
  • Pega Cloud ACV: That portion of total ACV attributable to clients purchasing Pegasystems' solutions via the Pega Cloud hosted environment.
