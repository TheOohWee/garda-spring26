SoundThinking (SSTI) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 3, 2026 at 5:38PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, March 3, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Ralph A. Clark
  • Chief Financial Officer — Alan R. Stewart

Takeaways

  • Full-year revenue -- $104.1 million, representing a 2% increase, with growth offset by $9 million of non-renewed Chicago revenue replaced by other product sales.
  • Q4 revenue -- $24.8 million, up 6%, reflecting sustained sales momentum in key offerings.
  • Annual recurring revenue (ARR) -- Exited at $95.4 million, with management targeting ~15% growth, or $14.6 million, net of $3.1 million anticipated 2026 attrition.
  • ShotSpotter segment ARR -- $67.6 million at year-end, with potential $8.3 million addition targeted, including $2.7 million Puerto Rico renewal and $5.6 million new/expansion ARR; Chicago and sniper solution ARR excluded.
  • Crime Tracer ARR -- $8.1 million, with $3.1 million incremental ARR expected, including $2.5 million from 18 new agencies in a state currently experiencing execution delay.
  • PlateRanger ARR -- $1.5 million targeted by year-end, following new customer conversions from pilot deployments.
  • SafePoint bookings -- $1.6 million in bookings from 11 customers in 2025, with $800,000 booked in Q4 from six customers and $4 million in ARR targeted for 2026.
  • Q4 adjusted EBITDA -- $1.3 million, down versus $1.7 million due to delayed Crime Tracer and Puerto Rico renewals.
  • Full-year adjusted EBITDA -- $12.6 million compared to $14.4 million, reflecting lower Chicago ShotSpotter revenue and increased investments.
  • Q4 gross profit -- $12.6 million (51% margin), up from $11.7 million (50% margin).
  • GAAP net loss -- Q4 loss of $2.8 million ($0.22 per share); full-year loss of $9.4 million ($0.74 per share).
  • Operating expenses -- Full-year $65.4 million (63% of revenue), down 1% from $65.7 million; Q4 $15.1 million (61% of revenue), down from $15.5 million.
  • Cash and cash equivalents -- Ended year with $15.8 million, rising above $16 million after annual bonus payouts.
  • Share repurchases -- 225,334 shares bought for approximately $3 million at $13.15 average per share.
  • Revenue retention rate -- 99% for 2025, lowered by Chicago’s non-renewal.
  • 2026 revenue guidance -- Reduced to $109 million to $111 million, down from prior $114 million to $116 million, due to Crime Tracer and Puerto Rico contract delays.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin guidance -- Lowered to 16%-18% (from 18%-20%) to account for contract delays and increased AI investment.
  • Sales efficiency -- Sales and marketing spend per dollar of new annualized contract value decreased to $0.56 from $0.63, indicating improved efficiency.
  • Global sales leadership -- Kirk Arthur appointed Senior VP of Global Sales, accelerating execution and pipeline discipline.
  • Sales team expansion -- New hires include Manuel Nilan as VP of Sales for SafePoint and Bruno Bolorino as Vice President in Brazil for international momentum.

Summary

SoundThinking (SSTI 7.45%) reported a modest 2% annual revenue increase to $104.1 million, reflecting resilience despite Chicago contract losses, and set 2026 revenue guidance notably lower due to timing of large, delayed bookings. Management highlighted an expected inflection in ARR, projecting 15% growth led by its SafetySmart platform components and discussing robust bookings momentum in SafePoint and multi-product sales strategy. The company’s leadership expansion, particularly in global sales and SafePoint business, along with a board-initiated operational efficiency review, signal an organizational pivot for execution and cost control amid delayed contract revenue. Operating margins, adjusted EBITDA, and cash flow are expected to remain under near-term pressure until delayed contracts close. Management conveyed confidence in future recurring revenue and product expansion across new customer sectors.

  • Management said, "We are in a wait-and-see posture." on the Chicago contract, while reiterating that New York’s three-year ShotSpotter agreement remains "fully intact" with "no proposed reductions, no carve-outs, no structural changes."
  • SoundThinking is launching a perimeter-based sniper gunshot detection solution targeting utility substations, with initial pilots aimed at utility customers. Stated revenue potential is not assumed for 2026.
  • SafePoint growth is concentrated in healthcare customers seeking frictionless weapons detection, and over 50% of current customers are in this vertical; management noted significant hospital network expansion opportunity.
  • International growth focus includes expected new deployments in Brazil, Uruguay, and South Africa, with a sales leadership hire in Brazil to capture market share where "has a lot of gun violence, and we are doing quite well."

Industry glossary

  • Annual recurring revenue (ARR): The annualized value of contracted recurring revenue from subscription-based solutions, excluding one-time or non-recurring revenues.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: A non-GAAP financial measure reflecting earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, impairment, restructuring, stock-based compensation, and acquisition-related expenses.
  • ALPR (Automatic license plate recognition): Technology that automatically reads and identifies vehicle license plates for law enforcement and security purposes.
  • CJIS (Criminal Justice Information Services): An FBI division providing centralized databases and data aggregation for federal, state, and local law enforcement agencies.
  • RFP (Request for proposal): A formal process by which organizations solicit bids for specific solutions or services.
