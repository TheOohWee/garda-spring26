Earnings Call Transcript

Northwest (NWBI) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 10:54AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, April 29, 2025 at 9:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Louis J. Torchio
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Douglas E. Schosser

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $156 million, with 1.2% linked-quarter growth and 19% year-over-year growth.
  • Net Income -- $43 million, up $14 million, or 48%, from the first quarter of 2024.
  • Earnings Per Diluted Share -- $0.34, compared to $0.23 in the first quarter of 2024.
  • Net Interest Margin (NIM) -- 3.87%, a 45-basis-point expansion from the prior quarter, including a 39-basis-point interest recovery.
  • Commercial & Industrial (C&I) Loans -- Average balances up 20% year over year and $121 million, or 6.2%, from the previous quarter.
  • Total Loan Yield -- Increased 44 basis points quarter-on-quarter to 6.0%.
  • Consumer Non-Brokered Deposits -- Average balances increased $68 million quarter-on-quarter, with brokered deposits down $8 million.
  • Cost of Deposits -- Down 9 basis points quarter-on-quarter to 1.59%.
  • Non-Interest Income -- Decreased by $11.7 million quarter-on-quarter due to prior asset sales, but increased $0.4 million year over year.
  • Non-Interest Expense -- $92 million, up 2% year over year due to $1.1 million in merger-related costs; excluding these, expense was approximately $91 million.
  • Efficiency Ratio -- Improved to 57.7% from 59.6% in the previous quarter.
  • Annualized Net Charge-Offs -- 8 basis points, returning to historic levels after fourth-quarter write-downs.
  • Provision for Credit Losses -- $8.3 million recorded this quarter.
  • Non-Performing Loans (NPL) -- 53 basis points of total loans; non-performing assets at 52 basis points, each at five-quarter lows.
  • Quarterly Dividend -- $0.20 per share, declared for shareholders of record as of May 8, 2025.
  • Penns Woods Bancorp Merger -- All regulatory and shareholder approvals received, with closing and system conversion targeted for late July 2025.

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • The CFO stated, "Our fee income will likely be at the lower end of the range. We may not fully achieve that level."
  • Exposure to industries most impacted by tariffs, totaling approximately 8% of the loan portfolio, was highlighted, with ongoing monitoring but no material impact yet observed.
  • Management noted that "operating environment with significant market volatility and uncertainty over the economic outlook may be somewhat challenging," indicating external headwinds.

SUMMARY

The quarter delivered record first-quarter earnings and featured improved profitability metrics, driven by expanding net interest margin and disciplined expense control. Management confirmed the Penns Woods Bancorp merger remains on track for closing in late July, and noted anticipated scale benefits following integration. The shift toward commercial lending continued, with new verticals such as sports finance and franchise finance contributing to average commercial loan growth. Deposit costs fell, partly due to a strategic migration from certificates of deposit to lower-cost money market accounts. Credit quality indicators remained stable despite acknowledged macroeconomic and tariff-related uncertainties.

  • The efficiency ratio improvement reflected ongoing investments in talent alongside a containment of non-interest expenses, excluding merger costs.
  • Annualized net charge-offs normalized to eight basis points after earlier fourth-quarter elevated activity attributed to portfolio repositioning.
  • Loan yields increased sequentially, supported by interest recoveries and continued pricing discipline, even with recent Federal Reserve rate cuts.
  • The loan exposure to tariff-sensitive sectors, including manufacturing, transportation, and hospitality, amounted to close to 8% of the portfolio but has not yet resulted in elevated delinquencies or losses.
  • Acquisition economics for Penns Woods Bancorp may improve due to recent declines in rates and Northwest's stock price, although no updated financial guidance was given ahead of closing.
  • Management plans to provide detailed post-merger margin and purchase accounting guidance following second-quarter results.
  • Branch network expansion continues, with a new location opening in Fishers, Indiana—the first in six years—supporting further deposit and franchise growth.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • C&I Loans: Commercial and industrial loans, generally made to businesses and distinct from real estate or consumer lending.
  • CRE Portfolio: Commercial real estate loans, including office, retail, and other non-residential property financings.
