Pfizer (PFE) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 2:51PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, October 29, 2024 at 10:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Albert Bourla
  • Chief Financial Officer and Executive Vice President — Dave Denton
  • Chief Scientific Officer and President, Worldwide Research, Development and Medical — Mikael Dolsten

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Company Revenues -- $17.7 billion, reflecting 32% operational growth; includes PAXLOVID revenue of $2.7 billion and COMIRNATY revenue of $1.4 billion.
  • Non-COVID Product Revenues -- $13.6 billion, achieving 14% operational growth driven by key therapy areas and recent launches.
  • Oncology Revenue Growth -- 31% year over year, with strong performances from both legacy Seagen and legacy Pfizer (PFE 1.10%) products.
  • XTANDI Sales Growth -- Up 28%, driven by increased demand in advanced prostate cancer indications.
  • TALZENNA Sales Growth -- 77% growth in the quarter, accelerated by positive Phase III TALAPRO data in combination with XTANDI for metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer.
  • LORBRENA Operational Growth -- 31% in thoracic cancer, with expanded first-line usage following five-year CROWN trial data.
  • BRAFTOVI/MEKTOVI Combination Growth -- 32% year over year, mainly from metastatic non-small cell lung cancer indications.
  • ELREXFIO Sequential Revenue Growth -- 80% over prior quarter; U.S. new patient starts more than doubled since January.
  • CIBINQO Quarterly Operational Growth -- 74% globally, and 27% in the U.S. sequentially from Q2 2024, for severe eczema in patients aged 12 and up.
  • VYNDAQEL Family Operational Growth -- 63% in the third quarter, accompanied by a 10% increase in U.S. patients on treatment from Q2 2024.
  • Adjusted Gross Margin -- Approximately 70%, impacted by COVID-19 product mix, particularly profit split and royalty effects for COMIRNATY, with partial offset from cost management actions.
  • Adjusted Operating Expenses -- $5.8 billion, representing a 2% operational decrease; includes acquired Seagen spending.
  • Adjusted Diluted Earnings Per Share -- $1.06, influenced by top-line growth, operational efficiency, and a favorable tax rate profile.
  • 2024 Full-Year Revenue Guidance -- Raised by $1.5 billion to a range of $61 billion to $64 billion; COVID-19 product revenue forecast at $10.5 billion ($5 billion for COMIRNATY, $5.5 billion for PAXLOVID).
  • 2024 Operational Revenue Growth (Excluding COVID-19 Products) -- Maintained at 9%-11%, taking into account lower Oxbryta sales.
  • Net Cost Savings Target -- On track to achieve at least $4 billion in savings from the cost realignment program by year-end.
  • Dividend and Shareholder Returns -- $7.1 billion returned via dividends in the first nine months of 2024.
  • Operating Cash Flow and Helion Monetization -- Operating cash flows augmented by $3.5 billion from Helion share sales in October, reducing Pfizer's stake from 23% to 15%.
  • Seagen Revenue Contribution -- $854 million in Q3, with $2.3 billion year-to-date, representing 38% annualized pro forma growth; PADCEV delivered over $400 million in Q3 revenue.
  • Prevnar20 U.S. Market Share -- Achieved 83% share in pediatric and 97% in adult segments after new vaccination guidelines were issued.
  • ABRYSVO Retail and Clinic Sales Share -- Exceeded 50% for the quarter; administration share in retail settings reached 43% as of mid-October.

SUMMARY

Management raised full-year guidance for both total revenue and adjusted earnings, supported by 32% operational revenue growth and stronger-than-expected COVID-19 product sales contributions. Oncology, driven by assets such as XTANDI and TALZENNA, led with 31% growth and several positive clinical updates, while new product launches and key franchises outperformed across immunology, vaccines, and rare diseases. Productivity initiatives yielded improvements in cost base and cash flow, with net cost savings on track and further deleveraging from monetization of the Helion stake.

  • Albert Bourla referenced ongoing, direct dialogue with Starboard Value and acknowledged shareholder dissatisfaction with total return but maintained, "we believe we are executing on the best path forward."
  • Pfizer confirmed readiness to provide 2025 guidance by year-end, signaling clarity on "pushes and pulls" ahead, including expected IRA impacts and product LOEs.
  • The Seagen acquisition immediately yielded $854 million in Q3 revenue and ongoing pipeline acceleration, with management asserting the portfolio and integration are on track for future blockbuster potential pending readouts.
  • COVID-19 therapies (COMIRNATY and PAXLOVID) entered a stabilized period of commercial demand, with year-to-date PAXLOVID treatment volume nearly matching the prior year and both products viewed as durable businesses moving forward.
  • Internal reporting separates COVID and non-COVID performance, but management signaled an impending shift toward treating pandemic assets as core business with consistent commercial and operational focus.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ADC (Antibody-Drug Conjugate): Targeted therapy combining a monoclonal antibody with a cytotoxic agent for specific cancer cell delivery.
  • CDK4 Inhibitor: A drug targeting cyclin-dependent kinase 4, used for disrupting cancer cell growth in certain tumors.
  • 340B Program: U.S. federal policy requiring drugmakers to provide medications at reduced prices to eligible health care organizations, designed for underserved populations.
  • LOE (Loss of Exclusivity): The period when patent or exclusivity protection for a branded drug expires, allowing for generic competition.
  • SI&A (Selling, Information & Administration): Category of operating expenses composed of costs for commercial, informational, and administrative activities.
  • Pillar 2: OECD/G20 initiative for a global minimum tax framework impacting the effective tax rate for multinational corporations.
  • PCV (Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine): Vaccine targeting various serotypes of Streptococcus pneumoniae, often identified by the number of serotypes included (e.g., PCV20 for 20 types).
  • ABRYSVO: Pfizer’s RSV (respiratory syncytial virus) vaccine.
  • PAXLOVID: Pfizer’s oral antiviral for COVID-19.
  • COMIRNATY: Pfizer-BioNTech’s COVID-19 mRNA vaccine.
  • Operational Growth: Growth rate adjusted for currency and certain extraordinary impacts, reflecting business performance in constant local currency terms.
