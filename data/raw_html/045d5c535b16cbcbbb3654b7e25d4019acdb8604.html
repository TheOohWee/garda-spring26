TAKEAWAYS

  • Safety Performance -- Total recordable incident rate reached a company historic low in 2025, marking the safest year in LyondellBasell Industries (LYB +2.01%) history despite significant maintenance activity.
  • Value Enhancement Program -- Achieved $1.1 billion of recurring annual EBITDA in 2025, above the original target, with a new goal of $1.5 billion by 2028 based on mid-cycle margins.
  • Cash From Operations -- Generated $2.3 billion during 2025, driven by working capital discipline and cost management despite weak industry margins.
  • Cash Conversion Ratio -- Achieved 95% for the year, exceeding the long-term 80% target and reflecting efficient earnings-to-cash translation even under compressed spreads.
  • Workforce Reduction -- Reduced global workforce by 7%, or approximately 1,350 employees, to the lowest level since 2018.
  • Cash Improvement Plan -- Outperformed the $600 million target by $200 million for a total of $800 million in 2025; cumulative target raised from $1.1 billion to $1.3 billion through the end of 2026.
  • Free Cash Flow Generation -- Remained positive at the bottom of the industry cycle, with continued capital discipline enabling ongoing shareholder returns and project investment.
  • Dividend and Share Repurchases -- Returned $2 billion to shareholders in 2025 through dividends and buybacks amid challenging market conditions.
  • Available Liquidity -- Ended the year with $3.4 billion in cash and short-term investments and $8.1 billion in available liquidity, supported by a $1.5 billion bond issuance to address near-term maturities.
  • EBITDA (Q4 2025) -- Reported $417 million, with $164 million from Olefins and Polyolefins Americas, a $61 million loss in Olefins and Polyolefins Europe, Asia, International, $205 million from Intermediates & Derivatives, $38 million from Advanced Polymer Solutions, and $80 million from the Technology segment (component figures do not sum to the total due to excluded unallocated costs).
  • Olefins & Polyolefins Americas Operating Rates -- Ran at approximately 75% with crackers at 90% in Q4; expect to increase to 85% across assets in the first quarter of 2026.
  • Olefins & Polyolefins Europe, Asia, International -- EBITDA loss of $61 million in Q4; actively reducing working capital and inventories, with no major turnarounds planned for 2026 and an expected 75% operating rate in Q1.
  • Intermediates & Derivatives Segment -- Delivered $205 million EBITDA in Q4; January downtime at the La Porte acetyls unit is projected to reduce Q1 2026 EBITDA by approximately $20 million.
  • Advanced Polymer Solutions -- Fourth quarter EBITDA reached $38 million, up 55% year over year, with improved cash generation and fixed cost discipline.
  • Technology Segment -- Q4 EBITDA totaled $80 million, as catalyst demand increased and more previously sold licenses met revenue recognition milestones; licensing revenue expected to decline in the following quarter.
  • Capital Expenditures (2026) -- Planned at approximately $1.2 billion, with $800 million designated for maintenance and $400 million for growth projects; notable reduction from prior years due to deferred turnarounds and adjusted project schedules.
  • European Asset Divestment -- Material progress made on planned divestiture of four European assets, with transaction completion targeted for the second quarter of 2026.
  • Cost-Advantaged Feedstocks -- Secured new feedstock allocation in Saudi Arabia as part of strengthening the company's cost position in the Middle East.
  • MoReTec-1 Construction -- Project in Cologne advancing on track and expected to start up in 2027, demonstrating progress in circular and low-carbon solutions.
  • 2026 Outlook: Cash Improvement -- Targeting an additional $500 million of incremental cash versus 2025, contributing to the broader $1.3 billion cash plan through 2026.
  • Fourth Quarter Segment Impacts -- Identified items amounted to $61 million net of tax, including closure costs; LIFO inventory charges reduced results by $52 million, partially offset by lower bonus accruals.
  • Export Exposure -- Polyethylene exports accounted for 34%-38% of volumes in the past two years, 10%-15% lower than industry averages.

SUMMARY

LyondellBasell Industries executed on key cost and cash discipline initiatives in 2025, enabling sustained free cash flow and liquidity despite industry margins being approximately 45% below historical averages. Management advanced a strategic divestiture of European assets, a core component of its portfolio transformation, and made progress on value enhancement and circular economy projects. Segment-level challenges persisted, particularly in Europe and Polypropylene markets, but operational focus maintained essential margin, safety, and reliability benchmarks. Strategic capital expenditure deferrals and a clear 2026 cash improvement roadmap signal a continued emphasis on financial flexibility and prioritized investments as market conditions remain challenging but begin showing signs of cyclical normalization.

  • Peter Z. Vanacker stated that "2025 [was] the safest year in our company's history," highlighting operational discipline amid elevated maintenance activity.
  • The company does not include any potential proceeds from the pending European asset sale in its cash improvement targets.
  • Management affirmed board-level reviews on dividend policy, emphasizing that decisions are balanced with maintaining an investment-grade balance sheet and are reevaluated in regular board meetings.
  • Specific segment operational rates for Q1 2026 are guided at 85% for Olefins & Polyolefins Americas, 75% for European assets, and 85% for Intermediates & Derivatives assets.
  • Delayed growth investments such as Flex-2 and MoReTec-2, and project prioritization in regions with supportive regulation, reflect adjustments for prevailing depressed sector margins.
  • The company expects a typical seasonal uplift in catalyst sales for the Technology segment in Q1 2026 but projects licensing revenue will decline, citing "substantially lower demand for licenses."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • MoReTec: Proprietary advanced recycling process technology for converting plastic waste into feedstock suitable for new plastics production.
  • Oxyfuels: High-octane blending components predominantly produced from intermediate chemical streams, such as isobutylene, commonly used in gasoline blending.
  • VEP (Value Enhancement Program): Company-wide initiative designed to deliver recurring annual EBITDA improvements through operational efficiency, cost discipline, and reliability projects.
  • LIFO: An accounting method (Last In, First Out) for inventory valuation that, when prices are volatile, can materially impact reported segment results due to non-cash period-end adjustments.
  • Anti-involution: Refers to Chinese regulatory initiatives targeting industry overcapacity and promoting asset rationalization, under review by the National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC).
  • PO&TBA facility: Integrated facility producing propylene oxide (PO) and tertiary butyl alcohol (TBA), representing a key investment in chemical intermediates.
