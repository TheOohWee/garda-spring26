Legend Biotech (LEGN) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 1, 2026 at 9:28AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Monday, Aug. 11, 2025 at 8 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Ying Huang
  • President of CARVYKTI — Alan S. Bash
  • Interim Chief Financial Officer — Jessie Yeung
  • Chief Medical Officer — Mythili Koneru
  • President of Research & Development — Guowei Fang
  • Associate Director of Investor Relations — Caroline LeCates Paul

Takeaways

  • CARVYKTI Net Trade Sales -- $439 million for the quarter, representing 136% growth year over year and 19% sequentially from the prior quarter.
  • U.S. CARVYKTI Net Sales -- $358 million, a 114% increase year over year and 13% growth versus first quarter.
  • International (OUS) CARVYKTI Sales -- $81 million, quadrupling year over year with 59% sequential growth; driven by launches in Germany, Switzerland, Austria, Brazil, and several additional European and international markets.
  • Patients Treated With CARVYKTI -- Over 7,500 cumulative patients since launch, with approximately 60% of utilization in earlier line (second–fourth) myeloma treatment settings.
  • Manufacturing Success Rate -- 97% for CAR-T, described as highest in industry, with a 30-day turnaround time from apheresis to product delivery.
  • Number of Global Treatment Sites -- 213 activated worldwide, including 123 in the U.S.
  • Gross Margin -- 57% on net product sales; reflects improvements despite continued pipeline and launch investments.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $10 million, after excluding $111 million of unrealized FX losses, contrasted with a reported $125 million net loss under IFRS.
  • Operating Loss -- $22 million, halved from $41 million a year ago, attributed to operational efficiency gains and disciplined expense management.
  • R&D Expense -- $98 million, accounting for 39% of revenue, reflecting a slight decline year over year.
  • SG&A Expense -- $81 million, up 23% over the prior year, now 32% of revenue.
  • Cash Position -- $1 billion in cash, equivalents, and time deposits maintained at quarter end.
  • CARVYKTI Label Update -- FDA removed REMS requirements, reducing monitoring proximity from four to two weeks and decreasing driving restrictions from eight to two weeks post-therapy.
  • CARTITUDE-1 Efficacy Milestone -- "1/3 of patients with heavily pretreated relapsed, refractory multiple myeloma remain alive and progression free for 5 years or more after being treated with CARVYKTI without further multiple myeloma treatment." (Huang)
  • CARTITUDE-4 Subgroup PFS -- Median PFS of 37 months for high-risk cytogenetic patients compared to 10 months for standard of care.
  • Outpatient Utilization -- Slightly over 50% of CARVYKTI patients received therapy in outpatient settings across both community and academic centers.
  • Community Setting Penetration -- 70% of patients originated from the community setting, emphasizing ongoing community and regional hospital expansion.
  • Pipeline Advances -- Phase I LB1908 study demonstrated antitumor activity and tolerable safety; LB2102 (DLL3 CAR-T) showed no dose-limiting toxicity with efficacy observed up to four dose levels.
  • CARTITUDE-6 Trial Status -- Global enrollment completed, except for the final cohort in Japan; study completion is anticipated within the year.
  • Long-term Clinical Safety -- Incidence of delayed neurologic events (parkinsonism) decreased from 6% in CARTITUDE-1 to 1% in CARTITUDE-4; bridging therapy use linked to further reduction.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- $0.03 per share, compared to negative $0.01 per share in the same period last year.

Summary

Legend Biotech Corporation (LEGN 0.22%) delivered record CARVYKTI sales, establishing a new quarterly benchmark for CAR-T therapies and driving 136% annual revenue growth. Regulatory changes, including FDA elimination of REMS for BCMA- and CD19-directed autologous CAR-T, led to immediate label updates on monitoring and activity restrictions, anticipated to accelerate patient access. Median progression-free survival reached 37 months in high-risk subgroups under CARTITUDE-4, indicating a clinical efficacy advantage across key patient populations. Operational discipline halved the company's operating loss and produced positive adjusted net income despite continued R&D and international expansion. OUS sales contribution increased fourfold, driven by accelerated launches and the ramp-up of new European production capacity.

  • Legend reported a 97% CAR-T manufacturing success rate, achieving consistently rapid turnaround at 30 days, which management described as sufficient for current clinical demand.
  • Outpatient therapy accounted for over half of recent patient infusions, with 70% of patients originating in the community setting, supporting initiatives to decentralize and expand U.S. access.
  • LB1908 and LB2102 advanced in the pipeline, with Phase I results showing "encouraging antitumor activity with manageable safety" (Huang) and absence of dose-limiting toxicities, respectively.
  • Management stated, "We continue to anticipate achieving operational breakeven for CARVYKTI by the end of 2025 and company-wide profitability in 2026, excluding unrealized foreign exchange gains or losses." (Huang)
  • Major safety data highlighted that no parkinsonism or Guillain-Barré syndrome was observed in a large real-world cohort when bridging with TALVEY, contrasting with 2% incidence in broader datasets.
  • Legend and Johnson & Johnson (NYSE: JNJ) have activated 213 treatment sites globally, with rapid expansion particularly in Europe, supporting a multiyear pathway for ex-U.S. growth and market share gains.
  • The company maintains a $1 billion cash position, funding clinical, commercial, and infrastructure investments while targeting margin improvements through efficiency initiatives and lower operating losses.

Industry glossary

  • CARVYKTI: Ciltacabtagene autoleucel, a BCMA-directed autologous CAR-T cell therapy for multiple myeloma.
  • CARTITUDE: Series of Legend/Janssen-sponsored clinical trials evaluating CARVYKTI in various multiple myeloma treatment lines.
  • OUS: Abbreviation for "Outside the United States," used to refer to ex-U.S. revenue and business segments.
  • REMS: Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategies, FDA-mandated safety protocols for certain drugs and biologics.
  • PFS: Progression-Free Survival, a clinical trial endpoint measuring the time during and after treatment that a patient lives without disease progression.
  • BCMA: B-cell Maturation Antigen, a therapeutic target in multiple myeloma treatments including CAR-T therapies.
  • DLL3: Delta-like ligand 3, a molecular target investigated in certain CAR-T therapies for oncology.
  • SG&A: Selling, General & Administrative expenses, a line item in financial reporting.
  • LB1908: Legend's investigational CLDN18.2-targeted CAR-T cell therapy, in Phase I development for solid tumors.
  • LB2102: Legend's investigational DLL3-targeted CAR-T, licensed globally to Novartis.
  • TALVEY: Talquetamab, a bispecific antibody used in bridging therapy protocols.
  • ENF cohort: Frontline patient group in the CARTITUDE-2 study, specifically referenced in context of upcoming data.
