TAKEAWAYS

  • Quarterly Revenue -- RMB 243.8 million, up 48.4% year over year and 163.6% sequentially, driven by record eVTOL deliveries.
  • Full-Year Revenue -- RMB 509.5 million, increasing 11.7%, surpassing annual guidance set by management.
  • Q4 Gross Margin -- 62.1%, higher than 60.7% in Q4 the prior year and 60.8% in Q3, with full-year margin at 62% (up from 61.4% in 2024).
  • Quarterly eVTOL Deliveries -- 100 units in Q4 (95 EH216 series, 5 VT35), setting a new record based on finalized contracts.
  • Full-Year eVTOL Deliveries -- 221 units delivered, including 215 EH216 series and 6 VT35 series.
  • First Quarterly GAAP Profitability -- Net income reached RMB 10.5 million in Q4, marking the company’s first GAAP-profitable quarter.
  • Adjusted Net Income (Q4) -- RMB 71.5 million, an increase of 96.4%, signifying a sequential return to profitability (non-GAAP basis).
  • Adjusted Net Income (Full Year) -- RMB 29.4 million, reflecting the second consecutive year of non-GAAP profitability.
  • Q4 Adjusted Operating Expenses -- RMB 99.3 million, up 26% year over year and 11.4% sequentially, mainly from R&D investment and commercialization expansion.
  • Annual Revenue Guidance for 2026 -- Management projects RMB 600 million, representing a projected increase of approximately 18%.
  • Commercial Flight Operations -- Initial launch of ticketed pilotless eVTOL passenger service planned in Guangzhou and Hefei, with early-bird ticket fare set at RMB 299 per person.
  • Manufacturing Capacity Expansion -- Yunfu facility Phase II completed, raising total planned annual capacity to 1,000 eVTOL units.
  • New Product Progress -- VT35 completed critical flight tests and public demonstration; type certification in China targeted within two years.
  • Production and Delivery Support -- Supply chain maintained 100% on-time delivery rate for key components in Q4.
  • Flagship Partnerships -- Key contract deliveries included 30 EH216-S units to Guizhou Tourism Group in Q4, totaling 50 units to this customer.
  • Nonpassenger Business Diversification -- Delivered 8 firefighting aircraft in December and set a Guinness World Record with 22,580 formation drones at the China Spring Festival Gala (does not sum to total units).
  • Thailand Market Progress -- Expected first commercial operation license for pilotless passenger eVTOL from the Civil Aviation Authority of Thailand; Sandbox area deployment planned with up to 100 aircraft targeted across 20 sites by year-end 2026.
  • Operator Training and Certification -- Ground crew licensing program progressing with first classes expected in the first half of 2026; CAAC expanded special approval licenses for commercial operations.
  • OpEx Growth Outlook -- Management stated 2026 operating expense growth rate is expected to be lower than projected revenue growth rate.
  • Overseas Revenue Outlook -- Overseas revenue comprised a low single-digit percent in 2025 but may grow to double-digit percent in 2026, contingent on commercial launches.

SUMMARY

EHang Holdings Limited (EH 1.02%) reported record eVTOL sales and revenue growth, achieving its first-ever GAAP-profitable quarter and meeting full-year financial guidance. The company is commencing the initial ticketed pilotless passenger flight services in China, establishing a new operational model for urban air mobility and planning rapid capacity scaling. Management confirmed supply chain reliability and manufacturing output, enabling both product and service expansion, including nonpassenger applications such as firefighting and drone formation displays. The Thailand market is set to become the first overseas site for commercial pilotless eVTOL operations under a regulatory Sandbox, pairing with talent development programs and regulatory alignment for future international growth.

  • Company representatives stated, "expected to obtain the first commercial operation license for pilotless passenger eVTOL aircraft from the Civil Aviation Authority of Thailand," and detailed that commercialization in the Sandbox is expected to result in dozens of unit deliveries beginning in Q2 2026 if approved.
  • Service revenue from commercial flights will initially be modest, as management noted, "the initial contribution to the overall revenue won't be large," but is expected to diversify the revenue base as public ticket sales begin.
  • Quality metrics improved with the product passing the third zero-defect post-certification airworthiness review and the EN9100 audit.
  • Operational readiness for new deployments is supported by a professional training system for ground crews, with CAAC authorization expanding qualified staff hiring.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • eVTOL: Electric Vertical Take-Off and Landing aircraft, designed for urban air mobility and logistics applications.
  • OC: Operator Certificate; regulator-issued approval required to conduct commercial aerial operations.
  • CAAC: Civil Aviation Administration of China, the authority overseeing aviation regulation and certification in China.
  • AAM Sandbox: Regulatory environment allowing specific trial and initial commercial operations of advanced air mobility technologies under controlled conditions.
  • EN9100 audit: Quality management system certification for aviation, space, and defense organizations, indicating compliance with industry standards.
