Enerpac (EPAC) Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 26, 2026 at 9:16AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, March 26, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Paul E. Sternlieb
  • Chief Financial Officer — Darren M. Kozik

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $155 million, reflecting 2% organic growth, with a 6% rise in product sales offset by a 17% decline in service revenue.
  • Americas Growth -- 4% overall, with nearly 6% product revenue growth and an 8% service revenue decline.
  • EMEA Results -- Product revenue increased 7%, but regional revenue decreased 1% due to a 21% contraction in service revenue.
  • APAC Performance -- Modest growth led by products, with India reporting strong double-digit expansion in steel, process industries, and heavy equipment manufacturing.
  • Segment Performance – Cortland (Other segment) -- 27% growth, attributed to new project activity.
  • Industrial Tools & Services (IT&S) Product Orders -- Mid-single-digit gains reported in all three geographic regions.
  • Gross Margin -- Declined 410 basis points year over year, pressured by lower service volume.
  • SG&A Expense Ratio -- Adjusted SG&A decreased to 26.4% of revenue from 28.3% in the prior year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin -- 21.3%, down from 23.2%, as service margin pressure and FX impact offset product margin gains.
  • EPS (Reported) -- $0.31 per share, compared with $0.38; adjusted EPS held steady at $0.39.
  • Restructuring Charges -- $3.3 million in the quarter, targeted at the service business, with an expected payback in one year.
  • Net Debt -- $89 million, with a net debt to adjusted EBITDA ratio of 0.6 times.
  • Total Liquidity -- $499 million, including revolver capacity and cash.
  • Free Cash Flow (YTD) -- $23 million, up $18 million compared to the previous year's $5 million.
  • Share Repurchases -- $51 million repurchased during the quarter; $135 million remains authorized out of $200 million.
  • Fiscal 2026 Guidance – Net Sales -- Narrowed to $635 million–$650 million, implying 1%-3% organic growth, with mid-single-digit product growth offset by a low- to mid-teens service contraction.
  • Fiscal 2026 Guidance – Adjusted EBITDA -- $158 million–$163 million.
  • Fiscal 2026 Guidance – Adjusted EPS -- $1.85–$1.92.
  • Fiscal 2026 Guidance – Free Cash Flow -- Maintained at $100 million–$110 million.
  • Major Contract Win -- Secured a five-year service contract in the U.K. North Sea, valued at several million dollars annually, set to begin revenue contribution in the fourth quarter.
  • Innovation Pipeline -- Six new products launched year to date, with additional launches planned in the back half of the fiscal year.
  • Hydropack Acquisition -- Recently completed small asset purchase, filling a product gap in diesel-powered Split-Flow Pumps.
  • Order Pipeline Trends -- Company noted, "order rates in the quarter were strong, with strong growth in every single region year over year."
  • Middle East Revenue Exposure -- Approximately 10% of total company revenue is from the region; current conflict has led to a pause in service work due to facility access issues.
  • Tax Rate Guidance -- Held at 21%-26% for the full year.

RISKS

  • Gross margin contraction of 410 basis points attributed to "lower volume in our service business."
  • Management cited continued near-term pressure in the service business and a forecasted low- to mid-teens annual decline for the segment.
  • Direct impact from Middle East conflict mentioned: "some pause in service work in the Middle East, mainly due to inability to access facilities, customers shutting sites, deferring work."
  • Potential broader business risks acknowledged from "higher oil prices, inflation, and general economic headwinds that the conflict has created."

SUMMARY

Management reported the highest product sales growth in ten quarters within the IT&S segment and emphasized disciplined cost initiatives that produced an improved SG&A expense ratio. The company announced a substantial new multi-year U.K. North Sea contract and outlined a robust innovation pipeline, featuring six new products launched year to date and more planned. Liquidity stands at $499 million, supporting ongoing M&A exploration and significant share repurchases, while guidance reflects continued weakness in the service business and the effects of restructuring. Leadership also confirmed increased utilization of proprietary digital lead management programs, including ongoing pilot implementations of AI to support commercial initiatives.

  • Diversification was highlighted, with the Other segment (Cortland) growing 27% and strong results in multiple geographies, especially double-digit expansion cited in India.
  • The recent Hydropack asset acquisition addresses a strategic gap in portable Split-Flow Pump offerings for infrastructure and power generation sectors.
  • Incremental restructuring charges of $3.3 million target the EMEA service business, with anticipated annual cost savings materializing within the next fiscal year.
  • Management held free cash flow guidance steady after reporting significant year-to-date improvement, underscoring consistent cash generation despite continued service business pressures.
  • The tax rate range remains wide due to ongoing jurisdictional planning, as stated by Kozik, "There is obviously tax planning that is underway, and it is always difficult to determine when some of those things will happen."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • HLT (Heavy Lifting Technology): Engineered product line specializing in synchronized heavy-load handling and lifting solutions, often utilized in major infrastructure, power generation, and industrial projects.
  • IT&S (Industrial Tools & Services): Core segment encompassing branded hydraulic and mechanical tool production, service, and rental for industrial maintenance, repair, and operations.
  • PEP (Powering Enerpac Performance): Internal operational excellence initiative focused on procurement optimization and manufacturing efficiency across the company’s footprint.
  • ECX (Enerpac Commercial EX): Proprietary lead management and commercial excellence program using real-time CRM systems and analytics to drive pipeline conversion and global sales effectiveness.

We are now guiding to adjusted EBITDA of $158 million to $163 million and adjusted EPS of $1.85 to $1.92.
