Watts Water (WTS) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Aug 7, 2025 at 10:06AM EST

Date

Thursday, Aug. 7, 2025, at 9 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Robert J. Pagano Jr.
  • Chief Financial Officer — Shashank Patel
  • Chief Financial Officer — Ryan Lada

Takeaways

  • Revenue-- $644 million in reported sales for the second quarter of 2025, a record high, up 8% year-over-year and 6% organic sales growth, driven primarily by price, volume, and approximately $20 million in pull-forward demand before price increases.
  • Americas segment sales-- Organic sales in the Americas increased 10% and reported sales rose 11%, with the Icon acquisition contributing $7 million; Americas organic growth offset declines in other regions.
  • Europe segment sales-- Organic sales in Europe declined 8% and reported sales fell 3%, with weakness across all geographies despite favorable foreign exchange impact.
  • APMEA segment sales-- Declined 1% organically in APMEA; growth in Australia, New Zealand, and the Middle East was offset by a decline in China due to project timing.
  • Adjusted EBITDA-- Adjusted EBITDA was $153 million, up 22% year-over-year, with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 23.8%, representing a 280-basis-point increase in margin, driven by price-cost favorability and volume leverage in the Americas.
  • Adjusted operating income-- Adjusted operating income was $139 million, up 24%; adjusted operating margin of 21.6%, up 280 basis points, a record for the company.
  • Americas segment margin-- Increased 290 basis points to 27.2% for the Americas segment.
  • Europe segment margin-- Rose 170 basis points to 11.7%.
  • APMEA segment margin-- Held steady at 18.9% for APMEA segment margin.
  • Adjusted EPS-- Adjusted earnings per share of $3.09, up 26% year-over-year, reflecting operations, acquisitions, foreign exchange, and reduced interest expense.
  • Price realization-- Achieved approximately 3% company-wide price realization; price increases in the 5%-15% range are being implemented due to tariff-related actions, with full realization expected in the second half.
  • Tariff impact-- $40 million in direct tariff costs estimated for 2025, reflecting reductions from Chinese tariffs but new copper and European tariffs in effect as of 2025.
  • Adjusted effective tax rate-- 25.2% adjusted effective tax rate.
  • Free cash flow-- Free cash flow was $105 million year-to-date, down from $120 million year-to-date through the second quarter of 2024, due to working capital timing and increased capital expenditures; full-year 2025 free cash flow conversion target of at least 100% of net income remains in place.
  • Net debt to capitalization ratio-- Net debt to capitalization ratio was negative 10% at quarter end; net leverage stood at negative 0.4, reflecting a strong balance sheet.
  • Acquisition update-- EasyWater was acquired in June with expected to contribute $5 million in 2025 sales; integrations of Bradley, Josam, Icon, and EasyWater are ahead of synergy targets.
  • Nexa platform-- Rollout gaining traction, with installations in luxury multifamily, hotel, and commercial real estate sectors; migration of all smart/connected products onto Nexa will be complete by end of year or early next year.
  • Guidance increase-- Raised full-year organic sales growth outlook to flat to 3% (up two points) for 2025, and reported sales growth to 2%-5% (up three points from previous view).
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin guidance-- Full-year adjusted EBITDA margin expansion expected at 60-120 basis points for 2025, 30 basis points higher at the midpoint than prior guidance.
  • Adjusted operating margin guidance-- Full-year adjusted operating margin expansion expected at 50-110 basis points for 2025, with the midpoint raised by 50 basis points from the earlier outlook.
  • Third quarter sales guidance-- Organic sales expected to rise 2%-5% in the third quarter of 2025, with mid-single-digit organic Americas growth expected, low-single-digit organic APMEA growth expected, and high-single-digit organic sales decline in Europe expected.
  • Third quarter margin guidance-- Expected EBITDA margin of 19.7%-20.3% for the third quarter of 2025 (20-80 basis points higher) and operating margin of 17.1%-17.7% (flat to up 60 basis points), reflecting normalization post pull-forward and reduced price-cost benefit.
  • 80/20 initiatives-- Approximately one million dollars of sales eliminated and two million dollars expected in the third quarter of 2025 within the Americas as part of ongoing profitability improvement actions.
  • Data center exposure-- Now offsetting residential weakness; previously represented approximately 2% of total sales in the prior year.
  • Acquisition and restructuring costs-- $3.8 million in GAAP pretax charges incurred, primarily related to French facility exit and other Europe actions.
  • Shipping days impact-- Lower shipping days in the first quarter will be recovered in the fourth quarter, potentially boosting fourth-quarter volume.

Summary

Watts Water Technologies (WTS 0.38%) reported record quarterly revenue and profit, supported by strong pricing actions and significant Americas growth, including a notable effect from approximately $20 million in sales pulled forward ahead of price increases. Management raised full-year sales and margin guidance, factoring in incremental contributions from acquisitions, foreign exchange, and ongoing pricing actions that are expected to offset a $40 million direct tariff headwind and ongoing margin expansion.

  • Pagano emphasized, "Our second quarter performance was better than we anticipated, with record sales, operating income, and earnings per share due to strong performance in our Americas region," directly connecting momentum to the region's price realization and supply chain strengths.
  • Latest acquisitions including EasyWater and Icon are tracking ahead of original synergy targets, positioning the company for further portfolio and revenue expansion.
  • While Nexa's commercialization is described by Pagano as following a lengthy adoption cycle, customer pilots have begun translating into recurring platform and monitoring revenue streams, with complete product migration to a single application expected by early next year.
  • Volume guidance for later quarters remains cautious, as sequentially lower third quarter growth reflects pull-forward effects and what Pagano referred to as "residential softness" while noting continued outperformance in secular-growth pockets such as data centers.

Industry glossary

  • 80/20 initiatives: Business discipline to prioritize resources on top-performing products, customers, or processes, while systematically discontinuing or exiting lower-profit segments to enhance overall operating efficiency and margin.
  • Nexa Intelligent Water Management: Watts' proprietary digital platform for real-time water system monitoring, management, and issue detection, delivering data-driven operational insights, payback cycles, and recurring revenue through software- and service-enabled solutions.
  • Pull-forward demand: Acceleration of customer purchases ahead of scheduled price increases or supply chain disruptions, creating a temporary sales and volume increase in the reporting period, often followed by a related slowdown.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin: The ratio of earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for items like restructuring or acquisition costs, to total revenue—a key measure of operating profitability.
