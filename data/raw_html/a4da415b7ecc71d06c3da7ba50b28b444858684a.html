InvenTrust (IVT) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

TAKEAWAYS

  • Same Property NOI Growth -- 5.3% increase for the year, with contributors including embedded rent escalations (160 basis points), occupancy gains (80 basis points), positive leasing spreads (90 basis points), redevelopment (70 basis points), and net expense reimbursements (130 basis points); offset by a 20 basis point increase in bad debt reserves.
  • NAREIT FFO -- $1.89 per share for the year, representing 6.2% growth, and $0.47 per share in the fourth quarter, up 4.4%.
  • Core FFO -- $1.83 per share for the year, up 5.8%; $0.46 per share in the fourth quarter, a 7% increase.
  • Liquidity and Leverage -- Year-end liquidity totaled $480 million, consisting of $35 million in cash and $445 million available under the revolving credit facility; net debt to adjusted EBITDA stood at 4.5x, with a net leverage ratio of 26.3%.
  • Dividend -- Board authorized a 5% increase in the annual cash dividend for 2026 to $1 per share, effective in April.
  • Acquisitions & Dispositions -- Completed five California asset sales and acquired 10 properties in Sunbelt markets with $460 million in gross acquisitions; two assets in the fourth quarter totaled $109 million and were funded through liquidity and approximately $30 million of assumed debt.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Core FFO per share expected to grow mid-single digits, with guidance for NAREIT FFO at $1.97-$2.03 per share (up 5.8% at midpoint) and core FFO at $1.91-$1.95 per share (up 5.5% at midpoint); same property NOI growth projected at 3.25%-4.25%, incorporating 30-70 basis points of bad debt reserve.
  • Leasing Metrics -- 2025 blended leasing spreads reached 13.3%, with new lease spreads at 30.9% and renewals at 10.9%; small shop occupancy hit a record 94%.
  • Rent Escalators -- Annual rent escalators for new and renewal small shop leases in 2025 averaged over 3.1%, the highest since InvenTrust Properties Corp. listed.
  • Portfolio Occupancy and Retention -- Year-end lease occupancy was 96.7%, and the retention rate was 85%, impacted by one planned anchor departure; occupancy and retention would have been stable otherwise.
  • Acquisition Pipeline Visibility -- Nearly half of the $300 million net acquisition activity for 2026 is either under contract or has high certainty of closing early in the year.
  • Balance Sheet Strategy -- Financing for acquisitions in 2026 is expected to use the line of credit and long-term private placement market debt, with net debt to adjusted EBITDA targeted at up to 5.5x on a forward basis if needed.
  • Dividend Increase Implementation -- The first payment under the new $1 per share annualized dividend rate will begin with the April cycle.
  • Redevelopment Contribution -- Redevelopment expected to contribute 50-100 basis points of incremental NOI growth annually for the next couple of years.
  • Signed Not Open Leases -- 2% of ABR ($5.5 million) represents signed not open leases, with 95% expected to come online in 2026 and roughly half of that recognized in current-year revenue.

SUMMARY

InvenTrust Properties Corp. (IVT +0.72%) delivered above 5% same property NOI growth for the second consecutive year while maintaining sector-low leverage and expanding its Sunbelt-focused portfolio through $460 million of acquisitions funded by internally generated capital and disciplined use of balance sheet liquidity. Retail leasing outpaced prior years, with blended leasing spreads and small shop occupancy at their highest levels since listing, reflecting resilient tenant demand and effective asset management. The 2026 outlook retains a prudent approach, projecting mid-single-digit core FFO growth, more than 3% same property NOI growth, and disciplined capital deployment towards incremental redevelopment and targeted acquisitions, half of which are already actionable. Management highlighted that sector fundamentals, including below-average retail construction and heightened acquisition competition, support the strategy to prioritize tenant retention, limit CapEx, and recycle assets, anchoring guidance to achievable leasing activity and modest credit loss assumptions.

  • Management stated that nearly $150 million of the 2026 acquisition target is under contract or highly visible for closing early in the year, boosting execution confidence.
  • Private placement market and long-term fixed-rate bank debt are preferred funding sources for new acquisitions to manage refinancing risk and interest costs.
  • Leadership expects redevelopment, excluding major anchor repositionings, to be accretive but not a primary capital allocation pillar.
  • The company maintains flexibility to temporarily exceed its typical leverage targets for strategic growth but will keep net debt to adjusted EBITDA below 5.5x.
  • No exposure to Amazon brick-and-mortar locations exists, according to management, and Whole Foods stores in the portfolio are described as "very profitable and have high sales volumes."
  • Store closures industry-wide rose, but new construction remained at historic lows, creating a scarcity of quality space that reinforced pricing power and leasing momentum in target markets.
  • Retention rates were temporarily impacted by a planned anchor exit at Gateway Market Center, but occupancy and renewal metrics otherwise trended stable or up.
  • Signed not open leases will primarily add to revenue beginning in 2026, with the majority representing small shops.
  • Management does not anticipate any significant weather-related expense impacts or risks from its signed not open pipeline for the forthcoming year.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Same Property NOI: Net operating income generated from properties held in the portfolio throughout the comparison periods, excluding acquisitions and dispositions.
  • NAREIT FFO: Net income attributable to common shareholders, excluding gains or losses on sales of properties, plus real estate depreciation—reflects operating performance as defined by the National Association of Real Estate Investment Trusts.
  • Core FFO: A non-GAAP measure adjusting NAREIT FFO to exclude certain non-recurring or non-core items, reflecting normalized operating results.
  • Leasing Spread: The percentage difference between the rent per square foot of new or renewed leases and the rent per square foot of prior leases in the same space.
  • Small Shop: Retail space typically less than 10,000 square feet, often leased to specialty or niche retailers.
  • ABR: Annualized Base Rent; the total rent that would be received on an annual basis from executing leases as of the reporting date.
  • Signed Not Open: Lease agreements executed but not yet generating rent revenue because the tenant has not opened for business.
