Eaton (ETN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 3, 2026 at 12:16PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 3, 2026, 11 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Paulo Ruiz Sternadt
  • Chief Financial Officer — Olivier Leonetti
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Yan Jin

TAKEAWAYS

  • Electrical Americas Orders -- Orders accelerated 16% on a rolling twelve-month basis, with quarterly orders rising more than 50%, and a sequential increase of over 18%.
  • Electrical Americas Backlog -- Backlog grew 31% year over year to a record $13.2 billion, providing high visibility for future revenue.
  • Data Center Segment -- Data center orders in Electrical Americas increased approximately 200%, and sales rose by more than 40% compared to Q4 2024.
  • Total Company Revenue -- Quarterly revenue reached $7.1 billion, with organic growth of 9% driven by Electrical and Aerospace segments.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Adjusted earnings per share rose 18% to $3.33, in line with guidance midpoint.
  • Segment Margins -- Margins reached a Q4 record of 24.9%, up 20 basis points year over year.
  • Electrical Americas Operating Margin -- Operating margin in the segment declined 180 basis points to 29.8%, due to ramp-up costs from capacity expansion.
  • Aerospace Segment -- Organic sales growth of 20%, with margins up 120 basis points to 24.1%, and backlog expansion of 16% year over year.
  • Vehicle Segment -- Organic revenue declined 13%, and margins declined by 230 basis points, attributed to weakness in North America truck and light vehicle end markets.
  • eMobility Segment -- Revenue decreased 15% (organic down 17%, partially offset by 2% favorable FX), with $10 million in operating profit.
  • Total Company Backlog -- Electrical Americas and Aerospace business backlogs totaled $19.6 billion, representing 29% and 16% year-over-year growth, respectively.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Organic revenue growth projected at 7%-9%, with segment margins between 24.6% and 25%, and adjusted EPS of $13.00-$13.50 (midpoint up 10%).
  • Capital Allocation -- $13 billion in announced investments for 2025, including acquisitions of FiberBond, Resilient Power Systems, UltraPCS, and the pending Boyd Terminal deal.
  • Mobility Spin-Off -- Announced intent to spin off the entire mobility business (vehicle and e-mobility) into a separately listed company with approximately $3 billion in annual revenue.
  • Mega Project Pipeline -- Mega project backlog increased 30% to $3 trillion, with 866 tracked projects; data centers represent 54% of year-to-date activity.
  • Share Repurchase Suspension -- Company does not plan to repurchase shares in 2026, citing focus on the Boyd acquisition and keeping share count flat.
  • Operational Capacity Investments -- Around $1.5 billion deployed for strategic capacity expansion across two dozen projects, with half completed and half expected to ramp by late 2026-2027.
  • Q1 2026 Guidance -- Organic growth expected at 5%-7%, with operating margins between 20.6% and 22.2% due to front-loaded ramp-up costs in Electrical Americas.

This signals near-term margin pressures from capacity expansions.
  • Segment margin in Electrical Americas declined 180 basis points to 29.8%, explicitly attributed to capacity ramp-up costs.
  • Vehicle and eMobility segments reported double-digit organic revenue declines, and margin contraction due to ongoing market weakness.

SUMMARY

Eaton (ETN +0.64%) management reported continued acceleration in orders and record backlogs driven by surging demand in Electrical Americas and aerospace, supported in particular by a 200% increase in data center segment orders. Strategic clarity was provided on the company's decision to spin off the mobility (vehicle and eMobility) businesses, aiming to focus the remaining portfolio on higher-growth, higher-margin end-markets. Robust capital allocation, in the form of multiple acquisitions, as well as $1.5 billion for targeted capacity expansions, is positioned to capitalize on durable, secular demand underpinned by a $3 trillion mega project pipeline. Management reiterated 2026 organic revenue growth guidance of 7%-9%, and delivered a detailed breakdown of short-term margin headwinds resulting from accelerated capacity ramps, which are expected to ease over the course of the year. The guidance also highlights a planned pause in share repurchases as the Boyd acquisition is completed.

  • Paulo Ruiz Sternadt stated, "about the business is we are adding, you know, integrating companies, like FiberBond, etcetera, and we continue to deliver 30% margins as a business," emphasizing scale-up capability despite margin pressures.
  • The mobility spin-off is anticipated to be immediately accretive to organic growth and operating margin for the remaining business, as per management's comments.
  • The composition of data center orders has shifted, with management reporting that "50% of our orders were cloud-based data center growth. The other half were AI," indicating significant exposure to higher-value AI infrastructure markets.
  • Order durations have shortened, with deliveries scheduled "between twelve and eighteen months," contrasting with earlier multi-year advance bookings.
  • Q1 2026 earnings guidance incorporates a first-half tax rate of 20%-21%, easing to 16%-17% in the second half, partially explaining projected EPS trajectory within the year.
  • Ramp-up headwinds from expansion are expected to peak in the first half, with progress forecasted for Q2 and Q3, and "a stronger pace of backlog liquidation in Q4" per management.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Book-to-bill: Ratio of orders received to sales billed in the same period, indicating future demand relative to current sales.
  • Mega projects: Large-scale, capital-intensive industrial or infrastructure initiatives with multi-year timelines common in power management and data center markets.
  • Cold plate: Component used in liquid cooling systems to dissipate heat directly from high-power electronic devices, such as data center chips.
  • CDU (Coolant Distribution Unit): Central component of data center liquid cooling systems responsible for distributing and returning coolant fluid to maintain optimal operating temperatures in electronic hardware.
  • ESA margin: Refers to Electrical Sector Americas operating margin, a key profitability metric for this Eaton segment.
