Earnings Call Transcript

Kosmos Energy (KOS) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 2, 2026 at 12:15PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Monday, Mar. 2, 2026 at 11 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Andrew G. Inglis
  • Chief Financial Officer — Neal D. Shah

Takeaways

  • 1P Reserves Replacement -- 90% reported for the year; adjusting for the Equatorial Guinea asset sale, ratio increases to 120%.
  • Jubilee Production Performance -- Surpassed 70,000 barrels of oil per day gross; recent J-74 producer well contributing 13,000 barrels per day gross.
  • GTA Liquefied Natural Gas Output -- Averaged 2,900,000 tonnes per annum equivalent year to date, exceeding nameplate volume of 2,700,000 tonnes per annum in December.
  • GTA 2026 Volume Guidance -- Targeting 32 to 36 gross LNG cargoes and three gross condensate cargoes shipped; 6.5 cargoes already lifted year to date.
  • Operating Cost Reduction -- Targeting an absolute OpEx reduction of more than $100 million year over year, rising to approximately $250 million once Equatorial Guinea assets are sold.
  • 2025 Capital Expenditure -- $290 million, down nearly 70% year over year and the lowest since 2017.
  • 2026 Capital Expenditure Guidance -- $350 million expected, with $40 million for the TEN FPSO acquisition; 70% allocated to Ghana and 15% to Gulf of America projects.
  • Debt Actions -- Completed $350 million Nordic bond in January; proceeds used to pay down $250 million of 2027 notes and $100 million on the RBL facility.
  • Leverage Covenant Waiver -- Banks granted waiver for year-end 2025 and midyear 2026; midyear covenant threshold temporarily increased from 3.5x to 4.25x.
  • Hedging Program -- 8.5 million barrels of oil hedged for 2026 and 2 million for 2027; post-Equatorial Guinea sale, hedge exposure for 2026 exceeds 50%.
  • Jubilee Drilling Payback -- Last 12 wells averaged about nine months for payback; two newest wells showing closer to six-month payback periods.
  • TEN FPSO Purchase -- Sale and purchase agreement signed for acquisition upon lease expiration in early 2027; anticipated to drive significant OpEx reductions starting in 2026.
  • Ghana Licenses Extended -- Jubilee and TEN licenses formally extended to 2040.
  • GTA OpEx Per MMBtu -- Targeting a greater than 50% reduction due to higher throughput and FPSO refinancing; approximately half of the reduction attributable to each driver.
  • EG Asset Sale -- Producing assets in Equatorial Guinea being divested, unlocking liquidity and accelerating debt reduction.
  • 2026 Production and Cost Targets -- Management aims for 15% production growth and 20% total operating cost reduction, translating to an estimated 35% OpEx-per-barrel decrease.
  • Gulf of America Asset Developments -- Performance at Odd Job and Kodiak met expectations with minimal storm downtime, offset by underperformance at Winterfell, where an impairment was taken.
  • Gulf of America Growth Projects -- Advanced Tiberias development plan with partner Oxy; FID targeted for 2026, farm-down process to follow; strategic alliance formed with Shell to jointly explore the Norfolk play, with drilling at Trailblazer expected in 2027.

Risks

  • OpEx in the fourth quarter was higher than expected, driven by increased costs at Equatorial Guinea.
  • Winterfell asset underperformed, leading to a recorded impairment following a fair value assessment.
  • Production growth for 2025 was slower than originally planned, and net debt closed the year higher than targeted.
  • The sale of Equatorial Guinea assets will decrease the RBL borrowing base by approximately $100 million upon closing.

Summary

Kosmos Energy (KOS +0.78%) disclosed strategic execution on asset high-grading, including the pending sale of Equatorial Guinea assets and a focus on lower-cost production. Management confirmed significant progress in cost reduction and capital discipline, reflecting a multiyear-low CapEx baseline and aggressive 2026 OpEx targets. The company finalized a $350 million bond transaction to proactively address near-term maturities and support deleveraging efforts. Major growth activities in Ghana and at GTA underpin guidance for rising production and materially lower unit costs, while robust hedging coverage is in place for 2026. License extensions in Ghana and an expanded alliance with Shell extend the reserve runway and open access to new Gulf of America development opportunities.

  • GTA LNG production throughput year to date exceeded facility nameplate, with 6.5 gross LNG cargoes shipped year to date in 2026.
  • Management described the "constructive" banking environment in securing a leverage covenant waiver, which raises the midyear 2026 threshold to 4.25x and permits operational delivery to drive future deleveraging.
  • Chairman and CEO Inglis specified that Jubilee drilling returns are informed by enhanced seismic imaging, with the J-75 producer expected online by end of quarter and more high-return wells planned.
  • About 6,000 barrels per day of full-year 2026 production guidance originates from the divested Equatorial Guinea assets, with guidance to be updated upon transaction close.
  • Anticipated RBL base reduction of about $100 million upon EG asset sale will be offset by the over-collateralization from Ghana reserves; no near-term liquidity issues indicated.
  • Capital allocation remains tightly focused on rapid-payback wells in Ghana and select Gulf of America projects, with larger commitments for assets like Tiberias deferred until debt levels further improve.
  • Partner integration and infrastructure access with Shell in the Gulf of America create mutual technical and development synergies, with cost and tieback advantages explicitly cited by management.

Industry glossary

  • 1P Reserves: Proven reserves estimated to have a 90% probability of being produced with current technology and economics.
  • 2P Reserves: The sum of proven plus probable reserves, representing a 50% probability of recovery.
  • RBL: Reserve-based lending facility, a type of credit facility secured against the company's oil and gas reserves.
  • FPSO: Floating Production, Storage, and Offloading vessel, used for offshore oil production operations.
  • OBN Seismic: Ocean Bottom Node seismic survey, a geophysical data collection method improving reservoir imaging.
  • FLNG: Floating Liquefied Natural Gas facility, allowing processing and liquefaction of natural gas offshore.
  • MMBtu: One million British thermal units, a standardized energy measurement commonly used in natural gas markets.
  • FID: Final Investment Decision, the project's formal commitment to proceed with full-scale implementation.
  • ILX: Infrastructure-Led Exploration, exploration strategy focusing on tieback opportunities close to existing infrastructure.
