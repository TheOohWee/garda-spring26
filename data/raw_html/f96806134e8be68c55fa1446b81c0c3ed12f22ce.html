First Merchants (FRME) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 10:31AM EST

Date

Thursday, July 24, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Mark Hardwick
  • President — Michael Stewart
  • Chief Financial Officer — Michele Kawiecki
  • Chief Credit Officer — John Martin

Takeaways

  • Net Income -- $56.4 million, up $17 million or 43% as improved credit quality lowered provision expense.
  • Earnings Per Share -- $0.98 for the quarter and $1.92 year-to-date, representing a $0.44 or 30% increase for the first half.
  • Annualized Loan Growth -- 9.1% for the quarter, with year-to-date loan growth of $430 million, or 9% annualized.
  • Commercial Loan Growth -- $262 million in the quarter, over 10% annualized, driven by CapEx financing, revolver usage, M&A, and new business conversion.
  • Consumer Loan Growth -- $36 million increase in the quarter, led by residential mortgage, HELOC, and private banking.
  • Total Deposit Growth -- $347 million in the commercial segment, while consumer deposits declined.
  • Non-Maturity Deposit Balances -- Increased by $108 million year-to-date, marked as lowest cost deposit category.
  • Net Interest Income -- Increased $2.8 million quarter over quarter, with total of $139.2 million on a fully tax-equivalent basis.
  • Net Interest Margin -- 3.25%, up 3 basis points from the prior quarter, as yield on earning assets increased by 11 basis points.
  • Pretax Pre-Provision Earnings -- $70.7 million for the quarter, $138.1 million year-to-date, up 7.3% compared to last year.
  • Noninterest Income -- $31.3 million, customer-related fees up $1.6 million year over year driven by mortgage gains and treasury management.
  • Noninterest Expense -- $93.6 million, an increase of $700,000 from the prior quarter tied to higher marketing and data processing costs.
  • Efficiency Ratio -- 53.99% for the quarter.
  • Tangible Common Equity Ratio -- 8.92%, up 65 basis points year over year.
  • Common Equity Tier 1 Ratio -- 11.35%, showing continued capital strength.
  • Provision for Credit Losses -- $5.6 million, sharply reduced from $24.5 million in the same quarter last year.
  • Allowance for Credit Losses -- $195.3 million with a coverage ratio of 1.47%; including fair value marks brings total coverage to 1.58%.
  • Net Charge-Offs -- $2.3 million, equating to 0.07% of average loans annualized.
  • Investment Portfolio Reduction -- Lower-yielding bonds declined by $372 million over 12 months, reallocated to higher-yielding loans (+$654 million).
  • Share Repurchases -- $22.1 million this quarter, $31.7 million year-to-date at an average price of $38.68.
  • Loan Portfolio Yield -- Rose 11 basis points to 6.32%; new originations and refis averaged 7.04% yield, up 8 basis points sequentially.
  • Brokered Funding -- $1.2 billion, about 8% of total funding; management maintains a 10% internal limit.
  • Office CRE Concentration -- Top 10 non-owner-occupied office loans represent 53% of the office portfolio, with the largest at $25 million and a 67.2% loan-to-value.
  • Technology Enhancements -- Recent upgrades reduced branch account opening times to "5 or 10 minutes," increased treasury management capabilities, and improved private wealth platforms.
  • Commercial Borrower Base -- 82.7% located in core four-state Midwest region.
  • Sponsor Finance Portfolio -- $867 million outstanding across 95 companies; nearly 80% of borrowers have senior leverage under 3x, 70% with fixed charge coverage ratios above 1.5x.
  • Residential Mortgage Credit Quality -- 91% of the $1.9 billion portfolio had origination FICO scores above 669.
  • Fee Income Outlook -- Kawiecki said, "I would expect to see noninterest income grow into the mid-single digits in the back half of the year."
  • Expense Outlook -- Kawiecki said, "maybe 1% to 2% as we refine our incentive accruals and carry out some of our marketing initiatives."
  • Margin Outlook -- Kawiecki projected, "modest compression in margin in the back half of the year compared to this quarter."
  • Provision Outlook -- Management expects to "continue to provide for the growth that we see in our balance sheet" at the current coverage ratio, barring major macroeconomic changes.
  • Tax Rate Guidance -- Kawiecki advised, "plan for it to approach maybe 13% in the back half of the year."
  • Fixed Rate Loan Repricing -- About $200 million of fixed rate loans set to reprice in the back half of 2025 at an average yield of about 5%.
  • Asset Sensitivity -- Kawiecki stated, "each rate cut on an annualized basis, I would expect about 2 basis points of compression for each 25 basis point rate cut."
  • Hiring Activity -- Five key commercial and private wealth hires joined in the first half, with five more anticipated in the second half; impact on growth yet to materialize.

Summary

First Merchants Corporation (FRME 0.96%) reported 43% net income growth, mainly due to reduced credit loss provisions as credit quality normalized. The company delivered robust commercial loan expansion and continued to shift its balance sheet toward higher-yielding assets while maintaining strong capital ratios. Expense control persisted, with expectation for only minor increases ahead, as management reinforced confidence in ongoing organic growth through strategic hiring and process automation. Customer-related fees outpaced prior year, but management cautioned on compressed net interest margin stemming from heightened deposit competition and funding costs in the coming quarters.

  • Management continued to deploy excess capital via share repurchases, retiring $31.7 million at an average price of $38.68 year-to-date, while also keeping open the option for future acquisitions.
  • Commercial loan pipeline stability led management to project sustained growth levels, but consumer deposit outflows and ongoing competitive dynamics could affect future funding costs.
  • Credit metrics across commercial, commercial real estate, and consumer portfolios remained favorable; net charge-offs were 0.07% annualized and allowance ratios held steady, even as isolated nonaccruals surfaced.
  • Recent technology and talent investments have yet to materially impact results, but are expected to support longer-term productivity and fee income expansion.

Industry glossary

  • HELOC: Home Equity Line of Credit, a revolving credit facility secured by residential real estate, enabling homeowners to borrow against the equity in their property.
  • Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) Ratio: A regulatory capital metric representing core equity tier 1 capital as a share of risk-weighted assets, reflecting the bank's primary capital strength.
  • ORE: Other Real Estate owned by the bank, typically assets obtained through foreclosure or forfeiture.
  • Efficiency Ratio: Noninterest expense divided by net revenue, used to assess expense control and operational efficiency for banks.
