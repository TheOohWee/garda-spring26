Takeaways

Consolidated revenue -- $268 million in consolidated revenue for Q3 2025, rising 11.5% year-over-year.

GAAP earnings from operations -- $55 million in earnings from operations for Q3 2025, representing a 13.7% increase for the third quarter compared to the prior year.

Net income -- $40 million in net income for Q3 2025, a 19.1% year-over-year increase for the third quarter.

Diluted net earnings per share (GAAP) -- $1.24 diluted net earnings per share for Q3 2025.

Adjusted EBITDA -- $71 million in adjusted EBITDA for Q3 2025, up 11% year-over-year, with an adjusted EBITDA margin rate of 26.7%.

Adjusted net earnings -- $44 million in adjusted net earnings for Q3 2025, an increase of 19.1% from the prior year for the third quarter, or $1.35 per diluted share for the third quarter (up 22¢ or 19.5% compared to the prior year).

Gross margin dollars and rate -- $95 million (up 11.8%) for Q3 2025 with gross margin at 35.7% of sales, which is a 10 basis point improvement compared to the prior year.

Operating expenses -- $41 million in earnings from operations for the Human Nutrition and Health segment for Q3 2025, up from $37 million in the third quarter of the prior year, attributed to higher professional services and compensation costs.

Segment revenue (Human Nutrition & Health) -- $174 million for Q3 2025, advancing 14.3% year-over-year, with food ingredient business up nearly 7% and nutrient portfolio up about 30%.

Segment revenue (Animal Nutrition & Health) -- $56 million for Q3 2025, an increase of 6.6% compared to the prior year, led by both ruminant and monogastric businesses.

Segment revenue (Specialty Products) -- $36 million in Specialty Products segment sales for Q3 2025, up 7.5% year-over-year, with Plant Nutrition growing 13% compared to the prior year and Performance Gases up 7% compared to the prior year (combined, these do not sum due to their respective weighting in total segment sales).

Free cash flow -- $51 million in free cash flow for the third quarter of 2025, reflecting continued strong operational cash generation.

Net debt -- Net debt was $89 million as of Q3 2025, with a net leverage ratio of 0.3 as of the third quarter of 2025, and closing cash balance of $65 million as of Q3 2025.

Tax rate -- Effective tax rate at 22.6% for Q3 2025, with management expecting approximately 22.5% effective tax rate for the full year 2025.

Encapsulation product growth -- Encapsulation business achieved approximately 26% growth in Q3 2025, with a new facility scheduled for completion in early 2027 to double existing capacity.

Trade developments -- Preliminary European Union antidumping duties on China-origin choline chloride set between 120% and 195% as of late 2025, pending final approval by year end, to be finalized by year end and expected to last five years if enacted.

Marketing & scientific advances -- The NIH-funded choline biomarker study has completed and published results showing that combined plasma choline and betaine levels are strongly predictive of dietary choline intake.

Summary

Balchem (BCPC 0.16%) set multiple financial records for Q3 2025, including consolidated revenue, adjusted EBITDA (non-GAAP), and adjusted net earnings (non-GAAP), with all three main business segments showing year-over-year sales growth. The Human Nutrition & Health segment led gains, particularly driven by robust increases in both nutrient and food ingredient portfolios. Company leadership cited further momentum from current “better for you” consumer trends, with the food ingredient business seeing performance acceleration linked directly to those demand drivers. Specialty Products showed marked geographic expansion—especially in Plant Nutrition—supported by international market wins in Latin America and Asia-Pacific, while Performance Gases rebounded in Europe. Newly announced and pending European antidumping actions on choline chloride, if finalized, could materially shift competitive dynamics over the next five years. The animal and human encapsulation product category continues to grow rapidly, prompting investment in a new facility expected to support continued double-digit growth in this high-margin product line.

CEO Harris said, "2025 was the twenty-fifth consecutive quarter of quarterly year-over-year growth in adjusted EBITDA for Balchem."

Balchem fully offset tariff impacts through alternate supply chain measures and anticipates success handling future trade policy changes.

Adjusted debt repayment will depend in part on the pace and scale of upcoming mergers and acquisitions as well as share repurchases intended to keep the share count stable.

Management sees significant runway for penetration of minerals, chelated minerals, and nutrients—envisioning market opportunities at three to four times today’s scale.

Harris stated, "The encapsulation business, for example, grew about 26% in Q3 2025." and new facility construction is underway to support continued high growth.

Industry glossary

Encapsulation: Technology used to surround nutrients or other ingredients within a protective coating, promoting targeted delivery and controlled release in nutritional and health products.

Choline chloride: A nutrient additive used primarily in animal feed and increasingly in human health for its role in cellular function and metabolism.

Chelated minerals: Minerals chemically bonded to organic molecules, improving bioavailability and absorption in both human and animal nutrition formulations.

GLP-1 drugs: Medications that position glucagon-like peptide-1 agonists for metabolic disorders, with effects on dietary trends and nutritional supplementation needs.

Adjusted EBITDA: Non-GAAP measure reflecting earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, and certain other adjustments to better represent operational cash profitability.

NIH-funded choline biomarker study: A clinical investigation designed to establish reliable blood-based indicators (biomarkers) for dietary choline intake, supporting both product positioning and public health research.
