Earnings Call Transcript

GM (GM) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 12:44PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Oct. 22, 2024 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chair and Chief Executive Officer — Mary Barra
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Paul Jacobson
  • President, GM Financial — Daniel Berce

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $49 billion, reflecting 10% year-over-year growth driven by higher volumes in both ICE and EV segments.
  • EBIT adjusted -- $4.1 billion, representing an 8.4% EBIT-adjusted margin and a $400 million benefit from timing impacts shifted from the fourth quarter.
  • EPS diluted adjusted -- $2.96, up approximately 30% year over year.
  • Adjusted automotive free cash flow -- $5.8 billion, up $900 million from the prior year, attributable to EBIT growth, lower capital expenditures, and higher working capital.
  • North America EBIT adjusted -- $4 billion with a 9.7% margin, up $500 million year over year, aided by strong pricing and an EV valuation allowance benefit.
  • U.S. incentives -- Approximately 2.4 percentage points below the industry average, widening the gap from the prior year's 1 percentage point differential.
  • Pricing benefit -- $900 million increase year over year, with about half attributed to mid-size SUVs, especially Chevrolet Traverse; the remainder primarily from lapping earlier price adjustments on full-size SUVs and Corvette.
  • Warranty expense -- $700 million year-over-year increase due to inflation and specific high-volume vehicle claims, addressed by implemented fixes in production.
  • Dealer inventory -- 68 days for ICE vehicles and 10 to 12 EVs per dealer, with targeted year-end ICE inventory between 50 days and 60 days.
  • GM International EBIT adjusted -- $50 million, down $300 million year over year, attributed to ongoing challenges in China; South America and Middle East gains offset by competitive and FX headwinds.
  • China quarterly sales -- 14% increase sequentially; new energy vehicles surpassed ICE models in sales for the first time; dealer inventory reduced by over 50% since January.
  • GM Financial EBT adjusted -- $700 million, down $50 million year over year, with credit reserves in line with expectations and a $450 million dividend paid to GM.
  • Cruise expenses -- $400 million, representing a $350 million reduction year over year, via decreased operational activities.
  • Stock repurchase -- $1 billion spent in the quarter, retiring 23 million shares and reducing diluted share count by 19% year over year to 1.12 billion.
  • EV production and profitability -- On track for approximately 200,000 EVs produced and wholesaled in North America, with variable profit positive for the quarter.
  • Blazer and Equinox EV launches -- Full lineup expected in market in the first quarter of 2025, with a new affordable Blazer version added.
  • Guidance update -- Raised full-year 2024 EBIT adjusted to $14 billion to $15 billion and EPS diluted adjusted to $10 to $10.50; adjusted automotive free cash flow outlook increased to $12.5 billion to $13.5 billion.
  • Share count reduction target -- Commitment to decrease shares outstanding to below 1 billion in early 2025, with an additional 25 million shares expected to be retired in the fourth quarter.
  • Capital expenditure -- Remains near $11 billion, balanced between ICE and EV investment, with about one-third dedicated to ICE vehicle programs.
  • GM Financial penetration -- Approximately 39% for the quarter, with a stated long-term target of 40% to 45%.

Risks

  • China market pressure -- GM International EBIT-adjusted declined $300 million year over year, explicitly attributed to the "continued challenges in the China market."
  • Warranty cost inflation -- Jacobson cited a $700 million third-quarter year-over-year increase in warranty expense, driven by inflationary pressures and specific quality issues, despite implemented fixes.
  • Fourth quarter lower earnings -- Management expects lower fourth-quarter earnings due to "lower expected ICE wholesale volume," lost production days from supply chain disruptions, and "lower pricing in part due to higher seasonal industry incentives."

Summary

General Motors (GM 0.40%) delivered 10% year-over-year revenue growth to $49 billion and raised full-year financial guidance to the upper end of prior ranges. Management confirmed adjusted EBIT of $4.1 billion and adjusted automotive free cash flow of $5.8 billion, driven by resilient pricing and disciplined cost control in the ICE portfolio, while citing a $700 million rise in warranty expenses. Sequential sales in China rose 14%, marking the first quarter where new energy vehicle sales exceeded ICE, but ongoing market pressures led to a $300 million year over year EBIT-adjusted decline for GM International. Share count was further reduced by 19% year over year via repurchase, with an ongoing commitment to fall below 1 billion outstanding shares by early 2025. Variable profit positivity was achieved for the EV portfolio, with North American production and wholesales on track for 200,000 units, supported by ongoing product launches and margin improvements.

  • Chevrolet's new ICE models accelerated share growth and premium pricing, with Equinox attaining ATPs $6,000 higher than prior versions, and driving younger consumer acquisition.
  • GM's EV segment held the number two U.S. market share in the third quarter, nearing a 10% overall share, and exceeding 60% conquest rates.
  • Battery cost advantages were credited to vertical integration and partnerships with LGES in Ohio and Tennessee, yielding manufacturing credits and enhanced cell cost efficiencies.
  • Inventory management resulted in more than a 50% reduction in China dealer inventory since January, intended to support pricing discipline and cost control.
  • GM expects capital outlay stability at approximately $11 billion, prioritizing product development over infrastructure, and aims to adapt mix based on consumer demand between ICE and EV.

Industry glossary

  • ATP: Average transaction price — the mean price paid by consumers for a vehicle at retail, reflecting the pricing power of a model or brand.
  • Variable profit positive: Occurs when a product line (e.g., EVs) produces a positive profit contribution before fixed costs, reflecting improvement in core manufacturing and sales margins.
  • New energy vehicles: Vehicles powered in full or in part by alternative energy sources, such as plug-in hybrid and battery electric vehicles, as referenced in the China market discussion.
  • EBIT adjusted: Earnings before interest and taxes, modified to exclude selected non-core items, as stated directly by management for core operational assessment.
  • EBT adjusted: Earnings before taxes with adjustments for specific items, utilized by GM Financial to measure segment profitability.
