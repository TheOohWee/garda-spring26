TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $9.3 billion, representing 18% growth, with 4% sequential decline due to seasonality and lower AI Systems revenue.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $0.65, marking a record and exceeding the high end of the guidance range.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $708 million, notably strong for a typically outflow-heavy first quarter.
  • Networking Segment Revenue -- $2.7 billion, reflecting 152% growth reported and 7% normalized growth year over year; operating margin reached 23.7%.
  • Networking Orders -- Orders outpaced revenue, with data center switching up mid-40% and routing up mid-20% normalized, driven by AI data center demand.
  • WiFi 7 Access Points -- More than 10 times increase in units sold and 28% growth in devices connected to Mist and Aruba Central platforms.
  • Cloud & AI Segment Revenue -- $6.3 billion, down 3%, consistent with company outlook and primarily impacted by timing of AI server revenue and stable traditional server and storage performance.
  • Cloud & AI Operating Margin -- 10.2%, with operating profit up 18% year over year and 4% sequentially due to pricing discipline and cost actions.
  • AI Systems Backlog -- Entered the next quarter with a record $5 billion backlog, mainly from enterprise and sovereign customers; sales pipeline multiples of this figure.
  • Alletra MP Storage -- Orders up 42%, achieving five consecutive quarters of double-digit year-over-year growth.
  • GreenLake Adoption -- Nearly 50,000 customers on the platform, and company is on track for $3.5 billion ARR by fiscal year-end 2026.
  • Financial Services Return on Equity -- Achieved a record 27% return on equity in the quarter, with revenue growth roughly flat.
  • Juniper Networks (NYSE:JNPR) Integration -- Phase 1 completed and sales integration milestone achieved; cost and revenue synergies are on track for fiscal year targets.
  • Outlook Raised -- Full year EPS guidance increased to $2.30-$2.50 and free cash flow guidance raised to at least $2 billion; Networking full year revenue growth outlook raised to 68%-73% reported, or mid- to high single digit normalized.
  • AI Systems Orders for Networking -- Cumulative “networks for AI” order target increased to $1.7 billion-$1.9 billion by fiscal year-end 2026.
  • Operating Margin Guidance -- Company expects full year Networking operating margins in the low 20% range and Cloud & AI segment margins of 7%-9%, consistent with the updated outlook.
  • Commodity Cost Actions -- Multiple memory price increases implemented since November 2025, with ongoing supply agreements and shorter quoting cycles to protect margins against DRAM/NAND inflation.
  • Capital Returns -- $190 million returned via dividend and $158 million through share repurchases during the quarter.
  • Leverage Ratios -- Pro forma net leverage reduced from 3.1 times to 2.6 times due to improved cash and profitability post-Juniper Networks acquisition.
  • Fiscal Q2 Guidance -- Predicted total revenue between $9.6 billion-$10 billion; Networking revenue up 142%-152% year over year reported; EPS expected between $0.51-$0.55; GAAP EPS between $0.09-$0.13.

SUMMARY

Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE +0.24%) reported that orders continued to exceed shipments, leading to a significant backlog and indicating sustained strong customer demand across segments. Management detailed that supply constraints—especially in memory—mean current outlooks are based only on secured supply, not total market demand. Commodity price increases, primarily in DRAM and NAND, are being dynamically passed through to customers, affecting average unit prices, and are expected to persist through 2027. Segment guidance for Networking was raised on the back of market share gains in data center switching and routing, while the Cloud & AI growth outlook was trimmed to reflect margin-focused strategic prioritization. Management reported achieving planned integration milestones for the Juniper Networks (NYSE:JNPR) acquisition, with synergy realization progressing on schedule and driving both increased scale and operational leverage. The company underscored its pivot toward higher-margin product segments and disciplined cost actions as the foundation for raised free cash flow and earnings guidance despite persistent inflationary headwinds.

  • Marie Myers explained, "We implemented DRAM-related price increases starting in November 2025, shortened quote commitment cycles and are more tightly coordinated across our supply chain, pricing and sales organizations."
  • Operating expense declined 5% sequentially, supporting improved margins and demonstrating efficiency gains from the Catalyst initiative and Juniper Networks integration.
  • GreenLake subscription ARR is projected to be a key growth lever, underpinned by the expanding customer base and high attach rates in networking and storage.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • GreenLake: HPE’s consumption-based cloud platform for IT infrastructure, software, and services, offering as-a-service and subscription models both on- and off-premises.
  • Alletra MP: HPE’s own intellectual property (IP) storage platform focused on block and object-based solutions.
  • Networks for AI: HPE term for solution sets targeting networking infrastructure to support AI data center workloads, including data center switching and routing products supporting large-scale AI deployments.
  • Normalized Basis: A reporting standard where current and historical figures are adjusted to reflect the inclusion of acquired businesses (e.g., Juniper Networks) from the start of the prior fiscal period, facilitating direct year-over-year comparability.
  • AIOps: Artificial Intelligence for IT Operations, entailing the use of machine learning and analytics to automate and optimize IT network management processes.
