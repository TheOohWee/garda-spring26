  • AI and Digital Capabilities -- Management emphasized competitive differentiation and backlog growth driven by adoption of digital twins and predictive analytics.

Summary

Jacobs Solutions (J 3.35%) reported a 21% increase in backlog, marking a new high and supported by several large and complex program wins across multiple end markets. Management raised 2026 full-year net revenue, adjusted EPS, and free cash flow guidance, highlighting confidence in execution and pipeline conversion. The company also announced its intent to acquire the remaining stake in PA Consulting, which is expected to drive both cost synergies and accretive EPS performance once closed. Notably, PA Consulting posted a 27% gain in operating profit and is set to benefit from sustained demand in digital consulting and advisory services. The company raised its dividend by 12.5% and accelerated share repurchases in response to share price dislocation. Free cash flow topped $365 million, and management expects to maintain a minimum 60% capital return ratio to shareholders for the year.

  • CEO Bob Pragada said, "the strong start to the year gives us confidence to increase our FY 2026 outlook for net revenue, adjusted EPS, and free cash flow margin."
  • The high single‑digit water revenue growth was led by international markets, particularly the UK, Middle East, and Australia.
  • Growth in life sciences and advanced manufacturing was driven by programs in AI data centers and semiconductor sectors, with management expecting these to lead segment growth for the year.
  • Management expects margin progression in INAF during the year, supported by operating leverage, global delivery expansion, and favorable commercial mix.
  • The majority of backlog growth includes a high pass-through revenue component, especially from equipment sourcing in data center projects.
  • Book-to-bill ratio of 2.0 times in Q1 was attributed to “several large awards” and is anticipated to drive continued net revenue growth into FY 2026 and beyond.
  • Pass-through revenues are primarily tied to procurement in data center and large-scale infrastructure contracts, with fee-based arrangements on both trade contractors and equipment vendors.
  • International business revenue increased by more than 9%, reflecting balanced global growth.
  • The acquisition of the remaining PA Consulting stake will raise net leverage above the target range, but management expects deleveraging within the subsequent four quarters.
  • Management reiterated that the adoption of AI platforms enables simultaneous top line growth and operational efficiency in a resource-constrained environment.
  • Q1 free cash flow was supported by a customer prepayment for data center work and will decline in Q2 as related subcontractor payments are made.

Industry glossary

  • Book-to-Bill: Ratio of orders received to revenue billed for a given period, indicating business growth momentum or contraction.
  • Pass-Through Revenue: Revenue for third-party goods or services procured on behalf of a client, recorded without a significant margin component.
  • Adjusted EPS: Earnings per share after adjusting for non-recurring items, providing a clearer view of recurring profitability.
  • INAF (Infrastructure & Advanced Facilities): Jacobs Solutions' business segment covering infrastructure and complex facility design, engineering, and program management.
  • EPCM: Engineering, Procurement, and Construction Management—delivery model where the contractor manages project design, procurement, and construction without assuming construction risk.
  • Noncontrolling Interest (NCI): Portion of equity ownership in a subsidiary not attributable to the parent company.
