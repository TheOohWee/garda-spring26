McDonald's (MCD) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 8:38PM EST

Date

Feb. 11, 2026, 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Christopher Kempczinski
  • Chief Financial Officer — Ian Borden
  • Chief Restaurant Experience Officer — Jill McDonald

Takeaways

  • System-wide sales -- Nearly $140 billion for the year, marking a 5.5% increase in constant currency.
  • Global comparable sales -- Increased 5.7% in the fourth quarter, with positive comparable guest counts.
  • U.S. comparable sales -- Rose 6.8% in the fourth quarter, attributed to both check and guest count growth.
  • International operated market comp sales -- Increased 5.2% in the segment for the quarter, driven by the U.K., Germany, and Australia.
  • International developmental licensed markets comp sales -- Grew 4.5% in the quarter, led by Japan across all regions.
  • Net restaurant openings -- 1,880 net new restaurants in 2025, supporting a 4.5% unit growth expectation for 2026 from approximately 2,100 net additions.
  • Gross restaurant openings -- 2,275 in 2025; target of approximately 2,600 in 2026, with about 1,000 in China.
  • Adjusted earnings per share -- $3.12 for the fourth quarter, including a $0.10 benefit from foreign currency translation.
  • Operating margin -- Reported 46.9% adjusted operating margin for the full year and guided for mid- to high 40% range for 2026.
  • Restaurant margin dollars -- Exceeded $15 billion for the year.
  • Capital expenditures -- $3.4 billion in 2025; anticipated increase to $3.7 billion-$3.9 billion in 2026, primarily for new restaurant openings.
  • U.S. owner-operator cash flow -- Increased year over year, supported by value-oriented menu strategies.
  • Loyalty program size -- 210 million 90-day active users globally, with a target to reach 250 million by end of 2027.
  • Digital engagement -- 46 million 90-day active users in the U.S. app, and nearly 500 million MONOPOLY games played during the event.
  • Marketing campaign outcomes -- The Grinch campaign set a single-day sales record and sold almost as many units as the combined 2025 Minecraft and 2024 collector cups promotions; about 50 million Grinch socks sold globally in the campaign's first days.
  • Product and menu initiatives -- Continued rollout of Best Burger in more than 85 markets, permanent addition of Big Arch in the U.K., and introduction of menu innovations such as McWings and Matcha lattes in Australia.
  • Future capex guidance -- Estimated $300 million-$500 million annual capex increases consistent with 2023 Investor Day plans, reflecting acceleration of growth pipeline.
  • G&A expense outlook -- Targeting about 2.2% of system-wide sales for 2026, with investments focused on technology, digital, and Global Business Services (GBS).
  • Interest expense outlook -- Projected increase of 4%-6% in 2026 due to higher average interest rates.
  • Effective tax rate guidance -- Estimated full-year range of 21%-23% for 2026, with potential quarterly volatility.
  • Foreign currency impact -- Management expects a $0.20-$0.30 per share earnings benefit for 2026, given current rates.
  • Net income to free cash flow conversion -- Achieved 84% in 2025; targeting low to mid-80% range in 2026.
  • Category management structure -- Beef, beverages, and chicken verticals led by dedicated teams to accelerate innovation and execution speed.
  • Best Burger rollout -- In over 85 markets, with company on track for global coverage by end of 2026.
  • Chicken category share -- Management achieved 2025 target of deploying McCrispy Sandwich equity in nearly all major markets; aiming for at least a 1 percentage point market share increase by end of 2026 versus December 2023.
  • New beverage launch -- Successful U.S. test in 500+ restaurants exceeded expectations and will underpin a McCafe-branded rollout later in 2026.
  • Loyalty impact in U.S. -- U.S. loyalty members visit 2.5x more frequently (26 visits post-enrollment vs. 10.5 prior), and spend more over time.

Summary

The call highlighted McDonald's (MCD 1.25%) 2025 financial performance, new restaurant expansion, accelerating digital engagement, evolving menu and category strategies, and updated capital allocation plans. Management confirmed 2026 operating margin guidance in the mid- to high-40% range and outlined increases in capital spending, driven by accelerated unit growth, particularly in growth markets such as China. Structural changes, such as new category management and the scaling of digital and loyalty platforms, were emphasized as enabling factors for increased productivity, rapid innovation, and customer engagement.

  • Executives projected that net restaurant expansion will add approximately 2.5% to system-wide sales in 2026, with 2,600 gross openings targeted, and over 1,000 planned in China.
  • The U.S. value offerings, such as McValue and relaunch of extra value meals, produced measurable gains in low-income consumer share, and improved affordability scores.
  • U.S. digital marketing events, including MONOPOLY and The Grinch, generated record customer engagement and the highest single sales day in company history.
  • Management expects capex in 2026 to reach $3.7 billion-$3.9 billion, primarily for new unit development, consistent with multiyear targets set at the 2023 Investor Day.
  • The company expects annual effective tax rates for 2026 to fall between 21% and 23%, with G&A expenses at about 2.2% of system-wide sales.
  • Recent investments in AI-enabled restaurant technologies and a common global tech stack are intended to boost speed, operational consistency, and customer satisfaction systemwide.
  • Management stated, "we know that loyalty increases visit frequency," noting a nearly 2.5x rise in average visits among U.S. members following enrollment.
  • The 2026 beverage launch under the McCafe brand will leverage successful learnings from previous CosMc's tests, and will target all dayparts to drive incremental occasions and higher average check.

Industry glossary

  • EDAP (Everyday Affordable Price options): Value menu initiatives in international operated markets offering consistent lower-priced items and bundles.
  • EVM (Extra Value Meals): Bundled meal promotions at a discount to a la carte prices, central to U.S. and global value positioning.
  • IOM (International Operated Markets): Markets where McDonald's owns and operates the majority of restaurants directly, rather than franchising.
  • IDL (International Developmental Licensed Markets): Franchised markets where local licensees operate McDonald's restaurants under developmental agreements.
  • GBS (Global Business Services): Centralized functions streamlining global operations, technology, and support services.
  • Best Burger: A company-wide initiative focused on hotter, juicier, and improved-tasting burgers, operationalized globally.
  • McCrispy Sandwich Equity: The deployment and brand recognition of McDonald's premium chicken sandwich across major markets.
