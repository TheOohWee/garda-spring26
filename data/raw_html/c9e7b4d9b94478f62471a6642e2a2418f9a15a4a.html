Ferguson (FERG) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 2, 2026 at 3:46PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, March 11, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kevin Murphy
  • Chief Financial Officer — Bill Brundage

Takeaways

  • Net sales -- $6.9 billion, representing a 3% increase, with organic revenue up 2.1% and 1.2% from acquisitions.
  • Adjusted operating profit -- $449 million, down $71 million, with a 6.5% adjusted operating margin.
  • Adjusted diluted EPS -- $1.52, a decline of 12.6% due to lower adjusted operating profit, partially offset by share repurchases.
  • Gross margin -- 29.7%, a 70 basis point decrease, explained by ongoing deflation and sales mix shifts toward HVAC and Waterworks.
  • U.S. segment sales -- Grew 3%, split between 2% organic growth and 1% from acquisitions.
  • HVAC customer group sales -- Increased 17%, attributed mainly to organic growth and new counter conversions.
  • Waterworks customer group sales -- Rose 10%, with double-digit expansion in municipal, metering technology, geosynthetics, and water/wastewater treatment.
  • Residential trade plumbing revenues -- Flat, reflecting ongoing headwinds in new construction and deflation, with RMI (repair, maintenance, improvement) slightly better.
  • Commercial mechanical customer group sales -- Up 2%, propelled by large capital projects, partly offset by weakness in traditional non-residential activity.
  • Fire & fabrication and industrial/facility supply -- Combined net sales declined 6%, largely due to steel pipe deflation in Fire & Fabrication.
  • Operating costs -- Grew in line with 5% sales volume growth; 60 basis point operating leverage decline mainly due to 2% price deflation and selective investment in capabilities.
  • Net debt/adjusted EBITDA -- 1.2x, described as a strong balance sheet position.
  • Canada segment -- Net sales climbed 3.2% (3.1% organic, 5.4% acquisitions, -5.3% FX impact); adjusted operating profit $11 million, up $2 million.
  • First-half net sales -- Up 1.8%, with gross margin of 29.9%, a decrease of 40 basis points.
  • Adjusted first-half operating profit -- $1.2 billion, down 10.7%; first-half adjusted operating margin was 7.9%.
  • Working capital investment -- $200 million, an $87 million increase, driven by HVAC growth initiatives and efficiency standards transition, plus higher receivables.
  • First-half operating cash flow -- $685 million; free cash flow was $545 million, with $158 million in capital expenditures.
  • Dividend -- $0.83 per share quarterly, reflecting a 5% increase.
  • Share repurchases -- $508 million in the first half (up from $250 million), reducing share count by approximately 2.6 million; share repurchase authorization increased by $1 billion, leaving $1.4 billion authorized and outstanding.
  • 2025 guidance -- Expects total sales growth in the low single-digit range, operating margin of 8.3%-8.8%, interest expense of $180 million-$200 million, adjusted tax rate around 26%, and CapEx between $325 million-$375 million.
  • Capital allocation -- Focus remains on bolt-on M&A, with recent activity including completion of Templeton/TEMSCO and definitive agreement to acquire Independent Pipe & Supply.

Risks

  • Adjusted operating margins fell below management expectations due to persistent commodity-led deflation and subdued end markets.
  • Gross margin was 29.7%, a decrease of 70 basis points over last year, driven by weak end market demand, persistent deflation, and the sales mix impact of outsized growth in HVAC and Waterworks.
  • Management stated, "we still think the markets overall will be down in the low single-digits range," reflecting expectations for continued muted demand into the second half despite guidance for improvement.
  • Steel pipe deflation resulted in "a very challenged commodity-based input product," heavily impacting results in the Fire & Fabrication business.

Summary

Ferguson (FERG +1.16%) reported sales and volume growth but delivered lower profitability metrics amid ongoing commodity price deflation and unfavorable sales mix impacts. Management confirmed outperformance versus end markets and underscored confidence by raising share repurchase authorization, but cited multiple cost and margin pressures that tempered operating leverage and EPS. While growth in HVAC and Waterworks outpaced the remainder of the business and key strategic objectives advanced, the company revised full-year margin guidance downward, with persistent headwinds in both residential and non-residential segments.

  • Strategic initiatives for HVAC expansion included over 500 dual trade counter conversions, with a target surpassing 650 counters, and 20 new HVAC market entries within the United States.
  • Kevin Murphy said, "The vast majority of the growth in both of those customer groups is organic," referencing HVAC and Waterworks.
  • Free cash flow reached $545 million in the first half, even as working capital investment rose due to HVAC inventory expansion and new equipment standards.
  • Tariff implementation and announced steel price increases were described as having potential to stabilize but also add uncertainty to commodity pricing throughout the year.
  • Cost discipline was maintained, as indicated by nearly flat headcount despite 5% volume growth; non-customer-facing cost reductions are planned to accelerate in the second half.
  • The Ferguson Home platform was launched as a unified showroom and digital offering, serving as an omnichannel growth platform for residential building, remodel, and digital commerce groups.
  • Management explicitly stated, "we intend this business to be a 30 plus percent gross margin business," reliant on value-added services and business mix, pending commodity stabilization and normalized markets.

Industry glossary

  • RMI (Repair, Maintenance, Improvement): Revenue category encompassing non-new-construction spending on residential or commercial properties, often tracked as a cycle-resilient segment.
  • Dual trade counter: Sales counters serving both HVAC and plumbing professionals, designed for efficiency and increased cross-category sales.
  • Deflation: Sustained decline in commodity or product prices, directly impacting reported revenue and margin comparisons for a distributor reliant on pass-through pricing.
  • Free cash flow: Cash flow from operations minus capital expenditures, indicating the capital available after required investment for discretionary spending or shareholder returns.
  • CapEx: Capital expenditures used for property, plant, equipment, or supply chain project investments as part of organic growth strategy.
  • BIM (Building Information Modeling): Digital modeling process referenced in project workflow solutions, used for advanced planning and coordination on construction projects.
