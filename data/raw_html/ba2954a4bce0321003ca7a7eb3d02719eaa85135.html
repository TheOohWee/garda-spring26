Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $22.6 million, reflecting 53% growth year over year, driven by a 54% increase in prescriptions for the Assure system.
  • Gross margin -- 50.6%, up from 39.6% the prior year, marking the eighth consecutive quarter of sequential improvement.
  • Prescriptions written -- Approximately 4,700 prescriptions for the Assure system accepted in the quarter, indicating continued adoption momentum.
  • In-network patient rate -- In-network mix increased to the low 80s%, up from around 70% at the time of IPO nine months ago.
  • Conversion rate -- 48.8%, up from an adjusted 48.2% in the prior year, progressing from 38% in fiscal 2024 and 44% in fiscal 2025.
  • GAAP operating expenses -- $43.2 million, including $1 million of non-recurring professional fees and costs related to the recent equity offering.
  • Adjusted (excluding non-recurring costs and stock-based compensation) operating expenses -- $33.5 million, up from $23.8 million in the prior year, primarily due to commercial expansion and increased public company costs.
  • GAAP net loss -- $32.8 million, versus $20.6 million in the comparable period last year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA loss -- $19.7 million, compared to $16.1 million the prior year.
  • Cash position -- $175.4 million as of Oct. 31, 2025, excluding $148 million of net proceeds from the recent public equity offering.
  • Sales force expansion -- Approximately 100 active sales territories, up from about 80 at the end of April 2025, with hiring in line with plans.
  • Guidance update -- Fiscal year 2026 revenue guidance raised to $91 million, representing 52% growth, compared to prior guidance of $88 million.
  • ACE PAS study results -- The Assure system met both primary endpoints in a 21,000-patient prospective study, with results described as demonstrating a "safe and highly effective device."
  • Clinical adoption metrics -- Average daily wear time reported at 23.1 hours; 30% of patients wore the device more than 90 days.
  • False alarm rate -- 94% of patients were free from false alarms, the lowest among available WCDs according to the study results presented.
  • WCD market penetration -- Wearable cardioverter defibrillator (WCD) therapy reaches only 14% of the U.S. addressable market, highlighting significant underutilization.
  • Operational leverage -- Higher in-network mix and declining cost per fit (down 20% year over year) cited as contributors to margin expansion and efficiency.
  • Product innovation -- Management confirmed ongoing R&D within wearable, WCD, and digital platforms, but declined to disclose pipeline specifics.
  • Revenue model -- Rental business model enables device reuse, with 90% long-term fittings expected to be accomplished using existing fleet assets.

Summary

Kestra Medical Technologies (KMTS 7.17%) increased its fiscal year 2026 revenue guidance to $91 million following 53% revenue growth and significant margin expansion in the second quarter of fiscal year 2026. The company delivered ACE PAS post-approval clinical study results, enrolling over 21,000 patients, supporting the Assure system’s clinical effectiveness and wearability. Management identified expanding sales coverage and a higher in-network mix as central to sustained revenue and margin growth. They cited growing clinical evidence as a potential catalyst for future guideline changes, although current growth plans do not rely on such updates. A recent $148 million equity raise and improved operational metrics provide additional resources and flexibility for future investments.

  • The company noted WCD market growth has accelerated to low double digits%, with management estimating 13% market share based on competitive disclosures and claims data.
  • Average revenue per fit is benefiting from the increased in-network patient mix and strengthened revenue cycle management capabilities.
  • Expansion into new accounts and additional clinical specialist hires support ongoing market penetration beyond core physician prescriber segments.
  • Management attributed operating expense growth to investments aligning with commercial expansion and sustainable service levels.
  • The Assure system is the only WCD designed for women, with 34% female enrollment in ACE PAS, compared to 40% of indicated patient population.
  • Current annualized SCA incidence rate observed in ACE PAS was 7.5%, above rates documented in landmark ICD trials, informing risk assessment in clinical decision-making.

Industry glossary

  • WCD (Wearable cardioverter defibrillator): An external, non-invasive medical device worn by patients at risk of sudden cardiac arrest, designed to detect and treat life-threatening arrhythmias with automatic electrical therapy.
  • ACE PAS: FDA-mandated post-approval clinical study evaluating the safety, effectiveness, and real-world outcomes of the Assure wearable cardioverter defibrillator system.
  • Conversion rate: The percentage of patient prescriptions that result in a completed fitting of a device, a key indicator of commercial execution.
  • RCM (Revenue cycle management): Processes and capabilities used to manage healthcare provider revenue generation from patient intake to final payment, including billing and collections efficiency.
  • In-network mix: Proportion of patients covered by health payers with which Kestra has a contractual agreement, enabling lower administrative burden and typically higher reimbursement.
  • SCA (Sudden cardiac arrest): A rapid and unexpected loss of heart function resulting in cessation of blood flow, often fatal without immediate intervention.
  • ICD (Implantable cardioverter defibrillator): An implanted device for ongoing detection and automated correction of dangerous heart rhythms, typically for chronic risk management.
