SUMMARY

The call introduced Kevin Engel as new CEO, emphasizing continuity in strategic direction and a disciplined leadership approach. Management laid out plans for aggressive capital investment of $2.5 billion-$3 billion in 2026, heavily front-loaded for Arizona facility build-out and HDFO equipment expansion. Two new HDFO programs for AI data centers are advancing toward high-volume production launches in the second half of 2026, fueling a projected 20%+ surge in computing-related revenue. Customer prepayments, executed support agreements, and up to $2.85 billion in government incentives are expected to play key roles in funding planned investment before subsidy inflows materialize. The company forecasts Q1 2026 revenue at $1.65 billion midpoint, a 25% year-over-year increase, but with seasonal gross margin compression and anticipated higher R&D costs. Facility ramp in Vietnam achieved breakeven; reclaimed Korea manufacturing space will support HDFO and test ramps until Arizona is operational.

  • Debt capacity utilization remains a possibility—Faust said, "we do have access to debt capacity in various forms," with a debt-to-EBITDA ratio at 1.2x and minimum cash operations threshold described as $500 million.
  • Advanced packaging comprised approximately 20% of total 2025 revenue; further acceleration is anticipated with AI and HPC platform expansion.
  • Management described ongoing supply chain strategy, including migrating SiP production to Vietnam to free up capacity for advanced programs in Korea.
  • Customer demand for U.S.-based advanced packaging is reinforced by customer attendance and interest during the Arizona facility groundbreaking across multiple segments—communications, automotive, and consumer.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • HDFO (High-Density Fan-Out): Advanced semiconductor packaging process enabling dense interconnects, critical for AI and high-performance computing applications.
  • SiP (System-in-Package): Integration of multiple semiconductor dies and components into a single package, enabling miniaturization and complex functionality.
  • ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems): Automotive technologies supporting active safety and driver automation, increasing semiconductor content per vehicle.
  • 2.5D Packaging: Semiconductor integration approach featuring side-by-side dies interconnected with an interposer, enhancing bandwidth and performance, particularly for data center and AI solutions.
  • IDM (Integrated Device Manufacturer): Company designing, manufacturing, and selling integrated circuits, often contrasted with fabless or foundry models.
  • NPIs (New Product Introductions): The process of bringing new semiconductor products from R&D into manufacturing for volume production.
