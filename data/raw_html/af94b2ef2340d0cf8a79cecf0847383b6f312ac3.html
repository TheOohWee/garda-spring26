TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $10.9 million, growing 23% year over year, marking the third consecutive quarter of greater than 20% revenue growth.
  • Full-Year Revenue -- $41.1 million, up 22%, driven by gains in both Domestic and International Core businesses.
  • Domestic Core Revenue Growth -- 18% year over year for the quarter and 11% for the year, reflecting new patient starts and share gains.
  • International Core Revenue Growth -- 71% year over year for the quarter and 80% for the year; underlying growth for the year was 73% excluding distributor realignment effects.
  • PST Revenue -- Decreased 30% year over year in the quarter, attributed to milestone timing, not activity levels.
  • Gross Margin -- 62.3% full-year, a slight decline due to increased material costs, tariffs, and geographic sales mix.
  • Operating Expenses -- Increased just 3% for the year alongside 22% revenue growth, demonstrating operating leverage.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $600,000 positive for the full year, improving 124% and marking three consecutive quarters of positive adjusted EBITDA.
  • Cash Position -- Ended the year with $8.9 million in cash; achieved positive operating cash flow in the third and fourth quarters and for the year.
  • Guidance for 2026 Revenue -- $47.5 million to $50 million, reflecting targeted 15%-22% growth.
  • 2026 Gross Margin Guidance -- 61%-63%, supported by pricing and manufacturing efficiencies.
  • Patient Base -- Approximately 59,000 global chronic subcutaneous immunoglobulin (SCIg) patients, up 20% year over year.
  • Market Share in Europe -- Increased to 20% from approximately 10% in the $50 million European SCIg pump and consumables market.
  • Regulatory Milestones -- Received EU MDR certification for the FREEDOM60 with prefilled syringe compatibility, and FDA 510(k) clearance for RYSTIGGO on the Freedom Infusion System.
  • Pipeline Developments -- Nine subcutaneous drugs cleared for use and nine new drug pipeline opportunities, including two new pharma collaborations in nephrology (Phase III) and a multi-indication molecule (Phase I).
  • Oncology Entry -- 510(k) filed for Phesgo in Q4 2025, with anticipated market entry in the second half of 2026.
  • Product Innovation -- MDR-cleared FREEDOM60 and FreedomEdge pumps now available for prefilled administration in Europe; next-generation pumps and flow controller regulatory submissions planned for 2026 and early 2027.
  • RYSTIGGO U.S. Opportunity -- Estimated 20,000 annual U.S. infusions, potentially growing to over 100,000 by 2030, with initial channel expansion into infusion clinics.
  • Recurring Revenue Model -- Maintained across nearly 60,000 global patients, creating stable baseline growth.

SUMMARY

KORU Medical Systems (KRMD 3.66%) announced a planned CEO succession, with Adam Kalbermatten slated to become CEO on July 1 and Linda Tharby remaining as Board member and advisor through 2026. The company highlighted continued outperformance versus growth in the core SCIg market, particularly its European expansion and rising global penetration. Management explicitly guided to ongoing operating leverage, projecting positive adjusted EBITDA and cash flow for 2026, and outlined further diversification into non-IG indications, especially oncology and nephrology. Regulatory milestones such as EU MDR and FDA 510(k) clearances were emphasized as positioning the company for expanded pharma partnerships and channel entry. Forward-looking statements signal investment in new production lines and a significant addressable opportunity in both the U.S. and ex-U.S. markets as additional drugs and markets activate.

  • Kalbermatten said, "We have a proven strategy really built around 3 pillars: protecting and growing the Core Domestic business, expanding internationally and enabling more drugs to reach more patients."
  • Management noted up to 80% reduction in drug preparation tasks resulting from the shift to prefilled syringes in Europe, which may influence ongoing adoption rates.
  • Pharma partnerships in development span 9 active drug opportunities, with estimated combined potential of over 7 million annual infusions globally.
  • 2026 revenue cadence is anticipated to be weighted toward the second half due to timing of inventory builds and product clearances.
  • The company expects a new market addition for prefilled products in Europe every quarter during 2026, supported by expanded regulatory clearances.
  • Potential geographic expansion in Japan is anticipated as both pump and consumable approvals are in place and prefilled syringe adoption grows.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • SCIg (Subcutaneous Immunoglobulin): Immunoglobulin therapy administered via the subcutaneous route, commonly used for treating primary and secondary immunodeficiency disorders; KORU's core indication and revenue driver.
  • EU MDR (European Union Medical Device Regulation): European regulatory framework required for medical device commercialization, critical for market access and prefilled syringe initiatives in Europe.
  • 510(k) Clearance: U.S. FDA premarket notification required to demonstrate a medical device is safe and effective for its intended use; enables KORU's product and channel expansion.
  • Prefilled Syringe Conversion: The market trend and operational process of transitioning from drug vials to fully prepared, prefilled syringes for infusion, reducing preparation time and improving patient convenience.
