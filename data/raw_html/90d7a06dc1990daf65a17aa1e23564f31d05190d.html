TAKEAWAYS

  • First FDA Approval -- Arrowhead Pharmaceuticals (ARWR 1.65%) obtained FDA approval for REDEMPLO as an adjunct to diet for reducing triglycerides in adults with familial chylomicronemia syndrome (FCS).
  • International Approvals -- REDEMPLO also received regulatory approvals from Health Canada and the Chinese National Medical Products Administration, with anticipated launches in Canada, China, the EU, and the U.K. later in 2026.
  • Product Launch and Uptake -- Over 100 REDEMPLO prescriptions received from a diverse, geographically balanced U.S. prescriber base in the first 10 weeks of launch.
  • Payer Feedback -- Early conversations with major payers indicate a willingness to provide coverage for REDEMPLO to label, including both clinically and genetically diagnosed FCS.
  • Commercial Model -- Arrowhead implemented the One-REDEMPLO pricing scheme for a single price across present and future indications for FCS.
  • Clinical Results -- Phase III PALISADE study showed median triglyceride reduction of 80% from baseline with REDEMPLO, with lower acute pancreatitis incidence compared to placebo.
  • Regulatory Label -- The U.S. FDA-approved label for REDEMPLO states no contraindications, no warnings, and no precautions, with self-administration by subcutaneous injection every three months.
  • Revenue -- Total revenue for the quarter was $264 million, comprised mainly of $229 million from Sarepta collaboration activities and $34 million from the Novartis upfront payment.
  • Net Income -- Reported net income was $30.8 million, equating to $0.22 per share, versus a net loss of $173.1 million, or $1.39 per share, in the year-ago quarter.
  • Operating Expenses -- Total operating expenses reached $223 million, an increase of $59 million, driven by $40 million higher R&D and $19 million higher SG&A to support clinical trials and the REDEMPLO launch.
  • Balance Sheet -- Cash and investments totaled $917 million at quarter end, not including the $200 million DM1 milestone payment or the $50 million Sarepta anniversary payment pending receipt.
  • Capital Raised -- Gross proceeds from recent transactions totaled $1.33 billion, including a $200 million Novartis upfront, $200 million Sarepta milestone, $700 million 0% coupon convertible notes, and $230 million common stock offering.
  • Convertible Debt Terms -- The convertible notes carry a 0% coupon, a 35% conversion premium, and capped call features to prevent dilution up to roughly $119 per share (about an 85% premium).
  • Plozasiran Pipeline Progress -- The company remains on schedule to complete blinded Phase III SHASTA-3, SHASTA-4, and MUIR-3 studies in mid-2026, with topline data expected in Q3 2026 for severe hypertriglyceridemia.
  • Breakthrough Therapy Designation -- The FDA granted breakthrough therapy designation to investigational plozasiran as an adjunct to diet for reducing triglycerides in adults with severe hypertriglyceridemia.
  • Obesity Program Interim Data -- Early Phase I/II data showed that ARO-INHBE, combined with tirzepatide, achieved approximately twofold better weight loss at week 16 and approximately threefold reductions in total fat, visceral fat, and liver fat versus tirzepatide alone at week 12 in obese diabetics.
  • ARO-ALK7 Gene Silencing -- Dose-dependent reductions in adipose ALK7 mRNA observed, with a mean reduction of 88% at 200 mg at week 8, and maximum reduction of 94% in the ongoing Phase I/II study.
  • ARO-DIMER-PA Clinical Start -- Patient dosing began in the first Phase I/II trial of ARO-DIMER-PA, a dual RNAi therapeutic targeting PCSK9 and APOC3 for mixed hyperlipidemia; interim data anticipated in the second half of 2026.
  • CNS Platform Milestone -- First patients dosed in Phase I/II trial of ARO-MAPT, targeting tau protein for tauopathies including Alzheimer’s disease, using Arrowhead’s proprietary blood-brain barrier delivery system, with interim healthy volunteer data expected in 2026.
  • Strategic Collaboration -- Arrowhead entered a global licensing and collaboration agreement with Novartis for ARO-SNCA, with eligible milestone payments up to $2 billion and tiered royalties up to low double digits on commercial sales.
  • Product Adoption Programs -- A high proportion of patients have enrolled in the rely on REDEMPLO patient support initiative; eligible patients paid $0 out of pocket for prescriptions during the quarter.
  • Provider Engagement -- Outreach targets approximately 5,000 healthcare professionals, with early prescribing led by preventive cardiologists and endocrinologists, accounting for 70% of prescription volume.

SUMMARY

Arrowhead Pharmaceuticals (ARWR 1.65%) reported its transition to a commercial biopharmaceutical company with first regulatory approvals for REDEMPLO for FCS in the U.S. Canada, and China, and launch in the U.S. The company secured $1.33 billion in gross proceeds through collaborations and financings and finished the quarter with a strengthened liquidity profile and positive net income. Management confirmed that critical Phase III studies in plozasiran and expansion efforts in obesity and CNS pipelines are progressing as planned with multiple key data readouts and regulatory events expected in 2026.

  • Clinical and payer feedback on the initial REDEMPLO launch signals broad U.S. accessibility, enabled by rationalized pricing and omnichannel engagement.
  • Blinded Phase III trials for plozasiran are on track, with topline results scheduled for Q3 2026 and planned label expansion beyond FCS targeted by year-end.
  • Arrowhead’s CNS platform utilized systemic delivery for tauopathies, with first-in-human studies now enrolling and interim results projected in 2026.
  • Capital structure optimized through zero-coupon convertible notes and capped call features designed to limit shareholder dilution while raising non-dilutive funds.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FCS (Familial Chylomicronemia Syndrome): A rare genetic disorder marked by severely elevated triglycerides, leading to risk of recurrent, potentially fatal pancreatitis.
  • REDEMPLO: Arrowhead’s FDA-approved RNAi-based drug for reducing triglycerides in FCS patients.
  • TRiM Platform: Arrowhead’s proprietary targeted RNAi molecule platform enabling precise gene silencing in specific tissues, including CNS penetration.
  • SHASTA/MUIR Studies: Arrowhead’s Phase III clinical trials evaluating plozasiran for severe hypertriglyceridemia (SHTG) and acute pancreatitis endpoints.
  • APOC3/PCSK9: Genes targeted for RNA interference therapies by Arrowhead in treating hyperlipidemias and cardiovascular risk.
  • ARO-INHBE/ARO-ALK7/ARO-DIMER-PA: Arrowhead investigational RNAi therapies in clinical development for metabolic disease, obesity, and mixed lipid disorders.
  • ARO-MAPT: Arrowhead’s investigational RNAi therapy for tauopathies, delivered to the brain via proprietary BBB-penetrant technology.
