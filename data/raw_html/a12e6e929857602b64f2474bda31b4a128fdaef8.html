CVRx (CVRX) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 6:17PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 12, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Kevin Hykes
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jared Oasheim

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $16 million for the quarter, up 4%, and $56.7 million for the year, with growth of 410%.
  • United States Revenue -- $14.9 million for the quarter, rising 4%, with 478 revenue units sold, compared to 404.
  • Europe Revenue -- $1.1 million for the quarter, increasing 10%, with 49 revenue units versus 41 prior period.
  • Gross Profit -- $13.8 million, an increase of $1.1 million, or 8%.
  • Gross Margin -- 86%, up from 83%, attributed to higher average selling prices and improved manufacturing efficiencies.
  • Sales Territories -- U.S. territories expanded to 53 (from 48), and Europe maintained at five.
  • Implanting Centers -- 252 active U.S. centers, an increase from 223 year-end prior, and up from 250 sequentially.
  • SG&A Expense -- $22 million, up $1.8 million (9%), driven by increased compensation, advertising, and travel, partially offset by lower consulting.
  • Research & Development Expense -- $3 million, up $200,000 (7%), with growth in compensation from higher headcount and offset by lower clinical study costs.
  • Net Loss -- $11.9 million, or $0.46 per share, compared to $10.7 million, or $0.43 per share, on 26.2 million weighted-average shares outstanding.
  • Cash & Cash Equivalents -- $75.7 million at year-end; $40.8 million used in operating and investing activities (versus $40.5 million prior year).
  • Term Loan Facility -- Amended in January 2026 to increase commitment to $100 million, with $60 million drawn at close; maturity extended to 2031.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Full-year revenue projected at $63 million to $67 million; gross margin guidance of 84%-86%; operating expenses expected at $103 million to $107 million; Q1 revenue expected between $13.7 million and $14.7 million.
  • Barostim Market Opportunity -- 339,000 prevalence-based indicated patients in the U.S, representing a $10.5 billion opportunity, with less than 1% penetrated.
  • Key Reimbursement Milestone -- Category I CPT code for Barostim effective January 2026, with physician payment formalized at ~$560 and improved prior authorization outcomes expected.
  • Medicare Advantage Approvals -- 30-day prior authorization approval rates reached 46%, up from 31% in 2024; further improvement anticipated with new code.
  • BENEFIT HF Trial Initiation -- Category B IDE coverage approved, allowing Medicare reimbursement (~$45,000 per procedure); trial targets 2,500 randomizations at ~100 centers.
  • BENEFIT HF Market Expansion -- If successful, trial would raise the addressable market to over 980,000 patients and ~$30 billion.
  • Salesforce Structure -- Strategic shift to focus each rep on three to five high-potential accounts to deepen adoption, supported by training and leadership enhancements.
  • Top Account Adoption -- Top 20% of centers averaged annualized implant rate of approximately 19 in Q4; each has roughly 300 indicated patients, highlighting expansion runway.
  • Capital Resources -- $86 million available at start of 2026, combining year-end cash position and new debt draw; at least two years’ cash runway before raising additional capital would be needed.
  • Chief Human Resources Officer Transition -- Greg Morrison appointed to succeed Tanya Austin, effective March 2026.

SUMMARY

The company launched the pivotal BENEFIT HF trial after securing crucial CMS Category B IDE coverage, setting the stage for Medicare reimbursement and a significant market expansion if successful. Management stated that the new Category I CPT code for Barostim took effect in January and is already resulting in improved prior authorization rates, with expectations that approval trends will accelerate as payers and physicians adapt throughout the year. A deliberate transformation of the U.S. salesforce yielded 53 active territories and targeted selling strategies, with top-performing centers focusing on deep adoption to drive increases in annualized implant rates. Financial guidance for 2026 was established, with management highlighting high gross margin sustainability, controlled expense growth, and clear capital sufficiency to support commercial and clinical operations as the trial proceeds.

  • The guidance for 2026 incorporates a projected return to sequential growth after a Q1 seasonal decline, based on increasing salesforce productivity and territory expansion.
  • Medicare and Medicare Advantage will reimburse approximately 1,600-1,700 Barostim devices in connection with the BENEFIT HF trial, generating device revenue during the study period.
  • Management indicated that new center additions will continue but expects most 2026 growth to stem from deeper adoption and "same-store sales" at existing implant sites.
  • Debt facility amendments offer up to $40 million additional non-dilutive capital, contingent on revenue milestones, with no current plans to raise equity capital despite recent shelf and ATM filings.
  • The criteria for sustained high-volume adoption among centers include redundant clinical and administrative stakeholders, community cardiologist engagement, and workflow integration, now serving as the sales team's focus model.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Barostim: Neuromodulation device developed by CVRx, Inc. for heart failure patients, targeting improvement in symptoms and quality of life.
  • Category I CPT Code: Standardized medical billing code indicating national acceptance and improving reimbursement predictability and access for covered procedures.
  • Category B IDE Coverage: CMS approval for Medicare to reimburse devices used in FDA-approved clinical trials, critical for hospital participation and patient access.
  • BENEFIT HF Trial: Large, prospective, randomized controlled trial evaluating Barostim in heart failure patients, aimed at regulatory and commercial market expansion.
