Earnings Call Transcript

Trimble (TRMB) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 10, 2026 at 11:42AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Aug. 6, 2025 at 8:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Robert G. Painter
  • Chief Financial Officer — Phillip Sawarynski

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $876 million, up 9% organically, with all three segments outperforming internal outlook.
  • Annualized Recurring Revenue (ARR) -- $2.21 billion, up 14% organically, representing 63% of total revenue.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $0.71, up 15% year over year, exceeding original guidance by $0.09.
  • Software and Services Revenue Mix -- Accounted for 79% of total revenue.
  • Gross Margin -- Expanded by 210 basis points to 70.6% on continued business model progression.
  • EBITDA Margin -- 27.4%, marking a 170 basis point increase from the prior year.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $90 million year-to-date, after a $277 million agriculture divestiture tax payment.
  • Leverage Ratio -- 1.4x, well below the long-term target of 2.5x.
  • AECO Segment Performance -- $1.36 billion ARR and $350 million revenue, both up 16%; operating income reached 30.4%, a 400 basis point improvement.
  • Field Systems Segment Performance -- $393 million revenue, up 3% despite a 200 basis point model conversion headwind; ARR up 17% to $358 million; operating income 30.8%, a 190 basis point gain.
  • Transportation and Logistics Segment Performance -- $133 million revenue and $492 million ARR, up 8% each; operating margin 21.5% with>90% recurring revenue.
  • Share Repurchases -- $50 million repurchased with $323 million remaining authorization; plan to continue allocating at least one-third of free cash flow to buybacks.
  • Tuck-In Acquisition -- Acquired “Trimble Materials” to enhance contractor material management workflow and cross-sell ARR growth opportunities.
  • AI Adoption -- Deployed AI in nearly every function and product suite, with ProjectSight AI processing over 1.5 million drawings at a pace of 200,000 per month.
  • Full-Year Guidance Raised -- Revenue midpoint increased by $100 million to $3.52 billion; EPS midpoint up $0.11 to $2.98; ARR growth midpoint held at 14%.
  • Third Quarter Guidance -- Anticipates $850 million–$890 million revenue and $0.67–$0.75 EPS; organic revenue growth projected at 4%-9% for the quarter.
  • Tariff Impact -- Ongoing Field Systems tariffs total ~$10 million per quarter, fully offset by surcharges; no expected effect on profitability.
  • Cash Flow Benefit from Policy -- Section 174 repeal expected to add $50 million in 2025 cash flow, with an $80 million additional multi-year benefit.
  • TC1 Bundling Traction -- Two-thirds of AECO bookings from existing customers; one-third from new customers, reflecting cross-sell momentum.

RISKS

  • Painter said, "That federal business is down significantly year over year, year-to-date, and we would expect that for the overall year," indicating a substantial ongoing revenue headwind in the federal public sector.
  • Sawarynski noted, "being prudent on the back half, particularly in Field Systems since that's a book and burn business," and cited tougher year-over-year comps as a reason for conservative second-half guidance.

SUMMARY

Management at Trimble (TRMB 0.99%) confirmed elevated recurring revenue and significant segment margin improvement, emphasizing AI integration across products and operations as a driver for customer workflow and cross-selling enhancements. Leadership highlighted that Field Systems and Transportation achieved ARR and revenue gains despite federal sector declines, with strategic pricing and transformation initiatives sustaining margin and cash flow targets. Forward-looking commentary stressed disciplined capital allocation and prudent guidance, reflecting ongoing macroeconomic and tariff-related uncertainties unique to the back half.

  • Sawarynski stated, "Q1 was a bit higher because of the term licenses we recognized. Q2 was a bit lower," confirming that ARR growth rates can fluctuate quarter to quarter based on revenue recognition timing.
  • Phillip Sawarynski said, "we've seen some destocking in our -- particularly in our survey channel," clarifying inventory and demand dynamics had no evidence of tariff-driven channel stuffing.
  • Painter explained, "SMB is much less penetrated," supporting higher percentage growth and expansion opportunity within the AECO segment's down-market adoption.
  • Sawarynski highlighted, "we continue with the small tuck-ins," revealing active and ongoing M&A to add targeted functionality for upsell across the customer base.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ARR: Annualized Recurring Revenue — contracted recurring revenue expected over the next 12 months, reflecting core subscription strength.
  • ACV: Annual Contract Value — annualized value of bookings for subscription or recurring revenue contracts, indicating new and expansion business flows.
  • TC1: Trimble Construction One — the firm’s bundled suite of construction management, ERP, and workflow software solutions targeted for comprehensive digital project management.
  • Model Conversion: The firm’s transition of customer purchasing from traditional perpetual licenses/hardware sales toward recurring subscription pricing models, typically impacting Field Systems’ revenue cadence.
  • Book and Burn Business: A business in which revenue is recognized upon transaction booking, rather than over a contract term, often influencing variability in guidance and backlog analysis.
  • SLAM: Simultaneous Localization And Mapping — advanced 3D mapping technology enabling real-time spatial data capture, referenced for innovation in surveying and reality capture workflows.
