TAKEAWAYS

  • Home Closings -- 3,285 homes delivered with an average price of $596,000, generating approximately $2 billion in revenue for the quarter.
  • Full-Year Performance -- 12,997 homes delivered at an average price of $597,000, with total home closings revenue of about $7.8 billion.
  • Net Income -- Fourth-quarter reported net income was $174 million ($1.76 per diluted share); adjusted net income was $188 million ($1.91 per diluted share) after specific charges; full-year reported net income reached $783 million ($7.77 per diluted share), while adjusted net income was $830 million ($8.24 per diluted share).
  • Gross Margin -- Fourth-quarter home closings gross margin was 21.8%, above prior guidance and reflecting higher incentives and a greater mix of lower-margin spec home closings; full-year reported margin was 22.5% (23% adjusted), down from 24.4% reported (24.5% adjusted) in 2024.
  • Spec Home Inventory -- Ended the quarter with 2,956 specs under construction and 1,232 finished, representing a total spec count down 11% sequentially from the previous quarter; spec inventory reduced by 24% since 2025.
  • Sales Mix -- Spec homes accounted for 72% of sales and 66% of closings, up from 61% and 54%, respectively, in 2024; to-be-built sales in January gained 700 basis points in share compared to the fourth quarter.
  • Backlog -- Started 2026 with a lower-than-normal backlog of just over 2,800 homes.
  • Community Growth -- Expect well over 100 new community openings (including 20+ new Esplanade outlets) driving high single-digit outlet growth to 365–370 by year-end from the current 341.
  • Forward Guidance -- Project 2026 home deliveries near 11,000, with Q1 closings around 2,200; average closing price expected near $580,000 in Q1 and $580,000–$590,000 for the year.
  • Margin Outlook -- Q1 home closings gross margin expected to be approximately 20%, recovering gradually as to-be-built share rises and incentives ease.
  • SG&A Leverage -- Fourth quarter SG&A ratio was 9.9% of revenue; full-year ratio was 9.5%, resulting in 40 basis points of expense leverage over 2024.
  • Land Supply -- Owned or controlled 78,835 lots (54% controlled off-balance sheet), down from 86,153 lots (57% controlled) a year ago; controlled ratio is expected to increase toward a long-term target of 65%.
  • Land Investment -- Homebuilding land investment was approximately $2.2 billion, below the targeted $2.3 billion, with 2026 guidance set around $2 billion and a focus on core markets.
  • Financial Services -- Revenue reached about $49 million for the quarter, with an 88% capture rate and buyers' average credit score of 750, down payment of 21%, and average income above $183,000.
  • Liquidity and Capital Structure -- Ended the quarter with $1.8 billion in liquidity ($850 million cash and $928 million revolver capacity); net homebuilding debt-to-capitalization ratio improved to 17.8%, down from 20% a year ago.
  • Share Repurchases -- Repurchased 1.2 million shares in the quarter ($71 million) and 6.5 million shares ($381 million, 6% of prior share count) for the year; Board approved a new $1 billion share repurchase program through 2027.
  • Build-to-Rent Platform (Yardley) -- Yardley business represents 10,400 home sites across nine markets, supported by a $3 billion land bank with Kennedy Lewis; less than 10% of Yardley units are on balance sheet.
  • Innovation Initiatives -- Expanded proprietary digital and AI-powered sales and operational tools spanning purchasing, customer service, and financial functions.

SUMMARY

Taylor Morrison Home Corporation (TMHC 1.44%) highlighted resilient operational execution amid challenging consumer confidence and heightened industry incentives, focusing on margin protection, community expansion, and a shift toward higher-return segments. The move-up and resort lifestyle buyer groups remain central to the company’s strategy, with demographics and consumer preferences supporting sustained growth and margin differentiation. Management emphasized disciplined capital allocation, a selective approach to land investments, and ongoing cost controls as key drivers of future performance.

  • The company’s Esplanade resort lifestyle communities achieved 7% net order growth, while move-up sales registered a low single-digit decline and non-Esplanade orders decreased in the mid- to high-single-digit range.
  • Entry-level, move-up, and resort lifestyle buyers represented 31%, 49%, and 20% of orders, respectively, and these proportions held steady compared to the prior quarter.
  • Community-level activity was strongest in Florida, California, and Phoenix, while softness persisted in Texas, particularly Austin; management noted willingness to accept lower volumes in Texas to preserve margin quality.
  • Cycle times improved sequentially by one week during the quarter and ended more than five weeks faster year over year, enhancing operational flexibility.
  • Lot and option premiums comprised nearly 19% of base home price in 2025, underscoring value-add from customization, especially in move-up and resort lifestyle segments.
  • Leadership signaled continued pullback from land investment in fringe entry-level submarkets and reiterated a targeted exposure to these areas of 15% of the portfolio, down from as much as 25% during peak demand.
  • Management expects lot costs to increase in the mid-single-digit range, with stable construction costs aided by ongoing value engineering and supplier negotiations.
  • Customer demand in January and early February tracked ahead of seasonal norms, despite significant winter weather disruptions, supported by increased web traffic and robust pre-opening interest in new communities.
  • The Board of Directors extended and enlarged the share repurchase authorization to $1 billion, with an expected 2026 repurchase volume of approximately $400 million and average diluted share count guidance of 95 million for the year.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Spec Home: A home constructed by a builder without a pre-committed buyer, sold as inventory upon completion.
  • To-Be-Built Home: A home sold before construction, allowing buyers to customize features and finishes prior to building.
  • Absorption Pace: The average number of home sales per community per month, a key indicator of sales velocity.
  • Capture Rate: The proportion of homebuyers who choose the builder’s in-house mortgage or financial services.
  • Build-to-Rent (BTR): A housing business model where single-family homes are built specifically for rental purposes, not for sale.
