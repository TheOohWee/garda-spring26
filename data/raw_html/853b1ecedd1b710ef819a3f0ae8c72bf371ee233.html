Neurocrine (NBIX) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 6:16PM EST

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Net product sales -- $790 million with a 28% year-over-year growth rate driven by INGREZZA and CRENESSITY.
  • CRENESSITY sequential growth -- Net sales increased from $53 million in Q2 to $98 million in Q3, with 80% of prescriptions reimbursed and 540 new patient starts during the quarter.
  • INGREZZA net sales -- Achieved $687 million in Q3, fueled by a third consecutive quarter of record new patient additions; Q3 included a 14th ordering week, which added to reported results.
  • Combined net sales -- $785 million for INGREZZA and CRENESSITY, reflecting a record quarter for both brands but not summing to the $790 million total due to other product contributions.
  • Sales force expansion -- Approximately 30% increase in sales personnel across both products, with most incremental headcount allocated to INGREZZA, and both expansions to be completed by end of Q1.
  • SG&A guidance -- CFO Abernethy stated, "the expanded investment in both INGREZZA and CRENESSITY will result in an SG&A expense increase of around $150 million in 2026."
  • Formulary & market access -- INGREZZA expanded its formulary coverage mid-year; current coverage expected to be sustained through 2026 with no major anticipated access disruptions.
  • CRENESSITY patient access -- Nine out of ten individuals taking CRENESSITY receive insurance approval, and 90% pay $10 or less monthly out-of-pocket.
  • Pipeline progress -- Phase III studies of osavampator (major depressive disorder) and direclidine (schizophrenia) are meeting enrollment targets, with 4 new Phase I and 2 new Phase II studies initiating in the year.
  • Financial position -- Neurocrine Biosciences (NBIX 2.09%) holds over $2.1 billion in cash and has no debt, as stated by management.
  • CRENESSITY inventory build -- CFO Abernethy confirmed a $7 million inventory build for the quarter.
  • Gross-to-net discount (CRENESSITY) -- CFO Abernethy characterized the current gross-to-net discount as "less than a 20%" rate for the foreseeable future.

Summary

Neurocrine Biosciences delivered robust net product sales driven by growth in both INGREZZA and CRENESSITY, with management pinpointing expanded commercial execution as a catalyst. The company will increase its commercial investment, projecting an SG&A rise of approximately $150 million in 2026 to support broad sales force expansions for both primary assets, finalized by Q1 close. Clinical progress continued in late-stage programs, with all pipeline enrollment and study initiation targets reported as on track, underpinning guidance for ongoing expansion and portfolio diversification.

  • CRENESSITY's launch continued to see steady adoption, with pediatric and female populations showing higher uptake, and no major seasonal or reimbursement barriers identified in Q3.
  • INGREZZA captured the majority of new patient starts in its category, with management explicitly targeting maximization of patient share ahead of anticipated Payer and IRA dynamics through 2029.
  • Current formulary gains for INGREZZA, including recent parity across plans, are expected to persist into the next year, reflecting recent contract negotiations and ongoing market development efforts.
  • Management specified having received a Civil Investigative Demand from the Department of Justice regarding INGREZZA's sales, marketing, and promotion; company leadership affirmed full cooperation with the investigation.
  • An industry-wide warning letter regarding direct-to-consumer advertising was received for INGREZZA; management stated continued commitment to responsible advertising and ongoing business as usual.
  • CRENESSITY's inventory levels rose by $7 million in the quarter to match patient demand strength, with adherence and persistence metrics tracking favorably since launch.

Industry glossary

  • VMAT2 inhibitor: A class of drugs that inhibit vesicular monoamine transporter 2, used to treat tardive dyskinesia and Huntington's chorea.
  • CAH (Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia): A group of genetic disorders affecting the adrenal glands, with "classic" CAH being the most severe form treated by CRENESSITY.
  • Gross-to-net: The percentage reduction from gross invoiced sales to net recognized sales, reflecting rebates, discounts, and other deductions.
  • TRx / NRx: TRx is total prescriptions; NRx is new prescriptions, both critical for tracking drug uptake and growth trajectories.
  • SG&A: Selling, general, and administrative expenses, a key metric for assessing operating cost base expansion.
  • COE (Centers of Excellence): Specialized clinical centers with high concentration of expertise and patient volume in a specific disease area.
  • MFP (Maximum Fair Price): A price set under Medicare negotiation as part of the Inflation Reduction Act for certain drugs.
  • CID (Civil Investigative Demand): A legal request issued by the Department of Justice for documents and information as part of an investigation.
  • sNDA (Supplemental New Drug Application): A regulatory submission seeking approval for a new indication, formulation, or other change to an already approved drug.
  • DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) advertising: Marketing of prescription drugs directly to patients, typically through television, digital, or print media.
