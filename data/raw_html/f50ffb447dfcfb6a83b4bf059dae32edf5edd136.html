Uber (UBER) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 4, 2026 at 9:07AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Wednesday, Feb. 4, 2026, at 8 a.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Dara Khosrowshahi
  • Chief Financial Officer — Prashanth Mahendra-Rajah
  • Incoming Chief Financial Officer — Balaji Krishnamurthy
  • Senior Director of Investor Relations — Alex Wong

TAKEAWAYS

  • Gross Bookings -- Up 22% year over year to a $15 billion annual run rate, marking the fifth consecutive year with annual gross bookings greater than 20% growth.
  • Monthly Active Platform Consumers (MAPC) -- Exceeded 202 million, with MAPC growth accelerating from 14% at the start of the year to 18% by year-end.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Totaled $8.7 billion, representing 35% year-over-year growth.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Reached $9.8 billion, up 42% year over year.
  • Membership Program Growth -- Uber One membership grew 55% year over year, with 46 million members at year-end, nearing 50% of total gross bookings.
  • Autonomous Vehicle (AV) Expansion -- AV trips per vehicle per day were 30% higher on Uber's platform than standalone AV competitors, and AV operations are expected in 15 cities by the end of the year.
  • Geographic Revenue Mix -- 60% of mobility gross bookings originated outside the U.S., and nearly 75% of U.S. profits came from outside the top 20 largest cities.
  • Advertising Penetration in Delivery -- Delivery advertising exceeded the prior 2% penetration target, with enterprise year-over-year growth now outpacing SMBs.
  • Capital Returns -- Management stated, "We will continue to be aggressive buyers of our stock. And you should expect that it continues at a steady cadence, and we are on track to reducing our share count by a healthy amount as we go forward."
  • Key Partnership Announcements -- New exclusive agreement signed for the first 25,000 passenger vehicles produced by Wabi to operate solely on Uber's platform, and partnership with NVIDIA to collect over three million hours of real-world AV data.

SUMMARY

The call highlighted sustained acceleration in user growth and solid top-line performance, underpinned by rising monthly active users and substantial gains in free cash flow. Management emphasized the increasing contribution of non-core markets — both internationally and within less dense U.S. geographies — alongside a deliberate expansion of AV partnerships and related capital commitments. Uber Technologies (UBER 1.85%) reported that AVs are generating higher utilization and positive economics, while outlining a model in which further AV and fleet scaling will be enabled by both equity financing and third-party capital. The strategic focus extended to delivery segment growth, supported by near-term gains in both merchant base selection, advertising monetization, and further reinforced by international launches. Shareholder returns remain a priority, with a commitment to meaningful share buybacks as recurring cash flows expand.

  • Chief Executive Officer Dara Khosrowshahi said AV deployment is viewed as a "we are very confident that one, AVs will be a net positive to the mobility sector. In other words, it will expand the category. And, you know, we are the winner-take-most player as it relates to three p. And we think that the three p kind of marketplace will be very, very large and will be very, very healthy."
  • Chief Financial Officer Prashanth Mahendra-Rajah noted the company's first share repurchase program and achievement of “investment-grade status,” setting a platform for aggressive capital return.
  • Management explained insurance reform and consistent pricing as key drivers behind acceleration in U.S. trips and gross bookings, particularly as cost savings enable price stability and enhanced elasticity.
  • A multi-year exclusive with Kohl's, the largest grocer in Australia, was announced as part of continued efforts to expand delivery offerings into grocery and retail segments.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Autonomous Vehicle (AV): Self-driving vehicle that operates without direct human input for passenger or freight transportation.
  • MAPC (Monthly Active Platform Consumers): The number of unique customers engaging with the Uber platform within a given month.
  • Uber One: Uber’s paid membership program offering various benefits and incentives across its service portfolio.
  • Enterprise Advertising: Digital advertising sold to large businesses or brands, distinct from ad spend by smaller merchants (SMBs) on the platform.
  • SMB (Small and Medium-sized Business): Merchant accounts on the Uber platform classified by the company as small or mid-sized businesses.
  • Gross Bookings: The total dollar value, including taxes, tolls, and fees, of rides or deliveries facilitated on Uber's platform prior to certain deductions.
