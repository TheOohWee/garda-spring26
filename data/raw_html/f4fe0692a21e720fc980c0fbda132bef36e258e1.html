First Western Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Oct 24, 2025 at 4:33PM EST

Friday, October 24, 2025 at 12:00 p.m. ET

Call participants

Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Scott C. Wylie

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected].

Takeaways

Net Income -- $3.2 million net income, or $0.32 per diluted share.

Tangible Book Value per Share -- Increased 1.2% in the third quarter, reflecting improved capital management.

Loans Held for Investment -- Rose $50 million compared to the prior quarter, driven by $146 million in new loan production, primarily in residential and commercial real estate portfolios, and mostly accompanied by corresponding deposit relationships.

Average Rate on New Loan Production -- 6.38% for the third quarter, indicating disciplined pricing.

Total Deposits -- Increased $320 million sequentially, with both noninterest-bearing and interest-bearing categories growing due to new relationships and mortgage-driven inflows.

Assets Under Management (AUM) -- Decreased $64 million in assets under management primarily due to net withdrawals in low-fee product categories.

Trust and Investment Management Fees -- Rose $100,000 sequentially, driven by higher investment agency AUM.

Gross Revenue -- Gross revenue increased 8.7% sequentially and 15.5% year-over-year increase, supported by net interest income and non-interest income growth.

Net Interest Income -- Increased for a fourth consecutive quarter, up 8.9% sequentially; expansion attributed to higher average interest-earning assets from strong deposit growth.

Net Interest Margin (NIM) -- Decreased 13 basis points from the previous quarter to 2.54%, attributed to a shift toward higher-cost money market deposits; management expects NIM expansion as liquidity is deployed into loans in Q4 2025.

Non-Interest Income -- Grew over $500,000, or 8.5% sequentially (34% annualized), with all major fee categories contributing, including a noted increase in gain on sale of mortgage loans.

Non-Interest Expense -- Increased by less than $1 million sequentially, remaining consistent across categories; higher incentive compensation was noted.

Allowance Coverage -- Rose slightly from 75 basis points to 81 basis points, reflecting a specific provision for a downgraded C&I loan; net charge-offs remained minimal.

Spot Rate on Deposits at Quarter-End -- Spot rate on deposits at quarter-end was 3.04%.

Deposit Beta on Money Market Accounts -- Deposit beta on money market accounts was approximately 63% for the third quarter, consistent with management's fourth quarter expectations.

Bond Portfolio Investments -- Added $50 million to the portfolio, primarily in government-guaranteed floating-rate securities.

Summary

First Western Financial (MYFW 0.97%) management articulated that recent deposit growth, described as persistent rather than transitory, provides a larger base for future balance sheet expansion. Leadership maintained that operational leverage is improving, citing positive trends in both expense control and revenue growth across major business lines. They confirmed that nonperforming assets increased slightly due to a single C&I loan downgrade, but emphasized that allowance coverage and provisioning are adequate for this exposure. Executives stated, "As this liquidity is deployed into the loan portfolio during the fourth quarter, we expect to see NIM expansion," referring to Q4 2025, identifying near-term priorities for capital deployment and margin improvement. The company confirmed steady progress in its Arizona expansion, adding a senior leader from First Republic to build market share. Management highlighted that recent M&A-driven market disruption is providing both client and talent acquisition opportunities, with the loan and deposit pipelines characterized as "strong" and persisting into the next quarter.

CEO Wylie directly stated that a recent large influx of deposits should not be viewed as a one-time pre-funding event for 2026, but as part of an ongoing, company-wide focus on relationship-driven growth.

CFO Weber explicitly attributed the NIM decline to higher-cost money market deposit growth, while projecting a five basis point NIM increase in Q4 2025.

Leadership discussed that expense run rates in the coming quarter are expected to remain in line with the third quarter, accounting for variability tied to incentive compensation.

COO Courkamp and CEO Wylie highlighted that trust fee income, particularly from investment agency accounts, is now a greater management focus than the absolute level of AUM, given recent product mix shifts.

Executives indicated that organic growth and operational leverage remain management’s primary strategy rather than immediate engagement in M&A as a buyer, with CEO Wylie referencing the requirement to "get our stock price back to something reasonable" before pursuing acquisitions.

Industry glossary

Deposit Beta: The percentage of changes in market interest rates that is passed through to deposit rates; a measure of deposit rate sensitivity.

Money Market Accounts: Interest-bearing deposit accounts that allow limited check-writing and have variable interest rates, often used for institutional or high-value deposits.

C&I Loan: Commercial and industrial loan, a lending product extended to business entities for purposes other than real estate or personal use.

Basis Point: Equal to one one-hundredth of a percentage point (0.01%); used for measuring interest rate movements.

Allowance Coverage: Ratio of loan loss reserves to total loans or nonperforming assets, indicating loss-absorbing cushion.
