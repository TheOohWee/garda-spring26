Takeaways

  • SG&A expense ratio -- 17.5%, improving approximately 160 basis points compared to the previous year due to technology, AI initiatives, and cost management.

  • Membership mix shift -- Bronze plans increased to 39% (from 25%), gold plans rose to 25% (from ~4%), and silver dropped to 36% (from 71%).
  • Average member age -- 38 years, about one year younger than previous year.
  • Churn expectations -- A dip from 3.4 million enrolled in February to 3 million paid members by Q2, then monthly churn expected at 1%-2% for the remainder of the year.

  • Risk profile management -- Use of third-party clinical data to assess new member risk; migration from silver to bronze/gold plans after subsidy expiration noted.

  • Potential market contraction -- Mark Bertolini said that "market contraction may track toward the lower end of our original projection of 20% to 30%" and noted, "will they maintain coverage? Or will they drop out? And this is where the big."
  • Premium affordability and utilization uncertainty -- Mark Bertolini highlighted, "they were moved to or stayed in. They're going to start to say, wait a minute, this is expensive, and I'm not going to be able to afford this," signaling risk of higher disenrollment and financial hardship impacts.

Summary

Oscar Health (OSCR 0.62%) reported a 28% revenue increase to $11.7 billion and achieved record membership growth, ending open enrollment with 3.4 million members and projecting a 58% year-over-year rise in paid membership by the second quarter. Guidance for 2026 anticipates total revenues of $18.7 billion-$19 billion, operating earnings between $250 million and $450 million, and significant improvements in both medical loss and SG&A expense ratios. The company disclosed that risk adjustment accruals created meaningful earnings headwinds in 2025 and expects them to remain elevated for 2026. Recent membership mix shifts, including substantial increases in bronze and gold plan selections, combined with aggressive broker channel expansion, underscore Oscar Health's focus on retaining members and managing churn during a period of market contraction. AI investments and lifestyle product innovations contributed cost efficiencies and enhanced member retention, with direct technology benefits cited in administrative cost reductions and customer engagement metrics.

  • Mark Bertolini asserted the market is experiencing "a reset year," and Oscar Health responded with "decisive actions with a disciplined pricing, distribution and product strategy."
  • Management reframed risk adjustment forecasting as challenging due to limited visibility into market-wide morbidity and highlighted ongoing collaboration with Wakeley for improved transparency.
  • The shift in membership tier mix (bronze and gold up, silver down) reflects operational responses to subsidy expirations and consumer price sensitivity.
  • Richard Blackley stated its "capital position remains very strong," detailing substantial cash, investment, and new credit facility resources supporting planned growth.
  • The company indicated further reporting granularity may be expected, especially regarding ICRA and lifestyle product segment contributions over time.

Industry glossary

  • Medical loss ratio (MLR): The percentage of premium revenue spent on clinical services and quality improvement, excluding administrative and marketing costs.
  • Risk adjustment: A mechanism in ACA exchanges to balance the premium revenue by transferring funds from plans with lower-risk populations to those with higher-risk members.
  • SG&A expense ratio: Selling, general, and administrative expenses divided by total revenue, indicating operational efficiency.
  • IFP (Individual & Family Plan): Health insurance coverage sold directly to individuals and families, as opposed to group or employer coverage.
  • ICRA (Individual Coverage Health Reimbursement Arrangement): A type of employer-funded arrangement allowing employees to purchase their own health insurance with employer contributions.
  • Agentic AI: Oscar Health's proprietary artificial intelligence used for automating customer service and administrative workflows.
  • OEP (Open Enrollment Period): The annual window when individuals can enroll in or change health insurance plans.
