CVS Health (CVS) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 9:07AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Feb. 11, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — J. Joyner
  • Chief Financial Officer — Brian Newman
  • President, Aetna — Steven Nelson
  • Chief Medical Officer — Sreekanth Chaguturu
  • President, Pharmacy and Consumer Wellness — Prem Shah
  • Head of Pharmacy and Consumer Wellness — Len Shankman

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted Operating Income -- $2.6 billion for the fourth quarter, with performance above internal expectations but representing a modest year-over-year decline tied primarily to Health Care Benefits segment results.
  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share (EPS) -- $1.09 for the quarter; full-year adjusted EPS reached $6.75, approximately 15% above the initial guidance for the year.
  • Full-Year Revenue -- Over $400 billion, with more than $105 billion generated in the fourth quarter, representing more than 8% growth compared to the prior year quarter.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $3.4 billion generated in the fourth quarter and $10.6 billion for the full year, outpacing expectations due in part to the timing of certain payments.
  • Health Care Benefits Revenue -- Over $36 billion in the quarter, rising more than 10% year over year, led by Government programs and Medicare Part D impact from the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
  • Medical Membership -- Ended at approximately 26.6 million, a sequential decline and an annual decrease of about 500,000, reflecting individual Exchange and Government attrition partially balanced by commercial fee-based growth.
  • Health Care Benefits Segment Results -- Adjusted operating loss of $676 million in the quarter, modestly larger than the prior year, driven by Medicare Part D seasonality, negative risk adjustment in the Individual Exchange, and a flu provision, with improved Government segment offsets.
  • Medical Benefit Ratio (MBR) -- 94.8% in the quarter, with a full-year MBR of 91.2%, up approximately 20 basis points from prior guidance due to Medicaid pass-throughs, risk adjustment, and flu costs.
  • Health Services Revenue -- More than $51 billion in the quarter, up 9% year over year, led by drug mix and brand inflation, countered by pharmacy client price improvements.
  • Health Services Adjusted Operating Income -- Approximately $1.9 billion in the quarter, up over 9% from prior year, driven by purchasing economics.
  • Pharmacy & Consumer Wellness Revenue -- Nearly $38 billion in the quarter, rising over 12%, with prescription volume and Rite Aid transaction as contributors.
  • Same-Store Pharmacy Sales -- Grew more than 19% year over year in the quarter, driven by drug mix and a nearly 10% increase in prescription volume.
  • Retail Pharmacy Script Share -- Exceeded 29% in the quarter, reflecting organic growth and Rite Aid acquisition impact.
  • Pharmacy & Consumer Wellness Adjusted Operating Income -- Over $1.9 billion in the quarter, an increase of almost 9%, with annual adjusted operating income surpassing $6 billion, up 4.5% from the prior year.
  • Leverage Ratio -- Ended 2025 at approximately 4x due to improved financial performance.
  • Dividend Distribution -- Over $3 billion returned to shareholders via dividends during the year.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Revenue projected at least $400 billion, adjusted EPS expected in the $7.00-$7.20 range, and operating cash flow forecasted at a minimum of $9 billion.
  • 2026 Earnings Seasonality -- Anticipates a roughly 55%-45% first half to second half split; projects an 850-basis-point increase in MBR from Q1 to Q4, steeper than prior year.
  • Cost-Based Reimbursement Transition -- Transition to cost-based reimbursement completed in all major pharmacy lines, described as a "significant step in creating a more transparent and stable pharmacy market."
  • TrueCost Model Adoption -- Ongoing regulatory changes described as "closely aligned to where we believe the industry needs to go," and anticipated to accelerate TrueCost model adoption across Caremark.
  • Rite Aid Transaction Impact -- Welcomed nine million new patients and more than 3,500 new colleagues, contributing positively to pharmacy volume and market footprint.
  • Biosimilar HUMIRA Strategy -- Achieved 96% adoption of a low list-price biosimilar for HUMIRA, with more than 80% of members paying $0 out of pocket, generating over $1.5 billion in client and member savings.
  • Total Client and Member Savings -- Aetna network negotiations yielded $235 billion, and Caremark contributed $45 billion, for a cumulative $280 billion annual client and member savings figure.
  • Technology Investments -- Cited AI and platform technologies as drivers of cost savings, consumer engagement, and cross-segment integration, with product launches and partnerships planned for disclosure in 2026.
  • Market Recognition -- Aetna received the inaugural Press Ganey Health Plan of the Year award, noting technological innovation and high-quality offerings.

RISKS

  • CEO Joyner said, "The proposed rate simply does not match the level of medical cost trend in the industry," indicating concern over 2027 Medicare Advantage funding adequacy.
  • Management expressed that "medical cost trends in the quarter remained elevated across all products," with continuation expected through 2026.
  • Aetna business saw "a deterioration of our risk adjustment position in our Individual Exchange business and a provision for increased flu activity," contributing to adjusted operating losses.
  • Full-year operating cash flow guidance for 2026 was reduced to at least $9 billion due to payment timing, identified as "down slightly" compared to 2025.

SUMMARY

CVS Health Corporation (CVS +0.62%) reported revenue growth across all operating segments and maintained durable operating margins, ending the year with enhanced cash generation and an improved leverage ratio.

Management reaffirmed 2026 guidance for revenue and earnings, while disclosing that 2026 operating cash flow will be lower primarily due to accelerated payment receipts at year-end 2025. Strategic advances included full transition to cost-based pharmacy reimbursement, rapid TrueCost model adoption, and significant HUMIRA biosimilar penetration, each contributing to significant client and member savings. Advanced technology investments in AI, interoperability, and the open engagement platform were emphasized as enterprise-wide priorities positioned for measurable impact in 2026, with new partner collaborations anticipated. Aetna achieved notable commercial membership growth for the decade and received national quality recognition; however, management directly cited proposed 2027 Medicare Advantage rates as insufficient for medical cost trends, flagging ongoing advocacy as the company prioritizes maintenance of target segment margins.

  • The Rite Aid asset acquisition delivered immediate pharmacy market share gains by integrating nine million patients and expanding the national footprint.
  • CostVantage and digital capabilities were credited as bolstering pharmacy margins, contributing to a new long-term earnings baseline for the Retail/LTC segment.
  • Caremark’s role as pharmacy benefit manager remained described as "critical" given the branded drug price increases and annual client savings of $45 billion, underscoring the continuing pressure from pharmaceutical price inflation.
  • Fourth-quarter Health Care Benefits segment operating losses were attributed to Medicare Part D seasonality, risk adjustment deterioration, and elevated flu activity, with much of the pressure offset by Government business improvement and maintained reserve adequacy.
  • Management cited deliberate pricing discipline in commercial and Medicaid products, emphasizing retention, product innovation, and strong engagement with provider partners as levers for stability against high trend environments.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Adjusted Operating Income: Company-reported segment earnings prior to interest, taxes, and certain non-recurring items, used to measure operational segment profitability.
  • Medical Benefit Ratio (MBR): The percentage of premiums spent on medical claims, representing medical costs as a portion of premium revenue.
  • Cost-Based Reimbursement: A pharmacy reimbursement approach tying reimbursement directly to drug acquisition costs plus a handling fee, designed to enhance pricing transparency.
  • TrueCost Model: CVS Caremark’s pharmacy benefits model emphasizing price transparency, stable margins, and direct pass-through of costs to clients.
  • CostVantage: CVS’s pharmacy segment pricing model applying cost-based reimbursement across commercial, Medicare, and Medicaid pharmacy programs.
  • Biosimilar: A therapeutically comparable version of a branded biological drug, generally introduced at lower list prices to foster competition and savings.
  • Inflation Reduction Act (IRA): Recent federal legislation affecting Medicare drug program cost structures, notably Medicare Part D seasonality and reimbursement dynamics.
  • Prior Authorization: An insurance approval requirement for specific medical services or prescription drugs before coverage, often used for cost management and care appropriateness.
