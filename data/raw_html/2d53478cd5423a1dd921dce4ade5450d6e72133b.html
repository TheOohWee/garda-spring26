TAKEAWAYS

  • Pacific Premier (NASDAQ: PPBI) acquisition -- The acquisition closed Aug. 31, expanding the franchise to approximately $68 billion in assets and providing an 8-state Western footprint.
  • Deposit market share -- Management stated the company holds "nearly 10% deposit market share in the Northwest."
  • Operating pre-provision net revenue (PPNR) -- Operating PPNR increased 12% sequentially and 22% year over year, reaching $270 million.
  • Net interest margin (NIM) -- NIM expanded by 9 basis points to 3.84%, with Q4 expected to see a temporary increase above 3.90% due to $12 million in deposit premium accretion.
  • Operating earnings per share (EPS) -- Operating EPS was $0.85, exclusive of merger and restructuring expenses and certain fair value items.
  • Return on average tangible equity -- Operating return on average tangible equity was 18.2%.
  • Allowance for credit losses -- Coverage was 1.1% of loans, down from 1.17% prior quarter; inclusive of credit discount, the allowance stood at 1.34% of loans, up 3 basis points sequentially.
  • Provision for credit losses -- $70 million expense recorded due to purchase accounting related to the Pacific Premier acquisition.
  • Tangible book value per share -- Rose slightly to $18.57 after internal capital generation and favorable AOCI changes offset deal dilution.
  • Tangible book dilution -- The Pacific Premier acquisition resulted in 1.7% dilution, below the expected 7.6% at announcement.
  • Regulatory capital ratios -- Tier 1 Common was 11.6%, and Total Risk-Based Capital was 13.4%, both above long-term targets of 9% and 12%, respectively.
  • Operating expense -- Reported at $307 million, with a pro forma full quarter estimate of $375 million; $48 million of anticipated $127 million annualized cost savings achieved as of Sept. 30.
  • Share repurchase -- The Board authorized a $700 million share repurchase program, enabled by approximately $550 million in capital above target levels.
  • Organic deposit growth -- Customer deposits increased nearly $800 million organically, with approximately 30% from new clients and $150 million attributable to de novo branches.
  • Loan origination -- $1.2 billion in new loans originated, up 36% sequentially; commercial and owner-occupied real estate loans grew 5% annualized.
  • Noninterest income -- Operating noninterest income was $72 million, a 9% sequential increase, with $3 million from Custodial Trust Services acquired in the Pacific Premier transaction.
  • Targeted transactional loan reduction -- Management aims to reduce approximately $8 billion of inherited transactional loans organically over several years.
  • Loan pricing -- New loan originations are being generated at weighted average rates generally between 6.5% and 8%.
  • Systems integration -- Conversion is scheduled for Q1 2026, with the "clean expense run rate" expected by the third quarter of 2026.

SUMMARY

Management completed the strategic acquisition of Pacific Premier, delivering substantial scale, geographic reach, and new business line capabilities, while citing tangible book dilution below prior expectations and significant capital generation. A $700 million share repurchase program was initiated as regulatory capital ratios strengthened, supported by internal capital accumulation and enhanced profitability. Guidance called for further improvement in net interest margin in the coming quarters, with continued focus on organic growth, transaction portfolio run-off, and disciplined expense management. Management stated it has "0 interest in M&A for the foreseeable future," emphasizing share repurchases, profitability, and full integration as top priorities.

  • The company expects cost savings from the Pacific Premier deal to accelerate into 2026, with operating expenses (excluding core deposit intangible amortization) anticipated between $330 million and $340 million per quarter until a lower post-synergy baseline is achieved.
  • Deposit betas for interest-bearing accounts are currently tracking near 50%, with proactive repricing to capture future Federal Reserve rate cuts.
  • Management targets a sustainable high-teens return on tangible equity, with operating ROTCE already at 18%, and ambitions stated for top decile performance.
  • Loan growth will be muted by planned runoff of $8 billion of transactional loans, but remix into higher-yielding, relationship-based C&I loans is expected to support revenue growth even with flat total asset levels.
  • Balance sheet optimization includes sales of non-strategic securities, repayment of wholesale funding, and maintaining spring cash balances of $1.7 billion to $1.9 billion.
  • Revenue synergies from Pacific Premier’s HOA banking, custodial trust, and technology platforms are already contributing to noninterest income, with legacy branch referral activity exceeding 1,200 opportunities since deal closure.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PPNR (Pre-Provision Net Revenue): Income before loan loss provisions and taxes, used to assess operating profitability.
  • Transactional loans: Shorter-term loans typically not tied to core client relationships and targeted for runoff or remixing into high-value relationship loans.
  • AOCI (Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income): An equity account reflecting unrealized gains or losses on certain balance sheet items, often used to assess tangible book value impacts.
  • Deposit beta: The percentage of change in deposit rates relative to changes in market interest rates, important for margin modeling.
  • Core deposit intangible (CDI) amortization: The periodic expense associated with the intangible asset recorded in an acquisition for the value of core customer deposits.
