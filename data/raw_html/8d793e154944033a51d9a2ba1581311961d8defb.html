Earnings Call Transcript

Union Pacific (UNP) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 12:41PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, July 24, 2025 at 8:45 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Vincenzo James Vena
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jennifer L. Hamann
  • Executive Vice President, Marketing and Sales — Kenyatta G. Rocker
  • Executive Vice President, Operations — Eric J. Gehringer

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted Diluted Earnings Per Share -- $3.03, up 12%, after excluding a $115 million deferred tax benefit and a $55 million Brakeperson buyout labor expense.
  • Operating Revenue -- $6.2 billion, rising 2%, with freight revenue reaching $5.8 billion, a quarterly record, up 4%.
  • Operating Ratio (Adjusted) -- 58.1%, an improvement of 230 basis points, reflecting a 90 basis point impact from the labor agreement.
  • Freight Revenue Excluding Fuel Surcharge -- Grew 6%, achieving highest ever quarterly and year-to-date totals.
  • Core Pricing and Mix -- Delivered a 200 basis point benefit to freight revenue, with net price above inflation for the third consecutive quarter.
  • Volume Growth -- Drove 375 basis points of freight revenue increase, with Bulk segment volumes up 11%, Industrial up 3%, and Premium up 1%.
  • Fuel Expense -- Decreased 8% due to an 11% drop in fuel prices from $2.73 to $2.42 per gallon, while consumption rate improved 2%; fuel surcharge revenue fell $100 million to $569 million.
  • Compensation and Benefits Expense -- Rose 5%, driven by the $55 million Brakeperson buyout; excluding this, compensation and benefits rose 1% with a 3.5% increase in cost per employee on a 3% smaller workforce.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $4.3 billion returned via buybacks and dividends through the second quarter; 3% dividend increase announced, marking 19 consecutive years of increases.
  • Adjusted Debt-to-EBITDA Ratio -- Ended at 2.8x, with A-ratings maintained from three credit agencies.
  • Cash from Operations -- $4.5 billion, an increase of over $500 million.
  • Efficiency Metrics -- Freight car velocity improved 10% to 221 miles/day; train speed up 3%, terminal dwell down 7%; locomotive productivity up 5%; workforce productivity up 9% to all-time quarterly record; train length set a record near 9,700 feet.
  • Segment Trends -- Bulk revenue up 10% (volume: 11%); Industrial revenue up 4% (volume: 3%); Premium revenue down 4% (volume: 1% increase); automotive volumes fell due to reduced OEM production.
  • Future Outlook -- Management reaffirmed a high single to low double-digit 3-year EPS CAGR target and expects 2025 EPS growth to align with this range.
  • Capital Investment -- More than $1.4 billion invested since 2020 in intermodal expansion, with four new terminals (latest in Kansas City).
  • Business Combination Disclosure -- CEO Vena stated, "Union Pacific and Norfolk Southern are engaged in advanced discussions regarding a potential business combination," with no assurance of an agreement.

RISKS

  • Management guided for sequential volume declines in the back half, noting, "that's likely not going to happen. In fact, we're expecting some sequential declines through the back half."
  • Guidance cited "continued softness in the autos market and lower accessorials" as weighing on other revenue for the third quarter.
  • Premium segment saw a 4% revenue decrease, with the call noting, "strong comparisons and port shifts will challenge international and domestic intermodal volumes."
  • CEO Vena described "Unknowns remain," for the second half, explicitly noting external pressures including tariff implications and possible changes in consumer behavior.

SUMMARY

Union Pacific (UNP 0.26%) delivered its highest-ever quarterly freight revenue, operating income, and cash from operations, with management highlighting robust volume growth, pricing gains, and significant productivity improvements. The company confirmed it remains on track to achieve its multi-year EPS CAGR target and repeated confidence in its capital deployment plans despite projecting sequential volume moderation in the coming quarters. Notably, Union Pacific disclosed it is in advanced discussions with Norfolk Southern (NYSE: NSC) regarding a potential business combination, but no further comment or assurance of an agreement was provided.

  • Management attributed record core pricing gains and operating leverage to an elevated service product and cost discipline, with productivity records in both workforce and locomotive operations.
  • Industrial investments in the Gulf Coast and intermodal infrastructure, including the newly opened Kansas City terminal, underpin current and anticipated carload growth across several business segments.
  • CFO Hamann estimated the restoration of 100% bonus depreciation would provide a $250 million to $300 million annual cash benefit for the company.
  • Operational initiatives delivered a 2% improvement in fuel consumption rate and a 5% gain in other expense efficiency, supporting margin expansion.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Operating Ratio (OR): A key railway efficiency metric calculated as operating expenses divided by operating revenue; lower values indicate higher profitability and efficiency.
  • Accessorials: Fees charged for rail services that are outside of standard line-haul transportation, such as demurrage, storage, or special handling.
  • IMC (Intermodal Marketing Company): A third-party logistics provider that contracts freight for intermodal transport, coordinating between shippers and railroads.
  • LCRA (Lower Colorado River Authority): A bulk coal customer cited for new volume shipments contributing to segment growth.
  • BCO (Beneficial Cargo Owner): The ultimate owner of cargo being shipped, as distinct from intermediaries or logistics providers.
