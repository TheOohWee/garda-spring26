Broadstone Net Lease (BNL) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 12:32PM EST

  • President — Brent Maedl

Takeaways

  • AFFO per share -- $1.49, up 4.2%, driven by strong same-store rent growth of 2%, and $430 million in stabilized investments.
  • Portfolio leased rate -- 99% leased, with 99.8% of rents collected at year-end.
  • New investment activity -- $748.4 million deployed, including $429.9 million in acquisitions, $209.3 million in build-to-suit developments, $100.8 million in transitional capital, and $8.3 million in revenue-generating CapEx.
  • Weighted average initial cash cap rate (acquisitions and CapEx) -- 7%; weighted average lease term of 14.2 years; annual rent increases of 2.6% (noting contractual rent growth outpaces portfolio average by 50 basis points).
  • Build-to-suit pipeline -- $350 million in high-quality projects scheduled to stabilize in 2026, expected to generate $26 million in incremental ABR.
  • 2026 AFFO guidance -- Reaffirmed at $1.53 to $1.57 per share, a 4% midpoint increase, with embedded investment assumptions: $500 million-$625 million of investments, $75 million-$100 million of dispositions, core G&A at $30 million-$31 million, and 75 basis points of lost rent assumed.
  • Bad debt expense -- 31 basis points for the year, below reserve; no bad debt realized from American Signature bankruptcy due to successful lease assumption and recovery.
  • Dispositions -- 28 properties sold for $96 million at a 7.3% average cash cap rate (tenanted properties), with a focus on risk mitigation and clinical health care asset reduction.
  • Leverage -- Pro forma leverage ended at 5.8x; $11 million of unsettled equity; $700 million available revolver capacity.
  • Dividend -- Quarterly dividend raised to $0.2925 per share, up approximately 1%, targeting a mid-70% payout ratio by 2026.
  • Term loan amendments -- All-in rates reduced by 10 basis points on $1 billion in loans, with an additional 25 basis point reduction on the 2029 loan; the 2029 maturity pushed to Feb. 2031.
  • Project Triborough investment -- Approximately $100 million invested at year-end as a power-centered industrial development, maintaining multiple value-creation paths and optionality.
  • Red Lobster exposure -- 1.3% of ABR remains across 18 sites under a single master lease with expiration in 2042; company evaluating for-sale or for-lease options to minimize future exposure as foot traffic and profitability trends are closely monitored.
  • Equity capital activity -- $43 million raised in gross proceeds under the ATM since November; management does not plan substantial equity issuance at current price levels.
  • Cap rate environment -- Build-to-suit deals sourced in the mid-7% range for cash yields (8.6% straight-line), while competition continues to compress cap rates on traditional acquisitions, especially in industrial portfolios.
  • Lease rollovers -- 19 rollovers completed (over 1% ABR), with a 110% recapture rate and new lease terms averaging over seven years; 3.3% of ABR rolling in 2026 with negotiations underway.

Risks

  • Management cites ongoing concerns related to Red Lobster, noting, "continued underperformance at some of our sites," and disclosure that Red Lobster post-bankruptcy traffic and profitability remain mixed, prompting evaluation of disposition or re-leasing strategies to reduce exposure.
  • Persistently high competition for traditional acquisitions has led to cap rate compression on larger industrial and food processing portfolios, thereby impacting risk-adjusted return expectations.
  • Company expresses frustration with persistent "relative valuation," indicating current price levels remain below peer averages and are "not even being at the average level," which restricts further equity issuance for growth funding.

Summary

Broadstone Net Lease (BNL +0.81%) management highlighted a record year of operational execution with $748.4 million of capital deployed and minimal bad debt, supported by robust leasing and rent collection. The build-to-suit platform anchors future growth, with $350 million of stabilized development and over $142 million under advanced LOIs, providing near-term ABR expansion and visibility into 2027. Financial flexibility remains solid, as amendments to term loans reduced interest costs and extended maturities, while limited near-term debt and $700 million in liquidity underpin confidence in self-funding strategies. Dividend growth resumes in 2026, reflecting a return to sustainable payout expansion. Direct sourcing and longstanding tenant relationships differentiate Broadstone Net Lease’s positioning and mitigate increasing competition across both acquisition and development pipelines.

  • Current guidance assumes a lower incremental investment burden for achieving AFFO growth, as management stated required capital deployment for 2026 targets is "relatively insignificant" relative to existing pipeline momentum.
  • Stabilized acquisitions and revenue-generating CapEx in 2025 delivered a weighted average lease term of 14.2 years and outpaced portfolio-average contractual rent escalations by 50 basis points, enhancing duration and embedded growth.
  • Lease restructuring at American Signature generated no rent loss, as letters of credit covered missed rent and Gardner White assumed all six locations at unchanged rents, with pursuit of a master lease structure underway.
  • Of 19 lease rollovers, a weighted average recapture rate of 110% extended average lease term, and 3.3% of ABR is set for rollover in 2026, with current negotiations showing a positive outlook on outcomes.
  • Project Triborough positions Broadstone Net Lease for high-value exposure to anticipated hyperscale data center demand, with initial power delivery for the first 100+ megawatts targeted as early as 2027; management notes unsolicited proposals reflect values above current invested capital.
  • Transitional capital deployment, notably Project Triborough at $100 million, preserves strategic optionality and advances relationships that generate further pipeline opportunities beyond traditional net lease assets.
  • G&A efficiency improved, with full-year core G&A at $28.7 million, down 2%; 2026 guidance has been revised downward, expecting $30 million-$31 million, consistent with disciplined expense control.
  • Regular way acquisition volume, while recovering, remains below pre-2023 transaction levels, sustaining heightened competition and requiring continued discipline to avoid subpar risk-adjusted pricing.
  • Build-to-suit sourcing remains predominantly repeat business, as nearly all advanced projects under LOI involve existing partners or tenants, sustaining Broadstone Net Lease’s competitive access and funnel depth.
  • Regarding Claire's, the exercised lease termination right for June 2026 incurs no termination fee, with vacancy or disposition already incorporated in bad debt and guidance assumptions.
  • Management confirmed that current 2% internal growth assumptions remain reasonable for forward planning, with potential for upward momentum based on recent disclosure trends.

Industry glossary

  • Build-to-suit: A development arrangement where properties are constructed to meet the specifications of a particular tenant, usually under a long-term lease agreement, often enhancing yield and reducing lease-up risk.
  • Cap rate (Capitalization rate): The initial annual cash return on an investment property, calculated as net operating income divided by acquisition price, serving as a primary yield metric in real estate underwriting.
  • LOI (Letter of intent): A non-binding agreement outlining basic terms for a potential property acquisition or development transaction, typically preceding full due diligence and definitive documentation.
  • Transitional capital: Investments allocated to projects not yet stabilized or fully leased, often involving development or repositioning to drive future cash flow growth.
  • Recapture rate: Percentage of prior rent recovered or exceeded in a newly negotiated lease upon tenant turnover, reflecting leasing power and market conditions.
  • Unsettled equity: Proceeds from at-the-market equity sales that have been raised but not yet formally issued as shares outstanding.
  • ABR (Annualized base rent): The annual income generated by leased properties, normalized to reflect stabilized leasing levels and contractual rent rates.
