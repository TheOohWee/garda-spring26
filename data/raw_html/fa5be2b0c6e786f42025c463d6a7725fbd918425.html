Zoetis (ZTS) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Apr 8, 2026 at 2:03PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Nov. 4, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kristin Peck
  • Chief Financial Officer — Wetteny Joseph

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $2.4 billion, up 1% on a reported basis and 4% on an organic operational basis, primarily driven by price while volume was flat.
  • Adjusted Net Income -- $754 million, representing 5% growth on a reported basis and 9% on an organic operational basis.
  • Companion Animal Revenue -- $1.7 billion, up 2% operationally; includes Simparica franchise at $356 million (up 7% operationally) and Key Dermatology at $469 million (up 3% operationally).
  • OA Pain Franchise Revenue -- $138 million, down 11% operationally, with global Librela sales dropping 15% operationally and Solensia revenue declining 4% to $17 million.
  • Livestock Revenue -- $725 million, up 10% on an organic operational basis, aided by broad-based growth across geographies and species.
  • Simparica Trio International Sales -- $41 million, up 32% operationally, with double-digit international franchise growth and recent approval in Brazil.
  • U.S. Results -- U.S. revenue declined 2% on a reported basis and grew 3% on an organic operational basis; livestock up 14% operationally, companion animal segment flat.
  • International Segment -- Gained 3% reported and 6% organic operational revenue growth, with companion animal revenue up 4% operationally and livestock up 8% operationally.
  • Adjusted Gross Margin -- 71.6%, up 90 basis points on a reported basis, with foreign exchange impact contributing 20 basis points.
  • Full-Year 2025 Guidance -- Revised revenue target range to $9.4 billion to $9.475 billion, narrowing organic operational growth outlook to 5.5% to 6.5% and adjusted net income to $2.8 billion to $2.84 billion with 5.5% to 7% organic operational growth.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- Grew 7% operationally and 12% on an organic operational basis; guidance maintained at $6.30 to $6.40 for adjusted and $5.90 to $6.00 for reported diluted EPS.
  • Portfolio Expansion -- Over 130 geographic expansion and lifecycle innovation milestones achieved in the year, including approvals for Lenivia (dogs in Canada) and Portela (cats in Europe), both launching first half 2026.
  • R&D Leadership Transition -- Rob Polzer to retire at year-end, with Kevin Esch named successor as of January 1, 2026, and Polzer staying on as Scientific Adviser until end of 2026.

Summary

Zoetis (ZTS 1.77%) reported organic operational growth across revenue and adjusted net income, driven by companion animal and livestock portfolios, while pricing contributed meaningfully as volume remained flat. The company highlighted continued international expansion, with Simparica Trio and Key Dermatology franchises fueling sector growth, and marked significant geographic product approvals for OA pain management expected to enhance future revenue streams. Guidance for full-year 2025 was narrowed to reflect macroeconomic headwinds, subdued therapeutic clinic visits, and ongoing competitive promotional activities, particularly in dermatology and OA pain. Operational efficiency was demonstrated through cost discipline, a 90-basis-point gross margin expansion, and stable distributor inventory levels near historic lows. Zoetis outlined a smooth succession in R&D leadership and reaffirmed confidence in its innovation pipeline, targeting major new market approvals each year through multiple product launches and ongoing life-cycle advancements.

  • Zoetis confirmed steady livestock growth momentum, underpinned by vaccine-led expansion and strengthened field forces in poultry and cattle sectors.
  • International markets benefited from European approvals and new launches, supporting a broadened chronic pain management portfolio in both canine and feline segments.
  • The revised guidance explicitly incorporates assumed persistence of macro and operational challenges into year-end, with details on future forecasts deferred until February.
  • Phase IV independent studies launching in Q4 aim to provide further real-world support for Librela, as early trends signaled sequential stabilization following recent headwinds.
  • Pet owner and veterinarian satisfaction with Simparica and Librela franchises remained high, mitigating aggressive competitive actions and supporting retention.

Industry glossary

  • OA pain mAbs: Monoclonal antibody therapies indicated for the treatment of osteoarthritis-related pain in animals.
  • Simparica Trio: A triple-combination oral parasiticide for dogs, targeting fleas, ticks, and heartworm.
  • Librela: A monoclonal antibody product for canine osteoarthritis pain management.
  • Solensia: A monoclonal antibody product for feline osteoarthritis pain management.
  • Key Dermatology franchise: Product portfolio addressing allergic and other dermatological conditions in animals, notably Apoquel (including Chewable) and Cytopoint.
  • NUMELVI: Newly launched competitor product referenced in the European dermatology market.
  • Zenrelia: Competitor dermatology product in the U.S. and international markets.
  • MFA divestiture: Zoetis' sale of its medicated feed additive business, affecting year-over-year U.S. revenue comparability.
  • CVMP: Committee for Medicinal Products for Veterinary Use, the European Medicines Agency’s committee responsible for veterinary medicinal products.
  • Phase IV research: Post-marketing clinical studies intended to confirm effectiveness or monitor safety in a real-world setting.
  • Alternative channels: Non-clinic distribution avenues such as retail and home delivery, increasingly significant for pet medication sales.
