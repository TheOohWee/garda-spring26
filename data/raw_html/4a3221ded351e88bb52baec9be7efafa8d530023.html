Intercorp Financial (IFS) Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 12:08PM EST

Takeaways

  • Net income -- $1.9 billion for the year, representing a 49% increase year over year, setting an all-time high for Intercorp Financial Services (IFS 1.26%).
  • Return on equity (ROE) -- 16.8% for the full year; would have been 18.5% excluding the Ruta de Lima impairment; quarterly ROE reached 15% and would have been 19.1% without the impairment provision.
  • Bank segment earnings -- Interbank delivered $1.5 billion in net income, driven by lower cost of risk, decreased funding costs, and higher fee income.
  • Wealth management growth -- Inteligo posted a 68% revenue increase and a 21.5% ROE, with assets under management reaching a record $9.1 billion.
  • Insurance segment performance -- Interseguro posted 36% growth and achieved a 32.5% ROE, with written premiums increasing by 661% and contractual service margin up 22%.
  • Revenue growth -- Consolidated revenues increased 13% year over year, with Interseguro rising 33% and Inteligo advancing 29%.
  • Loan portfolio growth -- Total loan book expanded 4% (6.5% ex-FX), propelled by higher-yielding loans up 8% and small business lending up 25%.
  • Mortgage market share -- Mortgage portfolio increased over 8%, gaining 10 bps of market share to exceed 16%.
  • Retail primary customers -- Retail primary banking client base climbed 11%, now representing over 35% of total retail clients.
  • Risk-adjusted net interest margin (NIM) -- Rose 50 bps year over year to 4% in the last quarter; full-year risk-adjusted NIM was 3.7%, up from 2.9%.
  • Cost of risk -- Declined to 1.8% in the quarter and 2.3% for the year, marking a four-year low for quarterly levels; cost of risk in consumer lending dropped from nearly 9% to below 7% year over year.
  • Deposit base & funding structure -- Total deposits rose 5% (9% ex-FX), now comprising 81% of funding; loan-to-deposit ratio stands at 92%.
  • Cost-to-income ratio -- Stood at 36.8% for the year with operating expenses up 11%, reflecting continued investments in technology and talent.
  • Digital engagement -- 84% of retail customers and 74% of commercial clients are now digital; monthly active Plin users hit 2.6 million, up 33% in transactions per user, while digital insurance premiums grew 25%.
  • Payment ecosystem contribution -- Izipay flows increased 60% and supported a 26% deposit growth; Plin processed 48% more transactions, with P2M payments now 60% of volume.
  • Capital ratios -- Total capital ratio is 16% and core equity Tier 1 is 12.5%.
  • Guidance for 2026 -- Management projects ROE around 17%, high single-digit loan growth, and cost-to-income ratio stable at approximately 37%.
  • Ruta de Lima impairment -- An impairment charge of $2 million was recorded, leaving a residual value of $22 million; management does not anticipate further material impairments on this asset.
  • Coverage ratio -- Nonperforming loan coverage remains solid at about 140%, with management expecting this level to be maintained as higher-yield loans grow.

Risks

  • Management explicitly cited political volatility during the election year as a key risk, stating, "the risk is for someone that is not market friendly being elected and trying to change certain things that support growth, stability, the currency being stable, or issues that will come with inflation."
  • Cost of risk is expected to "gradually increase" as the share of high-yielding consumer and SME loans rises, which management noted may push the cost of risk up to the historic environment.
  • Management acknowledged continued short-term growth constraints from pension fund withdrawals, with CEO Castellanos noting, "pension fund releases and the severance deposit releases actually are a stopper to loan growth."

Summary

Intercorp Financial Services (IFS 1.26%) reported record financial results, highlighting a 49% year-over-year increase in net income and double-digit revenue and profit expansion across all core business units. Management emphasized digital adoption and payments ecosystem synergies as key growth catalysts fueling customer acquisition and operational leverage. Loan portfolio growth was driven primarily by higher-yield product expansion in consumer and SME segments, with asset quality improvements and increasing net interest margins supporting sustainable profitability. Strategic guidance points to further growth in returns and efficiency, even as political and macroeconomic uncertainties persist into the election cycle.

  • Management confirmed that the impact from the Ruta de Lima impairment should be fully absorbed, with no further material charges anticipated based on current information.
  • ROEs for Inteligo and Interseguro are structurally positioned at or above 20%, while the bank's ROE recovery will depend on the renewal of higher-yielding loan growth.
  • Growth in low-cost retail and wholesale funding, supported by Izipay and Plin, is expected to support margin expansion and efficient funding costs into 2026.
  • The company expects continued growth in digital activity—with innovations such as Plin Corredores and Interfondos app enhancements—driving higher customer engagement and cross-sell opportunities.
  • Management expects asset quality coverage ratios to remain stable even as the loan mix shifts toward higher-yielding, higher-risk assets.

Industry glossary

  • Izipay: A payment processing and merchant acquiring network operated by Interbank, integral to IFS's payments ecosystem.
  • Plin: A digital payment and transfer platform facilitating instant peer-to-peer and merchant transactions for banking clients in Peru.
  • Contractual service margin (CSM): The unearned profit reserve related to insurance contracts, reflecting future earnings recognition in the insurance segment.
  • P2M payments: Digital payments executed from a person to a merchant, particularly prominent in the context of Plin transaction volumes.
  • AFP withdrawals: Withdrawals from Peruvian private pension funds (Administradoras de Fondos de Pensiones), materially affecting consumer liquidity and credit demand dynamics in the banking system.
