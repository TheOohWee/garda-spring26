Teads (TEAD) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 5, 2026 at 11:15AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, March 5, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Co-Chief Executive Officer — David Kostman
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jason Kiviat

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $352 million, representing a 50% increase year over year on an as-reported basis due to the acquisition, but a 17% year-over-year decline on a pro forma basis.
  • Ex-TAC Gross Profit -- $152 million, up 122% as reported, but down 19% on a pro forma basis, as mix shifts favored more profitable streams following acquisition integration.
  • CTV Revenue -- Surpassed $100 million annualized, with 55% growth in Q4 and significant expansion in home screen placements.
  • Enterprise Performance Cross-Selling -- Sales to enterprise customers increased 300% sequentially from Q3, albeit totaling just a few million dollars per quarter.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $37 million in Q4, exceeding guided range.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $3 million for Q4 and $6 million for the full year, consistently positive throughout 2025.
  • Balance Sheet Position -- Ended the quarter with $139 million in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities, against $17.5 million in short-term debt and $628 million in long-term debt at a 10% coupon due 2030.
  • Annual Cost Savings -- December restructuring is projected to deliver between $35 million and $40 million in annual savings from personnel reductions.
  • Goodwill Impairment -- Recognized a $350 million non-cash impairment charge due to share price and market cap declines, with no effect on liquidity or covenants.
  • Q1 2026 Guidance -- Ex-TAC gross profit expected in the range of $102 million to $106 million; adjusted EBITDA expected to range from breakeven to $3 million.
  • Full-Year 2026 Guidance -- Targeting adjusted EBITDA of approximately $100 million, with potential for a small use of cash but overall confidence in liquidity and further positive free cash flow opportunities.
  • Global OEM Reach -- Teads Ad Manager now has access to over 500 million addressable TVs through expanded partnerships, including exclusivity with LG in Italy and Greece and Samsung TV in Asia-Pacific.
  • Operational Progress -- Stabilization observed in the U.K. top line and improved sales pipeline in the U.S. following leadership and organizational changes.
  • Inventory Quality Cleanup -- Deliberately exited lower-quality demand and supply sources, resulting in an anticipated $20 million ex-TAC headwind concentrated in H1 2026 and tapering off by Q4.
  • AI Advancement -- Integration of AI and LLMs contributed to lower cost per acquisition and improvement in advertising margins.
  • Omnichannel Results -- Recent partnership with Accor delivered quantifiable lifts: "23% lift in brand favorability" and a "17% increase in purchase intent."

RISKS

  • Kiviat stated, "the operational challenges have led us to a timetable longer than we initially anticipated, resulting in this impairment charge," referencing the $350 million goodwill impairment related to market cap and share price declines.
  • Kiviat said, "The year-over-year comparison impact for 2026 is expected to be a headwind of approximately $20,000,000 of ex-TAC with the vast majority of that in H1, phasing down to a minimal amount by Q4," due to exiting lower-quality supply and demand sources.
  • Kostman noted, "The 2025 trimming of our supply and demand sources to ensure higher quality will impact our year-over-year comparisons early on," with soft year-over-year performance expected through early 2026.
  • Kiviat highlighted that FX headwinds "a few million dollars that we see from the weakening of the dollar versus, you know, the euro and the shekel." will impact expenses despite ongoing cost reductions.

SUMMARY

Teads Holding Co. (TEAD 6.58%) delivered Q4 results at the high end of ex-TAC gross profit guidance, and adjusted EBITDA exceeded the outlook, amid continued positive adjusted free cash flow generation. Management noted annualized CTV revenue surpassed $100 million, with home screen expansion contributing to rapid segment growth and differentiated positioning through exclusive OEM partnerships. Strategic cost reductions are expected to yield $35 million to $40 million in annual savings following restructuring, while management recognized a $350 million non-cash goodwill impairment reflecting recent market valuation declines but with no liquidity impact. The company projects full-year 2026 adjusted EBITDA of approximately $100 million and expects to resume pro forma ex-TAC growth by Q4, as the impact of revenue quality cleanup moderates and integration synergies take hold.

  • Leadership emphasized stabilization in key geographies, with the U.K. and U.S. seeing improved pipelines due to organizational realignment.
  • Kostman said, "We crossed the $100 million annual revenue mark with growth hitting 55% in Q4, and with strong growth on the home screen placements."
  • Integration of AI and data infrastructure is "already seeing tangible results," leading to measurable improvements in advertiser KPIs and company margins.
  • Ex-TAC gross profit growth is outpacing revenue growth due to enhanced mix and premium inventory focus post-acquisition.
  • The inventory quality cleanup, including walking away from $20 million in lower-quality revenue, remains a temporary headwind, mainly impacting H1 performance comparisons.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Ex-TAC: Revenue after deducting traffic acquisition costs, a key profit measure for digital advertising platforms.
  • CTV: Connected TV; advertising inventory delivered via smart TVs or internet-connected TV platforms.
  • OEM: Original equipment manufacturer; device makers like LG, Samsung, or Vizio that provide home screen advertising placements.
  • TPV: Total platform volume; aggregate metric representing the scale of campaigns or impressions managed through the platform.
  • LLM: Large language model; advanced type of AI used for predictive analytics and ad content optimization.
  • ROAS: Return on ad spend; a measure of revenue generated per dollar spent on advertising.
