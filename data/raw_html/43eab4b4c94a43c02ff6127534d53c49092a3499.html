LiveRamp (RAMP) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 5:42PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 5, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Scott Howe
  • Chief Financial Officer — Lauren Dillard

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $212 million, up 9%, with performance exceeding the midpoint of company guidance by $1 million.
  • Subscription revenue -- $158 million, also up 9%, with fixed subscription revenue growing 8% and usage-based revenue up 13% year over year.
  • Annual recurring revenue (ARR) -- Increased by $11 million quarter over quarter, rising 7% year over year, driven by commerce media, connected TV (CTV), and measurement use cases.
  • Customer metrics -- Total customer count rose by 15 sequentially, marking the largest quarterly increase in over three years, while customers with $1 million-plus in annual spend grew by eight to 140.
  • Net new ARR -- Surpassed $11 million for a second consecutive quarter, attributed primarily to upsell wins with key enterprise clients.
  • Operating metrics -- Record quarterly operating margin of 29% (non-GAAP), operating income of $62 million (up 36%), and record free cash flow of $67 million.
  • Share repurchases -- $39 million deployed in the quarter, with $119 million repurchased year to date and $137 million remaining under authorization as of quarter end.
  • Balance sheet -- Cash and short-term investments of approximately $403 million, and no debt reported.
  • Revenue backlog (RPO) -- Total RPO rose 23% to $710 million, with current RPO at $471 million, up 9% sequentially due to renewal seasonality.
  • Subscription net retention -- Held at 101%, consistent with the stated 100%-105% near-term target.
  • Gross margin -- Came in at 74%, benefiting from customer migration timing to new platform infrastructure.
  • AI partnerships -- Over 20 AI-specific partners signed, with AI model integrations and usage-based pricing pilots noted as key growth initiatives.
  • Fiscal 2026 outlook (period ending March 31, 2026) -- Revenue guidance raised to $810-$814 million (~9% growth), with non-GAAP operating income expected at $180 million and gross margin forecasted at 72%-73% as platform upgrades complete.
  • Capital allocation -- Company continues to prioritize free cash flow for share buybacks, expected to reduce total share count this fiscal year.
  • Rule of 40 targets -- Company reiterated the fiscal 2028 (period ending March 31, 2028) goal of 10%-15% revenue growth and 25%-30% non-GAAP operating margin; expects to reach 'rule of 31' this fiscal year, with 9% revenue growth and 22% margin.

Summary

LiveRamp (RAMP 3.16%) reported record non-GAAP operating margin, free cash flow, and quarterly growth in high-value customers, attributing much of its performance to AI-driven demand and expansion in key verticals such as commerce media and CTV. Management emphasized a transition toward usage-based pricing, citing pilot results that lowered entry points for midsized and SMB clients and highlighted a notable contract expansion with Publicis. The outlook for fiscal 2026 includes increased top- and bottom-line guidance, with growing sales pipeline activity and broad-based customer additions fueling confidence in sustained acceleration. The company outlined specific plans to leverage offshoring and platform optimization to compress costs further, while maintaining aggressive share repurchase activity as a top capital priority.

  • Scott Howe said, "subscription usage revenue will now be more directly linked to the growing use of our platform by the partners and customers," referencing the economic neutrality between direct and reseller channels enabled by contract revisions.
  • Piloted usage-based pricing model resulted in average contract sizes that are lower than traditional contracts, indicating early traction with smaller new logos and anticipated future ARR growth as these clients scale usage.
  • Lauren Dillard noted bookings growth "up in the quarter strong double digits," driven by expansions with existing customers, particularly for clean room offerings supporting cross-media intelligence and commerce measurement.
  • Data marketplace revenue rebounded to double-digit growth rates from mid-November, reversing a slower start to the quarter and supporting management's high-single- to low-double-digit revenue outlook for Q4.

Industry glossary

  • ARR (Annual recurring revenue): A measure of predictable revenue generated from ongoing subscription contracts, annualized for financial analysis.
  • RPO (Remaining performance obligations): The contracted future revenue that has not yet been recognized; includes both current and future (non-current) obligations.
  • CTV (Connected TV): Television or device connected to the internet, enabling digital streaming, used for targeted advertising measurement and activation.
  • Clean room: Privacy-enhancing technology platform enabling multiple parties to collaborate on sensitive marketing data without exposing raw data to each party.
