Earnings Call Transcript

Academy Sports (ASO) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 17, 2026 at 11:19AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, March 17, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Steve Lawrence
  • Chief Financial Officer — Carl Ford

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $1.7 billion for the quarter, up 2.5% with comparable sales down 1.6%.
  • Transactions and Ticket -- Transactions fell 6.4%, while average ticket increased 5.1% in the quarter.
  • Gross Margin -- 33.6% for the quarter, 140 basis points higher, driven by supply chain efficiencies and fewer port-related costs.
  • Net Income -- $133.7 million for the quarter, equal to $1.98 diluted EPS; adjusted net income $132.9 million, or $1.97 adjusted diluted EPS.
  • SG&A Expenses -- 23.7% of sales, rising by $21 million or 70 basis points, with new store growth comprising 115 basis points and technology investments 20 basis points.
  • Inventory -- $1.5 billion at quarter-end, up 15%; per-store inventory dollars up 6.3% with flat units.
  • Cash and Free Cash Flow -- $330 million cash (up 14%), $263 million adjusted free cash flow generated, with $234 million returned to shareholders: $35 million via dividends and $199 million through share repurchases at an average $50.62 per share.
  • Dividend Policy -- Board approved a 15% dividend increase to $0.15 per share, payable April 10, 2026.
  • Store Growth -- 24 new stores opened; plans for 20 to 25 new stores in 2026, primarily infill within existing markets.
  • .com Sales Growth -- E-commerce sales grew 13.6%, with continued investments in AI capabilities and platform enhancements.
  • RFID Deployment -- RFID scanners now in all stores, covering approximately 25% of annual volume, improving in-stock rates company-wide by 500 basis points.
  • Loyalty Program -- My Academy Rewards surpassed 13 million members, with major program relaunch and integration with credit cards planned for Q2 2026.
  • Customer Segment Shift -- Household incomes over $100,000 now represent the largest and fastest-growing customer group, with 10% growth year over year in this cohort.
  • Guidance: 2026 Sales -- Full-year net sales expected at $6.18 billion to $6.36 billion, up 2%-5%, with comparable sales guidance of negative 1% to positive 2%.
  • 2026 Gross Margin and EPS Guidance -- Gross margin projected at 34.5%-35%, with GAAP diluted EPS of $5.65-$6.15 and adjusted diluted EPS of $6.10-$6.60, based on 67 million diluted shares.
  • CapEx and Free Cash Flow Outlook -- Capital expenditures budgeted at $200 million-$240 million, with adjusted free cash flow targeted at $250 million-$300 million.
  • Sales by Category -- Fourth quarter saw strength in bikes, fishing, outdoor cooking, apparel, electronics, and athletic footwear; softness in seasonal footwear, outerwear, drinkware, ride-ons, and ammunition.
  • Brand Initiatives -- Expansion of Nike and Jordan brands contributed high single-digit growth; Jordan Brand Shop rollout to expand to over 200 stores in 2026.
  • AI and Digital Initiatives -- Implementation of agentic AI for search and personalization (Scout assistant), additional AI platform partnerships, and more drop-ship partnerships to expand online assortment.
  • Macroeconomic Assumptions -- Guidance midpoint supported by internal initiatives; top and bottom ends of guidance reflect varying degrees of consumer pressure offset or aided by external events (tax refunds, World Cup, 250th US anniversary).

RISKS

  • Carl Ford stated, "I am seeing credit card delinquencies at double what they were at the end of 2024. I feel job growth in America is not going to be strong in 2026. I think that gas staying high—we are just really conscious of a headwind associated with financial health."
  • Guidance explicitly factors in ongoing discretionary consumer spending weakness and persistent inflationary pressure, especially in the first half, as negative drivers.
  • Management cited tariffs and "any prolonged impact to gas prices" as potential negative impacts on results.
  • Lower-income consumer segment is experiencing continuing high single-digit traffic declines, attributed to financial pressure and trading down.

SUMMARY

Academy Sports and Outdoors (ASO +1.24%) delivered top-line sales growth and marked its first annual market share increase since 2021, underpinned by new store performance, e-commerce expansion, and successful strategic investments including RFID and advanced analytics. Management disclosed quarter-to-date positive comparable sales into early March and emphasized ongoing growth in higher-income customer segments alongside persistent declines among lower-income shoppers. Initiatives like the loyalty program relaunch, expanded premium brand presence, and digital modernization are expected to provide measurable revenue and margin tailwinds, while external events—including the World Cup and increased tax refunds—offer additional but comparatively modest upside to guidance.

  • SG&A leverage is expected from normalized unit growth and completion of prior-year launch costs, with further efficiencies targeted through automation and AI initiatives.
  • The expected shape of fiscal 2026 is strongest in Q1, with back-half weighted store openings due to lease pauses last year, and comp growth bolstered by new credit card and loyalty program launches.
  • E-commerce investments have yielded session share gains and are projected to drive continued growth as further agentic AI and marketplace integrations roll out.
  • Inventory build reflects higher per-store dollar value, flat units, and strategic forward-buying to mitigate tariffs, positioning stores for better in-stocks and conversion rates.
  • Segment-level softness in the quarter was primarily in ammunition and seasonal categories, partly attributed to lapping elevated prior-year results and changing macro conditions.
  • Gross margin improvement and capital allocation strategy position the company to sustain cash returns to shareholders while self-funding investments in omnichannel and store growth.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RFID: Radio frequency identification technology used to track inventory at the item level, supporting accurate, real-time store counts and improved stock accuracy.
  • AUR: Average Unit Retail; calculated as total sales dollars divided by units sold, used to measure achieved pricing and product mix.
  • Agentic AI: Artificial intelligence agents that autonomously interface with users (e.g., "Scout" assistant) to enhance search, recommendation, and personalization on e-commerce platforms.
  • Drop-ship partnerships: Vendor arrangements enabling fulfillment of customer orders directly from third-party inventory, expanding the range of available products online without increasing owned inventory.
  • Comp base / comp store sales: Comparable sales growth metric based on stores open for at least 14 months, excluding new locations to reflect underlying store performance.
  • Shop doors: Stores featuring branded shop-in-shop presentations (e.g., Jordan Brand Shop) to showcase select vendor merchandise in dedicated in-store spaces.
