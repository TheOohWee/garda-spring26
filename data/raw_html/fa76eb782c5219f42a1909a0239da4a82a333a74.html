Marzetti (MZTI) Q2 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 3, 2026 at 11:00AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Feb. 3, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — David A. Ciesinski
  • Chief Financial Officer — Thomas K. Pigott

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated Net Sales -- $518 million, up 1.7%; adjusted net sales (excluding temporary supply agreement) increased 0.1% to $510 million.
  • Gross Profit -- $137 million, a 3.4% increase, setting a second-quarter record.
  • Reported Gross Margin -- Expanded by 40 basis points; adjusted gross margin grew 80 basis points, excluding non-core sales.
  • Retail Segment Net Sales -- Decreased 1.1%; prior quarter had 6.3% growth, with decrease attributed to softer demand during the government shutdown.
  • New York Bakery Frozen Garlic Bread Sales -- Grew 8.4%, gaining 300 basis points of market share for a total of 44.6% in category.
  • Sister Schubert's and Texas Roadhouse Frozen Dinner Roll Sales -- Combined growth of 7.1%, lifting market share by 40 basis points to 60.8%.
  • Licensed Chick-fil-A Sauces -- Sales up 6.7%, with 13 basis points increase in share, supported by expanded club channel distribution.
  • Marzetti Produce Dips -- Sales rose 0.3%, boosting market share by 130 basis points to 75.5%.
  • Adjusted Foodservice Net Sales -- Up 1.6%; foodservice volume in pounds shipped declined 0.4%.
  • Pricing Impact -- Net pricing added approximately 140 basis points; core volume and mix reduced results by 130 basis points.
  • Temporary Supply Agreement (TSA) Sales -- $8.2 million, or 160 basis points of total growth; TSA sales expected to end in the following quarter.
  • Selling, General, and Administrative Expenses (SG&A) -- Increased by $3.3 million, or 5.8%, mainly due to higher marketing spend and expanded Texas Roadhouse launch.
  • Restructuring and Impairment Charges -- Recorded at $1.7 million, driven by manufacturing asset impairment in foodservice and Lopitas, California facility closure plans.
  • Reported Operating Income -- Decreased $500,000, with gross profit growth offset by higher SG&A and impairment charges.
  • Diluted Earnings Per Share -- Increased by $0.37, or 20.8%, to $2.15; prior year included a $0.39 pension settlement charge and $0.05 acquisition-related cost, while current quarter includes $0.05 restructuring and impairment cost.
  • Quarterly Dividend -- $1 per share on December 31, a 5% increase, marking sixty-three consecutive annual rises; $27.6 million paid in dividends.
  • Share Repurchases -- $20.1 million in common stock repurchased during the quarter.
  • Balance Sheet & Cash -- Debt-free with over $201 million in cash at quarter's end.
  • Bachan's Acquisition -- Entered definitive agreement to acquire Bachan's for $400 million cash; cited as accretive to top-line and gross margin beginning year one.

SUMMARY

The Marzetti Company (MZTI 3.00%) disclosed a $400 million cash acquisition of Bachan's, targeting immediate enhancement to sales growth and gross margins as management aligned the deal with its strategic M&A and licensing agenda. Executives highlighted robust expansion in core categories, with New York bakery and Texas Roadhouse products outpacing overall retail sales performance. Management confirmed the company's shift back to a consistent dividend increase approach and an attritional share repurchase strategy given the acquisition, leveraging a strong, debt-free cash position. Bachan's was identified as high-margin, premium-priced, and synergistic with Marzetti's manufacturing supply chain and innovation capabilities. The company called out imminent TSA sales concluding next quarter and expects continued moderate cost inflation, with mitigation planned via pricing and cost savings focus.

  • David A. Ciesinski emphasized, "We see meaningful opportunities to accelerate Bachan's next chapter of growth by leveraging Marzetti's culinary capability, retail relationships, and foodservice partnerships."
  • Thomas K. Pigott clarified TSA (temporary supply agreement) sales are $8.2 million and will end by March 31, 2026, signaling their non-recurring nature in future results.
  • Ciesinski stated Bachan's sales grew "48%" annually from 2022 to 2025, predominantly through Costco and more recently through mass and retail channels.
  • Bachan's currently maintains minimal foodservice presence, which Marzetti plans to expand using existing national account relationships.
  • Ciesinski confirmed the Bachan's product margin is "accretive to our existing retail segment at gross margin."
  • Management projects low single-digit retail revenue growth for the second half, with quarterly cadence expected to remain even despite holiday impacts and club launch timing.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Temporary Supply Agreement (TSA): Short-term sales arrangement for product supply, not recurring in standard core revenue calculations.
  • Co-pack/Co-man: Contract manufacturing in which production is outsourced to third parties instead of in-house facilities.
  • SG&A: Selling, general, and administrative expenses, including marketing, administrative, and other non-production overhead.
  • Adjusted Operating Income: Operating income excluding restructuring, impairment, and certain acquisition-related expenses for clearer core business performance representation.
