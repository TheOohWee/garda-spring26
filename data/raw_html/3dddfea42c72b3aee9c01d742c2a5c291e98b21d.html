TAKEAWAYS

  • Q4 sales -- $1.38 billion, flat compared to the prior year, reflecting a $27 million reduction from the sale of the India commercial vehicle axle business, a $2 million decline from volume and mix, $6 million in pricing gains, and a net positive $38 million from metal market pass-throughs and FX.
  • Full-year 2025 sales -- $5.84 billion, down from $6.12 billion in 2024, primarily due to lower volumes, product mix, and the India business sale, partially offset by favorable FX and metal markets.
  • Q4 adjusted EBITDA -- $169 million, with a margin of 12.2%.
  • Full-year adjusted EBITDA -- $743.2 million, representing a 12.7% margin, a 50 basis point improvement from 2024.
  • Q4 adjusted earnings per share (EPS) -- $0.07 per share.
  • Full-year adjusted EPS -- $0.53, compared to $0.51 in 2024.
  • Q4 adjusted free cash flow -- $70.1 million.
  • Full-year adjusted free cash flow -- $213 million, versus $230 million in 2024.
  • Q4 GAAP net loss -- $75.3 million, or $0.63 per share; full-year loss of $75.3 million, compared to a $13.7 million loss in the prior year.
  • Net interest expense -- $50.8 million for 2025, up from $37.3 million in 2024, driven by new acquisition-related debt.
  • Net debt -- $1.8 billion at year-end, with a net leverage ratio of 2.5x, improved from 2.8x a year prior.
  • 2026 sales guidance -- Targeting $10.3 billion to $10.7 billion, including a partial-year contribution from the acquired businesses.
  • 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- Projected at $1.3 billion to $1.4 billion.
  • 2026 adjusted free cash flow guidance -- Expected at $235 million to $325 million.
  • Expected synergies -- $300 million identified, with $100 million run rate savings by the end of year one, 60% run rate by the end of year two, and the majority achieved by the end of year three.
  • Synergy cost profile -- Anticipated $100 million to $125 million in 2026 for synergy capture; restructuring outlays of $110 million to $150 million, with spend front-weighted over the first two years.
  • JV equity income -- $65 million to $75 million expected to flow through adjusted EBITDA, representing the 50% share of the China JV business.
  • 2026 CapEx estimate -- Forecasted at 4.5%-5% of sales to support major launches.
  • Full-year share count -- Approximately 243 million fully diluted shares expected for 2026.
  • Name change and acquisition -- Legal name changed from American Axle & Manufacturing Holdings to Dauch Corporation (DCH +3.64%) in January 2026; completed acquisition of Dauch Group plc and subsidiaries GKN Automotive and GKN Powder Metallurgy on Feb. 3, 2026.
  • Customer recognition -- Won Scout Motors business for SmartBar, plus electric drive units and rear beam axles; received Cherry Automotive’s Best Supplier Award and multiple GM supplier quality awards.
  • 2026 OEM and regional production assumptions -- North America at 15 million units, Europe at 17 million, China at 33 million, and global production at 93 million units; GM large pickup and SUV production forecasted between 1.3 million and 1.4 million units.
  • Reporting standards note -- Management emphasized material differences between IFRS (legacy Dauch) and US GAAP figures, especially around JV sales and EBITDA, preventing direct sum of line items.
  • Operational efficiency -- Both driveline and metal forming segments showed margin improvement through ongoing efficiency initiatives.

RISKS

  • Net interest expense increased by $13.5 million year over year due to acquisition-related borrowing, raising overall financing costs.
  • Chris May said, "Taking all these sales and cost drivers into account, our GAAP net loss was $75,300,000 or $0.63 per share in the 2025 compared to a net loss of $13,700,000 or $0.12," indicating a substantial increase in annual net loss.
  • Restructuring and synergy integration costs are expected to be "front weighted to the first two years" and will continue into 2027, leading to near-term cash outflows.
  • Management cited a "headwind for metal market and FX, in particular due to the strengthening of the Mexican peso," anticipated to pressure profitability in 2026.

SUMMARY

Dauch Corporation (DCH +3.64%) completed the acquisition of Dauch Group plc, GKN Automotive, and GKN Powder Metallurgy in early February 2026, establishing a larger global presence and diversified product portfolio. Management set 2026 sales targets at $10.3 billion to $10.7 billion and adjusted EBITDA guidance at $1.3 billion to $1.4 billion, reflecting partial-year contributions from acquired businesses and a focus on synergy realization. The company identified $300 million in expected synergies, with $100 million targeted by year-end and the majority realized within three years. Elevated interest expense and significant restructuring and integration costs were attributed to acquisition financing and transformation activities, resulting in a wider net loss for 2025. Management highlighted the importance of accounting differences between IFRS and US GAAP, particularly regarding JV sales and EBITDA, cautioning against direct aggregation of legacy and new entity results.

  • Capital allocation for 2026 includes targeted CapEx of 4.5%-5% of sales to support major launches, especially GM truck programs.
  • Management stated, "Synergy realization is a core priority," and described the establishment of an integration office and accountability frameworks for cost-saving execution across SG&A, purchasing, and operations.
  • Quarterly results are expected to show seasonality in Q1 2026, with management anticipating both revenue and cash flow to be softest in the first quarter due to "meaningful January downtime at key customers" and only a partial quarter of acquired sales.
  • JV equity income from the China business, with nearly $1.5 billion in sales, is expected to contribute $65 million to $75 million to adjusted EBITDA, with large, market-linked dividends forecast by management.
  • Chris May clarified, "you cannot simply add the two figures together, as the differences are significant," regarding legacy Dauch and Dauch Corporation financial statements, due to both accounting standard and joint venture reporting differences.
  • Pension cash contributions are expected at $40 million to $50 million in 2026, mainly related to legacy Dauch plans.
  • Debt at deal closing was projected at approximately $4.2 billion, consistent with management’s previous plans.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • SmartBar: An active stabilizer bar product that dynamically adjusts anti-roll performance in vehicles, enhancing on-road handling and off-road capability.
  • Metal market pass-throughs: Contractual adjustments reflecting changes in raw metal input prices passed directly to customers, impacting reported sales.
  • SG&A: Selling, general, and administrative expenses—a key category for synergy-driven cost reduction.
  • IFRS: International Financial Reporting Standards, used by legacy Dauch, with material differences versus US GAAP in key performance measures.
