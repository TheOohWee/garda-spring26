Robinhood (HOOD) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 10, 2026 at 6:44PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 10, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Vladimir Tenev
  • Chief Financial Officer — Shiv Verma
  • Head of Investor Relations — Chris Koegel

Takeaways

  • Total platform assets -- $324 billion, reflecting nearly 70% growth year over year.
  • Net deposits -- $68 billion for the year, a 35% increase and a company record; $16 billion in Q4, the eighth consecutive quarter above $10 billion.
  • Revenue -- $4.5 billion for the year, rising 52%; Q4 revenue reached a record $1.3 billion, up 27%.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $2.5 billion for the year, increasing 76%; adjusted EBITDA margin at 56%, hitting a new high.
  • Incremental adjusted EBITDA margins -- Exceeded 70% for the third consecutive year.
  • Earnings per share (EPS) -- $2.05 for the fiscal year, marking a record high.
  • Gold subscribers -- 4.2 million, up nearly 60% year over year; Q4 growth was 58% for this subscriber base.
  • Interest-earning assets -- Increased 39% in Q4, driven by growth in the cash sweep program, margin, and the credit card loan book.
  • Product expansion -- Prediction market volumes in 2025 exceeded 12 billion contracts, with over 4 billion contracts traded year-to-date in 2026; Robinhood Gold Card surpassed 600,000 customers, a fivefold increase, totaling $10 billion in annualized spend.
  • Customer asset mix -- Over 40% of total assets now reside in ETFs, advisory, retirement, and cash products.
  • Profitability discipline -- Adjusted operating expense plus stock-based compensation in Q4 was $597 million, approximately $15 million below the most recent outlook.
  • Expense outlook -- For the coming year, adjusted opex plus SBC is expected between $2.6 billion and $2.725 billion, representing 18% year-over-year growth at the midpoint.
  • Business diversification -- Eleven businesses now generate more than $100 million in annualized revenue, with several more near that threshold.
  • Share repurchase program -- Over $1 billion authorized for share buybacks, intended for use during periods of market volatility.
  • AI operational efficiency -- More than 75% of customer service cases resolved by AI; internal AI-driven productivity savings estimated in the nine-figure range for 2025.
  • International users -- Over 750,000 customers outside the US; recent rollout included ISAs in the UK and expansion into Indonesia.
  • Prediction markets run rate -- Business now above a $300 million annualized run rate in its first full year of operation.
  • Bitstamp integration -- Volumes doubled since the June acquisition; institutional and retail adoption both addressed in Q4 strategy.
  • Banking rollout -- Over 25,000 funded customers with more than $400 million in balances; over 50% of banking customers enrolled in direct deposit.
  • Expense growth strategy -- More than half of projected expense growth allocated to new and scaling businesses, prioritizing product velocity and innovation.

Risks

  • Shiv Verma said, "On the retail side, one big change we made last year is we moved to smart order exchange routing. Which really means we want to be competitive for all types of customers. What we had found out before is for the most competitive customers, we actually need to lower pricing, and so that's what we did, and now we have the volume tiers. And you saw a little bit of this in Q4 where there was less market volatility and the casual trader stepped away, but we saw really strong engagement from the more active traders. And because of that, they are on the higher tiers, and this pricing comes down a little bit. For us, we don't goal the teams on pricing. That's an output metric. We goal them on market share. We are continuing to win market share there. What we'd expect is during periods of lower market volatility, you'll probably see more traders engage, which will bring down their overall take rate. That's what we saw a little bit in Q4." This may indicate margin pressure due to lower pricing for more active traders.
  • Vladimir Tenev stated, "as we have integrated, we have actually seen an increase in market share and increase in revenues and business performance, which I think is relatively rare for acquisitions." While positive, the comment highlights typical acquisition risks and integration challenges that may impact future results.
  • Management acknowledged that launching additional prediction markets related to company fundamentals "need some regulatory relief," indicating potential regulatory hurdles to further product expansion.
  • Shiv Verma noted, "those are also good investments. Your question on can we stay nimble, absolutely. We are." This signals possible exposure to risk from fixed costs if conditions change or revenue does not meet growth expectations.

Summary

Robinhood Markets (HOOD 1.33%) reported significant acceleration in top-line growth, customer engagement, and platform diversification, underpinned by an expanding suite of active trading, advisory, and banking products. Management emphasized aggressive international expansion, product innovation—including AI-driven features, prediction markets, and tokenization—and the contribution of new business lines, now exceeding $100 million each in annualized revenues, to the platform’s evolving revenue mix. The company previewed substantial capital allocation into scaling novel offerings like Robinhood Social, family investing, private markets access, and DeFi integration while formally committing to sustaining profitable growth and operational discipline in 2026 amid an 18% targeted expense increase.

  • Tenev said, "Olympics are going on right now. World Cup coming in the summer. Continued growth in the non-sports categories, and of course, our Rothera, which is our JV with Susquehanna coming online," highlighting event-driven upside potential for the prediction markets business.
  • Growth in Robinhood Gold Card was driven by customer adoption of features such as "single-use cards and all the family features," establishing a foundation for future cross-selling and engagement beyond trading.
  • Tenev shared, "We now have 2,000 stock tokens available to customers in Europe," and forecasted the introduction of "permissionless twenty-four-seven tradable public company stock tokens" to reinforce the company's tokenization leadership abroad.
  • AI assistant Cortex and Robinhood Social products are nearing rollout, both seen as central to customer acquisition and retention by enhancing discovery, personalization, and streamlined trading experiences.
  • Direct deposit adoption for Robinhood Banking exceeded 50% among funded customers—an early indicator of product-market fit and deeper wallet share.
  • Shiv Verma confirmed underwriting all new investments to standalone ROIs and long-term scalability, noting, "It used to be, hey, a $10 million business moves the needle. Now it's can this be a $100 million business in a few years?"

Industry glossary

  • Prediction markets: Exchange markets offering contracts based on outcomes of real-world events, including sports, political, financial, or other occurrences, tradable on the Robinhood Markets platform.
  • Rothera: Joint venture between Robinhood Markets, Susquehanna, and Mayex, providing an internal prediction markets exchange with full monetization and vertical control.
  • Cortex: Robinhood Markets' proprietary AI assistant, integrated across the mobile app and Legend product, designed to augment trading, discovery, and advisory functions for customers and advisors.
  • Legend: Robinhood Markets' advanced trading platform targeting active traders, incorporating AI/automation features.
  • Robinhood Gold Card: Robinhood Markets’ proprietary credit card product offering 3% rewards and a suite of premium features to deepen ecosystem engagement.
  • Robinhood Ventures: Initiative to provide customers access to private markets and alternative asset classes via registered funds.
  • Tokenization: Conversion of real or financial assets into digital tokens, allowing trading, settlement, and custody on blockchain or DeFi infrastructure.
  • Bitstamp: European-based crypto exchange acquired by Robinhood Markets, servicing institutional and retail cryptocurrency trading.
  • DeFi: Decentralized finance—blockchain-based financial services removing intermediaries, underpinning initiatives like Robinhood Chain and stock token trading.
  • TradePMR: Source of adviser/referral program infrastructure and part of Robinhood Markets’ toolkit to connect customers with registered investment advisers (RIAs).
  • Robinhood Chain: Robinhood Markets’ proprietary layer-two blockchain focused on trading tokenized real-world assets and offering DeFi applications.
