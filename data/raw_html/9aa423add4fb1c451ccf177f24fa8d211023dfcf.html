Lisata (LSTA) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 9, 2025 at 12:19AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Nov. 6, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Dr. David J. Mazzo
  • Executive Vice President of Research and Development and Chief Medical Officer — Dr. Kristen K. Buck
  • Senior Vice President of Finance and Treasury and Chief Accounting Officer — James Nisco

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Operating Expenses -- $4.4 million, down $900,000 or 17.3% compared to $5.3 million for the same period in the prior year, due primarily to reduced R&D and general administrative spending.
  • Research and Development Expenses -- $2 million, a $600,000 or 22.9% decrease from $2.5 million in the comparable quarter, attributed to lower chemistry, manufacturing, and controls costs and reduced clinical department spend, partially offset by increased BOLTAR trial costs due to a prior year CRO refund.
  • General and Administrative Expenses -- $2.5 million, a decrease of $300,000 or 12.1% from $2.8 million for the same quarter last year, mainly due to lower consulting costs and elimination of a position.
  • Net Loss -- $4.2 million, reduced from $4.9 million for the corresponding period last year.
  • Cash and Cash Equivalents -- $19 million as of quarter-end, with management projecting a runway into 2027 for current operations and clinical activities, excluding expenditure for a Phase III ASCEND trial.
  • ASCEND Trial Development -- Preliminary Cohort B data exhibited a positive trend in progression-free survival, overall survival, and objective response rate for cerdepatide plus standard of care compared to placebo; a complete response was observed only in the treatment group.
  • ASCEND Safety -- Pooled analysis from both cohorts A and B indicated “no increased adverse events in the cerdepatide plus standard of care treated group beyond those experienced in the standard of care plus placebo-treated groups.”
  • ASCEND Regulatory Progress -- A completed end-of-Phase II meeting with FDA led to agreement on “the fundamental aspects of an acceptable global Phase III protocol including dose and dosing regimen, blinding, and primary endpoints.” Management confirmed readiness for Phase III subject to available capital.
  • BOLTAR Trial Update -- Enrollment for the first-line cholangiocarcinoma arm finished nearly six months ahead of plan; total enrollment in the second-line cohort was capped at 20 patients for efficiency, with this exploratory arm not statistically powered.
  • SENDFOX Trial Data -- Preliminary results in the pancreatic cancer cohort showed a 50% R0 resection rate and a 70% pathological partial response among patients completing therapy, with a reported 60% two-year overall survival rate.
  • IELISTA Trial Status -- Enrollment is complete, and interim analysis revealed “compelling positive preliminary data” for cerdepatide in combination with immunotherapy, with final outcomes expected in 2026.
  • GBM Trial Progress -- Enrollment is approximately two-thirds complete toward the 30-patient target, proceeding on schedule.
  • Chilu Licensee Update -- Development partner Chilu completed patient enrollment (90 subjects) in metastatic pancreatic cancer; initial data is anticipated in the next 12 to 18 months.
  • Catalent Licensing Agreement -- A nonexclusive global license was granted to Catalent (NYSE:CTLT) for use of cerdepatide and its analogs as payloads for the SmartTag ADC platform; Catalent’s preclinical results demonstrated enhanced tumor-selective penetration and efficacy.
  • Strategic Alliance with GATC Health -- Entered a collaboration to leverage AI-powered drug discovery for new indications and novel compounds, with several candidates targeted for clinical development in 2026.
  • Patent Extension -- The U.S. Patent and Trademark Office issued a new composition of matter patent for cerdepatide in July 2025, providing protection through March 2040 with potential extensibility.

SUMMARY

Lisata Therapeutics (NASDAQ:LSTA) reported material progress in both clinical and corporate initiatives, including a pronounced reduction in operating expenditures and strengthened capital position. Management advanced multiple cerdepatide clinical programs with new trial data indicating clinical benefit and maintained positive safety profiles across diverse oncology indications. Strategic alliances with Catalent and GATC Health, as well as an extended patent estate, reinforce near-term opportunities for product, platform, and non-dilutive capital expansion. The company articulated a clear regulatory and operational pathway for a Phase III ASCEND trial, though noted this is contingent on securing additional funding, with management actively seeking partnering solutions.

  • The financial runway extends into 2027, but no Phase III ASCEND expenditures are included in this projection, as acknowledged by management.
  • Management said, “The original trial was designed with an academic bench rather than one with commercial and regulatory objectives and was statistically powered based on a six-month progression-free survival primary endpoint. This endpoint is unusual in that it has not been previously used as the basis of approval for an anticancer drug.”
  • Lisata’s leadership emphasized direct engagement with potential licensing partners, citing recent Catalent and Chilu agreements as cornerstones for continued business development efforts.
  • Dr. Mazzo clarified that “in this current financing environment, it's going to be next to impossible to raise the amount of money necessary to unilaterally fund that phase three in a manner that would not be so overly dilutive to current shareholders,” underscoring reliance on strategic investment or partnering to execute next-phase trials.
  • Several ongoing and near-readout investigator-initiated studies may introduce future variability in timelines, with final ASCEND, IELISTA, and Chilu metastatic pancreatic cancer study data all targeted for 2026 or beyond.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • CD34+ cell therapy: A regenerative treatment utilizing hematopoietic stem cells expressing the CD34 marker, aimed at tissue repair and disease reversal.
  • CRO (Contract Research Organization): An external service provider managing clinical trial operations, data management, and protocol execution.
  • R0 resection: Surgical removal of a tumor with negative margins, indicating no microscopic residual cancer at the cut edge.
  • RECIST (Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumors): A standardized measurement framework for tumor response in clinical trials, based on changes in tumor size.
  • SmartTag ADC (Antibody-Drug Conjugate): Catalent’s targeted drug platform enabling tissue-selective delivery of cytotoxic agents via engineered antibodies tagged with customizable payloads.
  • PD-1/PD-L1: Immune checkpoint proteins targeted by immunotherapies to enhance anti-tumor immune responses.

As mentioned on previous quarterly calls, Lisata Therapeutics is focused on the development of its proprietary cyclic internalizing RGD peptide product candidate, cerdepatide, for the treatment of advanced solid tumors and other difficult-to-treat diseases. Cerdepatide is designed to activate a novel uptake pathway that allows co-administered or tethered anticancer drugs to selectively target and penetrate solid tumors more effectively. In addition, cerdepatide has been shown to modify the tumor microenvironment, making it less immunosuppressive and therefore increasing the tumor's susceptibility to immunotherapy and our body's own immune system while also inhibiting the metastatic cascade.

If you'd like more information regarding cerdepatide's mechanism of action, we encourage you to visit our website where you'll find an animated video and relevant slides within our corporate presentation. On the regulatory front, cerdepatide has secured multiple special designations from both the U.S. FDA and EMA, all of which are also listed on our website and in the corporate presentation for your easy reference. Now for an update on our individual development programs. The ASCEND trial is a Phase 2b 158 patient, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled clinical trial evaluating cerdepatide in combination with standard of care gemcitabine and nab-paclitaxel chemotherapy in patients with metastatic pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma, or M-DAC.

Patient recruitment was conducted at 25 sites in Australia and New Zealand and is sponsored by the Australasian Gastrointestinal Clinical Trials Group or AGITG in collaboration with the National Health and Medical Research Council Clinical Trial Center at the University of Sydney. As we mentioned in the past, this investigator-initiated trial was inherited upon our acquisition of Send Therapeutics. The original trial was designed with an academic bench rather than one with commercial and regulatory objectives and was statistically powered based on a six-month progression-free survival primary endpoint. This endpoint is unusual in that it has not been previously used as the basis of approval for an anticancer drug.

After the acquisition, we collaborated with the AGITG to modify the trial to ensure it measured clinical endpoints that would best support the next steps in the development of cerdepatide from a regulatory perspective. To that end, the ASCEND protocol was amended to include another cohort of patients, Cohort B, not statistically powered to evaluate an additional cerdepatide dosing regimen. The ASCEND protocol was also amended to capture overall survival outcomes for both cohorts A and B as overall survival is considered by regulatory authorities to be the gold standard endpoint in pancreatic cancer trials. Since the ASCEND protocol was amended following trial initiation, data from Cohort B are being analyzed sequentially following Cohort A data.

Cohort A with 95 patients receiving a single intravenous dose of cerdepatide or placebo in combination with standard of care completed enrollment in 2023. As announced in January, preliminary Cohort A data was presented at the 2025 ASCO GI Symposium which showed a positive trend in overall survival in the cerdepatide plus standard of care chemotherapy group, including four complete responses in this group compared to none in the standard of care plus placebo-treated group. Most recently, preliminary data from Cohort B with 63 patients receiving two intravenous doses of cerdepatide 3.2 mg/kg or placebo, administered four hours apart in combination with standard of care chemotherapy were presented at the ESMO Gastrointestinal Cancers Congress on July 2, 2025.

These data showed a positive trend in progression-free survival, overall survival, and objective response rate in the cerdepatide plus standard of care treatment group compared to the standard of care plus placebo-treated group. Another complete response was also noted in the cerdepatide plus standard of care treated group while none were observed in the standard of care plus placebo group. Additionally, pooled data from both cohorts A and B which was presented at the ESMO Congress on October 20, 2025, further corroborated previous findings and indicated no increased adverse events in the cerdepatide plus standard of care treated group beyond those experienced in the standard of care plus placebo-treated groups.

Final data and conclusions from both cohorts are anticipated for 2026. As we've previously announced, we have completed an end of Phase II meeting with the FDA and in conjunction with other correspondence with the agency, have an agreement on the fundamental aspects of an acceptable global Phase III protocol including dose and dosing regimen, blinding, and primary endpoints. Subsequently, we have begun preparation for Phase III trial initiation as contingent upon available capital and anticipated capital acquisition. The BOLTAR trial is our Phase 2a double-blind placebo-controlled multicenter randomized trial in the United States evaluating cerdepatide in combination with standard of care chemotherapy and immunotherapy in first and second-line cholangiocarcinoma on top of standard of care.

Enrollment was completed in the first-line cholangiocarcinoma arm nearly six months ahead of plan, accelerating anticipated top-line data readout to 2025. Based on encouragement from multiple investigators involved in the trial, a second cohort was added evaluating cerdepatide in subjects in second-line cholangiocarcinoma. Although originally planned to recruit 40 patients, enrollment was ultimately capped at approximately 20 patients to allow for quicker data analysis and a more efficient use of our capital. It should be noted that this exploratory trial is not statistically powered. However, the reduced number of patients in the second-line arm should be sufficient to determine if there is a treatment effect.

The SENDFOX trial is a Phase IbIIa open-label trial in the States evaluating cerdepatide in combination with neoadjuvant fulphuranox-based therapies in pancreatic, colon, and appendiceal cancers. In December 2024, the company announced enrollment completion in all three cohorts. This single-center study being conducted at the University of Kansas Cancer Center was designed with a three-cycle run-in period to ensure patients met specific criteria before receiving treatment. Of the 66 patients enrolled, 50 met these criteria and were treated with cerdepatide across three cohorts, including 24 with resectable or borderline resectable pancreatic cancer, 15 with high-grade colon or appendiceal cancers with peritoneal metastases, and 11 with oligometastatic colon cancer.

Preliminary data from the pancreatic cancer cohort presented at the AACR Special Conference in September 2025, showed that the combination of cerdepatide with fulphuranox was safe and feasible. In the ten patients who completed the therapy and underwent surgery, treatment resulted in a 50% R0 resection rate and a 70% pathological partial response alongside promising early survival data, including a 60% two-year overall survival rate. Importantly, the combination therapy appears to transform tumors from immuno-cold to immuno-hot by enhancing immune cell infiltration and increasing markers like PD-1 and PD-L1 which could significantly improve the effect of subsequent immunotherapies. Additional data from the SENDFOX trial are anticipated in the coming months.

The trial is funded by the University of Kansas Cancer Center and Lisata Therapeutics is supplying cerdepatide. Chiso Pharmaceutical, the licensee of cerdepatide in the Greater China territory, is running a parallel development program for cerdepatide in combination with gemcitabine and nab-paclitaxel as a treatment for metastatic pancreatic cancer. Chilu previously reported that they completed enrollment in the study of 90 subjects. However, we still await data from the study to be shared. Also, according to guidance last received from Chilu, data are expected in the next twelve to eighteen months and we anticipate ongoing dialogue to continue related to potential next steps in their development plan.

The IELISTA trial is a Phase 1b2a randomized placebo-controlled three-arm single-blind single-center trial evaluating the safety, early efficacy, pharmacodynamics of cerdepatide in patients with locally advanced non-resectable pancreatic cancer. The trial is being conducted in Australia in collaboration with AstraZeneca, and the funding sponsor WARP nine, combining cerdepatide with the checkpoint inhibitor durvalumab both standard of care gemcitabine and nab-paclitaxel chemotherapy versus cerdepatide in combination with standard of care chemotherapy without durvalumab versus standard of care alone. As recently announced, enrollment in the IELISTA study is complete. An updated interim analysis presented at ESMO GI Congress on July 3, 2025, revealed compelling positive preliminary data for cerdepatide.

These data are consistent with earlier findings from the 2025 ASCO GI meeting further reinforcing cerdepatide's potential to significantly enhance immunotherapy effectiveness by provoking significant RECIST responses improving overall response and disease control rates. Final data and key findings from this study are anticipated in 2026. A study of cerdepatide in combination with temozolomide in patients with glioblastoma multiforme, or GBM, brain cancer has been initiated with many patients already enrolled and treated. The study is designed as a Phase 2a double-blind placebo-controlled randomized proof of concept study evaluating cerdepatide when added to standard of care temozolomide versus temozolomide alone and matching placebo in subjects with newly diagnosed GBM.

This actively enrolling study is being conducted across multiple sites in Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania. The study is targeted to enroll 30 patients with a randomization of two to one plus standard of care versus placebo plus standard of care.
