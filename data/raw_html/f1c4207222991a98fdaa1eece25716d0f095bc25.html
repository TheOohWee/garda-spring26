TKO Group (TKO) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 5, 2025 at 7:05PM EST

Takeaways

Revenue -- $1.12 billion in revenue for Q3 2025, reflecting a 27% decrease on a reported basis, primarily driven by the absence of On Location revenue from the 2024 Paris Olympics.

Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA was $360 million, representing a 59% increase on a reported basis compared to the prior year period, with adjusted EBITDA margin rising to 32% from 15% in the prior year period.

UFC segment revenue -- $325 million in revenue for UFC in Q3 2025, down 8% compared to the prior year period, due to one fewer numbered event and timing/mix effects.

UFC segment adjusted EBITDA -- $166 million in adjusted EBITDA for UFC in Q3 2025, a 15% decline compared to the prior year period, with adjusted EBITDA margin at 51% versus 55% in the prior year period.

WWE segment revenue -- $402 million in revenue for the WWE segment in Q3 2025, up 23% compared to the prior year period, attributed to increased live event nights, ticket sales, site fees, and new event launches.

WWE segment adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA was $208 million, up 19% compared to the prior year period, with adjusted EBITDA margin decreasing to 52% from 54%, largely attributable to strategic investments in talent.

IMG segment revenue -- $337 million in revenue for the IMG segment in Q3 2025, a 59% decrease compared to the prior year period, reflecting the lack of Olympics revenue; offset by uplift from studio projects such as the Ryder Cup.

IMG segment adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA was $61 million, an improvement of $116 million from the prior year period, with adjusted EBITDA margin at 18% compared to negative 7% in the prior year period.

Corporate and other revenue -- $63 million in revenue for Corporate and Other in Q3 2025, a 17% increase compared to the prior year period, primarily from boxing management fees and promotional activity associated with the Canelo versus Crawford event.

Free cash flow -- $399 million in free cash flow generated in Q3 2025, with 111% free cash flow conversion of adjusted EBITDA, aided by the timing of boxing event cash receipts.

Media rights agreements -- UFC secured a seven-year, $7.7 billion deal with Paramount starting in 2026, doubling the average annual value of its prior U.S. agreement.

WWE media partnership -- WWE executed a five-year premium live events deal with ESPN, starting early with WrestlePalooza, and delivering more than 1.8 times value uplift versus the prior deal.

Stock buyback and dividend -- Launched a $1 billion buyback, with $800 million repurchased via an accelerated program, plus a 1% dividend increase paid in September, totaling approximately $150 million for the quarterly cash dividend paid on September 30.

Zufa Boxing JV and events -- Joint venture officially launches in 2026; management reported a landmark Paramount rights deal for Zufa Boxing in the U.S., Canada, and Latin America, and outlined $10 million service fees for each super fight in 2026, targeting two to four annually.

Full-year guidance raised -- Management increased full-year 2025 revenue guidance to $4.69-$4.72 billion and adjusted EBITDA to $1.57-$1.58 billion, primarily from UFC and WWE outperformance and early WWE-ESPN deal timing.

Summary

TKO Group Holdings (TKO +0.27%) reported a sharp contraction in total revenue on a reported basis for Q3 2025, principally due to the exclusion of loss-generating Olympics-related On Location business, which also drove a substantial increase in both adjusted EBITDA and margins. Management emphasized the long-term visibility from newly signed UFC and WWE media rights agreements, highlighting multi-year high-margin, escalator-driven revenue streams commencing in 2026. The company highlighted record attendance and revenue at key UFC and WWE events, launch of the Zufa Boxing joint venture with a key media rights agreement, and step-up in global partnership revenue targets. Shareholder returns advanced through a substantial buyback and increased dividend, funded via operating cash flow and a $1 billion term loan add-on.

Management described the transition to broader distribution through Paramount and ESPN as a lever to boost reach and high-margin partnership revenue, with expectations to achieve $450 million in partnership revenue in 2025, and longer-term company-wide targets of $1 billion by 2030.

Mark S. Shapiro said, "busy on the execution story and boxing. But if something else comes to market, that has scale, reach, significant demand, and we see as having upside opportunity, we will jump into an exploration of that."

The company is building out a dedicated team for site fee negotiations, with more open calendar flexibility expected to accelerate site fee and municipal incentive growth in 2026.

Lawrence Epstein stated, "there's going to be an increase in fighter pay, there's no doubt about that. But we feel like it's going to be in line with what be consistent with the margins that we've maintained over the last several years."

Industry glossary

PLE (Premium Live Event): Flagship WWE events broadcast directly to audiences as scheduled, high-profile productions driving ticket, sponsorship, and media rights revenue.

ASR (Accelerated Share Repurchase): A stock buyback arrangement in which a company repurchases shares rapidly, often from a financial intermediary, using an upfront payment in exchange for immediate share delivery and eventual price averaging.

Site fees: Monetary or in-kind incentives paid by host cities, municipalities, or venues to secure major live sporting or entertainment events.

Equity method investment: An accounting method in which a company recognizes its share of earnings or losses from joint ventures or affiliates, rather than reporting consolidated results.

Free cash flow conversion: The ratio of free cash flow to a company's adjusted EBITDA, indicating efficiency in turning operating profit into cash.
