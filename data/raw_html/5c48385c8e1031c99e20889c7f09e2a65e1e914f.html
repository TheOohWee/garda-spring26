Cognex (CGNX) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 6:13PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Oct. 30, 2025, 9:26 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Matt Moschner
  • Chief Financial Officer — Dennis Fehr

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $277 million, representing 18% year-over-year growth, or 16% in constant currency.
  • Revenue growth (underlying, constant currency) -- 13% increase when excluding the $30 million contribution from the commercial partnership and a $5 million Moritex benefit in the prior year quarter.
  • Americas revenue -- Grew 27% year-over-year in constant currency, led by logistics and the onetime commercial partnership.
  • Europe revenue -- Increased 24% year-over-year, aided by consumer electronics order shifts from China to Europe.
  • Greater China revenue -- Rose 9% year-over-year, with adjusted growth described as "very strong" outside automotive.
  • Other Asia revenue -- Declined 5%, but grew 4% after adjusting for prior-year Moritex impact.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin -- 22.1%, increasing 450 basis points year-over-year to the highest level since Q2 2023 (excluding the onetime commercial partnership benefit); adjusted EBITDA margin expanded 730 basis points including the onetime commercial partnership benefit.
  • Adjusted operating expenses -- Increased 1% year-over-year; declined 1% in constant currency, reflecting cost discipline and reorganization charges of $3 million excluded from this metric.
  • GAAP diluted EPS -- $0.10, down 39% due to a onetime $33 million discrete tax expense related to the One Big Beautiful Bill Act.
  • Adjusted diluted EPS -- $0.33, up by $0.13 or 69% year-over-year on an as-reported basis; $0.28 when adjusting for the onetime items, rising 47% year-over-year.
  • Trailing 12-month free cash flow -- Reached $214 million, an increase of 132% over the prior 12-month period, with a conversion rate of 133%.
  • Capital return -- $37 million returned to shareholders through buybacks and dividends during the quarter; $224 million returned over 12 months, exceeding free cash flow generated.
  • Net cash & investments -- $600 million at quarter-end, supporting future M&A possibilities and continued shareholder returns.
  • Q4 2025 guidance -- Revenue expected between $230 million and $245 million, with a midpoint implying 3% year-over-year growth and a seasonal sequential decline driven by consumer electronics.
  • Q4 adjusted EBITDA margin guidance -- Expected in the 17%-20% range, aligning with prior-year levels at the midpoint.
  • Q4 adjusted EPS guidance -- Projected between $0.19 and $0.24, with the midpoint equaling about 7.5% year-over-year growth.
  • Operational efficiency -- Cash conversion cycle declined sequentially for the sixth straight quarter.
  • AI strategy development -- Launched SLX product line focused on logistics, targeting applications such as object classification and side-by-side detection to expand into higher-value vision applications.
  • Market commentary: Consumer electronics -- Significant revenue growth driven by supply chain diversification and new device form factors, with 2025 expected as the first growth year since 2022.
  • Market commentary: Automotive -- Revenue continued to contract, but year-over-year declines moderated and management indicated the market may be nearing its bottom.
  • Market commentary: Packaging -- Solid revenue growth across geographies, with continued emphasis on capturing opportunities through sales coverage and new products.
  • Market commentary: Logistics -- Seventh consecutive quarter of double-digit year-over-year growth, mainly from automation in existing facilities.
  • Market commentary: Semiconductor -- Modest revenue increase on a difficult comparison, with a cautious near-term outlook but expectation of AI-related demand fueling long-term growth.

Summary

The quarter featured revenue and adjusted EBITDA margin at the top end of expectations, driven by broad-based growth across consumer electronics, logistics, and packaging. The launch of the SLX product line positions Cognex (CGNX 0.93%) to address expanding AI-driven logistics applications, signaling an evolution toward higher-value vision solutions. Management executed cost optimization, maintaining adjusted operating expenses growth at or below the pace of revenue expansion, and reinforced a disciplined capital return approach while preserving M&A flexibility. Forward guidance for the next quarter anticipates a typical seasonal step-down but maintains modest year-over-year growth, with no material negative effects from tariffs assumed as of the call date. Strategic clarity was reinforced through programmatic efficiency initiatives, technology-driven market penetration, and a clear framework for navigating early-cycle macroeconomic conditions.

  • Matt Moschner said, "we are making meaningful progress against our strategic objectives," referencing sales force improvements, CRM tools, and broadening into underpenetrated verticals such as packaging with AI-enabled products.
  • Purolator's adoption of SLX reduced processing costs and enabled scalable deployment across its logistics network.
  • Adjustments for comparative analysis were explicitly disclosed, with Dennis Fehr confirming, "Revenue growth, excluding the impact of both the commercial partnership and the additional months of Moritex a year ago was 13% on a constant currency basis."
  • Cost management included $3 million of reorganization charges, with underlying cost control cited as the combination of revenue growth and adjusted OpEx reduction for three consecutive quarters.
  • Operational initiatives delivered trailing 12-month free cash flow conversion at 133%, marking the fourth straight quarter above the 100% benchmark.
  • The company maintains a high threshold for M&A activity despite ample liquidity and signaled patience should strategic or financial criteria not be met.
  • The OneVision platform, announced in June, remains in limited release, with broader launch targeted in the first half of next year and positive early customer feedback reported.
  • Management stated current industrial macro indicators, like PMI measurements of 48-51, characterize the environment as early cycle, resulting in expectations for moderate growth rates in both 2025 and 2026, excluding onetime partnership benefits.
  • Adjusted gross margin was 67.7%, 170 basis points lower year-over-year, due to unfavorable mix and tariffs.
  • The commercial partnership in medical lab automation contributed a onetime $30 million in revenue this quarter, clarifying its discrete, nonrecurring nature.

Industry glossary

  • Adjusted EBITDA margin: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for discrete items, expressed as a percentage of revenue.
  • SLX: Newly launched AI-enabled logistics solutions experience product line focused on automating object classification and side-by-side detection within logistics environments.
  • Free cash flow conversion: Ratio of free cash flow generated to net income or another earnings metric over a trailing 12-month period, indicating cash generation efficiency.
  • PMI (Purchasing Managers’ Index): Macro indicator measuring the prevailing direction of economic trends in manufacturing, referenced to identify cyclical inflection points.
  • OneVision: Collaborative vision AI platform still in limited release, designed to integrate across embedded systems and drive new industrial vision applications.
