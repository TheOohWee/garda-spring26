Euroseas (ESEA) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 25, 2026 at 11:31AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 25, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — Aristides Pittas
  • Chief Financial Officer — Anastasios Aslidis

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Total net revenues -- $57.4 million, a 7.7% increase driven by higher charter rates, partially offset by a reduced average vessel count.
  • Net income -- $40.5 million for the quarter, including a $9.2 million gain on the sale of MV Marcus V, compared to $24.4 million for the prior year quarter.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $40.7 million, up 24% year over year, attributed to increased revenues from higher average charter rates.
  • Annual dividend -- Raised by 7% to $3.00 per share, resulting in a stated yield of approximately 5% based on the current share price.
  • Share repurchase -- 480,000 common shares bought back (6.8% of outstanding) since May 2022 for $11.4 million; repurchase program renewed in May 2025, and stated intent to continue.
  • Fleet composition -- 21 owned vessels with a carrying capacity of 1,000 TEUs as of year-end; four intermediate vessels under construction will add 18,000 TEUs on delivery in 2027–2028.
  • Charter coverage -- 87% of 2026 voyage days fixed at an average $30,700 daily, 71% coverage for 2027 at $31,900, and 41% of 2028 at $32,400, supporting visibility and earnings stability.
  • Daily operating expenses -- $8,284 per vessel in 2025 versus $7,728 in 2024; increase primarily tied to euro-dollar fluctuations and asset base changes.
  • Contracted cash flow breakeven -- $13,009 per vessel per day in 2025, improving versus $13,936 the previous year.
  • Outstanding debt -- $218.4 million at a 2% margin above benchmark as of year-end; additional $140 million–$150 million in expected newbuild financing not included in this figure.
  • Fleet market value -- Estimated at $664 million, implying a net asset value of $660 million ($93.70 per share) versus $62.40 closing price, reflecting a 33% market discount.
  • Charter rates and asset values -- As of Feb. 20, 2026, one-year time charter rate for 5,000 TEU ships at $36,000 per day; comparable secondhand values at $37.5 million, exceeding long-term medians.
  • Sale gain on vessels -- $19.4 million in full-year gains: $10 million from Diamandis and $9.2 million from Marcus V, offset by prior-year gain of $5.7 million from Lia Mastoria.
  • No idle or commercial off-hire -- Reported zero off-hire days for the period, indicating high utilization and operational consistency.
  • Newbuild orderbook for core segments -- Feeder vessel orderbook at 10% of fleet; intermediate segment at 17% as of Feb. 2026, with over 28% of each segment's fleet above 20 years old.
  • Dividend payout policy -- Pittas stated, "We do not have a steady payout ratio that some other companies have. But we do have a strategy of providing a very decent dividend to our shareholders," with 5% yield regarded as a likely floor.

Summary

Euroseas (ESEA +2.33%) reported top-line and bottom-line growth primarily due to higher charter rates and disciplined fleet management, with substantial forward charter coverage providing visibility into multi-year earnings. Management cited a 7% increase in annual net revenues and a 24% uplift in adjusted EBITDA, largely supported by a robust charter market and full fleet utilization, while raising the dividend by 7% and maintaining an active share repurchase policy. The fleet expanded to 21 vessels with significant capacity additions scheduled from 2027, underpinned by a conservative leverage profile and a well-staggered debt maturity schedule. Company leadership expressed clear strategic intent to balance shareholder returns with disciplined capital deployment, emphasizing continued dividend growth, measured leverage, and preference for newbuild exposure rather than secondhand market expansion at prevailing values.

  • The company achieved 87% voyage days secured for 2026 at an average rate of $30,700, which creates substantial earnings protection and limits near-term cash flow risk.
  • Pittas responded to capital allocation queries by stating the Board's focus on dividends over special distributions, preferring potential reinvestment into growth or newbuild asset opportunities.
  • Long-dated forward charter rates and high vessel asset values were highlighted as supporting a 33% discount of the share price to estimated net asset value, which management views as an implicit share price appreciation opportunity.
  • CFO Aslidis highlighted operating expense step-ups were primarily currency-driven, and noted future OpEx guidance assumes a 3% escalation, with euro-USD rate assumptions in the 1.15–1.20 range.
  • Management described current market dynamics as favoring vessel retention over scrapping, as even aged assets are expected to trade profitably until at least 2027–2028 if market conditions persist.

Industry glossary

  • TEU: Twenty-foot equivalent unit, a standard measure of containership cargo-carrying capacity.
  • Feeder vessel: A containership typically under 3,000 TEUs, serving regional and intra-regional trade routes.
  • Intermediate vessel: Containerships generally between 3,000 and 8,000 TEUs, positioned between feeder and mainline sizes.
  • Time charter equivalent (TCE): A daily rate metric accounting for voyage revenues, expenses, and off-hire, enabling comparison of performance across periods and vessels.
  • Balloon payment: A large, final repayment of principal on a loan, typically due at maturity.
