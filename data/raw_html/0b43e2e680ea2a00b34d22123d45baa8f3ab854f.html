UBS (UBS) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 11:31AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, October 29, 2025 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Group Chief Executive Officer — Sergio Ermotti
  • Group Chief Financial Officer — Todd Tuckner

TAKEAWAYS

  • Reported Net Profit -- $2.5 billion, marking a 74% increase year over year, with earnings per share of $0.76.
  • Underlying Pretax Profit -- $3.6 billion, up 50% year over year on 5% revenue growth, including net litigation reserve releases of $668 million.
  • Group Return on CET1 Capital -- 16.3%, with an adjusted rate of 12.7% excluding litigation, showing a 26% increase in pretax profits across the group.
  • Gross Run Rate Cost Saves -- Reached $10 billion cumulative, achieving this one quarter ahead of plan, driven by rightsizing the technology estate and streamlining functions.
  • Total Assets -- $1.6 trillion, representing a $38 billion decrease sequentially from the previous quarter.
  • Group Loan Balances -- $666 billion, with 92% secured by collateral, and group credit loss expense at $102 million, primarily from nonperforming positions in Switzerland.
  • Tangible Book Value Per Share -- Sequentially increased by 2% to $26.54, driven mainly by net profit partially offset by share repurchases.
  • Capital Ratios -- CET1 capital ratio of 14.8% and CET1 leverage ratio of 4.6%, both rising quarter over quarter and above stated targets.
  • Global Wealth Management (GWM) Invested Assets -- Increased by 4% sequentially to $4.7 trillion, with net new assets totaling $38 billion, largely attributed to Asia Pacific inflows.
  • GWM Net New Assets in APAC -- $38 billion, representing the entire division’s net new asset growth this quarter and driven by a mix of sizable strategic flows and regional momentum.
  • GWM Americas Net New Assets -- Negative $9 billion, attributed to adviser movement following compensation structure changes implemented last year.
  • Net Interest Income (NII) in GWM -- $1.6 billion, up 3% year over year and 5% quarter over quarter, with an outlook for stable NII next quarter as lending growth offsets lower rates.
  • GWM Net New Lending -- $3.5 billion, primarily driven by Lombard and mortgages in Switzerland and the Americas.
  • Personal & Corporate Banking (P&C) Pretax Profit -- CHF 668 million, up 1% or down 3% excluding litigation; net interest income decreased by 9% year over year.
  • Asset Management Invested Assets -- Grew by 5% sequentially, surpassing $2 trillion for the first time, with net new money at $18 billion and a 3.7% growth rate.
  • Investment Bank Pretax Profit -- $787 million, more than double year over year, with return on attributed equity at 17% and revenues up 23% to $3 billion.
  • Noncore and Legacy Unit -- Reduced nonoperational risk RWAs by nearly 90% since Q2 2023, closing 94% of its 14,000 books and decommissioning 65% of IT applications.
  • Integration Milestones -- Over two-thirds of Swiss client accounts (more than 700,000) migrated to UBS platforms, with substantial completion of Asset Management integration and major IT rationalization efforts, including shut down of 60% of legacy servers.
  • Employee Integration Sentiment -- Employee survey reports sentiment across UBS and former Credit Suisse staff well above industry benchmarks, signaling cultural and operational progress.
  • Regulatory and Legal Updates -- Settlement of Credit Suisse RMBS and UBS cross-border litigation, with $668 million in net litigation reserve releases and joint intention to appeal the AT1 ruling as disclosed this quarter.
  • U.S. National Bank Charter Application -- Filed application, with approval anticipated in 2026, described as "a pivotal milestone" for UBS’ long-term U.S. growth strategy.
  • AI Capabilities -- 340 live artificial intelligence use cases now implemented, targeting efficiency, resilience, and improved client experience.

RISKS

  • Macro uncertainties, strong Swiss franc, and higher U.S. tariffs are "clouding the outlook for the Swiss economy," and a prolonged U.S. government shutdown "may delay capital market activities," as directly mentioned in the call.
  • Management expects the CET1 capital ratio to decrease sequentially at year-end, driven by accruals for intended share repurchases in 2026 and the full-year dividend.
  • Guidance for Investment Bank and Global Markets activity in the fourth quarter includes an explicit expectation for normalization due to seasonality, transaction timing shifts, and potential delays from U.S. government shutdown effects.

SUMMARY

UBS Group AG (UBS +0.24%) reported significant profitability gains in the third quarter of 2025, with net profit up 74% and strong underlying pretax profit growth, supported by substantial cost savings and progress on the Credit Suisse integration.

Large net new asset inflows in Asia Pacific drove group-wide increases in invested assets, counterbalancing adviser-driven outflows in the Americas. Legal and regulatory outcomes—including settlement of Credit Suisse-related litigation and the announced appeal on the AT1 ruling—were resolved or proactively addressed. UBS filed for a U.S. national bank charter, a major step in its U.S. strategy, and continued to develop artificial intelligence across the platform. Risks to the outlook were flagged regarding Swiss macro headwinds, currency strength, and potential U.S. policy disruptions.

  • UBS confirmed achievement of $10 billion in gross run rate cost savings, with further technology and staff efficiencies expected to lift cost-income ratios by end of 2026.
  • Management stated integration expense is the leading driver of divisional pretax losses in the Global Wealth Management “Global” segment, with improvement anticipated as integration nears completion.
  • “No investment guidelines were breached” in UBS-managed funds affected by the First Brands issue; impact is mainly confined to sophisticated investor funds and is under active recovery efforts.
  • The parent bank’s stand-alone CET1 capital ratio was stable at 13.3%, with intercompany dividend pacing used to manage FX-driven leverage headwinds.
  • UBS plans to accrue for 2026 share buybacks in Q4, with ultimate buyback size contingent on integration progress and clarity on Swiss capital regulation.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AT1 (Additional Tier 1): Contingent convertible bonds designed as part of banks’ capital structure that can be written down or converted to equity in cases of severe financial distress or resolution events.
  • Lombard Lending: Loans secured by pledged liquid assets, often used by high-net-worth clients for collateralized borrowing in wealth management.
  • Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR): Regulatory metric requiring banks to maintain a stable funding profile in relation to their asset composition over a one-year period.
  • Noncore and Legacy (NCL): A unit for winding down or selling nonstrategic or high-risk assets, often from acquired entities or discontinued businesses.
  • Unified Global Alternatives (UGA): UBS’ platform for alternative investments, including hedge funds, private markets, and real assets, across multiple geographies and products.
  • LCM/ECM: Loan Capital Markets (LCM) and Equity Capital Markets (ECM); divisions facilitating capital raising in debt and equity markets, respectively.
  • Stage 3 Charges: Credit loss provisions for loans considered credit impaired under IFRS 9 accounting standards.
