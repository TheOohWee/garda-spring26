Earnings Call Transcript

Southern Copper (SCCO) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 7:15AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, July 29, 2025 at 11 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Vice President of Finance and Chief Financial Officer — Raul Jacob

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $3.1 billion, down 2% ($67 million) due to a 5% decrease in copper sales value and a 3% drop in copper volume, partially offset by higher zinc and silver sales.
  • Net Income -- $973 million, up 2% with a net margin of 32%; improvement driven by decreased operating costs, lower G&A and exploration expenses, and higher interest income.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $1,791 million, down 0.3%; margin improved to 59% from 58%.
  • Operating Cash Cost (Before By-Product Credits) -- $2.11 per pound, a 3% increase from the previous quarter on higher production costs and administrative expenses, partly offset by lower treatment and refining costs.
  • Operating Cash Cost (Including By-Product Credits) -- $0.63 per pound, an 18% decrease ($0.13 reduction) compared to the previous quarter; by-product credits totaled $756 million, up 15%.
  • Copper Production -- 238,980 tons, a 1.4% decrease driven by reduced output in Mexico and partially offset by increases at Toquepala and IMMSA mines; full-year guidance is 965,300 tons (down 0.9%).
  • Zinc Production -- 45,899 tons, a 56% quarter-on-quarter increase, primarily from a 126% increase at the Buenavista zinc concentrator; annual guidance is 173,400 tons (up 33%), with 110,700 tons from Buenavista.
  • Silver Production -- Mine output up 15%; refined silver production up 1% quarter-on-quarter; sales grew 28% due to higher prices and volumes.
  • Molybdenum Production and Sales -- Production increased 3.5%; average price fell 5% to $20.57 per pound; annual production guidance is 28,700 tons (down 1%).
  • Total Operating Costs and Expenses -- Decreased by $47 million, or 3%, the result of lower inventory consumption, constructors, freight, diesel, and fuel, partially offset by higher repair materials and translation differences.
  • Cash Flow from Operating Activities -- $1,698 million for the first six months, up 5% year over year, reflecting higher sales and cost efficiencies.
  • Capital Expenditures -- Full-year guidance remains $1.6 billion, with expenditures expected to significantly accelerate in the second half, especially for Tia Maria and maintenance projects.
  • Tia Maria Project Progress -- 90% completion of access roads and platforms; 1,376 new jobs created (802 local hires); initial production testing targeted for the first half of 2027.
  • Buenavista Zinc Plant Focus -- Facility is dedicated to zinc due to current ore grades, foregoing copper production for higher profitability.
  • Dividend Announcement -- Quarterly cash dividend of $0.80 per share and a stock dividend of 0.0101 shares per share; payable September 4, 2025.
  • Sustainability and ESG -- Achieved third-party verification of the sustainable development report, FTSE Russell inclusion, and 24% reduction in lost time injury frequency since 2023.

Risks

  • Management noted, "we believe an intense commercial war between the U.S. and China will affect economic growth worldwide, consequently impacting in copper demand."
  • Raul Jacob referenced operational sensitivity to tariffs, stating "it's very hard to predict at this point what is going to happen and how this is going to affect us" regarding possible U.S. tariffs on copper.
  • Segment copper production declined 2.5% in Mexico, primarily due to decreased copper production at Buenavista and La Caridad mines.

Summary

Southern Copper Corporation (SCCO +2.19%) delivered net margin and EBITDA margin improvements amid softer revenue and copper output, driven by significantly lower cash costs per pound attributable to strong by-product performance and cost reductions.

Strategic capital deployment centers on the Tia Maria mine and multiple Peruvian and Mexican projects, with clear evidence of regulated CapEx acceleration planned for the second half. The company reaffirmed disciplined dividend policy, operational resilience through value optimization at Buenavista, and advancing ESG credentials with new independent report verification.

  • Company maintains full-year production guidance for copper, zinc, and molybdenum, but copper volumes reflect an explicit tradeoff with zinc output at Buenavista for higher profitability.
  • Management confirmed that "Some of our contracts are under COMEX, and we are," but would not disclose the realized premium from the current price arbitrage.
  • Net sales lagged production due to the timing effect of prior inventory sales, as stated by Jacob: "On the copper sales, we had -- at the end of 2024, we had some copper inventories that were sold in the first quarter of this year. That's mainly -- that's the main difference that we have in the copper sales in the first quarter."
  • Tia Maria workforce buildout and infrastructure development are well advanced, underpinning launch expectations in 2027 and local economic impact metrics.

Industry glossary

  • TC/RC (Treatment and Refining Charges): Fees incurred by mining companies to process concentrate into refined metal, affecting cash cost calculations.
  • SX-EW (Solvent Extraction and Electrowinning): A hydrometallurgical process to extract and refine copper from low-grade ore without smelting.
  • Buenavista Zinc Concentrator: A processing facility at the Buenavista mine dedicated to extracting zinc, capable of switching to process copper depending on ore grades and company strategy.
  • COMEX: The primary U.S. commodities exchange where copper futures are traded, frequently used as a price reference in contracts.
  • FTSE4Good: Index series identifying companies that demonstrate strong environmental, social, and governance practices.
