  • Operating Businesses Distributable Earnings -- $336 million, or $0.15 per share, for the quarter, and $1.7 billion, or $0.72 per share, for the past 12 months.
  • Real Estate Portfolio Occupancy -- Supercore occupancy at 96% and Core Plus occupancy at 95% at quarter-end.
  • Office Lease Activity -- Signed 3 million square feet of office leases in the quarter, with rents averaging 15% above expiring leases.
  • Manufactured Housing Monetization -- Sold remaining U.S. manufactured housing portfolio assets for $2.5 billion, achieving a 25% IRR and a 3.5x multiple on invested capital.
  • Carried Interest Realizations -- Realized $154 million of carried interest into income during the quarter.

  • Stock Split -- Completed a three-for-two stock split on October 9, 2025; all per-share amounts are presented on a post-split basis.

  • Debt Issuance and Refinancing Activity -- Executed $140 billion in financings year-to-date, including $650 million of 10-year senior notes and two separate CMBS deals at over $1.25 billion each.
  • Major Transactions -- Agreed to acquire the remaining 26% of Oaktree (NYSE: OAK); $1.4 billion cash component, with $250 million to be settled in BN shares and nearly all Oaktree-related BAM consideration in cash; transaction expected to close in 1H 2026.
  • Brookfield Infrastructure IPO -- Completed the CAD 810 million IPO of Rockpoint Gas Storage, retaining significant ownership interest and realizing over 3x invested capital.
  • Energy Transition Projects -- Partnered with the U.S. government via Westinghouse to deliver $80 billion in nuclear reactors and announced a collaboration with Bloom Energy (NYSE: BE) for 1 gigawatt of fuel cell power generation.
  • AI and Robotics Initiatives -- Strategic partnership with Figure (NYSE: FIG) to provide real-asset environments for humanoid robotics development and announced plans to launch an AI infrastructure fund.
  • Dividend Declaration -- Declared a quarterly dividend of $0.06 per share, payable at the end of December to shareholders of record on December 16, 2025.
  • Monetization Activity -- Realized $75 billion year-to-date across the franchise: $22 billion in real estate, $14 billion infrastructure assets, $11 billion renewable assets, $7 billion private equity, and $21 billion credit/other; completed sales largely at or above carrying values.

SUMMARY

Management detailed substantial progress on international expansion, with new partnerships in the U.K. through Just Group and initial entry into Japan’s insurance market. Significant capital deployment was allocated toward infrastructure, renewable energy, and AI initiatives, including major U.S. nuclear and data infrastructure projects. The firm highlighted its disciplined investment strategy and cited robust real asset demand supported by record fundraising, rising fee-related earnings, and a broad set of global monetizations. Management confirmed that Oaktree’s full acquisition is proceeding as planned, with the transaction structure finalized and closing anticipated in the first half of 2026.

  • President Goodman said, "we expect continued growth in our results over the remainder of the year and into 2026."
  • The company completed an oversubscribed IPO of Rockpoint Gas Storage, noted as the largest Toronto Stock Exchange IPO since May 2022.
  • Management indicated that realized monetizations this year have moved mature funds materially closer to carried interest realization thresholds under the European "waterfall" model.
  • New investments were partly data-driven, with capital positioned to address secular trends: AI infrastructure, demographic changes in global savings, and ongoing real estate recovery.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Fee-bearing capital: Client assets under management that generate recurring management fees for Brookfield Corporation (BN 0.45%).
  • Distributable Earnings (DE): A non-GAAP measure used by Brookfield Corporation (BN 0.45%), representing cash earnings available for distribution to shareholders before certain items and realizations.
  • European "waterfall" model: A profit-sharing structure where carried interest is paid to investment managers only after all invested capital is returned to investors along with a preferred return.
  • CMBS: Commercial Mortgage-Backed Securities; bonds backed by mortgages on commercial real estate.
  • IRR (Internal Rate of Return): A performance metric calculating the annualized effective compounded return rate earned on an investment.
  • Supercore/Core Plus: Real estate investment strategies focusing on high-quality, stable assets ("Supercore") and slightly higher-yielding, lower-risk properties with strong fundamentals ("Core Plus").
  • PRT: Pension Risk Transfer; insurance products that transfer liability for defined benefit pension plans from sponsors to insurers.
  • Carried Interest: The share of profits accruing to managers of investment funds after a hurdle return for investors has been reached.
