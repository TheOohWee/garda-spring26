Zebra (ZBRA) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 10:20AM EST

Thursday, Feb. 12, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — William J. Burns
  • Chief Financial Officer — Nathan Andrew Winters
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Michael Steele

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $1.5 billion for the quarter, representing a 10.6% increase and 2.5% organic growth.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin -- 22.1% for the quarter, with operating expense leverage improvements of 60 basis points.
  • Non-GAAP diluted EPS -- $4.33, an 8% increase year over year and above the upper end of management's outlook.
  • Segment growth -- Connected Frontline increased 3.6% led by mobile computing; Asset Visibility and Automation rose 1.3% led by printing and supplies.
  • Geographic performance -- Asia Pacific up 13%, Latin America up 8% with Mexico showing double-digit gains, EMEA up 4%, and North America down 1% as large prior-year orders lapped tough comparisons.
  • Adjusted gross margin -- Declined 50 basis points to 48.2% due to reduced services and software margins.
  • Restructuring charges -- $76 million in the quarter related to the exit of the robotics business and productivity initiatives.
  • Free cash flow -- $831 million for the year with a conversion rate of 102%.
  • Capital return -- $300 million in share repurchases during the quarter; $587 million for the year; share repurchase authorization increased by $1 billion, totaling $1.1 billion after $100 million completed through early February.
  • Acquisitions -- Elo Touch and Fotoneo closed during the year, funded with cash on hand and existing credit, with early synergy progress cited.
  • Balance sheet -- Year-end cash of $125 million, debt leverage ratio of 2, and $1.2 billion in unused credit capacity.
  • 2026 outlook—Q1 sales guidance -- 11%-15% growth including about 10 points from acquisitions and FX.
  • 2026 outlook—Full year sales guidance -- 9%-13% growth, including a seven-point benefit from acquisitions and FX.
  • 2026 outlook—EBITDA/profitability -- Adjusted EBITDA margin expected around 22%; non-GAAP diluted EPS guided to $17.70-$18.30.
  • 2026 memory headwind -- Two-point gross margin headwind anticipated from memory pricing starting in Q2; management expects full mitigation within guidance through price increases, supply actions, and robotics exit.
  • RFID performance -- High double-digit growth projected in 2026 with broad-based adoption across multiple verticals.
  • Machine vision -- Sequential growth in Q4; anticipated return to annual growth in 2026, driven by diversified wins in manufacturing and T&L, and expanded offerings via Fotoneo acquisition.
  • AI solutions -- Launch of Frontline AI Suite, with paid pilots underway and scaled deployments expected in the current year; positioned as a differentiator.
  • Channel inventory -- Inventory position exiting the year is consistent with prior year; no material changes embedded in full year outlook.

Risks

  • Chief Financial Officer Winters stated that a "two-point gross margin headwind on a gross basis" is expected due to industry-wide memory component price increases beginning in Q2, though management anticipates full mitigation through targeted actions.
  • Adjusted gross margin declined 50 basis points to 48.2%, primarily attributed to reduced services and software margins driven by increased service repair costs.
  • North America revenue declined 1% due to challenging prior-year comparisons as large orders in transportation and logistics and retail were not repeated in the quarter.
  • Restructuring charges of $76 million recognized in the quarter relating to robotics business exit and productivity initiatives impact reported profitability.

Summary

Zebra Technologies Corporation (ZBRA 0.45%) reported above-guidance revenue and earnings growth, driven by strong execution, recent acquisitions, regional outperformance outside North America, and expanding demand for digital and AI-powered solutions. Operating leverage, full tariff mitigation, and disciplined capital allocation led to significant share repurchases and increased Board authorization. The 2026 outlook is underpinned by a robust sales pipeline, acquisition and FX tailwinds, a mitigated but pronounced gross margin headwind from memory price increases, and continued strength in core segments such as RFID and machine vision.

  • Management plans to complete roughly half of 2026's targeted $900 million free cash flow in share repurchases during the first half, maintaining substantial financial flexibility for future capital allocation.
  • Asia Pacific, EMEA, and Latin America all achieved solid or accelerating growth, while North America's decline reflected tough prior-year order cycles rather than underlying demand weakness.
  • Integration of Elo Touch and Fotoneo is enhancing Zebra Technologies Corporation’s offerings in Connected Frontline and machine vision, with early synergy benefits already realized.
  • The company’s AI portfolio, specifically the new Frontline AI Suite, is entering early customer adoption stages, with pilots converting to scaled deployments in 2026 across multiple industries and verticals.
  • Management expects current service and software margin pressures to moderate as platform consolidation is completed and services repair demand levels out during the year.
  • Robust customer intent to invest in modernization initiatives—including digitization, automation, and AI—was cited, with ongoing refresh cycles and large project discussions said to be progressing earlier than in prior years.

Industry glossary

  • RFID: Radio-frequency identification—technology enabling wireless identification and tracking of assets using radio waves and embedded tags.
  • Machine vision: Automated image capture and analysis used to guide operational processes, quality control, and workflow automation, particularly in manufacturing and logistics.
  • T&L: Transportation and logistics—a key customer vertical addressed by Zebra Technologies Corporation's solutions.
  • Frontline AI Suite: Zebra Technologies Corporation’s integrated platform of AI-driven enablers, blueprints, and companion agents designed to automate, inform, and optimize operational workflows for frontline workers.
