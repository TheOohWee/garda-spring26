Hillman Solutions (HLMN) Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 10:42AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Feb. 18, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Executive Chairman — Douglas Cahill
  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Jon Michael Adinolfi
  • Chief Financial Officer — Robert Kraft

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Risks

  • Market volume pressure -- Full-year 2024 saw a 5% reduction in market volume, and 2025 guidance incorporates a further 1% decrease, explicitly pointing to ongoing softness in retail foot traffic, existing home sales, and remodeling activity.
  • Free cash flow underperformance -- Robert Kraft stated, "Free cash flow came in a bit below our expectations, which was impacted by CapEx and our soft top line."
  • Adjusted SG&A escalation -- Adjusted SG&A as a percentage of sales increased to 31.6% in 2024 from 29.5% in 2023, driven by higher employee bonus expense, IT investments, and the True Value charge.
  • Customer concentration and attrition -- Management acknowledged expected attrition outside the top three Robotics and Digital Solutions customers, potentially creating "a little bit of negative pressure for the year."

Takeaways

  • Full-year net sales -- $1.473 billion, down slightly but above the midpoint of revised guidance, with flat organic sales; Koch Industries and Intex DIY contributed four percentage points to growth, new business wins added two percentage points, a 5% market volume reduction, and a 1% price headwind offset gains.
  • Fiscal Q4 net sales -- $349.6 million, up 25% over the prior-year quarter due to recent acquisitions and new business wins.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (full year) -- $241.8 million, up 10.2%, and includes an $8.6 million True Value receivables write-off following their Chapter 11 bankruptcy.
  • Fiscal Q4 adjusted EBITDA -- $56.3 million, up 3.5% versus the prior-year fiscal Q4 and in line with expectations.
  • Adjusted gross margin (full year) -- 48.1%, a 390 basis-point increase from the prior year driven by operational efficiencies, product mix, and lower cost of goods sold.
  • Fiscal Q4 adjusted gross margin -- 47.7%, down 50 basis points over the previous fiscal Q4, in line with expectations.
  • Adjusted SG&A as percentage of sales (full year) -- 31.6%, up from 29.5% last year, due to higher employee bonus expense, IT spending, and inclusion of the True Value charge.
  • Operating cash flow (full year) -- $183 million, down from $283 million in 2023, which benefited from over $100 million in inventory reductions.
  • Free cash flow (full year) -- $98.1 million, representing a decline from $172.3 million in 2023, partly due to higher capital expenditures and a softer top line.
  • Capital expenditures (full year) -- $85 million, increased from $66 million in 2023, primarily for MinuteKey 3.5 rollouts and fastener sets at key customers.
  • Net debt (year-end) -- $674 million, improved from $722 million the year prior, reflecting $48 million of debt repaid with free cash flow and acquisitions funded.
  • Net debt/trailing 12-month adjusted EBITDA -- 2.8x at year-end, better than 3.3x at prior year-end, with a long-term target at or below 2.5x.
  • Vendor recognition -- Received Vendor of the Year awards from both Home Depot and Lowe's within the year.
  • M&A activity -- Two bolt-on acquisitions completed: Koch Industries (January 2024) and Intex DIY (August 2024), both cited as immediately accretive and positioned for organic growth through Hillman Solutions' supply chain.
  • Hardware and Protective Solutions adjusted EBITDA -- Increased 23% year over year on a flat top line, attributed to the company’s service model advantages.
  • Robotics and Digital Solutions -- Management expects return to growth with MinuteKey 3.5 rollout, aiming for $50 million of annual free cash flow post-deployment, and expects RDS-related capital expenditures to peak in 2025 and drop by $20 million in 2026.
  • 2025 full-year net sales guidance -- $1.495 billion to $1.575 billion, midpoint $1.535 billion; price expected neutral, market volume -1%, new business +2.5%, Intex DIY +2.5%.
  • 2025 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- $255 million to $275 million, midpoint $265 million for an approximate 10% increase.
  • 2025 free cash flow guidance -- $90 million to $110 million, midpoint $100 million, with $90 million of planned capital expenditures influencing the figure.
  • 2025 leverage outlook -- Net leverage expected to end 2025 near 2.2x, assuming achievement of midpoint guidance levels and excluding further acquisitions.
  • 2025 Q1 free cash flow -- Guided to be negative due to inventory build for spring and summer, with positive cash generation expected in subsequent quarters.
  • Interest expense (2025 guidance) -- $45 million to $55 million; cash interest $40 million to $50 million, and cash taxes $15 million to $25 million.
  • Share count (2025 outlook) -- Approximately 201 million fully diluted weighted average shares anticipated.
  • Performance-based equity compensation -- Executives’ new compensation structure links 50% of annual equity grants to a return on invested capital metric starting in 2025.
  • Supply chain shifts -- Ongoing transition of sourcing from China to Vietnam, Taiwan, India, and other countries to support an efficient distribution network.
  • Technology investments -- Commitment to cloud migration and AI deployment to enhance operational efficiencies across 111,000 SKUs and nearly 30,000 retail locations.

Summary

Hillman Solutions (HLMN 1.15%) ended fiscal 2024 with record adjusted EBITDA, a 390 basis-point improvement in adjusted gross margin, and a strengthened balance sheet through reduced net debt and leverage. Management’s 2025 guidance targets net sales growth driven by new business wins and acquisitions, while price is expected to be a headwind in the first half and neutral for the full year. Executives announced a revised performance-based equity structure focused on return on invested capital and committed to technology and supply chain modernization, positioning the company for operational efficiency and scalable growth. Annual capital expenditures are expected to remain elevated, mainly to support the MinuteKey 3.5 rollout and category expansion at key customers.

  • Management expects the Robotics and Digital Solutions segment to resume growth in the first quarter, with the MinuteKey 3.5 deployment labeled as delivering a "healthy lift in revenue versus MinuteKey 3.0."
  • The company said its top new business wins for the year are "committed," while warning that realized growth may fluctuate depending on underlying market volume.
  • Hillman Solutions' Canadian operations are described as "we're running it well" despite ongoing market and industrial weakness, with profitability maintained by offsetting headwinds through new business wins.
  • Tactical sourcing diversification provides potential advantages in navigating evolving tariff environments; management highlighted flexibility as a reason Hillman Solutions may benefit should tariffs increase.
  • 2025 interest, tax, capital, and restructuring expense ranges are provided to support investor cash flow modeling, and the updated leverage outlook excludes possible M&A.

Industry glossary

  • MinuteKey 3.5: The latest Hillman Solutions automated key-duplication kiosk series with upgraded features including auto fob programming, enhanced payment capability, and an "endless aisle" inventory offering.
  • RDS: Robotics and Digital Solutions segment, which includes MinuteKey kiosks and related retail automation.
  • HPS: Hardware and Protective Solutions, Hillman Solutions' largest business unit providing core hardware and safety products.
  • COGS: Cost of goods sold, representing direct costs attributable to products sold.
  • CapEx: Capital expenditures, including investments in property, equipment, and technology.
