Columbia (COLM) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 3, 2026 at 6:20PM EST

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $1.1 billion for the quarter, a 2% decrease year over year driven by a 7% decline in wholesale net sales partly offset by a 1% increase in direct-to-consumer sales.
  • Gross Margin -- 51.6%, expanding by 50 basis points, primarily due to cleaner inventories and lower promotions, which more than offset higher U.S. tariffs.
  • SG&A Expense -- Increased 3% driven by higher direct-to-consumer expenses and nonrecurring costs related to a profit improvement program, partially offset by cost reductions in targeted business areas.
  • International Sales -- Grew across all major regions with constant currency net sales up 10% in Latin America Asia Pacific, low double-digit percent in China, high single-digit percent in Japan, low single-digit percent in Korea, high teens percent in LAP distributor markets, 3% in EMEA, and 3% in Canada.
  • U.S. Net Sales -- Declined 8% for the quarter; wholesale business down high teens percent, reflecting earlier shipments and a lower order book, with supply constraints due to curbed inventory purchases following tariff announcements.
  • Direct-to-Consumer U.S. Net Sales -- Down low single-digit percent in the quarter, with e-commerce also down low single-digit percent as reduced clearance and promotional activity was partly offset by improved marketing and website redesign.
  • Brand Performance -- Columbia net sales decreased 1% as international gains were more than offset by U.S. declines; prAna net sales increased 6% mainly from direct-to-consumer channel growth; SOREL net sales decreased 18% due to shipment timing and less clearance activity; Mountain Hardwear net sales decreased 5% as a result of lower clearance versus an elevated prior-year baseline.
  • Marketing Spend -- Rose to 6.5% of net sales, up from 5.9% in the previous year, primarily to support the Accelerate Growth strategy; to be maintained at 6.4% in 2026.
  • Inventory Position -- Flat at year-end, described as healthy, despite increased U.S. tariff costs.
  • Cash and Debt -- Ended the year with $791 million in cash and equivalents and no outstanding debt.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $201 million returned through share repurchases and $66 million through dividends in 2025.
  • 2026 Net Sales Outlook -- Management expects net sales growth of 1%-3% for the year, with foreign exchange providing a 0.5% to 1% tailwind.
  • 2026 Operating Margin Outlook -- Projected at 0.2%-6.9%, reflecting expected improvement from 2025 despite ongoing tariff headwinds.
  • 2026 Gross Margin Guidance -- Expected to contract 50-70 basis points to 49.8%-50%, driven by incremental unmitigated U.S. tariff costs.
  • Q1 2026 Guidance -- Anticipating net sales decline of 2.5%-4%, with anticipated earnings per share of $0.29-$0.37 due to lower gross margin and SG&A deleverage.
  • Pricing Actions -- High single-digit percent price increases planned for both Spring and Fall 2026 in the U.S. market to mitigate tariff impacts, not compounded but applied by season.
  • Fall 2026 Wholesale Order Book -- Over 80% of the global order book secured for Fall, with U.S. wholesale expected to return to low to mid-single-digit growth in the second half, while international growth outpaces U.S.
  • Impairment Charges -- Nonrecurring $29 million brand impairment recorded in 2025 for Crown Mount Hardware, not recurring in 2026.

RISKS

  • Timothy P. Boyle stated, "our 2025 financial performance was short of my personal growth and profitability goals," citing headwinds from unmitigated tariffs, brand impairments, and higher marketing spend, all contributing to operating margin contraction and earnings decline.
  • Jim Swanson confirmed, "on that. So I think there'll be a disproportionate impact on gross margin, particularly in the front part of the year, Q1 in particular," due to delayed effect of price hikes to offset tariffs on existing inventory.
  • Management noted ongoing retailer caution about tariff-induced price increases just beginning to affect the U.S. marketplace, which may dampen demand and unit growth.
  • Gross margin is forecast to contract by 50-70 basis points in 2026, and management flagged "the uncertainty of how consumers react to broad price-based increases" as a key risk in margin forecasts.

SUMMARY

Columbia Sportswear (COLM 2.65%) reported quarterly net sales of $1.1 billion, a 2% year-over-year decrease, citing strong international growth offset by weakness in the U.S. market and earlier timing of wholesale shipments. Gross margin expanded by 50 basis points to 51.6%, driven by lower promotions and cleaner inventories, despite incremental tariff pressures. Marketing spend rose to 6.5% of net sales, supporting product launches and new campaigns such as Engineered for Whatever. The year ended with no debt and $791 million in cash, as management returned over $260 million to shareholders via buybacks and dividends. For 2026, guidance calls for 1%-3% sales growth and operating margin improvement, but gross margin will contract due to higher tariffs, to be partially offset through high single-digit U.S. price increases and cost mitigation measures.

  • Jim Swanson attributed the "better conversion" of fall U.S. wholesale orders to lower cancel rates, tighter inventory buys, and strong sell-through for new collections and campaigns.
  • Peter Bragdon confirmed international markets—including China, Europe, and Latin America Asia Pacific—will lead growth, bolstered by direct consumer engagement and campaign activations like Omni Heat Infinity in China and a local influencer partnership in Korea.
  • Timothy P. Boyle described the Flat Earther and Nature Calls marketing campaigns as strategic levers to draw new, younger consumers, with initial results showing measurable increases in unaided brand awareness and search activity in the U.S.
  • SOREL and Mountain Hardwear brands faced year-on-year revenue declines due to lower clearance activity and shipment timing, while prAna saw 6% growth, led by an updated product offering and expanded lifestyle fitness distribution.
  • Management is maintaining a conservative store opening plan in 2026, expecting U.S. new store additions to be offset by closures, as part of ongoing fleet rationalization.
  • Timothy P. Boyle commented that weather has a greater impact on in-season order opportunities than any other variable, cautioning that only minor order book upside is possible despite low retailer and channel inventories.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • SG&A (Selling, General, and Administrative expense): Direct operating costs not tied to production or COGS, including marketing, administrative, and retail operations expenses, critical for assessing operating leverage and margin trends at COLM.
  • OutDry Extreme: Columbia Sportswear's proprietary waterproofing technology, highlighted for its PFAS-free, recycled-material construction and used to differentiate product lines in compliance-driven categories.
  • Engineered for Whatever: Multibrand marketing and product platform launched to refresh Columbia Sportswear’s consumer engagement, emphasizing innovation and expanding reach to younger consumers in the U.S. and internationally.
  • Direct-to-Consumer (DTC): Sales channel encompassing brand-owned retail and e-commerce operations, pivotal for brand margin management and consumer data access.
  • PFAS: Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, a class of chemicals subject to regulatory scrutiny that impact sourcing and product mix decisions for COLM and its brands.
