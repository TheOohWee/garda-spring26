Instacart (CART) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 6:53PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 12, 2026

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Chris Rogers
  • Chief Financial Officer — Emily Maher
  • Head of Investor Relations — Rebecca Yoshiyama

TAKEAWAYS

  • Gross Transaction Value (GTV) -- $9.85 billion, reflecting 14% year-over-year growth and the strongest pace in three years.
  • Total Orders -- 89.5 million, up 16% year over year.
  • Average Order Value -- Declined 1% year over year, attributed to higher mix of restaurant orders.
  • Transaction Revenue -- Rose 13% year over year, making up 7.1% of GTV, which was flat relative to prior year.
  • Advertising and Other Revenue -- Increased 10% year over year, driven by GTV growth, onboarding of more Carrot Ads partners, and expansion to over 9,000 active brand advertisers.
  • GAAP Net Income -- $81 million, down 46% due to nonrecurring legal and regulatory expenses, including a $60 million FTC settlement.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $303 million, reflecting 20% year-over-year growth.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $184 million, an increase of 20% year over year.
  • Share Repurchases -- $1.1 billion bought back in the quarter, contributing to a $1.4 billion program in the year.
  • Cash Balance and Buyback Capacity -- Ended with ~$1 billion in cash and similar assets and $671 million remaining under repurchase authorization.
  • Q1 2026 GTV Guidance -- Projected $10.25 billion to $10.275 billion, representing anticipated 11%-13% year-over-year growth, with GTV expected to outpace order growth.
  • Q1 2026 Advertising and Other Revenue Guidance -- Forecasted to grow 11%-14% year over year.
  • Q1 2026 Adjusted EBITDA Guidance -- Expected range of $280 million-$290 million, up 15%-19% year over year but sequentially below Q4 due to advertising seasonality.
  • Instacart+ Membership -- Paid membership growth continues, with Instacart+ now representing a majority of GTV and orders; members show higher engagement and retention.
  • Marketplace and Enterprise Retailer Reach -- Marketplace now spans 2,200+ retail banners with nearly 100,000 locations, while Enterprise powers over 380 e-commerce sites.
  • Carrot Ads Expansion -- Platform grew from 220 to over 310 retailer-owned sites year over year.
  • In-Store Advertising -- Caper Carts are driving nearly a 1 percentage point average lift in basket size from in-cart prompts, now deployed in 100 cities and 15 states.
  • AI Operational Impact -- Productivity per engineer increased nearly 40% year over year, with 10% of engineers increasing output by 80%; system reliability also improved.
  • International Expansion -- Storefront Pro and Caper Cart launched with Costco in France and Spain, and a pilot announced with Morrisons in the UK.

RISKS

  • Chief Financial Officer Maher said, "GAAP net income was $81 million, down 46% year over year. This decline was primarily due to higher G&A expenses related to nonrecurring legal and regulatory matters, including a $60 million settlement with the FTC."
  • CFO Maher stated, "expansion to moderate year over year as we reinvest to accelerate across our multiple growth engines and lap some of the more significant operating expense efficiencies realized in 2024 and 2025."
  • "there's still some macro uncertainty that we're seeing in the market and some ongoing changes in consumer preferences," according to CEO Rogers.

SUMMARY

Maplebear (CART 2.70%) reported its highest GTV growth in three years, supported by increasing order volumes and expanding partnerships with retail banners and advertisers. The company executed $1.1 billion in share repurchases during the quarter, and ended the year with substantial cash reserves and continued buyback authorization. Management guided to sustained double-digit GTV and advertising revenue growth, with adjusted EBITDA growth expected to moderate as investments scale across product and international initiatives.

  • CEO Rogers emphasized "multiple years of profitable growth ahead," outlining durable expansion in both core marketplace and Enterprise businesses, and re-affirmed differentiated positioning in large basket orders and omnichannel capabilities.
  • Active brands advertising count increased to over 9,000, as Carrot Ads' reach and off-platform partnerships expanded.
  • Costco and Sprouts were cited as flagship case studies for deepening retail partnerships and layering in advanced technology offerings, such as AI-powered Cart Assistant and in-store solutions like Caper Cart.
  • Generative AI investment delivered quantifiable productivity gains and accelerated onboarding of new retail partners, while also enabling faster and higher-quality technology deployments across the business.
  • International market strategy is focused on leveraging existing North American technology and white label offerings, with disciplined expense management shaping a gradual approach to new market entry.
  • Management confirmed that Instacart+ membership strength is driving higher platform retention and engagement, while highlighting cross-platform benefits including $0 delivery minimums and bundled value with key partners.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • GTV (Gross Transaction Value): Total dollar value of orders transacted on the Instacart platform before deductions for refunds, cancellations, or reductions.
  • Carrot Ads: Instacart's advertising platform, enabling brands and agencies to place digital ads both on the marketplace and on retailer-owned sites.
  • Caper Cart: AI-powered smart shopping cart providing in-store digital engagement and targeted advertising opportunities.
  • Storefront Pro: Enterprise technology product for online grocery retailers, managing e-commerce operations and digital storefronts.
  • Cart Assistant: Instacart’s AI-based shopping assistant designed to support personalized cart planning and customer experience.
