PNC (PNC) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 16, 2026 at 10:06AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Friday, January 16, 2026 at 9 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — William S. Demchak
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Robert Q. Reilly
  • Director of Investor Relations — Bryan Gill

Takeaways

  • Net Income -- $7 billion for the year, representing diluted EPS of $16.59 and 21% annual EPS growth.
  • Revenue -- Record annual revenue up $1.5 billion or 7%; Q4 revenue reached $6.1 billion, rising $156 million or 3% from the prior quarter.
  • Operating Leverage -- Positive 5% for 2025, with annual noninterest expense up 2% against 7% revenue growth.
  • Return of Capital -- $1.1 billion returned in the quarter, including $676 million in dividends and about $400 million in share repurchases; quarterly buybacks set to increase to $600 million-$700 million for 2026.
  • FirstBank Acquisition -- Closed on January 5 with a $4.2 billion purchase price (30% cash, 70% stock); estimated to add $1 per share in annualized earnings run rate by year-end 2026.
  • Average Loans -- $328 billion for Q4, up $2 billion or 1% linked quarter; 3% annual growth, driven by $10 billion increase in commercial loans, partially offset by $1 billion decline in consumer loans.
  • Deposits -- Average balances of $440 billion, up $8 billion or 2% sequentially; noninterest-bearing deposits at $95 billion comprise 22% of total average deposits.
  • Net Interest Income (NII) -- $3.7 billion for Q4, up $83 million or 2% quarter over quarter; net interest margin rose 5 basis points to 2.84%.
  • Noninterest Income -- $2.3 billion for Q4, increasing $73 million or 3% sequentially; full-year noninterest income reached $8.7 billion, up 8% from 2024.
  • Credit Quality -- Nonperforming loans rose $81 million or 4% from Q3; NPLs now represent 0.67% of total loans versus 0.73% a year ago.
  • Tangible Book Value -- $112.51 per common share, up 4% from the prior quarter and 18% year over year.
  • CET1 Ratio -- Estimated at 10.6% at year-end; expected to decline by 40 basis points post-FirstBank but remain near a 10% target.
  • Guidance for 2026 -- Projected average loan growth of 8%, total revenue up approximately 11%, net interest income up about 14%, noninterest income up roughly 6%, and noninterest expense excluding integration up 7%; effective tax rate anticipated at 19.5%.
  • Q1 2026 Expectations -- Average loans up 5%, NII up 6%, fee income down 1%-2%, total revenue up 2%-3%, and noninterest expense up 4% (excluding integration costs) compared to the prior year.
  • Continuous Improvement Program -- $350 million cost savings goal achieved for 2025 and targeted again for 2026, funding technology and business investments independently of FirstBank integration.
  • Technology Investments -- CEO Demchak explained, "new initiative CapEx expense...is higher this year than it's ever been," with technology spend projected to increase over 10%; artificial intelligence (AI) is 20% of that increase.
  • Capital Markets and Advisory -- Capital markets revenue rose $57 million or 13% QoQ, driven by M&A advisory; capital markets revenue expected to grow high single digits in 2026.
  • Asset Management and Brokerage -- Increased by $7 million or 2% QoQ, aided by positive markets and net flows.
  • Merger and Integration Costs -- FirstBank integration expenses expected to total approximately $325 million in 2026.
  • Share Count Impact -- Diluted shares outstanding to average about 406 million in Q1 2026, reflecting shares issued for the FirstBank acquisition.

Risks

  • Nonperforming loans increased $81 million or 4% on a linked-quarter basis, as reported by CFO Reilly.
  • Consumer loans declined by $1 billion or 1% for the year, attributed to weakness in residential real estate lending.
  • Provision for loan losses in Q4 totaled $139 million, indicating persistent, though modest, credit costs.

Summary

PNC Financial Services Group (PNC 0.91%) reported record annual results, driven by robust loan and deposit growth, disciplined expense control, and well-executed capital management. Management’s strategic focus is set on technology investment, efficiency initiatives, and completed integration of the FirstBank acquisition—projected to deliver meaningful earnings accretion and further geographic expansion. The outlook for 2026 includes double-digit revenue growth expectations and higher quarterly share repurchases, supported by capital ratios near targeted levels. Guidance assumes solid operating leverage, a significant increase in technology-related spending—especially in cloud and AI capabilities—and careful monitoring of credit quality as business volumes expand.

  • Management indicated FirstBank is expected to contribute an annualized earnings run rate of approximately $1 per share by the end of 2026, with operational efficiencies anticipated to amplify this impact.
  • CEO Demchak stated, "We are national in terms of our presence," outlining the company’s strategic distinction from regional peers and focus on nationwide market share expansion.

Industry glossary

  • CET1 Ratio: Common Equity Tier 1 capital ratio—a regulatory measure of a bank's core capital strength relative to its risk-weighted assets.
  • Operating Leverage: The difference between the rates of revenue growth and expense growth, reflecting efficiency improvements.
  • PPNR: Pre-Provision Net Revenue; income before accounting for credit loss provisions, used to assess underlying profitability.
  • MSR: Mortgage Servicing Rights; an asset representing the contractual right to service mortgage loans for a fee.
  • NPL: Non-Performing Loans—loans on which borrowers are not making interest or principal payments as scheduled.
  • AOCI: Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income; reflects unrealized gains and losses excluded from net income, such as mark-to-market changes on securities.
  • CIP: Continuous Improvement Program; PNC’s enterprise-wide initiative for recurring cost savings and process enhancements.
