Centene (CNC) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 29, 2025 at 10:42AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Oct. 29, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chief Executive Officer — Sarah London

Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Drew Asher

RISKS

Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer Drew Asher reported a $6.7 billion noncash goodwill impairment charge in Q3 2025, resulting in a loss per share of $13.5.

CEO London noted ongoing volatility and uncertainty around the expiration of Enhanced Advanced Premium Tax Credits (EAPTCs), adding, "our view of market contraction for 2026 is in the high teens to mid-thirties range. So again, that's based on sort of all of the different factors that could be at play, but it tells you that even that bottom end where, for example, the program integrity rules remain stayed and EAPTCs are extended, that there is going to be some degree of market contraction. And again, some of that is the roll through of 25 program integrity enrollment hurdles, but also a view that there will be some abrasion and breakage on members who get that letter and don't come back. But we are certainly prepared to do everything we can to go find those members and help them understand in the event of an extension that they do have access to affordable insurance.

TAKEAWAYS

Premium and Service Revenue -- $44.9 billion in premium and service revenue provided a topline basis for performance analysis.

Adjusted EPS -- Adjusted EPS of $0.50, exceeding prior internal expectations and reflecting operational improvements.

Adjusted EPS Guidance -- Adjusted EPS guidance raised to at least $2 from $1.75 for full year 2025, supported by Medicaid and investment income performance.

GAAP Loss per Share -- $13.5 loss per share, driven by a one-time, noncash $6.7 billion goodwill impairment with no impact on cash or adjusted EPS.

Medicaid HBR -- 93.4%, sequentially improved, helped by a $150 million revenue adjustment from the Florida Children's Medical Services program (with $90 million being retroactive and accounting for 40 basis points).

Full-Year Medicaid Composite Rate Adjustment -- 5.5% expected Medicaid composite rate adjustment for 2025 after all scheduled adjustments are finalized, up from a July assumption of 5%.

Medicaid Membership -- 12.7 million members at quarter-end with expected slight attrition in the coming quarters.

Marketplace Membership -- Closed at approximately 5.8 million members, slightly above plan.

Medicare Segment HBR -- 94.3% reported, reflecting cost of care seasonality in Medicare Advantage and Part D.

Cash Flow from Operations -- $1.4 billion in cash flow from operations, primarily from net earnings and timing of payments.

Adjusted SG&A Expense Ratio -- 7% in the third quarter, an improvement from 8.3% in the third quarter of 2024.

Medical Claims Liability -- $21.5 billion at quarter-end, representing 48 days in claims payable (up one day year-over-year).

Marketplace Earnings Impact from Morbidity -- No change to the $2.4 billion pre-tax impact assumption for full-year 2025, as updated Wakely data remains consistent with management's projection.

Marketplace Rate Filing for 2026 -- Rate increases averaged in the mid-30% range for 2026, reflecting pricing for elevated morbidity, trend, EAPTC sunset, and program integrity shifts, approved in states covering 95% of current membership.

Medicare Segment Pretax Favorability -- $700 million pretax favorability for the Medicare segment maintained as the outlook for full-year 2025 relative to the April forecast.

Adjusted EPS Tax Rate -- Lowered temporarily, adding approximately $0.10 to adjusted EPS; full-year 2025 guidance remains at an adjusted rate of 20%-21%.

Unregulated Cash -- $357 million on hand at quarter-end, with $200 million in subsidiary dividends expected in the fourth quarter.

SUMMARY

Centene Corporation (CNC 0.67%) increased its full-year adjusted EPS outlook to at least $2 for 2025, as management cited operational execution, especially within Medicaid, and higher-than-anticipated investment income as key factors supporting the guidance raise. Management highlighted a significant $6.7 billion noncash goodwill impairment, which drove the GAAP per-share loss but does not affect adjusted metrics or statutory capital. Company leaders emphasized disciplined rate-setting and program changes in Medicaid, resulting in sequential HBR improvement and finalized rate uplifts that will partially annualize into 2026. Strategic actions in Marketplace pricing for 2026 anticipate higher morbidity, EAPTC expiration, and program integrity impacts, with expected margin expansion despite the potential for double-digit market contraction based on policy developments. Medicare segment results met prior expectations, underpinned by ongoing Star rating improvements and risk-corridor containment in Part D, supporting stability as the segment targets breakeven in 2027. Management flagged that future guidance for 2026 will incorporate tailwinds from Medicaid rate actions and Marketplace repricing, but also headwinds from unwinding 2025 Part D outperformance, higher taxes, and net investment income normalization. These comments pertain to the company's initial outlook for 2026, as discussed on the third quarter earnings call.

CEO London said, “Marketplace delivered an in-line result for the quarter, but given the uptick in utilization we saw in September, we are holding the remaining $125 million of this provision in Q4 and are adding another $75 million given the volatility around EAPTCs.”

CFO Asher indicated Centene’s debt-to-capital stands at 45.5% post-impairment, well below the 60% covenant limit.

Drew Asher stated management expects “stability in that HBR relative to 93.7 for full year 2025. Expecting to be able to maintain that, in 2026 is the starting point of our forecasting for 2026.”

Vice President Asher reported zero utilization of the company’s $4 billion revolver maturing in 2030, reinforcing ample liquidity.

Centene does not anticipate any significant impact from Medicaid work requirements in 2026 but is monitoring regulatory developments for possible effects in 2027 and beyond.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

HBR (Health Benefits Ratio): Medical costs as a percentage of premium revenue, a key measure of medical cost management effectiveness in managed care.

EAPTC (Enhanced Advanced Premium Tax Credits): Temporary policy enhancement providing extra premium subsidies for Affordable Care Act marketplace enrollees, with uncertain renewal prospects.

PDP (Prescription Drug Plan): Medicare Part D plans offering prescription drug coverage, separate from Medicare Advantage medical coverage.

SG&A (Selling, General, and Administrative expenses): Operating expenses not directly tied to the delivery of care, serving as an efficiency metric.

Retroactive Adjustment (“Retro”): Revenue or cost adjustment reflecting changes for a prior period, often due to contract reconciliation or regulatory update.

Medicaid Composite Rate Adjustment: Weighted average rate change resulting from all Medicaid contract pricing updates within the period.

Star Ratings (Medicare): CMS-issued quality scores for Medicare Advantage and Part D plans, directly influencing plan bonuses and patient choice.
