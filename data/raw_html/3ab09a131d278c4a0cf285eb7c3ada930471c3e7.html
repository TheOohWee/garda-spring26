PureTech (PRTC) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Sep 2, 2025 at 5:23PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, August 28, 2025, at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Interim Chief Executive Officer — Robert Lyne
  • Co-Founder and President — Eric Elenko
  • Chief Accounting Officer — Michael Inbar
  • General Counsel — Chip Sherwood
  • Chief Executive Officer, Gallop Oncology — Luba Greenwood
  • Chief Executive Officer, Celea Therapeutics — Sven Dethlefs

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Cash Position-- Just under $320 million in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments as of the end of Q2 2025, with self‑funding operations maintaining "operational runway well into 2028."
  • Operating Expenses-- Operating expenses totaled just under $50 million for the first half of 2025. This figure is down from $66.7 million in the first half of 2024 due to Seaport's spinout, with R&D and G&A expected to continue decreasing as Gallop and Celea transition.
  • Seaport Therapeutics Stake-- PureTech holds a 35.1% equity stake in Seaport Therapeutics, with PureTech also entitled to tiered royalties (3%-5%), as well as milestone and sublicense payments on Seaport Therapeutics programs.
  • Cobenfy Economics-- PureTech receives 2% royalties on annual Cobenfy sales exceeding $2 billion, and is eligible for certain regulatory and commercial milestones; Consensus analyst modeling, based on third-party forecasts, indicates approximately $300 million in potential value to PureTech through 2033 (non-IFRS, based on consensus analyst forecasts for Cobenfy sales through 2033).
  • Core Founded Entities Transition-- Gallop Oncology and Celea Therapeutics remain 100% owned by PureTech but are actively seeking external funding, which will transfer R&D costs off PureTech’s balance sheet upon completion.
  • Deupirfenidone ELEVATE Data-- Celea reported that the ELEVATE Phase IIb study in IPF, completed in December 2024, demonstrated that deupirfenidone 825mg TID produced a 50% greater treatment effect versus pirfenidone and only a 32.8ml decline in lung function over 52 weeks, a level comparable to healthy older adults.
  • Phase III Planning-- End of Phase II FDA meeting for LYT-100 is anticipated at the end of Q3 2025, with Phase III initiation targeted for the first half of 2026, if alignment is achieved (as stated by management on the Q2 2025 earnings call).
  • Gallop Oncology Milestones-- LYT-200 has completed Phase Ib enrollment for AML and high-risk MDS, demonstrated clinical benefit as both monotherapy and in combination, and is expected to report top‑line efficacy results in Q4 2025 with additional data in the first half of 2026.
  • Cost Allocation Strategy-- Management reiterated disciplined asset development, using external capital for progression and discontinuing projects not showing promising results early to limit exposure.
  • Planned Board Additions-- Up to two new nonexecutive directors with U.K. capital markets expertise will be appointed to strengthen PureTech’s board.

SUMMARY

PureTech Health (PRTC +0.36%) emphasized that a near-$320 million cash position as of Q2 2025, along with reduced operating expenses, has contributed to a strong operational runway well into 2028, while the company’s three core founded entities—Seaport Therapeutics, Gallop Oncology, and Celea Therapeutics—represent significant sources of future value and follow the company's hub-and-spoke externalization strategy. Management disclosed precise equity holdings and external valuations for Seaport, clarified the details and third-party consensus estimates of Cobenfy's economics, and shared detailed clinical data supporting deupirfenidone’s potentially superior efficacy in IPF. The call outlined concrete regulatory and data-readout timelines for both LYT-100 and LYT-200, reflected ongoing governance changes tailored to U.K. capital market priorities.

  • Management affirmed that shifting R&D spend to newly independent entities would further extend the existing 2028+ cash runway.
  • PureTech cited "very high-quality independent analysts" projecting Cobenfy sales through 2033, providing neutral third-party grounding for long-term royalty and milestone value estimation. These projections are based on non-IFRS (non-GAAP) measures, as clarified in the company’s financial disclosures.
  • Gallop Oncology's LYT-200 program received FDA Fast Track and Orphan Drug designations in AML, and its Phase Ib trials in solid tumors showed complete responses and stable disease, increasing the pool of partnered and indication opportunities.
  • Questions from investors confirmed that upcoming external financings for Gallop and Celea are limited to institutional investors, with transaction structures modeled after traditional private biotech rounds rather than open shareholder participation.
  • Management clarified that PureTech retains 100% ownership of Gallop and Celea prior to external funding, with public disclosure of fully diluted interests and valuation data points expected as transactions close.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • IPF: Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis, a progressive lung disease with limited treatment options and poor prognosis.
  • Deupirfenidone: A deuterated version of pirfenidone intended to provide improved efficacy and tolerability in IPF.
  • Fast Track Designation: An FDA status granted to facilitate the development of drugs treating serious conditions with unmet medical need.
  • Orphan Drug Designation: An FDA designation providing incentives for development of drugs for rare diseases affecting fewer than 200,000 patients in the U.S.
  • AML: Acute Myeloid Leukemia, an aggressive blood cancer and a lead indication for LYT-200.
  • ELEVATE Study: Phase IIb clinical trial of deupirfenidone in IPF conducted by Celea Therapeutics.
  • Glyph Platform: Proprietary PureTech technology enabling drugs to be "cloaked" as dietary fats, facilitating oral delivery and potentially reducing side effects.
  • TREK-2, LYT-100, LYT-200: PureTech program designations; LYT-100 (deupirfenidone) in IPF, LYT-200 in AML/solid tumors.
  • G&A Expense: General and Administrative Expenses, a category of operating costs exclusive of R&D.
