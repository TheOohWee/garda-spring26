Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $1.96 billion, up 7%, driven by both HOKA and UGG brand performance.
  • HOKA revenue -- $629 million, up 18%, with balanced growth across direct-to-consumer (DTC) and wholesale channels.
  • UGG revenue -- $1.3 billion, up 5%, achieving a record quarterly level.
  • International markets growth -- HOKA and UGG combined revenue rose 15% internationally and 5% in the United States.
  • HOKA DTC revenue -- Up 19% complemented by 18% wholesale growth, indicating broad channel momentum.
  • UGG DTC revenue -- Increased 5% as wholesale grew 4%, reflecting effective inventory allocation strategies.
  • Gross margin -- 59.8%, exceeding expectations due to lower than anticipated tariff effects and high full-price sales.
  • SG&A expense -- $557 million (28.5% of revenue), rising 4% with leverage aided by favorable foreign currency.
  • Diluted EPS -- $3.33, up 11% or 33¢ from last year, setting a record for the company.
  • Inventory -- $633 million, up 10%, including tariff costs on inventory received in the period.
  • Cash and equivalents -- $2.1 billion with no outstanding borrowings reported at quarter end.
  • Share repurchase -- $349 million in Q3 at an average $92.36 per share, contributing to approximately 8 million shares repurchased year-to-date.
  • Remaining buyback authorization -- $1.8 billion available for future share repurchases.
  • Fiscal 2026 revenue guidance -- Increased to $5.4 billion-$5.425 billion; HOKA to grow mid-teens percent, UGG mid-single digits.
  • Fiscal 2026 gross margin guidance -- Raised to ~57%, boosted by reduced net tariff impact.
  • Fiscal 2026 operating margin guidance -- Lifted to ~22.5%, 100 basis points above prior guidance.
  • Fiscal 2026 EPS guidance -- Now $6.80-$6.85, reflecting a 7%-8% increase.
  • Tariffs net impact -- Now expected at $25 million for fiscal 2026, mainly due to favorable timing and pricing actions.
  • Q4 HOKA growth guidance -- Expected at 13%-14%, with Q4 forecast to mark HOKA's highest-ever quarterly revenue.
  • Q4 UGG guidance -- Anticipated flat, as some orders shifted forward from Q4 to Q3.
  • Membership programs -- HOKA’s new membership initiative increased average revenue per user and unit transaction metrics.
  • Brand market share -- HOKA gained US road running category share above $140, and maintained top two specialty status in run channels.
  • Koolaburra brand phase-out -- Declines in the company’s overall segment revenue stemmed from exiting the Koolaburra brand.
  • No material exposure to Saks -- Management confirmed little or no risk from Saks as a wholesale partner.

Summary

The call revealed decisive upward revisions to Deckers Outdoor (DECK 1.74%)'s annual guidance in revenue, gross margin, and earnings, with all outlook improvements directly attributed to stronger-than-expected brand momentum and disciplined channel management. Management emphasized sustained DTC growth fueled by successful loyalty and membership programs, especially for HOKA, and clarified that recent outperformance was achieved without over-reliance on promotions. Strategic investments in pricing, inventory, and product innovation were cited as central to maintaining record profitability and market share expansion in priority performance and lifestyle categories. Company leadership highlighted robust international progress, signaled further capital return via continued share buybacks, and set expectations for ongoing channel balance and controlled expansion into fiscal 2027.

  • Management stated the UGG and HOKA brands “both actively scaling their respective addressable audiences through category expansion," underlining plans for market share capture and entry into new global segments.
  • The call specified that Deckers' higher-margin DTC channel would be used to manage end-of-season inventory, aiming to preserve future sell-through rates and launch effectiveness.
  • Steve Fasching highlighted, “we are on track to repurchase more than $1 billion in total by the end of the year, which is expected to contribute more than 20¢ of diluted earnings per share improvement.”
  • Stefano Caroti said, “the brands did show up, and this increases our optimism going into next year,” indicating confidence in consumer demand resilience despite generalized macro caution.
  • Deckers identified further distribution expansion opportunities across geographies, stating HOKA is represented in less than 20% of relevant European athletic specialty stores and occupies “a little less than one-third” of potential in China.

Industry glossary

  • DTC (Direct-to-Consumer): Sales made directly to customers via the company’s own retail or e-commerce channels, bypassing traditional wholesale distribution.
  • Sell-through: The percentage of inventory sold to end consumers relative to the amount shipped to retailers; an indicator of demand health and channel inventory quality.
  • Run specialty channel: A retail distribution segment focused on technical performance running footwear and apparel, often serving as a credibility builder for performance brands.
