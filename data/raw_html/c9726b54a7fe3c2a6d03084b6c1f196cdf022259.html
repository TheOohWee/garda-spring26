By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 7:54PM EST

Call participants

Takeaways

  • Total billings -- $2.37 billion, up 18%, led by strong growth in Unified SASE, OT security, and large enterprise deals in the U.S. and Europe.
  • Unified SASE billings -- Increased 40% and now represent 27% of total billings, supported by adoption across both Sovereign and Public SASE use cases.
  • Operational technology billings -- Rose more than 25%, reflecting customer demand for security solutions in industrial environments.
  • Large deal metrics -- Number of deals above $1 million grew over 30%, with aggregate value up more than 40%, and largest contributions from the U.S. and Europe.
  • Customer additions -- 7,200 new organizations selected the Unified FortiOS platform during the period.
  • Annual recurring revenue (ARR) -- Increased 21%; Unified SASE ARR reached $1.28 billion (up 11%), with FortiSASE ARR rising over 90%, and SecOps ARR up 21% to $491 million.
  • Total revenue -- $1.91 billion, up 15%; product revenue was $691 million, up over 20%, and service revenue was $1.21 billion, up 12%.
  • Hardware and software growth -- Both segments grew 20%, aided by technology upgrades, upsell, and expansion into new use cases.
  • Total gross margin -- 80.3%, exceeding expectations despite product mix shift.
  • Operating margin -- 37.3%, surpassing guidance on stronger-than-expected revenue and cost discipline.
  • Free cash flow -- $577 million, with adjusted free cash flow of $589 million, and a margin of 31%.
  • Share repurchase activity -- 730,000 shares repurchased for $57 million during the quarter, and 4.6 million more purchased for $356 million quarter-to-date; board approved a $1 billion increase in authorization, leaving $1.4 billion remaining.
  • Full-year billings -- $7.55 billion, up 16%.
  • Full-year revenue -- $6.8 billion, up 14%; product revenue advanced 16%, and service revenue 13% to $4.58 billion, 67% of total revenue.
  • Full-year gross margin -- 81.3%.
  • Full-year operating margin -- Increased 50 basis points to 35.5%; GAAP margin was 30.7%.
  • Full-year EPS -- $2.76, up 16%.
  • Full-year free cash flow -- Record of $2.21 billion with 33% margin; adjusted free cash flow was $2.5 billion, or 37% margin.
  • Rule of 45 -- Outperformed for sixth straight year.
  • Pricing actions -- CFO Ohlgart said, "Overall, it's -- I think it's between 5% and 20%," referring to appliance price increases, with service pricing tied to product list price.
  • Billings duration -- CFO Ohlgart noted, "it was slightly up, it's around 2.5 years" due to higher enterprise deal activity.
  • Service revenue outlook -- Guidance anticipates stronger second-half 2026 service revenue as higher recent product billings convert to services.
  • Q1 guidance -- Billings expected between $1.77 billion and $1.87 billion (14% growth midpoint); revenue between $1.7 billion and $1.76 billion (12% growth midpoint); non-GAAP gross margin of 80%-81%; non-GAAP operating margin of 30%-32%; non-GAAP EPS of $0.59-$0.63.
  • Full-year 2026 guidance -- Billings $8.4 billion to $8.6 billion (13% growth midpoint); revenue $7.5 billion to $7.7 billion (12% growth midpoint); service revenue $5.05-$5.15 billion (11% growth midpoint); non-GAAP gross margin 79%-81%; non-GAAP operating margin 33%-36%; non-GAAP EPS $2.94-$3.00.
  • Innovation initiatives -- Forthcoming product launches include FortiOS 8.0 and bundled SD-WAN/SASE features; partnership with NVIDIA, and introduction of a new ASIC chip are expected drivers.

Risks

  • CFO Ohlgart warned, "the recent weakness of the U.S. dollar may create a modest headwind in the first quarter."
  • CFO Ohlgart noted, "we plan to repay the first tranche in the amount of $500 million of our senior debt at maturity at the end of the first quarter. This, alongside lower market interest rates, will reduce net interest income for the year."

Summary

Fortinet (FTNT 4.91%) delivered double-digit billings and revenue growth, supported by adoption of Unified SASE and operational technology security, and sustained platform expansion in large enterprises. Management reaffirmed midterm growth targets above market rates and the Rule of 45, highlighting an integrated approach that leverages proprietary ASIC and OS technologies to increase platform stickiness and support long-term profitability. The call outlined expectations for continued service revenue acceleration in the second half of 2026 and described upcoming innovation catalysts, including FortiOS 8.0, enhanced AI security features, and leveraging AI infrastructure partnerships.

  • CFO Ohlgart stated, "service pricing is a percent of list price," indicating recent pricing actions may flow through to services revenue with a lag.
  • CEO Xie emphasized, "Sovereign SASE usually buy the product first, then deploying the customer or service provider data center and then where we're keeping supporting with additional service," clarifying differentiation versus peers and the pathway to recurring revenue.
  • The Q&A clarified Fortinet's approach to managing semiconductor memory exposure, with CEO Xie stating memory comprises "10% to 20% cost," and six months of inventory is maintained to buffer supply risks.
  • Enterprise refresh cycles are expected to generate incremental demand by combining hardware upgrades with adjacent platform solutions, particularly SD-WAN and SASE.
  • Management reaffirmed a disciplined M&A approach focused on technology or talent acquisitions, rather than acquiring for customer base scale.

Industry glossary

  • Unified SASE: Secure Access Service Edge offerings that converge networking and security functions, delivered either via public cloud or customer-operated (Sovereign) infrastructure.
  • OT: Operational Technology; hardware and software systems that monitor and control industrial devices and processes.
  • FortiOS: Fortinet’s proprietary integrated operating system unifying security and networking functions across its platform.
  • SD-WAN: Software-Defined Wide Area Network; technology automating and managing secure enterprise WANs.
  • ASIC: Application-Specific Integrated Circuit; custom hardware designed by Fortinet to accelerate security and networking operations.
  • PoP: Point of Presence; network access points enabling global distributed service delivery.
  • Rule of 45: A performance metric defined by Fortinet as the sum of revenue growth rate and operating margin equaling or exceeding 45%.
  • SecOps: Security Operations; tools and processes for detecting, responding to, and remediating cybersecurity threats.
  • FortiSASE: Fortinet’s branded SASE platform, integrating firewall, networking, and cloud-based security services.
