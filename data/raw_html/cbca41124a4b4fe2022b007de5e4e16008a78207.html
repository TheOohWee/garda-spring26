Shopify (SHOP) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 23, 2025 at 11:24AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, August 6, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President — Harley Finkelstein
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jeff J. Hoffmeister
  • Director, Investor Relations — Carrie Gillard

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $2.7 billion, up 31% year over year, driven by broad-based strength across geographies and verticals.
  • Gross Merchandise Volume (GMV) -- $88 billion, representing 31% growth (29% on a constant currency basis), with particularly strong performance among Plus merchants and in Europe.
  • Free Cash Flow Margin -- 16% for the quarter, marking eleven consecutive quarters of positive free cash flow, eight of which were double-digit.
  • International GMV Growth -- Up 42% year over year, led by Europe, which achieved 49% reported and 42% constant currency GMV growth, outpacing local e-commerce markets by 4-5x.
  • B2B GMV -- Increased 101% year over year, signaling material adoption in new merchant segments.
  • Offline GMV -- Rose 29% year over year, supported by new large retailers joining the platform and enhancements to Point of Sale software.
  • Shop Pay GMV -- Up 65% to $27 billion, with the user base more than doubling over a 2.5-year period.
  • Shop App Native GMV -- Surged 140% year over year; Shop Week sales more than doubled from last year's event.
  • Cross-Border GMV -- Represented 15% of total GMV, unchanged from past quarters.
  • Payment Penetration -- Global payments penetration reached 64%, climbing from 61% a year ago due to launches in 16 new countries.
  • Merchant Solutions Revenue -- Grew 37%, the majority driven by GMV expansion and increasing payments adoption.
  • Subscription Solutions Revenue -- Increased 17%, with a larger percentage of subscriptions from higher-priced plans and some headwind from extended paid trials.
  • Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) -- Rose 9%, supported by Plus plans which accounted for 35% of total MRR.
  • Gross Profit -- Increased 25%, exceeding internal expectations based on revenue performance, though overall gross margin declined to 48.6% from 51.1% last year due primarily to ongoing payments mix shifts.
  • Subscription Solutions Gross Margin -- Came in at 81.6%, remaining above the five-year historical median.
  • Merchant Solutions Gross Margin -- Registered at 37.9%, down from 39.1% the prior year, mainly attributable to partnership impacts.
  • Operating Expenses -- Totaled $1 billion or 38% of revenue, down from 39% in the prior year and reflecting continued operating leverage.
  • Operating Income -- Reported at $291 million, or 11% of revenue, a 56% year-over-year growth rate when adjusted for last year's legal accrual.
  • Stock-Based Compensation -- Recorded at $120 million in the quarter, with Q3 expected at $130 million.
  • Capital Business -- Expanded to Germany and the Netherlands, with loss rates unchanged from prior periods.
  • Shop Pay Installments GMV -- Increased 38%, aided by launches in new geographies and flexible payment offering adoption.
  • Q3 Outlook -- Revenue growth expected in the mid- to high-20% range and free cash flow margin projected in the mid- to high teens; operating expenses anticipated at 38%-39% of revenue.
  • Convertible Note Settlement -- $920 million principal to be settled in cash before next earnings call.
  • Product Innovation -- New launches included Universal Cart (early access), Catalog API, and AI-powered Sidekick and Store Builder, with Microsoft Copilot already integrating Checkout Kit.
  • Notable Merchant Wins -- Recently signed global brands Starbucks, Canada Goose, Burton Snowboards, Michael Kors, Miele, Woot, Beachbody, Signet Jewelers, and Boart Longyear, diversifying across verticals and geographies.

SUMMARY

Shopify (SHOP 1.42%) reported accelerating GMV and revenue growth rates across all major regions, with particular outperformance in Europe and among both larger and smaller merchants. Management emphasized that increased global payments penetration and continued product localization are driving merchant adoption and engagement, particularly as new geographies are unlocked. Innovations in agentic commerce and AI, as well as new platform components like Universal Cart and enhanced Checkout integrations, are cited as competitive differentiation and catalysts for future growth. Strategic wins with both consumer-facing and industrial enterprise clients underscore ongoing diversification and expansion of the total addressable market.

  • Shopify cited no observed demand pull-forward or tariff-related distortions in transaction volume, maintaining growth momentum into July.
  • The company noted only about 4% of GMV globally depends on de minimis import exemptions, with little impact from recent regulatory changes.
  • Extended paid trials affected MRR growth for standard merchants, as most new merchants remained within trial periods in the quarter.
  • Marketing investments are increasing year over year, focused on performance marketing, supported by "continued improvements in internal attribution and segmentation models."
  • Shopify highlighted success in new verticals such as mining/drilling services and emphasized B2B and large enterprise client wins as contributors to platform resilience.
  • Leadership reiterated the view that the company's business model aligns revenue and merchant success, reinforcing durable, long-term growth.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Plus merchants: Large-scale Shopify clients using the enterprise-grade Shopify Plus platform with advanced commerce features.
  • GMV (Gross Merchandise Volume): The total dollar value of orders processed via the Shopify platform in a given period, before deductions for returns or discounts.
  • Shop Pay: Shopify's proprietary accelerated checkout and payments solution available to merchants and consumers transacting on the platform.
  • De minimis exemption: A customs rule in the U.S. and other countries allowing low-value goods (below a prescribed threshold) to be imported without duties or formal customs clearance.
  • Agentic commerce: Commerce conducted via AI agents or digital assistants that interact conversationally with shoppers and orchestrate transactions.
  • Checkout Kit: A set of developer tools enabling partners to embed Shopify merchant checkouts directly within third-party applications or AI agents.
  • Universal Cart: A feature allowing shoppers to aggregate products from multiple stores into a single checkout experience, regardless of merchant.
  • Catalog API: An interface allowing real-time programmatic access to aggregated product data from Shopify’s global merchant base.
