Additionally, during the third quarter, we had $0.015 per share of favorability, resulting primarily from higher fee income, lower interest expense, and lower income tax expense.

Property revenues for the quarter were in line with our expectations. We are increasing the midpoint of our full-year core FFO from $6.79 to $6.81, which results entirely from the nonproperty component of our third-quarter outperformance. We also provided earnings guidance for the fourth quarter of 2024. This $0.01 per share increase in sequential property NOI is entirely offset by a combined $0.015 per share decrease in interest and other income, as we are no longer in a net cash position and fee and asset management income due to the timing of our third-party construction activity.
