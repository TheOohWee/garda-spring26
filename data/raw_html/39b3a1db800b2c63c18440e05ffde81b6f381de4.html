Cerus (CERS) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 2, 2026 at 5:29PM EST

March 2, 2026, 4:30 p.m. ET

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Obi Greenman
  • Chief Operating Officer — Vivek K. Jayaraman
  • Chief Financial Officer — Kevin D. Green
  • Vice President, Corporate Affairs — Tim Lee

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $233.8 million, a record annual result, up 16% from 2024.
  • Product Revenue -- Grew 14% for both the fourth quarter and full year, exceeding the top end of $220 million-$240 million prior guidance.
  • EMEA Product Revenue Growth -- Increased 25% in Q4 and 14% for the year, excluding currency impacts, with double-digit growth in both platelet and plasma kit sales.
  • FOREX Impact -- Provided a benefit of approximately 3% for Q4 and 1.6% for the full year on a consolidated basis.
  • IFC Q4 Product Revenue -- $4.2 million, up 40% from Q4 2024; full-year IFC product sales of $16.7 million, up 80%, with underlying volume demand rising about 110% due to a sales shift toward kits.
  • Installed INT 100 Base in EMEA -- Approximately 400 devices expected to be fully replaced by INT 200 systems over several years.
  • U.S. Platelet Franchise Market Share -- Estimated in the mid-60% range; BCA group purchasing agreement penetration currently about 30% for platelets across BCA centers.
  • Germany Opportunity -- DRK Baden-Württemberg-Hessen initiated the INITIATE Phase 4 study; expected $30 million in annual platelet market potential with initial revenue contribution projected for 2027.
  • 2026 Product Revenue Guidance -- Range reaffirmed at $224 million-$228 million, implying 9%-11% year-over-year growth, excluding potential tariff effects.
  • 2026 IFC Revenue Guidance -- Expected at $20 million-$22 million, targeting 20%-30% growth, with current U.S. IFC adoption at ~7% and anticipated penetration rate increase of over 50% during 2026.
  • Product Gross Margin -- 51.5% for Q4, down from 53.9% year over year, impacted by higher IFC production costs, import tariffs, and inflation; management expects low-50% range to continue in 2026, with variability possible.
  • Operating Expenses -- Increased 7% in Q4 and 10% for the year, attributed mainly to development costs for the red blood cell program, largely reimbursed by BARDA.
  • Government-Reimbursed R&D -- BARDA contract expires September 2026; R&D reimbursements and associated revenues will decrease over the year as major trial work concludes.
  • GAAP Net Loss Attributable -- $2.2 million for Q4 and $15.6 million for the year, a 25% year-over-year reduction.
  • Non-GAAP Adjusted EBITDA -- $3.4 million for Q4 (seventh straight positive quarter); $9.5 million for the year, marking a second consecutive year of positive results.
  • Cash and Short-Term Investments -- Nearly $83 million at year-end, with full-year operating cash flow of $4.8 million, including $6.2 million generated in Q4.
  • Regulatory Updates -- INT 200 received CE Mark and was commercially launched in Europe; U.S. PMA submission planned for mid-2026. For red blood cells, European regulatory review has advanced to ANSM; U.S. Phase 3 REDIS trial completed enrollment, with results expected by year-end.

SUMMARY

Cerus Corporation (CERS 0.52%) set new records for total and product revenue, led by EMEA and international platelet and plasma kit sales, and shifted a substantial share of IFC revenue to a kit-based model. The company reaffirmed its 2026 product revenue guidance and expects continued gross margin pressure from tariffs and inflation, but projects positive adjusted EBITDA for a third year. Regulatory milestones for INT 200 and the red blood cell program advanced, with European submissions progressing and U.S. trial enrollment completed. The new BCA agreement provides a framework for greater INTERCEPT adoption, especially in U.S. platelets and IFC, with inquiries and participation from additional BCA centers already underway.

  • Approximately 3 million INTERCEPT-treated blood components were produced for almost 600,000 patients in nearly 40 countries in 2025.
  • The EMEA region’s installed base of ~400 INT 100 devices is expected to be fully replaced with INT 200 units over several years as part of the technology rollout.
  • BCA—the largest U.S. blood supply cooperative—now covers about 50% of the nation's supply, representing the company's core near-term platelet growth opportunity.
  • Germany's INITIATE study is expected to provide real-world clinical and reimbursement evidence, initially contributing revenue during the study period and driving larger revenue impacts post-2027 pending completion.
  • Cash flow generation continues, with management indicating ongoing operating cash flow in 2026, supported by inventory investments for forecasted demand.
  • IFC product demand outpaced revenue growth due to the transition from direct sales to kit sales, a model management expects to be nearly exclusive by year-end.
  • BCA agreement and regional education efforts have led to expanded clinical engagement and early new customer inquiries.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • INTERCEPT Blood System: Cerus Corporation’s proprietary pathogen inactivation platform for platelets, plasma, red blood cells, and cryoprecipitation, designed to enhance blood safety in transfusion medicine.
  • IFC: INTERCEPT Fibrinogen Complex, a pathogen-reduced cryoprecipitated fibrinogen product for transfusion in bleeding and fibrinogen deficiency cases.
  • BCA: Blood Centers of America, a cooperative representing approximately half of the U.S. blood supply network, serving as a key contract partner for Cerus Corporation’s product access and adoption.
  • INT 100 / INT 200: First- and second-generation illuminator devices for the INTERCEPT Blood System; INT 200 utilizes LED technology and supports next-stage product innovation and operational efficiency.
  • BARDA: Biomedical Advanced Research and Development Authority, a U.S. government entity funding advanced R&D and clinical work, including Cerus Corporation’s red blood cell programs.
  • ANSM: French National Agency for Medicines and Health Products Safety, the competent authority reviewing certain EU regulatory dossiers for medical devices and pharmaceuticals.
  • DRK Baden-Württemberg-Hessen: The largest blood center in Germany; sponsor of the INITIATE post-market platelet study using INTERCEPT technology.
