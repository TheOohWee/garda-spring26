Karyopharm (KPTI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 11:28AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Feb. 12, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Richard Paulson
  • Chief Medical Officer — Reshma Rangwala
  • Chief Commercial Officer — Sohanya Cheng
  • Chief Financial Officer — Lori Macomber
  • Vice President, Corporate Communications and Investor Relations — Brendan Strong

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $34.1 million for the quarter, reflecting an 11.8% increase; full year revenue was $146.1 million, a slight year-over-year increase.
  • U.S. XPOVIO net product revenue -- $32.1 million in the quarter (up 9.6%) and $114.9 million for the full year (up 1.9%).
  • 2026 guidance -- Total revenue projected at $130 million to $150 million; U.S. XPOVIO net product revenue guidance set at $115 million to $130 million.
  • Operating expenses -- Research and development expense was $27.7 million for the quarter (down 17%) and $125.6 million for the year (down 12%); selling, general, and administrative expense was $22.8 million for the quarter (down 16%) and $105.2 million for the year (down 9%).
  • Cash position -- Ended 2025 with $64.1 million in cash, cash equivalents, restricted cash, and investments, compared to $109.1 million at prior year-end.
  • Interest and other expenses -- Full year interest expense increased to $45.8 million, reflecting higher outstanding debt and interest rates; $62.4 million loss on extinguishment of debt was recorded in 2025.
  • Net loss -- $102.2 million, or $5.71 per share, for the quarter; $196.0 million, or $17.93 per diluted share, for the full year; more than half of the full year loss was driven by non-operational, noncash items below the line.
  • Cash runway -- Guidance states cash is expected to fund operations into the second quarter of 2026.
  • Upcoming data readouts -- Phase III SENTRY trial (selinexor/ruxolitinib in myelofibrosis) top-line data expected March 2026; Phase III EXPORT EC042 trial in endometrial cancer top-line data expected mid-2026.
  • Myelofibrosis opportunity -- SENTRY trial baseline Total Symptom Score (TSS) of approximately 22.5, potentially the highest in a frontline myelofibrosis Phase III study; combination strategy intends to improve upon roughly one third SVR35 response rate with ruxolitinib alone.
  • Dose selection -- 60 mg selinexor identified as providing optimal efficacy and tolerability compared to 40 mg in myelofibrosis (per Phase I data), leading to its selection for ongoing Phase III.
  • Endometrial cancer trial design -- EXPORT EC042 focuses on p53 wild-type endometrial cancer, assessing progression-free survival as primary endpoint; accrual of PFS events is on track.
  • Commercial execution -- XPOVIO demand in 2025 was stable, with approximately 60% of sales derived from community settings; company highlights launch readiness for myelofibrosis franchise pending positive pivotal data.
  • Strategic focus -- Management emphasizes disciplined capital allocation, evaluation of financing and strategic options, and alignment of spend to near-term milestones.
  • Licensing revenue -- $15.0 million R&D reimbursement from Menarini was received in 2025, completing this revenue source; remaining 2025 licensing revenue totaled $16.2 million, generated through royalties and milestones (primarily from Menarini and other international partners).

Risks

  • Chief Financial Officer Lori Macomber said, "2025 was the last year we will receive this reimbursement," signaling a drop in future licensing revenue.
  • Interest expense rose to $45.8 million for the year, attributed to higher debt and refinancing, while a $62.4 million loss on extinguishment of debt contributed to a higher net loss in 2025; these factors may pressure the company's financial flexibility.
  • Management guided that existing liquidity will fund operations only into the second quarter of 2026, indicating an imminent need to secure additional financing or strategic alternatives.

Summary

Karyopharm Therapeutics (KPTI 1.96%) reported double-digit quarterly revenue growth and a stable commercial base for XPOVIO, but signaled rising debt-related expenses and diminishing nonrecurring revenue streams. Cash burn continues, with management projecting existing resources to support operations only into the second quarter, making near-term pivotal clinical data readouts in myelofibrosis and endometrial cancer essential for future growth and financing strategy.

  • Chief Medical Officer Reshma Rangwala stated, "our Phase I data suggests that the combination could more than double the SVR35 rate at 79%," underscoring differentiation potential over ruxolitinib monotherapy in myelofibrosis.
  • Chief Commercial Officer Sohanya Cheng reported, "our peak revenue opportunity may approach $1 billion annually in the U.S. alone." for frontline myelofibrosis, based on market size and physician intent surveys.
  • Research and development, as well as selling, general, and administrative expenses, declined year over year, reflecting strict cost control and portfolio prioritization around late-stage assets.
  • The endometrial cancer program's prior Phase III SIENDO trial demonstrated a median progression-free survival benefit of 39.5 months (hazard ratio 0.36) within a biomarker-defined subgroup, providing a foundation for the current ongoing Phase III study design.
  • License and royalty revenue from Menarini and other partners amounted to $31.2 million in 2025 but is expected to decline due to the end of R&D reimbursements.

Industry glossary

  • SVR35: Spleen Volume Reduction of at least 35%, a regulatory and clinical endpoint in myelofibrosis studies used to assess treatment efficacy.
  • TSS: Total Symptom Score, a quantitative assessment tool for symptom burden in myelofibrosis clinical trials.
  • SINE compounds: Selective Inhibitors of Nuclear Export compounds, a class of drugs that target the nuclear export protein XPO1.
  • XPO1: Exportin 1, a nuclear export protein and target for anti-cancer therapy in various malignancies.
  • p53 wild-type: Refers to tumors retaining the normal (non-mutated) p53 gene, with implications for therapy response in oncology.
  • Myelofibrosis: A chronic hematologic malignancy characterized by bone marrow fibrosis and proliferation of abnormal blood cells.
  • JAK inhibitor: A drug class that blocks Janus kinase proteins, standard treatment in myelofibrosis and related disorders.
  • PFS: Progression-Free Survival, a clinical endpoint measuring the length of time during and after treatment that a patient lives with the disease but it does not get worse.
  • R&D reimbursement: Payments from a partner offsetting research and development costs on collaborative programs.
  • SENTRY: The company’s Phase III trial of selinexor plus ruxolitinib in frontline myelofibrosis.
  • EXPORT EC042: The Phase III study of selinexor as maintenance therapy in p53 wild-type endometrial cancer.
