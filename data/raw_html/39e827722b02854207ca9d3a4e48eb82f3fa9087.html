Earnings Call Transcript

Independent Bank (IBCP) Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 23, 2026 at 10:26AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, October 24, 2024 at 11:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Brad Kessel
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Gavin Mohr
  • Executive President and Head of Commercial Banking — Joel Rahn

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Income -- $13.8 million, or $0.65 per diluted share, down from $17.5 million, or $0.83 per diluted share, in the prior year’s quarter.
  • Loan Growth -- Total loans increased by $90 million, or 9.3% annualized, with commercial loans rising $93 million, mortgage loans up $10 million, and installment loans down $12.5 million.
  • Deposit Growth -- Core deposits grew 8.9% annualized; total deposits reached $4.6 billion at quarter end. Retail deposits declined $21.3 million, business deposits rose $16.7 million, and municipal deposits increased $105.2 million (component change does not sum to total deposit growth).
  • Net Interest Income -- Increased $2.4 million from the prior year; net interest margin was 3.37%, up from 3.23% a year ago and down three basis points from the prior quarter.
  • Non-Interest Income -- $9.5 million, below the forecasted range of $11.5 million to $13 million, impacted negatively by a $3.1 million loss on mortgage loan servicing.
  • Non-Interest Expense -- $32.6 million, within the targeted forecast of $32.5 million to $33.5 million.
  • Credit Quality -- Nonperforming loans were $5.1 million (13 basis points of total loans), while past due loans were $4.8 million (12 basis points), both consistent with the prior quarter.
  • Commercial Portfolio Metrics -- 67% C&I lending, with manufacturing as the largest C&I concentration at 9% ($172 million); investment real estate at 33% of the commercial portfolio, led by industrial at 8% ($153 million); office exposure was $86 million (4.7% of commercial loans), primarily in low-rise suburban and medical offices.
  • Provision for Credit Losses -- $1.5 million expense (15 basis points annualized of average loans), attributed directly to loan growth.
  • Tangible Book Value -- Increased by 22% year over year.
  • Yield on New Commercial Loans -- 6.78% as the highest-yielding portfolio.
  • Deposit Costs -- Total funding costs increased 8 basis points to 2.10% this quarter; management stated, "we have seen a peak with the recent Fed move."
  • Regulatory Capital -- All capital ratios increased sequentially; no new share repurchases during the quarter.
  • Technology Investments -- Performance-based compensation rose by $25 million due to higher expected incentive payout, and data processing expenses increased $0.3 million, related to core processor and new solutions.
  • Outlook -- Management anticipates continued margin expansion and loan growth, supported by a "solid commercial pipeline" and ongoing hiring of commercial bankers.

SUMMARY

Management highlighted a disciplined focus on commercial lending, stating the commercial pipeline remains strong and supports further loan growth expectations. Executives emphasized the bank's current asset sensitivity and expect net interest margin to "grind higher" as more assets reprice over the next year. Core deposit remixing out of non-interest bearing into higher-yielding accounts slowed, but total funding costs rose slightly and were described as peaking. The bank maintained stable credit quality metrics and strengthened tangible book value, while non-interest income underperformed management’s targets primarily due to a loss in mortgage servicing results.

  • Management reported adding three bankers during the quarter, supporting broader expansion efforts in Southeast and Northern Michigan markets.
  • Executives reiterated the target securities-to-assets ratio in the 12%-15% range, with $25 million in securities maturities expected in Q4 and approximately $120 million distributed evenly across 2025.
  • Gavin Mohr stated noninterest expense control is ongoing, leveraging headcount reductions, automation, and AI, positioning the company to potentially drive future efficiency gains.
  • Executives clarified that recent reserve builds are directly tied to loan growth rather than underlying asset quality concern, and the allowance level includes a material subjective component to adjust for macroeconomic uncertainty.
  • Management affirmed there has been no regulatory pushback regarding current AI-related initiatives, emphasizing a structured governance-first approach as new technologies are deployed internally.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • C&I Lending: Commercial and industrial loans typically provided to businesses, not secured by real estate.
  • Basis Points: A unit equal to 0.01%, used in banking and finance to denote changes in interest rates.
  • Beta (Deposits): A measure of how quickly deposit costs adjust in response to changes in market interest rates.
  • Net Interest Margin (NIM): The difference between interest income generated and interest paid out, relative to average earning assets.
