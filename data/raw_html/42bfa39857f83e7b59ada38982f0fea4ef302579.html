Earnings Call Transcript

American Eagle (AEO) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Sep 3, 2025 at 7:14PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Sept. 3, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Executive Chairman — Jay Schottenstein
  • President, Executive Creative Director, Aerie — Jennifer Foyle
  • Chief Financial Officer — Michael Mathias
  • Chief Marketing Officer, American Eagle — Craig Brahmers

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • Tariff Impact— Chief Financial Officer Michael Mathias stated, "About $20 million of impact from Q3. $40 million to $50 million in tariff impact expected in Q4," which will pressure gross margin and earnings in the second half.
  • Store Closures— Michael Mathias said, "We now expect to close 35 to 40 American Eagle locations by year-end," citing ongoing rebalancing in the physical store fleet due to higher digital penetration.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total revenue-- Fiscal Q2 2025 revenue was $1.28 billion, representing a 1% decrease; this marked the company’s second-highest Q2 revenue ever.
  • Gross margin-- Gross margin was 38.9%, up from 38.6% last year; achieved despite "20 basis points of deleverage in buying, occupancy, and warehousing" in fiscal Q2 2025, according to Michael Mathias.
  • Operating income-- Operating income was $103 million, a 2% increase from last year, delivering an operating margin of 8%, compared to 7.8% last year.
  • SG&A expense-- $342 million, down 1% from last year in fiscal Q2 2025, and flat as a percentage of sales, driven by restructuring, cost control, and higher advertising investment.
  • EPS (diluted)-- Diluted EPS rose 15% compared to last year.
  • Comparable sales-- Comparable sales declined 1% in fiscal Q2 2025; Aerie comparable sales grew 3% in fiscal Q2 2025, American Eagle comps declined, but traffic increased across brands, with comps turning positive in August.
  • Inventory-- Ending inventory cost increased 8%, with units up 3% in fiscal Q2 2025, primarily due to tariff effects; inventory is described by management as "aligned with our plans."
  • Share repurchases-- $231 million in buybacks year-to-date, reducing shares outstanding by 20 million, or about 10%, year-to-date; this includes the completion of a $200 million accelerated repurchase program in fiscal Q2 2025.
  • Cash & liquidity-- Ending cash was $127 million in fiscal Q2 2025, and total liquidity near $400 million; $200 million was drawn from the revolver to support buybacks and seasonal cash needs during fiscal Q2 2025.
  • Store actions-- Plan to remodel 40-50 American Eagle stores, with 35-40 American Eagle store closures planned by year-end.
  • Q3-to-date performance-- Quarter-to-date, consolidated comparable sales are up mid-single digits in fiscal Q3 2025, with Labor Day described as record-breaking for the company.
  • Q3 and Q4 guidance-- Fiscal Q3 2025 comparable sales are guided to a low single-digit increase, operating income is forecast at $95 million to $100 million (including ~$20 million in tariffs) for fiscal Q3 2025; fiscal Q4 comparable sales are expected to increase by low single digits, operating profit is projected at $125 million to $130 million (with ~$40 million to $50 million in tariffs) for fiscal Q4 2025.
  • SG&A guidance-- SG&A is expected to rise by high single digits in fiscal Q3 due to advertising, with fiscal Q4 SG&A projected to be slightly down.
  • Marketing campaign results-- Noted "record-breaking" new customer acquisition: over 700,000 new customers, and 40 billion campaign impressions from the Sydney Sweeney, and Travis Kelce collaborations, generating national engagement and denim sellouts.
  • Digital channel-- "Foundational improvements" are underway, with digital traffic, and sales penetration cited as major drivers of growth, and distribution costs.

SUMMARY

American Eagle Outfitters(AEO 0.52%) reported that early second-half momentum in fiscal Q3 2025 is being driven by robust new customer growth from celebrity marketing campaigns, with increased traffic extending into August and Labor Day. Strategic supply chain adjustments, including country-of-origin shifts and vendor negotiations, have materially reduced an unmitigated $180 million tariff impact to an expected $70 million for the second half (fiscal Q3 and Q4 2025). Inventory was actively managed to align with category trends and anticipated demand, with management citing only minimal pull-forward related to tariffs during fiscal Q2 2025. A lower promotional cadence contributed to improved gross margins despite sales declines in fiscal Q2 2025. The company is prioritizing store fleet rationalization in parallel with an accelerated rollout of Aerie, and offline locations, dialing back on core American Eagle stores in response to evolving consumer behavior. Shareholder capital returns remain significant, with buybacks, and dividends year to date, and an intent to pay down seasonal debt by year-end.

  • Michael Mathias said, "Our unmitigated number was closer to $180 million, compared to the $70 million we are guiding to," explicitly confirming multiple simultaneous mitigation levers—country-of-origin remixing, cost negotiations, and selective price adjustments -- with pricing "not the largest mitigation strategy," as discussed for fiscal Q2 and the back half of the year.
  • The company stated that "digital is definitely a big driver" of fiscal Q3 and Q4 growth, but it also brings "variable expense" and "slight deleverage" in BOW lines as penetration rises.
  • Marketing investments are heavily weighted toward fiscal Q3, explicitly supporting ongoing campaign activity, with guidance indicating advertising as the primary driver of high single-digit SG&A growth.
  • American Eagle's men's, and Aerie's intimates businesses were identified as major focus areas, with new collections and merchandising shifts showing "nice upticks" and momentum continuing into the fall season, as noted in fiscal Q2 and into fiscal Q3 2025.
  • Store fleet optimization continues as management plans 35-40 American Eagle store closures in 2025, doubling recent annual closure rates, while simultaneously opening new Aerie, and offline stores to capture growth segments.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • BOW (Buying, Occupancy, and Warehousing): Cost components associated with product sourcing, distribution, and store occupancy, separate from cost of goods sold, and SG&A.
  • AUR (Average Unit Retail): The average selling price per unit of merchandise, factoring promotional discounts, and markdowns.
  • UPT (Units Per Transaction): The average number of items sold per customer transaction.
