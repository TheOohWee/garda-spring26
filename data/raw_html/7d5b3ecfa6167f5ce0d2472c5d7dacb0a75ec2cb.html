CACI (CACI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 22, 2026 at 9:49AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, August 7, 2025 at 8:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — John S. Mengucci
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jeffrey D. MacLauchlan

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $2.3 billion for the quarter, representing 13% year-over-year growth, with 5.3% organic growth.
  • EBITDA Margin -- 11.5% for the quarter, consistent with the prior year and slightly above internal expectations.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- $8.40 in the quarter, a 27% year-over-year increase, attributed to higher operating income, a lower tax provision, and reduced share count.
  • Tax Benefit Impact -- A $28 million IRS R&D tax credit resolution reduced the effective tax rate in the quarter, with future estimated tax liabilities also lowered as a result.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $139 million in the quarter, with fiscal year free cash flow totaling $442 million, marking a 16% increase in free cash flow per share.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio -- 1.1x for the trailing 12 months, supported by $10 billion of contract awards in the year.
  • Backlog -- Exceeds $31 billion, representing about 3.5 years of annual revenue; average new award duration is over 5 years.
  • Fiscal 2026 Revenue Guidance -- Projected at $9.2 billion to $9.4 billion, indicating 6.6%-8.9% growth.
  • Fiscal 2026 EBITDA Margin Guidance -- Expected in the mid-11% range, a 30 basis point rise at the midpoint.
  • Fiscal 2026 Adjusted Net Income Guidance -- Targeted between $605 million and $625 million, or $27.13-$28.03 adjusted diluted EPS.
  • Fiscal 2026 Free Cash Flow Guidance -- At least $710 million, implying more than 60% growth in free cash flow per share, with a conversion rate exceeding 100% of net income at the guidance midpoint.
  • Strategic Capital Deployment -- Completed 3 acquisitions and repurchased $150 million of shares at an average price of $344, alongside a $1 billion, 6.75% senior unsecured notes offering.
  • Customer Concentration -- 90% of revenue originates from U.S. national security customers, with only 6% from the federal civilian sector and 1% from the NASA NCAPS program.
  • Pipeline Metrics -- $16 billion of bids under evaluation (80% for new CACI business), with $11 billion to be submitted in the next two quarters (75% new).
  • Revenue Visibility for 2026 -- Forecasted breakdown: 84% from current programs, 11% from recompetes, 5% from new business.
  • Major Program Notes -- The TLS Manpack Army program has a raised $500 million ceiling and plans for a vehicle-mounted version; EITaaS ceiling reduction to $5 billion does not affect backlog, margins, or guidance.

Summary

CACI International (CACI 5.10%) reported fourth-quarter adjusted diluted EPS of $8.40, driven by improved operating income, a favorable tax credit resolution, and a smaller share base. The company’s backlog exceeded $31 billion with multi-year contract durations, indicating solid forward revenue visibility. Management initiated fiscal 2026 guidance for $9.2 billion to $9.4 billion in revenue, EBITDA margin in the mid-11% range, and more than 60% growth in free cash flow per share, reflecting strong pipeline visibility and cash conversion expectations.

  • John S. Mengucci emphasized that the corporate strategy for high-value growth “put in place years ago” allows the company to address current procurement and technology trends without transformation.
  • Management cited the passage of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act as a constructive industry development, with $25 billion allocated to Golden Dome and $170 billion toward border security within a $150 billion defense package, favorably affecting CACI’s core markets.
  • MacLauchlan stated the company is on track to achieve its three-year target of $1.6 billion in cumulative free cash flow, aided by ongoing IRS Section 174 tax benefits amounting to about $50 million annually for the next two years.
  • The TLS Manpack Army program, now with a $500 million ceiling, will add a vehicle-mounted variant not currently included in the awarded contract, indicating incremental contract opportunities.
  • Management described the company’s $16 billion bid pipeline as primarily “new work” to CACI and clarified that 84% of next year’s revenue is expected from existing programs, further reducing exposure to contract renewal risks.
  • The company reported modest DSO headwinds due to legacy Azure contracts, impacting DSO by about 4 days, but expects improvements as new business moves to standard terms.
  • Management highlighted minimal impact from federal civilian budget pressures due to an intentional, multi-year shift away from that customer base.

Industry glossary

  • TLS Manpack: Tactical program integrating signals intelligence (SIGINT) and electronic warfare (EW) functions in a software-defined, portable system used by the Army.
  • EITaaS: Enterprise IT as a Service; a long-term U.S. Air Force modernization program for information technology infrastructure.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: A metric comparing the value of new contract awards to revenue, indicating future growth potential.
  • DSO (Days Sales Outstanding): The average number of days required to collect payment after a sale.
  • One Big Beautiful Bill Act: U.S. federal legislation allocating large-scale funding for defense and border security, directly impacting defense contractors.
  • Section 174: U.S. tax code provision affecting the treatment of R&D expenses, influencing companies’ tax positions and cash flows.
