Earnings Call Transcript

ICF International (ICFI) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 1, 2025 at 6:29PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Oct. 30, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — John M. Wasson
  • Chief Financial Officer — Barry M. Broadus

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • Barry M. Broadus reported a 29.8% year-over-year decline in federal government revenues in Q3 2025, citing contract funding reductions and procurement delays.
  • John M. Wasson estimated the government shutdown would reduce monthly revenue by approximately $8 million and gross profit by about $2.5 million in October 2025, with the potential for a $25 million quarterly revenue impact if the shutdown continues.
  • Barry M. Broadus cited a higher tax rate of 22.7% in Q3 2025, up from 13.8% in Q3 2024, due to a one-time negative tax adjustment and new legislation.
  • Guidance for full-year 2025 operating cash flow was revised downward to a range of $125 million to $150 million (from approximately $150 million) due to anticipated collection delays caused by the shutdown.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $465.4 million in the third quarter, a decrease from $517 million in the prior-year period and relatively flat versus $476.2 million in Q2 2025.
  • Federal Government Revenue -- Federal government revenues were down 29.8% year-over-year in Q3 2025 and declined 3% sequentially.
  • Non-Federal Revenue Mix -- Commercial, state, local, and international clients represented 57% of total revenue in Q3 2025, up from 46% a year ago.
  • Commercial, State, Local, and International Revenue Growth -- Revenue from these segments increased 13.8% year-over-year in Q3 2025, driven by a 24.3% year-over-year increase in commercial energy revenues.
  • Commercial Energy Revenue -- Commercial energy revenue rose 24.3% year-over-year in Q3 2025 and contributed 30% of total revenues in Q3 2025 compared to 22% in the third quarter of the prior year.
  • Gross Margin -- Gross margin increased by 50 basis points year-over-year to 37.6% in Q3 2025, driven by higher margin commercial business and increased direct labor content.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA was $53.2 million (11.4% margin) in the third quarter, compared to $58.5 million in the third quarter of the prior year, reflecting a 10 basis point margin expansion compared to the third quarter of the prior year.
  • Net Income -- Net income was $23.8 million ($1.28 per diluted share) in the third quarter, compared to $32.7 million ($1.73 per share) the previous year.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- Non-GAAP EPS was $1.67 for Q3 2025, including a $0.04 per share negative impact from the tax adjustment (prior year: $2.13 non-GAAP in Q3 2024).
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio -- 1.53 for the quarter, resulting in $1.8 billion in year-to-date contract awards as of Q3 2025 and a $3.5 billion backlog (up $180 million sequentially) as of Q3 2025.
  • Backlog Composition -- As of Q3 2025, 52% of the $3.5 billion backlog is funded; the business development pipeline stands at $8.4 billion, about 4.3 times trailing 12 months revenue as of Q3 2025.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- Operating cash flow in the third quarter was $47.3 million, up from $25.5 million in the same period last year.
  • Debt -- Debt totaled $449 million at the end of Q3 2025, down $11 million from the end of the second quarter; with 39% of debt at a fixed rate as of Q3 2025 and a year-end target of about 45% fixed for 2025.
  • Capital Expenditures -- Capital expenditures were $5.5 million in Q3 2025 (prior year: $5.2 million in Q3 2024); full-year 2025 guidance for capital expenditures was lowered to $23 million to $25 million from $26 million to $28 million.
  • Full-Year Tax Rate -- The full-year 2025 tax rate is now expected to be approximately 18.5% (2026 estimate: 21% tax rate).
  • Leverage Ratio -- The adjusted net leverage ratio was 2.13 times in Q3 2025; and management expects a reduction of about 0.25 turn by year-end 2025 absent acquisitions.
  • Dividend -- Announced a quarterly cash dividend of $0.14 per share, payable January 9, 2026.
  • Management Transition -- Barry M. Broadus is retiring as CFO; James Morgan (current COO, previous CFO) will assume the CFO role. Ann Choate will become President in early 2026.
  • Executive Compensation During Shutdown -- Named executive officers to take a 20% salary reduction for the duration of the government shutdown.
  • Guidance for Fourth Quarter -- Year-over-year declines in revenue and non-GAAP EPS for Q4 2025 are expected to mirror the percentage declines seen in Q3 2025 if the shutdown persists.

SUMMARY

ICF International (ICFI 2.04%) management specifically attributed the overall year-over-year revenue decline in Q3 2025 to ongoing federal government headwinds, while non-federal businesses reported notable gains, led by commercial energy clients in Q3 2025. The company emphasized that federal contract cancellations and shutdown-related delays reduced both revenue and profit, but were described by management as "temporary" with most lost revenue expected to be recovered over the lifetime of affected contracts. Executives outlined their capital allocation priorities, reaffirming a disciplined approach to debt reduction, continued dividend payments, and a focus on acquisitions in the energy and disaster management sectors for 2026. New business awards, with half attributed to federal clients and a significant share from new work, resulted in a book-to-bill of 1.53 in Q3 2025 and an expanded backlog. Leadership changes were disclosed, with the CFO retiring and executive succession plans detailed to preserve continuity in both operations and growth strategy.

  • John M. Wasson described the impact of the government shutdown as "painful but manageable" and stressed measures to retain key staff to accelerate recovery when funding resumes.
  • The company expects temporary cash flow effects from delayed collections but projects $30 million in 2025 and an additional $40 million in 2026 in cash tax savings from new tax legislation.
  • CFO Barry M. Broadus highlighted that fixed price and time & materials contracts comprised 93% of revenue in Q3 2025, reducing exposure to cost-reimbursable contracts.
  • Management stated that "any material new cancellations" of federal contracts occurred since July 31, indicating stability in the magnitude of lost federal business in Q3 2025.
  • Industry tailwinds from data center and AI-driven electricity demand were cited as drivers.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: The ratio of new contract awards received to revenue recognized during a period; measures future growth visibility.
  • Backlog: The remaining revenue value of contracts awarded but not yet completed and recognized as revenue.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for non-recurring and non-cash items.
  • ICF Fathom: A proprietary suite of artificial intelligence solutions and services tailored for federal agency clients.
  • Recompete: A contract renewal process where existing work is open for competitive bidding at expiration.
