Earnings Call Transcript

Generac (GNRC) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 12:49PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Feb. 11, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Aaron P. Jagdfeld
  • Chief Financial Officer — York A. Ragen
  • Vice President, Corporate Development & Investor Relations — Kris Rosemann

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $1.1 billion for the quarter, representing a 12% decrease year over year primarily due to softer power outage environment that reduced home standby and portable generator shipments.
  • Global C&I Product Sales -- Increased 10% year over year to $400 million, driven by momentum in data center customer demand and initial domestic large megawatt generator sales.
  • Residential Product Sales -- Decreased 23% to $572 million, reflecting a pronounced impact from the lowest outage hours in a decade and reduced home standby and portable shipments.
  • International Segment Sales -- Increased 12% to $209 million, with a 6% gain attributed to foreign currency, and set a new record adjusted EBITDA margin of 16.1% for the segment.
  • Gross Profit Margin -- 36.3%, down from 40.6% in the prior year, due to unfavorable sales mix and a $15.6 million inventory provision related to a discontinued product contract settlement.
  • Operating Expenses -- Rose to $405 million, up 34%, mainly from a $104.5 million provision for settlement of portable generator product liability.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $185 million or 17% of net sales, versus $265 million or 21.5% in the prior year, reflecting margin compression.
  • GAAP Net Loss -- $24 million compared to $117 million net income in the prior year, primarily resulting from product liability and supplier contract settlements.
  • Cash Flow from Operations -- $189 million, declining from $339 million, with free cash flow at $130 million versus $286 million in the prior year.
  • Backlog for Data Center Products -- Increased to $400 million, reflecting accelerated orders from co-locators and developers, with the majority expected to ship in 2026.
  • Domestic Manufacturing Capacity (Large Megawatt Generators) -- Expected to surpass $1 billion in capacity by year-end, following acquisition of a new facility in Wisconsin.
  • 2026 Sales Guidance -- Projected mid-teens percentage increase in consolidated net sales, with C&I product sales anticipated to grow in the plus 30% range and residential in the plus 10% range.
  • Gross Margin Outlook -- Expected flat at 38%-39% for 2026, with margin improvement anticipated in second half driven by home standby sales mix.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin Guidance -- Targeted at 18%-19% for 2026, with seasonality expected to drive a second-half margin near 20%.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- Board approved up to $500 million over 24 months, replacing prior authorization.
  • Home Standby Market Penetration -- Category reported at 6.75% U.S. penetration at year-end, with each incremental 1% presenting an estimated $4.5 billion addressable opportunity.
  • Ecobee Performance -- Achieved mid-teens sales growth and positive EBITDA contribution, with connected homes reaching about 5 million.

RISKS

  • Operating expenses increased by 34% primarily due to a $104.5 million product liability settlement provision, directly impacting quarterly profitability.
  • Home standby shipments fell 25% in the quarter due to "the lowest level of total outage hours in a decade," negatively affecting residential dealer sentiment and market metrics.
  • Energy storage system shipments are expected to decrease in 2026 as the Department of Energy program in Puerto Rico winds down according to company remarks.
  • Gross profit margin declined due to "unfavorable sales mix, together with a $15.6 million net inventory provision" related to a discontinued product contract settlement.

SUMMARY

Generac Holdings (GNRC 0.15%) reported a year-over-year decline in overall net sales and profit margins for the quarter, caused by a significant decrease in residential product shipments following subdued power outages. The company highlighted robust momentum in its data center business, with commercial and industrial product sales rising and backlog for data center generators growing to $400 million, even as orders from hyperscale customers remain at the pilot stage. Management outlined material investments in expanded manufacturing capacity, especially for large megawatt generators, to support expected acceleration in data center-driven growth and confirmed a substantial increase in both gross and EBITDA margin guidance for 2026, coupled with a new $500 million share repurchase program.

  • Management confirmed that approximately half of the anticipated 2026 home standby growth will be driven by price realization and half by volume, as clarified during the call.
  • Generac’s CFO said, "We're seeing around mid-teens EBITDA margins or contribution margins for these projects in 2026," with a target to reach "more high teens margins in the 27%, 28% range" as scale increases.
  • Pilot programs with two hyperscaler customers are in progress, with management indicating possible supply agreements that could add to backlog if successfully completed by mid-2026.
  • Total debt was reported at $1.33 billion with a gross debt leverage ratio of 1.9x, within the company’s target range of 1x to 2x adjusted EBITDA.
  • Residential energy technology product sales reached $375 million in 2025, at the high end of the previous $300 million-$400 million guidance range, with break-even EBITDA targeted by 2027 across these products.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Hyperscaler: A large-scale operator of data centers that require significant investments in mission-critical backup power infrastructure (e.g., Amazon, Google, Microsoft).
  • Co-locator: A company providing shared data center space and backup power on behalf of multiple clients, distinct from hyperscalers that build dedicated facilities.
  • Home Standby Generator (HSV): A permanently-installed residential generator automatically supplying backup electricity during grid outages.
  • PWRcell: Generac’s proprietary residential energy storage system for solar and grid backup applications.
  • Ecobee: Generac’s connected home energy management platform, including smart thermostats and services integrated with other Generac products.
  • Pilot Phase: Customer-led product validation and testing prior to awarding major supply contracts, especially relevant with new hyperscaler relationships.
  • Gross Debt Leverage Ratio: Ratio of total debt to adjusted EBITDA, used to assess financial leverage against management’s stated targets.
