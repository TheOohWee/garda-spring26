Mirum (MIRM) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 1, 2026 at 9:34AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Aug. 6, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Christopher Peetz
  • President — Peter Radovich
  • Chief Medical Officer — Joanne M. J. Quan
  • Chief Financial Officer — Eric H. Bjerkholt

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $128 million, representing 64% growth year over year, driven by commercial progress across medicines.
  • Livmarli U.S. Net Product Sales -- $57 million, supported by higher-than-anticipated PFIC patient volumes and ongoing Alagille syndrome growth.
  • Livmarli International Net Product Sales -- $31 million, including launch-driven growth in Japan through Takeda and expanded reimbursement in partner markets.
  • Bile Acid Portfolio Revenue -- $40 million from CTEXLI and COBACHOLBAM, reflecting steady demand and benefit from CTEXLI approval earlier in the year.
  • Full-Year Revenue Guidance -- Raised to $490 million to $510 million, approaching 50% top line annual growth, led by Livmarli's performance in both U.S. and international segments.
  • Operating Expense -- $133 million, comprised of $46 million R&D, $63 million SG&A, and $23 million cost of sales, which includes $18 million in non-cash stock-based compensation and $6 million in intangible amortization and other non-cash items (largely in cost of sales).
  • Cash, Cash Equivalents, and Investments -- $322 million at quarter end, reflecting a $29 million increase from the end of last year.
  • Cash Flow -- Positive operating cash flow reported for the quarter, with expectations to remain cash flow positive for the full year.
  • Commercial Cash Contribution Margin -- Exceeded 50% during the quarter, signaling improved cash operating margins.
  • VISTAS PSC Study Progress -- Enrollment completion expected in the current quarter, with top line data targeted for the second quarter of 2026; study passed interim analysis last year.
  • Phase II Study in Fragile X Syndrome -- IND clearance received from FDA; first patient enrollment targeted by year-end with focus on males aged 16-45 confirmed for full mutation, and a separate open-label cohort for younger patients at lowest dose to assess PK.
  • Alagille Syndrome Penetration -- Approaching 50% in the U.S, with continued patient additions, including prevalent and incident cases.
  • Persistence Rates -- Approximately 70%-75% of Alagille patients remain on therapy after one year; attrition declines further in subsequent years.
  • Takeda Japan Order Impact -- Contributed $11 million in revenue for the quarter; large, periodic orders introduce quarter-to-quarter variability in international segment results.

SUMMARY

Mirum Pharmaceuticals (MIRM 3.74%) reported accelerated product revenue growth and raised full-year guidance as patient volumes exceeded expectations, particularly for Livmarli across multiple geographies and indications. Management highlighted the expansion of PFIC diagnosis driven by increased disease awareness and broader adoption of genetic testing, supporting both current and future commercial opportunities. Several pivotal pipeline programs advanced on schedule, with the VISTAS Phase IIb study in PSC nearing enrollment completion and the Fragile X syndrome Phase II trial cleared for launch before year-end.

  • Peter Radovich commented, "We believe Livmarli ultimately has the potential to be a $1 billion-plus revenue brand," citing the ongoing EXPAND label extension and international launches as drivers.
  • The commercial cash contribution margin surpassed 50%, reflecting efficient scaling of operations.
  • Livmarli gains from new oral tablet formulation launched in June are expected to impact future quarters, as Q2 results only reflect the oral solution.
  • Management emphasized operating cash flow positivity and indicated ongoing financial independence supporting pipeline development and global expansion efforts.
  • Takeda's Japanese launch in June generated immediate demand, with expectations for another large order within the year, implying continued revenue variability in the international segment.
  • Enrollment for the EXPAND study and Phase II VANTAGE trial remain on track, broadening Livmarli's addressable population and advancing potential new indications.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PFIC: Progressive Familial Intrahepatic Cholestasis, a rare genetic liver disorder.
  • Alagille Syndrome: A genetic disorder affecting the liver and other organs, leading to cholestasis.
  • PSC: Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis, a chronic liver disease causing inflammation and scarring of bile ducts.
  • PBC: Primary Biliary Cholangitis, a progressive autoimmune disease of the bile ducts.
  • IBAT Inhibition: Blocking the ileal bile acid transporter, a mechanism targeted by certain therapies for cholestatic disease.
  • MRM-3379: Mirum's investigational brain-penetrant PDE4D inhibitor being developed for Fragile X syndrome.
  • PK: Pharmacokinetics, the study of how drugs move through the body.
  • SG&A: Selling, General, and Administrative expenses.
  • IND: Investigational New Drug application, an FDA regulatory filing required before starting clinical trials in humans.
  • CTEXLI: Commercialized bile acid therapy in Mirum's portfolio.
  • COBACHOLBAM: Commercialized bile acid therapy in Mirum's portfolio.
