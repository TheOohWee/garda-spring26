Weyerhaeuser (WY) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Aug 5, 2025 at 3:22PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

  • Friday, July 25, 2025, at 2 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Devin Stockfish
  • Senior Vice President & Chief Financial Officer — Davey Wold

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • GAAP Earnings-- $87 million, or $0.12 per diluted share, in earnings for Q2 2025.
  • Net Sales-- $1.9 billion in net sales during Q2 2025.
  • Adjusted EBITDA-- Adjusted EBITDA totaled $336 million for Q2 2025, marking a slight sequential increase from the first quarter.
  • Share Repurchases-- $100 million in shares repurchased in Q2 2025, at an average price of $25.74, marking the highest quarterly repurchase level since late 2022.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization-- A new $1 billion share repurchase authorization was established following completion of the previous $1 billion program in May.
  • Timberlands Acquisition-- 117,000 acres in North Carolina and Virginia acquired for $375 million (announced in May 2025; expected to close in Q3 2025), with an anticipated 5.1% average annual free cash flow yield over five years (timber-only), expected to close in the third quarter.
  • Timberlands Segment Results-- $88 million in earnings and $152 million in adjusted EBITDA; a sequential adjusted EBITDA decline of $15 million, mainly due to higher Western operational costs.
  • Southern Timberlands EBITDA-- $69 million in adjusted EBITDA for Southern Timberlands, a slight decrease from the previous quarter primarily due to muted sawlog demand; average sales realizations increased slightly, and per unit log and haul costs increased moderately.
  • Real Estate, Energy & Natural Resources EBITDA-- $143 million in adjusted EBITDA, up $61 million sequentially, driven by the timing and mix of real estate sales.
  • Natural Climate Solutions Target-- $100 million in adjusted EBITDA targeted by year-end 2025; three forest carbon projects approved and six additional in progress as of Q2 2025
  • Wood Products Segment-- $46 million in earnings and $101 million in adjusted EBITDA; $11 million in adjusted EBITDA for lumber, a $29 million sequential decrease in adjusted EBITDA for the lumber business from Q1 to Q2 2025, with a 2% decrease in average sales realizations.
  • OSB (Oriented Strand Board) EBITDA-- $30 million in adjusted EBITDA, down $29 million in adjusted EBITDA due to a 12% fall in sales realizations and higher planned maintenance costs.
  • Engineered Wood Products (EWP) EBITDA-- $57 million in adjusted EBITDA, slightly higher sequentially, benefiting from lower raw material costs and improved MDF facility performance.
  • Capital Expenditures-- $107 million in capital expenditures, including $22 million for the EWP facility under construction in Monticello, Arkansas; expected total investment for Monticello is $500 million, to be incurred through 2027.
  • Cash Position and Debt-- $600 million in cash as of Q2 2025 and Total debt was just under $5.2 billion at quarter-end.
  • Real Estate Market Trends-- Continued realization of "significant premiums to timber value" in property transactions, according to management.
  • Princeton Mill Divestiture-- Announced sale of the British Columbia Princeton mill for approximately $120 million Canadian, with the mill portion of the transaction expected to close in Q3 2025, and an expected gain on sale plus a ~$15 million Canadian tax liability, to be recognized upon closing of the Princeton mill sale in Q3 2025.
  • Adjusted CapEx Guidance-- Guidance for typical capital spending (excluding Monticello project) reduced from $440 million to $400 million for 2025.
  • Dividend and Shareholder Returns-- Over $5.7 billion in cash returned to shareholders since the beginning of 2021 via dividends and share repurchases.
  • Lumber Market Dynamics-- A divergence was noted between Western SPF (Spruce-Pine-Fir) and Southern yellow pine pricing during Q2 2025. Southern yellow pine prices "decreased significantly" while Western SPF increased due to anticipated Canadian duty changes.
  • Q3 Guidance Highlights-- Timberlands segment earnings and adjusted EBITDA are anticipated to be approximately $10 million lower in the third quarter of 2025 due to weaker Western markets. The Real Estate segment is expected to see adjusted EBITDA approximately $80 million lower and earnings $60 million lower in Q3 2025 due to transaction timing. Wood Products segment adjusted EBITDA is expected to be comparable overall, adjusting for realized commodity prices.
  • Monticello Facility Update-- Construction started; Approximately $130 million of investment is planned in 2025 for the Monticello facility.

SUMMARY

The new $375 million Southern timberlands acquisition, announced in May 2025, is intended to be funded through noncore asset divestitures, which management expects to execute in a tax-efficient manner, largely within a 180-day regulatory window as required under IRS code section 1031. Weyerhaeuser (WY 0.72%) management reaffirmed its target to achieve $100 million in adjusted EBITDA in the Natural Climate Solutions business by year-end 2025, underpinned by three approved forest carbon projects as of Q2 2025, with more in development. Share repurchase activity accelerated, highlighted by the completion of a prior $1 billion authorization and the initiation of a new $1 billion program in May 2025, demonstrating continued capital return. Sequential segment trends included a notable increase in real estate adjusted EBITDA in Q2 2025, offset by a decline in both lumber and OSB adjusted EBITDA due to lower commodity pricing and higher operating costs. The company provided clear Q3 2025 guidance, projecting sequential declines in Timberlands and Real Estate earnings, but stable aggregate Wood Products operating results when adjusting for commodity price changes.

  • Stockfish stated, "Upon closing of our latest North Carolina and Virginia acquisition, we will have achieved the multiyear target to grow our Timberlands portfolio through $1 billion of investments by the end of 2025."
  • Excluding CapEx for Monticello, management has lowered guidance for the typical CapEx program from $440 million to approximately $400 million for 2025.
  • Stockfish confirmed, "we expect to recognize a gain on the sale and incur a tax liability of approximately $15 million Canadian," with the transaction expected to close in the third quarter of 2025.
  • The recent U.S. tax bill increases the limit on taxable REIT subsidiary holdings to 25%, creating greater structural flexibility for Wood Products expansion, up from the previous 20% limit.
  • Full-year 2025 adjusted EBITDA for the Real Estate, Energy & Natural Resources segment is expected to reach $350 million, driven by healthy real estate demand and climate solutions growth.
  • Export logs to Japan saw increased sales realizations, supported by lower imported European lumber shipments and local customers gaining share.
  • Plans for most of the Southern timberlands acquisition funding rely on Section 1031 like-kind exchanges, subject to the 180-day regulatory timeline outlined by management.
  • Wood Products segment operating rates in Q2 2025 were in the high 80s% for lumber, mid-90s% for OSB, and high 70s% for EWP, with an outlook for lower EWP operating rates next quarter.
  • In Q2, management noted that Southern yellow pine lumber producers are in a strong position to take U.S. market share, given the current price differential and structural trends.
  • Management indicated that lumber and OSB EBITDA sensitivities remain $50 million annually per $10 lumber price move and $32 million for every $10 OSB move, respectively, based on guidance as of Q2 2025.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • SPF (Spruce-Pine-Fir): A classification of softwood lumber predominantly used in construction, sourced primarily from western and northern forests in North America.
  • OSB (Oriented Strand Board): An engineered wood panel made from cross-oriented wood strands, widely used in structural applications such as sheathing and subflooring.
  • EWP (Engineered Wood Products): Manufactured structural lumber products engineered for strength and performance, including laminated veneer lumber, I-joists, and similar materials.
  • MDF (Medium-Density Fiberboard): A manufactured wood product made from compressed wood fibers, used in furniture, cabinetry, and building applications.
  • Southern yellow pine: A group of pine species native to the U.S. South, frequently used for lumber due to its strength and workability.
  • Section 1031 exchange: A U.S. tax code provision allowing investment property owners to defer capital gains tax by reinvesting proceeds in like-kind properties within specified timelines.
