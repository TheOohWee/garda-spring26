News Corp (NWS) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 27, 2026 at 3:39PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Nov. 6, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Robert Thomson
  • Chief Financial Officer — Lavanya Chandrashekar
  • Senior Vice President, Head of Investor Relations — Michael Florin

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $2.14 billion, up 2% year over year, driven by Dow Jones and Digital Real Estate Services, with digital and AI-related revenues providing incremental uplift.
  • Total Segment EBITDA -- $340 million, increased 5% year over year with 40 basis points improvement in margin to 15.9%.
  • Net Income from Continuing Operations -- $150 million, compared to $149 million a year ago.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $0.22, up from $0.20 in the same period last year.
  • Share Buyback Activity -- Repurchase rate surged to approximately $2.5 million per day, over 4x prior pace, enabled by a strong cash position and management’s stated belief that shares are undervalued.
  • Digital Revenue Mix -- Digital revenues represented 62% of total company revenue (fiscal 2025), and 89% of profits in the quarter were from Dow Jones and Digital Real Estate Services.
  • Dow Jones Segment Revenue -- $586 million, up 6% year over year, with digital contributing 84% of segment revenue, up 2 percentage points versus the prior year.
  • Dow Jones Segment EBITDA -- $144 million, rose 10% with margin near 25%, a 90 basis point improvement year over year.
  • Dow Jones Risk & Compliance Revenue -- $94 million, up 16% on new customers, products, and improved yield.
  • Dow Jones Energy Revenue -- $73 million, up 7%, with retention near 90%.
  • Factiva Business -- Returned to growth following settlement of a customer contract dispute.
  • The Wall Street Journal Digital-Only Subscriptions -- Up 11% year over year and increased 91,000 sequentially.
  • Digital Circulation Revenue Share -- 75% of circulation revenues, up from 72% a year ago.
  • Advertising Revenue Mix (Dow Jones) -- Digital advertising contributed 68% of advertising revenues, rising 1 percentage point; digital advertising was up 2%, and print down 4%.
  • Digital Real Estate Segment Revenue -- $479 million, up 5%, with segment EBITDA at $158 million, up 13% year over year.
  • Realtor.com Revenue -- $152 million, gaining 9%, the strongest quarterly growth in nearly four years; growth driven by new homes, rentals, and sellers, making up 22% of revenue, a rise of 3 percentage points.
  • REA Group (ASX:REA) Revenue -- $327 million, up 3% (5% in constant currency), with residential yield growth of 13%; segment margin improved from 30.6% to 33%.
  • Book Publishing Segment Revenue -- $534 million, declined 2%; segment EBITDA at $58 million, down 28% or $23 million, impacted by a $13 million distributor receivable write-off.
  • HarperCollins Digital Revenue -- Down 9%, with audiobooks falling 11%; digital comprised 23% of consumer revenues, versus 25% last year.
  • News Media Segment Revenue -- $545 million, up 1%, while segment EBITDA jumped 67% to $30 million; margin climbed from 3.3% to 5.5%.
  • New York Post Advertising Growth -- Advertising revenue grew 19%, with digital making up nearly 90% of ad revenue; digital advertising grew 23%.
  • UK Times and Sunday Times Digital Subscribers -- Increased by 40,000 to 640,000; News Corp Australia digital subscriptions up 3% to 1.162 million.
  • Capital Allocation -- Management expects $380 million repayment of Foxtel shareholder loans to benefit pacing and reiterated intent to sustain elevated repurchase rates.
  • Upcoming Strategic Announcements -- Management expects additional AI partnership deals in the near term, with anticipated positive financial impact.
  • Legal Proceeds -- Management referenced a $1.5 billion award against Anthropic for use of “pirated books,” with expectations for further cases and financial benefit.
  • Book Publishing Month-to-Date Trends -- Recovery seen in orders late in the quarter, especially after the release of bestsellers and notable titles.
  • Capital Expenditures -- Expected to moderately increase, mainly due to investment in HarperCollins supply chain logistics and ongoing technology initiatives.

SUMMARY

News Corporation (NWS 0.49%) signaled a heightened commitment to shareholder returns through accelerated buybacks, citing undervaluation and optionality to further expand the repurchase pace. Multiple segments showed pronounced digital momentum, with digital revenues now comprising a majority of the revenue mix and driving a large portion of overall profitability. Announced and anticipated legal settlements and AI-related partnerships could contribute meaningful, incremental profits, as management directly tied strategic value to proprietary content and data. The quarter also saw margin expansion across key businesses and pointed to diversified growth drivers beyond advertising, especially as cost management yielded sustained EBITDA improvements.

  • A tangibly higher rate of buybacks follows board authorization and available liquidity, with future increases possible.
  • News Corporation leadership directly challenged the perceived undervaluation of its shares and highlighted asset sum valuation.
  • Shareholder value accretive M&A activity was positioned as supporting ongoing portfolio optimization.
  • Dow Jones’ professional information growth benefitted from product innovation and new verticals, with GenAI capabilities underscored for Factiva.
  • Management is testing digital subscription price elasticity at The Wall Street Journal following implemented increases.
  • Factiva’s return to growth stemmed from resolution of a material contract dispute and new customer acquisition.
  • REA Group and Realtor.com delivered outsized growth in higher-yield segments and continued to capture user engagement leadership.
  • Book Publishing faces elevated prior-year comparables but improving recent ordering trends and high-profile releases may portend near-term recovery.
  • New York Post is positioned for further advertising and content expansion with the upcoming California Post launch.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization; a widely used measure of operating profitability.
  • Risk & Compliance: Business segment at Dow Jones providing regulatory, risk management, and compliance data solutions, including Know Your Client (KYC) services.
  • GenAI: Generative artificial intelligence; refers to AI technologies that can produce new content or data, applied here in Factiva search capabilities.
  • B2B: Business-to-business; refers to products and services sold directly to other businesses rather than consumers.
  • Foxtel: Former Australian pay-TV business; now divested by News Corporation in exchange for a stake in DAZN.
  • REA Group: News Corporation-owned digital real estate business based in Australia.
  • Rightmove (LSE:RMV): UK-based real estate portal and a past acquisition target by News Corporation’s real estate division.
  • RealPro Select: Realtor.com’s premium product for large brokers, driving higher annualized contract values.
  • Backlist: Books published in prior periods that continue to generate ongoing sales, as opposed to new releases ("frontlist").
  • Yield: Revenue generated per customer or product unit; here refers to price realization in real estate listings and advertising.
