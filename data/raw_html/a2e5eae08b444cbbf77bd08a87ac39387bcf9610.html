SUMMARY

Management outlined a trajectory in which infrastructure is expected to become MaxLinear (MXL +4.33%)'s largest revenue segment, with optical and storage accelerator momentum reinforced by recent design wins at major data center and carrier customers across North America. The Keystone optical DSP product line is anticipated to generate $100 million to $130 million in 2026 revenue, and the Rushmore next-generation PAM4 products are on track for production revenue by the end of 2026, addressing evolving data center needs. The company reported continued improvements in operating efficiency, reflected in both gross margin and working capital metrics, and signaled additional operating leverage from investments in high-growth, multiyear opportunities such as Wi-Fi 7, Ethernet, and AI-driven data center technologies.

  • Bookings remain robust, visibility continues to improve, and management stated that the company is entering 2026 with strong momentum across its portfolio.
  • The Panther 5 hardware storage accelerator SoC and portfolio expansion into rack management and server interconnects position the company for broader AI data center exposure.
  • Second major Tier 1 North American PON revenue ramp is expected to build from Q2 and Q3 2026.
  • The company articulated that data center opportunities extend across optical, control-plane PON, rack telemetry, and power management, with several of these verticals still in early adoption stages.
  • Share buyback activity reflects board and management confidence in business performance and outlook.
  • While Q1 2026 guidance suggests sequential broadband softness, increasing visibility in infrastructure offsets this, and management expects both Wi-Fi and Ethernet within connectivity to grow during the year.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PAM4 DSP: Digital signal processors utilizing four-level pulse amplitude modulation, central to high-speed optical data center interconnects.
  • PON: Passive optical network, a cost-effective fiber-optic telecommunications technology for broadband access and control plane connectivity.
  • LRO / LPO / CPO: Varied optical architectures—Linear Receive Optics, Linear Pluggable Optics, and Co-Packaged Optics—targeting next-generation data center network deployments.
  • Panther Storage Accelerator: MaxLinear’s hardware-based compression and memory access silicon solution for storage and AI-intensive network applications.
