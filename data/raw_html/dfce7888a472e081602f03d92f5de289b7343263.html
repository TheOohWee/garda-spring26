Earnings Call Transcript

Leslie's (LESL) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 10:35AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Dec. 2, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Jason McDonell
  • Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer — Jeffrey White

Takeaways

  • Net sales -- $389.2 million, declining 2.2% compared to the prior year fourth quarter.
  • Same-store sales -- Decreased 6.8% on a 13-week comparable basis, with chemicals down 7.1% and equipment down 7.6%, reflecting demand weakness.
  • Inventory reduction -- Year-end inventory was $208 million, a reduction of $26 million year over year and over $100 million over two years.
  • Gross margin -- Rose to 38.6% from 36% in the prior year quarter, driven by improved vendor rebates and lower freight costs.
  • SG&A expense -- Increased 50 basis points as a percentage of sales, primarily from higher store payroll to support peak selling periods.
  • Impairment charges -- $184 million recorded, mainly due to goodwill impairment and partial impairment from announced store closures.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $45.2 million compared to $43 million in the prior year quarter.
  • Store closures -- 80 to 90 underperforming locations to be closed, resulting in an annual revenue reduction of $25 million to $35 million and net EBITDA improvement of $4 million to $10 million.
  • Annual cost savings -- Targeting $7 million to $12 million in cost optimization, annualized, from planned initiatives beginning in fiscal 2026.
  • SKU rationalization -- Over 2,000 SKUs being eliminated, expected to drive $4 million to $5 million in incremental annual EBITDA savings.
  • Customer traffic -- Reported net loss of over 160,000 residential customers, with residential traffic down 8.6% for the year.
  • Pricing action -- Core chemical product pricing to be adjusted to “everyday value,” with an expected gross margin impact of 100-150 basis points starting in Q2 2026.
  • Fiscal 2026 guidance -- Sales projected at $1.1 billion to $1.25 billion, with adjusted EBITDA between $55 million and $75 million.
  • Capital expenditures -- Expected to be $20 million to $25 million in 2026, focused on maintenance, productivity, and supporting positive free cash flow.
  • Distribution network -- Denver warehouse closed in Q3; Illinois facility to close in January, each yielding approximately $500,000 in annualized savings.
  • Conversion rate -- Conversion rate after water testing increased by 500 basis points year over year, reflecting customer engagement with technical expertise.
  • Loyalty program -- Pool Perks loyalty program captured data on over 85% of transactions, enabling targeted marketing and retention efforts.

Risks

  • A $184 million impairment charge was recorded due to “current business condition” and store closures, with further charges of $12 million to $17 million expected in Q1 and Q2 2026 from additional store closures.
  • Net loss for the quarter was $162.8 million, or $17.54 per diluted share, compared to $9.9 million, or $1.07 per diluted share, in the prior year quarter.
  • Residential customer traffic fell by 8.6% for the year, driven by price value challenges, and resulting in a net loss of more than 160,000 customers.
  • Management expects “meaningful headwinds” in Q1 2026 as the prior-year quarter benefited from a hurricane-driven 2%-4% boost in same-store sales.

Summary

Leslie's (LESL 2.26%) detailed a broad operational overhaul, including strategic pricing adjustments designed to remedy eroded price competitiveness, comprehensive store and SKU rationalizations, and the closure of two distribution centers to align its cost structure and asset base with demand dynamics. Management confirmed that new investments in targeted marketing and core category pricing will be counterbalanced by aggressive cost initiatives yielding $7 million to $12 million annualized in net EBITDA benefit. Proactive actions such as reducing inventory by $26 million, leveraging loyalty data for personalized customer outreach, and rapidly integrating new market leadership frameworks were described as central to stabilizing traffic and rebuilding market share. A fiscal 2026 revenue outlook of $1.1 billion to $1.25 billion with adjusted EBITDA in the $55 million to $75 million range was provided and incorporates anticipated effects from announced cost actions, price investments, and asset optimization.

  • Management emphasized that store closures will remove “the majority of the unprofitable stores” on a four-wall basis, and that remaining locations are generally profitable under current conditions.
  • Jeffrey White said, "at the midpoint of the guide, we're assuming free cash flow positivity," signaling that adjusted EBITDA is expected to translate into improved cash generation.
  • A continuing shift toward “everyday value” pricing is set to impact gross margin by 100-150 basis points, but efforts such as renegotiating vendor and landlord contracts are underway to offset this reduction.
  • EBITDA margin expansion for fiscal 2026 is projected to result from a balance of gross margin pressure and SG&A reductions, with the latter aided by significant vendor, supplier, and lease renegotiations.
  • Company leaders stated current strategic marketing and retention efforts do not require incremental spend, and are achieved by redeploying existing budgets into higher-ROA, data-driven channels.
  • Local fulfillment center optimization and expanded same-day delivery initiatives are designed to further enhance omnichannel capabilities and improve fulfillment speed, particularly in high-volume regions.
  • The call revealed the company is utilizing Kirkland & Ellis, Centerview Partners, BRG, and A&G Real Estate for external advice on capital structure and operational efficiency improvements.

Industry glossary

  • SKU (Stock Keeping Unit): A unique identifier for each product and service, used for tracking inventory, sales, and profitability in retail operations.
  • Four-wall EBITDA: A measure of a store's profitability accounting only for direct earnings generated within its physical location, excluding corporate and indirect costs.
  • BOPIS (Buy Online, Pick Up In Store): An omnichannel retail model where customers purchase products online and retrieve them from a physical store location.
  • PRO Fulfillment: Order fulfillment process dedicated to professional or commercial customers requiring higher-volume or specialized product segments.
  • Zero-party data: Information intentionally and proactively provided by customers, such as preferences or purchase intentions, to enable personalized marketing.
