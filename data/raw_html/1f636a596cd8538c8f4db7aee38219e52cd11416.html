Omega Healthcare (OHI) Q3 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 31, 2025 at 11:22AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Friday, Oct. 31, 2025 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

Chief Executive Officer — C. Taylor Pickett

Takeaways

Adjusted funds from operations (AFFO) -- $0.79 per share AFFO in the third quarter; raised full-year adjusted funds from operations (AFFO, non-GAAP) guidance for 2025 to $3.08-$3.10 per share, up from $3.04-$3.07.

Funds available for distribution (FAD) -- $0.75 per share FAD in the third quarter of 2025; FAD payout ratio declined to 89% in the third quarter of 2025.

Year-over-year AFFO growth -- 8% year-over-year AFFO growth based on the $3.09 midpoint of 2025 guidance.

Revenue -- $312 million in revenue in the third quarter due to investment activity.

Net income -- $185 million, or $0.59 per share, in the third quarter of 2025.

NAREIT FFO -- $242 million, or $0.78 per share, in the third quarter.

Dividend track -- CEO Pickett said, "There's a pathway in the near term to get to a dividend increase," as coverage rapidly improves.

Portfolio coverage -- Core EBITDAR coverage increased to 1.55x as of June 30, 2025, up from 1.51x in the first quarter; Sabre portfolio rent coverage exceeded 1.46x as of October 2025 and trends higher.

Portfolio composition -- 1,024 core facilities, with 60% U.S. skilled nursing/transitional care and 40% U.S. senior housing/U.K. care homes.

Genesis exposure -- Genesis bankruptcy ongoing as of July 2025, with Omega's lease covering thirty-one facilities ($52 million annual rent) and $125 million in secured loans; Genesis continues to pay full contractual rent post-filing.

Investment activity -- Over $978 million in new investments year-to-date through October 2025, including $151 million in the third quarter, 87% of which were on-balance-sheet real estate assets.

Sabre transactions -- $222 million invested for a 49% equity stake in a 64-facility real estate joint venture (leased to Sabre at a 9.3% initial yield, 2% annual escalators; investment made in October 2025), plus $93 million for 9.9% equity in Sabre's operating company (closing expected January 2026, 8% minimum cash yield).

Loan investment returns -- A 2024 Connecticut assisted living loan generated a 74% IRR upon refinancing in October 2025, demonstrating potential for outsized returns in loan structures.

Balance sheet -- $737 million in cash as of September 30, 2025. Entered into a new $2.3 billion credit facility (including a $2 billion revolver) in the third quarter of 2025; leverage reduced to 3.59x as of September 30, 2025. Fixed charge coverage ratio was 5.1x in the third quarter of 2025.

Share issuance -- 9 million new common shares issued in the past two quarters, with consideration for new investments partially funded via operating partnership units.

Dividend policy -- Board reviews payout policy quarterly, and tax constraints may result in a dividend increase if payout ratio moves into the low 80s.

Pipeline -- Favorable acquisition environment in U.S. skilled nursing and U.K. care homes; majority of opportunities sourced through existing relationships and focus on underperforming assets below replacement cost.

G&A expense outlook -- Projected at $13.5-$14.5 million in the fourth quarter of 2025.

Medicare/Medicaid exposure -- Anticipated 4% Medicare cut in early 2026 is expected to have minimal portfolio impact, netted with a 3.2% rate increase; skilled nursing was carved out from recent Medicaid reductions.

Staffing regulation repeal -- Krull confirmed minimum staffing mandate repeal with CMS drafting an interim final rule to rescind standards for long-term care facilities.

Occupancy -- Sabre joint venture occupancy was in the low 90% range as of the third quarter of 2025; Maplewood portfolio occupancy was 96% as of the third quarter of 2025.

Debt profile -- Majority of joint venture portfolio debt is HUD-sourced; ongoing efforts to refinance non-HUD debt to obtain lower rates.

Summary

Omega Healthcare Investors (OHI +0.15%) delivered a third quarter marked by strong AFFO and FAD growth, underpinned by substantial investment deployment. Expanded investments in strategic partnerships, notably with Sabre Healthcare, introduced diversified deal structures and elevated potential for risk-adjusted returns through equity, joint ventures, and loan participations. Capital management actions—including credit facility expansion, debt refinancing, and a new ATM program—further bolstered funding flexibility for an active acquisitions pipeline. Regulatory clarity around staffing mandates and reimbursement rates enhanced near-term portfolio stability and sector visibility.

Company leadership stated that the initial annual cash yield for facility acquisitions in the third quarter of 2025 was 10%, with annual escalators ranging from 2% to 2.5%.

Robert O. Stephenson explained that the $222 million Sabre joint venture investment, completed in October 2025, was funded entirely with operating partnership units to provide sellers a tax-efficient structure and create shareholder alignment.

Guidance for fiscal 2025 excludes the effects of additional prospective investments or asset sales beyond those closed by October 30 and assumes unchanged interest rate conditions.

Megan M. Krull highlighted minimal labor cost escalation, noting wage growth "matching inflation" and a sustained reduction in temp/agency usage since the COVID period.

Asset sales in the third quarter of 2025 reached $81 million.

Industry glossary

AFFO (Adjusted funds from operations): Adjusts FFO by excluding recurring capital expenditures and straight-line rent, representing cash available for distributions in REITs.

FAD (Funds available for distribution): Profit metric after deducting capital expenditures from AFFO to measure cash available for dividends.

Triple net lease: Lease structure requiring tenants to pay property taxes, insurance, and maintenance costs in addition to rent.

EBITDAR coverage: Ratio of tenants’ EBITDAR to lease obligations, used to assess operator rent coverage strength in healthcare real estate.

RIDEA structure: REIT Investment Diversification and Empowerment Act structure, allowing REITs to participate in operating income and share business risks in senior housing properties.

HUD debt: Mortgage financing insured or provided by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development, typically for senior care or healthcare facilities.

ATM program: At-the-market stock offering enabling the company to issue new shares directly into the market at prevailing prices.

OP units (operating partnership units): Partnership interests often used by REITs in acquisitions, exchangeable for common shares under certain conditions.

Sabre: Refers to Sabre Healthcare Holdings, a private skilled nursing and assisted living operator and Omega's investment partner.

Genesis: Reference to Genesis HealthCare, an Omega tenant currently in Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings.

CCRC: Continuing care retirement community, a senior housing asset providing various levels of care within one site.
