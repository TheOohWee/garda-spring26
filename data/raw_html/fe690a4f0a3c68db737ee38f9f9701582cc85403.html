Oatly (OTLY) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 12:37PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, February 11, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Jean-Christophe Flatin
  • Chief Operating Officer — Daniel Ordonez
  • Chief Financial Officer — Marie-Jose David
  • Head of Investor Relations — Brian Kearney

TAKEAWAYS

  • Full-Year Profitability -- The company achieved its first full year of profitable growth, with adjusted EBITDA of $6.8 million.
  • Revenue Growth -- Full-year revenue increased 4.7%, or 2.2% on a constant currency basis; fourth-quarter revenue grew 9.1%, or 4.3% constant currency.
  • Gross Margin -- Gross margin reached 34.5% in the quarter, up 580 basis points from the prior year’s fourth quarter.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (Q4) -- Adjusted EBITDA was $11 million for the quarter, an improvement of $17.1 million compared to the same period last year.
  • Segment Performance -- Europe and International volume grew 13.9% in the quarter, driving $9.9 million increase in segment adjusted EBITDA; North America excluding the largest foodservice customer saw 10% revenue growth, while the segment’s adjusted EBITDA reached $4.4 million, its highest on record.
  • Cost Optimization -- Cost of goods sold per liter declined 23% since management changes, and total SG&A was reduced by nearly $100 million, equal to 21% of revenue.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Full-year free cash flow was a net outflow of $39 million, a $117 million improvement from the previous year.
  • 2026 Guidance -- The company forecasts constant currency revenue growth of 3%-5% and adjusted EBITDA of $25 million to $35 million, with capital expenditures anticipated at $20 million to $30 million.
  • Strategic Review -- Plans remain to complete a strategic review of the Greater China segment in 2026, including considering a potential carve-out.
  • Product Innovation -- New Barista flavors such as churros and coconut will launch in 2026, along with a cold form Barista product for both hot and cold beverages, and an expanded matcha line in retail.
  • Portfolio Focus -- The company now derives around 95% of its business from drinks, having shifted away from other categories in its U.S. portfolio.
  • Retail Market Share -- Oatly expanded its retail market share in every measured European market during the second half, recently becoming the top plant-based drink brand in Germany.
  • Household Penetration -- Household penetration increased in most major markets, with growth attributed to younger “Gen Z and Alpha” consumers.
  • Working Capital -- The firm identified further working capital optimization as a principal lever for driving free cash flow toward positive territory.
  • North America Customer Mix -- Exposure to its largest North American foodservice customer now represents less than 10% of the segment’s revenue.

RISKS

  • Marie-Jose David stated, "While we do not expect to deliver positive free cash flow for the full year 2026, we do expect to improve from 2025 level."
  • Corporate guidance reflects a roughly 200 basis point headwind to 2026 revenue growth from a large North America customer.
  • The CFO acknowledged, "Greater China constant currency revenue declined slightly."

SUMMARY

Oatly Group AB (OTLY +8.75%) reported a pivotal fourth quarter, achieving full-year profitability for the first time and marking the company's entry into a self-described "profitable growth era." Management highlighted significant gross margin gains from supply chain efficiencies and strategic portfolio shifts, underpinned by robust innovation in beverages and targeted investments behind brand and category expansion. Guidance for 2026 anticipates higher capital expenditures to enable European growth and continued disciplined investment, while acknowledging ongoing headwinds in Greater China and U.S. foodservice exposure.

  • Leadership emphasized new product launches and market penetration among younger demographics as critical ongoing growth drivers.
  • The company is executing a refreshed growth playbook concentrated on beverage relevance, menu expansion, and digital engagement to advance consumer adoption globally.
  • Further working capital improvement and supply chain leverage are intended to close the gap to sustainable, positive free cash flow, although that milestone is not expected in the current year.
  • Management reiterated plans for a strategic review in Greater China with carve-out options, providing future optionality for asset allocation.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Barista Product: A plant-based oat milk item formulated for specialty coffee use, notable for its ability to froth and perform well in coffee shop applications.
  • Cold Form Barista: A new beverage topping innovation designed for both hot and cold drinks, introduced as a plant-based alternative to traditional milk foams.
  • Carve-Out: A corporate action where a company separates part of its business, possibly for sale or to operate more independently, commonly used in strategic reviews.
  • SG&A: Selling, General, and Administrative Expenses; a key operational cost metric directly referenced in Oatly's turnaround initiatives.
