Montrose (MEG) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 5, 2025 at 8:10PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Nov. 5, 2025, at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Vijay Manthripragada
  • Chief Financial Officer — Allan Michael Dicks

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $224.9 million in the quarter, a 25.9% increase, driven primarily by organic growth across all three segments and moderate acquisition contributions.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $33.7 million (15% adjusted EBITDA margin), reflecting a 19% year-over-year increase and improved operating performance.
  • Net Income -- Positive $8.4 million net income for 2025, reversing a $10.6 million net loss in the prior year period.
  • GAAP EPS -- $0.21 earnings per diluted share versus a $0.39 net loss per diluted share compared to the prior year period, with the $0.60 improvement in earnings per share for 2025 is attributed to higher revenues, margin expansion, and a $10.6 million Series A-2 redemption gain, offset by increased interest and taxes.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $55.5 million operating cash flow for the first nine months of 2025, up $65.3 million year-over-year, reflecting a 60.2% conversion of consolidated adjusted EBITDA.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $38.8 million free cash flow (non-GAAP) year-to-date, an increase of $77.4 million over the prior year, equating to 42% conversion of adjusted EBITDA year-to-date.
  • Leverage Ratio -- Ended the quarter at 2.7 times, with liquidity of $198.5 million.
  • Guidance Raised -- 2025 revenue guidance increased to $810 million-$830 million and adjusted EBITDA to $112 million-$118 million, representing 18% revenue and 20% EBITDA growth at the midpoint over 2024.
  • Segment Performance: Assessment, Permitting, and Response -- Revenue grew 75% to $91.1 million, with adjusted EBITDA of $20.4 million (22.4% margin), a 90 basis point improvement in adjusted EBITDA margin for the Assessment, Permitting, and Response segment over the previous year.
  • Segment Performance: Measurement and Analysis -- Revenue increased 7.5% to $63 million; adjusted EBITDA of $17.3 million (27.5% margin), a 460 basis point margin improvement for the Measurement and Analysis segment over the prior year period.
  • Segment Performance: Remediation and Reuse -- Revenue increased to $70.8 million; adjusted EBITDA declined to $9.4 million, with margin down 380 basis points to 13.3%, primarily due to losses from the renewables business wind-down.
  • Series A Preferred Stock Redemption -- Final $62.6 million redeemed July 1, 2025, completing balance sheet simplification ahead of schedule.
  • Water Treatment Business -- Delivered "really healthy organic growth" and margin accretion compared to 2024, with ongoing regulatory clarity around PFAS contributing to a growing multi-industry pipeline.
  • Renewables Business Wind-Down -- Business being fully exited by year-end due to policy-driven demand decline, with de minimis revenue according to Allan Michael Dicks and losses impacting Remediation and Reuse segment margins.
  • Acquisition Strategy -- Acquisition pause in 2025 to highlight organic performance; plan to restart M&A activity in 2026, targeting both capability and geographic expansion, and evaluating larger asset opportunities.
  • Organic Growth Rate Outlook -- Management anticipates sustaining 7%-9% annual organic revenue growth, with EBITDA growth outpacing revenue.
  • 2026 Early Outlook -- Management offered guidance for at least $125 million in EBITDA and margin improvement in 2026.
  • Exposure to U.S. Federal Government -- Less than 5% of revenue, with no material impact from recent U.S. government shutdown; state and local government activity has created incremental growth opportunities.
  • Cross-Selling Effect -- Emergency response assignments are generating substantial downstream consulting, testing, and remediation revenue, increasingly forming a recurring revenue base.

SUMMARY

Montrose Environmental (MEG 3.68%) delivered record results for Q3 2025 in revenue, adjusted EBITDA, net income, and free cash flow, with broad-based organic growth reinforced by cross-selling and ongoing margin expansion year-to-date. Management raised full-year 2025 guidance for revenue and adjusted EBITDA while providing an "at least $125 million" EBITDA outlook for 2026, reflecting confidence in the business's momentum and visibility. Segment level data show outperformance in the Assessment, Permitting, and Response segment, record Measurement and Analysis margins, and material margin drag in Remediation and Reuse from renewables exit losses embedded in current and projected results. The balance sheet was strengthened via a completed $62.6 million Series A preferred redemption and leverage reduction to 2.7 times, with ample available liquidity to fund future investment and restart M&A. The company is benefiting from state-level environmental regulations and international market forces, notably the EU methane rules, which have counteracted U.S. federal policy headwinds and provided additional growth opportunities. Strategic clarity was provided on business line exits, near-term reinvestment priorities, and the planned resumption of acquisitions in 2026 targeting both scale and capability.

  • CEO Manthripragada said, "we observed that state and local governments have and continue to step in to address gaps and uncertainties left by the U.S. Federal government, creating additional opportunities for growth that we did not anticipate at the start of this year."
  • Water treatment is expanding beyond PFAS-driven demand into pharma, semiconductors, and waste, supported by proprietary technology and broad regulatory trends.
  • Cross-segment commercial and technical team investments are included in raised guidance, aiming to drive share-of-wallet expansion and enhanced retention across a predominantly industrial client base.
  • Allan Michael Dicks stated, "The Assessment, Permitting, and Response segment's adjusted EBITDA was $20.4 million or 22.4% of revenue, a 90 basis point improvement over the previous year due to favorable revenue mix."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PFAS: Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, a class of persistent environmental contaminants regulated under evolving water quality standards.
  • Cross-Selling: Generating revenue by selling additional or complementary services to existing clients, often after an initial major engagement.
