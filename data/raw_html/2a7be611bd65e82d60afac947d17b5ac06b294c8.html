Earnings Call Transcript

Kimberly-Clark (KMB) Q1 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 12:04PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, April 22, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Michael D. Hsu
  • Chief Financial Officer — Nelson Urdaneta
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Christopher Jakubik

TAKEAWAYS

  • Organic Sales Growth -- Company organic sales were slightly below internal expectations, primarily due to softer weighted average category growth in the 1.5%-2% range and one less day of shipments, which represented a roughly 100 basis point headwind in the quarter.
  • Tariff Impact -- Nelson Urdaneta reported a new $300 million gross cost from tariffs, with about two-thirds related to US tariffs on China at 145%, 10% from new US reciprocal tariffs, and the remainder driven by retaliatory tariffs imposed on US products in other countries.
  • Profitability and Outlook -- CFO Urdaneta stated, "profitability was in line with what we expected, supported by strong productivity delivery both in costs and overheads," despite the organic sales coming in softer than anticipated.
  • Guidance Revision -- Updated company guidance for the year was described as "about flat in operating profit and EPS," reflecting an expected $200 million net headwind from tariffs and mitigations underway.
  • Productivity Savings -- Urdaneta noted Q1 productivity savings at 5.2%, with full-year efforts targeted at the top end of the 5%-6% range and year-to-date SG&A as a percent of sales reduced to 13% from 13.2% last year, demonstrating ongoing progress toward the multiyear $3 billion goal.
  • Marketing and Innovation Investment -- The company maintained "about a 6% level of advertising," in line with last year, and signaled no intention to reduce innovation or brand support even amid cost headwinds.
  • Strategic Pricing and Mix -- Targeted price-pack architecture changes and increased promotional activity were implemented to drive trial for new products such as Huggies Snug & Dry and Kleenex, aligning with the strategy to cascade premium innovation across value tiers.
  • Supply Chain Adjustments -- Urdaneta confirmed management aims to address a third of tariff-driven cost impacts in the current year, with the balance mitigated through supply chain and sourcing moves into 2026.

RISKS

  • Nelson Urdaneta stated, "The reason why we are calling or changing our guidance for the year to about flat in operating profit and EPS has to do with the fact that we do not intend to cut investments behind our innovation and our plans," acknowledging that the $300 million incremental tariff headwind creates significant near-term earnings pressure, particularly concentrated in Q2.
  • "Q2 will be the biggest impact from a headwind related to the tariffs. They're in full swing right now," Urdaneta said, highlighting that the acute financial impact is expected to peak next quarter before mitigation efforts ramp up.
  • "Thirdly, we're facing lower year-on-year North America private label shipments outside of the private label diaper business exit we've been highlighting, and this as a whole represents about 40 basis points to total company organic sales. And in the case of North America, which you asked, it's about two times that, about 80 basis points of a headwind," Urdaneta reported, pointing to continued market share and volume challenges in that region.

SUMMARY

Kimberly-Clark Corporation (KMB 0.69%) reported softer-than-expected organic sales in Q1, driven by lower category growth and fewer shipment days, but maintained profitability in line with internal targets due to robust productivity initiatives. Management identified a $300 million gross tariff headwind, with two-thirds stemming from US-China tariffs, which prompted a downward revision to the company's full-year guidance for both operating profit and EPS—now projected to be roughly flat year-on-year as mitigation actions are underway. Despite these cost pressures, the company reiterated its commitment to sustained innovation, brand investment, and accelerated supply chain transformation, emphasizing that tariff-driven effects are being addressed through global sourcing adjustments and productivity savings without reducing strategic marketing spend.

  • Hsu emphasized the company is treating tariffs as a discrete, external event—"There's an externality that we don't think will continue to recur over time."—and reinforced that "we want to do is run the play, run our plan as intended."
  • Management expects volume and mix to accelerate in subsequent quarters, citing easier comparisons and new product launches, with additional tailwinds anticipated from retail restocking and upcoming innovation pipelines.
  • The supply chain transformation—anchored by integrated margin management—remains a cornerstone of the long-term strategy, targeting at least 40% gross margin and 18%-20% operating profit margin by decade end.
  • "affordability has become paramount," Hsu said, underscoring a sharpened focus on competitive value propositions and cascading premium innovation to reach both value-seeking and premium consumers.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PNOC (Pricing Net of Commodity): Internal metric for assessing price realization after factoring in changes in commodity costs.
  • Price Pack Architecture: Strategy involving different package sizes or formats at varying price points to align with consumer and channel preferences.
  • Integrated Margin Management: Enterprise-wide approach to optimizing profitability through a combination of cost control, productivity improvements, and disciplined pricing or mix actions.
