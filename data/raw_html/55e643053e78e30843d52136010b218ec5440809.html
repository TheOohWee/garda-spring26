Earnings Call Transcript

Oatly (OTLY) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 12:28PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, April 30, 2025 at 7:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Jean-Christophe Flatin
  • Chief Operating Officer — Daniel Ordoñez
  • Chief Financial Officer — Marie-José David
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Brian Kearney

TAKEAWAYS

  • Volume Growth -- 9.2% companywide, led by Greater China and supported by Europe and International, which offset declines in North America.
  • Revenue -- Down 0.8% on a reported basis, with constant-currency revenue growth of 0.7%, driven by volume gains but negatively impacted by an 8.5% price mix decline and 1.5% foreign exchange headwind.
  • Gross Margin -- Expanded by 450 basis points year over year to 31.6%, achieving the company's highest quarterly margin as a public company.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Loss of $3.7 million, a $9.5 million year-over-year improvement, primarily from an $8.4 million increase in gross profit and $1.1 million better SG&A and other costs (partially offset by higher brand investments).
  • Cost Efficiencies -- Cost of goods sold per liter declined 15% year over year and 6% sequentially from the prior quarter, translating to a total COGS reduction of $10 million.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Positive $21 million, representing a $25 million improvement versus last year, with $5.5 million in restructuring/severance and $7.6 million in annual incentive plan payments included in this result.
  • Trade Working Capital -- Reduced by $2 million, with trade receivables dropping below $100 million for the first time since early 2022.
  • Guidance -- Full-year constant-currency revenue growth expected at 2%-4%, and adjusted EBITDA between positive $5 million and $15 million, with no major changes to this outlook.
  • Segment Commentary -- Europe and International outperformed the plant-based and oat milk categories, with Germany accelerating to nearly 8% volume growth in the most recent 12 weeks; Greater China achieved growth in foodservice and early traction in retail through club channels.
  • North America Update -- All of the segment’s year-over-year sales decline was attributed to the largest customer’s sourcing changes and frozen SKU rationalization; excluding frozen, scanner data would have shown only a 1% sales decline for the region.
  • Brand Initiatives -- Launches such as the Nespresso collaboration, blind taste test activations, and targeted retail strategies demonstrated measurable awareness gains and increased shelf space, especially in core beverage categories.
  • 2025 Strategic Execution -- "We believe this progress keeps us on track to deliver our first full year of profitable growth, as a public company," indicating management’s continued commitment to disciplined resource allocation and efficiency-led investment.

RISKS

  • "we continued to face some challenging dynamics in North America, where we have not yet fully deployed resources to ignite positive momentum," and headwinds from the largest customer and the frozen business are expected to continue impacting growth rates throughout the year.
  • The first quarter saw a 270 basis point revenue headwind attributable to a key North American foodservice customer’s sourcing shift, and these pressures are framed by management as temporary but significant in 2025.
  • Price mix deterioration, including a 4% price mix decline in Europe primarily from customer renegotiations in the first quarter, affected reported top-line growth, though management expects these issues to normalize going forward.
  • Despite improved fundamentals, the broader North American oat milk category remains "soft," with recovery dependent on the full deployment of the company’s commercial playbook later in the year.

SUMMARY

The first quarter marked Oatly Group AB (OTLY +8.75%)’s best reported figures for gross profit, gross margin, adjusted EBITDA, and free cash flow since becoming a public company, with management reiterating full-year profitability and growth guidance. Investments in efficient supply chain management and targeted brand activations, particularly in Europe and Greater China, underpinned key volume gains against persistent North American headwinds related to customer sourcing and SKU rationalization. Improving product mix, reductions in SG&A, and cash flow discipline positioned the company to accelerate playbook deployment, especially in North America, anticipated to shift trajectory in the second half.

  • Management stated that "second quarter adjusted EBITDA is likely to be comparable to Q1 level," reflecting higher brand activation spending in anticipation of region-wide rollout.
  • Segmental analysis showed that European expansion markets such as France, Spain, Belgium, Mexico, and UAE are "contributing to our total company growth in a meaningful manner"—distinct from the more mature UK and German businesses.
  • Greater China’s retail gains are tied to entry into club channels, with management citing early but positive momentum following the recent strategic reset in the region.
  • The company affirmed that capital expenditures were expected in the $30 million to $35 million range for 2025, with liquidity supported by $74 million in cash and $211 million in credit facilities at quarter-end.
  • Jean-Christophe Flatin emphasized ongoing cost and margin initiatives by stating, "gross margin is expected to increase compared to the 28.7% we reported in 2024," with long-term targets set at 35%-40%.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Barista (as used by Oatly): A specialized product line of oat-based beverages formulated for optimal performance in professional coffee drink preparation, often emphasizing foam-ability and flavor for café-style drinks.
  • Club Channel: A retail distribution format involving large, membership-based warehouse stores that offer products in bulk quantities, often a significant volume driver for consumer packaged goods.
  • SKU Rationalization: Strategic reduction or streamlining of stock keeping units (SKUs) in a product portfolio to improve efficiency, profitability, and alignment with channel or customer needs.
  • Velocity: A measure of product sales per point of distribution (e.g., per store, per week), used to gauge how quickly items sell relative to their availability on shelves.
  • Absorption (in COGS context): The process of allocating fixed manufacturing overhead costs over a larger production volume, thereby reducing the unit cost of goods sold.
