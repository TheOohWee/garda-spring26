Earnings Call Transcript

Neogen (NEOG) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Apr 9, 2026 at 9:01AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, April 9, 2025 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — John Adent
  • Chief Financial Officer — David Naemura

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $221 million reported, with foreign currency a negative 310 basis point impact compared to the prior year.
  • Core Revenue Growth -- 0.2% growth, with "headwinds in genomics and sample collection" contributing over 400 basis points of drag to consolidated core revenue growth.
  • Food Safety Segment Revenue -- $153 million, a decrease of 3.2% with core revenue growth of 1.5% offset by foreign currency.
  • Food Safety Core Revenue (Ex-Sample Collection) -- 7% growth, driven by biosecurity, pathogen detection, food quality, and Petrifilm products.
  • Animal Safety Segment Revenue -- $68 million, with a core revenue decline of 2.6%; core business outside genomics declined just over 1%.
  • Global Genomics Core Revenue -- Down mid-single digits; differentiated bovine segment achieved low single-digit growth.
  • Gross Margin -- 49.9%, representing a 120 basis point decrease from 51.1% last year; excluding special items, margin was down 30 basis points due to lower revenues.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $49 million, or a 22% margin, flat sequentially and declining year over year on lower revenue.
  • Adjusted Net Income and EPS -- $21 million and $0.10, respectively, compared to $26 million and $0.12 a year ago, mainly due to lower EBITDA and a higher tax rate.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Outflow of approximately $14 million, improving by $49 million compared to Q3 2024.
  • Refinancing Activity -- Term Loan A refinanced, extending maturity by 2.5 years and achieving a 60 basis point interest rate reduction.
  • Full-Year Guidance Update -- Revenue outlook revised to approximately $895 million, adjusted EBITDA to $195 million, and free cash flow now projected as a $20 million outflow.
  • Tariff Exposure -- CFO David Naemura stated: "If you just took kind of the last 12 months run rate and straight up applying tariffs ... you'd probably be around $30 million, close to it, $29 million. And of that, two-thirds of it roughly would be China ... this is predominantly Animal Safety."
  • Leadership Changes -- A new Head of R&D, a new North America Commercial Organization head, a new commercial Head for North American Food Safety, and a new Chief Human Resources Officer were appointed; Chief Commercial Officer search to conclude in Q1 2026.
  • Petrifilm Integration -- First two production lines installed; initial test production remains on track for fall 2025, with a phased SKU-by-SKU ramp-up model described as "a more gradual transition."
  • Cost Actions -- Portfolio changes in late-stage consideration with net proceeds intended for debt reduction; further cost alignment actions underway targeting Q4 and fiscal 2026 impact.
  • Regional Performance -- Latin America achieved double-digit core revenue growth; Asia Pacific grew mid-single digits; Europe and U.S./Canada decreased mid-single digits, with declines concentrated in genomics, sample collection, and associated categories.

RISKS

  • CFO David Naemura said, "the macroeconomic uncertainty has led to somewhat of a pause taken by many customers and channel partners," contributing to a revenue shortfall of approximately $6 million in the quarter.
  • John Adent noted, "the Food Safety market soften a bit at the end of the quarter," leading to underperformance relative to internal expectations and impacting full-year guidance.
  • Core gross margin decreased by 120 basis points year over year; excluding integration and restructuring costs as well as the reclassification of certain expenses in the prior year period, Q3 gross margin was down 30 basis points year over year, primarily due to lower revenues.
  • Free cash flow for the year guided to a $20 million outflow, with integration CapEx expected to remain elevated before tapering in subsequent fiscal years.

SUMMARY

The Neogen (NEOG 6.13%) call announced CEO John Adent's resignation, concurrent with material leadership transitions including new appointments across R&D, commercial, and HR positions. The company's integration of the Petrifilm product line continues, with initial test production planned for fall 2025 and a phased transition strategy expected to generate duplicate costs during the ramp-up. Management highlighted that macroeconomic softness, customer inventory caution, and global tariff volatility drove approximately $6 million of quarterly revenue underperformance and led to a reduction in full-year financial guidance. Strategic portfolio actions are entering advanced phases, with explanations that proceeds will be used for deleveraging, while free cash flow is pressured by ongoing capital expenditures and margin compression. The outlook for fiscal 2026 includes further cost realignment as the company responds to lower-than-anticipated revenue levels and heightened macroeconomic risk.

  • Food Safety segment core revenue excluding sample collection rose 7%, yet "disappointed" management due to falling short of double-digit growth expectations amid muted distributor stocking.
  • Global Genomics weakness persisted, particularly outside bovine, offsetting stability in core Animal Safety and prompting further focus on bovine as "that's really the business that our restructuring is aimed at focusing around."
  • Guidance and commentary suggest the bulk of the revenue miss stemmed from macro-driven distributor hesitancy and China softness, rather than execution missteps or integration delays.
  • Recent completion of term loan refinancing and launch of portfolio actions demonstrated continued prioritization of debt reduction and balance sheet flexibility in a "very dynamic" trade and policy environment.
  • Management signaled that Petrifilm integration costs will be dimensionalized and disclosed as SKU transfer progresses, indicating temporary cost overlap for fiscal 2026 as new capacity comes online.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Petrifilm: Proprietary ready-to-use microbiological testing products developed as part of the acquired 3M Food Safety business for quantifying microorganisms in food and environment samples.
  • Bovine Genomics: Genetic testing and analysis services focused on cattle, enabling trait selection, disease resistance, and productivity improvements for livestock producers.
