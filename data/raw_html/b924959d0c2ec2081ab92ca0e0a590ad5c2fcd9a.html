Klaviyo (KVYO) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 9:08AM EST

Date

Tuesday, Feb. 10, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Co-Founder and Co-CEO — Andrew Bialecki
  • Co-CEO — Chano Fernandez
  • Chief Financial Officer — Amanda Whalen
  • Director of Investor Relations — Ryan Plain

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $1.234 billion for the fiscal year, up 32% year over year.
  • Q4 revenue -- $350 million, representing 30% year-over-year growth, with annualized run rate now $1.4 billion.
  • Non-GAAP operating margin -- 14% for the year and 15% in Q4, expanding 170 basis points and 900 basis points, respectively.
  • Free cash flow -- $87 million in Q4, up 61% year over year; free cash flow margin was 16% for the year.
  • Customer metrics -- 193,000+ customers in over 100 countries; customers with over $50,000 in ARR rose 37% to 3,912, with 349 net new additions in the cohort.
  • Net revenue retention (NRR) -- 110% for the year, expanding over 200 basis points.
  • International revenue growth -- 42% for the year; Q4 international revenue led by Italy grew 41% year over year and now exceeds one-third of total business.
  • Product adoption -- 60% of ARR from multiproduct customers; over 15% of ARR from customers adopting at least three products.
  • AI engagement -- More than half of campaigns by marketing agent users are AI-generated, performing as well or better than manual campaigns and reducing campaign creation time.
  • Enterprise pipeline -- The number of customers generating at least $1 million ARR doubled in the year, supported by new partnerships and expanded leadership in enterprise sales.
  • Text and WhatsApp usage -- 29% of SMB plus customers utilize text and WhatsApp; WhatsApp launch contributed to international adoption trends.
  • Guidance -- Raised 2026 revenue outlook to $1.501 billion-$1.509 billion, representing 21.5%-22.5% projected growth, with a non-GAAP operating margin guidance of 14.5%-15%.
  • Platform scale -- Processed half a trillion customer interactions annually, with 3.7 billion daily signals across 8 billion consumer profiles.
  • Q1 2026 expectations -- Revenue between $346 million and $350 million (23.5%-25% growth), non-GAAP operating income of $50 million-$53 million; seasonality and product ramps expected to weight revenue to the year’s second half.
  • Operational expenses -- Non-GAAP operating expenses were 58% of revenue, the lowest level since IPO, attributed to AI-driven internal productivity gains.

Summary

Klaviyo (KVYO 7.76%) highlighted a surge in large-customer and international adoption, underscoring strategic wins in enterprise and a record expansion of high-value accounts. Management detailed the integration of autonomous agents and AI-driven workflows, emphasizing record multiproduct penetration and the positive impact on customer automation outcomes. Multiple operational milestones were discussed, including notable platform scale—processing 3.7 billion daily signals—and significant internal efficiency gains linked to AI, which enabled margin expansion alongside reinvestment for growth. The company’s guidance incorporated only minimal contribution from new AI and service products, framing these as “embedded upside” to its already derisked 2026 outlook. Strategic partnerships, such as with Accenture, were framed as critical to future enterprise momentum, with increasing adoption of SMS, WhatsApp, and agent solutions across all segments enabling deeper market penetration and new revenue streams.

  • Chano Fernandez stated, "the number of customers generating at least $1 million of ARR doubled last year," highlighting deepening enterprise traction.
  • CFO Amanda Whalen noted, "there is minimal contribution from service built in. The," with continued growth in the core business and cross-sell channels providing a strong baseline.
  • Co-founder Andrew Bialecki asserted, "Open rates increased by 50%, and revenue per campaign grew by 40%," for customers using the marketing agent to automate campaigns.
  • The Q4 expansion of 349 net new customers in the $50,000+ ARR cohort set a new record, exceeding the previous high by more than 25%.
  • Amanda Whalen described the key structural advantage as "Our infrastructure is what allows agents and AI tools to operate consistently as volume and complexity increase," positioning the company within the broader AI ecosystem.
  • International business mix now exceeds one-third of total revenue, propelled by accounts such as Kiko Milano and Bayer, and significant growth in markets like Italy and Spain.
  • Agent-powered service adoption demonstrated a "75% resolution rate" and over "100% increase in average order value," according to case study data cited by management.
  • Management described sales and operational strategies as moving toward greater rigor, discipline, and alignment to drive repeatability and performance in enterprise segments.

Industry glossary

  • Klaviyo Attributed Value (KAV): Metric representing incremental revenue generated for customers as a result of engagement activities run through the Klaviyo platform.
  • MCP server: Klaviyo's integration tool that connects external models or agents with first-party data and messaging infrastructure.
  • NRR (Net Revenue Retention): Measures year over year recurring revenue growth from existing customers, including upgrades, downgrades, and churn.
  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): The value of contracted recurring revenue from customers, annualized.
