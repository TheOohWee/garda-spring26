Kymera (KYMR) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 26, 2026 at 3:57PM EST

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 26, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Nello Mainolfi
  • Chief Medical Officer — Jared Gollob
  • Chief Financial Officer — Bruce Jacobs

TAKEAWAYS

  • Cash Position -- Year-end cash balance was $1.6 billion, described as sufficient to support operations into 2029, including the completion of KT-621 Phase IIb trials and initial Phase III activities.
  • Capital Raise -- Nearly $1 billion was raised in 2025, materially strengthening liquidity and enabling execution of broad development plans.
  • Operating Expenses -- R&D expenses for the quarter were $83.8 million, of which $7.6 million was noncash stock-based compensation; adjusted cash R&D spend increased 16% sequentially from Q3 2025 to $76.2 million.
  • G&A Costs -- G&A expenses were $16.9 million, with $6.9 million in noncash stock-based compensation; adjusted cash G&A rose 1% from Q3 2025 to $10 million.
  • Collaboration Revenue -- $2.9 million in collaboration revenue was recognized for the quarter, attributable to the Gilead partnership.
  • Milestone Potential -- The current Gilead deal includes up to $750 million in total development and commercial milestones, with a $45 million payment triggered by exercise of the option on the CDK2 program; Sanofi partnership presents up to $1 billion in potential milestones.
  • STAT6 Program Progress -- KT-621 showed "robust STAT6 degradation" and "excellent safety and tolerability" in Phase I and "highly encouraging impact on efficacy endpoints" in Phase Ib in atopic dermatitis patients.
  • Phase IIb Trials Initiated -- Two placebo-controlled Phase IIb trials for KT-621 are underway, targeting atopic dermatitis (AD) and asthma, with data expected mid-2027 for AD and late 2027 for asthma.
  • Patient Opportunity -- Management estimates 140 million diagnosed Type 2 patients across the U.S. EU5, and Japan, with 50 million classified as moderate to severe, but only approximately 2 million treated with advanced systemic therapies.
  • Addressable Market -- The current advanced systemic therapies segment for Type 2 diseases is valued at $20 billion annually, predominantly captured by dupilumab; management sees market expansion potential based on new oral options.
  • IRF5 Degrader (KT-579) -- IND clearance received; Phase I healthy volunteer dosing initiated, with first-in-human data expected in the second half of 2026 and lupus patient proof-of-concept as a development focus.
  • Preclinical Evidence for KT-579 -- Data in lupus and rheumatoid arthritis (RA) animal models were described as "compelling" and presented at a major scientific meeting.
  • Clinical Endpoints for KT-621 -- Measures included significant reductions in Type 2 biomarkers (TARC, Eotaxin-3, FeNO), with robust improvements in key clinical endpoints (EASI, Pruritus NRS, IGA, SCORAD, and PROs).
  • Safety Profile of KT-621 -- Chronic 6- to 9-month toxicology studies in rats and primates did not observe any adverse findings of any type across all doses and concentrations tested.
  • Future Pipeline Expansion -- One new development candidate is targeted for announcement during the second half of the year.

SUMMARY

Kymera Therapeutics (KYMR 2.63%) emphasized strong pipeline progress, with KT-621 advancing into parallel Phase IIb studies for atopic dermatitis and asthma, supported by previously reported efficacy and safety data. Management clarified that significant market expansion could result if oral agents like KT-621 shift advanced therapies from niche to mainstream use in Type 2 diseases. The company disclosed major potential milestone payments from partnerships with Gilead and Sanofi, which are not reflected in the current cash runway guidance. First-in-human dosing began for KT-579, an oral IRF5 degrader, within a Phase I study, aiming to address multiple autoimmune conditions, with lupus as an initial proof-of-concept indication. Operational discipline was highlighted by management, referencing measured growth in both R&D and G&A spending levels. Significant leadership expansion occurred with the addition of Neil Graham as Chief Development Officer, bringing extensive clinical development experience.

  • Management stated, "our year-end cash balance to $1.6 billion," and this "extends our runway into 2029" with the capacity to execute planned late-stage clinical development.
  • CEO Mainolfi referred to the oral STAT6 program KT-621 as a "breakout year" accomplishment and cited the opportunity to reach "millions of patients who would benefit from more effective therapies."
  • Gollob reported that KT-621 demonstrated "data were in line with or numerically exceeded published data for dupilumab at 4 weeks," on clinical endpoints.
  • Jacobs noted that the company "received an upfront payment of $40 million" in the Gilead deal, but milestone receipts are pending and not included in the current runway forecast.
  • No interim analyses are planned for KT-621 Phase IIb studies to maintain blinded study integrity, with top-line results anticipated after study completion.
  • IRF5 selectivity was highlighted, with Mainolfi stating, "Our selectivity is pristine because we've been able to find this molecule that is actually not functional. So it does not inhibit anything. It only binds to IRF5, all the IRF5s splicing variants, but not other IRFs. And this allows us to give the utmost selectivity."
  • Gollob explained functional biomarker targets for KT-579, aiming for "a 50% to 80% reduction in these biomarkers across the 3 TLR pathways assessed if we're engaging IRF5 effectively."
  • Preclinical asthma data for KT-621 showed activity "always at least as well and in many cases, better than dupilumab," according to Mainolfi.
  • Operational strategy includes starting all registrational Phase III trials in parallel post-Phase II, subject to regulatory discussions.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • STAT6: Signal Transducer and Activator of Transcription 6, a transcription factor involved in IL-4 and IL-13 mediated immune signaling, targeted for degradation in atopic dermatitis and asthma.
  • IRF5: Interferon Regulatory Factor 5, a genetically validated transcription factor central to immune pathway regulation in autoimmune diseases; KT-579 is designed to selectively degrade IRF5.
  • Type 2 Diseases: Immune-mediated diseases characterized by Type 2 inflammation, including atopic dermatitis and asthma, often treated with systemic or biologic therapies.
  • TLR Pathways: Toll-Like Receptor pathways (e.g., TLR7, 8, 9), key components of innate immunity relevant for immune activation and targeted in functional biomarker assays in IRF5 development.
  • EASI: Eczema Area and Severity Index, a clinical endpoint for atopic dermatitis measuring extent and severity of the disease.
  • PROs: Patient-Reported Outcomes, direct patient assessments of their health condition, functioning, or quality of life used as clinical trial endpoints.
  • FeNO: Fractional exhaled Nitric Oxide, a biomarker of airway inflammation, used as a measure in the KT-621 clinical trials.
  • BROaDEN2: The name of the Phase IIb clinical trial evaluating KT-621 in atopic dermatitis patients.
  • BREADTH: The Phase IIb clinical trial evaluating KT-621 in moderate to severe eosinophilic asthma patients.
