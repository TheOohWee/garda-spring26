Takeaways

  • Cloud ARR -- $433.6 million, an increase of 31% year over year, now comprising 81% of total ARR.
  • Total ARR -- $535 million, up 22% with growth attributed to both new client additions and expansion within existing clients.
  • SaaS revenue -- $102.5 million, a 28% year-over-year rise, now 73% of total revenue.
  • Total revenue -- $140.2 million, up 16% year over year, driven by increased adoption of cloud solutions.
  • License revenue -- $25.4 million, reflecting a decrease of 9% year over year, consistent with the ongoing cloud transition.
  • Professional services revenue -- $12.3 million, down 7% year over year, attributed to the shift toward cloud offerings.
  • Non-GAAP gross margin -- 78.1%, improved from 76.7% year over year due to product mix and efficiency gains.
  • Non-GAAP operating expenses -- $81.8 million, compared to $74.1 million in the prior year period, reflecting continued investment in growth and go-to-market initiatives.
  • Non-GAAP operating income -- $27.7 million, up from $18.9 million last year.
  • Non-GAAP diluted EPS -- $0.33, based on approximately 83 million diluted shares.
  • Free cash flow -- $22.2 million for the quarter.
  • Cash and cash equivalents -- $191.2 million at quarter-end, including the impact of the $100 million share repurchase.
  • Remaining performance obligations (RPO) -- $777.1 million, up 26% year over year, providing incremental revenue visibility.
  • Large client base -- 834 clients now generate at least $100,000 in ARR, up from 728 the prior year, representing 30% of total clients.
  • Cloud net revenue retention rate -- 124%.
  • Share repurchase activity -- $100 million repurchased in the quarter (2.3 million shares); the total $150 million program completed with 3.4 million shares repurchased; new $200 million authorization initiated in January 2026.
  • Partner ecosystem expansion -- Over 145 partners; partners directly involved in 7 of the 10 largest fiscal Q2 deals; service partner certifications increased 35% year over year.
  • AI product innovation -- More than 70 new AI capabilities in DealCloud and significant new AI features released for Intapp Time, contributing to client migrations and first-time purchases.
  • Guidance -- Fiscal Q3 SaaS revenue expected at $105 million-$106 million; total revenue at $143.8 million-$144.8 million; non-GAAP operating income at $23.1 million-$24.1 million; non-GAAP EPS at $0.27-$0.29, approximately 83 million diluted shares.
  • Full-year guidance -- SaaS revenue expected between $415 million-$419 million; total revenue at $570.3 million-$574.3 million; non-GAAP operating income at $99.9 million-$103.9 million; non-GAAP EPS at $1.20-$1.24, approximately 83 million diluted shares.

Summary

Intapp (INTA 4.23%) reported accelerated adoption of its cloud platform and a rising mix of recurring SaaS revenue, supported by significant new AI offerings and expanding engagement with enterprise clients. Management highlighted a deepening strategic partnership with Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT), with Azure Marketplace playing a material role in both deal origination and sales cycle acceleration. Intapp completed and launched a $100 million share repurchase in the quarter and authorized a new $200 million buyback, signaling continued capital discipline amid robust cash generation.

  • The company indicated large law, accounting, and investment banking firms are modernizing compliance and operational workflows, adopting purpose-built AI for competitive differentiation.
  • AI-driven product releases, such as enhancements to DealCloud and Intapp Time, acted as catalysts for new cloud migrations, expansion agreements, and customer wins across regulated verticals.
  • Management stated, "we are growing by adding new capabilities and increasing our global enterprise go-to-market reach," and sees monetization of AI capabilities as a continuing opportunity with experimentation in new pricing models.
  • The fiscal Q2 client base included a notable increase in clients generating at least $100,000 in ARR, attributed to upsell, cross-sell, and multi-product growth under the enterprise go-to-market motion.
  • Board-approved buybacks were positioned as primarily anti-dilutive, with further details on broader capital allocation to be disclosed at the February investor event.
  • Management described durable market demand, highlighted by 26% RPO growth and new relationships enabled by its growing partner ecosystem.
  • Intapp is preparing for a major product release at its upcoming Intapp Amplify event, described by management as "the most consequential release that the company is setting up to bring to everybody."

Industry glossary

  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): Subscription revenue expected to repeat annually under current contracts, excluding non-recurring or services-based components.
  • RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations): The contracted, yet unrecognized, revenue under signed agreements still to be delivered.
  • DealCloud: Intapp's proprietary AI-powered relationship and deal management platform for financial and professional services firms.
  • Intapp Time: Intapp's AI-augmented timekeeping solution, designed for professional services firms to automate and optimize billing and compliance.
  • Azure Marketplace: Microsoft’s cloud-based marketplace where firms can purchase and deploy certified third-party software like Intapp's solutions.
