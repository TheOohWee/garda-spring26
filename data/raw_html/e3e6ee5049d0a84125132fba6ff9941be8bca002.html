Earnings Call Transcript

McKesson (MCK) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 4, 2026 at 5:45PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 4, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Brian Tyler
  • Chief Financial Officer — Britt Vitalone

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted EPS Guidance Raised -- Full-year adjusted EPS guidance increased to $38.80–$39.20, reflecting 17%-19% growth.
  • Revenue -- Consolidated revenue rose 11% to $106.2 billion, supported by broad-based business growth.
  • Adjusted Operating Profit -- Operating profit climbed 13% to $1.7 billion, attributed to increased demand in access solutions and specialty distribution volumes.
  • Gross Profit -- Gross profit was $3.7 billion, representing a 10% increase, led by provider growth within oncology and multispecialty.
  • Operating Expenses -- Expenses grew 7% to $2.1 billion, principally due to acquisitions and growth platforms, with a 138 basis point improvement in expenses as a percentage of gross profit.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Adjusted diluted EPS increased 16% to $9.34.
  • North American Pharmaceutical Segment Revenue -- Segment revenue climbed 9% to $88.3 billion, driven by prescription volume growth and specialty distribution strength.
  • GLP-1 Distribution Revenue -- GLP-1 distribution revenue reached $14 billion, up 26% year over year and 7% sequentially.
  • North American Pharmaceutical Operating Profit -- Operating profit for the segment increased 6% to $872 million.
  • Oncology and Multispecialty Segment Revenue -- Revenue grew 37% to $13 billion; acquisitions of Prism and Core Ventures contributed 13 percentage points to segment revenue growth.
  • Oncology and Multispecialty Operating Profit -- Operating profit expanded 57% to $366 million; excluding acquisitions, organic operating profit increased 15%.
  • Prescription Technology Solutions Segment Revenue -- Revenue in this segment rose 9% to $1.5 billion, driven by access and affordability solutions.
  • Prescription Technology Solutions Operating Profit -- Operating profit increased 18% to $277 million.
  • Medical-Surgical Solutions Segment -- Revenue in this segment grew 1% to $3 billion, with operating profit declining 10% to $265 million due to lower physician office volumes and softer seasonal illness incidence.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Third-quarter free cash flow totaled $1.1 billion, including $175 million in capital expenditures.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $781 million was returned to shareholders, comprising $680 million in share repurchases and $101 million in dividends.
  • Norwegian Business Divestiture -- Divestiture completed in January, marking full exit from European operations; contributed $0.05 to third-quarter adjusted EPS.
  • Medical-Surgical Separation Progress -- Transition service agreements completed, with IPO targeted for the second half of 2027, subject to market conditions and regulatory approvals.

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • Medical-Surgical operating profit declined 10% due to lower physician office volumes and a softer seasonal illness environment, which management noted can introduce variability and impact quarterly and annual results.

SUMMARY

McKesson (MCK 0.90%) reported double-digit growth across key financial metrics in the quarter, driven by performance in oncology, biopharma services, and North American distribution. Management highlighted meaningful contributions from recent acquisitions and technology investments that enhanced operating efficiency and patient support capacity. The company has finalized its European exit and announced important progress toward a planned medical-surgical segment IPO, with preparation milestones on track. Guidance for fiscal 2026 was raised and narrowed for adjusted EPS, revenue, and operating profit, reflecting observed business momentum and stable utilization trends.

  • Britt Vitalone stated, We delivered strong operational execution and enhanced efficiency, driving a 138 basis point improvement in operating expenses as a percentage of gross profit as compared to the prior year.
  • GLP-1 medications drove significant revenue growth, with management confirming the continued variability of this segment from quarter to quarter.
  • Integration of Florida Cancer Specialists and Prism Vision remains on pace, with volumes and business performance at or slightly ahead of prior expectations.
  • Technology and automation investments increased staff productivity in annual verification programs, allowing each full-time employee to support 120 more patients than the prior year.
  • Recent U.S. policy changes, including the Inflation Reduction Act’s Medicare Part D modifications, are already included in updated guidance, and the implications for McKesson Corporation are quite navigable.
  • Operating profit and adjusted EPS have delivered five-year compound annual growth rates of 11% and 18%, respectively, with reported return on invested capital now exceeding 30%.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • GLP-1: Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists; a class of drugs primarily used to treat type 2 diabetes and increasingly prescribed for weight management.
  • DSCSA: Drug Supply Chain Security Act; a U.S. federal law establishing track-and-trace requirements for the pharmaceutical supply chain.
  • MSO: Management Services Organization; an entity providing administrative and support services for medical practices.
