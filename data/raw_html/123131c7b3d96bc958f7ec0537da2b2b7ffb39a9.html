Culp (CULP) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 7, 2026 at 9:32AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Sep. 5, 2024 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Robert Culp
  • Chief Financial Officer — Kenneth Bowling
  • President, Upholstery Fabrics — Mary Beth Hunsberger
  • President, Mattress Fabrics — Tommy Bruno

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated Net Sales -- $56.5 million, down 0.2%, with sales flat year over year despite industry softness and sequential improvement across segments.
  • Mattress Fabrics Segment Sales -- $28.1 million, down 3.9%; sequentially up 9%, attributed to higher order levels and new product placements.
  • Upholstery Fabrics Segment Sales -- $28.5 million, up 3.7%; sequential increase of 19.7% supported by strong residential and hospitality demand.
  • Upholstery Fabrics Operating Margin -- 6%, up from 4.8%, reflecting a 120 basis point improvement on higher sales and reduced fixed costs.
  • Mattress Fabrics Operating Loss -- $3.5 million, worsening from $1.4 million due to restructuring-related inefficiencies and lower volumes.
  • Total Operating Loss -- $6.9 million, including $2.7 million in restructuring charges, up from $3.1 million with $517,000 in charges previously.
  • Adjusted Loss from Operations -- $4.1 million, compared with $2.6 million previously, adjusting for restructuring items.
  • Net Loss -- $7.3 million or $0.58 per diluted share, higher than $3.3 million or $0.27 per share last year.
  • Hospitality/Contract Sales Mix -- Represented 33% of upholstery fabrics segment sales, supporting segment profitability and growth.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (Trailing 12 Months) -- Negative $5.7 million, showing a 66% improvement from negative $16.7 million for the same period last year.
  • Inventory Reduction -- Both segments reduced inventory from prior fiscal year-end despite increased sequential sales, assisting cash management.
  • Total Cash and Liquidity -- $13.5 million in cash and $19.2 million in domestic borrowing availability, with $4 million outstanding under the China credit line.
  • Cash Flow (Q1) -- Cash flow from operations negative $206,000; free cash flow negative $550,000, both improved from negative $4.4 million and negative $4.2 million, respectively, last year.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $501,000 for first three months; full-year projected at $4.8 million, mainly targeting maintenance and efficiency enhancements in mattress fabrics.
  • Restructuring Plan Charges -- Total expected restructuring-related charges revised to $5.1 million (from $8 million), with $3 million in expected cash outflows.
  • Annualized Savings from Restructuring -- Anticipated $10 million to $11 million, mostly from mattress fabrics, with approximately $1 million from corporate and shared services reductions.
  • Canadian Facility Sale -- Projected after-tax proceeds of approximately $9 million to $10 million highlighted as a major liquidity event.
  • Guidance for Next Quarter -- Management expects consolidated net sales in the upcoming quarter to be comparable to this quarter and projects a near breakeven adjusted EBITDA, excluding restructuring charges.
  • Return to Profitability Target -- Company expects positive consolidated adjusted operating income, excluding restructuring and related charges, in the third quarter.
  • Asset-Light Strategy Expansion -- Management confirmed transition of mattress fabrics damask business to a sourcing model and consolidation of North American manufacturing as core restructuring elements.

RISKS

  • Chief Financial Officer Kenneth Bowling reported, "Our overall operating performance for the first quarter as compared to the prior year period was pressured by inefficiencies primarily related to the significant restructuring activity underway in the mattress fabric segment."
  • President Robert Culp acknowledged, "mattress fabrics operating results are being pressured by these restructuring actions in the first half of the year, especially in the first quarter."
  • CEO Robert Culp stated, "We anticipate macro industry conditions may remain pressured during fiscal 2025, although we also believe there is some stabilizing of industry trends."
  • Company confirmed no share repurchases in the first quarter and does not expect any activity in the second quarter, citing a continued focus on liquidity preservation.

SUMMARY

Management at Culp (CULP +5.78%) executed sequential sales growth in both segments, with upholstery fabrics delivering stronger profitability and operating margins. The restructuring plan is progressing with key milestones, including consolidation of manufacturing and transition to asset-light models, expected to yield annualized savings of $10 million to $11 million and significant liquidity from the anticipated Canadian facility sale. Operational inefficiencies and costs tied to restructuring continued to negatively affect the mattress fabrics division and contributed to overall losses. Guidance signals expectations of flat sales next quarter, breakeven adjusted EBITDA, and a return to positive adjusted operating income in the following quarter, assuming restructuring plans stay on track despite subdued macro demand.

  • Management expects to complete most restructuring transitions by the end of the next quarter, enabling the business to achieve cost reductions and process improvements without dependence on a broader market recovery.
  • Upholstery fabrics' hospitality and commercial mix, including expanded roller shade production and new hotel partnerships, is positioned to drive future segment growth.
  • Effective working capital management, including meaningful inventory reductions despite higher sales, improved cash flow performance versus last year.
  • Restructuring cost estimates decreased from $8 million to $5.1 million, reflecting better asset utilization and lower-than-forecasted cash outflows.
  • Guidance for capital spending indicates a focus on selective maintenance and quick payback investments, mainly in mattress fabrics to support efficiency gains.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Damask: A type of woven fabric commonly used in mattress covers, often featuring elaborate patterns and requiring specialized manufacturing.
  • Asset-light model: A business strategy emphasizing reliance on sourcing and partnerships over internal manufacturing, aimed at reducing fixed costs and capital intensity.
  • Read Window: Culp’s hospitality window treatment and roller shade business, referenced as a driver of hospitality segment growth.
  • CHF: Culp Home Fashions, referring specifically to the company’s mattress fabrics segment.
  • CUF: Culp Upholstery Fabrics, referring to the company’s upholstery fabrics segment.
