Sealed Air (SEE) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 27, 2025 at 6:57PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Nov. 4, 2025 at 11:56 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Dustin Semach
  • Chief Financial Officer — Kristen Actis-Grande

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Risks

  • Accelerating Volume Declines — Management stated "market pressures accelerating in the fourth quarter, resulting in volumes lower than anticipated, particularly in North America Food, along with further competitive pricing pressure in both businesses."
  • Negative Net Price Realization — Dustin Semach reported that negative net price realization, totaling negative $75 million for the full year, will be "a $10 million step-up" in the fourth quarter.
  • Macroeconomic Uncertainty — Dustin Semach cited "uncertainty is increasing" due to softer global growth outlooks and weakening consumer demand in the U.S. as a risk for forward demand.
  • U.S. Beef Production Pressure — U.S. beef production fell approximately 10.5% year-over-year in the third quarter, with a "The steeper-than-anticipated decline in beef production is pressuring our industrial exposed volumes." and full year 2025 beef slaughter expected to be down by about 5%, with a similar decline anticipated in 2026.

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $1.35 billion, up 0.5% as reported, reflecting a 1% decline in constant currency.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $287 million, up 4% as reported and 3% in constant currency, with a margin of 21.3%, up 80 basis points year-over-year.
  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share -- $0.87, up 10% as reported and 9% in constant currency, driven by higher adjusted EBITDA and lower interest expense.
  • Volumes -- Down less than 1% overall; Food was flat, while Protective fell less than 2%; foodservice volume rose 4% year-over-year.
  • Food Segment Net Sales -- $910 million, flat in constant currency with balanced volume and pricing; adjusted EBITDA of $215 million, up 3% constant currency, and margin at 23.6%, up 70 basis points.
  • Protective Segment Net Sales -- $442 million, down $12 million or 3% in constant currency; adjusted EBITDA $78 million, up approximately 3% as reported, with a 17.7% margin, up 80 basis points.
  • Protective Materials Growth -- Protective materials volume grew 1% year-over-year, the first positive inflection since 2021; this was offset by lower equipment volumes.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $120 million generated in the quarter, up 4% year-over-year; full-year guidance reaffirmed at approximately $400 million.
  • Liquidity Position -- $1.3 billion in total liquidity at quarter end, including $282 million in cash and the remainder in committed revolver capacity.
  • Net Leverage -- Net leverage ratio is 3.5x; target to reach approximately 3x by the end of 2026.
  • Capital Expenditures -- Full year CapEx guidance lowered to $175 million, a $25 million reduction, attributed to increased capital discipline tied to transformation priorities.
  • Sales and EBITDA Guidance -- Full-year sales targeted at a $5.3 billion midpoint; adjusted EBITDA range raised to $1.12 billion to $1.14 billion, up $5 million from the prior midpoint, with implied Q4 adjusted EBITDA of $274 million.
  • Share Count and Tax Rate -- Full-year shares outstanding expected at approximately 147 million; updated full-year tax rate guidance at 26%.
  • Protective National Accounts -- Management landed "multiple 7-figure competitive wins at national accounts" in Protective, primarily in fulfillment, contributing to future volume stabilization.
  • Network Optimization Initiative -- A holistic evaluation is underway across both segments, aimed at facility, asset, and logistics optimization; management will provide more detail in 2026.

Summary

Sealed Air Corporation (SEE +0.02%) delivered modest revenue growth and margin expansion under persistent demand headwinds, with notable volume outperformance in its foodservice and Protective materials businesses. Management cited accelerating volume and pricing pressures, especially in North America Food, prompting a $5 million upward revision to adjusted EBITDA guidance and a further cut to expected capital expenditures. The company closed a refinancing of its revolving credit facility, enhancing liquidity and extending access to low-cost capital. Full-year free cash flow guidance was reaffirmed despite lower CapEx, due to a planned reduction in accounts payable and a seasonally weighted inventory drawdown in the fourth quarter. The protective segment demonstrated its first material volume inflection since 2021, aided by new large account wins that are expected to support stabilization into 2026.

  • Kristen Actis-Grande stated free cash flow benefits from both stronger EBITDA and lower CapEx, but is held flat at the $400 million guide because "reductions in accounts payable" are offsetting improvements.
  • Operating expenses benefited from ongoing back-office restructuring, highlighted by over 300 employees now at the Manila G&A center and reductions in IT costs in "the high double digits."
  • North American food segment faces further headwinds as beef production is now projected down around 5%-6% for both 2025 and 2026, compounding persistent demand softening among lower- and middle-income U.S. consumers.
  • Current productivity and transformation initiatives are producing measurable cost savings, with a projected Q4 benefit "a little bit under $30 million" and a full-year contribution of roughly $150 million in restructuring and cost control.
  • Management is monitoring the near-term impact of a potential U.S. government shutdown on SNAP funding, noting it "may continue to exacerbate the trade downs we are seeing" but expects the effect to be temporary.
  • Further details on the timing of transformation initiatives and the 2026 outlook are deferred to the next earnings update in February.

Industry glossary

  • Shrink Bag: Flexible plastic packaging used for vacuum-sealing and preserving perishable foods, prominent in foodservice and industrial food processing.
  • Fulfillment Portfolio: Protective packaging product suite designed for e-commerce and large-scale distribution operations, including AUTOBAG and specialty foam.
  • Liquibox: Brand and product line focused on fluids and liquids flexible packaging, acquired to expand SEE’s offerings in beverage and dairy.
  • Formulated Pricing: Pricing contracts that automatically adjust product prices based on an index of underlying input costs such as resins or labor.
