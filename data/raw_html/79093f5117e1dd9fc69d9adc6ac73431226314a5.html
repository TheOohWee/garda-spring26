Cigna (CI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 9:53AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 5, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — David Cordani
  • President and Chief Operating Officer — Brian Evanko
  • Chief Financial Officer — Ann Dennison
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations — Ralph Giacobbe

TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted Revenue -- $275 billion for the full year, reflecting 11% growth and signaling consistent topline momentum driven by core business expansion.
  • Adjusted Earnings per Share (EPS) -- $29.84 for the year, representing a 9% increase and maintaining a multiyear growth trajectory.
  • Adjusted After-Tax Income -- $8 billion for the year, underpinning operational performance in both Evernorth and Cigna Healthcare.
  • Customer Net Promoter Score -- Increased in each of the largest businesses year over year, directly signaling improved customer experience.
  • Specialty and Care Services Revenue -- $26.7 billion for the quarter, up 14%, with adjusted earnings of $1 billion and performance attributed to elevated specialty volumes and biosimilar uptake.
  • Specialty Scripts Growth -- 13% year over year, primarily linked to increased demand and biosimilar adoption within the Evernorth platform.
  • EverNorth Pharmacy Benefit Services Revenue -- $36.3 billion for the quarter, with $1.2 billion in adjusted earnings, highlighting the effect of strategic investments and client retention above 97% for 2026 renewals.
  • Medicare Business Divestiture -- Completed, as management noted its role in portfolio repositioning and risk mitigation.
  • Global FTC Settlement -- $7 billion in out-of-pocket cost relief over the next decade for approximately 100 million customers, focused on lower insulin and brand-name drug prices and increased transparency.
  • Rebate-Free Pharmacy Model Adoption -- Complete adoption for Cigna Healthcare fully insured portfolio in 2027, with at least 50% of Evernorth clients by the end of 2028.
  • GLP-1 Medicines Initiatives -- Expanded patient assurance programs now cap member out-of-pocket costs and add new patient-support models for GLP-1 dispensing.
  • Clarity Product Launch -- Introduced as a new offering promising up to 10% client medical cost savings; features a copay-only structure and integrated digital interface for customers.
  • Medical Care Ratio (MCR) Guidance -- 2026 range expected between 83.7%-84.7%, incorporating recent pricing actions and factoring for continued elevated cost trends.
  • Cash Flow from Operations -- $9.6 billion generated in 2025; management expects $9 billion in 2026 operations cash flow, explaining the $600 million decrease is due to pharmacy benefit services client renewals and investment outlays.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $5.2 billion returned in 2025 via $3.6 billion share repurchase (11.9 million shares) and $1.6 billion in dividends, aligning with continued capital deployment priorities.
  • Debt-to-Capitalization Ratio -- Improved to 43% by year end, with a quarterly improvement of 190 basis points, and management restated a target of approximately 40% long-term.
  • 2026 Adjusted EPS Outlook -- Guidance for at least $30.25 EPS, representing management’s commitment to maintaining annual growth.
  • EverNorth 2026 Guidance -- Segment-level adjusted earnings expected to be at least $6.9 billion, with investment spending for infrastructure weighted to the back half of the year.
  • Cigna Healthcare 2026 Guidance -- Adjusted earnings anticipated at a minimum of $4.5 billion, expecting first quarter earnings to exceed 30% of the full-year total.
  • Effective Tax Rate Impact -- Relocation of GPO operations to the US may increase the effective tax rate by up to 1% once fully phased in, as stated by management, but “totally manageable.”.
  • Total Medical Customers -- Forecasted at approximately 18.1 million for year-end 2026, with growth in select, middle-market, and international offset by national account and exchange segment declines.

RISKS

  • Ann Dennison noted assumptions incorporate appropriate prudence given the continued elevated cost environment, with pricing actions in stop-loss and exchange businesses offset by persistent cost inflation in medical trends.
  • Cash flow from operations guidance for 2026 ($9 billion) reflects a projected $600 million decline due to lower pharmacy benefit services contributions and investment needs.
  • The move of GPO capabilities from Switzerland to the US, management indicated, could result in up to a 1% increase in the effective tax rate if unmitigated.

SUMMARY

The Cigna Group (CI 2.59%) reported double-digit top-line growth and high single-digit bottom-line growth, driven by strong specialty pharmacy and bio-similar uptake, successful client retention, and disciplined portfolio actions, including the Medicare business divestiture.

Management emphasized the resolution of major regulatory uncertainty, citing a $7 billion out-of-pocket cost relief settlement with the FTC and alignment with recently passed PBM legislation. Near-term earnings guidance for 2026 targets at least $30.25 per share in adjusted earnings, with the rollout of a rebate-free pharmacy benefit model and continued capital deployment through dividends and share repurchases. Year-end 2026 medical customer count is projected flat at 18.1 million, as segmental growth is offset by strategic repricing and mix management. Cigna highlighted ongoing investment in data-driven innovation—including GLP-1 program expansion, AI-driven digital tools, and the Clarity offering—to reinforce future profitability while recognizing prudent caution regarding medical cost trends and cash flow impacts from temporary working capital shifts.

  • The specialty and care services platform now comprises around 35% of company income, up from around 25% three years ago, with high-single-digit secular growth and biosimilar market penetration cited as enduring catalysts.
  • Brian Evanko said, The entire Cigna Healthcare fully insured book will be adopting the new model in 2027, and management expects at least half of Evernorth clients on the new model by year-end 2028.
  • Quarterly seasonality for earnings and medical care ratio was clarified, with first-quarter Cigna Healthcare MCR guided below 81% and over 30% of segment earnings expected in that period.
  • Cigna’s strategic capital uses for 2026 include $1.3 billion in capital expenditures and $1.6 billion in expected shareholder dividends, based on a raised quarterly payout.
  • Management reported no anticipated changes to pharmacy benefit service revenue recognition methodology with the shift to a fee-based model, maintaining comparable level of profitability between the legacy model and the new model.
  • Prescription benefit segment investment spending for infrastructure related to regulatory compliance and model transition is expected to be weighted toward the back half of 2026 and 2027.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PBM (Pharmacy Benefit Manager): A third-party administrator of prescription drug programs for health plans, employers, and government entities, managing formulary design, pricing negotiations, and distribution logistics.
  • Rebate-Free Model: A pharmacy benefit design where clients pay transparent, fixed administrative fees instead of relying on manufacturer rebates or spread pricing, enhancing predictability and transparency of prescription drug costs.
  • GLP-1: A class of drugs (glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists) primarily used to manage type 2 diabetes and obesity, increasingly important among specialty pharmacy products.
  • MCR (Medical Care Ratio): A measure of the percentage of premium revenue spent on claims for medical services, also referred to as medical loss ratio.
  • Biosimilar: A biologic medical product highly similar to an already approved reference product, often used to enhance market competition and lower drug costs.
  • Specialty Scripts: Prescriptions for drugs that treat complex or chronic conditions, often biologic or specialty pharmaceuticals requiring special handling or administration.
  • Clarity: Cigna Healthcare’s recently launched product offering consumers integrated digital access, cost transparency, and streamlined benefits across medical, pharmacy, and supplemental health.
