Zions Bancorp (ZION) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — Harris H. Simmons
  • President & Chief Operating Officer — Scott J. McLean

Risks

  • C&I Loan Charge-Offs — A $50 million charge-off and a $10 million reserve on two related commercial and industrial loans led to a nonrecurring $49 million provision for credit losses in Q3 2025, directly impacting net charge-offs and reducing the allowance for credit losses.
  • Expense Growth Outlook — Management projects a moderate increase in adjusted noninterest expense for Q3 2026 relative to Q3 2025, primarily due to higher marketing investments, ongoing technology costs, and expansion in revenue-generating business lines.
  • Net Interest Income Sensitivity — Guidance for net interest income growth in Q3 2026 is based on the assumption of substantial (225 basis points) Fed funds rate cuts over the next several quarters, introducing potential downside risk if monetary policy diverges from this path.

Takeaways

  • Net Interest Margin -- Increased 11 basis points sequentially to 3.28% in Q3 2025, marking the seventh consecutive quarterly expansion.
  • Net Interest Income -- Net interest income increased by $52 million, or 8%, compared to the third quarter of 2024, driven by fixed asset repricing and favorable funding mix.
  • Noninterest Income -- Adjusted customer-related noninterest income, excluding net credit valuation adjustment (CVA), grew 6% from the prior quarter and 8% year-over-year to $174 million in Q3 2025.
  • Diluted Earnings Per Share -- Reported at $1.48 in the third quarter of 2025, down from $1.63 in the prior quarter. Impacted by a $0.06 per share negative net CVA adjustment and the elevated credit provision in Q3 2025.
  • Pre-Provision Net Revenue -- Adjusted figure reached $352 million in Q3 2025, rising 11% from the prior quarter and 18% year-over-year reflecting adjusted revenue growth exceeding expense growth in Q3 2025.
  • Loan and Deposit Growth -- Average loans and customer deposits increased at annualized rates of 2.1% and 3.1%, respectively, over the prior quarter; period-end deposit balances rose by $1.1 billion while total borrowings fell by $1.8 billion in Q3 2025.
  • Deposits Mix Shift -- Average noninterest-bearing deposits increased by $192 million (0.8%) sequentially in Q3 2025, supported by a migration program at multiple affiliates; Total average deposits remained relatively flat in Q3 2025 with an 11.5% reduction in average broker deposits compared to the prior quarter.
  • Efficiency Ratio -- Improved to 59.6% sequentially in Q3 2025 as adjusted expenses declined by $1 million compared to the prior quarter.
  • Provision for Credit Losses -- $49 million was recorded for Q3 2025; Excluding the specific C&I charge-offs, net charge-offs were minimal at 4 basis points annualized for Q3 2025 and overall credit quality improved in Q3 2025.
  • CRE Portfolio Performance -- Nonperforming assets in the $13.5 billion commercial real estate portfolio remained low at 0.54% of loans and other real estate owned in Q3 2025 and classified loan balances declined by $282 million in Q3 2025.
  • Capital Ratios -- The Common Equity Tier 1 ratio reached 11.3% in Q3 2025. Tangible book value per share increased 17% year-over-year in Q3 2025.
  • Outlook for 2026 -- Management guides to moderate increases in net interest income, loan balances, fee income, and adjusted expenses for Q3 2026 compared to Q3 2025.

Summary

Zions Bancorporation (ZION 1.17%) explicitly addressed a one-time $50 million C&I charge-off and an accompanying $49 million provision in Q3 2025, clarifying that it considers these charges isolated following internal and external portfolio reviews. Management maintains its guidance for modest growth in net interest income and loans in Q3 2026, explicitly tied to expectations for significant Federal Reserve rate cuts, with a focus on commercial lending leading anticipated portfolio expansion. The call provided evidence of broad-based noninterest income growth across multiple business lines, supported by increased contributions from capital markets, treasury management, and mortgage activities in Q3 2025. Deposits remained stable, with management highlighting the retention and expansion of noninterest-bearing balances through strategic product migration programs.

  • Chief Financial Officer Richards noted, "net interest margin expanded for the seventh consecutive quarter to 3.28%," highlighting the durability of margin pressures and favorable asset mix.
  • President McLean confirmed, "A greater portion of growth will come from C&I loans in '26," supporting the loan mix shift seen in current guidance.
  • R. Richards stated, "Our organic earnings growth, when coupled with AOCI unrealized loss accretion, has enabled us to grow tangible book value per share by 17% compared to the prior year period in the third quarter of 2025," directly linking earnings performance to capital formation.
  • Management emphasized ongoing review of lending practices, disclosing that a review with an external party will be conducted to learn from the isolated charge-offs.

Industry glossary

  • Net Credit Valuation Adjustment (CVA): An adjustment reflecting current market valuation of counterparty risk, especially impacting capital markets and derivative activities.
  • NDFI: Nondepository Financial Institutions, a regulatory designation encompassing entities such as equipment leasing companies, capital call lines, and subscription lines within bank lending portfolios.
  • AOCI: Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income, a component of shareholders' equity reflecting unrealized gains and losses, often from the securities portfolio.
  • Classified Loans: Credits designated internally or by regulators as higher risk based on financial health or collateral adequacy, including categories such as "special mention," "substandard," or "doubtful."
