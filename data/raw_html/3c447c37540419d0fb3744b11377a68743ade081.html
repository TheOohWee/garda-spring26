RLJ Lodging (RLJ) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 27, 2026 at 11:17AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Friday, February 27, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President & CEO — Leslie D. Hale
  • Chief Financial Officer — Nikhil Bhalla
  • Chief Operating Officer — Thomas J. Bardenett

Takeaways

  • RevPAR -- $137, with a 1.5% decline driven by a 0.9% decrease in occupancy and a 0.7% decrease in ADR.
  • Occupancy -- 68.7% for the portfolio.
  • Average daily rate (ADR) -- $199 across the portfolio.
  • Urban market RevPAR -- San Francisco CBD achieved 52% growth; Northern California, Denver CBD, and New York City posted 18.5%, 10.1%, and 4.7% RevPAR growth, respectively.
  • Non-room revenue growth -- Increased by 7.2%, outpacing RevPAR by nearly 900 basis points, led by F&B, parking, and ancillary revenue streams.
  • Total revenue growth -- Up 0.2%, exceeding the contraction in RevPAR, due to non-room revenue outperformance.
  • Comparable hotel EBITDA -- $87.8 million with a comparable hotel EBITDA margin of 27%, 44 basis points below prior year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $80.4 million reported for the quarter.
  • Adjusted FFO per diluted share -- $0.32 for the quarter.
  • Total operating costs -- Increased by 0.8% in the quarter; full-year increase was 1.6%. Excluding $4.7 million of tax benefits, full-year cost growth was 2.1%.
  • Shareholder returns -- $120 million returned through both dividends and share repurchases.
  • Asset dispositions -- Three hotels sold for $73.7 million at a 17.7x multiple of projected 2025 hotel EBITDA including CapEx; proceeds recycled into share repurchases ($28.6 million for 300,000 shares) and debt paydown.
  • Balance sheet position -- $600 million available on undrawn revolver, $1 billion liquidity, $2.2 billion in debt, 84 out of 92 hotels unencumbered.
  • Debt profile -- All maturities addressed through 2028; revolver maturity extended to 2031; weighted average interest rate is 4.673%; post-payoff, weighted average debt maturity will be roughly 4.5 years.
  • Full-year 2026 guidance -- Comparable RevPAR growth of 0.5%-3%; comparable hotel EBITDA of $344 million-$374 million; adjusted EBITDA of $312 million-$342 million; adjusted FFO per diluted share of $1.21-$1.41.
  • Capital expenditure (CapEx) guidance -- $80 million-$90 million for 2026.
  • Cash G&A guidance -- $32.5 million-$33.5 million for 2026; net interest expense expected at $101 million-$103 million.
  • RevPAR growth catalysts -- World Cup events to deliver approximately 45 basis points of RevPAR growth; ramp-up of recent high-occupancy renovations in Waikiki, Deerfield, and Key West to add 40 basis points.
  • Conversions -- Recent four hotel conversions delivered 15% RevPAR growth for the year; all seven conversions averaged 5% RevPAR growth.
  • Wyndham Boston Beacon Hill conversion -- Anticipated to drive 40% EBITDA uplift post-conversion to Hilton Tapestry; projected returns are "well north of 50%."
  • F&B margin improvement -- 120 basis point year-over-year improvement attributed to margin-focused, beverage-centric renovation and increased community foot traffic at F&B outlets.
  • Expense growth assumptions for 2026 -- Total expenses forecast to rise around 3%; variable at 2%, fixed at 4%, and wage and benefits at 3%-4%.
  • Government business mix -- Government accounted for 3% in a normalized year but was down 20% in 2025; no change in government demand assumed for 2026 guidance.
  • Quarterly contribution expectation -- Q1 adjusted EBITDA forecast to represent roughly 22% of full-year total, reflecting seasonally soft demand and challenging comps.

Risks

  • The government shutdown reduced RevPAR and group demand in key markets including D.C. and Southern California, reflected in a 1.5% RevPAR decline and 20% reduction in government business for 2025.
  • Management noted the portfolio is "80% transient" with a short-term booking window, making forecast visibility subject to late-breaking changes in demand or event performance.
  • Potential underperformance of World Cup-related events, weaker urban market production, or slower ramp in conversions could result in achieving the lower end of guidance, according to CEO Hale.
  • Despite refinancing activities, management stated the company will incur a "minimal increase to our annual interest expense" due to the retirement of lower-cost debt in a higher interest rate environment.

Summary

RLJ Lodging Trust (RLJ 1.26%) reported quarterly results featuring a 1.5% RevPAR contraction alongside outperformance in non-room revenues, enabling total revenue to grow modestly despite macro headwinds. Strategic initiatives included three hotel sales at a 17.7x EBITDA multiple, targeted reinvestment in share repurchases and balance sheet strength, and high-impact capital investments such as conversions that delivered outsized RevPAR and margin gains. Guidance for 2026 anticipates modest RevPAR growth, continued out-of-room revenue expansion, and disciplined expense management, while updated debt transactions extended maturities through 2028 and preserved significant liquidity. Management explicitly discussed upside drivers such as World Cup events, milestone city celebrations, and ramping of high-performing renovations, and set expectations for revenue and margin growth to outpace the broader hotel REIT sector largely due to specialized urban exposure and ROI initiatives.

  • CEO Hale quantified that the World Cup alone is expected to contribute "about 45 basis points of pickup," with high-occupancy renovations adding an incremental 40 basis points to RevPAR at the guidance midpoint.
  • Portfolio optimization remains ongoing, with capital allocation favoring a combination of asset recycling, dividend payments, and opportunistic share repurchases, adjusted for current market conditions.
  • The Wyndham Boston Beacon Hill conversion is underwritten for a "40% upside in the EBITDA" and renovation returns "well north of 50%," per direct management comments.
  • Non-room revenue continued its outperformance, and F&B outlets are shifting to attract non-guest community traffic, as illustrated by the Mills House Black Door Cafe's "50% from our guests and 50% from the outside" mix.
  • Management signaled that expense growth for 2026 is embedded at roughly 3% overall, with no anticipated major renovation disruption, and expects F&B margin improvement to remain a focus via beverage-centric investments and operational efficiency.

Industry glossary

  • RevPAR: Revenue per available room, a primary hotel performance metric derived from occupancy and ADR.
  • ADR: Average daily rate, the average room rental rate per occupied room.
  • Adjusted FFO: Adjusted funds from operations, a key REIT cash flow metric representing net income plus non-cash charges and excluding certain items.
  • Comparable hotel EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization as measured on a like-for-like owned-hotel basis, excluding disposed assets.
  • Conversion: The renovation and rebranding of a hotel to a different flag or operator to drive new demand and margins.
  • CapEx: Capital expenditures; funds spent to acquire, maintain, or improve physical assets.
  • F&B: Food and beverage, a hotel segment generating revenue from dining, banquet, and bar operations.
  • Transient: Short-term guest segment (business or leisure), as opposed to group or contracted stays.
