Thermo Fisher (TMO) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 29, 2026 at 9:47AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, January 29, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Marc N. Casper
  • Senior Vice President, Chief Financial Officer — Stephen Williamson

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $12.21 billion for the quarter, up 7% year over year; full year revenue was $44.56 billion, up 4%.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $6.57 per share in Q4 (up 8%); $22.87 per share for the year (up 5%).
  • Adjusted Operating Income -- $2.88 billion in Q4 (up 6%); $10.11 billion annually (up 4%).
  • Adjusted Operating Margin -- 23.6% in Q4, down 30 basis points; 22.7% for the full year, up 10 basis points.
  • Segment Revenue Growth -- Life Science Solutions: Q4 up 13%, organic 4%; Analytical Instruments: Q4 up 1%, organic flat; Specialty Diagnostics: Q4 up 5%, organic 3%; Laboratory Products and Biopharma Services: Q4 up 7%, organic 5%.
  • End-Market Performance -- Pharma and Biotech: Q4 high single-digit growth, full year mid-single-digit; Academic and Government: declined low single digits for both periods; Industrial and Applied: Q4 down low single digits, full year up low single digits; Diagnostics and Healthcare: Q4 low single-digit growth, full year flat.
  • Geographic Organic Growth -- Q4: North America and Asia-Pacific up low single digits, Europe up mid single digits; China declined low single digits; full year China declined mid single digits.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $6.34 billion for the year after $1.48 billion net capital expenditures.
  • Capital Deployment -- $16.5 billion deployed in 2025, including $13 billion M&A and $3.6 billion to shareholders ($3 billion buybacks, ~$600 million dividends).
  • Major Acquisitions -- Completed acquisition of filtration and separation business from Solventa and the New Jersey sterile fill-finish site from Sanofi; announced $9 billion Clario definitive agreement in Q4, expected to close by end of 2026.
  • Clario Revenue Contribution -- Clario generated ~$1.5 billion revenue in 2025 and is expected to add ~$0.45 adjusted EPS in the first year upon closing.
  • FX and Tariff Impact -- Over 100 basis points headwind to margin in 2025 from tariffs and related FX; Q4 adjusted EPS included $0.14 upside vs. guidance midpoint, with $0.25 positive operational offset by $0.11 FX headwind.
  • Adjusted Operating Margin Guidance -- 50 basis points expansion expected in 2026, despite M&A dilution of 20 basis points.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Revenue: $46.3-$47.2 billion (4%-6% growth); organic growth 3%-4%; adjusted EPS: $24.22-$24.80 (6%-8% growth).
  • Additional 2026 Assumptions -- ~$500 million net interest expense, 11.5% adjusted tax rate, $1.8-$2 billion capital expenditures, $6.8-$7.3 billion free cash flow, $3 billion buybacks (completed January), $700 million dividends.
  • Q1 2026 Expectations -- Organic revenue growth a couple points below full year pace and low single-digit adjusted EPS growth anticipated.

RISKS

  • Williamson noted, "adjusted operating margin was 23.6% thirty basis points lower than Q4 last year. Which includes over 100 basis points of headwind from tariffs and related FX."
  • Cash flow was reported as "slightly lower than we'd assumed in the prior guide largely driven by temporary impacts in working capital and the timing of cash taxes."
  • Management highlighted continued macro headwinds in academic and government end markets and in China, leading to low single-digit revenue declines for both in the quarter and full year.
  • Guidance includes $60 million of adjusted operating income from 2025 acquisitions, which "adds a 20 basis point headwind to adjusted operating margins in 2026. After factoring in the financing costs they represented 7¢ of adjusted EPS dilution for 2026."

SUMMARY

Thermo Fisher Scientific (TMO 0.87%) closed 2025 with high single-digit quarterly and mid single-digit annual growth in its core pharma and biotech end market, while declaring a $9 billion definitive agreement to acquire Clario, a digital endpoint data provider generating ~$1.5 billion in 2025 revenue. The announced guidance for 2026 projects 4%-6% reported revenue growth, 3%-4% organic growth, and 6%-8% adjusted EPS growth, with a further ~$0.45 adjusted EPS uplift from Clario expected after completion by year-end. Management reported active M&A strategy deployment of $13 billion and capital returns to shareholders of $3.6 billion during 2025, alongside increased bioproduction, analytical innovation, and digital product introductions contributing to competitive positioning. Operational execution offset over 100 basis points of tariff and FX margin headwinds, but segment results, particularly in the Analytical Instruments division, were pressured by unfavorable mix, tariffs, and currency effects.

  • Management described customer sentiment in pharma and biotech as "quite positive" and reported strong adoption of integrated CDMO and CRO offerings, while maintaining guidance assumptions for flat to cautious academic and government market conditions.
  • The CFO clarified that the 2026 guidance does not yet include Clario's contribution, which could add 1 percentage point to adjusted EPS growth if the deal closes as planned.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • PPI business system: Thermo Fisher’s company-wide, proprietary operational framework focused on continuous process improvement, productivity enhancement, and cost management.
  • CDMO: Contract Development and Manufacturing Organization; provides outsourced drug development and manufacturing for pharmaceutical companies.
  • CRO: Contract Research Organization; an outsourced provider of clinical trial and research services for pharmaceutical and biotechnology industries.
  • Single-use bioreactor: Disposable vessel designed for culturing cells or microorganisms in biopharmaceutical production, minimizing cross-contamination and cleaning costs.
  • Digital endpoint data solutions: Technology platforms that collect, process, and deliver clinically validated digital outcomes or measurements used in drug development trials.
