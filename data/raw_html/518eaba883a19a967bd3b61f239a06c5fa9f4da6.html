Zepp (ZEPP) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – May 20, 2025 at 4:52PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Monday, May 19, 2025 at 7 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chairman of the Board of Directors and Chief Executive Officer — Wang Huang Wang

Chief Financial Officer — Leon Cheng Deng

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

RISKS

Operating expenses rose to $31.5 million in Q1 2025, up from $27.8 million year-over-year and $29.3 million in the previous quarter, driven by increased R&D and marketing spending, resulting in a higher adjusted operating loss of $17.2 million compared to $13.1 million in Q1 2024.

Gross margin was negatively impacted by a 20% U.S. tariff on China-made products. resulting in about a 1 percentage point reduction in gross margin, highlighting ongoing exposure to tariff-related cost headwinds.

Foreign exchange headwinds caused a $1.0 million increase in operating expenses, with management highlighting this impact as a factor in elevated Q1 costs.

TAKEAWAYS

Amazfit Revenue Growth: Amazfit-branded product sales increased 10.2% year-over-year in Q1 2025, marking the first year-over-year growth since the start of the company's transformation period, attributed primarily to the strong performance of new models Active 2 and Bip 6.

Gross Margin: Gross margin reached 37.3%, up from both Q4 2024 and Q1 2024, with leadership noting new product launches as the main driver.

Tariff Impact: CFO Leon Cheng Deng stated, "During the quarter, gross margin was negatively impacted by the additional 20% U.S. tariff on China-made products, which reduced our gross margin by approximately 1 percentage point." Excluding tariffs, gross margin would have been 38.4%.

Operating Expenses: Total operating expenses were $31.5 million, with a $2.2 million sequential rise driven mainly by higher R&D, digital marketing, and sales channel investments.

R&D Expenses: R&D spending was $11.5 million, down 3.4% year-over-year, reflecting "refined research and development approaches."

Selling and Marketing Spend: Selling and marketing expenses reached $13.8 million increasing 31% year-over-year with $1.7 million allocated to digital marketing and new product events and $1.4 million for channel strengthening.

Adjusted Operating Loss: Adjusted operating loss totaled $17.2 million, widening from $13.1 million in Q1 2024, attributed to seasonal sales and higher costs.

Cash Position: Cash balance was $104 million as of March 31, 2025 reflecting a $6 million decrease in cash balance from Q4 2024 to Q1 2025, which management credited to improved working capital and cash conversion cycle.

Debt Retirement: The company retired a cumulative $67.8 million debt as of Q1 2025, with $11.5 million repaid; refinancing shifted a significant portion of short-term debt to lower-cost, two-year instruments.

Q2 Revenue Guidance: Management guided Q2 2025 revenue to $50 million-$55 million, representing a 23%-35% year-over-year increase compared to Q2 2024, signaling continued growth momentum.

OpEx Target: CFO Leon Cheng Deng said, "We will maintain our cost-conscious approach in the upcoming quarters to keep overall operating costs between $25 million and $27 million per quarter (unaudited, GAAP)," with significant reductions anticipated from Q2 onward.

Global Supply Chain: The dual sourcing model between China and Vietnam, and forthcoming exploration in the NAFTA region, is designed to limit tariff risk and enhance production flexibility.

Product Launch Success: Newly launched Amazfit Active 2 and Bip 6 achieved record sales and high ratings on Amazon and offline channels, with notable traction in Italy and Spain.

Brand Strategy: Strategic branding through athlete endorsements and global event sponsorships expanded Amazfit brand presence, including visibility from Italian tennis player Jasmine Paolini's Rome Open win.

AI and Technology Integration: Zepp OS 4.5, featuring OpenAI, improved user interaction through voice control and meal tracking, and reduced food recognition costs by 90% via hybrid AI adoption.

Product Roadmap: Management confirmed plans to launch multiple new products across all major lines in the remaining quarters of 2025.

SUMMARY

Zepp Health Corporation highlighted the return to year-over-year sales growth for Amazfit-branded products, with sales increasing by 10.2% in Q1 2025, and reported a higher gross margin (GAAP, 37.3%) driven by new product launches despite tariff and cost headwinds in Q1 2025. Management described success in supply chain diversification efforts and confirmed a revenue outlook of $50 million-$55 million for Q2 2025. The company underscored its ongoing shift toward greater product innovation, AI-driven features, and expanded offline and digital distribution to increase global market share.

CFO Leon Cheng Deng said, "We expect our full year 2025 operating expenses to be at or below its level versus 2024." as continued cost discipline is anticipated to offset tariff headwinds.

The company is leveraging supply chain flexibility by front-loading U.S. inventory and monitoring tariff developments, with an expected full-year tariff cost of $2 million-$3 million for FY2025, based on a 10% rate.

The company explicitly reaffirmed its share repurchase program for 2025, citing confidence in long-term fundamentals and current valuation.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

CFK: Market research provider referenced for smartwatch sales rankings in Italy and Spain.

OpenAI: Artificial intelligence platform integrated into Zepp OS for voice and language features.

LLaMA: Open-source large language model technology used by Zepp Health.

Hyrox: International fitness event series with which Amazfit partners as an official wearable and timekeeping sponsor.
