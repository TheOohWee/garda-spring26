Earnings Call Transcript

Arcosa (ACA) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 27, 2026 at 9:47AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, Feb. 27, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and CEO — Antonio Carrillo
  • Chief Financial Officer — Gail Peck

TAKEAWAYS

  • 2025 Revenue -- $2.9 billion, up 12%, setting a new company record.
  • 2025 Adjusted EBITDA -- $583 million, representing 30% growth and a 20.2% margin, up 280 basis points.
  • Aggregate Unit Profitability -- 10% increase in 2025, with 8% sales price growth and 6% higher volumes, led by Stavola, though partially offset by regional declines.
  • Construction Products Q4 Revenue -- Decreased 2%; excluding freight, up 4% organically.
  • Construction Products Q4 Adjusted EBITDA -- Increased 3%, margin rose 140 basis points; freight-adjusted margin was roughly flat.
  • Aggregates Q4 Performance -- Freight-adjusted revenue rose 8% on 5% price and 2% volume gains; adjusted cash gross profit per ton up 3%.
  • Engineered Structures Q4 Revenue -- Increased 15%, driven by a 20% surge in utility and related structures; wind tower revenue up 3%.
  • Engineered Structures Q4 Adjusted EBITDA -- Up 22%, margin expanded 100 basis points to 18.5% from improved volumes and efficiency in utility structures.
  • Utility Structures Backlog -- Ended at $435 million, up 5%, providing clear demand visibility for 2026.
  • Wind Towers Backlog -- $628 million, with 42% expected to be recognized in 2026 and 53% in 2027; Q4 orders mostly for 2027 delivery.
  • Transportation Products Q4 EBITDA -- Rose 24% on 19% revenue growth, with tank barge volume and improved mix, raising segment margin by 90 basis points.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- Generated $120 million in Q4; full-year CapEx totaled $166 million, with free cash flow for the year at $22 million.
  • Net Leverage -- Net debt to adjusted EBITDA decreased to 2.3x from 2.9x, ending with $915 million in liquidity and full $700 million revolver available.
  • Barge Business Divestiture -- Signed agreement to sell for $450 million cash; anticipated close in 2026 subject to regulatory approval.
  • 2026 Financial Guidance -- Projected revenue of $2.95 billion to $3.1 billion and adjusted EBITDA of $590 million to $640 million, excluding barge; barge guidance of $410 million to $430 million revenue and $70 million to $75 million adjusted EBITDA until divestiture closes.
  • 2026 CapEx Outlook -- Capital expenditures expected between $220 million and $250 million, with $70 million to $80 million allocated to growth initiatives.
  • 2026 Segment Guidance -- Combined Construction Materials and Engineered Structures projected to deliver double-digit adjusted EBITDA growth and margin improvement.
  • Wind Towers 2026 Outlook -- Anticipated short-term step-down of approximately 25% in wind tower revenues compared to prior year, with volume recovery expected in 2027.
  • Utility Structures Capacity Expansion -- Illinois facility targeted for launch in second half 2026 and Tulsa facility preparing for transition; new galvanizing facility in Mexico operational in Q1 2026 for cost savings.

RISKS

  • Gail Peck stated, "Q1 will likely have a tough comp in unit profitability year over year," due to severe Northeast winter weather affecting Stavola operations, indicating lower first quarter contribution to annual EBITDA.
  • Management guided for "a short-term step-down in wind towers before recovering in 2027," with backlog data supporting a roughly 25% decrease in 2026 wind tower revenue.
  • Ongoing "policy uncertainty" cited by Antonio Carrillo as a source of noise for wind tower demand in 2026, with volume weakness explicitly acknowledged.
  • Flat residential volumes forecast for aggregates due to continued affordability challenges and weak activity in Phoenix and Florida, despite regional strength in Texas.

SUMMARY

Arcosa (ACA 0.37%) reported record 2025 results, highlighted by a double-digit percentage increase in revenue and significant expansion in adjusted EBITDA margin, primarily driven by growth in Construction Materials and Engineered Structures. The company announced the divestiture of its barge business for $450 million in cash, marking a strategic shift toward focusing fully on its two core growth segments. 2026 guidance calls for further revenue and EBITDA gains at the enterprise level, with double-digit adjusted EBITDA growth targeted in combined Construction Materials and Engineered Structures, despite a projected step-down in wind tower revenues. Liquidity and net leverage improved further as disciplined capital allocation and free cash flow enabled substantial debt repayment and full revolver availability.

  • Management stated the barge sale "raises our overall margin profile, and enhances the long-term resiliency of the company," positioning Arcosa for reduced cyclicality post-divestiture.
  • Aggregate business saw continued price-driven profitability, while regional softness in the Gulf and West offset some nationwide gains.
  • Utility structures backlog ended the year at "or near record highs," well-supported by sustained industry demand and multi-year customer investment plans, with both geographic and product-line expansion underway.
  • Capital expenditures are set for a first-half weighting in 2026 as new plant conversions and IT initiatives ramp, particularly in Construction Materials and Engineered Structures.
  • Antonio Carrillo explained that "bolt-on acquisitions," and organic capital projects will be the primary use of barge sale proceeds, with a clear focus on sustained value creation and disciplined capital deployment.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Aggregates: Construction materials including crushed stone, sand, and gravel used as foundational inputs for building and infrastructure projects.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, further adjusted for items such as non-cash and nonrecurring expenses for clearer profitability analysis.
  • Pass-through Freight: Shipping costs billed to customers without profit markup, excluded in certain internal profit measures.
  • Stavola: Acquired aggregates business driving inorganic and regional profitability growth for Arcosa.
  • Backlog: Unfilled confirmed orders scheduled for future delivery, providing visibility into segment demand and revenue pipeline.
  • Utility Structures: Engineered steel or concrete poles and related systems used for electricity transmission, telecommunication, and lighting infrastructure.

Full year freight-adjusted sales price grew 8% and adjusted cash gross profit per ton increased 10%, led by the accretive impact from Stavola.

Excluding freight, revenues were roughly flat, while adjusted EBITDA and margin declined slightly. Within Specialty Materials, strong profitability gains in lightweight aggregate were offset by volume-related decline in our specialty plaster business. In our asphalt business, revenues increased slightly as solid pricing gains offset lower volumes, resulting in modest unit profitability gains. Finally, revenues and adjusted EBITDA for our trench shoring business saw a double-digit increase year over year and had strong margin expansion driven by higher volumes and improved operating leverage.

For utility structures, volumes increased double digits while pricing was up high single digits. Steel pass-through was roughly flat year over year. Adjusted segment EBITDA increased 22% and margin expanded 100 basis points to 18.5%, driven by strong revenue growth and operating efficiencies in utility structures. This business executed well throughout the year, resulting in sequential margin improvement in each quarter of 2025.

For wind towers, adjusted EBITDA was roughly flat as we focused on rightsizing the business for lower production levels in 2026, resulting in a slight decline in margin year over year for the business. Customer reservations for utility structures that have not yet hit backlog remain strong, providing additional confidence in the demand outlook. For wind towers, we received orders of $190 million during the quarter, primarily for 2027 delivery. We ended the year with backlog of $628 million and expect to recognize 42% in 2026 and 53% in 2027.

Turning to Transportation Products, revenues were up 19% and adjusted segment EBITDA increased 24% primarily due to higher tank barge volumes and a more favorable mix, resulting in 90 basis points of margin expansion, building on the meaningful improvement delivered in the prior year.

I will now provide some comments on our cash flow performance and improved balance sheet position. During the quarter, we generated $120 million of operating cash flow. Excluding advanced billings, which can be uneven, net working capital days have improved sequentially each quarter in 2025 as we remain very focused on cash management. CapEx for the fourth quarter was $64 million, resulting in full year CapEx of $166 million, which was above the high end of our guidance range.

The increase was driven by deposits placed on some long lead-time equipment and the timing of spend on the wind tower plant conversion within our utility structures business. Free cash flow for the quarter was roughly $60 million and was $22 million for the full year. Reducing our wind tower capacity to two facilities right-sizes the business and redirects our resources to the higher multiple, higher margin utility structures with a sustained runway for growth.

As it relates to our capital allocation priorities, we are focused on investing in our growth businesses, both organically and through acquisitions. The divestiture of our barge business is a significant milestone in our company's evolution and will sharpen our focus on our key growth businesses, Construction Materials and Engineered Structures.

We are part of the mix, but it might take us years to see the orders from the time someone develops a data center.

Gail Peck: In terms of 2026, as we have said on the conversion for the wind tower facility, that is the second half of the year where that is going to start contributing.
