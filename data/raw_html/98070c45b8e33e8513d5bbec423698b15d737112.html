Axogen (AXGN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 24, 2026 at 3:12PM EST

Feb. 24, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer and Director — Michael Dale
  • Chief Financial Officer — Lindsey Hartley
  • Vice President, Commercial Operations — Rick Ditto

Risks

  • Gross margin and operating margin for Q4 and full year were negatively impacted by onetime stock-based compensation expenses linked to FDA BLA approval, totaling $1.9 million for gross profit and $7.2 million for operating expenses, reducing Q4 operating margin by 12.1%.
  • Net loss widened to $13.2 million in Q4 and $15.7 million for the year, primarily due to the aforementioned onetime expenses and increased R&D, sales, and G&A spending.
  • Guidance concedes that gross margin will face pressure during 2026 as the company transitions to higher cost Biologics Avance product beginning in Q2, with improvement not expected until 2027 following continuous improvement initiatives.

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $59.9 million for the quarter, up 21.3% year over year, and $225.2 million for the year, representing 20.2% annual growth.
  • Gross profit -- $44.4 million for the quarter (gross margin: 74.1%, down 2 percentage points); $167.4 million for the year (gross margin: 74.3%, a 1.5 percentage point decline), both affected by onetime costs from FDA Biologics License Application (BLA) approval.
  • Onetime expenses -- $1.9 million in Q4 gross profit (3.3%) and $7.2 million in Q4 operating expenses (12.1% of revenue) were directly tied to stock-based compensation related to the Avance BLA milestone, with $4.6 million allocated to R&D, $1.9 million to G&A, and $700,000 to sales and marketing.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $6.5 million for the quarter (margin: 10.9%, down 2.7 percentage points) and $27.9 million for the year (margin: 12.4%, up 1.8 percentage points), reflecting margin leverage and revenue growth after adjustment for onetime costs.
  • Net loss -- $13.2 million ($0.28 per share) in Q4; $15.7 million ($0.34 per share) for the year, as compared to net profit of $500,000 in Q4 2024 and net loss of $10 million in 2024.
  • Adjusted net income -- $3.5 million ($0.07 per share) for the quarter; $14.4 million ($0.29 per share) for the year, a marked increase from $5.9 million ($0.13 per share) in 2024.
  • R&D and commercial spending -- Full-year R&D expense grew 18.4% to $32.9 million (flat at ~15% of revenue), while sales and marketing expense reached 43.4% of revenue (up 1.5 percentage points).
  • Cash & capital -- Cash, equivalents, and investments increased $6 million to $45.5 million; $133.3 million raised via public offering in January, with $69.7 million used to retire all debt, leaving a debt-free capital structure.
  • FDA BLA approval -- Avance Nerve Graft received FDA BLA approval with 12 years of market exclusivity, positioning it as the only approved biologic for peripheral nerve discontinuity repair and immediate standard of care reference.
  • Commercial coverage -- Over 19.8 million additional U.S. lives gained coverage, moving commercial insurance coverage above 65% for the portfolio.
  • Sales force expansion -- Year-end count: 21 Breast sales reps (targeting ~30 in 2026), 117 Extremities reps (expanding to ~130), 3 Oral Maxillofacial/Head and Neck market managers, and 3 Prostate clinical development managers added.
  • High potential accounts -- 61% of total revenue growth attributed to high potential accounts; year-end universe consisted of 679 active high potential accounts from a universe of ~780, with 131 new active surgeons added in 2025.
  • Professional education -- 9 Extremities programs trained 170 surgeons, 3 Oral Maxillofacial/Head and Neck programs trained 59 surgeons, and 5 Breast programs trained 79 surgeon pairs; 2026 targets aim to increase programs and surgical trainees.
  • 2026 guidance -- Revenue growth of at least 18%, total revenue of at least $265.7 million, gross margin expected in 74%-76% range (matching 2025), and guidance to be free cash flow positive; seasonally higher cash burn anticipated in Q1.

Summary

Axogen (AXGN 3.21%) reported a 21.3% increase in quarterly revenue and significant annual growth, underpinned by higher adoption in all three commercial segments and culminating in a milestone FDA BLA approval for Avance Nerve Graft, which now enjoys 12 years of market exclusivity. The company used its recent $133.3 million equity raise to repay all outstanding debt, entering 2026 with a clean balance sheet and enhanced financial flexibility. Management outlined 2026 revenue guidance of at least 18% growth and a maintained gross margin range, while cautioning of temporary margin pressure due to higher biologics costs until operational improvements in 2027. The BLA approval led to significant onetime stock-based compensation expenses, which factored heavily into a quarterly and annual net loss, despite underlying adjusted EBITDA and net income improvements. The company’s commercial strategy focuses on expanding its sales force, accelerating high potential account productivity, and pursuing broad insurance coverage for its core graft platform.

  • Company leaders indicated that BLA approval status serves as a lever to re-engage non-adopter physicians and address payer concerns regarding experimental use, though payer response timelines are formally regulated and unpredictable.
  • Surgeon education efforts surpassed internal targets in all markets, targeting additional growth in trained surgeons and professional programs in 2026 for improved adoption curves.
  • CMS implemented a new outpatient payment classification for nerve procedures in January, potentially expanding care site flexibility but expected to impact primarily upper extremity procedures, not breast or head and neck cases.
  • Prostate is not forecast as a material revenue contributor in 2026, as the company awaits clinical signals from over 100 completed procedures before scaling further commercial investment.
  • Management projects the overall universe of high potential accounts and sales force coverage will continue to expand through 2028 as new indications and procedures are developed and commercialized.

Industry glossary

  • BLA (Biologics License Application): Submission to the FDA requesting approval to market a biologic product; approval confers regulatory exclusivity and can shift market positioning for products like Avance Nerve Graft.
  • High potential accounts: Designation for hospitals or physician practices identified as likely to drive a disproportionate share of revenue growth and targeted for prioritized commercial engagement and support.
  • K stock sales program: Company-specific sales channel for Avance prior to its discontinuation, previously used for inventory management and distribution, ended in 2025.
  • ASP (Average Selling Price): The average revenue attained by the company per procedure or product unit, varying by surgical segment based on graft volume and length.
