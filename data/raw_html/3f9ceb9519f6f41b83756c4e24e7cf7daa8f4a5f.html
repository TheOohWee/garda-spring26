Hershey (HSY) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 10:06AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 5, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kirk Tanner
  • Chief Financial Officer — Steven Voskuil
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Anoori Naughton

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales Growth Guidance -- Management signaled expectations for 4%-5% net sales growth, providing runway for investment and innovation.
  • EPS Growth Outlook -- Full-year guidance targets 30%-35% earnings growth as momentum builds in both core categories.
  • Elasticity Assumption -- Steven Voskuil stated elasticity is planned at 0.8, with actuals currently tracking more favorably than projected.
  • Portfolio Price Points -- Kirk Tanner noted, "75% of our portfolio is still under $4," framing product accessibility as a driver of consumer retention.
  • 2025 Pricing Actions -- Price increases taken in 2025 do not fully offset the expected cocoa cost inflation for 2026, resulting in a recovery path for gross margin.
  • Cocoa Hedging Position -- Steven Voskuil confirmed cocoa is hedged above current spot prices and just above 2025 levels, with downside opportunity remaining for 2026 due to flexible hedging structures.
  • Gross Margin Guidance -- Management projects 41% gross margin for 2026, with further gains possible in 2027 if cocoa prices stabilize or decline.
  • Q4 Salty Snacks Performance -- Q4 saw 18% organic growth in the salty snack segment, driven by double-digit volume increases.
  • Marketing Investment -- The company is deploying a double-digit percentage increase in advertising and brand-building in 2026.
  • Salty Snacks Segment Growth -- Fiscal guidance indicates mid-single-digit top-line growth and double-digit EBIT improvement in the salty snacks segment.
  • International Segment -- Forecasts point to low single-digit organic sales decline but double-digit EBIT growth, attributed to pricing in premium, cocoa-intensive products and portfolio optimization.
  • Q4 Gross Margin Drivers -- Outperformance was primarily due to lower-than-expected tariffs on supplier materials, offset by LIFO and inventory revaluation headwinds.
  • Promotional Strategy -- Steven Voskuil stated, "all the pricing's been sold in, so that's not a risk," and expects category pricing to remain rational, with promotions used for targeted excitement.
  • Brand Innovation Pipeline -- Significant new product introductions are slated across major brands including Hershey's, Reese's, Dots, Skinny Pop, and Jolly Rancher.
  • Seasonal and Cultural Activation -- Management highlighted 10 cultural and seasonal events in 2026 to drive engagement and "almost always-on" brand presence.
  • SNAP Impact -- Only two out of twelve approved states have implemented SNAP waivers for candy to date, and the headwind is characterized as "manageable" and incorporated into the outlook.
  • Capital Allocation -- Share repurchases remain under consideration as tariff and cocoa-related cash pressures ease, with dividend growth and normalized CapEx reaffirmed as priorities.
  • Protein and Functional Snacking -- Management described a strong outlook for the protein business and ongoing investments in R&D for protein and fiber-based snacks, with openness to M&A to expand presence.

SUMMARY

Management at Hershey (HSY 4.05%) communicated confidence in multi-year growth supported by a balanced approach to margin recovery and brand investment, with clear flexibility to adjust capital deployment and commercial levers as market and macro conditions evolve. Guidance explicitly contemplates current elasticity trends, hedged cocoa costs, tariff outcomes, and early but monitored impacts from SNAP waiver adoption in select states.

  • Incremental gross margin improvement in 2026 is expected to be weighted toward the second and third quarters, with lingering inventory and tariff headwinds limiting first-quarter profitability.
  • Hershey intends to maintain volume and pricing agility, deploying promotional and marketing dollars as needed to sustain competitiveness amidst stable category dynamics.
  • International results reflect focused market participation, premium positioning, and selective pricing to balance current volume softness against long-term EBIT expansion.
  • Growth and engagement strategies include leveraging core brands in major cultural moments and an active innovation pipeline spanning both confectionery and snacks.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Elasticity: A measure of demand sensitivity to price changes, commonly referenced as the ratio of percentage volume decline to percentage price increase in consumer goods industries.
  • LIFO (Last-In, First-Out): An inventory accounting method where the most recently acquired inventory is expensed first, affecting cost of goods sold and reported margin.
  • SNAP: Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program, a U.S. federal aid initiative, with policy waivers affecting eligibility of products like confectionery in certain states.
  • CMG (Candy, Mint, Gum): Industry term encompassing the confectionery sub-segments of candy, mint, and gum, often grouped for product and market analysis.
