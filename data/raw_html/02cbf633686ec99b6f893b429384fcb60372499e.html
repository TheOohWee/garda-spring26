SFL (SFL) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 10:51AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 11, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Ole Bjarte Hjertaker
  • Chief Operating Officer — Trym Otto Sjølie
  • Chief Financial Officer — Aksel C. Olesen
  • Senior Vice President — Espen Nilsen Gjøsund

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total operating revenues -- $176 million, reflecting a minor decrease from $178 million in the prior quarter.
  • EBITDA equivalent cash flow -- $109 million, consistent with the previous quarter, with a twelve‑month total of $450 million.
  • Net result (US GAAP) -- Net loss of $4.7 million, or $0.04 per share, due to non‑recurring and non‑cash items.
  • Dividend -- Declared $0.10 per share, representing the 88th consecutive quarterly payment and a yield of approximately 9% based on the prior day's share price.
  • Charter backlog -- $3.7 billion, with more than two‑thirds contracted to investment‑grade counterparties, underpinning cash flow visibility.
  • Book equity ratio -- 26% at quarter‑end, per company disclosure.
  • Cash and liquidity -- $151 million in cash and equivalents; $46 million available from undrawn credit facilities.
  • Fleet mixed asset base -- 57 maritime assets after asset sales, including two dry bulk carriers, 30 container vessels, 14 large tankers, two chemical tankers, seven car carriers, and two drilling rigs.
  • Fleet utilization -- Overall rate of 98.6%, improving to 99.8% when adjusted for unscheduled technical off‑hire only.
  • Container charter revenue -- $81 million, with profit share from fuel savings on seven large vessels.
  • Car carrier charter revenue -- $26 million, up from $23 million in the previous quarter due to full fleet service after drydocking.
  • Tanker charter revenue -- $42 million, lower than $44 million in the prior quarter attributed to a scheduled drydock.
  • Dry bulk charter revenue -- $2.7 million from two Camsimax vessels, or about $15,000 per vessel per day.
  • Energy asset revenue -- $23 million, primarily from the Linus rig's long‑term contract with ConocoPhillips through May 2029.
  • Sale of Suezmax tankers -- Two 2015‑built ships sold for approximately $57 million per vessel, generating a $11.3 million book gain and $26 million in net cash effect; a similar gain to be reported in the next quarter for the second delivery.
  • Suezmax charter terminations -- Two 2020‑built Korean vessels retained by SFL with $11.5 million per vessel compensation expensed in Q4 as a cost; vessels now book‑valued at $55 million each against broker‑estimated market value above $80 million per ship.
  • Profitability of Suezmax transactions -- "This transaction has been very profitable for us, with an annualized return on equity above 25%," according to Hjertaker.
  • Charter mix -- Majority of revenue from time charters, with four container ships on bareboat leases and the remainder on time or spot charters.
  • SFL Composer update -- "All of the damage repairs were covered by insurance, including also the off‑hire related to the incident. For SFL Corporation Ltd., we did not lose out on this at all."
  • Hercules drilling rig status -- Remained idle since November 2024; facility at year‑end repaid, rig is currently debt‑free, with $100 million new financing facility in preparation subject to closing conditions.
  • Fleet emissions/upgrades -- All six LNG dual‑fuel vessels operating on LNG, as part of the company's emissions reduction initiative.
  • Operating and G&A expenses -- $67 million, "broadly in line with the previous quarter," per Olesen.
  • Dividend distributions to date -- Over $2.9 billion returned to shareholders over 88 consecutive quarters.
  • Dry bulk segment focus -- Management stated, "We are segment diagnostics, so we would look at deals in all the segments, including the dry bulk segment," addressing potential portfolio reallocation.

RISKS

  • The fourth‑quarter net loss of $4.7 million under US GAAP resulted from non‑recurring costs, specifically the $23 million expensed as compensation for Suezmax charter terminations.
  • Hercules drilling rig remained idle throughout 2025 with no new employment secured during the reporting period, reducing energy segment cash generation.
  • Company noted potential "volatility in the profit and loss statement from quarter to quarter due to vessel positioning" for spot market vessels under US GAAP accounting.

SUMMARY

SFL Corporation (SFL 0.73%) completed key asset reallocation initiatives, including profitable Suezmax tanker sales and charter terminations, which materially raised cash yields on select assets. Management signaled ongoing strategic fleet renewal and efficiency upgrades, with all LNG dual‑fuel vessels operating on LNG and sustained high fleet utilization. Despite near‑term headline losses from non‑cash items, the company emphasized resilient cash flows anchored by a $3.7 billion backlog concentrated with investment‑grade counterparties and strong liquidity for future growth execution.

  • Hjertaker stated, "The two vessels that are remaining are generating more net cash flow than all four did in the previous chartering arrangement," underscoring the positive impact of recent asset redeployment.
  • Container segment generated $81 million in charter hire, benefiting from profit‑sharing features.
  • Olesen confirmed post‑quarter new $100 million financing in progress for the previously debt‑free Hercules rig, with execution pending closing conditions.
  • All six LNG dual‑fuel fleet vessels are now utilizing LNG, affirming the company's emissions reduction commitment and operational alignment with industry standards.
  • Management confirmed backlog composition following recent dry bulk sales: the fleet post‑quarter consists of 57 maritime assets, including two dry bulk, 30 container, 14 tankers, two chemical, seven car carriers, and two rigs.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Suezmax: A class of tanker vessel designed to maximize cargo capacity for transit through the Suez Canal, typically carrying about one million barrels of crude oil.
  • Camsimax: Mid-sized dry bulk carriers, often employed for versatile bulk cargoes and optimized for fuel efficiency.
  • Bareboat lease: Vessel leasing arrangement in which the charterer assumes full operational and maintenance responsibilities.
  • TD20 Index: Industry benchmark index tracking Suezmax spot charter rates, frequently referenced in tanker market hedging and contract pricing.
