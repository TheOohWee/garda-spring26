Axcelis (ACLS) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 17, 2026 at 6:25PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Feb. 17, 2026, 5:00 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Russell Low
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — James G. Coogan

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total revenue -- $238 million for the quarter, with systems revenue at $156 million and CS&I (Customer Support & Innovation) revenue at $82 million, both above internal expectations.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $1.49, surpassing outlook of $1.12, attributed to favorable aftermarket and upgrades mix.
  • Bookings -- $128 million for the quarter, described as "notably" higher than the prior quarter, led by power, general mature, and DRAM segments.
  • Backlog -- $457 million at quarter end.
  • Geographic sales mix (Q4) -- China at 32%, Europe 15%, U.S. 14%, Korea 13%, Japan 9%, Taiwan 3%, and rest of world 13%.
  • CS&I full-year revenue growth -- 14% increase, with upgrades and service contracts at record levels.
  • Non-GAAP gross margin -- 47.3% for the quarter, above outlook of 43%; full year at 45.2%, up 30 basis points year over year.
  • GAAP operating expenses -- $76 million for the quarter; non-GAAP operating expenses at $62 million, higher due to variable compensation from strong performance.
  • Free cash flow -- Negative $9 million in the quarter driven by late-quarter sales timing and $5 million in merger-related transaction costs; full-year free cash flow $107 million.
  • Share repurchases -- $25 million completed in quarter; $121 million for the year; $110 million remaining under authorization.
  • Cash position -- $557 million in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities at quarter end, including $182 million in long-term securities.
  • Q1 2026 revenue outlook -- $195 million, with sequential decline expected in both systems and CS&I segments.
  • Q1 2026 non-GAAP gross margin outlook -- 41%, with lower mix of upgrades and increased memory sales reducing margins, alongside seasonality and minor tariff effect.
  • Q1 2026 non-GAAP diluted EPS outlook -- $0.71.
  • 2026 full-year revenue guidance -- Forecast as approximately flat with 2025, with revenue second-half weighted due to memory growth offsetting power and general mature declines.
  • 2026 non-GAAP gross margin guidance -- Expected in the low to mid-40% range, lower than prior year on increased memory business and slight tariff impact (under 100 basis points).
  • Pending merger -- All shareholder approvals and several regulatory clearances for Veeco merger completed; final Chinese regulatory approval outstanding, with closing anticipated in 2026.
  • Product innovation -- Introduction of Purion H6 high-current implanter with advances in dose repeatability, precision, purity, and productivity for logic, memory, and mature nodes.
  • High-current shipments -- Highest quarter in two years for high-current system deliveries.
  • DRAM business -- Order secured from leading North American memory manufacturer, expanding presence beyond Korea.

RISKS

  • Q1 2026 revenue and gross margin are projected to decrease sequentially, primarily due to lower CS&I volume, reduced upgrades mix, and seasonal effects, as stated by James G. Coogan.
  • Power and general mature markets are described as "slightly down" for 2026, with overall demand described by management as muted and full-year revenue growth expected to be offset by declines in these segments.
  • China revenue decreased to 32% of total in the quarter from 46% in the previous period due to ongoing capacity digestion, and 2026 demand from the region is expected to remain flat to slightly down.
  • Management acknowledged a modest tariff impact on gross margins for 2026, estimated at less than 100 basis points, and additional potential pressure from higher cost inventory cycling into cost of goods sold in Q1.

SUMMARY

Axcelis Technologies (ACLS +2.01%) reported quarterly revenue and earnings above its internal outlook, with aftermarket and upgrades driving both gross margin strength and record CS&I growth. The company confirmed that full-year 2026 revenue is expected to remain flat, as projected memory market expansion, particularly in DRAM for AI applications, will balance softer conditions in power and general mature markets. High-current shipments in the quarter reached a two-year high, and an order from a major North American memory manufacturer extended Axcelis Technologies' geographic reach in DRAM. Integration planning for the Veeco merger advanced with all shareholder consents and most regulatory approvals in hand, though Chinese regulatory clearance is required before closing in 2026. Management guided to a sequential dip in revenue and gross margin for the coming quarter, mainly due to timing, product mix, and limited recurrence of Q4 upgrade activity.

  • The full-year non-GAAP gross margin improvement of 30 basis points occurred despite lower overall revenue, suggesting that mix and cost discipline buffered profitability.
  • Aftermarket CS&I recurring business, including services and upgrades, delivered record levels for both the quarter and full year, strategically supporting margin expansion and stability.
  • Management attributed Q4's negative free cash flow to both seasonal sales timing and transaction costs from the Veeco merger process.
  • The Purion H6 implanter marks a technological leap for high-current applications across varied process nodes, reflecting ongoing investment in differentiated innovation.
  • Chinese customers continue investing strategically, but both sales mix and management outlook suggest a cautious stance amidst ongoing capacity digestion and self-sufficiency objectives.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • CS&I (Customer Support & Innovation): Axcelis Technologies' aftermarket business segment encompassing installed base support, upgrades, services, parts, and related offerings.
  • High-current implanter: Ion implantation tool delivering high-current beams, used primarily in fast, large-volume semiconductor manufacturing steps—particularly for power and memory devices.
  • Purion Platform: Proprietary ion implantation system family from Axcelis Technologies designed for diverse wafer fabrication requirements.
  • Silicon carbide (SiC): Wide-bandgap semiconductor material increasingly used for high-efficiency power electronics and electric vehicle applications.
  • DRAM: Dynamic random-access memory, key in memory chip production and high-performance computing applications.
  • HBM: High Bandwidth Memory, a type of memory used in advanced, high-speed computing such as AI and graphics processing.
