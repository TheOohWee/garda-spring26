Veritone (VERI) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 6, 2025 at 6:08PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, November 6, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Ryan Steelberg
  • Chief Financial Officer — Michael L. Zemetra

RISKS

  • Chief Financial Officer Michael L. Zemetra said, “We do not expect the same level of one-time software revenue to recur in Q4 2025." As a result, the company is forecasting fiscal Q4 2025 non-GAAP gross margins to be closer to 60%, indicating margin compression next quarter.
  • “This negative trend in representation services is expected to continue through 2025 or until the macro economy shows demonstrated improvements over 2024," per Zemetra.
  • Chief Executive Officer Ryan Steelberg confirmed, “have we seen potentially some delays in some of the revenues that would have contributed in this quarter? We have,” referencing federal public sector business, and cited the government shutdown as a contributing factor to delays.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $29.1 million in revenue for fiscal Q3 2025 (period ended September 30, 2025), up 32% year over year, driven by $8.1 million growth in software products and services and offset by a $1 million decline in managed services.
  • Software products and services growth -- 55% year-over-year increase; excluding Veritone Hire, growth exceeded 200%.
  • VDR sales pipeline -- Over $40 million in qualified pipeline and bookings, representing a 100% sequential increase from fiscal Q2 2025 levels.
  • Annual recurring revenue (ARR) -- $68.8 million in ARR, up 9% from fiscal Q2 2024 and 12% sequentially from fiscal Q2 2025, with ARR from consumption-based customers rising 26% year over year and 74% sequentially.
  • Total bookings -- $21.5 million, up $5 million or 30% year over year, primarily driven by VDR deals.
  • Gross margin (Non-GAAP) -- Non-GAAP gross margin was 70.6%, down 60 basis points year over year; fiscal Q4 2025 projected non-GAAP gross margin is between 60%-61% due to less one-time revenue and a higher VDR mix.
  • Debt reduction -- Term debt fully retired and 50% of convertible debt repurchased, reducing consolidated debt principal from roughly $126.7 million as of September 30, 2025, to about $45 million, and annual debt service from over $14 million to approximately $800,000.
  • Equity capital raised -- $100 million secured via offerings in September and October 2025, raising cash and cash equivalents above $100 million post-transaction.
  • Fiscal Q4 2025 revenue guidance -- Expected between $33.4 million and $39.4 million, with the midpoint implying a 63% year-over-year increase and 25% sequential growth from fiscal Q3 2025.
  • Fiscal Q4 2025 non-GAAP net loss guidance -- Projected non-GAAP net loss between $1.5 million and $5 million, a 66% improvement year over year at the midpoint.
  • Customer count -- Total software products and services customers decreased by 9% year over year to 3,021, reflecting a shift in focus toward larger ARR opportunities and the impact of legacy client attrition.
  • Public sector pipeline -- $218 million in qualified public sector pipeline opportunities, up from $110 million earlier in 2025, with 82 contracts closed and 30 new agencies added.
  • Commercial agreements -- 27 signed in commercial enterprise, including new and expanded partnerships with ESPN, NCAA, Newsmax, and CBS Media Ventures.
  • VDR margins -- Approximately 40% gross margin on VDR revenue, with one-time high-margin software revenue positively affecting results.
  • Guidance for public sector and commercial enterprise in fiscal Q4 2025 -- Public sector revenue expected to grow nearly 50% year over year; commercial enterprise, led by VDR, projected to exceed 75% growth year over year.
  • Operating loss -- Operating loss of $15.8 million, improved by $6.7 million or 29% year over year; non-GAAP net loss from continuing operations at $5.8 million, a 48% year-over-year improvement.

SUMMARY

Veritone (VERI 0.53%) delivered a 32% year-over-year revenue increase for fiscal Q3 2025 and sharply accelerated software products and services growth, with revenue up $8.1 million or 55% year over year, and strategic execution underscored by aggressive debt reduction and successful capital raises that substantially lowered financial risk. Management cited a doubling of the VDR bookings and sales pipeline sequentially, extensive new commercial and public sector wins, and a 30% increase in total new bookings year over year as tangible drivers of future momentum. Significant improvements to liquidity and annualized debt service, as well as robust revenue and non-GAAP net loss guidance for fiscal Q4 2025, signal a strengthened financial foundation and solid market positioning. Public sector deals and a growing international presence contribute to record pipeline visibility, although margin compression and macro-driven headwinds in managed services warrant close watch.

  • Chief Executive Officer Steelberg said, “We’ve completed an agreement to retire the entirety of our term debt and repurchase approximately 50% of our outstanding convertible debt while maintaining material net cash on the balance sheet to fully fund operations over the next twelve months and achieve our profitability goal in the latter half of next year, as of November 6, 2025.”
  • Zemetra stated, “We started 2025 with one of our strongest quarters to date, with Q3 revenue surpassing our recent guidance, led by our software products and services growth of over 55% year over year.”
  • Management highlighted a “robust expanding pipeline” across both commercial and public sectors, with committed bookings, recent product launches, and selected agency wins serving as leading indicators for durable growth beyond 2025.
  • The company indicated, “VDR's accelerated momentum underscores not only the surging demand for clean, model-ready training data but also the expanding monetization potential of premium video and audio assets themselves.”

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • aiWARE: Veritone’s proprietary artificial intelligence operating system enabling the orchestration and processing of structured and unstructured data via AI models.
  • VDR (Veritone Data Refinery): A Veritone solution that converts raw unstructured multimedia (audio, video, text, images) into tokenized, model-ready datasets for AI training and monetization.
  • Veritone Hire: AI-powered human capital management product suite within Veritone’s software products and services segment.
  • Gross revenue retention: The percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers over a period, excluding the impact of upgrades, cross-sells, or expansions.
  • Consumption-based revenue: Revenue derived from pay-per-use contracts, as opposed to fixed recurring SaaS subscriptions.
  • IDEMS: Veritone’s product for investigative and evidentiary data management solutions, sold to public sector agencies.
