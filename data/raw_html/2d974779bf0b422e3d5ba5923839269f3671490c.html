Earnings Call Transcript

Dollar General (DG) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 12, 2026 at 12:01PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, March 12, 2026 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Todd Vasos
  • Chief Financial Officer — Donny Lau
  • Chief Operating Officer — Emily Taylor

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $10.9 billion, up 5.9% year over year, reflecting growth in both consumable and nonconsumable categories.
  • Same-Store Sales -- Increased 4.3% with growth in both customer traffic and average basket size; every month of the quarter was above 3.5% comp sales growth.
  • Gross Margin -- 30.4%, an increase of 105 basis points, primarily due to a 62 basis point reduction in shrink, higher inventory markups, and lower damages, offset by higher LIFO provision.
  • SG&A as Percentage of Sales -- 24.9%, a decrease of 165 basis points, mainly from fewer impairment charges and lower retail salaries, partially offset by higher incentive compensation.
  • Operating Profit -- $606 million, up 106%, with margin up 270 basis points to 5.6%; prior year included $232 million in impairment charges.
  • EPS -- $1.93, up 122%, with prior year negatively impacted by $0.81 from impairment charges.
  • Merchandise Inventories -- $6.3 billion, down 5.7% year over year and 7% per store, supporting improved in-stock levels and sales.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $3.6 billion for the year, up 21.3%, supporting $1.7 billion in senior note redemptions and $130 million in dividends.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Net sales growth of 3.7%-4.2%, same-store sales growth of 2.2%-2.7%, EPS of $7.10-$7.35, effective tax rate of ~25% including a $0.13 EPS reduction from Work Opportunity Tax Credit expiration.
  • Capital Spending Plans -- $1.4-$1.5 billion, including new stores, remodels (2,000 Project Renovate, 2,250 Project Elevate), and IT investments.
  • Delivery Sales Contribution -- Delivery sales drove approximately 80 basis points of the quarter's comp sales growth, supported by myDG and third-party platforms.
  • DG Media Network -- Retail media brought in roughly $170 million in network volume for the year, cited as highly accretive to gross margin.
  • Value Valley Performance -- Value Valley $1 item assortment delivered a 17.6% comp sales increase and outperformed the company average.
  • Store Expansion -- 581 new stores opened in 2025, with plans for 450 new stores in 2026; 80% of stores are in rural communities under 20,000 population.

RISKS

  • CFO Donny Lau said, "We anticipate a full year tax rate of 25%. That compares to 23% in 2025. As I alluded to in my prepared remarks, this includes about 150 basis point headwind from the exploration of the work opportunity tax credit at the end of 2025, and that will result in an approximate $0.13 reduction to EPS."
  • Management identified the negative impact of winter storms and temporary store closures in the first two weeks of February, which hurt Q1 sales at the start of the year.
  • Donny Lau noted, "we are watching the changing tariff environment. We are watching the potential for the changes in higher gas prices," identifying these as potential headwinds.
  • Management stated, "consumer sentiment does remain cautious and stagnant, and inflation remains sticky and the macro environment continues to evolve," emphasizing ongoing uncertainty in consumer behavior and economic variables.

SUMMARY

Dollar General Corporation (DG 3.36%) reported broad-based sales and profit gains across categories, with margin expansion driven primarily by systematic shrink reduction, improved inventory management, and targeted cost controls. The company is prioritizing digital engagement, with delivery and DG Media Network revenues noted as accretive profit pools, while substantial investments continue in remodels, brand launches, and technology to sustain momentum in core and discretionary categories. Management disclosed outsized operating cash flow enabled debt redemption, and outlined plans to align future capital spending with shareholder return priorities. Strategic direction includes a goal to increase nonconsumable penetration to up to 20% by 2029, with efforts supported by merchandising innovation, expanded delivery capabilities, and new store growth domestically and in Mexico. Guidance embeds a higher tax burden due to expiring credits, modest gross margin expansion, and incremental SG&A investments in key growth and modernization initiatives.

  • CFO Lau explained, "be partially offset by continued investments in key initiatives, including remodels and IT modernization."
  • The chain plans to launch at least 15 new nonconsumable brands, reinforce closeout buying, implement a loyalty program pilot, and integrate shoppable social marketing to accelerate sales in discretionary categories.
  • CEO Vasos noted improved operational metrics including in-stocks, on-time deliveries, and company-wide store manager turnover declining by more than 375 basis points, which management views as strengthening the sales and execution backdrop.
  • The company expects combined shrink and damages to provide an additional 50 basis points of gross margin expansion ahead, with AI-driven efficiency gains not yet included in the long-term SG&A leverage targets.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • DG Media Network: Dollar General’s in-house retail media platform enabling advertisers to reach Dollar General’s customer base across digital and in-store channels.
  • LIFO Provision: Last-In, First-Out accounting charge reflecting the impact of inventory cost inflation, predominantly in consumables.
  • Shrink: Inventory loss from theft, damage, or administrative error, directly impacting gross margin.
  • Project Renovate / Project Elevate: Remodel programs targeting different store-age groups for varied capital and merchandising upgrades to drive incremental comp sales.
  • Value Valley: Rotating Dollar General in-store assortment of low-priced items at or below $1 designed to drive customer traffic and value perception.
