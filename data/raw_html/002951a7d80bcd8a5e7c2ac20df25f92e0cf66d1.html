UiPath (PATH) Q4 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 12, 2026 at 9:50AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, March 11, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Founder and Chief Executive Officer — Daniel Dines
  • Chief Operating and Financial Officer — Ashim Gupta
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Allise Furlani

TAKEAWAYS

  • Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) -- $1.853 billion, up 11%, with net new ARR of $70 million, including a $14 million FX tailwind.
  • Revenue -- $481 million, up 14%; full-year revenue $1.611 billion, up 13%; normalizing for FX, quarterly and annual revenue growth were 10% and 11%, respectively.
  • Cloud ARR -- Over $1.2 billion, representing more than 20% growth and including both hybrid and SaaS models.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Income -- $150 million for the quarter (31% margin); full-year non-GAAP operating income $370 million (23% margin), up more than 600 basis points.
  • GAAP Profitability -- Achieved first full year of GAAP operating profit at $57 million; fourth quarter GAAP operating income $80 million; full-year GAAP net income $282 million.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $182 million in the quarter; $372 million for the year.
  • Customer Metrics -- Total customers reached 10,750; customers spending over $30,000 in ARR grew 7%; those at $100,000 or more total 2,565, and those at $1 million or more total 357; 90% of $1 million-plus customers and 60% of $100,000-plus customers use AI products.
  • AI Product ARR -- Nearly $200 million, fueled by agentic offerings; 25% growth in $100,000-plus ARR customers purchasing AI products and these customers spending almost 3x more than non-AI buyers.
  • Retention Metrics -- Dollar-based gross retention remained at 97%, dollar-based net retention at 107% (or 106% Fx-adjusted).
  • Operating Expenses -- Fourth quarter operating expenses were $263 million; company ended the year with 3,981 employees.
  • Share Repurchases -- 780,000 shares repurchased during the quarter; full-year total of 30.9 million shares at an average price of $10.92; an additional 14 million shares repurchased since January 31, 2026, completing the $1 billion buyback; new $500 million authorization approved.
  • Long-Term Margin Target -- Non-GAAP operating margin target increased to 30%, up from the prior 20% model.
  • Guidance -- Q1 revenue expected in the $395 million-$400 million range and ARR of $1.894 billion-$1.899 billion; full-year revenue guidance of $1.754 billion-$1.759 billion and ARR of $2.051 billion-$2.056 billion; fiscal 2027 non-GAAP operating income guided to $415 million.

RISKS

  • Ashim Gupta flagged variability in the macroeconomic environment, stating, "we do see pockets of strength and we see pockets of pressure or fluctuations that happen from a macroeconomic standpoint."
  • The shift to SaaS offerings is expected to create a "1% headwind to total revenue growth for the full year," as disclosed in prepared guidance.
  • "Middle East conflict is there, so there's uncertainty there," according to Ashim Gupta when addressing international market demand consistency.

SUMMARY

UiPath (PATH 5.68%) delivered a quarter marked by new GAAP profitability milestones and robust AI product adoption, with management raising the long-term non-GAAP operating margin target to 30%. The company closed its $1 billion share repurchase program and authorized an additional $500 million buyback, underscoring confidence in its cash flows. Strategic investments in agentic automation, healthcare, and financial services were highlighted as principal growth drivers. Pricing in local currency and minimal net FX effects were specifically cited, supporting transparency in constant-currency results. Management guided for continued expansion of AI product penetration and margin leverage as cloud adoption accelerates.

  • The WorkFusion acquisition was described as an immaterial "tuck-in," broadening agentic capabilities in financial services without material ARR impact, per Ashim Gupta.
  • Partner relationships, including with Deloitte and Accenture, are accelerating customer adoption of agentic workflows and driving broader Test Cloud deployment.
  • Vertical solutions in healthcare and financial crime compliance were referenced as areas with the highest willingness to spend on agentic AI, based on management commentary.
  • Management revealed that Maestro, the company’s orchestration engine, is architected for agent heterogeneity and integrates both UiPath and open-source agents within enterprise security and governance standards.
  • Pricing remains primarily subscription-based, so the SaaS revenue headwind is unrelated to a shift toward usage timing or revenue delays.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Agentic Automation: Automation capabilities where AI-driven agents make context-sensitive, goal-directed decisions, extending beyond rule-based workflows to deliver adaptive execution.
  • Maestro: UiPath’s enterprise workflow orchestration engine designed to manage deterministic, agentic, and human-involved automation with integrated governance and compliance controls.
  • IDP: Intelligent Document Processing, UiPath’s AI module for complex document understanding, classification, and extraction, embedded within the automation platform.
  • Test Cloud: UiPath’s cloud-based autonomous testing platform, used to automate and orchestrate large-scale software quality assurance across enterprise systems.
