Cal Water (CWT) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 26, 2026 at 12:16PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, Feb. 26, 2026 at 11 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Martin Kropelnicki
  • Chief Financial Officer — James Lynch
  • Chief Business Development Officer — Shilen Patel
  • Vice President, Rates and Regulatory Affairs — Greg Milleman

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $220,000,000 in Q4, down slightly from $222,000,000 in the prior year, impacted by weather-driven consumption declines.
  • Net Income (Q4) -- $11,500,000, or $0.19 per diluted share, versus $19,700,000, or $0.33 per diluted share, previously, due to a significant weather event affecting demand.
  • Full-Year Operating Revenue -- $1,000,000,000, up 5.4% compared to non-GAAP 2024 revenue of $949,300,000, with a $50,800,000 increase.
  • Full-Year Net Income -- $128,200,000, up $1,400,000, or 1%, from the non-GAAP prior year metric of $126,800,000.
  • Diluted Earnings Per Share (Full-Year) -- $2.15, essentially flat versus non-GAAP 2024 result of $2.16.
  • Capital Expenditures -- Record annual investment of $517,000,000, up 19.8% from 2024, with Q4 investment of $52,300,000.
  • Dividend -- Annual 2025 dividend increased by 10.7%, followed by a further 8% boost in 2026; 59 consecutive annual increases reported.
  • Return on Equity (California) -- 10.27% ROE extended through January 2028, cited as among North America’s highest for water utilities.
  • Interim Rate Increase (California) -- Implemented a 3% interim increase in January following regulatory delay.
  • Acquisitions -- Agreements executed for (1) Nexus Water’s Nevada and Oregon operations (about $109,000,000 rate base, purchase price ≈ 2x rate base), and (2) remaining minority interest in BVRT Holdings, granting California Water Service Group sole ownership of seven Texas utilities.
  • Liquidity Position -- $51,800,000 in unrestricted cash, $45,600,000 in restricted cash, and about $470,000,000 available on credit lines as of year-end.
  • Long-Term Financing -- Issued $370,000,000 in new Group notes and mortgage bonds on October 1, 2025.
  • Credit Ratings -- A+ stable maintained from S&P Global for both Group and Cal Water units.
  • PFAS Capital Expenditures -- Projected $235,000,000 in incremental PFAS-related investments to be deployed in coming years, not included in published 2026–2027 CapEx guidance.
  • Customer Growth Opportunity (Texas) -- Over 19,000 committed customers at year-end 2025, 5,000 connected, 20,000 likely in near-term, and approximately 100,000 potential customers under long-term development in the Texas corridor.
  • Dividend Declaration -- Announced 324th consecutive quarterly dividend of $0.33 per share in January 2026, with a scheduled 2026 annual dividend of $1.34, representing an 8.1% increase over 2025.

RISKS

  • James Lynch cited, “the financial results in December were clearly affected by wet, cold weather,” with a $14,600,000 consumption decline in Q4, which contributed to a $0.59 decrease in earnings per share for the quarter.
  • Expense true-up from the conclusion of a three-year conservation program reduced earnings by $0.10 per share in the quarter.
  • Management highlighted, “Affordability continues to be an issue,” indicating cost recovery and rate pressure could affect rate-setting outcomes, given increased regulatory scrutiny on customer rates.

SUMMARY

California Water Service Group (CWT 0.30%) reported stable full-year non-GAAP earnings per share, reflecting robust tariff rate adjustments and expanded capital deployment into infrastructure. The company closed agreements to acquire Nexus Water’s Nevada and Oregon utilities as well as full ownership of its Texas BVRT Holdings, substantially increasing its geographic and regulatory diversification. Management described meaningful progress in ongoing multi-state rate cases, and confirmed that the company maintained a disciplined approach to both acquisition strategy and capital spending, with new PFAS regulations requiring significant incremental investment over the next several years.

  • Chairman Kropelnicki stated the Nexus Water deal adds about 36,000 residential equivalents and, excluding BVRT, increases operations outside California by about 40%, thereby increasing regulatory diversification.
  • Vice President Milleman detailed that if the 2024 California General Rate Case is approved as filed, compounded annual rate base growth could approach 12% through 2027.
  • James Lynch projected 2026 PFAS capital outlays will range from $50,000,000 to $70,000,000, incremental to other planned capital expenditures.
  • Chief Business Development Officer Patel explained, “consolidating full ownership enhances governance, simplifies the structure, and allows us to fully capture the long-term growth and infrastructure investment opportunities within this market.”
  • Management emphasized strong state-level regulatory frameworks in Nevada (decoupling, construction work in progress, DSIC) and Oregon (hybrid ratemaking), enhancing future recovery prospects for capital investment beyond the company's traditional California base.
  • Grant applications and ongoing legal recoveries were identified as additional funding sources for required PFAS compliance activities.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • DSIC (Distribution System Improvement Charge): A regulatory mechanism allowing utilities to recover the costs of certain infrastructure investments through periodic, formula-driven surcharges, outside general rate cases.
  • Decoupling: Regulatory approach that separates a utility’s revenue from the volume of water sold, reducing revenue volatility due to fluctuations in consumption.
  • PUCT (Public Utility Commission of Texas): The Texas regulatory authority overseeing investor-owned utility transactions, rates, and operations.
  • GRC (General Rate Case): A formal, multi-year process in which regulated utilities request revised rates to cover operating costs and capital investments.
  • PFAS (Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances): A class of synthetic chemicals requiring regulation and specialized water treatment due to persistent environmental and health impacts.
  • MCL (Maximum Contaminant Level): Legally enforceable standard setting the maximum permissible concentration of a contaminant in public water systems.
  • ATM Program (At-the-Market Equity Offering Program): A mechanism permitting utilities to raise equity capital incrementally in the open market, at prevailing share prices.
