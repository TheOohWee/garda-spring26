TAKEAWAYS

  • Adjusted Earnings per Share -- $4.70 for the fourth quarter and $10.70 for the full year, as reported by the company.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $3.5 billion in the fourth quarter and $12 billion for the full year, with quarterly EBITDA $1.4 billion higher year over year driven by the Refining & Marketing segment.
  • Refining & Marketing Adjusted EBITDA per Barrel -- $7.15 for the quarter and $5.63 for the year, reflecting strength in margin capture and operational performance.
  • Refining Utilization -- 95% utilization in the quarter, with total throughput just over 3 million barrels per day; regional utilizations were 98% in Gulf Coast, 93% in Mid-Continent, 91% in West Coast.
  • Capture Rate -- 114% in the fourth quarter, described by Maria Currie as supported by "Solid commercial execution" and structural improvements in the commercial organization.
  • Midstream Segment Adjusted EBITDA -- Nearly $7 billion for the full year, a record level; fourth-quarter midstream adjusted EBITDA declined year over year due to divestitures of non-core gathering and processing assets.
  • Cash from Operations -- $2.7 billion in the fourth quarter, $8.3 billion for the full year including working capital; cash flow from operations excluding working capital was $2.7 billion in the quarter and $8.7 billion for the year.
  • Capital Returns to Shareholders -- $4.5 billion returned in 2025, inclusive of a 6.5% reduction in shares outstanding from share repurchases.
  • Refining Capital Investment Plans -- Roughly $700 million budgeted for 2026, nearly 20% lower than 2025, with 85% allocated to Galveston Bay, Garyville, Robinson, and El Paso refineries.
  • New Strategic Projects -- Three new projects announced totaling $190 million in 2026 spend (Garyville feedstock optimization, Garyville premium gasoline export flexibility, El Paso product upgrading), each with targeted returns of 25% or above.
  • MPLX Growth Investment -- $2.4 billion of growth capital planned, with 90% deployed to natural gas and NGL services, primarily in the Permian and Marcellus basins.
  • MPLX Distribution Guidance -- Projected distribution growth rate of 12.5% over the next two years, translating to more than $3.5 billion in expected future annual distributions to Marathon Petroleum Corporation.
  • Renewables Segment Performance -- 94% utilization in the quarter, with a one-time credit sale benefit from the Martinez JV offset by weaker margins compared to the prior-year quarter; planned Q1 turnaround at Martinez expected to reduce utilization to approximately 70%.
  • Cash Position -- Year-end consolidated cash was $3.7 billion, comprising $1.5 billion at the parent level and $2.1 billion at MPLX.
  • 2026 Turnaround Expense Guidance -- $1.35 billion expected for the full year, with further reductions targeted for 2027 and 2028.
  • Net Debt-to-Capital Ratio -- Remains within the 25%-30% range, with an ongoing target for annual cash balances of $1 billion.
  • Product Demand Trends -- Gasoline and distillate demand each grew by roughly 1%, and jet fuel demand increased nearly 4%, with management expecting similar patterns for 2026.
  • Feedstock Slate -- Approximately 50% sour crude, with system flexibility to switch between Canadian and Venezuelan barrels based on economics; company recently purchased two cargoes of Venezuelan crude due to advantageous pricing.
  • Export Markets -- Decreased product exports to Mexico, more than offset by higher demand from other Latin American countries, especially Brazil.

SUMMARY

Marathon Petroleum Corporation (MPC 0.40%) delivered strong financial performance in Q4 2025, reporting significant year-over-year gains in adjusted EBITDA, led by its Refining & Marketing segment and supported by record midstream results. Management announced three new projects targeting 25% returns, reflecting strict capital discipline and an emphasis on system upgrades at key refineries. The company reiterated its model of covering capital expenditures and dividends primarily with distributions from MPLX, supporting ongoing capital returns to shareholders as capital spending is set to decrease in coming years. Management indicated strategic flexibility in crude sourcing, including expanding sour crude intake and pivoting to opportunistic Venezuelan cargoes as economics allow. Expansion of MPLX’s natural gas and NGL services in the Permian and Marcellus, coupled with robust projected distribution growth, underpins confidence in the integrated value chain and future shareholder returns.

  • MPLX’s $2.4 billion growth investment is highly concentrated in the fastest-growing gas basins, and projects are expected to generate mid-teens returns upon completion.
  • The company’s renewable segment will experience a planned utilization decrease to approximately 70% at Martinez refinery because of a scheduled turnaround, while operational optimization of renewables remains ongoing.
  • Maryann Mannen said, "assuming current market cracks, that would be indicative of us being able to repeat a similar pattern that we did in 2025," signaling the intent to return a comparable level of capital to shareholders in 2026.
  • Pricing pressures from pipeline apportionment and increased Canadian and Venezuelan crude supply are seen as "significant tailwind" for the company’s margins, with management underscoring the strategic advantage of a system capable of maximizing optionality and exploiting wide sour crude differentials.
  • Capital allocation priorities remain focused on exercising strict investment discipline, with 2026, 2027, and 2028 refining capital spend guided lower each year, reinforcing a "durable, high-quality returns" approach per management’s characterization during the call.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Capture Rate: The percentage of available market refining margin that the company succeeds in capturing, reflecting operational and commercial efficiency.
  • MPLX: A master limited partnership controlled by Marathon Petroleum Corporation, operating midstream energy infrastructure such as pipelines and terminals.
  • Sour Crude: Crude oil with a relatively high sulfur content, requiring more complex refining processes but often available at discounts to comparable grades (e.g., Canadian WCS or Venezuelan Maya).
  • Turnaround Expense: Planned expenditures associated with periodic major maintenance or upgrades in refinery operations, typically resulting in temporary reductions in utilization rates.
  • NGL: Natural Gas Liquids, hydrocarbons such as ethane, propane, and butane separated from natural gas streams.
  • Feedstock Slate: The composition and proportion of different raw crude oil grades processed in a refinery’s operations.
