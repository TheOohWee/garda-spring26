Takeaways

  • Revenue growth -- 3.8% year-over-year increase in constant currency for the quarter, all organic, with full-year revenue reaching $21.1 billion, up 6.4% in constant currency.
  • Financial services segment -- Segment revenue grew 9% in constant currency in the quarter and 7% for the year, marking the highest annual growth since 2016.
  • Large deals -- 12 large deals signed in the quarter, each exceeding $100 million in total contract value (TCV), including one over $1 billion; large deal TCV up 60% versus prior year.
  • Adjusted operating margin -- 16% in the quarter, up 30 basis points year over year; full-year adjusted operating margin at 15.8%, up 50 basis points versus prior year and above guidance.
  • EPS and profitability -- Fourth-quarter adjusted diluted EPS was $1.35, up 12% year over year; full-year adjusted EPS increased 11% to $5.28.
  • Productivity metrics -- Trailing twelve-month revenue per employee rose 5%; adjusted operating income per employee increased 8% in the same period.
  • Bookings and backlog -- Fourth-quarter bookings grew 9% year over year, with trailing twelve-month bookings up 5% and a book-to-bill ratio of 1.3.
  • Cash flow -- Fourth-quarter free cash flow of approximately $800 million; full-year free cash flow totaled $2.7 billion, representing over 100% of net income.
  • AI engagements -- Over 4,000 active AI engagements company-wide; more than 30% of developer work is now AI-assisted.
  • Fixed bid and transaction-based work -- These contract types now comprise more than 50% of total revenue, up from 41%-42% three years prior.
  • Capital return -- Full-year capital return to shareholders totaled $2 billion via dividends and share repurchases.
  • M&A and strategic moves -- Three Cloud acquisition closed, adding over 1,200 Azure specialists; healthy M&A pipeline with $730 million restricted cash in escrow for recent deals as of quarter-end.
  • Segment performance -- Healthcare and BPO revenues both grew 9% year over year in the quarter and full year; digital engineering saw 8% growth for both periods; data and cloud practices reported mid-single-digit organic growth.
  • Geographic results -- North America grew over 4% in constant currency, leading all regions; Europe achieved 2% growth; growth outside these regions matched the company average, led by Middle East performance.
  • Headcount and talent -- Over 340,000 associates completed AI skilling in past two and a half years; 20,000 college graduates hired in India in 2025, with 2,000 campus hires targeted in the U.S. and 20,000 more in India for 2026.
  • 2026 guidance -- Full-year constant currency revenue growth expected at 4%-6.5%, with 150 basis points from inorganic sources; adjusted operating margin guidance is 15.9%-16.1%.
  • EPS guidance -- 2026 adjusted diluted EPS projected at $5.56-$5.70, reflecting 5%-8% growth, driven by revenue gains, margin expansion, and lowered share count, partially offset by higher tax rates and labor code costs.
  • Capital allocation plans -- Approximately $1.6 billion in capital return planned for 2026, favoring share repurchases and dividend distributions, with additional free cash flow available for M&A.
  • India listing evaluation -- Board and management are actively assessing a potential primary share offering and secondary listing in India; no final decision has yet been made.

Summary

Cognizant Technology Solutions (CTSH 2.16%) reported all-organic revenue growth exceeding the high end of guidance, outpacing most peers in both revenue and margin metrics. Management disclosed 5 mega deals (valued above $500 million) for the year, including a $1 billion engagement, with large deal TCV up nearly 50%. The company closed the Three Cloud acquisition post-quarter, reinforcing its AI and Azure capabilities, and maintained a healthy M&A pipeline supported by robust free cash flow exceeding 100% of net income. Strategic capital deployment—$2 billion returned to shareholders and expansion in both AI engagements and talent training—positions the firm to strengthen operating leverage, with 2026 guidance implying consistent organic growth, incremental margin expansion, and EPS growth superior to revenues despite anticipated tax and cost headwinds.

  • Net promoter scores achieved record highs, indicating improved client satisfaction and potentially enhancing client retention.
  • Variable compensation for associates is expected at the highest level since 2018, reflecting performance alignment but increasing compensation expenses.
  • A one-time increase in defined benefit liability—resulting from India's new labor codes—will lead to a modest, ongoing increase in defined benefit costs.
  • Annual contract value (ACV) saw a modest year-over-year decline, mainly from longer-duration deal mix and softness in smaller transactions, but backlog visibility remains steady.
  • Use of fixed price and transaction-based contracts continues to shift delivery risk to Cognizant, but management cites consistent margin realization compared to bid expectations.
  • Innovation-led deals are gaining share, broadening the growth profile beyond cost savings initiatives seen in previous years.
  • Board and management continue progressing on the evaluation process for an Indian listing, with ongoing engagement with regulators and advisors, but no timeline commitment provided.

Industry glossary

  • TCV (Total Contract Value): Aggregate value of a contract, including all committed revenue over its term; frequently referenced in IT services deal analysis.
  • Book-to-bill: The ratio of bookings (value of contracts signed) to revenue recognized in a specific period; an indicator of demand pipeline sustainability.
  • Agentic AI: AI systems designed to act as autonomous agents, driving outcomes in enterprise processes as opposed to acting solely as analytical or support tools.
  • Context engineering: Proprietary methodology focused on mapping an enterprise's operational context—principles, work patterns, culture—for AI integration and productivity.
  • Pyramid: Talent mix strategy emphasizing proportion of junior (lower-cost) to senior (higher-cost) personnel, crucial in cost management and long-term profitability for IT services firms.
  • BPO (Business Process Outsourcing): Contracting enterprise operations, often enabled by digital and AI tools, to third-party providers such as Cognizant.
