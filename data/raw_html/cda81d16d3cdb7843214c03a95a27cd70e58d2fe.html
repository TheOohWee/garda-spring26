Chevron (CVX) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 31, 2025 at 12:12PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Friday, October 31, 2025 at 11:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Michael K. Wirth

Chief Financial Officer — Eimear P. Bonner

Vice President, Investor Relations — Jake Spiering

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

Worldwide Production -- Production exceeded 4 million barrels of oil equivalent per day in Q3 2025, driven by upstream growth and asset reliability.

Adjusted Earnings -- Adjusted earnings were $3.6 billion, or $1.85 per share (adjusted), including special items totaling $235 million, such as severance and hedge-related costs, partially offset by the fair value measurement of Hess shares.

Organic CapEx -- Organic CapEx was $4.4 billion, with expected full-year spending of $17 billion to $17.5 billion including Hess, in line with guidance.

Adjusted Upstream Earnings -- Adjusted upstream earnings increased compared to the previous quarter, driven by higher liftings and a $150 million contribution from legacy Hess assets, partially offset by higher depreciation, depletion, and amortization (DD&A).

Adjusted Downstream Earnings -- Increased due to higher refining volumes, improved chemical margins, and favorable operating expense timing.

Cash Flow from Operations (Ex-Working Capital) -- $9.9 billion cash flow from operations, excluding working capital, representing a 20% increase compared to the same period last year even as crude prices were $10 lower than the prior year’s comparable period.

Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- Adjusted free cash flow was $7 billion, including a $1 billion loan repayment from Tengizchevroil (TCO).

Shareholder Returns -- $6 billion returned to shareholders, fully covered by adjusted free cash flow.

Production Growth -- Up 690,000 barrels per day sequentially, primarily from legacy Hess production and strong execution in the Permian, Gulf of America, and TCO.

Synergy Capture -- Approximately $1.5 billion in annual run rate savings achieved from restructuring, with further benefits expected in the next quarter.

ACES Green Hydrogen Project -- Achieved first production in Utah, expanding the company’s renewables footprint.

Ballymore Tieback Project -- Reached design capacity ahead of schedule, advancing toward more than 300,000 barrels per day capability in the Gulf of America.

2025 Production Guidance -- Full-year average production growth projected at the top end of the 6%-8% range, excluding legacy Hess assets.

Operational Update: El Segundo Refinery -- Fire occurred in October with no serious injuries; supply commitments continue to be met while investigations are in progress.

Permian Basin Production -- Surpassed 1 million barrels per day, currently 60,000 barrels per day above the targeted plateau, driven by productivity and efficiency gains with fewer rigs and completions.

Hess Integration -- Realizing targeted synergies and integrating assets smoothly, with legacy Hess asset performance exceeding expectations.

Equity Affiliate Distributions -- Outperformed guidance due to TCO results; Distributions are expected to decrease in the fourth quarter of 2025 as TCO undergoes planned maintenance and conserves cash for upcoming loan repayments.

Summary

Chevron (CVX 0.95%) delivered record upstream production and robust operational execution, with major asset contributions from the Hess acquisition and substantial gains in the Permian, Gulf of America, and TCO. Technology deployment, portfolio restructuring, and realized synergies drove significant capital and operating efficiencies, while disciplined organic capital spending matched prior guidance and maintained favorable shareholder returns. The company executed key renewables milestones and reaffirmed capital allocation discipline amid changing macro and policy dynamics.

Chairman and CEO Wirth described negotiations with Kazakhstan for an extension at TCO as complex and "just at the beginning," with future updates to occur only as progress warrants disclosure.

Bonner confirmed the $1 billion synergy target following the Hess acquisition will reach run rate savings by year-end 2025, citing contributions from NOL utilizations, canceled put options, and operating efficiencies.

Management noted that "new country entries" in exploration, including Namibia, Brazil, and Suriname, reflect a shift to more frontier opportunities, with capital allocation to expand beyond near-infrastructure focus.

Eimear P. Bonner said, "Third-quarter oil equivalent production was up 690,000 barrels per day from last quarter, primarily due to legacy Hess production," highlighting the material impact of the transaction on results.

The company’s portfolio weighting has returned to approximately 85% upstream and 15% downstream following the Hess acquisition, with long-term focus maintained on upstream returns and targeted downstream/petrochemicals growth.

Operating model restructuring in upstream divisions has aligned organization by asset class, enabling more rapid transfer of technical best practices and leveraging technology for further efficiency gains.

Chairman Wirth characterized the California refining market as structurally tightening due to policy-driven reductions in capacity and anticipated ongoing impact on supply chains and marine imports.

Permian gas marketing strategy currently provides approximately 70% U.S. Gulf Coast pricing; Waha hub exposure fluctuates quarterly as the company leverages excess firm transportation capacity for additional value.

Upcoming planned maintenance at TCO is expected to reduce affiliate distributions in the fourth quarter of 2025.

Industry glossary

Ballymore Tieback Project: A deepwater oil development in the Gulf of America connecting new production to existing infrastructure to accelerate time-to-market and lower costs.

DD&A: Depreciation, depletion, and amortization, a non-cash expense reflecting the allocation of capitalized costs for assets over their useful life in oil and gas accounting.

NOJV: Non-operated joint venture production, where Chevron holds an interest but does not serve as operator.

NOLs: Net operating losses, tax attributes acquired with Hess and used to offset income for tax efficiency.

TCO: Tengizchevroil, a Kazakhstan-based upstream joint venture, partially owned and operated by Chevron.

ACES Green Hydrogen Project: An integrated clean hydrogen production facility in Utah, marking Chevron’s entry into commercial-scale green hydrogen output.
