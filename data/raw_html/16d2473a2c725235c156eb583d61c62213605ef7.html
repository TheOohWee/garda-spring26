Western Union (WU) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 20, 2026 at 9:52AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, Feb. 20, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Devin McGranahan
  • Chief Financial Officer — Matthew Cagwin

TAKEAWAYS

  • GAAP Revenue -- $1 billion for the quarter; $4.1 billion full year.
  • Adjusted Revenue -- Down 5% year over year for the quarter, 2% decline full year (excluding Iraq), driven by growth in consumer services and branded digital, offset by declines in The Americas retail business.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $0.45 for the quarter, compared to $0.40 prior year; $1.75 full year, at the top end of guidance ($1.65-$1.75).
  • Adjusted Operating Margin -- 20% for the quarter, up from 17% prior year; 20% full year, increased from 19% prior year, due to cost discipline.
  • Consumer Money Transfer (CMT) Adjusted Revenue -- Down 9% for the quarter; down 6% full year (excluding Iraq).
  • Consumer Money Transfer Transactions -- Down 2% for the quarter; down 1% full year (excluding Iraq).
  • Branded Digital Business -- Adjusted revenue up 6% and transactions up 13% for the quarter; double-digit transaction growth driven by Middle East partnerships.
  • Account Payout Transactions -- Grew over 30% in the quarter.
  • Consumer Services Adjusted Revenue -- Up 26% for the quarter, ~30% full year; travel money and bill payment cited as drivers.
  • Consumer Services Contribution -- Accounted for 14% of total revenue this quarter.
  • Retail Business -- Overall transaction performance similar to Q3; slight revenue decline; North America transactions improved by several hundred basis points from Q3.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $544 million full year, including $220 million in transition tax payments; prior year cash flow was $406 million.
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow Conversion -- Over 100% for each of the past three years.
  • CapEx -- $151 million for the year, up 15% due to tech infrastructure and agent network development; expected to remain elevated in 2026.
  • Capital Return -- Over $500 million returned to shareholders ($305 million in dividends, $225 million share repurchase).
  • Leverage Ratios -- 2.9x gross, 1.6x net; cash and equivalents of $1.2 billion, debt of $2.9 billion.
  • U.S. Remittance Tax Implementation -- CEO McGranahan stated, "we have not seen a material impact on our business" through the first six weeks of 2026.
  • Wallet Expansion -- Over 30,000 U.S. Vigo Money Wallet customers onboarded since March; Brazil at 20,000 wallet customers since May; in Argentina, 17% of inbound remittances now go to company wallets.
  • Retail Digital Payments -- Debit cards account for 15% of all retail funding in the U.S. in January, a significant increase over recent months.
  • Digital Asset Pilot -- First U.S. Dollar payment token (USDPT) minted and piloted for on-chain settlement; market launch expected by midyear.
  • Major Retail Agent Wins -- Exclusive, multiyear deals signed with Deutsche Post (Germany), Canada Post, Vallarta Markets (California), and exclusivity renewed with Kroger; company expects a minimum of $100 million incremental annual retail revenue at full ramp.
  • 2026 Outlook -- Adjusted revenue growth of 6%-9% (inclusive of Intermex); adjusted EPS expected between $1.75 and $1.85, assumes ~$100 million in share repurchases and Q2 Intermex closure.
  • Consumer Services 2026 Growth -- CFO Cagwin expects double-digit annual growth in consumer services, with travel money business projected to near $150 million in revenue.
  • Intermex Acquisition -- Q2 closing targeted; anticipated accretion of $0.10 per share over the first full year after integration, with 2026 guidance including partial-year accretion.

SUMMARY

The Western Union Company (WU 1.33%) delivered fourth quarter results showing resilient digital and consumer services growth against continued macro headwinds in The Americas, supported by disciplined cost management and new exclusive partnerships. Despite adjusted revenue declines in traditional core markets, management outlined a clear path to mid- to high-single-digit revenue expansion for 2026, citing wallet adoption, digital innovation, and planned integration of Intermex. Implementation of the new U.S. remittance tax has not yet materially impacted transaction volumes, while the branded digital platform now comprises over 40% of global transaction principal. Strategic execution was further highlighted by the successful rollout of the company's own payment token, exclusive agency wins, and ongoing technology upgrades intended to accelerate digital transformation and customer retention initiatives.

  • Management underscored that exclusive agent wins were “not deals that we bought” but resulted from enhanced platforms and value proposition.
  • The gap between digital transaction and revenue growth rates widened due to strong Middle East growth from low-revenue-per-transaction partnerships, with further acceleration projected.
  • CFO Cagwin noted organic revenue in 2026 is expected to be “closer to flattish,” with Intermex contributing growth to achieve the full-year guidance range.
  • Customer acquisition outside The Middle East has flattened; management is adjusting marketing and go-to-market strategies to counter increased competition and changing digital discovery patterns.
  • Branded digital account payout transactions led growth (+30% in the quarter), leveraging expanded partnerships in the Middle East and APAC, while core retail softness remains a headwind.
  • Capital expenditures and cash flow are being redirected to support digital-first objectives, technology modernization, and timely integration of new acquisitions.
  • No strong demand observed from senders for stablecoin remittance, but management is pursuing partnerships to enable digital asset on-ramping and off-ramping due to perceived market opportunity.
  • Management described the business as operating in a “new normal” of increased month-to-month volatility, prompting a shift to more agile, dynamic management, especially regarding agent incentives and resource allocation.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Consumer Money Transfer (CMT): Core business segment comprising cross-border and domestic money transfers facilitated between consumers.
  • Branded Digital: The company’s digital transfer services conducted via company-owned digital properties (website, app), excluding white-label or third-party powered solutions.
  • Account Payout: Digital payments made directly to a recipient’s bank account or wallet, rather than physical cash pickup.
  • Prepaid Card: Reloadable payment card sold by the company for use in financial transactions and remittances.
  • Vigo Money Wallet: The Western Union Company-branded digital wallet allowing U.S. users to receive, store, and make payments or transfers.
  • USDPT: The Western Union Company’s proprietary U.S. Dollar payment token piloted for on-chain settlement and digital asset transactions.
  • Intermex: International Money Express, a U.S. and Latin America-focused money transfer company pending acquisition by The Western Union Company.
