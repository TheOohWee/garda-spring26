D-Wave (QBTS) Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 22, 2026 at 12:11PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, May 8, 2025 at 8 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Alan Baratz
  • Chief Financial Officer — John Markovich
  • Operator

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $15 million, a record and a 509% increase, with $12.6 million contributed by an Advantage system sale to the Julich Supercomputer Center.
  • Bookings -- $1.6 million, down 64%, primarily comprised of professional services.
  • GAAP Gross Profit -- $13.9 million, up 736%, driven by the high margin Advantage system sale.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Profit -- $14 million, a 644% increase from the prior year period.
  • GAAP Gross Margin -- 92.5%, up from 67.3%.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 93.6%, compared with 76.6% a year ago.
  • Net Loss -- $5.4 million, or $0.02/share, the company's lowest quarterly loss since going public; improved from $17.3 million, or $0.11/share.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Loss -- $6.1 million, a 53% reduction, reflecting both increased revenue and higher operating expenses due to ongoing investments.
  • Cash Position -- $304.3 million, a record level, following a $146.2 million equity raise and term loan payoff.
  • Customer Base -- Reached 133 customers across segments, up from 128, including 69 commercial, 52 research, and 12 government customers; 25% of commercial base are Forbes Global 2000 customers.
  • Production Applications -- Noted implementation by Ford Otosan, NTT DOCOMO, and Pattison Food Group, as well as a pharmaceutical proof-of-concept with Japan Tobacco.
  • Advantage2 Technical Milestones -- Calibration of 4,400+ qubit chips, double the coherence time versus previous generation, 40% increase in energy scale, and increased connectivity from 15 to 20-way.
  • Error Mitigation -- Demonstrated 10x coherence time improvement for simulation problems, with coherence times two times higher than previous systems.
  • Hybrid Solver Advancements -- Now supports problems with up to 2 million variables, including both integer and continuous types; expanded use cases to portfolio optimization and maintenance repair operations.
  • Blockchain Research -- Published result showing potential to reduce electricity costs in blockchain hashing by up to 1,000 times using quantum computation.
  • Leap Quantum Cloud -- Upcoming GA release of full-scale Advantage2 (4,400+ qubits) expected by end of the quarter; 1,200-qubit prototype already available to all users.
  • Equity Line Capacity -- $37.8 million available under Lincoln Park Capital Fund commitment through October 2025.
  • System Sale Model -- Management expects blockchain and AI use cases to drive this segment.
  • Customer Engagement -- Qubits 2025 conference saw record attendance, with in-person up 23% and virtual up nearly 100%; sales pipeline includes increasing interest from large enterprises.
  • Website and Media Metrics -- Website traffic up 153% from October 2024 to March 2025; media stories mentioning D-Wave increased 803% from February to March 2025.

RISKS

  • Bookings fell 64% to $1.6 million, with management directly linking the decline to ongoing sales cycles and the lumpy nature of system sales.
  • Limited US government interest in system purchases, with Baratz stating: "we are not there yet" and adding, "is falling way behind on this and really needs to get that sorted out."

SUMMARY

D-Wave Quantum (QBTS +2.74%) delivered record quarterly financial performance fueled by the first Advantage system sale, resulting in substantial gross margin expansion and the company's lowest net loss since public listing. Management emphasized continued execution on technical milestones, including the calibration of 4,400+ qubit Advantage2 processors and expanded hybrid solver capabilities. A new blockchain application was demonstrated, drawing inbound partnership interest and potentially expanding system sale opportunities. The customer base further diversified, highlighted by major enterprises moving quantum solutions into production while overall bookings declined sharply, underscoring the revenue concentration risk and elongated sales cycles associated with large system deals. Executives cited a robust sales pipeline and strong liquidity that they believe is adequate to achieve profitability.

  • Management confirmed the Julich Supercomputer Center's installation remains an Advantage system, with an upgrade to Advantage2 still pending.
  • Baratz noted D-Wave's proprietary cryogenic control is operational today and is essential for scaling qubit counts, with a significant patent portfolio in place.
  • The Leap Quantum Launchpad trial is driving first conversions from free trial to paid engagements in under two months.
  • The anticipated general availability of the full-scale Advantage2 system is positioned as a near-term catalyst for both cloud service and remote installation business segments.
  • Management did not disclose economics for the Davidson Technologies agreement or provide quantitative guidance on revenue break-even levels.
  • Error mitigation features are available for material simulation workloads, but Baratz clarified such enhancements do not universally extend to all problem classes.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • QPU (Quantum Processing Unit): Specialized computing hardware that manipulates quantum bits (qubits) to perform quantum calculations, analogous to CPUs in classical computing.
  • Hybrid Solver: Software technology combining quantum and classical algorithms to solve large, complex optimization problems beyond the native quantum system's qubit capacity.
  • Coherence Time: The duration a quantum system maintains its quantum state before decoherence disrupts computations, critical for sustained computation accuracy.
  • Annealing Quantum Computer: Quantum computer architecture leveraging quantum annealing to efficiently solve optimization problems using the system’s ground state.
  • Restricted Boltzmann Machine (RBM): Type of stochastic neural network commonly used for data pattern discovery, feature learning, and deep learning model pre-training.
  • Leap Quantum Cloud: D-Wave's proprietary cloud platform offering on-demand access to live quantum computing resources and developer tools.
  • QCAAS (Quantum Computer as a Service): D-Wave’s subscription and cloud-delivered quantum computing model for enterprise customers.
