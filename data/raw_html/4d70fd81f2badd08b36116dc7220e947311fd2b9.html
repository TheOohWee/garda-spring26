Avnet (AVT) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 28, 2026 at 11:50AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, August 6, 2025 at 12 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Philip R. Gallagher
  • Chief Financial Officer — Kenneth A. Jacobson

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $5.6 billion, up 6% sequentially and up slightly year over year, surpassing company guidance.
  • Adjusted operating margin -- 2.5% for the quarter, with Electronic Components at 3% and Farnell at 4.3%.
  • Asia sales growth -- 18% year over year, representing 48% of total sales compared to 41% in the prior-year quarter.
  • EMEA sales -- Down 17% year over year; in constant currency, down 21%.
  • Americas sales -- Decreased 2% year over year, while communications end market increased sequentially.
  • Electronic Components sales -- Increased 1% year over year and 6% sequentially across all regions.
  • Farnell sales -- Up 3% year over year and 5% sequentially, with first year-on-year operating margin improvement since fiscal first quarter ended Sept. 30, 2022.
  • Gross margin -- 10.6%, down 99 basis points year over year and 49 basis points sequentially, primarily due to regional mix shift toward Asia and unfavorable product/customer mix.
  • SG&A expenses -- $451 million, up $1 million year over year and $16 million sequentially; foreign currency impact accounted for $14 million sequentially and $10 million year over year.
  • Cash flow from operations -- $139 million for the quarter and $725 million for the fiscal year.
  • Inventory reduction -- Inventory dropped $185 million sequentially and over $400 million year over year (constant currency), mainly from Europe; overall inventory still targeted for further reduction.
  • Interest expense -- $58 million, down $6 million year over year and $3 million sequentially, aided by lower borrowings, improving adjusted diluted EPS by $0.05 year over year.
  • Share repurchases and dividends -- $415 million returned to shareholders during the fiscal year, achieving nearly 7% reduction in shares outstanding; $300 million remains in current repurchase authorization.
  • Book-to-bill ratio -- Improved above parity in all regions and in the IP&E segment, with growing backlog noted by management.
  • Turns business -- Orders within lead time increased across all regions, signaling inventory normalization at customers.
  • Fiscal first quarter 2026 guidance -- Sales expected in the $5.55 billion-$5.85 billion range and diluted EPS between $0.75 and $0.85, with guidance assuming 2% sequential sales growth at midpoint.
  • Capital expenditures -- $60 million in the quarter, including planned office building purchase; projected to normalize to $25 million–$35 million per quarter in fiscal 2026.
  • Book value per share -- Increased sequentially by $3, reaching about $59, driven primarily by foreign currency exchange effects.
  • Leadership changes -- Dave Youngblood appointed as Chief Digital Officer, and Gilles Beltran promoted to EMEA President as part of planned executive succession.
  • Farnell digital engagement -- Approximately 70% of transactions are digital, contributing more than 50% of revenue and expected to rise.

Risks

  • Chief Financial Officer Jacobson said, "Moving into fiscal year 2026, we expect some operating expense headwinds as a result of our decision to invest in our people by providing merit pay increases, which were not awarded in fiscal year 2025."
  • Gross margin fell 99 basis points year over year and 49 basis points sequentially, due mainly to increased sales mix in Asia and unfavorable product and customer mix.
  • EMEA sales declined 17% year over year, with constant currency showing a 21% decrease, as market recovery in the region remains slow.
  • Chief Executive Officer Gallagher stated, "there is still work to do" on reducing inventories, highlighting continuing excess in certain product categories.

Summary

Avnet (AVT +0.12%) reported $5.6 billion in sales for the quarter, driven by significant growth in Asia and sequential demand gains in most served markets. The company's backlog and book-to-bill ratios increased across all regions and segments, supported by improving order patterns and normalized lead times. Despite progress, gross margin compressed due to an increased Asia mix and product/customer dynamics, while EMEA sales remained notably weak. Capital allocation remained disciplined, with major cash returns to shareholders and clear operating expense headwinds flagged for fiscal 2026 tied to merit increases. Guidance for the next quarter anticipates slight sequential top-line growth and continued investment to position the business for a broader recovery.

  • Farnell's margin showed its first year-over-year improvement since fiscal first quarter ended Sept. 30, 2022, with management targeting a return to double-digit operating margins over the next two years while maintaining operational discipline in expense reductions.
  • Inventory management initiatives contributed to a year-over-year reduction exceeding $400 million on a constant currency basis, but management indicated elevated levels persist in select categories and targeted further progress in coming quarters.
  • Management reiterated that Asia's expansion is currently shifting the sales mix, which directly reduces overall margins, but expects eventual margin uplift as EMEA and Americas recover.
  • Avnet finished the fiscal year with 3.4x gross leverage and about $1.1 billion in committed borrowing capacity, reflecting continued focus on balance sheet strength amid uncertain market conditions.

Industry glossary

  • Book-to-bill ratio: A measure of incoming orders ("bookings") relative to product shipments ("billings"); a ratio above parity signals demand outpacing supply, indicating potential growth.
  • Turns business: Sales generated from customer orders placed within suppliers' lead times, indicating near-term, in-period demand rather than long-term backlog.
  • IP&E: Interconnect, Passive, and Electromechanical components—a segment covering connectors, capacitors, resistors, and other key hardware pieces in electronic systems.
  • Farnell: Avnet's distribution business specializing in electronic kits, tools, and industrial automation components targeted at engineers and entrepreneurs, operating largely online.
  • SG&A: Selling, General, and Administrative expenses, encompassing overhead and non-production operating costs.
