TAKEAWAYS

  • Divestiture Proceeds & Debt Repayment -- Array closed the spectrum sale to AT&T in January 2026 for $1.018 billion, with TDS repaying its final $150 million term loan that month.
  • Share Repurchase Activity -- $67 million was used to repurchase 1.8 million shares in Q4, totaling 2.8 million shares for 2025, with $524 million of buyback authorization remaining at year-end.
  • Fiber Expansion Results -- 58,000 new marketable fiber addresses were added in Q4, up 39% year over year, and 140,000 for the full year, growing the addressable base and expanding the broadband footprint.
  • Fiber Address Goal Update -- Long-term target for marketable fiber addresses increased from 1.8 million to 2.1 million, citing 300,000 incremental opportunities in about 50 new communities.
  • Residential Fiber Net Adds -- Q4 residential fiber net adds reached approximately 15,000, an 11% increase year over year, with 45,000 net adds for 2025 in total.
  • Operating Revenue -- Telecom operating revenues decreased 1% in Q4 and 2% for the full year; excluding divestitures, revenues were flat for both periods (offset by a $3 million decline from markets sold).
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Telecom adjusted EBITDA improved 6% in Q4 but declined 6% for the year, reflecting divestiture impact and noncash stock-based compensation adjustments in Q1.
  • Capital Expenditures (Telecom) -- CapEx was $406 million in 2025, with 2026 guidance increasing to $550 million-$600 million to support accelerated fiber builds.
  • 2026 Telecom Guidance -- Revenue expected between $1.015 billion and $1.055 billion; adjusted EBITDA projected at $310 million-$350 million; targeting 200,000-250,000 new fiber service addresses.
  • Business Transformation Savings -- The program remains on track to deliver $100 million in savings by year-end 2028.
  • Array Spectrum Monetization -- About 70% of spectrum holdings have been monetized or have signed agreements, with $1 billion transactions each to Verizon and AT&T, $178 million with T-Mobile, and remaining spectrum primarily in C-band.
  • Array Revenue and Growth -- Q4 cash site rental revenue increased 64% year over year from all customers, or 8% when excluding T-Mobile MLA sites, with a 96% increase when T-Mobile interim site revenue is included.
  • Array 2026 Guidance -- Array operating revenue forecasted at $200 million-$215 million, adjusted EBITDA at $200 million-$215 million, and capital expenditures at $25 million-$35 million.
  • DISH Revenue Exclusion -- 2026 Array guidance does not include any DISH revenue due to their non-payment and contesting of obligations, with $7 million recognized in 2025 but future collections uncertain.
  • Naked Towers Outlook -- When T-Mobile's MLA site selections finalize, Array anticipates 800-1,800 towers with no tenants and is focused on marketing and reducing costs for this portfolio.

SUMMARY

The call outlined significant balance sheet strengthening through major divestitures and aggressive debt repayment, setting the stage for further capital allocation flexibility. Management detailed material progress in fiber expansion, with increased marketable address targets and a stepped-up 2026 build plan, supported by higher CapEx guidance. Clarification was provided on the composition of new fiber address goals, prioritizing edge-out growth in adjacent markets. The Array business communicated substantial progress in spectrum monetization and tower integration, highlighted by the pipeline completion and the first full impact of T-Mobile MLA revenues. Guidance reflected disciplined exclusion of DISH Wireless revenue, signaling potential upside should resolution be achieved. Strategic focus was reinforced on optimizing underutilized tower assets while maintaining patience in monetizing retained spectrum and investment interests.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Marketable Fiber Addresses: Locations passed by fiber and ready for service activation, representing the accessible addressable market for fiber-based broadband.
  • Edge Out: Expansion of fiber infrastructure from existing service areas into adjacent, previously unserved markets to increase footprint and potential customer base.
  • C-band: Mid-band wireless spectrum commonly used for 5G deployment, considered valuable for its favorable propagation and capacity characteristics.
  • MLA (Master Lease Agreement): A comprehensive, multi-year lease contract between a tower company and mobile carrier, often covering a portfolio of tower sites and associated terms.
  • Naked Towers: Wireless tower sites with no active tenants (i.e., no lessees), requiring active marketing to generate future revenue or consideration for decommissioning.
  • EACAM: A multiyear fiber program referenced by TDS for expanding its fiber footprint, including designated service areas for federally- or state-supported broadband deployment.
