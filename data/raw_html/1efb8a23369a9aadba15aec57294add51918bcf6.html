Earnings Call Transcript

HP (HPQ) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 27, 2025 at 5:47PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, Aug. 27, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

President and Chief Executive Officer — Enrique Lores

Chief Financial Officer — Karen Parkhill

Global Head of Investor Relations — Orit Keinan-Nahon

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

RISKS

HP(HPQ 0.65%) reported that print hardware demand in the office segment was described as "slightly softer than anticipated," particularly across North America and parts of Europe, reflecting enterprise IT spending prioritizing other areas over print.

Gross margin declined year-over-year to 20.5% in the fiscal third quarter ended July 31, 2025, driven by a higher mix from personal systems, increased trade-related costs, and unfavorable currency impacts.

Management remarked on a "more aggressive pricing environment" in the print business, with competitive pressures limiting the effectiveness of industry-wide price increases.

TAKEAWAYS

Revenue-- Increased by 3% year-over-year, driven by strong performance in personal systems.

Personal Systems Revenue-- Grew 6% year-over-year and 10% sequentially from the prior quarter, with unit growth of 5% year-over-year and 13% sequentially.

AIPC Mix-- AI PC shipments reached over 25% of the personal systems mix, a full quarter ahead of target.

AIPC Pricing-- Lores stated, "we maintain the assumptions that we had shared before of the 5% to 10% price increase driven by AIPCs compared to non-AIPC units."

Operating Margin (Non-GAAP)-- Non-GAAP operating margin reached 7.1%, down year-over-year but within management's guidance.

Personal Systems Operating Margin-- Achieved a 5.4% personal systems operating margin, in line with the long-term target range after mitigation actions.

Print Revenue-- Declined 4% year-over-year (3% in constant currency), with market-driven hardware declines in both consumer and commercial segments.

Print Operating Margin-- Delivered a 17.3% operating margin, flat year-over-year and within guidance, with margin volatility explained by supplies mix seasonality.

Cash Flow-- Generated approximately $1.7 billion in cash from operations and $1.5 billion in free cash flow; returned over $400 million to shareholders via dividends and share buybacks.

Future-Ready Cost Savings-- On track to achieve $2 billion in annualized gross run-rate savings by the end of fiscal 2025 through cost reductions, manufacturing shifts, and process automation.

Trade-Related Costs-- Management stated that "nearly all products sold in North America are now built outside of China," reducing tariff exposure.

Q4 Guidance: EPS and Segment Performance-- Management expects fiscal fourth quarter 2025 non-GAAP diluted EPS of $0.87-$0.97 and GAAP diluted EPS of $0.75-$0.85. Both personal systems and print revenue are forecast to align with historical seasonality. Personal systems operating margin is expected in the 5%-7% range. Print operating margin is expected to be near the top of the 16%-19% range.

Free Cash Flow Guidance (Fiscal 2025)-- Projected free cash flow for fiscal 2025 is in the range of $2.6 billion to $3 billion, including approximately $400 million in restructuring charges.

Personal Systems Market Share-- Gained share in premium commercial, consumer, and AIPC sub-segments.

Print Consumer Subscriptions-- Delivered double-digit year-over-year growth in subscriber count for "all-in" print subscription services.

Geographic Performance-- Asia-Pacific and Japan constant currency revenue increased 11% year-over-year.

Sustainability Milestone-- Achieved 100% renewable electricity across U.S. operations; 99% of HP home and office printers, desktops, notebooks, displays, and workstations now incorporate recycled content.

SUMMARY

Management emphasized rapid progress in both operational cost savings and supply chain diversification, significantly mitigating the impact of tariffs and trade-related headwinds. Sequential acceleration in AIPC adoption outpaced forecasts, yielding a faster-than-expected mix shift to high-value personal systems and supporting above-guidance non-GAAP profitability. Despite flat print margins (operating margin of 17.3%, flat year-over-year), pricing pressure and muted hardware demand in commercial print persist as headwinds, although premium segments and subscription models continue to expand. Cash generation supported significant capital returns to shareholders, but leverage constraints led to more limited buybacks in the quarter. Looking ahead, the company projects sustained mid-single-digit PC market growth in the second half of fiscal 2025, with continued growth expected into fiscal 2026, and management expects fiscal fourth quarter segment performance to maintain historical seasonality.

Lores signaled that print demand softness resulted from enterprise and commercial clients prioritizing AI and PC investments over printing hardware, affecting near-term sales.

Parkhill clarified that leverage remained just above the 2x target at quarter end and that, "should we continue to operate at higher leverage levels, we would use cash to reduce debt as bonds mature."

Management cited ongoing advances in AI-powered solutions across products, with specific industry use cases highlighted to justify anticipated long-term AIPC demand and competitive differentiation.

This is reinforcing the shift to higher-margin categories despite industry headwinds.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

AIPC: “AI PC,” referring to personal computers with integrated AI acceleration hardware such as neural processing units for localized AI workloads.

Advanced Compute: Segment encompassing high-performance computing solutions including workstations, advanced desktops, and commercial premium products.

Big Tank: Ink tank printer models offering higher ink capacity and lower per-page costs, typically forming part of HP's strategy to drive more profitable consumer print units.

Channel Inventory: Product stock held by first-tier distributors or resellers, explicitly referenced as “tier one channel inventory.”

ISV: Independent Software Vendor; external software companies building or optimizing applications for HP hardware platforms.
