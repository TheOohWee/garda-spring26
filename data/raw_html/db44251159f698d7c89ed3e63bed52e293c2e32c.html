Earnings Call Transcript

Opendoor (OPEN) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 23, 2025 at 11:48AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, November 6, 2025 at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Kasra Nejatian
  • Chief Financial Officer — Christy Schwartz
  • Head of Investor Relations — Michael Judd

Takeaways

  • Home Purchases -- 1,169 homes acquired, with near doubling of weekly contract pace during the seven weeks ending late October (from 120 to 230 homes).
  • Revenue -- $915 million, exceeding the previously provided guidance range due to intentional clearing of older inventory.
  • GAAP Gross Profit -- $66 million versus $105 million in the prior-year quarter, reflecting margin headwinds from legacy inventory.
  • GAAP Gross Margin -- 7.2%, a 40 basis-point decrease year-over-year.
  • Contribution Profit -- $20 million, down from $52 million; contribution margin at 2.2% compared to 3.8% a year ago.
  • Net Loss -- $90 million; prior-year loss was $78 million, which included a $14 million gain from Mainstay deconsolidation.
  • Adjusted Net Loss -- $61 million, improved from the $70 million adjusted net loss in the prior year period.
  • GAAP Operating Expenses -- $134 million.
  • Adjusted Operating Expenses -- $53 million, representing a 41% reduction from $90 million a year ago.
  • Unrestricted Cash -- $962 million at quarter-end.
  • Equity Invested in Homes -- $187 million on the balance sheet.
  • Net Inventory -- 3,139 homes representing $1.1 billion.
  • Nonrecourse Asset-Backed Borrowing Capacity -- $7.6 billion, with $1.8 billion total committed.
  • ATM Equity Program -- $200 million raised in September 2025 by selling 21.6 million shares at a $9.26 average price to address maturing convertible notes.
  • Convertible Notes Refinancing -- Majority retired with equity; prior notes bore 7% interest and included an early repayment provision triggered by stock price increases.
  • Warrant Dividend -- Board declared a pro rata dividend: three series of freely tradable warrants for every 30 shares held, with strike prices at $9, $13, and $17.
  • Acquisition Growth Guidance -- Q4 2025 acquisitions expected to increase by at least 35% over Q3 driven by operational changes; target to exit Q4 2026 at approximately 6,000 homes bought per quarter.
  • Q4 Revenue Guidance -- Revenue projected to decline approximately 35% sequentially due to reduced Q3 inventory even as contract pace rises.
  • Contribution Margin Outlook -- Margins expected to bottom in October, improve by year-end, but remain below Q3 levels as legacy homes are cleared.
  • Adjusted Operating Expense Guidance -- For the twelve months ending June 2026: $255 million-$265 million (excluding CEO cash make-whole), representing a $62 million or 20% decrease from the prior-year period.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Guidance -- Q4 2025 adjusted EBITDA loss expected in the high $40 million to mid-$50 million range.
  • Profitability Target -- Management committed to achieving adjusted net income breakeven by the end of 2026, measured over a forward 12-month basis.
  • Product and Technology Initiatives -- Over a dozen new digital products launched, including AI-powered home assessments, D2C flows, automated title and escrow, and Opendoor Checkout for direct-to-consumer online home purchase.
  • Capital Structure Changes -- Use of ATM proceeds to address short-term note risks, effective elimination of external consulting expenses, and consolidation of software vendors underway.
  • Incentive Alignment -- CEO Nejatian said, "If we issue a share, it has only one job, to make every other share worth more for our existing shareholders."
  • AI Integration -- Home assessment time reduced from nearly one day to about 10 minutes and up to 750 AI-led assessments conducted weekly, significantly lowering manual touchpoints.
  • Operational Metrics -- D2C funnel conversion rate increased 6x over non-D2C funnel in recent test of more than 2,000 accounts.
  • Cost Structure Shift -- Commitment to transition fixed expenses toward variable, with elimination of over 20 software vendors to date.
  • Payment and Transaction Innovation -- Introduction of USDC payment acceptance and development work on real estate tokenization initiatives begun.

Risks

  • Christy Schwartz stated "Our priority since mid-September has been to clear old inventory homes selected under the previous strategy that prioritize spread over quality. That's pressured our contribution margin sequentially since April through October," highlighting negative margin impact from legacy inventory.
  • Revenue expected to decline approximately 35% quarter over quarter in Q4 due to lower inventory even as acquisition contract pace rises.
  • Q4 2025 contribution margin will remain below Q3, reflecting continued pressure until older inventory is cleared.
  • Q4 2025 adjusted EBITDA loss guidance in the high $40 million to mid-$50 million range flags ongoing short-term profitability challenges.

Summary

Opendoor Technologies (OPEN +0.23%) reported third-quarter results marked by revenue above guidance, but also disclosed significant margin deterioration and increased net loss due to accelerated sell-through of legacy inventory. Management executed substantial capital structure changes by raising $200 million in September, refinancing a substantial portion of the 2030 convertible notes with equity, and simultaneously declaring a warrant dividend to align shareholder interests. The company unveiled Opendoor 2.0, outlining a strategy focused on breakeven adjusted net income by end of 2026, increased home acquisition velocity, AI-driven operating model, and embedded financial services. Product launches—including direct-to-consumer home purchase, AI-powered home assessments, and support for transactions using USDC—signal an aggressive pivot to technology-centric real estate services. Near-term guidance calls for a material sequential revenue decline and continued low contribution margins as the remaining legacy homes are liquidated.

  • Management aims for fixed operating expense stability even as volume scales, shifting toward variable costs to support margin improvement.
  • Digital product releases, improved conversion via D2C, and a leaner, more AI-powered workforce underpin the refounding strategy.
  • Major operational and capital actions—including consultant elimination and software simplification—were implemented within weeks of CEO Nejatian's tenure.
  • Warrant dividend provides shareholders with direct financial upside through three series of freely tradable warrants at progressive exercise prices, reinforcing long-term equity alignment.
  • Ongoing development in asset tokenization and payment flexibility presents potential additional levers for future transaction and revenue growth.

Industry glossary

  • D2C: Direct-to-Consumer; refers to Opendoor's sales funnel enabling homeowners to transact directly through its platform rather than via intermediaries.
  • Contribution Margin: Profit measure reflecting revenues less costs directly attributable to property transactions, exclusive of indirect operational and fixed costs.
  • ATM Program: At-the-Market equity offering program, a method for raising capital via incremental public share sales at prevailing prices.
  • USDC: USD Coin, a stablecoin cryptocurrency pegged to the US dollar, now accepted by Opendoor for home purchases.
  • Tokenization: Process of converting a real estate asset into a digital token on a blockchain, enabling faster, potentially more liquid transactions.
