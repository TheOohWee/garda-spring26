Lear (LEA) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 4, 2026 at 10:29AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, February 4, 2026 at 9 a.m. ET

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Raymond Scott
  • Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Jason Cardew

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $23.3 billion for the full year, a 5% increase.
  • Core Operating Earnings -- $1.1 billion or 4.6% of net sales for the full year.
  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share -- $12.80, up 1%, and marking the fifth consecutive annual increase.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $1.1 billion for the year.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $527 million in 2025.
  • Share Repurchases -- $325 million in 2025, above the initial $250 million target.
  • Capital Returned to Shareholders -- Almost $500 million via dividends and buybacks.
  • Net Operating Performance Savings -- $195 million, exceeding the original $125 million target by 56%.
  • Headcount Reduction -- Global hourly headcount reduced by 7,000 during the year, and 22,000 over two years.
  • Seating Segment Full-Year Sales -- $17.3 billion, a $61 million (0.4%) increase.
  • Seating Segment Adjusted Operating Margin -- 6.4% for the year, down from 2024 due to lower volumes and unfavorable mix.
  • E-Systems Full-Year Sales -- $6 billion, a decrease of $108 million (2%).
  • E-Systems Adjusted Operating Margin -- 4.9%, down from 5.1% in 2024, pressured by lower volumes and wind-down of discontinued lines.
  • Thermal Comfort Awards -- 33 awards won, with $170 million peak annual revenue potential; 9 programs are already in production, and 14 additional launches secured for 2026.
  • Largest Seating Conquest Award -- Secured complete seats for a major American truck program and General Motors (NYSE:GM) full-size SUVs and pickups at Orion Assembly launching in 2027.
  • E-Systems New Business Awards -- Over $1.4 billion in awards secured for 2025, a decade-high, across all major global regions.
  • Digital Efficiencies -- AI and automation initiatives delivered $70 million in annual savings, and a 3%-5% efficiency gain in just-in-time facilities.
  • Restructuring Savings -- Achieved $85 million, surpassing the initial $55 million target.
  • 2026 Revenue Outlook -- Guidance range is $23.2 to $24 billion, with the midpoint representing a $351 million (2%) projected increase.
  • 2026 Core Operating Earnings Guidance -- $1.03 billion to $1.2 billion, midpoint implies 5% growth.
  • 2026 Free Cash Flow Target -- $600 million at midpoint, with conversion expected above 80%.
  • 2026 Share Repurchase Target -- Planned repurchases of more than $300 million.
  • Backlog -- $1.325 billion two-year backlog, up $125 million sequentially; about half of this is tied to ICE programs, and 85% of China backlog is from domestic automakers.
  • Adjusted EPS Growth -- 16% growth in 2025, driven by reduced share count and a lower tax rate.
  • Segment Margin Targets for 2026 -- Seating margin guided to rise by 10 basis points to 6.5%; E-Systems margin also guided up 10 basis points to 5%.
  • Automation and Digital Investments -- $75 million in further savings targeted in 2026 as these initiatives expand globally.
  • Restructuring Actions 2026 -- $80 million in expected savings from completed and planned projects.
  • Two-Year Backlog Composition -- Non-consolidated joint ventures account for $5.55 billion, of which 55% is with Chinese domestic automakers.
  • Balance Sheet -- Weighted average cost of debt under 4%, $3 billion of available liquidity, and share repurchase authorization of $775 million remaining through 12/31/2026.

SUMMARY

Lear Corporation (LEA 0.49%) reported notable revenue and cash generation growth, supported by surpassing margin improvement, savings, and key new business wins—highlighted by a record-setting seating conquest and major E-Systems awards. Management provided clear guidance for continued margin expansion and robust free cash flow in 2026, anchored by automation, digital transformation, and disciplined capital allocation. The two-year backlog increased to $1.325 billion with significant ICE exposure and strengthening relationships with Chinese domestic automakers, positioning the company for further share gains and growth acceleration into 2028 and beyond.

  • The Orion facility General Motors (NYSE:GM) award, called by management the largest conquest in company history, is excluded from the 2027 backlog and will primarily benefit 2029 results.
  • Management reaffirmed the 29% seating share target, attributing progress to successful differentiation via innovation and technology, rather than competitor weakness.
  • Thermal comfort business remains on track for $1 billion revenue and 10% margin, but timing has been adjusted due to delayed EV launches and lower associated volumes.
  • Commodity price fluctuations, including copper, are "Largely insulated" from earnings impact due to passthrough agreements, though revenue may show modest variability.
  • Major OEM program cancellations in North America resulted in negotiated settlements providing a one-time cash and net performance benefit, with no further material impact expected in 2026.
  • Share repurchase execution since program inception totals $5.9 billion, with over 85% of free cash flow returned to shareholders through buybacks and dividends.
  • First quarter 2026 is "shaping up pretty similar to how we exited the fourth quarter," with anticipated revenues near $6 billion, and operating income around $260 million.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Conquest Award: New customer business won by displacing an existing supplier.
  • ICE Programs: Internal combustion engine vehicle production contracts, as distinct from electric vehicle programs.
  • Net Performance: The aggregate operational savings realized after absorbing contractual price reductions, commodity changes, FX impacts, and labor rate variances.
  • Thermal Comfort Solutions (TCS): Integrated seating system features that manage heating, cooling, and comfort in automotive seats.
  • Margin Accretive Backlog: Awarded business expected to deliver profit margins above the company’s current average.
