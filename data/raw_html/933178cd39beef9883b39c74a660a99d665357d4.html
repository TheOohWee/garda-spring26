Minerals Tech Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Oct 24, 2025 at 4:28PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, October 24, 2025 at 11:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Chief Executive Officer — Douglas T. Dietrich

Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Erik C. Aldag

Group President, Consumer & Specialties — D.J. Monagle

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected].

RISKS

Erik C. Aldag stated, "we expect Environmental and Infrastructure sales to be 10% to 15% lower sequentially due to typical seasonality for large project activity."

Continued weakness in North American foundry and residential construction markets, with management noting they, "expect softer than normal residential construction volumes in the fourth quarter."

Litigation expenses of $7.5 million related to talc proceedings in Q3 2025 pressured operating income and were described by Douglas T. Dietrich as "a little bit higher in terms of activity" this quarter.

Segment margin impact driven by "temporarily higher operating costs at a few of our facilities" in Q3 2025, as well as higher logistics expenses linked to cat litter plant upgrades.

TAKEAWAYS

Revenue -- $532 million, up 1% sequentially and year-over-year in Q3 2025.

Operating Income -- Operating income was $78 million, down 1% sequentially and year-over-year.

Operating Margin -- 14.7% of sales in Q3 2025, reflecting temporary cost impacts primarily in Consumer and Specialties.

Earnings Per Share (EPS) -- $1.55 per share excluding special items, a company record in Q3 2025, up 3% from the prior year, with special items of $7.5 million related to litigation expenses.

EBITDA -- EBITDA was $100 million, up 1% sequentially and year-over-year in Q3 2025; EBITDA margin was 18.8% of sales in Q3 2025.

Free Cash Flow -- $44 million in free cash flow in Q3 2025, supporting capital investments and dividend payments.

Dividend -- 9% increase to the regular quarterly dividend, the third consecutive annual increase, as well as $20 million returned to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases in Q3 2025.

Consumer and Specialties Segment -- Sales of $277 million in Q3 2025, flat sequentially and down 1% year-over-year. Operating income of $37 million and a margin of 13.5% in Q3 2025.

Household and Personal Care -- Sales up 2% sequentially to $130 million in Q3 2025, with Cat litter volume improvement credited to promotional activity adjustments in Q3 2025.

Specialty Additives -- Sales at $148 million, 2% lower sequentially in Q3 2025 with Flat global Paper and Packaging volumes in Q3 2025 and North America volume declines offset by Asia growth in Q3 2025.

Growth in Oil Purification and Animal Health -- Edible oil and renewable fuel purification sales up 18% year-over-year in Q3 2025;

Organic Pet Litter Growth -- Compound annual growth rate of 9% over five years (adjusted for acquisitions) for the Pet Litter business; management expects a return to long-term rates of 3%-4% in North America, and 6%-8% in Asia.

Bleaching Earth (Turkey) -- 30% capacity expansion with a $9-$10 million investment in Turkey; Business has grown at a 20% compound annual rate since 2018, with a significant portion now in sustainable aviation fuel and renewable fuels.

Engineered Solutions Segment -- Sales of $255 million, up 2% sequentially and 4% year-over-year in Q3 2025; Operating income of $45 million with a record 17.6% margin in Q3 2025.

High Temperature Technologies -- Sales of $179 million, stable sequentially and up 2% year-over-year in Q3 2025; North America stable, Europe weak, and Asia foundry volumes up 17% year-over-year in Q3 2025.

Environmental and Infrastructure -- 5% sequential sales growth and 9% year-over-year, with both comparisons to the prior year, driven by offshore services and increased drilling demand.

Capital Expenditures -- $27 million in capital expenditures in the third quarter, with full-year capital spend on track for approximately $100 million.

Net Leverage -- 1.7x EBITDA, below the target of 2x EBITDA, indicating a solid balance sheet according to management as of Q3 2025.

Q4 Outlook -- Sales expected to decline 2%-4% sequentially, to $510-$525 million in Q4 2025; Operating income targeted at $65-$70 million and EPS of $1.20-$1.30 in Q4 2025.

Growth Investments -- $50 million in capital targeting $100 million in future incremental revenue over the next 12-18 months, mainly in Pet Care, Bleaching Earth, Paper & Packaging, and MINDScan installations.

SUMMARY

Mineral Technologies (MTX +0.06%) management introduced guidance anticipating softer volumes in Q4, with sequential declines in high-margin product lines due to typical seasonality and extended customer outages in North America. Capital was allocated both to support capacity expansion in Pet Care and Bleaching Earth and to facilitate new technology introduction, especially in Asian markets and engineered solutions. Margin impact in Consumer and Specialties was attributed to temporary cost increases in Q3 2025, with management confirming that rationalization efforts and operating leverage are expected to restore operating margins to targeted levels next year. Additionally, management reported successfully mitigating $10 million in higher commodity and logistics costs through productivity, price actions, and supply chain initiatives in Q3 2025.

Douglas T. Dietrich described the company's investments as already securing about $25 million to $30 million in contracts annually, which should begin to ramp up through the first quarter and reach the full run rate by the second quarter.

Management expects additional revenue from MINDScan contracts, with each installation cited as generating "$1 million to $2 million depending on the size" according to Douglas T. Dietrich and a market opportunity of approximately $200 million over time.

Free cash flow is projected to remain strong, supported by working capital normalization as temporary inventory builds in pet care and strategic positions in high temperature businesses are reduced in coming quarters.

Regarding talc litigation, management conveyed progress toward a 524(g) trust but offered no material update to timing or ultimate cost, maintaining that reserves remain sufficient.

Management confirmed the Pet Care segment's capacity expansions in China and North America are designed to support multi-year sector growth at targeted rates, with modular additions possible to scale output further.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Bleaching Earth: A clay-based absorbent used to remove impurities from edible oils and renewable fuels, including applications in sustainable aviation fuel processing.

MINDScan: A proprietary monitoring solution installed on electric arc furnaces to optimize refractory products and process efficiency.

524(g) trust: A legal arrangement under U.S. bankruptcy law for resolving asbestos (and similar) claims against a company, funded to compensate current and future claimants.
