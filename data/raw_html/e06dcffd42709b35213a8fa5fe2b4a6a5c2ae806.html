Earnings Call Transcript

Oshkosh (OSK) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 29, 2026 at 10:43AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, January 29, 2026 at 9:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — John Pfeifer
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Matthew Field
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Patrick Davidson

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated Revenue -- $2.7 billion for the quarter, up 3.5%, driven by improved pricing in Vocational and higher sales volume in Access.
  • Adjusted Operating Income -- $226 million, down approximately $20 million, reflecting unfavorable product mix and higher manufacturing overhead, partially offset by lower incentive compensation and higher volume.
  • Adjusted Operating Margin -- 8.4% for the quarter, down 100 basis points, with tariff and mix headwinds primarily in Access.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $2.26 in the quarter; full-year adjusted EPS of $10.79, slightly above the midpoint of guidance.
  • Full Year Revenue -- $10.4 billion, "in line" with guidance per CFO Field.
  • Share Repurchases -- 912,000 shares bought back for $119 million during the quarter.
  • Access Segment Revenue -- $1.2 billion, up 1%; orders exceeded $1.7 billion with a book-to-bill ratio of 1.5; backlog at $1.3 billion, seen as "reasonable" by management.
  • Access Segment Adjusted Operating Margin -- 8.8%, impacted by tariffs and adverse mix, partially offset by higher volume.
  • Vocational Segment Revenue -- $922 million in the quarter, with an adjusted operating margin of 16.2%; full year revenue exceeded $3.7 billion, up nearly 13%.
  • Fire Apparatus Sales -- Up about 17% for the year, with fire truck deliveries increasing 10% in the second half.
  • Vocational Backlog -- Exceeded $6.6 billion, providing visibility as capacity investments continue.
  • Transport Segment Revenue -- $567 million for the quarter; delivery vehicles accounted for $165 million, representing 30% of segment revenue and growing 13% sequentially.
  • Transport Segment Operating Margin -- 4% for the quarter, up from 2.8%, primarily benefiting from pricing on new contracts and improved contract cost changes but offset by NGDV ramp-up expenses.
  • Tariff Impact -- $25 million in the quarter and expected to reach $200 million in 2026, up $160 million; approximately three-quarters of the 2026 tariffs are assigned to Access.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Consolidated sales forecast at approximately $11 billion, mid-single digit growth; adjusted EPS expected to reach $11.50.
  • 2026 Segment Guidance -- Access sales forecast at $4.2 billion (down 6%-7%), adjusted margin at 10%. Vocational projected at $4.2 billion revenue and 17% margin. Transport expected at $2.5 billion revenue, 4% margin, with performance improving in the second half.
  • CapEx and Free Cash Flow Guidance -- $200 million in CapEx planned; free cash flow expected between $550 million and $650 million, about 80% of net income.
  • Dividend Announcement -- Quarterly dividend set at $0.57 per share; continuation of share repurchases planned through the year.
  • Innovation Recognition -- Several CES awards for JLG robotics, hybrid electric Volterra ARF, Volterra electric refuse/recycling vehicle, and collision avoidance mitigation system (CAMS), with field trials underway and commercialization planned.
  • NGDV (Next Generation Delivery Vehicle) -- Production surpassed 5,000 units and over 10 million miles driven; on-schedule performance with USPS, with delivery volumes expected to be higher in the second half of 2026 and about half of transport segment revenue for that year.
  • Vocational Production Investments -- $70 million spent to date of planned $150 million CapEx for fire truck throughput; targeted reductions in lead times expected from these investments.

RISKS

  • CEO Pfeifer cited "expected lower capital investments at certain of our industrial customers, notably in our access equipment and refuse businesses," contributing to planned declines in Access sales.
  • CFO Field stated, "we anticipate that the first quarter will be the lowest quarter of the year as we would traditionally see from seasonal factors," and forecast Q1 adjusted EPS could be "about half of last year."
  • Tariff headwinds are explicit, with Field reporting a "$160 million higher" tariff impact for 2026 versus prior year and stating approximately "three quarters is in access segment."
  • Vocational segment faces "some pressure in the environmental business with refuse and recycling," resulting in "a little bit of a lull in CapEx, so some downward pressure on that business" for 2026.

SUMMARY

Management reported that adjusted EPS for 2025 reached $10.79, driven by improved price-cost dynamics in the Vocational segment and strong demand pull-forward in Access. Executives forecast 2026 adjusted EPS of $11.50 on consolidated sales of approximately $11 billion, with each segment facing distinct demand and cost environments. Access is projected to decline 6%-7% due to weaker nonresidential construction and higher tariffs, while Vocational is expected to achieve 17% margins and stable revenue. Transport segment performance is set to improve as NGDV volumes increase during the second half, balanced against lower defense export volume and NGDV launch costs.

  • CFO Field stated, "We do anticipate price cost to turn positive in the back half of this year," supporting margin improvement later in 2026.
  • Innovation initiatives, highlighted by multiple CES and industry awards, are positioned by management as underpinning long-term profitable growth and future deliveries outside the USPS customer base.
  • CFO Field clarified capital allocation, noting plans to maintain quarterly dividends and execute further share repurchases, emphasizing Board confidence in sustained cash flow and profitability targets.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • NGDV (Next Generation Delivery Vehicle): Purpose-built USPS delivery vehicle project, featuring advanced safety, productivity, and electrification technologies.
  • CAMS (Collision Avoidance Mitigation System): AI-powered safety platform designed to anticipate roadway collisions for emergency vehicles and roadside operators.
  • Book-to-Bill Ratio: Orders received divided by units shipped in same period; a value above 1.0 indicates backlog growth.
  • RCV (Refuse Collection Vehicle): Specialized truck used for waste and recycling collection, a Vocational segment product category.
  • Throughput: Rate of vehicle production and delivery at manufacturing sites.
  • FHTV/FMTV (Family of Heavy Tactical Vehicles/Family of Medium Tactical Vehicles): Defense segment truck product programs supporting US armed forces.
  • Access Equipment: Includes aerial work platforms and telehandlers, primarily serving construction, rental, and maintenance end markets.
  • 8020 Philosophy: Operational improvement methodology that focuses on identifying and optimizing the most impactful 20% of business processes or products.
