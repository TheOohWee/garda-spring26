Granite (GVA) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 12:13PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Feb. 12, 2026 at 11:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Kyle T. Larkin
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Staci M. Woolsey
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Michael W. Barker

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $4.4 billion, a ten percent increase year over year, supported by organic growth and acquisitions.
  • Gross profit -- $711 million, up twenty-four percent from the prior year, driven by margin improvement in both segments.
  • Adjusted net income -- $276 million, marking growth of twenty-nine percent versus the previous year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $527 million, an increase of thirty-one percent; adjusted EBITDA margin rose to 11.9% from ten percent year over year.
  • Operating cash flow -- $469 million, three percent higher than the prior year, with a margin of 10.6% of revenue; excluding non-recurring collections, the operating cash flow margin aligned with the original nine percent target.
  • Construction segment revenue -- $940 million for the quarter, up $119 million or fourteen percent year over year; organic revenue growth was seven percent, with acquisitions contributing $59 million (note: subtotals do not sum to total due to rounding and additional factors).
  • Construction segment gross profit -- $143 million, a $15 million increase year over year, with segment gross profit margin at fifteen percent.
  • Materials segment revenue -- $225 million, up $69 million, primarily from recent acquisitions.
  • Materials segment cash gross profit -- $47 million for the quarter, up $10 million year over year, representing twenty-one percent of segment revenue; full-year cash gross profit margin rose 490 basis points to twenty-six percent.
  • Aggregate reserves and resources -- 2.1 billion tons, a thirty-four percent increase driven by acquisitions (Warren Paving, CinderLite, Pappage Construction), more than doubling reserves in five years.
  • Committed and awarded projects (CAP) -- $7 billion at year-end, the highest in company history; sequential quarter CAP increase was $632 million.
  • Best value work -- Forty-eight percent of CAP at quarter-end, nearly evenly balanced with bid-build contracts; management cited ongoing margin benefits from this mix.
  • 2026 revenue guidance -- Projected range of $4.9 billion to $5.1 billion, including a full year of 2025 acquisitions and organic growth at the high end of the six percent to eight percent 2027 target CAGR.
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin guidance -- Twelve percent to thirteen percent of revenue targeted for 2026, with a 2027 goal of 12.5% to 14.5%.
  • SG&A expense guidance -- Projected at 8.5% to nine percent of revenue, including $48 million in stock-based compensation.
  • 2026 CapEx guidance -- $140 million to $160 million, with about $50 million allocated to strategic investments in Materials.
  • Acquisition spend -- $778 million in 2025; three acquisitions expanded the Southeast, California, and Nevada platforms.
  • Cash and debt position -- $650 million in cash and marketable securities, $1.3 billion in debt, and $583 million available on the revolver at year-end.
  • Share repurchases -- Three hundred thousand shares repurchased to offset stock-based compensation dilution during the year.
  • 2026 operating cash flow margin target -- Ten percent of revenue, based on ongoing profitability improvement and disciplined working capital management.
  • Materials segment investments -- Cash gross profit margin improved from nineteen percent in 2023 to twenty-six percent in 2025 due to focused CapEx and integration efforts.
  • Pricing outlook -- Management expects mid-single-digit price improvements in aggregate and low single-digit in asphalt for 2026.
  • Best value/bid-build portfolio mix -- Approximately fifty/fifty split, providing revenue visibility and margin stability into 2027; management noted different revenue burn rates for contract types.
  • Federal opportunities -- Current border infrastructure contract in southeastern Texas near $200 million; a $40 billion federal border program is being pursued but not included in 2026 guidance.

Risks

  • Staci M. Woolsey noted that 2025 included non-recurring cash collections from contract retentions and disputed claims, which will not recur in 2026 and could affect comparability of operating cash flow.
  • Kyle T. Larkin said, "We still have to win and actually bid and build some of the work that we are going to need this year to hit our revenue numbers. So it is always a risk in the first half of the year of actually capturing that work and getting started on that work."
  • Kyle T. Larkin referenced that "weather can always be an opportunity or it can be a hindrance," highlighting execution and external factor risks for EBITDA margin achievement.
  • Border infrastructure program pursuit introduces a changing risk profile as contract sizes increase, requiring additional discipline and carrying higher potential execution complexity, as described in detail by Kyle T. Larkin.

Summary

Granite Construction (GVA 0.59%) reported record levels of revenue, profitability, and project backlog for the year, underlining the impact of acquisitions and disciplined market selection. Management flagged that the Materials segment's gross profit margin continued to improve as a result of targeted CapEx and successful integration of acquisitions, notably Warren Paving. The portfolio shift toward best value contracts and a balanced mix with bid-build projects has sharpened margin visibility and provided longer-term revenue stability. Guidance anticipates both revenue and margin expansion in 2026, with full-year benefit from all 2025 acquisitions and a strong pipeline of additional M&A. The company's cash generation and liquidity support substantial investment capacity, while management maintains a disciplined approach on leverage, targeting net debt at 2.5 times unless a compelling larger acquisition opportunity arises.

  • CEO Larkin said, "I believe our private markets, such as rail and commercial site development, remain robust and represent attractive incremental growth avenues for our Construction segment."
  • The home market strategy and focus on high-quality CAP was reinforced by management's disclosure that "The $7,000,000,000 is a really high-quality CAP. The margin profile within our CAP continues to improve, and that is going to also get to those 2027 targets."
  • Recent acquisitions have contributed to a thirty-four percent increase in aggregate reserves and doubled reserves over five years, positioning the company for sustained expansion in its Materials business.
  • Direct federal work, including a new southeastern Texas border infrastructure contract and opportunities under the evolving $40 billion program, may provide further upside if awarded, though currently excluded from revenue guidance.
  • Tight cost controls in the Materials segment allowed cost levels to remain flat in legacy business for two consecutive years, supporting net pricing gains and favorable cash gross profit trends.

Industry glossary

  • CAP (Committed and Awarded Projects): The total value of contracted and awarded, but not yet completed, construction and materials projects, used as a leading indicator for future revenue.
  • Best value (Construction Procurement): A project delivery method favoring overall value and contractor qualifications rather than lowest price; often results in higher margin, lower risk projects with longer revenue cycles.
  • Bid-build contract: Traditional procurement method in which design and construction are separate contracts, typically awarded to the lowest bidder; generally associated with shorter durations and different margin profiles versus best value work.
