PayPal (PYPL) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 28, 2025 at 10:01AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, Oct. 28, 2025 at 8 a.m. ET

Call participants

President and Chief Executive Officer — Alex Chriss

Chief Financial Officer and Executive Vice President, Finance and Administration — Jamie S. Miller

Senior Vice President, Investor Relations — Steven Eric Winoker

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

Transaction Margin Dollar Growth (Excluding Interest) -- On track for 6%-7% transaction margin dollar growth, excluding interest, for the full year 2025.

Revenue Growth Acceleration -- Revenue growth accelerated over the past two quarters, driven by a focus on profitable growth strategies.

Branded Experiences TPV Growth -- Rose 8% on a currency-neutral basis; includes PayPal Buy Now, Pay Later, Venmo, and debit program volumes.

US Branded Experiences TPV -- Achieved 10% branded experiences volume growth in the U.S, more than doubling the rate a year ago, propelled by omnichannel adoption and improved online checkout trends.

Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) Volume -- Sustained volume growth of more than 20% each quarter, led by U.S. performance; BNPL is projected to approach $40 billion in total payment volume (TPV) for 2025.

Venmo Revenue and User Metrics -- Venmo revenue is on track to reach $1.7 billion in 2025, excluding interest income, up more than 20% and representing a 10-point acceleration from two years prior; Monthly active accounts advanced 7% to nearly 66 million.

Venmo Debit and Pay with Venmo Adoption -- Venmo debit card monthly actives surged 43%, and Pay with Venmo monthly active accounts climbed 24%.

PSP Volume Growth -- Accelerated to 6%, up from 2% in the first half, indicating renewed momentum in enterprise and SMB processing.

Non-GAAP EPS -- Non-GAAP EPS rose 12%, attributed to transaction margin performance, strong credit, share repurchases, and favorable tax impacts.

Monthly Active Account and Engagement -- Active accounts grew 2% year over year to 227 million, with transactions per active account (ex-PSP) up 5%, evidencing increased user engagement.

Debit Card and Tap to Pay -- Spend increased 65% year over year, outpacing volume growth in branded experiences and reflecting rapid uptake of newer payment methods.

Total Payment Volume (TPV) -- Grew 8% at spot and 7% currency-neutral to over $458 billion.

Transaction Revenue and Value-Added Services -- Transaction revenue increased 6% to $7.5 billion; other value-added services revenue advanced 15% to $895 million, bolstered by credit performance.

Transaction Take Rate -- Decreased three basis points to 1.64%, mainly due to changes in product and merchant mix and reduced FX hedge impact.

Loan Receivables -- Net loan receivables stood at $6.4 billion, down 8% sequentially, following externalization of a portion of U.S. pay later receivables.

Shareholder Returns -- Share repurchases totaled $1.5 billion and $5.7 billion in share repurchases over the past four quarters; initiated a dividend with a 10% payout ratio relative to net income, complementing the buyback strategy.

Cash and Debt Levels -- Ended the quarter with $14.4 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and investments, and $11.4 billion in debt.

2025 Full-Year Guidance Increase -- Raised transaction margin dollar guidance by $100 million at the low end to $15.45-$15.55 billion (5%-6% growth) for 2025; non-GAAP EPS guidance increased to $5.35-$5.39 (15%-16% growth) for the full year; projected $6-$7 billion in adjusted free cash flow for the year and $6 billion in share buybacks.

Q4 Outlook -- Expects TM dollar growth at about 3.5% (midpoint) for Q4; non-GAAP EPS in the range of $1.27 to $1.31 (up 7%-10%) for Q4; currency-neutral revenue growth anticipated in the mid-single digits for Q4.

Capital Allocation -- Targets returning 70%-80% of free cash flow to shareholders, primarily via buybacks, with dividend serving as a supplementary return mechanism.

Product/Market Expansion -- Expanded BNPL to Canada and extended installment options in Italy and Spain; launched PayPal World pilot and established partnerships with OpenAI, Google, and Perplexity in agentic commerce.

Summary

PayPal (PYPL 1.61%) delivered meaningful acceleration in transaction margin dollar growth and revenue, highlighting sustained momentum across branded experiences, BNPL, Venmo, and PSP. Management articulated a clear strategy focused on omnichannel expansion, upstream BNPL presentment, and broadening high-value user engagement, supported by recent rollouts and key industry partnerships. The company initiated its first dividend, increased capital returns through share buybacks, and raised full-year guidance for both transaction margin dollars and non-GAAP EPS.

Jamie S. Miller stated, "Transaction margin dollars, excluding interest, grew 7%" with contribution from branded checkout, Venmo, PSP, and credit, partially offset by higher transaction losses due to a temporary service disruption in Germany.

Management indicated that ongoing investments in agentic commerce, branding, and product attachment initiatives in Q4 and 2026 "are likely to be a near-term headwind to how fast transaction margin dollars or earnings grow next year."

Expansion of the redesigned pay sheet has reached close to 25% of global checkouts, with "conversion rates increase 2% to 5%," according to Alex Chriss, demonstrating early success but also the complexity of legacy integrations.

BNPL originations remain global, with "less than 30% of originations in the U.S," according to Alex Chriss, BNPL monthly active accounts up 21%, underscoring the international scale and engagement uplift from BNPL adoption.

The company cited "basket sizes or average order value has decreased" according to Jamie S. Miller in both the U.S. and Europe, particularly among discretionary spend, and noted this trend is persisting into October, but did not alter long-term strategic initiatives as a result.

Industry glossary

Branded Experiences TPV: Total payment volume transacted through PayPal's proprietary branded solutions, including online, in-store, Venmo, and debit programs, used as a transformation progress metric.

PSP: PayPal's "Payment Service Provider" business encompassing enterprise and SMB processing, payouts, invoicing, and POS solutions.

TM (Transaction Margin): Profit on payment transactions after deducting direct costs (such as transaction losses and processing costs), used as a core profitability metric for PayPal.

ARPA (Average Revenue per Account): Average revenue generated per active account within a specific product or platform on an annualized basis.

Agentic Commerce: Commerce through AI-powered agents or large language models (LLMs), enabling consumers and merchants to transact in conversational, automated shopping interfaces.

Pay Sheet: The checkout interface shown to consumers during payment, whose optimization is intended to increase conversion rates.
