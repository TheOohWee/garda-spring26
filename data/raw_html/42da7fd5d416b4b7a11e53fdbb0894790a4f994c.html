Earnings Call Transcript

PotlatchDeltic (PCH) Q1 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 10:21AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Tuesday, April 29, 2025 at 12 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Eric Cremers
  • Vice President, Chief Financial Officer, and Treasurer — Wayne Wasechek

Takeaways

  • Total adjusted EBITDDA -- $63 million, up $10 million sequentially from the fourth quarter, driven by improvements in all business units.
  • Timberland segment adjusted EBITDDA -- $42 million, increasing from $34 million in the previous quarter, attributed to higher harvest volumes and increased sawlog prices in Idaho.
  • Idaho sawlog prices -- Increased by 9% per ton quarter over quarter, with higher cedar prices and favorable product mix contributing.
  • Southern harvest volumes -- 1.6 million tons harvested, exceeding both guidance and previous quarter by almost 170,000 tons due to favorable weather and increased stumpage demand.
  • Southern sawlog prices -- Declined by just over 2.5% sequentially, mainly from a higher mix of smaller diameter logs and lower hardwood volume.
  • Wood products adjusted EBITDDA -- Rose to $12 million from $9 million quarter over quarter, led by higher average lumber prices.
  • Average lumber price realization -- Increased to $454 per thousand board feet, up $9 or 2% sequentially, versus a 6% increase in the industry composite.
  • Lumber shipments -- 290 million board feet shipped, 7 million higher than the previous quarter and 10 million above Q1 guidance, mainly from reaching targeted capacity at Waldo, Arkansas.
  • Waldo sawmill ramp-up -- Ramp completed three months ahead of schedule, achieving production metrics, lowering cash processing costs by 30%, and expected to add $25 million in incremental EBITDDA annually in a mid-cycle environment.
  • Real estate segment adjusted EBITDDA -- $23 million, rising from $19 million quarter over quarter, with rural land sales exceeding 7,000 acres at an average $3,300 per acre.
  • Chenal Valley residential lots -- 11 lots sold at an average price of $113,000 per lot, consistent with inventory expectations.
  • Solar option acreage -- Expanded by 3,000 acres since year-end to a total of 38,000 acres under contract, with an estimated net present value of $475 million.
  • Lithium development -- Exclusive brine exploration and production rights granted on about 900 acres in Lafayette County, Arkansas, with an initial five-year term.
  • Share repurchases -- $8 million spent year-to-date, buying back 188,000 shares at an average price of $42 per share, with $82 million remaining under the $200 million authorization.
  • Liquidity position -- $447 million as of quarter-end, including $147 million in cash and a fully undrawn revolver.
  • Capital expenditures -- $23 million in the quarter; full-year guidance remains $60 million to $65 million, excluding project closeouts and acquisitions.
  • Second quarter guidance -- Company plans to ship 300 million to 310 million board feet of lumber; average lumber prices so far in Q2 are $475 per thousand board feet, about 5% above Q1 average.
  • Full-year rural land sales guidance -- 26,000 acres projected, with 8,000 acres expected in the second quarter.
  • Q2 total adjusted EBITDDA outlook -- Expected to be lower versus Q1, reflecting seasonally lower timberland harvests and higher forest management costs.

Summary

Management confirmed notable sequential improvement in adjusted EBITDDA across all business units, highlighting operational execution and the completion of the Waldo sawmill expansion ahead of schedule. Rural real estate sales delivered significant premiums per acre, and the company continued expanding its solar and lithium option acreage, signaling new long-term monetization paths. Capital allocation favored share repurchases as the stock is priced at what management describes as "a significant discount to our estimated net asset value." Outlook for the second quarter anticipates a modest decline in overall adjusted EBITDDA, driven mainly by seasonality in timber harvests and increased forest management costs.

  • Cremers stated, "I think our year-to-date volumes are running something like 4% above the prior year," in the home center channel for repair and remodel.
  • Vice President, Chief Financial Officer, and Treasurer Eric Cremers said the company maintains a liquidity reserve, with "$100 million of cash on the balance sheet as a sort of a safety net."
  • Wasechek indicated rural land demand remains strong in both small and larger tract transactions, with major buyers seeking conservation and recreational assets, and continued interest in M&A despite limited market supply.
  • Management cited regulatory bottlenecks in utility interconnection studies as causing delays for solar project monetization, with earliest conversions to royalty streams unlikely before next year.
  • The impact of Section 232 tariffs and changes in Canadian lumber duty rates could materially alter market supply dynamics and timberland valuations, but ultimate effects depend on the magnitude and duration of policy changes.

Industry glossary

  • SPF (Spruce-Pine-Fir): A classification of softwood lumber species primarily produced in Canada and the northern U.S., commonly used in construction framing.
  • Chenal Valley: PotlatchDeltic's master-planned residential and commercial real estate development located in Little Rock, Arkansas.
  • Stumpage: The price paid, usually per ton or board foot, for the right to harvest timber from landowner property.
  • MISO: The Midcontinent Independent System Operator, managing electric grid access and transmission across 15 U.S. states and one Canadian province.

This incremental volume was particularly advantageous in Idaho as we benefited from an increase in sawlog prices due to our index saw law agreements coupled with higher cedar prices driven by strong regional demand. In our wood products business, lumber markets were dominated by tariff discussions throughout most of the first quarter. The Random Lengths' Western SPF composite price rose by $60 during the quarter in anticipation of Canadian tariffs. As the tariff deadlines loomed, buyers refrained from building inventory to hedge their positions, preferring instead to continue to purchase for near term needs. With Canadian lumber tariffs currently on hold, it remains to be seen how much of this run-up of SPF prices in Q1 will unwind.

Conversely, southern yellow pine markets did not experience near the pricing benefit as SPF. Nonetheless, southern pine lumber markets were more active and prices remained relatively firm during the quarter. Lumber markets continue to face relatively tepid demand from end markets. Additionally, pending regulatory actions related to Canadian duties and potential tariffs have provided support to pricing thus far this year and that should continue as we move to the second half of the year. Canadian producers who supply approximately 25% of US demand were recently spared from reciprocal tariffs.

However, there are already well established softwood lumber duties on imported Canadian lumber, which are adjusted annually. Preliminary Canadian softwood lumber duty rates that will take effect later this year were announced and they are higher than current levels. The preliminary all others rate is set to increase from 14% to over 34%, more than double the current rates once finalized. Furthermore, on March 1st, the Secretary of Commerce initiated a Section 232 investigation to determine the effects of imports of lumber and derivative products on national security. Commerce will evaluate the extent to which US production can meet domestic demand and the feasibility of increasing domestic timber and lumber capacity.

The lease anticipates an initial five year term from planning, engineering and construction before potential production begins. Additionally, we are actively engaged in discussions on executing another large mineral rights lease in Southwestern Arkansas.

At this stage, we are currently conducting feasibility studies with reputable project developers that focus on potential projects in our southern timberlands. Due to the complexity and care required to develop a high quality carbon project, we would target to bring a meaningful project to market sometime in the next 18 to 24 months.

Turning our attention to the US housing market.

Overall macroeconomic conditions continue to constrain consumer confidence and challenge affordability leading to low buyer urgency in both new and existing home sale markets. While large US homebuilders have pointed to a slower start to the spring selling season, annualized US housing starts are stable, averaging nearly 1.4 million units. Single family home building starts remained resilient near the 1 million unit level as the larger public homebuilders continue to offer rate buydown incentives to drive home sales. The multifamily home building segment remains challenging due to the restrictive construction financing and an oversupply of units, which continue to be digested in the market.

For existing homes, inventory has risen but sales remain on pace with last year's low level as interest rates continue to be elevated and existing homeowners wanting to move are continuing to choose to stay in their current homes due to the lock-in effect of their low mortgage rates. Despite the current state of the housing market, the key drivers of inherent housing demand remain positive. These longer term structural tailwinds include the massive undersupply of homes, a substantial demographic shift as millennials transition to homeownership and strong household formations.
