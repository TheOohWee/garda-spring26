CleanSpark (CLSK) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 25, 2025 at 5:54PM EST

Date

Tuesday, Nov. 25, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Matt Shultz
  • President and Chief Financial Officer — Gary Vecchiarelli
  • Chief Strategy Officer — Harry Sudock

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $763.7 million for the fiscal year, more than doubling year-over-year, driven by increased Exahash and Bitcoin prices.
  • Bitcoin production -- Nearly 8,000 Bitcoin mined during the fiscal year, with end-of-period treasury holdings exceeding 13,000, generated solely from owned hashing power.
  • Gross margin -- 55% for the year, 56.5% in the fourth quarter, representing a slight 1% decrease year-over-year, attributed to the post-halving environment.
  • Non-GAAP adjusted EBITDA -- Over $800 million for the fiscal year, with normalized non-GAAP adjusted EBITDA from operations (excluding Bitcoin fair value gains) at approximately $305 million (40% margin).
  • Net income -- Approximately $365 million for the full year, supported by high operating margins and appreciation of Bitcoin holdings.
  • Quarterly revenue growth -- $25 million (13%) increase in Q4 versus Q3, with margins rising two points.
  • Hash rate -- Achieved and maintained 50 exahash per second in operations since June.
  • Digital asset management (DAM) program -- Generated $9.3 million in Q4 option premiums and blended annualized yields of 12% on covered call strategies and 8% on put writing.
  • Effective cash per Bitcoin -- Q4 average: spot sales price of $111,721; including option premiums, nearly $116,000 realized per Bitcoin.
  • S21X XP immersion deployment -- 19,000 new units with 13.5 joules per terahash scheduled for deployment, completion expected by calendar year 2026.
  • AI data center strategy & assets -- 285 MW Texas site secured for exclusive AI factory (with 271 acres and ERCOT-approved 285 MW power agreements), plus 250 MW Sandersville, Georgia site positioned for large-scale AI tenant hosting.
  • Power portfolio -- More than 1 gigawatt contracted and operational across data centers in the U.S; additional 300 MW contracted in Texas to start energizing in 2027, with a multi-gigawatt development pipeline.
  • Largest financing in company history -- $1.15 billion 0% convertible note issued, with a 27.5% conversion premium and 6.25-year term.
  • Share buyback -- $460 million stock repurchase, reducing outstanding shares by 10.9%.
  • Bitcoin-backed credit -- $400 million total credit facility capacity available, with outstanding lines recently paid down using proceeds from the convertible note.
  • AI-focused partnerships -- Memorandum of understanding signed with Submer to provide modular, liquid-cooled and prefabricated data center solutions for accelerated AI campus deployment.

Summary

CleanSpark (CLSK 1.76%) highlighted record financial results, significant expansion of operational scale, and a strategic evolution toward AI data center infrastructure. Management confirmed that over 1 gigawatt of power is now live under contract, while new AI-ready sites in Texas and Georgia are positioned for near-term commercialization. The company emphasized a blend of Bitcoin mining, energy infrastructure, and high-performance computing as the foundation for future revenue diversification. A $1.15 billion convertible note provided funding for both a major share buyback and investments in new facilities, while the DAM desk delivered material premium income and uplifted cash flows from Bitcoin treasury activities.

  • Chief Executive Officer Shultz stated, "achieved escape velocity. Reaching 50 exahash per second in operational hash rate with 100% U.S.-based infrastructure."
  • Vecchiarelli stressed that normalized adjusted EBITDA—excluding gains on Bitcoin’s fair value—was approximately $305 million, clarifying the sustainability of operating profitability.
  • Recent tenant inquiries for AI infrastructure, notably at the Sandersville and Sealy sites, may indicate that demand is materially exceeding prior market expectations, with intended lease execution timelines described as "much quicker than a year."
  • Digital Asset Management strategies generated $9.3 million of total premiums in Q4, with "over $5 million in cash premiums for the month of October alone," highlighting rapid post-year-end expansion.
  • S21X XP miners scheduled for full deployment by calendar 2026 further reinforce CleanSpark’s claim to best-in-class efficiency.

Industry glossary

  • Exahash: A measure of computational power equal to one quintillion (1018) hashes per second, frequently used to quantify aggregate Bitcoin mining capacity.
  • Hash rate: The total computational speed at which Bitcoin mining hardware operates, critical for determining mining output and network competitiveness.
  • ERCOT: Electric Reliability Council of Texas, the independent system operator managing electricity flow and market operations for most of Texas.
  • MEP: Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing—core components required for constructing and operating data centers, particularly relevant to AI and HPC deployments.
  • Covered call: An options trading strategy involving the writing of call options against held Bitcoin for additional income, as employed in CleanSpark’s DAM.
  • Immersion cooling: Technology that submerges mining hardware in a non-conductive liquid to improve efficiency and thermal management.
  • AI factory: Purpose-built facilities for hosting large-scale artificial intelligence computing workloads, integrating dedicated power, advanced cooling, and low-latency connectivity.
  • Offtake: Agreement to purchase power or host compute workloads, referenced in the context of CleanSpark’s tenant acquisition strategy for data center assets.
