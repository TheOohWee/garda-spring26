Applied Digital APLD Q4 2024 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 22, 2026 at 12:02PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, October 9, 2024 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Wes Cummins
  • Chief Financial Officer — David Rench

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $43.7 million, representing a substantial increase driven by expanded data center hosting capacity and new Cloud Services revenue.
  • Data Center Hosting Segment Revenue -- $26.9 million, emphasizing the core contribution from existing hosting operations.
  • Cloud Services Segment Revenue -- $16.8 million, demonstrating the impact of recent ramp-up in high-performance computing (HPC) and AI workloads.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $4.8 million, reflecting positive operating leverage inclusive of one-time items.
  • Net Loss -- $64.8 million, or $0.52 per share, attributed to transformer-related outages, increased depreciation, and significant SG&A tied to start-up and professional service costs.
  • Adjusted Net Loss (Non-GAAP) -- $45.3 million, or $0.36 per share, which adjusts for atypical items from the period.
  • Cost of Revenue -- $46.3 million, with primary drivers being higher energy use, depreciation, and personnel as new facilities came online.
  • Selling and General Administrative Expenses -- $31.3 million, up sharply due to cloud business ramp-up and associated start-up costs.
  • Ending Cash, Cash Equivalents, and Restricted Cash -- $31.7 million, with $125.4 million in debt on the balance sheet.
  • Post Quarter-End Funding -- Over $150 million in additional financing secured after the reported quarter, including Garden City contingency settlement.
  • Jamestown Facility Performance -- 106-megawatt site maintained full capacity and uninterrupted uptime for a seventh consecutive quarter.
  • Ellendale Power Outage Remediation -- Transformer failures at the 180-megawatt Ellendale facility were resolved with equipment upgrades by quarter-end.
  • Total Data Center Hosting Capacity -- 286 megawatts contracted post-remediation across two North Dakota locations.
  • Cloud Clusters Online -- Four at period end, ramping to six in the subsequent quarter; these six clusters running at an annualized $100 million–$110 million revenue rate.
  • HPC Campus Development -- 400 megawatts under active build-out in North Dakota, with Ellendale's 100-megawatt flagship progressing toward late 2024 or early 2025 completion.
  • Letter of Intent with Hyperscaler -- Executed for 400 megawatts at Ellendale campus, with due diligence reportedly complete and lease in finalization phase.
  • Planned Expansion -- Three new campuses totaling 1.4 gigawatts in early-stage marketing, all with anticipated power availability in 2026.
  • Asset-Level Financing Update -- Selected a bank for Ellendale project financing, expecting 80%-90% loan-to-cost structures upon lease execution.

RISKS

  • Wes Cummins stated, "the company has faced a multi-month outage at our Ellendale hosting facility and the financial burden of funding $1 billion data center while funding our cloud business," highlighting operational disruptions and significant financing strain.
  • David Rench reported "several onetime items significantly impacted our financial performance and comparability to prior quarters," specifically noting unplanned professional service costs and power outage losses.
  • Accelerated depreciation on GPU hardware, as discussed by management, "driven largely by the accelerated depreciation schedule," materially worsened bottom-line results relative to adjusted EBITDA.

SUMMARY

Applied Digital (APLD +2.62%) reported surging revenue, largely from the ongoing ramp of Cloud Services and steady data center hosting contributions, yet posted a significant net loss attributed to transformer-driven outages and high start-up expenses. The company confirmed that critical remediation at Ellendale is complete, restoring contracted capacity and enabling pursuit of major capex financing tied to a pending hyperscaler lease now 90%-plus complete. Executives outlined a sharp focus on converting pipeline demand for both data center and cloud businesses into growth, conditional on advancing asset-level financing structures to mitigate further balance sheet strain.

  • Management referenced a $200 million investment already made in Ellendale's 100-megawatt facility, with total project costs expected to exceed $1 billion and fit-out scheduled for late 2024 or early 2025 depending on customer go-live decisions.
  • Wes Cummins noted, "that business is roughly $110 million revenue run rate on an annual basis." for six AI cloud clusters, indicating continued demand but stressing further expansion awaits completion of more favorable financing.
  • Asset-level financing efforts are advancing for both data center and GPU infrastructure, with management citing engagement with multiple funding parties and the need to align depreciation terms with industry norms.
  • Expansion efforts now encompass 1.4 gigawatts of early-stage pipeline across three new campuses, each focused on hyperscaler customers and designed around rapid power availability and low-latency connectivity.
  • Future sequential growth in revenue is anticipated, with leadership asserting, "Q4 marked the bottom for our revenues and anticipate sequential improvements in the top-line as we enter the first quarter."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • HPC (High-Performance Computing): Advanced computing environments designed to aggregate massive computing power for complex scientific, AI, or analytics workloads, typically requiring specialized data centers.
  • Hyperscaler: A company, usually a global, cloud-scale technology provider, requiring vast computing resources and custom-built data centers under long-term lease agreements.
  • Cluster: In this context, a group of interconnected servers and GPU hardware, managed as a single entity to deliver scalable AI or HPC computing power within a facility.
  • Asset-Level Financing: Project-specific funding structures that align debt amortization and depreciation terms to the useful life and cash flows of discrete data center or hardware investments, minimizing direct impact on parent company balance sheets.
  • GPU (Graphics Processing Unit): Processor optimized for parallel computations, essential for AI, machine learning, and high-performance data analytics workloads.
  • Term Sheet: Nonbinding agreement outlining the key terms and conditions of potential financing, used here for project-level loans on campus developments.
  • SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle): Bankruptcy-remote legal entity created to isolate assets and revenue streams for securing dedicated project financing and customer contracts.
