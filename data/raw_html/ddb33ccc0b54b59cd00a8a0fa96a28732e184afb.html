Goosehead (GSHD) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Jul 23, 2025 at 8:44PM EST

DATE

  • Wednesday, July 23, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. EDT

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Mark Miller
  • Chief Financial Officer — Mark Jones Jr.
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Dan Farrell

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue: Total revenue was $94 million in Q2 2025, up 20% year over year.
  • Core Revenue: Core revenue was $86.8 million in Q2 2025, increasing 18% year-over-year (non-GAAP), driven by organic business expansion.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Adjusted EBITDA reached $29.2 million in Q2 2025, yielding a 31% adjusted EBITDA margin.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin (Ex-Contingent): 28%, clarifying underlying profitability apart from contingent commissions.
  • Total Written Premiums: Total written premiums were $1.2 billion in Q2 2025, up 18% year-over-year (non-GAAP), including franchise premiums of $959 million (+21%) and corporate premiums of $217 million (+6%).
  • Franchise New Business Premiums: Franchise new business premiums increased 13% in Q2 2025 compared to Q2 2024, attributable to ongoing network consolidation.
  • Policies in Force: Policies in force totaled 1.8 million as of Q2 2025. This represents a 13% increase compared to the previous year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin Dynamics: Sequential margin pressure is anticipated in Q3 2025 with improvement expected in Q4 2025, influenced by new producer onboarding and tech investment; full-year margin may compress slightly.
  • Commission Rate Outlook: Management expects the average commission rate to improve as business mix returns from state-run to admitted carriers, supported by a recent $1.5 million commission benefit expected in the second half of 2025.
  • Client Retention: Client retention stood at 84% in Q2 2025, with management reporting "consistent basis point rises" in client retention and anticipating further improvement in the second half of the year.
  • Contingent Commissions: Contingent commissions totaled $4.5 million in Q2 2025, a 103% increase year-over-year, with guidance for full-year 2025 contingent commissions at 40-65 basis points of total written premium.
  • Cash Flow from Operations: Cash flow from operations was $28.9 million in Q2 2025, up 53%, reinforcing financial flexibility.
  • Repurchase Program: $99.5 million remained available for share repurchases as of Q2 2025 following recent activity.
  • Debt Reduction: Term loan repriced to SOFR plus 300 basis points (from 350), lowering the annual interest burden by approximately $1.5 million.
  • Franchise Network Consolidation: 30 operating franchises consolidated into other agencies; purchasing agency productivity increased 21% over the past 12 months.
  • Corporate Sales Agents: 479 at quarter end, up 53%; comprising 379 traditional corporate agents and 100 enterprise sales agents as of Q2 2025.
  • Enterprise Sales and Partnerships: Unit new business production increased 88% year-over-year in Q2 2025 and 41% sequentially over Q1 2025; explicit mention of new strategic partnerships with Baird and Warner and Faye Servicing.
  • Cost Recovery Revenue: Cost recovery revenue was $1.4 million in Q2 2025, compared to $1.9 million in the year-ago period. This revenue is largely from initial franchise fees.
  • Technology and AI Deployment: Service costs are expected to decrease in the second half of 2025 due to AI-driven process optimization; investment in a direct-to-consumer marketplace under way.
  • Full-Year 2025 Guidance: Total revenue is projected at $350 million-$385 million (organic growth of 11%-22%) for the full year 2025; Total written premiums are guided to $4.38 billion-$4.65 billion (organic growth of 15%-22%) for the full year 2025.
  • One-Time Expense: A $4.7 million non-cash impairment charge related to real estate footprint changes in three cities was recorded in Q2 2025.

SUMMARY

Goosehead Insurance (GSHD 4.17%) management reaffirmed full-year revenue guidance while revising premium guidance downward for the full year 2025, explicitly attributing this to a temporary lag as premium moderation outpaces client retention recovery. New business momentum remains robust across both franchise and corporate channels, with enterprise partnerships representing a fast-growing and increasingly strategic revenue source. A $4 million recovery from a large carrier materially improved renewal commissions in Q2 2025, and a resulting $1.5 million incremental commission benefit is expected in the second half of the year. Management emphasized significant investments in technology and AI are beginning to reduce service costs and will continue to shape future cost structure and scalability. Leading indicators, including positive trends in carrier appetite and admitted-market availability, are driving visible improvements in commission rate outlook, with management now anticipating further upside from carrier engagement and business mix normalization.

  • Mark Jones Jr. specified that none of the earnings benefit from new enterprise partnerships -- including Baird, Warner, and Faye Servicing -- is incorporated in the 2025 guidance, so future upside remains unmodeled at this point.
  • Management expects a sequential increase in the third quarter as hiring accelerates in larger agencies.
  • The top 200 franchise agencies achieved over 30% growth in new business and gross earnings in Q2 2025, underlining effective concentration of growth among leading operators.
  • Management acknowledged that margin (excluding contingents) may decline slightly for the full year 2025, citing deliberate growth investments, particularly in expansion markets and technology.
  • Improvements in the product environment -- with admitted carriers coming back into markets—were described as meaningfully better than in prior quarters, specifically compared to Q1 2025 and the prior year, supporting optimism on future margin and growth.
  • Cash and cash equivalents stood at $92.4 million as of Q2 2025.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Admitted Carrier: Licensed insurer regulated by state authorities, typically offering greater policyholder protections and often higher commission rates versus non-admitted or surplus lines carriers.
  • Contingent Commissions: Performance-based payments from carriers to agents, paid in addition to standard commissions, often tied to underwriting profitability and loss ratios.
  • ASP (Agency Staffing Program): Goosehead's in-house program to recruit and place agents within franchise agencies, aimed at accelerating scalable growth for owner-operators.
  • Enterprise Sales and Partnerships: Goosehead's business development channel focused on institutional alliances beyond traditional loan officer and realtor sources, targeting real estate firms, mortgage servicers, and other large-scale partners.
  • Policies in Force (PIF): Total number of active insurance policies serviced by the agency at the end of a reporting period.
