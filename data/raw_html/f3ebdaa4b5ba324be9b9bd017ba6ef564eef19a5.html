Viatris (VTRS) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 6, 2025 at 10:57AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Nov. 6, 2025, at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Scott Andrew Smith
  • President, Research & Development — Philippe Martin
  • President, Global Commercial — Corinne Le Goff
  • Chief Financial Officer — Doretta Mistras

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Risks

  • Japan Sales Decline — Net sales in Japan decreased approximately 9%, driven by government price regulation impacts, reimbursement changes affecting off-patent brands, and heightened competition in Australia.
  • North America Revenue Pressure — The North American segment saw a 12% net sales decrease, primarily due to the indoor impact and increased competition in specific generic products.
  • Potential Loss of Exclusivity — Management cited "potential loss of exclusivity for Amitiza in Japan" according to Doretta Mistras as a key factor that may influence 2026 revenues.
  • Seasonality Headwind — Management expects "total revenues are expected to be lower across all of our segments due to normal product seasonality," indicating fourth-quarter revenue will be below the third quarter.

Takeaways

  • Total Revenues -- $3.76 billion, down approximately 1%; operational revenue growth was 1% excluding indoor, in line with internal expectations.
  • Segment Growth -- Europe net sales increased approximately 1%; generics in Europe rose 5%, while emerging markets grew 7% due to established brand strength in Turkey, Mexico, and Emerging Asia.
  • Greater China Performance -- Net sales increased 9%, surpassing expectations, attributed to commercial diversity and increased demand for patient-driven brands.
  • Adjusted gross margin -- Adjusted gross margin was 56% for the quarter, matching guidance; indoor impact contributed to year-over-year margin pressure.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $658 million reported, or $728 million excluding transaction-related costs; consistent performance supports capital returns.
  • Shareholders' Returns -- Over $920 million returned year-to-date, including $500 million in share repurchases; full-year capital return expected to exceed $1 billion.
  • Guidance Update -- Raised and narrowed 2025 guidance for revenues, adjusted EBITDA, and adjusted EPS, citing foreign exchange benefit and year-to-date buybacks.
  • Cost Savings Initiative -- Enterprise-wide strategic review is underway; significant net cost savings are targeted, with details and quantification to be disclosed at a 2026 investor event.
  • R&D Pipeline Progress -- Fast-acting Meloxicam NDA expected submission by year-end; low-dose estrogen weekly patch now under FDA review, with potential U.S. launch post-approval in 2026.
  • Clinical Milestones -- Selatogrel and cenerimod Phase 3 programs enrolling on pace; cenerimod Phase 3 for lupus nephritis initiated, first patient enrollment anticipated by year-end.
  • M&A and Portfolio Expansion -- Acquisition of Aculus Pharma in Japan adds two innovative CNS assets (ptolosan and SPIDIA), leveraging local infrastructure and enhancing the innovative brands segment.
  • Indoor Facility Update -- Initial remediation at the indoor facility is substantially complete; FDA reinspection timing is pending, with production redundancies established at other sites and third parties.
  • Operational Expenses -- Flat year-over-year as elevated R&D spend on selatogrel and cenerimod is offset by 2025 SG&A cost savings.

Summary

Viatris (VTRS 1.03%) reported modest operational revenue growth, excluding indoor impacts, supported by solid commercial performance in Europe, emerging markets, and Greater China. Management announced a raised and narrowed 2025 financial guidance, attributing improvements to currency tailwinds and capital allocation actions. The company highlighted progress in advancing its late-stage pipeline, particularly with the upcoming fast-acting Meloxicam NDA and ongoing major Phase 3 trial enrollments. Strategic updates included substantial completion of indoor remediation and execution of an enterprise-wide review aiming for significant cost savings. Portfolio expansion continued through the acquisition of Aculus Pharma, marking an increased focus on innovative CNS assets in Japan.

  • CEO Smith said, "We anticipate being able to deliver meaningful net cost savings over a multiyear period, while also being able to reinvest a portion of the savings back into the business to fund future growth opportunities."
  • Management does not expect the previously reported $100 million in indoor-related penalties and supply disruptions to recur next year.
  • Operating expenses remain stable, according to management, as "increased R&D spending driven by accelerated enrollment in our selatogrel and cenerimod clinical trial programs, which was offset by the continued benefit in SG&A from our 2025 cost savings initiatives."
  • FDA reinspection of the indoor facility remains unscheduled, but Viatris has built "operational redundancies by requalifying other sites and adding third-party vendors for products originally manufactured at Indoor," according to Scott Andrew Smith.

Industry glossary

  • Indoor impact: The operational and financial effect stemming from issues at Viatris's specific production facility referred to as "indoor," impacting sales and margins in several regions.
  • SGLT1/SGLT2 inhibitor: A compound that blocks sodium-glucose co-transporters 1 and 2, used to manage diabetes and cardiovascular conditions; referenced in context of sotagliflozin studies.
  • Paragraph IV challenge: In pharmaceuticals, a legal process initiated by a generic manufacturer to challenge the patent of a branded drug, permitting earlier entry to market if successful.
  • 505(b)(2) pathway: A regulatory approval route in the U.S. for drugs based in part on existing clinical data, used for Viatris's fast-acting Meloxicam submission.
  • JNDAs: Japanese New Drug Applications, required by Japan's PMDA for local pharmaceutical product approval.
  • IP exclusivity: The period during which a company holds exclusive intellectual property rights to a product, as referenced for fast-acting Meloxicam.
