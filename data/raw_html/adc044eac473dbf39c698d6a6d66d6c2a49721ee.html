GBTG Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 5, 2025 at 9:51AM EST

Call participants

Chief Executive Officer — Paul Abbott

Takeaways

Revenue-- Revenue reached $631 million for Q2 2025, up 1% year over year, with constant currency revenue flat year over year.

Total transaction value (TTV)-- Total transaction value reached $7.9 billion for Q2 2025, up 3% workday-adjusted, driven by transaction growth, higher average ticket prices, hotel room rates, and favorable FX impact.

Adjusted operating expenses-- Adjusted operating expenses were flat year over year in Q2 2025 and down 2% on a constant currency basis year over year; cost of revenue went down 2%, and general and administrative expense down 14%.

Free cash flow-- $27 million of free cash flow was generated in Q2 2025. Free cash flow declined year over year due to prior Agensia working capital benefits and increased investment.

Net debt reduction-- Net debt declined $70 million year over year as of Q2 2025; leverage ratio of net debt to last twelve months adjusted EBITDA down to 1.6x as of June 30, 2025 (from 2x one year ago and 3.5x two years ago).

Customer retention rate-- 95% customer retention rate over the last twelve months.

New wins value-- $3.2 billion total new wins value over the last twelve months, including $2.2 billion from SME customers.

Net new wins impact-- Two percentage points of transaction growth added in May and June 2025.

Transaction growth May/June-- Overall up 2% workday-adjusted in May and June 2025 (hotel transactions up 4% in May and June 2025, global multinational customers' transaction growth up 3% in May and June 2025, SME transaction growth up 2% in May and June 2025).

Regional performance-- Americas transaction growth reached 2% in May and June 2025 and EMEA transactions grew 3% in May and June 2025; APAC decelerated due to tariffs and mining sector in Australia.

Revenue yield-- 8%, down 10 basis points year over year, reflecting the mix shift to digital transactions.

Traveler care cost per transaction-- Improved 5% year over year in Q2 2025.

Full-year 2025 revenue guidance-- Revenue growth of 2%-4% for full-year 2025 guidance, midpoint $2.488 billion for full-year 2025 revenue guidance, raised from a prior range of -2% to +2% for full-year 2025 revenue growth guidance and incorporating 4% revenue growth expected in H2 2025.

Full-year adjusted EBITDA guidance-- Adjusted EBITDA growth of 6% to 13% for full-year 2025, adjusted EBITDA guidance of $505 million to $540 million for full-year 2025, with a 21% adjusted EBITDA margin at midpoint for full-year 2025 guidance.

Full-year free cash flow guidance-- $140 million to $160 million full-year free cash flow guidance for 2025, midpoint $150 million free cash flow guidance for full year 2025.

Cost savings program-- Ongoing execution targeting $110 million in savings.

CWT acquisition progress-- US DOJ dismissed its challenge; transaction expected to close in Q3 at a $540 million value on a cash-free, debt-free basis, with approximately 50 million shares to be issued at a fixed price of $7.50 per share and the rest paid in cash; CWT shareholders will own approximately 10% of the combined company upon closing.

Synergy guidance for CWT-- $155 million net synergies expected over three years, with 30% of identified net synergies expected to be realized in the first twelve months post-close.

Share repurchase plan-- Initiating a 10b5-1 plan under the $300 million stock repurchase program authorization, commencing with trading window opening, to accelerate buybacks over the coming months.

Meetings & events outlook-- Expected 5% year-over-year increase in the number of meetings scheduled for H2 2025.

Summary

Global Business Travel Group(GBTG 0.18%) delivered results at or above guidance midpoints for Q2 2025, demonstrating focus on operating leverage and cost control as well as improved transaction volumes from May 2025 onward. The pending CWT acquisition is progressing, with US DOJ dismissed its challenge; transaction expected to close in Q3 and a defined timeline for closing and synergy capture. Management is using improved visibility to activate the $300 million buyback program swiftly and raise full-year 2025 guidance in revenue, adjusted EBITDA, and free cash flow, even as demand patterns in some sectors remain uneven.

Chief Executive Officer Abbott said, "Total new wins value reached $3.2 billion over the last twelve months, including $2.2 billion from SME customers," providing evidence of broad-based commercial momentum.

Management indicated that technology and financial services clients are showing strong spend outlooks, while consumer, manufacturing, energy, and mining remain comparatively soft.

Forward commentary from Abbott pointed to positive July transaction trends but emphasized that September typically constitutes 40% of Q3 volumes, underscoring Q3 seasonality risk.

Industry glossary

TTV (Total transaction value): Sum of all travel booking and transaction volumes processed through the platform, combining both volume and pricing components.

SME: Small and medium enterprise clients, a core customer segment for managed business travel solutions.

Revenue yield: Revenue as a percentage of TTV, used as a measure of monetization per unit of transaction volume.

Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, excluding extraordinary items and standardized for comparability.
