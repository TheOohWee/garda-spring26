Takeaways

  • Fee-related earnings per share -- $1.08, as directly reported for the quarter.
  • Total operating earnings per share -- $1.42 for the quarter, as directly stated.
  • Adjusted net income per share -- $1.12 in the quarter; excluding the carried interest repayment obligation, adjusted net income per share was $1.30.
  • Management fees -- $1.1 billion for the quarter, representing a 24% increase year over year (22% excluding catch-up fees in both periods).
  • Annual management fees -- $4.1 billion for 2025, with private equity, real assets, and credit each contributing roughly one-third; component figures noted do not sum to total, as explained in-call.
  • Fee-related earnings (FRE) -- $972 million in the quarter, an increase of 15% year over year, with a FRE margin of 68% for the quarter and just over 69% for full-year 2025.
  • Insurance segment operating earnings -- $268 million in the quarter, which would have been $320 million including marks not captured under cash accounting; supplemental economic impact of Global Atlantic insurance segment is $1.9 billion in 2025, up 15% year over year.
  • Strategic holdings operating earnings -- $44 million reported for the quarter; operating earnings for strategic holdings more than doubled year over year, with management reiterating a forward target of $350 million-plus for 2026.
  • Embedded gains -- $19 billion at Dec. 31, a record high, up 19% year over year and over 50% from two years ago.
  • Capital raised -- $28 billion for the quarter and $129 billion for full-year 2025, the highest annual fundraising in the company's history, nearly double the figure from two years prior.
  • Credit fundraising -- $68 billion raised in 2025, setting a firm record driven by asset-based finance and insurance channels.
  • K Series AUM growth -- K Series vehicles surpassed $35 billion in AUM (as of Jan. 1), compared to $18 billion a year ago, with $4.5 billion raised in Q4 and over $16 billion for 2025, nearly double the 2024 total.
  • Total fundraising progress -- Over $240 billion, or greater than 80% of the $300 billion-plus fundraising target for 2024–2026, has been raised to date.
  • Dividend increase -- Annual dividend to rise from $0.74 to $0.78 per share, effective with Q1 2026 earnings; this represents the seventh consecutive annual increase post-C Corp conversion.
  • Gross monetization activity -- $2 billion in 2025 (excluding the carried interest repayment obligation), with gross realized carried interest up about 30% year over year.
  • Deployment -- $32 billion invested in Q4 and $95 billion in 2025, up 13% year over year; credit deployment was $44 billion (up 14%), ABF deployment was $19 billion, and infrastructure deployment reached nearly $15 billion (record, with over half outside the U.S.).
  • Dry powder -- $118 billion available, as reported.
  • Recent acquisition -- Agreement to acquire Arctos for $1.4 billion in equity and cash (with up to $550 million additional long-term vesting equity tied to share price and performance), immediately incorporating approximately $15 billion in AUM and establishing "KKR Solutions" as a new investing vertical targeting $100 billion-plus in AUM over time.
  • Visibility on monetizations -- Over $900 million in signed or completed monetization-related revenue expected to be recognized in the first half of 2026, up from about $400 million at the same time last year.
  • Software exposure -- Software constitutes approximately 7% of total AUM (using a highly inclusive definition), with stated concentration levels well below industry and broad market indices; management explicitly stated risk exposure as "low single-digit percentage" regarding tariffs.

Summary

KKR (KKR 1.73%) delivered record fundraising, with capital raised for 2025 nearly double that of two years prior and progress surpassing 80% of the stated $300 billion-plus 2024–2026 fundraising target. Fee-driven revenues, including both management fees and fee-related earnings, exhibited double-digit growth, while embedded gains across asset management and strategic holdings reached an all-time high of $19 billion. The firm announced the acquisition of Arctos, the leading investor in professional sports franchises, immediately providing scale in the rapidly expanding GP solutions space and forming a new "KKR Solutions" vertical with long-term AUM ambitions exceeding $100 billion. Private wealth fundraising, particularly through the K Series suite, demonstrated rapid growth and diversification, with AUM nearly doubling year over year. Management reiterated confidence in surpassing 2026 profitability and fundraising targets, citing meaningful operating leverage, diversified revenue streams, and disciplined investment deployment despite market volatility and AI-related sector anxieties.

  • Management confirmed the insurance segment's economic contributions are expected to rise meaningfully in 2026 and beyond, due to accrued income that has not yet converted to cash earnings but is projected to do so over the next several years.
  • Explicitly citing portfolio composition, management stated, "software is about 7% of our AUM," and framed tariff risk as "low single-digit percentage" exposure, both characterized as low-concern areas following proactive portfolio adjustments.
  • KKR emphasized ability to be patient on monetizations, stating, "We are not forced sellers. If markets are constructive, we will monetize. If conditions are less favorable, we can afford to be patient," thus highlighting significant flexibility amid exit market headwinds.
  • Fee-related earnings and management fees have grown at a faster pace than operating expenses since 2022, whereas management observed peer firms have seen the reverse; this supports management's FRE performance expectations for 2026 and operating leverage commentary.
  • K Series private wealth products raised $4.5 billion in Q4 and $16 billion in full-year 2025, with January's capital raise tracking 20% above last year's pace, signaling enduring inflows despite market volatility.
  • Deployment activity reached record levels globally in private equity, real assets, and credit, with leadership citing continued "linear pacing" and expectation of further increases in investment activity in 2026.
  • Arctos brings majority of its $15 billion AUM from sports investments, with rapid current growth in GP solutions and the intention to enter secondaries; KKR sees strategic upside via new origination and distribution channels leveraging its broader firm infrastructure.
  • AI-driven disruption and opportunity were addressed by outlining firmwide initiatives, including over 400 engineers and two dedicated cross-functional teams sharing best practices; management directly cited incremental EBITDA improvement at underlying companies from AI-driven operational enhancements.

Industry glossary

  • FRE (Fee-related earnings): Profits derived from recurring fee revenue streams such as management and monitoring fees, after direct expenses, but before performance-based income and carried interest.
  • Embedded gains: Unrealized investment gains from marked-up asset values across the firm's portfolio, inclusive of both balance sheet holdings and carried interests.
  • Dry powder: Unallocated committed investor capital available for future investment deployment.
  • K Series: KKR's suite of private wealth-oriented evergreen investment vehicles, designed particularly for individual investors seeking diversified alternatives exposure.
  • ABF (Asset-based finance): Credit strategy focused on lending or investing secured by specific pools of assets (e.g., receivables, inventory), often targeting higher-yielding, non-cyclical exposures.
