Earnings Call Transcript

Synopsys (SNPS) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Dec 10, 2025 at 8:21PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, Dec. 10, 2025, at 5 p.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Sassine Ghazi
  • Chief Financial Officer — Shelagh Glaser
  • Head of Investor Relations — Tushar Jain

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $7.05 billion for the year, up approximately 15%, including $757 million of ANSYS revenue.
  • Q4 revenue -- $2.25 billion at the high end of guidance; ANSYS segment contributed $668 million.
  • Fiscal 2026 revenue guidance -- Projected at $9.56 billion-$9.66 billion, with ANSYS expected to deliver $2.9 billion at the midpoint.
  • Backlog -- $11.4 billion, up from $10.1 billion sequentially, attributed to bookings strength.
  • Geographic performance -- China revenue down 18% year-over-year; excluding ANSYS, China declined 22%.
  • Operating margins -- Fiscal non-GAAP operating margin at 37.3%; Q4 non-GAAP operating margin at 36.5%.
  • EPS performance -- Fiscal non-GAAP EPS $12.91; Q4 non-GAAP EPS $2.90, both above guidance due to reduced expenses.
  • Design automation segment -- Fiscal revenue $5.3 billion, up 26%; excluding ANSYS, up 8% with hardware achieving record results.
  • Design IP segment -- Fiscal revenue $1.75 billion, down 8% due to a challenging second half; adjusted operating margin reported at 24%.
  • Free cash flow -- $1.35 billion for the year, exceeding guidance mainly due to earlier collections.
  • Debt and liquidity -- Total debt $13.5 billion; cash and short-term investments $2.96 billion, including $600 million in divestiture proceeds.
  • Planned debt repayment -- $2.55 billion term loan to be prepaid in 2026, which is reflected as accretive to fiscal 2026 EPS guidance.
  • Workforce reductions -- 10% reduction initiated as part of synergy realization; restructuring largely complete in 2026.
  • 2026 non-GAAP operating margin guidance -- 40.5% at the midpoint, up approximately 320 basis points from 2025.
  • Normalized non-GAAP tax rate -- 18% to be applied through 2028, up by 2 percentage points due to geographic earnings mix and tax law changes.
  • Capital expenditures -- $300 million projected for 2026, $130 million higher than 2025, primarily for compute infrastructure.
  • ANSYS integration -- Integration “well underway” post-divestiture of Optical Solutions and PowerArtist businesses, with joint R&D already consolidated.
  • Strategic partnership -- Nvidia invested $2 billion; Synopsys expects associated term loan repayments and direct synergy acceleration.
  • Design IP outlook -- “Muted growth” projected in 2026; long-term mid-teens growth target reiterated based on healthy pipeline and prioritized investment.
  • EDA outlook -- Double-digit growth remains long-term target, but near-term outlook incorporates China challenges and headwinds outside AI infrastructure markets.

Risks

  • China revenue decreased 18% year-over-year, and excluding ANSYS, declined 22%, reflecting ongoing market restrictions and “cumulative impact of restrictions.”
  • “Muted growth” projected for the Design IP segment in 2026, attributed to ongoing headwinds from China, foundry customer transitions, and completion gaps in key IP titles.
  • Management acknowledged “some share shift happening inside China” as customers unable to buy from Synopsys seek alternatives, mainly local organic competitors.
  • Operating margin for the Design IP segment is expected to remain under pressure in 2026 due to R&D investment and short-term revenue headwinds.

Summary

Synopsys (SNPS 3.11%) reported record annual revenue and a significant increase in backlog, supported by the integration of ANSYS and strategic divestitures. Management guided for robust double-digit growth in the ANSYS segment, cost synergies from restructuring actions, and planned debt repayment after a $2 billion strategic investment from Nvidia. The company expects geographic performance differences, particularly with ongoing challenges in China and a transitional year for the Design IP segment, resulting in projected “muted growth,” while maintaining long-term growth targets. Synopsys also outlined margin expansion, substantial capital expenditure investments in compute infrastructure, normalization of tax rates, and a renewed product roadmap focused on AI-enabled engineering solutions.

  • Management stated joint solutions between Synopsys and ANSYS will launch in 2026, targeting “one plus one is greater than two” monetization opportunities for customers adopting combined offerings.
  • The strategic partnership with Nvidia includes a $2 billion equity investment and new ratable revenue structures in select ANSYS product lines.
  • Synopsys acknowledged that China remains a constrained market, explicitly derisked in 2026 guidance with no near-term improvement assumed for sales environment or regulatory impacts.
  • Back half–weighted recovery in Design IP was indicated, depending on new title availability and customer engagement milestones by mid-2026.
  • Management described industry-wide challenges capitalizing on AI-driven demand in EDA, emphasizing a forthcoming shift to joint physics/software solutions and agentic AI workflows as the next monetization frontier.

Industry glossary

  • EDA: Electronic Design Automation; software and hardware tools used to design and verify electronic systems such as integrated circuits.
  • Design IP: Pre-developed circuits or blocks (intellectual property) integrated into semiconductors to accelerate product development.
  • HAV: Hardware-Assisted Verification; using hardware to accelerate chip design verification.
  • Agentic AI: AI systems capable of autonomous workflow changes and creative problem solving beyond traditional automation.
  • NRE: Non-Recurring Engineering; one-time costs for the development of custom products, often combined with use fees or royalties in semiconductor IP transactions.
  • Ratable revenue: Revenue recognized evenly over a contract period, rather than at delivery, commonly used for licences and subscriptions.
  • 3D IC: Three-dimensional integrated circuits; semiconductors with multiple layers of active electronic components stacked vertically.
