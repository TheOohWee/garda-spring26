  • General and administrative expenses (Full year) -- $95.9 million, down from $165.4 million the prior year.
  • Research and development expenses (Q4) -- $61.1 million, declining from $83.3 million in Q4 2024, mainly due to $14.1 million lower compensation and $7.6 million less in external costs.
  • Research and development expenses (Full year) -- $285.2 million, down from $348.2 million for the prior year.
  • Non-GAAP total expenses (Q4 and full year) -- $71.8 million and $323.4 million, respectively.
  • Share repurchase activity -- Approximately 10 million shares repurchased for $91.9 million (average price: $9.09 per share, including commissions and excise tax); program is now suspended with no further repurchases planned.
  • Cash runway guidance -- Management reiterated guidance that cash on hand is expected to fund operations into 2028.
  • ARV-102 (LRRK2 degrader) clinical update -- Achieved greater than 50% LRRK2 degradation in CSF during Phase 1 trials; next data to be presented at the Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s Diseases Conference.
  • ARV-806 (KRAS G12D degrader) -- Dose escalation in Phase 1 trial completed ahead of plan, with first data disclosure expected in 2026.
  • ARV-393 (BCL6 degrader) -- Phase 1 trial observed responses in both B- and T-cell lymphomas at sub-efficacious exposures; combination trial with glofitamab to begin in the first half of 2026.
  • ARV-027 (polyQ AR degrader) -- Phase 1 trial initiated in healthy volunteers; preclinical data showed reduced muscle polyQ AR, improved function, and extended survival in SBMA mouse models.
  • Vepdegestrant (ER degrader) -- Pfizer and Arvinas are seeking a third-party commercial partner, with goal to finalize an agreement by the June 5 PDUFA date.
  • Strategic refocusing -- Company is concentrating resources on four Phase 1 programs: ARV-102, ARV-806, ARV-393, and ARV-027.
  • ARV-6723 (HPK1 degrader) -- Preclinical results indicated deep HPK1 degradation and tumor growth control; first-in-human studies planned for later in 2026.
  • Pan-KRAS PROTAC program -- Preclinical data supported broad KRAS degradation, including wild-type and mutant variants, with non-RAS isoform selectivity; additional data to be presented at forthcoming scientific conferences.

Summary

Arvinas (ARVN 3.13%) delivered a call signaling a transition toward clinical execution, highlighting multiple imminent data readouts across its first-in-class PROTAC portfolio. The company gave strategic clarity by reallocating capital to early-phase assets and paused its share repurchase program following substantial buybacks. Management stated that pivotal clinical events, including initial ARV-102 and ARV-806 readouts, could establish clear differentiation among a competitive field. The call underscored explicit operational progress, including the rapid advance of ARV-393 and the first human dosing for ARV-027, while ongoing discussions with Pfizer and potential new partners set the stage for commercialization of vepdegestrant should regulatory timelines proceed as planned.

  • Management confirmed an active pursuit of new commercial partnerships for vepdegestrant in collaboration with Pfizer, aiming to be “launch-ready” by the PDUFA action date.
  • Preclinical and early clinical biomarker data were presented as foundational to the company’s differentiation claims for LRRK2, KRAS, and BCL6 programs, with further validation expected from upcoming scientific meetings.
  • The company described ARV-6723’s capacity to degrade both kinase and scaffolding functions of HPK1 as potentially paradigm-changing for immuno-oncology, with IND-filing planned in 2026.
  • For ARV-027, Arvinas outlined a translational strategy that includes muscle biopsies and MRI-based surrogate endpoints, targeting SBMA patients in a setting with no approved therapies.
  • On development strategy, management noted all new Phase 1 assets would be evaluated on their ability to demonstrate significant differentiation on safety, efficacy, or mechanistic grounds versus established competitors, with explicit go/no-go decisions based on near-term data events.

Industry glossary

  • PROTAC degrader: A small molecule that induces targeted protein degradation via recruitment of E3 ligases.
  • LRRK2: Leucine-rich repeat kinase 2, a protein implicated in neurodegenerative diseases such as Parkinson’s and PSP.
  • BCL6: A transcriptional repressor protein, therapeutic target in non-Hodgkin's lymphoma.
  • HPK1: Hematopoietic progenitor kinase 1, a negative regulator of immune cell signaling targeted in immuno-oncology.
  • SBMA: Spinal and bulbar muscular atrophy, a rare neuromuscular disease characterized by polyglutamine-expanded androgen receptor accumulation.
  • Glofitamab: A CD20-directed bispecific antibody utilized in certain combination oncology regimens.
  • PDUFA: Prescription Drug User Fee Act date, indicating the FDA deadline for decision on a drug application.
  • KRAS G12D: A specific KRAS gene mutation commonly driving various cancers.
  • Pan-KRAS: Refers to agents impacting multiple or all active KRAS mutations and wild-type forms.
  • PSPRS: Progressive Supranuclear Palsy Rating Scale, a clinical rating used to assess disease severity in PSP.
