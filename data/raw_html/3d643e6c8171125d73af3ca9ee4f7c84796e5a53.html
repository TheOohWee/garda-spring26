Lifetime Brands (LCUT) Q4 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 12, 2026 at 12:05PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, March 12, 2026 at 11 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Rob Kay
  • Chief Financial Officer — Laurence Winoker
  • Director of Investor Relations — Jamie Kirchen

Takeaways

  • Net income -- $18.2 million for 2025, up from $8.9 million the prior year.
  • Adjusted net income (Q4) -- $23 million, or $1.05 per diluted share, compared to $12 million, or $0.55 per share, in the prior-year quarter.
  • Income from operations -- $20 million for 2025, up from $15.5 million the previous year.
  • Adjusted income from operations (Q4) -- $26.4 million, up from $20.2 million a year ago.
  • Adjusted EBITDA (full year) -- $50.8 million, despite a 5% net sales decline.
  • Consolidated sales -- $204.1 million, representing a 5.2% decrease.
  • U.S. segment sales -- $185.3 million, down 5.5% year over year; offset partially by tableware increases.
  • International segment sales -- $18.8 million, a 2.3% decrease; decreased $1.4 million, or 6.8%, on a constant currency basis due to U.K. e-commerce weakness.
  • Gross margin -- Increased to 38.6% from 37.7% overall; U.S. segment margin rose to 38.8% from 37.6%; international margin fell to 30.8% from 38.6% due to higher customer support costs.
  • SG&A expenses -- Decreased 12% to $38 million; U.S. SG&A fell by $3.2 million, now 16% of sales versus 16.7% previously.
  • Distribution expenses (U.S. segment) -- 8.3% of goods shipped, down from 9.1%, attributed to labor efficiencies and higher tariff-induced pricing.
  • Adjusted EBITDA to net debt ratio -- 2.9x at year-end.
  • DALL E brand -- Achieved approximately $18 million in sales, up over 150% for the year.
  • International sales (full year) -- $56.7 million, a 1.7% reported increase; declined 17% on a constant currency basis due to declines in independent retail segments.
  • Project CONCORD -- Final phase delayed due to legal and structural constraints; full implementation expected in first half of 2026.
  • East Coast distribution center -- Relocation to Hagerstown, Maryland, underway; 1 million square feet replacing New Jersey, adding 327,000 square feet of capacity; operations start expected in 2026.
  • Interest expense -- Decreased by $600,000 due to lower average borrowings and reduced rates on variable-rate debt.
  • Liquidity -- $76.6 million at year-end, combining cash, credit facility, and receivable purchase agreement availability.
  • Tax rate guidance -- Expected to be 27%-28% in 2026, reflecting the combined U.S. federal and state rates.
  • 2026 CapEx (Maryland distribution center) -- Forecasted around $7 million, with a $1 million benefit in lower maintenance costs versus historical levels.
  • Major customer impact -- Costco rebounded in Q4 with resumed tabletop shipments after prior pullback, materially contributing to quarter results.
  • Strategic focus -- Management emphasized restoring sustainable top-line growth as the core 2026 focus.

Risks

  • CEO Kay stated, "The tariff escalations, retail customer disruption, consumers' reactions, and the operational demands were all significant," emphasizing that the 145% China tariffs led to widespread order cancellations and substantial Q2 and Q3 shipment disruptions.
  • CFO Winoker cited, "International segment sales decreased 2.3% to $18.8 million," with further constant currency decline attributed to UK e-commerce, and noted international gross margin fell steeply due to higher customer support spending.
  • Rob Kay acknowledged that "Costco, our largest year-over-year decline in any single customer through September. They pulled back sharply on tabletop programs as tariff uncertainty peaked," although Q4 saw partial recovery.
  • Rob Kay addressed that international operations were "at a loss" in 2025, with ongoing Project CONCORD delays and legal "roadblocks that slowed us down."

Summary

Lifetime Brands (LCUT 3.09%) reported material earnings growth amid significant tariff headwinds and softening sales, as decisive pricing action and cost controls offset shipment disruptions. The company’s pricing strategy resulted in higher margins and improved operating income, even as overall sales contracted due to tariff-related demand dampening and U.K. e-commerce declines. The DALL E brand and key accounts such as Costco provided positive momentum, while international performance was mixed, with Project CONCORD’s completion postponed into 2026. Management confirmed a continued focus on top-line recovery and strategic investment, with liquidity remaining solid and new distribution infrastructure set to commence operations in the upcoming year.

  • Management described the M&A environment as "the strongest I have seen in decades for strategic" and noted active deal evaluation amid favorable acquisition conditions.
  • CEO Kay stated that the impact of 2025 pricing actions will be "fully felt because they were fully implemented," setting the stage for 2026 results.
  • Higher SG&A efficiencies were characterized as sustainable under the current growth plan, with future increases tied to incremental investment needs only.
  • International restructuring, while partially complete, remains a work in progress with expected financial benefits pending the 2026 full realization of the CONCORD initiative.
  • Management forecasted that the capital spend for the Maryland distribution center should "beat" the $9 million budget, reinforcing operational discipline.
  • Management indicated incentive compensation payments will likely normalize in 2026, driving a partial rebound in SG&A from current lows.
  • Long-term ocean freight contracts provide some protection against rising container rates, though management allowed that extreme freight inflation could reduce these benefits.
  • Growth in new product categories, such as expanded KitchenAid storage, and cutlery initiatives are planned but were not included in disclosed forecasts.

Industry glossary

  • CONCORD: Lifetime Brands’ international restructuring initiative targeting operational improvements and cost efficiencies across overseas divisions.
  • DALL E: A proprietary or licensed Lifetime Brands product brand experiencing rapid growth in sales and channel expansion.
