Regional Management (RM) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 4, 2026 at 5:55PM EST

Wednesday, February 4, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. ET

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Latvir Lambda
  • Chief Financial and Administrative Officer — Harpreet Rana
  • Managing Director, ICR — Garrett Edson

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Income -- $12.9 million for the quarter, representing a 33% increase year over year and $1.30 in diluted earnings per share.
  • Full-Year Net Income -- $44.4 million, up 8%, landing toward the higher end of management's stated guidance.
  • Net Receivables Growth -- Ending net receivables rose by $248 million, or 13%, reaching the previously guided minimum of 10% annual growth.
  • Loan Portfolio Size -- Total loan portfolio reached $2.1 billion at year-end.
  • Originations -- Fourth-quarter originations totaled $537 million, a 13% increase year over year and a new record level.
  • Full-Year Originations -- Originations reached $2 billion, marking a 19% increase.
  • Credit Performance -- The 30-plus day delinquency rate improved by 20 basis points to 7.5%, reflecting actions taken to tighten credit and improved analytics.
  • Net Credit Loss Rate -- Adjusted annualized net credit loss rate improved 30 basis points for the quarter and 70 basis points for the year, after adjustments for previous disaster deferral and loan sale impacts.
  • Allowance for Credit Losses -- Increased by $8.9 million in the quarter to support portfolio growth, with a rate of 10.3%, up 20 basis points from the prior year period.
  • Operating Expense Ratio -- Annualized ratio at 12.4% for the quarter, a company record and a 160 basis point improvement year over year; full-year ratio at 13.1%, a 70 basis point improvement.
  • Capital Generation and Returns -- $74 million capital generated for the year; $36 million returned to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases.
  • Auto Secured Portfolio Growth -- This segment grew 42% year over year, becoming a larger component of the loan portfolio.
  • Branch Expansion -- Five new branches opened during the quarter in California and Louisiana, with further expansion planned and new states under review.
  • Dividend and Share Repurchase -- Board declared a 30¢ per share dividend for the first quarter; 197,000 shares repurchased in the quarter at $38.87 average price, and 702,000 shares repurchased for the year at $34.12 average price.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Management projects at least 10% ending net receivables growth, and 20%-25% net income growth for the full year.
  • Expense Management -- Ongoing focus on expense discipline expected to continue lowering the operating expense ratio over time, supported by scale and process improvements.
  • Digital and AI Investments -- Management plans investments to improve origination productivity, servicing experience, and cost efficiency in digital channels using analytics and AI.
  • Bank Partnership Initiative -- Still in development, this strategy is intended to expand market access, improve operational consistency across states, and optimize risk-adjusted yields.
  • Seasonality Expectations -- First quarter often features higher net credit losses and softer origination due to tax refund season, with guidance indicating this effect could be more pronounced in 2026 due to larger expected tax refunds.
  • Interest Expense -- Fourth-quarter interest expense was $22.6 million, equal to 4.3% of average net receivables on an annualized basis.

SUMMARY

Management highlighted a shift to full-year guidance, moving away from short-term precision as quarterly swings were considered less indicative of underlying business trends. Investment in digital origination and servicing, along with data analytics and AI, is expected to enhance both risk management and cost efficiency in upcoming periods. The company’s bank partnership initiative aims to deliver strategic advantages in product delivery, compliance uniformity, and faster entry into regulated markets. The auto secured loan product is set to receive continued focus, given its rapid growth and high risk-adjusted returns as noted by management. Management signaled ongoing expansion into new geographies and a disciplined approach to market entry and branch profitability evaluation.

  • Latvir Lambda said, "We delivered strong results while continuing to invest in the business, improve underlying credit performance, and drive operating leverage."
  • Chief Financial and Administrative Officer Harpreet Rana stated that "net income will be meaningfully higher in the second half of the year than the first half," citing seasonal factors and operational improvements.
  • The company expects the projected increase in tax refunds due to the One Big Beautiful Bill Act will "reduce balances through debt pay downs and improve collections and delinquencies in the first quarter."
  • Management noted that pricing remains closely aligned with market competition, and programmatic extension of loan duration has not occurred beyond shifts driven by product mix.
  • Ongoing improvements in credit and marketing efficiency are expected to be reinvested in business growth if demand remains favorable.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Net Credit Loss (NCL) Rate: Annualized net loan charge-offs as a percentage of average net receivables, measuring the effectiveness of credit risk management.
  • 30-Plus Day Delinquency Rate: Percentage of loan balances more than 30 days overdue, indicating early stage credit performance trends.
  • Operating Expense Ratio: Operating expenses divided by average net receivables, reflecting cost-efficiency in loan operations.
  • ENR (Ending Net Receivables): Net principal amount outstanding on all loans owned at period-end.
  • Auto Secured Portfolio: Segment consisting of loans secured by automobile collateral.
  • One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBA): Referenced federal legislation impacting tax refunds, directly influencing customer loan payoff patterns.
