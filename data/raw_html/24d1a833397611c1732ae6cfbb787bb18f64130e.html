Takeaways

  • Adjusted net income -- $30 million, or $0.06 per diluted share, compared to prior quarter's net loss of $0.07 per diluted share.
  • Net interest margin (NIM) -- Improved by 23 basis points quarter over quarter to 2.14% with a $20 million onetime hedge gain; excluding this gain, NIM was 2.05%, a 14 basis point increase.
  • Pre-provision net revenue -- Increased by $45 million quarter over quarter, reflecting positive operating leverage of approximately 900 basis points.
  • Net C&I loan growth -- Up 2% on a sequential basis, $343 million higher than the third quarter, and about 9% annualized.
  • C&I commitments and originations -- Total commitments increased 28% year over year to $3 billion; originations rose 22% to $2.1 billion.
  • CRE (commercial real estate) exposure -- Reduced by $2.3 billion in multifamily and CRE loans during the quarter, with 50% of payoffs classified as substandard; CRE concentration ratio now 381%.
  • Credit quality -- Nonaccrual loans declined by $267 million quarter over quarter to $3 billion; net charge-offs decreased by $27 million to $46 million, representing a 37% reduction.
  • CET1 capital ratio -- Rose to 12.83%, or about $2.1 billion above targeted operating minimum of 10.5% pretax.
  • Allowance for credit losses (ACL) -- Coverage ratio, including unfunded commitments, held steady at 1.79% quarter over quarter; $43 million decline driven by reduced balances and recoveries.
  • Operating expenses -- Core operating expenses declined by $700 million for full year 2025 compared to 2024; a $4 million severance accrual taken for FTE reductions in January 2026.
  • Brokered deposits and funding -- Brokered deposits reduced by $1.7 billion, leaving $2.3 billion at year-end; advances of $1 billion paid down, further lowering funding costs.
  • Retail CDs -- $5.4 billion matured in Q4 with a weighted average cost of 4.29%; 86% retained and rolled into lower-rate products.
  • Balance sheet -- Total CRE balances declined by $12.1 billion, or 25%, since end of 2023 to approximately $36 billion.
  • NYC regulated multifamily portfolio -- $9.2 billion in loans; 53% pass rated, 47% criticized or classified; $1.9 billion of criticized loans are nonaccrual, 16% charged off, with 21% total charge-off and reserve coverage.
  • 2026 and 2027 guidance -- Net interest income guidance reduced due to higher payoff activity; 2026 EPS forecasted at $0.65–$0.70, and 2027 at $1.90–$2.00.
  • Deposit costs -- Interest-bearing deposit costs decreased by 26 basis points sequentially; spot rate for all deposits at year-end was 2.56%.
  • Delinquencies -- 30- to 89-day delinquencies stood at $988 million at quarter-end, inflated by timing, with 70% already current by January 26, 2026.
  • Loan portfolio diversification -- Strategic plan targets balanced mix: one-third CRE, one-third C&I, one-third consumer.
  • Expense guidance -- Total operating expenses guided to $1.5 billion–$1.8 billion for 2026, with targeted continued reduction through cost optimization and real estate rationalization.

Summary

The call revealed that Flagstar Financial (FLG 0.64%) achieved its first profitable quarter since the third quarter of 2023, driven by C&I loan growth, CRE exposure reductions, and disciplined deposit cost management. Management outlined a multi-year plan focused on further strengthening credit quality, reducing nonperforming assets, and deploying excess capital—potentially including share repurchases—as conditions permit. The guidance revision for lower net interest income was attributed to accelerated loan payoffs, with an explicit expectation that about 40%-50% of future CRE paydowns will remain substandard, supporting continued portfolio derisking.

  • Smith said, "we reported net income attributable to common stockholders of $0.05 per diluted share" after accounting for a $9 million revaluation in Figure Technologies and $4 million in severance costs.
  • Otting stated, "our CET1 capital ratio has increased by almost 400 basis points" in the restructuring effort, emphasizing peer leadership in capitalization.
  • Smith clarified, "at this level, we have over $2 billion in excess capital pretax or $1.4 billion after tax relative to the low end of our target operating CET1 range of 10.5%."
  • Management stressed that 90% of NYC regulated multifamily criticized loans have repriced or will reprice within 18 months, giving increased clarity on embedded credit risk.
  • Management described the platform as interest rate neutral, but noted that a "declining rate environment, it helps our multifamily borrowers and so we think."
  • Smith provided average C&I loan origination spreads of 230 basis points over SOFR and noted 70% utilization rates on those facilities.
  • Otting guided a targeted reduction of at least $1 billion in nonperforming loans in 2026, with a large portion tied to resolution of a high-profile bankruptcy within the portfolio.
  • The share count for 2026 and 2027 is projected at 473 million and 479 million, fully diluted including warrants.

Industry glossary

  • CRE (commercial real estate): Loans secured by income-producing property such as offices, apartments, or industrial buildings, with risk metrics closely monitored due to concentration limits and regulatory focus.
  • NIM (net interest margin): The difference between interest income generated and interest paid out, expressed as a percentage of average earning assets; a principal profitability metric for banks.
  • C&I (commercial and industrial loans): Loans to businesses for commercial purposes, usually secured by business assets and distinct from real estate lending.
  • CET1 (common equity tier 1) capital ratio: A regulatory capital measure representing a bank's core equity capital compared with its total risk-weighted assets.
  • ACL (allowance for credit losses): The reserve established for expected credit losses on loans and off-balance-sheet exposures, reflecting management’s estimated inherent risk.
  • Pass rated / criticized / classified loans: Internal regulatory classifications; pass rated for healthy credits, criticized for elevated risk, classified for substandard or nonaccrual status.
  • Nonaccrual loans: Loans on which the bank has stopped accruing interest due to borrower payment issues or deteriorating credit conditions.
  • Par payoff: Repayment of a loan at face value, frequently invoked in discussions of portfolio reshaping or risk reduction.
  • SOFR (Secured Overnight Financing Rate): A benchmark interest rate for dollar-denominated derivatives and loans that has replaced LIBOR.
