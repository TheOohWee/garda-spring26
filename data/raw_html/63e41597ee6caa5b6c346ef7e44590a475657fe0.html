Earnings Call Transcript

Cullen/Frost (CFR) Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 10, 2026 at 12:32PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Oct. 30, 2025, 2 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — Phillip Green
  • Group Executive Vice President & Chief Financial Officer — Dan Geddes

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Income -- $172.7 million, or $2.67 per share, representing 19.2% growth over the prior year.
  • Return on Average Assets and Equity -- 1.32% and 16.72%, respectively, both increasing from a year ago.
  • Average Deposits -- $42.1 billion, up 3.3%, driven primarily by interest-bearing accounts.
  • Average Loans -- $21.5 billion, a 6.8% increase, with expansion markets contributing 38% of total loan growth.
  • Net Interest Margin (NIM) -- 3.69%, up 2 basis points sequentially, with a positive mix shift to higher-yielding assets.
  • Noninterest Income -- Full-year 2025 guidance raised to 6.5%-7.5% growth versus prior 3.5%-4.5%, following strong performance in insurance commissions and trust fees.
  • Noninterest Expense -- Grew 1.7% linked quarter due to higher incentive compensation, medical, and technology expenses, offset by a $3.9 million decline in advertising and marketing.
  • Expansion EPS Contribution -- $0.09 accretion in the quarter, with Houston 1.0 providing $0.14 per share, while Dallas and Houston 2.0 were near breakeven and Austin at a $0.04 per share loss.
  • Consumer Checking Household Growth -- 5.4% growth, described by management as "industry-leading" in organic new accounts.
  • Consumer Real Estate Loans -- $3.5 billion at period end, up $547 million, or 18.7%, year over year.
  • Commercial Loans -- Period-end balances rose 5.1%, with energy loans up 17% and commercial and industrial loans up 6.8%.
  • CRE (Commercial Real Estate) Loans -- Balances increased 2.7% but were impacted by paydowns in multifamily due to flexible capital structures.
  • Nonperforming Assets (NPAs) -- Declined to $47 million from $64 million last quarter and $106 million a year ago; now 22 basis points of period-end loans.
  • Net Charge-Offs -- $6.6 million, down from $11.2 million last quarter, annualizing to 12 basis points of average loans.
  • Total Problem Loans (Risk Grade 10 or Higher) -- $828 million, a $169 million decrease sequentially, primarily due to resolutions in multifamily lending.
  • Investment Portfolio -- Averaged $20.2 billion, with $430 million in municipal purchases at a 5.93% yield and a net unrealized loss of $1.14 billion, improved from $1.42 billion last quarter.
  • Tax-Exempt Municipal Portfolio -- Averaged $6.9 billion, up $269 million sequentially, with yields rising 12 basis points to 4.6%.
  • Stock Repurchases -- $69.3 million used to buy back approximately 549,000 shares under a $150 million authorization.
  • 2025 Guidance Updates -- Net interest income growth forecast lifted to 7%-8%; net charge-off guidance improved to 15-20 basis points; deposit growth expectation slightly increased to 2.5%-3.5%.
  • Commercial Pipeline -- Weighted pipeline ended at $1.9 billion, up 20% sequentially, CRE and C&I pipelines increased 29% and 11%, respectively.

RISKS

  • Phillip Green said, "There is in my view, some increasing competition. I think we called that last quarter," highlighting rising pricing pressure, though not yet dramatic.
  • Dan Geddes noted, "Fourth quarter, we -- it's a little bit lighter in terms of insurance business," and anticipated "pull forward" in public finance underwriting reducing fee income next quarter.
  • Expansion accretion may be affected by rate cuts, as Geddes stated, "for the fourth quarter, roughly around the same EPS accretion with the rate cuts, maybe that, that will impact the profitability for the fourth quarter for the expansion by $0.01 or $0.02."

SUMMARY

Cullen/Frost Bankers (CFR 1.70%) reported robust loan and deposit growth, while raising full-year guidance for net interest income, noninterest income, and deposit growth, alongside a sequential improvement in asset quality as nonperforming assets and net charge-offs declined. New commercial relationship wins and industry-leading consumer household growth supported a record commercial pipeline, with notable progress in expansion market performance driving meaningful earnings accretion and improved profitability in mature locations. Management reaffirmed a preference for organic growth strategies over M&A, citing confidence in branch-led expansion to deliver durable returns, and described elevated competitive pressures and deposit rate sensitivity but expressed limited concern over new entrants or larger bank competitors given the company's low-cost funding position and long-term relationship focus.

  • Management identified $860 million, or 4% of loans, as exposures to "NDFI" (Non-Depository Financial Institutions), stating a majority are on strong footing with substantial hedging and long-standing relationships.
  • Geddes clarified, "In '26, that number is going to be a little bit north of $2.5 billion at around a [ 3.60% ] yield," offering reinvestment opportunities at higher rates depending on curve movements.
  • Phillip Green said, "If the Fed brings rates down, just like he said, the value of really any intermediary that's asset sensitive will be somewhat less in terms of the current earnings but it doesn't reflect poorly on the success of what's happening," signaling that NIM and EPS are sensitive to interest-rate cycles.
  • With average branch age in new markets rising, Geddes stated, "expansion represented 38% of total loan growth and 39% of total deposit growth" year over year, highlighting a maturing contribution.
  • Noninterest income uplift was attributed to insurance, trust, and service charge growth, though insurance and underwriting results are expected to moderate in the next quarter.
  • Phillip Green commented that, based on current pipeline momentum, management expects to reach record mortgage lending levels and the company's $0.5 billion mortgage portfolio target by year-end.
  • CRE paydowns in multifamily were described as manageable, with both pipeline trends and customer dialogue indicating capacity to offset runoff and pursue new lending opportunities.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • NDFI (Non-Depository Financial Institution): Loan exposures to entities such as private credit intermediaries, subscription lines, family offices, insurance companies, and portfolio investors that do not accept deposits but engage in financial transactions.
  • PSF Insured: Designation for municipal securities insured by the Permanent School Fund of Texas, reducing credit risk.
  • OAEM: Banking industry risk-grade equivalent to "Other Assets Especially Mentioned," referring to loans with higher credit risk but not yet classified as substandard.
  • Houston 1.0 / Houston 2.0: Internal references to sequenced waves of branch expansions in the Houston, Texas, metro area.
