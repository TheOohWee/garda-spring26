Fuel Tech (FTEK) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 4, 2026 at 11:01AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, March 4, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Vincent J. Arnone
  • Chief Financial Officer — Ellen T. Albrecht

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- Consolidated revenue increased 37% to $7.2 million in Q4 and rose 6% to $26.7 million for the full year, driven by both APC and FUEL CHEM segment growth.
  • FUEL CHEM Segment -- Annual revenue for FUEL CHEM rose 28% to $17.8 million, attributed to strong legacy unit performance, the full-year contribution of a new commercial unit, and a new demonstration customer.
  • APC Segment Awards -- $8.8 million of APC awards were secured in 2025 from customers in the U.S., Europe, and Southeast Asia.
  • Backlog -- Consolidated APC segment backlog stood at $7.0 million at year end, up from $6.2 million, with $3.4 million domestic and $3.6 million foreign projects.
  • Gross Margin -- Consolidated gross margin for Q4 increased to 45% from 42%, while the annual figure rose to 46% from 42%, with both FUEL CHEM and APC segments higher year over year.
  • Operating Loss -- Operating loss narrowed to $1.4 million in Q4 from $2.1 million and to $3.7 million for the year from $4.7 million previously.
  • Cash and Investments -- End-of-year cash, cash equivalents, and investments totaled $31.9 million, with no outstanding debt.
  • Interest Income -- Interest income reached $288,000 for Q4 and $1.4 million for the year, reflecting investment of cash reserves.
  • Data Center Pipeline -- The APC sales pipeline related to data center projects approximates $75 million to $100 million per project, with current participation in opportunities involving more than ten data center integrators and OEMs.
  • FUEL CHEM Demonstration -- A six-month demonstration program with a new U.S. customer delivered reduced maintenance downtime and, if converted to commercial, could provide $2.5 million to $3 million annually at historic margins.
  • Dissimilar Segment Results -- APC segment revenue decreased annually despite higher Q4 results, reflecting project-award timing and customer-driven delays.
  • SG&A Expenses -- 2025 SG&A increased to $14.1 million from $13.8 million, but SG&A as a percentage of revenue decreased to 53% from 55%.
  • Dissolved Gas Infusion (DGI) -- Ongoing demonstrations led to a municipal wastewater trial converting to a six-month rental and a fish hatchery test expected to conclude in Q2 2026; the company anticipates the first commercial DGI contract in 2026.
  • Walco Asset Acquisition -- The Walco Inc. acquisition expanded Fuel Tech's APC portfolio, adding IP and customer assets, and has generated accelerated project inquiries.
  • Guidance -- Management expects 2026 revenues to surpass 2025, with FUEL CHEM steady and APC exceeding prior-year performance, exclusive of any potential data center awards.

RISKS

  • Net loss increased to $2.3 million ($0.08 per diluted share) from $1.9 million ($0.06 per diluted share), and adjusted EBITDA loss widened to $2.7 million from $2.2 million, despite top-line growth.
  • Management said, "The timing of these projects is very hard to predict." regarding data center opportunities, acknowledging unpredictability in converting the $75 million to $100 million pipeline to awards.
  • APC segment revenue declined annually due to customer delays and project-award timing, with near-term backlog realization dependent on closing now-slipped contracts.

SUMMARY

Fuel Tech (FTEK 1.54%) achieved its highest annual revenue since 2018, a 6% increase to $26.7 million, primarily driven by a recovery in FUEL CHEM segment performance and robust Q4 growth across both operating units. The company closed a $8.8 million volume of new APC awards, grew its backlog to $7.0 million, and maintained a debt-free balance sheet with $31.9 million in cash and investments. Management revealed a large data center project pipeline valued at $75 million to $100 million per project, although all described awards remain pending, and the company acknowledged precise award timing remains challenging to forecast.

  • Chairman Arnone stated, "we are in various stages of participation in project opportunities with more than ten different data center integrators and turbine and engine OEMs," signaling broadened customer engagement for upcoming projects.
  • The FUEL CHEM demonstration program resulted in "a material reduction in downtime and maintenance costs due to a reduction in offline cleaning," supporting the likelihood of full conversion.
  • The new NSPS federal emissions standards for gas turbines could drive future APC demand, but management disclosed, "we are not aware of this new regulation having a significant negative impact on decision-making regarding the implementation of pollution controls" at this stage.
  • Management highlighted that any near-term material growth will "likely derive from our success in addressing this opportunity," reinforcing its centrality to future expectations.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • APC: Air Pollution Control — technologies and systems designed to reduce or remove pollutants from industrial emissions.
  • FUEL CHEM: Segment focused on chemical programs to improve boiler efficiency and reduce emissions.
  • DGI: Dissolved Gas Infusion — technology for introducing dissolved oxygen into water systems for various industrial and environmental uses.
  • SCR: Selective Catalytic Reduction — process for reducing nitrogen oxide emissions using a catalyst.
  • SNCR: Selective Non-Catalytic Reduction — a process to reduce NOx emissions without a catalyst.
  • NSPS: New Source Performance Standards — federal EPA regulatory standards for new emission sources such as gas turbines.
  • MWC: Municipal Waste Combustion — refers to waste-to-energy facilities subject to specific emission rules.
  • OEM: Original Equipment Manufacturer — companies that produce engines or turbines incorporated into larger systems.
