Earnings Call Transcript

Mohawk (MHK) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 13, 2026 at 12:23PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Friday, February 13, 2026 at 11 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman & Chief Executive Officer — Jeffrey S. Lorberbaum
  • Chief Financial Officer — James Brunk
  • President, Flooring ROW — Paul De Cock

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $2.7 billion for the quarter, a 2.4% increase as reported and a 3.3% decrease on a constant currency basis, which matched company expectations.
  • Adjusted EPS -- $2.00 for the quarter, representing a 3% increase, with productivity, restructuring, improved mix, and lower interest costs offset by input cost increases and market pressures.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $270 million generated in Q4 and $620 million for the year, supporting $149 million in repurchases of 1.3 million shares.
  • Segment Performance -- Global Ceramic sales at just under $1.1 billion (6.1% increase as reported), Flooring North America sales of $893 million (4.8% decrease as reported), and Flooring Rest of the World sales of $737 million (6.5% increase as reported); constant-currency growth diverged from reported figures.
  • Adjusted Operating Income -- $152 million, or 5.6% of sales, showing only a 50 basis point decline despite restructuring and legal settlement charges of $84 million.
  • Tariff Management -- Tariff costs ranged from 15%-50%, mitigated by pricing actions, supply chain changes, and lower ocean freight, and the company expects to offset the approximately $100 million annual tariff impact.
  • CapEx -- $189 million for the quarter and $435 million for the year, noted as about 30% below depreciation; planned 2026 CapEx is $480 million, mainly targeting product innovation and cost reduction.
  • Leverage & Liquidity -- Gross debt of $2 billion, leverage ratio at 0.9x adjusted EBITDA, and cash & equivalents of $806 million; year-end inventory at $2.7 billion with 139 inventory days.
  • Pricing Actions -- Announced 3%-5% price increases across most categories for 2026, with some initiatives in higher-value segments and tariff-driven products.
  • Restructuring Savings -- $115 million in cumulative 2025 savings; $60 million expected to carry into 2026, with ~$30 million more expected to benefit 2027.
  • Guidance -- Q1 2026 adjusted EPS expected at $1.75-$1.85, factoring seasonality and four additional shipping days compared to the previous year.
  • Leadership Transition -- CFO James Brunk to retire in April, succeeded by Nick Manthe, formerly VP Corporate Finance and Investor Relations.
  • Hard Surface Outperformance -- Flooring North America hard surface products, including laminate, hybrid, and LVT collections, continued to outperform, with PureTech PVC-free hybrid experiencing market acceptance.
  • Commercial Channel -- Commercial demand outpaced residential, with ongoing strength in healthcare, education, and hospitality; backlog described as "solid."
  • Product Innovation -- Advanced design expertise, proprietary printing, and new product launches in premium ceramic created differentiated offerings and supported pricing power and margins.

RISKS

  • Residential demand remains "weak," with persistent low housing turnover and postponed discretionary home renovations due to affordability challenges and economic uncertainty.
  • Competition for volume continues to pressure pricing in residential channels, with management noting that "it is not easy" to fully pass through inflation and input costs, especially in soft surface and builder channels.
  • Paul De Cock stated, "geopolitical events in Europe are still impacting consumer confidence," leading to soft demand, strong price competition, and ongoing volatility in certain geographies.
  • Input costs, particularly wages, benefits, energy, materials, and tariffs, are rising and could vary materially across regions, with CFO Brunk citing wage and benefit increases as the primary inflation driver for 2026.

SUMMARY

Mohawk Industries (MHK +0.29%) delivered quarterly net sales of $2.7 billion, reporting both topline growth on a reported basis and contraction on a constant currency basis, reflecting mixed conditions globally. The company generated $620 million in free cash flow for the year and maintained a 0.9x adjusted EBITDA leverage ratio. Strategic restructuring and productivity measures produced $115 million in 2025 savings, with an additional $60 million projected for 2026, partially offsetting continued inflation and input cost pressures. Announced 2026 price increases of 3%-5% across most categories are intended to mitigate tariffs and inflation, though competitive intensity remains high in residential and builder markets. The company guided to first quarter 2026 adjusted EPS of $1.75-$1.85, highlighting early-year seasonality and an incremental four shipping days versus the prior year.

  • Company leadership transition will bring Nick Manthe into the CFO role following James Brunk's planned April retirement.
  • Segment breakdowns revealed Global Ceramic and Flooring Rest of World rising on a reported basis, but margin pressure continued in builder and residential channels, particularly in Flooring North America.
  • Premium product launches, proprietary design, and sustained innovation contributed to positive commercial momentum and mix in multiple business units.
  • Tariff and input cost management relies on pricing actions, lower freight costs, and ongoing restructuring, with management expressing commitment to covering the $100 million annual tariff cost impact.
  • The company identified a "transitional year" for 2026, expecting improvement in housing turnover and remodeling as lower mortgage rates, pent-up demand, and government actions potentially support recovery.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • LVT (Luxury Vinyl Tile): A high-durability, water-resistant flooring product commonly used in both residential and commercial applications as an alternative to traditional materials.
  • PureTech PVC-free hybrid flooring: A flooring category developed by Mohawk Industries that omits polyvinyl chloride for enhanced environmental and performance characteristics.
  • NAHB Remodeling Index: A quarterly survey-based measure of remodeling industry activity and sentiment, referenced as an industry indicator.
