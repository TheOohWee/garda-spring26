Alamos Gold (AGI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 19, 2026 at 10:51AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Feb. 19, 2026 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President & CEO — John A. McCluskey
  • Chief Financial Officer — Greg Fisher
  • Chief Operating Officer — Luc Guimond
  • Senior Vice President, Exploration — Scott R. Parsons

Takeaways

  • Annual gold production -- 545,000 ounces, below company guidance due to late December severe weather and Canadian operational challenges.
  • Annual revenue -- $1.8 billion, representing a 34% increase with record free cash flow exceeding $350 million.
  • Total 2025 gold sales -- 531,000 ounces at an average realized price of $3,372 per ounce.
  • Fourth quarter gold sales -- 142,000 ounces sold at an average realized price of $3,998 per ounce, resulting in $575 million quarterly revenue.
  • Free cash flow -- $157 million in Q4 and $352 million for the year, both records, while internally funding major growth projects.
  • Operating cash flow -- $285 million in Q4 before non-cash working capital changes; annual total rose 27% to $924 million.
  • Total 2025 capital expenditures -- $507 million, comprised of $318 million in growth capital, $50 million in sustaining capital, and $11 million in capitalized exploration.
  • All-in sustaining cost (AISC) -- $1,524 per ounce for the year, above prior guidance due to weather and operational disruptions in Canada.
  • Adjusted net earnings -- $228 million ($0.54 per share) in Q4; $587 million ($1.40 per share) for the year, excluding non-core asset gains, hedge derivative losses, and other adjustments.
  • Shareholder returns -- $81 million returned via $39 million in buybacks (1,300,000 shares) and $42 million in dividends, both doubled year over year.
  • Dividend increase -- 60% rise commencing this quarter, reflecting stronger free cash flow and a healthy balance sheet.
  • Debt reduction -- Paid down $50 million of debt, lowering the total to $200 million, and cash position rose by 90% to $623 million.
  • Hedge elimination -- Eliminated 230,000 of 330,000 ounces hedged through Argonaut Gold; 100,000 ounces remain hedged for 2026 and 2027.
  • Mineral reserves -- Year-end reserves rose 32% to 16 million ounces, the seventh consecutive annual increase.
  • Island Gold District reserve growth -- Nearly doubled reserves to over 8 million ounces, driven by 125% underground reserve increase and 56% open pit increase.
  • Production guidance -- 2026 outlook targets a 12% production increase, with further growth to 1 million ounces annually and 46% production growth at around 20% lower AISC by 2028.
  • Island Gold expansion -- Expected to reach average annual production of 534,000 ounces for the first ten years post-2028 at mine site AISC of $1,025 per ounce, more than doubling 2025 production at 30% lower costs.
  • Young-Davidson free cash flow -- Generated $250 million mine site free cash flow despite weather-related setbacks and ore pass repair needs.
  • Mulatos District performance -- Achieved record quarterly mine site free cash flow of $92 million in Q4 and $222 million annually, with production and costs in line with revised guidance.
  • 2026 exploration budget -- Increased to nearly $100 million, a 37% uptick supporting key district drilling and resource conversion.

Risks

  • Annual gold production and cost metrics missed guidance due to severe weather events and operational challenges at Canadian sites, specifically affecting Q4 performance.
  • Total cash cost and mine site AISC exceeded annual guidance, attributed to lower mill throughput at Magino and lower mining rates at Island Gold District.
  • Young-Davidson full-year production fell short of revised guidance due to adverse weather, rehabilitation requirements, and paste plug failure underground.

Summary

Alamos Gold (AGI +2.95%) posted record annual revenue and free cash flow in 2025 while facing operational headwinds, including weather-related disruptions and higher costs at Canadian mines. The company detailed a clear multi-year growth plan centered on the Island Gold District, with a 60% dividend increase funded by rising free cash flow and de-risked expansion projects. Alamos advanced its exploration initiatives, nearly doubling Island Gold District reserves and raising its total mineral reserves to 16 million ounces. Continuing hedge elimination and active debt reduction reflect management's focus on increasing gold price leverage and balance sheet strength amid significant upcoming capital expenditures. A strategic path to nearly doubling annual production by 2028 at lower unit costs positions the company for significant scalability in the gold sector.

  • Company leadership described a capital return strategy balancing dividends and share repurchases, with opportunistic buybacks based on market conditions and anticipated ongoing dividend growth.
  • Development at the Magino and PDA projects advances on schedule and budget, supported by procurement of long-lead items and timely construction milestones for mid-2027 target production at PDA.
  • Exploration success at Cline, Pickle, and Edwards mines, including record high-grade drill results at Cline, may provide additional pipeline ore for future district expansions.
  • Measured and indicated resources increased by 6% due to additions at Young-Davidson, Mulatos District, and Lynn Lake, with inferred resources down 63% owing to successful resource-to-reserve conversion at Island Gold District.
  • Expansion at Island Gold is expected to produce over $800 million of annual free cash flow at a $3,200 per ounce gold price, with a potential after-tax NPV of $12 billion at $4,500 per ounce.

Industry glossary

  • All-in sustaining cost (AISC): A comprehensive gold industry metric reflecting the total direct mining cost per ounce plus sustaining capital and other life-of-mine costs.
  • Ore pass: Vertical or inclined tunnel through which ore is transferred within an underground mine, critical for efficient ore movement and mining rates.
  • PDA project: Refers to the Puerto Del Aire deposit expansion within the Mulatos District, targeted for commercial gold production by mid-2027.
  • Phase 3 Plus shaft expansion: Large-scale capacity project at the Island Gold District designed to lift mining rates and support increased gold production.
