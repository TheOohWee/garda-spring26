GE (GE) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 22, 2026 at 12:31PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, July 22, 2025 at 7:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — H. Lawrence Culp
  • Chief Financial Officer — Rahul Ghai
  • Vice President of Investor Communications — Blaire Shoor

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $10 billion, up 23%, with Commercial Engines & Services (CES) revenue up 30% and Defense & Propulsion Technologies (DPT) revenue up 7%.
  • Profit -- $2.3 billion, increasing 23%, driven by volume and price, with overall margins reaching 23%.
  • Orders -- Up 27%, including CES service orders up 28% and equipment orders up 26%.
  • EPS -- $1.66, increasing 38% due to profit growth, lower interest expense, and a favorable tax rate.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $2.1 billion, nearly doubling year over year.
  • CES Service Revenue -- Rose 29%, underpinned by spare parts revenue up more than 25% and internal shop visit revenue up over 20%.
  • Total Engine Deliveries -- Up 45%, with commercial engine deliveries up 37% (LEAP up 38%) and defense engine deliveries up 84%.
  • Supply Chain Stability -- Priority suppliers delivered over 95% of committed volume, representing a twofold improvement compared to early 2024.
  • Guidance Raised -- 2025 outlook updated: total revenue growth now expected in the mid-teens (previously low double digits); operating profit guided to $8.2 billion–$8.5 billion, a $350 million midpoint increase from April guidance.
  • Free Cash Flow Outlook for 2025 -- Revised guidance to $6.5 billion–$6.9 billion, up from $6.3 billion–$6.8 billion, driven by improved profit expectations.
  • Record Backlog -- Total commercial services backlog exceeds $140 billion; 1,600+ engines in backlog, with production effectively sold out through the decade.
  • Major Wins -- Qatar Airways deal for 400+ GEnx and GE9X engines marked the company’s largest wide-body win; U.S. Air Force contract awarded for $5 billion in F110 engines.
  • Long-Term Outlook Raised -- 2028 guidance increased by $1.5 billion each for operating profit and free cash flow; targeted 2028 operating profit of $11.5 billion (21%+ margin), free cash flow $8.5 billion, and adjusted EPS ~$8.40.
  • CES Margin -- Expanded 50 basis points to 27.9% in the quarter.
  • Tariff Exposure -- 2025 tariff costs remain estimated at $500 million, with mitigation planned through cost controls and pricing actions.
  • Third-Party MRO Expansion -- Six premier partners added to the MRO network; significant increase in LEAP third-party shop visits this quarter.
  • R&D Investment -- $3 billion planned for 2025, representing 6%-8% of annual revenue.
  • Productivity Initiatives -- FLIGHT DECK and lean initiatives drove a 30%+ reduction in key MRO site lead times and a 50% decrease in AI-enabled blade inspection time.
  • Cash Return to Shareholders -- Planned increase from $19 billion to $24 billion in returns between 2024 and 2026, including ~$19 billion in buybacks and ~$5 billion in dividends (30% payout ratio).

RISKS

  • CFO Ghai said, "Assuming that reciprocal tariffs are implemented after the current pause, we still expect the net impact of tariffs to be roughly $500 million in 2025, which we are offsetting through cost controls and pricing actions."
  • Margins in DPT declined 20 basis points to 14.1% due to increased self-funded investments and inflation, partially offset by volume, productivity, and price.
  • The material delivery environment is expected to remain tight, with ongoing supply chain inflation risks and further improvement required in the second half of the year.

SUMMARY

GE Aerospace (GE 1.49%) delivered significant revenue and profit growth, raising guidance for revenue, operating profit, and free cash flow as commercial services demand and supply chain performance continued to improve. The company secured major wins, including a record order from Qatar Airways and a $5 billion defense contract, supporting a record backlog and sold-out production through the decade. Management announced increased capital returns to shareholders, robust R&D investment, and a structural expansion of MRO capacity. Tariff exposure, persistent supply chain tightness, and margin pressure in defense remain areas of explicit caution discussed by management.

  • The long-term outlook for operating profit and free cash flow was raised by $1.5 billion each, with anticipated operating margin expansion above 21% by 2028.
  • CEO Culp said, "Expect we'll return more than 100% of free cash flow to shareholders through 2026," highlighting dividend and buyback prioritization.
  • R&D spending of $3 billion is slated to sustain next-generation technology, with explicit focus on new propulsion programs and productivity systems.
  • FLIGHT DECK initiatives resulted in a doubling of supplier delivery reliability and substantial decreases in MRO lead times, with continued room for improvement identified.
  • Product ramp-ups in LEAP and GEnx engines, paired with growing external shop visits, drive profitability improvements and revenue mix shifts across the business.
  • Management projected "mid-teens EPS growth" through 2028 and expects sustained double-digit annual revenue growth, grounded in installed base growth and robust aftermarket demand.
  • Ongoing supply chain constraints, persistent inflation, and the risk of further tariff impacts represent acknowledged operational challenges cited in the call.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • FLIGHT DECK: GE Aerospace's proprietary lean operating model, focused on safety, quality, delivery, and cost optimization across manufacturing and service operations.
  • MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul): The set of services and facilities dedicated to maintaining, repairing, and overhauling aircraft engines and components.
  • LEAP: A family of high-bypass turbofan aircraft engines produced by CFM International, used on narrow-body commercial aircraft.
  • GEnx: GE Aerospace's high-bypass jet engine for wide-body aircraft, notably the Boeing 787 and 747-8.
  • CFM56: A widely used turbofan engine produced by CFM International, powering many narrow-body aircraft fleets globally.
  • Shop Visit: A scheduled engine overhaul event, typically involving disassembly, inspection, and part replacement, which directly generates significant aftermarket revenue.
  • Durability Package: Engine platform-specific upgrades introduced by GE Aerospace to extend time on wing and interval between shop visits.
  • HPT Blade: High-pressure turbine blade, a key engine component central to performance, efficiency, and durability enhancements referenced in call product updates.
  • CFM RISE: The CFM consortium's "Revolutionary Innovation for Sustainable Engines" program, developing an open fan engine architecture for next-generation narrow-body aircraft.
