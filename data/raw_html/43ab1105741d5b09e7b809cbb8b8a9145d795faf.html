SoFi (SOFI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 30, 2026 at 10:43AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Jan. 30, 2026 at 8 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Anthony Noto
  • Chief Financial Officer — Chris Lapointe

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Member Growth -- SoFi (SOFI 0.28%) total members reached 13.7 million, rising 35%, with a record 1 million new members added in the quarter.
  • Product Growth -- 1.6 million new products added, up 37%, bringing the total over 20 million.
  • Adjusted Net Revenue -- $1.013 billion for the quarter, up 37%, marking the company's first $1 billion quarter.
  • Financial Services and Tech Platform Revenue -- $579 million, growing 61%, and constituting 57% of total revenue.
  • Lending Segment Revenue -- $486 million in the quarter, up 15%, primarily driven by originations.
  • Total Loan Originations -- Fourth quarter record at $10.5 billion, up 46%, with personal loan originations at $7.5 billion (up 43%), student loans at $1.9 billion (up 38%), and home loans at $1.1 billion (nearly double year over year).
  • Fee-Based Revenue -- $443 million in the quarter, up 53%, now nearly $1.8 billion annualized, reflecting diversification into capital-light streams.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $318 million for the quarter, up 60%, with a 31% margin.
  • Net Income -- $174 million for the quarter at a 17% margin; earnings per share at $0.13.
  • Tangible Book Value -- Ended at $8.9 billion, up $4 billion year over year; tangible book value per share at $7.01, up 57%.
  • Rule of 40 -- Fourth quarter score of 68% was achieved.
  • Brand Awareness -- Unaided brand awareness reached 9.6%, up 250 basis points, a 33% improvement from prior year quarter.
  • SoFi Pay Expansion -- Launched in October using blockchain for international payments, now live in over 30 countries.
  • SoFi Crypto and Stablecoin -- Company re-launched crypto trading and issued its own stablecoin, SoFi USD, as the first national bank to do so on a public blockchain.
  • Deposit Growth -- Company deposits grew by $4.6 billion to $37.5 billion, driven primarily by direct deposits; 97% of deposits are from primary accounts.
  • Net Interest Margin -- Stood at 5.72% for the quarter, down 12 basis points sequentially, attributed to a shift from personal loans to home and student loans.
  • Personal Loan Credit Metrics -- Borrowers had a weighted average income of $158,000 and a FICO score of 746; charge-off rate at 2.80%, up 20 basis points sequentially but down over 50 basis points year over year.
  • Student Loan Credit Metrics -- Borrowers had a weighted average income of $149,000 and a FICO score of 765; charge-off rate at 0.76% for the quarter.
  • Capital Position -- Total capital ratio at 22.9%, more than double the regulatory minimum of 10.5%.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Adjusted net revenue expected at approximately $4.655 billion (30% growth), adjusted EBITDA at $1.6 billion (34% margin), adjusted net income at $825 million (18% margin), and adjusted EPS at $0.60, with at least 30% member growth projected.
  • Segment Guidance -- 2026 revenue growth anticipated at 40% or more for Financial Services, 23% for Lending, and 20% for Tech Platform (excluding a transitioned client).
  • Long-Term Growth Outlook -- Management expects 30% compounded annual net revenue growth and 38%-42% compounded annual EPS growth from 2025 to 2028.

Summary

Management emphasized the company's shift towards capital-light revenue streams, highlighting 44% of revenue from fees as of year-end. Strategic investments included the launch of blockchain-enabled SoFi Pay, re-entry into crypto trading, and SoFi USD stablecoin issuance, enabling immediate settlement and safe custody for members. The firm reported significant expansion in enterprise and international partnerships through its tech platform, supporting growing demand in blockchain and stablecoin technology. Management projected growth acceleration in 2027-2028 due to maturing products like crypto, brokerage, and business banking, positioning the company for further diversification. The company indicated continued willingness to invest in technology platform capabilities and international expansion, while maintaining a high acquisition bar for inorganic opportunities.

  • Anthony Noto said, "I really want us to get that escape velocity where the amount that we spend becomes more and more efficient," targeting meaningful improvements in customer acquisition cost via product quality and word of mouth.
  • With 97% of deposits from direct depositors, management described these relationships as "high-quality primary account relationships" and indicated stickiness in funding base.
  • Chris Lapointe highlighted, "we have the luxury of, a, choosing to drive capital-light fee-based revenue through our strong capital market pipeline, particularly in the loan platform business, as you mentioned, where we just signed a new partner, and we have several partners at final term sheet stages; or b, we have the opportunity to keep these higher returning assets on our balance sheet and putting our newly raised capital to work," citing strong capital optionality from recent capital raises.
  • The company disclosed plans to launch business banking in 2026, aiming to serve both fiat and cryptocurrencies and to provide infrastructure and custody, stablecoin as a service, and institutional solutions.
  • Management confirmed that its long-term goal remains a 20%-30% return on equity, but indicated that near-term priorities center on reinvesting for revenue growth over maximizing near-term margins.
  • Anthony Noto stated, "We still believe it's in the 20% to 30% range," while cautioning that aggressive reinvestment will continue as long as growth persists above 15% annually.

Industry glossary

  • Loan Platform Business (LTV): Third-party origination and distribution channel through which SoFi facilitates loans on behalf of partner institutions, generating fee-based, capital-light revenue.
  • Rule of 40: A financial metric calculated as the sum of revenue growth rate and EBITDA margin, commonly used to assess growth efficiency in technology and fintech firms.
  • Stablecoin: Digital asset pegged to a fiat currency, in this case SoFi USD, designed for use in blockchain-based payments and settlement.
  • Fee-Based Revenue: Income derived from non-lending sources such as interchange, referral fees, brokerage commissions, and technology platform services.
