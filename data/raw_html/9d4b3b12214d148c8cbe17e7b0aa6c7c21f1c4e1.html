Terex (TEX) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 9:54AM EST

Date

Wednesday, Feb. 11, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Simon Meester
  • Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Jennifer Kong-Picarello

Takeaways

  • Merger completion -- Terex (TEX 1.88%) concluded its merger with REV Group, resulting in a new specialty vehicle segment led by legacy REV management, with no organizational changes outside corporate functions.
  • Synergy targets -- Management expects to achieve $75 million in run-rate synergies from the REV merger by 2028, with approximately half realized within the next twelve months, primarily through eliminating duplicate corporate costs.
  • Q4 net sales -- Total net sales reached $1.3 billion, a 6% increase year over year; legacy sales excluding ESG grew by 5%.
  • Q4 operating margin -- Operating margin was 9.3%, up 150 basis points from the prior year, reflecting improvements across all three segments.
  • Q4 EBITDA -- EBITDA totaled $141 million or 10.6% of sales, 140 basis points higher than last year.
  • Q4 free cash flow -- Free cash flow was $172 million, $43 million higher than last year, attributed to higher operating income and improved working capital.
  • Full-year net sales -- Net sales grew 6% to $5.4 billion, supported by the ESG acquisition; legacy sales declined 11%.
  • Full-year EBITDA -- EBITDA reached $635 million or 11.7% margin.
  • Full-year operating margin -- Operating margin was 10.4%, down 90 basis points from the previous year due to lower volumes in Aerials and Materials Processing, and higher tariff costs, offset by ESG contributions.
  • Full-year free cash flow -- Free cash flow increased 71% to $325 million, resulting in a 147% cash conversion rate.
  • Interest and other expenses -- Interest and other expenses totaled $172 million for the year, up $89 million, mainly due to ESG-related financing costs; fourth quarter interest and other expenses were $43 million, $4 million higher than Q4 of the prior year.
  • Tax rate -- The full-year effective tax rate was 17.2%, consistent with last year; Q4 tax rate was 8.1% due to favorable one-time tax factors.
  • Environmental solutions segment Q4 -- Sales were $428 million, up 14.1% on a pro forma basis; operating margin grew by 90 basis points to 18.5%, led by utilities growth.
  • Environmental solutions segment full-year -- Sales rose 12.7% pro forma to $1.7 billion; full-year operating margin improved to 18.8%, up 220 basis points from 2024.
  • Materials processing Q4 -- Sales were $428 million, down 2.5%; excluding divested clean businesses, sales grew 2.8%; Q4 operating margin reached 13.7% after margin improvements and pricing actions.
  • Materials processing full-year -- Sales fell 11.6% to $1.7 billion, attributed to channel adjustments in the first half.
  • Aerials Q4 -- Sales grew 6.9%, with operating margin at 2.6%, up 200 basis points; tariff headwinds partially offset margin improvement, with limited mitigation in the period.
  • Bookings Q4 -- Total bookings of $1.9 billion increased 32% on a pro forma basis, including Environmental Solutions up 16%, Materials Processing up 24% (or 32% excluding clean businesses), and Aerials up 46% to $971 million.
  • Backlog -- Environmental Solutions segment ended with $1.1 billion backlog; Materials Processing backlog was up $71 million year over year ($100 million higher when adjusted for clean business divestiture); Aerials backlog at $906 million.
  • 2026 sales outlook -- Company forecasts 5% pro forma sales growth for 2026, targeting $7.5 billion-$8.1 billion in net sales for the combined entity, including eleven months of REV contribution.
  • 2026 EBITDA outlook -- Pro forma EBITDA expected to grow by approximately $100 million or 12% to a $930 million-$1 billion range (12.4% margin at midpoint), factoring in $28 million of projected synergies for 2026.
  • 2026 EPS guidance -- Forecasted EPS is $4.50-$5 with a weighted average share count of 111 million; guidance is 3% lower due to the dilutive impact from increased share count post-merger.
  • Interest and tax outlook for 2026 -- Interest and other expense for 2026 expected at approximately $190 million, with an effective tax rate of 21% due to higher U.S. dollar income.
  • 2026 cash conversion -- Full-year cash conversion rate expected at 80%-90% of net income, including transaction and synergy costs.
  • Segment guidance 2026 -- Environmental Solutions expected to grow mid-single digits; ESG subsegment guided flat, with potential upside in the second half pending fleet requirements and EPA regulations; Materials Processing expected to return to high single-digit growth with margin improvement; Specialty Vehicles segment targets high single-digit sales growth and EBITDA margin improvement to approximately 12.5% pro forma; Aerials expected to deliver flat sales and margin profile due to ongoing full-year tariff effects, offset by efficiency and pricing actions.
  • Capital allocation -- Over $118 million in 2025 capital expenditures focused on automation, innovation, throughput, and efficiency; $98 million returned to shareholders via dividends and repurchases; no near-term debt repayments expected as maturities extend until 2029.

Summary

The merger with REV Group positions Terex to expand its specialty vehicle segment, with management emphasizing operational continuity and synergy realization. The company projects sequential synergy capture, targeting $75 million in run-rate EBITDA by 2028, and continues to focus on incremental margin through productivity and aftermarket expansion. The 2026 outlook anticipates pro forma sales growth of 5%, with $930 million-$1 billion in projected EBITDA, but incorporates modest EPS dilution of 3% from an increased share base and a higher effective tax rate of 21%. Integration of ESG and full capture of targeted synergies have supported improved cash conversion, with working capital discipline and segment expansion expected to offset tariff and volume-related pressures.

  • Simon Meester described customer backlog visibility in specialty vehicles as "roughly two years," driven by strong bookings carrying forward operational momentum.
  • Jennifer Kong-Picarello addressed the margin impact of full-year tariffs in Aerials, stating, "we're offsetting that to productivity and price. For a net impact of flat. Throughout the year," and forecasted that price/cost neutrality would favor the second half of 2026.
  • Management noted capacity expansion plans in utilities, targeting a 20%-30% increase over two years, with roughly half expected online in 2026 to meet anticipated electric grid upgrade demand.
  • The active strategic review of the Aerials business continues, with Meester highlighting "strong inbound interest" and a deliberate evaluation process aimed at maximizing shareholder value.
  • Specialty Vehicles EBITDA margins are projected in the "12.5% on a pro forma basis," with incremental margin to be highest in Q2 and Q3 before seasonal moderation in Q4.
  • Management provided for optional use of cash proceeds from any potential Aerials divestiture, emphasizing flexibility between debt reduction, share repurchase, and reinvestment in utilities and specialty vehicles.

Industry glossary

  • ESG: Environmental Solutions Group, a division acquired by Terex focusing on refuse and utility vehicles.
  • SV: Specialty Vehicles segment, formed following the REV Group merger and comprising emergency and recreational vehicle businesses.
  • MP: Materials Processing segment, covering crushing, screening, and material handling solutions.
  • HRC: Hot-Rolled Coil, a commonly referenced steel input for manufacturing heavy equipment.
  • Book to bill: The ratio of orders received (bookings) to products shipped and billed, indicating demand momentum relative to current revenue.
