Earnings Call Transcript

Telefonica Brasil (VIV) Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Oct 31, 2025 at 10:25AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Friday, October 31, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

Call participants

Chief Executive Officer — Christian Gebara

Chief Financial Officer — David Melcon

Head of Investor Relations — Juan Pedro Suarez Carneiro

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

Total Revenue -- BRL 14.9 billion, up 6.5% year over year, driven by both mobile and fixed service growth.

Mobile Revenue -- Mobile service revenue grew 5.5% year over year with postpaid service revenue up 8% year over year and prepaid service revenue down 7.6% year over year.

Postpaid Segment -- Postpaid segment accesses increased 7.3% year over year, reaching approximately 50 million and making up 68% of the mobile base.

Fiber (FTTH) Accesses -- Rose 12.7% year over year to 7.6 million homes; footprint expanded to 30.5 million homes, with a 2.2 million increase in the last twelve months.

Fixed Service Revenue -- Fixed services revenue increased 9.6% year over year.

EBITDA -- Up 9% to BRL 6.5 billion, with a margin expansion to 43.4% (up 100 bps).

Operating Cash Flow -- BRL 11.2 billion for the first nine months, up 12.4% compared to the same period last year.

Net Income -- Reached BRL 4.3 billion for the first nine months, up 13.4% year over year.

Free Cash Flow -- Totaled BRL 6.9 billion, with a 15.6% margin.

Shareholder Returns -- BRL 5.7 billion was distributed through interest on capital, capital reduction, and buybacks from January to October; additional BRL 2 billion in interest on capital declared for payment before April 2026.

Churn -- Postpaid churn excluding machine-to-machine and dongles was 0.98%; fifth consecutive quarter of fiber churn reduction.

ARPU (Mobile) -- Increased 3.9% to a record BRL 31.5.

Vivo Total (Convergent Offer) -- Subscriber base up 52.7% year over year, now encompasses 62% of all fiber accesses; 85% of FTTH sales in stores bundle with mobile.

Take-Up Ratio (Fiber) -- Improved to 24.9%, reflecting better demand and conversion.

New Businesses -- Now represent 11.7% of total revenue over the last twelve months, up two percentage points year over year; B2C new business revenue up 15.3% over the last twelve months and B2B digital revenue up 34.2% over the past twelve months.

IoT Partnership -- Signed the world’s largest IoT deal with SAP SP for 4.4 million smart water meters to be installed by 2029.

CapEx -- BRL 6.9 billion for the first nine months, up 3% year over year; CapEx to revenue ratio down 60 bps to 15.7%, indicating improved efficiency.

Net Debt -- Net cash position at BRL 3 billion; net debt including IFRS 16 effects at BRL 11 billion as of September 30 with 0.5x leverage ratio on last twelve months’ EBITDA (including IFRS 16 effects).

Asset Sales -- BRL 232 million net gain from concession-related asset sales; proceeds included BRL 199 million from real estate and BRL 34 million from copper.

Buyback Program -- 48.4 million shares repurchased since January, equal to 1.5% of capital stock as of October; program of up to EUR 1.75 billion to be repurchased until February 2026.

Summary

Telefônica Brasil (VIV +1.30%) reported broad-based revenue and profitability gains in the first nine months, emphasizing double-digit year-over-year growth in fiber and B2B digital services and single-digit growth in mobile postpaid. Management highlighted record net additions in both postpaid and fiber, supported by declining churn and ARPU uplift driven by convergent offerings such as Vivo Total. The company executed Brazil’s largest IoT deployment agreement, underscoring expanding B2B digital penetration. Significant progress was made on concession-related asset sales, with management confirming expectations for continued positive cash effects through 2028. The call disclosed sequential improvements in prepaid revenues, and a proactive stance on future CapEx rationalization tied to both organic growth and pending M&A approvals.

Chief Executive Officer Gebara stated, “we have positive trends of mobile service evolution in the next quarters,” referencing both postpaid and a recovery in prepaid results.

Chief Financial Officer Melcon noted, “and is almost flattish compared to the last four quarters. So this is important because that shows positive trends resulting from the negotiation that we are having with the towers company.”

Chief Executive Officer Gebara described copper asset sales as entering “full speed starting next quarter,” projecting increased cash benefits through ongoing execution.

In response to fiber ARPU declines, Gebara said, “that our strategy is driven by selling more to the same customer, convergence being the key element.”

Management confirmed no material impact on CapEx from the pending Sea Brazil acquisition, stating the EBITDA improvement will offset incremental capital expenditure needs.

Industry glossary

FTTH: Fiber-to-the-home broadband service delivering high-speed internet directly to residential premises over optical fiber.

Vivo Total: Telefônica Brasil’s convergent product bundle combining fiber broadband and mobile postpaid services under a single offer.

IoT: Internet of Things; refers to the connected network of physical devices (e.g., smart water meters) communicating data over the company’s infrastructure.

Churn: The rate at which customers discontinue service within a period; lower churn indicates higher customer retention.
