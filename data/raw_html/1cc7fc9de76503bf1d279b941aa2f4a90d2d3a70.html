Workday (WDAY) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 25, 2025 at 5:40PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, Nov. 25, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Carl M. Eschenbach
  • Chief Financial Officer — Zane Rowe
  • President, Product and Technology — Garrett Katzmeyer
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Justin Allen Furby

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Subscription Revenue -- $2.244 billion, up 15% year over year.
  • Professional Services Revenue -- $188 million, contributing to total revenue of $2.432 billion, which grew 13%.
  • U.S. Revenue -- $1.825 billion, up 12%.
  • International Revenue -- $607 million, up 13%.
  • 12-Month Subscription Revenue Backlog (CRPO) -- $8.21 billion at quarter end, increasing 17.6%; the Paradox acquisition contributed over one point of CRPO growth and was not part of initial backlog guidance.
  • Total Subscription Revenue Backlog -- $25.96 billion, up 17% year over year, with gross revenue retention at 97%.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin -- 28.5% for the quarter; non-GAAP operating income of $692 million.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $588 million, representing 45% growth.
  • Share Repurchases -- $803 million repurchased during the quarter, and $1.4 billion year to date; company plans an additional $3.6 billion through fiscal 2027, targeting $5 billion in total.
  • Cash and Marketable Securities -- $6.8 billion at quarter end.
  • Employee Headcount -- 20,588 as of October 31, including about 600 from the Paradox acquisition.
  • AI Adoption -- Over 75% of core customers use Workday Illuminate AI, facilitating more than 1 billion AI actions this year; more than three-quarters of net new deals, and 35% of customer expansions, included at least one AI product.
  • AI Revenue Impact -- AI products contributed more than 1.5 percentage points of ARR growth this quarter, excluding Paradox.
  • Notable Product Highlights -- Eversort delivered a record quarter; XtendPro net new ACV grew over 50% year over year.
  • Workday Go Expansion -- Newly launched features include global payroll, an expanded partner network, and an AI deployment agent that can reduce implementation time by up to 25%.
  • Industry Milestone -- Healthcare surpassed $1 billion in ARR, becoming Workday (WDAY 0.49%)’s sixth industry to cross this threshold.
  • Partner Ecosystem -- Over 20% of net new ACV sourced through partners in the quarter; added new partners such as Chime, Spring Health, Strata, and expanded Microsoft partnership.
  • Acquisitions -- Closed Paradox (AI native recruiting), Sana (AI-native platform for learning and UI/UX), with announced intent to acquire Pipedream (low-code integration for AI agents).
  • International Developments -- Solid regional performance in EMEA, APAC, and Japan; launched Workday EU Sovereign Cloud and established an AI Center of Excellence in Dublin.
  • Q4 Subscription Revenue Guidance -- Projected to be $2.355 billion, representing 15% growth, including Sana and DIA contract contributions.
  • Fiscal 2026 Guidance (ending Jan. 31, 2026) -- Subscription revenue expected at $8.828 billion, growth of 14%; non-GAAP operating margin projected at approximately 29% for the full year.
  • Cash Flow Guidance -- Fiscal 2026 operating cash flow forecast raised to $2.9 billion; free cash flow expected at $2.7 billion, up 23%.
  • Long-Term Outlook -- Subscription revenue CAGR targeted at 12%-15% through fiscal 2028, with continued margin expansion; fiscal 2027 subscription revenue growth expected at approximately 13%.
  • CRPO Guidance for Q4 -- Expected to grow 15%-16%, with Sana and tenant contracts impacting growth by a combined 1.25 percentage points.

SUMMARY

Management attributed quarterly momentum to strong cross-industry demand, noting that half of net new global deals included both HR and finance solutions. Executives highlighted the rapid integration of recent AI-focused acquisitions, particularly the immediate revenue and expanded market reach from Paradox and Sana. Detailed commentary described robust federal sector adoption with a high-confidence $15 million DIA contract recognized in Q4, and Health Care’s new $1 billion ARR milestone. Guidance reflects confidence in sequential and annual revenue growth trajectories, with explicit expectations for double-digit subscription revenue growth and margin expansion through fiscal 2028.

  • CEO Eschenbach emphasized, "The narrative we've been hearing the last couple of quarters is changing, and it's coming back to those who are highly penetrated in the enterprise already providing value, and are deeply trusted by our customers."
  • President Katzmeyer said that 35% of sales back to the customer base included AI, with about $2.50 of HiredScore recruiting agent sold for every $1 of standard recruiting.
  • International business momentum was attributed to product localization, partner-led market entry, and targeted talent investments, with EMEA, APAC, and Japan all noted as outperforming.
  • Guidance reiterated sequential revenue growth will be flat in Q1 fiscal 2027 due to Q4 DIA contract timing, and the CRPO growth for Q4 includes approximately 0.25 points from Sana and over a point from tenants.
  • Management described a continued acceleration in share repurchases, with $4.4 billion remaining under authorization as of October 31.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ACV: Annual Contract Value; the annualized value of subscription contracts, often used to measure sales growth and expansion.
  • ARR: Annual Recurring Revenue; a key metric representing the value of recurring subscription revenues normalized to a one-year period.
  • CRPO: Current Remaining Performance Obligation; forward-looking metric measuring contracted subscription revenue expected to be recognized in the next twelve months.
  • FedRAMP: Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program; a U.S. government program for standardized security assessment, authorization, and monitoring for cloud products and services.
  • SLED: State, Local, and Education; a vertical market segment referring to public sector clients excluding federal agencies.
  • Workday Go: Workday’s offering for the medium enterprise market, bundled with features such as global payroll and AI deployment agents.
  • Workday Illuminate AI: Company’s AI-driven feature suite, integrated across HR and finance applications to automate and enhance business decisions and workflows.
