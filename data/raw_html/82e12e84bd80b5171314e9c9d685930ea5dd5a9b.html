Ollie's (OLLI) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Sep 2, 2025 at 5:31PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, August 28, 2025, at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Eric van der Valk
  • Chief Financial Officer — Robert F. Helm

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Net Sales-- $680 million in net sales, up 18%, driven by new store openings and comparable store sales growth.
  • Comparable Store Sales-- Up 5%, with increases attributed primarily to transaction growth and strong demand for consumer staples.
  • Gross Margin-- Increased 200 basis points to 39.9%, driven by lower supply chain costs, higher merchandise margins, and improved shrink.
  • SG&A Expense as % of Net Sales-- Rose 60 basis points to 25.8%, mainly due to higher medical and casualty claims and slightly higher store labor expenses.
  • Adjusted Net Income-- Adjusted net income was $61 million.
  • Adjusted Earnings Per Share-- Adjusted earnings per share increased 26.9% to $0.99.
  • Adjusted EBITDA-- Adjusted EBITDA increased 26% to $94 million, and adjusted EBITDA margin expanded 90 basis points to 13.8%.
  • Cash & Investments-- $460 million total, up over $100 million or 30%, with no meaningful long-term debt at quarter end.
  • Inventories-- Inventories increased 20% year-over-year, primarily due to accelerating store growth and higher in-transit inventory.
  • Capital Expenditures-- $26 million in capital expenditures, spent mainly on new store openings, bankruptcy-acquired store buildouts, and supply chain improvements.
  • Share Repurchases-- $12 million repurchased, with $304 million remaining on authorization.
  • Store Count-- 613 at period end, reflecting a 17% increase year-over-year, including 29 new stores opened during the quarter.
  • Ollie’s Army Members-- 16.1 million, up 10.6%, with Ollie’s Army members accounting for more than 80% of total sales.
  • Ollie’s Days Event Impact-- CFO Helm stated the revamped event “added approximately 100 basis points to comp store sales,” and customer acquisition for the week was up almost 60%.
  • 2025 Guidance Raised-- Updated full-year outlook for fiscal 2025 includes 85 new store openings, net sales of $2.631 billion to $2.644 billion, comparable store sales growth of 3%-3.5%, gross margin of 40.3%, operating income of $292-$298 million, adjusted net income of $233-$237 million, and adjusted EPS of $3.76-$3.84. The fiscal year ends on August 2, 2025.
  • New Store Economics-- CFO Helm said new stores in fiscal 2025 are "nicely performing above plan," and organic store payback periods remain consistent with historic levels.
  • Distribution Center Expansion-- The company plans to add approximately 200,000 square feet to both the Texas and Illinois facilities, expanding capacity by 100 stores over the next 18 months.
  • Closeout Market Position-- CEO van der Valk noted, "our inventory was up 20% at the end of Q2, which is a strong indicator of robust deal flow."

Summary

Ollie's Bargain Outlet (OLLI 2.68%) delivered results ahead of internal expectations across key performance metrics and raised its full-year sales and earnings outlook for fiscal 2025. Management highlighted the effectiveness of its accelerated unit expansion and Ollie’s Army loyalty strategy, with improved customer acquisition and strong new store performance fueling portfolio growth. Gross margin improvement in the second quarter of fiscal 2025 was driven by lower supply chain costs, favorable merchandise margins from robust deal flow, and reduced shrink. Inventory increased 20% year-over-year, underscoring the company's increased buying activity in a consolidating closeout market. The fiscal year ends on Aug. 2, 2025.

  • CFO Helm described SG&A pressure in the first half of fiscal 2025 as primarily due to “an unusually high number of severe medical cases,” with expectations for this cost to normalize as the year progresses.
  • The company is “committed to delivering double-digit annual unit growth moving forward,” with guidance for 85 new stores in fiscal 2025 and indications of further acceleration in 2026 as market opportunities persist.
  • Loyalty program members spend over 40% more per visit than nonmembers, according to CEO van der Valk.
  • New and acquired stores from bankrupt retailers such as Big Lots showed “not a noticeable difference” on top-line performance compared to organic openings, with better operating margins due to lower rents on acquired locations.
  • Management maintained a conservative stance by not updating fourth-quarter metrics beyond internal expectations for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2025, and cited long-term adherence to a 1%-2% comparable sales growth algorithm outside periods of market-driven opportunity.

Industry glossary

  • Dark Rent: Lease payments for store sites acquired but not yet fully operational, often related to locations obtained through retailer bankruptcies.
  • Warm Box: A recently vacated retail property retained in a partially operational or "turnkey" condition, facilitating rapid store openings with lower buildout costs.
  • 4 Walls: A performance metric referring to profits or margins generated by a store location, excluding allocation of corporate overhead.
  • Payback Period: The length of time required for a new store to recoup its original investment from cumulative profits.
  • IMU: Initial Markup, measuring the difference between merchandise cost and original retail price at the time of purchase.
