Earnings Call Transcript

RPM (RPM) Q3 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 8, 2026 at 3:22PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, April 8, 2026 10 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chair and Chief Executive Officer — Frank Sullivan
  • Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Rusty Gordon
  • Vice President, Controller and Chief Accounting Officer — Michael Laroche
  • Vice President — Matthew Schlarb

TAKEAWAYS

  • Sales Growth -- Consolidated sales increased nearly 9%, setting a record and driven by high-performance building solutions, M&A, and foreign exchange, partially offset by soft DIY demand.
  • Adjusted EBIT -- Adjusted EBIT rose nearly 50%, reaching a record as volume gains and SG&A optimization actions improved fixed cost utilization, offsetting higher health care costs.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Adjusted EPS also achieved a record, benefiting primarily from higher adjusted EBIT.
  • Regional Performance -- North America grew 6.3%, Europe grew over 20% (driven by M&A and FX), while emerging markets growth was led by Africa and Middle East.
  • Green Belt Program Savings -- Green Belt program generated more than $50 million in savings to date, with $30 million in the current pipeline.
  • SG&A Optimization Actions -- Cost initiatives generated approximately $5 million in savings in the quarter, with an anticipated favorable P&L impact of around $20 million in the fourth quarter.
  • Raw Material Inflation -- Company expects raw material inflation of 1%-2% in the fourth quarter and mid- to high single-digit range in the first quarter of fiscal 2027.
  • Cash Flow from Operations -- Year-to-date cash flow from operations totaled $656.7 million, the second highest in company history.
  • Capital Returns -- Dividends and share repurchases totaled $255.3 million through the first nine months, up 5.2% year over year.
  • Liquidity -- Liquidity stood at $1.02 billion, supporting continued M&A activity.
  • Kalzip Acquisition -- Closed acquisition of Kalzip, with 2024 sales of approximately EUR 75 million; expected to be accretive to margins after full integration.
  • Maintenance, Restoration, and Energy Efficiencies -- Approximately two-thirds of sales derive from these areas, highlighted as a key market differentiator during volatility.
  • Segment Highlights -- Construction Products and Performance Coatings Groups both delivered record sales and adjusted EBIT, aided by strong North American performance, improved mix, and continued margin expansion.
  • Plant Consolidation Costs -- Temporary inefficiencies from facility consolidations amounted to over $6 million, approximately two-thirds in Consumer Group, with most remaining impacts expected to end by fall.
  • Price Increases -- Price increases began in the third quarter (over 1%), with further actions under way to offset rising inflation; pricing initiatives are being dynamically adjusted by business and geography.

RISKS

  • Frank Sullivan said, "Recent geopolitical events have created supply chain disruptions and increased raw material costs," resulting in cost impacts most acute in Middle East, Africa, and Asia Pacific.
  • Adjusted EBIT outlook remains "wider-than-normal" due to "heightened uncertainty," with guidance maintained at low to high single-digit growth, reflecting volatility in raw material markets.
  • Organic sales in the Consumer Group have contracted for four consecutive quarters, with continued weakness in DIY demand and management noting, "we've been waiting for easier comps for 18 months, and they're not coming."
  • Temporary facility consolidation inefficiencies exceeded $6 million in the quarter and will persist into the fall, according to Gordon.

SUMMARY

Management reaffirmed mid-single-digit revenue growth targets for the upcoming quarter despite geopolitical-driven cost pressures and ongoing volatility, underpinned by strong M&A activity and operational discipline. Executives highlighted realized and pending SG&A savings, ongoing Green Belt program benefits, and sustained record-setting cash flows as core supports for financial flexibility and future margin upside. Pricing actions, contract coverage, and procurement strategies were identified as immediate levers to address anticipated acceleration in raw material inflation, particularly for the first quarter of fiscal 2027. The integration of Kalzip is expected to broaden system offerings, while maintenance and restoration continue to underpin the company's differentiated value proposition in volatile end markets.

  • Frank Sullivan indicated that although March results in the Middle East were strong, "That's going to come to a halt in April and May because that's the one area where supply is challenged in terms of getting raw materials back into our plants."
  • Rising health care expenses, particularly from the adoption of weight loss drugs into the benefit plan, added $4 million in the quarter and are expected to stabilize, not reverse, in fiscal 2027.
  • Europe's organic revenue excluding M&A and FX was slightly down, primarily due to the ongoing impacts of war in Ukraine and Middle East disruptions, although profit margins have improved through consolidation and strategic actions.
  • Performance Coatings Group is seeing strength in maintenance, fireproofing, and industrial coatings, with share gains in the Industrial Coatings Group and synergy development with Carboline and Stonhard businesses.
  • Management emphasized that the wide range in EBIT guidance is directly tied to "if things get worse in the Middle East, that could clearly impact our results in the next couple of months."
  • The company anticipates further benefit from procurement-led supply contracts and FIFO accounting, which delays the P&L impact of cost changes, providing the company additional time to respond.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • SG&A Optimization: Strategic actions focused on reducing selling, general, and administrative expenses to enhance efficiency and margin performance.
  • Green Belt Program: RPM International (RPM 1.59%)'s internal operational improvement initiative, training employees in process optimization and cost savings methodologies.
  • FIFO Accounting: Inventory valuation method (First-In, First-Out) that results in newer inventory costs being reflected later in the income statement, effectively delaying the recognition of current cost increases.
  • MAP: The company's "Margin Acceleration Plan" — a comprehensive multi-year initiative to drive margin improvement through operational efficiencies and cash flow enhancements.
