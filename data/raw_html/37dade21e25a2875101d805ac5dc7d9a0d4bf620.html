Shopify (SHOP) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 2:20PM EST

Date

Feb. 11, 2026, 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President — Harley Finkelstein
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jeff Hoffmeister
  • Director of Investor Relations — Carrie Gillard

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $11.6 billion for the year, up 30% from 2024, marking an acceleration from the prior year's 26% growth rate.
  • Q4 revenue -- $3.01 billion, the first quarter exceeding $3 billion, surpassing the total annual 2020 revenue in a single quarter.
  • Gross merchandise volume (GMV) -- $378 billion for the year, up 29% year over year, with Q4 GMV of $124 billion, a 31% increase (29% constant currency), the first quarter above $100 billion.
  • North America revenue -- Increased 28%, maintaining over 14% share of U.S. e-commerce, with significant merchant wins including Estee Lauder Companies, and Starbucks.
  • International revenue -- Grew 36% year over year, now representing nearly half of all merchants, with major new brands such as Kering Beauty and expanded presence in Europe.
  • Offline channel revenue -- $748 million, up 27%, driven by brands like Tom Ford adopting Shopify for physical retail.
  • Q4 offline GMV -- Increased 29% year over year, further broadening Shopify's ecosystem reach.
  • B2B GMV -- Rose 96% for the year and 84% in Q4, reflecting rapid platform expansion in the business-to-business segment.
  • Shop Pay GMV -- Processed $43 billion in Q4, representing over 50% of U.S. gross payment volume (GPV).
  • Merchant solutions revenue -- Grew 35% in Q4, driven by GMV growth and increased penetration of Shopify Payments.
  • Shopify Payments -- Processed $84 billion of GMV in Q4, or 68% of total, up 38% from the prior year and 4 percentage points above Q4 2024.
  • Subscription solutions revenue -- Increased 17% in Q4, due to a higher proportion of merchants on premium plans and higher platform fees.
  • Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) -- Q4 MRR increased 15%, with Plus plan contributing 34% of total MRR, one point higher than a year earlier.
  • Gross profit -- Q4 gross profit up 25% and full-year gross profit up 24%; subscription solutions gross margin reached 81%, merchant solutions at 36.8%.
  • Operating expenses -- $1 billion for Q4 (29% of revenue) and 35% for the year, both improving by 3 percentage points from 2024.
  • Free cash flow -- $2 billion for the year, a 26% increase and a 17% free cash flow margin; $715 million in Q4 (19% margin).
  • Share repurchase program -- Board approved up to $2 billion in share repurchases, following cash settlement of convertible notes.
  • AI search orders -- Orders from AI search channels increased 15x since January 2025, albeit from a small base.
  • Shop Campaigns -- Revenue doubled, and merchant adoption tripled in 2025, with advertising now spanning eight major digital channels.
  • Sidekick AI assistant -- In three weeks post-launch, generated almost 4,000 custom apps, over 29,000 automations, almost 355,000 task lists, and edited over 1.2 million photos.
  • Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) -- Launched as the first open standard for AI-based commerce, co-developed with Google and already adopted by leading retailers.
  • Q1 2026 outlook -- Projected revenue growth in the low 30% range, gross profit dollar growth in the high 20s, operating expenses at 37%-38% of revenue, and free cash flow margin in the low to mid-teens, with tax headwinds expected to affect intra-quarter cash flow.

Summary

Shopify (SHOP 1.42%) delivered record revenue and GMV in Q4 and the full year, powered by accelerated international expansion and strong B2B growth. AI-driven commerce, highlighted by the Universal Commerce Protocol, dramatically increased orders from AI search while Shop Pay and Shop Campaigns set new adoption records for payments and advertising reach. Substantial operating leverage enabled the company to achieve improved margins and drove a 26% year-over-year increase in free cash flow, with share repurchases authorized reflecting confidence in continued financial momentum. Management projects that 2026 will see further AI-enabled surface expansion, a continued global focus, and incremental efficiency gains despite near-term tax impacts on Q1 cash flow.

  • Shopify Payments expanded to 60 new countries, and local payment methods were broadened in Europe, the U.K., and Canada.
  • U.S. DC Stablecoin was introduced as the first built-in cryptocurrency option for Shopify Payments.
  • Shopify Capital entered eight countries, with Shop tracking now available in over 20, and AI-powered translations supporting eight more languages.
  • Managed Markets 2.0 launched, providing merchants with integrated local payout speeds and compliance checks for global sales.
  • Sidekick Pulse and simgym represent additional AI-based productivity features, driving merchant adoption and automation.
  • Shopify Product Network, an opt-in feature, allows cross-store product recommendations, creating new merchant-to-merchant commission opportunities.
  • Operating leverage was achieved across R&D, sales/marketing, and G&A, attributed to AI, automation, and disciplined headcount.
  • CFO Hoffmeister highlighted that Q4 transaction loans and losses returned to the historical trend of approximately 3% of revenue, and scale with payment products.
  • Management affirmed that agentic commerce transactions retain economic parity with online store transactions for Shopify merchants.
  • President Finkelstein stated, "LLM do not bypass Shopify's checkout. And in fact, I think this is really where Shopify shines," emphasizing Shopify's central role in complex commerce orchestration.

Industry glossary

  • GMV (Gross merchandise volume): Total transaction value of merchandise sold through the platform within a defined period, before deductions like returns or discounts.
  • GPV (Gross payment volume): Total value of payments processed through Shopify Payments during the period.
  • B2B: Business-to-business commerce segment, as opposed to direct-to-consumer.
  • Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP): Open technical standard, co-developed by Shopify and Google, that enables agentic (AI-based) commerce across any brand or AI agent, ensuring end-to-end transaction support and payment neutrality.
  • Agentic commerce: Digital commerce model where AI agents communicate and transact autonomously between buyers and sellers, typically across multiple platforms.
  • Sidekick: Shopify's proprietary AI assistant that automates tasks and provides data-driven business recommendations for merchants on its platform.
