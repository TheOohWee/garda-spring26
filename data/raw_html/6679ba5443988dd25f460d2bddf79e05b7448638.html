Earnings Call Transcript

Trimble (TRMB) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 10, 2026 at 11:23AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Wednesday, November 5, 2025 at 8 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President & Chief Executive Officer — Robert Painter
  • Chief Financial Officer — Phillip Sawarynski

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $901 million, representing 11% organic growth, outpacing previous outlook driven by AECO and Field Systems segments.
  • Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) -- $2.31 billion, up 15%, with AECO ARR reaching $1.42 billion, a 17% increase.
  • EPS -- $0.81, up 16% year over year and exceeding the high end of guidance by $0.06.
  • Recurring revenue mix -- Recurring revenue accounted for 63% of total, and software and services 78% of overall revenue.
  • Gross margin -- Improved by 90 basis points to 71.2%.
  • EBITDA margin -- 29.9%, expanding by 160 basis points year over year.
  • Field Systems ARR -- $386 million, up 18%; revenue grew 8% despite a 150 basis point subscription transition headwind.
  • AECO segment net retention -- Excluding SketchUp, net retention approximated 110%.
  • Transportation ARR -- $501 million, up 7%, delivering profitable growth in a weak freight market.
  • Operating margins by segment -- AECO at 31.8% (up 270 bps); Field Systems at 33.4% (up 40 bps); Transportation & Logistics at 25.8% (up 10 bps).
  • Share repurchases -- $50 million repurchased, leaving $273 million under current authorization.
  • Free cash flow -- $206 million year-to-date, after a $277 million cash tax payment in Q2 related to the agriculture divestiture.
  • Balance sheet position -- $233 million cash; leverage ratio at 1.2x, below the long-term target of 2.5x.
  • 2025 guidance update -- Increased revenue midpoint by $45 million to $3.565 billion and EPS midpoint by $0.10 to $3.08; maintained organic ARR growth midpoint at 14%.
  • 2027 financial targets -- On track to reach $3 billion ARR, $4 billion revenue, and 30% EBITDA margin ("3, 4, 30 framework") for fiscal 2027 (ending date to be provided by company).
  • Early fiscal 2026 revenue outlook -- Indicated mid- to high single-digit percentage growth, with further details to come next quarter for fiscal 2026 (ending date to be provided by company).

Risks

  • Painter said, "We correctly anticipated lower government revenue early in the year and have been able to contain the impact on the business, which we previously quantified as single-digit millions in the back half of 2025," reflecting continued exposure to U.S. federal government funding uncertainty.
  • Sawarynski stated the Field Systems segment would "Yes, we expect the transitions within Field Systems to continue through the next couple of years, through '27, which is what we talked about at Investor Day as far as our anchor year. So expect those to continue throughout the next couple of years," implying persistent conversion-related growth headwinds.

Summary

Trimble (TRMB 0.99%) delivered higher-than-expected top- and bottom-line results, with management raising both revenue and EPS guidance for 2025 and confirming progress toward fiscal 2027 (ending date to be provided by company) targets. The mix of recurring revenue and software/services increased, further strengthening gross and EBITDA margins and providing increased predictability. The company highlighted notable ARR and revenue growth in all segments—particularly within AECO—and strong net retention rates drove continued expansion. Share repurchase activity continued in the quarter, supported by a low leverage ratio and robust cash flows.

  • Management cited execution of the Connect & Scale strategy as a key enabler for momentum, stating that AI is "a logical extension" of this approach, with ongoing integration into both internal operations and customer solutions.
  • Trimble executed new partnerships, such as with Vermeer and Liverpool Football Club, and made workflow enhancements through product innovation including the launch of SketchUp 2026 and ProjectSight in Europe and Australia.
  • In response to analyst questions, Painter emphasized the company's unique AI data advantage, referencing "you've heard me reference before trillions, billions, millions and thousands. Trillions of dollars of construction run through Trimble, billions -- tens of billions of freight run through Trimble, millions of users of our software, hundreds of thousands of instruments and machines in the physical world run on Trimble." within Trimble's ecosystem.
  • Sawarynski clarified that growth headwinds from the Field Systems segment's subscription transition will persist through fiscal 2027 (ending date to be provided by company) and that the fiscal 2026 topline guidance of "mid- to high single digits" reflects both normalization and segment mix.

Industry glossary

  • AECO: Architecture, Engineering, Construction, and Owner segment—Trimble's unified suite serving building lifecycle management.
  • ARR: Annual Recurring Revenue—the yearly value of subscription-based or recurring revenue contracts.
  • Field Systems: Segment providing industrial IoT and data collection technology for construction and geospatial workflows.
  • EBITDA margin: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization as a percentage of total revenue, reflecting operational profitability.
  • Net retention: The rate at which recurring revenue from existing customers grows or contracts over a specific period, excluding new customer additions.
  • TC1: Trimble's bundled solution play for cross-segment subscription and workflow integration.
