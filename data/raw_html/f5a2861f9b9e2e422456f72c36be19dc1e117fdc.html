Keysight (KEYS) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 24, 2025 at 5:50PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Nov. 24, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Satish Dhanasekaran
  • Chief Financial Officer — Neil Dougherty
  • Senior Vice President, Global Marketing and Communications — Paulina Sims
  • Senior Vice President, Electronic Industrial Solutions Group — Kailesh Narayanan
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Jason Carey

RISKS

  • Chief Financial Officer Neil Dougherty said, "You know, those of you that have covered Keysight (KEYS +0.38%) for a while will know that our integration is complete, and we do a thorough integration that tends to take us on average twelve to eighteen months to complete. A significant driver of cost synergies for us is when we get systems aligned. Getting these acquisitions into our ERP environment is a big driver, and that does take time. So as you think about fiscal 2026, relatively low realized synergies in these first few quarters until we can get to that cutover point. Later in the year, I think you'll see a step function improvement in terms of synergy realization. Within a longer tail of smaller synergy realization into 2027."
  • Dougherty stated, "It will take us a while to climb back from these current 26% levels to the levels we were contemplating when we were at 29% back in fiscal 2023."

TAKEAWAYS

  • Q4 Revenue -- $1.419 billion, up 10% on a reported basis and above the high end of guidance, with core revenue growth at 9%.
  • Q4 Orders -- $1.533 billion, increasing 14% reported and 12% core, with $22 million contributed by completed acquisitions and $4 million from currency effects.
  • Earnings Per Share (EPS) -- $1.91 for the quarter, representing 16% year-over-year growth.
  • Full-Year Revenue -- $5.375 billion, up 8% as reported and 7% on a core basis.
  • Full-Year EPS -- $7.16, an increase of 14%.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Record $1.3 billion in fiscal 2025, with 30% used for $375 million in share repurchases during the year.
  • Share Repurchase Authorization -- New $1.5 billion program approved by the board.
  • Segment Performance -- Communications Solutions Group generated $990 million Q4 revenue (up 11% reported, 9% core), while Electronic Industrial Solutions Group posted $429 million (up 9% reported, 8% core).
  • Commercial Communications Revenue -- $660 million in Q4, up 12%, driven by continued strength in wireline and growth in wireless.
  • Software and Services Mix -- Accounted for approximately 37% of total revenue, with annual recurring revenue at 29%.
  • Acquisitions -- Spirent, Optical Solutions Group, and ANSYS PowerArtist closed in Q4, contributing $11 million of revenue and expected to add approximately $375 million in fiscal 2026 revenue.
  • Fiscal 2026 Guidance -- Revenue targeted at $1.53 billion to $1.55 billion in Q1 (up 19% at midpoint including acquisitions, and up 10% excluding acquisitions) and EPS guidance of $1.95 to $2.01 with approximately 173 million diluted shares.
  • Synergy Target -- Over $100 million in run-rate synergies and efficiencies anticipated post-acquisition, with the acquisitions expected to be accretive to earnings twelve months post-close.
  • Tariff Mitigation -- Both April and August tariff impacts expected to be fully offset in Q1 2026, one quarter sooner than previously communicated.

SUMMARY

Management highlighted that AI-driven demand and secular industry innovation produced double-digit order and revenue growth across both major business segments in Q4 fiscal 2025. Keysight (KEYS +0.38%) expects momentum to continue in core markets, with newly acquired assets accelerating growth in software, services, and precision navigation offerings. Tariff cost headwinds are expected to be mitigated faster than guided, and the company projects 2026 revenue expansion above its long-term 5%-7% target when excluding acquisitions. Segment leadership stated that the integration of acquired businesses will take twelve to eighteen months to fully realize profit margin improvements, resulting in "mild" EPS dilution early in the year before earnings benefit accrues. Keysight's financial model enables sustained high free cash flow, supporting large-scale capital returns and continued investment in R&D and product innovation.

  • Chief Executive Officer Satish Dhanasekaran noted that the AI and accelerated compute infrastructure build-outs drove increased test and design intensity, particularly in the wireline business, with "roughly half of our wireline business is seeing that impact."
  • Keysight's annual recurring revenue comprised 29% of the total.
  • Dhanasekaran emphasized that wireless "exceeded expectations" in 2025, aided by 5G stabilization and early traction in advanced technologies, and that growth may continue ahead of major 6G inflection points.
  • Dougherty explained, "You know, those businesses were, as we inherited them, were operating at profit levels that were lower than Keysight's. But we have committed that on a post-integration basis, we expect an accretive decrease site operating margin. So over that twelve to eighteen month period of time, we're gonna make a pretty significant increase in driving improved profitability in those businesses via this $100 million synergy and other efficiency capture."
  • Dougherty clarified that any EPS dilution from acquisitions in fiscal 2026 would be "on a percentage basis as low single digits."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Core Basis: Financial results adjusted for foreign currency impacts and acquisitions/divestitures to reflect organic growth trends.
  • Wireline: Refers to networking and communications solutions involving physical cabling or fiber (not wireless/radio-based connectivity).
  • 6G: Sixth-generation wireless communication technology, currently in research and early standardization phases.
  • LEO: Low Earth Orbit, relating to satellite constellations used in communications and navigation technologies.
  • NTN: Non-terrestrial Networks, encompassing technologies such as satellite-enabled communications beyond traditional terrestrial infrastructure.
  • CPO/LPO: Co-packaged Optics/Linear-drive Pluggable Optics, next-generation optical interconnect technologies driving high-speed data transfer at lower power.
  • MINO: Multiple-Input Multiple-Output, an advanced antenna technology used to improve wireless communication performance.
  • EMSO: Electromagnetic Spectrum Operations, involving advanced military applications and monitoring of the RF spectrum.
