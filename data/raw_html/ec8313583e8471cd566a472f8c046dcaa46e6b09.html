Equifax (EFX) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

Risks

  • 2025 Q4 EBITDA margin of 32.8% was “slightly below our October guidance,” attributed to higher incentive compensation impacting corporate expenses.
  • International revenue performance was “slightly weaker than expected, principally reflecting end market weakness in Canada and European debt management.”
  • 2026 EBITDA margins (including FICO mortgage score revenue) projected down about 30 basis points at the guidance midpoint due to increased FICO royalties.
  • 2026 international revenue growth is expected at the lower end of the segment’s long-term framework “principally from weaker economic growth in Canada and the U.K.”

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $6.075 billion for 2025, reflecting 7% reported and organic constant currency growth.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- $7.65 per share in 2025, with 2026 guidance midpoint of $8.50, up 11%.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $1.13 billion for 2025, with a 120% free cash flow conversion rate, exceeding the long-term 95% target; over $1 billion projected for 2026 with at least 100% conversion.
  • EBITDA / Margins -- 2025 EBITDA of $2 billion, 2025 Q4 EBITDA of $508 million (margin 32.8%), and 2026 projected EBITDA of $2.122 billion; 2026 EBITDA margins expected up 75 basis points (ex-FICO impact).
  • Shareholder Returns -- $1.2 billion returned in 2025, including $927 million in share repurchases and $233 million in dividends.
  • FICO Mortgage Score Revenue Impact -- FICO mortgage represented 3% of 2025 revenue, projected at 6% for 2026, which reduces reported EBITDA margins by over 200 basis points due to zero-margin pass-through; “there is no profitability on the sale of FICO MortgageScore by Equifax.”
  • EWS Segment -- Workforce Solutions revenue grew 6% for 2025 and 9% in Q4, with verification services up 8% for the year and over 10% in the quarter; EBITDA margins exceeded 51%.
  • USIS Segment -- U.S. Information Solutions achieved 10% revenue growth and margin expansion of 70 basis points to 35.2% in 2025; Q4 revenue up 12% with mortgage revenue up 33%.
  • International Segment -- Constant dollar revenue growth of 6% in 2025 and 5% in Q4, with EBITDA margin expansion of nearly 100 basis points; Q4 performance below expectations in Canada and European debt management, offset by “very good” growth in Brazil and Australia.
  • Vitality Index -- 2025 Equifax vitality index reached 15%, 500 basis points above the long-term 10% target, representing approximately $900 million of new product revenue; Q4 vitality index at a record 17%.
  • Proprietary Data Revenue -- “About 90% of Equifax revenue was generated through the direct sale or through derivative products generated from our proprietary only Equifax data.”
  • AI Deployment -- Over 400 AI patents secured or pending, including 40 added in 2025; AI-driven models delivered nearly 30% lift over legacy models; “All new models in 2025 were built using efx.ai.”
  • Government Vertical -- EWS government revenue up low double digits in Q4; “We already contracted with a few states in the first quarter on our new continuous evaluation solution,” driving momentum for further adoption.
  • Twin Database -- Over 200 million active records at year-end 2025, representing an 11% increase, with over 800 million total records; Q4 additions included five new partners.
  • 2026 Revenue Guidance -- Total revenue at the guidance midpoint expected up about 10.6% reported and 10% constant currency; ex-FICO, revenue expected up approximately 7%.
  • EPS Guidance Q1 2026 -- Expected between $1.63 and $1.73 per share, up about 10% at midpoint compared to Q1 2025.
  • Vault Verify Acquisition -- Acquired in Q4 2025 by EWS using strong free cash flow.
  • FICO Mortgage Score Pass-Through Assumption -- 2026 guidance assumes no conversion to VantageScore and 100% FICO mortgage score delivery by nationwide agencies; “We are indifferent to TriMerge resellers calculating FICO scores under the new FICO direct model.”
  • Potential Impact of Full VantageScore Conversion (Scenario) -- Full conversion in mortgage to VantageScore in 2026 would reduce revenue guidance by about $270 million, increase EBITDA by about $160 million, and raise EBITDA margins by nearly 380 basis points, with EPS increasing by about $1 per share.
  • International Cloud Migration -- Cloud completion for international operations expected by mid-2026.

Summary

Equifax (EFX 2.16%) reported 2025 revenue of $6.075 billion with organic constant currency growth of 7%, finishing at the low end but within the company’s long-term 7%-10% growth objective. The Workforce Solutions and USIS segments notably drove above-consensus fourth quarter revenue, with the government and mortgage verticals significantly outperforming management’s guidance. AI-driven product innovation fueled a vitality index of 15% for 2025 and 17% in the fourth quarter, resulting in substantial new product revenue and measurable operating leverage. The company flagged significant pass-through revenue growth and margin dilution arising from zero-profit FICO mortgage score franchise expansion, an effect that is expected to double in 2026 without impacting EBITDA dollars but reducing reported margin rates. Management provided detailed 2026 guidance reflecting 10.6% reported revenue growth and 11% EPS expansion at the midpoint, with a baseline assumption of no VantageScore conversion and explicit scenario modeling for the outsized bottom-line benefits should a transition occur.

  • CFO Gamble indicated 2026 guidance centers at the midpoint for revenue and EPS, with capital deployment priorities including “attractive bolt-on M&A” and continued “substantial capacity for share repurchases.”
  • CEO Begor stated, “90% of our revenue comes from proprietary data,” highlighting barriers to competitor disintermediation through AI or alternative data sources.
  • Management confirmed commercial adoption of cloud-based AI models for new credit, fraud, and income products in mortgage, auto, and card, with “over 1,400 customers accessing these new only Equifax products” in mortgage.
  • The international segment’s cloud migration and rollout of Australian and Canadian proprietary data exchanges are key strategic milestones forecast for 2026.
  • Scenario modeling disclosed that if VantageScore fully replaced FICO in mortgage, the company would give up pass-through revenue but realize a boost of roughly $160 million in EBITDA and roughly $1 in EPS—even with current depressed mortgage origination levels.

Industry glossary

  • Vitality Index (VI): Percentage of total company revenue derived from new products introduced in the past three years.
  • Twin Database: Equifax’s proprietary employment and income verification database aggregating records from payroll processors and direct contributors.
  • TriMerge: Industry-standard process in which mortgage lenders acquire credit files from all three national credit bureaus for a comprehensive credit assessment.
  • FICO Mortgage Score Pass-Through: Zero-margin revenue from the sale of FICO mortgage scores, fully remitted to FICO, with no EBITDA contribution for Equifax.
  • VantageScore: Alternative credit scoring model developed by the three national credit bureaus, provided as a lower-cost and increasingly adopted substitute for FICO scores in consumer lending.
