Earnings Call Transcript

KB Home (KBH) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 25, 2026 at 9:06AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, March 24, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Executive Chairman — Jeffrey Mezger
  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Rob McGibney
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Rob Dillard
  • Senior Vice President and Chief Accounting Officer — Bill Hollinger
  • Senior Vice President and Treasurer — Thad Johnson

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenues -- $1.08 billion, reflecting a 23% decrease year over year.
  • Diluted Earnings Per Share -- $0.52, benefitting from a 13% reduction in weighted average diluted shares outstanding.
  • Home Deliveries -- 2,370 units, delivered during the quarter.
  • Average Selling Price (ASP) -- $452,000, down 10% year over year and 3% sequentially due to regional and product mix.
  • Housing Gross Profit Margin (GAAP) -- 15.3%; Adjusted Housing Gross Profit Margin -- 15.5% after excluding $2.2 million in inventory-related charges.
  • Net Orders -- 2,846 homes, a 3% increase year over year; average absorption pace was 3.5 per community.
  • Built-to-Order (BTO) Shift -- Share of BTO net orders increased from 44% in October to 68% by February, exceeding 70% in early March.
  • Active Communities -- 276 at quarter end, the highest in many years and up 8% year over year.
  • Community Openings -- 37 grand openings in the quarter; 30-35 additional openings projected for the next quarter.
  • Build Time Reduction -- Companywide built-to-order home build time reduced to 108 days, a 22% decrease year over year and a 9% sequential improvement.
  • Direct Construction Cost -- 8% per-unit decrease year over year, achieved through lower trade labor expense and process efficiencies.
  • SG&A Expense -- Decreased 14% driven by reduced selling expenses and fixed cost controls, with a favorable $8 million insurance recovery this quarter; SG&A ratio increased to 12.2% of housing revenue.
  • Homebuilding Operating Income -- $33 million, or 3.1% of homebuilding revenues.
  • Capital Returned to Shareholders -- Nearly $70 million in the first quarter, including $17 million in dividends and $50 million through repurchase of 843,000 shares.
  • Book Value Per Share -- Increased to over $61 compared to the same period last year.
  • Buyer Credit Metrics -- Average down payment 16% (~$72,000), average FICO 743, average household income $133,000; 11% of deliveries to all-cash buyers.
  • KBHS Home Loans Capture Rate -- 81% of buyers financed through KBHS.
  • Lots Owned or Controlled -- Over 63,000 at quarter end (41% controlled); 26,000 under option contracts.
  • Land Investment -- $567 million during the quarter, with 60% allocated to land already owned.
  • Liquidity -- $1.2 billion in total liquidity, comprising $201 million in cash and $1 billion available through the credit facility; no debt maturities until June 2027.
  • Headcount Reduction -- 10% year-over-year cut as part of cost-control initiatives.
  • Q2 2026 Guidance -- Expected housing revenues of $1.05 billion to $1.15 billion, deliveries between 2,250 and 2,450 units, and housing gross profit margin of 15%-15.6% (before inventory-related charges).
  • Full Year 2026 Guidance -- Housing revenues projected at $4.8 billion to $5.5 billion, based on 10,000 to 11,500 deliveries.
  • Share Repurchase Outlook -- $50 million to $100 million planned for the next quarter, with $850 million remaining under current authorization.
  • SG&A Ratio Guidance -- Anticipated at 12.4%-13% for Q2 2026, with expected decline in the second half as volume improves.
  • Tax Rate Guidance -- Forecasted at approximately 19% for Q2; expected to rise later in the year as energy credits decline.

RISKS

  • Management reduced full-year delivery and revenue guidance, citing "net orders in the first quarter were below the level we needed" and "a lot of noise out there" due to "the conflict in the Middle East" causing consumer uncertainty and softer sales in early March.
  • Adjusted housing gross profit margin fell 480 basis points year over year, attributed to "pricing pressure, higher relative land costs, regional mix and lower operating leverage."
  • Direct costs are experiencing "some pressure on material costs from lumber," with the company acknowledging potential cost increases depending on market direction.
  • Management observed "tepid consumer confidence, elevated mortgage interest rates and affordability pressures" as stifling demand in the near term.

SUMMARY

KB Home (KBH 1.19%) reported a 23% year-over-year decline in revenue and a marked shift in sales mix toward built-to-order homes, with BTO comprising over 70% of net orders in early March. Executives lowered annual delivery and revenue forecasts, pointing to first-quarter net orders not meeting internal targets and ongoing consumer caution influenced by recent geopolitical tensions. Companywide build times for built-to-order homes fell to 108 days, expanding the window for same-year deliveries, and management emphasized strategic cost reductions, including a 10% workforce cut. The firm highlighted an 8% year over year per-unit reduction in direct construction costs, active management of SG&A, and strong balance sheet liquidity supporting both land investment and capital returns. Guidance for the second half includes sequential delivery increases, margin improvement from higher BTO and Northern California deliveries, and continued share repurchases.

  • Executives identified the "temporary trough in deliveries for the first half of the year" as "a purposeful reset that positions us to be a stronger, more predictable company in the second half."
  • Of homes in production at quarter end, 70% were sold and 30% were unsold, supporting expectations for at least 70% BTO deliveries in the latter half.
  • SG&A benefited from "a favorable impact of an $8 million insurance recovery" in the quarter, which management noted was larger than usual.
  • The company abandoned contracts to purchase 3,400 lots at a cost of $2.2 million, reflecting a disciplined approach to land inventory quality.
  • In response to a question, Rob McGibney stated, "we exited February 68%. Early March, we're tracking above 70%, and we think we'll maintain that or get at least 75% as we move through Q2."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • BTO (Built-to-Order): Homes sold prior to construction start, allowing buyer customization and typically carrying higher gross margins than speculative (inventory) homes.
  • ASP (Average Selling Price): The average price at which homes are sold during the reporting period.
  • Absorption Pace: The average number of homes sold per community per month over a given period.
  • KBHS: KBHS Home Loans, the company's affiliated joint-venture mortgage lender.
