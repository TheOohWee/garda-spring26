Construction Partners (ROAD) Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 6, 2026 at 8:17AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Thursday, Aug. 7, 2025 at 10 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Fred Julius Smith
  • Chief Financial Officer — Gregory A. Hoffman
  • Executive Chairman — Ned N. Fleming

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- $779.3 million, up 51% with 5% organic growth and 46% from acquisitions.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $131.7 million, an 80% increase; margin reached a record 16.9%, up 280 basis points.
  • Net income -- $44 million GAAP; adjusted net income $45.2 million, or $0.81 per diluted share.
  • G&A expense ratio -- 6.6% of revenue, improving from 7.3% in the prior-year quarter.
  • Project backlog -- $2.94 billion as of June 30, 2025, covering 80%-85% of expected next-12-months revenue.
  • Cash & liquidity -- $114.3 million in cash; $493.5 million available on a $1.1 billion credit facility after extension to June 2030.
  • Leverage ratio -- Debt to trailing 12 months EBITDA at 3.17x, with a stated target of approximately 2.5x by late fiscal 2026.
  • Operating cash flow -- $83 million, up from $35 million in the same quarter prior year.
  • Capital expenditures -- $36.7 million in the quarter; full-year expected CapEx between $130 million and $140 million, with maintenance CapEx at approximately 3.25% of revenue.
  • Acquisitions -- Durwood Greene Construction added in Houston, operating three hot-mix asphalt plants and a rail service aggregates terminal.
  • Fiscal 2025 guidance -- Revenue expected at $2.77 billion–$2.83 billion; adjusted EBITDA at $410 million–$430 million; adjusted EBITDA margin at 14.8%–15.2%; 8%–10% organic revenue growth projected; net income guidance $106 million–$117 million; adjusted net income guidance $124 million–$135 million.
  • Q4 acquisition revenue impact -- $270 million–$280 million expected to roll over into fiscal 2026 at $240 million–$250 million.
  • Public contract awards -- Up about 14% in CPI’s states in fiscal 2025; similar growth expected for fiscal 2026 based on current budgets and contract awards.
  • Free cash flow conversion -- On pace to convert 80%–85% of EBITDA to cash from operations in fiscal 2025; trailing 12 months at 79%.
  • Labor market -- Labor availability has normalized post-COVID, with fewer shortages now than three to four years ago.

Summary

Management stated that acquisitive and organic expansion increased company scale by over 50% this year, with the integration of Durwood Greene in Houston expected to drive future organic growth in Texas. CPI is operating at full capacity across all its local markets, supported by a record backlog and strong July project volumes as reported on the call. The company intends to maintain both acquisitive activity and ongoing deleveraging, prioritizing long-term capital allocation and workforce retention strategies focused on culture, compensation, and career advancement. CPI confirmed plans to update long-term growth targets in light of transformative acquisitions and is positioned to capitalize on sustained public infrastructure funding, demographic trends, and reshoring-driven demand in the Sunbelt region.

  • CPI’s revenue mix remains stable between public and private jobs, with management reporting steady bidding activity for non-residential projects such as warehouses, schools, and manufacturing sites throughout its markets.
  • Hoffman said, "our backlog covers the next 80% to 85% of our 12-month revenue. And the cost is built in there for the appropriate margin as we think about 40 to 50 basis point improvement in margin," indicating margin expansion is factored into awarded work.
  • "we're talking to a lot of potential sellers," Smith stated, confirming an active acquisition pipeline of both hot-mix asphalt and service-focused construction companies aligned with CPI’s historic approach.
  • Federal and state transportation funding, including IIJA-related sources, are actively cited as drivers of double-digit increases in contract awards in CPI’s geographic footprint.
  • A recent credit facility amendment increased the term loan and extended the maturity date, enhancing liquidity and flexibility to support both growth and deleveraging objectives.
  • Hoffman noted that recent legislation allowing 100% bonus depreciation on qualifying acquisitions and equipment purchases post-January 15, 2025, will "dramatically" reduce cash taxes for qualifying future investments.

Industry glossary

  • Hot-mix asphalt (HMA): A thoroughly blended mixture of aggregate and asphalt binder, produced at high temperatures and used for road surface paving applications.
  • IIJA (Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act): U.S. federal legislation aimed at funding infrastructure projects, including transportation, that directly supports contract awards in the construction sector.
  • FOB sales: Sales in which the buyer takes delivery of goods at the seller's facility; common in the context of aggregates and asphalt plants.
