Citi Trends CTRN Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Jun 3, 2025 at 5:07PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, June 3, 2025, at 9 a.m. EDT

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Interim Chief Executive Officer — Kenneth Seipel
  • Chief Financial Officer — Heather Plutino

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

RISKS

  • Distribution center performance was reported as "below expectations," with management stating there is "significant room for improvement" and outlining process and personnel changes underway.
  • Full-year gross margin expansion guidance was revised slightly downward due to an "extended time line for the repair phase of our supply chain transformation."
  • Management highlighted "a significant degree of uncertainty in the macroeconomic environment" in updating their 2025 outlook, particularly referencing tariff risks and product flow.
  • Sales to EBITDA flowthrough is expected to decline in the second half of FY2025 due to the reinstatement of bonus expense accrual.

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Sales: $201.7 million, an increase of $15.4 million or 8.3% compared to Q1 FY2024, representing broad-based performance across all apparel and home categories.
  • Comparable Store Sales: 9.9% comparable store sales growth, producing a 13% two-year stack and marking the third consecutive quarter of sequential comparable store sales improvement.
  • Gross Margin: 39.6% gross margin, up 90 basis points year-over-year, driven by higher initial markups, lower freight, and a 70 basis point improvement in shrink, partially offset by higher in-season markdowns.
  • Operating Expense Leverage: Adjusted SG&A improved 220 basis points to 36.9% of revenue, attributed to disciplined cost control and improved top-line results.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Adjusted EBITDA was $5.4 million, up $6.2 million (adjusted EBITDA) versus Q1 FY2024 with a reported sales-to-adjusted EBITDA flowthrough of 40%, higher than the 20%-25% company target.
  • Diluted EPS: $0.11 (GAAP diluted EPS), or $0.17 as adjusted, versus a prior year loss per share of $0.42, or $0.32 as adjusted.
  • Inventory: Dollar value reduced by 7.6% at quarter end year-over-year, average in-store inventory down 4.9%, and a 45% decrease in product aged 7 months or more, reflecting fresher inventory and active markdown strategies.
  • Balance Sheet: No debt, $42 million in cash at quarter end, and full availability on a $75 million revolver, resulting in approximately $117 million in liquidity at quarter end.
  • Share Repurchase: 251,000 shares repurchased for $6.3 million, with $40 million remaining under the repurchase authorization.
  • Store Remodeling: 19 remodels completed in the quarter, 36 store remodels year-to-date, bringing 25% of the fleet to updated format; performance is in line with past remodel upticks.
  • 2025 Outlook Updates: Full-year comp store sales now guided to the high end of mid single-digits, gross margin expansion expectation revised to approximately 200 basis points versus FY2024, SG&A leverage guided to 60-80 basis points, and EBITDA (non-GAAP) forecast raised to $6 million-$10 million.
  • Capital Expenditures: Full-year capital expenditures are guided at $18 million-$22 million, with plans for up to 5 new stores, up to 5 closures, and 50 remodels.
  • Tariff Impact: Management reports product costs held flat despite tariff-related volatility, and sees opportunity from "extreme value deal flow" in the disrupted market.
  • AI-Based Allocation System: Testing phase exceeded expectations with company-wide rollout planned post-back-to-school and pre-holiday, intended to improve inventory efficiency.
  • Strategic Goal: Management reaffirmed the "clear path to achieving a target of $40 million to $50 million of EBITDA" (non-GAAP) as part of their three-year plan.

SUMMARY

Citi Trends (CTRN 0.97%) achieved broad-based growth in all major product and geographic segments in Q1 FY2025, with apparel and non-apparel categories both up significantly, with comp trends consistent across all climate zones and store volumes. Management described a sharpened focus on "off-price" and "extreme value branded product," aiming for this segment to drive an incremental 10% of top-line over time (long-term target; not yet achieved). The quarter reflected heightened operational discipline, including store refreshes, enhanced product allocation, detailed KPI tracking, and an evidence-based expansion strategy leveraging three years of transaction data and geolocation analytics. A potential EBITDA margin headwind was disclosed from bonus expense accruals returning after a pause, which is expected to lower flow-through in the second half. Real estate growth will rely on proprietary site selection tools and detailed customer profiling, as part of a disciplined approach to both in-market backfilling and entry into new markets.

  • Chief Executive Officer Kenneth Seipel stated the company is "gaining traction on our strategic transformation." citing a structured three-phase plan -- repair, execute, optimize -- supported by foundational retail process upgrades and enhanced product value equations.
  • Both Seipel and Chief Financial Officer Heather Plutino noted that all major product and regional categories contributed to growth, eliminating prior overreliance on any single line of business.
  • Management’s "data-driven expansion strategy" was emphasized, with integration of comprehensive transaction and geolocation data to inform real estate investment and minimize market entry risk.
  • Ongoing supply chain transformation was framed as requiring more time, impacting gross margin guidance, while evolving tariff conditions have led to both operational challenges and unique sourcing opportunities.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Off-Price: Product sourced and sold at a significant discount to the manufacturer's suggested retail price, often involving inventory closeouts or brand overstock, aimed at value-conscious consumers.
  • Extreme Value Product: Brand-name merchandise acquired at substantial discounts, typically at least 50%-70% off MSRP, designed to attract customers seeking perceived bargains on recognized brands.
  • Shrink: Loss of inventory from causes such as theft, error, or damage, expressed as a percentage of revenue or stock on hand.
  • Two-Year Stack: The sum of comparable sales growth over two consecutive years; a key metric for evaluating compounded performance trends.
