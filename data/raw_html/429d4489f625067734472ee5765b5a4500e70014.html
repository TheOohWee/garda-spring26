Bruker (BRKR) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 3, 2025 at 10:06AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Monday, Nov. 3, 2025, at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Frank Laukien
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Gerald Herman
  • Director of Investor Relations — Joe Kostka

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

Risks

  • Organic Revenue Decline — Bruker management guided to a 4%-5% organic revenue decline for 2025, citing weaker academic and research instrument demand, and order timing shifts into 2026.
  • Operating Margin Compression — Non-GAAP operating margin decreased 260 basis points year over year to 12.3%, impacted by lower revenue absorption, tariffs, and foreign exchange.
  • High Non-Cash Charges — The quarter included non-cash goodwill and intangibles impairment charges of $119.4 million, $34.5 million in restructuring charges, resulting in a loss per share of €0.41.
  • Softness in Key Segments — EMEA region revenue declined by over 20%, and the BioSpin segment posted a double-digit decline in organic revenue.

Takeaways

  • Reported revenue -- $860.5 million for the quarter, reported revenue was down 0.5%, with a 2.9% positive currency impact, and a 1.1% contribution from acquisitions.
  • Organic revenue performance -- Organic revenue decreased 4.5%, with Scientific Instruments organic revenue declining 5.4%, and BEST organic revenue increased 6.9% net of intercompany eliminations.
  • Non-GAAP operating margin -- 12.3% for the quarter, down 260 basis points year over year, but improved from 9% in the prior quarter.
  • Non-GAAP diluted EPS -- $0.45, a decrease of 25% from $0.60 in the third quarter 2024, but up from $0.32 in the previous quarter.
  • Book-to-bill ratio -- Scientific Instruments segment book-to-bill rose above 1.0x for the first time in several quarters, indicating a pickup in bookings.
  • Bookings growth -- Mid-single-digit percentage organic bookings growth driven by improved government, applied, and biopharma orders; ACAGOV segment orders grew in the high teens percentage, primarily outside the U.S.
  • China orders -- Scientific Instruments orders in China increased by double digits organically; management cited initial signs of stimulus funding beginning to be dispersed in China.
  • Diagnostic segment performance -- CALID Group revenue increased in the low double digits year-to-date, driven by strength in microbiology diagnostics, and molecular diagnostics franchises.
  • Cost savings initiatives -- Management is progressing toward the high end of its $100 million–$120 million cost-cutting target for 2026, and expects this to drive significant margin expansion and double-digit EPS growth in 2026.
  • Guidance update -- Full-year revenue guidance updated to $3.41 billion–$3.44 billion, reflecting a reported 1%-2% growth, but a 4%-5% organic revenue decline; non-GAAP EPS updated to $1.85–$1.90, with $0.07 dilution from the convertible preferred offering in non-GAAP EPS guidance.
  • Backlog position -- Backlog increased to approximately seven months, up from 6.5 months at the end of Q2 2025.
  • Operating cash flow -- Year-to-date operating cash outflow of $95.7 million for the first nine months, attributed to lower profitability, the timing of tax and vendor payments, and restructuring expenses.
  • M&A and tariff headwinds -- Operating margin guidance incorporates a 60 basis point headwind from M&A, and 60 basis points from tariffs.

Summary

Bruker (BRKR 1.03%) reported a sequential improvement in both revenue and non-GAAP earnings versus the previous quarter, despite a year-over-year decline resulting from weaker academic and research demand. Management highlighted stronger-than-expected order activity, particularly in international government and biopharma end markets, supporting a Book-to-Bill ratio above 1.0x in Scientific Instruments. The company reduced full-year revenue and non-GAAP earnings guidance due to late Q3 orders and customer site delays, shifting some expected Q4 revenue into 2026. Significant cost-saving actions are well underway, with management reiterating confidence in realizing $100 million–$120 million in annual savings, and double-digit non-GAAP EPS growth in 2026, even in a low-growth environment. The diagnostics and molecular division delivered double-digit revenue growth year-to-date, and the company noted emerging strength in applied markets such as security and defense, including robust demand for trace explosive detectors in Europe and South Korea.

  • Management stated, "mid-single-digit percentage organic bookings growth" occurred, with outperformance in non-U.S. ACAGOV orders driving overall order growth.
  • Gerald Herman highlighted that non-GAAP gross margin fell 110 basis points to 50.1% year over year.
  • The quarter was impacted by a $0.01 non-GAAP EPS dilution from the September mandatory convertible preferred stock offering, which is expected to have a €0.20 dilutive impact on 2026 non-GAAP EPS.
  • Orders in China, while cited by management as "green shoots" of stimulus, totaled less than $10 million and require monitoring for sustainability.
  • The company reported aftermarket revenue in Scientific Instruments increased by a mid-single-digit percentage organically year over year.
  • Frank Laukien indicated that "improved bookings in Q3 2025 and Scientific Instruments book-to-bill ratio above 1.0 make us optimistic that we may be past the trough in demand."
  • Ongoing restructuring and non-cash impairment charges drove results to a loss.
  • Management noted that their outlook does not fully factor in potential impacts from a prolonged U.S. government shutdown, but stated that "our Q4 guidance is now appropriately conservative to absorb some of that."

Industry glossary

  • Book-to-bill ratio: A metric that compares orders received (bookings) to the amount of revenue billed within a period; a ratio above 1.0x indicates demand is exceeding supply and signals future revenue growth.
  • ACAGOV / AGA GOV / PACAGOV / Accagov / AGAGAV / ECAGov / Aka Gov / Akagao / AgaGov / CAGAF / ACOGAR / AGAGOV: Transcript uses multiple typo variants referring to the company's Academic and Government market segments; management references these collectively for institutional (primarily academic and government research) demand.
  • BEST: Bruker Energy & Supercon Technologies; segment focused on superconducting materials and instrumentation.
  • CALID: Segment focused on Clinical, Applied, and Life Science instruments, including diagnostics.
  • BioSpin: Segment focused on NMR (Nuclear Magnetic Resonance) and EPR (Electron Paramagnetic Resonance) instruments and solutions.
  • NMR: Nuclear Magnetic Resonance, an advanced technique used in chemical, biochemical, and medical research; ultra-high-field gigahertz class systems are high ASP (average selling price) capital instruments referenced in the call.
  • MALDI Biotyper: Bruker's platform for rapid pathogen identification via matrix-assisted laser desorption/ionization (MALDI) mass spectrometry.
  • TIMSSOMNI / TIMS Metabo: Recently launched mass spectrometry products mentioned as gaining early traction.
  • MANDATORY CONVERTIBLE PREFERRED (MCP): Hybrid security instrument issued in September 2025, flagged as EPS dilutive in both 2025 and 2026.
  • Cosmic / Cellscape / Painscape: Proprietary products in the spatial biology platform discussed by management as contributors to growth in the quarter.
