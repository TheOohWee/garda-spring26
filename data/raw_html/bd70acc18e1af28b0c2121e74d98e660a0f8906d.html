Earnings Call Transcript

LiveWire (LVWR) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 10, 2026 at 10:24AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, February 10, 2026 at 9:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Arty Scars
  • Chief Financial Officer — Jonathan Root

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated Q4 Revenue -- Down 28% year over year, driven by a 10% decline at HDMC and a 59% decline at HDFS.
  • Q4 Consolidated Operating Loss -- $361 million loss, compared to a $193 million operating loss in the prior year, with HDMC at a $260 million loss and HDFS at an $82 million loss.
  • Q4 Loss per Share -- $2.44, compared to a $0.93 loss per share in the prior year.
  • Full-Year 2025 Revenue -- $4.5 billion, a 14% decrease, with the decline primarily from lower wholesale motorcycle volumes and flat net pricing over the year.
  • Full-Year 2025 Operating Income -- $387 million, compared to $417 million in 2024, reflecting weaker margins and lower volumes.
  • Global Retail Sales Q4 -- 25,287 motorcycles, down 1%, with North America up 5% and international down 10%.
  • Full-Year 2025 Global Retail Sales -- Down 12%, with North America and international markets both showing similar declines.
  • Dealer Inventory Reduction -- Ended 2025 down 17% globally and down 16% in North America, exceeding the 10% global reduction target.
  • Tariff Costs -- $22 million incurred in Q4 and total $67 million for full year 2025; guidance for 2026 is $75 million to $105 million in tariff expense.
  • HDFS Transaction -- Sale of approximately $6 billion of HDFS loan receivables completed, transforming HDFS into a capital-light, derisked business model; HDFS loan assets fell 74% year over year.
  • HDFS Full-Year 2025 Operating Income -- $490 million, a record, up from $248 million, due to a favorable provision for credit losses and higher other income.
  • HDFS 2026 Guidance -- Expected operating income of $45 million to $60 million, reflecting structural changes and lower volumes after the receivables sale.
  • HDMC 2026 Guidance -- Projected operating income ranging from positive $10 million to a loss of $40 million; retail and wholesale volumes guided at 130,000 to 135,000 units.
  • LiveWire Segment Full-Year Results -- Electric motorcycle unit growth of 7% and Stasic units up 15%, yet consolidated revenue declined 3% on increased incentives.
  • LiveWire 2026 Outlook -- Operating loss forecasted at $70 million to $80 million; focus is on the S4 Honcho launch and new capital sourcing.
  • Cost Savings Plan -- At least $150 million of annual run-rate savings targeted for 2027 and beyond, exclusive of LiveWire; some savings to begin in the back half of 2026.
  • Operating Cash Flow -- $569 million for full year 2025, down from $1.064 billion, attributed to lower shipments, higher tariff/manufacturing costs, and classification of held-for-sale retail finance receivables as outflows.
  • Share Repurchases -- $347 million repurchased in 2025, including a $200 million accelerated share repurchase; represents about 11% of shares outstanding as of December 31, 2024.
  • Strategic Initiatives -- Management emphasized: "restoring our relationship with dealers," improving inventory management, realigning product/price portfolio, cost discipline, and a May 2026 strategic plan update.
  • Promotional and Incentive Activity -- Interest rate assistance, low APR, customer cash, and dealer credits applied to support sell-through and manage channel inventory, especially in touring models.

RISKS

  • CEO Scars cautioned, "view 2026 as a transition year," signaling continuation of operating and margin pressure from deliberate inventory and production discipline.
  • CFO Root disclosed incremental tariff headwinds: "we forecast the cost of between $75 million to $105 million of new or increased tariffs based on current tariff levels" for 2026, compared to $67 million in 2025.
  • Management stated, "we expect margins to be under pressure in the near term as production runs below wholesale, creating operating deleverage."
  • Full-year 2025 consolidated operating loss driven by intentional inventory and promotional actions may persist into upcoming quarters as described by management's outlook.

SUMMARY

Consolidated Q4 revenue dropped sharply, driven by lower wholesale shipments and a pronounced decline at HDFS. Dealer inventory was substantially reduced, exceeding management's targets and setting up a leaner 2026. Leadership signaled that 2026 is a transition year, with continued margin pressure from inventory discipline and full-year tariff exposure. The transformation of the HDFS segment to a capital-light model after a significant receivables sale has reset its earnings trajectory. LiveWire continues to deliver unit growth in electric motorcycles, but is forecasted to generate significant operating losses in the next fiscal year.

  • Management expects cost savings of at least $150 million on an annual run-rate basis starting in 2027, with details of ongoing restructuring to be shared alongside the May strategy update.
  • Dealer promotional incentives and interest rate supports are expected to remain in place until inventories, especially touring models, reach desired levels, impacting near-term financials.
  • The overhaul of HDFS resulted in record operating income for 2025, yet forecasts show a dramatic decline to $45 million to $60 million in 2026, highlighting the near-term effects of the new model and lower asset base.
  • LiveWire will focus on new product launches and capital sourcing as it pursues network and cost improvements but maintains high expected operating losses.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • HDMC: Harley Davidson Motor Company, the segment representing motorcycle and related products manufacturing and retailing.
  • HDFS: Harley Davidson Financial Services, the financing segment providing retail and commercial loans to motorcycle customers and dealers.
  • Stasic: Harley Davidson’s youth electric balance bike product line sold under the LiveWire/Harley Davidson brand.
  • ASR: Accelerated Share Repurchase, an agreement to repurchase shares on an expedited basis, often with a financial intermediary.
  • CECL allowance: Current Expected Credit Losses allowance, a provision for estimated future loan losses under accounting standards.
