Earnings Call Transcript

3M (MMM) Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 11, 2025 at 5:13PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Tuesday, October 22, 2024 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — William Brown
  • Chief Financial Officer — Anurag Maheshwari
  • Senior Vice President, Investor Relations — Bruce Jermeland

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Non-GAAP EPS was $1.98 in the third quarter, reflecting an 18% increase and directly attributed to a combination of operational performance, productivity, and restructuring benefits.
  • Adjusted Operating Margin was 23% in the third quarter, an increase of 140 basis points with margin expansion across all business groups noted as a significant contributor.
  • Organic Revenue Growth was 1% in the third quarter, with a further improvement to 2% when excluding “geographic prioritization and product portfolio initiatives.”
  • Adjusted Free Cash Flow -- $1.5 billion, with an adjusted free cash flow conversion rate of 141% for the quarter, and year-to-date adjusted free cash flow at $3.5 billion with 102% conversion.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $1.1 billion returned in the quarter via dividends and share repurchases, and $2.7 billion year to date, including $1.1 billion of repurchases.
  • Updated Full-Year Adjusted EPS Guidance -- Lower-end raised by $0.20 to a range of $7.20 to $7.30 per share, reflecting increased confidence in operational execution.
  • Segment Sales Breakdown -- Safety and Industrial at $2.8 billion (0.9% organic growth), Transportation and Electronics at $1.9 billion adjusted sales (2% organic growth), and Consumer at $1.3 billion adjusted sales (organic sales down 0.7% including a 2.3-point portfolio headwind).
  • On-Time In-Full (OTIF) Performance -- Improved to 89% at quarter end, a 5-point improvement since the year's start and 10 points above Q4 2022.
  • Supplier On-Time Performance -- Rose from low 60% to low 70% range, signaling tighter supplier management.
  • Operating Equipment Efficiency (OEE) -- Major assets in 38 largest facilities averaged about 50% utilization since the year’s start, highlighting further productivity opportunities.
  • New Product Introductions -- Expected to rise approximately 10% for the year, with acceleration projected for next year as new R&D alignment increases output.
  • Portfolio Actions -- Several small businesses are in early sale processes with stated intent for ongoing strategic evaluation.
  • Insurance Recoveries -- $54 million realized in the quarter and over $175 million year-to-date on Combat Arms and public water supplier claims.
  • China Organic Growth -- Up about 11% year-to-date, with electronics identified as the key growth driver over automotive.
  • Data Center Revenues -- Reported at approximately $600 million, with mid-teens growth, and described as well-aligned with current company priorities.
  • Expected Q4 Margin Seasonality -- Anticipated sequential step-down of 300-350 basis points, linked to typical factors such as lower revenues, factory shutdowns, and timing of investments.
  • Net Leverage -- 0.8x at the end of the quarter, with a reported cash balance of $7.3 billion, more than 2x the business’s working capital requirements.
  • 2024 Margin Guide Update -- Full-year adjusted operating margins now projected to be up 250-275 basis points, higher than the prior range.
  • Productivity Target -- Management aims for 2% net productivity, translating to approximately $260 million annual efficiency improvement within cost of goods sold.

SUMMARY

Management outlined improved delivery metrics and ongoing shifts in R&D focus, including redeployment of staff and facility upgrades supporting product pipeline acceleration. The company highlighted margin and earnings momentum, citing both realized and anticipated effects from productivity, operational discipline, and restructuring actions. Specific growth platforms such as electronics, data centers, and China were identified as core demand contributors, while the leader confirmed ongoing portfolio divestitures and incremental insurance recoveries. In addition, executives addressed anticipated Q4 margin seasonality, supplier performance trends, and OEE rollout as foundational to future consolidation and capital allocation decisions.

  • CEO Brown described current portfolio reviews as focused strategically on leveraging core technology for product differentiation and clarified that near-term divestitures will be relatively small revenue components.
  • Brown confirmed that while injury rates improved, the safety culture requires additional advancements, and a new “journey to zero” campaign has begun.
  • CFO Maheshwari stated that year-to-date margin expansion of 380 basis points and 30% EPS growth occurred on just 1% organic revenue growth.
  • Brown indicated that supplier rationalization remains a top operational focus, with efforts underway to consolidate the base of 25,000 direct suppliers and 4,000 contract manufacturers.
  • He specified that improvement initiatives such as OEE and demand forecast accuracy sprints are at pilot stage in major divisions, with expansion to follow successful results.
  • Large-scale restructuring is still in progress, with $165 million spent in the first half, $110 million expected in the second, and some residual spend carrying into next year.
  • China’s growth was clarified by Jermeland as concentrated in electronics, with other local divisions up roughly 3% organically year-to-date, excluding electronics.
  • R&D spending is currently being redistributed toward near-term new product launches and incremental extensions, with the majority of new introductions concentrated in Safety and Industrial plus Transportation and Electronics segments.
  • Brown said, “as I look out the next 1, 2, 3 years, we should continue to see margin growth coming out of a lot of what's happening across our factory network.”
  • Both Brown and Maheshwari emphasized that margin expansion will be driven primarily by productivity and disciplined capital deployment, and they noted minimal risk from below-the-line items aside from wage inflation and FX volatility.
  • Management stated that any ongoing portfolio pruning will impact SKUs or small segments, with a commitment to maximizing value if full divestitures occur.
  • Improved pricing power was highlighted, with half the year’s organic growth attributed to price, and Brown noted increased focus on tightening net-to-gross realization practices.
  • No major liability-related cash outflows are expected in the coming quarters, as recently booked items are already reflected on the balance sheet.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • OTIF (On-Time In-Full): Logistic performance metric measuring supply deliveries’ punctuality and completeness versus agreed schedules.
  • OEE (Operating Equipment Efficiency): Standard manufacturing measure of actual production output relative to the maximum possible, capturing utilization, performance, and quality loss.
  • PFAS: Class of per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances targeted for regulatory and legal actions due to environmental persistence.
  • TSA (Transition Service Agreement): Temporary contractual support arrangement following a business divestiture.
  • NPI (New Product Introduction): Internal process or metric tracking the number and scope of formally launched new products to the market.
