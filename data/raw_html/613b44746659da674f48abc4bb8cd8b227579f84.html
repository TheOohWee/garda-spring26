Altria (MO) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 29, 2026 at 10:05AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

Date

Jan. 29, 2026 at 9:00 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Billy Gifford
  • Chief Financial Officer — Salvatore Mancuso
  • Investor Relations — Mac Livingston

Takeaways

  • Adjusted diluted EPS -- Increased by 4.4% for the full year; 2026 guidance range is $5.56 to $5.72, representing 2.5%-5.5% growth from the $5.42 base.
  • Shareholder returns -- Returned $8 billion to shareholders in 2025 through $7 billion in dividends and $1 billion in share repurchases, with $1 billion remaining under the current buyback authorization.
  • Smokeable products adjusted OCI -- Reached over $11 billion for the year, with margins expanding by 1.8 percentage points to 63.4% year over year; fourth-quarter adjusted OCI declined 2.4% and margins contracted by 0.8 percentage points to 60.4%.
  • Net price realization (smokable) -- Achieved an annual increase of 8.4%.
  • Domestic cigarette volumes -- Decreased 7.9% in the fourth quarter and 10% for the year; after adjusting for calendar and inventory, volumes declined 7% (Q4) and 9.5% (full year).
  • Marlboro retail share -- Dropped 1.5 share points in the fourth quarter and 1.2 share points for the year; Marlboro's premium share increased by 0.1 to 59.4% annually but dropped to 59.2% in the fourth quarter.
  • Basic brand performance -- Retail share rose 0.6 share points sequentially and 1.9 share points year over year in the fourth quarter.
  • Oral tobacco product segment adjusted OCI -- Fourth-quarter adjusted OCI declined 4.6% with a 5 percentage-point margin contraction to 64.5%; full-year adjusted OCI grew 1.3% and margins improved by 0.1 percentage points to 67.9%.
  • ON nicotine pouch shipments -- ON reported shipment volume increased 11% for the year to more than 177 million cans; reached over 44 million cans in the fourth quarter.
  • ON retail share -- Accounted for 7.7% of the total oral tobacco category in Q4 and 8.2% for the year.
  • ON PLUS FDA authorization -- FDA authorized ON PLUS Mint, Wintergreen, and Tobacco (six and nine milligram strengths), with twelve milligram pending review; resumed shipments in Florida, North Carolina, and Texas.
  • Nicotine pouch category retail price trends -- Category competitor average retail prices declined 3% sequentially and 12% year over year; ON price increased approximately 4% sequentially and 3% year over year.
  • Illicit e-vapor market -- Illicit products represented 70% of the e-vapor category; e-vapor category grew approximately 30%, with disposable e-vapor volumes also up 30% (both declines from 2024 rates).
  • Noncash impairment charges -- Recorded $1.3 billion in noncash impairment charges for e-vapor intangible assets and goodwill due to continued illicit market pressure and slow regulatory enforcement.
  • Capital expenditures guidance -- 2026 CapEx expected to rise to $300-$375 million, primarily for manufacturing investments linked to import-export and smoke-free product capabilities.

Risks

  • Salvatore Mancuso reported, "we recorded noncash impairment charges of $1.3 billion" on e-vapor intangible assets and goodwill due to insufficient enforcement lowering illicit e-vapor volumes.
  • Adjusted OCI in the Oral Tobacco Products segment declined 4.6% in the fourth quarter, and adjusted OCI margins contracted by five percentage points to 64.5% over the same period.
  • Billy Gifford described ongoing "significant headwinds" in e-vapor from illicit disposable proliferation, slow FDA authorizations, and uncertain intellectual property landscape.

Summary

Altria Group (MO 0.12%) reported higher full-year adjusted earnings per share, strong capital returns, and margin growth in core tobacco, but faced significant volume declines across both smokeable and oral segments. Management emphasized the maturing regulatory environment, with stepped-up federal enforcement targeting illicit e-vapor, alongside new FDA authorizations for ON PLUS pouches and international expansion of the Fumi brand. The company announced increased manufacturing investment to facilitate import-export capabilities, contributing to elevated capital expenditures and a large one-time $1.3 billion e-vapor impairment charge, while reaffirming a disciplined approach to smoke-free category investment amid persistent regulatory bottlenecks. Expectations for 2026 highlight margin progress, back-half weighted growth, and continued prioritization of a smoke-free portfolio, though guidance assumes no NJOY ACE market reentry.

  • Billy Gifford described the industry as, "Total nicotine industry equivalized volumes increased for the third consecutive year and grew by approximately 2% over the past five years on a compounded annual basis."
  • The company added three Fumi flavor line extensions, and expanded the brand to 40,000 retail locations across seven international markets.
  • Average retail pricing strategies diverged sharply, with ON maintaining sequential price increases even as broader competitor category pricing fell.
  • Helix submitted PMTA applications for ON PLUS in six additional flavor varieties across three nicotine strengths during November.
  • Domestic cigarette discount segment retail share rose by 2.6 share points in the fourth quarter, and 2.2 share points for the year, further pressuring Marlboro brand retail share.
  • The company continues to anticipate that effective enforcement against illicit e-vapor will develop over time "at a more gradual pace."

Industry glossary

  • Adjusted OCI: Operating Companies Income excluding non-recurring items, providing insight into segment profitability.
  • PMTA: Premarket Tobacco Product Application, a regulatory submission required for authorization of new tobacco products in the U.S.
  • MRTPA: Modified Risk Tobacco Product Application, a filing with the FDA for claims of reduced risk in tobacco products.
  • Duty drawback: Refund of federal excise taxes when products are exported, enhancing import-export efficiency.
  • NJOY ACE: Altria's e-vapor device brand subject to regulatory status.
  • Helix: Altria's subsidiary involved in the manufacture and sale of ON and ON PLUS nicotine pouches.
