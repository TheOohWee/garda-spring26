Earnings Call Transcript

Roblox (RBLX) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 5, 2026 at 5:22PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 5, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — David Baszucki
  • Chief Financial Officer — Naveen Chopra

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $1.4 billion, representing 43% year-over-year growth, was reported for the quarter.
  • Bookings -- $2.2 billion, up 63% year over year, were delivered.
  • Global DAUs -- Grew 69% year over year in the quarter, crossing the 100 million threshold.
  • Engagement Hours -- Totalled 35 billion in the quarter, increasing over 88% year over year.
  • Monthly Unique Payers -- Nearly 37 million users, almost double the prior year and up sequentially; United States and Canada up 34%.
  • DevEx (Developer Exchange) -- $477 million paid in Q4, up 70% year over year, reflecting the 8.5% rate increase effective September; over $1.5 billion paid to creators for the year.
  • Top Thousand Creators' Average Earnings -- $1.3 million per creator, rising over 50% from the prior year.
  • Regional Strength -- Asia-Pacific bookings up 96% year over year, led by Japan at 160%, India at 110%, and Indonesia exceeding 700% growth.
  • Age Demographic Expansion -- 18 and older cohort grew over 50% year over year and monetizes 40% higher than younger users.
  • AI Deployment -- Over 400 proprietary AI models now operate internally, including facial tracking, moderation, translation, and experience generation.
  • Discovery Impact -- Management noted, "experiences outside of our top 10 are growing at an even faster rate than they were in Q3 ... that's true both with respect to engagement and bookings."
  • Safety Technology -- Facial age estimation now live, reaching 45% of global DAUs; 60% of DAUs in key initial markets have been age-checked, with continuous enhancement ongoing.
  • 2026 Bookings Guidance -- Management projected 22%-26% growth in bookings, not factoring in any unanticipated viral hits.
  • 2026 Free Cash Flow Outlook -- Midpoint guidance implies 26% free cash flow growth, despite slightly higher capital expenditures for data center and memory investments.
  • Gross Margin Upside -- CFO Chopra cited both success steering Robux purchase activity to lower-cost platforms and robust bookings as key margin drivers in the quarter.
  • Product Innovations -- Native streaming for 2D/3D, Slim for dynamic asset delivery, rollout of custom matchmaking, and an expanded Avatar system are being released.
  • AI-Driven Personalization -- Double-digit increase in unique experiences surfaced for users via recommendation algorithms was achieved in the quarter.
  • Advertising -- CFO Chopra said, "we expect our advertising business to show pretty healthy growth in 2026 ... it is still modest" and "not a major contributor to the top line."
  • Guidance Approach -- Management announced a transition to quarterly guidance after 2026 due to unpredictability of viral hit dynamics.

RISKS

  • CFO Chopra warned, "margin expansion may not be linear, as you can see with investments that we are making in 2026," citing higher DevEx rates and AI infrastructure spend.
  • Guidance for 2026 bookings does not assume a major viral hit, indicating potentially conservative outlook amid acknowledged forecasting uncertainty.
  • CFO Chopra stated, "At the low end of bookings, we are estimating a slight year-over-year decline in margin," reflecting structural cost pressures.

SUMMARY

Roblox Corporation (RBLX +0.62%) delivered substantial year-over-year growth in revenue, bookings, and platform engagement, highlighting particular strength in international markets and among older user cohorts. Management cited accelerating adoption of advanced AI and safety features, including new age verification technologies, as strategic drivers supporting expanding monetization and genre diversification. Product pipeline disclosures detailed initiatives in dynamic content streaming, creator tools, and continuous platform reinvestment, while acknowledging margin pressures and increased capital intensity expected in 2026.

  • Roblox Corporation executives confirmed transition to quarterly guidance post-2026 due to the difficulty of forecasting viral hit frequency, which signals elevated uncertainty regarding long-range financial predictability.
  • AI supplementation across moderation, translation, and personalization is now embedded at scale, with internal analytics and engagement trends supporting management's conviction in top-line expansion potential.
  • Advertising is projected to grow but remains a secondary revenue stream, while significant operating leverage is expected to partly offset heightened investment in developer payouts and data infrastructure.
  • China remains an area of potential, with discussions around partnership and infrastructure allowing for possible air-gapped platform deployment; management reiterated infrastructure flexibility as an enabler for geographic diversification.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Bookings: Measurements of virtual currency sales or other in-platform spending committed by users, regardless of revenue recognition timing.
  • DAUs: Daily Active Users, a core operational metric indicating the distinct number of users engaging with the platform each day.
  • DevEx: Roblox Corporation's Developer Exchange program, through which creators convert their earned Robux to real-world currency.
