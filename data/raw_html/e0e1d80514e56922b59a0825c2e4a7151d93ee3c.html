Absci (ABSI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Mar 25, 2026 at 9:12AM EST

Tuesday, March 24, 2026 at 4:30 p.m. ET

  • Founder and Chief Executive Officer — Sean McClain
  • Chief Financial Officer and Chief Business Officer — Zach Jonasson

TAKEAWAYS

  • ABS-201 Clinical Progress -- First three single ascending dose (SAD) cohorts were dosed in the ongoing Phase 1/2a trial for androgenetic alopecia, with "favorable emerging safety data" and no observed on-target or off-target safety signals to date.
  • Endometriosis Program Expansion -- ABS-201 is being advanced as a second indication, with initiation of Phase 2 trial planned for Q4, supported by data from current studies.
  • Phase 1/2a Trial Design -- The randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial is enrolling up to 227 healthy volunteers and includes both SAD and multiple ascending dose (MAD) cohorts, with primary endpoints of safety and tolerability and secondary endpoints including pharmacokinetics (PK), pharmacodynamics (PD), immunogenicity, and quantitative hair metrics.
  • Upcoming Clinical Readouts -- Preliminary safety, tolerability, and PK data are expected in the first half of 2026, a 13-week proof-of-concept efficacy readout in the second half, and full 26-week data in early 2027 for the AGA indication.
  • Dosing Regimen and Durability -- ABS-201 was engineered with a three- to four-fold longer half-life than a competitor antibody in preclinical testing, aiming for two to three injections yielding durable hair regrowth for multiple years.
  • KOL and Patient Network Initiatives -- Absci Corporation formed dedicated advisory boards and engaged advocacy groups in both AGA and endometriosis, influencing trial design and endpoint selection.
  • Market Survey Results -- Among survey respondents, 87% of men and 69% of women indicated they would be extremely or very likely to ask a healthcare professional about ABS-201 if available; these rates rose to 92% (men) and 89% (women) among current oral minoxidil users.
  • Target Product Profile Expectations -- Zach Jonasson stated, the growth in terminal area hair count—is consistent with high-dose oral minoxidil, so 35 to 40 hairs per square centimeter, we think that is a home-run product.
  • Addressable Market Sizing -- The estimated total addressable U.S. market for ABS-201 in androgenetic alopecia could exceed $25 billion annually, with a potential population of 15 to 18 million and 5 to 9 million annual patients assuming two- to three-year durability.
  • OriginOne Platform Publication -- Absci Corporation published details on OriginOne, asserting it as the first demonstration of de novo, full-length antibody design to zero prior epitopes. and noting its application for rapid, atomically accurate antibody generation.
  • R&D Efficiency Claims -- Absci Corporation advanced its first two programs from AI design to IND in roughly two years at $15 million per program, compared to the industry standard four to six years and $50 million or more.
  • Revenue -- Reported revenue of $700,000 for the quarter, attributed to progress in partnered programs.
  • Research and Development Expenses -- R&D expenses were $25.3 million, up from $18.4 million from the prior-year period, linked to increased internal program activity, especially ABS-101 and ABS-201.
  • Selling, General, and Administrative Expenses -- Selling, general, and administrative expenses were $8.6 million, slightly down from $8.8 million for the prior-year period.
  • Contingent Consideration Settlement -- The company recorded a $5.1 million gain from settling a contingent consideration, generating $8.7 million in unrestricted cash.
  • Cash Position -- Cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities totaled $144.3 million at period-end, down from $152.5 million at September 30, 2025.
  • Runway Guidance -- Management stated, "securities will be sufficient to fund our operations into 2028."
  • Pipeline Prioritization -- ABS-101, ABS-301, and ABS-501 programs are being positioned for partnership, allowing focus on ABS-201 and new early-stage pipeline assets.
  • Ongoing Partnership Discussions -- Management reported "productive discussions" with pharmaceutical companies regarding platform partnerships and stated an intent to prioritize optimal deal structures over immediate transactions.

SUMMARY

Absci Corporation (ABSI +1.19%) reported clinical and operational advancements, emphasizing the ongoing Phase 1/2a trial of ABS-201 for androgenetic alopecia, which has progressed through three SAD cohorts without identifying any safety concerns to date. The company additionally expanded the program to target endometriosis, planning a Phase 2 trial launch in the fourth quarter, while highlighting the differentiated long-acting profile of ABS-201. Market research cited by management revealed "extremely likely" interest in ABS-201 among both existing and prospective hair-loss patients and quantifies a large, premium-priced addressable market for this asset. Financially, the company ended the period with $144.3 million in cash, with runway guidance extending into 2028 and a continued focus on nondilutive funding to advance its pipeline and platform partnership opportunities.

  • ABS-201 development strategy is designed to potentially accelerate advancement from Phase 1/2a directly into registrational trials contingent on FDA engagement and data outcomes.
  • ABS-201’s mechanism targets a prolactin receptor pathway, supported by preclinical animal data and ex vivo human scalp studies indicating localized engagement without need for exogenous prolactin.
  • Absci Corporation’s OriginOne AI platform is said to deliver de novo antibody candidates with reduced screening requirements and rapid validation, aiming for competitive differentiation in the antibody discovery space.
  • Management provided guidance that the company expects to update partners and investors with interim proof-of-concept data and additional early-stage pipeline developments during the current year.
  • The company reiterated that its Phase 1/2a clinical trial for ABS-201 efficiently supplies both safety and early efficacy data for multiple indications, maximizing capital allocation and trial utility.
  • Survey data referenced in the call suggest that current limitations with daily or twice-daily administration of standard therapies contribute to high patient interest in ABS-201’s infrequent dosing profile.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • OriginOne: Absci Corporation’s generative AI platform designed for de novo, full-length antibody discovery against targets lacking prior structural data.
  • SAD (Single Ascending Dose): A clinical trial phase where participants receive increasing doses of a drug to evaluate safety and pharmacokinetics.
  • MAD (Multiple Ascending Dose): A clinical trial phase in which study participants are administered increasing multiple doses of a drug to assess safety, pharmacokinetics, and preliminary efficacy.
  • AGA (Androgenetic Alopecia): A form of hair loss driven by genetic and hormonal factors, commonly known as male or female pattern baldness.
  • IND (Investigational New Drug): Regulatory status allowing a new drug candidate to enter clinical studies following preclinical evaluation.
  • TPP (Target Product Profile): A document outlining the intended features and attributes of a developing drug, used to guide clinical development objectives.
  • Prolactin Receptor (PRLR): The cellular target of ABS-201, relevant to the proposed mechanism of hair regrowth and disease modification in endometriosis.
  • Contingent Consideration: A financial arrangement in which additional payment is made upon occurrence of specified future events, resolved here in Absci Corporation’s favor.
