Ferroglobe (GSM) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 18, 2026 at 11:13AM EST

Wednesday, February 18, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Marco Levi
  • Chief Financial Officer — Beatriz García-Cos Muntañola

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $329 million for the quarter, up 6% sequentially, driven by higher silicon-based and manganese-based alloys volumes.
  • Shipments -- Increased 13% to 165,000 tons, reflecting pronounced growth in silicon-based and manganese-based alloys.
  • Silicon-Based Alloys Volume -- Rose 19% sequentially to 51,000 tons, with European Union and North America markets up 25% and 14%, respectively.
  • Silicon-Based Alloys Revenue -- $104 million, up 12% quarter over quarter, with a 6% decline in average selling prices to $2,020 per ton.
  • Manganese-Based Alloys Volume -- Increased 16% to 81,000 tons; EU sales grew by 18% and accounted for more than 90% of segment volume.
  • Manganese-Based Alloys Revenue -- $93 million, an increase of 10% sequentially, with ferromanganese and silicomanganese index prices rising 16% and 21%.
  • Silicon Metal Volume -- Decreased by 3% sequentially to 33,000 tons; U.S. shipments fell 8%, partly offset by a 5% increase in EU shipments.
  • Silicon Metal Revenue -- Declined 3% sequentially to $96 million; segment EBITDA dropped from $12 million to $1 million, with margins down to 1%.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $15 million for the quarter, down 20% sequentially; full year adjusted EBITDA was $28 million, compared to $154 million in 2024.
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin -- Fell to 4% for the quarter, with full-year margin declining to 2% from 11%.
  • Free Cash Flow -- Negative $19 million in the quarter and negative $12 million for the full year, with cash from operations totaling $51 million over the year.
  • Dividend -- Increased by 8% in the first quarter of 2025, and by 7% to $0.015 per share for first quarter 2026, payable March 30 to shareholders of record as of March 23.
  • Share Repurchases -- 1.2 million shares acquired at an average price of $3.55 per share during early 2025 (no buybacks in the second half).
  • Capital Expenditures -- $14 million in the quarter, a $5 million reduction from the prior quarter, with full-year CapEx curtailed by 20% to $63 million.
  • Net Debt -- Position increased to $30 million by year-end 2025.
  • Trade Measures -- Implementation of EU safeguard reducing imports by 25% relative to 2022-2024 averages and U.S. antidumping and countervailing duties imposed on major ferrosilicon exporters.
  • Furnace Conversion -- Three furnaces converted from silicon metal to ferrosilicon: one in the U.S. and two in Europe, enabling production optimization.
  • Long-term Energy Contract -- New 10-year French power agreement effective January 1, 2026, allows full-year production flexibility in France.
  • Corcel Battery Investment -- $10 million invested in 2025 for advanced silicon-rich EV batteries, with initial shipments to defense and robotics customers expected in Q1 2026.
  • 2026 Outlook -- Revenues projected between $1.5 billion and $1.7 billion, representing a 20% midpoint increase, primarily driven by expected strong volume growth in silicon-based and manganese-based alloys.
  • Future EU Tariffs -- Proposal effective July 1, 2026, to further cut import quotas by 50% and double tariffs to 50% for quota-exceeding volumes.

RISKS

  • CEO Levi cited, "outlook for silicon metal remains more measured due to its exclusion from new safeguards and continued aggressive imports from China and increasingly from Angola."
  • Full-year adjusted EBITDA fell from $154 million to $28 million, as management stated, "price decline driven by weak demand and increased imports to Europe had a significant impact on adjusted EBITDA; accounting for more than 80% or $104 million of the decline."
  • Beatriz García-Cos Muntañola noted, "Fourth quarter raw materials and energy as a percentage of sales increased from 58% to 67%, primarily due to temporary hiding in France," highlighting adverse cost pressure impacts.
  • Energy rebates totaling $7 million in the quarter will not recur under the new French energy agreement, potentially reducing available cash flow offsets going forward.

SUMMARY

Ferroglobe PLC (GSM +0.24%) reported an increase in quarterly revenues and shipments, primarily attributed to strong performances in the silicon-based and manganese-based alloys segments, with significant volume and pricing momentum in core European and North American markets. Management highlighted substantial trade protection actions in the European Union and United States, including EU safeguards that reduce imports by 25% and U.S. antidumping duties, creating new competitive advantages for domestic producers. The company executed critical strategic actions, such as converting furnaces in both geographic regions to optimize production mix in response to favorable trade dynamics and signing a 10-year energy contract in France to provide year-round manufacturing flexibility. Significant capital investments were made in advanced silicon-rich battery development, signaling an ongoing commitment to product innovation and diversification. Projections for 2026 include revenue growth of 20% at the midpoint, supported by volume gains and further regulatory catalysts, while selective dividend increases and the continuation of share repurchases reinforce a shareholder-focused capital allocation strategy.

  • New safeguard measures in the U.S. and E.U. are expected by management to drive improved earnings and increase confidence in 2026 performance.
  • Pricing surged after the E.U. safeguard announcement, with European ferrosilicon and manganese alloy indices jumping approximately 20%; current ferrosilicon prices remain over 10% above pre-announcement levels.
  • Cost-saving initiatives included a hiring freeze, cuts in discretionary and capital spending, and tighter working capital management, resulting in a $48 million net working capital reduction for the year.
  • Some European silicon metal furnaces remain idle, with management indicating selective restarts will depend on contracted demand in the coming quarters.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Silicon-Based Alloys: Alloy products containing significant silicon content, used in steel and aluminum manufacturing and various industrial applications.
  • Ferrosilicon: An alloy of iron and silicon used primarily as a deoxidizer in steel production and as an additive in casting industries.
  • Manganese-Based Alloys: Ferroalloys containing manganese, including ferromanganese and silicomanganese, utilized in steelmaking to improve strength, hardness, and deoxidation.
  • PPAs (Purchase Price Agreements): Long-term contracts for energy purchasing, which may introduce mark-to-market accounting impacts on reported financials.
  • Corcel: Ferroglobe’s initiative developing advanced silicon-rich materials for electric vehicle battery applications and other high-performance uses.
