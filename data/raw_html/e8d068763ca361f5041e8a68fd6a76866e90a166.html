  • Backlog -- Funded backlog at year-end was approximately $162,000,000, stable with 2024 funding levels.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Revenue forecasted between $70,000,000 and $76,000,000 for Q1, with gross margin in the 27%-32% range, product gross margin 34%-39%, and development at ~8%.
  • 2026 Adjusted EBITDA Guidance -- Expected in the $5,000,000 to $10,000,000 range for the first quarter.

  • Share Count -- Anticipated to be approximately 55 million for the first quarter of 2026, post-equity raise.

RISKS

  • Joseph Corso stated, "We expect development revenue to decline sequentially in 2026 given the successful delivery of our 50 kilowatt laser weapons module," indicating a headwind for near-term development income.
  • $25,000,000-$30,000,000 revenue headwind projected from the exit of cutting and welding segment, with impact realized during the transition through early 2026.
  • Gross margin decline in the fourth quarter was attributed primarily to "a slightly less favorable mix, lower factory utilization, and higher inventory charges related to the exit of the cutting and welding markets."
  • Management emphasized continued execution challenges and disclosed, "we cannot control the specific timing of government programs," reflecting inherent uncertainty and risk in defense contract revenue realization.

SUMMARY

nLIGHT (LASR +4.22%) achieved record full-year and quarterly revenues, fueled primarily by contract wins and deliveries in the aerospace and defense segment, especially from directed energy and laser sensing programs. The company’s mix-shift toward higher-margin defense and microfabrication projects resulted in a substantial gross margin improvement and positive annual adjusted EBITDA. Bookings momentum is supported by a stable $162,000,000 backlog, with company guidance highlighting anticipated top-line and adjusted EBITDA growth for 2026, despite known revenue and gross margin headwinds from the completed defense development contract and exit from the cutting and welding business. Management deployed proceeds from a $190,000,000 equity raise toward a new Colorado manufacturing site and targeted supply chain investments to strengthen anticipated demand, while cautioning that future revenue is subject to the timing of government program awards and execution of new customer contracts.

  • Management expects new directed energy program contract awards in the coming quarters, as increased U.S. and international demand for non-kinetic missile defense systems is anticipated, including from the Golden Dome executive order mandates.
  • The decision to expand Longmont, Colorado capacity was driven by the need to "develop and deliver multiple copies of high energy lasers and laser weapon modules," per Corso, without direct quantification of its revenue effect.
  • Low-rate initial production began on a classified sensing program alongside a new $50,000,000 contract for a missile guidance program, both supporting near-term sensing revenue.
  • Management stated the overhead released from cutting and welding is being repurposed to A&D and advanced manufacturing, minimizing expected near-term margin impact.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Directed Energy: Use of high-powered lasers as non-kinetic weapons for defensive and offensive applications, including counter-UAS and missile defense.
  • DE M-SHORAD: Directed Energy Maneuver-Short Range Air Defense, a U.S. Army program integrating high-energy lasers onto mobile platforms for close-in air threat defense.
  • HELSI-2: High Energy Laser Scaling Initiative, phase two; a government contract to develop a 1 MW laser system for defense applications.
  • LRIP: Low-Rate Initial Production, the first step in manufacturing beyond prototyping but before full-rate production, primarily for defense contracts.
  • CBC: Coherent Beam Combining, a technology that merges output from multiple lasers into a single, more powerful beam.
  • Additive Manufacturing: Industrial process, also known as 3D printing, that creates components by adding material layer by layer for precision and efficiency.
