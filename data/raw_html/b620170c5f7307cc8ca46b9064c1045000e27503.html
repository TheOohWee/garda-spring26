Penumbra (PEN) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Nov 6, 2025 at 11:48AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, November 5, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. ET

  • Chief Executive Officer — Adam Elsesser
  • Chief Financial Officer — Maggie Yuen
  • Chief Medical Officer — Shruthi Narayan
  • Vice President, Investor Relations — Cecilia Furlong
  • Executive Vice President, Strategy — Jason Mills

TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $354.7 million for Q3 2025, up 17.8% year-over-year and 16.9% in constant currency, with underlying U.S. growth of 21.5% and international growth of 6.6% (3% in constant currency).
  • Gross Margin -- 67.8% gross margin for Q3 2025, expanding 130 basis points year-over-year and 180 basis points sequentially, driven by favorable regional and product mix as well as productivity improvements.
  • Operating Income -- $48.8 million (non-GAAP) for Q3 2025, representing 13.8% of revenue, compared to $40.3 million and 13.4% in Q3 2024.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA was $66.7 million, or 18.8% of total revenue, matching the EBITDA margin of the prior year quarter.
  • U.S. Thrombectomy Revenue -- $192 million in U.S. Thrombectomy sales, up 18.5% year-over-year with VTE franchise delivering 34% year-over-year growth and highest sequential increase in VTE case volume in 2025.
  • U.S. Embolization and Access Revenue -- $83 million in U.S. Embolization and Access revenue, up 29.2% year-over-year, driven by the launch of Ruby Excel and successful expansion of the U.S. Embolization sales team.
  • International Revenue -- $79.7 million in international revenue, up 6.6% year-over-year (3% in constant currency), with the remainder outside China growing in double digits.
  • Global Thrombectomy Revenue -- $236.4 million, increasing 15.8% reported (15.1% constant currency) compared to the same period last year; international thrombectomy revenue up 5.6% year-over-year.
  • Embolization and Access Revenue (Global) -- $118.3 million in Embolization and Access revenue, up 22% reported and 20.8% in constant currency.
  • Operating Expense -- $191 million (non-GAAP, 54% of revenue), up from $160 million (53.1% of revenue) (non-GAAP) in Q3 2024 due to embolization team expansion and variable spending; SG&A was $168.9 million (47.6% of revenue), rising $8.9 million sequentially from sales force investment and variable spend.
  • Research and Development Expense -- $22.7 million (6.4% of revenue), nearly flat year-over-year versus Q3 2024 with $3.6 million in savings from wind-down of the immersive business offset by ongoing product development.
  • Cash Position -- $470.3 million in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities as of the end of Q3 2025 with no debt, up $45.7 million sequentially due to improving working capital ratios and profitability.
  • STORM PE Trial Results -- Shruthi Narayan said, "The trial proved CAVT superior in reducing right heart strain in intermediate high-risk PE patients, with a comparable safety profile to the current standard of care, as presented at the TCT Conference and published in Circulation in early November 2025," with statistical significance across multiple secondary endpoints and strong initial adoption momentum reported.
  • FDA Clearances -- The company received FDA clearance for Lightning Bolt 16 and Lightning Flash 3.0, which introduce modulated aspiration technology and hardware/software algorithm improvements, respectively.
  • Thunderbolt Regulatory Progress -- Adam Elsesser said, "we submitted thorough responses to all the outstanding questions received from the FDA during the 510(k) review process," and that the company is now awaiting any final clarifications from the FDA.
  • Patent Dispute Resolution -- The company achieved "a victory from the Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit, which summarily and unanimously affirmed the Patent and Trademark Appeal Board's IPR decisions invalidating several competitive patents challenged by Penumbra."
  • 2025 Revenue Guidance -- Guidance raised to $1.375 billion-$1.380 billion, with 20%-21% U.S. Thrombectomy year-over-year growth reiterated and maintenance of gross and operating margin expansion guidance.

SUMMARY

Management stated that the dedicated expansion of the U.S. Peripheral Embolization sales team resulted in 21.2% sequential growth in embolization revenue, marking a structural shift to separate focus on CABT and embolization sales objectives. Rapid physician adoption and positive market response following the publication and presentation of STORM PE data were cited, with hospital protocols expected to shift toward increased CABT use for PE. Thunderbolt is described as being in the final stages of FDA review; no delays from the ongoing regulatory environment were reported. Cash flow remains positive, with increases in cash and marketable securities attributed to improving working capital ratios and strong profitability. Guidance suggests double-digit growth continuity internationally, as China-specific headwinds are anticipated to diminish by early next year.

  • Management emphasized, "we are optimistic that with the anticipated introduction of Thunderbolt, we will see a similar dynamic play out" in stimulating the U.S. stroke thrombectomy market.
  • Conversion of U.S. arterial cases to CABT continues, supported by physician migration from traditional open surgery and lytic therapies.
  • Growth in neuro embolization, especially for MMA procedures, was attributed to "extremely strong above-market procedure growth" according to Adam Elsesser and increased coil utilization.
  • Management reiterated that no "there was nothing one time about this quarter." according to Adam Elsesser or citing sustainable sales team and portfolio dynamics.
  • Tariff impacts on gross margins were described as non-material, with recent trends already absorbing related headwinds.
  • Integration of the expanded U.S. Embolization sales force is now complete, and future SG&A leverage is expected as incremental investments normalize.
  • The company confirmed strong interest from non-interventionalists in updating hospital protocols to include CABT solutions, spurred by recent clinical evidence and physician feedback.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • CABT (Computer-Assisted Blood Thrombectomy): Technology-enabled mechanical thrombectomy platform used in the removal of blood clots, integrating advanced algorithmic aspiration with procedural automation.
  • VTE (Venous Thromboembolism): Condition comprising deep vein thrombosis and pulmonary embolism, targeted by Penumbra’s thrombectomy therapies.
  • PE (Pulmonary Embolism): Blockage of a pulmonary artery, treated via mechanical thrombectomy solutions discussed on the call.
  • STORM PE: Prospective multicenter randomized controlled trial evaluating CABT plus anticoagulation versus anticoagulation alone for acute intermediate high-risk pulmonary embolism.
  • Pert Consortium: Network of clinical teams organized to improve treatment of pulmonary embolism (rarely mentioned outside Pulmonary Embolism Response Teams context).
  • DVT (Deep Vein Thrombosis): Blood clot formation in deep veins, often addressed with thrombectomy devices.
  • MMA (Middle Meningeal Artery) Embolization: Neurovascular procedure to treat various cranial pathologies, a growing market for specialized embolization coils.
