Earnings Call Transcript

Moderna (MRNA) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Dec 11, 2025 at 12:24PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, November 6, 2025 at 8:00 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Stéphane Bancel
  • President — Stephen Hoge
  • Chief Financial Officer — James Mock

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $1.0 billion for the quarter, primarily driven by COVID-19 vaccines Spikevax and mNEXSPIKE, and RSV vaccine mRESVIA.
  • Net Loss -- $200 million loss for the quarter, with loss per share of $0.51.
  • Cash and Investments Balance -- $6.6 billion at quarter end, down from $7.5 billion at the end of the previous quarter, mainly due to seasonal working capital impacts.
  • Cost Reductions -- 34% year-over-year reduction in combined cost of sales, R&D, and SG&A compared to Q3 2024.
  • Cost of Sales -- $207 million for the quarter, a 60% decrease year-over-year, reflecting lower inventory write-downs, improved manufacturing productivity, and volume decline.
  • R&D Expenses -- $801 million for the quarter, down 30% year-over-year due to lower clinical trial costs and efficiency gains.
  • SG&A Expenses -- $268 million for the quarter, down 5% year-over-year, mainly due to reduced consulting, digital, and facility costs.
  • U.S. Product Revenue -- $800 million in the quarter, with COVID vaccines comprising the majority.
  • International Product Revenue -- $200 million in the quarter, approximately half of which was delivered to Canada through local manufacturing.
  • Full-Year Revenue Guidance -- Narrowed to $1.6 billion-$2.0 billion from the prior $1.5 billion-$2.2 billion.
  • Full-Year U.S. Revenue Guidance -- Updated to $1.0 billion-$1.3 billion, revised from $1.0 billion-$1.5 billion, reflecting a 15%-33% year-over-year decline in expectations.
  • Full-Year International Revenue Guidance -- Raised to $600 million-$700 million, from $500 million-$700 million previously, tied mostly to contracted volumes.
  • Market Share in U.S. COVID Retail -- 42% as of October 24, up 2 percentage points year-over-year.
  • mNEXSPIKE COVID Vaccine Uptake -- Now accounts for 55% of Moderna's COVID vaccination volume in the U.S., overtaking Spikevax.
  • Guidance for Cost Structure -- Cash cost guidance for 2025 reduced by $900 million, now expected at $4.6 billion, with GAAP operating expenses projected at $5.3 billion at the midpoint.
  • Projected Year-End Cash Balance -- Revised up to $6.5 billion-$7.0 billion from previous guidance of approximately $6.0 billion.
  • COVID Retail Vaccinations in U.S. -- 13.2 million through October 24 in the retail channel, down roughly 30% year-over-year and aligned with expectations of a 20%-40% decline.
  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV) Vaccine Program -- Discontinued for congenital CMV after failure to meet primary efficacy endpoints in Phase III; continued evaluation in bone marrow transplant patients.
  • Strategic Partnerships -- Manufacturing facilities operational in Canada with first domestic deliveries; facilities licensed in the U.K. and Australia, with ramp-up of shipments expected in Q4 2025 (Australia) and Q1 2026 (U.K.).
  • Regulatory Approvals -- Spikevax 2025-2026 update approved in 40 countries; mNEXSPIKE approved in the U.S. and Canada and filed in additional territories.
  • Flu Plus COVID Vaccine Filing -- Combination flu/COVID product (mRNA-1083) filing under review in Europe, with plans to refile in Canada and await FDA guidance in the U.S.
  • Oncology Pipeline -- Expanded Phase III and Phase II studies for intismeran in multiple cancer types; mRNA-4359 advanced to Phase II based on encouraging Phase Ib data.
  • Rare Disease Pipeline -- Registrational study for propionic acidemia fully enrolled and MMA program progressing towards a registrational trial in 2026.

RISKS

  • Net Loss and Cash Burn -- CFO James Mock reported a $200 million net loss for the quarter, with cash and investments decreasing by $0.9 billion sequentially, primarily due to working capital seasonality.
  • CMV Vaccine Program Failure -- Phase III CMV efficacy trial did not meet primary endpoint for prevention of infection; CEO Stéphane Bancel said, "We will discontinue the development of our CMV vaccine in this indication."
  • Declining COVID Vaccine Market -- CFO James Mock noted, "The 45% year-over-year decline in revenue was expected and primarily reflects lower COVID vaccine demand."
  • Guidance Reflects Lowered Vaccination Rates -- Management cited U.S. COVID vaccination rates as the largest variable and reduced guidance to reflect 20%-40% declines in shots administered from the previous year.

SUMMARY

Moderna (MRNA 0.62%) reported a quarterly net loss and further decline in COVID vaccine demand despite cost cuts and expanded product approvals. Management narrowed full-year revenue guidance and raised projected year-end cash balance due to deeper operating expense reductions. The company discontinued its congenital CMV program after Phase III failure, while advancing filings and trials for its flu, RSV, rare disease, and oncology pipeline. Strategic partnerships led to first in-country COVID vaccine deliveries in Canada, with similar international ramp-ups anticipated. Cost structure discipline, ongoing pipeline prioritization, and cautious expense allocation were repeatedly highlighted as central to the company's path toward targeted cash breakeven by 2028.

  • CEO Bancel projected, "Next year, our commercial business will benefit from the full year contribution from our strategic partnership in Canada, U.K. and Australia."
  • CFO Mock emphasized, "In just 2 years, we expect to reduce our cash cost by approximately 50% from nearly $9 billion in 2023 to $4.6 billion in 2025."
  • President Hoge clarified that further reductions in R&D costs are expected to occur without near-term program discontinuations, as legacy Phase III infectious disease trials conclude.
  • Management reiterated that any new Phase III infectious disease investments are being deferred until achieving its targeted breakeven in 2028.
  • International expansion and new product introductions were flagged as necessary for future revenue growth, with further strategic details to be provided at the upcoming Analyst Day.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • mNEXSPIKE: Moderna's next-generation COVID-19 vaccine, approved and commercialized in 2025.
  • mRESVIA: Moderna's respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) vaccine, approved in adults in multiple countries.
  • Spikevax: Moderna's original COVID-19 vaccine product.
  • intismeran: Moderna's personalized cancer vaccine, developed in partnership with Merck, under clinical development for several oncology indications.
  • propionic acidemia (PA): An inherited rare metabolic disorder being targeted by Moderna's rare disease program.
  • mRNA-1083: Moderna's investigational combination influenza and COVID-19 vaccine candidate.
  • mRNA-1010: Moderna's investigational mRNA-based influenza (flu) vaccine candidate.
  • MMA: Methylmalonic acidemia, a rare disease addressed in Moderna's pipeline.
  • CMV: Cytomegalovirus, a virus for which Moderna's vaccine program (mRNA-1647) failed in its primary congenital infection efficacy endpoint.
