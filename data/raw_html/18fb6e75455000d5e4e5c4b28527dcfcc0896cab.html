TAKEAWAYS

Revenue: $6.9 million in revenue for Q1 2025 (basis not specified), reflecting 30% year-over-year growth driven by both platform and solutions segments.

Platform Revenue: $2.3 million in platform revenue for Q1 2025, up 23% year over year, with growth attributed to higher transaction volumes and new carrier onboarding.

Solutions Revenue: $4.6 million in solutions revenue for Q1 2025, representing a 33% year-over-year increase, supported by enterprise procurement solutions and SaaS expansion.

Gross Margin: IFRS gross margin improved to 66.8%, rising from 62.6% a year ago; non-IFRS gross margin climbed to 73.7%, up from 70.3% a year ago.

Adjusted EBITDA: Adjusted EBITDA was negative $3.0 million, an improvement from negative $3.6 million a year ago, reflecting operational efficiency and cost discipline.

Cash Balance: $36.4 million in cash and cash equivalents at quarter end, supporting continued investment and progress toward breakeven.

Transaction Volumes: 370,000 transactions completed, increasing 25% year over year, marking a 21st consecutive quarter of record transaction growth.

Carrier Network: Four new carriers onboarded, bringing the total to 71 carriers, further extending platform coverage.

Buyer Growth: Unique buyer users reached 19,700, up 10% year over year.

Enterprise Solutions: Launched a unified Freightos Enterprise Suite post-quarter, integrating Shipsta to address complex shipper needs and cross-selling opportunities.

Data Retention: Data solutions segment reported 100% customer retention.

Q2 2025 Guidance: Anticipated 380,000-385,000 transactions (20%-22% growth), GBV of $278 million-$285 million (37%-40% growth), revenue of $7.0 million-$7.1 million (23%-25% growth), and adjusted EBITDA loss of $2.8 million-$2.9 million.

Full-Year 2025 Guidance: Prior financial targets reiterated, reflecting anticipated market stability and core growth drivers.

Truck Partnership: Signed an agreement with a major North American ground transportation provider to enable air-forwarders to book integrated trucking services through the platform.

SUMMARY

Freightos(CRGO +0.00%) highlighted record revenue and transaction volumes, underpinned by ongoing digital adoption in a volatile trade environment. Management stated, "we met or exceeded our guidance across key metrics this quarter," referring to Q1 2025, and noted continued operational progress despite short-term headwinds from tariff shifts and macroeconomic uncertainty. The addition of new carriers and enterprise customers, as well as the successful integration of Shipsta, expanded the company’s competitive positioning and digital suite, while reinforced guidance points to business resilience and confidence in long-term digitalization trends. Guidance for Q2 2025 and full year 2025 reflects management’s view that sector volatility—driven by shifting tariffs, a suspended U.S. de minimis exemption, and unsettled ocean routes—will have a mixed effect, but should ultimately not materially alter Freightos’ pathway to breakeven and expanded market opportunity.

Pinillos emphasized, “a large portion of our transactional bookings is based on a flat fee,” clarifying why GBV and revenue growth rates diverge as transactions increase.

Schreiber explained that major macroeconomic uncertainty or a serious trade war “would be a headwind” for closing large enterprise solutions deals, although recent tariff de-escalation offers some stabilization.

Carrier network expansion now spans 71 carriers, covering “carriers responsible for 70% of global capacity,” per call remarks, supporting enhanced network effects for sustained user growth.

Management expects the comprehensive enterprise suite to offer “significant efficiency gains” and enable synergies with the platform business through cross-selling to multinational shippers.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

GBV (Gross Booking Value): The total dollar value of transactions booked on the company’s platform within a reporting period, including both platform and solutions segments.

FAX Index: Freightos Air Index, a proprietary metric measuring global airfreight rates used to track pricing movement in the air cargo market.

FPX-01 Index: Freightos Platform Index-01, the company’s bellwether, proprietary metric for tracking ocean freight rates, especially on the core China–U.S. transpacific route.

De Minimis Exemption: A U.S. customs policy waiving duties and simplified clearance for low-value imports, mainly affecting e-commerce parcel flows.

Blanking Sailings: The practice by ocean carriers of canceling scheduled sailings to manage vessel capacity and stabilize rates in response to weak demand or excess supply.

Pablo Pinillos: Okay. And we actually do have another question. What constitutes the economic mode for freighters? Zvi Schreiber: Yes. So look, the biggest mode for a platform or a marketplace is the network effect. That's why I emphasize them in my remarks every quarter. And if you want to sell your secondhand toaster, then you go to eBay because that's what the buyers are. So our network is -- our customers are the moat, the network of buyers and sellers.

And that's why every quarter we report not just how many transactions we have, but how many carriers are selling and how many users are buying and so we give you all the tools every quarter to really follow how we're deepening that moat. Can someone else do it? Well, I mean, we do have some competition, for example, for air bookings, which is our biggest volume.
