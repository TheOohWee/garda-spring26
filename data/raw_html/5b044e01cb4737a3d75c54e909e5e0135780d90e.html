S&P Global (SPGI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 10, 2026 at 11:05AM EST

Date

Tuesday, Feb. 10, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

Call participants

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Martina Cheung
  • Chief Financial Officer — Eric Aboaf
  • Head of Investor Relations — Mark Grant

Takeaways

  • Revenue -- Reported revenue grew 9% with organic constant currency revenue up 8% for the quarter.
  • Adjusted Operating Margin -- Margin expanded by 60 basis points year over year to 47.3%.
  • Adjusted Diluted EPS -- EPS grew 14% in the quarter; full-year adjusted diluted EPS reached the higher end of guidance and exceeded initial projections.
  • Segment Results -- Every division delivered revenue growth within or above its original guidance range, and all divisions reported operating margins at or above the high end of original guidance.
  • Shareholder Returns -- The company returned 113% of adjusted free cash flow to shareholders and repurchased more than $5 billion in stock in 2025.
  • Dividend -- S&P Global announced its 53rd consecutive year of dividend increases.
  • Private Markets Revenue -- Grew 16% year over year, with Ratings as the largest contributor.
  • Energy Transition and Sustainability Revenue -- Decreased 3% to $101 million in the quarter due to customer spending slowdowns, particularly in consulting and one-time transactions.
  • Vitality Revenue -- Generated $470 million in the fourth quarter, maintaining a Vitality Index of 12%.
  • Market Intelligence Revenue -- Reported revenue grew 7%, with organic constant currency revenue up 5%; subscription revenue, about 85% of the segment, grew roughly 7% organically and as reported.
  • Ratings Revenue -- Increased 12% year over year, or 10% on an organic constant currency basis, with a 12% rise in transaction revenue and an 11% increase in non-transaction revenue.
  • Energy Revenue -- Increased 6%, driven by demand for Platts benchmarks and differentiated data; sanctions created a $3 million headwind for the quarter.
  • Indices Revenue -- Rose 14%, with asset-linked fees up 13% and exchange-traded derivatives revenue up 20%.
  • Mobility Revenue -- Grew 8% year over year, including double-digit growth in dealer, financial, and other revenue lines.
  • Acquisition Integration -- With Intelligence integration achieved over 75% of fund manager and investor data sets linked within a month of close, enabled SSO on Capital IQ Pro, and generated over 200 cross-sell opportunities in 60 days.
  • Margin Expansion Guidance (2026) -- On an organic constant currency basis (excluding OSTTRA), company expects margin expansion of 50-75 basis points; including OSTTRA, 10-35 basis points.
  • EPS Guidance (2026) -- Adjusted diluted EPS range of $19.40 to $19.65, representing 9%-10% year-over-year growth.
  • Division Revenue Guidance (2026) -- Market Intelligence expects 5.5%-7% organic constant currency growth; Ratings 4%-7%; Energy 5.5%-7%; Mobility 7.5%-9%; Indices 10%-12%.
  • Billed Issuance Outlook (2026) -- Assumes low to mid-single-digit increase and expects revenue growth to fluctuate by quarter due to a challenging comparison from prior year highs.
  • AI and Productivity -- Over half of total data workflows now automated; more than 10% of applications eliminated in 2025, with a targeted run-rate expense reduction in the Enterprise Data Office exceeding 20% by end of 2027.
  • Mobility Spin-Off -- Public Form 10 filing targeted for Q2 2026, with a separate Investor Day and roadshow planned; S&P Global intends to provide recast financials and standalone guidance for post-spin periods upon completion.
  • Buybacks (Q1 2026) -- On track to repurchase approximately $1 billion in Q1, up from $650 million in Q1 last year.
  • ACV Growth (Market Intelligence) -- Achieved 6.5%-7% for two consecutive quarters; prior first half averaged 6%-6.5%.

Risks

  • Energy Transition and Sustainability revenue declined 3% to $101 million due to "ongoing uncertainties that have led many customers to slow spending in this area, particularly in consulting engagements and onetime transaction spend in certain geographies."
  • Sanctions in the second half created a $3 million headwind to fourth quarter Energy revenue; company does not expect a near-term recovery and anticipates lapping the impact by end of Q3 2026.
  • Bank loan volumes remained soft, both in Ratings and Market Intelligence, impacting volume-driven revenue and reflected in expectations for continued near-term softness in that segment for 2026.
  • Management noted, "In the event of macroeconomic distress, elevated market volatility or uncertainty or a slowdown in economic growth, we would expect Billed Issuance to be lower than our forecast."

Summary

S&P Global (SPGI 2.10%) delivered top-end revenue, margin, and EPS performance for the year, supported by robust subscription growth and strategic product innovation, while executing a disciplined capital return program and integrating key acquisitions ahead of schedule. Management introduced 2026 guidance marked by 6%-8% organic constant currency revenue growth, continued adjusted margin expansion, and a 9%-10% EPS increase, all while previewing the upcoming Mobility spin and affirming commitment to data-driven productivity and AI integration across the enterprise. The company expects Billed Issuance to increase modestly in 2026, with quarterly fluctuations reflecting challenging prior-year comparables and persistent softness in bank loan volumes, and positions its Indices segment for double-digit growth while maintaining cautious outlooks in Energy due to sanctions and consulting demand variability.

  • By automating over half of data workflows and consolidating technology platforms, the Enterprise Data Office is positioned for a targeted 20% run-rate expense reduction by late 2027.
  • New AI product launches and workflow integrations, such as CAP IQ Pro expansions and the Kensho platform, are enabling value-based pricing discussions and cross-sell opportunities, including rapid post-close momentum from the With Intelligence acquisition.
  • Asset-linked fee revenue growth within Indices of 13%, bolstered by strong equity market inflows and appreciation, is a key driver behind above-industry operating margins and Index segment outperformance.
  • Guidance for Energy assumes persistent sanctions-related revenue headwinds and a measured recovery timeline for Upstream operations, with client platform upgrades and expanded partnerships intended to stabilize and reposition the product suite.

Industry glossary

  • Billed Issuance: The total value of securities for which S&P Global Ratings has billed clients for credit ratings in a period, often a leading business indicator for transaction-driven revenue.
  • Vitality Index: Percentage of revenue from products or services launched within the past three years, measuring the company's innovation pace.
  • ACV (Annual Contract Value): The annualized value of recurring revenue from current contract commitments, used to track growth of subscription streams.
  • EDO (Enterprise Data Office): An internal S&P Global function dedicated to optimizing data operations, integrating data workflows, and driving automation and cost efficiencies company-wide.
