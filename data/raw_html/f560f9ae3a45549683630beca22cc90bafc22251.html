SharkNinja (SN) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 9:56AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Feb. 11, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer — Mark Barrocas
  • Chief Financial Officer — Adam Quigley

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales Growth -- Net sales rose 17.6% year over year to $2.1 billion, representing the fastest quarterly growth rate of 2025.
  • Domestic vs. International Sales -- Domestic net sales increased 15.7% to over $1.37 billion, while international net sales grew 21.4% to $729 million.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Adjusted EBITDA climbed 36% year over year to $395 million for the quarter, yielding an 18.8% margin, up approximately 250 basis points from the prior year.
  • Adjusted EPS -- Adjusted earnings per share for the quarter grew 38% year over year to $1.93; GAAP EPS nearly doubled to $1.80 from $0.91 in the prior year period.
  • Gross Margin -- Adjusted gross margin increased nearly 40 basis points to 48.2% of net sales; GAAP gross margin rose roughly 90 basis points to 47.9%.
  • Operating Expense Leverage -- Adjusted operating expenses were $645 million or 30.7% of net sales, compared to 33.5% in the prior year, resulting in nearly 280 basis points of year-over-year favorability.
  • Cash and Debt Position -- Cash and cash equivalents rose to over $777 million at quarter-end, up more than 100% year over year; total debt was $739 million.
  • Share Repurchase Program -- The board authorized a $750 million share repurchase program, intended for both opportunistic buybacks and offsetting stock-based compensation dilution.
  • Category Performance -- Cleaning category net sales rose 3.4% to $670 million; cooking and beverage increased 11.7% to $667 million; frozen treats business expanded 28.1% to $438 million; beauty and home environment grew 3.2% to $326 million, with highest growth rate of the year in this segment.
  • International Segment Acceleration -- International net sales growth accelerated to 23.2% in the second half of 2025 from 17.3% in the first half.
  • Supply Chain Diversification -- Nearly 100% of US volume can now be manufactured outside China, with a multi-country sourcing footprint across Southeast Asia.
  • Social Media Reach -- SharkNinja reached 3.9 million followers across Instagram and TikTok in 2025, growing 119% year-over-year versus peers' 8% growth on a much smaller base.
  • 2026 Outlook -- Management expects net sales to increase 10%-11%, adjusted EPS to reach $5.90-$6.00 (up 12%-14%), and adjusted EBITDA of $1.27-$1.28 billion (up 12%-13%) for 2026, assuming persistent tariffs.
  • Omnichannel Expansion -- The company deepened retailer partnerships and enhanced direct-to-consumer capabilities, including a major redesign of sharkninja.com, which is driving higher engagement, conversion, and average order value.

SUMMARY

SharkNinja (SN +0.06%) delivered record revenue and earnings growth across both domestic and international operations, with margin expansion supported by disciplined cost management and operating leverage. Management emphasized the completion of a global supply chain transformation, enabling near-complete manufacturing decentralization outside China and cited enhanced international scale following distributor-to-direct model transitions in several geographies. The company announced a $750 million share repurchase program, reflecting increased financial flexibility. Expansion in global beauty, outdoor, and frozen treat categories contributed meaningfully to overall growth, alongside improved direct-to-consumer and social media-driven demand generation. The 2026 guidance affirmed double-digit top- and bottom-line growth expectations while accounting for fully phased-in tariffs, with management reiterating their dedication to ongoing margin improvement and product category innovation.

  • Management said, "We have more products, more channels, and more geographies, giving us multiple paths to expand."
  • Mark Barrocas highlighted, "we're now a direct operator in more markets than ever before," underscoring global reach.
  • CEO Barrocas stated, "we established Shark Beauty as a disruptive innovator in skincare with the global launch of Shark TrioGlow," indicating growing traction in beauty categories.
  • Adam Quigley confirmed, "we achieved $634 million of cash from operating activities and ended the year with over $777 million of cash and cash equivalents, up more than 100% year over year."
  • Management described 2026 as "our first full year of optimization," and noted plans to introduce at least two new product categories and 25 new products in the upcoming year.
  • The company stated, "adjusted EBITDA growing 36% year over year to $395 million, roughly double the rate of top-line growth," signaling substantial operating leverage in Q4.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, excluding non-recurring items for clearer operational comparability.
  • DTC: Direct-to-consumer; sales model where the company sells products directly to end customers, bypassing traditional retail channels.
  • SKU: Stock keeping unit; a unique identifier for each distinct product and service that can be purchased.
  • GAAP: Generally Accepted Accounting Principles; standardized guidelines for financial accounting and reporting.
  • EMEA: Europe, Middle East, and Africa; a regional designation used in international financial reporting.
  • Basis Point: Equal to 0.01%, commonly used for margin or rate changes in finance.
