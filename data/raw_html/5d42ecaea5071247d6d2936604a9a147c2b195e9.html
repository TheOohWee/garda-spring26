NiSource (NI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 11, 2026 at 11:56AM EST

Takeaways

  • Adjusted EPS -- $0.51 for the quarter, totaling $1.90 for the year, above internal guidance.
  • FFO to debt -- 16.1%, surpassing the targeted 14%-16% range and reflecting a 150 basis-point increase from the prior year.
  • 2026 EPS guidance reaffirmed -- $2.02 to $2.07, representing 8% year-over-year growth with base business guidance of $2.01 to $2.05 (6%-8% annual increase versus $1.90 achieved).
  • Dividend growth -- 7.1% increase approved for 2026, aligning with a target payout ratio of 55%-65%.
  • Amazon agreement -- $1 billion expected to be returned to Indiana NIPSCO customers, estimated at $7 to $9 per customer per month over fifteen years upon full ramp.
  • Capital investment commitment -- $28 billion planned over five years, with $21 billion base utility investment focused on modernization and safety, plus $6-$7 billion toward the Amazon data center project through 2032 (majority within the five-year window).
  • Rate increase management -- Targeting average annual customer bill increases below 5%, using rate design innovations and regulatory mechanisms in Pennsylvania and Ohio to mitigate volatility.
  • Operational advancements -- Over 545,000 smart meters installed and 41,000 miles of pipeline surveyed with advanced leak detection, both exceeding targets.
  • Data center pipeline -- Active negotiations for one to three gigawatts of new capacity, and an identified pipeline of up to three additional gigawatts, signaling ongoing expansion in data center services.
  • Regulatory outcomes -- Approval of the Pennsylvania rate case ($55 million revenue increase at 10% allowed ROE) and new supportive legislation in Ohio, reducing regulatory lag and improving cost recovery alignment.
  • O&M cost control -- Commitment to flat operating and maintenance costs over the plan horizon to support affordability.
  • Dividend track record -- Five consecutive years outperforming upper guidance end; 8.5% adjusted EPS CAGR since 2021 versus a 6.4% peer median.
  • Genco earnings initiation -- Genco expected to contribute $0.01 to $0.02 per share beginning in 2026, with future incremental disclosures as it becomes material.
  • Shaker coal plant order -- Federal mandate received in December to continue operation beyond planned retirement, with a cost recovery filing submitted to FERC.
  • Severe weather response -- Use of storage allowed 75% of total load to be served at below-market rates, reducing customer bill impacts and limiting service disruptions during the recent winter storm.

Summary

NiSource (NI 0.87%) highlighted robust execution on strategic initiatives, including a landmark $1 billion customer value return agreement with Amazon (NASDAQ:AMZN) and substantial progress on grid modernization. The management outlined the Amazon project as a central pillar for near-term and long-term growth, with key regulatory approvals—such as Jasper County zoning—already achieved, and further milestones pending Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission decision. Regulatory outcomes in Pennsylvania and new Ohio legislation were positioned as drivers for future revenue and reduced regulatory lag, while NIPSCO’s operational resilience was demonstrated during significant winter events without substantial outages. Management emphasized disciplined capital deployment and confirmed adaptable funding strategies, including maintenance ATM equity and project-level debt options at Genco. Strategic focus areas included expansion in data center services, economic development support, and advancing innovative operations, such as AI-enabled system monitoring and supply chain enhancements.

  • During Q&A, management stated strategic negotiations for additional data center capacity are not contingent upon IURC approval of the Amazon special contract.
  • The company identified a $2 billion reservoir of upside capital investments supporting utility operations, with ongoing evaluation of long-range transmission, PHMSA compliance, and advanced metering initiatives for potential inclusion in base plans.
  • Shawn Anderson said, "We have not inquired incorporated any upside from economic development or the procurement of large load customers into the Columbia Gas Ohio forecast yet," clarifying COH outlook excludes potential benefits from Ohio's recent legislative changes.
  • Lloyd Yates stated, "no pending rate cases outstanding," but indicated that some regulatory activity remains likely as per standard business practice.
  • Future reporting will provide incremental financial disclosure for the Genco business as it reaches materiality in 2026 results, with no segment break-out in 2025 reporting.

Industry glossary

  • Genco: NiSource’s internal entity created to execute large customer electricity supply agreements, particularly for data centers, and manage associated infrastructure investments.
  • FFO to debt: Ratio of funds from operations to total debt, a key utility sector leverage and credit quality metric.
  • Capital tracker: A regulatory mechanism allowing utilities to recover costs of specific capital investments outside of general rate cases, improving cost recovery timeliness.
  • IURC: Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission, the primary state regulator overseeing utility rates and service for NIPSCO and related projects.
  • PHMSA: Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration, a federal agency setting requirements relevant for gas infrastructure compliance.
  • MISO: Midcontinent Independent System Operator, responsible for regional electric grid planning and reliability standards impacting NiSource’s generation planning.
