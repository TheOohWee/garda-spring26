iRhythm (IRTC) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Aug 1, 2025 at 9:45AM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, July 31, 2025 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

President & CEO — Quentin S. Blackford

Chief Financial Officer — Daniel G. Wilson

Need a quote from one of our analysts? Email [email protected]

RISKS

Remediation Expenses: Chief Financial Officer Wilson said, "we continue to incur incremental legal and consulting fees as well as other company expenses related to FDA remediation efforts and DOJ subpoena activities."

Free Cash Flow: Wilson stated, "we continue to anticipate being slightly free cash flow negative (non-GAAP) for full year 2025 and expect to become free cash flow positive (non-GAAP) in full year 2026." He cited headwinds from prioritized investments and inventory buildup.

Gross Margin Pressure: Wilson said, "We anticipate that third quarter gross margin will decline slightly from second quarter due to hiring within our clinical operations teams ahead of anticipated volume growth."

Tariff Impact: Wilson reported, "The expected negative impact of tariffs on full year 2025 gross margin (non-GAAP) is approximately 20 to 40 basis points, with a greater effect in the second half of the year."

TAKEAWAYS

Revenue: $186.7 million, up 26.1% year-over-year, driven by long-term monitoring business, innovative channel partners, and Zio AT product line.

Adjusted EBITDA Margin: 8.4% in Q2 2025, a 500 basis point improvement over the prior year.

Gross Margin: 71.2% (non-GAAP) in Q2 2025, supported by volume leverage and operational efficiencies, offset by higher Zio AT mix cost per unit.

Adjusted Net Loss: $10.2 million, or $0.32 per share, improved from $18.8 million and $0.61 per share, respectively, in Q2 2024.

New Account Volume Growth: Accounts open less than 12 months contributed approximately 68% of year-over-year volume growth in Q2 2025 (non-GAAP).

Home Enrollment for Zio Services: Accounted for 23% of U.S. volume in Q2 2025.

Full-Year Revenue Guidance Raised: Full year 2025 non-GAAP revenue guidance is now $720 million–$730 million, citing first-half outperformance and continued momentum.

Adjusted EBITDA Outlook: Full year adjusted EBITDA margin outlook updated to 8%-8.5%, with Q3 margin expected at 9%-9.5%.

International Expansion: Eight new commercial accounts launched in Japan and continued growth in four European markets, as discussed in Q2 2025.

Zio MCT Regulatory Pathway: Next-generation Zio MCT is expected to have a 510(k) submission in the third quarter of 2025.

Cash Position: $545.5 million in unrestricted cash at the end of Q3 2025.

CMS Rate Impact: Preliminary CMS rates for calendar year 2026 are expected to be flat to slightly up for Zio monitor and up approximately 2% for Zio AT, with CMS comprising roughly 24% of revenues.

Aura (Epic EHR) Integration: Over 40 health systems implementing or preparing integrations, with average reductions in project implementation time by over 50% and observed prescribing pattern increases of 20%-40% post-integration in some accounts, as reported by iRhythm management.

Innovative Channel Partners: Contribution (non-GAAP) increased quarter-over-quarter in Q2 2025, with 12 partners under contract covering about 2 million potential patients as of Q2 2025; pilots demonstrated 80%-90% arrhythmia detection yields using Lucem Health's AI.

SUMMARY

iRhythm Technologies, Inc.(IRTC 2.47%) attributed its full year 2025 revenue guidance increase primarily to accelerated onboarding of large accounts and expanded integration activity in its core monitoring business. Management expects operational efficiencies to offset tariff-related headwinds in gross margin guidance, with clinical operations hiring temporarily lowering margins in Q3 2025. The company highlighted a strategic focus on AI-enabled, integrated, and population health solutions, including a partnership with Lucem Health for AI-powered patient identification. The next-generation Zio MCT, featuring extended wear times and advanced detection capabilities, is positioned as a future growth driver, with a 510(k) submission expected in Q3 2025. Ongoing FDA remediation and a planned third-party audit reflect a commitment to regulatory compliance supporting operational scalability and new product launches.

President & CEO Blackford said, "this partnership is a first-of-its-kind offering utilizing Lucem's predictive AI to help identify arrhythmias sooner in patient populations at elevated risk."

Chief Financial Officer Wilson emphasized, "Incremental remediation expenses are expected to be approximately $15 million in 2025."

Home enrollment represented 23% of quarterly Zio volume, indicating a shift in patient monitoring modalities.

New store growth, defined as accounts opened within the last 12 months, accounted for about 68% of year-over-year volume increase in Q2 2025 (non-GAAP).

Management has committed to submitting the next-generation Zio MCT for 510(k) review in the third quarter of 2025, while continuing ongoing FDA warning letter remediation in parallel.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

Zio AT: A patch-based, wire-free cardiac arrhythmia monitoring device providing real-time data transmission for ambulatory patients.

Zio monitor (Zio XT): A single-use, extended-wear patch device that continuously records ECG data for up to 14 days in ambulatory cardiac monitoring.

MCT (Mobile Cardiac Telemetry): A category of ambulatory cardiac monitoring devices offering near real-time arrhythmia detection by transmitting ECG data wirelessly.

Aura (Epic Aura): iRhythm's integration solution with Epic EHR systems, streamlining order workflow and report delivery for Zio services within health systems.

IDN (Integrated Delivery Network): A network of healthcare facilities and providers under a single parent organization, facilitating coordinated patient care, a major commercial focus for iRhythm.

510(k) submission: Regulatory process by which medical device manufacturers seek U.S. FDA clearance to market new or modified devices by demonstrating substantial equivalence to a legally marketed device.
