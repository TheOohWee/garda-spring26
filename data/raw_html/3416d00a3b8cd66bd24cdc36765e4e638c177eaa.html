Aurora Cannabis (ACB) Q3 2026 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Feb 4, 2026 at 8:58AM EST

Call participants

  • Chief Executive Officer — Miguel Martin
  • Chief Financial Officer — Simona King

Takeaways

  • Net revenue -- $94.2 million, up 7%, with global medical cannabis contributing a record $76.2 million and representing 81% of total net revenue.
  • International medical revenue -- Increased 17%, driven by growth in Germany and new product launches in Poland.
  • Adjusted gross margin -- Rose 100 basis points to 62% overall; medical cannabis segment margin remained high at 69%.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $18.5 million, reflecting continued profitability focus.
  • Adjusted net income -- $7.2 million, holding steady against the prior-year quarter's $7.4 million.
  • Free cash flow -- $15.5 million, albeit down from $27.4 million last year due to a lower working capital recovery.
  • Cash position -- $154 million in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments, with zero cannabis business-related debt reported.
  • Consumer cannabis segment -- Net revenue declined 48% to $5.2 million, a result explicitly tied to strategic market exits and prioritization of medical channels.
  • Plant propagation segment -- Revenue rose 27% to $11.3 million, but adjusted gross margin declined to 16% from 40% due to higher contract labor, utilities, and a $1.1 million inventory write-off.
  • Adjusted SG&A -- Increased 14.5% to $35.8 million, reflecting higher professional fees and expanding headcount in Europe and Australia.
  • Strategic actions -- Company will exit select lower-margin Canadian consumer cannabis markets and divest its controlling stake in the Bevo plant propagation business.
  • At-the-market (ATM) equity program -- Established with flexibility to issue and sell up to $100 million in common shares for "strategic and accretive purposes," including cultivation expansion and potential M&A.
  • Geographic revenue mix -- More than half of total net revenue generated outside Canada, showing growing international scale.
  • Production profile -- Approximately 90% of annual manufacturing capacity from European and TGA GMP-certified facilities, a competitive barrier for most peers.
  • Full-year guidance -- Global medical cannabis net revenue expected between $269 million and $281 million; consolidated adjusted EBITDA guided to $52 million to $57 million, a 5%-10% annual increase.

Summary

Aurora Cannabis (ACB 1.44%) prioritized higher-margin international medical cannabis markets with record segment revenue and a majority of net revenue now outside Canada, confirming ongoing strategic realignment. The company will further streamline operations by exiting select Canadian consumer markets and divesting its controlling interest in the lower-margin plant propagation business, reallocating resources for global medical cannabis growth and operational efficiency. Management established a $100 million at-the-market equity program, emphasizing a commitment to accretive use of capital and selective M&A pursuits.

  • Management confirmed, "we generated positive free cash flow of $15.5 million and maintained our strong balance sheet with over $150 million in cash and the absence of cannabis business-related debt."
  • Strategic guidance anticipates global medical cannabis net revenue of $269 million to $281 million and consolidated adjusted EBITDA growth of 5%-10%, positioning for further profitability improvement.
  • Plant propagation operations will be deconsolidated in Q4, with future financial focus shifting away from discontinued lower-margin business lines.
  • Company highlighted its certification in three major GMP facilities, a stringent regulatory advantage aimed at supporting growth throughout Europe, Australia, and new markets with established science-based frameworks.
  • Australian and Polish business segments are undergoing product mix upgrades, which may temporarily pressure margins but are intended to accelerate premium-category growth and market share.

Industry glossary

  • GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice): International quality-control certification required for pharmaceutical and regulated cannabis production, critical for market access in Europe and Australia.
  • TGA (Therapeutic Goods Administration): The regulatory authority in Australia responsible for certifying medical cannabis manufacturing and products.
  • ATM (At-the-market) equity program: A flexible financing structure allowing a company to sell shares directly into the market over time, rather than in a single offering, optimizing timing and pricing.
