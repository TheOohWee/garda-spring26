Dutch Bros (BROS) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Updated Feb 12, 2026 at 6:24PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, February 12, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chief Executive Officer and President — Christine Barone
  • Chief Financial Officer — Joshua Guenser
  • Chief Shop Officer — Jen Summers

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $1.64 billion for 2025, reflecting a 28% increase.
  • Q4 Revenue -- $444 million, up 29% or $101 million.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $303 million for 2025, a 31% increase and more than threefold growth since 2022.
  • Q4 Adjusted EBITDA -- $73 million, reflecting a 49% or $24 million increase.
  • System Same Shop Sales Growth -- 5.6% for 2025; Q4 system same shop sales up 7.7%.
  • Company-Operated Same Shop Sales Growth -- 9.7% in Q4, primarily driven by 7.6% transaction growth.
  • System Transaction Growth -- 5.4% in Q4 and 3.2% for the year, both called out as main growth drivers.
  • System Shop Count -- 1,136 at year-end, following 154 new shop openings in 2025.
  • Company-Operated Shop Mix -- Over 70% of the system shop count is now company-operated.
  • System Average Unit Volume (AUV) -- Reached a record $2.1 million; cited as evidence of strong shop-level economics.
  • Company-Operated Shop Contribution Margin -- 28.9% for 2025, up more than 400 basis points since 2022, and 27.6% in Q4.
  • 2025 CapEx per Shop -- Averaged $1.3 million in Q4, down from $1.8 million in Q4 2024.
  • Adjusted SG&A Leverage -- Improved by 140 basis points in 2025; guidance calls for another 70 basis points of leverage in 2026.
  • Order Ahead Program Penetration -- 14% of Q4 transactions, with a higher mix at the LA walk-up location.
  • Dutch Rewards Membership -- Exceeded 15 million at year-end, with 72% of 2025 transactions attributed to the program—up four points from 2024.
  • New Food Program Rollout -- Expanded to 300+ shops across 11 states by year-end, with early results indicating an approximate 4% comp lift in participating shops.
  • Clutch Coffee Bar Acquisition -- 20-location acquisition in North and South Carolina for approximately $20 million, to convert and integrate in 2026.
  • 2026 Guidance -- Total revenues projected at $2.0 billion-$2.03 billion (up 22%-24%); 181+ new system shops planned; system same shop sales growth targeted at 3%-5%; adjusted EBITDA expected at $355 million-$365 million; and capital expenditures planned at $270 million-$290 million including Clutch conversion.
  • Total Liquidity -- $705 million at year-end: $269 million in cash and cash equivalents, and $435 million available under the revolver.

RISKS

  • Joshua Guenser said, "we are expecting about 200 basis points of margin headwind in Q1," primarily due to elevated coffee costs and the food rollout, contributing to anticipated 60 basis points of full-year EBITDA margin pressure.
  • Company-Operated shop beverage, food, and packaging costs were 27% of revenue in Q4, an unfavorable increase of 160 basis points year over year, attributed to higher coffee costs and food program expenses.
  • "nearly 300 legacy shops may not be able to accommodate the new food program," which could limit full systemwide comp lift potential from the initiative.
  • Occupancy and other costs are expected to rise in 2026 as more leases shift to build-to-suit, partially offsetting other margin improvements.

SUMMARY

Dutch Bros (BROS +0.05%) reported accelerated growth in system-wide AUVs driven by refined real estate selection, operational efficiencies, and record transaction volumes across a broadened customer base. The company advanced its CPG strategy, growing retail presence for packaged products to supplement in-shop engagement and drive brand awareness. Management stressed the importance of loyalty, with Dutch Rewards now surpassing 15 million members and driving a larger share of total transactions. Strategic investments in food program rollouts and order ahead features are gaining traction, with early metrics providing confidence in further transaction and ticket growth as these initiatives reach scale. Free cash flow generation strengthened, marking the second consecutive year of consistent net cash addition, and the company expects to sustain robust liquidity while executing further expansion in 2026.

  • Retail innovation extended to urban corridors with the LA walk-up format, which quickly became a top-performing location and may inform future non-drive-thru entry strategies.
  • Pipeline depth for regional operator candidates increased to 475 by year-end, supporting the aspirational goal of 2,029 total shops by 2029 and enhanced expansion visibility.
  • Chief Shop Officer Jen Summers was onboarded to focus on scaling operations and customer experience as new shop formats, food offerings, and digital initiatives roll out.
  • Management confirmed that the $20 million cost for the Clutch Coffee Bar acquisition is included in the 2026 capital expenditure plan.
  • "We are not really seeing anything on a local level," Barone said regarding competitive threats, describing consistent value proposition strength and stable real estate access despite new entrants.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • AUV (Average Unit Volume): Total annual sales per store, used to measure shop-level revenue performance and benchmark new shop openings.
  • Build-to-Suit Lease: Lease agreement where a developer builds and leases a custom facility to the tenant (Dutch Bros Inc.), shifting capital intensity away from the company while increasing occupancy costs.
  • Four-Wall Economics: Measurement of shop-level profitability, excluding corporate overhead, to gauge unit margin strength.
  • Order Ahead: Mobile or digital pre-order channel that allows customers to place and schedule orders before arriving at the shop, cited as a catalyst for higher transaction volume and Dutch Rewards adoption.
  • Contribution Margin: Margin after subtracting direct operating costs (like product, labor, and occupancy) from revenue at individual shop level, exclusive of corporate overhead and other indirect expenses.
  • CPG (Consumer Packaged Goods): Packaged Dutch Bros Inc. products, such as creamers and ready-to-drink beverages, available in external retail outlets beyond company shops.
  • Comp Lift: Year-over-year increase in same shop sales, used to measure incremental benefit from initiatives like the food program.
