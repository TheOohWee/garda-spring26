Risks

  • Dave Schaeffer said, "we are still experiencing significant monthly and quarterly sequential degradation in that business," confirming ongoing revenue attrition despite integration efforts.
  • Revenue declines remain attributed to acquired Sprint wireline corporate and enterprise segments, with acquired corporate revenues dropping 79% from $39 million per quarter at closing to $8.1 million this quarter.
  • For enterprise, acquired revenues decreased from $60 million per quarter at close to $26.4 million, indicating persistent structural decline in the acquired base.
  • "EBITDA as adjusted margins were 30% for the full year 2025, down from 33.6 for the full year 2024 because of the reductions that I just previously mentioned," highlighting margin contraction related to transaction-related cost reductions rolling off.

Takeaways

  • Total revenue -- $200.1 million for the quarter and $975.8 million for the year; quarterly revenue declined sequentially by $1.4 million or 0.6%, a smaller decline compared to the previous quarter.
  • On-net revenue -- $146.4 million for the quarter, up 7.8% year over year and 0.6% sequentially; represented 58.4% of total revenue for the year, increasing from 54.4% in 2024.
  • Off-net revenue -- $92.9 million for the quarter, declined by 17.9% year over year and 2.3% sequentially; down to 40.7% of revenues for the year, from 43.8% previously.
  • Wavelength revenue -- $12.1 million this quarter, a 74% year-over-year increase and 18.8% sequential growth; full-year wavelength revenue reached $38.5 million, doubling from 2024.
  • EBITDA (as adjusted) -- $76.7 million for the quarter, up $3 million sequentially; EBITDA margin rose 140 basis points sequentially to 31.9%.
  • EBITDA classic -- $192.8 million for the year, up $70 million, with margin increasing to 19.8% from 11.9% year over year (an improvement of 790 basis points).
  • Gross margin -- $112.5 million for the quarter, up $1.6 million sequentially; quarterly gross margin rose 100 basis points to 46.8%. Full-year gross margin was $442.7 million, up 720 basis points to 45.4%.
  • IPv4 leasing revenue -- $64.5 million for the year, increasing 44% year over year; addresses leased were 15.3 million, up 17%.
  • Churn rates -- On-net monthly churn improved to 1.2% from 1.3% last quarter; off-net churn improved to 1.9% from 2.1%; wavelength churn remained below 0.5%.
  • CapEx -- $37 million for the quarter and $187.6 million for the year; materially lower compared to prior period, driven by data center reconfiguration completion.
  • Debt and leverage -- Gross debt at par was $2.4 billion; net debt $1.9 billion; most restrictive covenant leverage ratio at year end was 6.13; secured leverage 3.8; fixed charge coverage 2.38.
  • Sprint wireline revenue -- The acquired revenue base declined by 64% to $43 million per quarter since acquisition; acquired Sprint revenues now represent 18% of year-end total revenue, down from 42% at transaction close.
  • Sales rep productivity -- Averaged 4.1 units this quarter, down from 4.6 last quarter but up from 3.5 in 2024; long-term average is 4.8.
  • On-net buildings -- Increased by 126 during the year, reaching 3,579 total on-net buildings.
  • Wavelength footprint -- Wavelength services offered in 1,096 locations as of call date, up from 1,068 at year end; 2,064 connections at quarter end, up 18% sequentially.
  • 2026-2027 T-Mobile transit payments -- 23 remaining monthly payments of $8.3 million each to be received; additional $28 million payment in four installments expected December 2027 to March 2028.
  • NetCentric revenue -- Represented 43% of revenues this quarter, up from 40.3% in 2024; quarterly NetCentric revenues up 10.4% year over year and 3.1% sequentially.
  • Corporate revenue -- 42.7% of quarterly revenue; decreased 9.1% year over year, 2.3% sequentially, and declined 9.7% for full year.
  • Enterprise revenue -- 14.3% of quarterly revenue; quarterly decline of 24.7% year over year and 5.8% sequentially; declined 20.3% for full year.

Summary

Cogent Communications Holdings (CCOI 0.64%) delivered sequential quarterly revenue growth in its core Cogent Classic operations, offsetting accelerated attrition of the Sprint-acquired revenue base, which now comprises only 18% of total revenue compared to 42% at acquisition. The company executed a mix shift toward on-net services, driving year over year gross margin expansion of 720 basis points and pushing on-net revenue to 58.4% of the total for the full year 2025. Wavelength revenue expanded 74% year over year with 18.8% sequential growth, while NetCentric revenues rose 10.4% year over year as the product mix continues to diversify. Capital intensity declined materially following completion of data center conversions, and management reiterated a firm target to delever to four times net leverage before resuming elevated return of capital to shareholders.

  • Cogent expects multi-year average revenue growth in the 6%-8% range and approximately 200 basis points of EBITDA margin expansion annually, though management specified that "The nearly 800 basis points that we delivered this year was due to some extraordinary cost savings," which are not expected to recur.
  • Refinancing plans for $750 million in unsecured 2027 notes involve an equal-value secured debt issuance with execution expected post-June make-whole period expiration, independent of potential data center sale proceeds.
  • Wavelength business trajectory is forecast to accelerate, as "nearly 1,100 sites wave-enabled" and only "less than 2% market share in North America" provide a significant addressable opportunity relative to current penetration.
  • IPv4 leasing continues to show demand, with 44% annual revenue growth and 17% address growth, although QOQ revenue declined due to timing of block conversions to wholesale contracts.
  • Management confirmed, "we absolutely expect our capital expenditures to go down," and indicated unlevered and levered free cash flow will be positive even accounting for higher coupon rates post-refinancing.
  • Current sale negotiations for surplus data centers are ongoing after a prior LOI was terminated due to an "unacceptable" shift toward owner financing; backup offers may cover multiple sites, and timing of a transaction announcement is expected within a few months.
  • Cogent completed the integration of the Sprint and Cogent networks, with all targeted Sprint-acquired facilities now converted, materially reducing capital intensity for 2026 and beyond.
  • Top 25 customers accounted for 17% of revenue, underscoring limited customer concentration risk and stable revenue diversification.

Industry glossary

  • On-net: Services provided within facilities directly connected to Cogent's own backbone network, yielding higher margins due to direct infrastructure control.
  • Off-net: Services delivered to customer locations not directly on Cogent's network, typically utilizing third-party carriers for last-mile connectivity and yielding lower margins.
  • Wavelength: High-capacity optical transport service characterized by dedicated bandwidth at 10 Gbps, 100 Gbps, or 400 Gbps increments, often sold to carriers and hyperscalers.
  • EBITDA classic: Cogent's core adjusted EBITDA metric excluding acquisition and IP transit agreement items, reflecting underlying operating profitability after integration of acquired businesses.
  • NetCentric: High-volume, bandwidth-intensive customer segment such as streaming media providers, content networks, and platforms utilizing Cogent's IP or backbone services.
  • ARPU: Average revenue per user, a performance measure indicating the mean revenue generated per connection or address in a given segment.
  • IRU (indefeasible right of use): A long-term lease of network or fiber infrastructure, providing exclusive usage rights over the contract term, commonly used in telecommunications asset finance.
