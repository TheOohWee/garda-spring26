TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $15.3 billion, up 10% year over year, achieving a company quarterly record.
  • Product Revenue -- $11.6 billion, up 14% year over year, with networking leading segment growth.
  • Networking Revenue Growth -- 21% increase, driven by AI infrastructure and campus refresh demand.
  • Services Revenue -- $3.7 billion, down 1% year over year.
  • Non-GAAP Net Income -- $4.1 billion, up 10% year over year.
  • Non-GAAP EPS -- $1.04, an increase of 11%, representing EPS growth faster than revenue.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin -- 67.5%, down 120 basis points year over year, driven by mix and higher memory costs.
  • Non-GAAP Product Gross Margin -- 66.4%, down 130 basis points year over year primarily due to adverse product mix and increased memory costs, partially offset by productivity gains.
  • Non-GAAP Services Gross Margin -- 70.9%, down 70 basis points year over year.
  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin -- 34.6%, above the high end of guidance.
  • Total RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations) -- $43.4 billion, up 5% with product RPO up 8% and long-term product RPO $11.8 billion, up 11%.
  • Total ARR (Annualized Recurring Revenue) -- $31 billion, up 3%, with product ARR up 6%.
  • Subscription Revenue -- $7.8 billion, representing 51% of total revenue.
  • Total Software Revenue -- $5.7 billion, up 2% year over year.
  • Product Orders -- Up 18% year over year, highest growth from service provider and cloud at 65% and double-digit increases across all segments and geographies.
  • AI Infrastructure Orders -- $2.1 billion taken from hyperscalers, up from $1.3 billion in the previous quarter and matching full-year fiscal 2025 levels.
  • Full-Year AI Order Expectation -- Now projected to exceed $5 billion, with more than $3 billion in fiscal 2026 revenue to be recognized from hyperscalers.
  • Dividend Increase -- Quarterly dividend raised by $0.01 to $0.42 per share, with management reiterating commitment to return at least 50% of free cash flow annually to shareholders.
  • Capital Returned to Shareholders -- $3 billion during the quarter, totaling $6.6 billion year-to-date.
  • Advanced Purchase Commitments -- Increased $1.8 billion over the last 90 days and 73% year over year, primarily for memory components.
  • Security Segment Revenue -- Down 4% year over year, with new and refreshed security products accounting for roughly one-third of the portfolio and reporting over 1,000 new customers in the quarter.
  • Splunk Business -- Continued shift to cloud subscriptions, which management stated "is creating a drag on revenue growth" and is expected to persist into the second half of the fiscal year.
  • Guidance: Q3 Fiscal 2026 -- Revenue projected at $15.4 billion-$15.6 billion; non-GAAP gross margin 65.5%-66.5%; non-GAAP operating margin 33.5%-34.5%; and non-GAAP EPS $1.02-$1.04.
  • Guidance: Full-Year Fiscal 2026 -- Revenue expected in the range of $61.2 billion-$61.7 billion; non-GAAP EPS between $4.13 and $4.17.
  • Silicon One Milestone -- Shipped 1 millionth chip; new 102.4 terabit G300 chip and four accompanying systems introduced this week.
  • Acacia Bookings -- Achieved triple-digit growth with all major hyperscalers deploying its coherent pluggable optics.
  • Industrial IoT -- Reported double-digit growth for the seventh consecutive quarter, supported by U.S. manufacturing onshoring and edge AI workloads.
  • Campus Networking Product Orders -- Excluding hyperscalers, up 10% year over year globally; enterprise orders up 8%, and public sector up 11% across all geographies.
  • Joint Venture Announcement -- Plans with AMD and HUMAIN to deliver up to 1 gigawatt of AI infrastructure by 2030, starting with a 100-megawatt build in Saudi Arabia this year.

SUMMARY

Cisco Systems (CSCO 1.14%) highlighted accelerating AI infrastructure demand, noting a surge in orders and pipeline visibility, while operational leverage sustained profit outpacing revenue growth. Management attributed gross margin compression to component cost increases, especially memory, and outlined actions to mitigate these pressures through price adjustments and revised contractual terms. Strong double-digit growth in networking and ongoing transitions in security and software, particularly the Splunk move from on-premise to cloud subscriptions, shaped segment performance throughout the period.

  • CFO Patterson cited that "advanced purchase commitments that just in the last 90 days are up $1.8 billion." with the "If you look at it on a year-over-year basis, they're up about 73%. A big chunk of that is around memory."
  • The G300 and P200 products, as clarified by Robbins, "are not in the $5 billion expected for fiscal '26," allowing for upside in future AI contributions.
  • Security platform adoption, including Hypershield and AI Defense, gained pace, with management referencing 1,000 net new customers in the quarter.
  • Campus and enterprise networking refresh "is just getting started," with management viewing it as a multiyear and multibillion-dollar opportunity.
  • Cisco operators stated that recent and planned increases in product pricing have not driven significant customer pull-forwards or order deferrals.
  • Management disclosed that Q3 and full-year guidance assumes current tariffs and related exemptions remain through fiscal 2026.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations): The total value of contracted revenue yet to be recognized, including both short- and long-term customer agreements.
  • ARR (Annualized Recurring Revenue): The run-rate value of recurring revenue components at the end of a reporting period, often used to measure growth in software and subscription sales.
  • Hyperscalers: Large-scale cloud service providers (such as Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud) that purchase substantial volumes of networking, compute, and storage infrastructure.
  • SASE (Secure Access Service Edge): A security framework combining wide-area networking and network security functions delivered from the cloud to securely connect users, systems, and endpoints.
  • AI Defense: Cisco's designated suite of AI-driven cybersecurity solutions, designed for vulnerability scanning and centralized governance of AI models.
  • Silicon One: Cisco’s proprietary family of networking semiconductors and associated hardware platforms aimed at high-speed, high-efficiency data center networking and AI workloads.
  • G300 Chip: Cisco’s newly introduced 102.4 terabit per second switching chip, intended to serve next-generation data center and AI infrastructure demands.
  • Acacia: Cisco’s optoelectronics and optical interconnect subsidiary, focused on high-speed coherent optics for cloud and service provider clients.
