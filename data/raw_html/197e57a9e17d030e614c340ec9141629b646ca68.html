Aurora Mobile (JG) Q3 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Nov 13, 2025 at 11:29AM EST

Thursday, Nov. 13, 2025 at 7:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Weidong Luo
  • Chief Financial Officer — Shan-Nen Bong
  • General Manager — Guanyang Chen
  • Host — Rene Vanguestaine

Need a quote from a Motley Fool analyst? Email [email protected]

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- RMB 19,900,000, representing 15% year-over-year growth and 1% sequential increase, at the high end of company guidance.
  • EngageLab ARR -- RMB 53,700,000 as of September 2025, up 160% year-over-year, marking an all-time high for the product.
  • Financial Risk Management Revenue -- RMB 22,600,000, achieving 33% year-over-year growth and reaching a historic quarterly record for the segment.
  • Gross Profit -- RMB 63,800,000, up 20% year-over-year and 7% sequentially, the highest in the past fifteen quarters.
  • Gross Margin -- Improved both year-over-year and sequentially, driven by strong high-margin revenue performance.
  • GAAP Net Profit -- Company delivered its first-ever consecutive quarters of GAAP net profit.
  • Deferred Revenue -- RMB 166,300,000, a historical high, signaling strong future revenue recognition and robust cash collection.
  • Net Operating Cash Inflow -- RMB 33,300,000, the best quarterly result since 2020.
  • Developer Subscription Revenue -- RMB 57,300,000, up 11% year-over-year and 7% sequentially, marking a record for the business line.
  • Value-Added Services Revenue -- RMB 7,100,000, up 22% year-over-year but down 34% sequentially, affected by the absence of a traditional quarter online shopping festival.
  • Net Dollar Retention Rate (NDR) -- 104% for core developer service business over the trailing twelve months, surpassing the 100% milestone.
  • Global Customer Growth (EngageLab) -- Customer count rose 156% year-over-year to 1,312, covering 52+ countries and regions.
  • Q4 2025 Revenue Guidance -- Company projects RMB 94,000,000 to RMB 96,000,000, representing expected year-over-year growth of 1%-3%.
  • Share Repurchase Program -- Board authorized up to US$10,000,000 in share repurchases over twelve months, doubling the previous limit.
  • Accounts Receivable Turnover -- Maintained at forty-nine days, denoted as industry-leading by management.
  • Operating Expenses -- Increased 12.8% year-over-year and 5.8% quarter-over-quarter, outpaced by revenue and gross profit growth.

SUMMARY

The company signaled sustained global expansion for EngageLab, including new infrastructure investment, highlighted by a recently launched data center in Turkey. Management attributed EngageLab’s success to continuous product and service upgrades, which enabled strong global customer momentum and high contract values. Results showed positive trends in client retention and upselling, evidenced by NDR above 100% for the first time in company history.

  • CFO Shan-Nen Bong said, "This 33% year-over-year revenue growth mainly due to a strong 44% in customer number growth."
  • Developer subscription services notched their highest-ever quarterly revenue, attributed to both new customer additions and higher ARPU.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ARR: Annual Recurring Revenue; a SaaS metric that annualizes the value of subscription contracts as of a specific point in time.
  • Net Dollar Retention (NDR): The percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers over a period, including expansions, downgrades, and churn.
  • EngageLab: Aurora Mobile’s cloud-based customer engagement platform, offering multi-channel messaging capabilities for global enterprise clients.
