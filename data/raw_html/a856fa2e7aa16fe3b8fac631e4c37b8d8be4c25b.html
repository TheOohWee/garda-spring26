Takeaways

  • Passengers transported -- Over 22.9 million passengers were transported, supporting LATAM Airlines Group S.A. (LTM +0.19%) position as South America's leading airline group.
  • Capacity expansion -- Available seat kilometers grew 9.3% year over year, reflecting continued regional growth.
  • Load factor -- Consolidated load factor reached 85.4%, indicating healthy network utilization.
  • Passenger unit revenue -- Passenger RASK increased 8.4% year over year in U.S. dollars, demonstrating improved revenue generation.
  • Adjusted operating margin -- Adjusted operating margin expanded to 18.1%, reflecting margin improvement.
  • Adjusted EBITDAR -- Adjusted EBITDAR totaled $1.15 billion for the quarter, contributing to cash generation and financial position.
  • Net income -- Net income reached $379 million, up 26%, despite a $105 million negative nonoperational income statement impact from a liability management exercise.
  • Premium revenue growth -- Revenue from premium travelers increased by more than 15%, outpacing overall passenger revenue growth.
  • Cargo revenue -- Cargo revenues grew 6.3% year over year, supporting total revenue expansion.
  • Fleet expansion -- LATAM Airlines Group S.A. signed for up to 74 Embraer E2 aircraft, with 24 scheduled for delivery through 2027 to enhance regional connectivity and fuel efficiency by 30% per seat.
  • Aircraft order book -- The group's aircraft order book now exceeds 140 aircraft through 2030, underpinning the long-term fleet modernization strategy.
  • Share repurchase -- A $433 million share repurchase program was executed, aligning with the company's disciplined capital allocation targets.
  • Liquidity -- Quarter-end liquidity was 25.8%, slightly above the upper end of LATAM's financial policy range, brought back toward target by the share repurchase program.
  • Net leverage -- The adjusted net leverage ratio at quarter-end stood at 1.5x, aligned with full-year guidance and under the policy cap.
  • Adjusted operating cash flow -- Adjusted operating cash flow generation was $859 million, reflecting strong underlying business fundamentals.
  • Interest expense -- Interest payments totaled $52 million, reduced by refinancing activity in 2024, with expected annual savings of $151 million in 2026 versus the prior year.
  • Full-year 2025 guidance update -- Adjusted EBITDAR guidance was refined upward to $4 billion-$4.1 billion, approximately 9% above previous estimates.
  • Cost outlook -- Updated adjusted passenger CASK ex-fuel stands at $4.35-$4.40, revised in response to FX variation.
  • Capacity guidance -- High single-digit consolidated capacity growth is targeted for next year, driven by a robust fleet delivery and efficiency focus.
  • Shareholder returns -- Management reaffirmed capital allocation priorities focus first on business growth, with shareholder returns driven by results and opportunities.
  • Recognition -- The group was named APEX Five-Star Global Airline for the fourth consecutive year, while LATAM Cargo Group won Air Cargo Airline of the Year by Air Cargo News.
  • Fuel hedge position -- Fuel hedges covered 47% of needs for Q4 and 33% for Q1 2026 as of November 14, 2025, consistent with the existing policy.
  • Booking curve and fleet for next year -- Initial bookings for early 2026 trend similarly to third-quarter patterns, and the group expects to end 2026 with approximately 410 aircraft.
  • Premium product initiatives -- The company will launch a Premium Comfort Class for long-haul routes in 2027, broadening its cabin offering to support premium revenue growth.
  • Regulatory development in Brazil -- The lower chamber in Brazil passed a law for mandatory free bag and seat selection on both domestic and international flights; the bill awaits Senate action and potential presidential veto.
  • Potential union action -- Management stated, "At this point in time, we have no clarity of the potential impact" regarding the ongoing Chile pilot strike.

Summary

LATAM Airlines Group S.A. highlighted an expanded network and strong financial execution, reinforced by higher passenger volumes and capacity. The company advanced its fleet modernization with a major Embraer E2 aircraft order, targeting further efficiency and access to new destinations. Management updated full-year guidance upward on adjusted EBITDAR and capacity, reflecting better margins and operating performance. Significant capital was returned via a $433 million share repurchase, bringing liquidity back toward internal policy targets. The group continued its shift toward premium revenue, supported by targeted customer experience enhancements and new product launches.

  • CEO Alvo Milosawlewitsch cited ongoing cost discipline and execution focus as cornerstones of LATAM's strategy.
  • The Embraer E2 addition is expected to open up to 35 new destinations, boosting future revenue prospects and regional reach.
  • LATAM Airlines Brazil expanded its domestic network with six new routes and an over 12% increase in capacity, leading to a 2.2 percentage-point improvement in load factor there.
  • Management attributed Spanish-speaking affiliate RASK growth (18%) to a positive mix shift toward premium products and rising customer preference, even as Chile's market remained flat.
  • International demand trends showed relative softness in South America-U.S. leisure traffic, yet management indicated no material impact or "fundamental slowdown in demand".
  • Updated guidance projects year-end leverage at 1.4x, with liquidity above $4 billion, supporting future fleet investments and growth targets.
  • Premium revenue growth was described as "growing faster than capacity" and supported by enhanced passenger experience offerings and exclusive airport facilities.
  • Potential regulatory headwinds in Brazil, including new baggage and seat-selection rules, remain unresolved pending Senate and presidential action.
  • The call flagged the upcoming Investor Day in New York on December 9, 2025, with more guidance and disclosure expected then.

Industry glossary

  • RASK: Revenue per available seat kilometer, a measure of unit revenue in the airline industry.
  • CASK: Cost per available seat kilometer, a key unit cost measure for airlines, with ex-fuel indicating the figure before fuel expense.
  • EBITDAR: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, and aircraft rental, an operating profitability metric adjusted for airline sector specifics.
  • Load factor: The percentage of available seating capacity that is filled with passengers, an indicator of demand and network efficiency.
