Scholastic (SCHL) Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 6, 2026 at 1:37PM EST

DATE

Thursday, December 19, 2024 at 4:30 p.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer — Peter Warwick
  • Chief Financial Officer — Haji Glover
  • Chief Growth Officer — Jeffrey Mathews

TAKEAWAYS

  • Consolidated Revenue -- $544.6 million, down 3% in the second quarter, primarily due to timing-related factors in the Children's Book Publishing segment.
  • Operating Income -- $78.9 million in the second quarter, a decrease from $101.3 million in the prior-year period.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $108.7 million in the second quarter compared to $124 million in the prior-year period.
  • Net Income -- $52 million in the second quarter versus $76.9 million in the prior-year period.
  • Diluted EPS -- $1.82, down from $2.45 in the prior-year period.
  • Children’s Book Publishing and Distribution Segment Revenue -- $367 million, down 6%, reflecting trade publishing and book fair timing shifts.
  • Book Fairs Revenue -- $231 million, down 5%, with decreased fair count and revenue per fair, partly due to the late Thanksgiving holiday and hurricane impact in the U.S. South.
  • Book Clubs Revenue -- $33.2 million, up 2%, driven by higher revenue per sponsor and student per sponsor.
  • Trade Publishing Revenue -- $102.8 million compared to $117.9 million in the prior year.
  • Entertainment Segment Revenue -- $16.8 million, contributed by the 9 Story Media Group acquisition; segment operating loss of $3.9 million, including $2.3 million in increased amortization expense for intangible assets.
  • Education Solutions Segment Revenue -- $71.2 million, down 12%, reflecting lower spending on supplemental curriculum products and reduced state-sponsored program revenue.
  • International Segment Revenue -- $86.7 million, flat with prior year; excluding $1.9 million from currency gains, revenues declined $1.7 million, mainly driven by Australia retail softness.
  • Segment Operating Income (International) -- $7.1 million, slightly down from $8 million last year.
  • Unallocated Overhead Costs -- $25.9 million, up from $23.3 million, due to higher employee benefit costs.
  • Net Cash from Operating Activities -- $71.2 million, compared to $109.7 million a year ago, due to higher inventory purchases, increased interest, and lower customer remittance.
  • Free Cash Flow -- $42.4 million, down from $88.6 million, reflecting lower operating cash flow.
  • Revolver Credit Facility -- Upsized to $400 million, with $250 million drawn at quarter-end and maturity extended to November 2029.
  • Net Debt -- $120.8 million, compared to a net cash position of $107.7 million at fiscal 2024-end, mainly due to the 9 Story Media acquisition and shareholder returns.
  • Shareholder Returns -- 185,000 shares repurchased for $5 million; over $10 million returned including regular dividend.
  • Fiscal 2025 Guidance -- Revenue growth of 4%-6% and adjusted EBITDA of $140 million-$150 million reaffirmed.
  • Full-Year Free Cash Flow Guidance -- Expected between $20 million and $30 million, due to planned capital expenditures and larger working capital investments.
  • Strategic Cost Actions -- Discretionary, non-revenue expenses cut, hiring frozen in non-priority functions, and global departmental reorganizations implemented.
  • Key Product Launches -- Dog Man: Big Jim Begins released, debuting as the top bestseller in the U.S. and Canada; fifth Hunger Games book, Sunrise on the Reaping, slated for release next quarter.
  • New Digital and Literacy Products -- Explore ELA for grades 6-8 and Scholastic Knowledge Library for grades K-5 set to launch for the 2025-26 school year.
  • Entertainment Pipeline -- Goosebumps live action series season two set to air January 2025 on Disney+; 16 Children’s and Family Emmy nominations, including nine for Goosebumps.

SUMMARY

Scholastic Corporation (SCHL +0.33%) reported decreased revenues and operating profits, explicitly attributed by management to timing factors rather than underlying demand shortfalls. The company highlighted high-performing new releases, notably Dog Man: Big Jim Begins, which achieved number-one status across key markets and is expected to drive both frontlist and backlist sales. Management specifically identified the expanded credit facility and elevated net debt as mechanisms to fund acquisitions and seasonal working capital, while supporting both investment and shareholder returns. Looking ahead, leadership expressed confidence in delivering modest growth via rising school reading event counts and major trade publishing releases, underpinned by a reaffirmed outlook and cost discipline measures.

  • Chairman Warwick emphasized proactive supply chain risk mitigation, stating inventory needs are substantially locked in, limiting near-term exposure to changing U.S. trade policies.
  • The company indicated that most education policy and funding changes are occurring at the state and local level, minimizing near-term risk from federal shifts, with Warwick noting, "only about 13% to 14% of funding for education being done from federal funding."
  • Management cited delayed greenlights and curtailed content budgets across studios as temporary industry headwinds in the Entertainment segment, while underscoring long-term synergy potential from the 9 Story Media Group acquisition.
  • Growth investments include targeted new offerings for charter and independent schools, direct-to-family products, and digital content initiatives for YouTube and streaming platforms.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Adjusted EBITDA: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted to exclude one-time or non-recurring items for a clearer view of ongoing operational profitability.
  • Frontlist/Backlist: "Frontlist" refers to newly published titles; "backlist" consists of older titles that continue to sell after initial release.
  • Supplemental Curriculum Products: Instructional materials provided in addition to a school's core curriculum, often focused on skill development, enrichment, or remediation.
  • Trade Publishing: The business of publishing and distributing general-interest books to the retail market, distinct from educational or specialized segments.
  • School Reading Events (SRE): Scholastic's program involving school book fairs and similar events that promote and sell books directly within educational institutions.
