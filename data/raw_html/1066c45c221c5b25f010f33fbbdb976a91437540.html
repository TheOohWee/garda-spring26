TAKEAWAYS

  • Equipment rental revenue -- Increased 24% year over year, driven by the H&E acquisition, mega project activity, and growth in specialty solutions.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- Rose 19% compared to the prior year’s quarter, supported by higher rental revenue and a 53% increase in used equipment sales.
  • REBITDA (excluding used equipment sales) -- Up 17% with a margin of 45%, reflecting dilution from the acquired business’s lower margin profile.
  • Branch optimization completion -- 80% finished as of quarter end; expected to reach 100% in the next month, expanding co-located and specialty branches by about 25%.
  • Specialty location growth -- Approximately 50 new specialty branches added, increasing the specialty footprint by about 25%.
  • Fleet investment and disposals -- 2025 fleet expenditures held flat year over year, while disposals increased 67%, reflecting acquisition-driven realignment.
  • Proceeds from used equipment disposals -- $342 million in Q4 disposals generated realized proceeds equal to 44% of OEC, up from 41% in Q3 2025 (not all proceeds sources provided).
  • Free cash flow -- $521 million generated for the year, net of transaction costs.
  • Leverage ratio -- Pro forma leverage at 3.95x, projected to decline to 2x-3x by year-end 2027 as synergies accrue.
  • 2026 revenue guidance -- Rental revenue growth expected in the 13%-17% range.
  • 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance -- Targeted at $2.0 billion-$2.1 billion, reflecting 10%-16% growth.
  • 2026 free cash flow guidance -- Forecast at $400 million-$600 million.
  • 2026 net CapEx guidance -- Planned at approximately $650 million, with gross CapEx near $950 million and fleet disposals around $700 million.
  • Cost synergy targets -- $125 million in cost synergies forecast for 2026.
  • Revenue synergy targets -- Anticipated incremental revenue synergies of $100 million-$120 million for the full year.
  • Digital channel growth -- Digital revenue rose over 50% during the year, with hercrentals.com showing significant customer traction.
  • Telematics coverage -- 80% of eligible fleet equipped, providing broader utilization and performance data for customers through ProControl.
  • 2025 safety performance -- Over 97% “perfect days” across all operations and a total recordable incident rate below the 1.0 industry benchmark.
  • Mega project market share -- Captured a 10%-15% share of targeted mega projects, aiming for the upper end of this range as further opportunities ramp up.

SUMMARY

Herc Holdings (HRI 0.41%) reported significant topline and margin expansion, attributing the June H&E acquisition as a primary driver and emphasizing systematic progress in integration across network, fleet, and salesforce. Management indicated that branch optimization was 80% complete, with full completion expected in the following month, and highlighted the trajectory toward 200 specialty locations in early 2026. The capital plan maintains disciplined, flat year-over-year expenditures, increases specialty fleet allocation, and projects lower disposals, supporting improved capital efficiency for 2026. Leadership reaffirmed pro forma revenue acceleration in the back half of the year, underpinned by targeted cost and revenue synergies and growth in digital channels. Guidance points to double-digit rental revenue, EBITDA, and free cash flow growth, with leverage projected to revert to a 2x-3x band by 2027.

  • CEO Lawrence H. Silber stated, "we have completed 80% of the planned branch optimization which will be finished next month."
  • Lawrence H. Silber reported, "cost synergies are now tracking ahead of plan."
  • CEO Lawrence H. Silber said, "digital revenue grew by more than 50% last year with hercrentals.com giving our customers an easy way to reserve gear 24/7."
  • President Aaron D. Birnbaum noted, "Our specialty lines generated double-digit rental revenue growth in December."
  • During Q&A, management identified H&E integration, synergy capture, and mega project positioning as central to 2026 target attainment.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • OEC (Original Equipment Cost): The initial cost Herc paid for equipment, used to measure recovery on disposals and residual fleet value.
  • REBITDA: EBITDA excluding used equipment sales, focused on recurring rental profitability.
  • Dollar utilization: A key rental industry metric indicating equipment rental revenue as a percentage of OEC over a period.
  • Specialty fleet/solutions: High-margin, application-specific rental equipment and services—such as power generation, pumps, and climate control—often housed in dedicated branches.
