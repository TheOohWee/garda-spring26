Earnings Call Transcript

PCA (PKG) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Jan 26, 2026 at 12:17PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Thursday, October 23, 2025 at 9 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer — Mark W. Kowlzan
  • President — Thomas A. Hassfurther
  • Chief Financial Officer — Kent A. Pflederer

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Income (GAAP and Non-GAAP) -- GAAP net income was $227 million, or $2.51 per share; non-GAAP net income, excluding special items, was $247 million, or $2.73 per share.
  • Net Sales -- Net sales were $2.3 billion, up from $2.2 billion in the prior-year quarter.
  • EBITDA (Excluding Special Items) -- Company EBITDA reached $503 million, compared to $461 million in the same quarter a year ago.
  • Special Items Impact -- Special items, primarily related to the Greif Containerboard acquisition, reduced earnings per share by $0.22.
  • Greif Containerboard Acquisition -- The company completed the acquisition on September 2, including one month of results, which affected earnings per share by $0.11 after special items.
  • Packaging Segment EBITDA Margin -- Packaging segment's EBITDA margin improved to 23.1%, up from 22.2% in the comparable quarter and 22.6% in the previous quarter, based on EBITDA of $492 million and sales of $2.1 billion.
  • Corrugated Shipments (Legacy and Acquired) -- Total shipments per day rose 3.7% with the acquisition included; overall shipments increased 5.3%.
  • Legacy Corrugated Shipments Per Day -- Shipments per day declined 2.7% versus the record prior-year quarter, which followed an 11% increase from 2023.
  • Total Shipments (Legacy) -- Legacy plant shipments were down 1.1%, reflecting one extra workday this quarter.
  • Export Containerboard Volumes -- Export containerboard volumes decreased by 8,000 tons from the prior quarter, and 32,000 tons from the previous year.
  • Containerboard Production -- Production in the legacy system fell by 38,000 tons year over year, while increasing by 59,000 tons sequentially from the prior quarter.
  • Containerboard Inventory (Legacy System) -- Inventory increased by 15,000 tons in preparation for a fourth quarter outage.
  • Acquired Mill Production -- Newly acquired mills contributed 47,000 tons during the first month of ownership.
  • Paper Segment EBITDA Margin -- Paper segment reported EBITDA of $40 million on $161 million sales, for a margin of 24.9%, versus $43 million EBITDA and a 27.1% margin on $159 million sales last year.
  • Paper Segment Volumes -- Paper volumes decreased 1% compared to last year, but rose 10% from the prior quarter.
  • Cash Provided by Operations -- Operating cash flow reached an all-time quarterly high of $469 million.
  • Free Cash Flow -- After $192 million of CapEx, free cash flow reached a record $277 million.
  • Ending Cash Balance and Liquidity -- Quarter-end cash, including marketable securities, was $806 million, with total liquidity around $1.4 billion.
  • Capital Expenditure Forecast -- Full-year CapEx was revised downward to approximately $800 million, from a prior forecast of $840 million to $870 million, mainly due to timing of expenditures; capital plan remains unchanged.
  • Greif Purchase Accounting -- Preliminary Greif purchase accounting figures included $870 million for property, plant, and equipment, $530 million in intangibles, and $280 million in goodwill.
  • Depreciation and Amortization (Greif Assets) -- $12 million in Q3 depreciation/amortization was recorded for Greif assets, with annual run rate expected near $130 million.
  • Net Interest Expense (Acquisition Related) -- Annual net interest expense is expected to increase by $95 million, with $8 million added in Q3.
  • OCC Use Post-Acquisition -- Kent Pflederer said, "we typically ran around ... low 20 percentage furnish OCC. That's going to move up about 10% on the whole to 30-ish going forward [with Greif assets included]."
  • Guidance for Q4 EPS (Excluding Special Items) -- Management expects fourth quarter earnings of $2.40 per share, excluding special items.
  • Shutdown and Outage Expenses -- Fourth quarter legacy system outage expense will be $0.45 per share, including $0.29 more than in Q3; acquired business will add $0.02 per share in outage expense.
  • Synergy Target (Greif) -- Kent Pflederer said synergy projections remain at a $60 million run-rate after the second year, with Greif's historical EBITDA at about $240 million annually.

RISKS

  • Thomas Hassfurther cited "Actually, George, outside of a couple of end markets, our business has been very, very good. Those couple of markets that were -- that we've struggled -- it's not we've struggled. They've struggled in the marketplace. I mean, everybody has read about beef. That's a big segment for us. And the cattle herds are down to a 70-year low. So there's a lot of struggles going on there, and we even have the administration looking at other ways to solve that problem. And then the other area is the building materials. We all know what's happened with housing starts and where that stands. So those 2 segments have been a drag on us. Other than that, we've been very pleased with the results in all of our other sales segments.
  • Chairman Kowlzan indicated that "electricity rates are up 50% to 75%" at some facilities, resulting in sustained energy cost pressures with no expectation of near-term relief.
  • Legacy segment corrugated shipments per day decreased 2.7% compared to last year's record quarter, signaling competitive and demand challenges.
  • Export containerboard volumes dropped both sequentially and year over year, with third quarter volumes down by 32,000 tons from the previous year.

SUMMARY

PCA closed the Greif Containerboard acquisition on September 2, incorporating one month of acquired operations, which affected reported earnings. Management confirmed synergy targets for the Greif business remain at $60 million run-rate after year two, with historical EBITDA of $240 million, and cited rapid integration with immediate operational improvements at key acquired mills. Record cash from operations and free cash flow were achieved, supporting a downward revision of annual CapEx to $800 million, attributed primarily to timing. Company guidance for fourth quarter EPS, excluding special items, was set at $2.40, with management planning for elevated outage expenses and a full-quarter operational contribution from acquired assets.

  • Shipments in beef and building materials end markets, large segments for PCA, are under pressure due to historic beef herd lows and challenging housing starts, with management stating these trends could linger over the next several years.
  • Energy inflation, especially electricity, represents a major structural cost concern, with PCA accelerating self-generation projects aimed at reducing grid dependency at three mills within 2.5 years.
  • Company OCC (Old Corrugated Container) utilization is projected to increase by 10 percentage points system-wide due to the Greif integration, potentially altering cost dynamics and segment exposures.
  • Preparation for fourth quarter maintenance outages resulted in higher legacy system inventories and forward guidance for elevated expenses, but management expects continued improvement from Greif asset ramp-up and ongoing optimization of inventory and production strategies.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • OCC (Old Corrugated Container): Recycled containerboard material used as fiber furnish in paper and packaging production, whose share in total furnish impacts cost structure and operating flexibility.
  • CorrChoice: The former Greif converting division now integrated into PCA, specializing in corrugated products and sheet feeders.
