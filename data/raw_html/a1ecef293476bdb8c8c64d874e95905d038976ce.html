Takeaways

  • Total Revenue -- $726,000,000 for 2025, as stated by Frank Lee, attributed to growth across multiple product lines and the five-by-30 strategic plan.
  • EXPAREL Sales (Q4) -- $155,800,000, up from $147,700,000 in 2024, reflecting approximately 7% volume growth, partially offset by "a shift in buy mix and discounting from our third GPO going live," per Shawn M. Cross.
  • ZILRETTA Sales (Q4) -- $33,000,000, reported as "essentially flat versus 2024."
  • ioverao Sales (Q4) -- $7,000,000, an increase from $6,500,000 in 2024.
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin (Q4) -- 80%, up from 79% last year, attributed by Cross to "better-than-expected yields from both of our enhanced, larger scale 200-liter EXPAREL facilities."
  • Fourth Quarter Non-GAAP R&D Expense -- $34,400,000, compared to $22,000,000 last year, a rise due to a $5,000,000 upfront payment to AmacaThera and increased pipeline development expenses.
  • Fourth Quarter Non-GAAP SG&A Expense -- $91,900,000, versus $70,600,000 in 2024, with Cross noting "unanticipated costs associated with business development due diligence and litigation."
  • Year-End Cash and Investments -- $238,000,000 at reporting date, with ongoing "significant operating cash flow."
  • Share Repurchases -- $150,000,000 returned to shareholders during 2025, retiring approximately 2,000,000 shares; outstanding shares reduced to approximately 41,000,000 as of year-end.
  • EXPAREL Patent Runway -- Settlement with Fresenius secures U.S. exclusivity through 2039 and strengthens intellectual property estate to 21 patents across two families.
  • Payer Coverage -- EXPAREL now has 102,000,000 covered lives outside the surgical bundle reported at year-end, which Teehan identifies as a "notable shift in policy for some of the largest payers, including Aetna, Cigna, TRICARE, and Humana."
  • Five-by-30 Strategic Progress -- Lee specified progress on manufacturing efficiencies, five new pipeline programs, and five strategic partnerships, including J&J MedTech and LG Chem.
  • LG Chem Partnership -- Announced distribution agreement for EXPAREL in select Asia Pacific countries with "revenues from this agreement to begin in 2027 and to extend through the life of our patents in the 2040s."
  • 2026 Financial Guidance -- Projected total revenue between $745,000,000 and $770,000,000; EXPAREL sales guidance of $600,000,000 to $620,000,000; non-GAAP gross margins forecast at 77%-79%.
  • Pipeline Milestones (2026) -- Interim analysis planned for ZILRETTA trial in shoulder osteoarthritis, top-line results expected for ioverao spasticity study before year-end, and data from PCRX-201 Phase II ASCEND Part A to be reported near year-end.
  • NO PAIN Act Impact -- Teehan cited survey results: "82% view NO PAIN as important for advancing non-opioid stewardship. 92% believe NO PAIN is already contributing to reduced opioid prescribing."
  • GPO Contracting Effect -- Third GPO agreement resulted in "discounting" and "shift in buy mix," which equally affected revenue growth relative to volume growth sequentially from 2024.
  • Commercial Infrastructure -- ZILRETTA commercial expansion tripled reach in the U.S. via J&J MedTech partnership, with clear growth objectives incorporated into partner incentives.
  • Phase II ASCEND Study of PCRX-201 -- Enrollment of 49 patients completed ahead of schedule for Part A, with top-line safety and efficacy-trend data to be reported at year-end; Part B scheduled to enroll approximately 90 patients starting around midyear.

Summary

Pacira BioSciences (PCRX 0.13%) delivered record annual revenue and the highest gross margins in company history, underpinned by expanded payer coverage and renewed commercial momentum for EXPAREL. The LG Chem partnership opens long-term revenue potential in Asia Pacific, while the intellectual property settlement with Fresenius extends U.S. market exclusivity of EXPAREL to 2039 and further strengthens the company's patent estate. Full-year guidance details modest mid-single-digit top-line growth for 2026, supported by margin improvements and continued disciplined capital deployment, including continued share buybacks. Management highlighted upcoming clinical milestones for ZILRETTA, ioverao, and PCRX-201 as pivotal for pipeline visibility in the near to medium term.

  • The NO PAIN Act contributed to widespread updates in reimbursement practice, according to survey data cited by Teehan, which may accelerate provider adoption trends.
  • Management cited a completed volume-limited settlement with Fresenius that materially lengthens EXPAREL's U.S. exclusivity runway and establishes 21 supporting patents, substantially evolving its IP position from a single patent at the initial paragraph IV process.
  • A mix shift and discounting from a third GPO agreement led to slower revenue growth compared to volume for EXPAREL, with management expecting this delta to narrow post-anniversary of the GPO deal mid-2026.
  • For ZILRETTA, management attributed flat performance to commercial restructuring, with an assertive outlook linked to the full activation of the J&J MedTech sales force and targeted account selection for the upcoming year.
  • Development for PCRX-201 remains primarily focused on safety, though Part A interim data may inform future efficacy endpoints and program acceleration; Part B is set to start enrolling with a target of 90 patients and will use commercially viable product.

Industry glossary

  • NO PAIN Act: U.S. federal legislation to expand reimbursement for non-opioid pain management alternatives, impacting payment policy for drugs such as EXPAREL.
  • GPO: Group Purchasing Organization—a third-party that negotiates pricing and contracting for healthcare providers, influencing buy mix and revenue realization.
  • WOMAC: Western Ontario and McMaster Universities Osteoarthritis Index, a common endpoint in clinical trials assessing pain, stiffness, and physical function.
  • KOOS: Knee Injury and Osteoarthritis Outcome Score, a clinical measure for function and symptoms in musculoskeletal trials.
  • IGOR registry: Pacira BioSciences, Inc.'s real-world registry collecting outcome data on osteoarthritis and pain management products.
  • HCAD: Hydrophobic cationic ampholytic drug delivery platform proprietary to Pacira BioSciences, Inc.
  • HKF platform: Pacira BioSciences, Inc.'s proprietary local gene therapy drug delivery platform referenced in context of PCRX-201 development.
  • RMAT: Regenerative Medicine Advanced Therapy designation by FDA, facilitating expedited development and review of novel therapeutics such as PCRX-201.
