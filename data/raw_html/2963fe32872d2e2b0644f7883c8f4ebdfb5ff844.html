TAKEAWAYS

  • Total Revenue -- $756.4 million for the quarter, an increase of 32.2%, marking a quarterly record.
  • Ex-OTC Revenue -- $641.9 million, up 12.2%, and also a quarterly record excluding the OTC acquisition.
  • Full-Year Revenue -- Approaching $3.0 billion, growing by 30%.
  • EPS Growth -- GAAP EPS increased 24%, and adjusted earnings per share rose 19% to $1.18 per share for the year.
  • Brokerage Revenue -- $694.6 million, up 34.6%, driven by all asset classes.
  • ECS Revenue -- $257.5 million, an increase of 92%, attributed to the OTC acquisition and organic growth; ex-OTC, ECS grew 10%.
  • Rates Revenue -- $197.4 million, increasing 16.4%, with strong growth in G10, emerging markets, and repo products.
  • Foreign Exchange Revenue -- $102.8 million, up 9.8%, supported by growth in emerging market currencies and FX forwards.
  • Credit Revenue -- $64.3 million, rising 3%, attributed to higher emerging markets and European credit volumes.
  • Equities Revenue -- $72.7 million, up 29%, reflecting volatility and market share gains.
  • Data, Network, and Post-Trade Revenue -- $36.7 million, up 14.2% excluding Capitalab; including Capitalab, growth was 12.5%.
  • Fenics Revenue -- $163.9 million, up 15.4%, a quarterly record; Fenics Markets contributed $136.7 million, a 15.1% gain.
  • FMX UST Market Share -- Rose to a record 39% in the quarter (from 37% last quarter, and 30% last year), with ADV of $58.7 billion, a 12% increase.
  • FMX Futures ADV and Open Interest -- ADV grew 8%, and open interest increased 297% from the previous quarter; January ADV reached approximately 40,000 contracts, with approximately 200,000 contracts in open interest.
  • FMX FX ADV -- Increased 40% to a record $15.5 billion.
  • Portfolio Match ADV -- Grew 68%, and now represents nearly 20% of the U.S. credit sweep market.
  • Lucera Revenue -- Expanded 24.1% due to FX and rates demand, and new client onboarding.
  • Regional Revenue Breakdown -- EMEA up 39.2%, Americas up 25.7%, and Asia-Pacific up 24.2%.
  • Adjusted Pretax Earnings -- $161.3 million, up 24.5%; margin was 21.3% (23.2% ex-OTC, 23.7% ex-OTC and ex-FX impact).
  • Post-tax Adjusted Earnings -- $149.6 million, up 21.1%; adjusted earnings per share of $0.31.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $190.6 million, down 0.8% due to cost reduction charges.
  • GAAP Income from Operations -- Declined 8% to $25.0 million, impacted by $54.8 million in cost reduction charges.
  • Compensation and Employee Benefits -- GAAP increased 71.8%, adjusted up 40.1%, reflecting acquisition, commissions, and U.S. dollar weakness.
  • Non-Compensation Expenses -- GAAP increased 25.5%, adjusted up 27.1%; ex-OTC, up 13.5% (GAAP), and 14.7% (adjusted).
  • Cost Reduction Program -- First phase completed, targeting $25 million in annualized savings for 2026.
  • KACE Business Sale -- Sold for up to $119 million, or 28 times post-tax profit, at year-end.
  • Liquidity -- $979.1 million at year-end, up from $897.8 million the prior year.
  • Share Count -- Fully diluted weighted average for adjusted earnings was 490.4 million, down 0.8% from 2025, and one percent from a year ago.
  • 2026 Guidance (Q1 Revenue) -- $860 million to $920 million (up from $664.2 million), implying 34% growth at the midpoint; ex-OTC, Q1 revenue growth guidance midpoint is 15%.
  • 2026 Guidance (Q1 Pretax Adjusted Earnings) -- $202 million to $222 million (up from $160.2 million), midpoint representing over 32% growth.
  • Tax Rate Guidance -- Adjusted earnings tax rate expected between 11% and 14% in 2026.

SUMMARY

The record revenue performance was fueled by both organic growth and the OTC acquisition, with notable expansion in ECS, rates, and equities. FMX platforms reported milestone gains in market share and volumes, with FMX futures ADV surpassing one percent market share for the first time in January, and UST market share doubling over thirteen quarters. Portfolio optimization continued, highlighted by strategic business divestitures, and the completion of a cost reduction phase, which is expected to further improve margins in 2026. Regional results showed particularly pronounced strength in EMEA, and the elevated liquidity position provides flexibility for further expansion or investment. Management signaled ongoing investment in Fenics' high-growth segments, with plans to launch additional fixed income products, and reaffirmed commitment to "focus on those higher-growth things" within the portfolio.

  • Windeatt said, "We have become the largest ECS broker globally, diversified our customer base, and introduced competition to the U.S. interest rate futures market," indicating structural market positioning shifts benefiting BGC.
  • Abularrage stated that ECS business growth was supported by "a proliferation of new players," and market share outperformance driven by targeted asset class investments, confirming both cyclical and structural growth elements.
  • The FMX futures platform is approaching a fee structure change, with new fees beginning "the beginning of this summer" for early adopters, potentially shifting monetization of recent volume growth.
  • Management consistently highlighted the impact of volatility and higher trading volumes for both listed and OTC segments, with Aubin noting, "volatility remains the best friend of BGC business."
  • Management maintained an open stance on further asset sales, stating any future divestiture decisions will be "all about shareholder value," indicating a continued focus on portfolio optimization.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • ECS (Energy, Commodities, and Shipping): BGC's division covering brokerage across physical and derivative energy, commodity, and shipping products.
  • FMX: BGC's multi-asset exchange platform for electronic trading and clearing, including both U.S. Treasury (UST) and SOFR futures.
  • SOFR: Secured Overnight Financing Rate-based interest rate futures contracts, used as a benchmark in U.S. financial markets.
  • Fenics: BGC's electronic brokerage, data, and network infrastructure business line.
  • OTC (OTC Global Holdings): Global energy and commodity derivatives brokerage, acquired and integrated into BGC.
  • ADV (Average Daily Volume): The average number of contracts or quantity traded per day over a given period.
  • Capitalab: Post-trade optimization and compression business divested by BGC during 2024.
  • KACE: Risk analytics and trade automation business sold by BGC at the end of 2025.
  • Portfolio Match: BGC's electronic platform for matching large credit trades, notably in the U.S. credit sweep market.
  • Lucera: BGC’s network and trading infrastructure services provider focused on electronic trading connectivity.
