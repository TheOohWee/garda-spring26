Turning to today's press release, our Gemini II TTFT benchmarks discussed preliminary results showing a three-second time to first token for a multimodal model at the edge using video and text inputs at approximately 30 watts of system power. TTFT is how long it takes the system to produce the first response, which is critical for drones and unmanned systems. The threshold for a useful TTFT in video surveillance to ensure nothing is missed is three seconds.
That means we are sampling the video image every three seconds. If the TTFT is ten seconds, it takes too long, so the surveillance video could miss something. In preparation for the Sentinel demo, we will continue improving TTFT over the next five months to further reduce Gemini II's first time to response. What's exciting for GSI about these Gemini II preliminary benchmark results is that they demonstrate what compute and memory can provide for physical AI: faster time to first token and materially lower chip power. This will help enable a broader set of viable, cost-effective deployments.
