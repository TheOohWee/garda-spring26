For the 2025 second quarter, which we refer to as the 2025 quarter, total revenues were $547.5 million compared to $593.4 million in the second quarter of 2024, which we refer to as the 2024 quarter. Compared to the sequential quarter, total revenues increased $7 million due primarily to increased coal sales volumes. Driven by the continued roll-off of higher-priced legacy contracts from the 2022 energy crisis, and revenue mix with a higher proportion of Illinois Basin tons in the 2025 quarter. Total coal inventory at quarter-end was 1.2 million tons, 200,000 tons lower than the sequential quarter.

Coal sales volumes in Appalachia were down 16.8% and 0.7% compared to the 2024 and sequential quarters, due to continued challenging mining conditions at Tunnel Ridge, which led to lower recoveries. Tunnel Ridge did start its longwall move to a new section of the mine late in the 2025 quarter. As a result, we expect second-half results from Appalachia to be much better than the first half. In Appalachia, despite the challenging conditions at Tunnel Ridge, segment adjusted EBITDA expense per ton continued its improvement relative to recent quarters, declining 5.8% sequentially.
