Takeaways
Revenue
-- $29.8 million, with double-digit year-over-year growth when excluding the Grid Expertise customer.
Drone revenue outlook
-- Management raised full-year drone revenue expectations to $8 million-$12 million from the prior $5 million-$10 million range due to accelerating momentum.
Non-GAAP EPS
-- $0.04, remaining within previously communicated guidance.
GAAP gross margin

Operating expenses
-- GAAP OpEx was $14 million, down approximately 6% sequentially and 9% compared to $15.4 million the prior year.
Non-GAAP net income
-- $1.6 million, equating to $0.04 per share, up from $1.5 million in the previous quarter.
Operating cash flow
-- Nearly $2.2 million of positive operating cash flow generated during the period.
Debt reduction
-- $1 million was paid down, leaving $9.7 million outstanding as of Dec. 31, 2025, compared to $14.7 million a year ago.
Cash and equivalents
-- $23 million, representing an $800,000 increase sequentially.
Inventory
-- Net inventories stood at $27.1 million, up from $26.8 million sequentially and down from $29.1 million year over year.
Guidance
-- For the upcoming quarter, revenue guidance is $28.5 million-$32.5 million with non-GAAP EPS expected at $0.03-$0.04.
Software and services mix
-- Accounted for approximately 6% of total revenue over the last 12 months, with management forecasting the mix may more than double over the mid-term.
Average selling price (ASP) for drone solutions
-- Currently in the $400-$500 range per module for Group 2 drones, with higher ASPs anticipated for turnkey system offerings.
Customer base
-- Over 15 OEMs engaged for drone programs, with expanded partnerships including Red CAT, Flightwave, and Trillium Engineering.
Major U.S. mobile network operator deployment
-- Revenue continues to be recognized from a key critical infrastructure monitoring program, described as foundational for recurring revenue strategy.
Growth in embedded compute and infrastructure switches
-- Cited as primary drivers of year-over-year growth apart from drones.
IoT systems sequential decline
-- Revenue from IoT box products declined quarter over quarter due to seasonal government buying patterns and the near end of a major deployment.
Operating expense outlook
-- Management indicated near-term quarterly GAAP OpEx should range from $11.8 million to $12.3 million.
Supply chain
-- Management acknowledged industry-wide memory shortages and rising prices but stated they "don't see a big issue in the short term to even the midterm."
Summary

Management signaled further expansion in software and services, expecting recurring revenue to become a larger portion of total sales. The company cited diversification across drones, critical infrastructure, and embedded solutions, describing a unified edge AI platform strategy intended to drive operating leverage and customer stickiness.
Management said drone revenue could reach 15%-20% of total revenue in fiscal 2027, implying $20 million-$30 million, and described a multi-year federal drone program opportunity with an estimated 300,000 units over 18 months.

Management noted product ASP and gross margin expansion opportunities tied to platform migration and deeper solution integration, with longer-term gross margin uplift expected as the software mix grows.
The call also referenced U.S. policy changes, such as bans on new Chinese drones and federal budget allocations, accelerating demand for domestic solutions, which management believes increases their addressable opportunities.
Industry glossary
OEM
: Original Equipment Manufacturer; a company producing components or products used in another firm's end products.
SWaP
: Size, Weight, Power; key design criteria in drone and defense systems indicating efficiency and suitability for specific use cases.
SOM
: System on Module; a compact module that integrates a complete computing system, enabling rapid deployment of embedded solutions.
MNO
: Mobile Network Operator; a telecommunications company providing wireless communications services and associated infrastructure.
DFR
: Drone as First Responder; category where drones are used in real-time public safety or emergency response roles.
ASP
: Average Selling Price; the average unit price a company receives for products sold in a category.
ARR
: Annual Recurring Revenue; a measure of predictable revenue expected from ongoing contractual service agreements, typically in SaaS models.
