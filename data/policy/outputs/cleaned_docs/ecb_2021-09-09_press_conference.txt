Based on a joint assessment of financing conditions and the inflation outlook, the Governing Council judges that favourable financing conditions can be maintained with a moderately lower pace of net asset purchases under the pandemic emergency purchase programme (PEPP) than in the previous two quarters. We stand ready to adjust all of our instruments, as appropriate, to ensure that inflation stabilises at our two per cent target over the medium term. The current rise in inflation is expected to be largely temporary and underlying price pressures will build up only gradually. We expect the economy to rebound firmly over the medium term. The new staff projections foresee annual inflation at 2.2 per cent in 2021, 1.7 per cent in 2022 and 1.5 per cent in 2023, being revised up compared with the previous projections in June. The recovery of growth and inflation still depends on favourable financing conditions for all sectors of the economy. We have not discussed what comes next, and this is something for which we will prepare in the months to come. The day when PEPP is over will presumably indicate that financing conditions are favourable, and that the economy will have recovered in such a way that the downward impact of the pandemic on our inflation outlook has been resorbed. We believe that we can maintain those favourable financing conditions with a moderately lower pace of purchase. The inflation outlook has been revised slightly upwards, but in the medium term inflation is foreseen to remain well below our two per cent target. We are still targeting our 2 per cent, which is now clear and straightforward, as a result of our strategy review.
