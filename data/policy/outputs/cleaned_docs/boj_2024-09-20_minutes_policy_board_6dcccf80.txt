Members pointed out that there was a risk of inflation rising again due to factors such as prolonged geopolitical issues. Members agreed that downside risks to economic activity also warranted attention, particularly with regard to Germany, over the possibility of a structural decline in industrial strength and a protracted high youth unemployment rate. Members shared the view that, despite government support, the pace of improvement in the Chinese economy had slowed, with continued downward pressure from adjustments in the real estate market. Members shared the recognition that Japan's economy was likely to keep growing at a pace above its potential growth rate. One of these members was of the view that various indicators released since the previous meeting had been generally as expected or slightly stronger. The member then added that the current situation showed no signs that the Bank's policy changes so far would have a negative impact on economic and financial developments. Members shared the recognition that corporate profits had improved. Members agreed that it was necessary to monitor developments in financial markets and carefully assess how they would affect the outlook and risks for Japan's economic activity and prices. Members agreed that financial conditions in Japan had been accommodative. Members shared the basic thinking that if the outlook for economic activity and prices was realized, the Bank would accordingly continue to raise the policy interest rate and adjust the degree of monetary accommodation. Many members expressed the view that, in making policy decisions, it was important for the Bank to carefully assess not only developments in financial markets at home and abroad but also the factors underlying these developments. With regard to future communication, one member expressed the view that the Bank would need to communicate its policy stance and other factors to markets more carefully when conducting further policy interest rate hikes.
