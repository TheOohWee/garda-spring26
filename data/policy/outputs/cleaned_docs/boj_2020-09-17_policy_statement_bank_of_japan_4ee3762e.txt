To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, we will conduct purchases of long-term government bonds without setting an upper limit on the necessary amount. In doing so, the interest rate may fluctuate to some extent depending on economic and price conditions. We will continue to support corporate financing and maintain stability in the financial markets through (1) the special program for funding support in response to COVID-19, (2) ample supply of yen and foreign currency without setting upper limits through government bond purchases and dollar operations, and (3) active purchases of ETFs and J-REITs. We will closely monitor the impact of COVID-19 and, if necessary, will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. In the event of a rapid rise in interest rates, we will promptly and appropriately conduct government bond purchases. In terms of prices, the year-on-year change in consumer prices (excluding fresh food) is around 0% due to the impact of past declines in oil prices. As risk factors, there is a high degree of uncertainty regarding the outcome of the COVID-19 pandemic and the extent of its impact on both domestic and foreign economies. Corporate earnings and business sentiment have deteriorated, and capital investment is on a declining trend. Looking at the employment and income environment, weak movements are observed amid the ongoing impact of the pandemic. Personal consumption, particularly in services such as dining and accommodation, remains at a low level, but overall it is gradually recovering. Public investment continues to show a gradual increase.
