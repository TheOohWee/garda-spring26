The Bank will conduct purchases of long-term government bonds to keep the 10-year government bond yield around 0%. In the event of a rapid rise in interest rates, the Bank will promptly and appropriately conduct bond purchases. Member Harada opposed the decision, stating that allowing long-term interest rates to fluctuate to some extent is too ambiguous as a monetary market adjustment policy that should be decided by the Policy Board. Member Kataoka opposed the decision, arguing that it would be desirable to strengthen monetary easing by lowering the short-term policy interest rate.
