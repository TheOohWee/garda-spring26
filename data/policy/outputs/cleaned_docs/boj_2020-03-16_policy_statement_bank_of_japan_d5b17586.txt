Due to the impact of the spread of the novel coronavirus, uncertainty in the global economy has increased, and unstable movements continue in domestic and international financial capital markets. Therefore, at today's Policy Board and Monetary Policy Meeting, the following decisions were made. Implementation of more abundant fund supply Introduction of special operations for corporate financial support related to the novel coronavirus (unanimous agreement) A new operation will be introduced to supply funds at a zero percent interest rate for up to one year, secured by private corporate debt (approximately 8 trillion yen as of the end of February 2020). Increase in CP and corporate bond purchases (unanimous agreement) An additional purchase limit of 2 trillion yen will be established for CP and corporate bonds, with approximately 3.2 trillion yen for CP and approximately 4.2 trillion yen for corporate bonds as the upper limit for purchases. Active purchases of ETFs and J-REITs (unanimous agreement) For ETFs and J-REITs, active purchases will be made at a pace corresponding to an increase in balance of approximately 12 trillion yen and 180 billion yen per year, respectively, as an upper limit. Regarding the monetary market adjustment policy until the next monetary policy meeting, the previous policy will be maintained under the operation of short and long-term interest rates (yield curve control) (7 in favor, 2 against). In the event of a rapid rise in interest rates, government bond purchases will be implemented swiftly and appropriately. The economy of our country has recently shown weak movements due to the impact of the spread of the novel coronavirus. The year-on-year consumer price index (excluding fresh food) is in the low 0% range. As for risk factors, there is significant uncertainty regarding the outcome of the spread of the novel coronavirus and the magnitude and duration of its impact on the domestic and international economy.
