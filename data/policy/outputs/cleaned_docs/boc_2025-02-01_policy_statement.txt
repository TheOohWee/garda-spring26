Bank Rate reduced to 4.5% - February At its meeting ending on February 2025, the MPC voted by a majority of 7-2 to reduce Bank Rate by 0.25 percentage points, to 4.5%. In support of returning inflation sustainably to the 2% target, the Committee judges that there has been sufficient progress on disinflation in domestic prices and wages to reduce Bank Rate to 4.5% at this meeting. The Committee will continue to monitor closely the risks of inflation persistence and what the evolving evidence may reveal about the balance between aggregate supply and demand in the economy. CPI inflation was 2.5% in 2024 Q4. GDP growth has been weaker than expected at the time of the November Monetary Policy Report, and indicators of business and consumer confidence have declined. The labour market has continued to ease and is judged to be broadly in balance. The Committee will decide the appropriate degree of monetary policy restrictiveness at each meeting. Two members preferred to reduce Bank Rate by 0.5 percentage points, to 4.25%.
