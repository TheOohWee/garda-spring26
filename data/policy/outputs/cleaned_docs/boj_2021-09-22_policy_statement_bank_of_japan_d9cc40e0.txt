To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, we will conduct purchases of long-term government bonds without setting an upper limit on the necessary amount. In addition, we have decided on the details of the funding supply operation to support climate change response, which was outlined in the previous monetary policy meeting (unanimous agreement). The economy of our country continues to be in a difficult state due to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic both domestically and internationally, but is showing signs of recovery overall. Looking at the employment and income environment, weak movements continue due to the impact of the pandemic. We will continue to support corporate financing and maintain stability in the financial markets through (1) the special program for COVID-19 response funding support, (2) abundant supply of yen and foreign currency without setting limits through government bond purchases and dollar operations, and (3) purchases of ETFs and J-REITs with annual increase limits of approximately 12 trillion yen and 180 billion yen, respectively. For the time being, we will closely monitor the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic and, if necessary, will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. Regarding the policy interest rate, we assume that it will remain at the current level of short- and long-term interest rates or lower. In favor: Members Kuroda, Amamiya, Wakabayashi, Suzuki, Adachi, Nakamura, Noguchi, Nakagawa. Opposed: Member Kataoka. Member Kataoka opposed, stating that it would be desirable to strengthen monetary easing by lowering short- and long-term interest rates to support positive capital investment by companies in the post-COVID era.
