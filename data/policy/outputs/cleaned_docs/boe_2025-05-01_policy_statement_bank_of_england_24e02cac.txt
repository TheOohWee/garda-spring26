Bank Rate reduced to 4.25% - May At its meeting ending on May 2025, the MPC voted by a majority of 5-4 to reduce Bank Rate by 0.25 percentage points, to 4.25%. The Committee remains focused on returning CPI inflation sustainably to target in the medium term. Progress on disinflation in domestic price and wage pressures is generally continuing. Underlying UK GDP growth is judged to have slowed since the middle of 2024, and the labour market has continued to loosen. Inflation is expected to fall back thereafter. The Committee will continue to monitor closely the risks of inflation persistence and what the evidence may reveal about the balance between aggregate supply and demand in the economy. The Committee discussed the outlook for a range of nominal indicators. The Committee would remain sensitive to heightened unpredictability in the economic environment and would continue to update its assessment of risks. Five members voted to reduce Bank Rate to 4.25%. Two members preferred to hold Bank Rate at 4.5%. Two members preferred to reduce Bank Rate by 0.5 percentage points, to 4%.
