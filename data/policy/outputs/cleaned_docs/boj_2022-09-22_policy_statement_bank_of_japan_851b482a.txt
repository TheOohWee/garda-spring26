The proper loans for small and medium-sized enterprises related to the response to the infectious disease will be extended for six months, ending at the end of March 2023. During this period, monthly funding for three-month terms will be implemented. The institutional loans for small and medium-sized enterprises related to the response to the infectious disease will be extended for three months, ending at the end of December 2022. During this period, monthly funding for three-month terms will be implemented. In order to support the funding of small and medium-sized enterprises even after the expiration of the above operations and to respond to a wider range of funding needs, the 'common collateral funding operations' that provide funds backed by a wide range of collateral will be implemented without a monetary limit (this will change from the next scheduled implementation on September 27). The monetary market adjustment policy until the next monetary policy decision meeting will be as follows. Short-term interest rate: A negative interest rate of -0.1% will be applied. Long-term interest rate: To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, necessary amounts of long-term government bonds will be purchased without setting an upper limit. To achieve the above monetary market adjustment policy, limit price operations for the 10-year government bond yield will be conducted at a yield of 0.25% on every business day, except in cases where bidding is clearly not expected. Regarding the purchase of assets other than long-term government bonds, the following will apply. For ETFs and J-REITs, purchases will be made as needed, with an upper limit of approximately 12 trillion yen and 180 billion yen per year, respectively, for the pace of balance increase. For CPs and corporate bonds, purchases will be made at a pace similar to that before the spread of the infectious disease, gradually returning the purchase balance to pre-infection levels (approximately 2 trillion yen for CPs and approximately 3 trillion yen for corporate bonds). The economy of our country is recovering, despite the impact of rising resource prices, under the circumstances where the suppression of the novel coronavirus infection and economic activities are progressing. The overseas economy is generally recovering slowly, but there are signs of a slowdown, particularly in advanced countries. Exports and industrial production are increasing as a trend, as the impact of supply constraints is easing. Corporate profits are generally at a high level. Under these circumstances, capital investment is recovering, although some sectors show weakness. The employment and income environment shows some weakness in certain areas, but overall it is gradually improving. Personal consumption is gradually increasing, despite being affected by the infectious disease. Housing investment is showing weak movements. Public investment is moving within a flat range. The financial environment in our country is generally relaxed, although some tightness remains in corporate funding. In terms of prices, the year-on-year consumer price index (excluding fresh food) is in the late 2% range due to rising prices of energy, food, and durable goods. In addition, the expected inflation rate is rising. Looking ahead at the economy of our country, it is expected to recover despite downward pressure from rising resource prices due to the situation in Ukraine, as the impacts of the novel coronavirus infection and supply constraints ease. After that, it is expected to continue growing above the potential growth rate as the positive circulation mechanism from income to expenditure gradually strengthens. The year-on-year consumer price index (excluding fresh food) is expected to increase due to rising prices of energy, food, and durable goods until the end of this year, and then the positive contribution from these factors is expected to diminish, leading to a reduction in the positive range. During this period, as the macroeconomic supply-demand gap improves and the medium- to long-term expected inflation rate and wage growth rate increase, the underlying inflationary pressure is expected to rise. Looking at risk factors, uncertainty surrounding the economy of our country remains extremely high, due to the ongoing trends and impacts of domestic and foreign infectious diseases, developments in the situation in Ukraine, resource prices, and overseas economic and price trends. Under these circumstances, it is necessary to closely monitor the trends in the financial and foreign exchange markets and their impact on our country's economy and prices. For the time being, we will closely monitor the impact of the novel coronavirus infection, strive to support the funding of businesses, and maintain stability in the financial markets, and if necessary, we will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. Regarding the policy interest rate, it is assumed that it will remain at the current level of short- and long-term interest rates or below that level.
