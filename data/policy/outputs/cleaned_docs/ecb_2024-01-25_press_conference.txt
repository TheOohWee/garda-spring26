The Governing Council today decided to keep the three key ECB interest rates unchanged. We are determined to ensure that inflation returns to our two per cent medium-term target in a timely manner. Our future decisions will ensure that our policy rates will be set at sufficiently restrictive levels for as long as necessary. We will continue to follow a data-dependent approach to determining the appropriate level and duration of restriction. The euro area economy is likely to have stagnated in the final quarter of 2023. Inflation rose to 2.9 per cent in December as some of the past fiscal measures to cushion the impact of high energy prices dropped out of the annual inflation rate. Inflation is expected to ease further over the course of this year as the effects of past energy shocks, supply bottlenecks and the post-pandemic reopening of the economy fade. The risks to economic growth remain tilted to the downside. Upside risks to inflation include the heightened geopolitical tensions, especially in the Middle East. High borrowing rates, with the associated cutbacks in investment plans and house purchases, led to a further drop in credit demand in the fourth quarter. We stand ready to adjust all of our instruments within our mandate to ensure that inflation returns to our medium-term target. The consensus around the table of the Governing Council was that it was premature to discuss rate cuts.
