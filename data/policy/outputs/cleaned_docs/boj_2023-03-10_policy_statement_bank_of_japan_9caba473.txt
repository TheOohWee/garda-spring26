Short-term interest rate: A negative interest rate of -0.1% will be applied. Long-term interest rate: To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, purchases of long-term government bonds will be made without setting an upper limit on the necessary amount. The fluctuation range of long-term interest rates will be set at around ±0.5%, and limit operations at a yield of 0.5% for 10-year government bonds will be conducted every business day, except in cases where bids are clearly not expected. To promote the formation of a yield curve consistent with the above monetary market adjustment policy, large-scale government bond purchases will continue, and flexibly increase the purchase amounts and limit operations at each maturity. Regarding the purchase of assets other than long-term government bonds, the following will apply. For ETFs and J-REITs, purchases will be made as needed, with an upper limit on the pace of balance increase of approximately 12 trillion yen per year for ETFs and approximately 180 billion yen per year for J-REITs. Commercial paper (CP) will maintain a balance of approximately 2 trillion yen. Corporate bonds will be purchased at a pace similar to that before the pandemic, gradually returning the purchase balance to the level before the pandemic (approximately 3 trillion yen). However, adjustments to the purchase balance of corporate bonds will be made with sufficient consideration of the issuance environment for corporate bonds. The economy of our country is recovering, despite the impact of high resource prices, as the suppression of COVID-19 and economic activities are progressing together. The overseas economy is experiencing a slowdown in its recovery pace. Despite these influences, exports and industrial production are moving within a flat range, supported by the easing of supply constraints. Corporate profits are generally at a high level, and capital investment is gradually increasing. The employment and income environment is gradually improving overall. Personal consumption is gradually increasing, despite the impact of rising prices, as the effects of the pandemic ease. Housing investment is showing weak movements. Public investment is moving within a flat range. The financial environment in our country is generally accommodative, although some tightness remains in corporate financing. In terms of prices, the year-on-year consumer price index (excluding fresh food) is around 4% due to rising prices of energy, food, and durable goods. In addition, the expected inflation rate is rising. Looking ahead at the economy of our country, it is expected to recover despite downward pressure from high resource prices and a slowdown in the overseas economy, as the impacts of COVID-19 and supply constraints ease. After that, it is expected to continue growing above the potential growth rate as the positive circulation mechanism from income to expenditure gradually strengthens. The year-on-year consumer price index (excluding fresh food) is expected to narrow its positive range by the middle of next fiscal year due to the effects of government economic measures to lower energy prices, as well as the diminishing impact of price pass-through stemming from rising import prices. After that, as the macroeconomic supply-demand gap improves and the medium- to long-term expected inflation rate and wage growth rate also rise, it is expected that the positive range will gradually expand again due to the rebound effect of the government economic measures to lower energy prices. Looking at risk factors, uncertainty surrounding the economy of our country remains extremely high, including overseas economic and price trends, developments in the situation in Ukraine, trends in resource prices, and the trends and impacts of domestic and foreign infectious diseases. Under these circumstances, it is necessary to closely monitor the trends in financial and foreign exchange markets and their impact on our country's economy and prices. For the time being, we will closely monitor the impact of COVID-19, strive to support corporate financing and maintain stability in financial markets, and if necessary, take additional monetary easing measures without hesitation. Regarding the policy interest rate, it is assumed that it will remain at the current level of short- and long-term interest rates or below that level.
