The Governing Council today decided to lower the three key ECB interest rates by 25 basis points. We are determined to ensure that inflation returns to our two per cent medium-term target in a timely manner. We will keep policy rates sufficiently restrictive for as long as necessary to achieve this aim. We will continue to follow a data-dependent and meeting-by-meeting approach to determining the appropriate level and duration of restriction. Inflation is expected to rise in the coming months, before declining to target in the course of next year. The risks to economic growth remain tilted to the downside. We decided to cut all our rates by 25 basis points because we believe that the disinflationary process is well on track. There was a unanimous decision to cut rates by 25 basis points. We are going to continue doing exactly the same thing. We still have risks on both sides of our forecast, upside and downside, probably a bit more on one than the other. It is a fact that the economic activity has come in, on the basis of the elements that we have and the indicators that are available, a bit below what we had anticipated. We don't recalibrate, we calibrate, and this is really the process through which we go.
