The Governing Council today decided to keep the three key ECB interest rates unchanged. We are determined to ensure that inflation returns to our two per cent medium-term target in a timely manner. We will keep policy rates sufficiently restrictive for as long as necessary to achieve this aim. We will continue to follow a data-dependent and meeting-by-meeting approach to determining the appropriate level and duration of restriction. The risks to economic growth are tilted to the downside. Inflation could turn out higher than anticipated if wages or profits increase by more than expected. The policy rate cut in June has been transmitted smoothly to money market interest rates. Credit standards for loans remain tight. We are not pre-committing to a particular rate path. We welcome the European Commission’s recent guidance calling for EU Member States to strengthen fiscal sustainability.
