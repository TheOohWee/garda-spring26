The Governing Council today decided to keep the three key ECB interest rates unchanged. We are determined to ensure that inflation returns to our two per cent medium-term target in a timely manner. Our future decisions will ensure that our policy rates will be set at sufficiently restrictive levels for as long as necessary. We will continue to follow a data-dependent approach to determining the appropriate level and duration of restriction. The risks to economic growth remain tilted to the downside. Inflation edged down to 2.8 per cent in January and, according to Eurostat’s flash estimate, declined further to 2.6 per cent in February. We will have the Q4 numbers for CPE, for compensation per employee, tomorrow. There was also a very broad agreement around the fact that we will not change our views on one single data [point]. We are not sufficiently confident, and we clearly need more evidence, more data. We have decided to update significantly that statement and we have unanimously agreed on a new statement which is more specific.
