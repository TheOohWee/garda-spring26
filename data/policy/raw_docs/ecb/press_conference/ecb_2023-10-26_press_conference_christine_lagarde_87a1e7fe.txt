The Governing Council today decided to keep the three key ECB interest rates unchanged. We are determined to ensure that inflation returns to our two per cent medium-term target in a timely manner. Our future decisions will ensure that our policy rates will be set at sufficiently restrictive levels for as long as necessary. We will continue to follow a data-dependent approach to determining the appropriate level and duration of restriction. The euro area economy remains weak. Inflation dropped to 4.3 per cent in September, almost a full percentage point lower than its August level. The risks to economic growth remain tilted to the downside. Higher borrowing rates, with the associated cuts in investment plans and house purchases, led to a further sharp drop in credit demand in the third quarter. The annual growth rate of loans to firms has dropped sharply, from 2.2 per cent in July to 0.7 per cent in August and 0.2 per cent in September. We are monitoring the situation. We have to take all these factors into account to see how possible higher costs of energy would actually affect both GDP and inflation.
