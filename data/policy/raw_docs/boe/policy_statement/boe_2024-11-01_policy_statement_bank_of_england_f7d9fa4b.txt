Bank Rate reduced to 4.75% - November 2024 At its meeting ending on 6 November 2024, the MPC voted by a majority of 8–1 to reduce Bank Rate by 0.25 percentage points, to 4.75%. CPI inflation fell to 1.7% in September but is expected to increase to around 2½% by the end of the year as weakness in energy prices falls out of the annual comparison. The MPC judges that the labour market continues to loosen, although it appears relatively tight by historical standards. Monetary policy has been guided by the need to squeeze remaining inflationary pressures out of the economy to achieve the 2% target both in a timely manner and on a lasting basis. The combined effects of the measures announced in Autumn Budget 2024 are provisionally expected to boost the level of GDP by around ¾% at their peak in a year’s time, relative to the August projections. At this meeting, the Committee voted to reduce Bank Rate to 4.75%, reflecting the continued progress in disinflation. Based on the evolving evidence, a gradual approach to removing policy restraint remains appropriate. The Chair invited the Committee to vote on the proposition that: Bank Rate should be reduced by 0.25 percentage points, to 4.75%. Eight members (Andrew Bailey, Sarah Breeden, Swati Dhingra, Megan Greene, Clare Lombardelli, Huw Pill, Dave Ramsden and Alan Taylor) voted in favour of the proposition. Catherine L Mann voted against the proposition, preferring to maintain Bank Rate at 5%.
