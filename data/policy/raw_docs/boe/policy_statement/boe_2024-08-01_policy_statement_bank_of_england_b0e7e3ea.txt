Bank Rate reduced to 5% - August 2024 At its meeting ending on 31 July 2024, the MPC voted by a majority of 5–4 to reduce Bank Rate by 0.25 percentage points, to 5%. The Committee expects the fall in headline inflation, and normalisation in many indicators of inflation expectations, to continue to feed through to weaker pay and price-setting dynamics. However, there is a risk that inflationary pressures from second-round effects will prove more enduring in the medium term. The Committee continues to monitor closely the risks of inflation persistence and will decide the appropriate degree of monetary policy restrictiveness at each meeting. Five members preferred a 0.25 percentage point reduction in Bank Rate at this meeting. Four members preferred to maintain Bank Rate at 5.25% at this meeting. The impact from past external shocks has abated and there has been some progress in moderating risks of persistence in inflation. Monetary policy would need to continue to remain restrictive for sufficiently long until the risks to inflation returning sustainably to the 2% target in the medium term have dissipated further.
