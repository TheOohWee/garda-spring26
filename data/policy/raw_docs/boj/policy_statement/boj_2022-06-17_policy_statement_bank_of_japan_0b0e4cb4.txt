Short-term interest rate: Apply a negative interest rate of -0.1%. Long-term interest rate: To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, we will purchase the necessary amount of long-term government bonds without setting an upper limit. To implement the above monetary market adjustment policy, we will conduct fixed-rate operations for 10-year government bonds at a yield of 0.25% on every business day, except when bids are clearly not expected. Regarding the purchase of assets other than long-term government bonds, the following will apply. For ETFs and J-REITs, we will purchase as needed, with an upper limit of approximately 12 trillion yen per year and approximately 180 billion yen per year for balance increases. For CPs and corporate bonds, we will purchase at a pace similar to that before the spread of the infection, gradually returning the purchase balance to pre-infection levels (approximately 2 trillion yen for CPs and approximately 3 trillion yen for corporate bonds). The economy of our country shows some signs of weakness due to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic and rising resource prices, but the underlying trend is recovering. The overseas economy shows some signs of weakness, but overall it is recovering. Exports and industrial production continue to increase on a trend basis, but recently, the impact of supply constraints has been strengthening. Business sentiment has recently plateaued due to the impact of supply constraints and rising resource prices. Corporate profits are generally at a high level. Capital investment is recovering, although some sectors show weakness. The employment and income environment shows some signs of improvement in parts, but overall remains weak. Personal consumption is recovering, mainly in service consumption, as the impact of the pandemic eases. Housing investment is moving within a flat range. Public investment is showing weak movements. The financial environment in our country remains accommodative overall, although some companies still face tight funding conditions. In terms of prices, the year-on-year consumer price index (excluding fresh food) is around 2%, mainly due to rising energy and food prices, as the impact of the reduction in mobile phone communication fees fades. In addition, the expected inflation rate is rising, particularly in the short term. Looking ahead at the economy of our country, it is expected to recover, supported by an increase in external demand, an accommodative financial environment, and the effects of government economic measures, despite downward pressure from rising resource prices due to the situation in Ukraine and the easing of the impacts of COVID-19 and supply constraints. After that, as the negative effects of high resource prices diminish and the positive circulation mechanism from income to expenditure gradually strengthens, it is expected that the economy of our country will continue to grow above its potential growth rate, albeit at a slower pace. The year-on-year consumer price index (excluding fresh food) is expected to remain around 2% for the time being due to the impact of rising energy and food prices, but is expected to narrow its positive range as the contribution of rising energy prices diminishes thereafter. During this period, the year-on-year consumer price index (excluding fresh food and energy) is expected to gradually expand its positive range due to improvements in the macroeconomic supply-demand gap, as well as rising medium- to long-term expected inflation and wage growth rates, along with the pass-through of rising raw material costs. Looking at risk factors, uncertainty surrounding the economy of our country remains extremely high due to the ongoing trends and impacts of domestic and foreign infections, developments in the situation in Ukraine, resource prices, and trends in the overseas economy. In this context, it is necessary to closely monitor the trends in financial and foreign exchange markets and their impact on our country's economy and prices. For the time being, we will closely monitor the impact of COVID-19, strive to support corporate funding and maintain stability in financial markets, and if necessary, we will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. Regarding the policy interest rate, it is assumed that it will remain at the current level of short- and long-term interest rates or below that level. In favor: Members Kuroda, Amamiya, Wakabayashi, Suzuki, Adachi, Nakamura, Noguchi, Nakagawa. Opposed: Member Kataoka. Member Kataoka opposed, stating that it would be desirable to strengthen monetary easing by lowering short- and long-term interest rates to support positive capital investment by companies in the post-COVID era.
