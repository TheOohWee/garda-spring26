Short-term interest rate: A negative interest rate of -0.1% will be applied. Long-term interest rate: To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, necessary amounts of long-term government bonds will be purchased without setting an upper limit. The upper limit for long-term interest rates is set at around 1.0%, and to promote the formation of a yield curve consistent with the above monetary market adjustment policy, large-scale government bond purchases will continue, and flexible measures such as increasing purchase amounts, fixed-rate operations, and common collateral fund supply operations will be implemented for each maturity. Regarding the purchase of assets other than long-term government bonds, the following will apply. For ETFs and J-REITs, purchases will be made as needed, with an upper limit on the pace of balance increase of approximately 12 trillion yen per year for ETFs and approximately 180 billion yen per year for J-REITs. Commercial paper (CP) will be maintained at a balance of approximately 2 trillion yen. Corporate bonds will be purchased at a pace similar to that before the spread of the infection, gradually returning the purchase balance to the level before the spread of the infection (approximately 3 trillion yen). To achieve the 'price stability target' and to sustain it stably until necessary, 'quantitative and qualitative monetary easing with yield curve control' will be continued. Regarding the monetary base, the expansion policy will continue until the year-on-year increase rate of the consumer price index (excluding fresh food) stabilizes above 2%. Efforts will continue to maintain the funding of businesses and the stability of financial markets, and if necessary, additional monetary easing measures will be taken without hesitation.
