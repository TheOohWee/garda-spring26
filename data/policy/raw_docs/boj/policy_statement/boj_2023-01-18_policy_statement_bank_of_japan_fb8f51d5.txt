Short-term interest rate: A negative interest rate of -0.1% will be applied. Long-term interest rate: To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, purchases of long-term government bonds will be made without setting an upper limit on the necessary amount. The fluctuation range of long-term interest rates will be set at around ±0.5%, and limit operations at a yield of 0.5% for 10-year government bonds will be conducted every business day, except in cases where bids are clearly not expected. Large-scale government bond purchases will continue, and flexibly increase the purchase amounts and limit operations will be implemented for each maturity. Regarding the purchase of assets other than long-term government bonds, the following will apply. For ETFs and J-REITs, purchases will be made as needed, with an upper limit of approximately 12 trillion yen per year for ETFs and approximately 180 billion yen per year for J-REITs. For CPs and corporate bonds, purchases will be made at a pace similar to that before the spread of the infection, gradually returning the purchase balance to pre-infection levels (approximately 2 trillion yen for CPs and approximately 3 trillion yen for corporate bonds). However, adjustments to the purchase balance of corporate bonds will be made with sufficient consideration of the issuance environment for corporate bonds. For the time being, we will closely monitor the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, strive to support the funding of businesses and maintain stability in financial markets, and if necessary, will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. Regarding the policy interest rate, it is assumed that it will remain at the current level of short- and long-term interest rates or below that level.
