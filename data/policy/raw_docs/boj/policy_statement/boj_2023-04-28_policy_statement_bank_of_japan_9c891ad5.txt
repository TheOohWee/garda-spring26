Short-term interest rate: Apply a negative interest rate of -0.1%. Long-term interest rate: To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, we will conduct purchases of long-term government bonds without setting an upper limit on the necessary amount. The fluctuation range of long-term interest rates will be set at around ±0.5%, and we will conduct fixed-rate operations at a yield of 0.5% for 10-year government bonds on every business day, except in cases where bids are clearly not expected. We will continue large-scale government bond purchases and flexibly implement increases in purchase amounts and fixed-rate operations for each maturity. Regarding the purchase of assets other than long-term government bonds, the following will apply. For ETFs and J-REITs, we will conduct purchases as needed, with an upper limit on the pace of balance increase of approximately 12 trillion yen per year for ETFs and approximately 180 billion yen per year for J-REITs. We will maintain a balance of approximately 2 trillion yen for CP and other assets. We will purchase corporate bonds at a pace similar to that before the pandemic and gradually return the purchase balance to the pre-pandemic level (approximately 3 trillion yen). In order to achieve the 'price stability target' and to sustain it stably, we will continue 'quantitative and qualitative monetary easing with yield curve control' until it is deemed necessary. Regarding the monetary base, we will continue the expansion policy until the year-on-year increase rate of the consumer price index (excluding fresh food) consistently exceeds 2%. We will continue to strive to maintain the funding of businesses and the stability of financial markets, and if necessary, we will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. Since the late 1990s, when our economy fell into deflation, achieving 'price stability' has been a challenge for 25 years. During this time, various monetary easing measures have been implemented. These monetary easing measures have been interrelated and have influenced a wide range of areas in our economy, prices, and finance. In light of this, we have decided to conduct a multifaceted review of monetary policy operations over a period of about one to one and a half years.
