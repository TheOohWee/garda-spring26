Short-term interest rate: Apply a negative interest rate of -0.1%. Long-term interest rate: To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, we will conduct purchases of long-term government bonds without setting an upper limit on the amount. The upper limit for long-term interest rates is set at around 1.0%, and to promote the formation of a yield curve consistent with the above monetary market adjustment policy, we will continue large-scale government bond purchases and flexibly implement increases in purchase amounts, fixed-rate operations, and common collateral funding operations for each maturity. Regarding the purchase of assets other than long-term government bonds, the following will apply. For ETFs and J-REITs, purchases will be made as needed, with an upper limit on the pace of balance increase set at approximately 12 trillion yen per year for ETFs and approximately 180 billion yen per year for J-REITs. Commercial paper (CP) will be maintained at a balance of approximately 2 trillion yen. Corporate bonds will be purchased at a pace similar to that before the pandemic, gradually returning the purchase balance to the pre-pandemic level (approximately 3 trillion yen). However, adjustments to the purchase balance of corporate bonds will be made with sufficient consideration of the issuance environment for corporate bonds. Furthermore, given the extremely high uncertainty surrounding domestic and international economic and financial markets, it is appropriate to enhance flexibility in the operation of the yield curve control to ensure smooth long-term interest rate formation in response to future changes in the situation. In this regard, given the current situation, it has been determined that implementing continuous fixed-rate operations at a yield of 1.0% every business day and strictly capping long-term interest rates may have significant side effects despite its strong effects, and therefore, interest rate operations will focus on large-scale government bond purchases and flexible operational management. In order to achieve the 'price stability target' and to sustain it stably until the necessary point, we will continue 'quantitative and qualitative monetary easing with yield curve control.' Regarding the monetary base, the expansion policy will continue until the year-on-year increase in the consumer price index (excluding fresh food) stabilizes above 2%. We will continue to strive to maintain the funding of businesses and the stability of financial markets, and if necessary, we will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. In favor: Members Ueda, Himino, Uchida, Adachi, Noguchi, Nakagawa, Takada, Tamura. Opposed: Member Nakamura. Member Nakamura expressed support for further flexibility in the operation of yield curve control but opposed it, stating that it would be preferable to confirm the improvement in corporate earnings capacity based on corporate statistics before proceeding.
