The economy of our country is gradually recovering, although some weak movements are observed. The overseas economy is generally growing slowly. With the improvement in corporate profits, capital investment is showing a gradual increasing trend. The employment and income environment is gradually improving. Personal consumption is steady, although it is being pressured by rising prices and the suspension of shipments by some manufacturers affecting automobile sales. In terms of prices, looking at the year-on-year change in consumer prices (excluding fresh food), the impact of price pass-through from previous import price increases is diminishing, but service prices are continuing to rise gradually due to wage increases, with the current level being in the low 2% range. Looking ahead at the economy of our country, it is expected to continue growing above the potential growth rate, supported by a gradually strengthening positive circulation mechanism from income to expenditure, against the backdrop of a mildly growing overseas economy and an accommodative financial environment. Looking at risk factors, uncertainties surrounding the economy and prices in our country remain high, including trends in overseas economic and price movements, resource prices, and corporate wage and price-setting behavior.
