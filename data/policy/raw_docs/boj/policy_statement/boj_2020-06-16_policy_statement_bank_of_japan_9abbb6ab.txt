To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, we will conduct purchases of long-term government bonds without setting an upper limit on the necessary amount. In doing so, the interest rate may fluctuate to some extent depending on economic and price conditions. We will continue to support corporate financing and maintain stability in the financial markets through (1) the special program for supporting funding related to COVID-19, (2) ample supply of yen and foreign currency without setting upper limits through government bond purchases and dollar operations, and (3) active purchases of ETFs and J-REITs. We will closely monitor the impact of COVID-19 and, if necessary, will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. Regarding the policy interest rate, we assume that it will remain at the current level of short- and long-term interest rates or below. In the event of a rapid rise in interest rates, we will promptly and appropriately conduct government bond purchases. Member Kataoka opposed, stating that it would be desirable to strengthen monetary easing by lowering short- and long-term interest rates in response to the increasing downward pressure on prices and to alleviate the interest burden on businesses and households.
