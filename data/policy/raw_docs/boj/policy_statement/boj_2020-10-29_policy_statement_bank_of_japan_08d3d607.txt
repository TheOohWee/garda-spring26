To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, we will conduct purchases of long-term government bonds without setting an upper limit on the necessary amount. In doing so, the interest rate may fluctuate to some extent depending on economic and price conditions. We will continue to support corporate financing and maintain stability in financial markets through (1) the special program for funding support in response to COVID-19, (2) ample supply of yen and foreign currency without setting upper limits through government bond purchases and dollar operations, and (3) active purchases of ETFs and J-REITs. For the time being, we will closely monitor the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic and, if necessary, will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. In the event of a rapid rise in interest rates, we will promptly and appropriately conduct government bond purchases. Member Kataoka opposed, stating that it would be desirable to strengthen monetary easing by lowering short- and long-term interest rates to address the increasing downward pressure on prices and to alleviate the interest burden on businesses and households.
