The special program for funding support in response to COVID-19 will be extended for six months until the end of March 2022. To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, we will conduct purchases of long-term government bonds without setting an upper limit. Regarding ETFs and J-REITs, we will conduct purchases as needed, with an upper limit of approximately 12 trillion yen and 180 billion yen in annual balance increase pace, respectively. The economy of our country continues to be in a difficult situation due to the impact of COVID-19 both domestically and internationally, but the underlying trend is recovering. We will continue to support corporate funding and maintain stability in the financial markets through (1) the special program for funding support in response to COVID-19, (2) abundant supply of yen and foreign currency without setting upper limits through government bond purchases and dollar operations, and (3) purchases of ETFs and J-REITs with annual increase pace limits of approximately 12 trillion yen and 180 billion yen, respectively. We will continue to monitor the impact of COVID-19 and, if necessary, will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. Regarding the policy interest rate, we assume that it will remain at the current level of short- and long-term interest rates or lower. Member Kataoka opposed the decision, stating that it would be desirable to strengthen monetary easing by lowering short- and long-term interest rates to support positive capital investment by companies in the post-COVID era.
