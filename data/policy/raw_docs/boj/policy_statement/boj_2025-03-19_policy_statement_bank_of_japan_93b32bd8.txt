In terms of prices, looking at the year-on-year change in consumer prices (excluding fresh food), the impact of price pass-through from previous import price increases has diminished, but with a gradual rise in service prices due to wage increases, and the reduction of government energy burden relief measures, the current level is in the low 3% range. Looking ahead at the Japanese economy, it is expected to continue growing above the potential growth rate, supported by a gradually strengthening positive circulation mechanism from income to expenditure, against the backdrop of a relaxed financial environment and moderate growth in the overseas economy. Looking at risk factors, uncertainties surrounding the Japanese economy and prices remain high due to movements in trade policies of various countries, the impact on overseas economic and price trends, resource price movements, and corporate wage and price-setting behavior.
