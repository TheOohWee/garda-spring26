Short-term interest rate: A negative interest rate of -0.1% will be applied. Long-term interest rate: To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, necessary amounts of long-term government bonds will be purchased without setting an upper limit. To implement the above monetary market adjustment policy, limit order operations will be conducted every business day at a yield of 0.25% for 10-year government bonds, except in cases where bids are clearly not expected. Regarding the purchase of assets other than long-term government bonds, the following will apply. For ETFs and J-REITs, purchases will be made as needed, with an upper limit on the pace of balance increase equivalent to approximately 12 trillion yen per year for ETFs and approximately 180 billion yen per year for J-REITs. For CPs and corporate bonds, purchases will be made at a pace similar to that before the spread of the infection, gradually returning the purchase balance to pre-infection levels (approximately 2 trillion yen for CPs and approximately 3 trillion yen for corporate bonds). For the time being, we will closely monitor the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, strive to support corporate financing and maintain stability in financial markets, and if necessary, will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. Regarding the policy interest rate, it is assumed that it will remain at the current level of short- and long-term interest rates or below. In favor: Members Kuroda, Amamiya, Wakabayashi, Suzuki, Adachi, Nakamura, Noguchi, Nakagawa. Opposed: Member Kataoka. Member Kataoka opposed, stating that it would be desirable to strengthen monetary easing by lowering short- and long-term interest rates to support positive corporate investment in the post-COVID era.
