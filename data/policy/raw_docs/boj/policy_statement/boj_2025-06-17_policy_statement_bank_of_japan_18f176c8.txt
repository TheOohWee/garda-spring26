The Bank will encourage the uncollateralized overnight call rate to remain at around 0.5%. It was decided to reduce the planned monthly purchases of long-term government bonds by approximately 400 billion yen each quarter until January 13, 2026, and by approximately 200 billion yen each quarter after April 6, 2026, aiming for about 2 trillion yen by January 13, 2027. The economy is gradually recovering, although some weak movements are observed. The overseas economy is showing some weak movements due to the influence of trade policies in various countries, but overall it is growing gradually. With corporate profits improving, capital investment is showing a gradual increasing trend. Personal consumption is maintaining a gradual increasing trend, despite some weakness in consumer sentiment due to the impact of rising prices, supported by improvements in the employment and income environment. Housing investment is showing weak movements. Public investment is moving within a flat range. The financial environment in our country is in a relaxed state. In terms of prices, looking at the year-on-year change in consumer prices (excluding fresh food), it is currently in the mid-3% range due to the continued pass-through of wage increases to selling prices, along with the impact of past increases in import prices and rising food prices such as rice. The expected inflation rate is gradually rising. Looking ahead at the Japanese economy, it is expected that the overseas economy will slow down due to the influence of trade policies in various countries, which will also put pressure on corporate profits in Japan, although the accommodative financial environment will act as a supporting factor, leading to a slowdown in the growth pace. After that, it is expected that the overseas economy will return to a gradual growth path, leading to an increase in the growth rate. Regarding consumer prices (excluding fresh food), it is expected that the impact of past increases in import prices and recent rises in food prices such as rice, which have pushed up the inflation rate, will diminish. During this period, while the underlying inflation rate for consumer prices is expected to stagnate due to the slowdown in growth pace, it is anticipated that as the growth rate increases and labor shortages become more pronounced, the medium- to long-term expected inflation rate will gradually rise, aligning with the 'price stability target' during the latter half of the forecast period in the 'Outlook Report'. There are various risk factors, but particularly, the uncertainty surrounding the future developments of trade policies in various countries and their impact on overseas economic and price trends is extremely high, and it is necessary to closely monitor their effects on the financial and foreign exchange markets as well as on the Japanese economy and prices. In favor: Members Ueda, Himino, Uchida, Nakamura, Noguchi, Nakagawa, Takada, Koeda. Against: Member Tamura. Member Tamura proposed that the formation of long-term interest rates should be left to the market and market participants, suggesting to reduce the planned monthly purchases by approximately 400 billion yen each quarter until January 13, 2027, which was rejected by a majority vote. In addition to this decision, measures will be taken to expand the eligible securities for easing the requirements for the reduction measures related to supplementary supply in the government bond market to improve liquidity. Regarding the plan for reducing long-term government bond purchases. The planned monthly purchases of long-term government bonds will be reduced by approximately 400 billion yen each quarter until January 13, 2026, and by approximately 200 billion yen each quarter after April 6, 2026, aiming for about 2 trillion yen by January 13, 2027. In the June 2026 Monetary Policy Meeting, a mid-term evaluation of the plan to reduce long-term government bond purchases will be conducted. In the mid-term evaluation, the basic principle will be to maintain the current reduction plan, but if deemed necessary after examining the trends and functionality of the government bond market, adjustments to the plan will be made as appropriate. At the same time, the policy for purchasing long-term government bonds after April 2027 will be examined, and the results will be presented. In the event of a rapid rise in long-term interest rates, regardless of the planned monthly purchase amount, flexible measures such as increasing the purchase amount, conducting fixed-rate operations, and providing common collateral funding operations will be implemented. If necessary, the reduction plan may also be reviewed in the Monetary Policy Meeting.
