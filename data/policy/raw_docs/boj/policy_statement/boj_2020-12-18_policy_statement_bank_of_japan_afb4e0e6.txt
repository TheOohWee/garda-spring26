The deadline for the increased purchases of CP and corporate bonds will be extended by six months until the end of September 2021. The deadline for the special operations for financial support in response to COVID-19 will be extended by six months until the end of September 2021. To ensure that the 10-year government bond yield remains around 0%, purchases of long-term government bonds will be made without setting an upper limit on the necessary amount. For the time being, active purchases will be made with an upper limit on the pace of balance increase equivalent to approximately 12 trillion yen per year for ETFs and approximately 180 billion yen per year for J-REITs. In the event of a rapid rise in interest rates, government bond purchases will be conducted swiftly and appropriately. We will closely monitor the impact of COVID-19 and, if necessary, will not hesitate to implement additional monetary easing measures. In terms of prices, the year-on-year change in consumer prices (excluding fresh food) is negative due to the impact of the pandemic, previous declines in oil prices, and the Go To Travel campaign. As a risk factor, there is a high degree of uncertainty regarding the outcome of the COVID-19 pandemic and its impact on the domestic and global economy. In order to support the economy and achieve the 2% 'price stability target', we have decided to review the implementation of more effective and sustainable monetary easing. Under this framework, we will review various measures and aim to publish the results by the monetary policy meeting in March next year.
